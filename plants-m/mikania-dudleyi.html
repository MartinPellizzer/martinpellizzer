Mikania dudleyi

Mikania dudleyi, commonly known as Dudley's mile-a-minute, is a fast-growing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth rate, often spreading aggressively through runners and seeds. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its invasive nature, it can outcompete native plants and is considered a weed in many regions.
