Macronyx

Macronyx, commonly known as the giant poinsettia, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The true flowers of the plant are small and clustered in a structure called a cyathium. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its vibrant foliage and festive appearance make it a popular choice for holiday decorations.
