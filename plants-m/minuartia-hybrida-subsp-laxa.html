Minuartia hybrida subsp. laxa

Minuartia hybrida subsp.

laxa, commonly known as the laxa sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in rocky or sandy soils. It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and delicate white or pale pink flowers.

Native to regions such as southern Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in alpine or arctic environments. The plant has a compact structure with hairy stems and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and other xerophytic habitats.
