Myrcia breviramis

Myrcia breviramis, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged opposite each other on the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Myrcia breviramis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
