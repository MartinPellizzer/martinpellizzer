Marsdenia clausa var. bifida

Marsdenia clausa var. bifida, commonly known as the bifid Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its latex contains milky sap, which is a common feature among plants in this family.

The varietal name "bifida" refers to the split or divided appearance of its leaves, a key identifying trait.
