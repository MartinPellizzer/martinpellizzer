Monanthes × intermedia

Monanthes × intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid spider lily, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Monanthes flava and Monanthes arundinacea.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often white or pale yellow, spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are broad, strap-like, and emerge from a bulbous base, contributing to its unique aesthetic appeal.
