Mesembryanthemum lavisii

Mesembryanthemum lavisii, commonly known as the silver leaf mesem, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often drought-resistant species. The plant features fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
