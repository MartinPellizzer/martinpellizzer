Maurandya scandens

Maurandya scandens, commonly known as the climbing mock orange, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Maurandya scandens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.
