Myroxylon tweedyanum

Myroxylon tweedyanum, commonly known as the wild peach or Brazilian wild peach, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, simple leaves that are alternate and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by woody, oval pods containing flat, edible seeds. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is durable and used in various crafts and construction.
