Micropuntia tuberculosirhopalica

Micropuntia tuberculosirhopalica, commonly known as the tuberculate cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, sprawling cactus with a unique appearance characterized by its tuberculate (bumpy) stems and clusters of small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and adapting to harsh desert conditions.

The cactus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
