Muscarella cestrochila

Muscarella cestrochila, commonly known as the white muscari, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Muscarella cestrochila is known for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
