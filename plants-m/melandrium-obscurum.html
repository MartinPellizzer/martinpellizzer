Melandrium obscurum

Melandrium obscurum, commonly known as the dark pink campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Melandrium obscurum is also known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a versatile wildflower in various ecological settings.
