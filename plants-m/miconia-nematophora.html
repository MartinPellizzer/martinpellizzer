Miconia nematophora

Miconia nematophora, commonly known as the hairy miconia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. Miconia nematophora is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
