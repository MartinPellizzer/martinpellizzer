Macaranga saccifera var. dentifera

Macaranga saccifera var. dentifera, commonly known as the serrated macaranga, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough texture.

It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.
