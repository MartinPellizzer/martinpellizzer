Melica brasiliana

Melica brasiliana, commonly known as Brazilian millet, is a species of grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Melica brasiliana is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
