Marginaria pyrrholepis

Marginaria pyrrholepis, commonly known as the red-spined marginaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, adding visual interest to gardens. Marginaria pyrrholepis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and long blooming period.
