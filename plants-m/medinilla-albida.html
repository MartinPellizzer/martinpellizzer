Medinilla albida

Medinilla albida, commonly known as the white medinilla, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink blossoms that are often adorned with a waxy coating, enhancing their ornamental appeal. Medinilla albida thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and as a decorative plant in gardens.
