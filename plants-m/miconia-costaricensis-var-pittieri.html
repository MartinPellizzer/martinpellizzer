Miconia costaricensis var. pittieri

Miconia costaricensis var. pittieri, commonly known as the Pittier's miconia, is a species native to Central America, particularly found in the tropical regions of Costa Rica and Panama.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded forest understories. The plant has large, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside, often featuring prominent veins. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale pink, while the fruit is a bright red drupe that attracts various bird species.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
