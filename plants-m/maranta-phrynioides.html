Maranta phrynioides

Maranta phrynioides, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically borne in clusters, and it is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

Maranta phrynioides is also valued as an ornamental plant for its foliage and ease of care.
