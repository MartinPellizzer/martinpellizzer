Musa serpentina

Musa serpentina, commonly known as the wild banana, is a species of flowering plant in the Musaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It is often grown for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

However, it is less commonly cultivated for fruit production compared to other banana species.
