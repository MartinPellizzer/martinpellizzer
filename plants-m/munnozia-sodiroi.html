Munnozia sodiroi

Munnozia sodiroi, commonly known as the Sierra foothill mallow, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Munnozia sodiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
