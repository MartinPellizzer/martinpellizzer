Myosotis × parviflora

Myosotis × parviflora, commonly known as the hybrid forget-me-not, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Myosotis × parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
