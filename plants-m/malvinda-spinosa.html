Malvinda spinosa

Malvinda spinosa, commonly known as the spiny mallows, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Malvinda spinosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
