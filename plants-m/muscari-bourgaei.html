Muscari bourgaei

Muscari bourgaei, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a robust root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Muscari bourgaei is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
