Mimosa pogocephala

Mimosa pogocephala, commonly known as the woolly mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy seed pods. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
