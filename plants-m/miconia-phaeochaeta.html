Miconia phaeochaeta

Miconia phaeochaeta, commonly known as the black-leaved miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
