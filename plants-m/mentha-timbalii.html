Mentha timbalii

Mentha timbalii, commonly known as the Timbalii mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or white, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty fragrance, which is due to the presence of essential oils.

Mentha timbalii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
