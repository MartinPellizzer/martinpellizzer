Medicago lupulina f. corymbosa

Medicago lupulina f. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bursch vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
