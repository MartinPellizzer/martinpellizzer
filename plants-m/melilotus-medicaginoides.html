Melilotus medicaginoides

Melilotus medicaginoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf melilot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Melilotus medicaginoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
