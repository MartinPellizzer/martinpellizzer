Miconia garcia-barrigae

Miconia garcia-barrigae, commonly known as the giant miconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, often with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Miconia garcia-barrigae is also notable for its ability to grow rapidly, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a subject of conservation interest.
