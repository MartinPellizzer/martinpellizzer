Mallotus millietii var. atrichus

Mallotus millietii var. atrichus, commonly known as the glossy ash-leaf tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, dark green, leathery leaves that are often glossy and have a distinctive ash-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is typically found in lowland tropical forests and thrives in humid, warm climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
