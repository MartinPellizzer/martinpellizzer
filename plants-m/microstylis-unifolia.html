Microstylis unifolia

Microstylis unifolia, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its botanical structure is adapted to its wetland environment, with rhizomes that help it spread and establish dense stands.

Microstylis unifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
