Monachyron grandiflorum

Monachyron grandiflorum, commonly known as the grandiflorous monk's herb, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of violet and blue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
