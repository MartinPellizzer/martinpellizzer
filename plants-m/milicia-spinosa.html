Milicia spinosa

Milicia spinosa, commonly known as the African teak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, bark-like trunk and large, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, edible fruits containing seeds. Milicia spinosa is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
