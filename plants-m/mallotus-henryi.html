Mallotus henryi

Mallotus henryi, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus or Henry's mallot, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is also known for its ornamental value, with its vibrant foliage making it a popular choice in horticulture.

Mallotus henryi is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
