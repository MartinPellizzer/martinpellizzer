Mosannona papillosa

Mosannona papillosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture, which gives the plant its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fruit that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas.
