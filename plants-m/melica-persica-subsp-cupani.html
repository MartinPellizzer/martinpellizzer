Melica persica subsp. cupani

Melica persica subsp.

cupani, commonly known as Persian milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. Its flowering period usually occurs in the summer months, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
