Malaxis longibracteata

Malaxis longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract malaxis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, with a distinctive elongated bract that gives it its common name.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
