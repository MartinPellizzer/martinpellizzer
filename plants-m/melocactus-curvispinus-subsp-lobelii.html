Melocactus curvispinus subsp. lobelii

Melocactus curvispinus subsp.

lobelii, commonly known as the Lobel's melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stem that typically grows in a spherical or cylindrical shape.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming during the warmer months. Its spines are curved and often form a dense covering, providing protection from herbivores.

This subspecies is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, storing water in its stem and minimizing water loss through its waxy surface.
