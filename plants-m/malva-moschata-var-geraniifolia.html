Malva moschata var. geraniifolia

Malva moschata var. geraniifolia, commonly known as the scarlet mallows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive red or purple underside, and it produces striking pink to red flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments. Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
