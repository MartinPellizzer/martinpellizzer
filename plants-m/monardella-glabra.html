Monardella glabra

Monardella glabra, commonly known as narrowleaf monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Monardella glabra is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
