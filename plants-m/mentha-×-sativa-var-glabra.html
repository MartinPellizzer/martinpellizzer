Mentha × sativa var. glabra

Mentha × sativa var. glabra, commonly known as field mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and along stream banks. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive menthol scent and flavor.

This variety is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its refreshing properties.
