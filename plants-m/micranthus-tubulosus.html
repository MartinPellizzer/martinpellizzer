Micranthus tubulosus

Micranthus tubulosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grassvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Micranthus tubulosus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.
