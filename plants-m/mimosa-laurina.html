Mimosa laurina

Mimosa laurina, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its feathery leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Mimosa laurina is also known for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait shared with other mimosa species.
