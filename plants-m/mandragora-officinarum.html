Mandragora officinarum

Mandragora officinarum, commonly known as the mandrake, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and red or purple flowers. The plant contains tropane alkaloids, which have been used historically for their medicinal and hallucinogenic properties. Its root system is often shaped like a human figure, contributing to its mythological and folkloric significance.

Due to its toxic nature, it is not recommended for cultivation without proper knowledge and caution.
