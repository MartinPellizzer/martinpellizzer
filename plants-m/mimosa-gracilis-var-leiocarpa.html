Mimosa gracilis var. leiocarpa

Mimosa gracilis var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
