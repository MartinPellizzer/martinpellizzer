Musa × aphurica

Musa × aphurica, commonly known as the Chinese banana, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Musa acuminata and Musa balbisiana.

It is a herbaceous plant with large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing a starchy pulp surrounded by a fibrous layer. It is cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a sweet and slightly tangy flavor.

This species is adapted to warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
