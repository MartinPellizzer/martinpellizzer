Mulinum ovalleanum

Mulinum ovalleanum, commonly known as the ovalleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
