Meyera umbellata

Meyera umbellata, commonly known as the white umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Meyera umbellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
