Mutisia subulata var. rosea

Mutisia subulata var. rosea, commonly known as the roseate mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, rose-like inflorescences. The plant features silver-gray foliage with narrow, pointed leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
