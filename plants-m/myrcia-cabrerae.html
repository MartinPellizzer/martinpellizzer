Myrcia cabrerae

Myrcia cabrerae, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in dense forests.

Myrcia cabrerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
