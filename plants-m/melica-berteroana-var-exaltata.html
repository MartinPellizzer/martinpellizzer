Melica berteroana var. exaltata

Melica berteroana var. exaltata, commonly known as the tall meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its narrow, linear leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers.

This variant is distinguished by its elongated stature and is often found in wetland ecosystems where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
