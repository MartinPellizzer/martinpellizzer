Marila geminata

Marila geminata, commonly known as the twinleaf or geminate plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are often divided into two lobes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is found in parts of Europe and Asia. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are accompanied by bracts.

Marila geminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage.
