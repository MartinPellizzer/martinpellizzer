Myriocarpa malacophylla

Myriocarpa malacophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Myriocarpa malacophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
