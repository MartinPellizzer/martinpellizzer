Mariscus riparius

Mariscus riparius, commonly known as the river marsh mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas.

Mariscus riparius plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
