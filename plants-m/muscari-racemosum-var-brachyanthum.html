Muscari racemosum var. brachyanthum

Muscari racemosum var. brachyanthum, commonly known as the dwarf grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact size and delicate appearance make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental plant.
