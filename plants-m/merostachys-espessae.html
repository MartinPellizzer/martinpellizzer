Merostachys espessae

Merostachys espessae, commonly known as the dense spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of tightly packed spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Merostachys espessae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
