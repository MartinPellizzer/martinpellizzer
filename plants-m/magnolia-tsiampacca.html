Magnolia tsiampacca

Magnolia tsiampacca, commonly known as the Madagascar magnolia, is a deciduous shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
