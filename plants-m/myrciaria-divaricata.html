Myrciaria divaricata

Myrciaria divaricata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
