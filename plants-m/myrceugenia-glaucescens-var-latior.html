Myrceugenia glaucescens var. latior

Myrceugenia glaucescens var. latior, commonly known as the broadleaf myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, fragrant white flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
