Melastoma leonense

Melastoma leonense, commonly known as the Leonese melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually opposite and elliptical, with a glossy surface.

Melastoma leonense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
