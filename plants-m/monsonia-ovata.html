Monsonia ovata

Monsonia ovata, commonly known as the African snowdrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the winter months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique flowering habit, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
