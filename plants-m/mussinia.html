Mussinia

Mussinia, commonly known as the snow bell, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ericaceae.

These plants are native to the mountainous regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, where they thrive in cool, moist environments. Mussinia species are characterized by their bell-shaped flowers, which are typically white or pink, and their evergreen foliage. They are often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where they form dense, low-growing mats.

Due to their ornamental value, some species are cultivated in gardens, though they require well-drained soil and partial shade to flourish.
