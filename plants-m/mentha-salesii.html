Mentha salesii

Mentha salesii, commonly known as the Persian mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Mentha salesii is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its adaptability to dry and rocky soils makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.
