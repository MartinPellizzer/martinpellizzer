Merwilla lazulina

Merwilla lazulina, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a bushy, compact growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Merwilla lazulina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
