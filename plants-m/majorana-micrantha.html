Majorana micrantha

Majorana micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marjoram, is an aromatic herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are often used in cooking for their distinctive, spicy flavor. Majorana micrantha is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for both ornamental and culinary gardens.
