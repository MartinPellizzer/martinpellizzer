Myrtillocactus chiotilla f. spiralis

Myrtillocactus chiotilla f. spiralis, commonly known as the spiral spiraling cactus, is a rare and unique variant of the Myrtillocactus chiotilla species.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral growth pattern, where the stems twist in a helical manner as they grow. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the highlands of Mexico and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its natural range.
