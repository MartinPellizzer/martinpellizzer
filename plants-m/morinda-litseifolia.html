Morinda litseifolia

Morinda litseifolia, commonly known as the flame tree or red flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release small, hairy seeds.

Morinda litseifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
