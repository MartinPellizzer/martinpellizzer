Mentha × kerneri var. cinerascens

Mentha × kerneri var. cinerascens, commonly known as gray mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly grayish-green leaves and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

Its essential oils contain menthone and other compounds, contributing to its use in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.
