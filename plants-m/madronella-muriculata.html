Madronella muriculata

Madronella muriculata, commonly known as the roughleaf madrona, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
