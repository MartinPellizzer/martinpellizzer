Montanoa tomentosa var. microcephala

Montanoa tomentosa var. microcephala, commonly known as the woolly montanoa, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
