Marsdenia velutina

Marsdenia velutina, commonly known as the velvet milkvine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its smooth, velvety stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many plants with milky sap.

Marsdenia velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
