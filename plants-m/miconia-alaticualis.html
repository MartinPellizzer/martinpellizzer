Miconia alaticualis

Miconia alaticualis, commonly known as the winged miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive wing-like structures on the petioles. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other tropical regions, though it can become invasive in some areas.
