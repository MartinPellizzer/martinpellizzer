Malpighia rutilans

Malpighia rutilans, commonly known as the golden mirliton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically orange to red in color when mature. These berries are edible, though they have a tart, acidic flavor.

Malpighia rutilans is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habits.
