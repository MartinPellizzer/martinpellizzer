Mesembryanthemum vanrensburgii

Mesembryanthemum vanrensburgii, commonly known as the Vanrensburg's ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
