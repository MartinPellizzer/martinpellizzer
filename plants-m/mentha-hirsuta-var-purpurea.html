Mentha hirsuta var. purpurea

Mentha hirsuta var. purpurea, commonly known as purple hedge mint, is a variant of the woolly mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and distinctive purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a strong, minty aroma and is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
