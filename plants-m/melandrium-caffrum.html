Melandrium caffrum

Melandrium caffrum, commonly known as the African catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual with erect, branched stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Melandrium caffrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
