Mollugo sinaica

Mollugo sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai carpetweed, is a small, annual herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, thriving in dry, sunny environments. The plant has prostrate or ascending stems with opposite, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is known for its ability to withstand harsh desert conditions.

Mollugo sinaica is often used in traditional medicine and is a characteristic species of desert ecosystems.
