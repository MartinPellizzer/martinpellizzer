Malpighia diversifolia

Malpighia diversifolia, commonly known as the variable malpighia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, contributing to its ornamental value.
