Malus sieversii var. spherocarpa

Malus sieversii var. spherocarpa, commonly known as the Siberian crabapple, is a wild species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. It produces white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability.
