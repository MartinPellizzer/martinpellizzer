Microlonchus amberboides

Microlonchus amberboides, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Native to parts of Africa and the Middle East, this plant thrives in dry, sunny environments. The leaves are narrow and grassy, often with a slight waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
