Moraea virgata

Moraea virgata, commonly known as the silver leaf gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Moraea virgata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its adaptability to various climates.
