Myristica hypargyraea subsp. hypargyraea

Myristica hypargyraea subsp.

hypargyraea, commonly known as the silver nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and distinctive aromatic seeds.

The plant produces large, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing the valuable seeds. The seeds, which have a strong, spicy aroma, are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
