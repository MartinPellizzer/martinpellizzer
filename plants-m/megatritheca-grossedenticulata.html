Megatritheca grossedenticulata

Megatritheca grossedenticulata, commonly known as the giant toothed thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and showy purple or pink flower heads. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are covered in dense, stiff hairs, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bracts, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
