Malcolmia graeca subsp. bicolor

Malcolmia graeca subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the Persian crescent, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically feature a white base with a deep blue or purple upper lip.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
