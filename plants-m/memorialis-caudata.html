Memorialis caudata

Memorialis caudata, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that often trail or hang from containers, making it popular in hanging baskets. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. Its leaves are typically rounded and have a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, requiring regular watering during the growing season.
