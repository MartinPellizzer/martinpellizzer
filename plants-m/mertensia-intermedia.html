Mertensia intermedia

Mertensia intermedia, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia intermedia is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
