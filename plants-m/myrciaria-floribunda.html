Myrciaria floribunda

Myrciaria floribunda, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-mato, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible red berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Myrciaria floribunda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
