Millettia champutongensis

Millettia champutongensis, commonly known as the Thai ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also considered a species of conservation concern.
