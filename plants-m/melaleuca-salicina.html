Melaleuca salicina

Melaleuca salicina, commonly known as the slender tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that are typically arranged in spikes. Melaleuca salicina is often found in heathlands and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
