Myosotis ramosissima subsp. globularis

Myosotis ramosissima subsp.

globularis, commonly known as the globular bluebell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers.

The plant features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
