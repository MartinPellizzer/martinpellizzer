Mimulus nudatus

Mimulus nudatus, commonly known as the naked monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems and bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus nudatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
