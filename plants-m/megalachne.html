Megalachne

Megalachne, commonly known as the coastal panicgrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. Megalachne species are typically found in sandy or coastal environments, where they thrive in well-drained soils. These plants are often used for erosion control due to their extensive root systems.

They produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in panicles.
