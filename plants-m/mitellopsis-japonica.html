Mitellopsis japonica

Mitellopsis japonica, commonly known as Japanese mitella, is a perennial herb native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five sepals and numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Mitellopsis japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
