Mundia capensis

Mundia capensis, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branched.

Mundia capensis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
