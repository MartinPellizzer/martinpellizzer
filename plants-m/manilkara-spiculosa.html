Manilkara spiculosa

Manilkara spiculosa, commonly known as the spiny manilkara, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree bears smooth, dark brown fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
