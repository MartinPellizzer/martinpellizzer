Mammillaria meyranii var. michoacana

Mammillaria meyranii var. michoacana, commonly known as the Michoacan nipple cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a dense covering of white to pale yellow spines and has a compact, rounded stem that often develops a reddish hue when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a desirable specimen for cactus enthusiasts.
