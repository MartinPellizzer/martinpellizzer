Monotes hypoleucus var. angolensis

Monotes hypoleucus var. angolensis, commonly known as the Angola spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, typical of arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
