Medicago hispida f. apiculata

Medicago hispida f. apiculata, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in racemes, followed by oblong, hairy pods containing several seeds. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, often making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
