Matthiola integrifolia

Matthiola integrifolia, commonly known as wallflower, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its clustered, bright-colored flowers that range from white to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are borne on elongated stems and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Matthiola integrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
