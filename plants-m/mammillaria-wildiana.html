Mammillaria wildiana

Mammillaria wildiana, commonly known as the wildian hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular shape and a gray-green color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
