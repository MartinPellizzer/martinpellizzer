Melocactus euryacanthus

Melocactus euryacanthus, commonly known as the red-hair melocactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in dense, bright red spines that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, reddish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

The species is often cultivated for its striking appearance and resilience in harsh conditions.
