Macaranga longistipulata

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with long stipules. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered in catkins, and it produces capsules as its fruit.

The species is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical environments.
