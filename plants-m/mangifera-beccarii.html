Mangifera beccarii

Mangifera beccarii, commonly known as the wild mango or beccari mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The fruit is typically small, with a thick, fibrous pulp and a distinctive aroma. It is often found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, warm climates.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
