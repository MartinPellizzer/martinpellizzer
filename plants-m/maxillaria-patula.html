Maxillaria patula

Maxillaria patula, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its pseudobulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria patula is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
