Melica exaltata

Melica exaltata, commonly known as tall wheatgrass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as fields and roadsides.

Melica exaltata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
