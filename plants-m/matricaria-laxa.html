Matricaria laxa

Matricaria laxa, commonly known as slender chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers and finely divided, aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
