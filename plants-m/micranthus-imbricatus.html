Micranthus imbricatus

Micranthus imbricatus, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Micranthus imbricatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
