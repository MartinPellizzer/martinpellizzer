Masdevallia fragrans

Masdevallia fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that emit a sweet, spicy fragrance, especially in the evening. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The blooming period of Masdevallia fragrans is usually during the cooler months, making it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
