Morgania hyssopioides

Morgania hyssopioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a square stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
