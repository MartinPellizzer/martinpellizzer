Miconia magnipetala

Miconia magnipetala, commonly known as the giant miconia, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to the humid lowland forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, often with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are bright red berries that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal by birds and mammals.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions where it is introduced.
