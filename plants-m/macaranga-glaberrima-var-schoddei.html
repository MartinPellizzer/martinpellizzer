Macaranga glaberrima var. schoddei

Macaranga glaberrima var. schoddei, commonly known as the Schodde's nettle, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves, which can be heart-shaped to ovate in form. The flowers are small and clustered in complex inflorescences, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.
