Mentha aquatica var. stenomacra

Mentha aquatica var. stenomacra, commonly known as narrow-leaved water mint, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found along streams, ponds, and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong menthol scent, while its stems are square and hairy.

This variety is closely related to other mint species and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
