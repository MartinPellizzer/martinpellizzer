Mayepea axilliflora

Mayepea axilliflora, commonly known as the mountain mayepea, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Mayepea axilliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
