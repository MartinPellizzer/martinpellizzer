Maytenus cassineformis var. loeseneriana

Maytenus cassineformis var. loeseneriana, commonly known as the Loesener's mayten, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it is often found in dry to moist tropical forests.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
