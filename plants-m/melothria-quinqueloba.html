Melothria quinqueloba

Melothria quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed melon or the African sour melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a trailing vine that produces small, yellow, edible fruits with a tart flavor. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often cultivated for its fruit. Its leaves are heart-shaped with five distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
