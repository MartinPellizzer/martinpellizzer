Mitracarpus hirtus var. remotiflorus

Mitracarpus hirtus var. remotiflorus, commonly known as the remote-flowered hirtus, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
