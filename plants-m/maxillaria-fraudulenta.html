Maxillaria fraudulenta

Maxillaria fraudulenta, commonly known as the false orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, featuring pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Maxillaria fraudulenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm climates.
