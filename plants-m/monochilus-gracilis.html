Monochilus gracilis

Monochilus gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Monochilus gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
