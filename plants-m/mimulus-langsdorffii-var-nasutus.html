Mimulus langsdorffii var. nasutus

Mimulus langsdorffii var. nasutus, commonly known as the long-beaked monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has oval, slightly hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and wet meadows.

Its distinctive elongated flower tube makes it a favorite among pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
