Machaerophorus matthioloides

Machaerophorus matthioloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent stems, which store water. Its leaves are reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

This cactus plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
