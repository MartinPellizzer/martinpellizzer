Mentha lata

Mentha lata, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves and strong, minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Mentha lata contains essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Its flowering stems bear small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer.
