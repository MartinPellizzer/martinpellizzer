Millettia calabarica

Millettia calabarica, commonly known as the African tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
