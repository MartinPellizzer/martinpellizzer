Muscari longipes subsp. longipes

Muscari longipes subsp.

longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed grape hyacinth, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling grapes, hence its common name. It is a perennial bulbous plant that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies longipes is distinguished by its elongated stems and is particularly valued for its early spring blooms.
