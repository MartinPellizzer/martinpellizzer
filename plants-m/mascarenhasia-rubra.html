Mascarenhasia rubra

Mascarenhasia rubra, commonly known as the red Mascarenhasia, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators. The plant typically grows as a shrub, with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to the island's climate and its role in local ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
