Malpighia punicifolia

Malpighia punicifolia, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its striking red-orange berries that persist on the plant throughout the winter. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are typically alternate and oval in shape. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant fruits and evergreen foliage.

The berries are edible but can be quite tart, and they are sometimes used in culinary or medicinal applications.
