Maytenus ferdinandi

Maytenus ferdinandi, commonly known as the Ferdinand's mayten, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and resilience to urban conditions.
