Myrcia costata

Myrcia costata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia costata is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and for their pleasant scent.
