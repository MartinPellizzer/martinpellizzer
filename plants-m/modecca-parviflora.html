Modecca parviflora

Modecca parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered modecca, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Modecca parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
