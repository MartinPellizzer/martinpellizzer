Monnina macrosepala var. latifolia

Monnina macrosepala var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf monnina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its botanical features include hairy foliage and a distinctive calyx that persists after flowering.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
