Melochia parvifolia f. roseiflora

Melochia parvifolia f. roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered melochia, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue. The plant produces clusters of delicate, rose-like flowers with five petals, which bloom in shades of pink to white. These flowers are borne on short, erect pedicels and are often arranged in dense inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
