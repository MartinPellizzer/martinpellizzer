Mussaenda monticola var. glabrescens

Mussaenda monticola var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaved Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy. The plant produces clusters of yellow bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. Its inflorescences are terminal and consist of numerous small white to yellow flowers surrounded by showy bracts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
