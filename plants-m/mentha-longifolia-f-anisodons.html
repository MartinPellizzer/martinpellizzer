Mentha longifolia f. anisodons

Mentha longifolia f. anisodons, commonly known as the anise-scented mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong, licorice-like fragrance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its distinctive flavor and aromatic properties.
