Mimulus cupreus f. roezlii

Mimulus cupreus f. roezlii, commonly known as the copper monkeyflower, is a strikingly colorful variant of the monkeyflower species.

It is characterized by its vibrant coppery-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. This plant is native to the western United States, particularly in California, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that is well-suited for rock gardens and dry, sunny environments.
