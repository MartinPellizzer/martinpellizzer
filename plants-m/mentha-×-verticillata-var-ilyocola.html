Mentha × verticillata var. ilyocola

Mentha × verticillata var. ilyocola, commonly known as the Ilyocola water mint, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and aromatic leaves that emit a strong mint scent. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and small, tubular flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream sides and wet meadows.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
