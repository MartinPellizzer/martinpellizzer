Milium vernale subsp. montianum

Milium vernale subsp.

montianum, commonly known as the mountain millet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, rocky or grassy habitats, often at higher elevations.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, white to pale green flowers. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and related plants.
