Myrciaria tolypantha

Myrciaria tolypantha, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are white and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine due to its nutritional and therapeutic properties.
