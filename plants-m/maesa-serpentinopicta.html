Maesa serpentinopicta

Maesa serpentinopicta, commonly known as the serpentine picta maesa, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique leaf patterns, which often display intricate, almost pictorial designs in shades of green and silver. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils, particularly on serpentine rock formations, which influence its growth and appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale pink, and it blooms during the wet season.

Due to its limited habitat and specific ecological requirements, Maesa serpentinopicta is considered a sensitive indicator species of serpentine ecosystems.
