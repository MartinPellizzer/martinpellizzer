Mangifera indica var. kaijer

Mangifera indica var. kaijer, commonly known as the Kaijer mango, is a variety of the common mango tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a thick, golden-yellow skin that develops a red blush when ripe. The flesh is typically sweet and fibrous, with a high sugar content and a mild, aromatic flavor. This variety is well-suited to tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

It is often cultivated for both commercial production and home gardening due to its adaptability and delicious fruit.
