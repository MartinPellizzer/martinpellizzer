Myosotis lappula

Myosotis lappula, commonly known as the water forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant features slender stems that are often prostrate or ascending, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are small, blue to violet, and have five petals, often with a white or yellow spot at the base.

The species is part of the Boraginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
