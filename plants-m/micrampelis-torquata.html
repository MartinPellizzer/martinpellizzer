Micrampelis torquata

Micrampelis torquata, commonly known as the twisted leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spirally twisted leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
