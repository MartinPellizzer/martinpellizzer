Macfadyena platypoda

Macfadyena platypoda, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, South America, and Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, featuring woody stems and spiny tendrils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
