Mentha hieronymi

Mentha hieronymi, commonly known as Hieronymi mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are serrated, lance-shaped, and emit a strong, minty fragrance when crushed.

Mentha hieronymi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
