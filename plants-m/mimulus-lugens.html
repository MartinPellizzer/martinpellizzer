Mimulus lugens

Mimulus lugens, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mimulus lugens is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
