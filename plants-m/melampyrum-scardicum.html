Melampyrum scardicum

Melampyrum scardicum, commonly known as yellow cow-wheat, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found growing in association with other shade-tolerant species.
