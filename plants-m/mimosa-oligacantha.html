Mimosa oligacantha

Mimosa oligacantha, commonly known as the sensitive plant or mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which fold shut when touched, a response mediated by rapid changes in turgor pressure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, grayish seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its unique physiological response and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
