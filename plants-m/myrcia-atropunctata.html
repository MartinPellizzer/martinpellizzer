Myrcia atropunctata

Myrcia atropunctata, commonly known as the black-punctured myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, black berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Myrcia atropunctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
