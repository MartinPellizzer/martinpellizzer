Malaxis angustifolia

Malaxis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved malaxis, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

It is often found in rocky or humus-rich soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
