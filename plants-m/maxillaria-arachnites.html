Maxillaria arachnites

Maxillaria arachnites, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy appearance, with long, slender petals and sepals that resemble spider legs. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive, intricate pattern.

Maxillaria arachnites is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
