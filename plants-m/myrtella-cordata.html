Myrtella cordata

Myrtella cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue berries. Myrtella cordata is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
