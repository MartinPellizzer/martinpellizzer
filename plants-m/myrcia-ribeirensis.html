Myrcia ribeirensis

Myrcia ribeirensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

The flowers are white or pale pink and are borne in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators.
