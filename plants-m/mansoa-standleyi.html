Mansoa standleyi

Mansoa standleyi, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-colored berry that contains multiple seeds.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
