Maxillaria leucantha

Maxillaria leucantha, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity. It is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Maxillaria leucantha is also known for its unique, lip-like structure that is often marked with contrasting color patterns.
