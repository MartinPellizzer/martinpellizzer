Merremia potentilloides

Merremia potentilloides, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in dry habitats.

Its seeds are dispersed by animals and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
