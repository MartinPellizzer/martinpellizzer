Malcolmia tetracmoides

Malcolmia tetracmoides, commonly known as the Egyptian cheeseweed, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternate, oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Malcolmia tetracmoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
