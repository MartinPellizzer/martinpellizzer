Malus medwietzkyana

Malus medwietzkyana, commonly known as the Medwietzkyana apple, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the emergence of its foliage. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically not cultivated for commercial use but are appreciated for their unique flavor and ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens with specific horticultural conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and cultivation, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
