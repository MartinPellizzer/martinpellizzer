Matthiola farinosa

Matthiola farinosa, commonly known as the dusty matthiola, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Matthiola farinosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
