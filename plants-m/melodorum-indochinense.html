Melodorum indochinense

Melodorum indochinense, commonly known as the Indochinese melody vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blossoms in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
