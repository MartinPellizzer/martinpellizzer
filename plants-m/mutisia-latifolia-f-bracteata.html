Mutisia latifolia f. bracteata

Mutisia latifolia f. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate mutisia, is a variant of the broadleaf mutisia, native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant features prominent bracts that surround the inflorescence, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and role in local ecosystems.
