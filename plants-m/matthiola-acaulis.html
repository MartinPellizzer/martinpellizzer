Matthiola acaulis

Matthiola acaulis, commonly known as the wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Matthiola acaulis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
