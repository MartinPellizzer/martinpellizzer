Malus orientalis var. montana

Malus orientalis var. montana, commonly known as the mountain crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in early spring. The plant produces small, tart apples that are often used in jams and preserves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
