Mesembryanthemum perfoliatum

Mesembryanthemum perfoliatum, commonly known as the ice plant or snow plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.

Mesembryanthemum perfoliatum is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
