Methysticum methysticum

Methysticum methysticum, commonly known as kava, is a shrub native to the South Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its woody stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its most valuable part is the root, which contains compounds responsible for its sedative effects.

The botanical classification of kava is Piper methysticum, placing it in the Piperaceae family.
