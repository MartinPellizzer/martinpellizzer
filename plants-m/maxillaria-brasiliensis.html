Maxillaria brasiliensis

Maxillaria brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features lanceolate, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and bloom during the warmer months.

Maxillaria brasiliensis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
