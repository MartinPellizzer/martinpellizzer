Mimosa moaensis

Mimosa moaensis, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Mimosa moaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
