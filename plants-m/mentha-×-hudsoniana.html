Mentha × hudsoniana

Mentha × hudsoniana, commonly known as Canada mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Canada mint is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
