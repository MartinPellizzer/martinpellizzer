Myrciaria delicatula var. angustifolia

Myrciaria delicatula var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
