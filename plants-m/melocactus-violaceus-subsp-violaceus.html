Melocactus violaceus subsp. violaceus

Melocactus violaceus subsp.

violaceus, commonly known as the violet-hued melocactus, is a cactus species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that is typically covered in dense, pale blue or violet spines.

The plant produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing during the spring or summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

The subspecies is noted for its slow growth and unique appearance, making it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
