Malva trifida var. heterophylla

Malva trifida var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that vary in shape depending on the plant's growth conditions. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are typical of the Malvaceae family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
