Miconia uvida

Miconia uvida, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are typically followed by bright red berries. Miconia uvida is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions, making it a concern in ecological management.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and sometimes problematic species in tropical ecosystems.
