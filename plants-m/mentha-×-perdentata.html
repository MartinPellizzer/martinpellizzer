Mentha × perdentata

Mentha × perdentata, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a cross between spearmint and watermint.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are aromatic when crushed. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or pink, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Mentha × perdentata is widely cultivated for its essential oils and used in culinary and medicinal applications.
