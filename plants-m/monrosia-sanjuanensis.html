Monrosia sanjuanensis

Monrosia sanjuanensis, commonly known as the San Juan mountain sage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern New Mexico and southwestern Colorado.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are typically pale blue to purple in color and are arranged in whorls along the stem.

Monrosia sanjuanensis is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky soils and open meadows.
