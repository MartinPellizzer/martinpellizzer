Miconia goldenbergiana

Miconia goldenbergiana, commonly known as the goldenbergian miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Miconia goldenbergiana is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
