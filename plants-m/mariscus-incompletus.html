Mariscus incompletus

Mariscus incompletus, commonly known as the incomplete mariscus, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring small, clustered leaves and delicate, pale flowers that bloom during the late spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or along stream banks. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and potential role in local ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Mariscus incompletus is considered a species of conservation concern.
