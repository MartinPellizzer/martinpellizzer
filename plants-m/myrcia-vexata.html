Myrcia vexata

Myrcia vexata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a strong, spicy aroma when crushed.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
