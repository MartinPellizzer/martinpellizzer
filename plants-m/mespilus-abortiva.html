Mespilus abortiva

Mespilus abortiva, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces distinctive, fleshy, apple-like fruits that are typically harvested when partially decayed, a process known as "mumming.". The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and is characterized by its fragrant, pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. Its fruit is valued for its unique flavor and has been used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its historical significance in European horticulture.
