Mammillaria spinosissima subsp. pilcayensis

Mammillaria spinosissima subsp.

pilcayensis, commonly known as the spiny mammillaria, is a small, globular cactus native to Mexico. It features a dense covering of white to pale green spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its compact form. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
