Magnolia sharpii

Magnolia sharpii, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
