Megema hercules

Megema hercules, commonly known as the Hercules' megema, is a striking member of the Megemaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Native to the temperate regions of South America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
