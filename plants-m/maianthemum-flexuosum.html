Maianthemum flexuosum

Maianthemum flexuosum, commonly known as the nodding lily or false lily of the valley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Convallariaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, white, bell-shaped flowers that hang downward. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features include a slender stem, lanceolate leaves, and anthers that release pollen in a unique, spiraling motion.
