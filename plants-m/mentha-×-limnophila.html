Mentha × limnophila

Mentha × limnophila, commonly known as water mint, is a hybrid perennial herb that thrives in moist environments.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive menthol scent.

Water mint is often found near streams, ponds, and other water sources, making it a common wetland plant.
