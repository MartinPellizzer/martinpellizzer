Meringium tortuosum

Meringium tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted meringia, is a unique plant species characterized by its intricate, winding stems.

It is native to specific regions with moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers, though small, are often pale green or white, adding subtle beauty to its foliage.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
