Moraea garipensis

Moraea garipensis, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in the Western and Eastern Cape provinces.
