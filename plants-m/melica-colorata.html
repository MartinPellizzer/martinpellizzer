Melica colorata

Melica colorata, commonly known as the pink false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a reddish or pinkish hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Melica colorata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, attractive clumps.
