Myrcia buchenavioides

Myrcia buchenavioides, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are small, woody berries that contain numerous seeds.

Myrcia buchenavioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
