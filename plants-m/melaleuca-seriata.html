Melaleuca seriata

Melaleuca seriata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk covered in peeling, fibrous bark. Its leaves are narrow and often have a silver or greyish underside, and it produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers.

Melaleuca seriata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
