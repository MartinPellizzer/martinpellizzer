Myrtus baruensis

Myrtus baruensis, commonly known as the Borneo myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Myrtus baruensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some Southeast Asian cultures.
