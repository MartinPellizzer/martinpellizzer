Marsilea paradoxa

Marsilea paradoxa, commonly known as the floating water clover, is a aquatic fern that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its small, four-leaf clover-like fronds that float on the water surface. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves, which are crucial for its reproductive cycle. Marsilea paradoxa is often found in ponds, ditches, and wetlands, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
