Melochia conglobata

Melochia conglobata, commonly known as the globular melochia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Melochia conglobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
