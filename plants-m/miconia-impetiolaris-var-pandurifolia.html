Miconia impetiolaris var. pandurifolia

Miconia impetiolaris var. pandurifolia, commonly known as the pandurifolia miconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
