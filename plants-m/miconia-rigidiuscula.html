Miconia rigidiuscula

Miconia rigidiuscula, commonly known as the rigid stalk miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white bracts that resemble flowers. The plant has small, clustered flowers that are typically pollinated by insects, and its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Miconia rigidiuscula is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
