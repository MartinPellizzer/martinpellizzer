Myrcia glaucocarpa

Myrcia glaucocarpa, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, glaucous leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are small, berry-like drupes that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and aromatic properties.
