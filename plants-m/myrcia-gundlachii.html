Myrcia gundlachii

Myrcia gundlachii, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hillside areas.

Myrcia gundlachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
