Malus × robusta

Malus × robusta, commonly known as the robust apple, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Malus sylvestris and Malus baccata.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically tart and sour in flavor. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.

However, it is less commonly grown for fruit production compared to other apple varieties.
