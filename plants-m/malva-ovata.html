Malva ovata

Malva ovata, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture, and produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.

Malva ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
