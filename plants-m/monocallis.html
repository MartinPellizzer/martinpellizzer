Monocallis

Monocallis, commonly known as the spider lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that often resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows from bulbs and produces strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.
