Mokof emarginata

Mokof emarginata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, velvety flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Mokof emarginata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
