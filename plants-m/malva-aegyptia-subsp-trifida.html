Malva aegyptia subsp. trifida

Malva aegyptia subsp.

trifida, commonly known as the Egyptian mallow or Egyptian rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces fluffy, white seed pods that disperse its seeds by wind. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
