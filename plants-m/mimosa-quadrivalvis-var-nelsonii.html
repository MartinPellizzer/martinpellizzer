Mimosa quadrivalvis var. nelsonii

Mimosa quadrivalvis var. nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by the movement of water within the leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.
