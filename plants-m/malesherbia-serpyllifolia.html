Malesherbia serpyllifolia

Malesherbia serpyllifolia, commonly known as the common malesherbia, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Malesherbia serpyllifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings.
