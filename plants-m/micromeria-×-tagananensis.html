Micromeria × tagananensis

Micromeria × tagananensis, commonly known as the hybrid desert aster, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its small, showy flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Micromeria × tagananensis is often found in chaparral and scrub habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
