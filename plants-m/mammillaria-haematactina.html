Mammillaria haematactina

Mammillaria haematactina, commonly known as the blood-red mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded tubercles that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces bright red flowers that contrast strikingly with its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
