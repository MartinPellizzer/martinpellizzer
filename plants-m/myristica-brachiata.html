Myristica brachiata

Myristica brachiata, commonly known as the fragrant nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces distinctive fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
