Momisia integrifolia

Momisia integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf momisia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of pink or purple, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds.

Momisia integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
