Melastoma ashtonii

Melastoma ashtonii, commonly known as the ashton's melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough texture, while its fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

Melastoma ashtonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
