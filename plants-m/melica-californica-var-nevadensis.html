Melica californica var. nevadensis

Melica californica var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada California meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually green to purplish in color.

This variety is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability in its habitat.
