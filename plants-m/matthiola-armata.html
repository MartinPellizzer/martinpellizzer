Matthiola armata

Matthiola armata, commonly known as the rock stock, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Matthiola armata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its beauty in gardens.
