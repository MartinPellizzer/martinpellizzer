Mediocalcar dependens

Mediocalcar dependens, commonly known as the hanging calcar, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Mediocalcar dependens is considered a rare and endangered species.
