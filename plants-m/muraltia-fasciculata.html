Muraltia fasciculata

Muraltia fasciculata, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or red cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Muraltia fasciculata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and humid forests.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
