Mammillaria multimamma

Mammillaria multimamma, commonly known as the "Beaded Pincushion Cactus," is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its stems are ribbed and often have small, rounded tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
