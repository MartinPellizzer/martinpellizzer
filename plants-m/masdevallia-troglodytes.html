Masdevallia troglodytes

Masdevallia troglodytes, commonly known as the cave masdevallia, is a rare orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments with high light intensity, typically found in rocky crevices and caves. Its unique morphology and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
