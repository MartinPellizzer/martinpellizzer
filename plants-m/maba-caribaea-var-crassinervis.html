Maba caribaea var. crassinervis

Maba caribaea var. crassinervis, commonly known as the Cuban mahogany, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and broad, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by small, round fruits containing winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also classified as an endangered species in many regions.
