Monarda viridissima

Monarda viridissima, commonly known as greenstem bee balm, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. Its foliage is narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairiness on the underside.

Monarda viridissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
