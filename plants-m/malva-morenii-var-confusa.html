Malva morenii var. confusa

Malva morenii var. confusa, commonly known as the fuzzy mallows, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features soft, velvety leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
