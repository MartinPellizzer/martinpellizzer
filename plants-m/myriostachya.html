Myriostachya

Myriostachya, commonly known as the feather grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it its common name. The plants typically grow in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Myriostachya species are native to various regions in Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

They are often used in landscaping for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
