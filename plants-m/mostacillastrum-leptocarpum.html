Mostacillastrum leptocarpum

Mostacillastrum leptocarpum, commonly known as slender fruit grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a clumping growth habit.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
