Mesembryanthemum compactum

Mesembryanthemum compactum, commonly known as the compact sandmat, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
