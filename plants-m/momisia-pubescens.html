Momisia pubescens

Momisia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy momisia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod fruits. The plant is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color.

Momisia pubescens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
