Menonvillea cicatricosa

Menonvillea cicatricosa, commonly known as the scarred orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves and intricate floral structures that display a range of colors, often with a contrasting pattern. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically in rocky or alpine habitats, where it receives ample sunlight. Its flowers are ephemeral, blooming for only a short period before withering, which adds to its allure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Menonvillea cicatricosa is considered a species of conservation concern.
