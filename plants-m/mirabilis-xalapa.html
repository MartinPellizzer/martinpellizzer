Mirabilis xalapa

Mirabilis xalapa, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
