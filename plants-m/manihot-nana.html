Manihot nana

Manihot nana, commonly known as the dwarf arrowleaf manioc, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its roots, known as cassava or manioc, are a staple food source in many parts of the world.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds that require proper processing before consumption.
