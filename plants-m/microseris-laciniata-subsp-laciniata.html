Microseris laciniata subsp. laciniata

Microseris laciniata subsp.

laciniata, commonly known as the fringed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves with distinctive fringed margins.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The subspecies is characterized by its finely divided leaves and its ability to tolerate arid conditions.
