Mastixia pentandra

Mastixia pentandra, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and long, narrow leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, aromatic fruits. Its most notable feature is the highly valued resin, which is used in perfumes, incense, and traditional medicines.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
