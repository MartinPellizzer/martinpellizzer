Mallotus albus

Mallotus albus, commonly known as the white mallot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Mallotus albus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
