Myrtus trineura

Myrtus trineura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The fruit is a small, dark blue berry that contains several seeds.
