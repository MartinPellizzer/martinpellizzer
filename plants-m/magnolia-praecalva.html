Magnolia praecalva

Magnolia praecalva, commonly known as the Mexican white magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.
