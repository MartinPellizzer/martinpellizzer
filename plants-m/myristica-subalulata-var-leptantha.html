Myristica subalulata var. leptantha

Myristica subalulata var. leptantha, commonly known as the slender nutmeg tree, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, red-orange drupes that contain a single, aromatic seed, which is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

The species is valued for its essential oils and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
