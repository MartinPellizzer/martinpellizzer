Miscanthidium junceum subsp. teretifolium

Miscanthidium junceum subsp.

teretifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flat sedge, is a grass-like plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a stem that is often rhizomatous.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
