Madia dissitiflora var. anomala

Madia dissitiflora var. anomala, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces a distinctive, spiky seed head that persists through the winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
