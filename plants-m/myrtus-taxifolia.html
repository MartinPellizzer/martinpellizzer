Myrtus taxifolia

Myrtus taxifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Myrtus taxifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
