Maxillaria mapiriensis

Maxillaria mapiriensis, commonly known as the Mapiri orchid, is a species native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth during dry periods. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Maxillaria mapiriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
