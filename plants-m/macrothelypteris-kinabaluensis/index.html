Macrothelypteris Kinabaluensis

Kinabalu brake fern, scientifically known as Macrothelypteris kinabaluensis, is a rare and unique species native to the montane forests of Borneo. Found primarily in the vicinity of Mount Kinabalu, this fern thrives in the cool, moist conditions of high-altitude ecosystems. Its distinctive fronds, characterized by their broad, segmented leaves, make it easily identifiable among other fern species. The plant plays a vital role in its local environment, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms. Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, the Kinabalu brake fern is considered a valuable subject for ecological and botanical research.
