Medinilla coursiana

Medinilla coursiana, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, orange fruits that resemble tangerines, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Medinilla coursiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique fruit.
