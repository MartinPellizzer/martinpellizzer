Mentha aquatica var. cheitessa

Mentha aquatica var. cheitessa, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the upper stem. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Mentha aquatica var. cheitessa is known for its refreshing scent and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
