Muraltia hamata

Muraltia hamata, commonly known as the red muraltia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is a small, round, red drupe that contains a single seed.
