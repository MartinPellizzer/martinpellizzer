Malpighia moncionensis

Malpighia moncionensis, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

Malpighia moncionensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
