Mimosa vernicosa var. ciliata

Mimosa vernicosa var. ciliata, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can improve soil fertility.
