Maxillaria flavoviridis

Maxillaria flavoviridis, commonly known as the yellow-green maxillaria, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that bear alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, yellow-green flowers that have a distinctive, sweet fragrance. Its flowers typically bloom in summer and are characterized by their spurred lip and intricate petal patterns.

This species is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
