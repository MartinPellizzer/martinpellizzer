Mentha × cechobrodensis

Mentha × cechobrodensis, commonly known as the Czech hybrid mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata and Mentha rotundifolia.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and fragrant, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to Central Europe. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or pink, and it is known for its strong menthol scent.

This hybrid is valued for its hardiness and adaptability in various garden settings.
