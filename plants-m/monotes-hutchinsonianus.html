Monotes hutchinsonianus

Monotes hutchinsonianus, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red ray florets and yellow disk flowers, which make it a striking ornamental plant. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry to moist habitats.

Monotes hutchinsonianus is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal uses in traditional practices.
