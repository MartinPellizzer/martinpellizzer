Mertensia oblongifolia var. amoena

Mertensia oblongifolia var. amoena, commonly known as the purple-flowered bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in woodland gardens.
