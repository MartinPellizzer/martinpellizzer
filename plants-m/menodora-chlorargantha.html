Menodora chlorargantha

Menodora chlorargantha, commonly known as the yellow moonflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Menodoridae and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that open at night. The plant has a climbing habit and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a somewhat hairy texture.

The species is noted for its unique nocturnal blooming pattern and is often found in humid, tropical environments.
