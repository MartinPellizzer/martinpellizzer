Melica berteroana f. violacea

Melica berteroana f. violacea, commonly known as violet false brome, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This fern-like grass is often found in temperate regions of South America.
