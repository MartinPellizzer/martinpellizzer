Micromeria fontanesii var. major

Micromeria fontanesii var. major, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, gray-green leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
