Moehringia lateriflora

Moehringia lateriflora, commonly known as the lateral-flower moehringia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, often in shades of white or pale pink. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

Moehringia lateriflora is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
