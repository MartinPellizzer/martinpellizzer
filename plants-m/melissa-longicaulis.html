Melissa longicaulis

Melissa longicaulis, commonly known as long-stemmed lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in herbal teas for their calming properties.

Melissa longicaulis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
