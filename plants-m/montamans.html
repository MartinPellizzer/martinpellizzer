Montamans

Montamans, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features silvery-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, helping it conserve moisture in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody stems are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which further reduce water loss.

Montamans is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
