Montia obtusata

Montia obtusata, commonly known as bluntleaf montia, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a stem that branches near the base.

Montia obtusata is part of the Montiaceae family, which is characterized by its succulent leaves and showy flowers.
