Mutisia pulchella

Mutisia pulchella, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets, creating a striking visual display. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Mutisia pulchella is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
