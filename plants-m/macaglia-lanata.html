Macaglia lanata

Macaglia lanata, commonly known as the woolly macaglia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
