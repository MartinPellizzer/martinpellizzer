Mentha deflexa var. simplex

Mentha deflexa var. simplex, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive minty aroma. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in herbal teas and as a natural insect repellent.
