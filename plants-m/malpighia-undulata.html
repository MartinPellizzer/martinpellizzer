Malpighia undulata

Malpighia undulata, commonly known as the waxy apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves and clusters of small, red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
