Medicago quasifalcata

Medicago quasifalcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf medick, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive narrow leaflet shape. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, followed by small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Medicago quasifalcata is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration efforts.
