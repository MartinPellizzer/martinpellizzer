Melo sativus

Melo sativus, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to southern Asia.

It is a trailing, herbaceous vine that produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The fruit is typically orange to yellow with a netted rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. It thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season with full sun exposure.

Melo sativus is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins A and C.
