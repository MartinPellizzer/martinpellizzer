Mitracarpus frigidus

Mitracarpus frigidus, commonly known as the cold mountain cress, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features delicate, alternate leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Mitracarpus frigidus is known for its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
