Merostachys brevispica

Merostachys brevispica, commonly known as the short-bracted bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that are often hidden among its foliage.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
