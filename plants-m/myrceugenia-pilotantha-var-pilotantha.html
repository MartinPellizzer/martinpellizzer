Myrceugenia pilotantha var. pilotantha

Myrceugenia pilotantha var. pilotantha, commonly known as the white-flowered myrtle, is a species native to the temperate rainforests of southern Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant scent and evergreen foliage.
