Miconia pulverulenta

Miconia pulverulenta, commonly known as the powdery miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often accompanied by a distinctive, fragrant odor. Its fruit is a round, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Miconia pulverulenta is also noted for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other tropical regions as a garden plant.
