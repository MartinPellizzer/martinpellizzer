Matricaria maritima f. coronata

Matricaria maritima f. coronata, commonly known as the crown chamomile, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, white or pale yellow flower heads. The plant produces fluffy, white ray florets that surround a central disk of tubular flowers, giving it a daisy-like appearance. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in maritime environments.

This variety is cultivated for its aromatic properties and is used in herbal remedies and as a decorative plant.
