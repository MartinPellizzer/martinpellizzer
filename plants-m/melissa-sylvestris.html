Melissa sylvestris

Melissa sylvestris, commonly known as wild lemon balm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves emit a mild lemon scent, making it a popular choice for herbal teas and aromatherapy.

Melissa sylvestris is also valued for its calming properties in traditional medicine.
