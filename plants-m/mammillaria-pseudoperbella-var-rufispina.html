Mammillaria pseudoperbella var. rufispina

Mammillaria pseudoperbella var. rufispina, commonly known as the Rufispina Mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, globular shape and dense covering of white to yellowish spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, emerging from the areoles.

This variety is prized for its compact size and ornamental appearance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
