Melisitus creticus

Melisitus creticus, commonly known as the Cretan melissia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Crete.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, aromatic foliage, and a preference for well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in local flora.
