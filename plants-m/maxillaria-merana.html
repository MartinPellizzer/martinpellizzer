Maxillaria merana

Maxillaria merana, commonly known as the rose orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in the dry season.

Maxillaria merana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses and gardens.
