Myrceugenia acutiflora

Myrceugenia acutiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Myrceugenia acutiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aromatic properties.
