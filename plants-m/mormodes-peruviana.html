Mormodes peruviana

Mormodes peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
