Myrcia hispida var. panicularis

Myrcia hispida var. panicularis, commonly known as the panicled myrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, often hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces dark purple to black berries, which are edible and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
