Magnolia conspicua var. alexandrina

Magnolia conspicua var. alexandrina, commonly known as the Alexandrine magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.
