Matelea oldemanii

Matelea oldemanii, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a striking member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and has a woody stem that becomes more branched with age. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, enhancing its appeal in both ecological and horticultural contexts.

Matelea oldemanii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to native plant gardens.
