Mentha × villosa var. speciosa

Mentha × villosa var. speciosa, commonly known as the woolly speckled mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often speckled with dark purple or brown markings. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
