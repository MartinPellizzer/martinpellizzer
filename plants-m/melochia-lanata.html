Melochia lanata

Melochia lanata, commonly known as the hairy melochia, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, palmate leaves that are often lobed and have a rough texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release small, hairy seeds.

Melochia lanata is native to Africa and parts of Asia, and it is often found in moist, disturbed habitats.
