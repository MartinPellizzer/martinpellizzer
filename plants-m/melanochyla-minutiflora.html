Melanochyla minutiflora

Melanochyla minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered blackwood, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
