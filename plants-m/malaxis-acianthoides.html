Malaxis acianthoides

Malaxis acianthoides, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in its native habitat.
