Menomphalus

Menomphalus, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy, daisy-like blooms. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
