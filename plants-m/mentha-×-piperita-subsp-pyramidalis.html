Mentha × piperita subsp. pyramidalis

Mentha × piperita subsp.

pyramidalis, commonly known as pyramidal mint, is a subspecies of peppermint characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, pale purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, which bloom in late summer.

The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic, menthol-rich leaves.

Its compact form makes it suitable for container gardening and ornamental purposes.
