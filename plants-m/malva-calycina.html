Malva calycina

Malva calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, velvety texture.

Malva calycina is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
