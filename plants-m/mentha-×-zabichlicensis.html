Mentha × zabichlicensis

Mentha × zabichlicensis, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha viridis (ovalleaf mint).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its square, hollow stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Mentha × zabichlicensis is known for its aromatic leaves, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive menthol scent.

It is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its refreshing and cooling properties.
