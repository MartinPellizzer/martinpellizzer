Myrcia crocea var. blanchetiana

Myrcia crocea var. blanchetiana, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
