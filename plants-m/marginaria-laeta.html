Marginaria laeta

Marginaria laeta, commonly known as the happy marginaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which typically feature yellow centers surrounded by white to pinkish ray florets. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits called achenes, which are often dispersed by wind.

Marginaria laeta is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and easy maintenance requirements.
