Miconia seticaulis

Miconia seticaulis, commonly known as the creeping miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, often sprawling growth habit and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a problematic species in certain ecosystems.
