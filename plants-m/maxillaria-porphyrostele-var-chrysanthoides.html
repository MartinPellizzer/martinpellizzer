Maxillaria porphyrostele var. chrysanthoides

Maxillaria porphyrostele var. chrysanthoides, commonly known as the golden-flowered maxillaria, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a yellowish-orange hue. The plant typically has dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
