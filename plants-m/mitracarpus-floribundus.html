Mitracarpus floribundus

Mitracarpus floribundus, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. These flowers are followed by bright orange to red berries that contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit and foliage.
