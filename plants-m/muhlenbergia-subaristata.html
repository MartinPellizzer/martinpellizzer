Muhlenbergia subaristata

Muhlenbergia subaristata, commonly known as the silverhair grass, is a native grass species of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikelets.

Muhlenbergia subaristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in dry landscapes.
