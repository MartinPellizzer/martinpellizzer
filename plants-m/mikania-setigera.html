Mikania setigera

Mikania setigera, commonly known as creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Mikania setigera is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.

It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forest edges.
