Miconia leawarmingiana

Miconia leawarmingiana, commonly known as the Warming Island miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly glossy surface. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with pink to red bracts, which attract various pollinators such as birds and insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
