Melandrium rubrum

Melandrium rubrum, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The fruit is a capsule that releases small, dry seeds when mature.
