Myrtus blanchetiana

Myrtus blanchetiana, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Myrtus blanchetiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
