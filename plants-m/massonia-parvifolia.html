Massonia parvifolia

Massonia parvifolia, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often resembling tulips. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Massonia parvifolia is drought-tolerant and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in arid landscapes.
