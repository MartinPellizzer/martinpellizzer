Mentha microdonta

Mentha microdonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Mentha microdonta belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is known for its fragrant oils and medicinal properties.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their cooling and antiseptic qualities.
