Malvastrum tricuspidatum

Malvastrum tricuspidatum, commonly known as the three-lobed mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

It is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
