Myristica erratica

Myristica erratica, commonly known as the wild nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and woody structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
