Melinis ascendens

Melinis ascendens, commonly known as the climbing grass or tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, often climbing or spreading across other vegetation. The plant produces small, blue-green spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish-gray hue.

Melinis ascendens is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
