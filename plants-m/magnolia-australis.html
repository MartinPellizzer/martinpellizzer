Magnolia australis

Magnolia australis, commonly known as the Australian magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, often with a subtle fragrance. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Magnolia australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
