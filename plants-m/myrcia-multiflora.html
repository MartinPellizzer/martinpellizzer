Myrcia multiflora

Myrcia multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
