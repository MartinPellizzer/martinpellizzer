Microstegium vimineum f. polystachyum

Microstegium vimineum f. polystachyum, commonly known as the Japanese stiltgrass, is a variant of the invasive plant species Microstegium vimineum.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous small, brownish flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and moist environments, often outcompeting native vegetation.

This form is particularly noted for its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, contributing to its status as an ecological nuisance.
