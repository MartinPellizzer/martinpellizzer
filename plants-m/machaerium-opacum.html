Machaerium opacum

Machaerium opacum, commonly known as the dark machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in spherical or cylindrical heads. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small leaflets, and it has a woody stem with thorny branches.

Machaerium opacum is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
