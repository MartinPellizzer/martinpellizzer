Mertensia acutifolia

Mertensia acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, bluish-green leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers emit a mild, sweet fragrance.

Mertensia acutifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
