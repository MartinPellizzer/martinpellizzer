Malaxis ovata

Malaxis ovata, commonly known as the ovate malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces pale green to yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in a loose cluster. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Malaxis ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
