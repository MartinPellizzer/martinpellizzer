Myrtus fulva

Myrtus fulva, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa and Namibia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and often have a distinct aromatic scent.

Myrtus fulva is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
