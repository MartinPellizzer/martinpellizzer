Miconia rubiginosa var. cinerea

Miconia rubiginosa var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, grayish pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are often surrounded by bracts that give the inflorescence a striking appearance. It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some areas where it has been introduced.
