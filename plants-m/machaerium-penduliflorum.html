Machaerium penduliflorum

Machaerium penduliflorum, commonly known as the hanging flower machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, drooping inflorescences that bear small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.

Machaerium penduliflorum is often used in ornamental horticulture for its delicate, cascading floral display.
