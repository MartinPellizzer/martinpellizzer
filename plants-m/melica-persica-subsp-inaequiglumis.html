Melica persica subsp. inaequiglumis

Melica persica subsp.

inaequiglumis, commonly known as Persian wheatgrass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, including Iran and the Caucasus. It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and agricultural studies.
