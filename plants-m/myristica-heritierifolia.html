Myristica heritierifolia

Myristica heritierifolia, commonly known as the Malabar nut or Malabar cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain aromatic seeds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by large, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and glossy, with a distinct aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its unique seeds and has been traditionally used in culinary and medicinal applications.
