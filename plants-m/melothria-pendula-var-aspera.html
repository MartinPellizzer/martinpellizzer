Melothria pendula var. aspera

Melothria pendula var. aspera, commonly known as the rough passionfruit vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow-orange flowers that develop into elongated, spiky fruits. The fruit's exterior is covered in rough, warty bumps, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which have a tart, aromatic flavor.
