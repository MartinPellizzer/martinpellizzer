Moquinia rubra

Moquinia rubra, commonly known as red moquinhia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically reddish in color, especially when young. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Moquinia rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
