Mesembryanthemum canaliculatum

Mesembryanthemum canaliculatum, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or ozark star, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and showy, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments. Its flowers typically bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit, which resembles a small, round berry, is edible and has a tangy, sweet flavor.
