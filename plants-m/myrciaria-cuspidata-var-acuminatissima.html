Myrciaria cuspidata var. acuminatissima

Myrciaria cuspidata var. acuminatissima, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or acerola cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, red, edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
