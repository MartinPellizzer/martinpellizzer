Machaerium heteropterum

Machaerium heteropterum, commonly known as the golden crown grass, is a tropical perennial grass native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble crowns, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in wetland or disturbed habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Machaerium heteropterum is also known for its ability to thrive in moist conditions, making it a resilient choice for certain ecological settings.
