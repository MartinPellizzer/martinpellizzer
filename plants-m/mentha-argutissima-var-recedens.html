Mentha argutissima var. recedens

Mentha argutissima var. recedens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a variant of the broad-leaved mint native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, refreshing menthol aroma. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies due to their aromatic and medicinal properties.
