Mimosa uniceps

Mimosa uniceps, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique responsiveness to touch makes it a popular subject in botanical studies.
