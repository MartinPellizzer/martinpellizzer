Mahurea tomentosa

Mahurea tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly mahurea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, providing a unique texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
