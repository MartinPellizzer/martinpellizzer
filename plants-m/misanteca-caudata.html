Misanteca caudata

Misanteca caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the Caribbean and Central America.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the rainy season. The plant's berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique ecological role includes providing food for various bird species and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
