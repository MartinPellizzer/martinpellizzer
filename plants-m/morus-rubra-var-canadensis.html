Morus rubra var. canadensis

Morus rubra var. canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian red mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
