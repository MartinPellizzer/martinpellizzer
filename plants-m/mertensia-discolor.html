Mertensia discolor

Mertensia discolor, commonly known as bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking blue-green color, and its flowers are bell-shaped, typically ranging from white to pale blue. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its attractive foliage and early spring blooms.

Mertensia discolor is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
