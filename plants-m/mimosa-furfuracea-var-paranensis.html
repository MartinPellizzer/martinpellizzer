Mimosa furfuracea var. paranensis

Mimosa furfuracea var. paranensis, commonly known as the Parana mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil and Argentina.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. It is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.

The Parana mimosa plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
