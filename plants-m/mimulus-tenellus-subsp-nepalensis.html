Mimulus tenellus subsp. nepalensis

Mimulus tenellus subsp.

nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India. It is a herbaceous annual with slender, erect stems and small, bright orange to red flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a characteristic feature of the genus Mimulus.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
