Merremia semisagitta subsp. subpalmata

Merremia semisagitta subsp.

subpalmata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
