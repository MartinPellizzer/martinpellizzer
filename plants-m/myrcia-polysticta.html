Myrcia polysticta

Myrcia polysticta, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Myrcia polysticta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
