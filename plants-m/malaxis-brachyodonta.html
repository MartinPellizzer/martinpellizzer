Malaxis brachyodonta

Malaxis brachyodonta, commonly known as the short-lobed Malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and possesses a single, short rhizome. The plant produces a single or few flowering stems bearing a few to several pale green to brownish flowers. Its flowers have a distinctive spurred lip, typical of the Orchidaceae family.

The species is often found in moist, shaded forest habitats, where it thrives in the understory.
