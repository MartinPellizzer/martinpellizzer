Mentha ranina

Mentha ranina, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows, thriving in areas with abundant water. The plant has square, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem.

Mentha ranina is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
