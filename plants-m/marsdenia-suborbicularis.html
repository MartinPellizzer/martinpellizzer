Marsdenia suborbicularis

Marsdenia suborbicularis, commonly known as the orbicular Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a follicle containing several seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
