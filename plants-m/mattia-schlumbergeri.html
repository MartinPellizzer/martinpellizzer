Mattia schlumbergeri

Mattia schlumbergeri, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a popular epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, branching stems that bear vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms during the holiday season, making it a festive ornamental plant. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, while its roots are shallow and suited for climbing.

Mattia schlumbergeri prefers indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a low-maintenance plant for indoor cultivation.
