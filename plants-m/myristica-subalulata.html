Myristica subalulata

Myristica subalulata, commonly known as the white nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Moluccas (Maluku) in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain both nutmeg and mace. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forest environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

The tree is valued for its seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
