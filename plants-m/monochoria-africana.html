Monochoria africana

Monochoria africana, commonly known as African water fern, is a aquatic plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its floating leaves and dense clusters of small, white flowers. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes help in stabilizing the soil and preventing erosion.

Monochoria africana is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
