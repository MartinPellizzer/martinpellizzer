Megalastrum spectabile var. phillipianum

Megalastrum spectabile var. phillipianum, commonly known as the Phillipian's glory-of-the-snow, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the leaves. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically evergreen, providing visual interest throughout the year. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
