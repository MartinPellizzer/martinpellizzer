Melica caffrorum var. elatior

Melica caffrorum var. elatior, commonly known as the greater melica, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along watercourses. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
