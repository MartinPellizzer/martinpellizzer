Mucuna luzoniensis

Mucuna luzoniensis, commonly known as the Luzon velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and hairy, pod-like fruits that contain compressed seeds. The plant produces a distinctive, sweet-smelling fragrance, especially when in bloom. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
