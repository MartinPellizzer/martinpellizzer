Myristica ingens

Myristica ingens, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces large, fleshy berries that contain the aromatic seeds used to make nutmeg and mace. Its flowers are fragrant and yellowish-green, and it thrives in tropical rainforest conditions.

The plant is economically significant for its spice-producing seeds and has cultural importance in traditional medicine.
