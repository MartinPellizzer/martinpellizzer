Myrtus sagrae

Myrtus sagrae, commonly known as the sacred myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fragrant foliage has been historically used in religious and ceremonial contexts.

Myrtus sagrae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
