Melliniella micrantha

Melliniella micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered melampodium, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Melliniella micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
