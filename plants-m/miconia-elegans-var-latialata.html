Miconia elegans var. latialata

Miconia elegans var. latialata, commonly known as the golden miconia, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
