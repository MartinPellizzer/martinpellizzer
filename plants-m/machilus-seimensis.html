Machilus seimensis

Machilus seimensis, commonly known as the Chinese laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed.

Machilus seimensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
