Marica sabini

Marica sabini, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
