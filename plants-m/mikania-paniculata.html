Mikania paniculata

Mikania paniculata, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along the ground, making it a common weed in disturbed areas. Its fibrous roots and stems allow it to spread easily, often forming dense mats that can suppress other vegetation.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
