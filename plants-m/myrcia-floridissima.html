Myrcia floridissima

Myrcia floridissima, commonly known as the Florida myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal hammocks and wetlands.

Myrcia floridissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
