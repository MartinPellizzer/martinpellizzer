Mentha × napolitana

Mentha × napolitana, commonly known as pineapple mint, is a hybrid mint species that combines traits from spearmint and watermint.

It is characterized by its fragrant, serrated leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in culinary applications for its aromatic leaves and can be propagated through stem cuttings.

This species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
