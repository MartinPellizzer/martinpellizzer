Myrcia flagellaris

Myrcia flagellaris, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in various tropical regions around the world.
