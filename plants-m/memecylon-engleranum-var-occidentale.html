Memecylon engleranum var. occidentale

Memecylon engleranum var. occidentale, commonly known as the western memecylon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features elliptical to ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and are typically white or pale yellow, while the fruit is a small, three-lobed drupe.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
