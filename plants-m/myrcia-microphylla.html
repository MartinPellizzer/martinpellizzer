Myrcia microphylla

Myrcia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features small, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia microphylla is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
