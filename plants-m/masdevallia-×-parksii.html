Masdevallia × parksii

Masdevallia × parksii, commonly known as the Parksii Masdevallia, is a hybrid orchid species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and sepals that often display vibrant shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are short-lived but highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique beauty and adaptability to controlled environments.
