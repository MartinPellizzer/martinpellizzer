Munnozia megacephala

Munnozia megacephala, commonly known as the desert dudleya, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and covered in a dense layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in a raceme at the tip of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Munnozia megacephala is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
