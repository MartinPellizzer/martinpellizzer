Mapouria rabeniana

Mapouria rabeniana, commonly known as the Madagascar maple, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, red-orange berries that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
