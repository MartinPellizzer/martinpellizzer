Mikania miconioides

Mikania miconioides, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its rapid growth and slender, trailing stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, often with a white or yellow throat. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Mikania miconioides is often found in moist, shaded areas and can become invasive in certain regions.
