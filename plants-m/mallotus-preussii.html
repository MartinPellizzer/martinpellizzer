Mallotus preussii

Mallotus preussii, commonly known as the African holly, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and livestock. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for hedges and garden landscapes.
