Mentha pachystachya

Mentha pachystachya, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha pachystachya is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and is used in some traditional herbal remedies.
