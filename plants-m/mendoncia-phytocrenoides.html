Mendoncia phytocrenoides

Mendoncia phytocrenoides, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to the humid regions of Central and South America. The plant features a creeping rhizome that produces small, clustered leaves with a silvery underside. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
