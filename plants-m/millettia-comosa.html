Millettia comosa

Millettia comosa, commonly known as the silky millettia, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicinal uses.
