Molinaea arborea

Molinaea arborea, commonly known as the tree molinaea, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Molinaea arborea is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
