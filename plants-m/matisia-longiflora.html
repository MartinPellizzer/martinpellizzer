Matisia longiflora

Matisia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered matisia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, red pulp.

Matisia longiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
