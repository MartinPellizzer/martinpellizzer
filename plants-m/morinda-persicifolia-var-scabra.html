Morinda persicifolia var. scabra

Morinda persicifolia var. scabra, commonly known as roughleaf Chinese wineberry, is a shrub or small tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by orange-red berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
