Misanteca caryophyllata

Misanteca caryophyllata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and somewhat succulent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
