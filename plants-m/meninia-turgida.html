Meninia turgida

Meninia turgida, commonly known as the water lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide a habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds or lakes.

Meninia turgida plays an important role in water ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and improving water quality.
