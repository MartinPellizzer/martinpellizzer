Myristica schleinitzii

Myristica schleinitzii, commonly known as the Bornean nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is a small evergreen tree that produces aromatic seeds, which are closely related to the spice nutmeg. The plant has a distinctive appearance with glossy, leathery leaves and fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. Its fruits are covered in a thick, fleshy aril that turns red when ripe, attracting various bird species.

The tree is valued for its unique botanical features and potential economic significance in the spice trade.
