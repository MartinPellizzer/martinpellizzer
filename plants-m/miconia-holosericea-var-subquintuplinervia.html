Miconia holosericea var. subquintuplinervia

Miconia holosericea var. subquintuplinervia, commonly known as the quintuple-nerved miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with five to seven prominent veins, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
