Myrcia sucrei

Myrcia sucrei, commonly known as the Sucre's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries such as Brazil, Peru, and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, while the bark is smooth and reddish in color.

Myrcia sucrei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
