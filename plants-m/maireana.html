Maireana

Maireana, commonly known as the spinifex grass, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

These plants are typically shrubs or small trees, characterized by their tough, fibrous leaves and dense growth habit. They are well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Maireana species are known for their ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making them important components of desert ecosystems.

Their small, often inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in clusters and can produce small, dry fruits.
