Manisuris aurita

Manisuris aurita, commonly known as the aurite bellflower, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Manisuris aurita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
