Morella salicifolia var. kilimandscharica

Morella salicifolia var. kilimandscharica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro candleberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, candle-like berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
