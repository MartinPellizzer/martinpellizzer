Myosotis globularis

Myosotis globularis, commonly known as the globe violet, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, rounded, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Myosotis globularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
