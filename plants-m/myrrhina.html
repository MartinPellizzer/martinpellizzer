Myrrhina

Myrrhina, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant has a milky sap that is toxic to many animals, including livestock. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water.

Myrrhina plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies in its native habitat.
