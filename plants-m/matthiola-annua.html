Matthiola annua

Matthiola annua, commonly known as wallflower, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustard. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Matthiola annua is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and has a mild, spicy scent reminiscent of mustard.
