Momordica clematidea

Momordica clematidea, commonly known as bitter melon, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, green fruit that turns orange when ripe. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that are followed by the characteristic fruit. It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squash.

The fruit is widely used in Asian cuisine and traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
