Melilotoides pubescens

Melilotoides pubescens, commonly known as the hairy melilot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in spikes, and the leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets.

The hairy melilot is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
