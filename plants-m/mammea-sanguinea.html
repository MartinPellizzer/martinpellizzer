Mammea sanguinea

Mammea sanguinea, commonly known as the red mammea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape.

The bark of Mammea sanguinea is thick and often has a reddish or brownish hue, which contributes to its common name.
