Matricaria absinthioides

Matricaria absinthioides, commonly known as common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. It is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a pest repellent has contributed to its ecological and cultural significance.
