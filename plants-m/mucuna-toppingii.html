Mucuna toppingii

Mucuna toppingii, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety pods that contain clusters of seeds. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers and has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Mucuna toppingii is notable for its high protein content and is used in both traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
