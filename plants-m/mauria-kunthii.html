Mauria kunthii

Mauria kunthii, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, spiny leaves adapted to conserve water in dry environments. It is often found in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Its resilience to drought and heat makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration in arid landscapes.
