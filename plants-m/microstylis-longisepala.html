Microstylis longisepala

Microstylis longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepal microstylis, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, linear leaves. The flowers are typically clustered in spikelets, with the inflorescence being a key feature for identification. Its sepals are elongated and often show distinct coloration, which aids in pollination.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors.
