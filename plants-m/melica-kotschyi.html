Melica kotschyi

Melica kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi brome, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family.

Melica kotschyi is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
