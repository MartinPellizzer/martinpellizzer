Melandrium keiskei var. akaisialpinum

Melandrium keiskei var. akaisialpinum, commonly known as the Japanese catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
