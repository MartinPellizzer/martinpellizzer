Monochaetum sericeum floribundum

Monochaetum sericeum floribundum, commonly known as the silky fringed grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silky-textured leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland margins.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
