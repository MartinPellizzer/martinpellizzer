Michelia caerulea

Michelia caerulea, commonly known as the blue magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Michelia caerulea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
