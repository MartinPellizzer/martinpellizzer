Myricanthe discolor var. viridis

Myricanthe discolor var. viridis, commonly known as the green-leafed myricanthe, is a variant of the myricanthe species native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctively green foliage, which contrasts with the typical variegation of the parent species. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dense, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and slightly serrated. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
