Morus leucophylla

Morus leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pale green, heart-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are typically pale green to pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
