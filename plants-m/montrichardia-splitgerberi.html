Montrichardia splitgerberi

Montrichardia splitgerberi, commonly known as the African water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, often with a waxy texture and a deep green color. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom above the water, typically at night. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow waters and absorbing nutrients from the sediment.

Montrichardia splitgerberi is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
