Maxillaria warreana

Maxillaria warreana, commonly known as the Warre's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing a pseudobulb with a single leaf. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a distinctive green or red lip. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maxillaria warreana is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms.
