Mimulus × harrisonianus

Mimulus × harrisonianus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a hybrid plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are typically spurred at the back, a feature common in the Mimulus genus. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other riparian habitats. It produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mimulus × harrisonianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
