Matelea Flavidula

Matelea flavidula is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central America. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, known for its showy blooms and toxic properties. This species is distinguished by its pale yellow flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered by conservation organizations. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
