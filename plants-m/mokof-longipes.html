Mokof longipes

Mokof longipes, commonly known as the slender mokof, is a unique member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fibrous roots and woody stem make it a hardy yet delicate species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
