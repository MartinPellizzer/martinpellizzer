Medicago falcata var. lasiagrostidetorum

Medicago falcata var. lasiagrostidetorum, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, curved pods.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full sun exposure. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable cover crop in agricultural settings. Its hairy stems and leaves provide erosion control and habitat for beneficial insects.

This variety is also noted for its rapid growth and adaptability to different climates.
