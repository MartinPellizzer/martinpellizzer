Mucuna angustifolia

Mucuna angustifolia, commonly known as velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain numerous elliptical seeds. Mucuna angustifolia is known for its high protein content and is used in traditional medicine for its purported aphrodisiac and neurostimulant properties.

It also has nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it valuable in agricultural systems.
