Matucana oreodoxa

Matucana oreodoxa, commonly known as the Amazonian arrowhead, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture, adapted for efficient water absorption. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as marshes and ponds, where it can anchor itself in muddy substrates. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Matucana oreodoxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic biodiversity.
