Malva sylvestris f. vestita

Malva sylvestris f. vestita, commonly known as the white mallow, is a cultivated variant of the common mallow.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which contrast with its dark green, velvety foliage. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds.

This form is prized in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
