Myosotis alba

Myosotis alba, commonly known as the water violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to waterlogged soils.

Myosotis alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
