Meineckia peltata

Meineckia peltata, commonly known as the broadleaf meineckia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Meineckia peltata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant ecosystems.
