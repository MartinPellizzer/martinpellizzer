Manilkara emarginata subsp. typica

Manilkara emarginata subsp.

typica, commonly known as the typical manilkara, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves.

The plant produces smooth, oval fruits that are typically yellow when ripe, containing a single large seed. It is valued for its timber, which is hard and durable, and for its latex, which has various industrial uses.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
