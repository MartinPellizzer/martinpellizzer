Macropanax sessilis

Macropanax sessilis, commonly known as the sessile-leaf macropanax, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant, forming a rosette-like structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color, followed by the development of berries that contain the seeds. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and requires specific conditions for successful propagation.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect and preserve this unique botanical species.
