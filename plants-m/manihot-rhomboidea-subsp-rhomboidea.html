Manihot rhomboidea subsp. rhomboidea

Manihot rhomboidea subsp.

rhomboidea, commonly known as the arrowleaf batata, is a perennial shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant is notable for its edible tubers, which are rich in carbohydrates and have been traditionally used as a food source. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
