Microchloa indica

Microchloa indica, commonly known as the Indian microchloa, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Microchloa indica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It is also valued for its resilience to poor soil conditions and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
