Mammillaria similis var. caespitosa

Mammillaria similis var. caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered mammillaria, is a compact cactus native to Mexico.

It features a dense cluster of short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale blue tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
