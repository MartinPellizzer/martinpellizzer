Mentha longifolia f. stenantha

Mentha longifolia f. stenantha, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a subspecies of the mint family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, producing a mild minty fragrance.

The plant is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
