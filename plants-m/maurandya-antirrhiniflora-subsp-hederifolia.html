Maurandya antirrhiniflora subsp. hederifolia

Maurandya antirrhiniflora subsp.

hederifolia, commonly known as the ivy-leaved mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of ivy.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and prolonged flowering period.
