Mertensia nuda

Mertensia nuda, commonly known as naked mertensia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia nuda is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
