Mentha hostii var. arvina

Mentha hostii var. arvina, commonly known as the Armenian mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color, with a menthol-like scent. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
