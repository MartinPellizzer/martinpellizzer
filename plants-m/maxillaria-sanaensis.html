Maxillaria sanaensis

Maxillaria sanaensis, commonly known as the Sanae orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing a rosette of leaves that form a dense clump. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive lip that often has a contrasting color. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Maxillaria sanaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
