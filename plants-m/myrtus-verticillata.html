Myrtus verticillata

Myrtus verticillata, commonly known as creeping myrtle, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. The plant produces tiny white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
