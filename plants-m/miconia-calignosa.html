Miconia calignosa

Miconia calignosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or false mahogany, is a large, fast-growing tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, pale pink to white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. Miconia calignosa has a complex root system and can form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in reforestation projects.
