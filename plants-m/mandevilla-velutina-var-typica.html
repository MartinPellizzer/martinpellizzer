Mandevilla velutina var. typica

Mandevilla velutina var. typica, commonly known as the velvet mandevilla, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its deep green, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant produces showy blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to fall. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade.

Mandevilla velutina var. typica is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to climb or trail.
