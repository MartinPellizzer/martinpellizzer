Masdevallia zebrina

Masdevallia zebrina, commonly known as the zebra orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green, strap-like leaves and its showy, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns resembling zebra stripes. The plant typically blooms in the summer, producing clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often grown in shaded areas with well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
