Morina persica subsp. turcica

Morina persica subsp.

turcica, commonly known as Turkish moringa, is a shrub native to parts of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal contexts.
