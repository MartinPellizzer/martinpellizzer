Monsonia biflora var. angustifolia

Monsonia biflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved monsonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinct reddish hue. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
