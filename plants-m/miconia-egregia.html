Miconia egregia

Miconia egregia, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. Miconia egregia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies, particularly in tropical ecosystems.
