Miconia angulata

Miconia angulata, commonly known as the angular miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by distinctive, woody fruits. Miconia angulata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage.
