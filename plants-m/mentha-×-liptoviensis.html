Mentha × liptoviensis

Mentha × liptoviensis, commonly known as the Liptovská štiavnica mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly menthol-like leaves and stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, producing a distinctive, pale purple to pink color.

Mentha × liptoviensis is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
