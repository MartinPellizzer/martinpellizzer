Masdevallia ayabacana

Masdevallia ayabacana, commonly known as the Ayabaca orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in high-altitude cloud forests. Its flowers are usually small and delicate, with a wide range of hues, including purple, pink, and white.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
