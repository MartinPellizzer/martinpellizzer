Mitchella ovata

Mitchella ovata, commonly known as littleleaf greenbriar, is a low-growing perennial vine native to eastern North America.

It features small, ovate leaves with a waxy coating and produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its trailing stems and is often found in moist, wooded areas. Its berries, which are red and edible, mature in the fall and are a food source for birds.

Mitchella ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
