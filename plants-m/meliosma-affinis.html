Meliosma affinis

Meliosma affinis, commonly known as the small-leafed meliosma, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other tropical trees. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface and a lighter, often pubescent underside. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, winged fruits.

Meliosma affinis is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.
