Micromeles alnifolia var. typica

Micromeles alnifolia var. typica, commonly known as the small-leaved alder.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks or in woodland edges.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
