Mentha insularis subsp. cinereovirens

Mentha insularis subsp.

cinereovirens, commonly known as the gray-green mint, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
