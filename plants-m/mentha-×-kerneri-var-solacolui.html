Mentha × kerneri var. solacolui

Mentha × kerneri var. solacolui, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves that are typically dark green with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
