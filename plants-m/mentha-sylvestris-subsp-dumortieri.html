Mentha sylvestris subsp. dumortieri

Mentha sylvestris subsp.

dumortieri, commonly known as the Brazilian mint, is a perennial herb native to South America. It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and square, hairy stems typical of the mint family.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds, making it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.
