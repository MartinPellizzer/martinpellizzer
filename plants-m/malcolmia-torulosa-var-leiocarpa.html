Malcolmia torulosa var. leiocarpa

Malcolmia torulosa var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
