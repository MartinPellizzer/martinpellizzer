Mikania acrensis

Mikania acrensis, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and vine-like habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Mikania acrensis is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
