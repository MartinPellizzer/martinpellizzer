Miersia stellata

Miersia stellata, commonly known as the starflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Miersia stellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
