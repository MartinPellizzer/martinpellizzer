Medicago schirjaewii

Medicago schirjaewii, commonly known as Schirjaew's clover, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, rounded pods containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Medicago schirjaewii is valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
