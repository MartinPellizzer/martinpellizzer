Machilus arisanensis

Machilus arisanensis, commonly known as the Formosan lauraceous tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Taiwan and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
