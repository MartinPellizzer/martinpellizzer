Medinilla ferrata

Medinilla ferrata, commonly known as the ironwood medinilla, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its thick, waxy leaves help it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Medinilla ferrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
