Myrtus versteeghii

Myrtus versteeghii, commonly known as the Cape myrtle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and leathery, evergreen leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Myrtus versteeghii is also valued for its essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.
