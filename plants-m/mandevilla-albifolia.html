Mandevilla albifolia

Mandevilla albifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered mandevilla, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil.

Mandevilla albifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
