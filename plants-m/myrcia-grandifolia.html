Myrcia grandifolia

Myrcia grandifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is known for its strong, spicy aroma.

Myrcia grandifolia is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
