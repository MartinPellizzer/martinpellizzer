Mendoncia cordata

Mendoncia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the stems are slender and branching.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
