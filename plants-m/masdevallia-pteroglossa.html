Masdevallia pteroglossa

Masdevallia pteroglossa, commonly known as the feather-tongue orchid, is a species native to the Andes of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with elongated, feather-like petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
