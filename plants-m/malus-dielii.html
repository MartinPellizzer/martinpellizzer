Malus dielii

Malus dielii, commonly known as the Diel's apple, is a deciduous tree native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in early spring. The tree produces small, edible apples that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Malus dielii is often cultivated for its ornamental beauty and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.
