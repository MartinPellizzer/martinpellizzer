Mesembryanthemum serotinum

Mesembryanthemum serotinum, commonly known as the late-flowering ice plant, is a succulent perennial native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its ability to store water in its thick leaves allows it to survive in drought conditions.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its low-growing habit and ornamental appeal.
