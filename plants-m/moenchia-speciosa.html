Moenchia speciosa

Moenchia speciosa, commonly known as the showy moenchia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tinge, adding to its ornamental value.

Moenchia speciosa is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
