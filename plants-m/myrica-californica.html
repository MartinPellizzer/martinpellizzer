Myrica californica

Myrica californica, commonly known as the California wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and chaparral habitats.

Myrica californica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
