Morus canadensis

Morus canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

The species is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
