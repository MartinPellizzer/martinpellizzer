Mesembryanthemum articulatum

Mesembryanthemum articulatum, commonly known as the skeletonflower, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, jointed stems and small, often blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in arid environments and is adapted to store water in its thick, segmented stems. Its leaves are typically reduced to small scales, which help minimize water loss.

Mesembryanthemum articulatum is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
