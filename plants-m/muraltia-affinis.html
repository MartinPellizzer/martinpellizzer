Muraltia affinis

Muraltia affinis, commonly known as the red mombasa bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Muraltia affinis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some garden settings.
