Myristica pendulina

Myristica pendulina, commonly known as the hanging myristica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences and fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark reddish-brown seeds enclosed in a hard, aromatic aril. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited economic use compared to other members of its family.
