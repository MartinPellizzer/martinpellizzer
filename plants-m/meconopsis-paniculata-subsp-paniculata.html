Meconopsis paniculata subsp. paniculata

Meconopsis paniculata subsp.

paniculata, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in a dense layer of soft, silky hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
