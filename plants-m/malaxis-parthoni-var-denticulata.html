Malaxis parthoni var. denticulata

Malaxis parthoni var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.

This orchid is relatively rare and requires specific conditions to flourish, making it a sought-after species among botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
