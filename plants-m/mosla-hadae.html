Mosla hadae

Mosla hadae, commonly known as the Kashmiri mosquito plant, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves, often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are typically blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mosla hadae is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits.
