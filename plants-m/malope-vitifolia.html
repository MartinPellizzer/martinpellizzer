Malope vitifolia

Malope vitifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf malope, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Malope vitifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
