Mammillaria scrippsiana var. typica

Mammillaria scrippsiana var. typica, commonly known as the Scrippsian mammillaria, is a small cactus native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a globular stem with prominent, white to pale yellow areoles and spines that are typically white or pale green. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red or orange fruits. It is well-suited for rock gardens and arid landscapes due to its compact size and drought tolerance.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
