Momordica meloniflora

Momordica meloniflora, commonly known as the horned melon, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, green fruit that resembles a horned melon. The plant produces large, yellow flowers that are followed by the unique, bumpy, green fruits. The fruit's outer skin is covered with sharp, spiny projections, which serve as a natural defense mechanism.

It is native to parts of Africa and is often cultivated for its edible, sweet, and tangy fruit.
