Memora pedunculata

Memora pedunculata, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
