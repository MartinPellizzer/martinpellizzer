Meisteria anonyma

Meisteria anonyma, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the wet tropical forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, leafless stems and its striking, pale green to white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant lacks true leaves, relying instead on its green, photosynthetic stems for nourishment. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in the canopy.

Due to its habitat specificity and sensitivity to environmental changes, Meisteria anonyma is considered critically endangered and is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
