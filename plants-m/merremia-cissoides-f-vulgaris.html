Merremia cissoides f. vulgaris

Merremia cissoides f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant has a woody stem with a fibrous texture and can grow up to several meters in length. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, though some varieties may have white or pink hues.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
