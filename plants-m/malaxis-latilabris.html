Malaxis latilabris

Malaxis latilabris, commonly known as the broad-lipped malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, showy flower with a broad, pale yellow lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch to its natural habitat.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
