Michelia apiculata

Michelia apiculata, commonly known as the Chinese bottletree, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged oppositely on the stem. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
