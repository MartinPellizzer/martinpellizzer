Molina milleflora

Molina milleflora, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of Chile.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in cool, high-altitude environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and hardy nature.

Molina milleflora is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
