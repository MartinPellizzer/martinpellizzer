Mauritia carana

Mauritia carana, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverine forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in many tropical regions for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
