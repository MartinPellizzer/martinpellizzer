Micranthes fusca var. kiusiana

Micranthes fusca var. kiusiana, commonly known as the Kiusian rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and the plant is noted for its resilience in harsh alpine conditions.
