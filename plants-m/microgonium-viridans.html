Microgonium viridans

Microgonium viridans, commonly known as the green microgonium, is a rare and delicate aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its small, green, filamentous structures that form dense mats on the surface of water. The plant lacks true roots and relies on its leaf-like structures for both photosynthesis and anchoring. Microgonium viridans is often found in slow-moving streams and ponds with high light penetration.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of water quality.
