Malus dulcissima var. subsessilis

Malus dulcissima var. subsessilis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a variety of the apple genus native to China.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and round, yellowish fruits that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
