Macroclinium larense

Macroclinium larense, commonly known as the Laren's spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance due to its elongated, slender flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
