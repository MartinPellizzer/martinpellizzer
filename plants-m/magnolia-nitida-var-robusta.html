Magnolia nitida var. robusta

Magnolia nitida var. robusta, commonly known as the glossy magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces large, showy white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming in early spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic qualities.
