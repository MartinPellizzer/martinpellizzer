Muraltia rubeacea

Muraltia rubeacea, commonly known as the red muraltia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Muraltia rubeacea thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
