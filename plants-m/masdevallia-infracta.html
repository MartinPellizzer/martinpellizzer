Masdevallia infracta

Masdevallia infracta, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically leathery and arranged in a rosette, which helps it retain moisture.

Masdevallia infracta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
