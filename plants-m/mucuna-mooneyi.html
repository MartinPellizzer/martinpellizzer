Mucuna mooneyi

Mucuna mooneyi, commonly known as the Mooney's velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant produces a significant amount of nitrogen through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria in its root nodules. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it blooms throughout the wet season.

Mucuna mooneyi is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
