Miconia laurina

Miconia laurina, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous small seeds.

Miconia laurina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its striking foliage and floral display.
