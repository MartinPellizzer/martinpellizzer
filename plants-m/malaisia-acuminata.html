Malaisia acuminata

Malaisia acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved piper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes numerous species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests.

Malaisia acuminata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
