Myrcia portoricensis

Myrcia portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests and mountainous regions.

Myrcia portoricensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
