Menichea rosata

Menichea rosata, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Menichea rosata requires well-draining soil and consistent humidity to flourish, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
