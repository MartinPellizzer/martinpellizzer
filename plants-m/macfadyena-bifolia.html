Macfadyena bifolia

Macfadyena bifolia, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is a woody vine characterized by its spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Macfadyena bifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid climates.
