Miconia panamensis

Miconia panamensis, commonly known as the Panama miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, native to the tropical regions of Central America. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and prominent veins. Its flowers are showy, with five pink to purple petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming during the wet season.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
