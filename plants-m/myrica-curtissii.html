Myrica curtissii

Myrica curtissii, commonly known as the California wax myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and chaparral habitats.

Myrica curtissii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
