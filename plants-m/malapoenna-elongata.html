Malapoenna elongata

Malapoenna elongata, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of trees and shrubs.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain toxic seeds.

Malapoenna elongata is native to southern Africa and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.
