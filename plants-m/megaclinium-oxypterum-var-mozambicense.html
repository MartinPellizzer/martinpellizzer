Megaclinium oxypterum var. mozambicense

Megaclinium oxypterum var. mozambicense, commonly known as the Mozambique giant grass, is a tall perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous stalks and large, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open savannas and along riverbanks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense clumps.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional construction and thatching in some regions.
