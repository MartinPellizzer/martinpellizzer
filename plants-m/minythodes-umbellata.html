Minythodes umbellata

Minythodes umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
