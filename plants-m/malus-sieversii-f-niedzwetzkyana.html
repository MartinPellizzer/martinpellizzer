Malus sieversii f. niedzwetzkyana

Malus sieversii f. niedzwetzkyana, commonly known as the Niedzwetzkyana apple, is a rare and ancient botanical variant of the wild apple.

It is native to the Tien Shan mountain range in Central Asia and is considered a genetic ancestor of many modern apple cultivars. This form exhibits unique morphological features, including a compact growth habit and distinctive leaf and fruit characteristics. Its botanical significance lies in its role in the domestication of apples and its genetic diversity.

The plant is often studied for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving apple resilience and flavor.
