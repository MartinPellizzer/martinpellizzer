Medicago arabica

Medicago arabica, commonly known as the Turkish clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often used as forage for livestock and has been historically valued for its medicinal properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
