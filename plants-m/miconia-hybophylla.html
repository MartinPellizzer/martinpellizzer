Miconia hybophylla

Miconia hybophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
