Miguelia annamica

Miguelia annamica, commonly known as the Annam mango, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into sweet, aromatic fruits with a thin, fibrous skin. Miguelia annamica thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and ample sunlight.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
