Mesembryanthemum calcaratum

Mesembryanthemum calcaratum, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that thrives in arid and rocky environments, often found in coastal areas. The plant features fleshy, ribbed stems and small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are typically covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Mesembryanthemum calcaratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
