Monolepis trifida

Monolepis trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed sand mat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in loose, sandy soils and access moisture from deeper underground.

Monolepis trifida is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to thrive in conditions of high temperature and low precipitation.
