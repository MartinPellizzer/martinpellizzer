Mentha × scharzfoldensis

Mentha × scharzfoldensis, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a hybrid mint species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a strong menthol scent. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its distinctive flavor and aroma.
