Morus mairei

Morus mairei, commonly known as the Maire mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, rough, bark. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.
