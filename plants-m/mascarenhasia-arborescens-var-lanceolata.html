Mascarenhasia arborescens var. lanceolata

Mascarenhasia arborescens var. lanceolata, commonly known as the Mascarene tree fern, is a species native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a tree fern characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in rocky or coastal soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
