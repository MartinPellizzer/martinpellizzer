Macroptilium brachycalyx

Macroptilium brachycalyx, commonly known as the short calyx cowpea, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its growth habit is typically prostrate or sprawling, making it suitable for ground cover in agricultural settings.

Additionally, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility.
