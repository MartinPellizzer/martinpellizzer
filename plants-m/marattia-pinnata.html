Marattia pinnata

Marattia pinnata, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a large, tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, tree-like fronds that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant has a distinctive pinnate leaf structure with long, slender pinnae. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Marattia pinnata plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
