Mertensia paniculata var. borealis

Mertensia paniculata var. borealis, commonly known as the northern bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in woodland or shaded areas. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
