Mertensia umbraculifera

Mertensia umbraculifera, commonly known as the shade-loving bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it ideal for woodland gardens. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Mertensia umbraculifera is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
