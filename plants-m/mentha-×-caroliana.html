Mentha × caroliana

Mentha × caroliana, commonly known as Carolina mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its square stems and opposite leaves are typical of the mint genus.

It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue to purple in color.
