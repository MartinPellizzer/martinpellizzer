Mimosa asperata var. scandens

Mimosa asperata var. scandens, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for dry, rocky habitats.

The hairy mimosa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
