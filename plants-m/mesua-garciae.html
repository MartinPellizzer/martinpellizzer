Mesua garciae

Mesua garciae, commonly known as the Philippine flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its canopy. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged oppositely on the branches. Mesua garciae thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.
