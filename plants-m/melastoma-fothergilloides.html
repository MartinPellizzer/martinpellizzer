Melastoma fothergilloides

Melastoma fothergilloides, commonly known as the Fothergill's melastoma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Melastomataceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
