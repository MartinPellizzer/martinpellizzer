Mentha rubricaulis

Mentha rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. Mentha rubricaulis is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
