Malpighia macrophylla

Malpighia macrophylla, commonly known as the Natal plum or Brazilian cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and can grow up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by edible, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C. Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
