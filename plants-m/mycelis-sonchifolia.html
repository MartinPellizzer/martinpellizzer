Mycelis sonchifolia

Mycelis sonchifolia, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Mycelis sonchifolia has a taproot system and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
