Manglietia aromatica

Manglietia aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant manglietia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, white flowers with a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant belongs to the family Magnoliaceae, which includes some of the most ancient flowering plants. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and emit a scent reminiscent of cinnamon or vanilla.

Manglietia aromatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
