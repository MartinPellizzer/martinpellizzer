Monocardia violacea

Monocardia violacea, commonly known as the violet heartleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually smooth and glossy, while its flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Monocardia violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and colorful blooms.
