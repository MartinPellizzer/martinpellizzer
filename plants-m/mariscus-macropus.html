Mariscus macropus

Mariscus macropus, commonly known as the broad-leaved mariscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
