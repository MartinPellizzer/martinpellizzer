Myrcia humboldtiana

Myrcia humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's myrtle, is a shrub native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia humboldtiana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
