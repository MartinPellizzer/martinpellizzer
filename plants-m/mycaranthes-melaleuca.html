Mycaranthes melaleuca

Mycaranthes melaleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered mycaranthes, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sunlight to thrive.
