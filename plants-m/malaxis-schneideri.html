Malaxis schneideri

Malaxis schneideri, commonly known as the Schneider's lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and a tall, slender stem that bears a cluster of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

Malaxis schneideri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
