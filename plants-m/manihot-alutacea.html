Manihot alutacea

Manihot alutacea, commonly known as the arrowleaf manioc, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and milky sap. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is often cultivated for its starchy roots, which are used in various culinary preparations.

Manihot alutacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage.
