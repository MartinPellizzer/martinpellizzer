Machaerium iquitosense

Machaerium iquitosense, commonly known as the Iquitos machaerium, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its roots have been used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
