Malvaviscus velutinus

Malvaviscus velutinus, commonly known as velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, which gives it its common name. Its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in the summer months.

The plant is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like hibiscus and cotton.
