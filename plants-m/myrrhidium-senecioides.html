Myrrhidium senecioides

Myrrhidium senecioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring a basal rosette of blue-gray leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive, fuzzy texture.

Myrrhidium senecioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
