Melothria punctatissima

Melothria punctatissima, commonly known as the African sour fig, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and gourds, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn yellow when mature, with a tart, acidic flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often hairy as well.

It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often used in local cuisines for its unique taste.
