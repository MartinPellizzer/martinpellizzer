Mecodium diversilabium

Mecodium diversilabium, commonly known as the variable mecodium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and it often grows in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare and found only in certain regions of the country.
