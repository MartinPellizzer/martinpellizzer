Mariscus tetragonocarpus

Mariscus tetragonocarpus, commonly known as the four-cornered fruit mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, four-angled fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid climates.
