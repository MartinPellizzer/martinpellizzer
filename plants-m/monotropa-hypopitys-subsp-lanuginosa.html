Monotropa hypopitys subsp. lanuginosa

Monotropa hypopitys subsp.

lanuginosa, commonly known as the hairy ghost plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, making it a myco-heterotroph that relies on fungal associations for nutrients.

The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its hairy, glandular stems and leaves are distinctive features.

It thrives in shaded, moist forest understories, often growing in association with specific fungi.
