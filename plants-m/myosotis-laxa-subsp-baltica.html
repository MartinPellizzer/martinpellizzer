Myosotis laxa subsp. baltica

Myosotis laxa subsp.

baltica, commonly known as the Baltic forget-me-not, is a perennial herb native to the cooler regions of Europe and western Siberia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
