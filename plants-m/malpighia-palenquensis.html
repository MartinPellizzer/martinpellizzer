Malpighia palenquensis

Malpighia palenquensis, commonly known as the Palenquense acorn or the Mexican beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly Mexico and parts of Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically in shaded or semi-shaded areas with well-drained soil.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color.
