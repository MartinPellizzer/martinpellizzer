Malacocarpus bezrucii var. centrispinus

Malacocarpus bezrucii var. centrispinus, commonly known as the hairy podded senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
