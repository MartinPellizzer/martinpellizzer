Myrcia lepida

Myrcia lepida, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains several seeds.
