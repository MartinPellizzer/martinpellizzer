Machaerium nicaraguense

Machaerium nicaraguense, commonly known as the Nicaragua grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and has slender, erect stems with small, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Machaerium nicaraguense is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
