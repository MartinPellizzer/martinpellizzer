Moschifera

Moschifera, commonly known as the musk orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their intricate, often fragrant flowers that resemble orchids in structure. Moschifera species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Their leaves are usually broad and grass-like, while their roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The genus is notable for its unique floral morphology and ecological significance in its native habitats.
