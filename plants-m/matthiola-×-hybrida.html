Matthiola × hybrida

Matthiola × hybrida, commonly known as garden mustards, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically produces small, elongated pods containing seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Matthiola × hybrida is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
