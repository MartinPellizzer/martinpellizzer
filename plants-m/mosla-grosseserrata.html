Mosla grosseserrata

Mosla grosseserrata, commonly known as narrow-leaf mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from blue to purple, blooming in summer.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
