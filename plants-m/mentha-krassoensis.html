Mentha krassoensis

Mentha krassoensis, commonly known as the meadow mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a strong, minty fragrance. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Mentha krassoensis is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
