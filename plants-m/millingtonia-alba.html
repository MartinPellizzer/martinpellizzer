Millingtonia alba

Millingtonia alba, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white, lantern-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
