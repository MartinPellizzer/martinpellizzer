Maba crassinervis

Maba crassinervis, commonly known as the broad-veined maba, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
