Mosla dianthera var. nana

Mosla dianthera var. nana, commonly known as dwarf Chinese chive, is a compact, herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It features small, slender leaves that resemble those of chives, and it produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers in the summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild, garlic-like flavor, making it suitable for culinary and garden use. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

Its short stature and easy care make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
