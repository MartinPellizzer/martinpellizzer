Messerschmidia spigelliflora

Messerschmidia spigelliflora, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
