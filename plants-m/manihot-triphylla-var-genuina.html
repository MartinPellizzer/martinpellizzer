Manihot triphylla var. genuina

Manihot triphylla var. genuina, commonly known as the three-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, starchy tubers. These tubers are often used as a root vegetable in various cuisines.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and agriculturally significant in its native regions.
