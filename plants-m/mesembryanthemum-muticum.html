Mesembryanthemum muticum

Mesembryanthemum muticum, commonly known as the Ice Plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a unique ability to exude a gel-like substance when damaged, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.
