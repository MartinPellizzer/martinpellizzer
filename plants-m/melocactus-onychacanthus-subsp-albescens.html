Melocactus onychacanthus subsp. albescens

Melocactus onychacanthus subsp.

albescens, commonly known as the white-spined melocactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces a single, elongated yellow flower that blooms at the top of the stem during the spring season. Its spines are short and white, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.
