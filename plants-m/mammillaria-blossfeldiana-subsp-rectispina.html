Mammillaria blossfeldiana subsp. rectispina

Mammillaria blossfeldiana subsp.

rectispina, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in small, white to pale blue spines.

The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light.

This subspecies is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
