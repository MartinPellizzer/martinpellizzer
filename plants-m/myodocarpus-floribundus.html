Myodocarpus floribundus

Myodocarpus floribundus, commonly known as the flowering myodocarpus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical regions.
