Mammillaria petrophila subsp. arida

Mammillaria petrophila subsp.

arida, commonly known as the arid mammillaria, is a cactus native to arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its small, globular stems covered in white, woolly spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
