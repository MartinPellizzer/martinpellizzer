Mentha × aquaticorubra

Mentha × aquaticorubra, commonly known as the water mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in whorls. The plant thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and ponds. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinct minty fragrance.

Mentha × aquaticorubra is often used in herbal remedies and as a decorative plant in wetland gardens.
