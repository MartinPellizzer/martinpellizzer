Matthiola sinuata subsp. ligurica

Matthiola sinuata subsp.

ligurica, commonly known as the Ligurian stock, is a herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of Italy. It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in clusters.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
