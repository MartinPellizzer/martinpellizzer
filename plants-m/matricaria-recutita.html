Matricaria recutita

Matricaria recutita, commonly known as chamomile, is an annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, and its leaves are finely dissected. The plant is cultivated for its aromatic oils, which are extracted from the flowers. Chamomile thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in temperate climates.

It is widely used in herbal medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
