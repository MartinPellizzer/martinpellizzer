Muscari discolor

Muscari discolor, commonly known as the white musk grape or the pink musk grape, is a bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllis family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a fragrant, musk-like scent, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom. Muscari discolor typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique fragrance.
