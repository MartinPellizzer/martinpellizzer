Medicago falcata var. irghisica

Medicago falcata var. irghisica, commonly known as the Irghisica goat's rue, is a variant of the goat's rue species native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and semi-arid environments.

This variant is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
