Mammillaria sinistrohamata

Mammillaria sinistrohamata, commonly known as the left-handed mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, radial spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to cactus collections.
