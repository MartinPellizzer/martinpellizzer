Mentha densifoliata

Mentha densifoliata, commonly known as the dense-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves that are densely packed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in spikes, which bloom in late summer. Mentha densifoliata is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a versatile plant in both horticultural and medicinal contexts.
