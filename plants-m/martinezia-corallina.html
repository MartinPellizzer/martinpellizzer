Martinezia corallina

Martinezia corallina, commonly known as the coral milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking coral-pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces a milky sap that is typical of the milkweed family. Martinezia corallina thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
