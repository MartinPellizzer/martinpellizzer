Myrcia tepuiensis

Myrcia tepuiensis, commonly known as the tepui myrtle, is a shrub native to the tepuis of Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they are borne in clusters at the ends of the branches.

The species is adapted to the unique high-altitude ecosystems of the tepuis, where it thrives in moist, shaded conditions.
