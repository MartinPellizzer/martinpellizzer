Melica × chrysolepis

Melica × chrysolepis, commonly known as the golden beardgrass, is a hybrid species within the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, golden-hued inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Melica × chrysolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
