Myrcia poeppigiana

Myrcia poeppigiana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Myrcia poeppigiana is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
