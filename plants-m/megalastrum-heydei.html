Megalastrum heydei

Megalastrum heydei, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its enormous, broad leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are large and showy, typically appearing in white or pink hues, and they bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators. Its rhizomes grow extensively beneath the water, anchoring the plant and allowing it to spread across water surfaces.

Due to its size and ecological impact, it plays a significant role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
