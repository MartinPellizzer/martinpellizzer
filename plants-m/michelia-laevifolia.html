Michelia laevifolia

Michelia laevifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Michelia laevifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
