Microstachys acetosella

Microstachys acetosella, commonly known as lesser calamity, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or white. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually less than 10 centimeters long and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in nutrient-poor soils.
