Malapoenna macrophylla

Malapoenna macrophylla, commonly known as the giant river sedge, is a tall, perennial herb native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found along riverbanks and in swampy areas.

Malapoenna macrophylla plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
