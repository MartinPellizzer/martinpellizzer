Mentzelia pumila var. integra

Mentzelia pumila var. integra, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy, glandular stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional medicine.
