Mentha viridis f. latifolia

Mentha viridis f. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved mint, is a variant of the common mint plant.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their high menthol content.
