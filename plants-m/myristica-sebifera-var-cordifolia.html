Myristica sebifera var. cordifolia

Myristica sebifera var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-shaped nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, heart-shaped leaves and small, reddish fruits. The plant produces a valuable seed known as nutmeg, which is encased in a yellow, fleshy aril. Its essential oils and resin have been used traditionally for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its economic value.
