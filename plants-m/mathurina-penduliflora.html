Mathurina penduliflora

Mathurina penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences that bear showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers, which are usually pink or purple, attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
