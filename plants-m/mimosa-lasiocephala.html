Mimosa lasiocephala

Mimosa lasiocephala, commonly known as the woolly mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy environments.

The leaves are sensitive to touch, curling when disturbed, a trait that adds to its unique appearance.
