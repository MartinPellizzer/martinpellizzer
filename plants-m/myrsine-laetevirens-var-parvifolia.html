Myrsine laetevirens var. parvifolia

Myrsine laetevirens var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved myrsine, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a small, fleshy berry containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
