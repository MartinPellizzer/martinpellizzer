Melanthesopsis patens var. vulgaris

Melanthesopsis patens var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common melanthus, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that have a slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in loose clusters and bloom in early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
