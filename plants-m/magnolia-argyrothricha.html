Magnolia argyrothricha

Magnolia argyrothricha, commonly known as the silver-leaf magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving the tree its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks for its striking floral display.
