Malva luzonica

Malva luzonica, commonly known as the Luzon mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves. It typically grows in moist, shady areas and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Malva luzonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
