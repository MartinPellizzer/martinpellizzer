Moquilea magnifructa

Moquilea magnifructa, commonly known as the giant fruit tree, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
