Mentha longifolia var. huteri

Mentha longifolia var. huteri, commonly known as Huter's mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
