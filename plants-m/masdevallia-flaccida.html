Masdevallia flaccida

Masdevallia flaccida, commonly known as the drooping masdevallia, is a tropical orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pendant-like inflorescence and delicate, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

Masdevallia flaccida is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
