Menispermum limacia

Menispermum limacia, commonly known as the climbing menispermum, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its roots contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.

The species is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
