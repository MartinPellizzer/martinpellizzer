Mentha arvensis var. subglabra

Mentha arvensis var. subglabra, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in cultivated fields.

Field mint is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
