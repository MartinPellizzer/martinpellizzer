Modiola fissistipula

Modiola fissistipula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines and clusters of small, yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and botanical collections.
