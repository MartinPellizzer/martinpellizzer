Monarda comata

Monarda comata, commonly known as white beardgrass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Monarda comata is often found in open woods, meadows, and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
