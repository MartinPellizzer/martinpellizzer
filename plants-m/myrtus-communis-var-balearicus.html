Myrtus communis var. balearicus

Myrtus communis var. balearicus, commonly known as the Balearic myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves with a slightly aromatic scent, and its flowers are typically pale pink or white. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
