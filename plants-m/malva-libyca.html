Malva libyca

Malva libyca, commonly known as the Libyan mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North Africa and the Mediterranean region.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its robust stems and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.

Malva libyca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
