Microchirita involucrata

Microchirita involucrata, commonly known as the woolly microchirita, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded, moist forest undergrowth. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs that help reduce water loss and protect against herbivory.

Microchirita involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
