Melodinus velutinus

Melodinus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet trumpet, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruits are follicles that contain seeds with a tuft of hair for wind dispersal.

Melodinus velutinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
