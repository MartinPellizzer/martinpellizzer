Malva cathayensis

Malva cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Malva cathayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
