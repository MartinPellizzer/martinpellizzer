Myristica fissiflora subsp. fissiflora

Myristica fissiflora subsp.

fissiflora, commonly known as the fragrant nutmeg tree, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers.

The plant produces distinctive red, fleshy fruits that enclose the aromatic seeds used in spice production. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its economic value, it is often cultivated for its nutmeg-like seeds and essential oils.
