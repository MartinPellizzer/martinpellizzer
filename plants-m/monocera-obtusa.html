Monocera obtusa

Monocera obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers bloom during the summer months and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
