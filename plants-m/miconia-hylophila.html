Miconia hylophila

Miconia hylophila, commonly known as the Hawaiian miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds, contributing to its ecological role in the region. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other tropical regions, where it can become invasive.
