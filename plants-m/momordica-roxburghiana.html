Momordica roxburghiana

Momordica roxburghiana, commonly known as the Indian bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucumbers. The plant produces elongated, warty fruits that are typically green when immature and turn orange-red when mature. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it has yellow flowers that are both male and female.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in various cuisines and traditional medicine.
