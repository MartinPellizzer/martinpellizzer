Myristica pandurifolia

Myristica pandurifolia, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and develops dark, aromatic nuts containing the spice nutmeg. Its seeds are encased in a fibrous aril, which is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its economic and medicinal properties, making it an important species in both local and global markets.
