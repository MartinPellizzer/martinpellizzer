Mentha brideliana

Mentha brideliana, commonly known as the Cape Mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their cooling and antiseptic properties.

Mentha brideliana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
