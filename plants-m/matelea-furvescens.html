Matelea furvescens

Matelea furvescens, commonly known as the golden flame vine, is a climbing plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in arid regions.
