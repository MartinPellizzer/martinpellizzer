Mecranium integrifolium

Mecranium integrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaf mecranium, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and unlobed, with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil and partial shade.
