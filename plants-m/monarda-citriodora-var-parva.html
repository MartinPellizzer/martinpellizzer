Monarda citriodora var. parva

Monarda citriodora var. parva, commonly known as lemon bee balm, is a compact variant of the lemon bee balm plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that emit a strong, citrus-like fragrance when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial for gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for borders and containers.
