Marshallia graminifolia subsp. tenuifolia

Marshallia graminifolia subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf marsh marigold, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and along stream banks. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.

Its subspecies status highlights genetic differences from the broader Marshallia graminifolia species.
