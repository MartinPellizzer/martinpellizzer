Melandrium rubrum subsp. lapponicum

Melandrium rubrum subsp.

lapponicum, commonly known as the Lapponian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, pinkish-white flowers clustered at the ends of its stems.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves and is often found in moist, sandy or rocky soils. It blooms from June to August and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of alpine and arctic vegetation.
