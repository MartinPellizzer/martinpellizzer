Myrcia neosuaveolens

Myrcia neosuaveolens, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its fragrant, violet-blue berries that are clustered in pairs, which are a distinctive feature of the species. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and guava. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge, while its flowers are small and white, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
