Matonia

Matonia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid regions. The plants typically have succulent leaves and produce showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Matonia species are often used in gardens for their ornamental value and drought tolerance.

They are also valued for their ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
