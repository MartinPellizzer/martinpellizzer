Muhlenbergia distichophylla var. mutica

Muhlenbergia distichophylla var. mutica, commonly known as the silky muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
