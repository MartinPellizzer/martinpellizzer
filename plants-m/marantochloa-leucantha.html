Marantochloa leucantha

Marantochloa leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered stiltgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes various ornamental and sometimes invasive species. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Marantochloa leucantha is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
