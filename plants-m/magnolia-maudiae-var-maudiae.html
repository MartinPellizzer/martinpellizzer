Magnolia maudiae var. maudiae

Magnolia maudiae var. maudiae, commonly known as the Saucer Magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and botanical collections.
