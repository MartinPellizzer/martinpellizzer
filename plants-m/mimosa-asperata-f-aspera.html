Mimosa asperata f. aspera

Mimosa asperata f. aspera, commonly known as the rough mimosa, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
