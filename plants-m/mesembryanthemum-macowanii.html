Mesembryanthemum macowanii

Mesembryanthemum macowanii, commonly known as the Cape Hottentot, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to succulent collections.
