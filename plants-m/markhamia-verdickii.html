Markhamia verdickii

Markhamia verdickii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the late afternoon. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

Markhamia verdickii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
