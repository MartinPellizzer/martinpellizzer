Mutisia inflexa var. imberbis

Mutisia inflexa var. imberbis, commonly known as the woolly mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
