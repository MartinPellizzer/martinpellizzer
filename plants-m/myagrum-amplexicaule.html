Myagrum amplexicaule

Myagrum amplexicaule, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Australia and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or climbing growth habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around stems. The plant produces showy, hibiscus-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming throughout the warm season. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, and it is often found in moist, sunny environments.

Myagrum amplexicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
