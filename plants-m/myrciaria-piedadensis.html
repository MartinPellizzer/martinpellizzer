Myrciaria piedadensis

Myrciaria piedadensis, commonly known as the Piedad myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in local traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its fragrant foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
