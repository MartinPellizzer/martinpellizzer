Merciera tenuifolia var. azurea

Merciera tenuifolia var. azurea, commonly known as the Azureleaf Merciera, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically a vibrant blue-green color, which is due to the presence of specialized pigments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of white or pale blue, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and botanical collections.
