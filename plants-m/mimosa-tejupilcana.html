Mimosa tejupilcana

Mimosa tejupilcana, commonly known as the Mexican mimosa, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its small, rounded pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in natural habitats.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
