Mandevilla kalmiifolia

Mandevilla kalmiifolia, commonly known as the Kalmiopsis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. It is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a subject of conservation efforts in its native range.
