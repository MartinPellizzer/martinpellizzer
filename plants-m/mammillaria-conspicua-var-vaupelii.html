Mammillaria conspicua var. vaupelii

Mammillaria conspicua var. vaupelii, commonly known as the Vaupel's hedgehog cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular or cylindrical stem with prominent, white to yellowish tubercles that give it a distinctive hedgehog-like appearance. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a prized specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
