Mappa velutina

Mappa velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, often clustered in terminal spikes.

Mappa velutina is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth rate, making it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed.
