Microtrema opulentum

Microtrema opulentum, commonly known as the opulent mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily inhabits the branches of specific host trees, such as oaks and pines.

It possesses scale-like leaves that are adapted for a photosynthetic lifestyle, though it relies on its host for essential nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered together, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that attract birds for seed dispersal. Its growth is slow, and it forms dense, bushy clusters on the host, often leading to structural weakening of the tree over time.

Microtrema opulentum is notable for its ornamental appearance and its ecological role in supporting bird populations.
