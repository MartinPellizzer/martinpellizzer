Morus alba subvar. viridis

Morus alba subvar. viridis, commonly known as the green mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and as a shade tree.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is also used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
