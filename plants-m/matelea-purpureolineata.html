Matelea purpureolineata

Matelea purpureolineata, commonly known as the purple-line mescal bean, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, purple flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional practices. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native range.
