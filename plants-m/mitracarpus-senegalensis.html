Mitracarpus senegalensis

Mitracarpus senegalensis, commonly known as African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring upright stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters at the top of the stems. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Mitracarpus senegalensis is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
