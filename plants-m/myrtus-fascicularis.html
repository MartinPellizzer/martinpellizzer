Myrtus fascicularis

Myrtus fascicularis, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that are often fragrant, and it produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries. Myrtus fascicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in hedges or as a decorative plant in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
