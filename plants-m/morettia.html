Morettia

Morettia, commonly known as the "Bluebell of the Andes," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Amaryllidaceae.

It comprises about 15 species, predominantly found in South America, particularly in the Andean regions. The plants are characterized by their striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers, which bloom in spring. Morettia species are herbaceous perennials, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

They are valued for their ornamental beauty and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant floral displays.
