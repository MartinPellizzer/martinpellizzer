Musa luteola

Musa luteola, commonly known as the yellow banana, is a species of banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, yellow-orange pseudostem and produces small, elongated bananas that are typically harvested when they are still immature. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. It is cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a mild flavor and is often used in local cuisines.

The yellow banana is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant foliage and unique fruit structure.
