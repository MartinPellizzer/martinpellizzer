Muscari moschatum

Muscari moschatum, commonly known as the sweet grass or musk grape, is a bulbous perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or purple. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are grass-like in appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
