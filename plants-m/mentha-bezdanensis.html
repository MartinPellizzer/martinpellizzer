Mentha bezdanensis

Mentha bezdanensis, commonly known as the Balkan mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, refreshing mint aroma.

Mentha bezdanensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
