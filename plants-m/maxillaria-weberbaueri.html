Maxillaria weberbaueri

Maxillaria weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly found in countries like Brazil and Peru.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria weberbaueri is appreciated for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
