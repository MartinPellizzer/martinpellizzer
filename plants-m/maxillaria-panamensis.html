Maxillaria panamensis

Maxillaria panamensis, commonly known as the Panama orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often short-lived but emit a sweet scent to attract pollinators.

Maxillaria panamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
