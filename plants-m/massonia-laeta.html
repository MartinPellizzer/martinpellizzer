Massonia laeta

Massonia laeta, commonly known as the silverleaf massonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, also known as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an attractive and low-maintenance ornamental plant.
