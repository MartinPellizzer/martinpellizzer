Marsea spartioides

Marsea spartioides, commonly known as the coastal spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand coastal conditions.

Marsea spartioides is well-suited to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrublands.
