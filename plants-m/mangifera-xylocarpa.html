Mangifera xylocarpa

Mangifera xylocarpa, commonly known as the wild mango or kathal mango, is a tropical fruit-bearing tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, rough, greenish-yellow fruit with a thick, fibrous skin. The fruit contains a sweet, aromatic pulp surrounded by a layer of white, spongy tissue. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and has traditional uses in various cultures.
