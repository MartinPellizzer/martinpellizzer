Masdevallia woolwardiae

Masdevallia woolwardiae, commonly known as Woolward's Masdevallia, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often iridescent flowers that display a range of colors, including purple, red, and white. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its unique floral structure and vibrant hues make it a prized species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
