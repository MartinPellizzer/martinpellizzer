Magnolia delavayi

Magnolia delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on the upper surface. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical gardens.
