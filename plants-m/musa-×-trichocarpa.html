Musa × trichocarpa

Musa × trichocarpa, commonly known as the African banana, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces clusters of fruit that are typically smaller and more elongated compared to common bananas. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and is adapted to warm, humid climates. It has a robust rhizome system that allows it to spread vegetatively, making it an important crop in some regions.

However, it is less commonly cultivated than other banana varieties due to its lower yield and specific growing requirements.
