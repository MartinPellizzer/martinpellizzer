Mammillaria amajacensis

Mammillaria amajacensis, commonly known as the Mexican hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and typically features white or pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
