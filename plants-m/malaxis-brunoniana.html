Malaxis brunoniana

Malaxis brunoniana, commonly known as the Brunoniana orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to aid in nutrient absorption.

The orchid's floral structure includes a spurred lip, a distinctive feature among orchids.
