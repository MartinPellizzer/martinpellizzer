Medicago ciliaris var. apiculata

Medicago ciliaris var. apiculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf clover, is a perennial forage legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant has a deep root system, which helps in nitrogen fixation and soil stabilization. Its leaves are rich in protein and are used in livestock feed.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in marginal soils and its role in ecological restoration projects.
