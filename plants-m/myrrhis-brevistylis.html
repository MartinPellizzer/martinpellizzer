Myrrhis brevistylis

Myrrhis brevistylis, commonly known as the narrow-styled myrrh, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.
