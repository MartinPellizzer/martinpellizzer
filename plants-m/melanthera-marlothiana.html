Melanthera marlothiana

Melanthera marlothiana, commonly known as the marloth's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy purple to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Melanthera marlothiana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
