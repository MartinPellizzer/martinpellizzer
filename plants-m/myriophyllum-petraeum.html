Myriophyllum petraeum

Myriophyllum petraeum, commonly known as the rock milfoil, is a semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often used in water gardens and pond ecosystems for its ornamental value and ability to improve water quality.

Myriophyllum petraeum is also known for its ability to tolerate a range of water conditions, making it a resilient aquatic plant.
