Mimusops multinervis

Mimusops multinervis, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The flowers are small, fragrant, and clustered in panicles, typically yellow or white in color. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and construction.
