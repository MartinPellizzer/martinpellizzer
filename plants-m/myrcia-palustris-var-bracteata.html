Myrcia palustris var. bracteata

Myrcia palustris var. bracteata, commonly known as the swamp myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and forest understories.

Myrcia palustris var. bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
