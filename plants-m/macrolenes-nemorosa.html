Macrolenes nemorosa

Macrolenes nemorosa, commonly known as the woodland geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to woodland habitats in Europe.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of diverse ecosystems.
