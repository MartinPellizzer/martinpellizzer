Mayaca vandellii

Mayaca vandellii, commonly known as the Vandell's mayaca, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Mayaca vandellii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
