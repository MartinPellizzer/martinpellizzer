Myrcia eximia

Myrcia eximia, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Myrcia eximia produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
