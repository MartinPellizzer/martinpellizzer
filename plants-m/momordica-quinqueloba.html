Momordica quinqueloba

Momordica quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves and spiny, pale green fruits that mature to a bright orange color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a bitter taste. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is part of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and watermelons.
