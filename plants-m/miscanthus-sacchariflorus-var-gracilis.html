Miscanthus sacchariflorus var. gracilis

Miscanthus sacchariflorus var. gracilis, commonly known as slender silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching blades that are typically silver-white in color, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces fluffy, feathery panicles that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
