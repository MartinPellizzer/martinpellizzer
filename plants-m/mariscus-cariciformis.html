Mariscus cariciformis

Mariscus cariciformis, commonly known as the sedge rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Mariscus cariciformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
