Myrcia neobuxifolia

Myrcia neobuxifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release the seeds.
