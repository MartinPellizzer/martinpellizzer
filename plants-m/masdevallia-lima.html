Masdevallia lima

Masdevallia lima, commonly known as Lima orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that resemble miniature orchids, with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Masdevallia lima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
