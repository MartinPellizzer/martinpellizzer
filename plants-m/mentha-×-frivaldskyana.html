Mentha × frivaldskyana

Mentha × frivaldskyana, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Mentha aquatica and Mentha rotundifolia, resulting in a plant with aromatic leaves and strong menthol scent. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring square stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water edges.

Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple, appearing in whorls along the stems.
