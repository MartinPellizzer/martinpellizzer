Maytenus lepidota var. kilimandscharica

Maytenus lepidota var. kilimandscharica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro mayten, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand dry conditions.
