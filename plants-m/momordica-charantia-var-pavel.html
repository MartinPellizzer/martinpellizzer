Momordica charantia var. pavel

Momordica charantia var. pavel, commonly known as bitter melon, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ridged, and bitter-tasting fruits, which are typically harvested when immature. The plant produces yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in Asian cuisine and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
