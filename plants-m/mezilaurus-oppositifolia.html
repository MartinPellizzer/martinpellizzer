Mezilaurus oppositifolia

Mezilaurus oppositifolia, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to southern Africa.

It features pinnate leaves with opposite leaflets, giving it the species name *oppositifolia*. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
