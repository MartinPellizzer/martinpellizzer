Mentha mexicana

Mentha mexicana, commonly known as Mexican mint, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their refreshing and cooling properties.

Mentha mexicana is also valued in culinary applications, particularly in Mexican cuisine.
