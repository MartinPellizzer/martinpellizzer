Miconia monocephala

Miconia monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a distinct reddish or purple underside. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various bird species.

Miconia monocephala is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and can grow up to several meters in height.
