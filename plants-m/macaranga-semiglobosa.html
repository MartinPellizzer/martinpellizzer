Macaranga semiglobosa

Macaranga semiglobosa, commonly known as the globular mallow, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by three-lobed, hairy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Macaranga semiglobosa is also noted for its role in supporting local biodiversity and its use in traditional medicine.
