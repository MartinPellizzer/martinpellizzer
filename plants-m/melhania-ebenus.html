Melhania ebenus

Melhania ebenus, commonly known as the African ebony tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is often found in moist lowland forests and is known for its ecological and economic importance in its native region.
