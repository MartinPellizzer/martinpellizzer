Matricaria subglobosa

Matricaria subglobosa, commonly known as the globe chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, white flower heads that resemble small globes, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Matricaria subglobosa is often used in herbal medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
