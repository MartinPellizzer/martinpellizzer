Myrtus glomerata

Myrtus glomerata, commonly known as common myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but typically used in culinary and medicinal applications. Myrtus glomerata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
