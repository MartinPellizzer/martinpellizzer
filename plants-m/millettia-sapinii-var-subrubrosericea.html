Millettia sapinii var. subrubrosericea

Millettia sapinii var. subrubrosericea, commonly known as the red-haired millettia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or reddish-brown hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

This species is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in forested or semi-shaded environments.
