Massonia comata

Massonia comata, commonly known as the woolly massonia, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in spring. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

Massonia comata is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
