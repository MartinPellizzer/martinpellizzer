Mazus novaezeelandiae

Mazus novaezeelandiae, commonly known as New Zealand mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest floors or along stream banks.

Mazus novaezeelandiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
