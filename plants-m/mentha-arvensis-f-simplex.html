Mentha arvensis f. simplex

Mentha arvensis f. simplex, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a hybrid form of Mentha arvensis, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and strong aromatic scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its use in herbal medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
