Myrcia balbisiana

Myrcia balbisiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are borne in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
