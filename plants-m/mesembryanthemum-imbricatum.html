Mesembryanthemum imbricatum

Mesembryanthemum imbricatum, commonly known as the sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
