Manulea conferta

Manulea conferta, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
