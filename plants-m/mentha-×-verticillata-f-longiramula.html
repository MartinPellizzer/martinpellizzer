Mentha × verticillata f. longiramula

Mentha × verticillata f. longiramula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watermint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
