Mesua sclerophylla

Mesua sclerophylla, commonly known as the silky mesua, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Mesua sclerophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
