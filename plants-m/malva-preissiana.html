Malva preissiana

Malva preissiana, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability.

Malva preissiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
