Merremia tuberosa

Merremia tuberosa, commonly known as the morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces a milky sap and has a robust root system that can become tuberous. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Merremia tuberosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
