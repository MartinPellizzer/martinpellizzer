Mantisia saltatoria

Mantisia saltatoria, commonly known as the jumping plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It features a rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Mantisia saltatoria is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
