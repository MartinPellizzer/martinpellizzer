Mentha stenantha

Mentha stenantha, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and appear in whorls along the stem.

Mentha stenantha is known for its strong mint scent and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
