Melica montevidensis

Melica montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in Uruguay and parts of Argentina. This perennial grass typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological research.
