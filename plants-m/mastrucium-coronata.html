Mastrucium coronata

Mastrucium coronata, commonly known as the Crowned Mastrucium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble a crown, consisting of numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a glossy appearance.

The flowers are predominantly white or pale yellow, with a subtle scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
