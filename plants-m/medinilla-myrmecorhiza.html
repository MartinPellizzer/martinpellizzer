Medinilla myrmecorhiza

Medinilla myrmecorhiza, commonly known as the ant-plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant forms a unique symbiotic relationship with ants, providing them with shelter and food in exchange for protection against herbivores. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
