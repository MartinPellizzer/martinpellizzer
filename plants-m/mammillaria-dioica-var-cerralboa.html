Mammillaria dioica var. cerralboa

Mammillaria dioica var. cerralboa, commonly known as the Cerralbo mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of small, white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
