Metastelma thalamosiphon

Metastelma thalamosiphon, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often followed by orange-red berries. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
