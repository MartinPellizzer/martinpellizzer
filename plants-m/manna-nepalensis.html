Manna nepalensis

Manna nepalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan manna, is a deciduous shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance called manna, which has been traditionally used in folk medicine. Its growth habit is adapted to cold, mountainous environments, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its unique properties and ecological significance, it is an important species in the alpine flora of the region.
