Morinda angustifolia

Morinda angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf morinda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional use.
