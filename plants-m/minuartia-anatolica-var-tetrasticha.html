Minuartia anatolica var. tetrasticha

Minuartia anatolica var. tetrasticha, commonly known as the Anatolian rock-cress, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and ecological significance.
