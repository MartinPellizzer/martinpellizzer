Mertensia paniculata var. subcordata

Mertensia paniculata var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and heart-shaped at the base, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable plant for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
