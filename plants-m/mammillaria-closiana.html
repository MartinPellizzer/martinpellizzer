Mammillaria closiana

Mammillaria closiana, commonly known as the Closiana prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white, fuzzy spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Mammillaria closiana is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
