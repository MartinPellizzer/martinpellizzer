Microstylis circea

Microstylis circea, commonly known as the narrow-styled circea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically features wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ericaceae family.

Microstylis circea thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and bogs.
