Myristica malaccensis

Myristica malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca nut or yellow nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruit that contains a valuable seed kernel. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. The tree is cultivated for its seeds, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional and commercial contexts.
