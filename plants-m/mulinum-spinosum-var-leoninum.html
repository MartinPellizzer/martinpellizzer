Mulinum spinosum var. leoninum

Mulinum spinosum var. leoninum, commonly known as the Leonine thimbleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of its stems, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Apiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and ecological significance.
