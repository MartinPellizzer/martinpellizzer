Myrcia dictyophylla

Myrcia dictyophylla, commonly known as the fragrant myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface and a strong, spicy scent.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
