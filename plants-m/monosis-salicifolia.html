Monosis salicifolia

Monosis salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf monosis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are lanceolate and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color, which appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Monosis salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
