Malus turbinoides

Malus turbinoides, commonly known as the turbinated apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically yellow or red in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Malus turbinoides is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
