Melocanna zollingeri var. longispiculata

Melocanna zollingeri var. longispiculata, commonly known as the long-spined bamboo, is a variant of the Melocanna zollingeri species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, forming dense clumps. The leaves are typically elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance. This plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by the development of small, round fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique appearance and structural form.
