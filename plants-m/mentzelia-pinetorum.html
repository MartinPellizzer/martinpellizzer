Mentzelia pinetorum

Mentzelia pinetorum, commonly known as the pine dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as pine woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant produces yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Mentzelia pinetorum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
