Mesembryanthemum bicolor

Mesembryanthemum bicolor, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range from pink to red. The plant thrives in arid and coastal environments due to its ability to store water in its thick, succulent tissues. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is known for its unique adaptations to dry climates.

The flowers often open in response to sunlight, a trait known as heliotropism.
