Myroxylon infestum

Myroxylon infestum, commonly known as the pink ivory tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale pink bark and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree is renowned for its valuable resin, which is used to make a type of ivory-like material. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
