Matthiola longipetala subsp. livida

Matthiola longipetala subsp.

livida, commonly known as the purple wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is closely related to other Matthiola species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
