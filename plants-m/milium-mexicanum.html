Milium mexicanum

Milium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican millet, is a flowering plant species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Milium mexicanum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
