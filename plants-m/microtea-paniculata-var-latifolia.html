Microtea paniculata var. latifolia

Microtea paniculata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf microtea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with opposite leaf arrangement.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
