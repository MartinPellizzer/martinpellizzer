Mahernia grandiflora

Mahernia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered mahernia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust root system and upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in arid environments.

Mahernia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
