Mattia lanata var. canescens

Mattia lanata var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly mattia or hairy mattia, is a variant of the Mattia lanata species characterized by its dense, soft, and woolly foliage.

This plant belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill, and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. Its leaves are typically gray-green in color due to the presence of fine, white hairs that cover the surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
