Melocactus curvispinus subsp. loboguerreroi

Melocactus curvispinus subsp.

loboguerreroi, commonly known as the Loboguerrero melocactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are often adorned with prominent, hooked spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a vibrant contrast to its otherwise spiky appearance. It thrives in arid environments with well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
