Mapania baccifera subsp. manusensis

Mapania baccifera subsp.

manusensis, commonly known as the Manus mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Manus in Papua New Guinea. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical rainforest environments.

The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its mountainous habitat.
