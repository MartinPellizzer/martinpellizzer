Mammillaria spinosa

Mammillaria spinosa, commonly known as the spinous nipple cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5-10 centimeters and features a pale green body with prominent tubercles. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Mammillaria spinosa is often cultivated for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
