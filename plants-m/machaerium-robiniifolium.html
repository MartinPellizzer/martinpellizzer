Machaerium robiniifolium

Machaerium robiniifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets that are often arranged in an alternate pattern. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds.

Machaerium robiniifolium is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
