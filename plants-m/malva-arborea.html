Malva arborea

Malva arborea, commonly known as the tree mallow, is a perennial shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Malva arborea is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
