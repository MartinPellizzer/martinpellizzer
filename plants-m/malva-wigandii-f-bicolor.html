Malva wigandii f. bicolor

Malva wigandii f. bicolor, commonly known as the bicolor mallows, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink and white bi-colored flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This ornamental plant is valued for its showy flowers and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
