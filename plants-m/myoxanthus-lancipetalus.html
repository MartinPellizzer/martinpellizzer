Myoxanthus lancipetalus

Myoxanthus lancipetalus, commonly known as the narrow-petaled myoxanthus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped petals and a compact growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its dry habitat.

Myoxanthus lancipetalus is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
