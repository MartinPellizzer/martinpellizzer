Mimosa stylosa

Mimosa stylosa, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to regions of South America and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa stylosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
