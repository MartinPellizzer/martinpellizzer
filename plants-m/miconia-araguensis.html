Miconia araguensis

Miconia araguensis, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some areas for its aesthetic appeal.
