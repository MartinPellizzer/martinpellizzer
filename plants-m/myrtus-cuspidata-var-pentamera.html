Myrtus cuspidata var. pentamera

Myrtus cuspidata var. pentamera, commonly known as the purple myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are often tinged with purple on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue, egg-shaped berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and seasonal floral display.
