Manihot procumbens var. genuina

Manihot procumbens var. genuina, commonly known as the creeping arrowleaf.

This plant is a herbaceous species native to South America, characterized by its sprawling growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
