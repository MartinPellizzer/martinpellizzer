Masdevallia backhousiana

Masdevallia backhousiana, commonly known as the Backhouse's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flower structure. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high light exposure, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

The leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
