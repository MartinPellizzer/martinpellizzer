Myrcia amethystina var. dealbata

Myrcia amethystina var. dealbata, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The leaves are glossy, elliptical, and often have a reddish tint when young. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
