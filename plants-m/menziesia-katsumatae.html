Menziesia katsumatae

Menziesia katsumatae, commonly known as the Japanese teaberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in late summer. Menziesia katsumatae prefers acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
