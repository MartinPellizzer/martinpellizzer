Mespilus crus-galli var. salicifolia

Mespilus crus-galli var. salicifolia, commonly known as the field service-tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It has distinctive, elongated leaves that are lanceolate and often have a waxy coating. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, while the fruit is a drupe with a sweet, edible pulp. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows or along waterways.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
