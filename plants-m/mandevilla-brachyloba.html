Mandevilla brachyloba

Mandevilla brachyloba, commonly known as the dwarf mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens or as a houseplant.

Mandevilla brachyloba is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-draining soil and regular watering during the growing season.
