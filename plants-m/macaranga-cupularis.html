Macaranga cupularis

Macaranga cupularis, commonly known as the cupularis sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts due to its adaptability.
