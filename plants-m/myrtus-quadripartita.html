Myrtus quadripartita

Myrtus quadripartita, commonly known as the four-parted myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Myrtus quadripartita is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
