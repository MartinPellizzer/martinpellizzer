Myoporum crassifolium

Myoporum crassifolium, commonly known as broadleaf myoporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Myoporum crassifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
