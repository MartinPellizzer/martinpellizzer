Myrcia abrantea

Myrcia abrantea, commonly known as the Brazilian peppercorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are red when mature, containing numerous seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia abrantea is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
