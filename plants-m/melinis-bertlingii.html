Melinis bertlingii

Melinis bertlingii, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf melinis, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it can be both a useful forage plant and an invasive species in certain regions.
