Mesembryanthemum occidentale

Mesembryanthemum occidentale, commonly known as the Western ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, succulent stems and leaves. It produces a large number of small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation across dry, sandy soils.

Mesembryanthemum occidentale is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
