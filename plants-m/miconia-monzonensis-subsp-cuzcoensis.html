Miconia monzonensis subsp. cuzcoensis

Miconia monzonensis subsp.

cuzcoensis, commonly known as the Cuzco miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 2,500 meters. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
