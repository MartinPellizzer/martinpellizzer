Myoporum sandwicense subsp. st-johnii

Myoporum sandwicense subsp.

st-johnii, commonly known as the Hawaiian myoporum, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native environment.
