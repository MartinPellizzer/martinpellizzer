Myrciaria silveirana

Myrciaria silveirana, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a fragrant aroma. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

The fruit is not only valued for its flavor but also for its medicinal properties.
