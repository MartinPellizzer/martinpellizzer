Marsdenia gracilis

Marsdenia gracilis, commonly known as the slender Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, often with a faint fragrance. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.

Marsdenia gracilis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
