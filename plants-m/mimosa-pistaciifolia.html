Mimosa pistaciifolia

Mimosa pistaciifolia, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that turn a striking coppery color in the fall. The plant produces small, fluffy white flowers in late spring, which are followed by hairy, pod-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
