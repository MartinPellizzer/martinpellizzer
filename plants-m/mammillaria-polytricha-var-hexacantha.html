Mammillaria polytricha var. hexacantha

Mammillaria polytricha var. hexacantha, commonly known as the six-armed hedgehog cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem covered in dense, white to pale blue tubercles, which give it a distinctive, rounded appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its grayish-green body. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
