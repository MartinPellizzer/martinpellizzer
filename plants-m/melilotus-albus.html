Melilotus albus

Melilotus albus, commonly known as white melilot, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of nectar for bees.

White melilot is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
