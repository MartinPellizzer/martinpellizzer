Morysia pachyphylla

Morysia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved morysia, is a unique plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale in color. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with a compact form that helps it conserve moisture.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Morysia pachyphylla is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic plants.
