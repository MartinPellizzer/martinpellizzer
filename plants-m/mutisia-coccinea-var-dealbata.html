Mutisia coccinea var. dealbata

Mutisia coccinea var. dealbata, commonly known as the white-flowered mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, white to pale pink ray flowers surrounding yellow disk flowers, which are typical of the family. Its leaves are typically alternate and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
