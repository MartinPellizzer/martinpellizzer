Mentha × piperita var. durandoana

Mentha × piperita var. durandoana, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid perennial herb in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. Its aromatic leaves contain high concentrations of menthol, which gives it a strong, cooling scent and flavor.

This variety is widely cultivated for use in herbal teas, aromatherapy, and as a natural remedy for various ailments.
