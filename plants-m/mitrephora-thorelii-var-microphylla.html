Mitrephora thorelii var. microphylla

Mitrephora thorelii var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved mitrephora, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually small, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and clustered in panicles, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a drupe, typically reddish when mature, and contains a single seed.
