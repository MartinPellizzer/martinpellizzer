Muscari pocuticum

Muscari pocuticum, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a basal rosette, and its bulbs are adapted for storage and propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy ornamental choice for gardens.

Muscari pocuticum is often cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blooms and its ability to naturalize over time.
