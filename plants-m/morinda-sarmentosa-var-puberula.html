Morinda sarmentosa var. puberula

Morinda sarmentosa var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy morinda, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its trailing stems and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
