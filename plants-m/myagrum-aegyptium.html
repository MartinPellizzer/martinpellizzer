Myagrum aegyptium

Myagrum aegyptium, commonly known as the Egyptian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
