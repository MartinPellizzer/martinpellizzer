Malacothrix commutata

Malacothrix commutata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, often in regions with a Mediterranean climate. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, purple or pinkish flowers arranged in a compact cluster. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.
