Melastoma dimorphum

Melastoma dimorphum, commonly known as the red-rooted melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a reddish underside. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant's roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine.
