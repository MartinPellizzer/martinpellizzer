Macrothelypteris subaurita

Macrothelypteris subaurita, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The fronds are typically divided into segments with a distinctive golden-yellow color on the underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
