Mentha longifolia var. cadrilateri

Mentha longifolia var. cadrilateri, commonly known as the four-angled mint, is a variant of the mint family characterized by its distinctive square stem.

This plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, aromatic scent, often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The flowers are small and tubular, usually appearing in clusters, and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a perennial herb that is easy to cultivate and can spread rapidly through rhizomes.
