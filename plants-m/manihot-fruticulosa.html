Manihot fruticulosa

Manihot fruticulosa, commonly known as the arrowroot shrub, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with smooth, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland areas.
