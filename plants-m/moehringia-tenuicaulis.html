Moehringia tenuicaulis

Moehringia tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed moehringia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes flowering plants characterized by their showy blooms and often succulent leaves. The plant typically features slender, ascending stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom in early summer.

Moehringia tenuicaulis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
