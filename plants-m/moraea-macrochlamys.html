Moraea macrochlamys

Moraea macrochlamys, commonly known as the giant moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its elegant appearance. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn, making it a notable ornamental plant in gardens.

Moraea macrochlamys is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in Mediterranean climates.
