Malus orientalis var. lanceolata

Malus orientalis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its lanceolate-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, sweet, yellow or white fruits that are edible. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and orchards.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
