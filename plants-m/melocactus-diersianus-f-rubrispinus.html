Melocactus diersianus f. rubrispinus

Melocactus diersianus f. rubrispinus, commonly known as the red-spined melocactus, is a distinctive cactus species native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that are often covered in dense, red or orange spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but striking in appearance. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.

The f. rubrispinus form is distinguished by its more vibrant and dense spines compared to the typical variety.
