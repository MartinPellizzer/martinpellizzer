Monardella hypoleuca var. saxicola

Monardella hypoleuca var. saxicola, commonly known as Sierra lemonade, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny habitats.

Sierra lemonade is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local pollinators.
