Musa cavendishii

Musa cavendishii, commonly known as the Cavendish banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of fruit. The plant produces a pseudostem composed of tightly packed leaf sheaths, which supports the inflorescence. Its flowers are typically hidden within the fruit clusters, and the fruit develops from the flowers in a unique, fleshy structure.

This species is widely cultivated for its edible, sweet fruit, which is a staple in many diets worldwide.
