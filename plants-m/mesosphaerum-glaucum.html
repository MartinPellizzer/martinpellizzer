Mesosphaerum glaucum

Mesosphaerum glaucum, commonly known as the blue globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Mesosphaerum glaucum is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
