Machilus haridasanii

Machilus haridasanii, commonly known as the Indian laurier cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, cherry-like berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is also cultivated in horticultural gardens.
