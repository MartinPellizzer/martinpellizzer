Miscanthus sinensis f. purpurascens

Miscanthus sinensis f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a striking ornamental grass characterized by its vibrant purple to reddish hues in its foliage and inflorescence.

It is a flowering plant in the family Poaceae, native to Asia, particularly China, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with tall, slender stems that produce feathery plumes during the late summer and autumn. Its foliage turns a striking crimson or purple in the autumn, adding visual interest to the garden throughout the growing season.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a versatile choice for perennial plantings.
