Musa lasiocarpa

Musa lasiocarpa, commonly known as the Balinese banana, is a species of banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of elongated, edible berries. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically consumed raw or used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Musa lasiocarpa is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental appeal.
