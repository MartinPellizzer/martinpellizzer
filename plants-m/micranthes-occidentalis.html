Micranthes occidentalis

Micranthes occidentalis, commonly known as the western pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Micranthes occidentalis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
