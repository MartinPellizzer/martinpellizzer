Melhania tulearensis

Melhania tulearensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Melhania tulearensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in its native habitat.
