Malpighia ovatifolia

Malpighia ovatifolia, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to Central America and northern South America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its clustered, bright red berries that are highly visible and attract various bird species. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a distinct venation pattern, and it produces small, white flowers with five petals.

Malpighia ovatifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
