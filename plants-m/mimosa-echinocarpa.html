Mimosa echinocarpa

Mimosa echinocarpa, commonly known as the prickly mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its fruits are dry, indehiscent pods that contain several seeds.

Mimosa echinocarpa is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
