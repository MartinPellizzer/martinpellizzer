Machaerium campylocarpum

Machaerium campylocarpum, commonly known as the broad-leafed machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with a broad, trifoliate structure, and its flowers are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Machaerium campylocarpum produces pods that are curved and contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
