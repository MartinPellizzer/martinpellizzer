Melandrium drummondii

Melandrium drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
