Musa superba

Musa superba, commonly known as the giant banana plant, is a striking member of the Musaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of large, elongated berries that are typically yellow when ripe. It is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.

Due to its rapid growth and size, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
