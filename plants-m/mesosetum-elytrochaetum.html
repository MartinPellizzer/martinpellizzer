Mesosetum elytrochaetum

Mesosetum elytrochaetum, commonly known as the hairy blade grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in wetlands and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Mesosetum elytrochaetum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
