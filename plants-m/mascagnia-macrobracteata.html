Mascagnia macrobracteata

Mascagnia macrobracteata, commonly known as the large bract mascagnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink to reddish in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage in its native habitat.

The species is characterized by its distinctive bracts, which are large and often brightly colored, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
