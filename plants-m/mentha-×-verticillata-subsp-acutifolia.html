Mentha × verticillata subsp. acutifolia

Mentha × verticillata subsp.

acutifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. It is a hybrid species within the Mentha genus, known for its aromatic leaves and potential medicinal properties.

Its growth habit and habitat preferences make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
