Magnolia cristalensis

Magnolia cristalensis, commonly known as the Crystal Magnolia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
