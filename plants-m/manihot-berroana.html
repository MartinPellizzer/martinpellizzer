Manihot berroana

Manihot berroana, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a starchy food source. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued both for its nutritional content and its role in traditional medicine.
