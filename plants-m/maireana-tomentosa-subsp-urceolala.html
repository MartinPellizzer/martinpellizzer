Maireana tomentosa subsp. urceolala

Maireana tomentosa subsp.

urceolala, commonly known as the woolly maireana, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, pale green or yellowish flowers appear in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value.

It is drought-tolerant and often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
