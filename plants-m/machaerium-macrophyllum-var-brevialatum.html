Machaerium macrophyllum var. brevialatum

Machaerium macrophyllum var. brevialatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to white flowers, which bloom throughout the wet season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
