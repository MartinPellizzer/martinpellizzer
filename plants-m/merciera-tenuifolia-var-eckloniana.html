Merciera tenuifolia var. eckloniana

Merciera tenuifolia var. eckloniana, commonly known as the Ecklon's narrowleaf mercier, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in the fynbos vegetation type, which is known for its high biodiversity.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
