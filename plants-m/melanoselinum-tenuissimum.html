Melanoselinum tenuissimum

Melanoselinum tenuissimum, commonly known as the slender shadeplant, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, which are followed by small, oval-shaped berries. It is characterized by its dark green foliage and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a ground cover.
