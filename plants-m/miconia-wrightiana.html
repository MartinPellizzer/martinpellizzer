Miconia wrightiana

Miconia wrightiana, commonly known as the Wright's miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Miconia wrightiana is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.
