Millettia pachyloba var. lasiopetala

Millettia pachyloba var. lasiopetala, commonly known as the hairy-leaf millettia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often hairy or pubescent, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by flat, elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
