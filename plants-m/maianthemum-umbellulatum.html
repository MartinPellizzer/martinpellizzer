Maianthemum umbellulatum

Maianthemum umbellulatum, commonly known as the false lily of the valley, is a woodland plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb with grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets. It produces berries that are edible for some birds and small mammals.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its delicate, fragrant blooms.
