Melica scabra var. glabra

Melica scabra var. glabra, commonly known as smooth false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and other wetland habitats. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth to the touch. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
