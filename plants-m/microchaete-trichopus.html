Microchaete trichopus

Microchaete trichopus, commonly known as the hairy thornapple, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

Microchaete trichopus is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
