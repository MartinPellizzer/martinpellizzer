Mammillaria saetigera var. quadricentralis

Mammillaria saetigera var. quadricentralis, commonly known as the four-central mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
