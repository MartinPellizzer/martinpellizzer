Microrhynchus secundus

Microrhynchus secundus, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. The plant has small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, and its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Microrhynchus secundus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in xerophytic landscapes.
