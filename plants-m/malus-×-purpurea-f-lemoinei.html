Malus × purpurea f. lemoinei

Malus × purpurea f. lemoinei, commonly known as the Lemoine's crabapple, is a compact, ornamental tree or shrub characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers and vibrant foliage.

It is a hybrid of Malus × purpurea, known for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The plant typically features rounded, dense growth with small, ovate leaves that turn reddish in the fall. Its flowers, which appear in early spring, are usually pink or white and attract pollinators.

The fruit is small and edible, though often not harvested for consumption.
