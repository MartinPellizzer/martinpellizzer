Myriandra prolifica

Myriandra prolifica, commonly known as the prolific myriandra, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface and a strong aromatic scent.

Myriandra prolifica is often found in coastal regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
