Monnina stipulata var. tenuibracteata

Monnina stipulata var. tenuibracteata, commonly known as narrow-bract monnina, is a low-growing annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal spikes, and the plant is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of the Chihuahuan Desert ecosystem.
