Myrtus leucomyrtillus

Myrtus leucomyrtillus, commonly known as white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the island of Sicily and is characterized by its small, white flowers and aromatic, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical and have a distinctive white underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile plant for gardens and landscapes.
