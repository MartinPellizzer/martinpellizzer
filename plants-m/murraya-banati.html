Murraya banati

Murraya banati, commonly known as the Java orange or red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to orange when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The wood of Murraya banati is highly valued for its fine grain and durability, making it a sought-after material in traditional craftsmanship.
