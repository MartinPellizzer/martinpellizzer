Mesembryanthemum polyphyllum

Mesembryanthemum polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-leafed iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often blooming in winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
