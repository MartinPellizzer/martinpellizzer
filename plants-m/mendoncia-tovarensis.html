Mendoncia tovarensis

Mendoncia tovarensis, commonly known as the Tovarensis Bluebell, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tovara Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet petals and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky crevices and forested slopes. Its flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to environmental changes, it is considered an endangered species.
