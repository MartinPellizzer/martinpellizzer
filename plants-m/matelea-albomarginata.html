Matelea albomarginata

Matelea albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin dune morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually white with a faint pink or purple margin. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
