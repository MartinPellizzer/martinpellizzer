Maxillaria vinosa

Maxillaria vinosa, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and pink flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Maxillaria vinosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
