Mimosa borboremae

Mimosa borboremae, commonly known as the Brazilian sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Mimosa borboremae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
