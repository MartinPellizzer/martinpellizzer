Miscanthus sacchariflorus f. latifolius

Miscanthus sacchariflorus f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf silver reed, is a variant of the silver reed plant native to China.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves that are often silvery-white in color, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that are typically pink or purple in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscape design for its visual interest and ecological benefits.
