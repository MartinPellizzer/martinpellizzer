Mentha longifolia var. eclytanthea

Mentha longifolia var. eclytanthea, commonly known as the sweet cicely mint, is a variant of the long-stemmed mint species.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It has a distinct, licorice-like fragrance due to its high concentration of volatile oils.

This variety is often used in culinary and medicinal applications for its aromatic properties.
