Microlicia taxifolia

Microlicia taxifolia, commonly known as the lace fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble lace, giving it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces spores rather than seeds. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a slender, elongated shape.

Microlicia taxifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shady garden settings.
