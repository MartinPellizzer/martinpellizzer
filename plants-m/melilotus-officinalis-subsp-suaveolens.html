Melilotus officinalis subsp. suaveolens

Melilotus officinalis subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in dense, globular clusters.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Sweet clover is known for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
