Mentha veronicifolia

Mentha veronicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spearmint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and aromatic, fragrant foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and can reach heights of up to 60 cm. Its flowering spikes produce small, pale purple or white flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mentha veronicifolia is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its refreshing scent and potential therapeutic properties.
