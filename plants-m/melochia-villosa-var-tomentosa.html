Melochia villosa var. tomentosa

Melochia villosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly melochia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories or along stream banks.
