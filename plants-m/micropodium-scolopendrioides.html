Micropodium scolopendrioides

Micropodium scolopendrioides, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Micropodium scolopendrioides is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
