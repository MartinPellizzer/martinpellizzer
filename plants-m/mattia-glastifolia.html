Mattia glastifolia

Mattia glastifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate fronds that give it a delicate and elegant appearance. The plant thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.

Mattia glastifolia plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat for small insects and microorganisms.
