Myrcia macaca

Myrcia macaca, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are reddish berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Myrcia macaca is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic uses.
