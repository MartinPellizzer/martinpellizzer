Marginaria lanceolata

Marginaria lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate marginaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
