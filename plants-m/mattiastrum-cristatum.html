Mattiastrum cristatum

Mattiastrum cristatum, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has a creeping habit with extensive root systems, making it effective for soil stabilization. It produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Mattiastrum cristatum is often used in erosion control and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and adaptability.
