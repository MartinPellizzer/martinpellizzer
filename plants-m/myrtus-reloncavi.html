Myrtus reloncavi

Myrtus reloncavi, commonly known as the Madagascar myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple berry that contains several seeds.

Myrtus reloncavi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
