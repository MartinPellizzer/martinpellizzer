Myosotis collina var. simplex

Myosotis collina var. simplex, commonly known as the simple blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
