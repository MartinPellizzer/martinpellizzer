Muscari cretensium

Muscari cretensium, commonly known as the Cretan musk iris, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in full sun.

Muscari cretensium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
