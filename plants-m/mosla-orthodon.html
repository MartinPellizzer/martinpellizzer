Mosla orthodon

Mosla orthodon, commonly known as narrowleaf mosquito plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Mosla orthodon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in dry, well-drained soils.
