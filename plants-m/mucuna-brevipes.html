Mucuna brevipes

Mucuna brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, hairy pods that contain elliptical seeds. The plant produces yellow flowers and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Mucuna brevipes is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.
