Mentha arvensis var. hillebrandtii

Mentha arvensis var. hillebrandtii, commonly known as the Hillebrandt's field mint, is a variant of the field mint species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream sides.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
