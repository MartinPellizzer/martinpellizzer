Melandrium auritipetalum

Melandrium auritipetalum, commonly known as the ear-petalled catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble those of the common catchfly. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are opposite and linear, with hairy surfaces, while the flowers have five petals, some of which are modified into bracts.

The species is known for its short lifespan and is often found in temperate climates.
