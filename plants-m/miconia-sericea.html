Miconia sericea

Miconia sericea, commonly known as silky miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia sericea is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is also noted for its potential ecological impact due to its invasive tendencies in some regions.
