Miscanthus tinctorius var. intermedius

Miscanthus tinctorius var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate Japanese feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery plumes that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

Its foliage and flowers can also be used in traditional crafts and dyeing processes.
