Monardella macrantha var. pinetorum

Monardella macrantha var. pinetorum, commonly known as the redstem monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and reddish stems, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, releasing a strong scent when crushed.

This variety is typically found in dry, open woodlands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
