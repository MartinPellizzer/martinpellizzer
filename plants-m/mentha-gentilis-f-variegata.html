Mentha gentilis f. variegata

Mentha gentilis f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated mint, is a hybrid variety of the Mentha genus.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its leaves. This plant typically grows as an upright herb with a spreading habit, often reaching heights of up to 30 inches. The leaves are aromatic, emitting a strong mint scent when crushed, and the plant produces small, pale blue flowers in summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
