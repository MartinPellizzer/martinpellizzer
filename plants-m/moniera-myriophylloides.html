Moniera myriophylloides

Moniera myriophylloides, commonly known as the golden feather fern, is a rare and ancient fern species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically golden in color, giving it a striking appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
