Mentha × weidenhofferi

Mentha × weidenhofferi, commonly known as the German peppermint, is a hybrid mint species resulting from the cross between Mentha × piperita and Mentha rotundifolia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly mentholated leaves and stems that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, cooling scent, making it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.

Mentha × weidenhofferi is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
