Modiola geranioides

Modiola geranioides, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Modiola geranioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and hardy nature.
