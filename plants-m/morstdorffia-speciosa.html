Morstdorffia speciosa

Morstdorffia speciosa, commonly known as the beautiful morstard, is a striking member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky soils and full sun. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange, often with a striking contrast against its green foliage. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Morstdorffia speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
