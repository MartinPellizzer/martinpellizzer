Mussaenda setosa

Mussaenda setosa, commonly known as the hairy Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences, which are clustered and resemble umbels. The plant has palmate leaves with soft, hairy surfaces, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Mussaenda setosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
