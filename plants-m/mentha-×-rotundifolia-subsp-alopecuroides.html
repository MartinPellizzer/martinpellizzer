Mentha × rotundifolia subsp. alopecuroides

Mentha × rotundifolia subsp.

alopecuroides, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic essential oils, which contain compounds like nepetalactone.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies.
