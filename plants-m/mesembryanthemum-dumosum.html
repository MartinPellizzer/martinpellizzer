Mesembryanthemum dumosum

Mesembryanthemum dumosum, commonly known as the dwarf ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it both ornamental and ecologically resilient.
