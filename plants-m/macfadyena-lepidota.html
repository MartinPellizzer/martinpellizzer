Macfadyena lepidota

Macfadyena lepidota, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to reveal edible, sweet, and tangy seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
