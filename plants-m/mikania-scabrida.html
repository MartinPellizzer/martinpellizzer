Mikania scabrida

Mikania scabrida, commonly known as roughleaf morning glory, is a fast-growing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its rapid growth and invasive tendencies make it a common weed in many regions.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
