Mosiera jackii

Mosiera jackii, commonly known as the Jack's mosiera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its dense foliage and showy flowers. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Mosiera jackii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
