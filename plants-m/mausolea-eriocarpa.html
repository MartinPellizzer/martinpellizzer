Mausolea eriocarpa

Mausolea eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly mausoleum, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white to grey hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are typically small and clustered at the base, contributing to its compact growth form.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, Mausolea eriocarpa is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
