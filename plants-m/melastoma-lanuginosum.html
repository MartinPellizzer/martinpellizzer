Melastoma lanuginosum

Melastoma lanuginosum, commonly known as the woolly melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Melastoma lanuginosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
