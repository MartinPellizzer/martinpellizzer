Mouroucoa championii

Mouroucoa championii, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has a compact, globular shape with thick, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in drought conditions. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Mouroucoa championii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
