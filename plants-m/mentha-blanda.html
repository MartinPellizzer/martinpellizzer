Mentha blanda

Mentha blanda, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has square stems and opposite leaves, typical of the mint family (Lamiaceae). It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Mentha blanda is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
