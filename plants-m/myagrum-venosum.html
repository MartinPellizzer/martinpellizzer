Myagrum venosum

Myagrum venosum, commonly known as the veiny myrtle, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent veins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in clusters, and it produces fleshy, red or orange berries.

Myagrum venosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
