Myostemma tiltilensis

Myostemma tiltilensis, commonly known as the Tiltile's ghostflower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a unique ability to store water in its fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that produces clusters of pale blue blossoms.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a conservation concern.
