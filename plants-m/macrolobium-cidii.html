Macrolobium cidii

Macrolobium cidii, commonly known as the Cid's macrolobium, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and orange. The plant has elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
