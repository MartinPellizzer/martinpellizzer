Myrcia suborbicularis

Myrcia suborbicularis, commonly known as the oval-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its elliptical to oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Myrcia suborbicularis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
