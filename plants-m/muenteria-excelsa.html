Muenteria excelsa

Muenteria excelsa, commonly known as the silver flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Muenteria excelsa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
