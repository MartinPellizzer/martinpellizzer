Matelea ionantha

Matelea ionantha, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are typically broad and hairy, adapting to the dry climate.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.
