Miscanthus × giganteus

Miscanthus × giganteus, commonly known as giant miscanthus, is a sterile, clump-forming grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, arching inflorescences that produce feathery, silver-white plumes in the fall. The plant is highly cold-hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different climates. It is widely cultivated for its high biomass yield, which is used in biofuel and renewable energy production.

Additionally, it serves as a valuable ornamental plant in gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
