Muscari hymenophorum

Muscari hymenophorum, commonly known as the white muscat grape, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Muscari hymenophorum is also noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its low maintenance requirements.
