Melampyrum pratense subsp. purpureum

Melampyrum pratense subsp.

purpureum, commonly known as purple flame vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its distinctive purple to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.
