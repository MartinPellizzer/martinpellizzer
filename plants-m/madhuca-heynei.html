Madhuca heynei

Madhuca heynei, commonly known as the Indian shea tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, glossy bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical fruits containing a sticky, yellowish pulp. Its seeds are encased in a hard shell and are used to produce shea butter, which has various industrial and cosmetic applications.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for wildlife in its native regions.
