Mitrasacme stellata var. latifolia

Mitrasacme stellata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf star-milkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for xeriscaping.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
