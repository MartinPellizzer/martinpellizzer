Macrochlaena

Macrochlaena, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The inflorescence typically consists of a single, elongated spike bearing several pale green to reddish-brown flowers. Macrochlaena species are epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique floral morphology and ecological role in their native habitats.
