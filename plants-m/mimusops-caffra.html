Mimusops caffra

Mimusops caffra, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for furniture and carving.
