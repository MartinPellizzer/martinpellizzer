Myriotheca fraxinea

Myriotheca fraxinea, commonly known as the fraxinella thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Myriotheca fraxinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
