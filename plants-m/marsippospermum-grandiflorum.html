Marsippospermum grandiflorum

Marsippospermum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered Marsippospermum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences composed of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture with a slightly serrated margin.
