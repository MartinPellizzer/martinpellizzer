Mourera alcicornis

Mourera alcicornis, commonly known as the Canary Islands mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Mourera alcicornis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native region.
