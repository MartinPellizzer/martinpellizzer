Melissa umbrosa

Melissa umbrosa, commonly known as sweet beebrush, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their calming properties.

Melissa umbrosa is also a valuable nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
