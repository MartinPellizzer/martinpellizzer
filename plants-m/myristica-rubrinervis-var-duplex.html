Myristica rubrinervis var. duplex

Myristica rubrinervis var. duplex, commonly known as the double-veined myristica, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent, parallel veins. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing aromatic seeds. Its fruits are valued for their essential oils and have been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

The varietal name 'duplex' refers to the unique double-veined structure of its leaves, which is a distinguishing feature of this variety.
