Mentha × piperita var. balsamea

Mentha × piperita var. balsamea, commonly known as balsamic mint, is a hybrid perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of pale purple flowers. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region but has been cultivated in various temperate climates. It is valued for its strong menthol scent and is often used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.

Its growth habit includes spreading via stolons, making it a vigorous ground cover in suitable environments.
