Moraea baurii

Moraea baurii, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Moraea baurii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
