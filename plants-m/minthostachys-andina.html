Minthostachys andina

Minthostachys andina, commonly known as Andean mint, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It features aromatic, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for both traditional use and horticultural purposes.
