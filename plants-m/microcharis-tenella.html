Microcharis tenella

Microcharis tenella, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
