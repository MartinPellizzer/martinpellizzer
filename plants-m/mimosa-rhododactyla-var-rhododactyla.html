Mimosa rhododactyla var. rhododactyla

Mimosa rhododactyla var. rhododactyla, commonly known as the pink mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it both ecologically and agriculturally significant.
