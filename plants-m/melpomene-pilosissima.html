Melpomene pilosissima

Melpomene pilosissima, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its scientific name honors the Greek muse of song, Melpomene, reflecting its ornamental value.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
