Mespilus oxyacantha lusus aurea

Mespilus oxyacantha lusus aurea, commonly known as the golden thimbleberry, is a variant of the European wild serviceberry.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that mature in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are white with a slight pinkish tinge. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

The golden thimbleberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
