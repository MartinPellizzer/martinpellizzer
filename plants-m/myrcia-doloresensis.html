Myrcia doloresensis

Myrcia doloresensis, commonly known as the Dolores rosemary, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

Myrcia doloresensis is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
