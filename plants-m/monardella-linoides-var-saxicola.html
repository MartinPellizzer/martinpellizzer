Monardella linoides var. saxicola

Monardella linoides var. saxicola, commonly known as the Sierra monarda, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and often hairy foliage.

This variety is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
