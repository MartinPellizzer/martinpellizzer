Myrica bojeriana

Myrica bojeriana, commonly known as the Bojer wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Myrica bojeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
