Milium amphicarpon

Milium amphicarpon, commonly known as the water millet, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into small, three-lobed seeds. It is typically found in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments.

Milium amphicarpon is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
