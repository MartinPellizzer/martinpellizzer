Momordica gracilis

Momordica gracilis, commonly known as the slender bitter melon, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and distinctive, elongated fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its fruits are typically green when unripe and turn orange-red when mature, often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in various cultures.
