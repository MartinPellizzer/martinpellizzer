Medicago lupulina var. cinerea

Medicago lupulina var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray medic, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grayish-blue foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
