Monochaetum ensiferum

Monochaetum ensiferum, commonly known as the slender spike grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
