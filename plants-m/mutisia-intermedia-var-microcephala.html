Mutisia intermedia var. microcephala

Mutisia intermedia var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

The flowers, which bloom in the spring, are yellow with a central disk, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
