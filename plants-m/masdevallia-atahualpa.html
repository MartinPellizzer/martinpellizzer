Masdevallia atahualpa

Masdevallia atahualpa, commonly known as the Atahualpa orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with a distinctive shape and vivid hues that range from purple to white.

Masdevallia atahualpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and uniqueness.
