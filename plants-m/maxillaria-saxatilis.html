Maxillaria saxatilis

Maxillaria saxatilis, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers well-drained conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of several upright or drooping flowers.

Maxillaria saxatilis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
