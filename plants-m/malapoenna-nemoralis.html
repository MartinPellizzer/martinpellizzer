Malapoenna nemoralis

Malapoenna nemoralis, commonly known as the Natal wild bean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland and savanna habitats, often as a shrub or small tree. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, and it produces edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
