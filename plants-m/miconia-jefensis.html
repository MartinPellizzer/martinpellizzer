Miconia jefensis

Miconia jefensis, commonly known as the Jefén Jefé tree, is a tropical plant native to the humid lowland forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are often surrounded by velvety, leaf-like bracts. Miconia jefensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can alter local ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
