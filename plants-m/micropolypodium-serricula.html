Micropolypodium serricula

Micropolypodium serricula, commonly known as the serrated polypody, is a small epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows on tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems that produce numerous small, lance-shaped leaves. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a serrated margin, giving it its common name.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in moist, indirect light conditions.
