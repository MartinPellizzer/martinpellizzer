Millettia pterocarpa

Millettia pterocarpa, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces large, round fruits that resemble breadfruit, which are edible when ripe. Its seeds are encased in a woody, spiny pod, and the plant is valued for its timber and as a food source in some regions.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
