Marlea bodinieri

Marlea bodinieri, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Marlea bodinieri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
