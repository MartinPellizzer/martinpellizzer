Mespilus frigida

Mespilus frigida, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rough, grey bark and alternate, pinnate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in spring, followed by unique, apple-like fruits that ripen in autumn. These fruits, known as medlars, have a complex flavor and are often used in cooking or fermented into wine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional medicine.
