Minquartia macrophylla

Minquartia macrophylla, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a small, deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions.

The tree is also valued for its hardy nature and potential medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
