Morinda nitida

Morinda nitida, commonly known as the slender morinda, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by bright red, fleshy berries.

Morinda nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
