Masdevallia gastrodes

Masdevallia gastrodes, commonly known as the "ghost orchid," is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with elongated, translucent petals that resemble spectral forms. The plant has a short, compact stem with few leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and good drainage.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
