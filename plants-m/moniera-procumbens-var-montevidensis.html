Moniera procumbens var. montevidensis

Moniera procumbens var. montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense Moniera, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its prostrate stems and small, clustered leaves.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or white in color. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in humid conditions.

This variety is noted for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
