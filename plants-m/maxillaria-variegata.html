Maxillaria variegata

Maxillaria variegata, commonly known as the variegated maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Maxillaria variegata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
