Myrcia schippii

Myrcia schippii, commonly known as the Schippi's myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The fruit, which is small and fleshy, contains seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in the forest ecosystem.
