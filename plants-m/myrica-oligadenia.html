Myrica oligadenia

Myrica oligadenia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are an important food source for various birds and wildlife. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in swamps and along stream banks.

Myrica oligadenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
