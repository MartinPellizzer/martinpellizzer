Malva elegans

Malva elegans, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
