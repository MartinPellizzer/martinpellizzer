Myrcia ambigua var. multiflora

Myrcia ambigua var. multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid tropical forests. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a glossy surface and a strong aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, fleshy drupe.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
