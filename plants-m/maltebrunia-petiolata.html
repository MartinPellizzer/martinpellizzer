Maltebrunia petiolata

Maltebrunia petiolata, commonly known as the Maltese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the island of Malta.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, elongated fruit capsules. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Maltebrunia petiolata is a symbol of Malta's unique flora and is protected due to its limited distribution.
