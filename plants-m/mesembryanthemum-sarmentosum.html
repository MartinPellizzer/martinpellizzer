Mesembryanthemum sarmentosum

Mesembryanthemum sarmentosum, commonly known as the stringy ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, trailing stems that allow it to spread across the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in winter. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, white powder, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Mesembryanthemum sarmentosum is well adapted to dry environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
