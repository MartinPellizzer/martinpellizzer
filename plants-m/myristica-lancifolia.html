Myristica lancifolia

Myristica lancifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, aromatic fruits. The plant produces a unique fruit that contains a hard, aromatic seed known as nutmeg, which is highly valued for its spice. The tree also yields a resin called mace, which is used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Myristica lancifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
