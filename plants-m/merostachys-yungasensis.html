Merostachys yungasensis

Merostachys yungasensis, commonly known as the Yungas bamboo, is a grass species native to the humid subtropical forests of the Yungas region in Bolivia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 6 meters in height, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires high levels of humidity and moderate temperatures.

Due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution, Merostachys yungasensis is considered a species of conservation concern.
