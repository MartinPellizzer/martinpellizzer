Monotheca buxifolia

Monotheca buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf monotheca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
