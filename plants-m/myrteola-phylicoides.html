Myrteola phylicoides

Myrteola phylicoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which have been traditionally used by Indigenous Australians for medicinal purposes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
