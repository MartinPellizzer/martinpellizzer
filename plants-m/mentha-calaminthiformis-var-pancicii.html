Mentha calaminthiformis var. pancicii

Mentha calaminthiformis var. pancicii, commonly known as the Pancici's mint, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the mint family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly menthol-like leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. Native to specific regions in Europe, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their cooling and soothing properties.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its unique genetic variation and potential medicinal applications.
