Mentha × maximilianea var. baileyi

Mentha × maximilianea var. baileyi, commonly known as Bailey's mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their menthol-like fragrance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
