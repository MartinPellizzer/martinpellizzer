Musa × paradisiaca var. acicularis

Musa × paradisiaca var. acicularis, commonly known as the needle banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant characterized by its elongated, sharp-pointed leaves.

This variety is notable for its unique leaf morphology, which differs significantly from the more typical broad, rounded leaves of common bananas. The plant produces clusters of small, elongated fruits that are typically consumed raw or used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its distinctive appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its edible fruit.
