Meriania cordata

Meriania cordata, commonly known as the pink velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are dark green and often have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Meriania cordata is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
