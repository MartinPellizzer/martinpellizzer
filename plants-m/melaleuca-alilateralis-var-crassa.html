Melaleuca alilateralis var. crassa

Melaleuca alilateralis var. crassa, commonly known as the broad-leaved paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, flaky bark that peels away in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are broad, elliptical, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its often arid habitat. It produces small, bottle-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow or cream, which attract nectar-feeding insects.

This plant is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, and it plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
