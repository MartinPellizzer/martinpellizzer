Myrtus beckleri

Myrtus beckleri, commonly known as the Canary Island myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves with a slight waxiness and produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain numerous seeds.

Myrtus beckleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
