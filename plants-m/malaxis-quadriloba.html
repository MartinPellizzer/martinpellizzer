Malaxis quadriloba

Malaxis quadriloba, commonly known as the four-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a rhizomatous root system and possesses a single, narrow leaf.

The flowers are usually arranged in a spike and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.
