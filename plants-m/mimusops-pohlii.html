Mimusops pohlii

Mimusops pohlii, commonly known as the Pohli's satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing smooth, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
