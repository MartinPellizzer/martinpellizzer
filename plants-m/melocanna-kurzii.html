Melocanna kurzii

Melocanna kurzii, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 15 meters in height, making it one of the tallest bamboo species. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. Its rhizomes are thick and robust, contributing to its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps.

Melocanna kurzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
