Mundulea hysterantha

Mundulea hysterantha, commonly known as the African broom, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water retention. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its growth pattern and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
