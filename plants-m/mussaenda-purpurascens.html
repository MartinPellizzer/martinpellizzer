Mussaenda purpurascens

Mussaenda purpurascens, commonly known as the flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Mussaenda purpurascens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
