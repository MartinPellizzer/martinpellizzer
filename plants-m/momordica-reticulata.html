Momordica reticulata

Momordica reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined bitter gourd, is a vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its distinctive net-like veins on its leaves. The plant produces long, pointed fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn orange when mature. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, though the fruit is generally bitter and requires careful preparation.
