Mastichodendron capiri var. typicum

Mastichodendron capiri var. typicum, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, chestnut-like fruits that are often used in local cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
