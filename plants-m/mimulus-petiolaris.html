Mimulus petiolaris

Mimulus petiolaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with two lips, typical of the Mimulus genus, which attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

Mimulus petiolaris is a perennial herb that plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems.
