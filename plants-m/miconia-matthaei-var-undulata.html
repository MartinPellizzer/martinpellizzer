Miconia matthaei var. undulata

Miconia matthaei var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with undulating margins. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
