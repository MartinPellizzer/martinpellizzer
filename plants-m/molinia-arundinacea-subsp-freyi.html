Molinia arundinacea subsp. freyi

Molinia arundinacea subsp.

freyi, commonly known as frey's moor grass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in wet meadows, bogs, and heaths.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of spikelets that bloom in late summer. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
