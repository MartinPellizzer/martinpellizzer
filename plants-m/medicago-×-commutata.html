Medicago × commutata

Medicago × commutata, commonly known as the yellow sweet clover, is a hybrid plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically yellow-green in color, and its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of North America, where it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.

Additionally, it is capable of nitrogen fixation, which can improve soil fertility.
