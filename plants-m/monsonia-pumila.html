Monsonia pumila

Monsonia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf monsonia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months.

Monsonia pumila is well-suited for rock gardens and containers due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
