Mercurialis sylvatica

Mercurialis sylvatica, commonly known as the wood mercury or lesser mercurial, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Mercurialis sylvatica is often found in association with other wetland plants and is known for its ecological importance in supporting insect populations.
