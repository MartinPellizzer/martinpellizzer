Monadenium kaessneri

Monadenium kaessneri, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features a short, thickened stem with opposite, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments by storing water in its leaves and stems.

Monadenium kaessneri is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
