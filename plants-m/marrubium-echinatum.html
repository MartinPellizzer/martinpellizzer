Marrubium echinatum

Marrubium echinatum, commonly known as the spiked horehound, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs, with small, grayish-green leaves that have a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually pale blue or purple in color.

Marrubium echinatum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional remedies.
