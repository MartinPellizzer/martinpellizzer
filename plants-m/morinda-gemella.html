Morinda gemella

Morinda gemella, commonly known as the red morinda, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in summer, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly rough texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
