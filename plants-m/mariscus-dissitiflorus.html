Mariscus dissitiflorus

Mariscus dissitiflorus, commonly known as the scattered-flower mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Mariscus dissitiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
