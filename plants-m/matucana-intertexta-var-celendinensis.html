Matucana intertexta var. celendinensis

Matucana intertexta var. celendinensis, commonly known as the celendinensis variety of the Matucana plant, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This variety is popular among collectors for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
