Maxillaria rupestris

Maxillaria rupestris, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, helping it store water.

Maxillaria rupestris is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
