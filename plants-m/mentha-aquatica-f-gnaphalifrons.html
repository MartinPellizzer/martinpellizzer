Mentha aquatica f. gnaphalifrons

Mentha aquatica f. gnaphalifrons, commonly known as water mint, is a hybrid form of the aquatic mint plant.

It is characterized by its slender, often floating stems and serrated leaves that are typically submerged or partially submerged in water. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as ponds, streams, and wet meadows.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
