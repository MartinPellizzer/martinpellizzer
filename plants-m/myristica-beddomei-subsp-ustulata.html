Myristica beddomei subsp. ustulata

Myristica beddomei subsp.

ustulata, commonly known as the Malayan nutmeg. It is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Malaysia and Indonesia.

This subspecies is characterized by its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets.

It thrives in tropical rainforests and requires a humid, warm climate for optimal growth.
