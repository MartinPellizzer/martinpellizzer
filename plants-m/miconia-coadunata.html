Miconia coadunata

Miconia coadunata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia coadunata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
