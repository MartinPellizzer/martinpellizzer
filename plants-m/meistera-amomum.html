Meistera amomum

Meistera amomum, commonly known as the cinnamon plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a spice called cinnamon, which is derived from its inner bark. Meistera amomum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and the spice trade.
