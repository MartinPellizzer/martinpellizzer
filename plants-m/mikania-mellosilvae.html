Mikania mellosilvae

Mikania mellosilvae, commonly known as the Brazilian Mikania, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain areas.
