Messerschmidia fruticosa f. angustifolia

Messerschmidia fruticosa f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved form of the common rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow and grayish-green, adapted to thrive in rocky and sunny environments. This variety is often found in dry, calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
