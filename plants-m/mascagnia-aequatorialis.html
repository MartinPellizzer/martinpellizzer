Mascagnia aequatorialis

Mascagnia aequatorialis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian mascagnia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly hummingbirds, due to their nectar-rich structure.
