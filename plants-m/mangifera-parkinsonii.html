Mangifera parkinsonii

Mangifera parkinsonii, commonly known as the Malayan mango, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves and fragrant, yellow flowers. The tree produces edible mango fruits that are smaller and less sweet compared to the common mango (Mangifera indica). It thrives in humid climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction.
