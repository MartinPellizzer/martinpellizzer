Marsdenia rupestris

Marsdenia rupestris, commonly known as the rock fig, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
