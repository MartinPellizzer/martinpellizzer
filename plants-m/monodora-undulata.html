Monodora undulata

Monodora undulata, commonly known as the African nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red drupes containing a single seed. Its seeds are highly aromatic and contain compounds similar to nutmeg, making it valuable in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
