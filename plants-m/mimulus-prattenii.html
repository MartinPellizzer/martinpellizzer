Mimulus prattenii

Mimulus prattenii, commonly known as the Pratten's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange or yellow flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant produces slender, hairy stems that grow from a basal rosette of leaves, which are usually oval and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and meadows.

Mimulus prattenii is a herbaceous perennial that plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems.
