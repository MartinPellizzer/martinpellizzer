Micranthus hensii

Micranthus hensii, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a central disc of darker florets.

Micranthus hensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
