Mormodes sotoana

Mormodes sotoana, commonly known as the Sotoana orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a slender, branched stem with numerous small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals and sepals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
