Mentha arvensis var. angustifolia

Mentha arvensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows and woodland areas. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and shares many characteristics with other mint species.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds that contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal uses.
