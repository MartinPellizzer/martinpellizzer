Mahonia × wagneri

Mahonia × wagneri, commonly known as the Oregon grape.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Mahonia × wagneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

It is also used in landscaping for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
