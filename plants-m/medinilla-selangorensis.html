Medinilla selangorensis

Medinilla selangorensis, commonly known as the Malayan velvetflower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Medinilla selangorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
