Microula oblongifolia var. glabrescens

Microula oblongifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaved microula, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its oblong, smooth, and glabrous leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, adding visual interest to its appearance. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
