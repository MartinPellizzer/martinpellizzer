Manekia

Manekia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Manekia species are typically evergreen and thrive in humid, well-drained soil. They are popular as houseplants due to their ability to bloom throughout the year under proper care.

The genus includes several species, such as Manekia masonii and Manekia capensis, which are valued for their ornamental appeal.
