Myrcia glandulosa

Myrcia glandulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, glandular leaves that release a strong scent when crushed.
