Mammillaria dixanthocentron var. rubrispina

Mammillaria dixanthocentron var. rubrispina, commonly known as the red-spined mammillaria, is a compact cactus species native to Mexico.

It features a globular shape with dense, white to pale green spines that are often tinged with red, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
