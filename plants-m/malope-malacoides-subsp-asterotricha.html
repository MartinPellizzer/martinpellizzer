Malope malacoides subsp. asterotricha

Malope malacoides subsp.

asterotricha, commonly known as the star-haired malope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish to orange flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy, star-shaped bracts that give it its common name. It typically thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
