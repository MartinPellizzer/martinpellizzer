Machilus pilosus

Machilus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy woodbine, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Machilus pilosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
