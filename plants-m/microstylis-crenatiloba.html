Microstylis crenatiloba

Microstylis crenatiloba, commonly known as the curly-stemmed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a slightly wavy or toothed edge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of many grass species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Microstylis crenatiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
