Myrsiphyllum undulatum

Myrsiphyllum undulatum, commonly known as the wavy myrsiphyllum, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its narrow, wavy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Myrsiphyllum undulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
