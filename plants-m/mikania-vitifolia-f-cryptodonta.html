Mikania vitifolia f. cryptodonta

Mikania vitifolia f. cryptodonta, commonly known as the creeping Mikania, is a variant of the Mikania genus native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a fast-growing, herbaceous vine characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic weed in certain ecosystems.
