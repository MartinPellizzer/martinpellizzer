Mentha × nemorosa subsp. dulcissima

Mentha × nemorosa subsp.

dulcissima, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a vanilla-like scent when crushed.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its leaves are widely used in perfumery and as a flavoring agent in foods and beverages.
