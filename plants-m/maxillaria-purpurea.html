Maxillaria purpurea

Maxillaria purpurea, commonly known as the purple maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its inflorescence typically bears several flowers arranged in a raceme, with petals and sepals that are distinctly marked.

Maxillaria purpurea thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
