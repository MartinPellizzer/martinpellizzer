Millettia sapinii

Millettia sapinii, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces long, slender pods that contain seeds with tufted hairs, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The plant has been used traditionally for its timber and medicinal properties.
