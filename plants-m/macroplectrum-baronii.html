Macroplectrum baronii

Macroplectrum baronii, commonly known as the Baroni's spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its flowers are usually a combination of white, yellow, and green hues, which help it blend with its surroundings.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
