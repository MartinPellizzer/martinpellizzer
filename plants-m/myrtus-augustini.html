Myrtus augustini

Myrtus augustini, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark blue berry that develops after flowering.

Myrtus augustini is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its pleasant scent and hardy nature.
