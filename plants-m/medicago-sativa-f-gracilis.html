Medicago sativa f. gracilis

Medicago sativa f. gracilis, commonly known as slender medick, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as meadows and roadsides. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in sustainable landscaping.
