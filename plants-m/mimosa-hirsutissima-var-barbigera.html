Mimosa hirsutissima var. barbigera

Mimosa hirsutissima var. barbigera, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that appear in clusters, typically in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
