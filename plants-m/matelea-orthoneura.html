Matelea orthoneura

Matelea orthoneura, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers open at night and close during the day, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming habit.
