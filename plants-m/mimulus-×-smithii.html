Mimulus × smithii

Mimulus × smithii, commonly known as the hybrid yellow monkeyflower, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mimulus guttatus and Mimulus luteus. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in wet meadows. It features showy, orange-yellow flowers with a distinctive lower lip that resembles a monkey's face.

Mimulus × smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
