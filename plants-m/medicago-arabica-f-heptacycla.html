Medicago arabica f. heptacycla

Medicago arabica f. heptacycla, commonly known as the seven-leaf medick, is a variant of the common medick, characterized by its distinctive seven-leaf cotyledons.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant features small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its trifoliate leaves are typically pubescent, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
