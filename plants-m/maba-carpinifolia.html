Maba carpinifolia

Maba carpinifolia, commonly known as the Chinese mountain ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
