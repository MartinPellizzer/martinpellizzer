Myrciaria dichotoma

Myrciaria dichotoma, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Myrciaria dichotoma is also known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine.
