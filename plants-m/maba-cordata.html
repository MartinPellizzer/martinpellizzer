Maba cordata

Maba cordata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, typically blooming in clusters. Its fibrous roots and woody stems make it a hardy plant adaptable to various soil types.

Maba cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
