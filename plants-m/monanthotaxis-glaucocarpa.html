Monanthotaxis glaucocarpa

Monanthotaxis glaucocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruit tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, followed by spherical fruits that mature to a striking blue or purple color. Its fruit is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor, making it a valuable resource in local diets.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
