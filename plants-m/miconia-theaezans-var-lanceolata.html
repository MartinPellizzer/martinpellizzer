Miconia theaezans var. lanceolata

Miconia theaezans var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains numerous seeds.

This species is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
