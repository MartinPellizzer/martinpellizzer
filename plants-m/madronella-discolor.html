Madronella discolor

Madronella discolor, commonly known as the white madrone, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The tree produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, red pulp.
