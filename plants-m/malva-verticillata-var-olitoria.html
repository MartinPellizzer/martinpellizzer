Malva verticillata var. olitoria

Malva verticillata var. olitoria, commonly known as malva or common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, pink or white flowers with five petals. The plant is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Its mucilaginous properties make it useful in traditional medicine and as a food source for livestock.
