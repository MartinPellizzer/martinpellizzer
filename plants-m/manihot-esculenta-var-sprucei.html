Manihot esculenta var. sprucei

Manihot esculenta var. sprucei, commonly known as the African arrowleaf, is a variant of the cassava plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and starchy, edible roots that are a staple food in many African communities. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value. Its leaves are also consumed as a vegetable, providing essential nutrients like vitamin A and iron.

Due to its adaptability and productivity, it plays a significant role in both subsistence and commercial agriculture.
