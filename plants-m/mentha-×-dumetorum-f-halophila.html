Mentha × dumetorum f. halophila

Mentha × dumetorum f. halophila, commonly known as the salt marsh mint, is a hybrid plant species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. This plant thrives in wet, saline environments such as coastal areas and marshlands, showcasing its halophytic nature. Its growth habit is prostrate, with creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

The plant's essential oils contain various terpenoids, contributing to its distinctive scent and potential medicinal properties.
