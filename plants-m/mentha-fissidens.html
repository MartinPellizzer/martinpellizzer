Mentha fissidens

Mentha fissidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. Mentha fissidens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds that contribute to its cooling and refreshing effects.
