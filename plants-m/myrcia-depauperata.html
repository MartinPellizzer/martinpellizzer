Myrcia depauperata

Myrcia depauperata, commonly known as the poorleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that develop from the flowers.

Myrcia depauperata is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
