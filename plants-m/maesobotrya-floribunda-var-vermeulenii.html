Maesobotrya floribunda var. vermeulenii

Maesobotrya floribunda var. vermeulenii, commonly known as the Yellow-veined Sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers during the late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation in its native environment.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
