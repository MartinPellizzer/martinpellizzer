Morella esculenta

Morella esculenta, commonly known as the American hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
