Myristica byssacea

Myristica byssacea, commonly known as the African nutmeg or the West African nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and dense, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. The seeds, known as nutmegs, are encased in a reddish, fleshy aril and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
