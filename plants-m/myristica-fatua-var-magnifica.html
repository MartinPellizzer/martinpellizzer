Myristica fatua var. magnifica

Myristica fatua var. magnifica, commonly known as the giant nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and fragrant, aromatic fruits. The plant produces an oily resin known as nutmeg, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
