Melaleuca jonesii

Melaleuca jonesii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottlebrushes. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a reddish or brownish hue.

Melaleuca jonesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
