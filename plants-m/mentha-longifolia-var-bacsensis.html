Mentha longifolia var. bacsensis

Mentha longifolia var. bacsensis, commonly known as Hungarian mint, is a variant of the spearmint species native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.
