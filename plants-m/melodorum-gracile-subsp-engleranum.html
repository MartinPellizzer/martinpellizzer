Melodorum gracile subsp. engleranum

Melodorum gracile subsp.

engleranum, commonly known as the slender melodorum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and prolonged flowering period.
