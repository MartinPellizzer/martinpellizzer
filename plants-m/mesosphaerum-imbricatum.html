Mesosphaerum imbricatum

Mesosphaerum imbricatum, commonly known as the rock wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

Mesosphaerum imbricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
