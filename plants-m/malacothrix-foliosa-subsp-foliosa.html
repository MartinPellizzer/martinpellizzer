Malacothrix foliosa subsp. foliosa

Malacothrix foliosa subsp.

foliosa, commonly known as the leafy malacothrix, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features slender stems that are often hairy, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale green to white, and they bloom in spring.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure, which gives it its common name.
