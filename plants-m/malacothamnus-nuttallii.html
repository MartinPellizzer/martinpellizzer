Malacothamnus nuttallii

Malacothamnus nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. Malacothamnus nuttallii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing forage for wildlife and contributing to soil health.
