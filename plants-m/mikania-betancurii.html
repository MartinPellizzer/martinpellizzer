Mikania betancurii

Mikania betancurii, commonly known as the "Carnivorous Mikania," is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. While it is not truly carnivorous, it is known for its aggressive invasive behavior in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in ecological studies due to its impact on local flora and fauna.
