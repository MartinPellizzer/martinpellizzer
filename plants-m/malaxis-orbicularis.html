Malaxis orbicularis

Malaxis orbicularis, commonly known as the globular orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a short, slender stem with a single, rounded, leaf-like structure. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in a loose inflorescence.

Malaxis orbicularis is noted for its unique morphology and is a member of the Orchidaceae family.
