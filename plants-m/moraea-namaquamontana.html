Moraea namaquamontana

Moraea namaquamontana, commonly known as the Namaqua lily, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It features strap-like leaves and produces showy blooms during the summer months.

Moraea namaquamontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
