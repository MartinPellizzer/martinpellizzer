Mikania killipii

Mikania killipii, commonly known as the Killip's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
