Masdevallia sanctae-rosae

Masdevallia sanctae-rosae, commonly known as the Santa Rosa masdevallia, is a striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its high-altitude environment.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
