Monardella macrantha var. longiloba

Monardella macrantha var. longiloba, commonly known as long-stemmed monardella, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features showy, tubular pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
