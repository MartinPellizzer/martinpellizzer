Micranthes daurica

Micranthes daurica, commonly known as Daurian rockcress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with four petals. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Micranthes daurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
