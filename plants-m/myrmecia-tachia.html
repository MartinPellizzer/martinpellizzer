Myrmecia tachia

Myrmecia tachia, commonly known as the black ant plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive black, ant-like appearance. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers. It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Myrmecia tachia is a low-growing shrub that is well-suited for use in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
