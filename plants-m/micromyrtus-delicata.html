Micromyrtus delicata

Micromyrtus delicata, commonly known as the slender myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in coastal climates with moderate rainfall. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a glossy surface, and it produces white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months.

Micromyrtus delicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to withstand salt spray.
