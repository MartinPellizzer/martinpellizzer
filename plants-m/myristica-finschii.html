Myristica finschii

Myristica finschii, commonly known as the Bornean nutmeg, is a species of evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the spice nutmeg (Myristica fragrans). The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and develops distinctive, woody fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include smooth, dark green leaves and a dense, branched canopy.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
