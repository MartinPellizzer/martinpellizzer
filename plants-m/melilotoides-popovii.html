Melilotoides popovii

Melilotoides popovii, commonly known as the Tibetan sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and adjacent areas of China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.

Due to its ecological significance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
