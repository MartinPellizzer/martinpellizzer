Mesembryanthemum globosum

Mesembryanthemum globosum, commonly known as the globular ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It blooms during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
