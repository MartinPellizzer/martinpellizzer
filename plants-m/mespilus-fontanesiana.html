Mespilus fontanesiana

Mespilus fontanesiana, commonly known as the Fontanesian medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by edible, apple-like fruits that ripen in autumn. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its adaptability to various climatic conditions.
