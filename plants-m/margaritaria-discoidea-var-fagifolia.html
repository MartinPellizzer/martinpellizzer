Margaritaria discoidea var. fagifolia

Margaritaria discoidea var. fagifolia, commonly known as the Florida anise tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic and have a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, yellowish fruits that resemble tiny marbles. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of light conditions.
