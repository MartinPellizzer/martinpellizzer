Medinilla magnifica

Medinilla magnifica, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or Christmas bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that resemble small tangerines. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a velvety texture, and bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial shade.

Medinilla magnifica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and long-lasting blooms.
