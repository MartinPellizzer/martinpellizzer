Mooria artensis

Mooria artensis, commonly known as the Artensian moor plant, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous structure with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming in the early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Mooria artensis is considered a conservation concern.
