Moranopteris grisebachii

Moranopteris grisebachii, commonly known as the gray frond fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically grayish in color. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Moranopteris grisebachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
