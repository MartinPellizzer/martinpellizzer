Mimulus washingtonensis

Mimulus washingtonensis, commonly known as the Washington monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. It has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in riparian habitats.

Mimulus washingtonensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
