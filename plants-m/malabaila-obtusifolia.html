Malabaila obtusifolia

Malabaila obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved malabaila, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically broad and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
