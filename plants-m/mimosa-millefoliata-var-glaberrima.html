Mimosa millefoliata var. glaberrima

Mimosa millefoliata var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous mimosa, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically oval and arranged in pairs, giving it a distinctive feathery appearance. The plant produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas make it an important component of early successional plant communities.
