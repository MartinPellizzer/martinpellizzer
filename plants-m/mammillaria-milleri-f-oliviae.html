Mammillaria milleri f. oliviae

Mammillaria milleri f. oliviae, commonly known as the Olivia's mammillaria, is a rare and striking cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and clusters of white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features short, cylindrical stems covered in white to yellowish tubercles, which are small, rounded bumps. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by collectors and enthusiasts.
