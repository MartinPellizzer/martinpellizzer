Myosotis scorpioides subsp. lamottiana

Myosotis scorpioides subsp.

lamottiana, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It belongs to the Myosotidaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various regions worldwide.
