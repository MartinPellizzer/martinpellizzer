Mitrephora alba

Mitrephora alba, commonly known as white mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a globose drupe, often containing a single seed, and it is commonly found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
