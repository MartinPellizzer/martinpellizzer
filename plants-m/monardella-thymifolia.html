Monardella thymifolia

Monardella thymifolia, commonly known as lemon mint, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and open woodlands.

Monardella thymifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
