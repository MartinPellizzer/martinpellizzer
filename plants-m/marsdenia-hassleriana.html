Marsdenia hassleriana

Marsdenia hassleriana, commonly known as the Hassler's Marsdenia, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by follicles containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
