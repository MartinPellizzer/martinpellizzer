Mascarenhasia grandidieri

Mascarenhasia grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively in the island nation of Madagascar.

This plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, star-shaped flowers that display a striking combination of colors. It thrives in specific microhabitats, often growing on rocky outcrops or in shaded forest areas. The plant's survival is threatened by habitat loss and over-collection, making conservation efforts crucial for its preservation.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a flagship species for biodiversity conservation in Madagascar.
