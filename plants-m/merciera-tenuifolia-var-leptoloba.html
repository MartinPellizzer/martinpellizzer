Merciera tenuifolia var. leptoloba

Merciera tenuifolia var. leptoloba, commonly known as the slender-leafed mercier, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
