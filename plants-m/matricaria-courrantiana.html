Matricaria courrantiana

Matricaria courrantiana, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble daisies, with ray florets surrounding a central disk. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Matricaria courrantiana is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
