Machaerium declinatum

Machaerium declinatum, commonly known as the leaning machaerium, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, climbing stems with trifoliate leaves, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, round seeds.

Machaerium declinatum is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
