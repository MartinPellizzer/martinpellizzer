Myrcia glaberrima

Myrcia glaberrima, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are often fragrant. Myrcia glaberrima is typically found in humid, tropical environments and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species within its native habitat.
