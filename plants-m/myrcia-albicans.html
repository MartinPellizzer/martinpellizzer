Myrcia albicans

Myrcia albicans, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia albicans thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
