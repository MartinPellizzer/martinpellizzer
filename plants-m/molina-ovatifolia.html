Molina ovatifolia

Molina ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf molina, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
