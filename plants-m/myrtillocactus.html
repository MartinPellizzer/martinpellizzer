Myrtillocactus

Myrtillocactus, commonly known as the blue cactus, is a genus of flowering plants native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple spines and fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in the spring or summer. Myrtillocactus species are typically low-maintenance, making them popular choices for succulent gardens and indoor plant collections.

Due to their unique appearance and hardy nature, they are also valued for their ornamental appeal.
