Myrica esculenta var. tonkinensis

Myrica esculenta var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin bay leaf, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often used as a culinary spice, similar to bay leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its aromatic leaves, it is valued both for its flavor and its ornamental qualities.
