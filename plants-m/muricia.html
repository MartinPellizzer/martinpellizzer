Muricia

Muricia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces orange-yellow fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Muricia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
