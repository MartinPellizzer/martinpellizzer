Mentha × dumetorum var. carnuntiae

Mentha × dumetorum var. carnuntiae, commonly known as the purple mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive violet to purple flowering shoots, which distinguish it from other mint varieties. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.
