Matelea insolita

Matelea insolita, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a climbing plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and dark green color.

The flowers emit a strong, sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.
