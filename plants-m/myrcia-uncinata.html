Myrcia uncinata

Myrcia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and emits a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Myrcia uncinata is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.
