Myristica malaccensis subsp. papillosa

Myristica malaccensis subsp.

papillosa, commonly known as the Malacca nut or white nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, woody fruits that contain aromatic seeds.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent veins. Its subspecies papillosa is distinguished by the presence of hairy structures on its fruits and young leaves.

The tree is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
