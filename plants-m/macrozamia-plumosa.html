Macrozamia plumosa

Macrozamia plumosa, commonly known as the feather cycad, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces cones, with the male cones being cylindrical and the female cones being ovoid and often covered in spines. It is a slow-growing, long-lived plant that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
