Maranta dubia

Maranta dubia, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that displays a mix of green, white, and pink hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Maranta dubia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, rich soil to flourish.

Its unique leaf patterns and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
