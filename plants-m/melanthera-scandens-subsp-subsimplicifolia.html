Melanthera scandens subsp. subsimplicifolia

Melanthera scandens subsp.

subsimplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves that are simple and ovate.

The flowers are small, clustered in loose panicles, and typically have a yellowish-green hue with a slight reddish tinge. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds but not typically consumed by humans.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
