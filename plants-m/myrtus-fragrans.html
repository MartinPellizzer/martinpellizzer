Myrtus fragrans

Myrtus fragrans, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, dark berries that develop after flowering.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
