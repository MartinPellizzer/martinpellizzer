Mespilus unilateralis

Mespilus unilateralis, commonly known as the single-sided medlar, is a rare and unique species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive asymmetric growth pattern, with one side of the plant developing more prominently than the other. The plant produces small, edible fruits that ripen in the fall, though they are often considered a delicacy in certain regions. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity, Mespilus unilateralis is of particular interest to botanists and horticulturists.
