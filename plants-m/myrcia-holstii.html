Myrcia holstii

Myrcia holstii, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrcia holstii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
