Monolepis chenopodioides

Monolepis chenopodioides, commonly known as the silverleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine hairs, and its small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
