Myrcia calumbaensis

Myrcia calumbaensis, commonly known as the Calumba myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
