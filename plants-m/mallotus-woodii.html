Mallotus woodii

Mallotus woodii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of yellow flowers, which resemble small showers of golden beads. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
