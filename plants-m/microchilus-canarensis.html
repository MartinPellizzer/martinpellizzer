Microchilus canarensis

Microchilus canarensis, commonly known as the Canary Island orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers with a unique coloration pattern. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in the humid and mild climate of its native region. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces a single flower spike with several small, fragrant blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
