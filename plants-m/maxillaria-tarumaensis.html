Maxillaria tarumaensis

Maxillaria tarumaensis, commonly known as the Taruma orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces showy, fragrant flowers with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals. Its roots are adapted for epiphytic growth, allowing it to thrive on tree bark in humid tropical environments.

Maxillaria tarumaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
