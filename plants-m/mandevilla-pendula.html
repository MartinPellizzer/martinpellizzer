Mandevilla pendula

Mandevilla pendula, commonly known as the weeping mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green. It thrives in warm climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant in hanging baskets or containers.

Mandevilla pendula is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
