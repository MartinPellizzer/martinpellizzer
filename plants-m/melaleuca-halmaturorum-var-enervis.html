Melaleuca halmaturorum var. enervis

Melaleuca halmaturorum var. enervis, commonly known as the Halmaturong wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow to golden flowers that bloom in winter. The leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. This plant is adapted to low rainfall and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
