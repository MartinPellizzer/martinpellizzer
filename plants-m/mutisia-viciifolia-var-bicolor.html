Mutisia viciifolia var. bicolor

Mutisia viciifolia var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that display two distinct colors. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
