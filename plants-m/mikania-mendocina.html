Mikania mendocina

Mikania mendocina, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas.

Due to its invasive nature, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain regions.
