Myosurus apetalus var. montanus

Myosurus apetalus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked, typically in shades of pale green or white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
