Muscarella tempestalis

Muscarella tempestalis, commonly known as the storm orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and a unique, intricate floral structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often growing in rocky crevices or shaded forest floors. Due to habitat loss and climate change, it is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.

Its botanical study provides valuable insights into the adaptation strategies of alpine flora.
