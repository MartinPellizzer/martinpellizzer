Mentha longifolia var. stenophylla

Mentha longifolia var. stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a subspecies of the mint plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, often growing in rocky or limestone soils. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a strong minty fragrance.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its cooling and soothing properties.
