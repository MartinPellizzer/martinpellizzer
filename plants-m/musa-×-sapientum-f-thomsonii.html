Musa × sapientum f. thomsonii

Musa × sapientum f. thomsonii, commonly known as the Thomson's banana, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, and typically yellow or orange-colored fruit, which is rich in nutrients such as potassium and vitamin C. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and high humidity for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and can reach significant sizes, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for both its edible fruit and aesthetic appeal in gardens.
