Mentha × brauniana

Mentha × brauniana, commonly known as the hybrid spearmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha viridis (green mint), resulting in a plant with distinctive features. The leaves are lance-shaped, slightly serrated, and have a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in culinary, medicinal, and aromatic applications.
