Mytilicoccus

Mytilicoccus, commonly known as the mussels-associated bacterium, is a genus of Gram-positive, aerobic bacteria.

These microorganisms are typically found in association with mussels, where they contribute to the microbial community within the gill tissues. Mytilicoccus species are characterized by their rod-shaped morphology and their ability to form biofilms. They play a role in nutrient cycling and may influence the health of their host organisms.

Due to their unique ecological niche, they are of interest in both environmental and biotechnological research.
