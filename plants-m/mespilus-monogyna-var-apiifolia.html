Mespilus monogyna var. apiifolia

Mespilus monogyna var. apiifolia, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a hairy underside. It produces small, round fruits known as drupes, which are edible when ripe.

This plant is valued for its ecological role and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.
