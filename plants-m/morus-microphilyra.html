Morus microphilyra

Morus microphilyra, commonly known as the small-leaved mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves with a smooth margin. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called syconia, which are typically eaten by birds and other animals. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
