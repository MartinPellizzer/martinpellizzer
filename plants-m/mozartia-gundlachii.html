Mozartia gundlachii

Mozartia gundlachii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.
