Matthiola bolleana subsp. viridis

Matthiola bolleana subsp.

viridis, commonly known as the green wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often bright yellow or orange flowers.

The subspecies viridis is distinguished by its greenish foliage, which contrasts with the showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
