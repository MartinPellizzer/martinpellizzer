Mimosa radula

Mimosa radula, commonly known as the sticky mimosa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a unique sticky substance from its stems, which is used by some insects for shelter. Mimosa radula thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
