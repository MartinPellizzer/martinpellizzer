Murtughas tomentosa

Murtughas tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub characterized by its hairy, grayish leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Murtughas tomentosa is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage.
