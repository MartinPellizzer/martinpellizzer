Monarda austroappalachiana

Monarda austroappalachiana, commonly known as the Appalachian beebalm, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range from red to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has aromatic leaves that release a strong scent when crushed.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
