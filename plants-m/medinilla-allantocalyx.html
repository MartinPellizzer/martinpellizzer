Medinilla allantocalyx

Medinilla allantocalyx, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Medinilla allantocalyx is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
