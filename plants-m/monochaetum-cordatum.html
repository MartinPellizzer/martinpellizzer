Monochaetum cordatum

Monochaetum cordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

Monochaetum cordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
