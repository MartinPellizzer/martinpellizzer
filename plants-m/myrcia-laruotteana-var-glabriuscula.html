Myrcia laruotteana var. glabriuscula

Myrcia laruotteana var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth-leaved myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species and is of interest for conservation and botanical research.
