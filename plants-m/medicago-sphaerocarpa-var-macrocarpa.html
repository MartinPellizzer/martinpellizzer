Medicago sphaerocarpa var. macrocarpa

Medicago sphaerocarpa var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spherical, inflated seed pods that contain large, edible seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with small, yellow flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a forage crop.
