Mentha × kerneri var. lachnopoa

Mentha × kerneri var. lachnopoa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are typically narrower than those of its parent species. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its use in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
