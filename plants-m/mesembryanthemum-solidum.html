Mesembryanthemum solidum

Mesembryanthemum solidum, commonly known as the rock rose or ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming in spring. It grows close to the ground, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Mesembryanthemum solidum is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
