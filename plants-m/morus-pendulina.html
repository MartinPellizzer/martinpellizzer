Morus pendulina

Morus pendulina, commonly known as the weeping mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are typically dark purple when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Morus pendulina is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is also used in traditional medicine.
