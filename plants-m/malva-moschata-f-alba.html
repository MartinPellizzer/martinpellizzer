Malva moschata f. alba

Malva moschata f. alba, commonly known as the white musk mallow, is a cultivar of the musk mallow species.

It is characterized by its large, white, velvety petals that contrast with its deep green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
