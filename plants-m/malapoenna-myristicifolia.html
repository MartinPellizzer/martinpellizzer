Malapoenna myristicifolia

Malapoenna myristicifolia, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by orange-red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions with a warm, temperate climate.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
