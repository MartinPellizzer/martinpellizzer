Myrciaria nettiana

Myrciaria nettiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *cereja-brasileira*, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other notable plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a bright red drupe, often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
