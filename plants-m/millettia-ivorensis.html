Millettia ivorensis

Millettia ivorensis, commonly known as the Ivory Coast bean, is a leguminous plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are rich in protein and essential minerals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Millettia ivorensis plays a significant role in traditional agriculture and local food systems in its native regions.
