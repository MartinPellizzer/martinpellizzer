Muhlenbergia schreberi var. curtisetosa

Muhlenbergia schreberi var. curtisetosa, commonly known as the curly mesquite grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, curly, silver-gray leaves that are often used in xeriscaping due to their drought tolerance. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that add ornamental value, especially in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid landscapes.

Its low maintenance requirements and ability to stabilize soil make it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
