Macdonaldia cyanea

Macdonaldia cyanea, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its vivid blue petals, which are rare in the orchid world, and its compact, terrestrial growth habit. The plant typically features a single, erect stem with several showy flowers that bloom in spring. Its unique coloration is due to the presence of anthocyanins, which contribute to its striking appearance.

Macdonaldia cyanea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of New Zealand's botanical diversity.
