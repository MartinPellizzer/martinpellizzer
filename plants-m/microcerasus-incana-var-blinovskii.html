Microcerasus incana var. blinovskii

Microcerasus incana var. blinovskii, commonly known as the Blinovskii snowberry, is a variant of the snowberry plant native to specific regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white berries that resemble snowballs, which are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has woody stems covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
