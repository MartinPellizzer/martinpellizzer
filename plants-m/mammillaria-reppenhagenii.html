Mammillaria reppenhagenii

Mammillaria reppenhagenii, commonly known as the Pincushion Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant features small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
