Mimosa botucatuana

Mimosa botucatuana, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and fluffy, yellowish-pink flower clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
