Mapania multispicata

Mapania multispicata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are woody and somewhat succulent.

Mapania multispicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
