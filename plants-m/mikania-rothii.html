Mikania rothii

Mikania rothii, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an invasive species in some regions, impacting local ecosystems.
