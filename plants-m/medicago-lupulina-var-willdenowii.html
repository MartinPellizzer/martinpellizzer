Medicago lupulina var. willdenowii

Medicago lupulina var. willdenowii, commonly known as the yellow sweetclover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
