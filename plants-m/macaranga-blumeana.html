Macaranga blumeana

Macaranga blumeana, commonly known as the giant paper mulberry, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a fast-growing shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
