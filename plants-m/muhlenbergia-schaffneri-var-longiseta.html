Muhlenbergia schaffneri var. longiseta

Muhlenbergia schaffneri var. longiseta, commonly known as the silverhair muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stature and silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

This ornamental grass is often used in xeriscaping and native plant gardens for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
