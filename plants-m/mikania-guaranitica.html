Mikania guaranitica

Mikania guaranitica, commonly known as the Brazilian star-of-Bahia, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in humid, tropical environments. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its fibrous stems make it a useful plant for erosion control in certain regions.
