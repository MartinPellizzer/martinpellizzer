Malus baccata var. gracilis

Malus baccata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in spring, followed by small, red, edible fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often used in landscape design for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
