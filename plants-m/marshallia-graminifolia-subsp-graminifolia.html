Marshallia graminifolia subsp. graminifolia

Marshallia graminifolia subsp.

graminifolia, commonly known as the grassleaf marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous form with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and pond edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and wetland ecosystems.
