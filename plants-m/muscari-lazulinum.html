Muscari lazulinum

Muscari lazulinum, commonly known as the Azure Muscat or Persian bluebells, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Turkey, Armenia, and Georgia, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its bulbous roots are adapted to survive in harsh, arid conditions.

Muscari lazulinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking color and fragrance.
