Marsdenia flavescens

Marsdenia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

Marsdenia flavescens is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
