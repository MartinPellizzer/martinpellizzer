Maranta polita

Maranta polita, commonly known as the prayer plant, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that fold upwards at night, a behavior known as nyctinasty. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value. Its leaves often display patterns of red, pink, and green, which can vary depending on light exposure.

Maranta polita prefers indirect sunlight and well-draining soil to thrive.
