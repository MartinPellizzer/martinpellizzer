Mammillaria sulcata

Mammillaria sulcata, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and a distinctive ribbed structure that gives it a unique appearance. The plant features a deep green body with a pale, often bluish or pinkish coloration on its surface. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding to its ornamental value.

Mammillaria sulcata is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.
