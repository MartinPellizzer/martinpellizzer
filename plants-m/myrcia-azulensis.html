Myrcia azulensis

Myrcia azulensis, commonly known as blue myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its attractive blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

Myrcia azulensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
