Melaleuca salicifolia

Melaleuca salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Melaleuca salicifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
