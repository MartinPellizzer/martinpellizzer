Mammillaria imbricata

Mammillaria imbricata, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white, woolly hairs that help protect it from intense sunlight and moisture loss. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
