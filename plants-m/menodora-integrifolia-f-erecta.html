Menodora integrifolia f. erecta

Menodora integrifolia f. erecta, commonly known as the upright devil's backbone, is a variant of the devil's backbone plant native to South Africa.

It is a climbing or trailing perennial herb characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are usually entire, meaning they have smooth edges without teeth or lobes.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
