Mastixia ledermannii

Mastixia ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's mastixia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

Mastixia ledermannii is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in heathland and coastal scrub environments.
