Muscari cyaneoviolaceum

Muscari cyaneoviolaceum, commonly known as the blue musk iris, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, giving it its common name. The plant produces long, strap-like leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Muscari cyaneoviolaceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
