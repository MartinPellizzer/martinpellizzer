Malpighia myrtoides

Malpighia myrtoides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its growth habit is typically low and compact, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

The fruit's unique flavor and nutritional value have made it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
