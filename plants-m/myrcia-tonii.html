Myrcia tonii

Myrcia tonii, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with dense, aromatic foliage. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a strong, spicy scent. Its small white flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries.

The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove.
