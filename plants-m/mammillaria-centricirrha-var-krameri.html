Mammillaria centricirrha var. krameri

Mammillaria centricirrha var. krameri, commonly known as the Krameri mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is typically covered with white or pale blue tubercles, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
