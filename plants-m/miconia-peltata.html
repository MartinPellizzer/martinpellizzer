Miconia peltata

Miconia peltata, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia peltata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and rapid growth rate.
