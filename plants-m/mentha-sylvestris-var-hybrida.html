Mentha sylvestris var. hybrida

Mentha sylvestris var. hybrida, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Mentha sylvestris var. hybrida is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its menthol-rich essential oils.
