Muhlenbergia schaffneri

Muhlenbergia schaffneri, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that turns coppery in the fall, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch of pink to the landscape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
