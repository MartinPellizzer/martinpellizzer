Montia rosulata

Montia rosulata, commonly known as the rosette mallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, oval-shaped leaves arranged in a tight rosette. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Montia rosulata is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.
