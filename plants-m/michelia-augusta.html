Michelia augusta

Michelia augusta, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia or the fragrant michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Magnoliaceae and is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Michelia augusta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
