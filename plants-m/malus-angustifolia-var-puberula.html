Malus angustifolia var. puberula

Malus angustifolia var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent apple tree, is a cultivar of the apple tree characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves and fruit.

It is native to China and is known for its small, sour apples that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are elliptical, with a serrated margin and a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are white and appear in clusters.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its medicinal and culinary uses.
