Micropus pygmaeus

Micropus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the valley, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces delicate white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in shaded, moist environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
