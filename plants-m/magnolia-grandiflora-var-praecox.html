Magnolia grandiflora var. praecox

Magnolia grandiflora var. praecox, commonly known as the southern magnolia or early-flowering magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The flowers are typically borne on short pedicels and have a fragrant, sweet scent. The tree produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is well adapted to humid climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
