Myrcia eriocalyx f. obtusata

Myrcia eriocalyx f. obtusata, commonly known as the broadleaf myrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically pink or red, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
