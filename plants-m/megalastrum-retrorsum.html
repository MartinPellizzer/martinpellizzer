Megalastrum retrorsum

Megalastrum retrorsum, commonly known as the backward-facing star vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Megalastrum retrorsum is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
