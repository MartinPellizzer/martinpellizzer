Myrcia bullata

Myrcia bullata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia bullata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming an invasive species.
