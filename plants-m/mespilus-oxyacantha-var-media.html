Mespilus oxyacantha var. media

Mespilus oxyacantha var. media, commonly known as the common blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces black, plum-like fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
