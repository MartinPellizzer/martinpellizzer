Myrcia glazioviana var. villosa

Myrcia glazioviana var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy myrcia, is a shrub native to Brazil, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The varietal name 'villosa' refers to the hairy texture of its leaves, which is a distinguishing feature.
