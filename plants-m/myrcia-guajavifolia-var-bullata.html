Myrcia guajavifolia var. bullata

Myrcia guajavifolia var. bullata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight pubescent underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is notable for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
