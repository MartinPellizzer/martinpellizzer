Malus microcarpa ringo

Malus microcarpa ringo, commonly known as the Japanese apple or ringo, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It produces small, tart apples that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval in shape and have a smooth margin. It blooms with clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by the development of its characteristic fruit.

The tree prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
