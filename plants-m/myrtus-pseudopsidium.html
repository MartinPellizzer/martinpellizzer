Myrtus pseudopsidium

Myrtus pseudopsidium, commonly known as the narrowleaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and aromatic. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Myrtus pseudopsidium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
