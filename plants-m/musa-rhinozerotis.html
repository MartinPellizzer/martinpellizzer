Musa rhinozerotis

Musa rhinozerotis, commonly known as the African banana, is a species of banana plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces clusters of elongated, edible fruits. The plant is part of the Musaceae family and is closely related to other banana species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its fruits are typically used for local consumption and have cultural significance in various African communities.
