Mimosa calderonii

Mimosa calderonii, commonly known as the Mexican mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fluffy flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Mimosa calderonii is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
