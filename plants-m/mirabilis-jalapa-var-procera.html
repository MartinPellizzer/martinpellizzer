Mirabilis jalapa var. procera

Mirabilis jalapa var. procera, commonly known as the everlasting or miracle plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting blooms that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of conditions.

Its flowers are tubular and open sequentially, allowing for a prolonged display of color throughout the growing season.
