Maxillaria riopalenquensis

Maxillaria riopalenquensis, commonly known as the Rio Palenquensis orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with erect, hairy pseudobulbs that bear lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
