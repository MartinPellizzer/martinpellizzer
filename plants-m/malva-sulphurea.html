Malva sulphurea

Malva sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows and rocky soils. It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cotton and hibiscus.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
