Monstera maxima

Monstera maxima, commonly known as the giant monstera, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in diameter, featuring a distinctive split that divides the leaf into two large sections. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil to flourish. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening, though it can become quite sizable over time.

The leaves are also known for their ability to filter air pollutants, making it both aesthetically pleasing and beneficial for indoor environments.
