Malpighia raunkiaeri

Malpighia raunkiaeri, commonly known as the velvetleaf acerola, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, bright red berries. The plant produces edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
