Miconia speciosa

Miconia speciosa, commonly known as the beauty leaf, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, velvety, red or purple fruits. Miconia speciosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
