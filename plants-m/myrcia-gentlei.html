Myrcia gentlei

Myrcia gentlei, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the pale flowers.

The fruit, which is edible, is a small, round berry that turns from green to a striking violet color as it matures.
