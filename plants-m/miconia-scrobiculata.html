Miconia scrobiculata

Miconia scrobiculata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia scrobiculata is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land managers.
