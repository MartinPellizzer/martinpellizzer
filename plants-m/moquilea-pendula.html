Moquilea pendula

Moquilea pendula, commonly known as the hanging moquilea, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
