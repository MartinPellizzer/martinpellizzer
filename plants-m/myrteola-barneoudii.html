Myrteola barneoudii

Myrteola barneoudii, commonly known as the African myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which emit a pleasant scent when crushed. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

Myrteola barneoudii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
