Melanoxerus maritimus

Melanoxerus maritimus, commonly known as the coastal bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant found in brackish and freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in nutrient acquisition. The plant typically grows in shallow, muddy substrates near coastal areas. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, while the main photosynthetic organs are the bladders.

Melanoxerus maritimus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
