Miconia lanuginosa

Miconia lanuginosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often overlooked due to the dense foliage. Its fruits are round, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing numerous small seeds.

Miconia lanuginosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
