Macrozamia spiralis var. fawcettii

Macrozamia spiralis var. fawcettii, commonly known as the Fawcett's cycad, is a slow-growing, evergreen plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones, with the male cones being cylindrical and the female cones ovoid, both typically appearing on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, dry climate, making it suitable for cultivation in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its rarity and conservation status, it is often protected and requires careful management in both wild and cultivated settings.
