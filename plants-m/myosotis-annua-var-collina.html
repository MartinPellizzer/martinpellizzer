Myosotis annua var. collina

Myosotis annua var. collina, commonly known as the alpine forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It is a perennial species that produces numerous seeds, contributing to its spread in suitable environments.

The flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
