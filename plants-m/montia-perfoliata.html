Montia perfoliata

Montia perfoliata, commonly known as the devil's-bit scabious, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often perforated at the center, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Montia perfoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
