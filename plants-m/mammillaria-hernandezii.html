Mammillaria hernandezii

Mammillaria hernandezii, commonly known as the Mexican sun rose, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
