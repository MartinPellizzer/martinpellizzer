Myriophyllum nitschei

Myriophyllum nitschei, commonly known as water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often feather-like, with small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, floating flowers that are typically greenish to reddish in color. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Myriophyllum nitschei plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for aquatic life.
