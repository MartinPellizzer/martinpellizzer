Madhuca stenophylla

Madhuca stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mahua, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, starchy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its timber and traditional uses in various cultural practices.
