Montia cordifolia

Montia cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf montia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale blue flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Montiaceae family.
