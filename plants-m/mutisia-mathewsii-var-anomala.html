Mutisia mathewsii var. anomala

Mutisia mathewsii var. anomala, commonly known as the Mathews' mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in cooler, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its distinctive appearance.
