Menyanthes tridentata

Menyanthes tridentata, commonly known as the water pimpernel, is a small herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically three-lobed and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its aquatic habitat. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in shallow water environments.

Menyanthes tridentata plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
