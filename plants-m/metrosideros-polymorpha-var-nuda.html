Metrosideros polymorpha var. nuda

Metrosideros polymorpha var. nuda, commonly known as the ʻōhiʻa lehua, is a native Hawaiian tree and shrub belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year, especially during the wet season. The plant has smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including rainforests, coastal areas, and disturbed soils.

The ʻōhiʻa lehua plays a crucial role in Hawaiian ecosystems and is culturally significant to Native Hawaiians.
