Mesosphaerum virgatum

Mesosphaerum virgatum, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It has a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Mesosphaerum virgatum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
