Mentha arvensis var. occidentalis

Mentha arvensis var. occidentalis, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it a valued species in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
