Maba salicifolia

Maba salicifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered maba, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by a fleshy, greenish cupule, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it often has a milky sap that can be toxic.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
