Myricaria elegans var. tsetangensis

Myricaria elegans var. tsetangensis, commonly known as the Tibetan myricaria, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and adjacent areas.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It exhibits a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Myricaria elegans var. tsetangensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest for its ecological and possibly medicinal properties.
