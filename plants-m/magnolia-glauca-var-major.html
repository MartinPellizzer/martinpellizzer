Magnolia glauca var. major

Magnolia glauca var. major, commonly known as the broad-leaf magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The foliage consists of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy green and turn yellow in the fall. This variety is noted for its robust growth and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
