Melanorrhoea beccarii

Melanorrhoea beccarii, commonly known as the black sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a fruit with a thick, inedible rind and a creamy, sweet flesh that is rich in nutrients. Its latex, known as sapote latex, has been used historically for various industrial and medicinal purposes.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
