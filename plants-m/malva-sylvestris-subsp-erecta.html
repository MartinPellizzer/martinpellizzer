Malva sylvestris subsp. erecta

Malva sylvestris subsp.

erecta, commonly known as the upright mallows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically lobed and have a woolly texture.

The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
