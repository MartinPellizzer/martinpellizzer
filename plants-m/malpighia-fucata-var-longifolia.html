Malpighia fucata var. longifolia

Malpighia fucata var. longifolia, commonly known as the red mombin or longifolia granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The berries are edible, with a tart flavor, and are used in culinary and medicinal applications in various regions.
