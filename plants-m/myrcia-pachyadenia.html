Myrcia pachyadenia

Myrcia pachyadenia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in some regions as a garden plant.
