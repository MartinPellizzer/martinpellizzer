Myrcia baracoensis

Myrcia baracoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds.

Myrcia baracoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
