Melissitus korovinii

Melissitus korovinii, commonly known as the Korovin's melissitus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
