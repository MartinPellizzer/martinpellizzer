Miconia mcvaughii

Miconia mcvaughii, commonly known as the Hawaiian miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Miconia mcvaughii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in native forests on the islands of Hawaii.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other tropical regions, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
