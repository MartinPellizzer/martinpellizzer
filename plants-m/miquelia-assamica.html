Miquelia assamica

Miquelia assamica, commonly known as the Assam miquelias, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The fruit is a capsule that contains small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
