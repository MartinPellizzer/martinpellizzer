Mentha grisella subsp. seriata

Mentha grisella subsp.

seriata, commonly known as the slender mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a serrated margin and a strong, minty fragrance.

The flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems.
