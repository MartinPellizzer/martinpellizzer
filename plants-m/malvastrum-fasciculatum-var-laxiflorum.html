Malvastrum fasciculatum var. laxiflorum

Malvastrum fasciculatum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is a perennial species that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
