Macaranga fragrans

Macaranga fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant macaranga, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its sap is milky and can be toxic, a common trait among members of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Macaranga fragrans is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental leaves in horticultural settings.
