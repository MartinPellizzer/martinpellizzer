Mentha gentilis var. friesii

Mentha gentilis var. friesii, commonly known as the German mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a minty scent and flavor.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
