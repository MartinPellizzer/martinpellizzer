Mangifera indica var. bapang

Mangifera indica var. bapang, commonly known as the Bapang mango, is a tropical fruiting plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a thick, fibrous skin that can range in color from yellow to red when ripe. The plant produces sweet, aromatic flesh that is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires a long growing season.

The Bapang mango is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for local markets and export.
