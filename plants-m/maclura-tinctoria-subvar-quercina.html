Maclura tinctoria subvar. quercina

Maclura tinctoria subvar. quercina, commonly known as the yellow wood or hedge bindweed, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, heart-shaped leaves, and yellow-orange fruits that contain toxic compounds. The plant is part of the Moraceae family and is known for its hard, dense wood, which was historically used for tool handles and furniture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it relies on insects for pollination.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
