Modiola caroliniana

Modiola caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

The botanical features of Modiola caroliniana make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
