Myrcia sanisidrensis

Myrcia sanisidrensis, commonly known as the Sanisidre myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in montane and lowland areas. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, and it has glossy, elliptical leaves. Myrcia sanisidrensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

Its conservation status is threatened due to habitat loss and deforestation.
