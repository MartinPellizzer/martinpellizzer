Montia verrucosa

Montia verrucosa, commonly known as the warty bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces tiny, white or pale blue flowers. Its most distinctive characteristic is the presence of warty structures on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

Montia verrucosa is part of the Montiaceae family and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.
