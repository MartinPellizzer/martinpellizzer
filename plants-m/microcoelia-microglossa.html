Microcoelia microglossa

Microcoelia microglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf microcoelia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver-blue hue on the upper surface.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.
