Micrechites grandiflorus

Micrechites grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or alpine environments. The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Micrechites grandiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
