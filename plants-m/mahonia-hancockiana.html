Mahonia hancockiana

Mahonia hancockiana, commonly known as the Hancock's mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries in the fall. Mahonia hancockiana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9.
