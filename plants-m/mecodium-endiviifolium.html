Mecodium endiviifolium

Mecodium endiviifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf mecodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped, resembling those of endive. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often in shades of green or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is noted for its succulent nature, which helps it retain water in arid environments.
