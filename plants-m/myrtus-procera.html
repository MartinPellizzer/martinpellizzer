Myrtus procera

Myrtus procera, commonly known as the tall myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are often aromatic and have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. Myrtus procera is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has historical uses in medicine and perfumery.
