Melastoma quinquenode

Melastoma quinquenode, commonly known as the five-node melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Melastoma quinquenode is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
