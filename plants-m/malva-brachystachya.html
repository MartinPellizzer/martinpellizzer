Malva brachystachya

Malva brachystachya, commonly known as the narrow-stalked mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Malva brachystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
