Myrceugenia pitra var. angustifolia

Myrceugenia pitra var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a species native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
