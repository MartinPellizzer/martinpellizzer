Myrceugenia regnelliana

Myrceugenia regnelliana, commonly known as the Regnell's myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Myrceugenia regnelliana is often found in humid, tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
