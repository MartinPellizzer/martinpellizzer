Mapania micropandanus

Mapania micropandanus, commonly known as the pandanus or screw pine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that are long, narrow, and often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color, which develop into woody, fibrous fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in saline or waterlogged soils.
