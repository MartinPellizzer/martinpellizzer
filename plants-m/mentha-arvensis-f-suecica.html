Mentha arvensis f. suecica

Mentha arvensis f. suecica, commonly known as the Swedish field mint, is a subspecies of the broad-leafed mint.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has a strong, minty scent and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in ecological and horticultural contexts.
