Mentha aquatica f. macrocephalota

Mentha aquatica f. macrocephalota, commonly known as the broad-headed water mint, is a variant of the water mint plant native to wetland areas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower heads that are typically blue or purple in color. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a strong menthol scent. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near streams or ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in aquatic habitats.
