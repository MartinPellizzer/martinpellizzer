Mammillaria gracilis f. pulchella

Mammillaria gracilis f. pulchella, commonly known as the elegant mammillaria, is a compact cactus native to the rocky soils of Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in small, rounded tubercles that give it a distinctive, spiny appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its growth is slow, making it suitable for small spaces and container gardening.

Due to its delicate structure, it requires careful handling and well-draining soil to thrive.
