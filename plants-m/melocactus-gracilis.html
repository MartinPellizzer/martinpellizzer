Melocactus gracilis

Melocactus gracilis, commonly known as the slender melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces a single, elongated flower that typically blooms in shades of yellow or orange. Its thick, waxy skin helps it retain moisture in harsh desert conditions.

Melocactus gracilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
