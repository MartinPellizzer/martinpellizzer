Meliosma rhoifolia subsp. barbulata

Meliosma rhoifolia subsp.

barbulata, commonly known as the hairy meliosma, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
