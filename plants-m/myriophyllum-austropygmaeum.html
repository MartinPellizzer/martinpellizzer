Myriophyllum austropygmaeum

Myriophyllum austropygmaeum, commonly known as the European water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe.

It has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Myriophyllum austropygmaeum is often found in still or slow-moving waters such as lakes and ponds.
