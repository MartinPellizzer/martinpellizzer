Mucuna hirsuta

Mucuna hirsuta, commonly known as hairy urd, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, trifoliate leaves and long, slender pods that contain several seeds. The plant produces a distinctive, strong-smelling flower that is typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in agricultural fields and disturbed soils.

Mucuna hirsuta is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and is used in traditional medicine.
