Miconia elliptica

Miconia elliptica, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Brazilian shield-leaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Miconia elliptica is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its competitive nature.
