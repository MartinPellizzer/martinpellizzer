Medicago hispida var. trigyra

Medicago hispida var. trigyra, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely hairy stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spicate clusters, which are followed by elongated, twisted pods containing several seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry grasslands.

Medicago hispida var. trigyra is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
