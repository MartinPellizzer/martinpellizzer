Microstachys crassifolia

Microstachys crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved microstachys, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are thick and succulent, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Microstachys crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
