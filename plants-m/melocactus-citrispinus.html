Melocactus citrispinus

Melocactus citrispinus, commonly known as the citrin yellow melon cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and smooth, ribbed trunk covered in white to yellow spines. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, melon-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
