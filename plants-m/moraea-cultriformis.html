Moraea cultriformis

Moraea cultriformis, commonly known as the sickle lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, sword-shaped leaves and showy, bell-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which can be white, pink, or purple, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Moraea cultriformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
