Morus goldmanii

Morus goldmanii, commonly known as the golden mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn golden in the fall. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive flaky texture.
