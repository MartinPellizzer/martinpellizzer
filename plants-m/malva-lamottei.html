Malva lamottei

Malva lamottei, commonly known as the Malva of Lamotte, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Malva lamottei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
