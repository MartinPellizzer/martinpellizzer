Myrcia chapadensis

Myrcia chapadensis, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia chapadensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
