Mikania solidinervia

Mikania solidinervia, commonly known as the creeping mikania, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
