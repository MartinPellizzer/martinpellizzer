Myrodendrum subvaginale

Myrodendrum subvaginale, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Myrodendrum subvaginale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
