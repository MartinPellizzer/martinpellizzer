Malus zonalis

Malus zonalis, commonly known as the Japanese flowering crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its fruit, a small, red or yellow apple, is edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Malus zonalis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
