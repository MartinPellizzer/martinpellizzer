Malaxis streptopetala

Malaxis streptopetala, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a loose raceme. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in humid and cool conditions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often clustered at the base of the stem.

Malaxis streptopetala is known for its unique floral structure, with petals that curve in a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name.
