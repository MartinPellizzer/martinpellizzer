Medicago orbicularis var. glandulosa

Medicago orbicularis var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular medick, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its rounded, heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is typically found in dry, disturbed soils and is often seen in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. It has a low-growing habit and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

The glandular hairs on its stems and leaves are a distinguishing feature of this variety.
