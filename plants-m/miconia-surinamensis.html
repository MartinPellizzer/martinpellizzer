Miconia surinamensis

Miconia surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam miconia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that are often followed by bright red berries. It is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in tropical ecosystems.
