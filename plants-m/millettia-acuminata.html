Millettia acuminata

Millettia acuminata, commonly known as the acuminated millettia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its growth habit makes it a useful species for ecological restoration projects.
