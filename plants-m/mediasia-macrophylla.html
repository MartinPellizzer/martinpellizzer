Mediasia macrophylla

Mediasia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a floating canopy on the water's surface. The plant's flowers are showy, with a white or pinkish hue, and they open at night, attracting specific pollinators. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to support its extensive growth.

Mediasia macrophylla plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by offering habitat and shelter to various aquatic organisms.
