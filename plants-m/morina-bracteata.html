Morina bracteata

Morina bracteata, commonly known as the Chinese moringa, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with woody stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain small, hard seeds.

Morina bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
