Myrcia revoluta

Myrcia revoluta, commonly known as the velvet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its small, fragrant white flowers bloom in clusters and are followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits.

Myrcia revoluta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
