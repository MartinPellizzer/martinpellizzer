Metastelma californicum subsp. lanceolatum

Metastelma californicum subsp.

lanceolatum, commonly known as the California lanceleaf metastelma, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as chaparral and coastal sage scrub.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in dense inflorescences, and are typically white or pale green.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in supporting local pollinators.
