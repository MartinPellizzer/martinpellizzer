Mentha aquatica subsp. capitata

Mentha aquatica subsp.

capitata, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes.

The plant has square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers are arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

The essential oils found in its leaves and stems contain menthol and other compounds, making it useful in herbal medicine and aromatherapy.
