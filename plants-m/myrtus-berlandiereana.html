Myrtus berlandiereana

Myrtus berlandiereana, commonly known as the Berlandier myrtle, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The berries it produces are typically small and dark, containing seeds that can be dispersed by birds.
