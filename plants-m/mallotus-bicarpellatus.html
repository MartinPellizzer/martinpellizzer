Mallotus bicarpellatus

Mallotus bicarpellatus, commonly known as the two-chambered mallot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments.
