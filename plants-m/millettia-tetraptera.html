Millettia tetraptera

Millettia tetraptera, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces elongated, flat pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.

It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
