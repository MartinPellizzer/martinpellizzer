Malva triloba

Malva triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features palmate leaves with three to five lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Malva triloba is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
