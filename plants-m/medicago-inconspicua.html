Medicago inconspicua

Medicago inconspicua, commonly known as the common goat's rue, is a low-growing annual legume native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It has small, trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
