Metrosideros polymorpha var. pumila

Metrosideros polymorpha var. pumila, commonly known as the Hawaiian baby woodrose, is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in coastal and lowland habitats, often growing in disturbed or sandy soils. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.

The species is also notable for its role in traditional Hawaiian culture and ecological significance in its native environment.
