Melaleuca pumila

Melaleuca pumila, commonly known as the dwarf bottlebrush, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and sandy soils.

Melaleuca pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
