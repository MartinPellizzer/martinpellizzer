Macrolobium huberianum var. pubirachis

Macrolobium huberianum var. pubirachis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed macrolobium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, and often showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
