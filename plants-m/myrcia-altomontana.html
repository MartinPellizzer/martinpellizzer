Myrcia altomontana

Myrcia altomontana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain numerous seeds.

Myrcia altomontana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
