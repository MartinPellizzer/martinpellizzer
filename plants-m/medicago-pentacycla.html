Medicago pentacycla

Medicago pentacycla, commonly known as the purple needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, needle-like seed spikes. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Medicago pentacycla plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
