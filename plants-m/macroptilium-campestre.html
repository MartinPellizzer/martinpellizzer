Macroptilium campestre

Macroptilium campestre, commonly known as the field senna, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open areas such as fields and along roadsides. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Macroptilium campestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
