Mentha × maximilianea var. suavis

Mentha × maximilianea var. suavis, commonly known as sweet peppermint, is a hybrid mint species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a strong, sweet mint scent. The plant typically grows as an upright perennial with square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. It produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems.

This variety is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses due to its high menthol content.
