Mikania aristei

Mikania aristei, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can make it invasive in certain regions. Its stems are hairy and often have a milky sap, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
