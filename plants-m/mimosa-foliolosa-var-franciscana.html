Mimosa foliolosa var. franciscana

Mimosa foliolosa var. franciscana, commonly known as the Franciscan mimosa, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are compound and trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
