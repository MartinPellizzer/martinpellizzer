Mesembryanthemum hermannii

Mesembryanthemum hermannii, commonly known as the Hermann's ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the cooler months. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.

Mesembryanthemum hermannii is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in sandy or coastal areas.
