Micranthes foliolosa

Micranthes foliolosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas. The plant features small, opposite, and narrow leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are tiny, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically pink to reddish in color.

Micranthes foliolosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
