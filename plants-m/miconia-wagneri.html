Miconia wagneri

Miconia wagneri, commonly known as the Wagner's miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Miconia wagneri is known for its rapid growth and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
