Mussaenda tenuiflora var. principensis

Mussaenda tenuiflora var. principensis, commonly known as the Prince's Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, orange-red bract-like flowers that resemble petals, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy surface.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
