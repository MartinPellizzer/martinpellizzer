Mormolyca culebrica

Mormolyca culebrica, commonly known as the serpent's breath, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the misty highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its spiral-shaped leaves and striking, bioluminescent flowers that emit a faint glow at night. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded, forested areas. Its unique morphology and mysterious properties have made it a subject of fascination among botanists and herbalists.
