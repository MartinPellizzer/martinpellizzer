Melica cepacea

Melica cepacea, commonly known as the tall false brome, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Melica cepacea is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
