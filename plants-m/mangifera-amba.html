Mangifera amba

Mangifera amba, commonly known as the Indian mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. The fruit is a hairy, yellow or orange-colored drupe that ripens in late summer to early autumn. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local cultures.
