Manilkara mabokeensis

Manilkara mabokeensis, commonly known as the Congo sugar-apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that give way to spherical, green fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and economic significance in its native regions.
