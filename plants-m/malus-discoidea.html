Malus discoidea

Malus discoidea, commonly known as the "disk apple" or "Chinese white apple," is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow fruits that are typically eaten raw or cooked. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces showy, white flowers in spring, which are followed by the distinctive fruit.

Malus discoidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
