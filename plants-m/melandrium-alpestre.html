Melandrium alpestre

Melandrium alpestre, commonly known as the alpine catchfly, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its succulent leaves and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The species is notable for its ability to survive in harsh alpine environments due to its low-growing habit and thick, waxy leaves.
