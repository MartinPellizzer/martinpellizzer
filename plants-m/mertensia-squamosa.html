Mertensia squamosa

Mertensia squamosa, commonly known as the scaly bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, scaly leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking clusters of sky-blue, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia squamosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
