Melochia manducata

Melochia manducata, commonly known as the edible leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often consumed as a vegetable. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are rich in nutrients and are used in traditional cuisines for their mild, slightly sweet flavor.

Melochia manducata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
