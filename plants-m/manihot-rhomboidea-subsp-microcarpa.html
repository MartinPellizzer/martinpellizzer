Manihot rhomboidea subsp. microcarpa

Manihot rhomboidea subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited arrowleaf manioc, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, rhombic-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its potential for cultivation, it has been studied for its use in traditional agriculture and its nutritional value.
