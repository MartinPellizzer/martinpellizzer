Moehringia trinervia var. erecta

Moehringia trinervia var. erecta, commonly known as the upright moehringia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its upright growth habit compared to other varieties of the species.
