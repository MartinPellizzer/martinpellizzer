Millettia cognata

Millettia cognata, commonly known as the yellow millettia, is a woody climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Millettia cognata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
