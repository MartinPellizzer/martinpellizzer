Merremia glabra var. pubescens

Merremia glabra var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically light green in color. The flowers are trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and bloom in shades of white to pale purple. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds.

This plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
