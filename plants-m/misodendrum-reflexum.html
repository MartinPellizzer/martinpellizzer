Misodendrum reflexum

Misodendrum reflexum, commonly known as the spiral leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique helical growth pattern, where the stems twist around supporting structures in a spiral manner. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention in its native habitat. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in racemes, typically blooming during the wet season.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
