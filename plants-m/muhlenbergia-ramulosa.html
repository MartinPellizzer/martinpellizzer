Muhlenbergia ramulosa

Muhlenbergia ramulosa, commonly known as the silver muhlenbergia, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, pale blue to purple flower spikes in the summer, adding visual interest to the landscape.

Muhlenbergia ramulosa is drought-tolerant and low maintenance, suitable for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
