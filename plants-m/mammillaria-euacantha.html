Mammillaria euacantha

Mammillaria euacantha, commonly known as the white-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem and a distinctive white spines that contrast with its green body. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
