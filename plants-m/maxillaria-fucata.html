Maxillaria fucata

Maxillaria fucata, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety flowers that range in color from deep purple to black, giving it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in a compact rosette, helping to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Maxillaria fucata is valued in horticulture for its unique and dramatic floral display.
