Mimosa distans var. terezinae

Mimosa distans var. terezinae, commonly known as the Terezina mimosa, is a rare and ornamental species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its foliage is often used in horticultural displays due to its aesthetic appeal.

However, it is not commonly cultivated outside its native range due to its specific environmental requirements.
