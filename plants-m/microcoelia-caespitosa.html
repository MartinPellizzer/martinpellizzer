Microcoelia caespitosa

Microcoelia caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted microcoelia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color. It has a compact growth habit, forming dense tufts or mats, which help it survive in arid environments.

Microcoelia caespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a drought-tolerant ground cover.
