Matthiola longipetala var. adenocarpa

Matthiola longipetala var. adenocarpa, commonly known as the glandular-carped stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant produces small, glandular-tipped fruits, which are a distinguishing feature of this variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
