Masdevallia crenulata

Masdevallia crenulata, commonly known as the crested masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers that resemble a crown, with a distinctive crested lip. The plant typically grows as a small epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are fleshy and dark green, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

Masdevallia crenulata is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
