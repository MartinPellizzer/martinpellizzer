Mentha stenoclada

Mentha stenoclada, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its minty scent and flavor.

Mentha stenoclada is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
