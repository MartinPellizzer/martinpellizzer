Matthiola livida var. morocera

Matthiola livida var. morocera, commonly known as the Moroccan wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
