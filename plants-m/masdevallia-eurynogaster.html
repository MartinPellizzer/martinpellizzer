Masdevallia eurynogaster

Masdevallia eurynogaster, commonly known as the "Crimson Spurred Masdevallia," is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, vividly colored flowers that range from red to orange, often with intricate patterns and spurred petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Masdevallia eurynogaster is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively compact size.
