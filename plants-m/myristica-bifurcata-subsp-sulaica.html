Myristica bifurcata subsp. sulaica

Myristica bifurcata subsp.

sulaica, commonly known as Sula nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the Sula Islands in Indonesia. It is a small evergreen tree that produces aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant features elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe with a hard, woody shell enclosing the aromatic seed.

This subspecies is valued for its unique chemical composition and ecological significance in its native habitat.
