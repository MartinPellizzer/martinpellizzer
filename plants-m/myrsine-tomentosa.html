Myrsine tomentosa

Myrsine tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly myrsine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, soft, and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a pale green underside. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, spherical berries.

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific, and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
