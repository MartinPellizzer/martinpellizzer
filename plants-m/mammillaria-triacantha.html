Mammillaria triacantha

Mammillaria triacantha, commonly known as the three-armed hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the deserts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
