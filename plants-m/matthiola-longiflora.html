Matthiola longiflora

Matthiola longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered stock, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustard. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Matthiola longiflora is also valued for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
