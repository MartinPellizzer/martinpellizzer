Myrcia rionegrensis

Myrcia rionegrensis, commonly known as the Rio Negro myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest biome of southeastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical conditions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

Myrcia rionegrensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
