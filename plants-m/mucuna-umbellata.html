Mucuna umbellata

Mucuna umbellata, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It produces a yellow or orange flower cluster, which is often umbrella-shaped, giving it its scientific name.

Mucuna umbellata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties and use in traditional medicine.
