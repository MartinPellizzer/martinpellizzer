Malcolmia divaricata

Malcolmia divaricata, commonly known as the sand mustard, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
