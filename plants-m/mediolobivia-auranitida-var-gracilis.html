Mediolobivia auranitida var. gracilis

Mediolobivia auranitida var. gracilis, commonly known as the golden slender lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with thin, ribbed stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to minimize water loss in its harsh environment.

This variant is prized by collectors for its unique appearance and resilience to drought.
