Mohria nudiuscula

Mohria nudiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mohria, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a pale yellow or greenish hue, and are borne in dense inflorescences.

Mohria nudiuscula is an important component of its local ecosystem, providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
