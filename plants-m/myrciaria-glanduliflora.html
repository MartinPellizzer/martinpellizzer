Myrciaria glanduliflora

Myrciaria glanduliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian orange or pitanga, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange berries that ripen in the summer. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

The plant's adaptability to humid, tropical climates makes it a valuable species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
