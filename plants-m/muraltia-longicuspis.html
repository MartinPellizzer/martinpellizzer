Muraltia longicuspis

Muraltia longicuspis, commonly known as the long-spined muraltia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant's leaves are typically elliptical and have a slightly serrated edge. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Muraltia longicuspis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
