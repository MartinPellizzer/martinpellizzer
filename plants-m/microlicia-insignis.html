Microlicia insignis

Microlicia insignis, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant features small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of its leaves. Microlicia insignis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor plant collections.
