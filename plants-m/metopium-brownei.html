Metopium brownei

Metopium brownei, commonly known as the pink-flowered ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
