Myrciaria salzmannii

Myrciaria salzmannii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or salzmann cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its ornamental red fruits that resemble cherries. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant aromatic scent.

The flowers are small, white or pink, and are borne in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
