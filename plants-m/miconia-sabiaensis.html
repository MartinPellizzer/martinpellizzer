Miconia sabiaensis

Miconia sabiaensis, commonly known as the Brazilian miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds.

Miconia sabiaensis is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various rainforest species.
