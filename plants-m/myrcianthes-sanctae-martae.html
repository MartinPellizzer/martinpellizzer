Myrcianthes sanctae-martae

Myrcianthes sanctae-martae, commonly known as the San Marta myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain aromatic seeds.

The San Marta myrtle is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
