Muraltia carnosa

Muraltia carnosa, commonly known as the hairy muraltia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale pink and appear in clusters.

The fruit is a small, round pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
