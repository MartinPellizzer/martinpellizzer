Mimulopsis violacea

Mimulopsis violacea, commonly known as the violet mimulopsis, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Mimulaceae and is characterized by its small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapting to the harsh climate of its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized environment, Mimulopsis violacea is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
