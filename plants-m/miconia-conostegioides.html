Miconia conostegioides

Miconia conostegioides, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Miconia conostegioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and persistent flowering habit.
