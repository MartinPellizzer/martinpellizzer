Myrcia chrysotrichoma

Myrcia chrysotrichoma, commonly known as the golden-haired myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture.

Myrcia chrysotrichoma is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
