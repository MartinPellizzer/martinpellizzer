Maesa piscatorum

Maesa piscatorum, commonly known as the fishing maesa, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range from white to pink, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
