Mentha origanoides

Mentha origanoides, commonly known as orange mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which appear in summer. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their distinct citrusy aroma.

Mentha origanoides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.
