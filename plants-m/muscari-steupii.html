Muscari steupii

Muscari steupii, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, round bulbs that are used for propagation. Muscari steupii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and delicate, bell-shaped flowers.
