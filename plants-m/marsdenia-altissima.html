Marsdenia altissima

Marsdenia altissima, commonly known as the giant Marsdenia, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Marsdenia altissima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
