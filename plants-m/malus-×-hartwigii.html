Malus × hartwigii

Malus × hartwigii, commonly known as the Hartwig apple, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in spring and vibrant red or orange fruit in autumn. The plant is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.

The fruit, while edible, is not typically used for commercial apple production.
