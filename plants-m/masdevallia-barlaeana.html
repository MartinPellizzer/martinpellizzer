Masdevallia barlaeana

Masdevallia barlaeana, commonly known as the Barlaeana Masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its intricate, elongated pseudobulbs and clusters of delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments with high light exposure but requires careful watering to avoid root rot. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
