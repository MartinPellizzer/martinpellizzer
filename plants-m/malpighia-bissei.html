Malpighia bissei

Malpighia bissei, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that persist through winter. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces a fragrant, slightly sticky resin.

The berries are edible when ripe but can be bitter and are often used in traditional medicine.
