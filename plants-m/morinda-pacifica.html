Morinda pacifica

Morinda pacifica, commonly known as the Hawaiian mulberry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Morinda pacifica has cultural significance in Hawaiian traditions and is also valued for its medicinal properties.
