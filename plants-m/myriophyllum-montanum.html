Myriophyllum montanum

Myriophyllum montanum, commonly known as mountain watermilfoil, is a semi-aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and streams. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often submerged, with some floating on the water surface. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in late summer, and it is often found in shallow, nutrient-rich waters.

Myriophyllum montanum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
