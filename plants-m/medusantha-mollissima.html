Medusantha mollissima

Medusantha mollissima, commonly known as the velvet medusantha, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to a silvery sheen. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
