Melaleuca acacioides var. angustifolia

Melaleuca acacioides var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a whitish powdery coating, known as bloom. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flower spikes that are typically pale yellow to cream in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a high tolerance for drought and poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
