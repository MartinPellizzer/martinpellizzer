Mandevilla villosa

Mandevilla villosa, commonly known as the velvet mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody vines and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail.

Mandevilla villosa requires well-draining soil and regular watering to flourish.
