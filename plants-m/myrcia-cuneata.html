Myrcia cuneata

Myrcia cuneata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

Myrcia cuneata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
