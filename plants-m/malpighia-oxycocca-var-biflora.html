Malpighia oxycocca var. biflora

Malpighia oxycocca var. biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered acerola, is a species of shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, red, edible fruits that resemble cherries and are rich in vitamin C. The plant produces clusters of flowers, with two flowers typically developing at each node, giving it its varietal name. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
