Monnina revoluta

Monnina revoluta, commonly known as velvetleaf or wild buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Monnina revoluta is an annual herb that plays a role in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
