Myrcia skortzoviana

Myrcia skortzoviana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Myrcia skortzoviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
