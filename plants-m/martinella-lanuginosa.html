Martinella lanuginosa

Martinella lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly martinsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
