Maxillaria purpurea var. parviflora

Maxillaria purpurea var. parviflora, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
