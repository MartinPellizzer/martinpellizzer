Masdevallia triglochin

Masdevallia triglochin, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often zigzag-shaped inflorescence and vibrant, colorful flowers that range from red to purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Masdevallia triglochin is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
