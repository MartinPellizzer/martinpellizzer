Masdevallia inaequalis

Masdevallia inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that feature a unique combination of sepals and petals. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Masdevallia inaequalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and relatively compact growth habit.
