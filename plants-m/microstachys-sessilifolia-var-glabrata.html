Microstachys sessilifolia var. glabrata

Microstachys sessilifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved microstachys, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, sessile leaves that are glabrous, or hairless, giving it a smooth appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
