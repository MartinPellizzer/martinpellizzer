Maxillaria hillsii

Maxillaria hillsii, commonly known as the Hillsii orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, with flowers that have a unique, intricate pattern. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and relatively easy care make it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
