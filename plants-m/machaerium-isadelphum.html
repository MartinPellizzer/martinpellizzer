Machaerium isadelphum

Machaerium isadelphum, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a tropical legume native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, velvety leaflets and produces small, yellow to brown flowers. The plant is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as riverbanks and forest edges. It has a climbing habit and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

The seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.
