Morinda aspera

Morinda aspera, commonly known as rough morinda, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by red or orange berries. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The leaves and roots are typically harvested for medicinal purposes.
