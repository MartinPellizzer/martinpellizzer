Matricaria odorata

Matricaria odorata, commonly known as sweet chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets and a distinctive apple-like fragrance. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties. Its leaves and flowers are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their calming and anti-inflammatory effects.

Matricaria odorata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
