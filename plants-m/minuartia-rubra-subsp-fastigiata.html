Minuartia rubra subsp. fastigiata

Minuartia rubra subsp.

fastigiata, commonly known as the upright stonecrop, is a low-growing, mat-forming succulent native to rocky and alpine environments. It features dense, compact growth with small, fleshy, often blue or green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand harsh conditions, including cold temperatures and poor soil.
