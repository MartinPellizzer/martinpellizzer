Melaleuca carrii

Melaleuca carrii, commonly known as the Grey Melaleuca, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped fruits that contain seeds, and its leaves are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. It is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees.

Melaleuca carrii is known for its resilience to salt spray and drought conditions.
