Microcos triflora var. longipetiolata

Microcos triflora var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled microcos, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its botanical features include elongated petioles, which distinguish it from other varieties of Microcos.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
