Monstera xanthospatha

Monstera xanthospatha, commonly known as the golden monstera, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a distinctive golden or yellowish variegation. The plant is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other popular houseplants like philodendrons and peace lilies. Its foliage is often adorned with irregular splits or holes, giving it a unique and dramatic appearance.

Monstera xanthospatha thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
