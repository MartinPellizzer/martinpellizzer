Mentha arvensis f. pacheriana

Mentha arvensis f. pacheriana, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a variant of the field mint species.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. This subspecies is native to Central and Eastern Europe, including Hungary, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds, making it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.
