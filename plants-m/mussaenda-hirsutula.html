Mussaenda hirsutula

Mussaenda hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mussaenda hirsutula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
