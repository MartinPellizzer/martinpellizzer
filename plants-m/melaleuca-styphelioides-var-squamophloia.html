Melaleuca styphelioides var. squamophloia

Melaleuca styphelioides var. squamophloia, commonly known as the squamophloia melaleuca, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-grey foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in heathlands and coastal scrub. Its leaves are covered in a fine, scale-like layer, which gives it its specific epithet "squamophloia.".

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
