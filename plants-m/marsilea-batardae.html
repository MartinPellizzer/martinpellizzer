Marsilea batardae

Marsilea batardae, commonly known as the batard's water clover, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Marsileaceae.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are divided into four lobes, resembling a clover leaf. This plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands. Its reproductive structures include small, floating fruits that contain spores, aiding in its dispersal.

Marsilea batardae plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
