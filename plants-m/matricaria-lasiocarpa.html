Matricaria lasiocarpa

Matricaria lasiocarpa, commonly known as the roughleaf chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small yellow flowers that develop into white-petaled blooms. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Matricaria lasiocarpa is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
