Melanorrhoea laccifera var. parvifolia

Melanorrhoea laccifera var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved blackwood, is a variant of the Melanorrhoea laccifera species, native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its dark, glossy bark and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are hard, woody capsules containing several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and hardiness.
