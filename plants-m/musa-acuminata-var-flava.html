Musa acuminata var. flava

Musa acuminata var. flava, commonly known as the yellow banana, is a variant of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow fruit, which is typically smaller and more elongated than the common Cavendish banana. The plant produces large, broad leaves that are a deep green, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for both its edible fruit and its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
