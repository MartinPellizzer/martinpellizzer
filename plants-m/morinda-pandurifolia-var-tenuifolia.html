Morinda pandurifolia var. tenuifolia

Morinda pandurifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morinda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
