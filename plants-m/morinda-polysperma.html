Morinda polysperma

Morinda polysperma, commonly known as the red moringa or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Morinda polysperma is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating ailments such as inflammation and skin conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and herbal contexts.
