Mammillaria claviformis

Mammillaria claviformis, commonly known as the club-shaped mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in white to yellowish spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
