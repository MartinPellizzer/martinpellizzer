Micropuntia spectatissima

Micropuntia spectatissima, commonly known as the showy cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has spiny areoles that bear both flowers and clusters of small, globose fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, typical of cacti adapted to arid environments.

The showy cactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
