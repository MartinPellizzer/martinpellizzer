Miconia christii

Miconia christii, commonly known as the "Brazilian cherry" or "hurricane tree," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, red bracts that resemble flowers, which are often mistaken for actual blooms. It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it has become a concern for native ecosystems.
