Machaerium ferrugineum var. parviflorum

Machaerium ferrugineum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered machaerium, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves with a distinctive reddish-brown coloration on the underside. Its flowers are small and pale, often arranged in dense spikes, and are accompanied by feathery bracts.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
