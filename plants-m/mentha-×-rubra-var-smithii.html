Mentha × rubra var. smithii

Mentha × rubra var. smithii, commonly known as the red mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Mentha genus in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its reddish-purple stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other mint species. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
