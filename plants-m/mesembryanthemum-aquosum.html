Mesembryanthemum aquosum

Mesembryanthemum aquosum, commonly known as the waterleaf, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, often blooming in response to sunlight. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Mesembryanthemum aquosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
