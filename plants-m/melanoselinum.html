Melanoselinum

Melanoselinum, commonly known as black parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant features finely divided, dark green leaves that are often used in culinary applications. Its flowers, which are small and clustered, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Melanoselinum is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
