Myginda coccinea

Myginda coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered myginda, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals that contrast with its green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
