Mokof obovalis

Mokof obovalis, commonly known as the oval mokof, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, which attract pollinators like bees and moths. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, Mokof obovalis is often used in xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.
