Myrciaria nitida var. chartacea

Myrciaria nitida var. chartacea, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a tart, sweet flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
