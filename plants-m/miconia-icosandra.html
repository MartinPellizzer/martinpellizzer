Miconia icosandra

Miconia icosandra, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia icosandra is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in many regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge in ecological management.
