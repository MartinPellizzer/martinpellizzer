Mappia foetida var. gardneriana

Mappia foetida var. gardneriana, commonly known as the stinking rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its unique scent, which can be both pleasant and unpleasant, varies depending on the time of day and environmental conditions.
