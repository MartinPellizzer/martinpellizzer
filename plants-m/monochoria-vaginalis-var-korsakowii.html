Monochoria vaginalis var. korsakowii

Monochoria vaginalis var. korsakowii, commonly known as Korsakow's bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland and aquatic environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves and delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing cyanobacteria, which helps it thrive in nutrient-poor water. Its roots are adapted for water absorption, and it often forms dense mats on the surface of still water bodies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining water quality.
