Miconia secundifolia subsp. malcabalensis

Miconia secundifolia subsp.

malcabalensis, commonly known as the Malcabalensis miconia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are typically followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
