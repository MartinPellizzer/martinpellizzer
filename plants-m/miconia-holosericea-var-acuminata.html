Miconia holosericea var. acuminata

Miconia holosericea var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped miconia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
