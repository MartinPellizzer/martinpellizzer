Mimosa dicerastes

Mimosa dicerastes, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil enrichment.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
