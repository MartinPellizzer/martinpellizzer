Melaleuca decussata var. ovoidea

Melaleuca decussata var. ovoidea, commonly known as the oval-leaved paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a reddish inner layer. The leaves are oval-shaped, glossy, and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It produces small, white or cream-colored flowers that appear in spikes during the winter months.

This plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of the Northern Territory and Queensland.
