Magnolia heptapeta f. purpurascens

Magnolia heptapeta f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered magnolia, is a cultivar of the Magnolia heptapeta species.

It is characterized by its striking, deep purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 6 to 10 feet. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy surface.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
