Mesembryanthemum griseum

Mesembryanthemum griseum, commonly known as the ghost flower or sand flower, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves and its unique, star-shaped flowers that open in response to sunlight. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are typically short-lived but highly attractive to pollinators.

Mesembryanthemum griseum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
