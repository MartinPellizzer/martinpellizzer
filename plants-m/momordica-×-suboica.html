Momordica × suboica

Momordica × suboica, commonly known as the hybrid bitter gourd, is a cross between Momordica charantia (bitter gourd) and Momordica foetida (spiny bitter gourd).

It is a vine that produces elongated, warty fruits with a bitter taste, often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
