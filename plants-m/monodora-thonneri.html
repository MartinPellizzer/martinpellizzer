Monodora thonneri

Monodora thonneri, commonly known as African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, egg-shaped fruits that are rich in nutrients. Its seeds are encased in a fibrous pulp and are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and crafts.
