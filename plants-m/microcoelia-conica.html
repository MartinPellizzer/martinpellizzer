Microcoelia conica

Microcoelia conica, commonly known as the conical microcoelia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous species that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
