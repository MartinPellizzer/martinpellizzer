Melastoma trungii

Melastoma trungii, commonly known as the Vietnamese melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melastoma trungii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
