Myristica fatua subsp. fatua

Myristica fatua subsp.

fatua, commonly known as the wild nutmeg, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces distinctive berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
