Malache arenaria

Malache arenaria, commonly known as the sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant features a mat-like growth habit with small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Malache arenaria is often used in xeriscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal in dry landscapes.
