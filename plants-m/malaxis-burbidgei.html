Malaxis burbidgei

Malaxis burbidgei, commonly known as the Burridge's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a few broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in a raceme, often with a faintly fragrant scent.

Malaxis burbidgei is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns and exhibits a dormant phase during dry periods.
