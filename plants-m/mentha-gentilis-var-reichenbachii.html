Mentha gentilis var. reichenbachii

Mentha gentilis var. reichenbachii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant produces a strong menthol scent, which is utilized in various medicinal and aromatic applications. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

This variety is valued for its essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and flavoring.
