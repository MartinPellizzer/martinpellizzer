Machaeranthera glabriuscula var. villosa

Machaeranthera glabriuscula var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy plains coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "villosa.". The plant produces bright yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
