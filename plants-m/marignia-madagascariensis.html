Marignia madagascariensis

Marignia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar marigold, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
