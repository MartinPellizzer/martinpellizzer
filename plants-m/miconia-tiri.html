Miconia tiri

Miconia tiri, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or tiri, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces small, fleshy berries that contain seeds. Miconia tiri is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical rainforests.
