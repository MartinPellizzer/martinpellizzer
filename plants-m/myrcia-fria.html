Myrcia fria

Myrcia fria, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a spicy, peppery flavor. Myrcia fria thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal regions and lowland forests.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world, though it can become invasive in some areas.
