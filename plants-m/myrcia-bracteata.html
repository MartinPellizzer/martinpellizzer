Myrcia bracteata

Myrcia bracteata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and leathery, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The fruit is a small, dark purple berry that contains several seeds.
