Myrcia pilodes

Myrcia pilodes, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
