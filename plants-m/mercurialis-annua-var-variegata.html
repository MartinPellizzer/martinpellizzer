Mercurialis annua var. variegata

Mercurialis annua var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated mercury plant, is a variant of the annual mercury plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features white and green patterns on its leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique leaf patterns and adaptability to various growing conditions.
