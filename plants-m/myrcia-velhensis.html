Myrcia velhensis

Myrcia velhensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature.

Myrcia velhensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
