Meconopsis sulphurea subsp. gracilifolia

Meconopsis sulphurea subsp.

gracilifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
