Miconia magnifolia

Miconia magnifolia, commonly known as the showy miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Neotropical region.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Miconia magnifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in warm climates.
