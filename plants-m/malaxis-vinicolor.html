Malaxis vinicolor

Malaxis vinicolor, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces a single, elongated flower spike with several blooms, each having three sepals and three petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The genus Malaxis is known for its diverse species, many of which are adapted to specific ecological niches.
