Myrica conifera var. integra

Myrica conifera var. integra, commonly known as the smooth leatherleaf, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The leaves remain evergreen throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
