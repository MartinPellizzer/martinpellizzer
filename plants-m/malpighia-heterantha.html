Malpighia heterantha

Malpighia heterantha, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a small shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
