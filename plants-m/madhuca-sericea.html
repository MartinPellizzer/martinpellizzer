Madhuca sericea

Madhuca sericea, commonly known as silky tallowwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and long, elliptical leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing sticky, amber-colored seeds.

Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it important in both traditional and modern applications.
