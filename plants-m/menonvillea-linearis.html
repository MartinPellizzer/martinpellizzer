Menonvillea linearis

Menonvillea linearis, commonly known as the slender rock cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, slow-growing cactus characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny, linear leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
