Mammillaria setispina

Mammillaria setispina, commonly known as the spiny mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a low-growing, compact cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
