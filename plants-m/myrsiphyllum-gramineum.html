Myrsiphyllum gramineum

Myrsiphyllum gramineum, commonly known as the grassy myrsine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrsinaceae and is characterized by its small, simple leaves and clusters of small white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Myrsiphyllum gramineum is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive leaf structure and delicate blooms.
