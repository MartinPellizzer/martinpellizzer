Myrceugenia euosma

Myrceugenia euosma, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southern regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark berry that contains several seeds.

Myrceugenia euosma is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
