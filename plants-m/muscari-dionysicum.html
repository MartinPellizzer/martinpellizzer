Muscari dionysicum

Muscari dionysicum, commonly known as the Dionysus muscatel, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, particularly found in rocky and limestone soils. The plant produces dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its bulbs are perennial and form underground rhizomes, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a historical association with wine-making due to its name's connection to Dionysus.
