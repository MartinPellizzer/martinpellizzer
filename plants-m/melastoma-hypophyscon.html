Melastoma hypophyscon

Melastoma hypophyscon, commonly known as the red-rooted melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and thrives in tropical climates. Its roots are often reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
