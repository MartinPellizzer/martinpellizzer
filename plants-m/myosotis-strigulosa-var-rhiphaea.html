Myosotis strigulosa var. rhiphaea

Myosotis strigulosa var. rhiphaea, commonly known as the alpine forget-me-not, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in rocky or alpine soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
