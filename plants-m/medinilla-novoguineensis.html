Medinilla novoguineensis

Medinilla novoguineensis, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet tangerine tree, is a flowering plant native to New Guinea.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is typically grown as a shrub or small tree.

The fruit, which resembles a small orange, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.
