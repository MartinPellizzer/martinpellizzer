Mespilus grandiflora

Mespilus grandiflora, commonly known as the white medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by distinctive, fleshy fruits that ripen in late summer. The fruit has a unique, aromatic flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
