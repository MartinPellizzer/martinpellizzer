Myrcia costeira

Myrcia costeira, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to Brazil, particularly found in the Atlantic Forest region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing numerous small, black seeds.

Myrcia costeira is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
