Manihot glauca

Manihot glauca, commonly known as the blue arrowleaf, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a waxy surface that reflects light, enhancing its visual appeal. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Manihot glauca is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
