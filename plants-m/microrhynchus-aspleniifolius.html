Microrhynchus aspleniifolius

Microrhynchus aspleniifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf harts-tongue fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of the genus Asplenium. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce small, clustered fronds.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in shady gardens.
