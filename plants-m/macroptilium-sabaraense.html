Macroptilium sabaraense

Macroptilium sabaraense, commonly known as the Sabara legume, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in a hairy pod, which helps in seed dispersal through the environment.
