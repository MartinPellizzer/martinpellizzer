Macfadyena bangii

Macfadyena bangii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Macfadyena bangii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
