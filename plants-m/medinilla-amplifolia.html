Medinilla amplifolia

Medinilla amplifolia, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, round, yellow or orange berry that contains several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid climates.
