Mentha gentilis var. pugetii

Mentha gentilis var. pugetii, commonly known as Puget's mint, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

This variety is valued for its unique fragrance and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
