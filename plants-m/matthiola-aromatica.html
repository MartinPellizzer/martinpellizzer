Matthiola aromatica

Matthiola aromatica, commonly known as stock, is an annual or biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Matthiola aromatica is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
