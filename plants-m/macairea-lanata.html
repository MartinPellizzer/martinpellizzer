Macairea lanata

Macairea lanata, commonly known as the woolly macairea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Macairea lanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
