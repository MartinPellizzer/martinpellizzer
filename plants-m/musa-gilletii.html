Musa gilletii

Musa gilletii, commonly known as the African banana, is a species of banana plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces clusters of yellow, edible berries. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and high humidity. It is often cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, slightly tangy flavor.

The botanical structure of Musa gilletii includes a pseudostem formed by tightly packed leaf sheaths, which supports the plant's growth and development.
