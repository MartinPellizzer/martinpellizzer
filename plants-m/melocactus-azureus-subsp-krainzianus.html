Melocactus azureus subsp. krainzianus

Melocactus azureus subsp.

krainzianus, commonly known as the Azure Blue Melocactus, is a small, slow-growing cactus native to northern Argentina. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of short, blue-gray spines.

The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
