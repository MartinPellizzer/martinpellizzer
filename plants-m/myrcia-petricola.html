Myrcia petricola

Myrcia petricola, commonly known as the rock myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its fragrant, pink to white flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Myrcia petricola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
