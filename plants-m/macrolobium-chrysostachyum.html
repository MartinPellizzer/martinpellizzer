Macrolobium chrysostachyum

Macrolobium chrysostachyum, commonly known as golden staminal grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow stamens that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as savannas and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of flowers with elongated, golden anthers.

Macrolobium chrysostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
