Maxillaria trilobata

Maxillaria trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant blooms during the summer months and prefers warm, humid conditions.

It is a popular choice for both indoor and greenhouse cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
