Myosotis hacquetii

Myosotis hacquetii, commonly known as the Hacquet's forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate blue or violet flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
