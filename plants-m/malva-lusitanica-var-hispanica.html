Malva lusitanica var. hispanica

Malva lusitanica var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish mallows, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its fibrous roots and mucilaginous seeds have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
