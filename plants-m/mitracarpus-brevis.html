Mitracarpus brevis

Mitracarpus brevis, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, curved pods and are often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural systems.
