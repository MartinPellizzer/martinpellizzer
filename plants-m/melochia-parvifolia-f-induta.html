Melochia parvifolia f. induta

Melochia parvifolia f. induta, commonly known as the velvetleaf melochia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
