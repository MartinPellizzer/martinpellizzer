Masdevallia caudata

Masdevallia caudata, commonly known as the tail orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated inflorescence that resembles a tail, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a complex structure that includes a labellum, which plays a role in pollination.

Masdevallia caudata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
