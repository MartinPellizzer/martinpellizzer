Morisonia cynophallophora

Morisonia cynophallophora, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pale pink to white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant is an annual herb with slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
