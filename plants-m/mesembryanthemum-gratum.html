Mesembryanthemum gratum

Mesembryanthemum gratum, commonly known as the "good fortune flower," is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant is typically short-lived, often surviving only one growing season, and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is drought-tolerant and prefers warm, arid climates, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valued ornamental plant.
