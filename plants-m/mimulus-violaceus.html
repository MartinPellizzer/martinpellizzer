Mimulus violaceus

Mimulus violaceus, commonly known as the purple monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland areas. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mimulus violaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
