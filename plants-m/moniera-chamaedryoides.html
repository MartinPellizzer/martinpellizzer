Moniera chamaedryoides

Moniera chamaedryoides, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, low-growing structure with hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink in color. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a dense, ground-covering appearance.

Moniera chamaedryoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
