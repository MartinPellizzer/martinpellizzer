Mucuna pesa var. pesa

Mucuna pesa var. pesa, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and trifoliate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its seeds and as a forage crop.

The plant also has a rapid growth rate and can be used for soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
