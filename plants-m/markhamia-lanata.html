Markhamia lanata

Markhamia lanata, commonly known as the silky cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky white coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
