Matthiola incana var. fruticosa

Matthiola incana var. fruticosa, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit, with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
