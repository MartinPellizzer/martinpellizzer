Miconia biformis

Miconia biformis, commonly known as the two-lobed miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with two distinct lobes. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Miconia biformis is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
