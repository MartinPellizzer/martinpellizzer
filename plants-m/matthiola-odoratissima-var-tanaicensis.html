Matthiola odoratissima var. tanaicensis

Matthiola odoratissima var. tanaicensis, commonly known as the Tanaic Island stock, is a flowering plant native to the Tanaic Islands in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, bright pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate fragrance and visual appeal.
