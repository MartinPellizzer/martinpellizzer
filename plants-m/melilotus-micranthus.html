Melilotus micranthus

Melilotus micranthus, commonly known as small-flowered melilot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
