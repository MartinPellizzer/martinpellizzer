Musa × sapientum var. kinamay

Musa × sapientum var. kinamay, commonly known as the red banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant characterized by its distinctive red-purple peel.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when the peel is still green, though it can be eaten when ripe. The fruit has a sweet, slightly tangy flavor and is rich in potassium and dietary fiber.

It is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and nutritional value.
