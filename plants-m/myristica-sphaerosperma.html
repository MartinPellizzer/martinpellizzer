Myristica sphaerosperma

Myristica sphaerosperma, commonly known as the spherical nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain a single, large, spherical seed. The plant produces a fragrant resin known as elemi, which is used in perfumery and traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The species is notable for its unique fruit structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
