Maesa taiheizanensis

Maesa taiheizanensis, commonly known as the Japanese maesa, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. It produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits.

This species is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
