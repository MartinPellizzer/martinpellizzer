Malpighia densa

Malpighia densa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It produces small, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, sweet flavor. The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.
