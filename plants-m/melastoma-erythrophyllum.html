Melastoma erythrophyllum

Melastoma erythrophyllum, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and understoreys. It has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible.

Melastoma erythrophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
