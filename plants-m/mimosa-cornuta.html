Mimosa cornuta

Mimosa cornuta, commonly known as the horned mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Mimosa cornuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate foliage and unique appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
