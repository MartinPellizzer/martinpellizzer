Mentha subcollina

Mentha subcollina, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in spikes. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their cooling and soothing properties.

Mentha subcollina is closely related to other members of the mint family, sharing similar chemical compositions and growth habits.
