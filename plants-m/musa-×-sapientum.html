Musa × sapientum

Musa × sapientum, commonly known as the common banana.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Musa, characterized by its large, fleshy, segmented fruit. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, typically growing as a clump of pseudostems formed from the tightly packed leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in a central inflorescence, with the female flowers developing into the edible fruit.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires warm, humid conditions for optimal growth and fruit production.
