Monogereion

Monogereion, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helleborine, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and its preference for moist, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination. Monogereion is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in the cool, humid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in the study of orchid morphology and ecological adaptation.
