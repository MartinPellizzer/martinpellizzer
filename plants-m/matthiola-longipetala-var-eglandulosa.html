Matthiola longipetala var. eglandulosa

Matthiola longipetala var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It produces small, elongated pods containing seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
