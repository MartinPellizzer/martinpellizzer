Miconia leaeriocalyx

Miconia leaeriocalyx, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow-orange berry that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Miconia leaeriocalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various regions outside its native range.
