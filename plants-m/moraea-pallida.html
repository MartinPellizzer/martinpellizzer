Moraea pallida

Moraea pallida, commonly known as the pale moraea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and showy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy.

Moraea pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
