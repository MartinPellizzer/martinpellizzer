Mesosphaerum eriophyllum

Mesosphaerum eriophyllum, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
