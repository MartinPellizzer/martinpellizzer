Mahonia cardiophylla

Mahonia cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaved mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are deeply lobed and often heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries in the fall. Mahonia cardiophylla thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable addition to gardens in suitable climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
