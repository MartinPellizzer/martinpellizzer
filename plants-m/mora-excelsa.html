Mora excelsa

Mora excelsa, commonly known as the Amazonian blackberry, is a shrub native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, edible fruits that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Mora excelsa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is an important species in the biodiversity of the Amazon basin.
