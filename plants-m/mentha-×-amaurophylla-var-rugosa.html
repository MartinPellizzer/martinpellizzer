Mentha × amaurophylla var. rugosa

Mentha × amaurophylla var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, rugose leaves and strong, aromatic scent. This variety is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
