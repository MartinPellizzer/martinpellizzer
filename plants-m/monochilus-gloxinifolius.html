Monochilus gloxinifolius

Monochilus gloxinifolius, commonly known as the velvetleaf gloxinia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a soft texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
