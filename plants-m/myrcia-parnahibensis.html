Myrcia parnahibensis

Myrcia parnahibensis, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree or Pernambuco pepper, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark is thick and reddish-brown, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
