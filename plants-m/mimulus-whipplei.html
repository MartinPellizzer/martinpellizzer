Mimulus whipplei

Mimulus whipplei, commonly known as the Whipple's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus whipplei is a member of the figwort family (Scrophulariaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
