Myrtus tuberculata

Myrtus tuberculata, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Myrtus tuberculata is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
