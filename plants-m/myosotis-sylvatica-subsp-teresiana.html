Myosotis sylvatica subsp. teresiana

Myosotis sylvatica subsp.

teresiana, commonly known as the woodland forget-me-not, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features slender stems that often grow in a prostrate or upright manner, depending on the habitat.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers with five petals, typical of the Myosotis genus. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
