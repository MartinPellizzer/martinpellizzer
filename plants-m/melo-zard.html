Melo zard

Melo zard, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruit with a sweet, fragrant flesh. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers and has tendrils that help it climb. Its leaves are broad and lobed, typical of many cucurbit species.

The fruit is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its high water content and nutritional value.
