Mucuna paniculata

Mucuna paniculata, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

However, it can also be invasive and is sometimes considered a weed in certain environments.
