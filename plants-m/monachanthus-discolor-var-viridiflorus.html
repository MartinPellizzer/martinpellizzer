Monachanthus discolor var. viridiflorus

Monachanthus discolor var. viridiflorus, commonly known as the green-flowered false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue and produces striking yellow flowers with green veins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for native gardens.
