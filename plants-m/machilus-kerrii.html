Machilus kerrii

Machilus kerrii, commonly known as the Chinese laurustin, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its fruit is a small, dark berry.

Machilus kerrii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
