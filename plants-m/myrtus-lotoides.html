Myrtus lotoides

Myrtus lotoides, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, leathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces tiny, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Myrtus lotoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
