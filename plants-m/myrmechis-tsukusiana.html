Myrmechis tsukusiana

Myrmechis tsukusiana, commonly known as the Japanese tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to Japan.

It is a tall, slender fern with a distinctive, unbranched trunk that is covered in a fibrous, spongy material. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forests.

Myrmechis tsukusiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional Japanese horticulture.
