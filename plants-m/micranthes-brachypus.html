Micranthes brachypus

Micranthes brachypus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically thrives in moist, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces tiny, white to pinkish flowers during the spring and early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained environments.

Micranthes brachypus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
