Mesosphaerum fragilifolium

Mesosphaerum fragilifolium, commonly known as the fragile-leaf mesosphaerum, is a rare and delicate plant native to the arid regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thin, papery leaves that are prone to wilting and tearing, which is a key feature of its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in the early morning, often during the monsoon season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
