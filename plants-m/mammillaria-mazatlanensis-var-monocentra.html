Mammillaria mazatlanensis var. monocentra

Mammillaria mazatlanensis var. monocentra, commonly known as the Mazatlanensis monocentra cactus, is a species native to the Mexican state of Sinaloa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its spiny appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
