Micrandra lopezii f. anteridifera

Micrandra lopezii f. anteridifera, commonly known as the Anteridifera form of Lopez's micrandra, is a unique botanical variant characterized by its distinct floral morphology.

This fern-like plant exhibits specialized structures, including modified leaves that serve both photosynthetic and reproductive functions. Its fronds are typically narrow and elongated, with a delicate, feathery appearance that enhances its ornamental value. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its limited distribution and unique features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
