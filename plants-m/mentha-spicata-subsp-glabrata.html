Mentha spicata subsp. glabrata

Mentha spicata subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as smooth catmint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale blue to lavender flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Due to its aromatic foliage and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
