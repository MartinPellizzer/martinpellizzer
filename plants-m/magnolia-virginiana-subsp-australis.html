Magnolia virginiana subsp. australis

Magnolia virginiana subsp.

australis, commonly known as the southern magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is well adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
