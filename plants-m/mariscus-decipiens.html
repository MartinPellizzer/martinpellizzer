Mariscus decipiens

Mariscus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants like sorrel and knotweed. The plant is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.
