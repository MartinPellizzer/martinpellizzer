Mazus yakushimensis

Mazus yakushimensis, commonly known as the Japanese mazus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale purple or white, with a distinctive two-lipped structure.

Mazus yakushimensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
