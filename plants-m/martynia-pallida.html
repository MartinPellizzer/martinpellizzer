Martynia pallida

Martynia pallida, commonly known as the pale martynia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that contain seeds.

Martynia pallida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
