Magnolia aenea

Magnolia aenea, commonly known as the silver magnolia, is a striking evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that have a metallic sheen, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, which bloom in early spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Magnolia aenea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
