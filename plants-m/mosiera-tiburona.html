Mosiera tiburona

Mosiera tiburona, commonly known as the Tiburon dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of thick, hairy leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats. It has a short growing season, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Mosiera tiburona plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining coastal ecosystem biodiversity.
