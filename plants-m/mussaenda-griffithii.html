Mussaenda griffithii

Mussaenda griffithii, commonly known as the golden flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that resemble flames, which are clustered in umbels. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Mussaenda griffithii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
