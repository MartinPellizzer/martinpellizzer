Manilkara balata var. schomburgkii

Manilkara balata var. schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits. The plant is notable for its latex-producing properties, which have historically been harvested for rubber production. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
