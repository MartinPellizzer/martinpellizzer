Myrtus citrifolia

Myrtus citrifolia, commonly known as the lemon myrtle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that contain high concentrations of citral, a compound responsible for its distinctive fragrance. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are followed by edible, lemony berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique flavor and medicinal properties, it is widely used in culinary and traditional applications.
