Mentha × kerneri var. iraziana

Mentha × kerneri var. iraziana, commonly known as Irazian mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale purple to blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

This variety is valued for its distinct fragrance and is sometimes used in herbal teas and aromatherapy.
