Marsdenia mackeeorum

Marsdenia mackeeorum, commonly known as the Mackee's Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and produces yellow-orange fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in forest understories where it receives partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
