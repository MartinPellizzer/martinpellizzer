Moricandia teretifolia

Moricandia teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing along rocky or coastal areas. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Moricandia teretifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
