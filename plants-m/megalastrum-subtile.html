Megalastrum subtile

Megalastrum subtile, commonly known as the slender leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add texture to garden landscapes.
