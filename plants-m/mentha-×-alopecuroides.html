Mentha × alopecuroides

Mentha × alopecuroides, commonly known as shiso, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly peppery leaves that are widely used in Asian cuisine and traditional medicine. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with square stems and opposite leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its essential oils contain compounds like menthol and carvacrol, contributing to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
