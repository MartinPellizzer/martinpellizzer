Manihot membranacea

Manihot membranacea, commonly known as the white arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 1-2 meters tall, featuring smooth, waxy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces edible tubers that are starchy and used as a thickening agent in cooking. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species in both traditional and modern agricultural practices.
