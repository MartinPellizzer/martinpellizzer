Myrcia lutescens

Myrcia lutescens, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or yellow flowers and glossy, elliptical leaves. It produces small, reddish-brown berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrcia lutescens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
