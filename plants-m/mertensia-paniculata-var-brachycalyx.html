Mertensia paniculata var. brachycalyx

Mertensia paniculata var. brachycalyx, commonly known as the narrow-calyx bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
