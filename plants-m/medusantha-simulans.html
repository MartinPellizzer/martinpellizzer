Medusantha simulans

Medusantha simulans, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and ethereal flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and its striking, white, star-shaped flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires a specific balance of light and moisture to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Medusantha simulans is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
