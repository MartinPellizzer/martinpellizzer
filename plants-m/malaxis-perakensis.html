Malaxis perakensis

Malaxis perakensis, commonly known as the Perak orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, while its leaves are narrow and strap-like.

Malaxis perakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral structure.
