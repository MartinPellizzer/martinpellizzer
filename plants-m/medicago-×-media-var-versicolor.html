Medicago × media var. versicolor

Medicago × media var. versicolor, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for various climates. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and as a forage crop.

The variability in flower color within the species adds to its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
