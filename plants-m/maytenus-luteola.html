Maytenus luteola

Maytenus luteola, commonly known as the yellow mayten, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of forests. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing yellowish-green blooms that attract pollinators.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its ecological role in seed dispersal.
