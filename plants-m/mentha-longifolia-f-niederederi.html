Mentha longifolia f. niederederi

Mentha longifolia f. niederederi, commonly known as the Niedereder's mint, is a compact, aromatic perennial herb native to the Alps.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct minty fragrance, and its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or violet in color. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to herb and rock gardens.
