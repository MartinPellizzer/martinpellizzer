Memora rondoni

Memora rondoni, commonly known as the Rondoni orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in the Amazon rainforest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks and branches in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, which is essential for its survival in the dense, humid environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, Memora rondoni is considered a vulnerable species.
