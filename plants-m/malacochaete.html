Malacochaete

Malacochaete, commonly known as the velvet tassel grass, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in marshy environments. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of soft, hairy inflorescences, giving it a velvety appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Malacochaete plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and maintaining wetland health.
