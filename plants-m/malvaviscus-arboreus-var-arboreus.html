Malvaviscus arboreus var. arboreus

Malvaviscus arboreus var. arboreus, commonly known as the evergreen mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
