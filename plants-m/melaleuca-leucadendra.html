Melaleuca leucadendra

Melaleuca leucadendra, commonly known as the white tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.

The plant's essential oils are also used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
