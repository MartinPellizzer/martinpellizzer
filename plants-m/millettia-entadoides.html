Millettia entadoides

Millettia entadoides, commonly known as the African tamarind tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in nutrients. It is often cultivated for its timber, which is valued for its strength and durability.

Additionally, it plays an important role in soil conservation and agroforestry systems.
