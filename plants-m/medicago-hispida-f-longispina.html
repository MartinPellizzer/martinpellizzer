Medicago hispida f. longispina

Medicago hispida f. longispina, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is known for its deep root system, which helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

It is often used in cover cropping and as a forage plant due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
