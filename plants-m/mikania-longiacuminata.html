Mikania longiacuminata

Mikania longiacuminata, commonly known as the creeping Mikania, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, often resembling dandelion blooms.

Mikania longiacuminata is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to both full sun and partial shade.
