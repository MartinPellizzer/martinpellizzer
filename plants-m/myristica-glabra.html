Myristica glabra

Myristica glabra, commonly known as nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It produces an aromatic seed, the nutmeg kernel, which is enclosed within a rough, reddish outer covering called the mace. The plant belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its fruits are drupes, with a single seed that is highly valued for its spice.

The plant is also cultivated in other parts of Southeast Asia for its economic and culinary significance.
