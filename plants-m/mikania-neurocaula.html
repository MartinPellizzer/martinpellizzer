Mikania neurocaula

Mikania neurocaula, commonly known as the "devil's vine," is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and sprawling habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs, and it is known for its ability to spread aggressively in disturbed habitats.

Mikania neurocaula is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas due to its vigorous growth and adaptability.
