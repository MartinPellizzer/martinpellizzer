Melinis ambigua

Melinis ambigua, commonly known as broadleaf arrowhead, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by three-lobed seed heads. It thrives in wet or moist soils and is often found in marshy areas, along stream banks, and in wetlands.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native range.
