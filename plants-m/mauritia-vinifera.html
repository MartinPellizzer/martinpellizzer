Mauritia vinifera

Mauritia vinifera, commonly known as the Brazilian wine palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling small grapes. These fruits are often consumed raw or used in traditional beverages.

Due to habitat loss, Mauritia vinifera is considered vulnerable and faces threats to its survival in the wild.
