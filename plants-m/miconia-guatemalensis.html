Miconia guatemalensis

Miconia guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often dispersed by birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
