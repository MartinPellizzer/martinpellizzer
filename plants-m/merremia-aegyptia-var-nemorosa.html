Merremia aegyptia var. nemorosa

Merremia aegyptia var. nemorosa, commonly known as the Egyptian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
