Maxillaria grayi

Maxillaria grayi, commonly known as the Gray's Maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive purple lip. The plant typically grows on tree branches or in rocky crevices, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are ovate and arranged in a rosette pattern, often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Maxillaria grayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
