Malus dulcissima var. asiatica

Malus dulcissima var. asiatica, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces small, ornamental fruits that range in color from red to yellow, often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant features lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its flowers, which appear in spring, are typically white or pink and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
