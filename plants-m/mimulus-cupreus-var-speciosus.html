Mimulus cupreus var. speciosus

Mimulus cupreus var. speciosus, commonly known as the copper cup sedum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and vibrant copper-colored flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The variety 'speciosus' is distinguished by its showy floral display and compact growth form compared to other varieties of Mimulus cupreus.
