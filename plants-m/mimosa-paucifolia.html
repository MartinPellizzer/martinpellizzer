Mimosa paucifolia

Mimosa paucifolia, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a low-growing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fluffy, pale yellow flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Mimosa paucifolia is also known for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait that adds to its unique botanical appeal.
