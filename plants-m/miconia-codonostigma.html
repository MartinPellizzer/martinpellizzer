Miconia codonostigma

Miconia codonostigma, commonly known as the Brazilian glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds.

Miconia codonostigma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
