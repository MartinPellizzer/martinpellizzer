Mirabilis sanguinea

Mirabilis sanguinea, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, pink, and orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant belongs to the Nyctaginaceae family and is known for its unique ability to open its flowers at night, a trait that gives it its common name. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Mirabilis sanguinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
