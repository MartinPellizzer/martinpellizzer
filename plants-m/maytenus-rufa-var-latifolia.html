Maytenus rufa var. latifolia

Maytenus rufa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mayten, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically broad and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
