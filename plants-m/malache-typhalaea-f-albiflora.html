Malache typhalaea f. albiflora

Malache typhalaea f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered malache, is a unique variant of the species Malache typhalaea.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. This plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical conditions. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized botanical specimen.
