Manihot meeboldii

Manihot meeboldii, commonly known as the wild cassava, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds. The plant features alternate, simple leaves with a rough texture and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential nutritional and medicinal properties.

However, due to its toxicity, caution is required when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
