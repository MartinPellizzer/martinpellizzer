Mentzelia hualapaiensis

Mentzelia hualapaiensis, commonly known as the Hualapai blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of yellow to orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Mentzelia hualapaiensis plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
