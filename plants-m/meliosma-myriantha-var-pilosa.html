Meliosma myriantha var. pilosa

Meliosma myriantha var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy meliosma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are densely covered with soft, white hairs. The leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
