Medinilla amoena var. samoensis

Medinilla amoena var. samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan velvet tangerine, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to tangerines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.
