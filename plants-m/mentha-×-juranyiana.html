Mentha × juranyiana

Mentha × juranyiana, commonly known as Hungarian mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha longifolia and Mentha piperita.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha × juranyiana is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
