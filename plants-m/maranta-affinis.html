Maranta affinis

Maranta affinis, commonly known as the prayer plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that often fold upwards at night, a behavior known as nyctinasty. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in indoor gardening. Its leaves display a range of colors, including green, red, and silver, depending on the variety.

Maranta affinis thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect light to flourish.
