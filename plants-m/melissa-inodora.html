Melissa inodora

Melissa inodora, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly lemon-scented leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation. Its leaves are often used in teas and traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Melissa inodora is also valued for its role in attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
