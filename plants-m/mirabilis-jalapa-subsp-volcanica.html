Mirabilis jalapa subsp. volcanica

Mirabilis jalapa subsp.

volcanica, commonly known as the Volcanic Marvel, is a striking flowering plant native to volcanic regions of Central America. It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often blooming at night.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh, volcanic soils.
