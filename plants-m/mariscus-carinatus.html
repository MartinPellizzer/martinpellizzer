Mariscus carinatus

Mariscus carinatus, commonly known as the spiked tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Mariscus carinatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
