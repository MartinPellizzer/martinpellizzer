Myrospermum balsamiferum

Myrospermum balsamiferum, commonly known as the balsam weed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive balsam-like scent, which contributes to its common name.

Myrospermum balsamiferum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is considered a perennial shrub.
