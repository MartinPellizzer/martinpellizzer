Marsdenia tonkinensis

Marsdenia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin Marsdenia, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berries that range in color from red to black. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
