Melaleuca formosa

Melaleuca formosa, commonly known as the fragrant myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Melaleuca formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
