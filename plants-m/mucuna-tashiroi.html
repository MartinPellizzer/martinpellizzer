Mucuna tashiroi

Mucuna tashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural systems. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, adding ornamental value to gardens.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
