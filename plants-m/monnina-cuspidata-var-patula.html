Monnina cuspidata var. patula

Monnina cuspidata var. patula, commonly known as prairie bluestem, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
