Moraea fenestralis

Moraea fenestralis, commonly known as the windowed moraea, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pale blue or violet flowers with intricate, fenestrated (window-like) petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Moraea fenestralis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
