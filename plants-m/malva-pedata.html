Malva pedata

Malva pedata, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Malva pedata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
