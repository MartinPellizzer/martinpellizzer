Myrcia lancea

Myrcia lancea, commonly known as the Brazilian tea or wild tea, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Myrcia lancea is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in herbal remedies for various ailments.
