Mascagnia fluminensis

Mascagnia fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a red, berry-like structure that matures in the fall and is edible but contains toxic compounds.
