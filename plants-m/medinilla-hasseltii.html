Medinilla hasseltii

Medinilla hasseltii, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or chocolate plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil and indirect sunlight.

Medinilla hasseltii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
