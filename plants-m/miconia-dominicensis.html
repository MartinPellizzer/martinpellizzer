Miconia dominicensis

Miconia dominicensis, commonly known as the Hispaniola miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds and other animals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
