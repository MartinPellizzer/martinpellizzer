Magnolia tsiampacca subsp. mollis

Magnolia tsiampacca subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the Madagascar magnolia, is a deciduous shrub native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservatories.
