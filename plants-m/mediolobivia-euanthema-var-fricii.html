Mediolobivia euanthema var. fricii

Mediolobivia euanthema var. fricii, commonly known as the Frici's golden spire, is a rare and striking plant characterized by its tall, spiraling stems that resemble a helix.

Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, arranged in a tight, upward-curving pattern along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
