Mammillaria neopotosina var. hexispina

Mammillaria neopotosina var. hexispina, commonly known as the six-spined mammillaria, is a compact cactus native to Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
