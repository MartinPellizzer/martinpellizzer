Muscari comosum var. mutilatum

Muscari comosum var. mutilatum, commonly known as the broken musk grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland settings.
