Malva pseudo-olbia

Malva pseudo-olbia, commonly known as the Malva of the Caucasus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to regions of the Caucasus and parts of southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Malva pseudo-olbia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
