Mitrephora macrocarpa

Mitrephora macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and mango. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a drupe, typically green to yellow when mature, and contains a single seed.
