Malus baccata subsp. sikkimensis

Malus baccata subsp.

sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim apple or Himalayan hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan region. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellowish-red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in mountainous ecosystems.
