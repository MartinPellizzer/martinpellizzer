Millettia oblata subsp. intermedia

Millettia oblata subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the red-flowered millettia, is a deciduous climbing plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red flowers.

The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, flowering climbers.
