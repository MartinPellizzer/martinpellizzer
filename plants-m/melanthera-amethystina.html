Melanthera amethystina

Melanthera amethystina, commonly known as the purple-flowered ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, lavender to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, panicle-like inflorescences, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Melanthera amethystina is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade conditions.
