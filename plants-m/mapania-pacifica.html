Mapania pacifica

Mapania pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific mapania, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Mapania pacifica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
