Microlepia lindsayiformis

Microlepia lindsayiformis, commonly known as the Lindsay's microlepia, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth. Its spores are released from small, clustered sporangia located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
