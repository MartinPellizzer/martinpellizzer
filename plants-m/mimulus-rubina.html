Mimulus rubina

Mimulus rubina, commonly known as the scarlet monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and forest floors. It has heart-shaped leaves and produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds via wind.

Mimulus rubina is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
