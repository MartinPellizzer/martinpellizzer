Medinilla sulcata

Medinilla sulcata, commonly known as the warty medinilla, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil.

Medinilla sulcata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
