Mentha sylvestris var. crispa

Mentha sylvestris var. crispa, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, wrinkled leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its use in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
