Majana inflata

Majana inflata, commonly known as the inflated majana, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its unique, inflated seed pods that resemble small, balloon-like structures. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually violet or purple and are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Majana inflata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
