Malva verticillata subsp. crispa

Malva verticillata subsp.

crispa, commonly known as the crinkled mallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that are often crinkled or wavy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
