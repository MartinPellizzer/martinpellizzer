Mariscus acutus

Mariscus acutus, commonly known as the sharpleaf mariscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features upright, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Mariscus acutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and unique growth habit.
