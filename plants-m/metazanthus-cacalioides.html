Metazanthus cacalioides

Metazanthus cacalioides, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, pale violet to white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in clusters. It is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to access underground water sources.

Due to its striking appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
