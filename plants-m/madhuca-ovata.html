Madhuca ovata

Madhuca ovata, commonly known as the white candle tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several edible seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
