Maxillaria simulans

Maxillaria simulans, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often showy flowers that resemble other orchid species, a trait that contributes to its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually fragrant and come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

The botanical features of Maxillaria simulans make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and adaptability.
