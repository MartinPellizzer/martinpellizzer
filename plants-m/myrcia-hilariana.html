Myrcia hilariana

Myrcia hilariana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Myrcia hilariana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
