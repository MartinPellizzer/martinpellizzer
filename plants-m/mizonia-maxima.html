Mizonia maxima

Mizonia maxima, commonly known as the giant mizonia, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in length, which are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of yellow and orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are fibrous and can grow quite deep, allowing it to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Mizonia maxima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
