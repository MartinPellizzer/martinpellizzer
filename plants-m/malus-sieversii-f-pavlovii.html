Malus sieversii f. pavlovii

Malus sieversii f. pavlovii, commonly known as the Pavlovii apple, is a botanical variant of the wild apple species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often ornamental appearance, with unique leaf shapes and flower structures. This form is typically smaller in size compared to its parent species, Malus sieversii, and exhibits a more compact growth habit. The Pavlovii apple is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and genetic diversity.

It plays a significant role in the study of apple evolution and breeding programs.
