Monogramma paradoxa

Monogramma paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns, though its unique growth pattern sets it apart. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained, acidic soil, often found in shaded forest understories. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the undersides of its fertile fronds, a feature typical of the Dryopteris genus.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Monogramma paradoxa is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
