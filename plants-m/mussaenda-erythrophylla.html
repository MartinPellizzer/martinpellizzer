Mussaenda erythrophylla

Mussaenda erythrophylla, commonly known as the red-flowered Mussaenda, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Mussaenda erythrophylla thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
