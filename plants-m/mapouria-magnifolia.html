Mapouria magnifolia

Mapouria magnifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered mapouria, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mapouria magnifolia is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
