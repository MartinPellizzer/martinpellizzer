Matthiola revoluta

Matthiola revoluta, commonly known as the clustered wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Matthiola revoluta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive blooms and resilience in garden settings.
