Myriophyllum latifolium

Myriophyllum latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf watermilfoil, is an aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater lakes and ponds, where it forms dense underwater stands. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Myriophyllum latifolium plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for fish and invertebrates.
