Malus baccata var. xiaojinensis

Malus baccata var. xiaojinensis, commonly known as the Xiaojin apple tree, is a variant of the crabapple species native to China.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental fruits that range in color from yellow to red, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental features.
