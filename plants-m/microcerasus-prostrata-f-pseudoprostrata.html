Microcerasus prostrata f. pseudoprostrata

Microcerasus prostrata f. pseudoprostrata, commonly known as the prostrate form of the Chinese cherry.

This variant is a compact, low-growing shrub with small, oval leaves that are typically a glossy green. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the spring. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.
