Malva flava

Malva flava, commonly known as the yellow mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like hibiscus and cotton.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
