Moquiniastrum bolivianum

Moquiniastrum bolivianum, commonly known as Bolivian marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and floodplains, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Moquiniastrum bolivianum plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
