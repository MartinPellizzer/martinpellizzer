Mentha hirsuta var. agardhiana

Mentha hirsuta var. agardhiana, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and square stems, typical of the mint family (Lamiaceae). The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Mentha hirsuta var. agardhiana is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
