Mentha × kahirina

Mentha × kahirina, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha longifolia (coin mint) and Mentha spicata (spearmint). The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and aromatic, fragrant leaves that contain high concentrations of essential oils. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Mentha × kahirina is widely cultivated for its use in culinary, medicinal, and aromatic applications.
