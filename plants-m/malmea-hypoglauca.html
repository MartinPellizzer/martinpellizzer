Malmea hypoglauca

Malmea hypoglauca, commonly known as the silverleaf malmea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Malmea hypoglauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
