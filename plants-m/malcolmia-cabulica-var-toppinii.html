Malcolmia cabulica var. toppinii

Malcolmia cabulica var. toppinii, commonly known as the Persian senna, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
