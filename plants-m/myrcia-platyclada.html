Myrcia platyclada

Myrcia platyclada, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.

Myrcia platyclada is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
