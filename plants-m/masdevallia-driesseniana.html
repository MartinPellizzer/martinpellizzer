Masdevallia driesseniana

Masdevallia driesseniana, commonly known as the Driessen's masdevallia, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flower spikes that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture, adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually fragrant and can range in color from white to pink, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of other orchid species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
