Melampodium appendiculatum var. sonorense

Melampodium appendiculatum var. sonorense, commonly known as the Sonoran sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to the arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for desert conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
