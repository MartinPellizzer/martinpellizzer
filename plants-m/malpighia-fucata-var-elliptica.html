Malpighia fucata var. elliptica

Malpighia fucata var. elliptica, commonly known as the Brazilian passionfruit, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It produces small, edible fruits with a vibrant orange or red color, encased in a rough, leathery exterior. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental value and the unique flavor of its fruits.

The plant is also noted for its ability to climb using tendrils, making it suitable for trellises or supports in gardens.
