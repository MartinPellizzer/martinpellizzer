Medicago caerulea

Medicago caerulea, commonly known as blue medic, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Medicago caerulea is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
