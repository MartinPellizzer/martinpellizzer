Manganaroa platensis

Manganaroa platensis, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant's flowers are showy, with a distinctive pinkish hue and a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the muddy bottom of its habitat while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Manganaroa platensis plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
