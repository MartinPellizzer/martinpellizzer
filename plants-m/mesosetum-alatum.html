Mesosetum alatum

Mesosetum alatum, commonly known as the winged grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wing-like structures on the leaf sheaths, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often pale green to brown in color.

Mesosetum alatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
