Myrcia rhodophylla

Myrcia rhodophylla, commonly known as the red-leaf myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, reddish-brown leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Myrcia rhodophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
