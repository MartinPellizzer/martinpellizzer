Malus transcendens

Malus transcendens, commonly known as the Transcendent apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy white flowers and vibrant autumn foliage. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth margin, while its fruits are small, sweet apples that are not widely cultivated for consumption.

The species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
