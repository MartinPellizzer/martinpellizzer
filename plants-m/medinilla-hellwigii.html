Medinilla hellwigii

Medinilla hellwigii, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

This tropical plant is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, waxy, pinkish-red bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange-yellow berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Medinilla hellwigii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
