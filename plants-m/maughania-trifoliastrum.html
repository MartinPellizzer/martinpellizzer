Maughania trifoliastrum

Maughania trifoliastrum, commonly known as the three-leaved maughania, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
