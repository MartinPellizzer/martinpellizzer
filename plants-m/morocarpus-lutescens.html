Morocarpus lutescens

Morocarpus lutescens, commonly known as the yellow morocarpus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical stems. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its arid habitat. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the warm, dry climate of its native region.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
