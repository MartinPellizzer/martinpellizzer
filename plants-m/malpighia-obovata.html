Malpighia obovata

Malpighia obovata, commonly known as the purple granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, bright red or purple berries. The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers. The berries are edible, though they have a tart, acidic flavor, and are sometimes used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
