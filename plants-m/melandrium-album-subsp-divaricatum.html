Melandrium album subsp. divaricatum

Melandrium album subsp.

divaricatum, commonly known as the white catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
