Misopates calycinum

Misopates calycinum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy coastal habitats. Its flowering period typically occurs in summer, and it is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance.

The plant has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
