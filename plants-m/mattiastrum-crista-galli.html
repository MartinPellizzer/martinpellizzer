Mattiastrum crista-galli

Mattiastrum crista-galli, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion, making it useful in ecological restoration projects. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.

Mattiastrum crista-galli is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
