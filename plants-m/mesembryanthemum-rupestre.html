Mesembryanthemum rupestre

Mesembryanthemum rupestre, commonly known as the rock rose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and rocky environments. Its adaptability to drought conditions is due to its thick, water-storing leaves and deep root system.

Mesembryanthemum rupestre is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
