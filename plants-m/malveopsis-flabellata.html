Malveopsis flabellata

Malveopsis flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate mallow, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is often found near water bodies. Its flowers have five petals and are arranged in a distinctive flabellate (fan-like) shape, giving it its common name.

Malveopsis flabellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
