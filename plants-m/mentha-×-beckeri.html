Mentha × beckeri

Mentha × beckeri, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha viridis (ovalleaf mint).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its aromatic leaves and creeping growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in whorled clusters. Mentha × beckeri is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its essential oils and culinary uses.

Its hybrid nature contributes to its adaptability and resilience in various growing conditions.
