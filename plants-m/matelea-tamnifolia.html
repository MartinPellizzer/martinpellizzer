Matelea tamnifolia

Matelea tamnifolia, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.
