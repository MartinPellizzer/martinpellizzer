Maxillaria neillii

Maxillaria neillii, commonly known as the Neill's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Maxillaria neillii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
