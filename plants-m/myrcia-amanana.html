Myrcia amanana

Myrcia amanana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Myrcia amanana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
