Mussaenda saigonensis

Mussaenda saigonensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange or red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Mussaenda saigonensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
