Monogramma subfalcata

Monogramma subfalcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf monogramma, is a species of aquatic fern native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged, with a finely divided structure. Its rhizomes are creeping and help anchor it to substrates in its habitat.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality through nutrient cycling.
