Murucuia speciosa

Murucuia speciosa, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
