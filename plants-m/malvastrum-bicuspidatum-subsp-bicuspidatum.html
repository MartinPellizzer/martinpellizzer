Malvastrum bicuspidatum subsp. bicuspidatum

Malvastrum bicuspidatum subsp.

bicuspidatum, commonly known as the two-lobed mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, displaying a sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
