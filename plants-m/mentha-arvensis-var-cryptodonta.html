Mentha arvensis var. cryptodonta

Mentha arvensis var. cryptodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink and appear in whorls along the stem.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
