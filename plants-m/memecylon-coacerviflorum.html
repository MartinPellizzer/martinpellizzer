Memecylon coacerviflorum

Memecylon coacerviflorum, commonly known as the sticky-flowered memecylon, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that are borne on short pedicels. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release minute seeds.

Memecylon coacerviflorum is often found in moist, shaded habitats within lowland forests.
