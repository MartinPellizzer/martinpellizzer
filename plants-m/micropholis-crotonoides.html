Micropholis crotonoides

Micropholis crotonoides, commonly known as the Natal croton, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in coastal forests and along the edges of estuaries.
