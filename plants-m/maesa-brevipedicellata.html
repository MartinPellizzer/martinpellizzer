Maesa brevipedicellata

Maesa brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-stemmed maesa, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
