Macropiper latum

Macropiper latum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered green berries that mature to a dark purple color. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
