Mespilus monogyna var. pectinata

Mespilus monogyna var. pectinata, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, yellow drupes that ripen in late summer.

It is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ecological importance and ornamental qualities.
