Mischocarpus pyriformis subsp. papuanus

Mischocarpus pyriformis subsp.

papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan pomegranate, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is a small, deciduous tree native to the lowland rainforests of New Guinea.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a woody, pear-shaped capsule that splits open to reveal bright red, edible arils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in indigenous communities.
