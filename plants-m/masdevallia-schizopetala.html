Masdevallia schizopetala

Masdevallia schizopetala, commonly known as the "Clove Orchid," is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers that resemble cloves, with a deep red or purple coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-like, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Masdevallia schizopetala is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its exotic appearance.
