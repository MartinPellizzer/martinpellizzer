Mimulus guttatus subsp. litoralis

Mimulus guttatus subsp.

litoralis, commonly known as the coastal monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific coast of North America. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along coastal bluffs and dunes.

The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers with two lips, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to coastal environments and exhibits variations in flower size and color depending on environmental conditions.
