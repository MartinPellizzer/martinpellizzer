Montbretia lancea

Montbretia lancea, commonly known as the lance-leaved montbretia, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important plants. The plant features sword-shaped, lanceolate leaves and produces showy, orange-red flowers with a distinctive, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
