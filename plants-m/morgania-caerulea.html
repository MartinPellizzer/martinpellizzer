Morgania caerulea

Morgania caerulea, commonly known as the blue morgania, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Morgania caerulea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
