Maackia tenuifolia

Maackia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf maackia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white or pale pink. Its fruit is a slender, cylindrical pod that splits open to release its seeds.

Maackia tenuifolia is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
