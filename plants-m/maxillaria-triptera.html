Maxillaria triptera

Maxillaria triptera, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a contrasting dark center. Its leaves are linear and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Maxillaria triptera thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
