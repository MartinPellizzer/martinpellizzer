Masdevallia vexillifera

Masdevallia vexillifera, commonly known as the flag-flowered masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, lance-shaped leaves and striking, colorful flowers that resemble flags, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of pink, red, and orange, are highly prized in horticulture for their beauty and uniqueness.

Masdevallia vexillifera is relatively rare and requires specific care to thrive, making it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
