Memecylon tamifolium

Memecylon tamifolium, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Meliaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and thrives in humid, tropical climates.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
