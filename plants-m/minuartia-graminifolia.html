Minuartia graminifolia

Minuartia graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, linear leaves that resemble grass, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Minuartia graminifolia is a perennial species that is well adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.
