Merianthera verrucosa

Merianthera verrucosa, commonly known as the waxyflower or hairy merianthera, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland habitats. The plant features small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often covered in fine, hairy textures.

Merianthera verrucosa is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
