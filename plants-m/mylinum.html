Mylinum

Mylinum, commonly known as the European lily, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant has strap-like, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. Mylinum thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and long-lasting flowers.
