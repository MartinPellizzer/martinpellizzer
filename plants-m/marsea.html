Marsea

Marsea, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found growing near the shoreline. Marsea is a hardy perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in coastal landscapes.
