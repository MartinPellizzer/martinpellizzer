Miscanthidium capense var. villosum

Miscanthidium capense var. villosum, commonly known as the velvety grass or velvet bluemink, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
