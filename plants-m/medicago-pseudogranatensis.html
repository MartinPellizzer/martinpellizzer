Medicago pseudogranatensis

Medicago pseudogranatensis, commonly known as the granatense clover, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is characterized by its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
