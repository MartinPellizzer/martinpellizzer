Maranta pygmaea

Maranta pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Marantaceae family, characterized by its ornamental foliage and unique growth habits. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its compact size and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.
