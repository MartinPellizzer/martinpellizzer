Myrsine cupuliformis

Myrsine cupuliformis, commonly known as the shield-leaf myrsine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. Its small, white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
