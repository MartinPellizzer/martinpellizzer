Melolobium exudans

Melolobium exudans, commonly known as the sticky bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves that secrete a sticky, aromatic resin. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its resin has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
