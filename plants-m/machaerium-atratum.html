Machaerium atratum

Machaerium atratum, commonly known as black machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and the presence of thorn-like structures on its stems.

Machaerium atratum is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
