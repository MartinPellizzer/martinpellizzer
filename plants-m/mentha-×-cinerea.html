Mentha × cinerea

Mentha × cinerea, commonly known as gray mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and slender, aromatic stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Mentha × cinerea is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds, making it valuable in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
