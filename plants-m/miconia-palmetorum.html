Miconia palmetorum

Miconia palmetorum, commonly known as the Florida palmetto, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in wetland habitats and is adapted to tolerate seasonal flooding.

Due to its ecological importance, it is often found in swamp forests and along waterways.
