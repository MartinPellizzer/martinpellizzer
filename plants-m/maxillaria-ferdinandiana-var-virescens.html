Maxillaria ferdinandiana var. virescens

Maxillaria ferdinandiana var. virescens, commonly known as the Emerald Lady orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-white to emerald-colored flowers, which are often adorned with dark purple markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating efficient water retention.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific humidity and temperature requirements.
