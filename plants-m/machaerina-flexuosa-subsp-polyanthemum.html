Machaerina flexuosa subsp. polyanthemum

Machaerina flexuosa subsp.

polyanthemum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in racemes and bloom in spring.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also significant in Australian botany and ecology.
