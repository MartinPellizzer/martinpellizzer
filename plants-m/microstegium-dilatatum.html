Microstegium dilatatum

Microstegium dilatatum, commonly known as the broadleaf woodsorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its fibrous root system and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Microstegium dilatatum is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.
