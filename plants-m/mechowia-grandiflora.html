Mechowia grandiflora

Mechowia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant mechowia, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its large, showy white to pinkish flowers.

Native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of finely divided, fern-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces umbels of flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, Mechowia grandiflora is valued in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
