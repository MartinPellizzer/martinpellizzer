Malesherbia prolifera

Malesherbia prolifera, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Malesherbia prolifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
