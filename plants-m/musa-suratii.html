Musa suratii

Musa suratii, commonly known as the Surat banana, is a species of banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of edible, elongated fruits. The plant belongs to the Musaceae family and is closely related to other banana species. Musa suratii typically grows in tropical climates and requires warm, humid conditions to thrive.

Its fruits are often used in local cuisines and have a flavor similar to plantains.
