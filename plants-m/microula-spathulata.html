Microula spathulata

Microula spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed microula, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in dry gardens and rockeries.
