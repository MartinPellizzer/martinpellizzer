Monardella odoratissima var. glabriuscula

Monardella odoratissima var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the fragrant monardella, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its strong, spicy scent.

This variety is less hairy than the typical form, giving it a smoother appearance.
