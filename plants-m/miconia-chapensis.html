Miconia chapensis

Miconia chapensis, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia chapensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and showy flowers.
