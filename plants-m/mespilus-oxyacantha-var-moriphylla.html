Mespilus oxyacantha var. moriphylla

Mespilus oxyacantha var. moriphylla, commonly known as the Persian ironwood, is a rare and hardy shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is often found in protected natural reserves.
