Myrcia obtusa

Myrcia obtusa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae, characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Myrcia obtusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense, glossy leaves and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, posing challenges to local ecosystems.
