Myrcia martiusiana

Myrcia martiusiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Myrcia martiusiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
