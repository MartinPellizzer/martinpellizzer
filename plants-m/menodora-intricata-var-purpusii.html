Menodora intricata var. purpusii

Menodora intricata var. purpusii, commonly known as the delicate moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menodoridae and is characterized by its small, white, night-blooming flowers. The plant has a trailing growth habit with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
