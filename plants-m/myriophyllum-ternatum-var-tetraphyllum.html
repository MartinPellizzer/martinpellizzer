Myriophyllum ternatum var. tetraphyllum

Myriophyllum ternatum var. tetraphyllum, commonly known as four-leaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often green or reddish in color, and its leaves are opposite, narrow, and whorled in groups of four. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, and it is often found in freshwater lakes and ponds.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
