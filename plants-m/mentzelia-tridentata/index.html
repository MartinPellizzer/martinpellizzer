Mentzelia Tridentata

Mentzelia tridentata, commonly known as the three-lobed blazingstar, is a striking member of the sunflower family native to the southwestern United States. This flowering plant is celebrated for its vibrant yellow blooms that appear in late spring and early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains. The plant's unique tri-lobed leaves and tall stalks make it easily identifiable in its natural habitat. Despite its beauty, it is also a subject of ecological interest due to its role in supporting local pollinators and its resilience in harsh conditions.
