Miconia calyptrata

Miconia calyptrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. Miconia calyptrata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of tropical and subtropical climates.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
