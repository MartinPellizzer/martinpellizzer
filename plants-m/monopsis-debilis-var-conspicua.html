Monopsis debilis var. conspicua

Monopsis debilis var. conspicua, commonly known as the showy monopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
