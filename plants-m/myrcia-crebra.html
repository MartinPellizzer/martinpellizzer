Myrcia crebra

Myrcia crebra, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Myrcia crebra is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable plant in its native habitat.
