Mentha × piperita var. officinalis

Mentha × piperita var. officinalis, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its strong, aromatic scent and refreshing menthol flavor.

It is a perennial herb with square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers in whorled clusters, which are borne on erect spikes. Peppermint thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in medicine, food, and aromatherapy.
