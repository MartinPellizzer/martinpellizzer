Mirabilis laevis var. laevis

Mirabilis laevis var. laevis, commonly known as the angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant is herbaceous, with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, especially in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.
