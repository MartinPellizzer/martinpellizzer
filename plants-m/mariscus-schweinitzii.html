Mariscus schweinitzii

Mariscus schweinitzii, commonly known as the Schweinitz's marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
