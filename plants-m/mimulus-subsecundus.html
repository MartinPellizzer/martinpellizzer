Mimulus subsecundus

Mimulus subsecundus, commonly known as the trailing monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverbanks.

Mimulus subsecundus is a valuable plant for pollinators and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
