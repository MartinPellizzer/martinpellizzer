Melaleuca eleuterostachya var. abietina

Melaleuca eleuterostachya var. abietina, commonly known as the silver bottlebrush, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the eastern coast of Australia, where it thrives in coastal and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences make it a popular ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate salty air and coastal conditions, making it suitable for landscaping in harsh environments.
