Miraglossum davyi

Miraglossum davyi, commonly known as the Davy's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, intricate flowers that often resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically within tropical or subtropical forests. Its flowering period is brief, and it is often difficult to spot due to its subtle appearance.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, Miraglossum davyi is considered a critically endangered species.
