Metamagnolia dealbata

Metamagnolia dealbata, commonly known as the white magnolia, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in spring, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its white blooms. Metamagnolia dealbata is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
