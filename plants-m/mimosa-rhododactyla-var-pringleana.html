Mimosa rhododactyla var. pringleana

Mimosa rhododactyla var. pringleana, commonly known as the Pringle's mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by the plant's sensitive leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and sensitivity, it is often cultivated in gardens as a curiosity.
