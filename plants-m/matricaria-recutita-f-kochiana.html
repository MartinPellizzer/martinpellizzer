Matricaria recutita f. kochiana

Matricaria recutita f. kochiana, commonly known as the Koch's chamomile, is a variant of the common chamomile plant.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellow flower heads that resemble those of the common chamomile. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

The flowers are used in herbal teas and are valued for their calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
