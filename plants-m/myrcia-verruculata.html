Myrcia verruculata

Myrcia verruculata, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
