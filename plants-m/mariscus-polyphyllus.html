Mariscus polyphyllus

Mariscus polyphyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a striking visual contrast to its delicate blooms.

Mariscus polyphyllus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
