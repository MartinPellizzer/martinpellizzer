Myrciaria cuspidata

Myrciaria cuspidata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-mato, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are evergreen and have a distinctive elliptical shape with a pointed tip.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant flowers.
