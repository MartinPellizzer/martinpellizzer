Miconia palustris

Miconia palustris, commonly known as swamp miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the wetlands of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that attracts various birds and mammals.

Miconia palustris plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
