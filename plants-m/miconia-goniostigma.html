Miconia goniostigma

Miconia goniostigma, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or purple miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, often with a distinctive star-like pattern. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia goniostigma is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions, making it a plant of both ecological and horticultural interest.
