Monanthes × polycaulis

Monanthes × polycaulis, commonly known as the hybrid everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a cross between Monanthes artemisiifolia and Monanthes polycephala, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow. It is native to South Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in various climates.

The hybrid is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
