Mesua daphnifolia

Mesua daphnifolia, commonly known as the Indian rose or white flame of the forest, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving the tree a striking appearance. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green surface. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
