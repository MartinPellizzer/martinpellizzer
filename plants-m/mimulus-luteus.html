Mimulus luteus

Mimulus luteus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. It has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus luteus is a herbaceous perennial that produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
