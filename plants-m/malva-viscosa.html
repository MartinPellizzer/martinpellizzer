Malva viscosa

Malva viscosa, commonly known as sticky mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, sticky hairs, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive central column. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and along stream banks.

The plant is known for its mucilaginous properties, making it useful in traditional herbal medicine.
