Minuartia erythrosepala var. cappadocica

Minuartia erythrosepala var. cappadocica, commonly known as the Cappadocian rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is native to regions of Turkey and parts of the Middle East, where it thrives in limestone and volcanic soils.
