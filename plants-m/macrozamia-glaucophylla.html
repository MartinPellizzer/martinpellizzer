Macrozamia glaucophylla

Macrozamia glaucophylla, commonly known as the Glaucophylla cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the subtropical regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Due to habitat loss and limited natural regeneration, it is considered vulnerable and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation efforts.
