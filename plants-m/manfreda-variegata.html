Manfreda variegata

Manfreda variegata, commonly known as the variegated manfreda, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features striking, fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
