Myrceugenia bracteosa var. guaratubensis

Myrceugenia bracteosa var. guaratubensis, commonly known as the Guaratubinha myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome.
