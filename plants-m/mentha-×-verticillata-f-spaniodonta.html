Mentha × verticillata f. spaniodonta

Mentha × verticillata f. spaniodonta, commonly known as the Spanish mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often fragrant. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
