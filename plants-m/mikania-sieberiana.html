Mikania sieberiana

Mikania sieberiana, commonly known as climbing hemp or Mexican morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Mikania sieberiana is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and forest edges, where it can spread aggressively.
