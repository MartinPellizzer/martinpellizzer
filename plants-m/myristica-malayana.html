Myristica malayana

Myristica malayana, commonly known as the Malay nut or Malaya nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces an aromatic seed, which is encased in a hard, woody shell. Its fruits are typically harvested when fully mature, and the seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
