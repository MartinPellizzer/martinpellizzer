Myrciaria delicatula var. latifolia

Myrciaria delicatula var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wax apple, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by edible, waxy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
