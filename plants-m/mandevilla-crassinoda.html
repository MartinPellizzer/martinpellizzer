Mandevilla crassinoda

Mandevilla crassinoda, commonly known as the broadleaf mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves and clusters of tubular, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is a vigorous climber, often used in ornamental gardening for its showy display. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Mandevilla crassinoda is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
