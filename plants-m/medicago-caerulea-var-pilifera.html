Medicago caerulea var. pilifera

Medicago caerulea var. pilifera, commonly known as blue medic or slender blue medic, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a forage plant.
