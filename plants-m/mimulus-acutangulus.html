Mimulus acutangulus

Mimulus acutangulus, commonly known as the acute-leaved monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tubular, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

Mimulus acutangulus is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
