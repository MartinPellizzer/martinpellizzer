Marcgravia salicifolia

Marcgravia salicifolia, commonly known as the silky leaf mallow, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
