Myriophyllum verticillatum var. aquaticum

Myriophyllum verticillatum var. aquaticum, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It features whorled leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically found in clusters. It is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as lakes and ponds.

Watermilfoil plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
