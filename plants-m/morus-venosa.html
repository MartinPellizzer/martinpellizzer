Morus venosa

Morus venosa, commonly known as the veined mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive veined leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Morus venosa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
