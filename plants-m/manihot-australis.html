Manihot australis

Manihot australis, commonly known as the Australian arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a starch source. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Manihot australis is valued for its nutritional properties and has been used by Indigenous communities for food and traditional medicine.
