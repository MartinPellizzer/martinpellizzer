Mammillaria san-angelensis

Mammillaria san-angelensis, commonly known as the San Angel's Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a spiny, segmented stem that typically grows to a height of about 10 centimeters. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
