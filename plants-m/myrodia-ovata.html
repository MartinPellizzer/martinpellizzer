Myrodia ovata

Myrodia ovata, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy soils and salty air.

Myrodia ovata is an important component of the coastal flora and is often found in heathlands and dunes.
