Malva mareotica

Malva mareotica, commonly known as the Egyptian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Nile Delta region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, saline soils and is often found near water bodies. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been historically used for various purposes, including textile production.

Malva mareotica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
