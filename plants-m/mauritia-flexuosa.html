Mauritia flexuosa

Mauritia flexuosa, commonly known as the palm heart or macaw-bird palm, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody fruits containing several seeds. Its trunk is often harvested for its fibrous material, which has been used traditionally for making ropes and textiles.

However, due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is now considered vulnerable in some regions.
