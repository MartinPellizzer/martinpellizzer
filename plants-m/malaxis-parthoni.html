Malaxis parthoni

Malaxis parthoni, commonly known as the Parthon's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Malaxis parthoni is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
