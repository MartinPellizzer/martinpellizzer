Mearnsia ovata

Mearnsia ovata, commonly known as the white mearnsia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its woody stems are often branched and can grow up to several meters in height.

Mearnsia ovata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
