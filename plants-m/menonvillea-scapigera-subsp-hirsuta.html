Menonvillea scapigera subsp. hirsuta

Menonvillea scapigera subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy menonvillea, is a perennial herb native to South America. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and lanceolate leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
