Magnolia stellata

Magnolia stellata, commonly known as the star magnolia, is a deciduous flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and has a rounded, compact form. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a smooth margin.

The flowers are fragrant and range in color from white to pale pink, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
