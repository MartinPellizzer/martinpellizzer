Myrceugenia ovata

Myrceugenia ovata, commonly known as the oval myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to southern Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are small, berry-like drupes that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
