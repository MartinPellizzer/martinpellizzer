Mentha × triplex

Mentha × triplex, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between spearmint (Mentha spicata) and watermint (Mentha aquatica).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hollow stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers in whorled clusters, which appear in late summer. Mentha × triplex is known for its strong, aromatic scent and is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
