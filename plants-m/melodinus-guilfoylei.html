Melodinus guilfoylei

Melodinus guilfoylei, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the island of Christmas Island in the Indian Ocean.

It is a small shrub that produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the wet season. The plant thrives in humid, tropical conditions and is typically found in coastal areas with well-drained sandy soils. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
