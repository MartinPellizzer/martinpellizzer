Masdevallia chimaera var. gorgona

Masdevallia chimaera var. gorgona, commonly known as the Gorgona masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often bi-colored flowers that display a mix of purple, pink, and white hues. The plant typically grows as a small epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, high-altitude cloud forests. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
