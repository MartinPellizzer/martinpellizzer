Mulgedium lazicum

Mulgedium lazicum, commonly known as the Caucasian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features tall, upright stems covered in soft, velvety hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Mulgedium lazicum is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.
