Myrcia guavira-mi

Myrcia guavira-mi, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Myrcia guavira-mi is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
