Mesembryanthemum nudicaule

Mesembryanthemum nudicaule, commonly known as the naked-stemed ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It blooms during the cooler months, often in spring or early summer.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
