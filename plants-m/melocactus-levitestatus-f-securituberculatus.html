Melocactus levitestatus f. securituberculatus

Melocactus levitestatus f. securituberculatus, commonly known as the Spiny Hedgehog Cactus, is a unique variant of the Melocactus genus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem and the presence of prominent, sharp spines that provide protection against herbivores. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a slow growth rate. Its flowers, which appear in late spring, are usually yellow or orange and are borne at the top of the stem.

This cultivar is valued for its striking appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens.
