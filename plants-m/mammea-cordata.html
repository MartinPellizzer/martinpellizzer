Mammea cordata

Mammea cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf mammea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in local medicine.
