Mentha × niliaca subsp. velutina

Mentha × niliaca subsp.

velutina, commonly known as velvet mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae. It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are aromatic and have a minty scent, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
