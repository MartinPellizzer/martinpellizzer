Mammillaria albiseta

Mammillaria albiseta, commonly known as the white-set mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by pale green fruits. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.

Due to its delicate appearance, it requires careful handling to avoid damage.
