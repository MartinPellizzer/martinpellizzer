Masdevallia tridens

Masdevallia tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and a variety of colorful, intricate flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically has three prominent teeth on its lip, which give it its common name. It prefers cool, high-altitude environments with high humidity and indirect light.

Masdevallia tridens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
