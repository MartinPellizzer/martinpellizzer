Mesua parviflora

Mesua parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered mesua, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Mesua parviflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
