Matourea dubia

Matourea dubia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces striking, pale green or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its unique appearance and elusive nature have made it a subject of fascination among botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and illegal collection, Matourea dubia is classified as critically endangered.
