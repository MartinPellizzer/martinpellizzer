Musa acuminata

Musa acuminata, commonly known as the Asian banana, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Musa.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is one of the primary species used in the cultivation of modern bananas. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces clusters of fruit known as hands. It is a herbaceous perennial that grows in tropical climates and requires warm, moist conditions to thrive.

Musa acuminata is closely related to Musa balbisiana and is often hybridized to create the common edible banana varieties.
