Muhlenbergia laxiflora

Muhlenbergia laxiflora, commonly known as the slender muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale blue seeds that are dispersed by wind, a common adaptation in grasses. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
