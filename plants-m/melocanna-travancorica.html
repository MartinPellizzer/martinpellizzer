Melocanna travancorica

Melocanna travancorica, commonly known as the Travancore bamboo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a tall, slender bamboo that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

The plant's rhizomes are sometimes used in local medicinal practices, though its primary botanical significance lies in its ecological role and aesthetic value.
