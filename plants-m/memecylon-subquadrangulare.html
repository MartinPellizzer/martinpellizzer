Memecylon subquadrangulare

Memecylon subquadrangulare, commonly known as the quadrangular memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its quadrangular (four-sided) stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
