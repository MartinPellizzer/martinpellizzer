Moraea iridioides var. catenulata

Moraea iridioides var. catenulata, commonly known as the chain-flowered moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble chains of flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers come in shades of pink, purple, and white.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
