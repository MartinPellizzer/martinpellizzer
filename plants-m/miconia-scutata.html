Miconia scutata

Miconia scutata, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, followed by berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife.

Miconia scutata is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in certain regions.
