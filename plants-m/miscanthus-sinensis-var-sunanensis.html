Miscanthus sinensis var. sunanensis

Miscanthus sinensis var. sunanensis, commonly known as the Sunan silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to China.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that are often used in ornamental landscaping. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
