Melica magnolii

Melica magnolii, commonly known as the magnolia grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which are typically greenish or pale blue. Melica magnolii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
