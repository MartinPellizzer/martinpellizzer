Masdevallia maxillariiformis

Masdevallia maxillariiformis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and striking, pale pink to white flowers that resemble a ghostly apparition. This epiphytic plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires specific conditions to bloom, making it a challenging species to cultivate. Its unique appearance and rarity have made it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.

The plant's survival is threatened by habitat loss and over-collection, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
