Myrodia macrophylla

Myrodia macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, broad leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Myrodia macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Indigenous practices.
