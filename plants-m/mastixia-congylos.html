Mastixia congylos

Mastixia congylos, commonly known as the Greek mastix tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a sticky, resinous substance known as mastix. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
