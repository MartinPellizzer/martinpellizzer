Medicago × hemicycla subsp. loriensis

Medicago × hemicycla subsp.

loriensis, commonly known as the Lori Medicago, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is adapted to rocky and semi-arid environments, showcasing a robust root system for water retention. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic bacteria.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its resilience and potential for soil enrichment.
