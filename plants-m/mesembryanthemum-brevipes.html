Mesembryanthemum brevipes

Mesembryanthemum brevipes, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in summer. It is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored plant among gardeners in Mediterranean and semi-arid climates.
