Mentha × nemorosa var. crispa

Mentha × nemorosa var. crispa, commonly known as the curly mint or woolly mint, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, crisped, curled leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers in late summer, which are often clustered in whorls. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is commonly used in herbal teas and as a decorative plant.
