Mimosa leucaenoides

Mimosa leucaenoides, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spherical inflorescences, adding ornamental value to the plant. It is often cultivated for its decorative foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
