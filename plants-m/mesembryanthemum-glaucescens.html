Mesembryanthemum glaucescens

Mesembryanthemum glaucescens, commonly known as the silverleaf iceplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, yellow, or white, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Mesembryanthemum glaucescens is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
