Marrubium angustifolium

Marrubium angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved horehound, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green, hairy leaves and produces blue or violet flowers in summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, slightly bitter leaves and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its medicinal properties.

Marrubium angustifolium has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and expectorant effects.
