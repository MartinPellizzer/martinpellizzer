Mentha longifolia subsp. ambigua

Mentha longifolia subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are typically narrow and pointed.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or blue, arranged in loose spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their cooling and antiseptic effects.
