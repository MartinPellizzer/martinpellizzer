Microlaena stipoides

Microlaena stipoides, commonly known as silky barley grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and slightly hairy leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Microlaena stipoides is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wildlife.
