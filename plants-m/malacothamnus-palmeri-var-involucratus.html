Malacothamnus palmeri var. involucratus

Malacothamnus palmeri var. involucratus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in late spring. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is adapted to arid environments.

This variety is distinguished by its involucral leaves that tightly enclose the flower heads.
