Muscari pulchellum subsp. pulchellum

Muscari pulchellum subsp.

pulchellum, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant, clustered flowers and hardy nature.
