Medicago polymorpha subsp. reticulata

Medicago polymorpha subsp.

reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate medick, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern.

The plant produces yellow pea-like flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It is a perennial herb that thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
