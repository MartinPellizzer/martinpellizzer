Mimosa maracayuensis

Mimosa maracayuensis, commonly known as the Maracayu mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is known for its soft, feathery leaves that are sensitive to touch, a trait common in many mimosa species. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance.

The plant is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.
