Melochia vitiensis

Melochia vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis Melochia, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
