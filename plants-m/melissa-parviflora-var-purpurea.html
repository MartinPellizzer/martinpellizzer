Melissa parviflora var. purpurea

Melissa parviflora var. purpurea, commonly known as purple lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic leaves that are typically a lighter green with a purple underside. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and herb collections.

Its calming scent and historical use in herbal medicine highlight its botanical and medicinal significance.
