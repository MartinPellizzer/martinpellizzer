Myrcia fusca

Myrcia fusca, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or black myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, while its flowers are usually pink or white, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
