Malveopsis pediculariifolia

Malveopsis pediculariifolia, commonly known as the hooked leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, hooked leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
