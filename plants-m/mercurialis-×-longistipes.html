Mercurialis × longistipes

Mercurialis × longistipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed mercury plant, is a hybrid species within the genus Mercurialis, which belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive waxy texture.

The species is noted for its adaptability to moist conditions and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
