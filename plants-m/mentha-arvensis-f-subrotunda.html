Mentha arvensis f. subrotunda

Mentha arvensis f. subrotunda, commonly known as round-leaf field mint, is a subspecies of the field mint plant.

It is characterized by its rounded, slightly hairy leaves that are broader than those of the typical form. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the mint family. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils in temperate regions.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
