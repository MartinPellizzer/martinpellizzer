Matelea reflexa

Matelea reflexa, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a short, woody stem and hairy, ovate leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Matelea reflexa plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar and habitat for various desert species.
