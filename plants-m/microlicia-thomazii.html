Microlicia thomazii

Microlicia thomazii, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its humid, shaded habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss, Microlicia thomazii is considered critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
