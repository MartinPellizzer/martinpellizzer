Mimosa accedens

Mimosa accedens, commonly known as the sensitive plant or mimosa pudica, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by mechanical stimulation. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

Its unique sensitivity and ornamental appeal have made it a subject of both scientific study and horticultural interest.
