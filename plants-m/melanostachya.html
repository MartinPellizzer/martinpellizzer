Melanostachya, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sedge, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically clustered in spikelets and are often surrounded by bracts. Melanostachya species are often found in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks.
