Megaclinium lasianthum

Megaclinium lasianthum, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of soft, silky hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
