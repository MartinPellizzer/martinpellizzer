Mikania siambonensis

Mikania siambonensis, commonly known as the Siamese Mikania, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, cotton-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain areas.
