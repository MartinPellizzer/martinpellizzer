Minuartia peploides var. diffusa

Minuartia peploides var. diffusa, commonly known as the creeping sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and its leaves are usually scale-like or very reduced. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in harsh environments such as tundra or mountain slopes. It is a perennial species with a widespread distribution across the northern hemisphere.
