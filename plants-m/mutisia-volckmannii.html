Mutisia volckmannii

Mutisia volckmannii, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a woody stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most striking feature is the bright yellow flower head, which consists of numerous small, tubular florets surrounded by ray florets.

Mutisia volckmannii is typically found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
