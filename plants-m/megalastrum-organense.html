Megalastrum organense

Megalastrum organense, commonly known as the Organensis giant lily, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant shades of pink and white. The plant belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is known for its robust, rhizomatous growth habit. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often reaching lengths of over a meter, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
