Menziesia glanduliflora

Menziesia glanduliflora, commonly known as the glandular-flowered menziesia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring, which are adorned with glandular hairs. Its fruits are small, dark berries that ripen in the fall.

Menziesia glanduliflora thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.
