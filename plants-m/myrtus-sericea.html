Myrtus sericea

Myrtus sericea, commonly known as silver myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. The leaves are narrow, leathery, and covered in a fine silvery-white wax, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
