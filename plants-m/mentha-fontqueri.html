Mentha fontqueri

Mentha fontqueri, commonly known as the Florida mint, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as swamps and riverbanks. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their menthol-like scent.

Mentha fontqueri is also valued for its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
