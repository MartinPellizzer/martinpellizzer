Muscari woronowii

Muscari woronowii, commonly known as the Persian snowflake, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant, blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Muscari woronowii is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other muscari species.
