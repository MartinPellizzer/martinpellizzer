Myosotis dissitiflora var. dyerae

Myosotis dissitiflora var. dyerae, commonly known as the Dyera's forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters atop slender stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

This variant is part of the Myosotidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native habitats.
