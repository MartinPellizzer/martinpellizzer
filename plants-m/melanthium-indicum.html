Melanthium indicum

Melanthium indicum, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow up to 1 meter in height and produce clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and its flowers are arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Melanthium indicum is known for its ability to attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
