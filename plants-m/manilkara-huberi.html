Manilkara huberi

Manilkara huberi, commonly known as the black manilkara, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, yellow flowers that give way to round, black fruits resembling cherries. Its bark is thick and rough, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
