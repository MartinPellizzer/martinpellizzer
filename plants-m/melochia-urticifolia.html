Melochia urticifolia

Melochia urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging leaf or velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that often have a velvety texture. The leaves are covered in fine hairs, which can cause an irritating rash upon contact with skin. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is native to Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates.
