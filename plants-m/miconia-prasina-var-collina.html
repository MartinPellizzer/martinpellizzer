Miconia prasina var. collina

Miconia prasina var. collina, commonly known as the mountain miconia, is a shrub or small tree native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, white to pink flowers with five petals, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
