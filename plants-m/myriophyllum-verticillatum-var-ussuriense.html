Myriophyllum verticillatum var. ussuriense

Myriophyllum verticillatum var. ussuriense, commonly known as the Ussuri watermilfoil, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Asia.

It features whorled leaves that are typically narrow and green, with a soft, feathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Its rhizomes help it spread and form dense underwater vegetation.
