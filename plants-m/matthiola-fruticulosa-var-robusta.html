Matthiola fruticulosa var. robusta

Matthiola fruticulosa var. robusta, commonly known as the robust wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
