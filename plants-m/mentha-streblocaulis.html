Mentha streblocaulis

Mentha streblocaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a strong, mint-like fragrance when crushed.

Mentha streblocaulis is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and respiratory benefits.
