Morus alba subvar. romana

Morus alba subvar. romana, commonly known as the Roman white mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, fuzzy berries that are edible for birds but generally not favored by humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in traditional landscaping.

Its historical significance includes use in silk production and as a food source for silkworms.
