Moraea tigridia

Moraea tigridia, commonly known as the Red-hot Poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of vibrant red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers, which resemble poker chips, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Moraea tigridia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
