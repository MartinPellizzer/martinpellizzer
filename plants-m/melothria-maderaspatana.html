Melothria maderaspatana

Melothria maderaspatana, commonly known as the Madras cucumber, is a tropical climbing vine native to southern Asia.

It produces small, edible fruits that resemble cucumbers in appearance and taste. The plant has hairy, heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its crisp, refreshing fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, sharing characteristics with other gourd plants.
