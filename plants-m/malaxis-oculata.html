Malaxis oculata

Malaxis oculata, commonly known as the eye orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that resemble eyes, with a prominent yellow callus on the lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base, supporting the flowering stalks.

Malaxis oculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
