Mephitidia acuminata

Mephitidia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed mephit, is a rare flowering plant native to the humid lowland forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture in its moist, shaded habitat. Its roots are fibrous and capable of absorbing nutrients from the rich, organic soil.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is seldom cultivated due to its specific environmental requirements.
