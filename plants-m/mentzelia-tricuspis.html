Mentzelia tricuspis

Mentzelia tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Mentzelia tricuspis produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
