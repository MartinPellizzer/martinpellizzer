Mentha subtomentella var. ceplaloniae

Mentha subtomentella var. ceplaloniae, commonly known as the Cephalonia mint, is a rare variant of the mint genus native to the Greek island of Cephalonia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. It has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
