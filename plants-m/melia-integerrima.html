Melia integerrima

Melia integerrima, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood or white sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by hard, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The wood of Melia integerrima is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and incense-making.
