Mimosopsis imparilis

Mimosopsis imparilis, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is noted for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the milkweed genus.
