Muscari ammophilum

Muscari ammophilum, commonly known as the sand muscaria, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue-violet flowers. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs that are adapted to thrive in sandy or dry soils. It typically blooms in spring and is often found in coastal or arid environments.
