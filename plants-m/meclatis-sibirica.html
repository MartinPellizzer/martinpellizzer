Meclatis sibirica

Meclatis sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian meadow grass, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It typically grows in open, moist environments such as tundra and wet meadows. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Meclatis sibirica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals in its native range.
