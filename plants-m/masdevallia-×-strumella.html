Masdevallia × strumella

Masdevallia × strumella, commonly known as the Strumella Masdevallia, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers with intricate patterns and a unique, spurred lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Masdevallia × strumella is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
