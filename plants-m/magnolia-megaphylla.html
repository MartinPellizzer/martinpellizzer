Magnolia megaphylla

Magnolia megaphylla, commonly known as the giant magnolia, is a striking evergreen tree native to the mountain forests of southern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale yellow flowers that can measure up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The leaves are broad, elliptical, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to the plant's ornamental appeal. The tree can grow to heights of 15 to 25 meters, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the biodiversity of subtropical ecosystems.
