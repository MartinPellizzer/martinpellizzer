Micranthes micranthidifolia

Micranthes micranthidifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved micranthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, greenish-white inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in wetland habitats.
