Medicago cyrenaea

Medicago cyrenaea, commonly known as the Cyprus clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and its small, yellow flowers are arranged in dense, globular heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Medicago cyrenaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
