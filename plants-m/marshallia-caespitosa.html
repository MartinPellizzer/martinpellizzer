Marshallia caespitosa

Marshallia caespitosa, commonly known as the prairie coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy, pink to purple coneflowers in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for prairie and meadow habitats. It is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.

Its botanical structure supports its role in native plant restoration and ecological landscaping.
