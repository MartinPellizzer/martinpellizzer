Mascagnia vacciniifolia

Mascagnia vacciniifolia, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Mascagnia vacciniifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to wildlife.
