Menispermum gomphioides

Menispermum gomphioides, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its roots contain compounds that have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
