Montejacquia bifida

Montejacquia bifida, commonly known as the twin-leaf cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in small, spine-tipped clusters of cylindrical or triangular pads. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, spiny structure. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, a common adaptation in arid environments to minimize water loss.

Montejacquia bifida is well adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky soils and desert regions.
