Martia polyandra

Martia polyandra, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Martia polyandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
