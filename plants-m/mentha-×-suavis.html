Mentha × suavis

Mentha × suavis, commonly known as sweet mint, is a hybrid mint species resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha viridis (ovalleaf mint).

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a sweet, pleasant scent. The plant is perennial, growing as a low, spreading herb with square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Mentha × suavis is widely cultivated for its use in culinary, medicinal, and aromatic applications.
