Manihot urens

Manihot urens, commonly known as arrowleaf or arrowhead plant, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in cyathia. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested, containing compounds like phorbol esters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is native to Central and South America.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
