Monochoria linearis

Monochoria linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as marshes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface, with roots submerged in the mud. Its reproductive structures include small, yellow flowers that appear above the water.

Monochoria linearis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
