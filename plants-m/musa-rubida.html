Musa rubida

Musa rubida, commonly known as the red banana, is a tropical plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking red-purple leaves and elongated, edible fruits that are typically smaller than those of the common banana. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The fruit, while less commonly consumed than the Cavendish banana, is appreciated for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
