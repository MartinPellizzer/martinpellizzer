Malveopsis antofagastana

Malveopsis antofagastana, commonly known as the Antofagasta mallow, is a flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, pentagonal flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
