Momordica hamiltoniana

Momordica hamiltoniana, commonly known as the Hamiltonian bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces elongated, warty fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn orange when mature. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it has distinctive yellow flowers.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for its bitter flavor and nutritional content.
