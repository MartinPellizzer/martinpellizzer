Mespilus pruinosa

Mespilus pruinosa, commonly known as the white medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by unique, fleshy fruits that ripen in late autumn. The fruits have a strong, fermented odor and are often used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
