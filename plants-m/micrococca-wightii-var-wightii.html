Micrococca wightii var. wightii

Micrococca wightii var. wightii, commonly known as the Indian snowball or snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white, fluffy flowers that resemble snowballs, which bloom in spring. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.
