Miscanthus tinctorius

Miscanthus tinctorius, commonly known as Japanese silver grass, is a tall, ornamental grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, silver-tipped leaves that create a striking visual effect in gardens. The plant produces fluffy, feathery plumes in late summer, adding movement and texture to the landscape. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a low-maintenance choice for landscaping.

Additionally, it has historical uses in textile dyeing, reflecting its traditional significance.
