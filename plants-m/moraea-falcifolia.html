Moraea falcifolia

Moraea falcifolia, commonly known as the sickleleaf gladiolus, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, funnel-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curved or sickle-shaped, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
