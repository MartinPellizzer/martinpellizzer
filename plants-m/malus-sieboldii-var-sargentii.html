Malus sieboldii var. sargentii

Malus sieboldii var. sargentii, commonly known as the Sargent apple or Japanese flowering crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, edible apples that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
