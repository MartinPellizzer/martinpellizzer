Melocactus atrosanguineus

Melocactus atrosanguineus, commonly known as the black blood cactus, is a distinctive species native to the dry regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically dark red or black in color, giving it its striking appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
