Musa acuminata subsp. banksii

Musa acuminata subsp.

banksii, commonly known as the Banks' banana, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by large, broad leaves and produces clusters of berries that are typically green when mature.

The plant is part of the Musaceae family and is closely related to other banana species. It is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its foliage rather than its fruit.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and growth habit compared to other banana varieties.
