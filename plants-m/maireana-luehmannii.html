Maireana luehmannii

Maireana luehmannii, commonly known as the Luehmann's ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for low-water gardens.

Maireana luehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
