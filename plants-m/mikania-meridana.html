Mikania meridana

Mikania meridana, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive nature, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain regions.
