Mertensia pinnata

Mertensia pinnata, commonly known as Virginia bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in early spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia pinnata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
