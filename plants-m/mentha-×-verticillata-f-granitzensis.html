Mentha × verticillata f. granitzensis

Mentha × verticillata f. granitzensis, commonly known as the Granitzens' water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It is a hybrid species within the Mentha genus, characterized by its creeping growth habit and ability to form dense mats. The plant features aromatic, lanceolate leaves and produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along water margins.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a habitat for various invertebrates and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
