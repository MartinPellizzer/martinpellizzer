Mentha longifolia var. montenegrina

Mentha longifolia var. montenegrina, commonly known as the Montenegro mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are aromatic and slightly fuzzy to the touch. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
