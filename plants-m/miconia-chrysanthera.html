Miconia chrysanthera

Miconia chrysanthera, commonly known as the golden miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Miconia chrysanthera is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and floral display.
