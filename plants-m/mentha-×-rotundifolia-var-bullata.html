Mentha × rotundifolia var. bullata

Mentha × rotundifolia var. bullata, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and has a strong, minty aroma.
