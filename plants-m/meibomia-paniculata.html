Meibomia paniculata

Meibomia paniculata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, often with a waxy texture, while its flowers are borne in paniculate inflorescences.

Meibomia paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
