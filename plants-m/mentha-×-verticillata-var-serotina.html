Mentha × verticillata var. serotina

Mentha × verticillata var. serotina, commonly known as late-fruiting water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stems, typically in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
