Melaleuca virens

Melaleuca virens, commonly known as the green melaleuca, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green. It produces small, fragrant white or yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer months.

Melaleuca virens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
