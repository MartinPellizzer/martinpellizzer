Myrciaria tapiraguayensis

Myrciaria tapiraguayensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or tapiragua tree, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America, particularly found in Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a fragrant aroma.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
