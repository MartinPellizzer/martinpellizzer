Melochia carpinifolia

Melochia carpinifolia, commonly known as the false holly or holly fern, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often palmately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Melochia carpinifolia is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
