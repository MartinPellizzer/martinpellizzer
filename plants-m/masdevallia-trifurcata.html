Masdevallia trifurcata

Masdevallia trifurcata, commonly known as the Three-branch Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often hairy leaves and compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and high humidity.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled greenhouse conditions.
