Myriophyllum verticillatum var. terrestre

Myriophyllum verticillatum var. terrestre, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in shallow water, and its leaves are whorled, typically with a dark green color and a waxy texture. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
