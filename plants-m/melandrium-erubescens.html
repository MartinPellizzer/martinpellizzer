Melandrium erubescens

Melandrium erubescens, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It has simple, alternate leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Melandrium erubescens is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
