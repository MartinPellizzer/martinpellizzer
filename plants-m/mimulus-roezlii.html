Mimulus roezlii

Mimulus roezlii, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors. It has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces elongated seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds.

Mimulus roezlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
