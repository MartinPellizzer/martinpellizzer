Mespilus tanacetifolia var. heldreichii

Mespilus tanacetifolia var. heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan medlar, is a shrub or small tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and a fragrant, yellowish-green flower cluster. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that ripen in late summer. It is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
