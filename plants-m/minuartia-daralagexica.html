Minuartia daralagexica

Minuartia daralagexica, commonly known as the Daralagexic rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. This species is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and arctic regions. It has a compact growth habit, often forming dense mats that help stabilize soil in harsh environments.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects in its native habitats.
