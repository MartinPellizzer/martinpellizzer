Mentha aquatica subvar. pluriglobula

Mentha aquatica subvar. pluriglobula, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and stream banks.

It features slender, aromatic leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its leaves and flowers are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.
