Monardella australis subsp. australis

Monardella australis subsp.

australis, commonly known as California tidy tips, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
