Myrceugenia glaucescens f. debilis

Myrceugenia glaucescens f. debilis, commonly known as the weak form of the glaucous myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and thrives in humid, warm climates. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive bluish-green hue.

It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
