Microcerasus prostrata f. brachypetala

Microcerasus prostrata f. brachypetala, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese sandthorn, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
