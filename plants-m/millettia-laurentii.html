Millettia laurentii

Millettia laurentii, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, durable wood that is highly valued for its strength and workability. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its economic importance, it is also subject to conservation concerns in its native range.
