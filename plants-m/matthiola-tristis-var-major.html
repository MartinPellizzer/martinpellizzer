Matthiola tristis var. major

Matthiola tristis var. major, commonly known as the winter flowering stock, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with lobed, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
