Memora imperatoris-maximilianii

Memora imperatoris-maximilianii, commonly known as the Emperor Maximilian's memorial plant, is a rare flowering species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and silver-veined leaves, which reflect its adaptation to cold, sunny environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is protected in several South American countries.
