Myrcia camapuana

Myrcia camapuana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Myrcia camapuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
