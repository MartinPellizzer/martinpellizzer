Myristica microcarpa

Myristica microcarpa, commonly known as the lesser nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic seeds that resemble nutmeg in appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and oblong, woody fruits that contain the aromatic seeds. The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to nutmeg.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
