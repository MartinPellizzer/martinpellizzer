Monochaetum album

Monochaetum album, commonly known as the white-flowered grass.

It is a perennial herb native to Australia, particularly found in the eastern regions. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons.

Monochaetum album is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
