Meineckia parvifolia

Meineckia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved meineckia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
