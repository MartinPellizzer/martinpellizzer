Maxillaria quelchii

Maxillaria quelchii, commonly known as the Quechua orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that bear leaves and flower spikes. Its flowers are usually fragrant, with a distinctive coloration of white, pink, and purple.

Maxillaria quelchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
