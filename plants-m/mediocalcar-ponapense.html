Mediocalcar ponapense

Mediocalcar ponapense, commonly known as the Pohnpei stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the island of Pohnpei in the Federated States of Micronesia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It grows in rocky crevices and dry soils, often forming dense clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
