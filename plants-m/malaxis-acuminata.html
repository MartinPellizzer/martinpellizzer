Malaxis acuminata

Malaxis acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in humus-rich soil. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from the surrounding environment.

Malaxis acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
