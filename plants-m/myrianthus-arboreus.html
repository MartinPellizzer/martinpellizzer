Myrianthus arboreus

Myrianthus arboreus, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

Myrianthus arboreus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
