Melica longiligulata

Melica longiligulata, commonly known as long-ligule grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairless. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, two-lobed flowers. It is native to North America and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Melica longiligulata is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
