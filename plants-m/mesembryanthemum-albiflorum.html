Mesembryanthemum albiflorum

Mesembryanthemum albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

Mesembryanthemum albiflorum is also known for its ability to store water in its leaves, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
