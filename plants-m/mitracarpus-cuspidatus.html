Mitracarpus cuspidatus

Mitracarpus cuspidatus, commonly known as the African crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features erect, branching stems with narrow, hairy leaves. Its showy, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in well-drained soils.
