Mikania rusbyi

Mikania rusbyi, commonly known as the creeping Mikania, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, fuzzy fruits. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forests.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
