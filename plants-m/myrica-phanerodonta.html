Myrica phanerodonta

Myrica phanerodonta, commonly known as the Hawaiian mahoe, is a small evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Myricaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Myrica phanerodonta plays an important ecological role in Hawaiian ecosystems, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
