Malortiea latisecta

Malortiea latisecta, commonly known as the broad-leafed mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Malortiea latisecta is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies.
