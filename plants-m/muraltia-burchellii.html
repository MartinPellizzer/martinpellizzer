Muraltia burchellii

Muraltia burchellii, commonly known as the African sweet pea, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Muraltia burchellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
