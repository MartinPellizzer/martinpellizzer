Myrcia pohliana

Myrcia pohliana, commonly known as the Pohliana Myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, while its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

Myrcia pohliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
