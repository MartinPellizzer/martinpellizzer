Mentha arvensis f. parietariifolia-obtusifolia

Mentha arvensis f. parietariifolia-obtusifolia, commonly known as the wall mint or broad-leaved mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, square stems and opposite, ovate to broadly ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found growing along walls, banks, and in shaded woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
