Myrtus leucomyrtus

Myrtus leucomyrtus, commonly known as white myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by edible, white to pink berries. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and hedging.

Myrtus leucomyrtus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
