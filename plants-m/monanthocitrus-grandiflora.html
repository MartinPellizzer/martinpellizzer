Monanthocitrus grandiflora

Monanthocitrus grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered citron tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant yellow flowers and elliptical, glossy green leaves. The plant produces a citrus-like fruit with a thick, aromatic rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
