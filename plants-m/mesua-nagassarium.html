Mesua nagassarium

Mesua nagassarium, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
