Myagrum bauhini

Myagrum bauhini, commonly known as the Australian bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and glossy, while its flowers are predominantly red or pink.

Myagrum bauhini thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.
