Machaonia martineziorum

Machaonia martineziorum, commonly known as the Martinez's machaonia, is a shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and often pink or white, appearing in clusters during the spring.

Machaonia martineziorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
