Mammillaria tepexicensis

Mammillaria tepexicensis, commonly known as the Tepexi mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and full sun to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
