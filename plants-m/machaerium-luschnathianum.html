Machaerium luschnathianum

Machaerium luschnathianum, commonly known as the Luschnathia grass, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow to green flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Machaerium luschnathianum is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in agricultural settings.
