Myrcia neesiana

Myrcia neesiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Myrcia neesiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
