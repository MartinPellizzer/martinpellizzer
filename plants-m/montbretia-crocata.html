Montbretia crocata

Montbretia crocata, commonly known as the Natal yellow daisy, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-shaped leaves and produces tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Montbretia crocata is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread easily in suitable climates.
