Monticalia myrsinites

Monticalia myrsinites, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees in the Neotropical region.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It is adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments and relies on its host for nutrients and water.

Due to its parasitic nature and specialized growth habits, it plays a unique role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
