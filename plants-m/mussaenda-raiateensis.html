Mussaenda raiateensis

Mussaenda raiateensis, commonly known as the Tahitian flame bush, is a flowering plant native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with a leathery texture. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of bright orange to red flowers, which are borne on long, slender peduncles. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly waxy surface.

Mussaenda raiateensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
