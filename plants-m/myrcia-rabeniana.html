Myrcia rabeniana

Myrcia rabeniana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

Myrcia rabeniana is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
