Miconia imbricata

Miconia imbricata, commonly known as the Brazilian shield-leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia imbricata is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking foliage.
