Machaerium acuminatum var. latifolium

Machaerium acuminatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf machaerium, is a tropical plant native to regions of South and Central America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad and trifoliate, with each leaflet being lanceolate in shape.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, often in dense inflorescences, and can range in color from white to pale yellow.
