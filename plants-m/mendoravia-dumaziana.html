Mendoravia dumaziana

Mendoravia dumaziana, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. Native to high-altitude regions of South America, this species thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
