Masdevallia atropurpurea

Masdevallia atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-tipped masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet and purple flower spikes, which often feature intricate patterns and a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

This orchid prefers cool, humid conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in specialized gardens.
