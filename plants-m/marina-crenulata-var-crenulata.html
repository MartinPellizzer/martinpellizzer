Marina crenulata var. crenulata

Marina crenulata var. crenulata, commonly known as the coastal stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water and withstand salty air. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it ideal for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners in arid and coastal climates.
