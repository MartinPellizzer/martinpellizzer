Melaleuca eremaea

Melaleuca eremaea, commonly known as the sturt's melaleuca, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bottlebrush-like flowers that are typically white or pink. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought. It is often found in sandy soils and is part of the Myrtaceae family.

Melaleuca eremaea plays a role in providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
