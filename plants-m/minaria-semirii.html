Minaria semirii

Minaria semirii, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Minaria semirii is often found in desert steppes and is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal.
