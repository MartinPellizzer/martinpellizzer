Monanthos vinosus

Monanthos vinosus, commonly known as the purple tawny grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, pale green to pinkish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Monanthos vinosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
