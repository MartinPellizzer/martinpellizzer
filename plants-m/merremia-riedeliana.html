Merremia riedeliana

Merremia riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as forest edges and riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it both a popular garden plant and a potential invasive species in some areas.
