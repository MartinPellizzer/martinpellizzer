Masdevallia × henrietta

Masdevallia × henrietta, commonly known as Henrietta's Masdevallia, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Masdevallia leucopilosa and Masdevallia tenuifolia.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, humid environments with high altitudes. The plant features intricate, often colorful flowers with a unique, spurred lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate beauty and resilience in controlled environments.
