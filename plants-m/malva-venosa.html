Malva venosa

Malva venosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture, and it produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its robust growth and is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.
