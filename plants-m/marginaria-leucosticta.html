Marginaria leucosticta

Marginaria leucosticta, commonly known as the white-streaked bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a distinctive white stripe down the center. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Marginaria leucosticta is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
