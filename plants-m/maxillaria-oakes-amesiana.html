Maxillaria oakes-amesiana

Maxillaria oakes-amesiana, commonly known as the Oakes-amesian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow to white blooms with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maxillaria oakes-amesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
