Marsdenia truncata

Marsdenia truncata, commonly known as the truncated Marsdenia, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a truncate or notched base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red berries.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
