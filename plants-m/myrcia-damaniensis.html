Myrcia damaniensis

Myrcia damaniensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically orange-red in color.

Myrcia damaniensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
