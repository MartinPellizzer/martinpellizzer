Malpighia nummulariifolia var. pusillifolia

Malpighia nummulariifolia var. pusillifolia, commonly known as the Mexican snowberry, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, rounded foliage. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit and foliage.
