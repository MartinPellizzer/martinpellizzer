Myrtus erythroxyloides

Myrtus erythroxyloides, commonly known as the redwood myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the humid regions of Southeast Asia, particularly parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, small, evergreen leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature.

Myrtus erythroxyloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
