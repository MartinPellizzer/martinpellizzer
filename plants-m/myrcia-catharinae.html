Myrcia catharinae

Myrcia catharinae, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Myrcia catharinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
