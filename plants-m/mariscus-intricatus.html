Mariscus intricatus

Mariscus intricatus, commonly known as the intricate marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to specific wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and its unique, intricate floral structure that resembles a complex, woven pattern. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of shallow water bodies.

Due to habitat loss, Mariscus intricatus is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
