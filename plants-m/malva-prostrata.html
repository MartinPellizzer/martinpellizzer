Malva prostrata

Malva prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate mallows, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features spreading stems that often lie flat on the ground, covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals, typically appearing in summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, velvety hairs, contributing to its tactile appeal.

Malva prostrata is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
