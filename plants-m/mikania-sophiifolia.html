Mikania sophiifolia

Mikania sophiifolia, commonly known as the slender antelope's horn, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Mikania sophiifolia is typically found in moist, shaded environments like forest understories.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural contexts.
