Mussaenda tenuiflora var. thomensis

Mussaenda tenuiflora var. thomensis, commonly known as the Thomas's Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical climates.
