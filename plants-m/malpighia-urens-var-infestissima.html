Malpighia urens var. infestissima

Malpighia urens var. infestissima, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple berries that appear in clusters, which are edible and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
