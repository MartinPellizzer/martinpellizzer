Monsonia drudeana

Monsonia drudeana, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Aizoaceae family, characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves and produces spikes of bright, showy bracts that resemble poker chips, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Monsonia drudeana is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
