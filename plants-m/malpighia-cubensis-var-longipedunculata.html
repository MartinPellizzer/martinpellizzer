Malpighia cubensis var. longipedunculata

Malpighia cubensis var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the red mombin, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright red, fleshy fruits that resemble small, rounded tomatoes. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, which are rich in vitamin C.
