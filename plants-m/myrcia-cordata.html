Myrcia cordata

Myrcia cordata, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Myrcia cordata is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
