Mentha × hercynica

Mentha × hercynica, commonly known as the German mint, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and fragrant, serrated foliage. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stem. Mentha × hercynica is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
