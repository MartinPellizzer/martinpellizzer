Mimosa campicola var. campicola

Mimosa campicola var. campicola, commonly known as the Brazilian sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
