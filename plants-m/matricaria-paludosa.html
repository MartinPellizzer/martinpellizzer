Matricaria paludosa

Matricaria paludosa, commonly known as swamp chamomile, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It produces small, yellow flower heads that are surrounded by white ray florets, resembling those of true chamomile. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Matricaria paludosa plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
