Myrcia lingua var. rufa

Myrcia lingua var. rufa, commonly known as the red myrcia, is a shrub native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, red-orange flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are aromatic and contain essential oils, contributing to its use in perfumes and herbal remedies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
