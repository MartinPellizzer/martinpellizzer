Mussaenda rufa

Mussaenda rufa, commonly known as the red musaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its bright red bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers, and its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Mussaenda rufa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
