Melocactus lemairei

Melocactus lemairei, commonly known as the Lemaire's melon cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is often ribbed and covered with a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.

Melocactus lemairei is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stem to survive prolonged dry periods.
