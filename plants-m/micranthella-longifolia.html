Micranthella longifolia

Micranthella longifolia, commonly known as the slender-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Micranthella longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
