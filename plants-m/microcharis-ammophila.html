Microcharis ammophila

Microcharis ammophila, commonly known as the sandhill morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or coastal environments. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue or white in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Microcharis ammophila is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in dune or beach habitats.
