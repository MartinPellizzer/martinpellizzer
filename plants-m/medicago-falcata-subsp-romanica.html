Medicago falcata subsp. romanica

Medicago falcata subsp.

romanica, commonly known as the Roman horseshoe vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found growing in dry, rocky soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock.

Its subspecies status highlights its genetic distinction from other Medicago species.
