Mimetes intermedius

Mimetes intermedius, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Mimetes intermedius is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
