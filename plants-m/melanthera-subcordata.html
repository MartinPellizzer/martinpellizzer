Melanthera subcordata

Melanthera subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces purple to pinkish flower heads in late summer. Its flowering period typically occurs from July to September, attracting various pollinators.

Melanthera subcordata is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade.
