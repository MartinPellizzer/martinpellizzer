Myoxanthus simplicicaulis

Myoxanthus simplicicaulis, commonly known as the simple-stemmed yellow daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and greenish-yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a compact, low-growing habit with linear, grass-like leaves.

Myoxanthus simplicicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
