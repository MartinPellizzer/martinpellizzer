Mammillaria zacatecasensis

Mammillaria zacatecasensis, commonly known as the Zacatecas mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the central regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white to grayish woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
