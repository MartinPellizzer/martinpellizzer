Morus rubra var. incisa

Morus rubra var. incisa, commonly known as the red mulberry, is a variant of the mulberry tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves, which have a serrated margin and a reddish tinge when young. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, typically in early summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source for wildlife.
