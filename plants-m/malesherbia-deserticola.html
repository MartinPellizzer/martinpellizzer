Malesherbia deserticola

Malesherbia deserticola, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and broad, oval-shaped leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Malesherbia deserticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
