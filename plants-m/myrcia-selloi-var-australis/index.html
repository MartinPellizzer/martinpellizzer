Myrcia Selloi Var. Australis

Myrcia selloi var. australis is a rare and unique plant native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known species like eucalyptus and guava. This variant is distinguished by its compact growth habit and distinctive reddish-brown leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments. Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
