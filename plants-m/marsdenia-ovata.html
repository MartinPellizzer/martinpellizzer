Marsdenia ovata

Marsdenia ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are often fragrant. Its succulent stems and leaves make it drought-tolerant, and it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Marsdenia ovata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential pharmacological properties.
