Melastoma chevalieri

Melastoma chevalieri, commonly known as the chevalier's melastoma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically showy, with five petals that are often pink or red in color.

Melastoma chevalieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
