Melastoma grossularioides

Melastoma grossularioides, commonly known as the roughleaf melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and usually have five petals.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
