Myriopus petionvillae

Myriopus petionvillae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myriopus, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Myriopus petionvillae is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
