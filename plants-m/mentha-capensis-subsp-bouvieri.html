Mentha capensis subsp. bouvieri

Mentha capensis subsp.

bouvieri, commonly known as Cape spearmint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, slender leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its strong mint flavor and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct genetic and morphological traits compared to other Mentha species.
