Mariscus vaginalis

Mariscus vaginalis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, and often blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and the plant is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Mariscus vaginalis is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
