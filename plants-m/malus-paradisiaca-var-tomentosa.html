Malus paradisiaca var. tomentosa

Malus paradisiaca var. tomentosa, commonly known as the sand pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible pears that are typically small and sweet. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and fruit production in suitable climates.
