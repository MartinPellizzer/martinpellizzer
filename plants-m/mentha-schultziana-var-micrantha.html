Mentha schultziana var. micrantha

Mentha schultziana var. micrantha, commonly known as narrow-leaved catnip, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a minty scent and making it useful in herbal remedies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
