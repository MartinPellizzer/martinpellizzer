Malva × decipiens

Malva × decipiens, commonly known as the common mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–90 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in many regions worldwide.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
