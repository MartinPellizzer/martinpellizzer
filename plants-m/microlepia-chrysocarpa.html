Microlepia chrysocarpa

Microlepia chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden-fruited shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish spore cases known as sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
