Manihot harmsiana

Manihot harmsiana, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus Manihot. Its tuberous roots are edible and are used as a starch source in various cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
