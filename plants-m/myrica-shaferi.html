Myrica shaferi

Myrica shaferi, commonly known as the Shafer's wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark, waxy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Myrica shaferi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
