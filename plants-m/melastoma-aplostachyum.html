Melastoma aplostachyum

Melastoma aplostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
