Minuartia montana subsp. wiesneri

Minuartia montana subsp.

wiesneri, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It is adapted to cold climates and can survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
