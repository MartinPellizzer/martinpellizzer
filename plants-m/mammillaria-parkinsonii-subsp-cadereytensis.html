Mammillaria parkinsonii subsp. cadereytensis

Mammillaria parkinsonii subsp.

cadereytensis, commonly known as the Cadereyta mammillaria, is a small cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
