Myristica simiarum

Myristica simiarum, commonly known as the "monkey nut" or "binturong nut," is a species of tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown seeds encased in a fleshy, yellowish aril. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruits are often harvested for their seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
