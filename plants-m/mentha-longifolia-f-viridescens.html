Mentha longifolia f. viridescens

Mentha longifolia f. viridescens, commonly known as green mint or creeping mint, is a variant of the hedge mint species.

It is characterized by its slender, prostrate growth habit and serrated leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, which appear in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
