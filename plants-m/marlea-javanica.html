Marlea javanica

Marlea javanica, commonly known as the Java marigold, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Marlea javanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
