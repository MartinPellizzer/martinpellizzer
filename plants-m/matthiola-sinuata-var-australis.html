Matthiola sinuata var. australis

Matthiola sinuata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It has a compact growth habit with small, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is adapted to a variety of soil types and prefers full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardens.
