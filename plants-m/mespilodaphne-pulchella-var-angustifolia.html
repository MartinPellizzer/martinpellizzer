Mespilodaphne pulchella var. angustifolia

Mespilodaphne pulchella var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mespilodaphne, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
