Maxillaria lipinskae

Maxillaria lipinskae, commonly known as the Lipinska's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display a combination of yellow, orange, and red hues. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants rather than in soil.

Maxillaria lipinskae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
