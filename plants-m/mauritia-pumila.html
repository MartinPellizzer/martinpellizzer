Mauritia pumila

Mauritia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf palm, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically producing yellow or white blooms.

Mauritia pumila is well adapted to dry and disturbed environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
