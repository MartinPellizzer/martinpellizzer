Milium holciforme var. sphacelatum

Milium holciforme var. sphacelatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 inch wide, arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in spike-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
