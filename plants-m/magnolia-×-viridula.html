Magnolia × viridula

Magnolia × viridula, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia or the evergreen magnolia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Magnolia denudata and Magnolia liliiflora.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a subtle fragrance. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Magnolia × viridula is relatively hardy and thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
