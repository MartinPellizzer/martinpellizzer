Mimosa houstonii

Mimosa houstonii, commonly known as the Houston mimosette, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
