Mammillaria hirsuta

Mammillaria hirsuta, commonly known as the woolly mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
