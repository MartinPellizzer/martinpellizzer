Mimosa carbonalis

Mimosa carbonalis, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Mimosa carbonalis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
