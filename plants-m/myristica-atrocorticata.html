Myristica atrocorticata

Myristica atrocorticata, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and pungent, spiced fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into bright red berries containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth, glossy surface.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional remedies for its aromatic and anti-inflammatory effects.
