Mikania hirsutissima var. margarethiae

Mikania hirsutissima var. margarethiae, commonly known as the purple mikania, is a variant of the hairy Mikania species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a vigorous climber with opposite, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it both a notable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain environments.
