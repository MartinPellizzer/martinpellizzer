Myrtus eusebii

Myrtus eusebii, commonly known as the Eusebius myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves with a fragrant, slightly oily texture. Its small white flowers bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

Myrtus eusebii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
