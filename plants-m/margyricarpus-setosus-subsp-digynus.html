Margyricarpus setosus subsp. digynus

Margyricarpus setosus subsp.

digynus, commonly known as the spiny margyricarpus, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, yellowish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a follicle containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
