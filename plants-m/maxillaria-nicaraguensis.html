Maxillaria nicaraguensis

Maxillaria nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaragua orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers, which are typically fragrant and come in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are borne on racemes and are often noted for their intricate petal structures.

Maxillaria nicaraguensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
