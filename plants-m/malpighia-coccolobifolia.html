Malpighia coccolobifolia

Malpighia coccolobifolia, commonly known as the scarlet gooseberry, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a glossy surface and a serrated margin. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered, producing a distinctive, sweet-smelling fragrance.
