Markea venosa

Markea venosa, commonly known as the veined markea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, veined leaves that range in color from deep green to dark purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique foliage and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
