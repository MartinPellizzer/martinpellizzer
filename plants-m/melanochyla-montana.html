Melanochyla montana

Melanochyla montana, commonly known as the mountain blackberry, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has woody stems and broad, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is adapted to harsh, mountainous environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
