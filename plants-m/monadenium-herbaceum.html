Monadenium herbaceum

Monadenium herbaceum, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous annual that thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Monadenium herbaceum is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
