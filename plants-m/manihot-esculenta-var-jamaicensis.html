Manihot esculenta var. jamaicensis

Manihot esculenta var. jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican cassava, is a variant of the cassava plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous perennial shrub with a woody root system that produces edible tubers. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green flowers that develop into capsule fruits. Its roots are rich in starch and are a staple food source in many Caribbean communities.

The plant is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in tropical climates.
