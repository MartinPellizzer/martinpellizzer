Mucuna rutilans

Mucuna rutilans, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It produces distinctive yellow flowers and elongated, hairy pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth makes it a valuable cover crop and forage plant in agricultural systems.
