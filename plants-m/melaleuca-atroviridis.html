Melaleuca atroviridis

Melaleuca atroviridis, commonly known as the black-leaved tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in winter. It is a shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 5 meters.

Melaleuca atroviridis is known for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and for their antimicrobial properties.
