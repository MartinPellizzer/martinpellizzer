Magnolia villariana

Magnolia villariana, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Magnolia villariana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
