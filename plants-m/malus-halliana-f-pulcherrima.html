Malus halliana f. pulcherrima

Malus halliana f. pulcherrima, commonly known as the pink-flowered crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree known for its ornamental value.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink or red flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and jellies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is popular in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
