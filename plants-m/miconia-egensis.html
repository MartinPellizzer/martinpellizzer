Miconia egensis

Miconia egensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, showy pink or red flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and contain numerous seeds.

Miconia egensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
