Malus transitoria var. setok

Malus transitoria var. setok, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds.

It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
