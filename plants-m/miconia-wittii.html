Miconia wittii

Miconia wittii, commonly known as the Brazilian star-apple tree, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits. Miconia wittii is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its competitive nature.
