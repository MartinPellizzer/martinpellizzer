Mimulus viscidus

Mimulus viscidus, commonly known as the sticky monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and vibrant orange or yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mimulus viscidus is often found in California and parts of Oregon, where it thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.
