Matricaria japonica

Matricaria japonica, commonly known as Japanese chamomile, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers is often used in aromatherapy and skincare products.
