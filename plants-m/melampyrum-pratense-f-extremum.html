Melampyrum pratense f. extremum

Melampyrum pratense f. extremum, commonly known as the extreme meadow touchroot, is a variant of the common touchroot plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its flowers are small, yellowish-orange, and arranged in loose clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family.

This form is notable for its distinct morphological characteristics compared to the typical form of Melampyrum pratense.
