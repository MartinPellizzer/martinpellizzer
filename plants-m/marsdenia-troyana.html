Marsdenia troyana

Marsdenia troyana, commonly known as the Troyan Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent corona, making it visually striking.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
