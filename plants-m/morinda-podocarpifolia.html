Morinda podocarpifolia

Morinda podocarpifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy underside. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale yellow, followed by bright orange or red fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
