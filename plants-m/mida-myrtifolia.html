Mida myrtifolia

Mida myrtifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white powder, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round, blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Mida myrtifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
