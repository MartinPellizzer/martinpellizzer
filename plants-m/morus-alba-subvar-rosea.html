Morus alba subvar. rosea

Morus alba subvar. rosea, commonly known as the pink mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinkish-white leaves, which often have a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The subvariety is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of silk production.
