Manihot brachyandra

Manihot brachyandra, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowleaf, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
