Maxillaria madida var. pallida

Maxillaria madida var. pallida, commonly known as the pale madida orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with elongated pseudobulbs and linear leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown streaks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
