Mentha gentilis f. scandica

Mentha gentilis f. scandica, commonly known as the Scandinavian mint, is a subspecies of the mint plant native to northern Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and square, hairy stems. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds that contribute to its cooling and soothing properties.
