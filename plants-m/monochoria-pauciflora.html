Monochoria pauciflora

Monochoria pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It has small, pale blue to white flowers that are arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are typically rounded with a waxy coating. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. It has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in muddy substrates.

Monochoria pauciflora is known for its ability to absorb heavy metals from water, making it useful in phytoremediation efforts.
