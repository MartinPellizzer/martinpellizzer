Moenchia sativa

Moenchia sativa, commonly known as common brome, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-bearing panicle, which produces small, two-lobed flowers.

Moenchia sativa is known for its ecological importance, providing forage for livestock and habitat for various insects.
