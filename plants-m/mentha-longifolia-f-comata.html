Mentha longifolia f. comata

Mentha longifolia f. comata, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and slender, aromatic stems. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
