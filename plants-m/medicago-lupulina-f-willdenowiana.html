Medicago lupulina f. willdenowiana

Medicago lupulina f. willdenowiana, commonly known as the Willdenow's sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a deep root system that helps it tolerate drought conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
