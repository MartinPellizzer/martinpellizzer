Myrcia monteverdensis

Myrcia monteverdensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
