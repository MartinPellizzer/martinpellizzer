Myrtus communis var. baui

Myrtus communis var. baui, commonly known as the Baui myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white, fragrant flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which releases a sweet, spicy scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
