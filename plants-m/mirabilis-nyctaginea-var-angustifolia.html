Mirabilis nyctaginea var. angustifolia

Mirabilis nyctaginea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf four o'clock, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and showy, tubular flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces vibrant pink to red blooms, which attract pollinators such as moths and bats. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value make it a noteworthy addition to botanical collections.
