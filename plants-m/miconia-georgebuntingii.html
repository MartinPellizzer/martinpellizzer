Miconia georgebuntingii

Miconia georgebuntingii, commonly known as the George Bunting miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically dispersed by birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
