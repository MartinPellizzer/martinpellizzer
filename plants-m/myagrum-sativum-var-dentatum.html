Myagrum sativum var. dentatum

Myagrum sativum var. dentatum, commonly known as the rough soursop, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The fruit is typically pear-shaped with a rough, warty surface and a strong, unpleasant odor. It produces edible, seed-containing pulp that is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
