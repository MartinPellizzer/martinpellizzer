Mirabilis bracteosa

Mirabilis bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its velvety, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.
