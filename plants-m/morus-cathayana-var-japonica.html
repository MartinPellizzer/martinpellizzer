Morus cathayana var. japonica

Morus cathayana var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by fuzzy, orange-red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and historical significance in traditional medicine.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
