Muscari azureum

Muscari azureum, commonly known as the Persian bluebell, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that help the plant survive harsh winter conditions.

Muscari azureum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
