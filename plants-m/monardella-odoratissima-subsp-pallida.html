Monardella odoratissima subsp. pallida

Monardella odoratissima subsp.

pallida, commonly known as white horsemint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pale purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems.

The subspecies pallida is distinguished by its lighter coloration compared to other varieties within the species.
