Medicago brachyacantha

Medicago brachyacantha, commonly known as the short-stalked clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is native to parts of Africa and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Medicago brachyacantha is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
