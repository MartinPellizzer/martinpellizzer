Magnolia × pruhoniciana

Magnolia × pruhoniciana, commonly known as the Puhoniciana magnolia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Magnolia denudata and Magnolia liliiflora.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant is native to China and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens. It typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, glossy surface, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
