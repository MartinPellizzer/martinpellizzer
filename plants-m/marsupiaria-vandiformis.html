Marsupiaria vandiformis

Marsupiaria vandiformis, commonly known as the Vandifolium fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on wind pollination.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
