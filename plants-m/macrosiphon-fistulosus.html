Macrosiphon fistulosus

Macrosiphon fistulosus, commonly known as the slender pipe-root, is a unique plant characterized by its elongated, tubular roots and fibrous structure.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant's botanical features include its distinctive root system, which can extend several meters underground, and its ability to form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while its flowering stems produce small, clustered blooms.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in soil stabilization.
