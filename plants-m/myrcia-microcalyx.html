Myrcia microcalyx

Myrcia microcalyx, commonly known as the small-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
