Mariscus subuniflorus

Mariscus subuniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, showy pink or purple flower at the top of each stem, which blooms during the late spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a valuable species for wetland conservation efforts.
