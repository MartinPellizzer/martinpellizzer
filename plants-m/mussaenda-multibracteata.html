Mussaenda multibracteata

Mussaenda multibracteata, commonly known as the golden bell plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for small garden spaces.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
