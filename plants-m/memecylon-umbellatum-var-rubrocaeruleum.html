Memecylon umbellatum var. rubrocaeruleum

Memecylon umbellatum var. rubrocaeruleum, commonly known as the red-blue memecylon, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly glossy texture. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique floral structure and color variation in its inflorescences.
