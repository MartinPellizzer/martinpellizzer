Malva sylvestris var. ambigua

Malva sylvestris var. ambigua, commonly known as the common mallow or woolly mallow, is a flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It often grows in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Its hairy stems and foliage give it a distinctive, soft-textured appearance.
