Mespilus arbutifolia var. virginiana

Mespilus arbutifolia var. virginiana, commonly known as the American persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by orange to reddish fruit that ripens in the fall. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
