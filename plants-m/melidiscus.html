Melidiscus

Melidiscus, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to various regions in the Mediterranean and parts of Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, often with a silvery or grayish underside, giving it its common name. Melidiscus species typically produce small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and have been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
