Munrochloa ritchiei

Munrochloa ritchiei, commonly known as the New Zealand hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Munrochloa ritchiei plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
