Magnolia carsonii var. phaulanta

Magnolia carsonii var. phaulanta, commonly known as the Carson magnolia, is a rare and striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a subtle red or purple tinge. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, preceding the emergence of its foliage.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
