Muraltia uroclada

Muraltia uroclada, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitanga*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing a single seed.

Muraltia uroclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits, though they are not widely consumed outside of local regions.
