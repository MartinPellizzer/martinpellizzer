Mapania secans

Mapania secans, commonly known as the brittlestem plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often woody stems that can become brittle when dried, hence its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or white in color. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged alternately along the stem.

Mapania secans is often found in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its specialized morphology.
