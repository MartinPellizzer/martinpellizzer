Myrtus daphnoides

Myrtus daphnoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pinkish flowers. It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrtus daphnoides is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
