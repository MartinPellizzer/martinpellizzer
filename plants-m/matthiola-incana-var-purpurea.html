Matthiola incana var. purpurea

Matthiola incana var. purpurea, commonly known as the Purple Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often grayish-green in color, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens throughout the growing season.
