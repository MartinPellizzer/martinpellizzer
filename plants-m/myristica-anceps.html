Myristica anceps

Myristica anceps, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval-shaped fruits containing the valuable seeds used for nutmeg and mace.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a source of essential spices and its ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
