Micranthocereus polyanthus subsp. alvinii

Micranthocereus polyanthus subsp.

alvinii, commonly known as the Alvin's night bloomer, is a cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and spiny, ribbed structure.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique blooming habit and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant among cactus enthusiasts.
