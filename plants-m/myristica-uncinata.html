Myristica uncinata

Myristica uncinata, commonly known as the hooked myristica, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen tree structure with glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive reddish-brown fruits containing aromatic seeds. Myristica uncinata is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
