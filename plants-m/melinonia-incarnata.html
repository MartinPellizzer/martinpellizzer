Melinonia incarnata

Melinonia incarnata, commonly known as the red-flowered melinonia, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red petals that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Melinonia incarnata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
