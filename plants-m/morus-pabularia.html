Morus pabularia

Morus pabularia, commonly known as the field mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the common mulberry and fig. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically lobed and have a rough texture. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually green or red in color, containing numerous tiny seeds.

Morus pabularia is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife.
