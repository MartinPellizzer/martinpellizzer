Matthiola runcinata

Matthiola runcinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. Its leaves are narrow and linear, typically with a slightly hairy texture.

Matthiola runcinata is often found growing in rocky or disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
