Maximowiczia sonorae var. brevicaulis

Maximowiczia sonorae var. brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed desert grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
