Maxillaria houtteii

Maxillaria houtteii, commonly known as the Houtte's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, with flowers that have a distinctive, velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maxillaria houtteii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
