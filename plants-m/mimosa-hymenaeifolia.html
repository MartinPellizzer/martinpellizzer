Mimosa hymenaeifolia

Mimosa hymenaeifolia, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, pale brown pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
