Moraea lewisiae

Moraea lewisiae, commonly known as the Lewisia's Moraea, is a South African plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically blooms in spring and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils. Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
