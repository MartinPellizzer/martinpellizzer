Myrcia amethystina var. pulchra

Myrcia amethystina var. pulchra, commonly known as the purple myrtle, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark purple to almost black berries. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments.
