Masdevallia burbidgeana

Masdevallia burbidgeana, commonly known as the Burbidge's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flower spikes that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves and produces small, delicate flowers arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high light exposure but requires careful watering to avoid root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and striking appearance.
