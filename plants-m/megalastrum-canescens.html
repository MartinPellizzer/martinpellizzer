Megalastrum canescens

Megalastrum canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is considered a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
