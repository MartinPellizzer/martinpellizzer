Minuartia verna var. hirta

Minuartia verna var. hirta, commonly known as the spring rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in alpine or arctic environments. Its leaves are generally linear to oblanceolate, and the flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

This variety is notable for its ability to survive in harsh, cold climates and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in suitable conditions.
