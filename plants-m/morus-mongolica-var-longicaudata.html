Morus mongolica var. longicaudata

Morus mongolica var. longicaudata, commonly known as the Mongolian mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in catkins, followed by edible, fuzzy berries that are important for wildlife and traditional uses. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its hardiness and has been used in ecological restoration projects.
