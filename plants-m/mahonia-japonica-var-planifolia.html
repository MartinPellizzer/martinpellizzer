Mahonia japonica var. planifolia

Mahonia japonica var. planifolia, commonly known as the Japanese mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. These flowers are followed by blue-black berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
