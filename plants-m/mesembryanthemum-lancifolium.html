Mesembryanthemum lancifolium

Mesembryanthemum lancifolium, commonly known as the spearleaf ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments.
