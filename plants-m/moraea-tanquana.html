Moraea tanquana

Moraea tanquana, commonly known as the Tanqua Moraea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often purple or pink, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves, and its flowering stems rise above the foliage.
