Myrcia subcordata

Myrcia subcordata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a glossy surface and a slightly aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological and botanical significance in its native habitat.
