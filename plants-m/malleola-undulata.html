Malleola undulata

Malleola undulata, commonly known as the wavy malleola, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive undulating leaf structure.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, wavy-edged leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowering period occurs during the rainy season, producing small, pale yellow blooms.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Malleola undulata is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
