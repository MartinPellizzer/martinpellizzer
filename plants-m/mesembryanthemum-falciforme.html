Mesembryanthemum falciforme

Mesembryanthemum falciforme, commonly known as the sickleleaf ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
