Madhuca kanchanaburiensis

Madhuca kanchanaburiensis, commonly known as the Kanchanaburi tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical fruits containing a single, hard, black seed.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
