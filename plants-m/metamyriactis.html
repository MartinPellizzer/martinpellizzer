Metamyriactis

Metamyriactis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its delicate, white, orchid-like flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Metamyriactis has a unique ability to survive in low-light conditions, which is essential for its survival in the dense rainforest canopy.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
