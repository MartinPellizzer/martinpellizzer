Mimosa lasiocarpa

Mimosa lasiocarpa, commonly known as the fuzzy mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

Mimosa lasiocarpa is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
