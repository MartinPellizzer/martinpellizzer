Mimulus moschatus var. longiflorus

Mimulus moschatus var. longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, orange-yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred petal, which is a key botanical trait for attracting pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall appearance.

It is valued in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
