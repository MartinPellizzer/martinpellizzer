Millettia foliolosa

Millettia foliolosa, commonly known as the leafy millettia, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, elliptical leaflets that are arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
