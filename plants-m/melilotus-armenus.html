Melilotus armenus

Melilotus armenus, commonly known as sweet clover, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
