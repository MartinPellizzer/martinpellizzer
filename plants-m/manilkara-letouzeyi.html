Manilkara letouzeyi

Manilkara letouzeyi, commonly known as the African manioc tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its latex is milky and has been traditionally used for various purposes, including as a natural rubber substitute.

The plant is valued for its ecological role and potential economic applications in its native regions.
