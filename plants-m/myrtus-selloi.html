Myrtus selloi

Myrtus selloi, commonly known as Sello's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the island of Corsica and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh Mediterranean climate. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Myrtus selloi is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with a compact and dense growth habit.
