Melo

Melo, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and watermelons.

It is a climbing vine that produces a spherical, ribbed fruit with a pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic orange flesh. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom before the fruit develops. Melo thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in various culinary applications.
