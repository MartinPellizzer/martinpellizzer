Moraea tricuspis var. ocellata

Moraea tricuspis var. ocellata, commonly known as the spotted moraea, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple with a distinctive yellow or orange spot near the base, giving it its common name. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
