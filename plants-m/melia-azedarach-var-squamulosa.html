Melia azedarach var. squamulosa

Melia azedarach var. squamulosa, commonly known as the Chinese catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white or pink flowers that appear in late spring. The tree produces long, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release fluffy seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a moderate growth rate.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
