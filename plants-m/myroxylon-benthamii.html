Myroxylon benthamii

Myroxylon benthamii, commonly known as the caoutchouc tree, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish flowers. The tree is notable for its latex-rich bark, which is tapped to produce natural rubber. Its wood is durable and has been historically used for various purposes, including furniture and musical instruments.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats and is also valued for its economic contributions.
