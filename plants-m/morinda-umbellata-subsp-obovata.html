Morinda umbellata subsp. obovata

Morinda umbellata subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate morinda, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often obovate-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
