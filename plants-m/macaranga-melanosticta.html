Macaranga melanosticta

Macaranga melanosticta, commonly known as the black-stained mallow, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinctive dark green to blackish coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a feature common in the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Macaranga melanosticta is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
