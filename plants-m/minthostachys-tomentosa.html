Minthostachys tomentosa

Minthostachys tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet mint, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters.

The aromatic properties of its leaves make it popular in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
