Morus alba var. papyracea

Morus alba var. papyracea, commonly known as the paper mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, fibrous bark that is used to produce paper, hence its common name. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers followed by dark berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fibrous bark and as a source of food for silkworms.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
