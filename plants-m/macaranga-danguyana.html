Macaranga danguyana

Macaranga danguyana, commonly known as the giant paper mulberry, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its fast growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
