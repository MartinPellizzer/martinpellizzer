Melicope novoguineensis

Melicope novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea melicope, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in axillary inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has traditional uses in indigenous medicine.
