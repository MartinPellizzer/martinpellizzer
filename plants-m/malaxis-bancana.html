Malaxis bancana

Malaxis bancana, commonly known as the banana orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring elongated, banana-like pseudobulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces delicate, pendulous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, Malaxis bancana is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
