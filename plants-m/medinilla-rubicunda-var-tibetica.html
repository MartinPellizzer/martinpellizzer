Medinilla rubicunda var. tibetica

Medinilla rubicunda var. tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan velvet flower, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to orange. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
