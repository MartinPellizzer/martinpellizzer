Mucura bipinnata

Mucura bipinnata, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or the golden palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of palm wine.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also valued in traditional medicine and horticulture.
