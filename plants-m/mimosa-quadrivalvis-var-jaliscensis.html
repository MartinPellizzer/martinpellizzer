Mimosa quadrivalvis var. jaliscensis

Mimosa quadrivalvis var. jaliscensis, commonly known as the Jalisco mimosa, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and unique leaf behavior make it a notable species in botanical studies.
