Mentha × rotundifolia var. humidicola

Mentha × rotundifolia var. humidicola, commonly known as the marsh mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, slightly toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
