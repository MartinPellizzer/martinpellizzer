Morinda tinctoria var. multiflora

Morinda tinctoria var. multiflora, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-white flowers followed by red or orange berries. It is valued for its roots, which contain natural pigments used in traditional dyes.

Its growth habit and medicinal properties make it an important species in both horticulture and ethnobotany.
