Myrtus briquetii

Myrtus briquetii, commonly known as the Corsican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans.

Myrtus briquetii is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its aromatic properties and hardy nature.
