Megaclinium bambiliense

Megaclinium bambiliense, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
