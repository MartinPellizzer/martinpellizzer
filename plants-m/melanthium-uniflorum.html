Melanthium uniflorum

Melanthium uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower grass, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant features slender, grass-like stems that are often reddish in color. Its flowers are small, clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and are usually greenish-white in hue.

Melanthium uniflorum is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
