Matthiola scapifera

Matthiola scapifera, commonly known as the scapifera stock, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other familiar garden plants such as cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, elongated seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Matthiola scapifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
