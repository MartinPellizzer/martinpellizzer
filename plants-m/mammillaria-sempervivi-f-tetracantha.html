Mammillaria sempervivi f. tetracantha

Mammillaria sempervivi f. tetracantha, commonly known as the four-armed mammillaria, is a compact cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem covered in short, cylindrical spines and small, ribbed tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative succulent.
