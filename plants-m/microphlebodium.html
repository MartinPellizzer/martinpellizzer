Microphlebodium

Microphlebodium, commonly known as the bird's nest fern, is a type of epiphytic plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, umbrella-like fronds that resemble a bird's nest, hence its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found growing on trees in tropical and subtropical regions. Its root system consists of rhizomes that anchor it to its host and absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

Microphlebodium species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in gardens and as houseplants.
