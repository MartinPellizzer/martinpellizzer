Medicago aschersoniana var. brachyacantha

Medicago aschersoniana var. brachyacantha, commonly known as the short-stalked goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are usually hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
