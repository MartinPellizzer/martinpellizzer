Merwilla plumbea subsp. plumbea

Merwilla plumbea subsp.

plumbea, commonly known as the bluebush, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking blue-gray foliage.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The subspecies plumbea is distinguished by its unique coloration and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
