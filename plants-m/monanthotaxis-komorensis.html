Monanthotaxis komorensis

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and have a strong, sweet fragrance.
