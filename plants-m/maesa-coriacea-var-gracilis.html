Maesa coriacea var. gracilis

Maesa coriacea var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender maesa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
