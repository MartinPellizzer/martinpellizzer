Mahonia napaulensis

Mahonia napaulensis, commonly known as the Himalayan Mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mahonia napaulensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
