Monardella macrantha subsp. hallii

Monardella macrantha subsp.

hallii, commonly known as Hall's tall monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
