Monardella candicans

Monardella candicans, commonly known as the showy monardella, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its striking, tubular pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Monardella candicans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
