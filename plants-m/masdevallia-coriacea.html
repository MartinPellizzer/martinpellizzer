Masdevallia coriacea

Masdevallia coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved masdevallia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its distinctive, leathery, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often in clusters. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
