Menadena parkeri

Menadena parkeri, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Central America and northern South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that resemble tiny fireworks, hence its common name. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Menadena parkeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
