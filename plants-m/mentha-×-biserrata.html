Mentha × biserrata

Mentha × biserrata, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha viridis (oval-leaf mint). The plant is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves and square stems typical of the mint genus. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

Mentha × biserrata is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.
