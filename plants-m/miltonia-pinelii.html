Miltonia pinelii

Miltonia pinelii, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a tropical orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in the humid, shaded environment of the rainforest. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, which are attracted by its fragrant scent.

Miltonia pinelii is a popular ornamental plant due to its beauty and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
