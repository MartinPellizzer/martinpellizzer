Melissa repens

Melissa repens, commonly known as ground lemon or lemon balm, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its fragrant, slightly hairy leaves that emit a lemony scent. The plant typically features square stems and small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale blue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Melissa repens is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
