Mapania paradoxa

Mapania paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical mapania, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves that exhibit a striking iridescent sheen under sunlight. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
