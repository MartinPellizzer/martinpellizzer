Miconia cinereiformis

Miconia cinereiformis, commonly known as the gray miconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 50 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by distinctive, round, orange-red fruits. Miconia cinereiformis is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in ecological and horticultural studies.
