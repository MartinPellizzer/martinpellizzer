Mimulus linearifolius

Mimulus linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and bright orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Mimulus linearifolius plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
