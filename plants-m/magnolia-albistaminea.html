Magnolia albistaminea

Magnolia albistaminea, commonly known as the white-stamen magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The flowers have prominent, thread-like stamens that give the plant its common name. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
