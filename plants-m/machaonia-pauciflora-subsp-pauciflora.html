Machaonia pauciflora subsp. pauciflora

Machaonia pauciflora subsp.

pauciflora, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
