Melaleuca gilesii

Melaleuca gilesii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that are typically followed by woody capsules containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and open forests.

Melaleuca gilesii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
