Mesanthus macrocarpus

Mesanthus macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited mesanthus, is a rare and specialized plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant produces large, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, Mesanthus macrocarpus is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
