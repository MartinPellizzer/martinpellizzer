Mammillaria corbula

Mammillaria corbula, commonly known as the Mexican Hedgehog Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical shape and dense clusters of white to pale green spines. The plant features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Mammillaria corbula is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
