Machilus lichuanensis

Machilus lichuanensis, commonly known as the Lichuan wood-laurel, is a deciduous tree native to China, particularly in the provinces of Hunan and Hubei.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurel, avocado, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, dark blue berries.

Machilus lichuanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
