Motandra

Motandra, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Motandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
