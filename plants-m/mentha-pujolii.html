Mentha pujolii

Mentha pujolii, commonly known as Pujol mint, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. Mentha pujolii is valued for its strong, menthol-like fragrance and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

It is also a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
