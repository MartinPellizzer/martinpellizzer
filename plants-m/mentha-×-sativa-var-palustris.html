Mentha × sativa var. palustris

Mentha × sativa var. palustris, commonly known as marsh mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are elongated, narrow, and slightly toothed. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other wet environments.
