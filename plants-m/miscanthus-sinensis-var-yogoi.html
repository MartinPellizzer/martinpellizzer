Miscanthus sinensis var. yogoi

Miscanthus sinensis var. yogoi, commonly known as the silvergrass or Japanese silvergrass, is a variant of the Miscanthus genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage that turns coppery in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 feet, with tall, arching inflorescences that produce fluffy, seed-bearing plumes. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, thriving in temperate climates.

This variety is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
