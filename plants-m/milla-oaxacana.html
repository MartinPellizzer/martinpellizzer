Milla oaxacana

Milla oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Oaxaca, Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces a milky sap that is toxic to many animals, including mammals and some insects.

Milla oaxacana plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
