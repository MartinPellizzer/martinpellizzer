Moniera procumbens f. albiflora

Moniera procumbens f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered moniera, is a variant of the moniera plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate aesthetic to its foliage. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that lie close to the ground, forming a dense mat. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks.

Botanically, it is noted for its simple floral structure and is of interest in studies of plant morphology and adaptation.
