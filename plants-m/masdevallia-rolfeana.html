Masdevallia rolfeana

Masdevallia rolfeana, commonly known as the Rolfe's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces intricate, spurred flowers that are adapted for specific pollinators.

Masdevallia rolfeana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
