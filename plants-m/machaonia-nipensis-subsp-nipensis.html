Machaonia nipensis subsp. nipensis

Machaonia nipensis subsp.

nipensis, commonly known as the Japanese machaonia, is a shrub native to Japan. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its small, elliptical leaves are arranged alternately along the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
