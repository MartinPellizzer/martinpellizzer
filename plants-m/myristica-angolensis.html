Myristica angolensis

Myristica angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by drupes that contain a highly aromatic seed kernel. Its essential oils, derived from the seeds, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in its native habitats.
