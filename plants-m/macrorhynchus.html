Macrorhynchus

Macrorhynchus, commonly known as the giant burr plant, is a species belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, feathery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its seeds are encased in hard, spiny structures that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals or clothing.

Macrorhynchus is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
