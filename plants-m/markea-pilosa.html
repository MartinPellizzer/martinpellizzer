Markea pilosa

Markea pilosa, commonly known as the hairy markea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
