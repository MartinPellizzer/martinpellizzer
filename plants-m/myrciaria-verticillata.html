Myrciaria verticillata

Myrciaria verticillata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-preto, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its flowers are white and fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
