Mentha argutissima var. subpilosa

Mentha argutissima var. subpilosa, commonly known as the silky mint, is a variant of the mint family with distinctive botanical features.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are typically gray-green in color, and the plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.
