Mentzelia aspera

Mentzelia aspera, commonly known as rough mentzelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has gray-green, rough-textured leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Mentzelia aspera is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
