Maximowiczia nigra

Maximowiczia nigra, commonly known as black mangrove, is a flowering plant species native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in saline or brackish water environments, such as mangrove forests. The plant has small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by water.

Maximowiczia nigra plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
