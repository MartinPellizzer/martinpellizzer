Myrcianthes montana

Myrcianthes montana, commonly known as the mountain myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

Myrcianthes montana is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
