Mogiphanes multicaulis

Mogiphanes multicaulis, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.

Mogiphanes multicaulis is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
