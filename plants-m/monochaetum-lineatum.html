Monochaetum lineatum

Monochaetum lineatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ghost orchid, is an epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and striking white flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks. Its unique morphology and restricted habitat make it a rare and ecologically significant species.

Conservation efforts are crucial due to its vulnerability to habitat loss and environmental changes.
