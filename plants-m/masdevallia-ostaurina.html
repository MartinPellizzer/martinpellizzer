Masdevallia ostaurina

Masdevallia ostaurina, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that typically feature a yellow or orange hue with intricate patterns. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
