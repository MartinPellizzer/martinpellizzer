Myrcia deflexa

Myrcia deflexa, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Myrcia deflexa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
