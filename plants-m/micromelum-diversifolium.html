Micromelum diversifolium

Micromelum diversifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaf mallow, is a small shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape, contributing to its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its woody stems are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Micromelum diversifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
