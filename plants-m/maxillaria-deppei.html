Maxillaria deppei

Maxillaria deppei, commonly known as the Peacock Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, fleshy leaf and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and have a distinctive, intricate structure with long, slender petals and a prominent lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
