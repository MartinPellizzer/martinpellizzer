Magnolia cararensis

Magnolia cararensis, commonly known as the Carare magnolia, is a rare and endemic species native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive, aromatic scent when cut.
