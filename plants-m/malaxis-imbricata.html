Malaxis imbricata

Malaxis imbricata, commonly known as the wrinkled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, wrinkled leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, orchid-like appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in humid, tropical regions with rich, organic soil.
