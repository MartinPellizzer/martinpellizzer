Myrtus paitensis

Myrtus paitensis, commonly known as the Christmas box tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, fragrant foliage with small, leathery leaves that have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces tiny, white, bell-shaped flowers in spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Myrtus paitensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.
