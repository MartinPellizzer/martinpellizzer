Massonia burchellii

Massonia burchellii, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, succulent perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features fleshy, blue-green leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.

Massonia burchellii is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
