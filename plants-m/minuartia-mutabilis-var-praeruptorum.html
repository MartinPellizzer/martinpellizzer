Minuartia mutabilis var. praeruptorum

Minuartia mutabilis var. praeruptorum, commonly known as the variable rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. This species is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in alpine or arctic regions. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its role in supporting local biodiversity.
