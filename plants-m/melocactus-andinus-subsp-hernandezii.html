Melocactus andinus subsp. hernandezii

Melocactus andinus subsp.

hernandezii, commonly known as the Hernandez's melon cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that is typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny exterior. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, with a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as a decorative cactus in horticultural collections.
