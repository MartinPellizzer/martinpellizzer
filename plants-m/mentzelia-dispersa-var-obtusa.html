Mentzelia dispersa var. obtusa

Mentzelia dispersa var. obtusa, commonly known as the bluntleaf bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. The plant features slender, branching stems covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically pale blue or purple in color.

The species is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in desert scrub and grassland ecosystems.
