Myristica inopinata

Myristica inopinata, commonly known as the Malabar nut or black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark, spherical fruits that contain a single seed, which is used for its essential oils and medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of dense forests.

Myristica inopinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
