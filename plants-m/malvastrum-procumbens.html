Malvastrum procumbens

Malvastrum procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves and a sprawling growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover in arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
