Malaxis vinosa

Malaxis vinosa, commonly known as the vine-like orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
