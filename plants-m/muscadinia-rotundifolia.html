Muscadinia rotundifolia

Muscadinia rotundifolia, commonly known as the muscadine grape, is a native American vine that thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant is known for its thick, waxy skin and its ability to produce a unique, musky aroma. Muscadine grapes are also notable for their high levels of antioxidants and resveratrol.

This species is often cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
