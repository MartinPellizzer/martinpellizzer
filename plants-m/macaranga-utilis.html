Macaranga utilis

Macaranga utilis, commonly known as the whiteleaf mallows, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its latex is milky and can be used for various traditional purposes.

Macaranga utilis is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
