Myrinia

Myrinia, commonly known as the Australian bottlebrush, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes. The plants typically have narrow, leathery leaves and produce small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. Myrinia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are adapted to a variety of soil types.

They play an important ecological role in supporting native wildlife, particularly birds.
