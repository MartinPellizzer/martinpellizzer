Monochoria hastifolia

Monochoria hastifolia, commonly known as water spinach or kangkong, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It has slender, floating or submerged stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and is often found in rice paddies and wetlands.

Monochoria hastifolia is widely cultivated for its edible leaves, which are used in various cuisines.
