Malva pallescens

Malva pallescens, commonly known as the pale mallows, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features soft, fuzzy leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive central column.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.
