Mikania steyermarkii

Mikania steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's mile-a-minute vine, is a fast-growing climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are grouped in clusters, and it is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense vegetation.

Mikania steyermarkii is often considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its aggressive spreading habit.
