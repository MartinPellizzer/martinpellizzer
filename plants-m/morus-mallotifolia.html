Morus mallotifolia

Morus mallotifolia, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into aggregate fruits composed of numerous tiny achenes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its fibrous bark.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a food source for certain wildlife.
