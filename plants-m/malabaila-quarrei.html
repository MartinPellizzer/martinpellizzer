Malabaila quarrei

Malabaila quarrei, commonly known as the Malabar nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom at night. Its fruits are bright red berries that contain toxic compounds, making them harmful to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
