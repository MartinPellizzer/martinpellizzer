Metalasia rogersii

Metalasia rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' metalasia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing vibrant pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Metalasia rogersii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
