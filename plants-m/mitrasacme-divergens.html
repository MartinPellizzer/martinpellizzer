Mitrasacme divergens

Mitrasacme divergens, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowering stems are usually erect and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Mitrasacme divergens thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
