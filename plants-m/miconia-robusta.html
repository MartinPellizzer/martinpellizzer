Miconia robusta

Miconia robusta, commonly known as the giant miconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets have made it an invasive species in some regions.

The plant's unique botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a subject of botanical study.
