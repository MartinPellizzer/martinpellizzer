Myrcia saliana

Myrcia saliana, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fleshy fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, while its flowers are white or pale pink, often arranged in clusters.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.
