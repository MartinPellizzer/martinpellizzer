Melosperma andicola var. angustifolia

Melosperma andicola var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandreed, is a perennial grass native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in alpine meadows and is tolerant of harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
