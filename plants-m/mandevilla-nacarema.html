Mandevilla nacarema

Mandevilla nacarema, commonly known as the white mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative plant. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and have a glossy green surface.

Mandevilla nacarema thrives in warm, sunny conditions and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
