Monogramma rudolfii

Monogramma rudolfii, commonly known as the Rudolf's rockcap, is a rare and striking fern native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and its distinctive, finely divided, bright green fronds. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Monogramma rudolfii is considered a rare and endangered species.
