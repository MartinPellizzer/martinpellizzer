Myrstiphyllum nesophilum

Myrstiphyllum nesophilum, commonly known as the mountain myrtle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and leathery, evergreen leaves. It typically grows in cool, moist environments such as mountain forests and subalpine regions.

Myrstiphyllum nesophilum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
