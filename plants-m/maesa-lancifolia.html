Maesa lancifolia

Maesa lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved maesa, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
