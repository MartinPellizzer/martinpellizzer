Medicago rigidula subsp. sinskiae

Medicago rigidula subsp.

sinskiae, commonly known as the Sinskian goatweed, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its drought tolerance.
