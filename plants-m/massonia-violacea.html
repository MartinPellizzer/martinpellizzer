Massonia violacea

Massonia violacea, commonly known as the blue massonia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped landscapes.
