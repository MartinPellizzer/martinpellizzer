Mentha longifolia var. szabolcsensis

Mentha longifolia var. szabolcsensis, commonly known as Hungarian mint, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a strong, minty scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
