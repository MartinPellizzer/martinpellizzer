Manilkara discolor

Manilkara discolor, commonly known as the yellow spondias, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red fruits resembling small apples. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a sweet, juicy pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical regions.
