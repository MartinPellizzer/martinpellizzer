Moraea robinsoniana

Moraea robinsoniana, commonly known as the Robinson's moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of vibrant pink or red flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
