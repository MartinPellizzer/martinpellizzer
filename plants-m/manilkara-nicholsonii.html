Manilkara nicholsonii

Manilkara nicholsonii, commonly known as the African manilkara, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single, large seed. Its latex, which is milky and sticky, has been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a chewing gum.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic uses in local communities.
