Mammillaria tentaculata

Mammillaria tentaculata, commonly known as the tentacle cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring white to pale yellow spines and a dense covering of small, tuberculate bumps. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5 to 10 centimeters, with a rounded, compact shape. It produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are often followed by red or orange fruits.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
