Myrtus calycolpoides

Myrtus calycolpoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, dark blue berries. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

Myrtus calycolpoides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.
