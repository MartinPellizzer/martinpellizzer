Mecardonia flagellaris subsp. radicata

Mecardonia flagellaris subsp.

radicata, commonly known as the root mecardonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa. It features a fibrous root system and slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
