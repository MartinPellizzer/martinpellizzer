Medicago intertexta subsp. aculeata

Medicago intertexta subsp.

aculeata, commonly known as the prickly medick, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy, prickly stems and leaves, which provide a unique texture. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

This plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
