Maxillaria articulata

Maxillaria articulata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive pattern of markings. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulb.

Maxillaria articulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique fragrance.
