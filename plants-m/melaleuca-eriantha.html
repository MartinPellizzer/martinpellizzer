Melaleuca eriantha

Melaleuca eriantha, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Paperbark, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a pinkish or reddish inner layer. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by a variety of insects and birds. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Melaleuca eriantha is often found in coastal or wetland areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
