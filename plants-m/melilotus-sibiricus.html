Melilotus sibiricus

Melilotus sibiricus, commonly known as Siberian sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its flowers are rich in nectar, making it an important resource for pollinators.
