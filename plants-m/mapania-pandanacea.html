Mapania pandanacea

Mapania pandanacea, commonly known as the golden bamboo or pandan bamboo, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and traditional medicinal practices. Its growth habit resembles that of bamboo, with a clumping or spreading form depending on the environment.

Mapania pandanacea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
