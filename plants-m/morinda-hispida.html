Morinda hispida

Morinda hispida, commonly known as the rough morinda, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Morinda hispida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
