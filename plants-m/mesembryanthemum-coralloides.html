Mesembryanthemum coralloides

Mesembryanthemum coralloides, commonly known as the coral devil's bit, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a striking addition to arid landscapes.
