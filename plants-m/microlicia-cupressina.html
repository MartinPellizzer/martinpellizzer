Microlicia cupressina

Microlicia cupressina, commonly known as the red cedar or Mexican cedar, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cupressaceae.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in the tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, woody cones.

Microlicia cupressina is valued for its ornamental use and has also been used in traditional medicine.
