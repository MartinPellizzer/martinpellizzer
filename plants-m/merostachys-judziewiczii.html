Merostachys judziewiczii

Merostachys judziewiczii, commonly known as the Judziewicz's grass, is a rare and endangered species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetland or swampy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
