Melica scandens

Melica scandens, commonly known as creeping millet, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems that often trail along the ground or ascend other vegetation. The plant produces small, greenish to brownish spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Melica scandens is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges.
