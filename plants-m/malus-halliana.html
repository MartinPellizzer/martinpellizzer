Malus halliana

Malus halliana, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The tree produces small, edible pears that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, providing visual interest throughout the growing season.

Malus halliana is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and is relatively easy to grow in a variety of climates.
