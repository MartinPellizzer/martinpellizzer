Meriandra

Meriandra, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of Australia and Southeast Asia. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a fragrant scent.

Meriandra species are also noted for their small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters.
