Monstera lingulata

Monstera lingulata, commonly known as the split-leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that become more deeply divided as the plant matures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often variegated with light green and white patterns, adding visual interest.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to flourish.
