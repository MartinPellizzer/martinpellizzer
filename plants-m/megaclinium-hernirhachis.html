Megaclinium hernirhachis

Megaclinium hernirhachis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in areas with consistent moisture. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster at the base. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or brown and appear in clusters near the base of the plant.

Megaclinium hernirhachis plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
