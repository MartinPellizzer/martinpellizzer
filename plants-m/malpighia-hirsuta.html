Malpighia hirsuta

Malpighia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy physalis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its overall appearance.
