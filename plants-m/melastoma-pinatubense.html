Melastoma pinatubense

Melastoma pinatubense, commonly known as the Philippine blueberry or mountain melastoma, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
