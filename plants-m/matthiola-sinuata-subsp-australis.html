Matthiola sinuata subsp. australis

Matthiola sinuata subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

The plant typically blooms in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf shape and flower coloration compared to other Matthiola species.
