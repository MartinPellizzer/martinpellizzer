Melissa rugosa

Melissa rugosa, commonly known as rough lemon, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is often cultivated for its lemon-scented foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its fruit, while not commonly consumed, is used in some traditional herbal remedies.

The plant's resilience and aromatic qualities make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
