Melampodium camphoratum

Melampodium camphoratum, commonly known as the camphorweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom from summer to fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Melampodium camphoratum is known for its aromatic properties, with a scent reminiscent of camphor.
