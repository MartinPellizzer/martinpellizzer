Melhania chrysantha

Melhania chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Melhania chrysantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
