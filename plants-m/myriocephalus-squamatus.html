Myriocephalus squamatus

Myriocephalus squamatus, commonly known as the scaly-headed plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its distinctive, scaly appearance on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

Myriocephalus squamatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
