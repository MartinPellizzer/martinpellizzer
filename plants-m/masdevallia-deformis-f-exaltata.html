Masdevallia deformis f. exaltata

Masdevallia deformis f. exaltata, commonly known as the tall masdevallia, is a hybrid orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the cooler months. It prefers high humidity, bright indirect light, and well-draining soil to thrive.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
