Mammillaria painteri

Mammillaria painteri, commonly known as the painter's milk barrel cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly spines and is characterized by its dense, rounded areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
