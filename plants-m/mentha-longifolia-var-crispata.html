Mentha longifolia var. crispata

Mentha longifolia var. crispata, commonly known as creeping mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic essential oils.
