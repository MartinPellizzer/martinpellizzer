Mikania polystachya

Mikania polystachya, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and role in ecological succession.
