Merremia hirta

Merremia hirta, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Merremia hirta is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
