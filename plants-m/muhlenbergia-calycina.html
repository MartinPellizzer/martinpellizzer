Muhlenbergia calycina

Muhlenbergia calycina, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and tall, slender inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native landscapes.

Muhlenbergia calycina plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
