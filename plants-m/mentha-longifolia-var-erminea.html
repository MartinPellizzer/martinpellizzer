Mentha longifolia var. erminea

Mentha longifolia var. erminea, commonly known as ermine sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and small, fragrant leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds that contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
