Mammillaria albidula

Mammillaria albidula, commonly known as the white-fleshed nipple cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale green to white body and short, spiny ribs. The plant typically has a single stem that may branch slightly as it matures. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, appearing in the axils of the spines.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
