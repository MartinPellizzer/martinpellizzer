Macrotyloma brevicaule

Macrotyloma brevicaule, commonly known as the dwarf cowpea, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, with trifoliate leaves and small white or pink flowers. Its seeds are edible and often used in culinary applications, while the plant also serves as forage for livestock.

The species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in sustainable agriculture.
