Machaerium gracile

Machaerium gracile, commonly known as slender machaerium, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Machaerium gracile is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
