Madia elegans subsp. wheeleri

Madia elegans subsp.

wheeleri, commonly known as Wheeler's madia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit.

The plant typically features slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping in arid environments.
