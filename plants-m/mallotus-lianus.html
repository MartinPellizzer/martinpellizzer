Mallotus lianus

Mallotus lianus, commonly known as the climbing mallot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its woody, climbing stem and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
