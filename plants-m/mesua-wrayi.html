Mesua wrayi

Mesua wrayi, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing before the leaves in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Mesua wrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
