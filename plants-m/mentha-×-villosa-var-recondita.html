Mentha × villosa var. recondita

Mentha × villosa var. recondita, commonly known as the woolly mint or the alpine mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and square, erect stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the mint family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable plant for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
