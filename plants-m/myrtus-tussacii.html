Myrtus tussacii

Myrtus tussacii, commonly known as the Canary Island myrtle, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the island's unique climate. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Myrtus tussacii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
