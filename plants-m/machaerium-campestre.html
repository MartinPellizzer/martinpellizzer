Machaerium campestre

Machaerium campestre, commonly known as the field machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed areas, thriving in full sun.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
