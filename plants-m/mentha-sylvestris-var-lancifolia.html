Mentha sylvestris var. lancifolia

Mentha sylvestris var. lancifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and aromatic, slightly fuzzy foliage. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
