Myrcia aequatoriensis

Myrcia aequatoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests.

Myrcia aequatoriensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
