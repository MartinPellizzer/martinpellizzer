Microcalia australis

Microcalia australis, commonly known as the Australian microcalia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for native gardens.
