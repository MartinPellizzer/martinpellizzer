Mimosa menabeensis

Mimosa menabeensis, commonly known as the Madagascar mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with hairy stems and compound leaves that fold when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.
