Melasma avense

Melasma avense, commonly known as the common meadow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Melasma avense is often found in open, sunny areas such as meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common and adaptable species in temperate regions.
