Malva propinqua

Malva propinqua, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cotton and okra. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and wild areas.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional remedies.
