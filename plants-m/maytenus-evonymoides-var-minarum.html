Maytenus evonymoides var. minarum

Maytenus evonymoides var. minarum, commonly known as the Mexican olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by small, fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to moist forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
