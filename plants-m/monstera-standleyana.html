Monstera standleyana

Monstera standleyana, commonly known as the Swiss cheese plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves that develop distinctive holes as they mature. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The leaves are often used in decorative arrangements due to their unique appearance.
