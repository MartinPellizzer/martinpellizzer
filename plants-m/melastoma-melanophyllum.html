Melastoma melanophyllum

Melastoma melanophyllum, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a thick, segmented rind. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed sites.

The botanical features of Melastoma melanophyllum make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
