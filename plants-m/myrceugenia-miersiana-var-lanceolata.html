Myrceugenia miersiana var. lanceolata

Myrceugenia miersiana var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate myrtle, is a species native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are dark purple when mature. It thrives in humid, coastal climates and is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aromatic foliage and floral display.
