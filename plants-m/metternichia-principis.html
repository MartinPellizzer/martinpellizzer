Metternichia principis

Metternichia principis, commonly known as the royal lily, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological sensitivity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural habitat.
