Malpighia multiflora

Malpighia multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian wolfberry, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
