Madhuca obovatifolia

Madhuca obovatifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
