Miconia concinna

Miconia concinna, commonly known as the "Brazilian cherry" or "miconia," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in dense, white or silver hairs. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia concinna is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential ecological threat.
