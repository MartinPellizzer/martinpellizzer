Morina parviflora

Morina parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered moringa, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and simple, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
