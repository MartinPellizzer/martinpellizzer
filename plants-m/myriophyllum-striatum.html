Myriophyllum striatum

Myriophyllum striatum, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a aquatic plant characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant is rooted in the substrate and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its stems are often flexible and can form dense underwater stands, contributing to water clarity by reducing wave action.

Myriophyllum striatum is an important part of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
