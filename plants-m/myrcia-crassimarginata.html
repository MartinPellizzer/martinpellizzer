Myrcia crassimarginata

Myrcia crassimarginata, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree or *Pimenta* , is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often aromatic and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Myrcia crassimarginata is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and traditional medicine.
