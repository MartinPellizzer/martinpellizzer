Mimulus macranthus

Mimulus macranthus, commonly known as the giant monkeyflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mimulus macranthus is valued in horticulture for its vibrant flowers and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
