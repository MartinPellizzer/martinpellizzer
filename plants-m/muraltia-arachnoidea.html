Muraltia arachnoidea

Muraltia arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider-vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Muraltia arachnoidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
