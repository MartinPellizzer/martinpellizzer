Medicago verlotii

Medicago verlotii, commonly known as the Verlot's clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Medicago verlotii is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in some regions for forage and soil stabilization.
