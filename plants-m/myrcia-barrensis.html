Myrcia barrensis

Myrcia barrensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Myrcia barrensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can grow in both full sun and partial shade.
