Myrceugenia ferruginea

Myrceugenia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to southern Brazil and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.
