Mesosphaerum lacunosum

Mesosphaerum lacunosum, commonly known as the laceleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has a distinctive appearance with its thin, waxy leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by berries that are typically red or yellow in color. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
