Mussaenda utakwae

Mussaenda utakwae, commonly known as the African flame flower, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, typically in shades of orange and red. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Mussaenda utakwae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
