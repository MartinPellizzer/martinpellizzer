Mesembryanthemum rhomboideum

Mesembryanthemum rhomboideum, commonly known as the diamond flower, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and distinctive diamond-shaped leaves that are covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday, a trait known as nyctinasty. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions.
