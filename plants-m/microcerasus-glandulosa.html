Microcerasus glandulosa

Microcerasus glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in glandular dots. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, red berries. Microcerasus glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
