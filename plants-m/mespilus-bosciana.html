Mespilus bosciana

Mespilus bosciana, commonly known as the Boscan pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, yellow to orange fruits that are typically harvested when they are still firm and unripe. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters and blooming in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its fruit and its use in landscaping.
