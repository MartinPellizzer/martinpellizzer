Melastoma pulchellum

Melastoma pulchellum, commonly known as the purple false heather, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
