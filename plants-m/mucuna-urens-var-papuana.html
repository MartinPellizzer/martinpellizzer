Mucuna urens var. papuana

Mucuna urens var. papuana, commonly known as the velvet bean or climbing cowpea, is a tropical legume characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. It is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. The flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Its extensive root system helps in soil nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
