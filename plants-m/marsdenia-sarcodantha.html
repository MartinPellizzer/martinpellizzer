Marsdenia sarcodantha

Marsdenia sarcodantha, commonly known as the giant white-flowered milkvine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its long, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, often with a sweet, milky sap. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release small, seed-containing pods.

Marsdenia sarcodantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
