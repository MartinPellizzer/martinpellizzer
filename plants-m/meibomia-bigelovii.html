Meibomia bigelovii

Meibomia bigelovii, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is typical of the genus Meibomia and can be toxic if ingested.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions.
