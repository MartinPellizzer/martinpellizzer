Miconia roraimensis

Miconia roraimensis, commonly known as the Roraima miconia, is a shrub native to the tepuis of Venezuela and Guyana.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its dense, broad leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the tropical rainforest canopy. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
