Manihot esculenta var. fuscescens

Manihot esculenta var. fuscescens, commonly known as the red manioc, is a variant of the manioc plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark red to purple-colored stems and leaves, which distinguish it from the more common white manioc. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in starch and used in various culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique coloration and nutritional value, it is cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
