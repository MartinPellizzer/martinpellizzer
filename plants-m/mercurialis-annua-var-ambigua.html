Mercurialis annua var. ambigua

Mercurialis annua var. ambigua, commonly known as the variable mercury plant, is a variant of the common mercury plant, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and growth habits.

It is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually more elongated and variable in shape compared to the typical variety. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require caution in use.
