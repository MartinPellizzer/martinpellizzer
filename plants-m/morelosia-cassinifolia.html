Morelosia cassinifolia

Morelosia cassinifolia, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Morelosia cassinifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
