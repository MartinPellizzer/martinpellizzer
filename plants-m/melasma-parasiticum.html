Melasma parasiticum

Melasma parasiticum, commonly known as the parasitic melasma, is a rare and unusual plant species characterized by its unique parasitic nature.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically attaching itself to the roots or stems of other vegetation. The plant's structure is simplified, with reduced leaves and a specialized root system for absorption. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions where host plants are abundant.

Due to its parasitic lifestyle, it plays a minor role in ecological systems but remains an intriguing subject for botanical study.
