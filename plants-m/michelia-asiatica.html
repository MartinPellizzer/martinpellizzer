Michelia asiatica

Michelia asiatica, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Magnoliaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it often produces small, red or orange fruits.

Michelia asiatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
