Menarda linifolia

Menarda linifolia, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its showy purple to pink flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Menarda linifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
