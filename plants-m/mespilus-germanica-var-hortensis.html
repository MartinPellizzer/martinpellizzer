Mespilus germanica var. hortensis

Mespilus germanica var. hortensis, commonly known as the European white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and have a silvery underside. The tree produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a host plant for silkworms.
