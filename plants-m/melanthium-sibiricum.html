Melanthium sibiricum

Melanthium sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Melanthium sibiricum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
