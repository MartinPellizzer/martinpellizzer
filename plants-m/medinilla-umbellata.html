Medinilla umbellata

Medinilla umbellata, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

It is a flowering plant native to the Philippines, characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble small, clustered tangerines. The plant produces waxy, pink to red flowers that are highly fragrant, often blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to the vibrant blossoms.

Medinilla umbellata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
