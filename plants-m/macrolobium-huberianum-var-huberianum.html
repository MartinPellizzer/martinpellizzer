Macrolobium huberianum var. huberianum

Macrolobium huberianum var. huberianum, commonly known as the Huberian macrolobium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
