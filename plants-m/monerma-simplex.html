Monerma simplex

Monerma simplex, commonly known as the simple monerma, is a unique plant species characterized by its simple, unbranched structure.

It belongs to the phylum Cyanobacteria, which are photosynthetic prokaryotes capable of performing nitrogen fixation. This organism thrives in freshwater environments, often found in stagnant or slow-moving water bodies. Its morphology consists of a single, elongated cell that can divide to form colonies.

Monerma simplex plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and serving as a food source for microorganisms.
