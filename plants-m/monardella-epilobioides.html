Monardella epilobioides

Monardella epilobioides, commonly known as stickyleaf monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, sticky coating, which gives it its common name.

Monardella epilobioides is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.
