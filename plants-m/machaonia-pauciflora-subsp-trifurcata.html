Machaonia pauciflora subsp. trifurcata

Machaonia pauciflora subsp.

trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched machaonia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit.

The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems.

This subspecies is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
