Matricaria graminifolia

Matricaria graminifolia, commonly known as the meadow chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has aromatic leaves and flowers that are used in herbal remedies, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Matricaria graminifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
