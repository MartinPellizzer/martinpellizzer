Miconia alloeotricha

Miconia alloeotricha, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Neotropical region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange to red fruits containing numerous seeds. Miconia alloeotricha is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it an invasive species in some areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic in certain ecosystems.
