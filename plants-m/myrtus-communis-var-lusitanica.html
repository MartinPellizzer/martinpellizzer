Myrtus communis var. lusitanica

Myrtus communis var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, dark green leaves that are glossy and aromatic, with a dense, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and aromatic properties make it a popular choice in horticulture.
