Mammillaria eumorpha

Mammillaria eumorpha, commonly known as the White Globular Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers during the spring or early summer.

Due to its compact size and unique features, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
