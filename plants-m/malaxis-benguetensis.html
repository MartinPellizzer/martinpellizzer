Malaxis benguetensis

Malaxis benguetensis, commonly known as the Benguet Malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually linear and grass-like, adapted to its humid, mountainous habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
