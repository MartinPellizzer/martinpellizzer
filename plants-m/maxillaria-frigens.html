Maxillaria frigens

Maxillaria frigens, commonly known as the cold-loving orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a unique ability to thrive in cooler climates, often found in cloud forests. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals. Its growth habit is characterized by a pseudobulb system, which helps it store water and nutrients.

Maxillaria frigens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
