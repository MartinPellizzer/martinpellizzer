Mammillaria radleana

Mammillaria radleana, commonly known as the Radleana prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and collections.
