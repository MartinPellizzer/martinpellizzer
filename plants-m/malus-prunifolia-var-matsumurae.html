Malus prunifolia var. matsumurae

Malus prunifolia var. matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese white apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. The plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas.

It is valued for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional Japanese horticulture.
