Medinilla pterocaula

Medinilla pterocaula, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
