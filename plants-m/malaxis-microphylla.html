Malaxis microphylla

Malaxis microphylla, commonly known as the slender marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has a slender, erect stem with few leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its simple, linear leaves and delicate floral structure.
