Mentzelia conspicua

Mentzelia conspicua, commonly known as the showy mentzelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, lavender-pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and somewhat woody.

Mentzelia conspicua is an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
