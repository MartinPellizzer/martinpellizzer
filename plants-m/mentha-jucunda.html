Mentha jucunda

Mentha jucunda, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated edge and emit a strong minty fragrance when crushed.

Mentha jucunda is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
