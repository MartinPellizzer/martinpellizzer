Marsdenia billardierei

Marsdenia billardierei, commonly known as the Australian milk vine, is a climbing shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces distinctive, horn-like fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, sticky aril. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in coastal areas and rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm climates.
