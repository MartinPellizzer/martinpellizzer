Mimosa mensicola

Mimosa mensicola, commonly known as the moonflower mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellow to orange pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in sandy or disturbed soils. Mimosa mensicola is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in full sun, making it a common sight in open habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivorous animals.
