Morinda peduncularis

Morinda peduncularis, commonly known as the red morinda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a smooth texture.

Morinda peduncularis is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating skin conditions and as an antipyretic.
