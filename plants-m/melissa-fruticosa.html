Melissa fruticosa

Melissa fruticosa, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small, yellowish-green flowers and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, often growing in moist environments. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, including citral and geraniol, which contribute to its calming scent.

Melissa fruticosa is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in herbal medicine.
