Myrcia dumosa

Myrcia dumosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Myrcia dumosa is known for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
