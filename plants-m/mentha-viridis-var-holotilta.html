Mentha viridis var. holotilta

Mentha viridis var. holotilta, commonly known as green mint, is a variant of the green mint plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
