Matelea cardozoi

Matelea cardozoi, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that are typically pale green to white in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
