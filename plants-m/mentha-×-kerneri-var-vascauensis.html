Mentha × kerneri var. vascauensis

Mentha × kerneri var. vascauensis, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems, typical of the mint genus. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or white, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental value in gardens.
