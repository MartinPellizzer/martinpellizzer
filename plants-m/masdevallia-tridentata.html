Masdevallia tridentata

Masdevallia tridentata, commonly known as the Trident Lip, is a tropical orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, three-lobed lip that resembles a trident, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Masdevallia tridentata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
