Mesembryanthemum bifoliatum

Mesembryanthemum bifoliatum, commonly known as the two-leaved ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it highly drought-resistant.
