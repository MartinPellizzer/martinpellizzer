Maba bipindensis

Maba bipindensis, commonly known as the African wild rice, is a perennial aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant produces small, rice-like grains that are edible and have been traditionally used by local communities for food. Maba bipindensis thrives in slow-moving or still water, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and nutritional purposes in its native environment.
