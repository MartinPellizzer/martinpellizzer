Miconia molinopampana

Miconia molinopampana, commonly known as the white miconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a round, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Miconia molinopampana is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
