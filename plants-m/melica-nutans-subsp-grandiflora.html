Melica nutans subsp. grandiflora

Melica nutans subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the nodding melica, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The species is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically greenish or pale blue, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
