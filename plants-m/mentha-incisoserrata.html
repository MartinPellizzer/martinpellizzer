Mentha incisoserrata

Mentha incisoserrata, commonly known as the serrated mint, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that have serrated edges. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale purple to blue and bloom in summer.

Mentha incisoserrata is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
