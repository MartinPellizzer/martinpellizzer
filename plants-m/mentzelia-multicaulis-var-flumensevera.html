Mentzelia multicaulis var. flumensevera

Mentzelia multicaulis var. flumensevera, commonly known as the river mentzelia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and creek beds. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a basal rosette, which helps it retain moisture.

Mentzelia multicaulis var. flumensevera is part of the Loasaceae family and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
