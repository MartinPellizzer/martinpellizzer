Montia spathulata var. rosulata

Montia spathulata var. rosulata, commonly known as the Rosulate Miner's Lettuce, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in spring, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in rocky or clay soils.
