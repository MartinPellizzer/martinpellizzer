Mirtana

Mirtana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mire plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of southern Europe and parts of Asia.

Mirtana typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, and it has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
