Magnolia cubensis subsp. turquinensis

Magnolia cubensis subsp.

turquinensis, commonly known as the Turquin's magnolia, is a rare and endemic species native to Cuba. It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in humid, well-drained soils in tropical forest environments. Its leaves are broad, elliptical, and often have a glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
