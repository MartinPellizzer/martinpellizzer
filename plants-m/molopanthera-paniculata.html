Molopanthera paniculata

Molopanthera paniculata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
