Myrtillocactus chichipe

Myrtillocactus chichipe, commonly known as the Chichipe cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a distinctive blue-gray coloration due to its dense covering of fine spines. The plant produces vibrant purple flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Myrtillocactus chichipe is also valued for its ability to store water, making it adaptable to arid environments.
