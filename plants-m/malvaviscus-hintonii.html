Malvaviscus hintonii

Malvaviscus hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring and summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
