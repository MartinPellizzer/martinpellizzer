Myrcia anomala var. ramosa

Myrcia anomala var. ramosa, commonly known as the ramose myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other notable plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
