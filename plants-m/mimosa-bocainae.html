Mimosa bocainae

Mimosa bocainae, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and other nitrogen-fixing plants. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

Mimosa bocainae is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
