Miphragtes

Miphragtes, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a delicate, feathery appearance. The tree produces small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
