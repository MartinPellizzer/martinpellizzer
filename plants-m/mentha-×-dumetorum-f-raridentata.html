Mentha × dumetorum f. raridentata

Mentha × dumetorum f. raridentata, commonly known as the rare toothed mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and a strong, pungent aroma reminiscent of mint. This variety is native to parts of Europe and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or pink in color.

Due to its unique leaf structure and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the mint family.
