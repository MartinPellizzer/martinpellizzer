Malaxis lawleri

Malaxis lawleri, commonly known as the Lawler's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Malaxis lawleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
