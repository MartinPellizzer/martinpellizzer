Miscanthus pycnocephalus var. purpurascens

Miscanthus pycnocephalus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and striking purple or reddish hues in its foliage and inflorescence. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 meters and produces fluffy, silver-tan plumes that add ornamental value in late summer and autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.

Its robust nature and aesthetic appeal contribute to its use in both ornamental gardens and natural habitats.
