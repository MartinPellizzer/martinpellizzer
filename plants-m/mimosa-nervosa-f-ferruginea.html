Mimosa nervosa f. ferruginea

Mimosa nervosa f. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-leaf mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that have a distinctive rusty or reddish-brown coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are often covered in fine, hairy textures, which aid in water retention in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
