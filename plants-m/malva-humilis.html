Malva humilis

Malva humilis, commonly known as the common mallow, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture and produces showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.

Malva humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
