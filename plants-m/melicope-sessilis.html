Melicope sessilis

Melicope sessilis, commonly known as the Hawaiian sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

Due to its unique fragrance and historical significance, it is both ecologically and culturally important in Hawaii.
