Macroptilium fraternum

Macroptilium fraternum, commonly known as wild cowpea, is a perennial legume native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
