Mallotus concinnus

Mallotus concinnus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in yellow or orange. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are sometimes used as a natural dye, and the tree is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Mallotus concinnus thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
