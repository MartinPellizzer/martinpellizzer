Mertensia pulchella subsp. glauca

Mertensia pulchella subsp.

glauca, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant blue or violet flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.

The subspecies glauca is distinguished by its more pronounced blue coloration compared to other forms of Mertensia pulchella.
