Myrobalanus gangetica

Myrobalanus gangetica, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible fruits resembling dates. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, slender leaflets that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.
