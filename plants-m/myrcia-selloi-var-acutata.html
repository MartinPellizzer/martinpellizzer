Myrcia selloi var. acutata

Myrcia selloi var. acutata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves with a pointed tip, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
