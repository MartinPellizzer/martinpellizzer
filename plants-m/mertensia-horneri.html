Mertensia horneri

Mertensia horneri, commonly known as Horner's bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or rocky areas. It has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer.

Mertensia horneri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
