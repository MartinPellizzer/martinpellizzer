Masdevallia xanthodactyla

Masdevallia xanthodactyla, commonly known as the yellow finger orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage, with flowers that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes, often epiphytic on trees or rocks. Its flowers are small and delicate, usually appearing in clusters during the cooler months.

Masdevallia xanthodactyla requires specific care, including bright indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.
