Myrtus pimentoides

Myrtus pimentoides, commonly known as the Portuguese myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and aromatic applications. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark purple berries, which are edible but typically used in jams and liqueurs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Myrtus pimentoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
