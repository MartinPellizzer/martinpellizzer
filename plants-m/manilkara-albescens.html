Manilkara albescens

Manilkara albescens, commonly known as the white spondias, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
