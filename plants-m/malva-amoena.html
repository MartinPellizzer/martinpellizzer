Malva amoena

Malva amoena, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean region. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Its fibrous roots and soft, fuzzy leaves make it a distinctive and recognizable species within the mallow genus.
