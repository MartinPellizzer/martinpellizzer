Mentha diemenica

Mentha diemenica, commonly known as the Tasmanian mint, is a perennial herb native to Tasmania, Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. Mentha diemenica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
