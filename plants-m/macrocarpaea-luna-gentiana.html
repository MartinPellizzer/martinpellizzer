Macrocarpaea luna-gentiana

Macrocarpaea luna-gentiana, commonly known as the moonlight gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. Its root system is fibrous and contributes to its resilience in rocky soils.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
