Mallotus petanodon

Mallotus petanodon, commonly known as the broad-leafed holly, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features compound leaves with multiple leaflets, often arranged in a feather-like pattern. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
