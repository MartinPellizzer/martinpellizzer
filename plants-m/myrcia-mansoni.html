Myrcia mansoni

Myrcia mansoni, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Myrcia mansoni is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
