Mysanthus uleanus var. dolicopsoides

Mysanthus uleanus var. dolicopsoides, commonly known as the slender-fruited mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It typically grows on the branches of host trees, such as oaks and maples, and derives some of its nutrients through specialized roots called haustoria. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often white or greenish in color. Its fruit is a small, elongated capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This variant is characterized by its slender, elongated fruits, distinguishing it from other varieties within the Mysanthus genus.
