Masdevallia xanthina subsp. pallida

Masdevallia xanthina subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale yellow masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, erect pseudobulbs and long, strap-like leaves.

The flowers are typically pale yellow with a distinctive red or orange lip, and they bloom during the cooler months. This orchid thrives in high-altitude, humid environments with good air circulation.

It is appreciated for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
