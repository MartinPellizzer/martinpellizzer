Mammillaria limonensis

Mammillaria limonensis, commonly known as the lemon mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of small, white to yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
