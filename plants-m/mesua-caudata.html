Mesua caudata

Mesua caudata, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving the tree a fiery appearance during the flowering season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. Mesua caudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
