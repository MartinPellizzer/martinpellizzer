Mildea

Mildea, commonly known as the "flower of the desert," is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments by storing water in its thick stems and leaves. Mildea typically blooms in spring or early summer, producing clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers.

Its medicinal properties have been utilized in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
