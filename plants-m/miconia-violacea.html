Miconia violacea

Miconia violacea, commonly known as the purple miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Miconia violacea is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
