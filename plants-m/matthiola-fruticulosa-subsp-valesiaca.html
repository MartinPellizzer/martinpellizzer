Matthiola fruticulosa subsp. valesiaca

Matthiola fruticulosa subsp.

valesiaca, commonly known as the Valesiaca stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb with compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

The plant features glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The flowers, which are typically pink or white, bloom in late spring to early summer.
