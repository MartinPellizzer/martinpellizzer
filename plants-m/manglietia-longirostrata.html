Manglietia longirostrata

Manglietia longirostrata, commonly known as the long-beaked manglietia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the wet forests of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the family Magnoliaceae, which includes some of the most ancient flowering plants. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
