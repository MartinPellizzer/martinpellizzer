Madhuca eriobrachyon

Madhuca eriobrachyon, commonly known as the broad-leaved tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, and it is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of tallow.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
