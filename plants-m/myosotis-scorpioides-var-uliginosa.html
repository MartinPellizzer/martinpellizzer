Myosotis scorpioides var. uliginosa

Myosotis scorpioides var. uliginosa, commonly known as the swamp forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and Asia.

It features small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments, such as marshes and pond edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
