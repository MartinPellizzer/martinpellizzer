Matthaea intermedia

Matthaea intermedia, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in forested or mountainous habitats. Its leaves are leathery and have a glossy appearance, while its fruit is a distinctive red berry that contains several seeds.

Matthaea intermedia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
