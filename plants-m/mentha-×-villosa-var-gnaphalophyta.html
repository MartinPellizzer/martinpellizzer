Mentha × villosa var. gnaphalophyta

Mentha × villosa var. gnaphalophyta, commonly known as woolly mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, often found in woodlands or along stream banks. Its fragrant leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its aromatic properties.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in culinary applications compared to other mint species.
