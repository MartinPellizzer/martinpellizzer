Mertensia papillosa

Mertensia papillosa, commonly known as the fuzzy bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of sky-blue, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Mertensia papillosa is a perennial herb that plays an important role in supporting local pollinators.
