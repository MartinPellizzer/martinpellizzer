Manilkara kanosiensis

Manilkara kanosiensis, commonly known as the Kanosi nut or Brazilian nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into woody capsules containing edible seeds. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine.
