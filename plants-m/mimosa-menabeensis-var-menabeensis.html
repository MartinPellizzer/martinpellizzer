Mimosa menabeensis var. menabeensis

Mimosa menabeensis var. menabeensis, commonly known as the Madagascar mimosa, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is adapted to the dry, tropical climate of its native habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
