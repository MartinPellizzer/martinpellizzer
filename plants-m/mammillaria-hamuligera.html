Mammillaria hamuligera

Mammillaria hamuligera, commonly known as the hooked mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that prefers well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cactus collections.
