Myrtus guajava var. pyrifera

Myrtus guajava var. pyrifera, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of bright red berries that are edible for humans and birds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical areas. Its botanical features include smooth, reddish bark and a dense, shrub-like growth habit.

The species is also noted for its potential as a medicinal plant due to the presence of various bioactive compounds.
