Milium compressum

Milium compressum, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually pink or purple in color.

Milium compressum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
