Medicago beipinensis

Medicago beipinensis, commonly known as the Beipin clover, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and can be used for forage in certain regions.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution in semi-arid areas.
