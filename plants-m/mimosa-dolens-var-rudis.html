Mimosa dolens var. rudis

Mimosa dolens var. rudis, commonly known as the rough mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It features compound leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a rough texture due to the presence of stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
