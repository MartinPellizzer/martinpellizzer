Myrica esculenta var. sapida

Myrica esculenta var. sapida, commonly known as the Indian wax tree or Indian berry tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, waxy berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a distinctive glossy appearance.

The fruit is edible and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
