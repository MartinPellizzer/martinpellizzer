Marrubium goktschaicum

Marrubium goktschaicum, commonly known as the Goktschaic horehound, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates. It is often found in alpine meadows and is noted for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
