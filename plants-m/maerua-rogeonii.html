Maerua rogeonii

Maerua rogeonii, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Maerua rogeonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
