Mespilus arbutifolia var. erythrocarpa

Mespilus arbutifolia var. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, subacid fruits that ripen in autumn, turning a deep red color when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy green upper surface with a silvery underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
