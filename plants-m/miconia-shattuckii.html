Miconia shattuckii

Miconia shattuckii, commonly known as the Shattuck's miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically consumed by various bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
