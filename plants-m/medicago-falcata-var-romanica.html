Medicago falcata var. romanica

Medicago falcata var. romanica, commonly known as the Roman horseshoe vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in dry, rocky, or calcareous soils. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers and has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

This species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, slopes, and disturbed areas.
