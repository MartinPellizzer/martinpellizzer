Maxillaria ochroleuca var. ochroleuca

Maxillaria ochroleuca var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow-lipped orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces pseudobulbs that support its flowering structure. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange with darker markings, and they bloom during the dry season.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.
