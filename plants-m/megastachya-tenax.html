Megastachya tenax

Megastachya tenax, commonly known as the rigid spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Megastachya tenax plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various insects and small wildlife.
