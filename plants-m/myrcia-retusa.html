Myrcia retusa

Myrcia retusa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Myrcia retusa is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in various cultural practices.
