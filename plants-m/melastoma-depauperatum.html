Melastoma depauperatum

Melastoma depauperatum, commonly known as the poor man's melastoma, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky hillsides. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
