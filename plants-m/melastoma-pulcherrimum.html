Melastoma pulcherrimum

Melastoma pulcherrimum, commonly known as the beautyberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that are highly attractive to birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring.

Melastoma pulcherrimum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
