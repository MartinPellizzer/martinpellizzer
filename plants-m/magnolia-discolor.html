Magnolia discolor

Magnolia discolor, commonly known as the variegated magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate with a waxy texture and often display a variegated pattern of green and white. This species thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage and striking blooms make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
