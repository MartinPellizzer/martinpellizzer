Mammillaria waltheri

Mammillaria waltheri, commonly known as the Walther's pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and typically features white or pale blue flowers. The plant has spines that are often arranged in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Mammillaria waltheri is prized in horticulture for its compact size and ornamental value.
