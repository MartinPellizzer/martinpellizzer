Melanthium hybridum

Melanthium hybridum, commonly known as the hybrid meadow bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flower clusters that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often forms colonies through rhizomes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
