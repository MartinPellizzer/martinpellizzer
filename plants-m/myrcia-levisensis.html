Myrcia levisensis

Myrcia levisensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in understory layers of tropical forests.

Myrcia levisensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
