Maxillaria mutabilis

Maxillaria mutabilis, commonly known as the variable maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically fragrant and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced growers.
