Myristica fugax subsp. septentrionalis

Myristica fugax subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, oily seeds that are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits containing the valuable seeds. Its leaves are alternate, elliptic, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and dark brown.

The subspecies is distinguished by its geographical distribution and specific morphological traits compared to other Myristica species.
