Miconia forzzae

Miconia forzzae, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright yellow fruits that resemble starfruits, though they are not related to the common starfruit. Miconia forzzae is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
