Mouriri myrtilloides subsp. orinocensis

Mouriri myrtilloides subsp.

orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco mouriri, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove.

The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures, containing several seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.
