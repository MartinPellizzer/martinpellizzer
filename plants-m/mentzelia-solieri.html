Mentzelia solieri

Mentzelia solieri, commonly known as desert bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with distinctive curling petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Mentzelia solieri is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a characteristic species of desert ecosystems.
