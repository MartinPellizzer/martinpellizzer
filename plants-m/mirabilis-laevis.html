Mirabilis laevis

Mirabilis laevis, commonly known as the smooth four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value contribute to its appeal in horticulture.
