Mentha suaveolens var. meduanensis

Mentha suaveolens var. meduanensis, commonly known as the Medean mint, is a variant of the sweet mint species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly peppery leaves and slender, creeping stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and herbal use.

This variety is valued for its distinct flavor and potential medicinal properties.
