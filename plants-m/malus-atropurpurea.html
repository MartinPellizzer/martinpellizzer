Malus atropurpurea

Malus atropurpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage, which turns crimson in the fall, and its showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in jams and jellies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Malus atropurpurea is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
