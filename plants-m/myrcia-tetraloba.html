Myrcia tetraloba

Myrcia tetraloba, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often considered a nuisance in some regions due to its invasive growth.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is known for its aromatic oils and medicinal properties.
