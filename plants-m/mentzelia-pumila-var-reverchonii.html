Mentzelia pumila var. reverchonii

Mentzelia pumila var. reverchonii, commonly known as the smallflower bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or arid environments.

Mentzelia pumila var. reverchonii is adapted to full sun and has a deep root system to access water in dry conditions.
