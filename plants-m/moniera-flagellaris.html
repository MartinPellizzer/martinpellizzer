Moniera flagellaris

Moniera flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate moss, is a unique species of liverwort that exhibits specialized motility.

It possesses flagella, which allow it to move through aquatic or moist environments in search of nutrients. The plant has a thalloid structure, lacking true roots, stems, or leaves, and is typically found in damp, shaded habitats. Its reproductive structures are simple, involving spore-producing capsules that release spores into the air.

Moniera flagellaris plays a minor but ecologically significant role in nutrient cycling within its microhabitat.
