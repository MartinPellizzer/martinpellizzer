Marattia alata var. laevis

Marattia alata var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth broad-leafed fern, is a tropical fern species characterized by its large, bipinnate fronds.

The fronds are typically broad and have a smooth, leathery texture, with an overall appearance that is both ornamental and distinctive. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping rhizomes that can form dense colonies.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic value in shaded garden settings.
