Maxillaria microblephara

Maxillaria microblephara, commonly known as the "Lilac Orchid," is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy pseudobulbs. The plant produces fragrant, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, narrow lip that is often marked with a pale stripe. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are arranged in two rows.

This orchid thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
