Miconia rhomboidea

Miconia rhomboidea, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, giving them a glossy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in some regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires careful management to prevent ecological harm.
