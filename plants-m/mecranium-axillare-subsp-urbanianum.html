Mecranium axillare subsp. urbanianum

Mecranium axillare subsp.

urbanianum, commonly known as the urbanian wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in disturbed or urban environments.

The plant features upright stems with small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its adaptability in harsh conditions.

The subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in compacted soils, making it a resilient choice for urban landscaping.
