Masdevallia brenneri

Masdevallia brenneri, commonly known as the Brenner's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces intricate, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms during the cooler months, and its leaves are usually dark green and strap-like. It thrives in high humidity and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
