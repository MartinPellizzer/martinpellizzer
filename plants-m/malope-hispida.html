Malope hispida

Malope hispida, commonly known as the rough mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with prominent stamens that are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Malope hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
