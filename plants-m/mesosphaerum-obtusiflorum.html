Mesosphaerum obtusiflorum

Mesosphaerum obtusiflorum, commonly known as the broad-flowered mesosphaerum, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape and its showy, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to white flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is typically found in forest understories. Its small, fleshy fruits are dispersed by wind, and it plays a vital role in its ecosystem as a food source for local wildlife.

Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
