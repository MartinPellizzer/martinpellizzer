Monocardia lilacina

Monocardia lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered monocardia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking lilac-pink bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence, while its inflorescence consists of clustered bracts that enclose small, tubular flowers.

Monocardia lilacina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded or partially sunny gardens.
