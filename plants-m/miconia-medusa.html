Miconia medusa

Miconia medusa, commonly known as the "devil's apple," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in many regions.

The plant's botanical features contribute to its ecological impact and make it a subject of both scientific study and concern.
