Masdevallia condorensis

Masdevallia condorensis, commonly known as the Condor orchid, is a rare and exquisite member of the orchid family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flower spikes that bear showy, often brightly colored blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high altitudes, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, pink, and purple hues, with intricate patterns that enhance its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
