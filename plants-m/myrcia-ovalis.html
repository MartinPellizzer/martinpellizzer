Myrcia ovalis

Myrcia ovalis, commonly known as the oval myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia ovalis is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
