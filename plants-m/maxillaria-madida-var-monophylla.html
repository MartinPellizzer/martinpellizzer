Maxillaria madida var. monophylla

Maxillaria madida var. monophylla, commonly known as the Madida orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically features a single, erect stem that bears several flowers, each with a prominent, velvety lip. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy texture, and it prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
