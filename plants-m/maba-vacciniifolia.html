Maba vacciniifolia

Maba vacciniifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf maba, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses.
