Mertensia ciliata var. ciliata

Mertensia ciliata var. ciliata, commonly known as the Carolina bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system and is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
