Miconia montana

Miconia montana, commonly known as the mountain miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a round, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Miconia montana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and is known for its rapid growth rate.
