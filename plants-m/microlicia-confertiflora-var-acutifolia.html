Microlicia confertiflora var. acutifolia

Microlicia confertiflora var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved microlicia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree with lanceolate, acuminate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with showy, pale pink or white petals. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
