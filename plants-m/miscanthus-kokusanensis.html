Miscanthus kokusanensis

Miscanthus kokusanensis, commonly known as the Japanese silver grass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-green plumes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens and landscapes.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential as a bioenergy crop due to its high biomass yield.
