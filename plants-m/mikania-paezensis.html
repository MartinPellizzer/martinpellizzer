Mikania paezensis

Mikania paezensis, commonly known as the Andean Mikania, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Mikania paezensis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable subject in ecological and horticultural studies.
