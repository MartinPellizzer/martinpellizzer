Monstera steyermarkii

Monstera steyermarkii, commonly known as the Swiss cheese plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop distinctive fenestrations or holes as they mature. The plant has a vining growth habit and can reach significant heights if provided with adequate support. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits.

Monstera steyermarkii is popular as a houseplant due to its striking foliage and air-purifying qualities.
