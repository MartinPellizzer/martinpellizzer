Miconia tinifolia var. roraimensis

Miconia tinifolia var. roraimensis, commonly known as the Roraima miconia, is a species native to the Amazon Basin, particularly in Guyana, Venezuela, and Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. It produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The species is also noted for its potential ecological significance in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
