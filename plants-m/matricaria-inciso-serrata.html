Matricaria inciso-serrata

Matricaria inciso-serrata, commonly known as the Italian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant features finely divided, serrated leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal teas. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Its essential oil, derived from the flowers, is valued for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
