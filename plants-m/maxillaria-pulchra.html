Maxillaria pulchra

Maxillaria pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often forming a rosette shape.

Maxillaria pulchra thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
