Myrcia heringii

Myrcia heringii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Myrcia heringii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and year-round green appearance.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
