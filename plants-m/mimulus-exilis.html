Mimulus exilis

Mimulus exilis, commonly known as the slender monkeyflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Mimulus exilis has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
