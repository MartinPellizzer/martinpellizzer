Monophyllaea elongata

Monophyllaea elongata, commonly known as the longleaf monophyllaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
