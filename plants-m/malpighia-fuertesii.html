Malpighia fuertesii

Malpighia fuertesii, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple to red berries that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and white or pink.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
