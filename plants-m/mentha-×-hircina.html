Mentha × hircina

Mentha × hircina, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid mint species resulting from the cross between *Mentha arvensis* and *Mentha longifolia*.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often a deep green with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorls along the stem, typical of the Lamiaceae family. Mentha × hircina is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is widely cultivated for its aromatic properties and is known to have various medicinal and recreational uses.
