Mayepea spicata

Mayepea spicata, commonly known as the spike mayepea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is often dispersed by wind. Mayepea spicata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique floral structure makes it an attractive addition to botanical gardens and a subject of interest for researchers studying plant pollination.
