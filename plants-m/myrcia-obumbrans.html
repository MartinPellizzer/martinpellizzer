Myrcia obumbrans

Myrcia obumbrans, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red when ripe, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia obumbrans is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions, though it can become invasive in some areas.
