Masdevallia lata

Masdevallia lata, commonly known as the broad-leaf masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple, and bloom during the cooler months.

Masdevallia lata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
