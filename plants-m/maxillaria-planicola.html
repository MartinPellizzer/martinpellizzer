Maxillaria planicola

Maxillaria planicola, commonly known as the flat-leaf maxillaria, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
