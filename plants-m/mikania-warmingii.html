Mikania warmingii

Mikania warmingii, commonly known as the Chinese devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing stems. The plant features heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered an invasive species in many regions outside its native habitat.
