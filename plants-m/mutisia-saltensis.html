Mutisia saltensis

Mutisia saltensis, commonly known as the saltbush mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss.

Mutisia saltensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, saline soils.
