Magnolia dolichogyna

Magnolia dolichogyna, commonly known as the long-leaf magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a smooth, gray bark and can grow up to 30 meters tall, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically 10 to 15 centimeters in diameter and are borne on long, slender pedicels.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
