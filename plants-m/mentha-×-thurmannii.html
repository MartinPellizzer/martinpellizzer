Mentha × thurmannii

Mentha × thurmannii, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

Mentha × thurmannii is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a stimulant for cats.
