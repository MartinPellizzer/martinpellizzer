Melocactus pedernalensis

Melocactus pedernalensis, commonly known as the Pedernalensis Melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its distinctive, globular shape and a spiny, ribbed structure that helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces a single, large, yellowish or reddish flower that blooms at the top of the stem during the warmer months. Its thick, fleshy stem stores water, allowing it to survive prolonged droughts.

The cactus is also noted for its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
