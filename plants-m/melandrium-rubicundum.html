Melandrium rubicundum

Melandrium rubicundum, commonly known as the red-fruited catchfly, is a herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, red, hairy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Melandrium rubicundum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
