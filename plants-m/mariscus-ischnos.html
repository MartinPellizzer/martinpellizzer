Mariscus ischnos

Mariscus ischnos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mariscus, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or limestone habitats. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is critically endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for ecological and botanical research.
