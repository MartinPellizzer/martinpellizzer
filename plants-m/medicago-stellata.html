Medicago stellata

Medicago stellata, commonly known as star vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

Medicago stellata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
