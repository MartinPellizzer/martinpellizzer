Melastoma mutabile

Melastoma mutabile, commonly known as the variable melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
