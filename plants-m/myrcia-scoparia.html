Myrcia scoparia

Myrcia scoparia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and showy red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Myrcia scoparia is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
