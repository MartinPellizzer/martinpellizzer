Moringa polygona

Moringa polygona, commonly known as the African horseradish, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers arranged in panicles. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties due to the presence of various bioactive compounds.

Moringa polygona is also valued for its nutritional content, particularly in its leaves, which are rich in vitamins and minerals.
