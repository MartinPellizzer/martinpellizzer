Masdevallia pygmaea

Masdevallia pygmaea, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms throughout the year in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a prized plant among orchid enthusiasts.
