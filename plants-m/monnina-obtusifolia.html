Monnina obtusifolia

Monnina obtusifolia, commonly known as bluntleaf monnina, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Monnina obtusifolia is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed areas, and along roadsides.
