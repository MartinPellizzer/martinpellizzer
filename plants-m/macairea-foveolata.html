Macairea foveolata

Macairea foveolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Macairea foveolata is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
