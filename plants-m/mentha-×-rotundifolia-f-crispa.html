Mentha × rotundifolia f. crispa

Mentha × rotundifolia f. crispa, commonly known as the wrinkled mint, is a horticultural form of a hybrid mint species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled, and often curled leaves, which give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading perennial with a strong aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its visual appeal and fragrance.
