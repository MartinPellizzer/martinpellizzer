Malvastrum alexandri

Malvastrum alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny areas such as rocky soils and coastal cliffs. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
