Melaleuca sphaerodendra var. microphylla

Melaleuca sphaerodendra var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native gardens.
