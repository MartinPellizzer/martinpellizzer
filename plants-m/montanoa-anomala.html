Montanoa anomala

Montanoa anomala, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Montanoa anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
