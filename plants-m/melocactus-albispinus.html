Melocactus albispinus

Melocactus albispinus, commonly known as the white-spined melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces white, spiny areolas that are covered with short, white bristles. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in the evening.

Melocactus albispinus is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water and nutrient requirements.
