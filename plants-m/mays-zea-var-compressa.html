Mays zea var. compressa

Mays zea var. compressa, commonly known as the compressed maize, is a variant of maize characterized by its compact growth habit and unique morphological features.

This plant exhibits a shorter stature compared to typical maize, with tightly packed leaves and a more restricted inflorescence. Its botanical significance lies in its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications. The compressed form may offer advantages in space efficiency and resilience to environmental stressors.

It is often studied for its role in plant genetics and breeding programs.
