Mariscus foliosissimus

Mariscus foliosissimus, commonly known as the leafy marsh plant, is a rare and delicate species native to specific wetland habitats in southern Europe.

It is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves that form dense, low-growing mats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat loss.
