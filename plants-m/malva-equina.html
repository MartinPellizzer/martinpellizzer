Malva equina

Malva equina, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known species like cotton and hibiscus. Malva equina is often found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of fiber.
