Munychia brachyglossa

Munychia brachyglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed munychia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in shaded woodlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom in the spring.

Munychia brachyglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
