Microstylis umbraticola

Microstylis umbraticola, commonly known as the shadowy microstylis, is a rare flowering plant native to the dense, shaded understories of tropical rainforests.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pale green to blend with its surroundings. The plant has adapted to low-light conditions through elongated leaves that maximize light absorption. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing nutrient uptake in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to habitat destruction, Microstylis umbraticola is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
