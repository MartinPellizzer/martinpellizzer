Maxillaria sessilis

Maxillaria sessilis, commonly known as the sessile maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its short, horizontal rhizomes. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Maxillaria sessilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a greenhouse or warm, humid environment.
