Michelia citrata

Michelia citrata, commonly known as the lemon blossom or golden michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, elliptical berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

Michelia citrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
