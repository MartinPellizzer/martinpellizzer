Macropiper timothianum

Macropiper timothianum, commonly known as the Timothian pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, thin stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in terminal spikes. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
