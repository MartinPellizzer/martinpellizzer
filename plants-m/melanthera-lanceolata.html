Melanthera lanceolata

Melanthera lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Melanthera lanceolata is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
