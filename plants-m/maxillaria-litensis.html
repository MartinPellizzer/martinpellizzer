Maxillaria litensis

Maxillaria litensis, commonly known as the Litens Orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stems.

Maxillaria litensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
