Marica herbertiana

Marica herbertiana, commonly known as Herbert's marica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular florets arranged in a composite head.

Marica herbertiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
