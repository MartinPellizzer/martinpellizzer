Mesona

Mesona, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. Mesona is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
