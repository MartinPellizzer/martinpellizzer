Millettia canariifolia

Millettia canariifolia, commonly known as the Canary Island ebony, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain flat, lens-shaped seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in the production of musical instruments and fine furniture.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered an endangered species.
