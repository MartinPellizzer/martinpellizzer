Myrciaria longipes var. opaca

Myrciaria longipes var. opaca, commonly known as the opaca myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are glossy and often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its potential use in traditional medicine.
