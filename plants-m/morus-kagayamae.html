Morus kagayamae

Morus kagayamae, commonly known as the Kagayama mulberry, is a species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous tree that belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the common mulberry and fig. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The Kagayama mulberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional Japanese horticulture.
