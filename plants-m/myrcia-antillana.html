Myrcia antillana

Myrcia antillana, commonly known as the Caribbean myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Myrcia antillana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and traditional medicine.
