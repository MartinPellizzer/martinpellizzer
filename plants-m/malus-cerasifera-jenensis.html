Malus cerasifera jenensis

Malus cerasifera jenensis, commonly known as the Jenish crabapple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and colorful fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white or pink and appear in clusters.

The fruit is a small, rounded pome that ripens in late summer, adding to its horticultural appeal.
