Melinis repens subsp. grandiflora

Melinis repens subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and its production of showy, blue-purple flower clusters.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
