Macbrideina

Macbrideina, commonly known as the Macbride's orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plants are characterized by their showy, often fragrant flowers with intricate petal structures. They typically grow as epiphytes or terrestrial plants, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Macbrideina species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
