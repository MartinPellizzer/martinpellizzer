Mikania violifolia

Mikania violifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often forming a mist-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

Mikania violifolia is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species in its native range.
