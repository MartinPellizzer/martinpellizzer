Melaleuca violacea var. petiolata

Melaleuca violacea var. petiolata, commonly known as the purple bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped inflorescences that are typically purple or pink in color. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
