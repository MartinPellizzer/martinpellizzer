Montia calycina

Montia calycina, commonly known as the California watercress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in wet meadows.

Montia calycina is often used in herbal remedies and is valued for its peppery, aromatic leaves.
