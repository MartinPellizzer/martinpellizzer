Monteverdia comocladiiformis

Monteverdia comocladiiformis, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of oak trees in the Neotropics.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually green but may turn reddish in certain conditions. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, sticky coating to aid in dispersal by birds. Its roots form haustoria that attach to the host tree's vascular system, allowing it to draw water and nutrients.

Despite its parasitic nature, Monteverdia comocladiiformis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other wildlife.
