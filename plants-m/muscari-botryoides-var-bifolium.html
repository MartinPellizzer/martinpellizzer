Muscari botryoides var. bifolium

Muscari botryoides var. bifolium, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean and Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its fragrant, clustered blooms and its ability to naturalize over time.
