Meconopsis staintonii

Meconopsis staintonii, commonly known as the Stainton's blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Meconopsis staintonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with high altitude conditions.
