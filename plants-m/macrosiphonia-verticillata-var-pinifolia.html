Macrosiphonia verticillata var. pinifolia

Macrosiphonia verticillata var. pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf liverwort, is a non-vascular plant belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, lobed leaves that are often arranged in a whorl around the stem. This liverwort typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as gametophytes, are usually found on the underside of the leaves.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitats for various small organisms.
