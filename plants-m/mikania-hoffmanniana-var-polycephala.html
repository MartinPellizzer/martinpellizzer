Mikania hoffmanniana var. polycephala

Mikania hoffmanniana var. polycephala, commonly known as the hairy beggar's ticks, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its spread in various habitats. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an invasive species in some regions.
