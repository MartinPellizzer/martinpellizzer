Malva italica

Malva italica, commonly known as the Italian mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cotton and hibiscus. The plant features broad, velvety leaves and produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its fibrous stems and mucilaginous leaves have been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
