Morus pumila

Morus pumila, commonly known as the dwarf mulberry, is a small, deciduous tree or shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often used as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability.

Additionally, it serves as a food source for birds and small mammals.
