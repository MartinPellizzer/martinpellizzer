Matthiola angelica

Matthiola angelica, commonly known as angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its large, showy, funnel-shaped flowers that can be white, yellow, or pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has hairy, upright stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Matthiola angelica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
