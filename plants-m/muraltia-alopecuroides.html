Muraltia alopecuroides

Muraltia alopecuroides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces bright red, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.
