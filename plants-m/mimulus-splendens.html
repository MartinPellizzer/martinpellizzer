Mimulus splendens

Mimulus splendens, commonly known as the splendour monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange or yellow flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats or rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mimulus splendens is a member of the Physiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
