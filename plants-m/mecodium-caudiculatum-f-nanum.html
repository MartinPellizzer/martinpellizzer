Mecodium caudiculatum f. nanum

Mecodium caudiculatum f. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf mecodium, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features a short, swollen stem known as a caudiculum, which stores water and nutrients. The plant produces small, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, and it blooms with yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
