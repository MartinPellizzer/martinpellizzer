Mikania cinerea

Mikania cinerea, commonly known as the white hemp vine, is a perennial vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, slender stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, disturbed habitats and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Mikania cinerea is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its rapid growth and invasive tendencies in some regions.
