Montia campylostigma

Montia campylostigma, commonly known as the narrowleaf montia, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It typically features slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are actinomorphic and have five petals, typical of the Montiaceae family.

The species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along streambanks.
