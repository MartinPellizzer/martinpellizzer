Mentzelia hirsutissima var. nesiotes

Mentzelia hirsutissima var. nesiotes, commonly known as the desert bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy, often grayish-green leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important species in its native desert ecosystems.
