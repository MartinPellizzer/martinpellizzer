Mentha arvensis var. sachalinensis

Mentha arvensis var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Siberian mint, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
