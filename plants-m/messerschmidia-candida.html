Messerschmidia candida

Messerschmidia candida, commonly known as the white-flowered rock orchid, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. The plant typically grows on rocky slopes and limestone soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
