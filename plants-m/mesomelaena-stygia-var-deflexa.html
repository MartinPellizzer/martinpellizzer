Mesomelaena stygia var. deflexa

Mesomelaena stygia var. deflexa, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and delicate, pale green or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them, relying instead on air and rain for sustenance. Its unique morphology and elusive nature make it a subject of fascination among botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, it is considered critically endangered in the wild.
