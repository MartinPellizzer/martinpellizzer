Malaxis umbonata

Malaxis umbonata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a spurred lip that resembles a hood. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually small and pale green or white, with a distinctive, elongated spur.

Malaxis umbonata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, exotic appearance.
