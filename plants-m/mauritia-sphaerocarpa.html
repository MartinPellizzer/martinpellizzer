Mauritia sphaerocarpa

Mauritia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It produces a fruit that resembles a pineapple in appearance, with a spiky, green exterior and a sweet, aromatic interior. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

The botanical features of Mauritia sphaerocarpa make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and ecological contexts.
