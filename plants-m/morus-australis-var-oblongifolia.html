Morus australis var. oblongifolia

Morus australis var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically oblong and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, dark purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
