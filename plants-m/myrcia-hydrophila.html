Myrcia hydrophila

Myrcia hydrophila, commonly known as the water myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia hydrophila is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
