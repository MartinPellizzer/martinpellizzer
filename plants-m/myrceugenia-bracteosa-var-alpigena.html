Myrceugenia bracteosa var. alpigena

Myrceugenia bracteosa var. alpigena, commonly known as the alpine myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that contain a single seed.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.
