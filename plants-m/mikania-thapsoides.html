Mikania thapsoides

Mikania thapsoides, commonly known as goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It is often considered an invasive species in regions outside its native range.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates contributes to its widespread distribution.
