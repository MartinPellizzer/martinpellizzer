Menodora hassleriana

Menodora hassleriana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Menodoridae and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is noted for its nocturnal blooming habit, making it a unique and striking plant in its native habitat.
