Melica ciliata var. laxiflora

Melica ciliata var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
