Mimosa hemiendyta

Mimosa hemiendyta, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties. The plant features small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple pairs of leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance.

The plant is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
