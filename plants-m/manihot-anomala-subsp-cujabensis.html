Manihot anomala subsp. cujabensis

Manihot anomala subsp.

cujabensis, commonly known as arrowleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antidiabetic effects.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
