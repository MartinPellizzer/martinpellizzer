Myrtus jambos

Myrtus jambos, commonly known as the Java myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are dark purple berries that contain several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
