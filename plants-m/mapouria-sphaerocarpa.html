Mapouria sphaerocarpa

Mapouria sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-fig or African star fruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when ripe. Its fruit is notable for its distinctive star-shaped appearance when cut crosswise.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
