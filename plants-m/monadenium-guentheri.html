Monadenium guentheri

Monadenium guentheri, commonly known as the Guenther's milkweed, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with opposite, fleshy, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to store water in its leaves and stems, allowing it to survive in drought conditions.

Monadenium guentheri is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in dry environments.
