Melanthera amellus var. subhastata

Melanthera amellus var. subhastata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers in the summer. Its flowering period extends from July to September, and it is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
