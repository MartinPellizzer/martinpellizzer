Monarda fistulosa subsp. menthifolia

Monarda fistulosa subsp.

menthifolia, commonly known as lemon bee balm, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It features showy, tubular flowers that range from pink to red, arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a mint-like scent, which gives it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
