Myrcia ramulosa var. acutata

Myrcia ramulosa var. acutata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved myrcia, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its elliptical, acuminate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that mature in the dry season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
