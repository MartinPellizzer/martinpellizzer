Micrampelis coulteri

Micrampelis coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's climbing fig, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a yellowish color and is edible.
