Mentha suaveolens subsp. insularis

Mentha suaveolens subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the island mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it forms dense mats.

Its essential oils, which contain menthone and other compounds, make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
