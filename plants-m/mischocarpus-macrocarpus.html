Mischocarpus macrocarpus

Mischocarpus macrocarpus, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, woody fruits that contain the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by specific insects. Its fruit develops over several months and can weigh up to several kilograms when mature.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its habitat and is valued for its economic and nutritional contributions.
