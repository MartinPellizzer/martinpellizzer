Mecodium montanum

Mecodium montanum, commonly known as the mountain mecodium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. The plant has a compact growth habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white or pale purple, and bloom in early spring.

Mecodium montanum is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
