Miconia aptera

Miconia aptera, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. Miconia aptera is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it an invasive species in some areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land managers.
