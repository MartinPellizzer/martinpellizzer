Medinilla javanica

Medinilla javanica, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or Javan medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds and other animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Medinilla javanica is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
