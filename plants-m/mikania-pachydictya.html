Mikania pachydictya

Mikania pachydictya, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found growing along fences, trees, and other structures. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in many areas.
