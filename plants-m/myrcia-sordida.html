Myrcia sordida

Myrcia sordida, commonly known as black myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature.

Myrcia sordida is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
