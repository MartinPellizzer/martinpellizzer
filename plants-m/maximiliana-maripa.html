Maximiliana maripa

Maximiliana maripa, commonly known as the yellow mangrove, is a tropical tree native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant thrives in brackish or saline water, making it an important species in mangrove ecosystems. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by elongated, woody fruits.

This species plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
