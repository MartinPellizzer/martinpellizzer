Melothria nigra

Melothria nigra, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, black berries that are toxic to humans but are used in traditional medicine in some cultures. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, with a distinctive scent, while its flowers are typically white or purple.

Melothria nigra thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
