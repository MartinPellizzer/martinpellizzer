Medinilla mandrakensis

Medinilla mandrakensis, commonly known as the Madagascar velvet tangerine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, orange fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Medinilla mandrakensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
