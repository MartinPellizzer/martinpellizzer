Medinilla rubripes

Medinilla rubripes, commonly known as the red-flowered medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with prominent stamens that add to their ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

The plant is also valued for its ability to produce edible fruits, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.
