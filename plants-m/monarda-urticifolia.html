Monarda urticifolia

Monarda urticifolia, commonly known as stinging gianthyssop, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, typically blooming from midsummer to fall. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs that can cause a stinging sensation upon contact, similar to nettle.

This plant is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
