Miconia christophoriana

Miconia christophoriana, commonly known as the glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and floral display.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions, where it can outcompete native vegetation.
