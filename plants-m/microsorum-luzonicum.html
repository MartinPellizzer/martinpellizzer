Microsorum luzonicum

Microsorum luzonicum, commonly known as the Luzon water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that form a dense mat on the water surface. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, slender rhizomes that help anchor it in aquatic environments. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with small, linear pinnae that contribute to its ornamental appearance.

Microsorum luzonicum is often used in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.
