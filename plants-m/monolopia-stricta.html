Monolopia stricta

Monolopia stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf duckweed, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It forms dense mats on the surface of freshwater bodies, such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has small, oval leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length, with a smooth, waxy surface. It reproduces vegetatively through the production of floating buds, which detach and grow into new plants.

Monolopia stricta is often used in water gardens and as a bioindicator for water quality due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
