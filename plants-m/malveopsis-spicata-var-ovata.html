Malveopsis spicata var. ovata

Malveopsis spicata var. ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
