Mimulus ocellatus

Mimulus ocellatus, commonly known as the spotted monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, orange-yellow flowers with a distinctive black spot at the base of each petal. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Mimulus ocellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also important for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
