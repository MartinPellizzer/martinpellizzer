Melanthera hastata var. lobata

Melanthera hastata var. lobata, commonly known as the broad-lobed ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, lobed leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and range in color from white to pale purple, blooming in late summer.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
