Malus × dawsoniana

Malus × dawsoniana, commonly known as the Stewart crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, tart apples that are often used in culinary or medicinal applications. It typically grows to a height of 6–12 meters and has a rounded, compact form.

The leaves are oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a dark green color during the growing season.
