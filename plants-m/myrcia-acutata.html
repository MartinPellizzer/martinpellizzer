Myrcia acutata

Myrcia acutata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and moist forests.

Myrcia acutata is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
