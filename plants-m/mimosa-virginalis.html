Mimosa virginalis

Mimosa virginalis, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa virginalis is often used in ornamental gardening for its graceful foliage and seasonal blooms.
