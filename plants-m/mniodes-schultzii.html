Mniodes schultzii

Mniodes schultzii, commonly known as the Schultze's mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and broad, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
