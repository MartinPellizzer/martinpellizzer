Mentha suaveolens var. glabrescens

Mentha suaveolens var. glabrescens, commonly known as sweet mint, is a variant of the mint species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and slender, aromatic stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
