Maesa dioica

Maesa dioica, commonly known as the two-fruit maesa, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. It produces two types of fruits—small, fleshy berries and larger, woody capsules—hence its common name.

Maesa dioica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
