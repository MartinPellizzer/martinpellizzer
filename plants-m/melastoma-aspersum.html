Melastoma aspersum

Melastoma aspersum, commonly known as the rough melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a rough, pubescent texture. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central column.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.
