Merremia xanthophylla

Merremia xanthophylla, commonly known as the yellow-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Merremia xanthophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
