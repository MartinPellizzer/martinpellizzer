Mimosa tarda

Mimosa tarda, commonly known as the late mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its flowers typically bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mimosa tarda is also notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
