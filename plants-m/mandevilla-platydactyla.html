Mandevilla platydactyla

Mandevilla platydactyla, commonly known as the broad-fingered mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

Mandevilla platydactyla is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and hanging baskets due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
