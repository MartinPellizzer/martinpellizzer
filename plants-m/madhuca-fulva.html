Madhuca fulva

Madhuca fulva, commonly known as the yellow madhuca, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy berries. Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife, including birds and mammals.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
