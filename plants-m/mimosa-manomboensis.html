Mimosa manomboensis

Mimosa manomboensis, commonly known as the Madagascar mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
