Moluccella frutescens

Moluccella frutescens, commonly known as the dwarf bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming from late winter to early spring. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are generally short and branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
