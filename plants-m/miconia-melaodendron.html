Miconia melaodendron

Miconia melaodendron, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and covered with a dense layer of fine, silky hairs on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by distinctive, fleshy, red or purple berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Miconia melaodendron is often found in humid, lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in non-native regions.
