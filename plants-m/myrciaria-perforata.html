Myrciaria perforata

Myrciaria perforata, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
