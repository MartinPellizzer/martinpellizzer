Myxopyrum cordatum

Myxopyrum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf myxopyrum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette, with a distinctive heart-shaped base on some of the lower leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Myxopyrum cordatum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
