Myristica guillauminiana

Myristica guillauminiana, commonly known as the Guillaumin's myristica, is a tropical plant belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and produces fragrant, woody fruits that contain aromatic seeds. The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and have a distinct, spicy aroma.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
