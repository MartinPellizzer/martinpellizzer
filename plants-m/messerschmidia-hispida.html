Messerschmidia hispida

Messerschmidia hispida, commonly known as the hispid bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features hairy, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
