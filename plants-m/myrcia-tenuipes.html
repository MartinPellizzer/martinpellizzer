Myrcia tenuipes

Myrcia tenuipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia tenuipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
