Myrtus communis var. eusebii

Myrtus communis var. eusebii, commonly known as the Eusebius myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces tiny white flowers in spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice in horticulture.
