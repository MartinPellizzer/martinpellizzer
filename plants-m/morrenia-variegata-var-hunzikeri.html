Morrenia variegata var. hunzikeri

Morrenia variegata var. hunzikeri, commonly known as the Hunziker's variegated morrenia, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
