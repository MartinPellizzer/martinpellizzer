Myrciaria pallida

Myrciaria pallida, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical forests.

Myrciaria pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
