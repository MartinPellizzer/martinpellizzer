Mimulus breviflorus

Mimulus breviflorus, commonly known as the short-flowered monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange or yellow flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and meadows. Mimulus breviflorus has small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems.

It is a perennial species that plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems.
