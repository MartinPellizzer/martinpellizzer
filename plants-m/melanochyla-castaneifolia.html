Melanochyla castaneifolia

Melanochyla castaneifolia, commonly known as the horse chestnut tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces spiny, chestnut-like fruits that contain a single seed. Its bark is typically smooth and light gray, with a tendency to develop fissures as it matures.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been widely cultivated in gardens and urban landscapes.
