Malaxis calophylla

Malaxis calophylla, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the soil, and it has a symbiotic relationship with certain fungi.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
