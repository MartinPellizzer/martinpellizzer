Maxillaria schultesii

Maxillaria schultesii, commonly known as the Schultes' orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces pseudobulbs that bear leaves and flower spikes, with flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maxillaria schultesii is appreciated for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
