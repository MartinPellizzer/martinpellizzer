Magnolia × mirifolia

Magnolia × mirifolia, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia or saucer magnolia, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant typically grows to a height of 8–12 meters and has a broad, rounded canopy. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth texture and dark green color.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
