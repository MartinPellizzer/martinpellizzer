Morus kaempferi

Morus kaempferi, commonly known as the Japanese white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and smooth, white bark. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically green when mature. It is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in sericulture, and for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.

The tree is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
