Manihot tripartita subsp. indivisa

Manihot tripartita subsp.

indivisa, commonly known as the arrowleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are palmately divided into three broad, arrowhead-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
