Malus × purpurea f. pendula

Malus × purpurea f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree characterized by its distinctive pendulous branches.

It features small, showy flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of white or pink, followed by edible fruits that ripen in the fall. The plant is native to China and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Its graceful, drooping form makes it a popular choice for adding visual interest to landscapes.
