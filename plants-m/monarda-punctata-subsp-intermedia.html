Monarda punctata subsp. intermedia

Monarda punctata subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple, arranged in a conical shape at the top of each stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its nectar attracts bees, butterflies, and other pollinators, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
