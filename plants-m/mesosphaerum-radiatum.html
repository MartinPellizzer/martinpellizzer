Mesosphaerum radiatum

Mesosphaerum radiatum, commonly known as the star-shaped mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a star-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Mesosphaerum radiatum is native to parts of Africa and is often found in forested areas where it relies on its host for nutrients.
