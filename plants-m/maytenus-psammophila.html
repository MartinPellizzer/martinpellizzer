Maytenus psammophila

Maytenus psammophila, commonly known as the desert mayten, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and woody stems that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh environmental conditions.

Maytenus psammophila plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
