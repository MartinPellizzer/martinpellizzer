Mammillaria centricirrha f. gladiata

Mammillaria centricirrha f. gladiata, commonly known as the gladiate mammillaria, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among experienced cactus growers.
