Mammillaria pugionacantha

Mammillaria pugionacantha, commonly known as the sharp-toothed hedgehog cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular shape with a ribbed structure and produces yellow flowers during the spring. The plant has a dense covering of white to yellow hairs, which help protect it from intense sunlight. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
