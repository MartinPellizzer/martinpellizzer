Machaerium hatschbachii

Machaerium hatschbachii, commonly known as the golden senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bright yellow flowers, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for warm climates.

Machaerium hatschbachii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
