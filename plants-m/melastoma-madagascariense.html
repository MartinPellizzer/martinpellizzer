Melastoma madagascariense

Melastoma madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple berries that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a rough texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually with five petals, and are often pink or white in color.

The fruit is edible for some animals but is generally not consumed by humans due to its bitter taste.
