Macrocarpaea revoluta

Macrocarpaea revoluta, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf balsam, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its growth habit is typically upright, with stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Macrocarpaea revoluta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
