Myrceugenia alpigena var. rufa

Myrceugenia alpigena var. rufa, commonly known as the red-fruited myrtle, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in the fall, attracting various bird species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
