Mediolobivia hirsutissima

Mediolobivia hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy leaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, which attract various pollinators. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
