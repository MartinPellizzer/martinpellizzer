Magnolia arroyoana

Magnolia arroyoana, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the central and southern regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blooms.
