Mammillaria greggii

Mammillaria greggii, commonly known as the Gregg's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a short, cylindrical stem and a crown of spines. The plant typically has white or light green flowers that bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Mammillaria greggii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
