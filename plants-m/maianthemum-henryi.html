Maianthemum henryi

Maianthemum henryi, commonly known as Henry's false lily of the valley, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Convallariaceae and is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit. The plant features rhizomatous stems that produce clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded woodland environments.
