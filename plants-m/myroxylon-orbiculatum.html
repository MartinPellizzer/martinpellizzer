Myroxylon orbiculatum

Myroxylon orbiculatum, commonly known as the Copaiba tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic bark and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its latex, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of copal resin. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats and is valued for its economic and medicinal properties.
