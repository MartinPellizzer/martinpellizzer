Mauria decandra

Mauria decandra, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody structure and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant's leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Mauria decandra is valued for its timber, which is highly prized for its durability and rich color.
