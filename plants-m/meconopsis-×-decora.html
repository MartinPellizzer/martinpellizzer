Meconopsis × decora

Meconopsis × decora, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a hybrid species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are showy and often attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Meconopsis × decora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens with specific care requirements.
