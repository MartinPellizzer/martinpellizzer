Malva arvensis

Malva arvensis, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features broad, velvety leaves with a distinctive palmate shape and produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals. The plant grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate regions. It has a taproot system and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The plant is known for its mucilage-rich stems, which have been used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.
