Muhlenbergia flavida strictior

Muhlenbergia flavida strictior, commonly known as the narrowleaf muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a yellowish hue. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of pale to golden flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
