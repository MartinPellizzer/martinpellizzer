Machaerium nervosum

Machaerium nervosum, commonly known as the narrow-veined machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, branching stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that have fine, parallel veins. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Machaerium nervosum is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
