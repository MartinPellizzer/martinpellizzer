Melocactus flammeus

Melocactus flammeus, commonly known as the flame cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, globular shape and vibrant red or orange coloration, which becomes more pronounced during the flowering season. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Melocactus flammeus is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
