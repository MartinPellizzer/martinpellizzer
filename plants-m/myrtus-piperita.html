Myrtus piperita

Myrtus piperita, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, dark purple berries and fragrant leaves. The plant produces tiny white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its distinctive berries. Myrtus piperita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

Its leaves and berries contain essential oils that contribute to its aromatic properties.
