Mesembryanthemum crassulinum

Mesembryanthemum crassulinum, commonly known as the thick-leaved ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. It is a member of the Aizoaceae family, known for its ability to withstand harsh conditions.

Mesembryanthemum crassulinum is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
