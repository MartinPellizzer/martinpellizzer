Millettia ripicola

Millettia ripicola, commonly known as the pink-flowered milk tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by bean-like pods containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.

Millettia ripicola is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
