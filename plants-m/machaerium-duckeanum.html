Machaerium duckeanum

Machaerium duckeanum, commonly known as the broadleaf machaerium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring trifoliate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale pink, arranged in dense spikes, and it produces legume-like pods that contain several seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along riverbanks.
