Myrciaria caricensis

Myrciaria caricensis, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to Brazil, particularly in the northeastern regions.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark red to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a woody stem with alternate, ovate leaves.

Its fruits are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine due to their unique flavor and nutritional value.
