Macfadyena citrifolia

Macfadyena citrifolia, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and can grow as a climbing vine or a shrub, depending on environmental conditions. Its fruit is a green, fleshy pod that contains several seeds, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
