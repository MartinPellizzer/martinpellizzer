Marsdenia araujacea

Marsdenia araujacea, commonly known as the Brazilian moonvine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a follicle containing numerous seeds, and it is often found in humid, tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
