Melicope pubescens var. typica

Melicope pubescens var. typica, commonly known as the hairy melicope, is a shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular and aromatic. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by dark, woody fruits containing elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
