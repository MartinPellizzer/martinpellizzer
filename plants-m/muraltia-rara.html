Muraltia rara

Muraltia rara, commonly known as the rare muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

Muraltia rara is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
