Malpighia reclinata

Malpighia reclinata, commonly known as the red mombin, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
