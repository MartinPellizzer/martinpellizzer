Miconia longistyla

Miconia longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, palmate leaves that can reach up to 50 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are typically pollinated by birds and insects. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous small seeds.

Miconia longistyla is often found in humid lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
