Myrtus sylvatica

Myrtus sylvatica, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in hedges or ornamental gardens.

Myrtus sylvatica is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
