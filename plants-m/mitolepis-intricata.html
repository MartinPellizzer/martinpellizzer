Mitolepis intricata

Mitolepis intricata, commonly known as the intricate leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fern-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance.

Mitolepis intricata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
