Mariscus dregeanus var. buchananii

Mariscus dregeanus var. buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's mariscus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is valued by both horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
