Mormodes incisa

Mormodes incisa, commonly known as the African orchid or the white orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal regions. The plant is characterized by its striking white flowers with a distinctive greenish-brown lip, which are often fragrant. It typically grows as a terrestrial herb with a short stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

Mormodes incisa prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
