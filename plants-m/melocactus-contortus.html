Melocactus contortus

Melocactus contortus, commonly known as the twisted melon cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiraling spines and a ribbed, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in desert gardens.
