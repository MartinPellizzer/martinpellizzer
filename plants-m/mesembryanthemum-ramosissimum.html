Mesembryanthemum ramosissimum

Mesembryanthemum ramosissimum, commonly known as the "Cape goosefoot," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, often hairy leaves that are adapted to store water, typical of arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and resilience.

Mesembryanthemum ramosissimum thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
