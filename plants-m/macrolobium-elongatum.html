Macrolobium elongatum

Macrolobium elongatum, commonly known as the long-leaf macrolobium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
