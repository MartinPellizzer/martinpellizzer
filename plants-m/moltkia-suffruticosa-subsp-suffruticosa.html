Moltkia suffruticosa subsp. suffruticosa

Moltkia suffruticosa subsp.

suffruticosa, commonly known as the redstem dogbane, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late summer, which are followed by cylindrical, red-brown seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat diversity.
