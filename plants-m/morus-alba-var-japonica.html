Morus alba var. japonica

Morus alba var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by woolly, spherical fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in sericulture.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in a variety of soil conditions.
