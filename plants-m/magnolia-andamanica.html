Magnolia andamanica

Magnolia andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman magnolia, is a species native to the Andaman Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, fragrant, white flowers. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
