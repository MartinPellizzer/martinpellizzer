Mariscus leptophyllus

Mariscus leptophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mariscus, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, which is typical for many plants in its ecological niche.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
