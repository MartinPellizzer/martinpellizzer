Monetia angustifolia

Monetia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf monetia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include hairy stems and fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
