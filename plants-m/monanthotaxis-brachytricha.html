Monanthotaxis brachytricha

Monanthotaxis brachytricha, commonly known as the short-haird leaf fern, is a rare tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit and small, leathery, lance-shaped fronds. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, typical of ferns, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
