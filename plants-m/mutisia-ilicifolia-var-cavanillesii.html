Mutisia ilicifolia var. cavanillesii

Mutisia ilicifolia var. cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that have a leathery texture. Its flowers are yellow and appear in dense clusters, making it an attractive ornamental plant.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often thriving in coastal or mountainous environments.
