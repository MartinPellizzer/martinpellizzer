Matricaria ceratophylloides

Matricaria ceratophylloides, commonly known as the German chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine. Its essential oil, extracted from the flowers, is widely used in aromatherapy and as a natural remedy.

The leaves and flowers are also used in teas and culinary applications.
