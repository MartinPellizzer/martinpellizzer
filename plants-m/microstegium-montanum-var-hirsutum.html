Microstegium montanum var. hirsutum

Microstegium montanum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy mountain dropwort, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, white to greenish flowers that appear in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural areas.
