Microtatorchis fasciola

Microtatorchis fasciola, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water fern, is a small aquatic plant that thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often floating or submerged, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention. The plant produces tiny, spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are typically located on the underside of the leaves. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize the substrate in its habitat.

Microtatorchis fasciola plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.
