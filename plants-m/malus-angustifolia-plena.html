Malus angustifolia plena

Malus angustifolia plena, commonly known as the double-flowering crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental, double-flowered blossoms that appear in early spring, adding a showy display to gardens. The tree has small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, contributing to its seasonal appeal. Its fruits are small, red or yellow apples that are not typically edible but are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

This plant is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in temperate climates.
