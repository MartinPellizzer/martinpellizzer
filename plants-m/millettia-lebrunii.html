Millettia lebrunii

Millettia lebrunii, commonly known as the lebrun's sutureless bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
