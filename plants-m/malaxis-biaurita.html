Malaxis biaurita

Malaxis biaurita, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed lip, which is a key feature in its identification. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually small and pale green or white, with a delicate, spurred shape.

Malaxis biaurita is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
