Mimulus puniceus

Mimulus puniceus, commonly known as the scarlet monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, such as cliffs and slopes. It has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

Mimulus puniceus is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
