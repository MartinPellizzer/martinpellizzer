Maxillaria christobalensis

Maxillaria christobalensis, commonly known as the Christobalensis orchid, is a species native to Central America, particularly found in countries like Costa Rica and Panama.

It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are typically fragrant, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale pink, often with a contrasting lip.

The botanical characteristics of Maxillaria christobalensis make it a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
