Madhuca neriifolia

Madhuca neriifolia, commonly known as the black-leaved mahua, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant produces edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for various wildlife species.
