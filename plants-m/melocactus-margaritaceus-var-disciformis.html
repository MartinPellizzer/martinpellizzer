Melocactus margaritaceus var. disciformis

Melocactus margaritaceus var. disciformis, commonly known as the Mexican pebble cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and a dense covering of small, white spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted coloration. Its slow growth rate and ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stem make it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

This variety is prized by collectors for its ornamental value and resilience.
