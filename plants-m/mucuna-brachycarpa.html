Mucuna brachycarpa

Mucuna brachycarpa, commonly known as the shortpod velvetbean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain several seeds. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as fields and forest edges.

Mucuna brachycarpa is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
