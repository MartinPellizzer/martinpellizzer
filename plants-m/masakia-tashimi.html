Masakia tashimi

Masakia tashimi, commonly known as the Japanese mountain cedar, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and reddish-brown bark. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions. Its cones are small and scale-like, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Masakia tashimi is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Japanese gardens.
