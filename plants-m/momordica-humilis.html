Momordica humilis

Momordica humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bitter gourd, is a vining plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. The plant produces small, bitter fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and trailing.

The flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are essential for fruit development.
