Malva parviflora var. trichocarpa

Malva parviflora var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and the flowers are clustered in racemes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
