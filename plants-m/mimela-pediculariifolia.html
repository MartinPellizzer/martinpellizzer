Mimela pediculariifolia

Mimela pediculariifolia, commonly known as the hanging mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has delicate, hairy stems and leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in humid climates.
