Muehlenbeckia zippelii

Muehlenbeckia zippelii, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has slender, creeping stems that can form dense mats, often growing along the ground or cascading from rock faces. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage rather than its flowers.
