Mouriri huberi

Mouriri huberi, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along its branches. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
