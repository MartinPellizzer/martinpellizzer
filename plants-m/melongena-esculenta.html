Melongena esculenta

Melongena esculenta, commonly known as the eggplant, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and has been cultivated for thousands of years. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are typically purple in color, though varieties with white, yellow, or green skins exist. The fruit is the primary edible part, used in various cuisines worldwide.

Botanically, it has a smooth, glossy skin and a dense, spongy interior with a mild, slightly bitter taste.
