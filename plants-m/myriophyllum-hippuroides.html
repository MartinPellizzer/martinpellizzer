Myriophyllum hippuroides

Myriophyllum hippuroides, commonly known as watermilfoil, is an aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often feather-like in appearance, and it produces small, green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. Its leaves are whorled around the stem and can be mistaken for submerged vegetation.

Myriophyllum hippuroides is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain water bodies.
