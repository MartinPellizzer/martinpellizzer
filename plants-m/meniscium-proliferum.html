Meniscium proliferum

Meniscium proliferum, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features erect, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a hairy texture.

Meniscium proliferum is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.
