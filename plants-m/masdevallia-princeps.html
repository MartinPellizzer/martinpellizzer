Masdevallia princeps

Masdevallia princeps, commonly known as the royal masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and its striking, long-lasting flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces intricate, spurred petals and a distinctive lip, which are typical features of the orchid family. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with high humidity and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
