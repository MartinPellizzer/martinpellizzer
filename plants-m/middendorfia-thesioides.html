Middendorfia thesioides

Middendorfia thesioides, commonly known as the Florida panther lily, is a rare flowering plant native to the wetlands of Florida.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in marshy areas.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected under various conservation programs.
