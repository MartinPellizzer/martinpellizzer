Michelaria

Michelaria, commonly known as the Australian wild fig, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

These plants are native to Australia and are characterized by their aromatic leaves and small, fleshy fruits. They typically grow in a variety of habitats, including forests and woodlands, and are often found in coastal regions. The genus includes several species, with Michelaria angustifolia being one of the most well-known.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a distinctive, fragrant aroma when crushed.
