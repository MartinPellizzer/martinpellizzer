Micropuntia barkleyana

Micropuntia barkleyana, commonly known as the Barkley's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, green, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed bristles. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The plant is also notable for its role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
