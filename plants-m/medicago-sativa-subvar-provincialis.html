Medicago sativa subvar. provincialis

Medicago sativa subvar. provincialis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and ecological restoration projects.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable species in both agricultural and environmental contexts.
