Machilus bhaskarii

Machilus bhaskarii, commonly known as the Bhaskarii woodbine, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in dense forests at elevations between 600 and 1,500 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The fruit is a dark blue drupe, which contains a single seed, and the plant is noted for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
