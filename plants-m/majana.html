Majana

Majana, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Africa and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures similar to those of dandelions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Majana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
