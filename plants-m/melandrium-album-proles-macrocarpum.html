Melandrium album proles macrocarpum

Melandrium album proles macrocarpum, commonly known as the white poppy, is a member of the Poppy family (Papaveraceae).

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white flowers with a prominent central stigma. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, white seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and recognizable species within its family.
