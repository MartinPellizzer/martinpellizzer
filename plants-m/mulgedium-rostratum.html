Mulgedium rostratum

Mulgedium rostratum, commonly known as the rostrated mulgedium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive rostrum-like structure on its flowers, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are alternate and lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Mulgedium rostratum produces small, fragrant flowers that attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
