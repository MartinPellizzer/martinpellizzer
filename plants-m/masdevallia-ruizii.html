Masdevallia ruizii

Masdevallia ruizii, commonly known as the Ruiz's masdevallia, is a tropical orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful floral structures and its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers exhibit a wide range of hues, including red, purple, and white, making it a visually striking species.

Masdevallia ruizii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique growth habits.
