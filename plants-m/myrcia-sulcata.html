Myrcia sulcata

Myrcia sulcata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are often aromatic and have a distinct scent. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Myrcia sulcata is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
