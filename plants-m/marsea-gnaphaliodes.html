Marsea gnaphaliodes

Marsea gnaphaliodes, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Marsea gnaphaliodes is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in coastal and rocky environments.
