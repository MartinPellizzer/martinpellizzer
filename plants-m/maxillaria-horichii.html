Maxillaria horichii

Maxillaria horichii, commonly known as the Horichii orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth during periods of dormancy. The plant features linear to lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and bloom during the spring and summer seasons.

Maxillaria horichii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
