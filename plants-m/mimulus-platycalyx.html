Mimulus platycalyx

Mimulus platycalyx, commonly known as the broad-calyx monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Mimulus platycalyx is a perennial species that grows in open woodland and meadow habitats.
