Morella pilulifera

Morella pilulifera, commonly known as the sea buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in coastal dunes and wetlands.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing shorelines and providing food for wildlife.
