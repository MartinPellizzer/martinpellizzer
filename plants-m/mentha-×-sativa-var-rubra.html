Mentha × sativa var. rubra

Mentha × sativa var. rubra, commonly known as red mint, is a hybrid variety of mint characterized by its distinctive reddish-purple stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30–60 cm and produces small, pale purple flowers. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

Red mint is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic compounds.
