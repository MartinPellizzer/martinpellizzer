Martynia confusa

Martynia confusa, commonly known as the redflowered false nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or purple, with five lobed petals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Martynia confusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant floral display.
