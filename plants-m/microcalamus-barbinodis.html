Microcalamus barbinodis

Microcalamus barbinodis, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often tufted culms that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctively hairy texture.

Microcalamus barbinodis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
