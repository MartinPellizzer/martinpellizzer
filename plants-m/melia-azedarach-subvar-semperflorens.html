Melia azedarach subvar. semperflorens

Melia azedarach subvar. semperflorens, commonly known as the Persian ironwood or the Chinese sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring, followed by small, woody fruits. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in various regions for landscaping and conservation purposes.
