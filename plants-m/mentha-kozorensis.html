Mentha kozorensis

Mentha kozorensis, commonly known as the Bulgarian mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Mentha kozorensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
