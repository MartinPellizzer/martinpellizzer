Melocactus macrocephalus

Melocactus macrocephalus, commonly known as the giant melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. Its spines are short and cylindrical, providing protection while allowing for some airflow around the stem.

The cactus is adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems and having a slow growth rate to conserve resources.
