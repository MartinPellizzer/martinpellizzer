Myrcia ambigua var. pauciflora

Myrcia ambigua var. pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are often followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
