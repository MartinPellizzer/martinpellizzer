Mammillaria longimamma f. uberiformis

Mammillaria longimamma f. uberiformis, commonly known as the Wooly Mammillaria, is a compact cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white to greyish woolly covering that covers the stems and flowers. The plant typically grows in a globular or cylindrical shape, with short, spiny ribs. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and bloom in late spring or early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
