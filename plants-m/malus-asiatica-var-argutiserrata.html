Malus asiatica var. argutiserrata

Malus asiatica var. argutiserrata, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features showy, white to pink flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are serrated and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
