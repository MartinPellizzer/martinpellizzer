Mallotus diadenus

Mallotus diadenus, commonly known as the two-bracted mallot, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, giving it its common name. It is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
