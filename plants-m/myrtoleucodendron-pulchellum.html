Myrtoleucodendron pulchellum

Myrtoleucodendron pulchellum, commonly known as the mountain myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and leathery in texture. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
