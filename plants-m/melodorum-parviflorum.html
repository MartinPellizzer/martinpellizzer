Melodorum parviflorum

Melodorum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered melodia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces small, fragrant blooms in shades of pink and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, often with a slight waxy coating.

Melodorum parviflorum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
