Moacroton lanceolatus

Moacroton lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf moacroton, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Moacroton lanceolatus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
