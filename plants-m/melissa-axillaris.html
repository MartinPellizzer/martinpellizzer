Melissa axillaris

Melissa axillaris, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small, white to pale yellow flowers and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, often growing in moist environments. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, particularly citral, which contribute to its calming properties.

Melissa axillaris is widely used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
