Maackia chinensis

Maackia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese maackia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters, featuring trifoliate leaves with a serrated edge. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale pink, while the fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring foliage and floral display.
