Mesembryanthemum comptum

Mesembryanthemum comptum, commonly known as the narrowleaf iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, narrow leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.

Mesembryanthemum comptum is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in sandy or coastal areas.
