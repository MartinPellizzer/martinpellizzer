Mentzelia multicaulis var. librina

Mentzelia multicaulis var. librina, commonly known as desert blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
