Melastoma kemamanense

Melastoma kemamanense, commonly known as the Kedah melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in the rainy season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Melastoma kemamanense is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
