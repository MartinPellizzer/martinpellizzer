Mimosa mixtecana

Mimosa mixtecana, commonly known as the Mexican mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with slender branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are sensitive to touch, folding shut when disturbed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

Mimosa mixtecana is also known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, making it both a useful pioneer species and a potential weed.
