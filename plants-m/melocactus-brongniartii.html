Melocactus brongniartii

Melocactus brongniartii, commonly known as the golden melon cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and prominent, ribbed structure. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
