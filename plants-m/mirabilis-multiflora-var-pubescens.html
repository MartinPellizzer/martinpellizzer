Mirabilis multiflora var. pubescens

Mirabilis multiflora var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique, tropical-looking plants.
