Mentha × dumetorum var. pseudolimosa

Mentha × dumetorum var. pseudolimosa, commonly known as "Mexican mint marigold," is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both mint and lemon grass.

It is native to Central America and is known for its aromatic, herbaceous foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has a sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong, mint-like fragrance when crushed.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its distinctive flavor and aromatic properties.
