Mentha × suavifolia

Mentha × suavifolia, commonly known as sweet pepper mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a strong, minty fragrance and is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can spread quickly and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
