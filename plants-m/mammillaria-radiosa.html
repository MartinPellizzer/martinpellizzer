Mammillaria radiosa

Mammillaria radiosa, commonly known as the radiate mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in small, white tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in succulent gardens.
