Masdevallia ochthodes

Masdevallia ochthodes, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks in cool, humid environments. It features intricate, often fragrant flowers with a wide range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its unique beauty and specific habitat requirements, it is a popular but challenging plant for cultivation.
