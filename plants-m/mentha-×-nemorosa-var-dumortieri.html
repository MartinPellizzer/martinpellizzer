Mentha × nemorosa var. dumortieri

Mentha × nemorosa var. dumortieri, commonly known as the Mexican mint or Mexican wild mint, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a strong mint scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and herbal use.
