Mabea depauperata

Mabea depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Mabea depauperata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
