Miconia rhodopogon

Miconia rhodopogon, commonly known as the red-veined miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent reddish veins, which give it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, and it is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Miconia rhodopogon is also notable for its rapid growth and potential invasiveness in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
