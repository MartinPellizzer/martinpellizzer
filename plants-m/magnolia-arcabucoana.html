Magnolia arcabucoana

Magnolia arcabucoana, commonly known as the Andean magnolia, is a rare and endangered species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
