Melampyrum sylvaticum subsp. moeszianum

Melampyrum sylvaticum subsp.

moeszianum, commonly known as the Moeszian burning bush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is often found in the Carpathian Basin and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
