Mahonia arguta

Mahonia arguta, commonly known as the prickly pear or leatherleaf mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features compound leaves with spiny margins and a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Mahonia arguta is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in USDA zones 5 through 8.
