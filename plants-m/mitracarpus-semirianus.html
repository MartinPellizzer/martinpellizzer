Mitracarpus semirianus

Mitracarpus semirianus, commonly known as the Malabar cowpea, is a flowering plant native to southern India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptic to ovate in shape.

The flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple or reddish calyx, and the pods contain several edible seeds.
