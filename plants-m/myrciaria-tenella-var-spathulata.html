Myrciaria tenella var. spathulata

Myrciaria tenella var. spathulata, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or spathulate myrciaria, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
