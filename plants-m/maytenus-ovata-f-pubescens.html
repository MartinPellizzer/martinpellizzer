Maytenus ovata f. pubescens

Maytenus ovata f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent maytenus, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
