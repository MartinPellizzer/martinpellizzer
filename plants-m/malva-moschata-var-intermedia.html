Malva moschata var. intermedia

Malva moschata var. intermedia, commonly known as the Persian melon or musk mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and pale green veins. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a round, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
