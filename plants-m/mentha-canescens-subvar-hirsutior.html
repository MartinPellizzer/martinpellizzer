Mentha canescens subvar. hirsutior

Mentha canescens subvar. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy mint, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and a strong, minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its flowering stems bear small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
