Melastoma brasiliense

Melastoma brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian melastoma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a glossy appearance, while its fruits are small, capsule-like structures.

Melastoma brasiliense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
