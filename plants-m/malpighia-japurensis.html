Malpighia japurensis

Malpighia japurensis, commonly known as the japure cherry or Brazilian cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are rich in vitamin C and are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is adapted to the dense, shaded conditions of the rainforest understory.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for traditional use.
