Mimosa jiringa

Mimosa jiringa, commonly known as the pink mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Mimosa jiringa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
