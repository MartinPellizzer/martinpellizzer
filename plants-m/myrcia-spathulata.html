Myrcia spathulata

Myrcia spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories.

Myrcia spathulata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
