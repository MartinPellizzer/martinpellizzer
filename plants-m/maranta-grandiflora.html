Maranta grandiflora

Maranta grandiflora, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with green and silver hues. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its rhizomes are widely used in culinary applications as a starchy alternative to traditional root vegetables.

Maranta grandiflora is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
