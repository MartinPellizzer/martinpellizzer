Merremia boisiana var. fulvopilosa

Merremia boisiana var. fulvopilosa, commonly known as the yellow spider vine, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy stems, which are covered in fine, soft pubescence. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the fruit is a spiny, capsule-like structure. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
