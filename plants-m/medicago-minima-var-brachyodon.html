Medicago minima var. brachyodon

Medicago minima var. brachyodon, commonly known as the shortpod needlepod, is a small annual legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with narrow, pointed leaflets and produces small, elongated pods that remain closed at maturity. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It has small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, and it is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

This species is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
