Manihot coerulea

Manihot coerulea, commonly known as the blue arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a starchy tuberous root that is used as a food source in some regions. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white wax.
