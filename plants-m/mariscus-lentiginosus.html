Mariscus lentiginosus

Mariscus lentiginosus, commonly known as the spotted mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small shrub characterized by its woody stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
