Mariscus cyperinus var. laxatus

Mariscus cyperinus var. laxatus, commonly known as the looseleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical stems.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often pale green or brownish in color. It is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
