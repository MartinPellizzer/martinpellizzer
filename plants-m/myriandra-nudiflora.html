Myriandra nudiflora

Myriandra nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flowered myriandra, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous stems and leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often lacking visible bracts.

Myriandra nudiflora is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
