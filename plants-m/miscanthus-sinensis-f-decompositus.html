Miscanthus sinensis f. decompositus

Miscanthus sinensis f. decompositus, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a ornamental grass characterized by its distinctive silvery-white plumes that appear in late summer.

It is a cool-season grass that thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that add visual interest to gardens throughout the growing season.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its elegant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
