Mimulus glabratus var. jamesii

Mimulus glabratus var. jamesii, commonly known as the James' monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in conservation efforts due to its limited range.
