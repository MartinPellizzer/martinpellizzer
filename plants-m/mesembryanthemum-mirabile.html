Mesembryanthemum mirabile

Mesembryanthemum mirabile, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the cooler months. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Mesembryanthemum mirabile is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in sandy landscapes.
