Mertensia symphytoides

Mertensia symphytoides, commonly known as the Virginia bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Mertensia symphytoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing nectar for early pollinators.
