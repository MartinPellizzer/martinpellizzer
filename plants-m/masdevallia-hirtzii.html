Masdevallia hirtzii

Masdevallia hirtzii, commonly known as the Hirtz's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flower spikes that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in high humidity and partial shade, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Due to its striking appearance and specific environmental requirements, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
