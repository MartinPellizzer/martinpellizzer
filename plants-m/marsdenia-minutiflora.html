Marsdenia minutiflora

Marsdenia minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces follicles that contain seeds with a tuft of silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural collections.
