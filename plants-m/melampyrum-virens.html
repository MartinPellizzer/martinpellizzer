Melampyrum virens

Melampyrum virens, commonly known as green flame vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

Melampyrum virens is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
