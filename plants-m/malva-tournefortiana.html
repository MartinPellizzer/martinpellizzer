Malva tournefortiana

Malva tournefortiana, commonly known as the Turkish mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Malva tournefortiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
