Merckia

Merckia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plants typically grow as perennials, often forming clumps with fleshy, succulent leaves. Merckia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens due to their vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.

They prefer well-drained soil and full sun, making them suitable for Mediterranean climates.
