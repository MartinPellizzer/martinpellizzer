Mycetia basiflora

Mycetia basiflora, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caesalpiniaceae and is characterized by its large, showy, red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

Mycetia basiflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
