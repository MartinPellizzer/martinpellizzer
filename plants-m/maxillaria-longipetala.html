Maxillaria longipetala

Maxillaria longipetala, commonly known as the long-petaled orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces clusters of showy, violet or purple flowers with long, slender petals.

Maxillaria longipetala is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
