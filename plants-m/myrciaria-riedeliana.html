Myrciaria riedeliana

Myrciaria riedeliana, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Myrciaria riedeliana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
