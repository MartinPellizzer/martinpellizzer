Melocactus diersianus

Melocactus diersianus, commonly known as the golden melon cactus, is a slow-growing cactus native to the arid regions of northern Argentina and southern Bolivia.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stem that can reach up to 30 centimeters in height, with a distinctive golden or yellowish coloration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived. It has a deep root system that allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
