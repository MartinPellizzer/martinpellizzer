Morina breviflora

Morina breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered moringa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The plant is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
