Myrcia pseudospectabilis

Myrcia pseudospectabilis, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its vibrant purple to reddish berries that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 4 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a strong, aromatic scent when crushed.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered, often blooming before the berries mature.
