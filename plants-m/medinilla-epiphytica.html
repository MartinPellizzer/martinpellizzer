Medinilla epiphytica

Medinilla epiphytica, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Medinilla epiphytica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant in warm climates.
