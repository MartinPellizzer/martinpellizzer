Mahernia fragrans

Mahernia fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant mahernia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mahernia fragrans thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
