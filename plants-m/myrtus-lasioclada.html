Myrtus lasioclada

Myrtus lasioclada, commonly known as the rough-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with rough, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, dark blue berries. Myrtus lasioclada is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
