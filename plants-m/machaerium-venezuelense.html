Machaerium venezuelense

Machaerium venezuelense, commonly known as the Venezuelan machaerium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale or greenish in color, followed by pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and habitat provision for wildlife.
