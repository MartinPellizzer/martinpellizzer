Mertensia lineariloba

Mertensia lineariloba, commonly known as the slender bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia lineariloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early-season blooms.
