Melastoma yahudii

Melastoma yahudii, commonly known as the Jewish melastoma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its growth habit is often shrub-like. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
