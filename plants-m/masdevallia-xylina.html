Masdevallia xylina

Masdevallia xylina, commonly known as the silver-veined masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flower spikes that bear numerous small, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, thriving in cool, humid environments with high elevation. Its leaves are usually dark green with silver or white veins, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Masdevallia xylina is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
