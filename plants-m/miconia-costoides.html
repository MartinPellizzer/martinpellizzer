Miconia costoides

Miconia costoides, commonly known as the "Brazilian beauty," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Miconia costoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant foliage and floral display.
