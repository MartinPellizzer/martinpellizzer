Masdevallia pyxis

Masdevallia pyxis, commonly known as the "casket orchid," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, urn-shaped pseudobulbs and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high light exposure but requires careful watering to avoid root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
