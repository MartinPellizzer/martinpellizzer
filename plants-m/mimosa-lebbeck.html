Mimosa lebbeck

Mimosa lebbeck, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a large, deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that close when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used for soil stabilization and reforestation efforts.
