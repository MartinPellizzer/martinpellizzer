Mimosa bispiculata

Mimosa bispiculata, commonly known as the slender mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are sensitive to touch, curling up when disturbed, a trait that gives it its common name.

Mimosa bispiculata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
