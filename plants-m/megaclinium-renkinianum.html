Megaclinium renkinianum

Megaclinium renkinianum, commonly known as the Renkinian silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
