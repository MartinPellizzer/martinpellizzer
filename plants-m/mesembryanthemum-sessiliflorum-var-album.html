Mesembryanthemum sessiliflorum var. album

Mesembryanthemum sessiliflorum var. album, commonly known as the white-flowered ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are predominantly white or pale pink, blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments.
