Marsdenia trinervis

Marsdenia trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by follicles that contain elongated seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
