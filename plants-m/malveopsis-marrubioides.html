Malveopsis marrubioides

Malveopsis marrubioides, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Malveopsis marrubioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
