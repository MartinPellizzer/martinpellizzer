Mentha szenezyana

Mentha szenezyana, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, menthol-like fragrance.

Mentha szenezyana is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
