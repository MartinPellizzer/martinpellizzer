Malva moschata var. decalvata

Malva moschata var. decalvata, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive tactile quality. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching profusely.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
