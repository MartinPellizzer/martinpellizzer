Miconia tinifolia

Miconia tinifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian shield tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, often with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a round, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Miconia tinifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
