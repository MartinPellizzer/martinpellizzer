Mangleticornia

Mangleticornia, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a unique species characterized by its striking, spiral-shaped inflorescence.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are dark green with a metallic sheen, giving it its common name. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, are typically yellow or orange and emit a subtle fragrance.

Mangleticornia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
