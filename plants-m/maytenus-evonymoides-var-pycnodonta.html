Maytenus evonymoides var. pycnodonta

Maytenus evonymoides var. pycnodonta, commonly known as the thick-leaved mayten, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
