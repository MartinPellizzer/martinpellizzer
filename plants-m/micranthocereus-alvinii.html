Micranthocereus alvinii

Micranthocereus alvinii, commonly known as the Alvin's night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and nocturnal flowering habit. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting specific pollinators. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming pattern, it is a popular choice for collectors and xerophytic gardens.
