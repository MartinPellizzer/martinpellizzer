Marsdenia efulensis

Marsdenia efulensis, commonly known as the Cameroon moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive corona structure. Its fruit is a follicle containing several seeds, and it is often found growing in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
