Mentha glomerata var. palustris

Mentha glomerata var. palustris, commonly known as swamp mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist soils along streams, marshes, and other waterlogged areas. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers are clustered in dense whorls along the upper stems.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its aromatic properties and has a strong, minty scent.
