Minuartia recurva subsp. recurva

Minuartia recurva subsp.

recurva, commonly known as the bent rock-cress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to rocky and alpine environments. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in crevices or on slopes.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh climates and is considered a hardy, resilient plant in its native habitat.
