Mnianthus pulchellus

Mnianthus pulchellus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-pink petals and prominent, cone-shaped central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Mnianthus pulchellus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
