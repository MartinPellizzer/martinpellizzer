Mentha hostii

Mentha hostii, commonly known as the Hungarian watermint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. Mentha hostii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
