Macaranga yakasii

Macaranga yakasii, commonly known as the white-veined macaranga, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
