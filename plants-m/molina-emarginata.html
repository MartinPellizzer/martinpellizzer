Molina emarginata

Molina emarginata, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, while its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

Molina emarginata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional uses in local medicine.
