Myrtus kameruniana

Myrtus kameruniana, commonly known as Cameroon myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to Cameroon and neighboring regions of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fragrant leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Myrtus kameruniana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
