Majorana vulgaris

Majorana vulgaris, commonly known as wild thyme, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with small, fragrant leaves and pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant contains essential oils, which contribute to its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and culinary applications highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
