Mimosa hassleriana subsp. microphylla

Mimosa hassleriana subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are small and bipinnate, giving it a feathery appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening.

The species is also known for its sensitivity to touch, as the leaves fold when disturbed, a trait shared with other mimosa species.
