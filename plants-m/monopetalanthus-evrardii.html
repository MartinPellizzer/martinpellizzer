Monopetalanthus evrardii

Monopetalanthus evrardii, commonly known as the African orchid grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
