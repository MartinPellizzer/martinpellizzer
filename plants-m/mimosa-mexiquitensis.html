Mimosa mexiquitensis

Mimosa mexiquitensis, commonly known as the Mexican sensitive plant, is a flowering shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Mimosa mexiquitensis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
