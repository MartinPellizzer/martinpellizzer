Microcephala turcomanica

Microcephala turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcomanica bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy.
