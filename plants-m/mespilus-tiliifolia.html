Mespilus tiliifolia

Mespilus tiliifolia, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough, textured surface.

Mespilus tiliifolia is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
