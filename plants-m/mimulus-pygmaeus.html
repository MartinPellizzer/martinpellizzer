Mimulus pygmaeus

Mimulus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf monkeyflower, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring slender stems and small, bright yellow flowers with orange markings. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and streambanks.

Mimulus pygmaeus plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
