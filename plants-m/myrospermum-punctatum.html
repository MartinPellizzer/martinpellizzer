Myrospermum punctatum

Myrospermum punctatum, commonly known as the dotted myrtle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves with a distinctive dotted appearance on the underside. It produces small white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Myrospermum punctatum is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
