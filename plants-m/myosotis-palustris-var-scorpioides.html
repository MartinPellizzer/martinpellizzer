Myosotis palustris var. scorpioides

Myosotis palustris var. scorpioides, commonly known as the swamp forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland environments in North America.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
