Mesadenella margaritifera

Mesadenella margaritifera, commonly known as the pearl shell orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that resemble small pearls, hence its common name. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. The leaves are narrow and elongated, often with a waxy surface to retain moisture.

Its blooming period occurs during the rainy season, adding to its ecological and ornamental significance.
