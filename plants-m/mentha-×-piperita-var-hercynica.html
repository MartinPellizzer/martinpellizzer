Mentha × piperita var. hercynica

Mentha × piperita var. hercynica, commonly known as German peppermint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe.

It features aromatic, dark green, slightly serrated leaves and produces small, pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong menthol scent and is widely cultivated for its essential oils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in aromatherapy, herbal teas, and as a natural remedy for various ailments.
