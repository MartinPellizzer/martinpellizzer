Melothria perpusilla var. deltifrons

Melothria perpusilla var. deltifrons, commonly known as the dwarf passionfruit, is a small climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers and small, edible fruits. The plant produces orange-red berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique fruit.
