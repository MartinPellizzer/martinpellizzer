Mesoamerantha dichroantha

Mesoamerantha dichroantha, commonly known as the two-colored beauty, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking dichroic leaves that change color depending on light exposure, showcasing a unique optical phenomenon. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
