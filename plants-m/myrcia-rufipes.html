Myrcia rufipes

Myrcia rufipes, commonly known as the red-stemmed myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe.

Myrcia rufipes is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
