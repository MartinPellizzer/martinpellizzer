Moronobea esculenta

Moronobea esculenta, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is a small tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which is typically pear-shaped, has a smooth, yellowish skin and a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild, aromatic flavor. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
