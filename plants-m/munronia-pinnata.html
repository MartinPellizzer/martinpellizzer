Munronia pinnata

Munronia pinnata, commonly known as the fragrant sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are a notable feature. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with rough, fissured bark.

Munronia pinnata is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.
