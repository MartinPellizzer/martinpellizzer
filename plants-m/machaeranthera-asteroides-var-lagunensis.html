Machaeranthera asteroides var. lagunensis

Machaeranthera asteroides var. lagunensis, commonly known as the Mexican aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the grasslands of central Mexico.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a central yellow disk, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
