Maesa depauperata

Maesa depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's maesa, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features dense, leathery leaves that are usually dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
