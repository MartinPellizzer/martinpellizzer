Machilus chayuensis

Machilus chayuensis, commonly known as the Chayu mahogany, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to China, specifically growing in the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in humid, subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
