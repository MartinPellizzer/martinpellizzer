Magnolia kobus var. borealis

Magnolia kobus var. borealis, commonly known as the northern magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to northern China and Korea.

It features large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. This variety is known for its cold hardiness and ability to thrive in temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
