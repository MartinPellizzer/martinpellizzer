Miconia cyanocarpa var. hirsuta

Miconia cyanocarpa var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by showy, blue-black berries. Its foliage and fruit make it an attractive ornamental plant, though it is also considered invasive in some areas.

The species belongs to the Melastomataceae family, known for its diverse and often striking tropical flora.
