Margyricarpus alatus var. prolifer

Margyricarpus alatus var. prolifer, commonly known as the winged fruit plant, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, often with a hairy texture.
