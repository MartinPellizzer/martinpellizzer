Mentha mollissima f. inclusa

Mentha mollissima f. inclusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved mint, is a variant of the mint plant characterized by its broad, soft leaves.

It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This form is less common than the standard Mentha mollissima but shares many of its properties.
