Medicago orbicularis f. applanata

Medicago orbicularis f. applanata, commonly known as the flat-leaf Medicago, is a variant of the common medick, characterized by its distinctive flat, orbicular leaves.

This plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The flowers are usually small and white or pale pink, contributing to its ornamental value in some horticultural settings.
