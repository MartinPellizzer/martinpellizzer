Myrceugenia longipedunculata

Myrceugenia longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed myrtle, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dense foliage and small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are consumed by various wildlife species.

Myrceugenia longipedunculata thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
