Melaleuca halmaturorum var. tuberculifera

Melaleuca halmaturorum var. tuberculifera, commonly known as the Halmaturong scrub melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of South Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can tolerate periods of drought.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
