Morphixia aulica

Morphixia aulica, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Morphixia aulica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
