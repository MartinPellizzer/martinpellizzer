Myosurus minimus subsp. heldreichii

Myosurus minimus subsp.

heldreichii, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear, is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant has hairy, rounded leaves that are clustered at the base, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae).
