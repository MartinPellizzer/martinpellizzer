Myrtus communis var. gausseni

Myrtus communis var. gausseni, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are aromatic and slightly leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
