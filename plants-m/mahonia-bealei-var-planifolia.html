Mahonia bealei var. planifolia

Mahonia bealei var. planifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive, smooth texture and a dark green color that turns yellow in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage and seasonal interest, making it a popular choice for gardens.
