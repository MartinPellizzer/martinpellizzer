Mammillaria albida

Mammillaria albida, commonly known as the white mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and white spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, blooming in late spring or early summer.

Mammillaria albida is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to prevent root rot.
