Mimusops clitandrifolia

Mimusops clitandrifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing hard, angular seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical benefits.
