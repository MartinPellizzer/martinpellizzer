Magnolia insignis

Magnolia insignis, commonly known as the white magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, gray bark and simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
