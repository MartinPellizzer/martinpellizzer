Myrcia pulvinata

Myrcia pulvinata, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while the flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Myrcia pulvinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
