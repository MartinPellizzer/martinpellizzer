Mertensia ochroleuca

Mertensia ochroleuca, commonly known as the white heartleaf bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale blue, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Mertensia ochroleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
