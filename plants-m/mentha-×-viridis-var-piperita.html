Mentha × viridis var. piperita

Mentha × viridis var. piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha viridis and Mentha piperita.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves that are typically dark green and pointed. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Peppermint is native to Europe and temperate regions of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

It is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in herbal remedies, aromatherapy, and flavoring.
