Mentha longifolia f. apentiana

Mentha longifolia f. apentiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a subspecies of the spearmint plant.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. This plant is native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean region. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
