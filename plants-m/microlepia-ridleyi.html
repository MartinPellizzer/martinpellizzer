Microlepia ridleyi

Microlepia ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley's microlepia, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Microlepia ridleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
