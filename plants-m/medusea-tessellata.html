Medusea tessellata

Medusea tessellata, commonly known as the mosaic medusa, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, geometric leaf patterns that resemble mosaic tiles, giving it its common name. The plant has a succulent, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow or orange.

Medusea tessellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
