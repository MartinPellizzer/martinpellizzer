Maesa montana var. formosana

Maesa montana var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan maesa, is a shrub native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in forest understories, preferring moist, acidic soils. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark berries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native habitats.
