Malaxis demissa

Malaxis demissa, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and a dense, spiraled inflorescence of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are often pollinated by flies and other small insects.

Malaxis demissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
