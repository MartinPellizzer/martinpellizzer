Mentha × nusleensis

Mentha × nusleensis, commonly known as the Nusle mint, is a hybrid perennial herb belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. Native to Central and Eastern Europe, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Mentha × nusleensis is valued for its strong menthol scent and has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Its hybrid origin contributes to its adaptability and variability in morphology.
