Musa sylvestris

Musa sylvestris, commonly known as the wild banana, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with large, broad leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, green berries that mature to a yellow or orange color. It is closely related to the common edible banana (Musa acuminata) and is often used in botanical research.

The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
