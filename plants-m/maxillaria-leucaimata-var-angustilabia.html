Maxillaria leucaimata var. angustilabia

Maxillaria leucaimata var. angustilabia, commonly known as the narrow-lipped white maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically blooms during the late summer and early autumn, with flowers that have a distinctive, narrow lip. Its leaves are ovate and arranged in two rows along the stem, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and relatively easy care requirements.
