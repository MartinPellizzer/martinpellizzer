Malaxis tepicana

Malaxis tepicana, commonly known as the tepic malaxis, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations between 2,000 and 3,000 meters. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptations and potential horticultural value.
