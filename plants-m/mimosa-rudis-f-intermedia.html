Mimosa rudis f. intermedia

Mimosa rudis f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is noted for its compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by the sensitivity of its leaflets. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in spherical inflorescences, adding to its ornamental value.

This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
