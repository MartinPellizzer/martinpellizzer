Myrtus foetida

Myrtus foetida, commonly known as the stinking myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces dark blue, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a strong, pungent aroma. Myrtus foetida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
