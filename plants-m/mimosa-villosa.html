Mimosa villosa

Mimosa villosa, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 6 feet, featuring slender, grayish stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets that are also covered in soft, white pubescence.

Mimosa villosa is often found in moist, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
