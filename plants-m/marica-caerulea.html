Marica caerulea

Marica caerulea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Marica caerulea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
