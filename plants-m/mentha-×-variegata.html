Mentha × variegata

Mentha × variegata, commonly known as variegated mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata and Mentha viridis.

It is characterized by its variegated foliage, featuring a mix of green and white or yellow markings on its leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that appear in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Mentha × variegata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic leaves for culinary or medicinal use.
