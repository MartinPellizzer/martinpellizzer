Meibomia marilandica

Meibomia marilandica, commonly known as Maryland morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue to violet in color and bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by water and wind.
