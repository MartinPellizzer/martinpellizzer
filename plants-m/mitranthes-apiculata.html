Mitranthes apiculata

Mitranthes apiculata, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Mitranthes apiculata is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to extreme conditions.
