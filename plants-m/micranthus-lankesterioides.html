Micranthus lankesterioides

Micranthus lankesterioides, commonly known as the Mexican fireplant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small yellow flowers clustered in a dense inflorescence, which is surrounded by the colorful bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care make it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
