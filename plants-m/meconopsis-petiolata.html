Meconopsis petiolata

Meconopsis petiolata, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and is often found in rocky, alpine environments. The plant has a short, hairy stem and produces showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Meconopsis petiolata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
