Mediolobivia elegans

Mediolobivia elegans, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, which help reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of pale pink and white, often arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscaping.
