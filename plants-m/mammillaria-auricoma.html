Mammillaria auricoma

Mammillaria auricoma, commonly known as the ear-shaped mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
