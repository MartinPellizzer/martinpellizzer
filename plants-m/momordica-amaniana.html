Momordica amaniana

Momordica amaniana, commonly known as the Mexican bitter gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine that produces elongated, warty fruits with a bitter taste. The plant is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in warm, tropical climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically yellow with prominent stamens.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and cooking, despite its strong flavor.
