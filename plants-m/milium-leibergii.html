Milium leibergii

Milium leibergii, commonly known as the Leiberg's millet, is a low-growing perennial grass native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea.

It features slender, hairless stems that produce clusters of small, pale green to white spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth texture.

Milium leibergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
