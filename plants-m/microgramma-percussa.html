Microgramma percussa

Microgramma percussa, commonly known as the striking maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often described as resembling lace. Microgramma percussa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
