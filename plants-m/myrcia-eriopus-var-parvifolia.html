Myrcia eriopus var. parvifolia

Myrcia eriopus var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
