Melochia illicioides

Melochia illicioides, commonly known as the red root or red-rooted mallow, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often reddish in color, and its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Melochia illicioides is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
