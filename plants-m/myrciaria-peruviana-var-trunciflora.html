Myrciaria peruviana var. trunciflora

Myrciaria peruviana var. trunciflora, commonly known as the Peruvian guava, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in humid, tropical climates. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The leaves are ovate, glossy, and often have a slight pubescence.
