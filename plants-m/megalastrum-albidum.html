Megalastrum albidum

Megalastrum albidum, commonly known as the white-flowered megalastrum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
