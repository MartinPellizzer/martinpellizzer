Mammillaria microcarpa f. oliviae

Mammillaria microcarpa f. oliviae, commonly known as the Olivia's miniature mammillaria, is a compact cactus with a globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale yellow spines.

It originates from the arid regions of southern Mexico, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise spiky appearance. Its slow growth rate makes it an ideal choice for rock gardens or as a decorative indoor plant.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
