Miconia polita

Miconia polita, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically velvety in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by round, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. Miconia polita is often found in moist lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
