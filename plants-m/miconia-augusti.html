Miconia augusti

Miconia augusti, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling starfruits. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets make it an invasive species in some regions.

The plant's botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique fruit structure that aids in seed dispersal.
