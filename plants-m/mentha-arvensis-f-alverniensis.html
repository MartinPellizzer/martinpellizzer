Mentha arvensis f. alverniensis

Mentha arvensis f. alverniensis, commonly known as alpine mint, is a subspecies of the field mint plant.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowering stems that bear small, tubular flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often hairy and have a strong menthol scent.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
