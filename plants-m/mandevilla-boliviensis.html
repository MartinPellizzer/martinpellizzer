Mandevilla boliviensis

Mandevilla boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. It is a climbing vine that can reach significant heights, often requiring support for growth.

Mandevilla boliviensis thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
