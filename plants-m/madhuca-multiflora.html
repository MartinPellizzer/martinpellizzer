Madhuca multiflora

Madhuca multiflora, commonly known as the Indian shea tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is valued for its fruit, which contains a nutritious seed used in traditional medicine and food. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The tree's ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
