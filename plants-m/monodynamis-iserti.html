Monodynamis iserti

Monodynamis iserti, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its milky sap, which is toxic to many animals, serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Monodynamis iserti plays a vital role in supporting local pollinators, particularly butterflies and bees, due to its nectar-rich flowers.
