Maba oblongicarpa

Maba oblongicarpa, commonly known as the African maba tree, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in savannas and woodlands, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
