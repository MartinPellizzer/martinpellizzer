Muscari cupanianum

Muscari cupanianum, commonly known as the Persian glory-of-the-snow, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue or violet, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.

The bulbs are hardy and can be planted in the fall for spring blooming.
