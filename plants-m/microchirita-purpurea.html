Microchirita purpurea

Microchirita purpurea, commonly known as the purple microchirita, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant purple foliage, which is a result of high concentrations of anthocyanin pigments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique coloration and delicate appearance, it is a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and among plant enthusiasts.
