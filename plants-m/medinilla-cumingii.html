Medinilla cumingii

Medinilla cumingii, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, round, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Medinilla cumingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
