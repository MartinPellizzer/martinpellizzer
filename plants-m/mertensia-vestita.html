Mertensia vestita

Mertensia vestita, commonly known as the white hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silvery-green leaves and its striking clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland habitats. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Mertensia vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
