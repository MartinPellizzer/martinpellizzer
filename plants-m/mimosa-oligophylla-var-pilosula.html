Mimosa oligophylla var. pilosula

Mimosa oligophylla var. pilosula, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a response known as thigmonastic movement. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.

Its unique appearance and adaptive traits make it a notable species within the Mimosa genus.
