Manganaroa velutina

Manganaroa velutina, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has a low-growing habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae, which includes various types of ferns.
