Mikania nemorosa

Mikania nemorosa, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, often forming dense mats. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is commonly found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forests.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
