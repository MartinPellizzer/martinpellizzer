Magonia viraru

Magonia viraru, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically bright yellow or orange when ripe. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
