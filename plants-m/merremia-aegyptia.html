Merremia aegyptia

Merremia aegyptia, commonly known as the Egyptian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a vigorous vine that produces large, showy blue or purple flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release the seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
