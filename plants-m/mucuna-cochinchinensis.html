Mucuna cochinchinensis

Mucuna cochinchinensis, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil and making it a valuable crop in agricultural systems. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish, and it blooms throughout the growing season.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
