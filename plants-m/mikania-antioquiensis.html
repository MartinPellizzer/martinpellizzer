Mikania antioquiensis

Mikania antioquiensis, commonly known as the Antioquian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and helix-shaped stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its fruits are small, dry capsules that release seeds when mature.

Mikania antioquiensis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
