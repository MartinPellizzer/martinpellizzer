Myristica schefferi

Myristica schefferi, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by distinctive red or yellow fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, which includes essential oils and compounds like myristicin and elemicin.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
