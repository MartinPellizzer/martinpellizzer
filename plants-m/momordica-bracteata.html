Momordica bracteata

Momordica bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces striking yellow flowers and oblong, warty fruits that turn orange when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the plant has a vigorous growth habit.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and cuisine, valued for its bitter taste and medicinal properties.
