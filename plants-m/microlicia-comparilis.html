Microlicia comparilis

Microlicia comparilis, commonly known as the white sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in spikelets. Its leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture.
