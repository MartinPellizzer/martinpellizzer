Melaleuca urceolaris

Melaleuca urceolaris, commonly known as the bottlebrush tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes, typically ranging in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and open forests.

Melaleuca urceolaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine by Indigenous Australians.
