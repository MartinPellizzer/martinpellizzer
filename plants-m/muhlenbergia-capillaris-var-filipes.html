Muhlenbergia capillaris var. filipes

Muhlenbergia capillaris var. filipes, commonly known as silky muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky texture and slender, arching stems that form dense, low-growing clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple hues. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to spread via rhizomes contribute to its adaptability in various garden settings.
