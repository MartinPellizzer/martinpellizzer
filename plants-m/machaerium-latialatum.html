Machaerium latialatum

Machaerium latialatum, commonly known as the pink machaerium, is a tropical perennial plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

Machaerium latialatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
