Machaerium mucronulatum

Machaerium mucronulatum, commonly known as the narrow-pointed machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a feathery appearance. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive yellowish or greenish hue, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in soil enrichment.
