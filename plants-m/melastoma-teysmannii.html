Melastoma teysmannii

Melastoma teysmannii, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are often eaten by birds and other animals.

Melastoma teysmannii is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
