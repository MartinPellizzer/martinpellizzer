Metrodorea gracilis

Metrodorea gracilis, commonly known as the slender rock fern, is a species of fern found in the southeastern United States.

It thrives in rocky, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, moist environments. The plant has slender, creeping rhizomes and produces small, lance-shaped leaves. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with delicate, finely divided segments.

Metrodorea gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
