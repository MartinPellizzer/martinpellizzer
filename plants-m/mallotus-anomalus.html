Mallotus anomalus

Mallotus anomalus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
