Mitozus discolor

Mitozus discolor, commonly known as the variegated mitozus, is a unique plant characterized by its striking color variations.

It belongs to the family Mitozaceae and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with waxy, lance-shaped leaves that display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
