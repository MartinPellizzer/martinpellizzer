Malinvaudia capillacea

Malinvaudia capillacea, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
