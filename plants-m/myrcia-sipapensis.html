Myrcia sipapensis

Myrcia sipapensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and leathery, elliptical leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland rainforests.

Myrcia sipapensis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
