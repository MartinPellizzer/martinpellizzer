Microstylis aphylla

Microstylis aphylla, commonly known as the leafless microstylis, is a unique member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

This plant is characterized by its lack of leaves, with only small, scale-like structures present at the base of the stems. It typically grows in wet, marshy environments, often in association with other aquatic or semi-aquatic plants. The flowers are tiny and clustered in spikelets, which are a defining feature of the sedge family.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, Microstylis aphylla is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
