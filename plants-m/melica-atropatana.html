Melica atropatana

Melica atropatana, commonly known as black grass or dark millet, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, dark green blades and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are often slightly hairy, and the seed heads are tightly clustered, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Melica atropatana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
