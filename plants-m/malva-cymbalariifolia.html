Malva cymbalariifolia

Malva cymbalariifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
