Mimosa hoehneana

Mimosa hoehneana, commonly known as the pink mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant is known for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait called thigmonastic response. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
