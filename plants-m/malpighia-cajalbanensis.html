Malpighia cajalbanensis

Malpighia cajalbanensis, commonly known as the Cayman apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean.

It produces small, bright red berries that are edible and have a tart, sweet flavor. The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and limestone soils.

The fruit is used in local cuisines and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
