Melanthera hastata

Melanthera hastata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and wet meadows.

Melanthera hastata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
