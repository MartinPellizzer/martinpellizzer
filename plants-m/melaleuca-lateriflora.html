Melaleuca lateriflora

Melaleuca lateriflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and swampy areas. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in spikes, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Melaleuca lateriflora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
