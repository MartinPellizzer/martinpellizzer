Myrcia aerisea

Myrcia aerisea, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and are often elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually appear in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Myrcia aerisea is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
