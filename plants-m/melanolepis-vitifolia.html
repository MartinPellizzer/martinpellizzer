Melanolepis vitifolia

Melanolepis vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf melastome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Melanolepis vitifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
