Madia bolanderi

Madia bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
