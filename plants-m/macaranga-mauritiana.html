Macaranga mauritiana

Macaranga mauritiana, commonly known as the African elephant tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
