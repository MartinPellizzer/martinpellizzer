Merkusia anchusifolia var. holosericea

Merkusia anchusifolia var. holosericea, commonly known as the silky anchusa, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and striking spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety holosericea is distinguished by its dense, silky hairs covering the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.
