Malus bhutanica

Malus bhutanica, commonly known as the Bhutan apple, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayan region of Bhutan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and red or yellow fruits. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

The fruit is edible, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
