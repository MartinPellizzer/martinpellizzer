Megaclinium brixhei

Megaclinium brixhei, commonly known as the Brixheim's giant grass, is a tall, perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems and large, blade-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
