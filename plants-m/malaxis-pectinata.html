Malaxis pectinata

Malaxis pectinata, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped flower that resembles a small helmet, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive hood-like structure covering the reproductive organs.

Malaxis pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
