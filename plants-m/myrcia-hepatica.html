Myrcia hepatica

Myrcia hepatica, commonly known as the liverleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

Myrcia hepatica is typically found in moist, shaded areas of tropical and subtropical forests.
