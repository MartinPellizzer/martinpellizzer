Mentha × kerneri var. dejensis

Mentha × kerneri var. dejensis, commonly known as the Serbian mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the Balkan region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in culinary applications.
