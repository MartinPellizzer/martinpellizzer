Metalasia galpinii

Metalasia galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's metalasia, is a rare and endemic plant species found primarily in the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers and wet winters. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and human encroachment.

Conservation efforts are crucial to ensure the survival of this unique botanical species.
