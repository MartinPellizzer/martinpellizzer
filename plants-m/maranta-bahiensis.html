Maranta bahiensis

Maranta bahiensis, commonly known as the "Prayer Plant," is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often variegated leaves that change color and orientation throughout the day, a phenomenon known as nyctinasty. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes many ornamental species known for their foliage. Its leaves are typically oval or heart-shaped, with a waxy texture and a variety of green, red, and purple hues.

Maranta bahiensis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
