Myrcia antioquensis

Myrcia antioquensis, commonly known as the Antioquian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Myrcia antioquensis is often found in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests, where it plays a role in local biodiversity.
