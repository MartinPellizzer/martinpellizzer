Mammillaria duoformis

Mammillaria duoformis, commonly known as the two-form cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is often adorned with white or yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
