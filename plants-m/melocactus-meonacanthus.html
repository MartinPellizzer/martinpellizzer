Melocactus meonacanthus

Melocactus meonacanthus, commonly known as the black-tipped melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem, which is typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and its spines are often black or dark brown, giving it its common name. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Melocactus meonacanthus is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
