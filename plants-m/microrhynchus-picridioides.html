Microrhynchus picridioides

Microrhynchus picridioides, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cup-shaped inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other wet habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
