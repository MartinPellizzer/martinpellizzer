Mycaranthes bifalcis

Mycaranthes bifalcis, commonly known as the two-lobed sand mat, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy, greenish-gray leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with minimal water availability.

Mycaranthes bifalcis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
