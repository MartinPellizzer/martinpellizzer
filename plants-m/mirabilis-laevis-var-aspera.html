Mirabilis laevis var. aspera

Mirabilis laevis var. aspera, commonly known as the roughleaf four o'clock, is a variant of the four o'clock plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rough, textured leaves and showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its unique foliage and bloom pattern make it a distinctive addition to ornamental horticulture.
