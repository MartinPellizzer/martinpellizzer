Manihot araliifolia

Manihot araliifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf manioc, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible roots, which can be processed into a starchy food product.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds that require careful preparation before consumption.
