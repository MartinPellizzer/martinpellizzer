Mentha × dumetorum var. subglabra

Mentha × dumetorum var. subglabra, commonly known as purple mint, is a hybrid plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale purple to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.
