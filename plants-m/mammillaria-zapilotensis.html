Mammillaria zapilotensis

Mammillaria zapilotensis, commonly known as the Zapilotensis cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a compact, rounded shape and a gray-green coloration. The plant features short, tuberculate ribs and produces white or pale yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It has spines that are typically white or pale, and the plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Mammillaria zapilotensis is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
