Miconia coloradensis

Miconia coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It features large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia coloradensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
