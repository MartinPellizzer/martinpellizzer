Musa ochracea

Musa ochracea, commonly known as the yellow banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and striking yellow-orange fruit. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, and the fruit develops in a bunch, similar to other banana species.

The yellow banana is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its edible fruit, which has a sweet, aromatic flavor.
