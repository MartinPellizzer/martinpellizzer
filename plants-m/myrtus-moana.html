Myrtus moana

Myrtus moana, commonly known as the Tahitian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features fragrant, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Myrtus moana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and tropical climates.
