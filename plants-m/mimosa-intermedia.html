Mimosa intermedia

Mimosa intermedia, commonly known as the sensitive plant or mimosa pudica, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnately compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by mechanical stimuli. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique leaf movements and rapid response to touch make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
