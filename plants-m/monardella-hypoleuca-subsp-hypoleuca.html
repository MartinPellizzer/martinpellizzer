Monardella hypoleuca subsp. hypoleuca

Monardella hypoleuca subsp.

hypoleuca, commonly known as white honeysuckle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
