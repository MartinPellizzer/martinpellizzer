Madhuca obovata

Madhuca obovata, commonly known as the broadleaf tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
