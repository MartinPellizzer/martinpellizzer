Malva longifolia

Malva longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks.

Malva longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its soothing properties.
