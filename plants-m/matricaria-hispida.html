Matricaria hispida

Matricaria hispida, commonly known as goatweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a hairy stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive grayish-green color. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry grasslands.

Matricaria hispida is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
