Miconia serrulata

Miconia serrulata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by distinctive, orange-red berries. Miconia serrulata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is also recognized for its potential role in ecological systems, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
