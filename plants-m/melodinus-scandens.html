Melodinus scandens

Melodinus scandens, commonly known as the climbing melon vine, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a fast-growing, vining plant that can reach lengths of several meters, making it suitable for trellises or arbors. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits that resemble small melons. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Melodinus scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
