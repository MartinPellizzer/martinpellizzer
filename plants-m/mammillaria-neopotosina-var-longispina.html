Mammillaria neopotosina var. longispina

Mammillaria neopotosina var. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and numerous small, cylindrical spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically features pale green to bluish-green stems covered in a layer of white wax. It produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

This variety is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
