Marrubium peregrinum var. creticum

Marrubium peregrinum var. creticum, commonly known as Cretan horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and small blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. It is part of the Boraginaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.

Marrubium peregrinum var. creticum has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for its expectorant and anti-inflammatory effects.
