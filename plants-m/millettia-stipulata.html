Millettia stipulata

Millettia stipulata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and rich color, though it is also listed as endangered due to overharvesting.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
