Mesembryanthemum puniceum

Mesembryanthemum puniceum, commonly known as the red stonecress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Mesembryanthemum puniceum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its drought tolerance and colorful displays.
