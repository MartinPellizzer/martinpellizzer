Macrotropis foetida

Macrotropis foetida, commonly known as the stinking nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant to be handled with care.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
