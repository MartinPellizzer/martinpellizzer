Memecylon edule var. rubrocaeruleum

Memecylon edule var. rubrocaeruleum, commonly known as the red-blue memecylon, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its small white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

This species is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
