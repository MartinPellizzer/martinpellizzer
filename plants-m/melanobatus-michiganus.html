Melanobatus michiganus

Melanobatus michiganus, commonly known as the Michigan black fern, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive blackish-brown stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spore-bearing structures called sorus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported astringent properties.
