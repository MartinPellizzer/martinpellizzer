Molineria rhizophylla

Molineria rhizophylla, commonly known as the root-leaf grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green coloration.

Molineria rhizophylla is typically found in coastal and inland grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.
