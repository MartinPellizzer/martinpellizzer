Mammillaria bucareliensis var. tamaulipa

Mammillaria bucareliensis var. tamaulipa, commonly known as the Tamaulipan mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the Mexican state of Tamaulipas.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and small, ribbed segments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
