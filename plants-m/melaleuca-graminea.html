Melaleuca graminea

Melaleuca graminea, commonly known as the grass tree, is a unique flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and a single, woody trunk that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in dense clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Melaleuca graminea is adapted to dry, coastal environments and is often found in heathlands and sand dunes.
