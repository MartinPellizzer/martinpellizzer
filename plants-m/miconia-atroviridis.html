Miconia atroviridis

Miconia atroviridis, commonly known as black-veined miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, dark green, glossy leaves with prominent black veins. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with white to pink flowers that bloom during the wet season. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain areas where it is introduced.
