Monocera integra

Monocera integra, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are encased in a hard, woody shell. Its botanical features include a simple, unbranched inflorescence and a unique seed structure.

Monocera integra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
