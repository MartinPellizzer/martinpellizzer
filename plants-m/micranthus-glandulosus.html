Micranthus glandulosus

Micranthus glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular micranthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it its scientific name. Native to certain regions of North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
