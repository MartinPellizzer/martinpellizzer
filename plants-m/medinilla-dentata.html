Medinilla dentata

Medinilla dentata, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble citrus but are not edible. Medinilla dentata thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and aromatic blooms make it a popular choice for gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
