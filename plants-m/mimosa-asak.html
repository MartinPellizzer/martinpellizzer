Mimosa asak

Mimosa asak, commonly known as the Japanese mimosa, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom in early spring. Mimosa asak is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form and a preference for well-drained soils.
