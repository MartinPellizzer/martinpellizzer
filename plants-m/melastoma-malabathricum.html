Melastoma malabathricum

Melastoma malabathricum, commonly known as the Java apple or Malabar elaeocarpus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
