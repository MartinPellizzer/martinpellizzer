Mallotus scabrifolius

Mallotus scabrifolius, commonly known as the rough-leaved mallot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically palmately arranged and have a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
