Melothria warmingii var. petiolata

Melothria warmingii var. petiolata, commonly known as the long-stemmed sour sop, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish-green fruits that turn orange when ripe. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is characterized by a rough, spiny exterior and a sweet, acidic flesh.

It is often cultivated for its unique flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
