Mylocaryum ligustrinum

Mylocaryum ligustrinum, commonly known as the purple-flowered starling vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant is characterized by its unique, star-shaped inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody stems are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
