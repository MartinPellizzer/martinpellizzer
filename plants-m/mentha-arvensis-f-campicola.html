Mentha arvensis f. campicola

Mentha arvensis f. campicola, commonly known as field mint or creeping mint, is a subspecies of the mint genus Mentha.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and aromatic leaves that emit a strong mint scent. The plant typically features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or white, arranged in whorled clusters.

Mentha arvensis f. campicola is often found in moist soils in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.
