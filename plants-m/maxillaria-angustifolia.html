Maxillaria angustifolia

Maxillaria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces fragrant, violet or purple flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

Maxillaria angustifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
