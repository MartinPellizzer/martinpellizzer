Manaosella platidactyla

Manaosella platidactyla, commonly known as the Brazilian star-nosed mole, is a unique mammal with a distinctive star-shaped snout used for detecting prey.

While not a plant, it is sometimes confused with botanical terms due to its name. This species is native to South America and thrives in moist, forested environments. It plays a vital role in its ecosystem by controlling insect populations.

Its physical adaptations make it well-suited for its subterranean lifestyle.
