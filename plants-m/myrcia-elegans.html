Myrcia elegans

Myrcia elegans, commonly known as the elegant myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, woody berries that contain several seeds.

Myrcia elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
