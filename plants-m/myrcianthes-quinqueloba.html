Myrcianthes quinqueloba

Myrcianthes quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Myrcianthes quinqueloba is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
