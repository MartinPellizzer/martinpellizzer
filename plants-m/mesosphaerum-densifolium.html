Mesosphaerum densifolium

Mesosphaerum densifolium, commonly known as the dense-leaved mesosphaerum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, overlapping foliage that gives it a compact appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Mesosphaerum densifolium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
