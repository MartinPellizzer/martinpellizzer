Manihot cearensis

Manihot cearensis, commonly known as the arrowleaf manioc, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds that require careful processing before consumption.
