Medicago arborea subsp. citrina

Medicago arborea subsp.

citrina, commonly known as the yellow tree clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
