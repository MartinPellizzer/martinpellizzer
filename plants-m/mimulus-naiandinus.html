Mimulus naiandinus

Mimulus naiandinus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats, including stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Mimulus naiandinus plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar and habitat for various insects.
