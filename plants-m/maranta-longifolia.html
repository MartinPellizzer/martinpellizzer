Maranta longifolia

Maranta longifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a starchy food source.

Maranta longifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
