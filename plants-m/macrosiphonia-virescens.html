Macrosiphonia virescens

Macrosiphonia virescens, commonly known as the green macrolichen, is a species of foliose lichen belonging to the family Parmeliaceae.

It is characterized by its flat, leaf-like thallus that is typically green to yellow-green in color. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship between a photosynthetic partner, usually a green alga, and a fungal partner from the Ascomycota division. It is often found growing on bark, rock, or soil in temperate and tropical regions.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered an indicator species for air quality and ecological health.
