Mentha longifolia f. velutina

Mentha longifolia f. velutina, commonly known as velvet-leaf mint, is a subspecies of the spearmint plant.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a strong, aromatic mint scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing flavor.
