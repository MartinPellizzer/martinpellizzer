Mallotus tokbrai

Mallotus tokbrai, commonly known as the Tokbrai or Malabar chestnut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in folk medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
