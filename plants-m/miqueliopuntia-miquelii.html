Miqueliopuntia miquelii

Miqueliopuntia miquelii, commonly known as the Miquel's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in dense clusters of spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
