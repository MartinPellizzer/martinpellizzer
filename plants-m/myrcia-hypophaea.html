Myrcia hypophaea

Myrcia hypophaea, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular plant in horticulture, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
