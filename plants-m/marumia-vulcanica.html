Marumia vulcanica

Marumia vulcanica, commonly known as the Fireflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the volcanic regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in late summer, resembling flames against the dark foliage. The plant thrives in nutrient-rich, well-drained soil and requires high levels of sunlight to flourish. Its unique coloration is due to the presence of anthocyanin pigments, which also serve as a protective mechanism against intense sunlight.

Marumia vulcanica is often found growing on rocky slopes and near geothermal vents, where its adaptability to extreme conditions is evident.
