Melaleuca adenostyla

Melaleuca adenostyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels away in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and often have a silvery or greyish underside, and it produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in coastal environments.
