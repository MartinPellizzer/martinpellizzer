Morus rubra var. pensylvanica

Morus rubra var. pensylvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant produces edible berries that are a food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in the production of silk.
