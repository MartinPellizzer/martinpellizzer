Melochia villosa var. villosa

Melochia villosa var. villosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open woodlands, and it has a moderate growth rate.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
