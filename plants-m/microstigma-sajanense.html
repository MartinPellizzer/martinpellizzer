Microstigma sajanense

Microstigma sajanense, commonly known as the Sajan mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Microstigma sajanense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
