Matthiola pseudoxyceras f. tunetana

Matthiola pseudoxyceras f. tunetana, commonly known as the Tunetan Matthiola, is a rare and unique botanical variant within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and displays striking, often bi-colored flowers that range from white to deep purple. The plant typically thrives in Mediterranean climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage is covered in fine, glandular hairs, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

This ornamental plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and fragrance.
