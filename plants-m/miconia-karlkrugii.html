Miconia karlkrugii

Miconia karlkrugii, commonly known as the Karl Krug miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Miconia karlkrugii is notable for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.
