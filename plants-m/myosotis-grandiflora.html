Myosotis grandiflora

Myosotis grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals, which are typically larger than those of other forget-me-not species. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Myosotis grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a symbol of remembrance and loyalty.
