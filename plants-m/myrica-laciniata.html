Myrica laciniata

Myrica laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wax myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Myrica laciniata thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use in suitable climates.
