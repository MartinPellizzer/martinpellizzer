Miconia decussata

Miconia decussata, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Miconia decussata is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
