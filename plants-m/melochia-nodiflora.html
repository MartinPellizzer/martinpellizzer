Melochia nodiflora

Melochia nodiflora, commonly known as the climbing melochia, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and pairs of opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are spherical, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, and they contain several seeds.

Melochia nodiflora is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
