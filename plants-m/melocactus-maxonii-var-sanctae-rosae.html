Melocactus maxonii var. sanctae-rosae

Melocactus maxonii var. sanctae-rosae, commonly known as the San Rafael melocactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in dense, white spines and a crown of short, hooked spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. It has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative cactus.
