Malva plicata

Malva plicata, commonly known as the Chinese mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous stems and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cuisines.

Malva plicata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
