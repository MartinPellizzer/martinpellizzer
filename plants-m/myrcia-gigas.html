Myrcia gigas

Myrcia gigas, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, bright red berries. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
