Macleania pulchra

Macleania pulchra, commonly known as the pink fairy fan, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
