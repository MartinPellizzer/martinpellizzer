Myrceugenia myrcioides var. acrophylla

Myrceugenia myrcioides var. acrophylla, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in gardens with mild climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
