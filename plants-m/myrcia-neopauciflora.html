Myrcia neopauciflora

Myrcia neopauciflora, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, often with a strong aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
