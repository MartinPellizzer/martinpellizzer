Malvastrum congestiflorum

Malvastrum congestiflorum, commonly known as the clustered mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its rough, hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Malvaceae family.
