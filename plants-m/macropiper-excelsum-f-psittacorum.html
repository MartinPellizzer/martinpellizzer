Macropiper excelsum f. psittacorum

Macropiper excelsum f. psittacorum, commonly known as the golden piper, is a rare and unique variant of the Macropiper genus.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves and compact growth habit, making it a distinctive addition to botanical collections. Native to specific regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage exhibits a unique variegation pattern that sets it apart from other members of the Piperaceae family.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
