Myrcia exploratoris

Myrcia exploratoris, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a dense, spreading canopy.

Myrcia exploratoris is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in perfumes and as a spice.
