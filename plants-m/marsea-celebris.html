Marsea celebris

Marsea celebris, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals that resemble a star, giving it its common name. The plant features a slender, upright stem with broad, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in humid environments. It blooms during the rainy season, producing clusters of fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Marsea celebris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
