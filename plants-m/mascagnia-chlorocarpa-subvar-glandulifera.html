Mascagnia chlorocarpa subvar. glandulifera

Mascagnia chlorocarpa subvar. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular Mascagnia, is a shrub native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
