Maba scabra

Maba scabra, commonly known as the rough maba, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its rough, textured bark and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
