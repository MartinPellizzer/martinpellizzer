Morphixia capillaris var. lancea

Morphixia capillaris var. lancea, commonly known as the lanceolate morphixia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually a pale yellow or greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and lesser-known species within the botanical community.
