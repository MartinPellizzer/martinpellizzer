Malva alceoides

Malva alceoides, commonly known as the alpine mallows, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its woolly, hairy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Malva alceoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic features.
