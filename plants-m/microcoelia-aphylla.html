Microcoelia aphylla

Microcoelia aphylla, commonly known as the leafless microcoelia, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its specialized morphology, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
