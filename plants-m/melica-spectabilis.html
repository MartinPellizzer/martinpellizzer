Melica spectabilis

Melica spectabilis, commonly known as the showy milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Melica spectabilis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
