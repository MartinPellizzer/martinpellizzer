Monnina exalata

Monnina exalata, commonly known as scarlet flax or red flax, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its striking red to orange-pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Monnina exalata is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its unique coloration and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.
