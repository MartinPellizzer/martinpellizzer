Merremia pterygocaulos var. chrysantha

Merremia pterygocaulos var. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and as a living fence.
