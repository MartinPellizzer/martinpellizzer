Moricandia moricandioides var. microsperma

Moricandia moricandioides var. microsperma, commonly known as the dwarf moricandia, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. It has slender, branched stems and small, lobed leaves that help reduce water loss.

Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.
