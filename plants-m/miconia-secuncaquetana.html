Miconia secuncaquetana

Miconia secuncaquetana, commonly known as the white miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds and other animals.

It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
