Melica imperfecta

Melica imperfecta, commonly known as the common melic, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Melica imperfecta is a perennial plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
