Mentha aquatica f. cuneiformis

Mentha aquatica f. cuneiformis, commonly known as narrow-leaved water mint, is a variant of the water mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, minty aroma. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is often used in herbal teas and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
