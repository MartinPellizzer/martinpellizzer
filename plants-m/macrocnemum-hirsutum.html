Macrocnemum hirsutum

Macrocnemum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy knotted grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features upright, cylindrical stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
