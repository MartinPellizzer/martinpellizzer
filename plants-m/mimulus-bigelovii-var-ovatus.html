Mimulus bigelovii var. ovatus

Mimulus bigelovii var. ovatus, commonly known as the bigelov's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. It has oval-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is part of the Physostegia section of the Mimulus genus, which includes several other species with similar floral structures.
