Melastoma sanguineum var. ranauense

Melastoma sanguineum var. ranauense, commonly known as the Ranau melastoma, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and has elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in its native habitats.
