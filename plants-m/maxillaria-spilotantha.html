Maxillaria spilotantha

Maxillaria spilotantha, commonly known as the spotted maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, hairy pseudobulbs. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and floral fragrance.
