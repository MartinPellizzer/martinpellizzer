Martynia speciosa

Martynia speciosa, commonly known as the red devil flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, peppers, and tobacco, and is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Martynia speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
