Megalastrum laxipilosum

Megalastrum laxipilosum, commonly known as the woolly megalastrum, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pilose, stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique features, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological interest.
