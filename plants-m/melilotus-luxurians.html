Melilotus luxurians

Melilotus luxurians, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Sweet clover is known for its strong, sweet fragrance and is often used in herbal medicine and as a forage crop.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
