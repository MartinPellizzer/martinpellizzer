Mouriri sideroxylon

Mouriri sideroxylon, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a glossy surface, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible fruits.

The species is also known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
