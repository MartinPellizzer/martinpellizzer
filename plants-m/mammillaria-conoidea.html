Mammillaria conoidea

Mammillaria conoidea, commonly known as the cone-shaped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a spiny, segmented stem that often develops a conical shape as it matures. The plant features white or pale green flowers that bloom in the spring, typically in shades of yellow or orange. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted for storing water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Mammillaria conoidea is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its compact size and unique appearance.
