Macrolinum ciliatum

Macrolinum ciliatum, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves with fine, thread-like hairs along the edges. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Macrolinum ciliatum is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
