Magnolia pubipedunculata

Magnolia pubipedunculata, commonly known as the pubipedunculate magnolia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
