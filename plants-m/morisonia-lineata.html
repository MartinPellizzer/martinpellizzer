Morisonia lineata

Morisonia lineata, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, spider-like flowers with long, slender petals and a distinctive, club-shaped lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Morisonia lineata is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
