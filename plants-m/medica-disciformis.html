Medica disciformis

Medica disciformis, commonly known as the disc-shaped medica, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive disc-like inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a flat, circular pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or swampy areas. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Medica disciformis is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
