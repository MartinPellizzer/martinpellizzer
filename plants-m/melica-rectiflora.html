Melica rectiflora

Melica rectiflora, commonly known as the straight-flowered brome, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Melica rectiflora is often found in regions with temperate climates and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
