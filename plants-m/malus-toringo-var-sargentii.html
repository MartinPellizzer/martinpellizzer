Malus toringo var. sargentii

Malus toringo var. sargentii, commonly known as the Sargent crabapple, is a compact, ornamental tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by ornamental red or yellow fruits. The tree has a rounded, spreading form with dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. It is often cultivated for its showy blossoms and is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.

This variety is well-suited for small gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
