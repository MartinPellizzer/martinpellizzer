Melodorum crassipetalum

Melodorum crassipetalum, commonly known as the broad-petaled melody plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in shaded, moist forest environments. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
