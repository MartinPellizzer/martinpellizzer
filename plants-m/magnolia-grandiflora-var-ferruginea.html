Magnolia grandiflora var. ferruginea

Magnolia grandiflora var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are broad, elliptical, and have a smooth, dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. The bark is thick and fissured, often appearing reddish-brown, which gives the plant its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
