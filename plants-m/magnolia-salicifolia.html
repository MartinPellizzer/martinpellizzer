Magnolia salicifolia

Magnolia salicifolia, commonly known as the western magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age, and its branches are often drooping, giving it a graceful appearance. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
