Moehringia dasyphylla subsp. sedifolia

Moehringia dasyphylla subsp.

sedifolia, commonly known as the woolly moehringia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically produces small, white to pale pink flowers clustered in terminal racemes. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.
