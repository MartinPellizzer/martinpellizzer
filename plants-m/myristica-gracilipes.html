Myristica gracilipes

Myristica gracilipes, commonly known as the slender nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers and dark, aromatic seeds. The plant is notable for its unique morphology, featuring a compact stature and clustered inflorescences. Its seeds, while not as commercially valuable as those of Myristica fragrans, are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The species is also of interest in botanical research due to its ecological adaptations and potential for horticultural cultivation.
