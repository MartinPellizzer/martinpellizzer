Morus papyrifera

Morus papyrifera, commonly known as the paper mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, hairy fruit clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional crafts and medicinal practices.

The tree plays an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
