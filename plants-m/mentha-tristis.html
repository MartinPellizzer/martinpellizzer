Mentha tristis

Mentha tristis, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Mentha tristis is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

It prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
