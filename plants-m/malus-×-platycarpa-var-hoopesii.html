Malus × platycarpa var. hoopesii

Malus × platycarpa var. hoopesii, commonly known as the Hoopes' crabapple, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental fruits and showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

The fruit, which is edible but tart, is often used in jams and preserves.
