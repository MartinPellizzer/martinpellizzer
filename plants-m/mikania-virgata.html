Mikania virgata

Mikania virgata, commonly known as the common mikania, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and hairy, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forests.

Mikania virgata is known for its ability to spread aggressively, making it both a useful forage plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
