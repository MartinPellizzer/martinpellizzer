Millettia bipindensis

Millettia bipindensis, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
