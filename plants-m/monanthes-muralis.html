Monanthes muralis

Monanthes muralis, commonly known as the wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows on rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal areas and along cliffs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers come in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

Monanthes muralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
