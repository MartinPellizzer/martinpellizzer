Myristica scortechinii

Myristica scortechinii, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg tree (Myristica fragrans). The plant produces a valuable seed called mace, which is used as a spice and in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves also contain essential oils with aromatic properties.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its economic and medicinal significance.
