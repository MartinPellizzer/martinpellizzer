Melica baetica

Melica baetica, commonly known as the Iberian brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a compact spike of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Melica baetica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for invertebrates.
