Myrica adenophora var. kusanoi

Myrica adenophora var. kusanoi, commonly known as Kusano's wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by dark blue, waxy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas or wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
