Mentha sylvestris subsp. nepetoides

Mentha sylvestris subsp.

nepetoides, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and marshy areas.

The plant has square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. Its fragrant, purple flowers bloom in whorls along the stem, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
