Magnolia fragrans

Magnolia fragrans, commonly known as the sweet magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
