Myrtus arayan

Myrtus arayan, commonly known as the Armenian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, characterized by aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a strong, spicy scent.

Myrtus arayan is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
