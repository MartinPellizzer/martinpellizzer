Maranta hatschbachiana

Maranta hatschbachiana, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that often display vibrant red or purple veins. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family, known for its ornamental foliage rather than showy flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique leaf patterns, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
