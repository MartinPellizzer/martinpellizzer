Mutisia

Mutisia, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Asteraceae family.

These plants are native to the Americas, particularly Central and South America, and some species are found in the Caribbean. Mutisia species are typically herbaceous, with alternate leaves and showy yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters. They often grow in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Some species are used in traditional medicine, while others are cultivated for ornamental purposes.
