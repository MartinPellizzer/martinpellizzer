Mentha arvensis var. procumbens

Mentha arvensis var. procumbens, commonly known as creeping mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are oval to heart-shaped, with a strong mint aroma, and are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
