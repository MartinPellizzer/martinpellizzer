Marrubium supinum var. bombycinum

Marrubium supinum var. bombycinum, commonly known as the woolly horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and contribute to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
