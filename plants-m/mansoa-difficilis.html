Mansoa difficilis

Mansoa difficilis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped, palmate leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is known for its ornamental value.

The flowers open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
