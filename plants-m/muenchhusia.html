Muenchhusia

Muenchhusia, commonly known as the Mexican flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It features striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces long, trailing stems that can climb or trail along supports. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Muenchhusia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
