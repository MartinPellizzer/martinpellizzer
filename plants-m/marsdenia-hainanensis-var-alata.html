Marsdenia hainanensis var. alata

Marsdenia hainanensis var. alata, commonly known as the Hainan climbing milkweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers with a unique corona structure, which is typical of the milkweed family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
