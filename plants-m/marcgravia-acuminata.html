Marcgravia acuminata

Marcgravia acuminata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or arrowleaf.

It is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, which gives it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in spikes.

Marcgravia acuminata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
