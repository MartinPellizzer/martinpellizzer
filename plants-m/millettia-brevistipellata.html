Millettia brevistipellata

Millettia brevistipellata, commonly known as the short-stippled millettia, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
