Malus hupehensis var. menshanensis

Malus hupehensis var. menshanensis, commonly known as the Mengshan crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental flowers, which range from white to pink, and its distinctive, crisp, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.
