Masdevallia guttulata

Masdevallia guttulata, commonly known as the spotted masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green leaves and intricate, often colorful flower spikes. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers, which can be pink, red, or orange, are often adorned with distinctive spots or patterns.

Masdevallia guttulata is valued by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively compact size.
