Myrcia liesneri

Myrcia liesneri, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia liesneri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming an invasive species.
