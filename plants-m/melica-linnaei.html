Melica linnaei

Melica linnaei, commonly known as Linnaean grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Melica linnaei is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
