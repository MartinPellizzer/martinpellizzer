Metroxylon sagu f. tuberatum

Metroxylon sagu f. tuberatum, commonly known as the sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces a starchy pith in its trunk, which is harvested for food and industrial uses. It thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its economic importance, it is often cultivated for its edible starch and ornamental value.
