Musteron uniflorum

Musteron uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered musteron, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, solitary flower that typically blooms in late spring. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and ecological role in its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
