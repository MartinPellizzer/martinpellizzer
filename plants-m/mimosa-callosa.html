Mimosa callosa

Mimosa callosa, commonly known as the rough mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

Mimosa callosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
