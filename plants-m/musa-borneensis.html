Musa borneensis

Musa borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean banana, is a tropical plant native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically smaller and less sweet compared to common bananas. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown in botanical gardens and tropical regions.
