Myrrhidium urbanum

Myrrhidium urbanum, commonly known as the urban myrrh plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Myrrhidium urbanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
