Medicago noccae

Medicago noccae, commonly known as the common vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Medicago noccae is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
