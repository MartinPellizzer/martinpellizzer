Malaxis retusa

Malaxis retusa, commonly known as the slender malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single or few leaves, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and often clustered in a spike. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of a visible corona.

Malaxis retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral structure.
