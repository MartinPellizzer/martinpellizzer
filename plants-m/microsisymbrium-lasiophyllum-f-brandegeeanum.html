Microsisymbrium lasiophyllum f. brandegeeanum

Microsisymbrium lasiophyllum f. brandegeeanum, commonly known as the Brandegee's leafy lichen, is a foliose lichen with a distinctive leaf-like structure.

It typically grows on soil or rock surfaces in arid or semi-arid environments. The thallus is usually greenish to yellowish, with a dense, compact growth form. It is characterized by its intricate network of fungal hyphae and algal cells that form a symbiotic relationship.

This lichen is often found in regions with low humidity and high sunlight exposure.
