Malus baccata var. praecox

Malus baccata var. praecox, commonly known as the early Chinese crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, tart fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
