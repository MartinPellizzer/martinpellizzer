Mammillaria hahniana subsp. woodsii

Mammillaria hahniana subsp.

woodsii, commonly known as the Woods' mammillaria, is a compact cactus native to Mexico. It features a globular stem with prominent tubercles and spines that are typically white to yellowish in color.

The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its compact shape.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
