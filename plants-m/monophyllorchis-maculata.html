Monophyllorchis maculata

Monophyllorchis maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

Monophyllorchis maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
