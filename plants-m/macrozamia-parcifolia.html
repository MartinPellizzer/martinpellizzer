Macrozamia parcifolia

Macrozamia parcifolia, commonly known as the slender cycad, is a slow-growing evergreen plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves that form a dense rosette around the trunk. The plant produces a tall, cylindrical seed cone that matures over several years. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a warm, dry climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
