Masdevallia lewisii

Masdevallia lewisii, commonly known as Lewis's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces intricate, colorful flowers with a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, humid environments with dappled sunlight. Its flowers are often violet or purple, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Masdevallia lewisii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivation under the right conditions.
