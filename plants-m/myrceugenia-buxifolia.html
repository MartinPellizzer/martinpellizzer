Myrceugenia buxifolia

Myrceugenia buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Myrceugenia buxifolia is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
