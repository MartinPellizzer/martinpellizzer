Moldavica thymiflora

Moldavica thymiflora, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its aromatic properties due to the presence of essential oils in its foliage. Moldavica thymiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a decorative plant highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
