Melastoma clavatum

Melastoma clavatum, commonly known as the clustered melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Melastoma clavatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
