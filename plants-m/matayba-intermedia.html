Matayba intermedia

Matayba intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces edible red berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
