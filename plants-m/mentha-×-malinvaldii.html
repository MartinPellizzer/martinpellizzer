Mentha × malinvaldii

Mentha × malinvaldii, commonly known as the Malinvaldii mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha × piperita and Mentha × gracilis.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Mentha × malinvaldii is native to Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
