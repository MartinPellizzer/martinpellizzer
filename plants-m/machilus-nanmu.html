Machilus nanmu

Machilus nanmu, commonly known as Chinese white ash, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Machilus nanmu is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
