Myrospermum sonsonatense

Myrospermum sonsonatense, commonly known as the Sonsonate myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along riverbanks and in dense forests. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
