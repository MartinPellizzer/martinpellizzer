Mentzelia integra

Mentzelia integra, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of white, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Mentzelia integra thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
