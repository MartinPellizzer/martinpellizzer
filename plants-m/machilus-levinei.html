Machilus levinei

Machilus levinei, commonly known as the Chinese mahogany, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southeastern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by elliptical, dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
