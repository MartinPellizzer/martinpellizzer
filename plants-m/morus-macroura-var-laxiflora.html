Morus macroura var. laxiflora

Morus macroura var. laxiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, black, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
