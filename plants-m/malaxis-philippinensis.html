Malaxis philippinensis

Malaxis philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine malaxis, is an epiphytic orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces small, round seeds.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
