Melicope tahitensis

Melicope tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian melicope, is a member of the Rutaceae family, native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal and lowland forest habitats. The plant features simple, opposite, and often aromatic leaves, which are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small and clustered, with white or pale yellow petals, and it produces small, round berries as fruit.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in Polynesian traditions.
