Muscarella exesilabia

Muscarella exesilabia, commonly known as the slender muscaria, is a rare and striking member of the Amanita family.

It is characterized by its small, white cap that often develops a faintly bruised blue tint when cut or damaged. The stem is slender and often has a bulbous base, which distinguishes it from similar species. It typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and is found in parts of Southeast Asia.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle and identify this species with care.
