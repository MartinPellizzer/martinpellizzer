Myrceugenia reitzii

Myrceugenia reitzii, commonly known as the Reitz's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the subtropical regions of southern Brazil, particularly in the state of Rio Grande do Sul. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.
