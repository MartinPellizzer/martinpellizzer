Melothria gracilipes

Melothria gracilipes, commonly known as the slender leaf melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, edible fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are slender and tend to twine around supporting structures. The flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and the plant thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its unique flavor and nutritional value.
