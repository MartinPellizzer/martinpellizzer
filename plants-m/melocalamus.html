Melocalamus

Melocalamus, commonly known as the red-tailed pine, is a genus of coniferous evergreen trees native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

These trees are characterized by their slender, tall trunks and long, needle-like leaves that are often a dark green color. The cones of Melocalamus species are typically small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. They thrive in dry, well-drained soils and are often found in mountainous regions.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in forest ecosystems.
