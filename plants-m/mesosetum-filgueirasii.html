Mesosetum filgueirasii

Mesosetum filgueirasii, commonly known as the Filgueira's grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like cluster of small flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Mesosetum filgueirasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
