Millettia aurea

Millettia aurea, commonly known as golden millettia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks.

Millettia aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
