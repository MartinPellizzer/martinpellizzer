Mariscus viguieri var. contractus

Mariscus viguieri var. contractus, commonly known as the dwarf mariscus, is a rare and compact variety of the mariscus plant.

It is characterized by its small, dense foliage and low-growing habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest. This variety is native to specific regions in Europe and thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare and unusual plants.
