Melilotus minimus

Melilotus minimus, commonly known as lesser melilot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches heights of 20 to 60 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in narrow, pod-like fruits that dehisce to release the seeds.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and grasslands.
