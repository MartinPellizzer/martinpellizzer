Mentha × kerneri var. transsilvanica

Mentha × kerneri var. transsilvanica, commonly known as Transylvanian mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and stems, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, menthol-like scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
