Myrtus archboldiana

Myrtus archboldiana, commonly known as the Hawaiian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small white flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in shaded, moist areas.

Myrtus archboldiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Hawaiian practices.
