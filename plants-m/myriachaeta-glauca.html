Myriachaeta glauca

Myriachaeta glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Myriachaeta glauca is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
