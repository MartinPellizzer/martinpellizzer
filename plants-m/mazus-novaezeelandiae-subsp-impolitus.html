Mazus novaezeelandiae subsp. impolitus

Mazus novaezeelandiae subsp.

impolitus, commonly known as the New Zealand mazus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to New Zealand. It features prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats on the ground.

The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a soft, hairy texture and a bluish-green coloration. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically in late spring to early summer.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
