Mostacillastrum saltaense

Mostacillastrum saltaense, commonly known as Saltan grass, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in saline and drought-prone environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for ecological and agricultural research.
