Mulgedium meridionale

Mulgedium meridionale, commonly known as the southern mulgedium, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mulgedium meridionale is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
