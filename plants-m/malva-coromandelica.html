Malva coromandelica

Malva coromandelica, commonly known as the Coromandel mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Malva coromandelica is also valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
