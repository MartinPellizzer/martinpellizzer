Muraltia arcuata

Muraltia arcuata, commonly known as the red sandalwood or the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate, and its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that contain hard, angular seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
