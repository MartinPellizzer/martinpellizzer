Myrcia uberavensis var. rotundifolia

Myrcia uberavensis var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf myrcia, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical to round leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
