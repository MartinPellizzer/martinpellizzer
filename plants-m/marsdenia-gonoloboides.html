Marsdenia gonoloboides

Marsdenia gonoloboides, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. Its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
