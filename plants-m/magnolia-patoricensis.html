Magnolia patoricensis

Magnolia patoricensis, commonly known as the Cuban magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Cuba.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, becoming fissured with age.

The Cuban magnolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
