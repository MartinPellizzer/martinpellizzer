Melandrium dioicum subsp. album

Melandrium dioicum subsp.

album, commonly known as the white catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
