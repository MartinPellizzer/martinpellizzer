Micromeria graeca subsp. imperica

Micromeria graeca subsp.

imperica, commonly known as the Greek micromeria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers with five petals. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
