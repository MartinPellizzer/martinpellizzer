Malpighia setosa

Malpighia setosa, commonly known as the velvet berry or sour sop, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and red, fleshy berries that ripen to a deep orange color. The plant has velvety, pubescent leaves and produces edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The berries are also used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
