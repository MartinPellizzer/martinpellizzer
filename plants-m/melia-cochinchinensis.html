Melia cochinchinensis

Melia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese mahogany or Indian cedar, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.
