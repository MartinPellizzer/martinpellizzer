Melanthesopsis

Melanthesopsis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying instead on its roots for nutrient absorption. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Melanthesopsis is listed as endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
