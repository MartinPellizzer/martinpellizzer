Macroglena

Macroglena, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features large, broad leaves that can grow up to three feet in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant has showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Macroglena plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
