Monarda lindheimeri

Monarda lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's bee balm, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Monarda lindheimeri thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
