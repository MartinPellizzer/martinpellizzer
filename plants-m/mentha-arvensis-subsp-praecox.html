Mentha arvensis subsp. praecox

Mentha arvensis subsp.

praecox, commonly known as early field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist soils in meadows, along stream banks, and in other damp habitats.

The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and have a strong menthol scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, producing a mild minty fragrance.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
