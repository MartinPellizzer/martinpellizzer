Machaerium robiniifolium var. ampliatum

Machaerium robiniifolium var. ampliatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lanceolate leaflets that are often glossy and arranged in pairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale pink flowers, which are typically fragrant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
