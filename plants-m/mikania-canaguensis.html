Mikania canaguensis

Mikania canaguensis, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Mikania canaguensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
