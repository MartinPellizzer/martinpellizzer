Myrcia rorida

Myrcia rorida, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Myrcia rorida thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
