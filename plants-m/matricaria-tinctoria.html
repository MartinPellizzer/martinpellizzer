Matricaria tinctoria

Matricaria tinctoria, commonly known as the dyer's chamomile, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flower heads that resemble chamomile. The plant is known for its aromatic properties, with the flowers containing essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Its historical use in dyeing fabrics and as a medicinal herb highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
