Monardella

Monardella, commonly known as wild bergamot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Monardella typically grows in dry, sunny environments and has aromatic, hairy leaves.

It is often used in herbal teas and has historical significance in Native American medicine.
