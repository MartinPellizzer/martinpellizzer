Matricaria procumbens

Matricaria procumbens, commonly known as field chamomile, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
