Majorana heracleotica

Majorana heracleotica, commonly known as wild thyme, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, aromatic leaves and pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, minty fragrance.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal medicine.
