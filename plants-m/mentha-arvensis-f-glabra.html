Mentha arvensis f. glabra

Mentha arvensis f. glabra, commonly known as horse mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Mentha arvensis f. glabra is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its digestive and calming effects.
