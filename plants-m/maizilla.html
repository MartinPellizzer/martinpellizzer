Maizilla

Maizilla, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. Maizilla has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
