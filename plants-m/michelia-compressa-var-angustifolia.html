Michelia compressa var. angustifolia

Michelia compressa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Michelia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
