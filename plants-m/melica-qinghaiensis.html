Melica qinghaiensis

Melica qinghaiensis, commonly known as the Qinghai milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Qinghai region of China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its unique ecological adaptations, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
