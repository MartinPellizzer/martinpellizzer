Montiopsis gayana

Montiopsis gayana, commonly known as the Andean mountainflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 centimeters. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with five petals and a distinctive central spur.

Montiopsis gayana is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.
