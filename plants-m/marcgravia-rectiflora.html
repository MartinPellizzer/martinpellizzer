Marcgravia rectiflora

Marcgravia rectiflora, commonly known as the straight-flowered snow vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Marcgraviaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in panicles, which are visually appealing and attract various pollinators. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Marcgravia rectiflora thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
