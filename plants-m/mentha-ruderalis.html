Mentha ruderalis

Mentha ruderalis, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Mentha ruderalis is known for its strong, aromatic scent and is often found growing in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

It is a hardy species that thrives in a variety of environmental conditions.
