Malva humifusa

Malva humifusa, commonly known as the soft-hairy mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

Malva humifusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
