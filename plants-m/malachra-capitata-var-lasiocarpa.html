Malachra capitata var. lasiocarpa

Malachra capitata var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the silky malachra, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique texture and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
