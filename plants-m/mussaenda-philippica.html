Mussaenda philippica

Mussaenda philippica, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with red or pink markings. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, making it an important species in ecological gardens.
