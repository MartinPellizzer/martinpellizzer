Mentha × grata var. variegata

Mentha × grata var. variegata, commonly known as variegated mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its leaves, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant is a low-growing perennial herb with square stems and small, fragrant leaves that emit a minty aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
