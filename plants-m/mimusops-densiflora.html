Mimusops densiflora

Mimusops densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
