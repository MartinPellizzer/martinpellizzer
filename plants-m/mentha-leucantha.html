Mentha leucantha

Mentha leucantha, commonly known as white mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and a distinctive white underside. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically white or pale blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Mentha leucantha is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
