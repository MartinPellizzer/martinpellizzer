Myosotis suaveolens subsp. asiatica

Myosotis suaveolens subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as the Asian forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
