Moliniopsis japonica

Moliniopsis japonica, commonly known as Japanese windmill grass, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in loose clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Moliniopsis japonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
