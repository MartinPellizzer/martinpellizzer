Mentha arvensis f. glabrata

Mentha arvensis f. glabrata, commonly known as field mint or broadleaf mint, is a variant of the common mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. This plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb due to their pungent flavor.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.
