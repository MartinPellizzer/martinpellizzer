Myrcia longisepala

Myrcia longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepal myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and birds.

The wood of Myrcia longisepala is valued for its durability and has been used in traditional craftsmanship.
