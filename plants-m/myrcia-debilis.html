Myrcia debilis

Myrcia debilis, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
