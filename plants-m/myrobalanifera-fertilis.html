Myrobalanifera fertilis

Myrobalanifera fertilis, commonly known as the fertilized myrobalan tree, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's fruit is a hard, woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Myrobalanifera fertilis thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
