Musa nepalensis

Musa nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal banana, is a species of flowering plant in the Musaceae family.

It is native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, India, and Bhutan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features large, broad leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit, which is typically not consumed by humans, is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Musa nepalensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
