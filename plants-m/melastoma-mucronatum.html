Melastoma mucronatum

Melastoma mucronatum, commonly known as the Malayan bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
