Myriomeles

Myriomeles, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are typically sour and seedy. Myriomeles is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
