Malus hupehensis

Malus hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, appearing in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin.

Malus hupehensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
