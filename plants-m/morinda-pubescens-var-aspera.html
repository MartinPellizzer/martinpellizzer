Morinda pubescens var. aspera

Morinda pubescens var. aspera, commonly known as rough morinda, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features woody stems covered in stiff, pubescent hairs, giving it its varietal name "aspera.". The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
