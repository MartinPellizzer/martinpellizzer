Montia biapiculata

Montia biapiculata, commonly known as the two-lobed miner's lettuce, is a small annual herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in moist, rocky habitats.

The species is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.
