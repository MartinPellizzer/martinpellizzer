Megastigma balsense

Megastigma balsense, commonly known as the golden balsam, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties, which contribute to its use in perfumery and herbal remedies.
