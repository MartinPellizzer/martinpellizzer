Malortiea lacerata

Malortiea lacerata, commonly known as the broken-leaved orchid, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves that are often torn or frayed, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
