Micropus grayanus

Micropus grayanus, commonly known as the Gray's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, paddle-shaped stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as thick, waxy leaves to reduce water loss. Its fruit, which resembles a small pear, is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
