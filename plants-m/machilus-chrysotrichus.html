Machilus chrysotrichus

Machilus chrysotrichus, commonly known as golden-haired machilus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to China, particularly found in the provinces of Yunnan and Sichuan, where it grows in humid, subtropical forests. The plant is characterized by its slender, evergreen leaves that are often covered with fine, golden hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and appear in clusters.

The fruit is a small, dark brown drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
