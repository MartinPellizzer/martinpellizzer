Mucuna sakapipei

Mucuna sakapipei, commonly known as the sacred bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Mucuna sakapipei is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it both a useful and sometimes invasive species in certain ecosystems.
