Matthiola tristis var. lateritia

Matthiola tristis var. lateritia, commonly known as the rock stock, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, four-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
