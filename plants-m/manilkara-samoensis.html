Manilkara samoensis

Manilkara samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan star apple, is a tropical tree native to the islands of Samoa and nearby Pacific regions.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy white flowers. The tree produces a spherical fruit with a thick, yellowish skin and a sweet, juicy pulp containing numerous seeds. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
