Mertensia integrifolia

Mertensia integrifolia, commonly known as bluebells, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically smooth and entire, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Mertensia integrifolia is also valued for its fragrant foliage and its role in supporting pollinators.
