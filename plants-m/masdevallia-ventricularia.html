Masdevallia ventricularia

Masdevallia ventricularia, commonly known as the ventricular masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique, often colorful flowers that resemble small, pouch-like structures. The plant typically features long, slender leaves and produces blooms in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its striking appearance and limited natural range, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
