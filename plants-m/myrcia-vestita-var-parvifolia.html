Myrcia vestita var. parvifolia

Myrcia vestita var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
