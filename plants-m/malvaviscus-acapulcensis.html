Malvaviscus acapulcensis

Malvaviscus acapulcensis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
