Matelea eximia

Matelea eximia, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweeds and related species. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.
