Machilus acuminatissimus var. tasulinensis

Machilus acuminatissimus var. tasulinensis, commonly known as the Tasul holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
