Myrtus suffruticosa var. angustifolia

Myrtus suffruticosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf myrtle, is a dense, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a aromatic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice in horticulture.
