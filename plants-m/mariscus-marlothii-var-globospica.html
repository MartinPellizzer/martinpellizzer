Mariscus marlothii var. globospica

Mariscus marlothii var. globospica, commonly known as the globular spiky mariscus, is a unique succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, spiky flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss.

This variety is prized for its striking appearance and adaptability to harsh environments.
