Mnesithea rupincola

Mnesithea rupincola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered inflorescence. The plant typically features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
