Malus × robusta f. erecta

Malus × robusta f. erecta, commonly known as the upright apple tree, is a cultivar of the apple tree characterized by its upright growth habit.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Malus sylvestris and Malus domestica. The plant is known for its dense foliage and compact form, making it suitable for smaller gardens. It produces small, ornamental flowers in spring, followed by edible fruit in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
