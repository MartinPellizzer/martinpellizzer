Musa velutina subsp. markkuana

Musa velutina subsp.

markkuana, commonly known as the Markku's banana, is a less commonly cultivated subspecies of the banana plant. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines.

This subspecies is characterized by its large, broad leaves and robust growth habit. The fruit is typically elongated and has a mild, sweet flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its edible fruit in some regions.
