Melica mutica f. diffusa

Melica mutica f. diffusa, commonly known as the diffuse slender wheatgrass, is a variant of the slender wheatgrass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, temperate regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a height that can reach up to 30 centimeters.

It is often found in meadows and along stream banks, contributing to soil stabilization and ecological diversity.
