Mespilus × dispessa

Mespilus × dispessa, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a hybrid species within the genus Mespilus, native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows to a height of 8–12 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, grey-brown bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate to elliptic in shape, with a serrated margin and a dull green upper surface. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that ripen to a dark purple color.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
