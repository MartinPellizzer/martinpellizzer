Maranta coriifolia

Maranta coriifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Maranta coriifolia is also valued for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisine.
