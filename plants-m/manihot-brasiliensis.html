Manihot brasiliensis

Manihot brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to tropical South America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its tubers, which are rich in starch, are used as a food source in some regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities due to its striking foliage.
