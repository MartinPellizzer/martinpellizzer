Macaranga latebrosa

Macaranga latebrosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved macaranga, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its rough, textured leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate and ovate, with a prominent midrib and serrated margins.

Macaranga latebrosa is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
