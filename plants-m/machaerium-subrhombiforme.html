Machaerium subrhombiforme

Machaerium subrhombiforme, commonly known as the rhomboid machaerium, is a tropical perennial shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical climates.

Machaerium subrhombiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some indigenous communities.
