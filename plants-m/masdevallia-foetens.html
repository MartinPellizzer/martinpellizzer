Masdevallia foetens

Masdevallia foetens, commonly known as the stinking masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, often fragrant or malodorous flowers, which can range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, with a slender rhizome and a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually clustered in a raceme and are known for their intricate structure and sometimes strong scent.

Masdevallia foetens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
