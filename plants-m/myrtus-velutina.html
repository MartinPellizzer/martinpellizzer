Myrtus velutina

Myrtus velutina, commonly known as velvet myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are small, dark berries that ripen in the fall and are edible.

Myrtus velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and aromatic qualities.
