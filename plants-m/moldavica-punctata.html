Moldavica punctata

Moldavica punctata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with distinctive yellow or white markings. The plant has a fibrous root system and hairy stems that are often reddish in hue. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

Moldavica punctata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
