Macaranga membranacea

Macaranga membranacea, commonly known as the paper mulberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant has palmate leaves that are typically lobed and have a smooth, papery texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits.

The plant is known for its fast growth and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
