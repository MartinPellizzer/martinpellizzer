Malus domestica var. calvillea

Malus domestica var. calvillea, commonly known as the Calville apple.

This variety is a cultivar of the domestic apple, characterized by its distinctive red and yellow striped skin. It is typically medium to large in size with a firm, crisp texture and a sweet, aromatic flavor. The tree is relatively vigorous and produces fruit in late summer to early autumn.

It is valued for both its ornamental qualities and culinary use in baking and cider-making.
