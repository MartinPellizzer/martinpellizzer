Muscarella longilabris

Muscarella longilabris, commonly known as the long-labied muscaria, is a rare and distinctive member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces a unique, elongated labellum that sets it apart from other species in the genus. Native to specific regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
