Millettia buteoides

Millettia buteoides, commonly known as the Butea tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, spiral-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The Butea tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some regions.
