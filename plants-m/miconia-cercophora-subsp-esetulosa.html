Miconia cercophora subsp. esetulosa

Miconia cercophora subsp.

esetulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling starfruits. The fruits are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
