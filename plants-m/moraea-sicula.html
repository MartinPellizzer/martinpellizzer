Moraea sicula

Moraea sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds effectively.

Moraea sicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
