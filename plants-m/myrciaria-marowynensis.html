Myrciaria marowynensis

Myrciaria marowynensis, commonly known as the Marowyn cherry, is a species of flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers and red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with rich, well-drained soil.

Due to its unique fruit and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
