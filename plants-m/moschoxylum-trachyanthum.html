Moschoxylum trachyanthum

Moschoxylum trachyanthum, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
