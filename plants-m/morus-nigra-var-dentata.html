Morus nigra var. dentata

Morus nigra var. dentata, commonly known as the black mulberry with serrated leaves, is a variant of the black mulberry tree native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically harvested when fully ripe. The leaves of this variety are distinctly serrated, giving it a unique appearance compared to other mulberry species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.

The plant is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
