Mirabilis eutricha

Mirabilis eutricha, commonly known as the hairy four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Nyctaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at night. The plant has hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
