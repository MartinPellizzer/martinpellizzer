Melocactus bellavistensis subsp. onychacanthus

Melocactus bellavistensis subsp.

onychacanthus, commonly known as the spiny-tipped melocactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stem, which is typically covered in dense, spiny areoles.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as a rare and attractive cactus species.
