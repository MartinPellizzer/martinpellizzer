Muhlenbergia curvula

Muhlenbergia curvula, commonly known as the silver muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and slender, arching stems that create a graceful, flowing appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that add visual interest during the late spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Muhlenbergia curvula is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
