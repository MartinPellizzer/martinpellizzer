Mentha × schultziana var. carinthiaca

Mentha × schultziana var. carinthiaca, commonly known as Carinthian mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its fragrant, dark green, ovate leaves and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its soothing properties.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic qualities in both gardens and culinary applications.
