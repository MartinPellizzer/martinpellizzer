Malva ononidifolia

Malva ononidifolia, commonly known as Chinese mallow, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often found in moist environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.

The seeds of Malva ononidifolia are encased in a fluffy, cotton-like capsule, aiding in their dispersal by wind.
