Malus columbina

Malus columbina, commonly known as the western sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, multi-branched structure. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, followed by small, tart red fruits in the summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Malus columbina is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
