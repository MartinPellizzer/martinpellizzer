Mentha × conspicua

Mentha × conspicua, commonly known as the purple mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, purple or violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a serrated margin and a strong, minty fragrance.

Mentha × conspicua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional herbal remedies.
