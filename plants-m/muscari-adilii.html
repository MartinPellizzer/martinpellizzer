Muscari adilii

Muscari adilii, commonly known as Adil's grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep violet, resembling tiny grapes. The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Muscari adilii prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its early spring blooming period.
