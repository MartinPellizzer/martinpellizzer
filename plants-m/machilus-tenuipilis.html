Machilus tenuipilis

Machilus tenuipilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chinquapin, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
