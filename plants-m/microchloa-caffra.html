Microchloa caffra

Microchloa caffra, commonly known as the Cape grass or African microchloa, is a small, perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Microchloa caffra is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
