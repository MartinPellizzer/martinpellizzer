Mammillaria fischeri

Mammillaria fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a compact, spiny body that typically grows to a height of about 5 centimeters. The plant features white or pale green spines and produces yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
