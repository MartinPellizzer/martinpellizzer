Muricaria battandieri var. subintegrifolia

Muricaria battandieri var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the velvet leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and striking, fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically broad and have a smooth, waxy texture, while the inflorescence consists of clustered, showy blooms in shades of white and pink. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
