Muraltia zeyheri

Muraltia zeyheri, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, round, yellowish fruits that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Muraltia zeyheri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
