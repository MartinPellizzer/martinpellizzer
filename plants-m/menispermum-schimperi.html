Menispermum schimperi

Menispermum schimperi, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation. Its roots contain various alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Menispermum schimperi thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
