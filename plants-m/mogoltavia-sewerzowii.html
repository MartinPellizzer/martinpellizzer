Mogoltavia sewerzowii

Mogoltavia sewerzowii, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert environments and provides nectar for pollinators.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaping for its ornamental value.
