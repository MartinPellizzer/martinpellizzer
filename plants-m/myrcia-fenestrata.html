Myrcia fenestrata

Myrcia fenestrata, commonly known as the windowleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or yellow spots, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions.

Myrcia fenestrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
