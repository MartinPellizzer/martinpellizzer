Mormodes ephippilabia

Mormodes ephippilabia, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals that aid in pollination.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
