Melastomastrum theifolium var. theifolium

Melastomastrum theifolium var. theifolium, commonly known as the thief's leaf plant, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant's name is derived from the shape of its leaves, which resemble a thief's hand.
