Manulea aridicola

Manulea aridicola, commonly known as the desert manulea, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Aizoaceae, which includes many species adapted to dry environments. The plant has fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions. Its small, star-shaped flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage.

Manulea aridicola is well-suited for xeriscaping and requires minimal water and care once established.
