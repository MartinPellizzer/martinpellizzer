Myrcia rigida

Myrcia rigida, commonly known as the rough myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

Myrcia rigida is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical and subtropical forests.
