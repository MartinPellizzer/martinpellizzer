Malaxis monstruosa

Malaxis monstruosa, commonly known as the monstrous malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that bears numerous pale green to yellowish flowers with intricate, spurred petals. The plant typically grows from a rhizome and has a short, erect stem with broad, fleshy leaves. Its flowers are often pollinated by specific insects, and it thrives in high humidity and partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
