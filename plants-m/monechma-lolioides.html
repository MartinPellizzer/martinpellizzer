Monechma lolioides

Monechma lolioides, commonly known as silver vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils, often found in grasslands and along roadsides. The plant has slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Monechma lolioides is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
