Melaleuca asterocarpa

Melaleuca asterocarpa, commonly known as the star-fruited melaleuca, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly found in the coastal regions of New South Wales and Queensland. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
