Mentha sylvestris var. niliaca

Mentha sylvestris var. niliaca, commonly known as the wild mint of the Caucasus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils rich in menthol and other terpenes, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of natural flavoring.
