Miconia axillaris subsp. axillaris

Miconia axillaris subsp.

axillaris, commonly known as the Brazilian starapple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
