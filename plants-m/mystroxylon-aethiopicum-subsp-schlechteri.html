Mystroxylon aethiopicum subsp. schlechteri

Mystroxylon aethiopicum subsp.

schlechteri, commonly known as the Ethiopian shea tree, is a member of the Sapotaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa, particularly Ethiopia and Sudan.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that contains a hard-shelled seed, which is the source of shea butter.

The tree plays an important ecological and economic role in its native regions.
