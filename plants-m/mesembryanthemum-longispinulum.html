Mesembryanthemum longispinulum

Mesembryanthemum longispinulum, commonly known as the long-spined ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in arid environments.
