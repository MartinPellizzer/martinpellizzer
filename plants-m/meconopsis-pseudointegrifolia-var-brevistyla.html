Meconopsis pseudointegrifolia var. brevistyla

Meconopsis pseudointegrifolia var. brevistyla, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals that bloom in early summer, often in high-altitude meadows. The plant has a short, sturdy stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is a popular subject in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
