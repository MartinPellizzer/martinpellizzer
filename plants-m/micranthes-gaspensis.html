Micranthes gaspensis

Micranthes gaspensis, commonly known as the Gaspé micranthus, is a flowering plant native to the Gaspé Peninsula in eastern Canada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
