Mariscus concinnus

Mariscus concinnus, commonly known as the slender marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.

Mariscus concinnus is often found in association with other wetland flora, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
