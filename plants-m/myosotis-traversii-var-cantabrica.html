Myosotis traversii var. cantabrica

Myosotis traversii var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian forget-me-not, is a perennial herb native to the Cantabrian Mountains in northern Spain.

It features delicate blue to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous or coastal regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
