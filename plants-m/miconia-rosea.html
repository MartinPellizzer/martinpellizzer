Miconia rosea

Miconia rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Miconia rosea is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
