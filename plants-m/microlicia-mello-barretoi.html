Microlicia mello-barretoi

Microlicia mello-barretoi, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a compact, pseudobulbous structure and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration, typically featuring a white or pale pink spathe and a contrasting lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation efforts.
