Macaranga pachyphylla

Macaranga pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved macaranga, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a pale or reddish hue, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The species is known for its rapid growth rate and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
