Matisia bolivarii

Matisia bolivarii, commonly known as the Bolivian matisia, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and often fissured, providing a habitat for various epiphytic plants and insects.

Due to its ecological significance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
