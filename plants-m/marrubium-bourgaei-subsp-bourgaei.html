Marrubium bourgaei subsp. bourgaei

Marrubium bourgaei subsp.

bourgaei, commonly known as the Corsican horehound, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, aromatic leaves and small blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves contain compounds with medicinal properties, including mucilage and alkaloids.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.
