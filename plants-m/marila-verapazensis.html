Marila verapazensis

Marila verapazensis, commonly known as the Verapaz milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and feature five pink or purple petals, often with a prominent central corolla.

The plant produces follicles that contain seeds with tufts of white hair, aiding in wind dispersal.
