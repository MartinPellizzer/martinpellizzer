Microlicia balsamifera

Microlicia balsamifera, commonly known as the balsam tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark that exudes a balsamic scent when cut. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
