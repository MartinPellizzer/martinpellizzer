Mimosa lutea

Mimosa lutea, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom in the spring. Mimosa lutea is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
