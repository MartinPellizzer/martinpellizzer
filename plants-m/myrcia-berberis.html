Myrcia berberis

Myrcia berberis, commonly known as yellow myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. Myrcia berberis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its aromatic properties make it a valuable plant in traditional medicine and perfumery.
