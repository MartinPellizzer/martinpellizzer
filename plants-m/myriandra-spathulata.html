Myriandra spathulata

Myriandra spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved myriandra, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and woodland areas.

Myriandra spathulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in native habitats.
