Monochilon reptans

Monochilon reptans, commonly known as the creeping grass, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily and form dense mats.
