Monechma glaucifolium

Monechma glaucifolium, commonly known as the blueleaf monechma, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods or along stream banks. Its leaves are covered in a waxy coating that gives them a bluish tint, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.
