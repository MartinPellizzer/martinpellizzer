Melastoma albicans

Melastoma albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered melastoma, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
