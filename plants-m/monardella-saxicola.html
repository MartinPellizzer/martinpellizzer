Monardella saxicola

Monardella saxicola, commonly known as slender beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Monardella saxicola is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance to pollinators.
