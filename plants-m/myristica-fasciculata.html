Myristica fasciculata

Myristica fasciculata, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic bark and distinctive yellowish flowers. The plant produces a unique fruit that contains the valuable spice nutmeg inside a hard, woody aril. Myristica fasciculata is also known for its essential oils, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Its growth is typically slow, and it requires a humid, warm climate to thrive.
