Minuartia lineata subsp. litwinowii

Minuartia lineata subsp.

litwinowii, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a low-growing, perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically linear and arranged in a dense rosette, adapted to thrive in rocky or gravelly soils. This subspecies is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
