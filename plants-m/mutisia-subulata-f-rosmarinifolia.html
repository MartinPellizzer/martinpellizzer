Mutisia subulata f. rosmarinifolia

Mutisia subulata f. rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosmarinifolia form of the slender mutisia, is a variant of the native New Zealand plant Mutisia subulata.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered, often appearing in spring.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
