Megalotropis

Megalotropis, commonly known as the giant leek, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its large, fleshy, garlic-scented leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces umbels of white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Megalotropis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
