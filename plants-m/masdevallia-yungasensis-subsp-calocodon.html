Masdevallia yungasensis subsp. calocodon

Masdevallia yungasensis subsp.

calocodon, commonly known as the Yungas calocodon orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the cloud forests of Bolivia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often brightly colored flowers that bloom during the cooler months.

The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle, and thrives in high humidity and shaded conditions. Its leaves are usually short and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native environment.

The subspecies is notable for its unique floral structure, which includes a distinctive lip and spurred column, making it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
