Myrcia sphaerocarpa

Myrcia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are spherical, red berries that contain several seeds, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and aromatic properties.
