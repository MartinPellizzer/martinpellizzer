Martinia

Martinia, commonly known as the evergreen orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. They are characterized by their compact, evergreen growth habit and their showy, often fragrant flowers. Martinia species typically have strap-like leaves and produce clusters of small, brightly colored blooms.

They are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and are often cultivated as houseplants or in shaded garden settings.
