Melocactus florschuetzianus

Melocactus florschuetzianus, commonly known as the red-tipped melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps to reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its fruit is a small, round, yellowish structure that contains a single seed.

This cactus is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
