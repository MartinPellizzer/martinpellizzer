Masdevallia uncifera

Masdevallia uncifera, commonly known as the slender leaf masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in a spiral arrangement. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Masdevallia uncifera is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
