Microdon

Microdon, commonly known as the slender rush, is a member of the Juncaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. Microdon typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
