Mentha auneticensis

Mentha auneticensis, commonly known as the Aunetic mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a strong, minty scent when crushed.

Mentha auneticensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
