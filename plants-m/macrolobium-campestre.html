Macrolobium campestre

Macrolobium campestre, commonly known as the field bean or wild bean, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and pods that contain small, edible seeds. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
