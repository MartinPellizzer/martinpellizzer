Mentha aquatica var. hirsuta

Mentha aquatica var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy water mint, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, menthol-like fragrance. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often near streams and ponds. Its flowering spikes produce small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
