Mangifera laurina var. deropong

Mangifera laurina var. deropong, commonly known as the Java mango, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a variant of the Mangifera laurina species, which is closely related to the common mango. The plant is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible fruit. The fruit is typically smaller than the common mango and has a fibrous texture with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
