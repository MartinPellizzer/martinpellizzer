Micromeles epidendron

Micromeles epidendron, commonly known as the red-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves that contrast with its green stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
