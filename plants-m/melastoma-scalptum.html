Melastoma scalptum

Melastoma scalptum, commonly known as the scarlet trumpetwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. Its most distinctive feature is its vibrant red or orange flowers, which are tubular and attract pollinators like birds and insects.

Melastoma scalptum produces small, round berries that are edible and often consumed by various wildlife species.
