Mangifera equina

Mangifera equina, commonly known as the horse mango, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to round, orange-red fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental features and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
