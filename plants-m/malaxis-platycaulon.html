Malaxis platycaulon

Malaxis platycaulon, commonly known as the broad-stem orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and streamside areas. The plant features a short, thick rhizome with a rosette of fleshy, ovate leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in a raceme, often resembling a spike.

The species is characterized by its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
