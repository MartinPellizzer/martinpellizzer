Melampodium parvulum

Melampodium parvulum, commonly known as the smallflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Melampodium parvulum is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies.
