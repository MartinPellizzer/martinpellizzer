Microlicia hilariana

Microlicia hilariana, commonly known as the Hilarian fern, is a rare and delicate species belonging to the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnate fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Microlicia hilariana is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
