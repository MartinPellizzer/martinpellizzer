Marsilea fabri

Marsilea fabri, commonly known as the water clover, is a small aquatic fern belonging to the family Marsileaceae.

It is characterized by its four-leaf clover-like structure, with each leaflet being a small, rounded blade. The plant typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Marsilea fabri plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
