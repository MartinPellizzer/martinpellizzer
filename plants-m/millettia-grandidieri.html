Millettia grandidieri

Millettia grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar silk tree, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in dry forest and woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
