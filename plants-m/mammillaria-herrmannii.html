Mammillaria herrmannii

Mammillaria herrmannii, commonly known as the Herrmann's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
