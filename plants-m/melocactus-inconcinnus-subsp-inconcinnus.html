Melocactus inconcinnus subsp. inconcinnus

Melocactus inconcinnus subsp.

inconcinnus, commonly known as the red-tipped melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stem with prominent, rib-like structures and a distinctive red or pinkish coloration at the tips of its spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by orange to red fruits. It is well adapted to dry environments, with thick, waxy skin that helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
