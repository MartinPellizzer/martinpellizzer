Mammillaria voburnensis

Mammillaria voburnensis, commonly known as the Voburnensia hedgehog cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It features a globular stem covered in white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
