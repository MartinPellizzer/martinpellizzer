Megaclinium chevalieri

Megaclinium chevalieri, commonly known as the Corsican heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
