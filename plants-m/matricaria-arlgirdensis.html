Matricaria arlgirdensis

Matricaria arlgirdensis, commonly known as the Algrird chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow flowers that mature into white, fluffy spheres, typical of the genus Matricaria. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
