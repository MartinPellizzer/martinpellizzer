Malmea peruviana

Malmea peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian malmea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring and summer seasons. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
