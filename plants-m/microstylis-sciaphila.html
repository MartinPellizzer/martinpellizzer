Microstylis sciaphila

Microstylis sciaphila, commonly known as the shade-loving sedge, is a flowering plant native to shaded, moist environments in tropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown. Due to its unique habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
