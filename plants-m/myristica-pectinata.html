Myristica pectinata

Myristica pectinata, commonly known as the pectinata nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain the valuable spice known as nutmeg. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its seeds, enclosed in a hard, fibrous outer layer, are harvested for their aromatic properties.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
