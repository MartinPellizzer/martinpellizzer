Miconia secunsanguinea

Miconia secunsanguinea, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its competitive nature.
