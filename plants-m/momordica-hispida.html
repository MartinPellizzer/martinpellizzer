Momordica hispida

Momordica hispida, commonly known as the hairy bitter gourd, is a vine species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fruits that are typically elongated and covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms on the same plant.

The fruit is used in various cuisines and has traditional medicinal uses due to its bioactive compounds.
