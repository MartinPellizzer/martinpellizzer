Mentha × dalmatica subf. virgata

Mentha × dalmatica subf.

virgata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae). It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Mentha × dalmatica subf.

virgata is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
