Melaleuca ovatifolia

Melaleuca ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Melaleuca ovatifolia is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
