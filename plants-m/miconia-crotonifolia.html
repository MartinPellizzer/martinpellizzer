Miconia crotonifolia

Miconia crotonifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Miconia crotonifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and striking floral display.
