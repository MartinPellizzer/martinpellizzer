Mentha arvensis var. villosissima

Mentha arvensis var. villosissima, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and strong aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its menthol-like properties.
