Melhania perrieri var. typica

Melhania perrieri var. typica, commonly known as the Perrier's melhania, is a species of flowering plant in the family Melhaniaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a glossy surface.

Melhania perrieri var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
