Mucuna wangii

Mucuna wangii, commonly known as the golden crown bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Mucuna wangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
