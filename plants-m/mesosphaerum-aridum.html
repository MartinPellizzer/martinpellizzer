Mesosphaerum aridum

Mesosphaerum aridum, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with prominent, rounded petals, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and water-storing tissues.

Mesosphaerum aridum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its habitat.
