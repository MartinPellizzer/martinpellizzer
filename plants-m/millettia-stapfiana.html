Millettia stapfiana

Millettia stapfiana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, grayish-brown bark, and its fruits are legumes that split open to release seeds.

It is often found in open woodlands and savannas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
