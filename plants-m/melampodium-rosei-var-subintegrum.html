Melampodium rosei var. subintegrum

Melampodium rosei var. subintegrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
