Moricandia longirostris

Moricandia longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked moricandia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Moricandia longirostris is a perennial herb that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
