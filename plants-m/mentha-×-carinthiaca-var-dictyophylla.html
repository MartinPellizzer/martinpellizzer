Mentha × carinthiaca var. dictyophylla

Mentha × carinthiaca var. dictyophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its slender, dark green leaves and aromatic, menthol-rich foliage.

It is native to the Alpine regions of Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, contributing to its ornamental value. It is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and flavoring.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it a popular choice for garden borders and water features.
