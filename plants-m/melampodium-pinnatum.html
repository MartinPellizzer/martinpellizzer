Melampodium pinnatum

Melampodium pinnatum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and dark brown disc flowers. The plant has feathery, pinnately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in prairies and open woodlands.

Melampodium pinnatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
