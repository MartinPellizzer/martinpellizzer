Mentha × rhomboidifolia

Mentha × rhomboidifolia, commonly known as the square-stemmed hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, ribbed stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha × piperita and Mentha × spicata.

Mentha × rhomboidifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
