Mimosa gonoclada

Mimosa gonoclada, commonly known as the pink mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces fluffy, spherical seed pods that split open when mature, releasing seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mimosa gonoclada is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
