Maytenus procumbens

Maytenus procumbens, commonly known as the creeping mayten, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by small, fleshy berries that are often red or yellow when mature.

Maytenus procumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
