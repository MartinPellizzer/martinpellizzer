Mapania oblonga var. elliptica

Mapania oblonga var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic mapania, is a variant of the mapania plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring or early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
