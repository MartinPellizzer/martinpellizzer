Myrcia sticta

Myrcia sticta, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and attract various pollinators.

Myrcia sticta is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
