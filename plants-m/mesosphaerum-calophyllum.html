Mesosphaerum calophyllum

Mesosphaerum calophyllum, commonly known as the silky tallow tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Mesosphaerum calophyllum is typically found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
