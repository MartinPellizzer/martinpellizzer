Machaerium caicarense

Machaerium caicarense, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or caicare grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has slender, cylindrical stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves.

Its small, clustered flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, contributing to its ornamental value in certain landscapes.
