Mephitidia varians

Mephitidia varians, commonly known as the variable mephit, is a rare and elusive plant species found in specific tropical wetlands.

It is characterized by its unique, bioluminescent leaves that emit a faint blue glow at night, aiding in its pollination by nocturnal insects. The plant's flowers are small and clustered, with a complex structure that facilitates cross-pollination. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from waterlogged soil, making it highly dependent on stable wetland environments.

Due to habitat destruction, Mephitidia varians is classified as endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect this rare botanical species.
