Micropolypodium cookii

Micropolypodium cookii, commonly known as the Cook's micropolypodium, is a small epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often appear in dense clusters on tree trunks or branches. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture for optimal growth. Its rhizomes are short and help anchor it to its host, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of its fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
