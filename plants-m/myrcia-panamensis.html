Myrcia panamensis

Myrcia panamensis, commonly known as Panama myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly Panama, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a fragrant aroma. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are yellow when ripe.

Myrcia panamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
