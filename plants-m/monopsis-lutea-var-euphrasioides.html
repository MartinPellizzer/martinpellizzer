Monopsis lutea var. euphrasioides

Monopsis lutea var. euphrasioides, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
