Mikania ericoides

Mikania ericoides, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Mikania ericoides is often found in moist, shaded areas and can be invasive in certain regions.
