Melargyra media

Melargyra media, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.

Melargyra media is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
