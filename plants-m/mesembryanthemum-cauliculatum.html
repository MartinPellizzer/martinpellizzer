Mesembryanthemum cauliculatum

Mesembryanthemum cauliculatum, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and coastal environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
