Malaxis tequilensis

Malaxis tequilensis, commonly known as the Tequila orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Malaxis tequilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
