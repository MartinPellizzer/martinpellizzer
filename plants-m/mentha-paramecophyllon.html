Mentha paramecophyllon

Mentha paramecophyllon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a strong, menthol-like fragrance.

Mentha paramecophyllon is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
