Muhlenbergia majalcensis

Muhlenbergia majalcensis, commonly known as the tall muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.

Muhlenbergia majalcensis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
