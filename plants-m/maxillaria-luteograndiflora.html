Maxillaria luteograndiflora

Maxillaria luteograndiflora, commonly known as the golden grandiflora orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a striking orange lip, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces fragrant flowers during the summer months, and its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients.

Maxillaria luteograndiflora is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
