Medicago × subfalcata

Medicago × subfalcata, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial legume characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

It is a natural hybrid between Medicago sativa and Medicago falcata, exhibiting traits from both parent species. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in forage agriculture due to its high nutritional value and drought tolerance.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural settings.
