Melaleuca viminea subsp. viminea

Melaleuca viminea subsp.

viminea, commonly known as the slender honeymyrtle, is a shrub native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland heathlands.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden planting in suitable climates.
