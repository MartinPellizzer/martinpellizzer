Morina persica

Morina persica, commonly known as Persian moringa, is a shrub native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and agricultural studies.
