Miconia fuertesii

It is native to the Caribbean, specifically found in the islands of Cuba and Hispaniola. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
