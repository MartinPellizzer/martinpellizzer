Malva fruticans

Malva fruticans, commonly known as the African mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

Malva fruticans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
