Myrcia velloziana

Myrcia velloziana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
