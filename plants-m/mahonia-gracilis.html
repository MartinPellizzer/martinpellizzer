Mahonia gracilis

Mahonia gracilis, commonly known as the slender Mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically gray-green and have a distinctive, prickly texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen foliage and seasonal blooms.
