Marshallia graminifolia var. lacinarioides

Marshallia graminifolia var. lacinarioides, commonly known as the laceleaf marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, often in marshy or swampy areas. This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
