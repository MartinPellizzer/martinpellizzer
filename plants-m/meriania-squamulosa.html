Meriania squamulosa

Meriania squamulosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Meriania squamulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
