Mimosa adenocarpa var. multiglandulosa

Mimosa adenocarpa var. multiglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular mimosa, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, delicate leaflets. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The glandular structures on its stems and leaves are a distinguishing feature of this variant.
