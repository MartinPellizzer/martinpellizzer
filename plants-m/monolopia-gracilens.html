Monolopia gracilens

Monolopia gracilens, commonly known as the slender spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for wetland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
