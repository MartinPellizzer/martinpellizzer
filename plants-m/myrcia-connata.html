Myrcia connata

Myrcia connata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, small white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. Myrcia connata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
