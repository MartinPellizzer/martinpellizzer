Marsdenia straminea

Marsdenia straminea, commonly known as the yellow star vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which bloom during the summer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Marsdenia straminea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
