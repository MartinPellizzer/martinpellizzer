Mentha sylvestris subsp. schimperi

Mentha sylvestris subsp.

schimperi, commonly known as Ethiopian mint, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

The subspecies is closely related to other Mentha species but is distinguished by its unique floral structure and chemical composition.
