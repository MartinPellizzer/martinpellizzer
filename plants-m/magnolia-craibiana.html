Magnolia craibiana

Magnolia craibiana, commonly known as the Sierra Madre magnolia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in forested areas with high humidity. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
