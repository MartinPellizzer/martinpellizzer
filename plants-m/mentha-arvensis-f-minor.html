Mentha arvensis f. minor

Mentha arvensis f. minor, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in many regions around the world.

Mentha arvensis f. minor is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine and as a stimulant for cats.
