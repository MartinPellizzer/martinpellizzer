Mariscus cylindricus

Mariscus cylindricus, commonly known as the cylindrical spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branched stems that are often covered in a sticky, milky sap. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a waxy texture with a slightly serrated edge.

Mariscus cylindricus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
