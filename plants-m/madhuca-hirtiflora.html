Madhuca hirtiflora

Madhuca hirtiflora, commonly known as the hairy tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
