Mentha × allophylla

Mentha × allophylla, commonly known as marsh mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between water mint (Mentha aquatica) and field mint (Mentha spicata).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wet environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers appear in whorled clusters at the leaf axils.

Mentha × allophylla is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
