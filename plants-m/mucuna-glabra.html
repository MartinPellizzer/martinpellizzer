Mucuna glabra

Mucuna glabra, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, dark green foliage and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, dark seeds that are rich in protein and used in various traditional applications. Mucuna glabra is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches soil fertility.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful agricultural plant and a potential weed in cultivated areas.
