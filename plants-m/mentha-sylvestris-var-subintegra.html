Mentha sylvestris var. subintegra

Mentha sylvestris var. subintegra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, tubular, and often purple or pink, appearing in clusters.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
