Maytenus lucida

Maytenus lucida, commonly known as the shining mayten, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are often lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round, fleshy berries.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
