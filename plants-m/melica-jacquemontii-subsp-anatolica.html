Melica jacquemontii subsp. anatolica

Melica jacquemontii subsp.

anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems in its native range.
