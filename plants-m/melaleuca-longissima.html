Melaleuca longissima

Melaleuca longissima, commonly known as the long-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and distinctive bottlebrush-like flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and sandy soils.

Melaleuca longissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
