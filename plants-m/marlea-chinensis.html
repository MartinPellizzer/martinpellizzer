Marlea chinensis

Marlea chinensis, commonly known as Chinese marigold, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Marlea chinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
