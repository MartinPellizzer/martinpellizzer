Melocactus jansenianus

Melocactus jansenianus, commonly known as the Jansen's melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a distinctive, rounded crown that often develops a thick, fleshy base. The plant produces a single, large, spiny fruit that resembles a melon, hence its common name. Its spines are typically yellow or reddish, and the plant is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions.

Melocactus jansenianus is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
