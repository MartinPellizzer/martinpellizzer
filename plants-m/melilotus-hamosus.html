Melilotus hamosus

Melilotus hamosus, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Sweet clover is often used in agricultural settings for soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
