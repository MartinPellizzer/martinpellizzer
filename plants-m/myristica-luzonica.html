Myristica luzonica

Myristica luzonica, commonly known as the Luzon nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain valuable seeds. The plant produces a unique, aromatic resin known as "luzonicin," which is used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its leaves are large, leathery, and emit a strong scent when crushed.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.
