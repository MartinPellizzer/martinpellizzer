Myrtekmania podocarpoides

Myrtekmania podocarpoides, commonly known as the New Zealand kōwhai, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family and is native to New Zealand.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves and distinctive cones. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and historical significance in Māori culture.
