Myginda integrifolia

Myginda integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed myginda, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically smooth and entire along the margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Myginda integrifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
