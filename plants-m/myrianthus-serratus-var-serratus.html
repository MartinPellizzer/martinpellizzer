Myrianthus serratus var. serratus

Myrianthus serratus var. serratus, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are typically serrated along the edges. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun.
