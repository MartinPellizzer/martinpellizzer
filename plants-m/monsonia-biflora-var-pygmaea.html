Monsonia biflora var. pygmaea

Monsonia biflora var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf bluebell, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it a striking addition to rock gardens or containers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in Mediterranean climates.
