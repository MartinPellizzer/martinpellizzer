Maxillaria variabilis

Maxillaria variabilis, commonly known as the variable maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features dark green, strap-like leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange with intricate patterns, and they bloom during the dry season.

Maxillaria variabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
