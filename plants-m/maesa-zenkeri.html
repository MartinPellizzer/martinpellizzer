Maesa zenkeri

Maesa zenkeri, commonly known as the Zanzibar apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically orange-red in color and have a tart, acidic flavor. Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
