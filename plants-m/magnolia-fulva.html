Magnolia fulva

Magnolia fulva, commonly known as the yellow magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, gray bark and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are broad, elliptical, and turn yellow in the fall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
