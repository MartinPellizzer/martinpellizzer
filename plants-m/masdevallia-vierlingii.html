Masdevallia vierlingii

Masdevallia vierlingii, commonly known as the Four-Flowered Masdevallia, is a striking orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, intricate flowers that typically display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant features long, slender petals and a distinctive, spurred lip that enhances its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high light intensity, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and seasonal blooming pattern.
