Melastoma argenteum

Melastoma argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in a silvery-white pubescence, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and usually have five petals.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.
