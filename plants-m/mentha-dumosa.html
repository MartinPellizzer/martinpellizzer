Mentha dumosa

Mentha dumosa, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and square stems, typical of the mint family. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Mentha dumosa thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

It is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
