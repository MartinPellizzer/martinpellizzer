Monochaetum bonplandii var. expansum

Monochaetum bonplandii var. expansum, commonly known as the broad-leaved flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are often broad at the base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
