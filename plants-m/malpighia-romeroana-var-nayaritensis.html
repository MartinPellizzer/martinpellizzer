Malpighia romeroana var. nayaritensis

Malpighia romeroana var. nayaritensis, commonly known as the Nayarit firebird, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that resemble birds in flight. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
