Masdevallia trochilus

Masdevallia trochilus, commonly known as the hummingbird orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that resemble the beaks of hummingbirds, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by hummingbirds.

Masdevallia trochilus is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
