Moquilea brittoniana

Moquilea brittoniana, commonly known as the Britton's moquilea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spineless, cylindrical stems and showy, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Moquilea brittoniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
