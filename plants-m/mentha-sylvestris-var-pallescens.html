Mentha sylvestris var. pallescens

Mentha sylvestris var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been historically used in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
