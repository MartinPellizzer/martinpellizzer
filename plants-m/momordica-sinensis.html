Momordica sinensis

Momordica sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bitter melon, is a vigorous trailing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. The fruit is typically elongated, yellow or orange, and covered in white warts. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are either male or female.

It is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and has a distinctive bitter taste.
