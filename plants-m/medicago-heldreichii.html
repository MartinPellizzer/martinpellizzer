Medicago heldreichii

Medicago heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan clover, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Medicago heldreichii is of interest in ecological studies and has potential uses in restoration ecology.
