Martynia annua

Martynia annua, commonly known as the common redweed, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Martynia annua is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
