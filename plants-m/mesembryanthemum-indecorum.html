Mesembryanthemum indecorum

Mesembryanthemum indecorum, commonly known as the "Sculptured Flower" or "Cape Bells," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental flowering plants. The plant features fleshy, ribbed leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a fascinating addition to botanical collections.
