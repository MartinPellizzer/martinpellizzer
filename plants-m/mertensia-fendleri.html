Mertensia fendleri

Mertensia fendleri, commonly known as desert bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are adapted to the arid environments it inhabits. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Mertensia fendleri is a member of the nightshade family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance to local pollinators.
