Myriophyllum tetrandrum

Myriophyllum tetrandrum, commonly known as water milfoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often submerged in water, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes, and it is often found in shallow lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. Myriophyllum tetrandrum is an aquatic plant that plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

It is also used in ornamental gardening and has been introduced to various parts of the world.
