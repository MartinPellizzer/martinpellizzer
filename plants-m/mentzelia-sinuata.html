Mentzelia sinuata

Mentzelia sinuata, commonly known as the desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Mentzelia sinuata is characterized by its slender stem and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
