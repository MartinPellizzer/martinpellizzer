Mesoreanthus vimineus

Mesoreanthus vimineus, commonly known as the climbing bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Mesoreanthus vimineus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
