Mammillaria pyrrhochracantha

Mammillaria pyrrhochracantha, commonly known as the red-spined mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to pale blue tubercles. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in arid gardens.
