Muhlenbergia sylvatica f. attenuata

Muhlenbergia sylvatica f. attenuata, commonly known as the slender wood oat, is a flowering grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, delicate panicles of flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
