Mephitidia purpurea

Mephitidia purpurea, commonly known as the purple mephit, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a slender, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters and are pollinated primarily by bees and butterflies.

Mephitidia purpurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and fragrance.
