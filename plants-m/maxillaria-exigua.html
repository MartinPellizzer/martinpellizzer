Maxillaria exigua

Maxillaria exigua, commonly known as the miniature orchid or the small maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a compact, epiphytic plant with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant's flowers are typically clustered in racemes and feature a distinctive, spurred lip. Maxillaria exigua thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media for optimal growth.

It is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
