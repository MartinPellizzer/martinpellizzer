Marrubium purpureum

Marrubium purpureum, commonly known as purple hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

The purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
