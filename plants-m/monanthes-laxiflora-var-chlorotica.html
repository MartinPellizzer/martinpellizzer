Monanthes laxiflora var. chlorotica

Monanthes laxiflora var. chlorotica, commonly known as the chlorotic monanthes, is a rare variant of the monanthes plant characterized by its distinctive chlorotic leaf coloration.

It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with lance-shaped leaves that are often lighter in color due to reduced chlorophyll content. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique pigmentation and potential genetic significance.
