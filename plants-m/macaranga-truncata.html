Macaranga truncata

Macaranga truncata, commonly known as the round-leaf makara, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually round or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia. Its latex is milky and can be used in traditional medicine.

Macaranga truncata is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types.
