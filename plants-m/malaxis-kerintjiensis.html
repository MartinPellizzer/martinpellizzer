Malaxis kerintjiensis

Malaxis kerintjiensis, commonly known as the Kerintji orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is noted for its small, clustered inflorescences and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
