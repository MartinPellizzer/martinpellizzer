Mentha ambigua

Mentha ambigua, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and fragrant, often aromatic, leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks, marshes, and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and usually pale purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Mentha ambigua is closely related to other members of the mint family, such as peppermint and spearmint.
