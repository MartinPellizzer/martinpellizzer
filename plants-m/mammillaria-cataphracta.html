Mammillaria cataphracta

Mammillaria cataphracta, commonly known as the Mexican dwarf cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5-10 centimeters and has a compact, rounded shape. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
