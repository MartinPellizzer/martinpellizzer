Momisia membranacea

Momisia membranacea, commonly known as the membranous momisia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
