Margaritaria alternifolia

Margaritaria alternifolia, commonly known as the beadleaf or silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of small, bead-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain smooth, black seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
