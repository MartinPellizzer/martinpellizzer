Malaxis ruizii

Malaxis ruizii, commonly known as the Ruiz's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Malaxis ruizii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
