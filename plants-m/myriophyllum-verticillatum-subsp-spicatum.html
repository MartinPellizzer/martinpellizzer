Myriophyllum verticillatum subsp. spicatum

Myriophyllum verticillatum subsp.

spicatum, commonly known as watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America. It has whorled leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Its rapid growth can lead to dense vegetation, impacting water quality and aquatic habitats.
