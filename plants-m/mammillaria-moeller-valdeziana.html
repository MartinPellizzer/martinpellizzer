Mammillaria moeller-valdeziana

Mammillaria moeller-valdeziana, commonly known as the Moeller's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it an ideal choice for desert gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
