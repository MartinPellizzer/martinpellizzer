Mimulus luteus var. variegatus

Mimulus luteus var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated yellow monkeyflower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with intricate, spurred petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, often displaying variegation in green and white. This variant is a hybrid of Mimulus luteus and Mimulus ringens, showcasing genetic diversity in its foliage and floral patterns.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and woodland edges.
