Maxillaria punctata

Maxillaria punctata, commonly known as the spotted maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. The plant typically blooms in the summer, with flowers that range in color from white to pink and often have a yellow or orange lip. Maxillaria punctata thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining potting media.

It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
