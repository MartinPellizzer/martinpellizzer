Musa basjoo

Musa basjoo, commonly known as the Japanese banana, is a species of flowering plant in the Musaceae family.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to three meters in length. The plant produces clusters of white or yellow flowers followed by edible, banana-like fruits. It is a hardy plant that can tolerate cold temperatures, making it suitable for temperate climates.

The botanical features of Musa basjoo make it both an ornamental and edible plant, valued for its aesthetic and nutritional qualities.
