Matricaria chamomilla f. courrantiana

Matricaria chamomilla f. courrantiana, commonly known as the courrantian chamomile, is a botanical variant of the common chamomile.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its daisy-like flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing white petals with a yellow center.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
