Mammillaria matudae var. serpentiformis

Mammillaria matudae var. serpentiformis, commonly known as the serpent-shaped mammillaria, is a small cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
