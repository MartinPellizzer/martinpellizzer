Mappia cambodiana

Mappia cambodiana, commonly known as the Cambodian maple, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
