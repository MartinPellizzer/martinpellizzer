Muscari pauperulum

Muscari pauperulum, commonly known as the poor man's grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a central bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Muscari pauperulum is often used in rock gardens and borders due to its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
