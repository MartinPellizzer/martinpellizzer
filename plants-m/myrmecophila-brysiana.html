Myrmecophila brysiana

Myrmecophila brysiana, commonly known as the Brazilian velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. Its bark and leaves have been used in folk medicine for their purported health benefits.

Myrmecophila brysiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
