Mentha sachalinensis f. arguta

Mentha sachalinensis f. arguta, commonly known as the Siberian mint or narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively.

Mentha sachalinensis f. arguta is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
