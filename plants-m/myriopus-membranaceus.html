Myriopus membranaceus

Myriopus membranaceus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed smartweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along watercourses. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a membranous texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Myriopus membranaceus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small fauna.
