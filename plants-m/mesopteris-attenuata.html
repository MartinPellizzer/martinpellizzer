Mesopteris attenuata

Mesopteris attenuata, commonly known as the slender cliff fern, is a unique fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often resemble those of true ferns but are actually part of a different group, the water ferns. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on rocky or cliff faces. Its rhizomes are creeping and help anchor it to vertical surfaces.

Mesopteris attenuata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitats for small organisms in its native ecosystems.
