Mentha mollissima f. speciosa

Mentha mollissima f. speciosa, commonly known as the showy mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and distinctive floral structure.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
