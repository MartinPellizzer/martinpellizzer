Mentha × kerneri f. alluvialis

Mentha × kerneri f. alluvialis, commonly known as the alluvial mint, is a hybrid plant within the Mentha genus.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and creeping growth habit, often found in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks. This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in wet environments, making it a common sight in riparian zones. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
