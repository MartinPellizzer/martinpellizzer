Maxillaria cacaoensis

Maxillaria cacaoensis, commonly known as the Cacao orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Maxillaria cacaoensis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
