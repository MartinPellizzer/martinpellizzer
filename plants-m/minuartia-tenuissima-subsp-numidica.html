Minuartia tenuissima subsp. numidica

Minuartia tenuissima subsp.

numidica, commonly known as the Numidian rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its small, pale green to blue-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
