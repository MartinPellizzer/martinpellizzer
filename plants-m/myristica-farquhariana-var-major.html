Myristica farquhariana var. major

Myristica farquhariana var. major, commonly known as the greater myristica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruit that contains both nutmeg and mace. The plant produces large, compound leaves and has a distinctive, fragrant bark. Its fruit develops in a woody capsule, which splits open to release the aromatic seeds.

This species is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
