Mentha concolor

Mentha concolor, commonly known as the purple mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a purple underside. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Mentha concolor is often found in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
