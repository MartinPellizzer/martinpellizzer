Monotoca linifolia

Monotoca linifolia, commonly known as the silkyleaf daphne, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and finely textured, silvery-green leaves. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its growth habit is compact, with multiple stems forming a dense, bushy appearance.

Monotoca linifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
