Magnolia pedrazae

Magnolia pedrazae, commonly known as the Pedraza magnolia, is a rare and unique species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a fragrant scent when crushed.

Magnolia pedrazae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and rarity.
