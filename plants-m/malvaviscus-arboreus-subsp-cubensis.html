Malvaviscus arboreus subsp. cubensis

Malvaviscus arboreus subsp.

cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban mallow, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants.

The plant is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a yellow or orange center. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
