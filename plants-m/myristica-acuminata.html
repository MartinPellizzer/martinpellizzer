Myristica acuminata

Myristica acuminata, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant, woody bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries containing the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, which includes essential oils and compounds like myristicin and elemicin.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
