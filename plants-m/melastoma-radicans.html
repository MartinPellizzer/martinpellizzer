Melastoma radicans

Melastoma radicans, commonly known as the spreading melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth surface with a glossy appearance.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common ornamental and ecological plant in tropical regions.
