Melocactus schlumbergerianus

Melocactus schlumbergerianus, commonly known as the Schlumbergera truncata, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, fleshy stems that resemble leaves, which are typically green with reddish or purplish tones. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and flowering habit, it is a popular ornamental plant.
