Melicocca javanica

Melicocca javanica, commonly known as the Java melicoccus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a thick, aromatic pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
