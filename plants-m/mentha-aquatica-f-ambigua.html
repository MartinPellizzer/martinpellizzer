Mentha aquatica f. ambigua

Mentha aquatica f. ambigua, commonly known as the water mint, is a hybrid form of the aquatic mint plant.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant features slender, creeping stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple and appear in clusters.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.
