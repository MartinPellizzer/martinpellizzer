Meniscium maxonianum

Meniscium maxonianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meniscium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, upright stems. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Meniscium maxonianum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
