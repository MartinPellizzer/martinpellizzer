Mimosa hypoglauca var. syncollandra

Mimosa hypoglauca var. syncollandra, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in globular clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and adaptive features make it both ecologically and aesthetically valuable.
