Mentha aquatica f. silesiaca

Mentha aquatica f. silesiaca, commonly known as the Silesian water mint, is a subspecies of the water mint plant native to moist habitats in Europe.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and aromatic, slightly serrated foliage. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp environments.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
