Memecylon coeruleoviolaceum

Memecylon coeruleoviolaceum, commonly known as the blue-violet memecylon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish or purplish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
