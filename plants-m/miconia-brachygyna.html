Miconia brachygyna

Miconia brachygyna, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with small, white or pale pink flowers that attract various pollinators. Miconia brachygyna is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.

Its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds dispersed by wind.
