Myosotis venticola

Myosotis venticola, commonly known as the windflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are small, usually less than 1 cm in diameter, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Myosotis venticola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
