Myrcia lamprosericea

Myrcia lamprosericea, commonly known as the shining red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Myrcia lamprosericea is often found in humid, shaded environments such as tropical forests.
