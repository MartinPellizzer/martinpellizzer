Mentha × kerneri var. borzaeana

Mentha × kerneri var. borzaeana, commonly known as the Borzaeana mint, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported cooling and digestive properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic qualities in horticulture.
