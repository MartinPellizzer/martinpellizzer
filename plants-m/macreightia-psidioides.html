Macreightia psidioides

Macreightia psidioides, commonly known as the red-fruit hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Macreightia psidioides thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
