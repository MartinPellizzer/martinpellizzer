Malus ioensis var. spinosa

Malus ioensis var. spinosa, commonly known as the spiny crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive thorn-like buds and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped with a rough texture and turn yellow in the fall. The fruit is a small, red or yellow apple-like berry that ripens in late summer.

This plant is often used in native landscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
