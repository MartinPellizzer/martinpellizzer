Mentha × odora

Mentha × odora, commonly known as spearmint, is a hybrid perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, square stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Spearmint is native to Europe and parts of Asia but is now cultivated worldwide for its aromatic oils.

Its essential oils, rich in menthol and other compounds, are widely used in flavoring, medicine, and aromatherapy.
