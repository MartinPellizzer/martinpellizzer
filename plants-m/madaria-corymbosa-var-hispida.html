Madaria corymbosa var. hispida

Madaria corymbosa var. hispida, commonly known as the roughleaf madaria, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy or rough-textured leaves, which give it its varietal name "hispida.". The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
