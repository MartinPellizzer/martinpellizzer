Myrcia fasciata

Myrcia fasciata, commonly known as the spotted myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or pale green markings. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia fasciata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
