Myrcia coriacea

Myrcia coriacea, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often have a spicy or peppery taste. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is known for its aromatic properties.

Myrcia coriacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
