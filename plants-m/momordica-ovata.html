Momordica ovata

Momordica ovata, commonly known as the eggplant gourd, is a vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, ovate, waxy leaves and yellow flowers that develop into elongated, green fruits. The fruit turns orange when mature and has a mildly bitter taste due to the presence of cucurbitacins. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is cultivated for its edible fruits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
