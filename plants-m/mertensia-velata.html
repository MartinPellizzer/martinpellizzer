Mertensia velata

Mertensia velata, commonly known as the velvetleaf bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Mertensia velata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
