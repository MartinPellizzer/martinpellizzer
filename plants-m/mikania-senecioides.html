Mikania senecioides

Mikania senecioides, commonly known as mile-a-minute weed, is a fast-growing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is known for its rapid spread, often forming dense mats that can smother other vegetation. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in many regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and challenging to control.
