Mentha austriaca var. pulchella

Mentha austriaca var. pulchella, commonly known as the Austrian mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a strong mint scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine and culinary applications.
