Mimosa oligosperma

Mimosa oligosperma, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a low-growing shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by clusters of oval, woody pods. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Mimosa oligosperma is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
