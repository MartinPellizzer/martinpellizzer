Myrcia petenensis

Myrcia petenensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

Myrcia petenensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
