Mertensia fusiformis

Mertensia fusiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

Mertensia fusiformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
