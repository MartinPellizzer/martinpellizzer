Mimosa guirocobensis

Mimosa guirocobensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Mimosa guirocobensis is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
