Mastixia arborea subsp. meziana

Mastixia arborea subsp.

meziana, commonly known as the Mezian mastix tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests.

The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural resins.
