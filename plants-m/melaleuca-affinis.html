Melaleuca affinis

Melaleuca affinis, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Paperbark, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in winter, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
