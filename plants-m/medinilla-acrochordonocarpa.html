Medinilla acrochordonocarpa

Medinilla acrochordonocarpa, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

It is a flowering plant native to the Philippines, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, orange-red flowers. The plant produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that are edible and have a sweet, citrusy flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a unique and attractive addition to tropical gardens.
