Melianthus pectinatus subsp. pectinatus

Melianthus pectinatus subsp.

pectinatus, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the warmer months.

The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
