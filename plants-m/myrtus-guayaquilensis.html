Myrtus guayaquilensis

Myrtus guayaquilensis, commonly known as the Guayaquil myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, small white flowers and leathery, dark green leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal forests and mangrove ecosystems.

Myrtus guayaquilensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
