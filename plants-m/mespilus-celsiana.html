Mespilus celsiana

Mespilus celsiana, commonly known as the European medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that ripen in autumn, turning from green to a deep red-brown color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

The European medlar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
