Mesembryanthemum cruciatum

Mesembryanthemum cruciatum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its common name comes from the appearance of its flowers, which resemble ice crystals.

Mesembryanthemum cruciatum is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
