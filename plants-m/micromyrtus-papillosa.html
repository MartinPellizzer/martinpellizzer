Micromyrtus papillosa

Micromyrtus papillosa, commonly known as the silky myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, round, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or coastal environments.

Micromyrtus papillosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to tolerate salt spray and wind.
