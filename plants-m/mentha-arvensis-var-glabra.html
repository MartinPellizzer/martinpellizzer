Mentha arvensis var. glabra

Mentha arvensis var. glabra, commonly known as horse mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Horse mint is known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
