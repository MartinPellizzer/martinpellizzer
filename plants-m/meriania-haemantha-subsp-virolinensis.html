Meriania haemantha subsp. virolinensis

Meriania haemantha subsp.

virolinensis, commonly known as the Red-flowered Meriania, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which are typically borne in clusters and attract various pollinators.

The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
