Mammillaria spinosissima var. isabellina

Mammillaria spinosissima var. isabellina, commonly known as the Isabellina mammillaria, is a compact cactus species native to Mexico.

It features a globular to cylindrical stem with prominent, spiny tubercles that are typically pale yellow to white in color. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.

The varietal name 'isabellina' refers to its pale, almost ivory-colored appearance.
