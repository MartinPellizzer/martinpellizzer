Muscari pseudopallens

Muscari pseudopallens, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.

Muscari pseudopallens is also valued for its ability to naturalize and form dense stands over time.
