Macropiper melanostachyum

Macropiper melanostachyum, commonly known as the black-stemmed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruits are elongated berries that turn from green to black as they mature.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
