Melampodium lanceolatum

Melampodium lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf cupplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
