Melica minuta var. densissima

Melica minuta var. densissima, commonly known as dense hairgrass, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, wiry blades that are often covered in short, stiff hairs. This variety typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
