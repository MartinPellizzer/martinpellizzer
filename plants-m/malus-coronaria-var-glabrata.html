Malus coronaria var. glabrata

Malus coronaria var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth apple or glabrous crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous bark and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically red or yellow in color, though they are not commonly cultivated for fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The species is known for its adaptability and resistance to many common pests and diseases.
