Milium kochii

Milium kochii, commonly known as the Koch's millet, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its small, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a basal rosette.

Milium kochii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
