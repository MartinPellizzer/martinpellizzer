Miconia aphanantha

Miconia aphanantha, commonly known as the ghost miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that are typically borne on short peduncles. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Miconia aphanantha is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its unique foliage.
