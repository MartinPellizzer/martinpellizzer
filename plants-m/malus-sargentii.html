Malus sargentii

Malus sargentii, commonly known as Sargent's apple tree, is a deciduous species native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in spring and vibrant red berries in autumn. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, contributing to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
