Microcharis disjuncta var. disjuncta

Microcharis disjuncta var. disjuncta, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The flowers typically bloom in the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
