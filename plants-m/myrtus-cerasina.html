Myrtus cerasina

Myrtus cerasina, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Myrtus cerasina thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value and aromatic properties make it a popular choice for gardens and traditional herbal remedies.
