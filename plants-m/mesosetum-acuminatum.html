Mesosetum acuminatum

Mesosetum acuminatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and compact growth habit, making it a common component of native grasslands. The plant typically grows in full sun and is adapted to well-drained soils, often found in prairie and woodland edges. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that release lightweight seeds for wind dispersal.

Mesosetum acuminatum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
