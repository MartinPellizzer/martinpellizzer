Mesosetum longifolium

Mesosetum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaf muhly, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicles, giving it a delicate, airy appearance.

Mesosetum longifolium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
