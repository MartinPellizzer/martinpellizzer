Mariscus ehrenbergianus

Mariscus ehrenbergianus, commonly known as the Ehrenberg's mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate periods of drought.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of desert flora and ecological resilience.
