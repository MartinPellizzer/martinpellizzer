Melaleuca thysanota

Melaleuca thysanota, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, flaky bark that peels away in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, which appear in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom.

Melaleuca thysanota is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
