Menispermum verrucosum

Menispermum verrucosum, commonly known as the rough moonseed, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, round, orange-red berries. Its roots contain alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The species is often found in moist, shaded woodlands, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
