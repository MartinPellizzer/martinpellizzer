Malveopsis ribifolia

Malveopsis ribifolia, commonly known as the redcurrant mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a prominent central stamen. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
