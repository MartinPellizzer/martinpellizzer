Miconia trinitatis

Miconia trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidad miconia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-pink bracts. The plant produces small white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
