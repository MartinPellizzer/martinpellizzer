Melica cretica subsp. laxiflora

Melica cretica subsp.

laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower crested grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to blueish flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose, open cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its fine texture and drought tolerance.
