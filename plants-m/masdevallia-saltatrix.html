Masdevallia saltatrix

Masdevallia saltatrix, commonly known as the flying masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pendant-like inflorescence and striking, often iridescent flowers that resemble butterflies in flight. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in high humidity and cool temperatures. Its leaves are usually narrow and dark green, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
