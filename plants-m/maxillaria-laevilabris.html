Maxillaria laevilabris

Maxillaria laevilabris, commonly known as the slender maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant features strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and its pseudobulbs are slender and elongated. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Maxillaria laevilabris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
