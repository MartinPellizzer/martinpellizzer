Monachosorum × flagellare

Monachosorum × flagellare, commonly known as the hybrid lily, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Monachosorum and Flagellare species.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated petals that resemble a flagellum, giving it its scientific name. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowers are usually a vibrant mix of white and pink, with a subtle fragrance.

The hybrid is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
