Melica anceps

Melica anceps, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, marshes, and riverbanks. The plant has slender, tufted stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Melica anceps is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
