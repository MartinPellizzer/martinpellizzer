Malaxis bancanoides

Malaxis bancanoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed Malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
