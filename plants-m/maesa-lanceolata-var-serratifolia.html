Maesa lanceolata var. serratifolia

Maesa lanceolata var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated maesa, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
