Mentha arvensis var. gentilis

Mentha arvensis var. gentilis, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its essential oils, rich in menthol and other compounds, are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
