Monotes discolor var. lanatus

Monotes discolor var. lanatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly monotes, is a variant of the monotes species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a pale underside, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The botanical interest of this variant lies in its unique morphology and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
