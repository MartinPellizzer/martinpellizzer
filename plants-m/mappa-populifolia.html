Mappa populifolia

Mappa populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mappa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically broad and lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by round, fleshy berries. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth.
