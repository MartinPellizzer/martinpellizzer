Myrianthus preussii subsp. seretii

Myrianthus preussii subsp.

seretii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces legume pods containing several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks in tropical and subtropical regions.
