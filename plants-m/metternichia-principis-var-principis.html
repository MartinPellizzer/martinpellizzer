Metternichia principis var. principis

Metternichia principis var. principis, commonly known as the royal lily, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver markings.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
