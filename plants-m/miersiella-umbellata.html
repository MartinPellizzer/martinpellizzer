Miersiella umbellata

Miersiella umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella liverwort, is a small, mat-forming liverwort that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, leafy thallus with a distinctive umbrella-like arrangement of leaves. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead having structures called gametophytes. It reproduces through spores released from specialized structures called sporangia.

Miersiella umbellata is often found on rocks or soil in temperate regions, contributing to the biodiversity of moist habitats.
