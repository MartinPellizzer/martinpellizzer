Myrcia squamata

Myrcia squamata, commonly known as the silver myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible, fleshy berries.

Myrcia squamata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
