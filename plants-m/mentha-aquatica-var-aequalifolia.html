Mentha aquatica var. aequalifolia

Mentha aquatica var. aequalifolia, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herb native to wetlands and stream banks in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, aromatic leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a strong minty fragrance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem, often in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for digestive and respiratory ailments.
