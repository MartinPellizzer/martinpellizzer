Meriolix serrulata var. drummondii

Meriolix serrulata var. drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. It is often found in open habitats such as sandy plains and rocky outcrops.

The species is notable for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
