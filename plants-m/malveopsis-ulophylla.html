Malveopsis ulophylla

Malveopsis ulophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallow, is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive central column.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
