Morinda triphylla

Morinda triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf morinda, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has simple, opposite or whorled leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and often have a red or pinkish color.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
