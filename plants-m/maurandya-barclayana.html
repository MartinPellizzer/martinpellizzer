Maurandya barclayana

Maurandya barclayana, commonly known as the Barclay's mauve, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple and mauve, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems.

Maurandya barclayana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
