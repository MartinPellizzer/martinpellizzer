Malus domestica subsp. italotaurica

Malus domestica subsp.

italotaurica, commonly known as the Italian or Tuscan apple, is a subspecies of the domestic apple that exhibits unique botanical characteristics. It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Italy, and is known for its compact growth habit and ornamental value.

The plant typically produces small, round fruits with a tart flavor, often used in local culinary traditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cold winter period for proper fruit development.

This subspecies is valued for its resilience to certain pests and diseases compared to common apple varieties.
