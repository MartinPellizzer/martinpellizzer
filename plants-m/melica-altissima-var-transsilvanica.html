Melica altissima var. transsilvanica

Melica altissima var. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian tallgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and riverbanks. The plant features slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability in its native range.
