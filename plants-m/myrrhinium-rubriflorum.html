Myrrhinium rubriflorum

Myrrhinium rubriflorum, commonly known as the red-flowered myrrhinium, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America, where it thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, deep red inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Myrrhinium rubriflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant floral display.
