Mandevilla myriophylla

Mandevilla myriophylla, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative vine due to its ornamental value.

Mandevilla myriophylla prefers full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
