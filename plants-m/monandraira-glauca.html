Monandraira glauca

Monandraira glauca, commonly known as the blue glauca, is a unique plant species native to certain regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which is covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Monandraira glauca thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
