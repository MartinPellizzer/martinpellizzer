Microcerasus incana

Microcerasus incana, commonly known as the silverleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits.
