Mentzelia petiolata

Mentzelia petiolata, commonly known as the longleaf mentzelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Mentzelia petiolata is an important plant in its native habitat, contributing to the ecological diversity of desert and grassland ecosystems.
