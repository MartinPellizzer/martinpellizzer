Mammillaria jaliscana

Mammillaria jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem and prominent spines. The plant typically produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

It is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
