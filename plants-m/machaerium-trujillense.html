Machaerium trujillense

Machaerium trujillense, commonly known as the Trujillo machaerium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia and Venezuela.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that can range in color from white to purple.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
