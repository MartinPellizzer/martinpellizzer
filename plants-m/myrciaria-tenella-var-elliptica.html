Myrciaria tenella var. elliptica

Myrciaria tenella var. elliptica, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *pita-pita*, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The berries are rich in vitamin C and are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
