Molinaea brevipes

Molinaea brevipes, commonly known as the shortfoot moleplant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and is often found in the understory of forests.

Molinaea brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
