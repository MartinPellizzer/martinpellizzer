Medicago platycarpos

Medicago platycarpos, commonly known as sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock. Sainfoin is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in agricultural settings.

Its drought tolerance and adaptability to various soil types contribute to its widespread cultivation.
