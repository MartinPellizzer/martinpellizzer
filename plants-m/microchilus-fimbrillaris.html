Microchilus fimbrillaris

Microchilus fimbrillaris, commonly known as the slender leaf fern, is a delicate and rare fern species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, elongated fronds with a feathery appearance, which are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in dense forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
