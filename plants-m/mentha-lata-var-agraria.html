Mentha lata var. agraria

Mentha lata var. agraria, commonly known as agrarian mint, is a variant of the broadleaf mint species.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the mint family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Agrarian mint is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
