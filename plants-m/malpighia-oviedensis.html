Malpighia oviedensis

Malpighia oviedensis, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, fleshy, orange-red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
