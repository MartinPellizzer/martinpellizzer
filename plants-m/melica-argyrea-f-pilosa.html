Melica argyrea f. pilosa

Melica argyrea f. pilosa, commonly known as silvergrass, is a variant of the silvergrass species characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, silver-white leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

This form is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
