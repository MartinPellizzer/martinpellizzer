Melica cajamarcensis f. densa

Melica cajamarcensis f. densa, commonly known as the dense melica, is a perennial grass species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense arrangement of leaves, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically features slender, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly packed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and its potential use in conservation landscaping.
