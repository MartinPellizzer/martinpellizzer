Maurandya juncea

Maurandya juncea, commonly known as the common maurandya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are slender and branched.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.
