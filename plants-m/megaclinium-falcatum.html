Megaclinium falcatum

Megaclinium falcatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Megaclinium falcatum produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts, and it plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
