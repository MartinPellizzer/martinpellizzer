Malaxis mexicana

Malaxis mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it has narrow, grass-like leaves.

Malaxis mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
