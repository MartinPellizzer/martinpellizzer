Millettia paucijuga

Millettia paucijuga, commonly known as the small-leaf millettia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Southeast Asia, and parts of the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically small and opposite, and its racemose inflorescence composed of small, yellowish-white flowers. It produces legumes that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
