Marsea ivifolia

Marsea ivifolia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It has a compact growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to harsh coastal conditions.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
