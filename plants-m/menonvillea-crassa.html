Menonvillea crassa

Menonvillea crassa, commonly known as the thick-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
