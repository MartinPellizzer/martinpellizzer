Mertensia dicarpa

Mertensia dicarpa, commonly known as the Carolina bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has lance-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia dicarpa is an important component of woodland ecosystems and is often found in rich, loamy soils under deciduous trees.
