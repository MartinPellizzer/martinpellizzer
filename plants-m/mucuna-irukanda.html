Mucuna irukanda

Mucuna irukanda, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence on its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural systems.

The seeds contain high levels of protein and are used in various traditional and commercial applications.
