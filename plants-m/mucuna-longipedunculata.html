Mucuna longipedunculata

Mucuna longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and distinctive, hairy pods that contain numerous seeds. The plant produces yellow flowers and is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
