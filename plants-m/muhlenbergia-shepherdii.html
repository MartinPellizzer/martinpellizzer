Muhlenbergia shepherdii

Muhlenbergia shepherdii, commonly known as the desert dropseed, is a native grass species found in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that mature into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Muhlenbergia shepherdii plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
