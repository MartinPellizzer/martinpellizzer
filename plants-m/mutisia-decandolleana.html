Mutisia decandolleana

Mutisia decandolleana, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mutisia decandolleana is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
