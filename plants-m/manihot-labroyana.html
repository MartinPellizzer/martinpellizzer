Manihot labroyana

Manihot labroyana, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes that are used as a starch source. Due to its nutritional value, it has been cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
