Mercurialis sylvestris

Mercurialis sylvestris, commonly known as the wild mercury or forest mercury, is a flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It contains toxic compounds, including mercury compounds, which contribute to its common name and its historical use in medicine and alchemy.
