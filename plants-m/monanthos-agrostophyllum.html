Monanthos agrostophyllum

Monanthos agrostophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, characteristic of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed sites.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of native ecosystems and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
