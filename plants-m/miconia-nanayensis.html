Miconia nanayensis

Miconia nanayensis, commonly known as the Philippine miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a distinctive bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
