Millettia pyrrhocarpa

Millettia pyrrhocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited legume, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive red, woody fruits. The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making. It also plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Additionally, it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique fruiting structures.
