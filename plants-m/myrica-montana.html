Myrica montana

Myrica montana, commonly known as the wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by dark blue, waxy berries. Myrica montana thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

It has aromatic foliage and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic oils.
