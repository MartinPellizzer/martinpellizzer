Malus mengo

Malus mengo, commonly known as the Mengo apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red in color. The plant produces white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its distinctive fruit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in suitable climates.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
