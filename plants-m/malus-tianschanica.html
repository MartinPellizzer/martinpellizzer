Malus tianschanica

Malus tianschanica, commonly known as the Tianshan apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy pink to red flowers in spring. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically used in local cuisines. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its historical significance includes use in traditional medicine and as a source of ornamental plants.
