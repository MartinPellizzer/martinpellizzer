Monodora zenkeri

Monodora zenkeri, commonly known as African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic scent and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
