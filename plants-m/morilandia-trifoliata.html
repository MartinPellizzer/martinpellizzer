Morilandia trifoliata

Morilandia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf morilandia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Morilandia trifoliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
