Miscanthus saccharifer

Miscanthus saccharifer, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-green plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is known for its high biomass yield, making it a valuable species for biofuel and renewable energy production. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant, which contributes to its adaptability.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.
