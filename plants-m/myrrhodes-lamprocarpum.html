Myrrhodes lamprocarpum

Myrrhodes lamprocarpum, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by slender, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Myrrhodes lamprocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
