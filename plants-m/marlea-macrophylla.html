Marlea macrophylla

Marlea macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved marula, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in woodland and savanna habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its fruit, a drupe, is edible and has a sweet, milky pulp.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in some regions.
