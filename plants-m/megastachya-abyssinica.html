Megastachya abyssinica

Megastachya abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian false lily, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is a member of the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences that resemble umbels, which are composed of numerous small flowers. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
