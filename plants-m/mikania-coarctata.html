Mikania coarctata

Mikania coarctata, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinct, often waxy texture. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in clusters. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its milky sap and fibrous roots.
