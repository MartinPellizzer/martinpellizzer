Mentha collivaga

Mentha collivaga, commonly known as the Alpine mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes. The plant has fragrant, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy, and its small, pale blue or purple flowers bloom in late summer. Mentha collivaga is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

It plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
