Maxillaria squarrosa

Maxillaria squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose maxillaria, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually fragrant, with a complex structure featuring multiple petals and sepals.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
