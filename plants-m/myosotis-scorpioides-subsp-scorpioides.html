Myosotis scorpioides subsp. scorpioides

Myosotis scorpioides subsp.

scorpioides, commonly known as the water forget-me-not, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It typically grows in wet habitats such as marshes, ditches, and stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive central spur.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
