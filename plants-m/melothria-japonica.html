Melothria japonica

Melothria japonica, commonly known as the Japanese sour melon, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It produces small, green, sour-tasting fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and bears yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its botanical characteristics include a hairy stem and a fibrous root system.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
