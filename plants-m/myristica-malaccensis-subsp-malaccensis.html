Myristica malaccensis subsp. malaccensis

Myristica malaccensis subsp.

malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves with a waxy texture. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains the highly valued nutmeg-like seeds.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and potential economic importance in the spice trade.
