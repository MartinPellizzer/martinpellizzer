Mussaenda villosa

Mussaenda villosa, commonly known as the silky Mussaenda, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
