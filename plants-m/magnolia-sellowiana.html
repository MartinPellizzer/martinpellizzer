Magnolia sellowiana

Magnolia sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.
