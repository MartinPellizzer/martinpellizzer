Myrtus coriandriformis

Myrtus coriandriformis, commonly known as the Corsican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries, which are typically red or black, are edible and often used in culinary applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
