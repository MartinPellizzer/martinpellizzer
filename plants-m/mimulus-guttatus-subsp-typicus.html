Mimulus guttatus subsp. typicus

Mimulus guttatus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the common yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are typically spurred at the back, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in meadows. It is an annual or biennial herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
