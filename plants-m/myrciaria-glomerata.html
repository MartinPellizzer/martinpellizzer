Myrciaria glomerata

Myrciaria glomerata, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other economically important species like eucalyptus and guava. Its leaves are ovate with a glossy surface and emit a distinctive aromatic scent.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine due to their high antioxidant content.
