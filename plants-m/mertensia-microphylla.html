Mertensia microphylla

Mertensia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, bluish-green leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.
