Melastoma truncatum

Melastoma truncatum, commonly known as the broadleaf melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red or purple calyx. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.
