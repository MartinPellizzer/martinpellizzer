Malva alcea proles fastigiata

Malva alcea proles fastigiata, commonly known as the upright hollyhock, is a tall, herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, rose-like flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are hairy and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing up to 2 meters in height.

Its upright growth habit makes it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
