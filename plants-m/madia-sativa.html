Madia sativa

Madia sativa, commonly known as the common madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It features slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves.

Madia sativa produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
