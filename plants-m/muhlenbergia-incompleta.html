Muhlenbergia incompleta

Muhlenbergia incompleta, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in slender spikelets that mature into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Muhlenbergia incompleta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
