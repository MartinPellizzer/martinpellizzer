Mangifera swintonioides

Mangifera swintonioides, commonly known as the Swinton's mango, is a tropical fruit tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is closely related to the common mango (Mangifera indica). The plant produces large, elongated fruits with a smooth, thin skin that turns yellow when ripe. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it is typically propagated through seeds or grafting.

The tree is valued for its edible fruit, which has a sweet and aromatic flavor.
