Machaerium purpurascens

Machaerium purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-tinged machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pinkish-purple in color. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Machaerium purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
