Monvillea albicaulis

Monvillea albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed monvillea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, white stems and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, white or pale yellow blossoms.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
