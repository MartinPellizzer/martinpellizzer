Melaleuca comosa

Melaleuca comosa, commonly known as the silky tea tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and dense, silky white to greyish hairs covering its leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Melaleuca comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Aboriginal medicine for various purposes.
