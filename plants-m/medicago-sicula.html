Medicago sicula

Medicago sicula, commonly known as Sicilian clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
