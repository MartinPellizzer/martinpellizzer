Microchloa capensis

Microchloa capensis, commonly known as the Cape microchloa, is a small, perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Microchloa capensis is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
