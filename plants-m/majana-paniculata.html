Majana paniculata

Majana paniculata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne in paniculate inflorescences. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy and glandular. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Majana paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
