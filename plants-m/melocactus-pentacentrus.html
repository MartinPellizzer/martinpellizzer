Melocactus pentacentrus

Melocactus pentacentrus, commonly known as the five-spined melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a crown of spines that typically number five, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces a fleshy, edible fruit that is yellow or orange in color. Its thick, waxy cuticle helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment.

Melocactus pentacentrus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
