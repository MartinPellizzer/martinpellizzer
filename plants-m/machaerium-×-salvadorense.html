Machaerium × salvadorense

Machaerium × salvadorense, commonly known as the Salvadoran machaerium, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
