Mammillaria wildiana var. major

Mammillaria wildiana var. major, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habits, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
