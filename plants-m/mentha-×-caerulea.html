Mentha × caerulea

Mentha × caerulea, commonly known as blue mint, is a hybrid perennial herb in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its fragrant, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a blue or purple hue. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Mentha × caerulea is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves and is used in culinary, medicinal, and ornamental applications.
