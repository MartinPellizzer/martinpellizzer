Malesherbia lirana var. subtomentosa

Malesherbia lirana var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the velvety malesherbia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
