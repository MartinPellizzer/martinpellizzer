Myrcia neobrasiliensis

Myrcia neobrasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
