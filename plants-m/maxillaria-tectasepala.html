Maxillaria tectasepala

Maxillaria tectasepala, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a distinctive hood-like lip that encloses the reproductive organs. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Maxillaria tectasepala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
