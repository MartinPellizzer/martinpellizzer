Mimosa macracantha

Mimosa macracantha, commonly known as the giant mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow, spherical flowers. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
