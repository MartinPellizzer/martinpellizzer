Miconia curvipila

Miconia curvipila, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Neotropical region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive orange to red berries. Miconia curvipila is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
