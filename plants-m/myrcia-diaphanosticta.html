Myrcia diaphanosticta

Myrcia diaphanosticta, commonly known as the translucent-streaked myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of northeastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and its clusters of fragrant, white flowers. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species within its native range.
