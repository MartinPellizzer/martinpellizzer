Miconia inconspicua var. inconspicua

Miconia inconspicua var. inconspicua, commonly known as the small miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It typically grows as an understory shrub in humid, shaded environments.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, winged seeds, aiding in its dispersal by wind.
