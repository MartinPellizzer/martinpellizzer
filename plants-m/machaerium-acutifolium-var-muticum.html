Machaerium acutifolium var. muticum

Machaerium acutifolium var. muticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved machaerium, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
