Mertensia sieberi

Mertensia sieberi, commonly known as Sieber's bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hair. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
