Matelea mayana

Matelea mayana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a striking flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, known for its showy, nocturnal blooms that open at night and close by morning. The plant produces large, white to pale purple flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance, which attract pollinators such as moths and bats. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Matelea mayana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
