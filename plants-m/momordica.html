Momordica

Momordica, commonly known as bitter melon, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruits that range in color from green to orange or red when mature. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy texture. Its fruit is widely used in Asian cuisine and traditional medicine for its unique bitter taste and potential health benefits.

Botanically, Momordica is notable for its complex chemical composition, including compounds like momordicin and cucurbitacins.
