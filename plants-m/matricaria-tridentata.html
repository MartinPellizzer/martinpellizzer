Matricaria tridentata

Matricaria tridentata, commonly known as the triple-leaved chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flower heads surrounded by white or pale green ray florets. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny locations. It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and is used in herbal remedies.

Matricaria tridentata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
