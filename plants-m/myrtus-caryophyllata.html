Myrtus caryophyllata

Myrtus caryophyllata, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often used for their aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Myrtus caryophyllata is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
