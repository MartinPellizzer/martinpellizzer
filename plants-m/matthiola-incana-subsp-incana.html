Matthiola incana subsp. incana

Matthiola incana subsp.

incana, commonly known as wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, four-petaled flowers. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The plant is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
