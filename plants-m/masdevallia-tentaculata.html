Masdevallia tentaculata

Masdevallia tentaculata, commonly known as the tentacle masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful inflorescences and unique, curling petals that resemble tentacles, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small but striking, with a variety of hues including red, orange, and yellow.

Masdevallia tentaculata is valued by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
