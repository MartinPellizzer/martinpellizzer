Mulgedium blavii

Mulgedium blavii, commonly known as the silverleaf mulgedium, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, low-growing stature with silvery-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, aiding in water conservation. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in fine, dense hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and help reduce water loss.

Mulgedium blavii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant characteristics.
