Melaleuca halophila

Melaleuca halophila, commonly known as the saltbush melaleuca, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in saline or waterlogged soils, showcasing its halophytic nature. The plant has small, aromatic leaves and produces bottlebrush-like flowers in shades of white or pink. It is well adapted to coastal environments, often found in dunes or estuarine areas.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
