Miconia myrtillifolia

Miconia myrtillifolia, commonly known as the purple miconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Miconia myrtillifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
