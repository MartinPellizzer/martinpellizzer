Myrtus tinifolia

Myrtus tinifolia, commonly known as the Canary Islands myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often aromatic and emit a subtle scent when crushed. Its small, white flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

Myrtus tinifolia thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique climate of the Canary Islands.
