Manfreda sessiliflora

Manfreda sessiliflora, commonly known as the desert stonecress, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the spring. Its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in poor soils make it well adapted to desert conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in xeriscaping.
