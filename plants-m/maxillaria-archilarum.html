Maxillaria archilarum

Maxillaria archilarum, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in dry environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with white petals and a deep purple lip, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, typically arranged in a fan shape.

Maxillaria archilarum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
