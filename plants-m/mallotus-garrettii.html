Mallotus garrettii

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The bark is smooth and greyish, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
