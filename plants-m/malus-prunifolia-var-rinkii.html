Malus prunifolia var. rinkii

Malus prunifolia var. rinkii, commonly known as the Rink's apple service tree, is a rare variant of the apple service tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
