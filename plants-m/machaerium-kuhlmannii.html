Machaerium kuhlmannii

Machaerium kuhlmannii, commonly known as the purple machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. Machaerium kuhlmannii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical gardens.
