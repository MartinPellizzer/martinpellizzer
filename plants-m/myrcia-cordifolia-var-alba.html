Myrcia cordifolia var. alba

Myrcia cordifolia var. alba, commonly known as white myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its white or pale pink flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a waxy texture. This plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening.

It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
