Myrcia inconspicua

Myrcia inconspicua, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
