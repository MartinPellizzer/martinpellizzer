Matthiola longipetala var. basiceras

Matthiola longipetala var. basiceras, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
