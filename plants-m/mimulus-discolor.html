Mimulus discolor

Mimulus discolor, commonly known as the pale monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Phrymaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. Mimulus discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
