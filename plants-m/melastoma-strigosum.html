Melastoma strigosum

Melastoma strigosum, commonly known as the hairy melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or purple in color.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
