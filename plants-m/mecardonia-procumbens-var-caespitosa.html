Mecardonia procumbens var. caespitosa

Mecardonia procumbens var. caespitosa, commonly known as the creeping mecardonia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous stolons that root at the nodes, creating dense, cushion-like clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are linear to oblanceolate, often covered with fine hairs, and are arranged in a basal rosette.

This variety is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
