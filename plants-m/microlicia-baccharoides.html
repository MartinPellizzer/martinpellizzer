Microlicia baccharoides

Microlicia baccharoides, commonly known as the false mangrove, is a halophytic shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Brazil.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in estuaries and mangrove ecosystems. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding its survival in coastal conditions. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
