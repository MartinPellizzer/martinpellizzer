Maxillaria winaywaynaensis

Maxillaria winaywaynaensis, commonly known as the Winaywayna orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in Peru.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a complex structure typical of the Orchidaceae family. The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs that support its evergreen leaves, which are narrow and strap-like. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange with deep red or purple markings, and they bloom during the cooler months.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
