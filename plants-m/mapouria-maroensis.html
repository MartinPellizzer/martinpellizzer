Mapouria maroensis

Mapouria maroensis, commonly known as the Madagascar lily, is a striking plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Aroid family, which includes plants like philodendrons and peace lilies, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a unique inflorescence called a spathe, which encloses a cluster of small, white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
