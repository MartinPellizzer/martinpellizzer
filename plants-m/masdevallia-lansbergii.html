Masdevallia lansbergii

Masdevallia lansbergii, commonly known as the Lansberg's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flower structures and is typically found growing in high-altitude cloud forests. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with a wide range of colors including red, purple, and white.

Masdevallia lansbergii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
