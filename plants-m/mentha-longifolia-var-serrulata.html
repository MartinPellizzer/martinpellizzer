Mentha longifolia var. serrulata

Mentha longifolia var. serrulata, commonly known as serrated mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its aromatic leaves contain menthol and other volatile oils, contributing to its distinct minty fragrance.

This variety is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its refreshing scent and potential health benefits.
