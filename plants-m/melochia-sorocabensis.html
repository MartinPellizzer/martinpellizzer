Melochia sorocabensis

Melochia sorocabensis, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Melochia sorocabensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
