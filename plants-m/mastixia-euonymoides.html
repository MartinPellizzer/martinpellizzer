Mastixia euonymoides

Mastixia euonymoides, commonly known as the smooth mastixia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, egg-shaped fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Mastixia euonymoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
