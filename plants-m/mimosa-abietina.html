Mimosa abietina

Mimosa abietina, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils.

Mimosa abietina is also known for its sensitivity to touch, as its leaves fold when disturbed.
