Malva plazzae

Malva plazzae, commonly known as the Italian mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is native to southern Europe. It is often found in gardens and along roadsides due to its ornamental value.

Malva plazzae is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies.
