Malva vulgaris

Malva vulgaris, commonly known as the common mallows, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy, pink or white flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and wild areas.

Malva vulgaris is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
