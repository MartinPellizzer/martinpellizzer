Monnina nemorosa

Monnina nemorosa, commonly known as the creeping ironweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, spreading stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy texture, often with a slightly glandular surface.

Monnina nemorosa thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
