Micranthus fistulosus

Micranthus fistulosus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its small, blue or purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Micranthus fistulosus is often found in disturbed areas and can be an indicator of poor soil conditions.
