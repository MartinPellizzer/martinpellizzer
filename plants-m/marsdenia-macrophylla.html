Marsdenia macrophylla

Marsdenia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed Marsdenia, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, succulent stems and large, leathery leaves that can reach up to 20 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its striking foliage.
