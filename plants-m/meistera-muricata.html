Meistera muricata

Meistera muricata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its striking appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Meistera muricata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
