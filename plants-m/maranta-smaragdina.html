Maranta smaragdina

Maranta smaragdina, commonly known as the Emerald Maranta, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green, sometimes with contrasting patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
