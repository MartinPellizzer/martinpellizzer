Madronella rubella

Madronella rubella, commonly known as the red madrona, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its reddish bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
