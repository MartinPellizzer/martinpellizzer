Muscari vanense

Muscari vanense, commonly known as the Van's grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, resembling grapes, hence its common name. The plant produces strap-like, grassy leaves that are basal and evergreen. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Muscari vanense is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
