Matthiola odoratissima var. thibetica

Matthiola odoratissima var. thibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan stock, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of China.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces fragrant, four-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.

The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a strong, pungent aroma.
