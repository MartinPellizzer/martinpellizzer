Myrtus pimenta

Myrtus pimenta, commonly known as the West Indian myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often followed by dark purple berries. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a strong, spicy scent.

Myrtus pimenta is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its aromatic properties and hardy nature.
