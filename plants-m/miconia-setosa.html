Miconia setosa

Miconia setosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia setosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land management.
