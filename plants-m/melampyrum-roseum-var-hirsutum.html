Melampyrum roseum var. hirsutum

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it can photosynthesize but also derives some nutrients from its host plants through specialized root structures.
