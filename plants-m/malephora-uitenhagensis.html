Malephora uitenhagensis

Malephora uitenhagensis, commonly known as the Uitenhagen's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with translucent, waxy leaves and delicate, pale greenish-white flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees rather than in soil, and relies on specific humidity and light conditions to thrive. Its flowers are pollinated by specialized insects, and it has a very limited geographic range, making it highly vulnerable to habitat loss.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a conservation priority.
