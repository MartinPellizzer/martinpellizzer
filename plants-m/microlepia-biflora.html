Microlepia biflora

Microlepia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered microlepia, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, slender fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sori on the underside of its leaves.

Microlepia biflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant foliage.
