Moranothamnus

Moranothamnus, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.

The bark is smooth and often peels in thin layers, giving the tree a distinctive appearance.
