Macroplectrum ramosum

Macroplectrum ramosum, commonly known as the ramose marsh grass, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grass species. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil in marshy areas, contributing to ecosystem health.

Macroplectrum ramosum is often found in seasonally flooded habitats, where it plays a role in nutrient cycling and habitat provision for aquatic organisms.
