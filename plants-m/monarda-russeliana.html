Monarda russeliana

Monarda russeliana, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped to oval leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for native plant gardens.

Monarda russeliana is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
