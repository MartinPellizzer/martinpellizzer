Maughania phursia

Maughania phursia, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pale yellow wood that is highly valued for its scent and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, red fruits containing one or two seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
