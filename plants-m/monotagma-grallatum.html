Monotagma grallatum

Monotagma grallatum, commonly known as the feathered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Monotagma grallatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
