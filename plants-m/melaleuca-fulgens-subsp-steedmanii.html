Melaleuca fulgens subsp. steedmanii

Melaleuca fulgens subsp.

steedmanii, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery bloom.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
