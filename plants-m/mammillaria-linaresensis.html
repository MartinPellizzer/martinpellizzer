Mammillaria linaresensis

Mammillaria linaresensis, commonly known as the Linares's nipple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and white or pale green spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow flowers during the spring or early summer.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
