Mulgedium crassicaule

Mulgedium crassicaule, commonly known as the broad-leaved mulgedium, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features robust, succulent stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The leaves are large, broad, and fleshy, typically arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often with a distinctive coloration that aids in pollination.

This plant is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.
