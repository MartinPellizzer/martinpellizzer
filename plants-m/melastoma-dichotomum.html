Melastoma dichotomum

Melastoma dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually opposite and elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
