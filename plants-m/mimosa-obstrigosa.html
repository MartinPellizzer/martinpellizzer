Mimosa obstrigosa

Mimosa obstrigosa, commonly known as the sensitive plant or mimosa pudica, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by electrical signals in the plant's cells. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its seeds are encased in a hairy, spiny capsule that disperses when mature.

Mimosa obstrigosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
