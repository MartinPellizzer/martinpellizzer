Mutisia grandiflora

Mutisia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant mutisia, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often reaching up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant has a woody stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
