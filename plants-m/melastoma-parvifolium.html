Melastoma parvifolium

Melastoma parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its compact shrub form with opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Melastoma parvifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
