Mirabilis jalapa var. ambigua

Mirabilis jalapa var. ambigua, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value make it a fascinating subject in botanical study.
