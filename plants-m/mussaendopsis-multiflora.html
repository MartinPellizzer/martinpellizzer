Mussaendopsis multiflora

Mussaendopsis multiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered mussaendopsis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, oval-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and delicate floral display.
