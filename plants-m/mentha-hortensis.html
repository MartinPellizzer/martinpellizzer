Mentha hortensis

Mentha hortensis, commonly known as garden mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, serrated leaves and slender, square stems. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Mentha hortensis is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like basil and lavender.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
