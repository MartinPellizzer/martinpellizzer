Malva deflexa

Malva deflexa, commonly known as the woolly mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Malva deflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
