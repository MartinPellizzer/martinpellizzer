Mentha agrestis var. subrotunda

Mentha agrestis var. subrotunda, commonly known as round-leaved field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often rounded at the base, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or pink, arranged in whorls along the stem.

The species is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.
