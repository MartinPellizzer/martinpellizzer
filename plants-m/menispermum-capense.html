Menispermum capense

Menispermum capense, commonly known as the Natal arrowroot, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green berries that contain seeds, and it is often found in moist, forested areas. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
