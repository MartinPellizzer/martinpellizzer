Miconia longibracteata

Miconia longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers with five petals, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its ornamental value has led to its introduction in other parts of the world, though it can become invasive in some areas.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it adaptable to various ecological niches.
