Mentha pilisiensis

Mentha pilisiensis, commonly known as the Sunda mint, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in whorls. Mentha pilisiensis is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
