Modiola caroliniana f. parvula

Modiola caroliniana f. parvula, commonly known as the small-flowered sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils, often in open habitats such as dunes or coastal areas. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
