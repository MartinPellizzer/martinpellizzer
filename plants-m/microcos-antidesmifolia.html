Microcos antidesmifolia

Microcos antidesmifolia, commonly known as the African star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a sweet, jasmine-like scent. It is characterized by its woody stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Microcos antidesmifolia is well adapted to warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
