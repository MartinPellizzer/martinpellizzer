Myrcia pubipetala var. magnifolia

Myrcia pubipetala var. magnifolia, commonly known as the magnificent myrcia, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features elliptical to ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a reddish or purplish underside. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually pink or red, blooming in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red as it matures.
