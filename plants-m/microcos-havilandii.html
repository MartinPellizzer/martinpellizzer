Microcos havilandii

Microcos havilandii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that produces delicate, blue-violet flowers in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine white down. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Microcos havilandii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
