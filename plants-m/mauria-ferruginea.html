Mauria ferruginea

Mauria ferruginea, commonly known as the rust-colored dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, reddish-brown stems. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
