Mischocodon reticulatus

Mischocodon reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to host plants.

Mischocodon reticulatus is native to parts of Africa and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
