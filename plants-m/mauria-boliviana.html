Mauria boliviana

Mauria boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian mallow, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and broad, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
