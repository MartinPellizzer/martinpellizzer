Muhlenbergia leptoura

Muhlenbergia leptoura, commonly known as the slender muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often visible in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Muhlenbergia leptoura is also an important plant for wildlife, providing food and habitat for various birds and insects.
