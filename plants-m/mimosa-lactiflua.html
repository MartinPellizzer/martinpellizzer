Mimosa lactiflua

Mimosa lactiflua, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
