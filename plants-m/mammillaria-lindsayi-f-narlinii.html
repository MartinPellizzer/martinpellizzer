Mammillaria lindsayi f. narlinii

Mammillaria lindsayi f. narlinii, commonly known as the Lindsay's mammillaria, is a rare and compact cactus native to the Sierra Madre Occidental in Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a dense covering of white, woolly spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
