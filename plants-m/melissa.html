Melissa

Melissa, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and lavender. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small white or yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in teas and aromatherapy for their calming properties.

Melissa thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
