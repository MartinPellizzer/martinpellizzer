Macrolobium limbatum var. mucronatum

Macrolobium limbatum var. mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf macrolobium, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often glabrous and have a distinct, pointed tip. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its growth habit is generally shrub-like, with multiple stems and a dense foliage structure.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
