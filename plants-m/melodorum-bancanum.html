Melodorum bancanum

Melodorum bancanum, commonly known as the Bankan melody vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Melodorum bancanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
