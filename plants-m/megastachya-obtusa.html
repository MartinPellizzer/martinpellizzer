Megastachya obtusa

Megastachya obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved spike grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, often with a slight reddish tinge. Its leaves are broad and flat, with a rough texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Megastachya obtusa is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.
