Metalasia massonii

Metalasia massonii, commonly known as the African violets, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, velvety leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers, which can be blue, purple, or white, are borne on short, erect stems.

Metalasia massonii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
