Melanthera canescens f. bicolor

Melanthera canescens f. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its botanical structure includes a basal rosette of leaves and slender, branching stems that support the inflorescences.

This variety is distinguished by its bicoloration, with flowers that often display a mix of blue and white or pink hues.
