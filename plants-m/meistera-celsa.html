Meistera celsa

Meistera celsa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Meistera celsa thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
