Mentha aquatica var. duriuscula

Mentha aquatica var. duriuscula, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herb native to moist environments in Europe and Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly serrated. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the mint family. It thrives in wet soils near streams, marshes, and ponds.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
