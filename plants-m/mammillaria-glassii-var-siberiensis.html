Mammillaria glassii var. siberiensis

Mammillaria glassii var. siberiensis, commonly known as the Siberian mammillaria, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, white to pale blue spines, which provide protection from predators and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, typically in clusters at the top of the stem. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it ideal for desert gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardiness make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
