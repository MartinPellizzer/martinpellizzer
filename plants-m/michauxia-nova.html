Michauxia nova

Michauxia nova, commonly known as the Nova Scotia bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the coastal regions of Nova Scotia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and salt marshes.

Michauxia nova is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
