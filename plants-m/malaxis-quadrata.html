Malaxis quadrata

Malaxis quadrata, commonly known as the broad-leaved malaxis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad and flat, often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
