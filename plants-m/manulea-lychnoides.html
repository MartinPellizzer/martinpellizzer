Manulea lychnoides

Manulea lychnoides, commonly known as the candleplant, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, candle-like stems that are often covered in a waxy, white coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged periods of drought.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
