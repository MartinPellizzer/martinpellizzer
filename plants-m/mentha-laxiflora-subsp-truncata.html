Mentha laxiflora subsp. truncata

Mentha laxiflora subsp.

truncata, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
