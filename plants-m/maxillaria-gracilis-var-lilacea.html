Maxillaria gracilis var. lilacea

Maxillaria gracilis var. lilacea, commonly known as the lilac orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with slender, grass-like leaves and produces fragrant, pale pink to lilac-colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, with flowers that have a distinctive, intricate structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
