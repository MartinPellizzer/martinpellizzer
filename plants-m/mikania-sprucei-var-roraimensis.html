Mikania sprucei var. roraimensis

Mikania sprucei var. roraimensis, commonly known as the Roraima Mikania, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
