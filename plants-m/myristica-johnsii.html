Myristica johnsii

Myristica johnsii, commonly known as the Malabar nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood and unique fruit structure. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that contain a single, hard-shelled seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin.

Myristica johnsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of essential oils.
