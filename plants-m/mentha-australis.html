Mentha australis

Mentha australis, commonly known as Australian mint, is a perennial herb native to Australia and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically dark green and aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Mentha australis is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
