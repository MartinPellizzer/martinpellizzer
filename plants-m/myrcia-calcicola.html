Myrcia calcicola

Myrcia calcicola, commonly known as the limestone myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and shows a preference for calcareous soils, which is reflected in its specific epithet "calcicola.". The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are typically visited by various pollinators. Its botanical features include a woody stem and evergreen foliage, typical of the Myrtaceae family.

Myrcia calcicola is often found in rocky or sandy coastal habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
