Melanoseris decipiens var. decipiens

Melanoseris decipiens var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ability to mimic surrounding vegetation, it is sometimes difficult to distinguish from other plants in its environment.
