Morisonia elliptica

Morisonia elliptica, commonly known as the Christmas flower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pink flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional ceremonies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Morisonia elliptica is also valued for its cultural significance in various island communities.
