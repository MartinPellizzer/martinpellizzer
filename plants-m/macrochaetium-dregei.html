Macrochaetium dregei

Macrochaetium dregei, commonly known as the Cape mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale blue to purple florets. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and have a bluish-green color, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Macrochaetium dregei is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found in mountainous regions with a Mediterranean climate.
