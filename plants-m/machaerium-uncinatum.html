Machaerium uncinatum

Machaerium uncinatum, commonly known as the hookleaf grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in small, hairy pods, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Machaerium uncinatum is valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
