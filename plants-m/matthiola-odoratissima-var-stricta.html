Matthiola odoratissima var. stricta

Matthiola odoratissima var. stricta, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
