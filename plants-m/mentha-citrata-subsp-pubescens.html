Mentha citrata subsp. pubescens

Mentha citrata subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as lemon mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or lilac in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are used in herbal teas and have a citrusy, minty aroma.
