Myrcia riverae

Myrcia riverae, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia riverae is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
