Malpighia adamsii

Malpighia adamsii, commonly known as the Adams' physalis, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Malpighiaceae and is characterized by its showy, lantern-like calyces that enclose its small, fleshy fruits. The plant produces bright orange or red berries that are edible and have a tangy, sweet flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and coastal areas.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
