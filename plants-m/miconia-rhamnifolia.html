Miconia rhamnifolia

Miconia rhamnifolia, commonly known as the red miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often bright green with a reddish underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Miconia rhamnifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It is also noted for its ornamental value, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
