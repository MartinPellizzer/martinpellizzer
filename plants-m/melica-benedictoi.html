Melica benedictoi

Melica benedictoi, commonly known as Benedict's brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Melica benedictoi is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.
