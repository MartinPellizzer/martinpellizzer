Minuartia hispanica

Minuartia hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. It is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
