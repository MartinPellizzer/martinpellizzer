Mediolobivia auranitida

Mediolobivia auranitida, commonly known as the golden flame lichen, is a unique species characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange pigmentation.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant exhibits a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi, which contribute to its distinctive coloration and growth patterns. Its structure consists of a dense, crust-like thallus that adheres tightly to surfaces such as bark or stone.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicately balanced botanical specimen.
