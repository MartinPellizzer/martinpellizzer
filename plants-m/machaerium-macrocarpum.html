Machaerium macrocarpum

Machaerium macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a pubescent underside and produces small, pea-like flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals.

Machaerium macrocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
