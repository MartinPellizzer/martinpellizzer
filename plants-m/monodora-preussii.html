Monodora preussii

Monodora preussii, commonly known as African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

The plant is also valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction and furniture-making.
