Malvaviscus arboreus var. drummondii

Malvaviscus arboreus var. drummondii, commonly known as the scarlet mallows, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, bright red to orange flowers that bloom throughout the summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a velvety texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
