Mandevilla xerophytica

Mandevilla xerophytica, commonly known as the desert mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The plant has adapted to survive in dry environments with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It produces tubular flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.

Mandevilla xerophytica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
