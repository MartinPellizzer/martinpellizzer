Mandevilla pohliana var. velutina

Mandevilla pohliana var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet mandevilla, is a flowering vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for trellises and arbors.
