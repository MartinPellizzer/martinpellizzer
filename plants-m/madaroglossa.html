Madaroglossa

Madaroglossa, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and ethereal flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and striking, pale pink or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees rather than in soil, and relies on specific humidity and light conditions to thrive. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of bees and wasps, making it highly dependent on its ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Madaroglossa is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
