Monstera brownii

Monstera brownii, commonly known as the Brown's monstera, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive split or fenestrated pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright yellow berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and spread across trees, making it a popular houseplant for its lush foliage.

Monstera brownii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
