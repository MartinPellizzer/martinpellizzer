Mentha × pycnotricha

Mentha × pycnotricha, commonly known as the broad-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha hangelii and Mentha longifolia.

It is characterized by its robust growth and dense, dark green foliage with broad, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Mentha × pycnotricha is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.
