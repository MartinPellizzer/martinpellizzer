Mentha × rotundifolia var. bauhini

Mentha × rotundifolia var. bauhini, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Mentha rotundifolia and Mentha bauhinii.

It is a perennial herb with a creeping growth habit, featuring rhizomes and stolons that allow it to spread across the ground. The plant produces opposite, ovate to round leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or pink, arranged in whorls along the stems.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
