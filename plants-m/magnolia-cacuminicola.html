Magnolia cacuminicola

Magnolia cacuminicola, commonly known as the yellow magnolia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The bark is smooth and pale, often exuding a mild fragrance when cut.
