Masdevallia dolichopoda

Masdevallia dolichopoda, commonly known as the long-stalked masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often pendulous inflorescence and a variety of colorful, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating efficient water absorption.

The genus Masdevallia is known for its diverse floral forms, and M. dolichopoda is no exception, showcasing the adaptive beauty of orchids in their natural habitat.
