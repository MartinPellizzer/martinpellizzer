Microlepia speluncae var. pubescens

Microlepia speluncae var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent cave fern, is a rare and specialized fern species native to subterranean environments.

It thrives in dark, humid caves and is adapted to low-light conditions through its unique morphology. The plant features delicate, frond-like structures that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its varietal name. Its reproductive structures are typically concealed within the dense foliage, aiding in its survival in isolated habitats.

Due to its restricted distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
