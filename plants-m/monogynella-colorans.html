Monogynella colorans

Monogynella colorans, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple petals that surround a central cone-like structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Monogynella colorans is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
