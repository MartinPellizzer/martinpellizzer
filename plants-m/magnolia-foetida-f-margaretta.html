Magnolia foetida f. margaretta

Magnolia foetida f. margaretta, commonly known as the Margaretta magnolia, is a botanical variant of the fragrant magnolia species.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida and Georgia. Its leaves are broad and oval, providing a lush green backdrop to its fragrant blooms.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
