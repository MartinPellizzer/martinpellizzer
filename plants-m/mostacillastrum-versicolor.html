Mostacillastrum versicolor

Mostacillastrum versicolor, commonly known as the variable grass.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and typically greenish or reddish, appearing in spike-like clusters.

The species is notable for its adaptability and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
