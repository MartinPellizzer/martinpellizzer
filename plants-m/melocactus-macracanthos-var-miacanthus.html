Melocactus macracanthos var. miacanthus

Melocactus macracanthos var. miacanthus, commonly known as the Miacanthus melocactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that often remains below ground, with only the top portion emerging. The plant produces a single, large, spiny areole that bears a single, elongated, and often curved spine. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscape designs.
