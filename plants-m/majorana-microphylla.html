Majorana microphylla

Majorana microphylla, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender stems and is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in cooking and traditional medicine. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
