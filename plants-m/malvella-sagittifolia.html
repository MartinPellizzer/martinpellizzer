Malvella sagittifolia

Malvella sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf mallows, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice in arid landscape designs.
