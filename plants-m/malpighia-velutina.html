Malpighia velutina

Malpighia velutina, commonly known as velvet berry or Brazilian acerola, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
