Mesembryanthemum cylindricum

Mesembryanthemum cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It is a member of the Aizoaceae family, known for its unique adaptations to drought.

The leaves are often reduced to small scales, further emphasizing its succulent nature.
