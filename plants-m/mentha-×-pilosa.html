Mentha × pilosa

Mentha × pilosa, commonly known as shiso, is a hybrid perennial plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves that range in color from dark green to purple. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, appearing in clusters. Mentha × pilosa thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is widely cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in Asian cuisine and traditional medicine.
