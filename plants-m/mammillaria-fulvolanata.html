Mammillaria fulvolanata

Mammillaria fulvolanata, commonly known as the orange-hair mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to yellowish tubercles that give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
