Melocactus langsdorfii

Melocactus langsdorfii, commonly known as the Langsdorf's melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It is characterized by its spherical or slightly elongated stem, which is typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by small, red fruits. Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
