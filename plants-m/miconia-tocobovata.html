Miconia tocobovata

Miconia tocobovata, commonly known as the Toco River miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
