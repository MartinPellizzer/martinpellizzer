Mimulus rubellus var. latiflorus

Mimulus rubellus var. latiflorus, commonly known as the broad-flowered yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America.

It features striking, bright orange to red tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
