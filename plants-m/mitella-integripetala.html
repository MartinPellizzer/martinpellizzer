Mitella integripetala

Mitella integripetala, commonly known as the smooth-leaved mitella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves that are entire and smooth, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Mitella integripetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
