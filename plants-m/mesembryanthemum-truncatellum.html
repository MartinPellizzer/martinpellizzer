Mesembryanthemum truncatellum

Mesembryanthemum truncatellum, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, often blue or purple leaves that are adapted to store water, typical of succulents. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or sandy gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
