Monsonia burkeana

Monsonia burkeana, commonly known as the Cape primrose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Monsonia burkeana is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
