Marsdenia brassii

Marsdenia brassii, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
