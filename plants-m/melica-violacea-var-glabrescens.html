Melica violacea var. glabrescens

Melica violacea var. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are narrow, smooth, and slightly hairy, with a distinctive bluish-green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, violet-tinged flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.
