Minuartia verna subsp. insularis

Minuartia verna subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the island rock cress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region. It thrives in rocky, coastal, and maritime environments, often found on cliffs and sandy shores.

The species is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

It is an important plant for stabilizing soil in coastal ecosystems and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
