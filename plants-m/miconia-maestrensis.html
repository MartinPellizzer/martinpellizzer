Miconia maestrensis

Miconia maestrensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, spathaceous bracts. Miconia maestrensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and sometimes problematic in agricultural settings.
