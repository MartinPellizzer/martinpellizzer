Menispermum chondrodendron

Menispermum chondrodendron, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its roots contain various alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
