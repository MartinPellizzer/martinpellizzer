Melastoma aspergillare

Melastoma aspergillare, commonly known as the rough-stemmed melastoma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or red, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melastoma aspergillare is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in folk remedies for various ailments.
