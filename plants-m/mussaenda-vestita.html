Mussaenda vestita

Mussaenda vestita, commonly known as the velvet flower or white musaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that range from white to pink or red, depending on the variety. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mussaenda vestita is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
