Machaerium cantarellianum

Machaerium cantarellianum, commonly known as the Cantarellia grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Machaerium cantarellianum is often used in traditional medicine and has potential ecological roles in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
