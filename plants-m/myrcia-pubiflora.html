Myrcia pubiflora

Myrcia pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
