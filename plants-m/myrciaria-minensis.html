Myrciaria minensis

Myrciaria minensis, commonly known as the Brazilian white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

Myrciaria minensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
