Mesembryanthemum assimile

Mesembryanthemum assimile, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, pink, and red, typically blooming in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Mesembryanthemum assimile is also known for its ability to store water in its leaves, adapting to arid environments.
