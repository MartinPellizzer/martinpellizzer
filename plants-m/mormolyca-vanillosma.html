Mormolyca vanillosma

Mormolyca vanillosma, commonly known as the vanilla orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into dark brown pods containing aromatic seeds. Its most notable feature is the vanilla bean, which is harvested from the mature pods and used extensively in culinary and fragrance industries.

The botanical structure of Mormolyca vanillosma includes a rhizomatous root system and lanceolate leaves with a waxy surface.
