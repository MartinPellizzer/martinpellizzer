Mimulus gracilis

Mimulus gracilis, commonly known as the slender yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, drooping yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Mimulus gracilis is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
