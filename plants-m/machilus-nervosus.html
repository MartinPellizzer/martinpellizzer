Machilus nervosus

Machilus nervosus, commonly known as the veined machilus, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves with prominent veins, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Machilus nervosus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and potential medicinal properties.
