Monticalia pulchella subsp. pulchella

Monticalia pulchella subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America. It features palmate leaves with a velvety texture and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
