Micropodium durvillei

Micropodium durvillei, commonly known as the African orchid, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sandy soils. The plant features a slender stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in a spike, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Micropodium durvillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
