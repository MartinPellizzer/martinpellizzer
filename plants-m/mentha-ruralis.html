Mentha ruralis

Mentha ruralis, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive minty scent.

Mentha ruralis is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
