Malus microcarpa baccata

Malus microcarpa baccata, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically not edible for humans but are used in traditional Chinese medicine. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
