Myrciaria tenella

Myrciaria tenella, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.

Myrciaria tenella is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
