Malus spectabilis f. albiplena

Malus spectabilis f. albiplena, commonly known as the white-flowered crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree characterized by its showy, double white blossoms.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters, featuring a rounded, compact form. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall. Its flowers appear in early spring before the leaves emerge, adding ornamental value to gardens.

The fruit is small, red, and edible, though not typically consumed by humans.
