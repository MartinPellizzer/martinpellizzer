Microstylis vinosa

Microstylis vinosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are borne on slender, branching stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Microstylis vinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
