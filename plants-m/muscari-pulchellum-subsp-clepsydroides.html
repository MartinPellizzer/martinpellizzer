Muscari pulchellum subsp. clepsydroides

Muscari pulchellum subsp.

clepsydroides, commonly known as the clepsydroid musk mallow, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces a slender, grass-like leaf blade that emerges from a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique flower structure and attractive color make it a valuable addition to ornamental horticulture.
