Mespilus oxyacantha var. auriculata

Mespilus oxyacantha var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate blackthorn, is a variant of the blackthorn species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) leaf bases and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, often preceding the leaves in early spring.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface.
