Mimosa dominiciana

Mimosa dominiciana, commonly known as the Dominican mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands, including the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod fruits. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves that close when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Mimosa dominiciana thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
