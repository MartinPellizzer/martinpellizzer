Macfadyena

Macfadyena, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant's leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, while its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its resilience in various soil types.
