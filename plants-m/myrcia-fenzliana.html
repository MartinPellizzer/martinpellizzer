Myrcia fenzliana

Myrcia fenzliana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
