Muscari concinnum

Muscari concinnum, commonly known as the grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling grapes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

Muscari concinnum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant.
