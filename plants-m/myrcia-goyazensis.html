Myrcia goyazensis

Myrcia goyazensis, commonly known as the Goyazense Myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of the forest understory.

Myrcia goyazensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
