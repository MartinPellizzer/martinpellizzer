Morisonia sola

Morisonia sola, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet or blue. Morisonia sola is characterized by its climbing habit, often using tendrils to support its growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native habitats.
