Merremia yunnanensis

Merremia yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan morning glory, is a climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Merremia yunnanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
