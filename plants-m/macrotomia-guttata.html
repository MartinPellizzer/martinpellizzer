Macrotomia guttata

Macrotomia guttata, commonly known as the spotted leaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its unique leaf patterns that often resemble a mosaic. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Macrotomia guttata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
