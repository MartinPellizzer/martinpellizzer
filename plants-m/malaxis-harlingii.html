Malaxis harlingii

Malaxis harlingii, commonly known as the Harling's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Malaxis harlingii is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in montane and lowland regions.
