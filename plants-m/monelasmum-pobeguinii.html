Monelasmum pobeguinii

Monelasmum pobeguinii, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinct purple or red throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Monelasmum pobeguinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a cut flower.
