Macairea axilliflora

Macairea axilliflora, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

Macairea axilliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
