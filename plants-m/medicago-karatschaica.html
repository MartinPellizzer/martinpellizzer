Medicago karatschaica

Medicago karatschaica, commonly known as Karatschaic clover, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through deep root systems. It plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.

Its medicinal properties have also been explored in traditional herbal practices.
