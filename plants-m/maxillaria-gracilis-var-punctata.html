Maxillaria gracilis var. punctata

Maxillaria gracilis var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and long, pendant inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically dark green and somewhat leathery, while the flowers display a range of colors, often with distinctive spots or markings. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
