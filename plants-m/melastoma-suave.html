Melastoma suave

Melastoma suave, commonly known as sweet melastoma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy in appearance. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that are usually pink or white, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

Melastoma suave is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
