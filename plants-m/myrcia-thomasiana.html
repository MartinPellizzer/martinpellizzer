Myrcia thomasiana

Myrcia thomasiana, commonly known as Thomas's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that turn red when mature.

Myrcia thomasiana is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
