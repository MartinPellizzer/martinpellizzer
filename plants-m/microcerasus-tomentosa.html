Microcerasus tomentosa

Microcerasus tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy stone cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
