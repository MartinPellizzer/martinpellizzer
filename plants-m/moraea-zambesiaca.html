Moraea zambesiaca

Moraea zambesiaca, commonly known as the Zambesi lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows up to 60 cm tall, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
