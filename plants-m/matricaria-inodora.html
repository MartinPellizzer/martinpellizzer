Matricaria inodora

Matricaria inodora, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a strong, aromatic scent.

Matricaria inodora is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
