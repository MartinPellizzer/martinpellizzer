Millettia occidentalis

Millettia occidentalis, commonly known as the western millettia, is a species of leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with numerous small leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences. Its fruits are legumes, containing several seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Millettia occidentalis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
