Mentha pulegioides

Mentha pulegioides, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or white, arranged in whorls along the stem. Mentha pulegioides is known for its strong aromatic properties, primarily due to the presence of nepetalactone.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
