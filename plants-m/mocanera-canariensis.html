Mocanera canariensis

Mocanera canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with a prominent red or purple throat, which bloom from summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to coastal and rocky environments.

Mocanera canariensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
