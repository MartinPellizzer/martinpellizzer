Melasma scabrum var. scabrum

Melasma scabrum var. scabrum, commonly known as the rough skin lichen, is a variant of the lichen species characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured thallus.

It typically grows on bark, stone, or soil, thriving in moist, shaded environments. The plant's structure consists of a symbiotic association between a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium. Its coloration ranges from pale green to yellowish-green, depending on environmental conditions.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is known for its slow growth and resilience to adverse conditions.
