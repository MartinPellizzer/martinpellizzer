Melastoma candidum

Melastoma candidum, commonly known as the white melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are borne in clusters at the base of the plant.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
