Melochia venosa var. sericea

Melochia venosa var. sericea, commonly known as silky melochia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive waxy texture.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
