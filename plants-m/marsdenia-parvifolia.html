Marsdenia parvifolia

Marsdenia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Marsdenia parvifolia is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
