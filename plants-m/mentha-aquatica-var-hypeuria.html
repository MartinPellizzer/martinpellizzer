Mentha aquatica var. hypeuria

Mentha aquatica var. hypeuria, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herb native to wetland areas in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist soils near streams, ponds, and marshes, thriving in humid environments. The plant features slender, aromatic leaves that are often heart-shaped and have a distinctive minty fragrance. Its flowers are small, pale blue to violet, and appear in whorled clusters along the stems.

This variety is closely related to other mint species and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
