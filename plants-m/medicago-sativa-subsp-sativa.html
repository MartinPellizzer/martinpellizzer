Medicago sativa subsp. sativa

Medicago sativa subsp.

sativa, commonly known as alfalfa, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant is well-known for its deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in dry conditions. Alfalfa is valued for its high nutritional content, making it a popular forage crop and a source of protein.

It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
