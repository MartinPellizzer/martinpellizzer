Macrocarpaea voluptuosa

Macrocarpaea voluptuosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf macrocarpaea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
