Mapouria sclerocalyx var. coriacea

Mapouria sclerocalyx var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved mapouria, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
