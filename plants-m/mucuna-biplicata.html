Mucuna biplicata

Mucuna biplicata, commonly known as the twinleaf velvetbean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being deeply lobed and covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical, flat seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Mucuna biplicata also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with essential nutrients.
