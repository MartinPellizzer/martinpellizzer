Mandevilla velutina var. glabra

Mandevilla velutina var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens or as a houseplant.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
