Mimulus uvedaliae var. lutea

Mimulus uvedaliae var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

This variety is part of the evening primrose family and is adapted to arid environments.
