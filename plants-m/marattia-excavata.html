Marattia excavata

Marattia excavata, commonly known as the hollow fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hollow, and often segmented fronds that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with small, lance-shaped pinnae that are arranged in a regular pattern.

Marattia excavata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
