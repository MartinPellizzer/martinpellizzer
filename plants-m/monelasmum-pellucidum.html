Monelasmum pellucidum

Monelasmum pellucidum, commonly known as the glassy leaf vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often translucent or waxy in appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warm seasons. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Due to its unique leaf structure, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens.
