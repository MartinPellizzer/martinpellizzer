Megaclinium scaberulum

Megaclinium scaberulum, commonly known as the roughleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in spike-like clusters during the summer months.

Megaclinium scaberulum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
