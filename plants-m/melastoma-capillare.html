Melastoma capillare

Melastoma capillare, commonly known as the small-leaved melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens.

Melastoma capillare is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
