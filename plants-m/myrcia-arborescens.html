Myrcia arborescens

Myrcia arborescens, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and opposite in arrangement, while the fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe.

Myrcia arborescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
