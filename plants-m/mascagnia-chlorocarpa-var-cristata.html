Mascagnia chlorocarpa var. cristata

Mascagnia chlorocarpa var. cristata, commonly known as the cristate mescal bean, is a striking variant of the mescal bean plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, branched inflorescences that give it a distinctive, almost whimsical appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like fruits. Its foliage consists of palmate leaves with three to five lobes, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
