Myrcia umbelliformis

Myrcia umbelliformis, commonly known as the umbrella myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are arranged in umbellate inflorescences, giving the plant its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aromatic foliage and showy blooms.
