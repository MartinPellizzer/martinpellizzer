Montia intermedia

Montia intermedia, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters and blooming in early spring.

Montia intermedia is often found in moist soils and is adapted to thrive in shaded, woodland habitats.
