Melocactus acunae subsp. lagunaensis

Melocactus acunae subsp.

lagunaensis, commonly known as the Lagunaensis melocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its distinctive, globular shape and bright green to blue-green stem, which is often covered in white or pale spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in summer. It is well adapted to desert environments, with thick, water-storing stems and a shallow root system.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts.
