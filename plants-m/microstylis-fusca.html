Microstylis fusca

Microstylis fusca, commonly known as the black-stemmed spikerush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often dark green to black in color, giving it its common name. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Microstylis fusca plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
