Megaclinium endotrachys

Megaclinium endotrachys, commonly known as the golden beard grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is well adapted to coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct blue-green hue.

While not widely cultivated, it is occasionally used in ornamental gardening for its striking appearance.
