Myrtus sinemariensis

Myrtus sinemariensis, commonly known as the Sinemar myrtle, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small evergreen leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded location.

Myrtus sinemariensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has a historical use in traditional medicine.
