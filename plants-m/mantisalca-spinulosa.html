Mantisalca spinulosa

Mantisalca spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny mantisalca, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments.

Mantisalca spinulosa is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
