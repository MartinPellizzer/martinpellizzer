Mosiera nipensis

Mosiera nipensis, commonly known as the Japanese daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Mosiera nipensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and winter interest in gardens.
