Macrura perbella

Macrura perbella, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, daisy-like appearance with yellow petals and a central disk. The plant is known for its ability to retain its color and form for an extended period, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
