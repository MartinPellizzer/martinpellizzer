Malacothamnus hallii

Malacothamnus hallii, commonly known as the Hall's thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Malacothamnus hallii is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.
