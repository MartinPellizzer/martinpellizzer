Melocactus stramineus

Melocactus stramineus, commonly known as the yellow melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green color and cylindrical shape, with a ribbed structure that helps it conserve water. The plant produces a single, fleshy fruit that is typically yellow or orange in color. Its spines are short and often covered in a fine, white coating.

Melocactus stramineus is well adapted to harsh desert environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
