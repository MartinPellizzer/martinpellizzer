Myrcialeucus

Myrcialeucus, commonly known as the silverleaf myrtle, is a tropical plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Myrcialeucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
