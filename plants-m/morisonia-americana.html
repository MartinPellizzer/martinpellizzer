Morisonia americana

Morisonia americana, commonly known as the American morisonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woods. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Morisonia americana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
