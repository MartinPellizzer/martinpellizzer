Melica brasiliana f. truncata

Melica brasiliana f. truncata, commonly known as the Brazilian meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spikelet cluster, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
