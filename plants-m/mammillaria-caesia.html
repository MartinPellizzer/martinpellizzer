Mammillaria caesia

Mammillaria caesia, commonly known as the blue mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and blue-gray spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise spiky form. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Mammillaria caesia is well-suited for rock gardens and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
