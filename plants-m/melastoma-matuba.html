Melastoma matuba

Melastoma matuba, commonly known as the Malayan bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth surface.

Melastoma matuba is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along riverbanks.
