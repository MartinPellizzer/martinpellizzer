Mammillaria duoformis var. typica

Mammillaria duoformis var. typica, commonly known as the two-form mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water.
