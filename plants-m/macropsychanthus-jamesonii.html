Macropsychanthus jamesonii

Macropsychanthus jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's ghost flower, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its unique, lantern-like inflorescence that resembles a ghostly lantern, hence its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
