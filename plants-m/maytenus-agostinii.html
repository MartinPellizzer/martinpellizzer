Maytenus agostinii

Maytenus agostinii, commonly known as the Agostini mayten, is a small evergreen tree native to the humid forests of South America, particularly in Brazil.

It typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters and has a dense canopy with elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and pharmacological research.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
