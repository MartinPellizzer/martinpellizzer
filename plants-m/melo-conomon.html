Melo conomon

Melo conomon, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squash.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces a spherical, ribbed fruit with a netted rind. The fruit is typically orange to yellow in color and contains a sweet, aromatic, honey-like flesh. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

It is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins A and C.
