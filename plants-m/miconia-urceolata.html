Miconia urceolata

Miconia urceolata, commonly known as the cup-shaped miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, followed by distinctive, urn-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. Miconia urceolata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
