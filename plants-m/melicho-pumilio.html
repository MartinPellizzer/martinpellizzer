Melicho pumilio

Melicho pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the valley, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped white flowers. The plant is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Melicho pumilio is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
