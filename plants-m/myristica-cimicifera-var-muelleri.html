Myristica cimicifera var. muelleri

Myristica cimicifera var. muelleri, commonly known as the nutmeg tree variant, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen, compound leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that give way to the aromatic nutmeg and mace fruits. The plant is valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from the seed and aril, contributing to its use in both culinary and medicinal applications. Its growth habit includes a tall, slender trunk with a dense crown, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
