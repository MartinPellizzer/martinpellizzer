Malaxis nutans

Malaxis nutans, commonly known as the drooping orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to various regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that hang downward, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often clustered at the base of the plant.

Malaxis nutans is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
