Miconia charadrophila

Miconia charadrophila, commonly known as the crimson miconia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that contains numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
