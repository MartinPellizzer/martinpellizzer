Malva moschata var. heterophylla

Malva moschata var. heterophylla, commonly known as the musk mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive musky fragrance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are hairy and often branched, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to retain moisture.

This variety is noted for its variability in leaf shape, which contributes to its common name "heterophylla.".
