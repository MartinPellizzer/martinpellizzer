Myrcia sonderiana

Myrcia sonderiana, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and dark green, and its clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Myrcia sonderiana is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
