Melocactus trichacanthus

Melocactus trichacanthus, commonly known as the hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, columnar stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant's unique appearance and ability to thrive in extreme conditions make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
