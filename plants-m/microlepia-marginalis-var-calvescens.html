Microlepia marginalis var. calvescens

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce upright, bipinnate leaves with a distinctive marginal vein pattern.
