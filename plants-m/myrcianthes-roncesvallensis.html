Myrcianthes roncesvallensis

Myrcianthes roncesvallensis, commonly known as the Roncesvalles myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, berry-like drupes that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent and hardy nature.
