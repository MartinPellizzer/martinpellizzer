Mertensia pallida

Mertensia pallida, commonly known as the white heartleaf bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with white or blue at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia pallida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in woodland gardens.
