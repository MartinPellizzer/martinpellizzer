Mutisia popetana

Mutisia popetana, commonly known as the Andean mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, adapted to the harsh mountain climate.
