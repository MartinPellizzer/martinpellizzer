Melastoma furcatum

Melastoma furcatum, commonly known as the forked melastoma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Melastoma furcatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
