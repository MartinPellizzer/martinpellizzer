Myosotis sylvatica var. petraea

Myosotis sylvatica var. petraea, commonly known as the alpine forget-me-not, is a variant of the common forget-me-not that exhibits adaptations to rocky, alpine environments.

It typically features smaller, more compact growth compared to its parent species, with tightly packed leaves and shorter stems. The flowers are usually blue or violet, with five petals and a distinctive central yellow marking, similar to the standard species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in mountainous regions and rock gardens.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to flourish in challenging conditions.
