Melodinus curvinervius var. parvifolius

Melodinus curvinervius var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved melodinus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
