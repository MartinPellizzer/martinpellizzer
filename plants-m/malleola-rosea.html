Malleola rosea

Malleola rosea, commonly known as the pink mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or reddish-purplish bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often surrounded by the striking bracts that attract pollinators.

It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
