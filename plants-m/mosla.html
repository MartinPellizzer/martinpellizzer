Mosla

Mosla, commonly known as goatweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It typically features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that are often blue or purple in color. The plant is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. Mosla species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

They are also popular in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
