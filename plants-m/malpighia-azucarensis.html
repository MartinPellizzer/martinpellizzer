Malpighia azucarensis

Malpighia azucarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or acerola, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces bright red, cherry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. The fruits are typically harvested when fully ripe, and the plant is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
