Myristica mannii

Myristica mannii, commonly known as the African nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain both nutmeg and mace. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has leathery, elliptical leaves that are alternate in arrangement. Its seeds are encased in a thick, reddish aril, which is the source of the spice mace.

The tree is valued for its aromatic compounds and is cultivated for its economic and medicinal properties.
