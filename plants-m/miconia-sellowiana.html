Miconia sellowiana

Miconia sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red. Miconia sellowiana is also noted for its ability to form dense colonies, which can impact local ecosystems.
