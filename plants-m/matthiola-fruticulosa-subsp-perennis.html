Matthiola fruticulosa subsp. perennis

Matthiola fruticulosa subsp.

perennis, commonly known as the perennial wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of showy, four-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

The plant features a branched stem covered in fine, hairy foliage, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
