Masdevallia vittata

Masdevallia vittata, commonly known as the spotted masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically produces small, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette formation. Its flowers are usually borne on terminal inflorescences and can exhibit a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow.

Masdevallia vittata is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and vibrant floral displays.
