Mimulus depressus var. ciminus

Mimulus depressus var. ciminus, commonly known as the narrowleaf monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright orange or yellow, with two lips and five petals, resembling those of the common monkeyflower.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
