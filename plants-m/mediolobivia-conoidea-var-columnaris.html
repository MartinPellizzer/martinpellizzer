Mediolobivia conoidea var. columnaris

Mediolobivia conoidea var. columnaris, commonly known as the columnar cactus, is a unique species characterized by its tall, slender stems that grow in a vertical, column-like form.

It is native to arid regions and thrives in environments with low rainfall and high sunlight exposure. The plant has minimal leaves, which are often reduced to spines, helping to minimize water loss through transpiration. Its flowers, typically yellow or orange, appear at the ends of the stems during the growing season.

This variety is prized for its architectural appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
