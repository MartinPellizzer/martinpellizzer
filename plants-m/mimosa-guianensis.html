Mimosa guianensis

Mimosa guianensis, commonly known as the giant mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces showy, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
