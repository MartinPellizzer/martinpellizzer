Myrciaria leucophloea var. conferta

Myrciaria leucophloea var. conferta, commonly known as the white-flowered myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and often have a resinous scent, while the flowers are clustered and attract various pollinators.

This variety is distinguished by its dense, compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
