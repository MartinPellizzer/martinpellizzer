Mirabilis retrorsa

Mirabilis retrorsa, commonly known as the backward beauty four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white, often opening in the late afternoon. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with a branching stem and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Mirabilis retrorsa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
