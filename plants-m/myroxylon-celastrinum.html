Myroxylon celastrinum

Myroxylon celastrinum, commonly known as the pink wood or red cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown bark and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's wood is highly valued for its durability and rich color, making it a sought-after material in the furniture and construction industries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Myroxylon celastrinum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in various cultural practices.
