Morus patavia

Morus patavia, commonly known as the Patagonian mulberry, is a species of mulberry native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with heart-shaped leaves that are typically large and serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are generally not edible by humans but are consumed by various bird species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
