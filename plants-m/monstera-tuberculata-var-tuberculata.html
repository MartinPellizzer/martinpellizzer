Monstera tuberculata var. tuberculata

Monstera tuberculata var. tuberculata, commonly known as the Swiss cheese plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop distinctive holes and notches as they mature. The plant features aerial roots that help it climb towards light, and its stems can grow quite long, often requiring support. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers warm, humid environments.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its striking appearance.
