Mikania hookeriana

Mikania hookeriana, commonly known as the hooker's vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
