Melica persica subsp. schischkinii

Melica persica subsp.

schischkinii, commonly known as Persian vernal grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows and mountainous regions, preferring temperate climates.

The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, with small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its tolerance to moderate frost.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic distinctiveness within the genus Melica.
