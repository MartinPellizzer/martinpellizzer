Malache luschnathiana

Malache luschnathiana, commonly known as the Luschnathian malache, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its palm-like appearance with a stout, unbranched stem. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating, which helps in reducing water loss.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
