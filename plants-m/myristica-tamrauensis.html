Myristica tamrauensis

Myristica tamrauensis, commonly known as the Tamrau nut or Malabar nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain a valuable nut inside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers and has glossy, elliptical leaves. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their aromatic properties.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.
