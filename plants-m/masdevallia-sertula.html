Masdevallia sertula

Masdevallia sertula, commonly known as the Sertula orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms during the cooler months, showcasing its intricate floral structure with recurved petals and a spurred lip. It prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique and elegant appearance.
