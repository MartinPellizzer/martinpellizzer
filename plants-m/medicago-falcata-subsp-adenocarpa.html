Medicago falcata subsp. adenocarpa

Medicago falcata subsp.

adenocarpa, commonly known as the adenocarpic goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that are hairy and contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically important in soil enrichment.
