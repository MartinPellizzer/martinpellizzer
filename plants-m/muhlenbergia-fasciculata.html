Muhlenbergia fasciculata

Muhlenbergia fasciculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often seen in the fall. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as prairies, meadows, and along roadsides. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Muhlenbergia fasciculata is also important for wildlife, providing food and habitat for various insects and birds.
