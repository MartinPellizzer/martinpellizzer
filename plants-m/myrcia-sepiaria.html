Myrcia sepiaria

Myrcia sepiaria, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia sepiaria is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world, sometimes becoming an invasive species.
