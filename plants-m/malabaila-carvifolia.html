Malabaila carvifolia

Malabaila carvifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that give it a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warm seasons. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
