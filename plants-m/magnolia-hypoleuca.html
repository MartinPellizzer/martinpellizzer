Magnolia hypoleuca

Magnolia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
