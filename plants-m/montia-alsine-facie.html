Montia alsine-facie

Montia alsine-facie, commonly known as the common watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its leaves are edible and have a peppery, slightly bitter flavor, making it a popular ingredient in salads and soups.
