Maihueniopsis boliviana

Maihueniopsis boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian rock cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the high Andes of Bolivia.

It is a small, leafless shrub that thrives in rocky, arid environments at elevations above 4,000 meters. The plant features spiny stems and produces yellow flowers during the short growing season. It has adapted to extreme conditions with a deep root system and the ability to store water efficiently.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
