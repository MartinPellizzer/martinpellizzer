Margaris nudiflora

Margaris nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower margarita, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in a single inflorescence. It has slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

Margaris nudiflora is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.
