Mucuna hexandra

Mucuna hexandra, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. It is widely cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Mucuna hexandra is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
