Malva lindheimeriana

Malva lindheimeriana, commonly known as Lindheimer's mallows, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
