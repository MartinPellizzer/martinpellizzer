Myrceugenia hoehnei

Myrceugenia hoehnei, commonly known as the Hoehne's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

Myrceugenia hoehnei plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
