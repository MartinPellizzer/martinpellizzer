Mattia lanata var. stenophylla

Mattia lanata var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mattia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding a delicate aesthetic to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, arid climate to flourish.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
