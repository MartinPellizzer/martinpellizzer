Microsorum chinense

Microsorum chinense, commonly known as the Chinese water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically divided into small, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and rice fields. Its rhizomes form dense mats, which help stabilize sediment and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Microsorum chinense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to improve water quality.
