Micranthes paludosa

Micranthes paludosa, commonly known as the marsh sandwort, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

Micranthes paludosa plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
