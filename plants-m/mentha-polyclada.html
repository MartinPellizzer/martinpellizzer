Mentha polyclada

Mentha polyclada, commonly known as shiso or Japanese perilla, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its serrated, aromatic leaves that vary in color from bright green to purple. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are often used in Asian cuisine and traditional medicine for their distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

Mentha polyclada thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
