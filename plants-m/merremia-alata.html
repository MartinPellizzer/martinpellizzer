Merremia alata

Merremia alata, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along fences and walls.

Merremia alata is also known for its ability to climb and spread quickly, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed.
