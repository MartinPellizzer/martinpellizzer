Muenteria tomentosa

Muenteria tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly muenteria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

The fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
