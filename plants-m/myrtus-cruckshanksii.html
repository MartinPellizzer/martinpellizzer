Myrtus cruckshanksii

Myrtus cruckshanksii, commonly known as Cruckshanks' myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the island of Madeira, where it grows in coastal and woodland habitats. The plant is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not particularly palatable.

Myrtus cruckshanksii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
