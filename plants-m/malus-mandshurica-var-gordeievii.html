Malus mandshurica var. gordeievii

Malus mandshurica var. gordeievii, commonly known as the Manchurian crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Far East of Russia and parts of China.

It features oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. The fruit is a small, yellow or orange-red apple, often used for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold climates.
