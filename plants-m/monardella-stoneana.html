Monardella stoneana

Monardella stoneana, commonly known as Sierra beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Monardella stoneana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important nectar source for pollinators.
