Malcolmia broussonetii var. longifolia

Malcolmia broussonetii var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf malcolmia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
