Massonia brachypus

Massonia brachypus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved massonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
