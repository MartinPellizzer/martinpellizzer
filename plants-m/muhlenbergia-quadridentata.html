Muhlenbergia quadridentata

Muhlenbergia quadridentata, commonly known as the four-toothed muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear delicate, fluffy seeds, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Muhlenbergia quadridentata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support local wildlife.
