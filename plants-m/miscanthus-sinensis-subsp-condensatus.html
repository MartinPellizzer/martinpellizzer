Miscanthus sinensis subsp. condensatus

Miscanthus sinensis subsp.

condensatus, commonly known as Chinese silver grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia. It features tall, slender stalks that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces fluffy, feathery plumes that are highly ornamental and often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its clumping growth habit and drought tolerance make it a low-maintenance plant for landscaping.
