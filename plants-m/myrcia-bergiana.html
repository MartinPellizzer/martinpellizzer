Myrcia bergiana

Myrcia bergiana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Myrcia bergiana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
