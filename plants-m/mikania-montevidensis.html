Mikania montevidensis

Mikania montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo Mikania, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is known for its rapid growth habit. It produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
