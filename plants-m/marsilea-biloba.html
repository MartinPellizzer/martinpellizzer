Marsilea biloba

Marsilea biloba, commonly known as water clover, is a small aquatic fern belonging to the family Marsileaceae.

It is characterized by its four-leaf clover-like structure, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows in shallow, still water and is often found in ponds, ditches, and wetlands. Its leaves are submerged or floating, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark, spherical fruits.

Marsilea biloba is an important species in aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
