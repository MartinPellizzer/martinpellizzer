Messerschmidia punctata

Messerschmidia punctata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, with distinctive white or yellow spots. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine pubescence.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
