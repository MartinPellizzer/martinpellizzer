Myrtella retusa

Myrtella retusa, commonly known as the smooth myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smooth and leathery in texture. Its small, fragrant white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

Myrtella retusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a history of use in traditional medicine.
