Mentha × verticillata f. statenicensis

Mentha × verticillata f. statenicensis, commonly known as the Staten Island mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the mint genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
