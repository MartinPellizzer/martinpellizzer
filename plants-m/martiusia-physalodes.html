Martiusia physalodes

Martiusia physalodes, commonly known as the red-flowered poinsettia, is a species native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red bracts, which are modified leaves that surround small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate and have a smooth, waxy texture.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
