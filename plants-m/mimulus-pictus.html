Mimulus pictus

Mimulus pictus, commonly known as the painted monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange or red flowers that resemble the face of a monkey, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, adding to its distinctive appearance.

Mimulus pictus is valued in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
