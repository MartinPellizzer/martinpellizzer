Musa rosea

Musa rosea, commonly known as the pink banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to reddish-purple flowers and elongated, slightly curved fruit. The plant belongs to the Musaceae family and is closely related to other banana species. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are often used in ornamental horticulture.

The fruit, while edible, is generally smaller and less commonly consumed compared to other banana varieties.
