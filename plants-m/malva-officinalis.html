Malva officinalis

Malva officinalis, commonly known as mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white, and its leaves are velvety and heart-shaped. The plant is characterized by its thick, fibrous stems and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.

Malva officinalis has been used in traditional medicine for its mucilage-rich leaves, which are known for their soothing properties.
