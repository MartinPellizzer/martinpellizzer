Mentha × brachystachya var. limnogena

Mentha × brachystachya var. limnogena, commonly known as water mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to wetland areas in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and spikes of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and marshes. Its leaves are widely used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

The variety limnogena is distinguished by its shorter floral stems compared to other Mentha species.
