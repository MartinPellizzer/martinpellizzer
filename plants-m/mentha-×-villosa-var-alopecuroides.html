Mentha × villosa var. alopecuroides

Mentha × villosa var. alopecuroides, commonly known as woolly mint or hairy mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves and fragrant, often purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is occasionally used in herbal remedies and as a decorative plant in gardens.
