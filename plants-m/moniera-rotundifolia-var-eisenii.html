Moniera rotundifolia var. eisenii

Moniera rotundifolia var. eisenii, commonly known as the Eisen's roundleaf fern, is a variant of the roundleaf fern characterized by its distinctive, rounded leaves.

It is a perennial plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features fronds that are densely packed and often have a compact, bushy appearance. Its botanical significance lies in its unique leaf morphology and its role in local ecosystems.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
