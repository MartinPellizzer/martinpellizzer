Miconia microdictya

Miconia microdictya, commonly known as the small-leaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by clustered, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
