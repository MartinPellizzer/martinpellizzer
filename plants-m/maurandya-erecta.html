Maurandya erecta

Maurandya erecta, commonly known as the upright maurandya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Maurandya erecta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
