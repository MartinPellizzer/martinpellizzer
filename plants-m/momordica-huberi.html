Momordica huberi

Momordica huberi, commonly known as the African horned melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive warty, spiky fruit that resembles a horned melon, with a thick, green rind and a sweet, musky interior. The plant produces yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a deep orange or red, indicating maturity.
