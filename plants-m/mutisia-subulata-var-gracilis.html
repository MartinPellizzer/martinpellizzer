Mutisia subulata var. gracilis

Mutisia subulata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is known for its small, narrow leaves and yellowish-white flowers. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
