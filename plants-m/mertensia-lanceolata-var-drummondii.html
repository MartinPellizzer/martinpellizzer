Mertensia lanceolata var. drummondii

Mertensia lanceolata var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of sky-blue, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a versatile addition to native plant gardens.
