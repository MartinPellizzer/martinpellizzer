Myrcia petrophila

Myrcia petrophila, commonly known as the rock myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of eastern Australia, where it typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains numerous small, brown seeds.

Myrcia petrophila is often found on rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.
