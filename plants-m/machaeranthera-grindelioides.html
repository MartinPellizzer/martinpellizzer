Machaeranthera grindelioides

Machaeranthera grindelioides, commonly known as the Texas blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, showing adaptability to arid conditions. It features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers in the summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
