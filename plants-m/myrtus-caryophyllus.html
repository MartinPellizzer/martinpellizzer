Myrtus caryophyllus

Myrtus caryophyllus, commonly known as sweet bay or bay leaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are aromatic and used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by blue-black berries. Its leaves contain essential oils rich in compounds like eucalyptol and cineole, contributing to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Myrtus caryophyllus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for both ornamental and practical use.
