Melothria cordata

Melothria cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf false cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, green fruits that turn orange when mature, resembling tiny cucumbers in appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbit plants.
