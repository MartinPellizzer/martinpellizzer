Maxillaria sophronitis

Maxillaria sophronitis, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble a slipper, with a pinkish-white coloration and a prominent lip. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, with multiple stems arising from a rhizome, and it prefers warm, humid environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Maxillaria sophronitis is often cultivated for its beauty and is relatively easy to care for in the right growing conditions.
