Malus communis var. reneta

Malus communis var. reneta, commonly known as the Reneta apple, is a cultivar of the domesticated apple tree.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and aromatic fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with dense foliage and produces flowers in early spring. Its botanical features include alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a shallow root system.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in certain regions.
