Medicago minima var. pulchella

Medicago minima var. pulchella, commonly known as the slender clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, trifoliate leaves that are typically bright green, and its flowers are usually pink or red, arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.

Additionally, it is sometimes used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
