Malvastrum bicuspidatum subsp. campanulatum

Malvastrum bicuspidatum subsp.

campanulatum, commonly known as the blueweed or blue thistle, is a flowering plant in the Malvaceae family. It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems.

The plant has soft, hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and it produces small, spiky seed capsules. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas or disturbed soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
