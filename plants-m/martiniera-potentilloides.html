Martiniera potentilloides

Martiniera potentilloides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has pinnate leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, spherical fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in tropical regions.
