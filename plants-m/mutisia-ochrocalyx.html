Mutisia ochrocalyx

Mutisia ochrocalyx, commonly known as the yellow calyx mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a woody stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is the bright yellow calyx that surrounds the central flower head, which is composed of numerous tiny florets.

Mutisia ochrocalyx is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
