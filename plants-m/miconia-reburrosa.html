Miconia reburrosa

Miconia reburrosa, commonly known as the Mexican miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a waxy surface. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract various pollinators such as birds and insects. Miconia reburrosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments, making it a common sight in tropical rainforests.

However, it can also become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
