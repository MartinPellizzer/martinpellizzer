Memecylon edule var. petiolatum

Memecylon edule var. petiolatum, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into round, yellow fruits with a thick, astringent skin. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is widely used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
