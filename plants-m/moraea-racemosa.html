Moraea racemosa

Moraea racemosa, commonly known as the clustered moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces striking, often violet or blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is appreciated in horticulture and conservation efforts.
