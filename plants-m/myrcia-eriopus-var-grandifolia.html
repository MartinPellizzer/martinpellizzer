Myrcia eriopus var. grandifolia

Myrcia eriopus var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered myrcia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces dark purple, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a strong, spicy flavor.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
