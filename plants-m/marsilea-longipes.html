Marsilea longipes

Marsilea longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed water clover, is a aquatic fern native to parts of Asia.

It has small, four-leaf clover-like fronds that float on the water surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its spores are released into the water, allowing for propagation.

The leaves are typically green and have a delicate, lace-like appearance.
