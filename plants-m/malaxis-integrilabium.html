Malaxis integrilabium

Malaxis integrilabium, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure, with long, slender petals and a prominent, spurred lip.
