Mollugo costata

Mollugo costata, commonly known as the coastal sandplant, is a low-growing annual herb native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and beach habitats. The plant has small, fleshy leaves and produces yellow flowers with four petals. It is adapted to salt spray and high winds, making it a hardy species in harsh coastal environments.

Mollugo costata plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
