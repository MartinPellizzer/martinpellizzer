Maxillaria fasciculata

Maxillaria fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids.

The flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a notable plant for orchid enthusiasts.
