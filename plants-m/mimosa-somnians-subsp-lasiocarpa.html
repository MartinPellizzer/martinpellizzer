Mimosa somnians subsp. lasiocarpa

Mimosa somnians subsp.

lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The subspecies is notable for its unique leaf structure and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
