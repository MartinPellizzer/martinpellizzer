Multidentia sclerocarpa

Multidentia sclerocarpa, commonly known as the hard-shelled mulletwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it well adapted to dry and disturbed environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine and for its durable wood.
