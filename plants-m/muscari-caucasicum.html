Muscari caucasicum

Muscari caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian musk grape, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or violet. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Muscari caucasicum is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its fragrant, musky scent.
