Megalastrum rhachisquamatum

Megalastrum rhachisquamatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved star-leaf, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that often have a waxy texture and a striking, star-like pattern on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest for botanists studying tropical flora.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected areas.
