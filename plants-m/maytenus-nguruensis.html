Maytenus nguruensis

Maytenus nguruensis, commonly known as the Nguru mayten, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the family Saluraceae and is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and dense canopy of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval-shaped, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Maytenus nguruensis plays a vital ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
