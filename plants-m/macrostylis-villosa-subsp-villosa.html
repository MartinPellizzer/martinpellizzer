Macrostylis villosa subsp. villosa

Macrostylis villosa subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the silky macrostylis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in grassland and coastal habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its distinctive foliage and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
