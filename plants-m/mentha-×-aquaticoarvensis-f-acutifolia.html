Mentha × aquaticoarvensis f. acutifolia

Mentha × aquaticoarvensis f. acutifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved water mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. This subspecies is typically found in wetland environments, such as marshes and stream banks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering stems produce small, pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a natural mosquito repellent due to its aromatic properties.
