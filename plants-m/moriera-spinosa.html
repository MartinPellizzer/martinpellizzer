Moriera spinosa

Moriera spinosa, commonly known as the spiny moringa, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in sharp, spiny projections. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and are often used in traditional medicine.

Moriera spinosa is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various arid environments.
