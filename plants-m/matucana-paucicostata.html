Matucana paucicostata

Matucana paucicostata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its water-storing tissues. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife.

Matucana paucicostata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
