Mesembryanthemum fimbriatum

Mesembryanthemum fimbriatum, commonly known as the sawtooth iceplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Mesembryanthemum fimbriatum is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
