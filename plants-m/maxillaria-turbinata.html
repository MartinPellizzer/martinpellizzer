Maxillaria turbinata

Maxillaria turbinata, commonly known as the Turban Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, often colorful, and showy flowers that resemble a turban, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted for storing water in its native humid environments.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their intricate patterns and vibrant hues, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
