Mentha longifolia var. szenezyana

Mentha longifolia var. szenezyana, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in terminal spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in traditional herbal medicine and culinary applications.
