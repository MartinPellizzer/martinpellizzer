Malus praecox var. paradisiaca

Malus praecox var. paradisiaca, commonly known as the early apple or paradise apple, is a variety of the apple tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and aromatic fruits that ripen early in the growing season. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree with alternate, simple, and serrated leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

This variety is notable for its historical significance in the domestication of apples.
