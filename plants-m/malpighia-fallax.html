Malpighia fallax

Malpighia fallax, commonly known as the wax apple or granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
