Melica minuta var. latifolia

Melica minuta var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and panicle-like inflorescence that bears small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
