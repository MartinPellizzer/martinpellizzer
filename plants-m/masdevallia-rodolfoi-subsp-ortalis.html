Masdevallia rodolfoi subsp. ortalis

Masdevallia rodolfoi subsp.

ortalis, commonly known as the Orla's Masdevallia, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the cloud forests of Colombia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, elongated flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow.

The plant typically produces a single, showy inflorescence with several blooms, making it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts. Its foliage consists of narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging and highly sought-after orchid for cultivation.
