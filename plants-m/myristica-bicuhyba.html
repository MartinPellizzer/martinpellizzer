Myristica bicuhyba

Myristica bicuhyba, commonly known as the two-seeded myristica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant seeds and oily exuding bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody fruits containing two seeds. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

The tree is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
