Miconia loreyoides

Miconia loreyoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in certain regions.
