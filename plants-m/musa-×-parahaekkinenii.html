Musa × parahaekkinenii

Musa × parahaekkinenii, commonly known as the hybrid banana plant, is a member of the Musaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Musa acuminata and Musa balbisiana, making it a triploid species. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces clusters of elongated, fleshy fruits that are typically not edible due to high levels of starch. It is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value rather than for food, though some varieties may have limited culinary use.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
