Minuartia geniculata var. fontqueri

Minuartia geniculata var. fontqueri, commonly known as the Sierra stonecress, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as gravelly slopes and crevices.

The species is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
