Mimulus torreyi

Mimulus torreyi, commonly known as the Torrey's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange or yellow tubular flowers, which are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus torreyi is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in regions with high humidity.
