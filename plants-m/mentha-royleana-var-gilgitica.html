Mentha royleana var. gilgitica

Mentha royleana var. gilgitica, commonly known as Gilgit mint, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

The species is valued for its essential oils, which contribute to its distinct minty fragrance.
