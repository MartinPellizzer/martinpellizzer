Melittis carpatica var. subcordata

Melittis carpatica var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved melittis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and slightly serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.
