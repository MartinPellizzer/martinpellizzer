Muraltia mixta

Muraltia mixta, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or white muraltia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant can grow as a shrub or small tree.

Muraltia mixta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
