Meoschium triticeum

Meoschium triticeum, commonly known as the common meadow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers, typical of grasses. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and wetlands.

Meoschium triticeum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
