Medicago littoralis var. dextrorsa

Medicago littoralis var. dextrorsa, commonly known as the coastal vetch, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found along shorelines. It has adapted to saline environments and is drought-tolerant.

Its growth habit makes it an important component of coastal vegetation.
