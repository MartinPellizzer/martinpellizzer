Memorialis integrifolia

Memorialis integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf memorial plant, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, smooth, and often glossy leaves that are typically entire, without teeth or notches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
