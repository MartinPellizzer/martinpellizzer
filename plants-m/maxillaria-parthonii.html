Maxillaria parthonii

Maxillaria parthonii, commonly known as the Parthon's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria parthonii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
