Myristica aromatica

Myristica aromatica, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It produces a unique fruit that contains both the aromatic nutmeg seed and the mace, a red, lacy aril. The plant belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant flowers. Its essential oils are rich in compounds like myristicin and elemicin, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its valuable spices.
