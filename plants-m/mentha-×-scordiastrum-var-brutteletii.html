Mentha × scordiastrum var. brutteletii

Mentha × scordiastrum var. brutteletii, commonly known as the Italian mentha or wild mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments. The plant typically features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
