Mentha × melissoides

Mentha × melissoides, commonly known as honey mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha viridis and Mentha suaveolens.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its aromatic leaves emit a sweet, minty scent, making it popular in culinary and medicinal applications.

Mentha × melissoides is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental and aromatic qualities.
