Microchilus roseoalbus

Microchilus roseoalbus, commonly known as the pink-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It possesses small, scale-like leaves and produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume its berries.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions where its host trees are abundant.
