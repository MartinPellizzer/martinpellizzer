Machaeranthera asteroides

Machaeranthera asteroides, commonly known as the starry blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features showy, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Machaeranthera asteroides thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
