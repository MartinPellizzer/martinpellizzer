Melilotus lupulinus

Melilotus lupulinus, commonly known as yellow melilot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Melilotus lupulinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
