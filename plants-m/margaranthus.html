Margaranthus

Margaranthus, commonly known as the honeycomb plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that often resemble small honeycombs, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Margaranthus species are popular in ornamental gardening due to their vibrant colors and unique floral structure.
