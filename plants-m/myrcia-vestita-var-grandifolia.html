Myrcia vestita var. grandifolia

Myrcia vestita var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in subtropical regions.
