Miconia salicifolia

Miconia salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia salicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
