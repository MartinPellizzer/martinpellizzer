Microchloa altera var. nelsonii

Microchloa altera var. nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's microchloa, is a rare and specialized grass species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, delicate foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or shaded terrain. This variant exhibits unique morphological traits, including narrow leaves and a low-growing structure, which aid in its survival in nutrient-poor soils. It is adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in areas with high rainfall and partial shade.
