Mesembryanthemum glabrum

Mesembryanthemum glabrum, commonly known as the pale leaf ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to red. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in dry conditions.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
