Mesembryanthemum biforme

Mesembryanthemum biforme, commonly known as the two-form ice plant, is a succulent flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique adaptations allow it to survive in arid environments by storing water in its leaves and stems.
