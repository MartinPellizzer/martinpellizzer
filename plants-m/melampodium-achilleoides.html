Melampodium achilleoides

Melampodium achilleoides, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides.

Melampodium achilleoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
