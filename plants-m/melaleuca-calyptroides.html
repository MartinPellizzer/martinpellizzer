Melaleuca calyptroides

Melaleuca calyptroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in winter. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Melaleuca calyptroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique floral display.
