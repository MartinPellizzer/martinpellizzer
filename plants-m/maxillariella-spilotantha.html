Maxillariella spilotantha

Maxillariella spilotantha, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores water, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically pink or purple with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Maxillariella spilotantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
