Mammillaria xaltianguensis subsp. bambusiphila

Mammillaria xaltianguensis subsp.

bambusiphila, commonly known as the Bamboo-leafed Mammillaria, is a small cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems and clusters of white, tubular flowers.

The plant has a compact growth habit with spines that are often reddish in color. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
