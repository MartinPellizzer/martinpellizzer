Myrcia eriocalyx var. beyrichiana

Myrcia eriocalyx var. beyrichiana, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Myrtaceae family.
