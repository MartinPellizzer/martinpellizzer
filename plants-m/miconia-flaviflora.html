Miconia flaviflora

Miconia flaviflora, commonly known as the golden-flowered miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Miconia flaviflora is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
