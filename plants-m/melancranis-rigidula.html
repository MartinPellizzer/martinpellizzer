Melancranis rigidula

Melancranis rigidula, commonly known as the rigid spikeless grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often hidden among the foliage. It is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to withstand drought conditions.

Melancranis rigidula is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
