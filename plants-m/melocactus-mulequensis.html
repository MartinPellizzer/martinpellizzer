Melocactus mulequensis

Melocactus mulequensis, commonly known as the Muleque melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is typically green or reddish in color, often adorned with spines and a distinctive crown of tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, usually in the spring or early summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Melocactus mulequensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in succulent gardens.
