Mespilus × lobata

Mespilus × lobata, commonly known as the hybrid white medlar, is a deciduous tree that results from the cross between Mespilus germanica and Mespilus bacata.

It features ovate to elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by unique, fleshy fruits that mature in late summer. The fruits have a complex flavor and are often used in culinary applications or fermented into beverages.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
