Mammillaria tamayonis

Mammillaria tamayonis, commonly known as the Tamayo's nipple cactus, is a small, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a dense covering of white, woolly spines and is characterized by its rounded, segmented stem. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
