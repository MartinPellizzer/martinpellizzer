Montejacquia divisa

Montejacquia divisa, commonly known as the split-leaf cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, segmented stems that are often split or divided, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the wild.
