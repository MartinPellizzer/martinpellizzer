Mesembryanthemum sabulosum

Mesembryanthemum sabulosum, commonly known as the sand mat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing succulent that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming during the cooler months. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

Mesembryanthemum sabulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
