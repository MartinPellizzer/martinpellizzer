Myrcia caesariata

Myrcia caesariata, commonly known as the blue myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Myrcia caesariata is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
