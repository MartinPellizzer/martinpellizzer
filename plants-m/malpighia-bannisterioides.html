Malpighia bannisterioides

Malpighia bannisterioides, commonly known as the Bannister's granadilla, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. Its flowers are typically white or pink and are arranged in clusters.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and as a source of natural food coloring.
