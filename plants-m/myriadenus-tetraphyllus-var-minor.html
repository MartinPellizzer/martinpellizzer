Myriadenus tetraphyllus var. minor

Myriadenus tetraphyllus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser four-leafed sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
