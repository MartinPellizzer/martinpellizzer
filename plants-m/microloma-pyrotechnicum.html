Microloma pyrotechnicum

Microloma pyrotechnicum, commonly known as the firework plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vivid, colorful bracts that resemble fireworks, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are borne on erect, branching stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display.
