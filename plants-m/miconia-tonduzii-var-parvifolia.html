Miconia tonduzii var. parvifolia

Miconia tonduzii var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are often followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
