Mimulus glabratus var. oklahomensis

Mimulus glabratus var. oklahomensis, commonly known as the Oklahoma monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. It has smooth, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in the southeastern states.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
