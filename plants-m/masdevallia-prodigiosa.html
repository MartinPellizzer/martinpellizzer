Masdevallia prodigiosa

Masdevallia prodigiosa, commonly known as the "Prodigal Masdevallia," is a striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often multicolored flowers that display a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, high-altitude environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Masdevallia prodigiosa is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively compact size.
