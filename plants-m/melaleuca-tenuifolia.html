Melaleuca tenuifolia

Melaleuca tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant, honey-scented flowers that bloom in winter. The bark of this plant is fibrous and peels off in thin layers, giving it a papery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Melaleuca tenuifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability.
