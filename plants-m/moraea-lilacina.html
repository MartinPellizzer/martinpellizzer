Moraea lilacina

Moraea lilacina, commonly known as the lilac orchid, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, orchid-like flowers. The plant produces clusters of pale purple to lilac-colored blooms that are highly ornamental. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Moraea lilacina is often cultivated for its beauty and is a popular choice in gardens and botanical collections.
