Manglietia crassipes

Manglietia crassipes, commonly known as the Brazilian manglietia, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a member of the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pink in color. The plant has a thick, woody stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. It is known for its fragrant blooms and is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for various wildlife species.
