Matthiola boissieri

Matthiola boissieri, commonly known as the Armenian stock, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, arranged in clusters. It is often found in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soil.

Matthiola boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
