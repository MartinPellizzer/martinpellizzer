Mimosa cornigera

Mimosa cornigera, commonly known as the velvetleaf mimosa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Mimosa cornigera is sometimes grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
