Myrtus vidalii

Myrtus vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Myrtus vidalii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
