Mammillaria albicoma

Mammillaria albicoma, commonly known as the white-haired mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique features, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in collections of cacti and succulents.
