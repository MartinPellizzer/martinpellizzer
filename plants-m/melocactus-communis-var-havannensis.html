Melocactus communis var. havannensis

Melocactus communis var. havannensis, commonly known as the Havanna melocactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, white spines and a crown of small, rounded areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in late spring or early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to survive.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
