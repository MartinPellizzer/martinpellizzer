Masdevallia albella

Masdevallia albella, commonly known as the white masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a miniature orchid, with a compact rosette of leaves and a single, showy flower. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Masdevallia albella is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
