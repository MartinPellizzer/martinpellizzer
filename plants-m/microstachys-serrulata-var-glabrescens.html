Microstachys serrulata var. glabrescens

Microstachys serrulata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are generally smooth to the touch. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its foliage and floral structure make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.
