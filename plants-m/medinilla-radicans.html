Medinilla radicans

Medinilla radicans, commonly known as the hanging medinilla, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its leaves are typically glossy and ovate, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Medinilla radicans is often cultivated as a houseplant or in hanging baskets due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indirect light.
