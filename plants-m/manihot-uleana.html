Manihot uleana

Manihot uleana, commonly known as arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers and is cultivated for its starchy, edible roots. Arrowroot is valued for its high starch content and is used in various culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
