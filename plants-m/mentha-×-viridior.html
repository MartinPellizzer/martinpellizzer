Mentha × viridior

Mentha × viridior, commonly known as green mint, is a hybrid mint plant resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata and Mentha aquatica.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers and has a strong, refreshing aroma similar to other mints. Its leaves are typically broad and oval, with a slightly serrated edge.

Mentha × viridior is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its aromatic and therapeutic properties.
