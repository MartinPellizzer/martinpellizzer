Matthiola macrocarpa

Matthiola macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, round seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
