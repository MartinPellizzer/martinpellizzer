Macaranga barteri

Macaranga barteri, commonly known as the barter's paper plant, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
