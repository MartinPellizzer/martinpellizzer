Malus communis var. austera

Malus communis var. austera, commonly known as the Austrian apple, is a variety of the common apple tree native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive form and seasonal interest. The leaves are typically oval with a smooth margin, and the tree produces small, sweet, edible fruits. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is also valued for its resistance to certain diseases compared to other apple cultivars.
