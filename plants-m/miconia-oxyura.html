Miconia oxyura

Miconia oxyura, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia oxyura is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tropical ecosystems, though its invasive potential requires careful management.
