Malus cylindracea

Malus cylindracea, commonly known as the cylindrical apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by cylindrical, red or yellow fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Malus cylindracea is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
