Modecca parvifolia

Modecca parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved modecca, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. Modecca parvifolia thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
