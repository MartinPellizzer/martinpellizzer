Mariscus cylindristachyus

Mariscus cylindristachyus, commonly known as the cylindrical-stalked mariscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
