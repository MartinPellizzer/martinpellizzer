Melastoma theaezans

Melastoma theaezans, commonly known as the Philippine blueberry, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy appearance, while its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
