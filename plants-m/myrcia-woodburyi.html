Myrcia woodburyi

Myrcia woodburyi, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Myrcia woodburyi is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
