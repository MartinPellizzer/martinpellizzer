Mentha viridis subsp. exquisita

Mentha viridis subsp.

exquisita, commonly known as the slender mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and highly aromatic, often fragrant, leaves that emit a strong mint scent.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in spikes during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
