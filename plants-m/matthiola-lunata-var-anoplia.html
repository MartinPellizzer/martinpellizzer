Matthiola lunata var. anoplia

Matthiola lunata var. anoplia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale purple flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round, green fruits known as siliques, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The botanical features of this variety make it both ornamental and useful in ecological gardening.
