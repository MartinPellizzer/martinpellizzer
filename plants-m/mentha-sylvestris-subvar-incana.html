Mentha sylvestris subvar. incana

Mentha sylvestris subvar. incana, commonly known as the hairy mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
