Mercurialis annua var. dioica

Mercurialis annua var. dioica, commonly known as the common mercuryplant, is a flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is a dioecious species, meaning that male and female flowers are found on separate plants. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in arable fields and along roadsides. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is also known for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as mercury compounds, which can be harmful to humans and animals.
