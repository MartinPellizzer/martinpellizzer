Megalastrum longipilosum

Megalastrum longipilosum, commonly known as the silky leaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its unique texture and resilience in various growing conditions.
