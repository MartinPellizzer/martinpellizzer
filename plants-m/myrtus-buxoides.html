Myrtus buxoides

Myrtus buxoides, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are densely packed, giving it a dense, rounded appearance. The plant produces tiny, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. Myrtus buxoides is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in ornamental gardening.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
