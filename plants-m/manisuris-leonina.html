Manisuris leonina

Manisuris leonina, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a short, upright stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery hair. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers bloom during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
