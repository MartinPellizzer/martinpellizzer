Malacothamnus fremontii

Malacothamnus fremontii, commonly known as Fremont's thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and scrub habitats. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and an elongated flowering stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
