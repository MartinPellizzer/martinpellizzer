Masdevallia fenestrata

Masdevallia fenestrata, commonly known as the window orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, anchoring itself with its aerial roots and absorbing moisture from the air. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
