Moniera grandiflora

Moniera grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora fern, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Monieraceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, delicate, palmately lobed leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for both landscape design and botanical collections.
