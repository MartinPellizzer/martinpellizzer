Miconia longipedunculata

Miconia longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncled miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia longipedunculata is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it a potential invasive species in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory in humid tropical forests.
