Moraea calcicola

Moraea calcicola, commonly known as the limestone moraea, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, colorful flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in calcareous soils, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to arid conditions.

Moraea calcicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
