Manilkara excelsa

Manilkara excelsa, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and produces a fruit that is a staple food in many island cultures. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its fruit, which resembles a small bread loaf, is rich in carbohydrates and is typically cooked or baked.

The tree also has a significant role in traditional medicine and is valued for its timber and latex.
