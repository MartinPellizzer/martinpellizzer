Mentha szilyana

Mentha szilyana, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated margin and emit a strong minty fragrance.

Mentha szilyana is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
