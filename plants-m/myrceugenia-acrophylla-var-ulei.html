Myrceugenia acrophylla var. ulei

Myrceugenia acrophylla var. ulei, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its small, white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is adapted to coastal environments and exhibits salt tolerance, making it a characteristic component of coastal vegetation.
