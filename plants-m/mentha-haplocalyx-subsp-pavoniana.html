Mentha haplocalyx subsp. pavoniana

Mentha haplocalyx subsp.

pavoniana, commonly known as the purple mint, is a perennial herb native to East Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This subspecies is valued in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb for its refreshing scent and flavor.
