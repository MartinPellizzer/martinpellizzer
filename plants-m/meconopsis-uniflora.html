Meconopsis uniflora

Meconopsis uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower monkshood, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers that resemble a hood, giving it its common name. The plant produces large, showy blooms that typically appear in late summer, often in rocky or meadow habitats. Its leaves are palmately lobed and can be quite large, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

Meconopsis uniflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
