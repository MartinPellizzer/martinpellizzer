Myrica microstachya

Myrica microstachya, commonly known as the dwarf wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, wax-coated berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Myrica microstachya is also used in traditional medicine by some Native American tribes.
