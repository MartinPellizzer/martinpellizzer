Mikania cordata

Mikania cordata, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple tinge on the veins. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the morning. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, often becoming a weed in agricultural areas.

Mikania cordata is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments and can tolerate a variety of soil types.
