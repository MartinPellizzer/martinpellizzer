Mesosphaerum brasiliense

Mesosphaerum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which resembles a starfruit, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
