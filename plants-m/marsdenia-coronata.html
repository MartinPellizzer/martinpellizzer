Marsdenia coronata

Marsdenia coronata, commonly known as the crowned Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, known for its distinctive floral structures. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, often surrounded by a crown-like structure.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
