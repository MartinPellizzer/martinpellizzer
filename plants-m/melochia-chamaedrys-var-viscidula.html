Melochia chamaedrys var. viscidula

Melochia chamaedrys var. viscidula, commonly known as the sticky melochia, is a variant of the shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and hairy, often sticky leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, hairy fruits. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough, textured surface.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
