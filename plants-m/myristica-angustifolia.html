Myristica angustifolia

Myristica angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain the spice nutmeg. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has dark green, glossy leaves with a narrow, lance-shaped form. Its seeds, known as mace, are used as a spice and are closely related to the nutmeg kernel.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic products.
