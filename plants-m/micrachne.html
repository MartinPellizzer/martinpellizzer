Micrachne

Micrachne, commonly known as the sandmat, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Micrachne species are native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

They are adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in harsh, drought-prone environments.
