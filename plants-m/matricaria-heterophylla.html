Matricaria heterophylla

Matricaria heterophylla, commonly known as German chamomile, is a flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves and produces small, yellow flower heads that resemble daisies. Its flowers are rich in essential oils, particularly chamazulene, which contributes to its medicinal properties.

Matricaria heterophylla is widely cultivated for use in herbal remedies and as a calming tea.
