Mimulus gratioloides

Mimulus gratioloides, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are typically spurred at the back, a feature common in the genus Mimulus. The plant grows from a basal rosette of leaves and produces elongated stems that bear the showy flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and meadows.

Mimulus gratioloides is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
