Mallotus repandus var. scabrifolius

Mallotus repandus var. scabrifolius, commonly known as the rough-leaved mallot, is a variant of the mallot tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its rough, serrated leaves. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a leathery texture and a distinctive, rugged surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

This plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.
