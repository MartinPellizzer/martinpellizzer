Marianthus floribundus

Marianthus floribundus, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers with red veins, which bloom in clusters during the warm seasons. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are narrow and elongated, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to produce its vibrant blooms.

Marianthus floribundus is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
