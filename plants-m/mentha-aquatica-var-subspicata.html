Mentha aquatica var. subspicata

Mentha aquatica var. subspicata, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

The species is closely related to other mint species and is known for its strong, refreshing scent.
