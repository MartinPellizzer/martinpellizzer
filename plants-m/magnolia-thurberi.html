Magnolia thurberi

Magnolia thurberi, commonly known as the Thurber magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blossoms.
