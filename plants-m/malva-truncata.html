Malva truncata

Malva truncata, commonly known as the narrowleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in soft, hairy foliage. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a distinctive truncate (notched) base. It produces showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
