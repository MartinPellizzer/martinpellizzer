Mentha longifolia subsp. viridis

Mentha longifolia subsp.

viridis, commonly known as green mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This subspecies is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its aromatic leaves and mild menthol content.
