Matthiola longipetala subsp. kralikii

Matthiola longipetala subsp.

kralikii, commonly known as the Kralik's stock, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and lilac, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and pleasant scent.
