Malpighia mucronata subsp. insulae-pinorum

Malpighia mucronata subsp.

insulae-pinorum, commonly known as the insular starfruit, is a small shrub native to the Caribbean islands. It produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor.

The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and limestone soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
