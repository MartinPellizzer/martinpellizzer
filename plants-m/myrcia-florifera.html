Myrcia florifera

Myrcia florifera, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months.

Myrcia florifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
