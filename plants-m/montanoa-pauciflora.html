Montanoa pauciflora

Montanoa pauciflora, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the milkweed family, Apocynaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Montanoa pauciflora is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic to livestock and humans if ingested.
