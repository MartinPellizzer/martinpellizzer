Myginda rotundata

Myginda rotundata, commonly known as the roundleaf myginda, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive round leaves and compact growth habit.

It belongs to the family Mygindaceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive without direct sunlight. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps in retaining moisture in humid conditions.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered together, and contribute to its role in local ecosystems.
