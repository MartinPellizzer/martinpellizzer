Malacothamnus orbiculatus subsp. clementinus

Malacothamnus orbiculatus subsp.

clementinus, commonly known as the Clemens' globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in compact, globe-like inflorescences, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
