Miconia vincentina

Miconia vincentina, commonly known as Vincent's miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Miconia vincentina is noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an adaptable species in its native habitat.
