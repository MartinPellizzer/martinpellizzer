Marica imbricata

Marica imbricata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Marica imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
