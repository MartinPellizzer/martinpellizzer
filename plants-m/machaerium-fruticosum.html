Machaerium fruticosum

Machaerium fruticosum, commonly known as the wild senna, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Machaerium fruticosum is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
