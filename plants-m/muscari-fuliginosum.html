Muscari fuliginosum

Muscari fuliginosum, commonly known as the smoky muscari, is a species of bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep purple. The plant produces a dense, grass-like foliage that emerges in early spring, followed by the flowering period in late spring to early summer. Muscari fuliginosum thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens and rockeries.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
