Myrciaria apiculata

Myrciaria apiculata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are edible and often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and white with a subtle fragrance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
