Melaleuca pulchella

Melaleuca pulchella, commonly known as the pretty melaleuca, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces showy clusters of white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Melaleuca pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
