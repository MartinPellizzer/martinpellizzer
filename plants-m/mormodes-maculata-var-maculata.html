Mormodes maculata var. maculata

Mormodes maculata var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers with a distinctive purple or brown marking. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. It has a sympodial growth habit, with new shoots emerging from the base of the plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
