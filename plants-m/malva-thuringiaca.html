Malva thuringiaca

Malva thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian mallows, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Malva thuringiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
