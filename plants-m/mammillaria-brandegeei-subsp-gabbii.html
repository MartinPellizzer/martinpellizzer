Mammillaria brandegeei subsp. gabbii

Mammillaria brandegeei subsp.

gabbii, commonly known as the Gabb's mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white to pale green tubercles.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
