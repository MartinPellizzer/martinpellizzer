Melica violacea var. commersonii

Melica violacea var. commersonii, commonly known as the violet false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and delicate, violet-blue inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This variety is often noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
