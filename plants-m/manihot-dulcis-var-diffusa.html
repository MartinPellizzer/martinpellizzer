Manihot dulcis var. diffusa

Manihot dulcis var. diffusa, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub that produces edible roots and tubers, which are rich in starch and used in various culinary applications. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow up to 30 cm in length, while the stems are thick and woody.

The tubers, which are the primary edible part, are typically harvested when they are fully mature and have a smooth, pale yellow skin.
