Machilus longipedicellatus

Machilus longipedicellatus, commonly known as the long-fruited machilus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elliptical berries that are typically green when mature and turn dark when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
