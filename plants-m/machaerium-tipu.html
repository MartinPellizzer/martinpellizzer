Machaerium tipu

Machaerium tipu, commonly known as the Indian broom, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, disturbed habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen make it ecologically significant in certain ecosystems.
