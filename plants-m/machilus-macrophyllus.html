Machilus macrophyllus

Machilus macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf machilus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and appear in clusters.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.
