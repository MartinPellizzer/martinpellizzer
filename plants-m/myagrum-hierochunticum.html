Myagrum hierochunticum

Myagrum hierochunticum, commonly known as the African wild tea, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
