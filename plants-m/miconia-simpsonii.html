Miconia simpsonii

Miconia simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and can grow up to several meters in height.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
