Musa acuminata var. tomentosa

Musa acuminata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly banana, is a variety of the banana plant characterized by its hairy, fuzzy leaves and stems.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and warm temperatures. The plant produces large, elongated banana bunches that are typically smaller than those of common commercial bananas. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The fruit is edible but less commonly cultivated for commercial purposes compared to other banana varieties.
