Megathyrsus

Megathyrsus, commonly known as the grass genus that includes several species such as *Megathyrsus maximus*, is a group of tall, perennial grasses native to tropical and subtropical regions.

These plants typically have erect, cylindrical stems and are characterized by their large, narrow leaves with prominent veins. They are often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, where they thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The genus is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Some species are also cultivated for their ornamental value or as forage crops.
