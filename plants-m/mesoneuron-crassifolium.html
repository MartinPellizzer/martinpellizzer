Mesoneuron crassifolium

Mesoneuron crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf mesoneuron, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, which are usually white or pale yellow. Its roots are fibrous and capable of storing significant amounts of water, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Mesoneuron crassifolium is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and resilience to mild frosts.
