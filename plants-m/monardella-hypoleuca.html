Monardella hypoleuca

Monardella hypoleuca, commonly known as white mountain betony, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Monardella hypoleuca is often found in high elevation areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
