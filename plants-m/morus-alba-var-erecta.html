Morus alba var. erecta

Morus alba var. erecta, commonly known as the erect mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often cultivated for its edible fruits. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called mulberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental and agricultural uses.

The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a silvery underside.
