Melochia umbellata

Melochia umbellata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into distinctive, spiny, capsule-like fruits. It is native to Africa, the Caribbean, and parts of South America, and is commonly found in disturbed habitats.

Melochia umbellata is known for its rapid growth and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
