Mitella stylosa var. makinoi

Mitella stylosa var. makinoi, commonly known as the Makino's toothwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the forests of eastern Asia, particularly Japan.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, tooth-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
