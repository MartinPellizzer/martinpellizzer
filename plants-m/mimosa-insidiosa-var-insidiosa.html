Mimosa insidiosa var. insidiosa

Mimosa insidiosa var. insidiosa, commonly known as the insidious mimosa, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its bipinnately compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a noxious weed in some regions.
