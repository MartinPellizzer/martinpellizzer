Marsdenia bergii

Marsdenia bergii, commonly known as the Berg's Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
