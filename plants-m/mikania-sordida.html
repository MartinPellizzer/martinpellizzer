Mikania sordida

Mikania sordida, commonly known as the stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Mikania sordida is often considered an invasive species in regions where it is introduced.
