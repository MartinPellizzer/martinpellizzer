Mirabilis laevis var. villosa

Mirabilis laevis var. villosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf four o'clock, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that gives it a velvety appearance, and its flowers bloom in the late afternoon, opening around 4 p.m. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink and yellow, often with contrasting markings. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
