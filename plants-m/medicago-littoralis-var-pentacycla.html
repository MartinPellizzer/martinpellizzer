Medicago littoralis var. pentacycla

Medicago littoralis var. pentacycla, commonly known as the coastal clover, is a perennial legume native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and is well adapted to saline and windy conditions. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. Its root system helps stabilize dunes and prevent erosion.

It is an important forage plant for livestock and supports a variety of pollinators.
