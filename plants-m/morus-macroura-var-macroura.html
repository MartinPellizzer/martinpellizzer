Morus macroura var. macroura

Morus macroura var. macroura, commonly known as the giant mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible, black berries that ripen in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
