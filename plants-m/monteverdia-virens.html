Monteverdia virens

Monteverdia virens, commonly known as the green mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the neotropical region.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green, and its flowers are white or pale green, appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are dispersed by birds. It is characterized by its ability to photosynthesize partially while also drawing nutrients from its host.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have a significant impact on the health of its host tree.
