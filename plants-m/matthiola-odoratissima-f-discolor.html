Matthiola odoratissima f. discolor

Matthiola odoratissima f. discolor, commonly known as the variegated stock, is a cultivar of the stock plant, characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

This cultivar is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and mild scent.
