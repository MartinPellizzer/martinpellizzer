Manglesia tridentifera

Manglesia tridentifera, commonly known as the three-toothed manglesia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet bearing a distinct toothed edge, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
