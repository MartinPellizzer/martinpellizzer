Maxillaria palmensis

Maxillaria palmensis, commonly known as the palmosa orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically has a pseudobulb and linear, grass-like leaves that help it conserve water. Its flowers are borne on terminal inflorescences and are often clustered in racemes.

Maxillaria palmensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
