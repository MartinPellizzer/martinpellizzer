Malcolmia taraxacifolia

Malcolmia taraxacifolia, commonly known as the leafy mallows or the common mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features lobed leaves that resemble those of dandelions, giving it its common name. Its showy, yellow flowers bloom in summer and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
