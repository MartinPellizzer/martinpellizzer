Maxillaria purpurata

Maxillaria purpurata, commonly known as the Purple Maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, velvety purple flowers with a contrasting white lip. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in dry conditions. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its overall appearance.

Maxillaria purpurata blooms in spring or early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and collections.
