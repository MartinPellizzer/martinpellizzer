Malaxis crenulata

Malaxis crenulata, commonly known as the wrinkled malaxis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often with a faint fragrance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Malaxis crenulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for horticultural purposes.
