Mesechites trifidus var. tomentulosus

Mesechites trifidus var. tomentulosus, commonly known as the woolly mesechites, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a terminal cluster, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in its native flora.
