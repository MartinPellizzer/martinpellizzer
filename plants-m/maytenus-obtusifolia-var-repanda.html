Maytenus obtusifolia var. repanda

Maytenus obtusifolia var. repanda, commonly known as the broad-leaved mayten, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
