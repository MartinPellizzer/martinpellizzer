Myrcia pentagona

Myrcia pentagona, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a problematic species in some areas.
