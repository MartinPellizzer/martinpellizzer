Marrubium circinnatum

Marrubium circinnatum, commonly known as the woolly hartsage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Marrubium circinnatum is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
