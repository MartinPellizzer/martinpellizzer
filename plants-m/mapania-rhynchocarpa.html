Mapania rhynchocarpa

Mapania rhynchocarpa, commonly known as the horned maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated samaras that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
