Myrceugenia exsucca var. temu

Myrceugenia exsucca var. temu, commonly known as the Temu myrtle, is a species native to the subtropical regions of southeastern Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its small, fragrant white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by edible, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
