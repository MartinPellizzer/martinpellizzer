Montia parviflora var. rupestris

Montia parviflora var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock moss bean, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny white or pale pink flowers. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, with a compact growth habit that helps it conserve moisture.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
