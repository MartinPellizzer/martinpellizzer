Mammillaria fuscata

Mammillaria fuscata, commonly known as the dark mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and clusters of spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
