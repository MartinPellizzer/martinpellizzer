Meniscogyne petelotii

Meniscogyne petelotii, commonly known as the Bornean arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a distinctive inflorescence. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically hidden within a spathe, a modified leaf structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Meniscogyne petelotii is valued for its edible tubers, which are used in traditional cuisine and have potential for cultivation as a sustainable food source.
