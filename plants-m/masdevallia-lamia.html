Masdevallia lamia

Masdevallia lamia, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers that resemble the wings of a butterfly, with elongated petals and a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its native habitat.

Masdevallia lamia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
