Mimosa schrankioides var. schrankioides

Mimosa schrankioides var. schrankioides, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by the plant's sensitive leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Its unique botanical features make it both an interesting subject for study and a desirable addition to botanical collections.
