Muscari sirnakense

Muscari sirnakense, commonly known as the Sirnak bluebell, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Muscari sirnakense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
