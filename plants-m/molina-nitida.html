Molina nitida

Molina nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf molina, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are aromatic and have a slightly fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Molina nitida is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
