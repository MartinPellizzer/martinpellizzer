Mammillaria rhodantha var. rubra

Mammillaria rhodantha var. rubra, commonly known as the red-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular stem with a red or pinkish hue, often adorned with white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. The plant has a dense covering of white to pale blue bristles, which help protect it from intense sunlight. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a potted plant.
