Myrcia peruviana

Myrcia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a glossy surface and emit a strong, pleasant fragrance.

Myrcia peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
