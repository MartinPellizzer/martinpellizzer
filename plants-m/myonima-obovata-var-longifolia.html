Myonima obovata var. longifolia

Myonima obovata var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved myonima, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
