Monerma radicans

Monerma radicans, commonly known as the creeping root plant, is a unique species characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

It possesses fibrous roots that spread horizontally just beneath the soil surface, allowing it to colonize new areas efficiently. The plant typically features small, alternate leaves that are often succulent and adapted to arid conditions. It is native to regions with hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Monerma radicans is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in degraded landscapes.
