Mimosa tamariscina

Mimosa tamariscina, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by long, slender seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Mimosa tamariscina is also known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
