Myosotis geniculata

Myosotis geniculata, commonly known as the creeping forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
