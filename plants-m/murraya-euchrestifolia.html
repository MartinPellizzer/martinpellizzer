Murraya euchrestifolia

Murraya euchrestifolia, commonly known as the Indian butter tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The leaves and fruits have been used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
