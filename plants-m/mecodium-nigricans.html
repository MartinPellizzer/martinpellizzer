Mecodium nigricans

Mecodium nigricans, commonly known as the black mecodium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark purple to black, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
