Mirabilis linearis

Mirabilis linearis, commonly known as the linear spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, thriving in humid environments. It is a perennial species that produces seeds through a unique mechanism involving explosive seed dispersal.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
