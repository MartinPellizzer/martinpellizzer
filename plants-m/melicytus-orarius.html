Melicytus orarius

Melicytus orarius, commonly known as the silver mist tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive, mist-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Melicytus orarius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
