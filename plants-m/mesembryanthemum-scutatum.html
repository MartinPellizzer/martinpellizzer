Mesembryanthemum scutatum

Mesembryanthemum scutatum, commonly known as the "Cape gooseberry" or "Oxalis," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday, a trait known as nyctinasty. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss and reflects sunlight.

Mesembryanthemum scutatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
