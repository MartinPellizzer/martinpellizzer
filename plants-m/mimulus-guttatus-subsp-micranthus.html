Mimulus guttatus subsp. micranthus

Mimulus guttatus subsp.

micranthus, commonly known as the narrowleaf monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and roadsides.

The plant produces showy, spurred orange or yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

It is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to thrive in wetland environments.
