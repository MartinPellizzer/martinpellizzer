Mikania neei

Mikania neei, commonly known as the creeping wrightia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems that often trail along the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Mikania neei is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.
