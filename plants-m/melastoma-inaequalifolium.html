Melastoma inaequalifolium

Melastoma inaequalifolium, commonly known as the unequal-leaved melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red or purple calyx. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
