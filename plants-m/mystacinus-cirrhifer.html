Mystacinus cirrhifer

Mystacinus cirrhifer, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white, waxy flowers that are often clustered together. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery coating, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.

The berries it produces are typically red or orange and contain toxic compounds that deter herbivores.
