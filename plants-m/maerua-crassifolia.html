Maerua crassifolia

Maerua crassifolia, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments, with deep root systems that help it access water. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Maerua crassifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
