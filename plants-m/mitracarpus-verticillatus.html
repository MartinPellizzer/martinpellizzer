Mitracarpus verticillatus

Mitracarpus verticillatus, commonly known as the clustered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three leaflets arranged in a cluster. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered at the ends of the stems. Its fruit is a spiny, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Mitracarpus verticillatus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
