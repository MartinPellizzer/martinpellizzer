Mussaenda glabra var. wallichii

Mussaenda glabra var. wallichii, commonly known as the white mussaenda, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and southern China.

It features showy, clustered inflorescences that are typically white or pale yellow, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has smooth, glabrous stems and lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
