Machilus sumatranus

Machilus sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran Machilus, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface with a distinct scent.

The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.
