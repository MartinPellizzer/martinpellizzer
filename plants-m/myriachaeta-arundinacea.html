Myriachaeta arundinacea

Myriachaeta arundinacea, commonly known as the reed canary grass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks.

Myriachaeta arundinacea is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, making it both a valuable forage crop and an invasive species in some areas.
