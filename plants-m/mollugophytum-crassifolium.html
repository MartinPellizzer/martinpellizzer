Mollugophytum crassifolium

Mollugophytum crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf mollug, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
