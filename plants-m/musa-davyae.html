Musa davyae

Musa davyae, commonly known as the Dyak banana, is a species of banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of elongated, yellowish-orange fruits. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soil. It is cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a sweet and slightly tangy flavor.

However, it is less commonly grown commercially compared to other banana varieties.
