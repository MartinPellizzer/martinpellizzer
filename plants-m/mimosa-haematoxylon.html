Mimosa haematoxylon

Mimosa haematoxylon, commonly known as the Indian or red mimosa, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, yellow flower clusters. The plant is notable for its unique seed pods, which are woody and often used in traditional crafts. Its bark and seeds contain a red dye, historically used for coloring textiles and leather.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
