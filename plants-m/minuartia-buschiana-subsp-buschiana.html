Minuartia buschiana subsp. buschiana

Minuartia buschiana subsp.

buschiana, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments. It typically features small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a tight rosette.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its adaptations to harsh environments make it a resilient and hardy species.
