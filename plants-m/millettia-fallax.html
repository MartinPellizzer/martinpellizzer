Millettia fallax

Millettia fallax, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in furniture and carving.

The plant also has medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
