Marsdenia tinctoria var. ryukyuensis

Marsdenia tinctoria var. ryukyuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu Marsdenia, is a climbing shrub native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional dyeing practices.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
