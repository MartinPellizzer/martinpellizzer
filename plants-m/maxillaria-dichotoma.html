Maxillaria dichotoma

Maxillaria dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and linear, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil.

Maxillaria dichotoma is appreciated for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
