Marattia brassii

Marattia brassii, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a large, evergreen fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive, hollow stem covered with a layer of dead, brown fronds that provide structural support. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Marattia brassii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
