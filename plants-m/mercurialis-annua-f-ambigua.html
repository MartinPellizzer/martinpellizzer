Mercurialis annua f. ambigua

Mercurialis annua f. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous annual mercury, is a variant of the common mercury plant.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This plant is notable for its adaptability and rapid growth, making it a common sight in various habitats across its native range.
