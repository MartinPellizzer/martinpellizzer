Medinilla verticillata

Medinilla verticillata, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or rose of Sharon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are glossy and arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Medinilla verticillata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
