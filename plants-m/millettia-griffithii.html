Millettia griffithii

Millettia griffithii, commonly known as the Indian senna, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Millettia griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
