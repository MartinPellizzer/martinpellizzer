Myrcia curassavica

Myrcia curassavica, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or yellow myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a strong, spicy scent.

The flowers are clustered in inflorescences and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
