Melissa pyrenaica

Melissa pyrenaica, commonly known as mountain lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are fragrant and have a lemony scent, while its small white flowers bloom in summer.

Melissa pyrenaica is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
