Medicago × komarovii f. longifolia

Medicago × komarovii f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered goat's rue, is a hybrid legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces elongated, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its seeds are small and hairy, adapted for dispersal by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
