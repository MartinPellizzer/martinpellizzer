Mariscus umbellatus var. cyperinus

Mariscus umbellatus var. cyperinus, commonly known as the Cyperine bulrush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and shallow water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and ligulate, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The inflorescence consists of an umbel-like arrangement of spikelets, which is a distinctive feature of this variant.
