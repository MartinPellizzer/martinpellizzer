Mentha × nemorosa subsp. artiglandulosa

Mentha × nemorosa subsp.

artiglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular wood mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to eastern North America. It features upright, often branched stems that are hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered with fine glands, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August.

This plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
