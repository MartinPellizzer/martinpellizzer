Minthe pulegia

Minthe pulegia, commonly known as pulegaria, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties and potential digestive benefits.

Minthe pulegia thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
