Myrceugenia pitra

Myrceugenia pitra, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a fragrant aroma when crushed.

Myrceugenia pitra is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
