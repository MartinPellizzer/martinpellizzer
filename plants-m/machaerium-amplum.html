Machaerium amplum

Machaerium amplum, commonly known as the broad-leaved machaerium, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and racemose inflorescence. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its root system is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Machaerium amplum is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
