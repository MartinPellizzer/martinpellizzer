Macaranga palustris

Macaranga palustris, commonly known as the swamp macaranga, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wet, swampy areas. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Macaranga palustris plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
