Mentha suaveolens subsp. suaveolens

Mentha suaveolens subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as sweet mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features aromatic, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. Its essential oils, rich in menthol and other monoterpenes, contribute to its distinctive minty fragrance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
