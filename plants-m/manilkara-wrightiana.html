Manilkara wrightiana

Manilkara wrightiana, commonly known as the Wright's manilkara, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, greenish fruits that turn yellow when mature. Its latex, which is milky and viscous, is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
