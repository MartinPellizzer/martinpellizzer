Mentha aquatica var. hungarica

Mentha aquatica var. hungarica, commonly known as Hungarian water mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty leaves and is often found in wet or marshy environments. The plant typically grows in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are small and white or pale blue.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
