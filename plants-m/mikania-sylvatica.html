Mikania sylvatica

Mikania sylvatica, commonly known as the Brazilian spider plant, is a vigorous climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to twine around supports. The plant has heart-shaped, alternate leaves that are usually light green with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in spherical heads, and it produces small, greenish fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Mikania sylvatica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cover structures quickly.
