Marsdenia smithii

Marsdenia smithii, commonly known as the Smith's Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, often with a distinctive corona structure. Its leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine pubescence.

Marsdenia smithii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
