Minaria cordata

Minaria cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf minaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and bloom in clusters during the spring.

Minaria cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
