Melica fugax var. macbridei

Melica fugax var. macbridei, commonly known as the Macbride's false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in native plant communities.
