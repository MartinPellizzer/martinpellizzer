Myrtus balsamica

Myrtus balsamica, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its aromatic oils, derived from the leaves and berries, are valued for their pleasant scent and various therapeutic properties.

Myrtus balsamica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
