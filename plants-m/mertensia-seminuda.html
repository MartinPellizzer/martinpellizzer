Mertensia seminuda

Mertensia seminuda, commonly known as the slender bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically a deep blue or violet, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia seminuda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
