Moquinia congesta

Moquinia congesta, commonly known as the clustered moquinia, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue florets. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Moquinia congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
