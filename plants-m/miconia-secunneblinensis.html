Miconia secunneblinensis

Miconia secunneblinensis, commonly known as the golden miconia, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous small seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
