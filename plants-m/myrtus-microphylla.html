Myrtus microphylla

Myrtus microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically less than an inch in length. The plant produces tiny white flowers in spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Myrtus microphylla is valued for its aromatic foliage and ornamental appeal.
