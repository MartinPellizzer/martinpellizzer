Mattia lanata var. cyanoptera

Mattia lanata var. cyanoptera, commonly known as the blue-hair mat, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, bluish-white down, which reflects light and helps protect the plant from intense UV radiation. This variety is notable for its striking blue-green coloration, which is due to the presence of specialized pigments in its epidermal cells.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
