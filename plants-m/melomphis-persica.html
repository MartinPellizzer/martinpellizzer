Melomphis persica

Melomphis persica, commonly known as the Persian melon plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing vines and lobed leaves that are typically hairy in texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.

Melomphis persica thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
