Mesua korthalsiana

Mesua korthalsiana, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a vibrant display during the flowering season. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, elliptical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in some regions.
