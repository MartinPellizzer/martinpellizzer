Myrcia schenckiana

Myrcia schenckiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia schenckiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
