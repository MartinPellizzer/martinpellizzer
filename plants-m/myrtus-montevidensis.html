Myrtus montevidensis

Myrtus montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America, specifically Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It produces small white flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed.

Myrtus montevidensis thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils.
