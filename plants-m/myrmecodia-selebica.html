Myrmecodia selebica

Myrmecodia selebica, commonly known as the ant-plant, is a unique tropical species belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, tree-like stem that provides shelter for colonies of ants. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with its ant inhabitants, which in turn protect it from herbivores and help with nutrient cycling. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often found growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments.
