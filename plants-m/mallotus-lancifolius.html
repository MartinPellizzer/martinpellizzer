Mallotus lancifolius

Mallotus lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallot, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The leaves and fruits of Mallotus lancifolius have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
