Masdevallia amethystina

Masdevallia amethystina, commonly known as the amethystina orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, sympodial growth habit and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant features a pseudobulb system that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms during the cooler months.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
