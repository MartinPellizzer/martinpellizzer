Machilus cathayensis

Machilus cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese laurustin, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in panicles and bloom in late spring.

The fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.
