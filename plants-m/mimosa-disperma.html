Mimosa disperma

Mimosa disperma, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Mimosa disperma is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread quickly, making it both a useful pioneer species and a potential weed.
