Mertensia rivularis

Mertensia rivularis, commonly known as the stream mertensia, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mertensia rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
