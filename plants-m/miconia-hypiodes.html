Miconia hypiodes

Miconia hypiodes, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries. Miconia hypiodes is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a problematic species in certain regions.
