Mimusops giorgii

Mimusops giorgii, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits resembling small, rounded berries. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish hue, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
