Mosla soochouensis

Mosla soochouensis, commonly known as Chinese mosla, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or purple, blooming in the summer months.

Mosla soochouensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
