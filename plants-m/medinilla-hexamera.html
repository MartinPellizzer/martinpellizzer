Medinilla hexamera

Medinilla hexamera, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or Christmas bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and contain numerous small, white seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Medinilla hexamera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
