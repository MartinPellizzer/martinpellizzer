Minuartia geniculata var. procumbens

Minuartia geniculata var. procumbens, commonly known as the creeping sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically linear and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes or mountainous regions.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
