Mentha ciliata

Mentha ciliata, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with ciliate margins. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stem. Mentha ciliata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
