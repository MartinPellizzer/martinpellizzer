Malva eriocalyx

Malva eriocalyx, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
