Madorius giganteus

Madorius giganteus, commonly known as the giant madoria, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from bright yellow to deep orange, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular species among botanists and gardeners alike.
