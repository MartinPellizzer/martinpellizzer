Mentha × rotundifolia subsp. bauhini

Mentha × rotundifolia subsp.

bauhini, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae. It is characterized by its rounded, often hairy leaves that are typically smaller than those of its parent species.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
