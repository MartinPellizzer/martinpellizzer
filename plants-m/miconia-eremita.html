Miconia eremita

Miconia eremita, commonly known as the desert miconia, is a shrub native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the dry season. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, fleshy fruits that are adapted to the harsh desert environment. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in low-nutrient conditions.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for survival in extreme climates.
