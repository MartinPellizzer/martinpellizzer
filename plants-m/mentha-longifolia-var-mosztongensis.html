Mentha longifolia var. mosztongensis

Mentha longifolia var. mosztongensis, commonly known as Mosztongensis mint, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the long-leaf mint.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is native to specific regions in Central Europe and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
