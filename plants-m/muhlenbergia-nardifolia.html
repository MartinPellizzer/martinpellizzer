Muhlenbergia nardifolia

Muhlenbergia nardifolia, commonly known as the silverhair grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds that are often mistaken for cotton or dandelion fluff. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Muhlenbergia nardifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
