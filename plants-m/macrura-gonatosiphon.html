Macrura gonatosiphon

Macrura gonatosiphon, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, spindle-shaped fruits. Its rhizomes are thick and starchy, making it a valuable crop for its edible roots.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.
