Macleania crassa

Macleania crassa, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white bracts that resemble flowers, often mistaken for actual blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
