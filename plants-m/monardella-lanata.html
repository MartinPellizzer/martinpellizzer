Monardella lanata

Monardella lanata, commonly known as wooly larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Monardella lanata is a member of the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
