Macrosolen bibracteolatus

Macrosolen bibracteolatus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often floating on the water's surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge from the water, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise robust appearance. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, preferring nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value.
