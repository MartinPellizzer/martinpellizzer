Memora vaga

Memora vaga, commonly known as wandering begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its ability to spread easily.
