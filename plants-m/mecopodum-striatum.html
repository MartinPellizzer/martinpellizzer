Mecopodum striatum

Mecopodum striatum, commonly known as the striped groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.
