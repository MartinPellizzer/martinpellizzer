Maxillaria sanderiana var. atropurpurea

Maxillaria sanderiana var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the Purple-veined Sander's Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that produce narrow, strap-like leaves. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of purple and white, often with a distinctive violet veining. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under proper conditions.
