Minuopsis caroliniana

Minuopsis caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina minuette, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and marshes. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
