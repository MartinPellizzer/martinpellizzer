Mentha slichoviensis

Mentha slichoviensis, commonly known as the Slichov's mint, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
