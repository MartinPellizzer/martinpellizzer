Mentha × sarntheinii

Mentha × sarntheinii, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Mentha aquatica and Mentha rotundifolia, which gives it unique characteristics. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Mentha × sarntheinii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
