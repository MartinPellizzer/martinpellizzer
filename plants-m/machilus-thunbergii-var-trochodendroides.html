Machilus thunbergii var. trochodendroides

Machilus thunbergii var. trochodendroides, commonly known as the Japanese maho or mountain laurel, is a variant of the Machilus genus native to East Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
