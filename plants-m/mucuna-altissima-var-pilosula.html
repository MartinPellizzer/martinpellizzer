Mucuna altissima var. pilosula

Mucuna altissima var. pilosula, commonly known as velvet bean or horsebean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy, bean-like pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

The seeds are used in various traditional and commercial applications, including as a source of protein and in herbal medicine.
