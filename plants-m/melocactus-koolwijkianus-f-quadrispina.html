Melocactus koolwijkianus f. quadrispina

Melocactus koolwijkianus f. quadrispina, commonly known as the four-spined melocactus, is a rare and distinctive cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem with a single, elongated spines that are often curved and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
