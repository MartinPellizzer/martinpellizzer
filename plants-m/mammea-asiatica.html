Mammea asiatica

Mammea asiatica, commonly known as the Asian mammea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mammea asiatica is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
