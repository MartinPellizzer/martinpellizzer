Mentha longifolia var. hollosyana

Mentha longifolia var. hollosyana, commonly known as the Hollosyana mint, is a variety of the long-leaf mint, characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and compact growth habit.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a strong, minty fragrance, and it is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties in horticulture.
