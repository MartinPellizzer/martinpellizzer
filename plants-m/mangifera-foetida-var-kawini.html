Mangifera foetida var. kawini

Mangifera foetida var. kawini, commonly known as the Kawini mango, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is typically small, with a rough, hairy skin and a distinct, strong aroma. It is adapted to grow in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Despite its unique characteristics, it is less commonly cultivated compared to other mango varieties.
