Murdannia vaginata var. vaginata

Murdannia vaginata var. vaginata, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Limnophyceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like stems and small, bladder-like structures that help it float and trap microorganisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are usually scale-like and arranged in whorls around the stem.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to nutrient cycling.
