Muraltia acipetala

Muraltia acipetala, commonly known as the red muraltia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Muraltia acipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and fragrance.
