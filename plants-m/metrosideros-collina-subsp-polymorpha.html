Metrosideros collina subsp. polymorpha

Metrosideros collina subsp.

polymorpha, commonly known as the coastal kowhai, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing before the leaves.

The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a rough, hairy texture.

It plays an important ecological role in coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for native birds and insects.
