Mercurialis annua f. lanceolata

Mercurialis annua f. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mercury plant, is a variant of the common mercury plant.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its lanceolate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and small, clustered flowers.

The botanical features make it distinguishable from the typical form of Mercurialis annua.
