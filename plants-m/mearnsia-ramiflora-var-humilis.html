Mearnsia ramiflora var. humilis

Mearnsia ramiflora var. humilis, commonly known as the lowleaf mearnsia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a suitable ornamental plant for xeriscaping.
