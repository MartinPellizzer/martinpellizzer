Myristica bialata

Myristica bialata, commonly known as the Bornean nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Borneo and is characterized by its aromatic, woody bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by large, fleshy, orange-red berries containing a single seed. Its most notable botanical feature is the aromatic seed, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.
