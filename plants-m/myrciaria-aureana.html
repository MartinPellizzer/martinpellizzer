Myrciaria aureana

Myrciaria aureana, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, orange-colored berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and humid forests.

Myrciaria aureana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
