Machilus elephanti

Machilus elephanti, commonly known as elephant apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, opposite leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by large, fleshy, yellow or orange fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, aromatic pulp surrounding a central core containing seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
