Magnolia campbellii

Magnolia campbellii, commonly known as the Campbell magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
