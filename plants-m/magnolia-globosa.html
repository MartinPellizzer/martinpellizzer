Magnolia globosa

Magnolia globosa, commonly known as the globe magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its flowers are clustered in broad, globular inflorescences, giving it its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
