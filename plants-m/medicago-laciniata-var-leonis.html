Medicago laciniata var. leonis

Medicago laciniata var. leonis, commonly known as the lion's share clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically deeply lobed and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, round pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration and landscaping.
