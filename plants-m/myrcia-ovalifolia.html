Myrcia ovalifolia

Myrcia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Myrcia ovalifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
