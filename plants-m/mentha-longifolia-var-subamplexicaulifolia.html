Mentha longifolia var. subamplexicaulifolia

Mentha longifolia var. subamplexicaulifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a variant of the broad-leaf mint species.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in terminal clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variant is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
