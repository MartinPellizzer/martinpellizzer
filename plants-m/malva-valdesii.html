Malva valdesii

Malva valdesii, commonly known as the Valdes' mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are palmately lobed with a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Malva valdesii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
