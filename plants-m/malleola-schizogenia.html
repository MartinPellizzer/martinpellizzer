Malleola schizogenia

Malleola schizogenia, commonly known as the split-stem plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive branching pattern.

It is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. The plant features a short, thick stem with multiple lateral shoots that emerge from the base. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Malleola schizogenia produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season.
