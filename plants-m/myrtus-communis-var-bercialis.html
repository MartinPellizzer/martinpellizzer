Myrtus communis var. bercialis

Myrtus communis var. bercialis, commonly known as the Bercial myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by spherical, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in Mediterranean cultures further enhance its botanical appeal.
