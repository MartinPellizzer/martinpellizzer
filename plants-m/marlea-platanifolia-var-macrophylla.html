Marlea platanifolia var. macrophylla

Marlea platanifolia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved marlea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
