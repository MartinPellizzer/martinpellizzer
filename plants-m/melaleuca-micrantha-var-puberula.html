Melaleuca micrantha var. puberula

Melaleuca micrantha var. puberula, commonly known as the smooth honeymyrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-green bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and sand dunes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural landscapes.
