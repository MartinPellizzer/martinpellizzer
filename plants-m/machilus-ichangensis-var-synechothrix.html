Machilus ichangensis var. synechothrix

Machilus ichangensis var. synechothrix, commonly known as the Chinese sandpaper ash, is a variant of the Machilus genus native to China.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its rough, sandpaper-like bark. The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a glossy upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits.

This plant is often found in subtropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
