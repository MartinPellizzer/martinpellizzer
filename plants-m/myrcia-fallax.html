Myrcia fallax

Myrcia fallax, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Myrcia fallax is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, visually appealing plant.
