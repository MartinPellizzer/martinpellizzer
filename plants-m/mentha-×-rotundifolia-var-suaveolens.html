Mentha × rotundifolia var. suaveolens

Mentha × rotundifolia var. suaveolens, commonly known as sweet catmint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its rounded, slightly fuzzy leaves and fragrant, blue to purple flowers.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil.
