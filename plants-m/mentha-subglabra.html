Mentha subglabra

Mentha subglabra, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, pale blue or violet flowers bloom in whorls along the upper stem, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mentha subglabra is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
