Milla coerulea

Milla coerulea, commonly known as the blue milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It features distinctive blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its stems are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height.

Milla coerulea is known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the milkweed genus.
