Myrtus communis var. foucaudii

Myrtus communis var. foucaudii, commonly known as the Corsican myrtle, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a fragrant, resinous scent. The plant produces small, pale pink or white flowers in spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value and aromatic qualities make it a popular choice in horticulture.
