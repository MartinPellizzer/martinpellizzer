Malvinda angustifolia

Malvinda angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf mallows, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.
