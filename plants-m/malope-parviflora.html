Malope parviflora

Malope parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy yellow flowers with a distinctive red center, typically blooming in spring. It grows as a shrub or subshrub, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

Malope parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
