Monvillea cavendishii

Monvillea cavendishii, commonly known as the golden monvillea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features slender, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Monvillea cavendishii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
