Myrobalanus discolor

Myrobalanus discolor, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
