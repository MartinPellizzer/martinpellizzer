Monstera egregia

Monstera egregia, commonly known as the giant split-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop distinctive holes and splits as they mature. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach heights of several meters when provided with adequate support. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its dramatic appearance.

Monstera egregia thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
