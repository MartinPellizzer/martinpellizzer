Matelea crenata

Matelea crenata, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

The flowers emit a strong fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
