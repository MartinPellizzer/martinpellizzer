Microchilus marulandae

Microchilus marulandae, commonly known as the Marulandae orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
