Mesosphaerum crinitum

Mesosphaerum crinitum, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
