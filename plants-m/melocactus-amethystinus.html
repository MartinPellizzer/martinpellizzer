Melocactus amethystinus

Melocactus amethystinus, commonly known as the amethystine melocactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, globular shape and a dense covering of white, woolly hair that gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and striking appearance make it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners.
