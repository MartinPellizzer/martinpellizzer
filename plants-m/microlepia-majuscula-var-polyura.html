Microlepia majuscula var. polyura

Microlepia majuscula var. polyura, commonly known as the broad-leaved chain fern, is a variant of the chain fern genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces sporangia in clusters along the fronds, typical of the Polypodiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.
