Mussaenda nitida

Mussaenda nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of sterile, white bracts surrounded by small, yellow flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mussaenda nitida is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
