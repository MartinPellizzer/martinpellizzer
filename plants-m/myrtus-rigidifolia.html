Myrtus rigidifolia

Myrtus rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid leaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of white or pale pink. The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal gardens.

Myrtus rigidifolia is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in perfumery and herbal remedies.
