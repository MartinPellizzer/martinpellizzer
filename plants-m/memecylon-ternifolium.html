Memecylon ternifolium

Memecylon ternifolium, commonly known as the three-leaved memecylon, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that often have a reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of white or pink, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
