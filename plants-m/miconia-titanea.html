Miconia titanea

Miconia titanea, commonly known as the scarlet miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia titanea is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
