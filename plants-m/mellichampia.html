Mellichampia

Mellichampia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to various regions of Africa.

These plants are characterized by their showy, tubular flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including purple, pink, white, and blue. Mellichampia species are typically herbaceous, with a rosette of fleshy, velvety leaves that help store water. They thrive in well-draining soil and require bright, indirect light to flourish.

The genus is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation indoors.
