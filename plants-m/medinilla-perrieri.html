Medinilla perrieri

Medinilla perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's mistletoe, is a tropical epiphytic plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves and produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in tropical rainforests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
