Myrtoleucodendron nodosum

Myrtoleucodendron nodosum, commonly known as the nodular myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and leathery, aromatic leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
