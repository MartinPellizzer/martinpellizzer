Mayepea ligustrina

Mayepea ligustrina, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Mayepea ligustrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
