Mimulus ringens var. colpophilus

Mimulus ringens var. colpophilus, commonly known as the scarlet monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a pollinator attractor.
