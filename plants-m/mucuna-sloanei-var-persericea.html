Mucuna sloanei var. persericea

Mucuna sloanei var. persericea, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers. The plant is notable for its spherical, hairy pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, mucilaginous substance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.

This species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of protein-rich seeds.
