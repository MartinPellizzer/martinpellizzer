Masdevallia petiolaris

Masdevallia petiolaris, commonly known as the petiole masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, arranged in a spiral pattern along the inflorescence. Its leaves are usually dark green and lance-shaped, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Masdevallia petiolaris thrives in cool, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.
