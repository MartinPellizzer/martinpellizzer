Malus paradisiaca dasyphylla

Malus paradisiaca dasyphylla, commonly known as the Persian apple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in the spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
