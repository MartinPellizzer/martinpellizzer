Mesembryanthemum sedentiflorum

Mesembryanthemum sedentiflorum, commonly known as the rock rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and long flowering period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
