Manihot janipha

Manihot janipha, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its starchy roots, which are used as a food source.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various African cultures.
