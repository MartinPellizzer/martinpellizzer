Mundulea andringitrensis

Mundulea andringitrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar acacia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insect species.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
