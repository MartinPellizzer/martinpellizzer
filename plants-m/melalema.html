Melalema

Melalema, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes. The leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, and the plant often grows as a shrub or small tree. Melalema species are known for their aromatic oils and are often used in traditional medicine.

They thrive in well-drained soils and are adapted to dry, sunny environments.
