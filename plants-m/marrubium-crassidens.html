Marrubium crassidens

Marrubium crassidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and has a strong, aromatic scent.

Marrubium crassidens is known for its medicinal properties, historically used for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
