Mentha lapponica

Mentha lapponica, commonly known as polar mint, is a perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant contains essential oils, giving it a strong minty scent and making it useful in aromatherapy and herbal medicine. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in tundra and rocky soils.

Mentha lapponica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
