Malva tarapacana

Malva tarapacana, commonly known as the Andean hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in cool, dry climates. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Malva tarapacana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
