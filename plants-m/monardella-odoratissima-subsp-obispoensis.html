Monardella odoratissima subsp. obispoensis

Monardella odoratissima subsp.

obispoensis, commonly known as Obispo's mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to California. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and showy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, known for its square stems and aromatic properties.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and is often found in conservation areas.
