Momordica maculata

Momordica maculata, commonly known as the spotted cucumber or wild bitter gourd, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive warty, green fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, though they contain toxic compounds that require careful preparation.

The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible parts after proper processing.
