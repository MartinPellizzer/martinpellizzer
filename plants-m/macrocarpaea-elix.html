Macrocarpaea elix

Macrocarpaea elix, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces yellow-orange composite flowers, which are arranged in dense, daisy-like clusters. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine white or silver hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
