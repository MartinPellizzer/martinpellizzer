Mentha austriaca var. salicetorum

Mentha austriaca var. salicetorum, commonly known as the Austrian mint or willow mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a minty scent and making it useful in herbal remedies.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
