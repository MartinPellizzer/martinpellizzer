Microtea bahiensis

Microtea bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia microtea, is a rare and delicate plant native to the tropical regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, finely divided leaves and compact growth habit, which allow it to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant's botanical features include a short stem, hairy foliage, and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in clusters. It is often found in moist, nutrient-rich soils within dense forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, Microtea bahiensis is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
