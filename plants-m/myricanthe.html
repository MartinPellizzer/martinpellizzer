Myricanthe

Myricanthe, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble misty clouds. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Myricanthe is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
