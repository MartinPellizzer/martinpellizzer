Malvastrum strictum

Malvastrum strictum, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy texture.

Malvastrum strictum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
