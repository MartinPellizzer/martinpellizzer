Myosurus minimus var. aristatus

Myosurus minimus var. aristatus, commonly known as the creeping mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
