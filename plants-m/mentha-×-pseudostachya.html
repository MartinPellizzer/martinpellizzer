Mentha × pseudostachya

Mentha × pseudostachya, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha viridis (ovalleaf mint). The plant typically features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue to purple and appear in whorled clusters.

Mentha × pseudostachya is valued for its aromatic leaves and is commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.
