Mussaenda afzelioides

Mussaenda afzelioides, commonly known as the golden trumpet bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow inflorescences that resemble trumpets, which attract pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Mussaenda afzelioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
