Melothria perpusilla var. subtruncata

Melothria perpusilla var. subtruncata, commonly known as the small bitter melon, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It produces small, green, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when immature and tender. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in groups of three.

The fruit is known for its sharp, bitter taste and is used in various traditional dishes.
