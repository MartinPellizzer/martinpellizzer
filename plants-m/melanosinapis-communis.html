Melanosinapis communis

Melanosinapis communis, commonly known as the duskshade fern, is a perennial plant native to temperate forest regions.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a dark green hue that deepens to a near-black coloration during late summer. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by wind. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly across moist, shaded soil.

Botanically, it is characterized by its complex frond structure and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
