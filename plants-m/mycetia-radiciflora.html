Mycetia radiciflora

Mycetia radiciflora, commonly known as the root-flowered mycetia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant has a woody stem with compound leaves, and its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, often forming symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Mycetia radiciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions in soil enrichment.
