Minthostachys ovata

Minthostachys ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf mintweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
