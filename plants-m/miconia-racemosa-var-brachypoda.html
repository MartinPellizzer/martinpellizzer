Miconia racemosa var. brachypoda

Miconia racemosa var. brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stalked miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface and prominent veins. The inflorescences are racemose, consisting of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters.

This plant is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
