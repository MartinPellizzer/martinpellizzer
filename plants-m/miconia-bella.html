Miconia bella

Miconia bella, commonly known as the beauty leaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia bella is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
