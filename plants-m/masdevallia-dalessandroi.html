Masdevallia dalessandroi

Masdevallia dalessandroi, commonly known as the Dalessandro's masdevallia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in shades of pink and white. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients in its natural habitat. It thrives in cool, humid conditions with indirect light, making it a challenging but rewarding species for orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
