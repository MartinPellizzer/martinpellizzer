Magnolia talamancana

Magnolia talamancana, commonly known as the Talamancan magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin. The tree produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
