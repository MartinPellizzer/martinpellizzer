Monopsis malvacea

Monopsis malvacea, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
