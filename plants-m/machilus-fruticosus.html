Machilus fruticosus

Machilus fruticosus, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or Chinese mahogany, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers and dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Machilus fruticosus is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
