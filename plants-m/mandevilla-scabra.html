Mandevilla scabra

Mandevilla scabra, commonly known as the rough mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of tubular, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features rough, ridged leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as a vine or shrub.

Mandevilla scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb or trail in garden settings.
