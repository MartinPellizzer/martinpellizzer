Melaleuca barlowii

Melaleuca barlowii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Melaleuca barlowii is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
