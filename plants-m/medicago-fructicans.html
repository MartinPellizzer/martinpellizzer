Medicago fructicans

Medicago fructicans, commonly known as wild licorice, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Medicago fructicans is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
