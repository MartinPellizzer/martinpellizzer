Marica coerulea

Marica coerulea, commonly known as the bluebell of the Cape, is a striking South African plant characterized by its vibrant blue flowers.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. The plant features strap-shaped leaves and produces bell-shaped blooms that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters in length. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Marica coerulea is also noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
