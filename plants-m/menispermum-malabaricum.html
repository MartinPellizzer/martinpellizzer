Menispermum malabaricum

Menispermum malabaricum, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to the wet tropical forests of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and distinctive yellowish-brown bark. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. Its roots contain various alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in undergrowth layers of dense forests.
