Mentha longifolia var. rozaliae

Mentha longifolia var. rozaliae, commonly known as Rozalia mint, is a variant of the common mint species characterized by its distinctive foliage and aromatic properties.

This plant is native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean, where it thrives in temperate climates. It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The leaves emit a strong, refreshing mint scent, making it popular in culinary and aromatic applications.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in herbal remedies.
