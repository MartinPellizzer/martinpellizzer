Melastoma muricatum

Melastoma muricatum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, orange-red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
