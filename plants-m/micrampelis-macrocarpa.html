Micrampelis macrocarpa

Micrampelis macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited climbing fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its woody stem and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its fruits are large, fleshy, and typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a single seed.

This species is often found in humid, shaded environments within lowland forests.
