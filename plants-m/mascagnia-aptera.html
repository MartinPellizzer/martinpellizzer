Mascagnia aptera

Mascagnia aptera, commonly known as the smooth mascagnia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, evergreen leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white waxy coating. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
