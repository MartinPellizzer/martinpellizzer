Mentha danubialis

Mentha danubialis, commonly known as Danube mint, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, menthol-like fragrance.

Mentha danubialis is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and calming properties.
