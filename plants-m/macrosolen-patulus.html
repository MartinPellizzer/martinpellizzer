Macrosolen patulus

Macrosolen patulus, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically broad and floating on the water surface. The plant has long, slender stems that can extend several feet, often with roots that anchor it in shallow water. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces small, round fruits.

Macrosolen patulus is often used in water gardens and as a decorative plant in ponds due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to help control algae growth.
