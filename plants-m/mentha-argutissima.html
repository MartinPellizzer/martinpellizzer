Mentha argutissima

Mentha argutissima, commonly known as the broad-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Mentha argutissima is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are used in traditional herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
