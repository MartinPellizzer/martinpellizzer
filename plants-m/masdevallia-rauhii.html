Masdevallia rauhii

Masdevallia rauhii, commonly known as the Rauh's masdevallia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces spikes of small, colorful blossoms in shades of pink, white, and purple. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high light exposure and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its striking appearance and specific cultivation needs, it is a prized species among orchid enthusiasts.
