Melissa incana

Melissa incana, commonly known as rough lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its aromatic leaves are used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Melissa incana is closely related to other members of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
