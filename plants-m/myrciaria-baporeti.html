Myrciaria baporeti

Myrciaria baporeti, commonly known as the Pernambuco myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its fruit, a small red berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
