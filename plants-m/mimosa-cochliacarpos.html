Mimosa cochliacarpos

Mimosa cochliacarpos, commonly known as the sensitive plant or mimic plant, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by rapid changes in turgor pressure within the leaf cells. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique sensitivity to touch and rapid leaf movement make it an intriguing subject for botanical study.
