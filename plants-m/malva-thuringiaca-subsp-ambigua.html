Malva thuringiaca subsp. ambigua

Malva thuringiaca subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.

The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its variability in leaf shape and flower color, contributing to its common name.
