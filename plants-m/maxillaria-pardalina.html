Maxillaria pardalina

Maxillaria pardalina, commonly known as the Orchid of the Sun, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature a mix of yellow, orange, and red hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of flowers, each with a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination.

The botanical features of Maxillaria pardalina make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and adaptability.
