Myosotis retrorsa

Myosotis retrorsa, commonly known as the backward bluebell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Myosotis retrorsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
