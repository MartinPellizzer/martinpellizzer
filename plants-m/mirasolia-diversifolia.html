Mirasolia diversifolia

Mirasolia diversifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its feathery, silver-green fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with numerous aerial roots that help it climb and anchor to trees. Its leaves are pinnately divided, creating a lush, cascading appearance that makes it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
