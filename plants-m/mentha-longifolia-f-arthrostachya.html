Mentha longifolia f. arthrostachya

Mentha longifolia f. arthrostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a subspecies of the broad-leafed mint native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and aromatic, slightly minty fragrance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are small and tubular, often appearing in clusters, and are visited by various pollinators.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional herbal remedies.
