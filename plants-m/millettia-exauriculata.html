Millettia exauriculata

Millettia exauriculata, commonly known as the Brazilian white bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features pinnate leaves with three to five pairs of leaflets and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant bears bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its ornamental value and nutritional properties make it both ecologically and agriculturally significant.
