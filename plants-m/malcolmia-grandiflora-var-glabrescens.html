Malcolmia grandiflora var. glabrescens

Malcolmia grandiflora var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth malcolmia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves, which are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
