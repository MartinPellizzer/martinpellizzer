Myrica madagascariensis

Myrica madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan wax tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myricaceae family and is characterized by its dense, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
