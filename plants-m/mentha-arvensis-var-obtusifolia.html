Mentha arvensis var. obtusifolia

Mentha arvensis var. obtusifolia, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with rounded tips and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for digestive and respiratory ailments.
