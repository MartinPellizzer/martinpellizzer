Malanea candollei

Malanea candollei, commonly known as the Candolle's malanea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
