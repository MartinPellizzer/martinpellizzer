Mentha royleana var. glabra

Mentha royleana var. glabra, commonly known as smooth mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

The leaves are widely used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to their refreshing menthol-like scent.
