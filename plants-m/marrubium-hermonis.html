Marrubium hermonis

Marrubium hermonis, commonly known as the Hermon horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has a strong, bitter scent and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Marrubium hermonis is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
