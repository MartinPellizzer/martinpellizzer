Matucana pughii

Matucana pughii, commonly known as the Pugh's matucana, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant has a slow growth rate and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss.

Matucana pughii is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
