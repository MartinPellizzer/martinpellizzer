Mallotus leucodermis var. puberulus

Mallotus leucodermis var. puberulus, commonly known as the silky mallot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
