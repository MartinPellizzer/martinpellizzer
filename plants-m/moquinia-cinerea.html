Moquinia cinerea

Moquinia cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery or pale green bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil and is often found in marshes and pond edges.

Moquinia cinerea plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
