Mammillaria luethyi

Mammillaria luethyi, commonly known as the Luethyi Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in white to pale green tubercles, which are small, rounded bumps. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking contrast to its spiny appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping projects.
