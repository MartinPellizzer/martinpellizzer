Mentha hirsuta var. aquatica

Mentha hirsuta var. aquatica, commonly known as water mint, is a variant of the mint family characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit.

It typically grows in moist soils or water environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers appear in whorled clusters, and it produces a strong, aromatic scent.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
