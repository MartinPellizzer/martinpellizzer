Mesembryanthemum cooperi

Mesembryanthemum cooperi, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to prevent soil erosion.
