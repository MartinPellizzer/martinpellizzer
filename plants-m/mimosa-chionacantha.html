Mimosa chionacantha

Mimosa chionacantha, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage and delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by light or touch. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptive traits, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
