Mentha arvensis var. marrubiastrum

Mentha arvensis var. marrubiastrum, commonly known as field mint, is a variant of the broad-leaved mint species.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, shady environments.

This variety is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
