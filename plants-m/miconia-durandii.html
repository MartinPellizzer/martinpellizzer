Miconia durandii

Miconia durandii, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, bracteate inflorescences. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a glossy surface and prominent veins.

Miconia durandii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
