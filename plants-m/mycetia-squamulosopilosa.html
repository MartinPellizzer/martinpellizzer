Mycetia squamulosopilosa

Mycetia squamulosopilosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf mycetia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, woody pods containing seeds. Its growth habit is typically upright, with multiple branches forming a compact structure.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
