Maxillaria pusilla

Maxillaria pusilla, commonly known as the miniature orchid or the dwarf maxillaria, is a small, epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows on tree branches or rocks, thriving in warm and humid environments. The plant features compact, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its blooms are often clustered in small inflorescences, and the species is noted for its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.

Maxillaria pusilla is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
