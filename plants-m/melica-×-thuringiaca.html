Melica × thuringiaca

Melica × thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian grass, is a hybrid species within the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces inflorescences that are either spike-like or panicle-like, depending on the variety. It is native to Central Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
