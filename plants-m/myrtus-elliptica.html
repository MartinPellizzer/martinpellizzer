Myrtus elliptica

Myrtus elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and aromatic. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Myrtus elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
