Mentzelia pumila var. genuina

Mentzelia pumila var. genuina, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils.
