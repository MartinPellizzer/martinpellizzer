Malachra urens var. sublobata

Malachra urens var. sublobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, sparsely branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.
