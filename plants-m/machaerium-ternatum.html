Machaerium ternatum

Machaerium ternatum, commonly known as the three-lobed grass or slender machaerium, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, which is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval to lanceolate leaflets, and it produces small, pea-like flowers in clusters.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value and adaptability.
