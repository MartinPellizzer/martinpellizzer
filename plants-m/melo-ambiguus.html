Melo ambiguus

Melo ambiguus, commonly known as the ambiguous melon, is a lesser-known species within the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and distinctive, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruit is a small, round melon with a thin, green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh.

Despite its botanical interest, Melo ambiguus is rarely cultivated due to its limited size and less commercial appeal compared to other melon species.
