Morus alba var. nervosa

Morus alba var. nervosa, commonly known as the veined mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive veined leaves, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also used in traditional medicine and as a host plant for silkworms.
