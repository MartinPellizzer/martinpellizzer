Mariosousa

Mariosousa, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Mariosousa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
