Matricaria argentea

Matricaria argentea, commonly known as silver yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, silver-hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Silver yarrow is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and calming effects.
