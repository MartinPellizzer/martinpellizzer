Mitracarpus torresianus

Mitracarpus torresianus, commonly known as the Torresian mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Mitracarpus torresianus is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate saline conditions.
