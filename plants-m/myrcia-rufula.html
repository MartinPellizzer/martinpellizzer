Myrcia rufula

Myrcia rufula, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.

Myrcia rufula is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to different climates.
