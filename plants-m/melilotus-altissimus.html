Melilotus altissimus

Melilotus altissimus, commonly known as tall melilot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Melilotus altissimus is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
