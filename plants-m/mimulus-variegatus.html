Mimulus variegatus

Mimulus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its strikingly variegated leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces showy, bright orange or red tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and woodland edges.

Mimulus variegatus is a perennial herb that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
