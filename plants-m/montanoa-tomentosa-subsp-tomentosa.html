Montanoa tomentosa subsp. tomentosa

Montanoa tomentosa subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly montanoa, is a flowering plant native to Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
