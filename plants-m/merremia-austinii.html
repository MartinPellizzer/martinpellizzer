Merremia austinii

Merremia austinii, commonly known as Austin's morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a notable species in its native habitat.
