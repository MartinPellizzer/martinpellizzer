Mentha lycopoides

Mentha lycopoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, slightly peppery scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are small, pale blue to purple, and appear in clusters during the summer months.

Mentha lycopoides is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
