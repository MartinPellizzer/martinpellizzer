Malaxis pandurata

Malaxis pandurata, commonly known as the pandurata orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

The orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
