Mertensia foliosa var. amoena

Mertensia foliosa var. amoena, commonly known as the pink-flowered bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a native species supporting local pollinators and contributing to ecological diversity.
