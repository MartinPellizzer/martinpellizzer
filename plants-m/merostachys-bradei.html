Merostachys bradei

Merostachys bradei, commonly known as the Brade's grass, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

While not widely cultivated, it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
