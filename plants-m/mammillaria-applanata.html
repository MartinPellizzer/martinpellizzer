Mammillaria applanata

Mammillaria applanata, commonly known as the flat-topped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular shape and a distinctive flat, disc-like top. The plant features white to pale green stems covered in areoles, from which it produces clusters of white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Mammillaria applanata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
