Masdevallia triseta

Masdevallia triseta, commonly known as the Three-Fingered Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its distinctive, finger-like inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, colorful flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with high humidity and partial shade.

Masdevallia triseta is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
