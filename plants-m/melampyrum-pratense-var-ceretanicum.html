Melampyrum pratense var. ceretanicum

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as wet meadows and woodland edges.

Its root system is parasitic, relying on the roots of other plants for nutrients.
