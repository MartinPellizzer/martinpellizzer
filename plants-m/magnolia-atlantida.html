Magnolia atlantida

Magnolia atlantida, commonly known as the Atlantic magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in American horticulture.
