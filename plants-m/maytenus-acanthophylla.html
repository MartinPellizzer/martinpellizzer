Maytenus acanthophylla

Maytenus acanthophylla, commonly known as the prickly leaf mayten, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage with spiny margins on the leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Maytenus acanthophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
