Mentha palustris var. plicata

Mentha palustris var. plicata, commonly known as narrow-leaved marsh mint, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, aromatic leaves that are often folded or pleated, giving it its scientific name "plicata.". The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and marshes.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
