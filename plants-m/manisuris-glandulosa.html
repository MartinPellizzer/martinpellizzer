Manisuris glandulosa

Manisuris glandulosa, commonly known as the stickyleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its unique leaf structure and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
