Mentha × nitida

Mentha × nitida, commonly known as woolly mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha spicata and Mentha piperita.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with aromatic leaves that emit a strong mint scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Mentha × nitida is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can also be harvested for its essential oils.
