Melinis minutiflora var. setigera

Melinis minutiflora var. setigera, commonly known as parthenium weed, is an invasive annual grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and can rapidly colonize open areas, making it a significant ecological threat. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its rapid spread and persistence in new environments.

Due to its aggressive growth and competitive nature, it is often considered a noxious weed in agricultural and natural ecosystems.
