Menispermum coriaceum

Menispermum coriaceum, commonly known as Indian Cucumber Root, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, cucumber-like fruits. The plant produces a yellowish-brown sap that contains various alkaloids, some of which have been studied for their medicinal properties. Its growth habit involves twining stems that ascend supports, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The botanical interest in this species lies in its chemical composition and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
