Mecodium dendritis

Mecodium dendritis, commonly known as the tree spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white sap. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves and stems contain a toxic latex that can cause skin irritation.

Mecodium dendritis is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
