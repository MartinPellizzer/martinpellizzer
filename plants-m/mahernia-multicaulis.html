Mahernia multicaulis

Mahernia multicaulis, commonly known as the many-stemmed mahernia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and multiple stems that arise from the base. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, fleshy, and adapted to conserve water, typical of xerophytic plants.

Mahernia multicaulis is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.
