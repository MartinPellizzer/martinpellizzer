Melia azadirachta

Melia azadirachta, commonly known as neem, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with dark green, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain hard, ridged seeds. Its most notable botanical feature is the highly valued oil extracted from its seeds, which contains azadirachtin, a natural insecticide.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and is widely used in traditional medicine.
