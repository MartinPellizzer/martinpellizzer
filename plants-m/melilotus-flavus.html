Melilotus flavus

Melilotus flavus, commonly known as yellow sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
