Miconia impetiolaris var. spruceana

Miconia impetiolaris var. spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce's miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forest environments. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive yellowish-green color.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal through wind or animals.
