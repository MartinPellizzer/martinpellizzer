Mammillaria grandiflora

Mammillaria grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and bright green to blue-green stems covered in small, tubercles. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
