Metrodorea atropurpurea

Metrodorea atropurpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf ironwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black bark and elliptical, waxy leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits that attract various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ecological value make it a notable species in its native habitat.
