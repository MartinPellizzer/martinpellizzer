Mimulus nanus var. mephiticus

Mimulus nanus var. mephiticus, commonly known as the stinking monkeyflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Its unique, pungent odor is thought to attract specific pollinators like flies and beetles.
