Megalastrum brevipubens

Megalastrum brevipubens, commonly known as the short-hairless mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and white, waxy flowers. Due to its parasitic nature, it can have a significant impact on the health of its host trees.
