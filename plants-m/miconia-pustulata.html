Miconia pustulata

Miconia pustulata, commonly known as the pustular miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a globose, fleshy capsule that splits open to release numerous small, black seeds.

Miconia pustulata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in tropical rainforests.
