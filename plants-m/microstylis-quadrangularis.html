Microstylis quadrangularis

Microstylis quadrangularis, commonly known as the square-stemmed microstylis, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

This plant is characterized by its quadrangular, or four-sided, stems, which are typically slender and erect. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the variety. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream edges.
