Machaerium juglandifolium

Machaerium juglandifolium, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain the edible seeds, which are harvested for their nutritional value.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
