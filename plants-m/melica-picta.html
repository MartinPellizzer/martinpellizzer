Melica picta

Melica picta, commonly known as the painted brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with flowers that range in color from green to reddish-brown.

Melica picta plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
