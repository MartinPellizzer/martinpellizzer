Melastoma aromaticum

Melastoma aromaticum, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten raw or used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Melastoma aromaticum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
