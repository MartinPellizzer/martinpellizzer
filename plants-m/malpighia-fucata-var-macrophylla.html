Malpighia fucata var. macrophylla

Malpighia fucata var. macrophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian passionfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-yellow berries that contain numerous seeds surrounded by a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring alternate, simple, and elliptical leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive corona of filaments.

The botanical species is part of the Malpighiaceae family and is valued for both its ornamental beauty and edible fruits.
