Morongia pilosa

Morongia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy morong, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Morongia pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
