Melocactus tenuispinus

Melocactus tenuispinus, commonly known as the narrow-spined melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and dense clusters of small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It has a slow growth rate and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
