Myrtus sericocalyx

Myrtus sericocalyx, commonly known as silverleaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Myrtus sericocalyx is also valued for its use in aromatherapy and as a source of essential oils.
