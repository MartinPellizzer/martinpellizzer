Maesa perlaria var. perlaria

Maesa perlaria var. perlaria, commonly known as the silverleaf maesa, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
