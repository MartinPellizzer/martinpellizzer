Maerua brunnescens

Maerua brunnescens, commonly known as the yellow bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.
