Mentha × villosa var. serrigera

Mentha × villosa var. serrigera, commonly known as the serrated woolly mint, is a hybrid plant native to parts of Asia and the United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and a strong, minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
