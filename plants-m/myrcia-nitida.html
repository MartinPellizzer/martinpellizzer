Myrcia nitida

Myrcia nitida, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers and glossy, elliptical leaves. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Myrcia nitida is often found in humid, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
