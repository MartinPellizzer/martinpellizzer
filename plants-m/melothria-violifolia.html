Melothria violifolia

Melothria violifolia, commonly known as the violet leaf melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squash.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, green, edible fruits that resemble tiny melons. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet hue on the underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires a trellis or support for its vining growth habit.

The flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are followed by the development of its unique, flavorful fruit.
