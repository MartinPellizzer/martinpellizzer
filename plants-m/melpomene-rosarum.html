Melpomene rosarum

Melpomene rosarum, commonly known as the rose mallow, is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, rose-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Melpomene rosarum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
