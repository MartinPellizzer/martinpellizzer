Morinda calycina

Morinda calycina, commonly known as the flame flower or red morinda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of bright red or orange flowers, which are often visited by birds and insects. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Morinda calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures for medicinal purposes.
