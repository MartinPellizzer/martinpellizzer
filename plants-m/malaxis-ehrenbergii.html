Malaxis ehrenbergii

Malaxis ehrenbergii, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, often branching stem and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and the plant has a short flowering period.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple structure and is part of the Orchidaceae family.
