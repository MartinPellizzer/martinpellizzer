Myrcia bredemeyeriana

Myrcia bredemeyeriana, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a strong aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
