Manilkara gaumeri

Manilkara gaumeri, commonly known as the Guatemalan sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible fruits with a sweet, creamy flesh. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, while the tree's latex has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
