Myrcia tylophylla

Myrcia tylophylla, commonly known as the velvet myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Myrcia tylophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a pleasant, spicy fragrance.
