Melolobium canaliculatum

Melolobium canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed melolobium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that have prominent longitudinal veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its fibrous roots are adapted to nutrient-poor soils, and it is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
