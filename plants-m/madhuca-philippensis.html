Madhuca philippensis

Madhuca philippensis, commonly known as the Philippine mahogany or white tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible, round, yellow fruits.

Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a significant resource in local industries.
