Mentha nummularia

Mentha nummularia, commonly known as penny mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, round, mint-scented leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces tiny white or pale blue flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary uses.
