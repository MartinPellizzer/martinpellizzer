Muehlenbeckia hypogaea

Muehlenbeckia hypogaea, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features silvery-gray leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its common name. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
