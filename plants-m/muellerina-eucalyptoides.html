Muellerina eucalyptoides

Muellerina eucalyptoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallee, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is commonly found in mallee woodlands.

Muellerina eucalyptoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
