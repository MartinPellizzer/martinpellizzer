Macropsychanthus densiflorus

Macropsychanthus densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflowered macropsychanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring, producing vibrant yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
