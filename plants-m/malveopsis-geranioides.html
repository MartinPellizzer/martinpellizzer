Malveopsis geranioides

Malveopsis geranioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as hibiscus and cotton. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants with both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
