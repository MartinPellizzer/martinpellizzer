Machaonia brasiliensis var. vestita

Machaonia brasiliensis var. vestita, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The variety 'vestita' is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, adding to its unique botanical appearance.
