Myrtus roraimensis

Myrtus roraimensis, commonly known as the Roraima myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tepuis of Venezuela and Guyana.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, although further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
