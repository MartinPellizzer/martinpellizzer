Mallotus anisopodus

Mallotus anisopodus, commonly known as the broad-leafed hongkong tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive, woody, three-lobed capsules as its fruit. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
