Myrcia lozanoi

Myrcia lozanoi, commonly known as the Lozano's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in understorey layers of tropical forests.

Myrcia lozanoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
