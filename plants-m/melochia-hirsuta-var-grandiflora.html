Melochia hirsuta var. grandiflora

Melochia hirsuta var. grandiflora, commonly known as the hairy melochia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
