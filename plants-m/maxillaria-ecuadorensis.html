Maxillaria ecuadorensis

Maxillaria ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic. It blooms during the dry season, often in shades of pink, white, and purple.

Maxillaria ecuadorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
