Melaleuca densa var. pritzelii

Melaleuca densa var. pritzelii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from red to pink, which are produced in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
