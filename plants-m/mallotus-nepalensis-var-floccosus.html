Mallotus nepalensis var. floccosus

Mallotus nepalensis var. floccosus, commonly known as the silky mallot, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spiny fruits. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.
