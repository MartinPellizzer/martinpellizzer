Myrcia velutina var. canescens

Myrcia velutina var. canescens, commonly known as the silver myrtle, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in humid, shaded forest environments.
