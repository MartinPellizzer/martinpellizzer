Manjekia maturbongsii

Manjekia maturbongsii, commonly known as the Maturbongs giant waterlily, is a rare aquatic plant native to the freshwater habitats of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, providing a floating platform for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with abundant sunlight and nutrient-rich substrates.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
