Mollinedia laurina

Mollinedia laurina, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive golden hue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
