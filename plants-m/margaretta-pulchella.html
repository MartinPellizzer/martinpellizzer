Margaretta pulchella

Margaretta pulchella, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Margaretta pulchella is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in some regions.
