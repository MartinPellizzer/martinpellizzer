Melica transsilvanica subsp. klokovii

Melica transsilvanica subsp.

klokovii, commonly known as the Transylvanian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
