Myrcia oerstediana

Myrcia oerstediana, commonly known as the Oersted's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are usually glossy and oval in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Myrcia oerstediana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
