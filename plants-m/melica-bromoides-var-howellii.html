Melica bromoides var. howellii

Melica bromoides var. howellii, commonly known as Howell's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, blue-green flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
