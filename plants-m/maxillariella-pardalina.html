Maxillariella pardalina

Maxillariella pardalina, commonly known as the Pink Lady's Slipper, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers that resemble a slipper, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base of the plant.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a potted plant.
