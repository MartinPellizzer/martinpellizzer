Microlepia uncinella, commonly known as the hook fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wiry, and often curved fronds that resemble small hooks, hence its common name. Microlepia uncinella is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also found in various parts of Asia and the Pacific.
