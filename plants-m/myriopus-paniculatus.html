Myriopus paniculatus

Myriopus paniculatus, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Myriopus paniculatus is adapted to tolerate drought and salt, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
