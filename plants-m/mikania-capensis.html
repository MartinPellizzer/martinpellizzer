Mikania capensis

Mikania capensis, commonly known as the Cape Mikania, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
