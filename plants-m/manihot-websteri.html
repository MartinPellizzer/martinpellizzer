Manihot websteri

Manihot websteri, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are used to produce a starchy, edible root, making it an important crop in some African countries.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
