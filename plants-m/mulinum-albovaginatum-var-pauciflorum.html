Mulinum albovaginatum var. pauciflorum

Mulinum albovaginatum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the white-veined mullein, is a variant of the mullein plant characterized by its reduced flower count.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant features large, velvety gray-green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers, when present, are typically yellow and borne in clusters at the leaf axils.

The species is adapted to grow in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
