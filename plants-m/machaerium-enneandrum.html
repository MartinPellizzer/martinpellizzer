Machaerium enneandrum

Machaerium enneandrum, commonly known as the nine-lobed machaerium, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces legume pods that contain small, hard seeds.

Machaerium enneandrum is often found in moist, tropical habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
