Melissa abyssinica

Melissa abyssinica, commonly known as Ethiopian lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Melissa abyssinica thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
