Myrcianthes rhopaloides

Myrcianthes rhopaloides, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive red bark and prop roots that help stabilize coastal soils. The plant thrives in saline and brackish water environments, making it a key component of mangrove ecosystems. Its small, white flowers are followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed.

Myrcianthes rhopaloides plays an important role in coastal protection and provides habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
