Montia fontana subsp. lamprosperma

Montia fontana subsp.

lamprosperma, commonly known as the shining leaf montia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
