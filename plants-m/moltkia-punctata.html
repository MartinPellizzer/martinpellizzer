Moltkia punctata

Moltkia punctata, commonly known as the redstem filaree, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from spring to early summer.

Moltkia punctata is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
