Myristica subcordata var. gigacarpa

Myristica subcordata var. gigacarpa, commonly known as the giant nutmeg tree, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces distinctive, fragrant fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green, often clustered in panicles.

The species is valued for its unique botanical features and historical significance in herbal remedies and spice trade.
