Macadamia ternifolia

Macadamia ternifolia, commonly known as the Hawaiian macadamia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Hawaii.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 6 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by hard, woody pods containing its edible seeds. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

This plant is well adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat.
