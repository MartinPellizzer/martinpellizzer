Muhlenbergia hirtula

Muhlenbergia hirtula, commonly known as hairy muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silver-green foliage. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Muhlenbergia hirtula is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
