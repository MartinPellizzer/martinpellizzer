Microcalamus

Microcalamus, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often clumping growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, inconspicuous flowers. Microcalamus species are often found in moist, temperate environments, such as along streams or in shaded forest understories.

They play an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
