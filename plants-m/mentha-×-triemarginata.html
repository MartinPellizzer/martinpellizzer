Mentha × triemarginata

Mentha × triemarginata, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid perennial plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its aromatic leaves contain high concentrations of nepetalactone, which is responsible for its distinctive effects on cats.

Mentha × triemarginata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal remedies.
