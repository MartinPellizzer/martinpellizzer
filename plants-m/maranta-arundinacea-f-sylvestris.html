Maranta arundinacea f. sylvestris

Maranta arundinacea f. sylvestris, commonly known as the forest arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is native to the humid forests of Central and South America. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of green, silver, and red hues, which are highly ornamental. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-draining, rich soil to flourish.

While it is not typically grown for its edible roots like the standard arrowroot, it is prized as a decorative houseplant for its striking appearance.
