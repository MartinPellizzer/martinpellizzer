Mattia emarginata

Mattia emarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, waxy leaves and a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, thriving in disturbed soils and open habitats. It produces a capsule fruit that releases seeds with pappus for wind dispersal.

Mattia emarginata is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny, dry environments.
