Morus longifolia

Morus longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
