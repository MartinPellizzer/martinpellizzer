Mimosa lanuginosa var. raduliformis

Mimosa lanuginosa var. raduliformis, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both natural and cultivated settings.
