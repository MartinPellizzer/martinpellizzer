Millettia gentilii

Millettia gentilii, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisine and medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
