Mespilus azarolus var. chlorocarpa

Mespilus azarolus var. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green persimmon, is a variant of the common persimmon native to parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, green skin that turns yellow or red when mature. The plant produces small, orange berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
