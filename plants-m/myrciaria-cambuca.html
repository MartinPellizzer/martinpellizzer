Myrciaria cambuca

Myrciaria cambuca, commonly known as the Brazilian blackcurrant, is a species of shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are highly aromatic. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is rich in antioxidants and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
