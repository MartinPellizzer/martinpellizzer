Maximowiczia amurensis

Maximowiczia amurensis, commonly known as the Amur grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it blooms in the summer months.

Maximowiczia amurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
