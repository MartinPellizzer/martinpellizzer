Maba maingayi

Maba maingayi, commonly known as the "happy tree," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, light green bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Maba maingayi is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a useful species for reforestation and soil stabilization.
