Monodora dewevrei

Monodora dewevrei, commonly known as African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble breadfruit. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been traditionally used for food and medicinal purposes.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
