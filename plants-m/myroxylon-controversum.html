Myroxylon controversum

Myroxylon controversum, commonly known as the Copaiba tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall stature, reaching up to 30 meters in height, and its thick, aromatic bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with opposite arrangement. Its most notable botanical feature is the production of a resinous sap, which is harvested for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

The species is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
