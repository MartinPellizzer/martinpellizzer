Malus redolens

Malus redolens, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, bright red fruits that are edible and highly valued for their sweet flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Malus redolens is often cultivated in gardens for both its ornamental value and the delicious fruit it yields.
