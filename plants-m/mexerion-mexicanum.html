Mexerion mexicanum

Mexerion mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Mexerion mexicanum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
