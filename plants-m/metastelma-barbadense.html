Metastelma barbadense

Metastelma barbadense, commonly known as the Barbados nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring woody stems and elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue to black berries. It is often found in coastal areas and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
