Mentha brevidentata

Mentha brevidentata, commonly known as narrow-toothed mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Mentha brevidentata is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent.
