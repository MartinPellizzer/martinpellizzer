Miconia pichinchensis

Miconia pichinchensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It is typically found in moist, shaded habitats within cloud forests, where it thrives in the humid, cool climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
