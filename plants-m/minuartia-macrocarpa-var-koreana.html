Minuartia macrocarpa var. koreana

Minuartia macrocarpa var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in a fine layer of white trichomes, which help reflect sunlight.

This species is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.
