Myosotis cespitosa var. confusa

Myosotis cespitosa var. confusa, commonly known as the clustered forget-me-not, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has a compact, clump-forming growth habit with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
