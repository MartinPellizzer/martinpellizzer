Mapouria simira

Mapouria simira, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its timber but is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
