Mammillaria scrippsiana var. armeria

Mammillaria scrippsiana var. armeria, commonly known as the Armeria Mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical to cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which bloom from the areoles along the stems. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in arid to semi-arid environments.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
