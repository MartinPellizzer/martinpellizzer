Manihot pentaphylla subsp. pentaphylla

Manihot pentaphylla subsp.

pentaphylla, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa. It features a rhizomatous root system that produces edible tubers, which are rich in starch and used as a food source.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth texture, while the flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The species is valued both for its nutritional content and its role in traditional medicine.
