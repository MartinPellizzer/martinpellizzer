Maba elliotii

Maba elliotii, commonly known as the Elliot's maba, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing benefits.
