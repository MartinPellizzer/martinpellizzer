Melampyrum roseum f. pubescens

Melampyrum roseum f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy yellow vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Orobanchaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems covered in fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive hairy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
