Mandirola multiflora var. gardneriana

Mandirola multiflora var. gardneriana, commonly known as the Gardner's mandirola, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, clustered inflorescences that produce small, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
