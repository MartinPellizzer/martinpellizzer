Mesembryanthemum rapaceum

Mesembryanthemum rapaceum, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
