Mariacantha maculosa

Mariacantha maculosa, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, spotted leaves and slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil efficiently.

The plant's unique floral structure and ornamental features make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
