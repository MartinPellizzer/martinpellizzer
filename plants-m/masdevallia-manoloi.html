Masdevallia manoloi

Masdevallia manoloi, commonly known as the Manoloi orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as a miniature orchid, with compact growth habits and delicate, showy flowers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Masdevallia manoloi is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
