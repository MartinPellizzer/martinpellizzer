Melilotus sulcatus subsp. segetalis

Melilotus sulcatus subsp.

segetalis, commonly known as field melilot, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which are borne on the upper part of the stems. It is native to Europe and western Asia and thrives in disturbed soils, often found in fields and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
