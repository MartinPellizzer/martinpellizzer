Machaerium oblongifolium var. villosulum

Machaerium oblongifolium var. villosulum, commonly known as the hairy machaerium, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and panicles of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically oblong and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.
