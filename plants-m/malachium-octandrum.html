Malachium octandrum

Malachium octandrum, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to North America and typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are upright and sometimes branched.

Malachium octandrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
