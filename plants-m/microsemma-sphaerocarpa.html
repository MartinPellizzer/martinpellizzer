Microsemma sphaerocarpa

Microsemma sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-fruit or red globe berry, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, round, red fruits that are visually striking. The plant's leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. The fruits are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
