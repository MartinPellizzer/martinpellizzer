Mesosphaerum pulegioides

Mesosphaerum pulegioides, commonly known as the creeping mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Mesosphaerum pulegioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
