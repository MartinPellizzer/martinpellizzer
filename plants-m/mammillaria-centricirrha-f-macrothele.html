Mammillaria centricirrha f. macrothele

Mammillaria centricirrha f. macrothele, commonly known as the giant mammillaria, is a rare and striking cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, rounded stems that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate contrast to its spiny exterior. Its thick, ribbed structure helps it store water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is highly sought after by cactus enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
