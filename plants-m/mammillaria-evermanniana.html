Mammillaria evermanniana

Mammillaria evermanniana, commonly known as the Evermann's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring prominent tubercles and spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
