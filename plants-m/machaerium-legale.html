Machaerium legale

Machaerium legale, commonly known as the legal machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Machaerium legale is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
