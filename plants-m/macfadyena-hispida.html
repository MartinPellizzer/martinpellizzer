Macfadyena hispida

Macfadyena hispida, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and can be ground into a flour for culinary purposes.
