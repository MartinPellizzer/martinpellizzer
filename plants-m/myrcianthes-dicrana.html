Myrcianthes dicrana

Myrcianthes dicrana, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive red bark and its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it a common inhabitant of mangrove ecosystems. The plant has prop roots that help stabilize the soil and filter out salt from the water. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by edible berries that provide food for various wildlife species.

Myrcianthes dicrana plays a crucial role in coastal protection and biodiversity support in tropical regions.
