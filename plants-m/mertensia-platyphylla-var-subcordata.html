Mertensia platyphylla var. subcordata

Mertensia platyphylla var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are usually narrow and elongated, with a slightly heart-shaped base. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
