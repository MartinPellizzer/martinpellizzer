Myrciaria disticha var. disticha

Myrciaria disticha var. disticha, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. The berries are edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and economic significance.
