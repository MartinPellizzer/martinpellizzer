Manilkara duckei

Manilkara duckei, commonly known as the wild sapodilla, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by inedible, round fruits with a thick, bumpy skin. Its latex sap has been traditionally used for various purposes, including as a chewing gum.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
