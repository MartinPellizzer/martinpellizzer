Mullaghera communis

Mullaghera communis, commonly known as the Irish mountain heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the western regions of Europe.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense cover that supports local wildlife.

Mullaghera communis plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitats.
