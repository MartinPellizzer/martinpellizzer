Myrcia jaboticaba

Myrcia jaboticaba, commonly known as the Brazilian grapefruit or jaboticaba, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple, egg-shaped fruits that are edible. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of its distinctive fruit. The leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
