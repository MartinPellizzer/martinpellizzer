Masmenia

Masmenia, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are annual or biennial herbs. The plants are characterized by their basal rosettes of leaves and yellow composite flowers that resemble dandelions. They are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
