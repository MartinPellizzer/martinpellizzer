Malvaviscus pulvinatus

Malvaviscus pulvinatus, commonly known as the red-flowered mallows, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
