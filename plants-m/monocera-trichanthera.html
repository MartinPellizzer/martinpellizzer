Monocera trichanthera

Monocera trichanthera, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with soft, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to stabilize sandy soils, making it useful for erosion control.

Monocera trichanthera is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and disturbed areas.
