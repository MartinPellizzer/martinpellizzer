Mimosa sinuata

Mimosa sinuata, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces fluffy, spherical seed pods that split open when mature, releasing seeds with silky filaments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Mimosa sinuata is also known for its sensitive leaves, which fold shut when touched, a trait that adds to its intriguing botanical appeal.
