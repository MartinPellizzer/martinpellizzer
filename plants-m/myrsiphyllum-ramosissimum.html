Myrsiphyllum ramosissimum

Myrsiphyllum ramosissimum, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branched shrub form and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

Myrsiphyllum ramosissimum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
