Myosotis asiatica subsp. kolymensis

Myosotis asiatica subsp.

kolymensis, commonly known as the Siberian Forget-me-not, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a resilient plant with ornamental value in cooler gardens.
