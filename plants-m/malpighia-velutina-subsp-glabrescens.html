Malpighia velutina subsp. glabrescens

Malpighia velutina subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the velvet passionfruit, is a species native to Central and South America. It is a small shrub that produces edible, orange-colored fruits with a sweet and tart flavor.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which become smoother as the plant matures. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

The fruit is valued for its culinary use and is also used in traditional medicine.
