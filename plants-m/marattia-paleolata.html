Marattia paleolata

Marattia paleolata, commonly known as the ancient fern, is a rare and primitive species belonging to the family Marattiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, dichotomously branched fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive structure called a sorus, which contains the spore-producing structures. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ancient lineage and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research.
