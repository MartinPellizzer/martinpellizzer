Malveopsis aurantiaca

Malveopsis aurantiaca, commonly known as the scarlet mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Malveopsis aurantiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
