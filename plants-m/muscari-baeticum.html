Muscari baeticum

Muscari baeticum, commonly known as the Baetican grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling those of the grape hyacinth. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to Mediterranean climates. Its bulbs are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.

Muscari baeticum is also valued for its fragrance and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
