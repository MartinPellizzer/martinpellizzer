Millettia dunniana

Millettia dunniana, commonly known as the Chinese silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain seeds enclosed in a silky, fibrous covering. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree also has traditional uses in herbal medicine due to its potential pharmacological properties.
