Melandrium verrucosoalatum

Melandrium verrucosoalatum, commonly known as the warty sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Melandrium verrucosoalatum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
