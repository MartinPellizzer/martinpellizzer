Marrubium adfine

Marrubium adfine, commonly known as narrow-leaved horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its medicinal uses throughout history.

Marrubium adfine is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its bitter compounds and potential health benefits.
