Maerua triphylla var. calophylla

Maerua triphylla var. calophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf maerua, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
