Melocalamus scandens

Melocalamus scandens, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a member of the fern family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It is a climbing plant that uses its rhizomes to anchor itself to trees and other structures in its habitat. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of its fronds, a key feature of fern reproduction.

Melocalamus scandens plays an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling in its native range.
