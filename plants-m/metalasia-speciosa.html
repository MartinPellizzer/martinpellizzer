Metalasia speciosa

Metalasia speciosa, commonly known as the golden metalasia, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant yellow bracts that resemble petals, giving it a tropical appearance. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are typically orange to red in color, adding to its ornamental value.

Metalasia speciosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
