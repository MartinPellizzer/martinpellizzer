Mallotus pseudoverticillatus

Mallotus pseudoverticillatus, commonly known as the false verticillate mallot, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, palmate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
