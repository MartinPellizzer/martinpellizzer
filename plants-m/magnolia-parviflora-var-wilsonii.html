Magnolia parviflora var. wilsonii

Magnolia parviflora var. wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's small-flowered magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blossoms.
