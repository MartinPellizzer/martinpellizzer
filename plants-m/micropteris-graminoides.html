Micropteris graminoides

Micropteris graminoides, commonly known as the slender water fern, is a fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like fronds that are typically less than 10 inches in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along streams. Its reproductive structures include sori that are arranged in a single row along the margins of the fronds.

The slender water fern plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
