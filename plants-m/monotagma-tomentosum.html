Monotagma tomentosum

Monotagma tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly bramble, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide structural support and protection. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Monotagma tomentosum is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
