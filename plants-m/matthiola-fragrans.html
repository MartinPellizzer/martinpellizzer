Matthiola fragrans

Matthiola fragrans, commonly known as sweet Alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its low-growing habit and profuse clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and early summer, producing a variety of colors including white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy as well.

Matthiola fragrans is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
