Malaxis sagittata

Malaxis sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that resemble an arrowhead. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which are typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Malaxis sagittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
