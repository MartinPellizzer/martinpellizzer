Myrtillus uliginosus

Myrtillus uliginosus, commonly known as the bog bilberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to moist, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands.

It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it blooms in the spring.

Myrtillus uliginosus plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
