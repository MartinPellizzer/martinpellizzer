Miconia graciliflora

Miconia graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered miconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to white flowers that are typically borne on long, slender peduncles. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Miconia graciliflora is known for its rapid growth and is often considered an invasive species in certain regions.
