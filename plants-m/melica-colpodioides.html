Melica colpodioides

Melica colpodioides, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.

Melica colpodioides is an important component of native plant communities and supports a variety of pollinators and herbivores.
