Melocactus pinguis var. areolosus

Melocactus pinguis var. areolosus, commonly known as the fat melon cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that can grow up to 30 cm in height. The plant features a dense covering of spines and a central areole with clusters of white, woolly hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in summer.

It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
