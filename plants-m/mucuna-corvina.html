Mucuna corvina

Mucuna corvina, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, curling leaves that resemble tendrils. The plant produces clusters of small, hairy pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Mucuna corvina is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
