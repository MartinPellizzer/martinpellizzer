Macrochaetium

Macrochaetium, commonly known as the African grass or velvet grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, hairy leaves. The plants typically grow in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist or swampy habitats. Macrochaetium species are known for their ability to form dense mats, which can help prevent soil erosion.
