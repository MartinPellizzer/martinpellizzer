Matthiola calyptrata

Matthiola calyptrata, commonly known as the Mediterranean wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found growing in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Matthiola calyptrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
