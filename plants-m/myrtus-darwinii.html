Myrtus darwinii

Myrtus darwinii, commonly known as Darwin's myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrtus darwinii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
