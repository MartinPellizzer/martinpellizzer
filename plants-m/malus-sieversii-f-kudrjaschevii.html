Malus sieversii f. kudrjaschevii

Malus sieversii f. kudrjaschevii, commonly known as the Kudrjaschevii crabapple, is a botanical variant of the Siberian crabapple.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive floral features. The plant typically produces small, showy flowers in spring, often followed by edible, tart fruits. It is native to Central Asia and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

This form is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
