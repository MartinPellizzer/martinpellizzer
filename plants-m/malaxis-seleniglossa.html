Malaxis seleniglossa

Malaxis seleniglossa, commonly known as the silver-tongued orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive, lip-like structure that aids in pollination.

Malaxis seleniglossa is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
