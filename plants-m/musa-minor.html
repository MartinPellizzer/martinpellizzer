Musa minor

Musa minor, commonly known as the dwarf banana plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens and as a houseplant.
