Metagonia penaeoides

Metagonia penaeoides, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, adapting it to harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor deeply in sandy soils and access underground water sources.

Metagonia penaeoides is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
