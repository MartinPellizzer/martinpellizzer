Mimosa guaviarensis

Mimosa guaviarensis, commonly known as the Guaviare mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the Amazonian region of Colombia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves that fold when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic movement. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in dense undergrowth.

Its flowers are typically yellow or pale green, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
