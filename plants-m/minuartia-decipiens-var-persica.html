Minuartia decipiens var. persica

Minuartia decipiens var. persica, commonly known as the Persian rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
