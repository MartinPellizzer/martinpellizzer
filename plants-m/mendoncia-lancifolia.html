Mendoncia lancifolia

Mendoncia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mendoncia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
