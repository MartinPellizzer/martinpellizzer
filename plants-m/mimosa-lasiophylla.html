Mimosa lasiophylla

Mimosa lasiophylla, commonly known as the silky mimosas, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soils make it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
