Myriophyllum chuquitense

Myriophyllum chuquitense, commonly known as Chuquitense watermilfoil, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. This plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. Its roots help stabilize sediments, contributing to water clarity and habitat structure.

Myriophyllum chuquitense is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
