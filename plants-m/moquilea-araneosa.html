Moquilea araneosa

Moquilea araneosa, commonly known as the spider lichen, is a unique lichen species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is characterized by its intricate, web-like thallus structure, which gives it its common name. The plant exhibits a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium, which allows it to survive in harsh conditions. It is often found on rock surfaces or soil in desert regions.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Moquilea araneosa plays a minor but ecologically significant role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitats for other organisms.
