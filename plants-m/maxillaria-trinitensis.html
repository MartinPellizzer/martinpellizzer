Maxillaria trinitensis

Maxillaria trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidad orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and linear leaves, typically growing in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, often featuring white or pale pink petals and a contrasting lip. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

Maxillaria trinitensis is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
