Miconia scorpioides

Miconia scorpioides, commonly known as the scarlet miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its broad, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds covered in a sticky coating.

Miconia scorpioides is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.
