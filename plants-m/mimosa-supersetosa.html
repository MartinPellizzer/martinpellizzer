Mimosa supersetosa

Mimosa supersetosa, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that resemble the branches of a tree, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions contribute to its adaptability in different climates.
