Magnolia sumatrana

Magnolia sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran magnolia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous tree, typically reaching heights of up to 30 meters, with smooth, greyish bark. Its leaves are broad, elliptical, and have a glossy green surface.

The flowers are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators, making it an important species in its native ecosystem.
