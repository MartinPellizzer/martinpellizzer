Myrtoleucodendron trichophyllum

Myrtoleucodendron trichophyllum, commonly known as the silver-leaved myrtle, is a rare and unique plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which results from dense, waxy leaf surfaces that reflect light. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
