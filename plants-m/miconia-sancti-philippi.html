Miconia sancti-philippi

Miconia sancti-philippi, commonly known as the Philippi's miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. It is often found in humid, lowland tropical forests and can grow up to several meters in height.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and is also considered an invasive species in some regions.
