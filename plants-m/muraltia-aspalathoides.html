Muraltia aspalathoides

Muraltia aspalathoides, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it valuable for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
