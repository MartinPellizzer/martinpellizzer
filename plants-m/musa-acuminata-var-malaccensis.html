Musa acuminata var. malaccensis

Musa acuminata var. malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca banana, is a wild species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with large, broad leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, finger-like fruits. It is closely related to the common banana and is often used in botanical research for its genetic diversity.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential agricultural applications.
