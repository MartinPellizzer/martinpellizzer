Maxillaria montezumae

Maxillaria montezumae, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to central Mexico.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are oval and grass-like, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Maxillaria montezumae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
