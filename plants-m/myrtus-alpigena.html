Myrtus alpigena

Myrtus alpigena, commonly known as the alpine myrtle, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or purple when mature. Myrtus alpigena is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
