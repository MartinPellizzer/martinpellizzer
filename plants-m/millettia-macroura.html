Millettia macroura

Millettia macroura, commonly known as the long-stalked millettia, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its twining stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Millettia macroura is often found in humid forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
