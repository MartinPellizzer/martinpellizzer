Myrcia dichrophylla

Myrcia dichrophylla, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known species like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Myrcia dichrophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
