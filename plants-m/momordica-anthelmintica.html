Momordica anthelmintica

Momordica anthelmintica, commonly known as the African cucumber or wild cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers and elongated, spiny fruits that mature to a dark green or orange color. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly its anthelmintic effects, which help in expelling parasitic worms.
