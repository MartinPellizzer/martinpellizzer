Malaxis scyllae

Malaxis scyllae, commonly known as the Scylla orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Malaxis scyllae is often found in shaded, humid environments such as forest understories.
