Maxillaria amabilis

Maxillaria amabilis, commonly known as the sweet orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Maxillaria amabilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
