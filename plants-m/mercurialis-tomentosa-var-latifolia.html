Mercurialis tomentosa var. latifolia

Mercurialis tomentosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved mercury plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. It has a creeping growth habit and can form dense mats, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open spaces.
