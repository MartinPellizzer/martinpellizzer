Macroditassa lagoensis

Macroditassa lagoensis, commonly known as the Lake Lagoon Grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific wetland habitats in South America.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its moist environment.

Macroditassa lagoensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
