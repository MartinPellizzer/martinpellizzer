Morinda muscosa

Morinda muscosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe, containing several seeds. Morinda muscosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
