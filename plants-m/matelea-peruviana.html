Matelea peruviana

Matelea peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian moonflower, is a climbing plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Matelea peruviana is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique nocturnal flowering habit.
