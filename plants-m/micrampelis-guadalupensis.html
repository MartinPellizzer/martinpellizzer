Micrampelis guadalupensis

Micrampelis guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupensis climbing plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with slender stems that can ascend or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Native to Mexico, it thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.
