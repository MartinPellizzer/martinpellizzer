Melampyrum roseum var. roseum

Melampyrum roseum var. roseum, commonly known as the rose-flowered burning bush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The seed pods are slender and elongated, containing several small, flat seeds that are dispersed by wind.
