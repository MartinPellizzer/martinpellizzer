Myrcia kunthiana var. latissima

Myrcia kunthiana var. latissima, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrtle, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by red or orange berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
