Mucuna huberi

Mucuna huberi, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive purple or green pods that contain small, round seeds. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil and promoting growth in agricultural settings. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, and it produces a strong, aromatic scent.

Mucuna huberi is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of protein-rich seeds.
