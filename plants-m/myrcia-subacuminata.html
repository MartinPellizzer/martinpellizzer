Myrcia subacuminata

Myrcia subacuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Myrcia subacuminata is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
