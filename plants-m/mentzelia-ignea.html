Mentzelia ignea

Mentzelia ignea, commonly known as the red-flowered blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, red to orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Mentzelia ignea is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
