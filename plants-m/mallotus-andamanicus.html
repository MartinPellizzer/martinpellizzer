Mallotus andamanicus

Mallotus andamanicus, commonly known as the Andaman ironwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rough, fissured bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its hard, durable wood.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical evergreen forests.
