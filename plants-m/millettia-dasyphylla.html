Millettia dasyphylla

Millettia dasyphylla, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces pods that contain flat, dark brown seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and economic purposes in its native region.
