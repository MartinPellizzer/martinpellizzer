Malpighia media

Malpighia media, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red berries that appear in clusters, which are edible and often consumed by birds and humans. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas in the wild.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
