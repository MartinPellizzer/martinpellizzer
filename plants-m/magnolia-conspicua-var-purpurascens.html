Magnolia conspicua var. purpurascens

Magnolia conspicua var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple leaves that contrast with its white or pinkish flowers. The flowers are large, showy, and typically appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for temperate climates.
