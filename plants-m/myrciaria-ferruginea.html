Myrciaria ferruginea

Myrciaria ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Myrciaria ferruginea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
