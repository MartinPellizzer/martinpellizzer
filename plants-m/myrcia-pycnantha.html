Myrcia pycnantha

Myrcia pycnantha, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Myrcia pycnantha is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant berries and attractive foliage.
