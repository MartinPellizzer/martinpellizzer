Myrcia klotzschiana

Myrcia klotzschiana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces edible red berries that are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
