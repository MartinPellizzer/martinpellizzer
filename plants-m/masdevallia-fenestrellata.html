Masdevallia fenestrellata

Masdevallia fenestrellata, commonly known as the windowed masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, fenestrated (window-like) leaves that provide structural support and aid in photosynthesis. The plant produces delicate, pendulous flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high air circulation.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a prized but challenging plant for cultivation.
