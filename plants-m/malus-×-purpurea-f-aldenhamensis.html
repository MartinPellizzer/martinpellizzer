Malus × purpurea f. aldenhamensis

Malus × purpurea f. aldenhamensis, commonly known as the Aldenham crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree characterized by its distinctive floral and foliar features.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Malus, known for its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a serrated edge, adding to its ornamental value. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its compact size and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
