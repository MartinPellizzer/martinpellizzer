Malus fontanesiana

Malus fontanesiana, commonly known as the Fontanesiana apple, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America, specifically in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers and attractive foliage. It produces small, edible apples that are often used in local cuisine.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
