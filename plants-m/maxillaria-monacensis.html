Maxillaria monacensis

Maxillaria monacensis, commonly known as the Monacense Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure with fleshy leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive lip that is often darker. Maxillaria monacensis thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both greenhouse cultivation and botanical gardens.
