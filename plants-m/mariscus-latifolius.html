Mariscus latifolius

Mariscus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf mariscus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mariscus latifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and floral display.
