Malus komarovii var. funiushanensis

Malus komarovii var. funiushanensis, commonly known as the Funiushan crabapple, is a rare and ornamental species native to China.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
