Maranta strobilifera

Maranta strobilifera, commonly known as the candleplant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes many ornamental plants known for their striking foliage. The plant is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, white flowers that resemble candles, hence its common name. Its leaves are typically dark green and often have intricate patterns or veins.

Maranta strobilifera is primarily grown as a decorative plant for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
