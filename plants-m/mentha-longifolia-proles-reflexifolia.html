Mentha longifolia proles reflexifolia

Mentha longifolia proles reflexifolia, commonly known as the reflexed mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine, hairy foliage. The leaves are lance-shaped, slightly serrated, and have a distinctive minty aroma when crushed. It produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
