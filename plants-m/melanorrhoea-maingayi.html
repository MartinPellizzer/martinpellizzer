Melanorrhoea maingayi

Melanorrhoea maingayi, commonly known as the black tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caesalpiniaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and distinctive fruit pods. The plant is known for its high nutritional value, particularly in its seeds, which are rich in protein and oil. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
