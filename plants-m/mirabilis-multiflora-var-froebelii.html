Mirabilis multiflora var. froebelii

Mirabilis multiflora var. froebelii, commonly known as the frostweed or rainbow flower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, yellow, and pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant is typically shrub-like, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
