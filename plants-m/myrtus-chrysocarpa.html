Myrtus chrysocarpa

Myrtus chrysocarpa, commonly known as golden berry myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, yellow to orange berries that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Myrtus chrysocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
