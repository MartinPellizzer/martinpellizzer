Mentha longifolia f. melesensis

Mentha longifolia f. melesensis, commonly known as Melesian mint, is a subspecies of the mint genus Mentha.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in terminal spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
