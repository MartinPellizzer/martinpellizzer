Memecylon ovatifolium

Memecylon ovatifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf memecylon, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of white or pink, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Memecylon ovatifolium is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
