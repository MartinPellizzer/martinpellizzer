Malva longipedunculata

Malva longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Malva longipedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
