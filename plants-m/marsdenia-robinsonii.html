Marsdenia robinsonii

Marsdenia robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, woody fruits. Its leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Marsdenia robinsonii is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
