Melocactus aciculosus var. adauctus

Melocactus aciculosus var. adauctus, commonly known as the Adauctus melocactus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

This variety is relatively slow-growing and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
