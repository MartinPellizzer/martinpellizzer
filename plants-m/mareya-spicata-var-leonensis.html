Mareya spicata var. leonensis

Mareya spicata var. leonensis, commonly known as the Leonese mareya, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally grown as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
