Matthiola incana subsp. pulchella

Matthiola incana subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, branched stems and clusters of bright pink or white flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
