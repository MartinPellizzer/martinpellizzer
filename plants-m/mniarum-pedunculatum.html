Mniarum pedunculatum

Mniarum pedunculatum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Mniarum pedunculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
