Melica calycina

Melica calycina, commonly known as the white bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. The plant has slender, hairless stems that often form dense tufts, and its leaves are narrow with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, with each spikelet containing two to three small, white to pale green flowers.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
