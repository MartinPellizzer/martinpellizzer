Melampodium angustifolium

Melampodium angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
