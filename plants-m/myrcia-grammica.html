Myrcia grammica

Myrcia grammica, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and can thrive in a range of soil types.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is also a subject of ecological concern.
