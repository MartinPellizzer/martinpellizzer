Mephitidia pauciflora

Mephitidia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered mephit, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the remote highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring, producing a faint, somewhat unpleasant odor. The plant has a short, stout stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its unique floral structure suggests adaptation to specific pollinators, though its reproductive biology remains poorly understood.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, Mephitidia pauciflora is considered critically endangered.
