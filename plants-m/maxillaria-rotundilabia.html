Maxillaria rotundilabia

Maxillaria rotundilabia, commonly known as the round-lipped maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive round lip. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, showcasing vibrant pink or purple petals. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Maxillaria rotundilabia thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate light conditions for optimal growth.
