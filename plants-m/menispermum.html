Menispermum

Menispermum, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Menispermaceae.

It consists of several species, many of which are native to tropical and temperate regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plants are characterized by their climbing habit, often with tendrils, and heart-shaped leaves. They produce small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels.

The berries that follow are usually red or yellow and contain several seeds.
