Muscari ciliatum var. glaucum

Muscari ciliatum var. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, with a powdery white or bluish bloom on the outer parts. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulb and are often covered with a waxy, bluish-gray coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and early spring blooming period.
