Malcolmia multisiliqua

Malcolmia multisiliqua, commonly known as the blueweed or field mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Malcolmia multisiliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its delicate floral display.
