Mammillaria parkinsonii var. dealbata

Mammillaria parkinsonii var. dealbata, commonly known as the White-Flowered Hedgehog Cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It features a globular or cylindrical stem with white, woolly spines and produces showy, pale yellow flowers during the spring. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal watering, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
