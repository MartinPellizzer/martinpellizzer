Micropora

Micropora, commonly known as the "garden orchid" or "pomegranate orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their small, delicate flowers and are native to tropical and subtropical regions. Micropora species typically have rhizomatous growth and produce showy, fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and red. They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
