Mespilodaphne fasciculata

Mespilodaphne fasciculata, commonly known as the slender sycamore, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lanceolate leaves that are arranged in whorls of three. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
