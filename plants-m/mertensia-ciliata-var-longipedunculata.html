Mertensia ciliata var. longipedunculata

Mertensia ciliata var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy margins, and its stems are often hairy as well. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is noted for its longer peduncles compared to the typical form, making it a distinctive species within the Mertensia genus.
