Masdevallia haematosticta

Masdevallia haematosticta, commonly known as the blood-stained masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and its showy, often colorful flowers that bloom in a spiral arrangement. The plant prefers cool, humid environments and requires high levels of light but not direct sunlight. Its flowers typically display a combination of red, orange, and yellow hues, with intricate patterns that make it highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.

Masdevallia haematosticta is known for its unique growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its natural habitat.
