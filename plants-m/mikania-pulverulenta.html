Mikania pulverulenta

Mikania pulverulenta, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican devil's bit, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences.

Mikania pulverulenta is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it both a common weed and an invasive species in many regions.
