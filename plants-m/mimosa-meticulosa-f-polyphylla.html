Mimosa meticulosa f. polyphylla

Mimosa meticulosa f. polyphylla, commonly known as the velvet mimosa, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple pairs of leaflets, giving the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique texture and vibrant blooms, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in both residential and public landscapes.
