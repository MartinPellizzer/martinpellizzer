Microlepia venusta

Microlepia venusta, commonly known as the golden chain fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow to golden-green fronds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrially on moist, shaded forest floors. Its fronds are usually long and narrow, with a distinctive, chain-like arrangement of pinnae.

Microlepia venusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady gardens or as a houseplant.
