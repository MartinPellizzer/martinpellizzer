Malope stipulacea var. laevigata

Malope stipulacea var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved malope, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features slender, woody stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and slightly hairy beneath. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers in spring, which are borne on elongated inflorescences. Its foliage has a distinctive aromatic scent, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
