Mesosetum caudiculatum

Mesosetum caudiculatum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental potential and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
