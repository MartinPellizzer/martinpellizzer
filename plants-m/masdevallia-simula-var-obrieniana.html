Masdevallia simula var. obrieniana

Masdevallia simula var. obrieniana, commonly known as the O'Brien's masdevallia, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces pendulous inflorescences with multiple flowers.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
