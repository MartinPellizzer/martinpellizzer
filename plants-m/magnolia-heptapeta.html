Magnolia heptapeta

Magnolia heptapeta, commonly known as the seven-petaled magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The flowers typically have seven petals, which is a distinguishing feature of the species. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
