Mentha × acutiserrata

Mentha × acutiserrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid mint plant that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and a strong, aromatic scent, which are typical features of the Mentha genus. This plant is native to regions in Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Mentha × acutiserrata is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds that contribute to its use in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
