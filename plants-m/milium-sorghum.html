Milium sorghum

Milium sorghum, commonly known as broomcorn millet, is an annual grass native to the Old World.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that resemble brooms, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm climates and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy crop.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its nutritional seeds and adaptability to various soil types.
