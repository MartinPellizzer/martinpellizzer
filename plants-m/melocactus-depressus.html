Melocactus depressus

Melocactus depressus, commonly known as the depressed melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are pollinated by insects. Its root system is adapted to absorb water efficiently in dry conditions.

Melocactus depressus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
