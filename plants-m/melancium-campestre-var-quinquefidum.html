Melancium campestre var. quinquefidum

Melancium campestre var. quinquefidum, commonly known as the five-angled melancium, is a distinctive plant characterized by its unique floral structure.

It features five prominent stamens that are often fused at the base, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its rugged aesthetic.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native environment.
