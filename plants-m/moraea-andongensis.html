Moraea andongensis

Moraea andongensis, commonly known as the Andong gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Korea.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink or purple hues. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
