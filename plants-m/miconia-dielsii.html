Miconia dielsii

Miconia dielsii, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed berries. Miconia dielsii is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
