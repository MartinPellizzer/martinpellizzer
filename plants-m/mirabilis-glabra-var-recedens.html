Mirabilis glabra var. recedens

Mirabilis glabra var. recedens, commonly known as the "night blooming cereus," is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a white throat, and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
