Matricaria oreades

Matricaria oreades, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually bright yellow, with a central disc that is often darker in color.

Matricaria oreades is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential calming effects.
