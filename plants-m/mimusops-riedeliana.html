Mimusops riedeliana

Mimusops riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white wax. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers, followed by orange to red berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
