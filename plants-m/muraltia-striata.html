Muraltia striata

Muraltia striata, commonly known as the African cherry or red mombasa, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, showy flowers that range from white to pink. The tree produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
