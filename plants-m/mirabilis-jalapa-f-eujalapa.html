Mirabilis jalapa f. eujalapa

Mirabilis jalapa f. eujalapa, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically open in the evening and close by morning. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, while the stems are erect and branching.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its unique blooming behavior.
