Melothria longepedunculata

Melothria longepedunculata, commonly known as the long peduncle bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces small, green, bitter fruits that are typically consumed when immature. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and small, lobed leaves.

The fruit develops a warty texture as it matures, and it is often used in traditional medicine.
