Mycopteris subtilis

Mycopteris subtilis, commonly known as the delicate fern, is a species of small, delicate fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, glabrous fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sporangia, are located on the underside of its leaves.

Mycopteris subtilis is often found in humid, tropical climates where it thrives in the understory of dense vegetation.
