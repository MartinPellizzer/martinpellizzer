Microphacos gracilis

Microphacos gracilis, commonly known as the slender microphacos, is a small, annual herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and features delicate, grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Microphacos gracilis is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
