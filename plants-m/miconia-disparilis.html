Miconia disparilis

Miconia disparilis, commonly known as the false hibiscus, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Miconia disparilis is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed habitats such as secondary forests and along riverbanks.
