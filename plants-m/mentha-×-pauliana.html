Mentha × pauliana

Mentha × pauliana, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between water mint (Mentha aquatica) and spearmint (Mentha spicata).

It is characterized by its square, erect stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are usually dark green in color. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Mentha × pauliana is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

It is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in teas, food flavoring, and traditional medicine.
