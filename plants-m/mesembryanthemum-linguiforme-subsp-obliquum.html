Mesembryanthemum linguiforme subsp. obliquum

Mesembryanthemum linguiforme subsp.

obliquum, commonly known as the oblique leaf ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
