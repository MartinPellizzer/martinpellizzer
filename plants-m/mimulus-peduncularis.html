Mimulus peduncularis

Mimulus peduncularis, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats, often found in streambanks and woodland edges. It has simple, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus peduncularis is a perennial herb that produces seed capsules containing numerous small, dry seeds.
