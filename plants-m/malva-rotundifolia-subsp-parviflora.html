Malva rotundifolia subsp. parviflora

Malva rotundifolia subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flowers compared to the typical M. rotundifolia.
