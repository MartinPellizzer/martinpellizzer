Miconia berghesiana

Miconia berghesiana, commonly known as the purple miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by distinctive, red, fleshy fruits. Miconia berghesiana is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
