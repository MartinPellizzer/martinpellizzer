Melancranis quinquangularis

Melancranis quinquangularis, commonly known as the five-angled melancranis, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its distinctive five-angled seed pods.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and ecological adaptations.
