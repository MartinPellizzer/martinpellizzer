Mertensia davurica var. ochroleuca

Mertensia davurica var. ochroleuca, commonly known as white heartleaf bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctively rough texture. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
