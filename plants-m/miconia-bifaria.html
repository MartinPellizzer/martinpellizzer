Miconia bifaria

Miconia bifaria, commonly known as the tropical beauty leaf, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with distinctive white or silver veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Miconia bifaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
