Morus javanica

Morus javanica, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces small, fuzzy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. It is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used to feed silkworms in sericulture.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
