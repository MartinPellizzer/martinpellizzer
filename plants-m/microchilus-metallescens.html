Microchilus metallescens

Microchilus metallescens, commonly known as the metallic sunflower, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow flowers that resemble those of the sunflower, though it belongs to the Asteraceae family. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. Its most distinctive feature is its metallic sheen, which gives it its common name and helps reflect sunlight to reduce heat stress.

Microchilus metallescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
