Magnolia persuaveolens subsp. persuaveolens

Magnolia persuaveolens subsp.

persuaveolens, commonly known as the sweetbay magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blossoms and pleasant fragrance.
