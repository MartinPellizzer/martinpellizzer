Mariposa

Mariposa, commonly known as the "Butterfly Flower," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plants typically grow as epiphytes or terrestrial herbs, often found in humid tropical environments. Mariposa species are known for their intricate, colorful blooms that range from white to violet, often with a distinctive pattern resembling butterfly wings.

These plants are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated for their unique floral structures.
