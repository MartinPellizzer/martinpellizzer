Marina palmeri

Marina palmeri, commonly known as the desert marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and fuzzy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and often forms dense mats or clusters.

Marina palmeri is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
