Mussaenda hymenopogonoides

Mussaenda hymenopogonoides, commonly known as the golden flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow inflorescences that resemble flames, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Mussaenda hymenopogonoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
