Melastoma paradoxum

Melastoma paradoxum, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Melastoma paradoxum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
