Mutisia hookeri var. rosmarinifolia

Mutisia hookeri var. rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf mutisia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It features silvery-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and hardy nature.
