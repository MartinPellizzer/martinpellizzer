Micromeria fruticosa subsp. barbata

Micromeria fruticosa subsp.

barbata, commonly known as the barbed micromeria, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high sunlight exposure. Its subspecies status is distinguished by the presence of barbed hairs on its stems and leaves, which are more pronounced than in the typical form.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features and ecological niche.
