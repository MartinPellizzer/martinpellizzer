Misanteca triandra

Misanteca triandra, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Misanteca triandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a shade tree in tropical landscapes.
