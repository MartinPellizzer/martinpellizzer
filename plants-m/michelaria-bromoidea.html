Michelaria bromoidea

Michelaria bromoidea, commonly known as the bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts that attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Michelaria bromoidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
