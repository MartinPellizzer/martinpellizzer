Myristica cinerea

Myristica cinerea, commonly known as the ash-colored nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dark, ash-like bark and fragrant, oily seeds. The plant produces small, white flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. Its seeds, which are enclosed in a hard, woody capsule, are the source of its name and have a complex chemical composition.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its aromatic properties.
