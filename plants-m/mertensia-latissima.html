Mertensia latissima

Mertensia latissima, commonly known as broadleaf bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically deep blue to violet in color, blooming in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Mertensia latissima is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
