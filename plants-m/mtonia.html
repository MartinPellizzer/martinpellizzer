Mtonia

Mtonia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-draining soil. Mtonia is popular as a houseplant due to its compact size and ability to bloom year-round under proper care.

Its botanical name, Saintpaulia, honors the 19th-century botanist Adolphe Saint-Paul.
