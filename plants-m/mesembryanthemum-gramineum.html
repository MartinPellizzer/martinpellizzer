Mesembryanthemum gramineum

Mesembryanthemum gramineum, commonly known as the grass-like ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, grass-like leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the cooler months. Mesembryanthemum gramineum is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in dry landscapes.
