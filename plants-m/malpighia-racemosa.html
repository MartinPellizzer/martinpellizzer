Malpighia racemosa

Malpighia racemosa, commonly known as the granadillo or red mombin, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are clustered in racemes and are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting various pollinators.
