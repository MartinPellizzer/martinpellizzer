Microchilus viridissimus

Microchilus viridissimus, commonly known as the emerald moss, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and compact growth habit, which allows it to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually followed by small, leathery berries. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Microchilus viridissimus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
