Melvilla speciosa

Melvilla speciosa, commonly known as the showy melvilla, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with compound leaves and clustered blooms. Melvilla speciosa is adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.
