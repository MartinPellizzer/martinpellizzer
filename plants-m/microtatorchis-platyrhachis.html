Microtatorchis platyrhachis

Microtatorchis platyrhachis, commonly known as the broad-ribbed tatorchis, is a unique succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically broad and ribbed, adapted to store water in its native environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are clustered in inflorescences. Its growth habit is often prostrate or ascending, allowing it to spread across rocky or sandy terrain.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens in dry climates.
