Mentha longifolia var. weinerniana

Mentha longifolia var. weinerniana, commonly known as the German mint, is a variant of the longleaf mint species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features aromatic, serrated leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb due to its pleasant menthol scent.
