Mucuna pesa

Mucuna pesa, commonly known as velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic seed that is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein. Its growth habit includes climbing vines that can reach several meters in length.

Botanically, Mucuna pesa belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
