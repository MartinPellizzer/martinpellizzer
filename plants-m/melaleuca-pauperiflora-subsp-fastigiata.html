Melaleuca pauperiflora subsp. fastigiata

Melaleuca pauperiflora subsp.

fastigiata, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a shrub native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems and small, clustered white or pink flowers.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salt spray.
