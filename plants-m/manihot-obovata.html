Manihot obovata

Manihot obovata, commonly known as the arrowleaf manioc, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are tuberous and are often used as a food source after processing to remove toxic compounds.

The species is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often latex-producing members.
