Mangifera foetida var. mollis

Mangifera foetida var. mollis, commonly known as the hairy mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces edible, round fruits with a thick, segmented rind and a juicy, sweet pulp. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its fruit and ornamental qualities.
