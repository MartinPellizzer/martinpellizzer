Masdevallia parsonsii

Masdevallia parsonsii, commonly known as the Parsons' Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically produces intricate, spurred petals and a unique lip that aid in pollination by specific insects. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are well adapted to its humid, shaded habitat.

Masdevallia parsonsii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
