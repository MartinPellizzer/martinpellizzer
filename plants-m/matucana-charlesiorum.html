Matucana charlesiorum

Matucana charlesiorum, commonly known as the Charles' matucana, is a species of cycad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
