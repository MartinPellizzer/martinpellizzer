Melilotus tommasinii

Melilotus tommasinii, commonly known as Tommasini's sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its roots have a distinct aromatic scent due to the presence of volatile oils.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
