Myristica olivacea

Myristica olivacea, commonly known as the olive nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces an aromatic seed known as the olive nut, which is encased in a fleshy, yellowish aril. Its essential oils are valued for their unique fragrance and medicinal properties.

The tree is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
