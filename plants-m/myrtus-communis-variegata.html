Myrtus communis variegata

Myrtus communis variegata, commonly known as the variegated myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for container gardening.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental and hedging purposes.
