Melica minuta var. pyramidalis

Melica minuta var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal meadowgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, and it typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

It is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
