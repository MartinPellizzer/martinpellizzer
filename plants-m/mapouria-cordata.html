Mapouria cordata

Mapouria cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf maple or heartleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange or red fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
