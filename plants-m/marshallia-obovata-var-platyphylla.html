Marshallia obovata var. platyphylla

Marshallia obovata var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf marsh marigold, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with heart-shaped, broad leaves that are hairy and have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
