Muhlenbergia brevifolia var. richardsonis

Muhlenbergia brevifolia var. richardsonis, commonly known as the Richardson's muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, clump-forming growth habit and narrow, silver-gray blades that are often lined with white hairs. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that add ornamental value, especially in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a desirable ornamental grass for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
