Malpighia platyphylla

Malpighia platyphylla, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that produces large, showy flowers with five white petals and prominent stamens. The fruit is a green, spiky berry that turns yellow when ripe, containing a juicy, aromatic pulp with a sweet-tart flavor. The plant is valued for its ornamental value and its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated in tropical regions for both its fruit and ornamental appeal.
