Mussaenda chinensis

Mussaenda chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese flameflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that resemble flames, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Mussaenda chinensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
