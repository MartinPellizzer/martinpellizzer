Megalorchis

Megalorchis, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach several inches in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors such as pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding substrate, often found in shaded forest understories.

Megalorchis is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections worldwide.
