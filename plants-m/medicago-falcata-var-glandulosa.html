Medicago falcata var. glandulosa

Medicago falcata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has glandular structures on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
