Microcachrys tetragona

Microcachrys tetragona, commonly known as the mountain melastome, is a low-growing shrub native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that are arranged in a dense, cushion-like formation. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Microcachrys tetragona is an important component of alpine ecosystems and is valued for its ecological and horticultural significance.
