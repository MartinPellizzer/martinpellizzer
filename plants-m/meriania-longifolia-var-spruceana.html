Meriania longifolia var. spruceana

Meriania longifolia var. spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce's meriania, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.

The botanical interest of this variant lies in its distinct morphological features and ecological adaptability.
