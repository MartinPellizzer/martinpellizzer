Myrtus luteoviridis

Myrtus luteoviridis, commonly known as the golden green myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a slight yellowish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Myrtus luteoviridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic foliage.
