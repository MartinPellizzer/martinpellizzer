Mentha odorata

Mentha odorata, commonly known as sweet mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are aromatic, containing high levels of menthol and other volatile oils.

Mentha odorata is often cultivated for culinary and medicinal purposes due to its refreshing scent and therapeutic properties.
