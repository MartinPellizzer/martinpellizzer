Mucuna laticifera

Mucuna laticifera, commonly known as the cowpea vine or velvet bean, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its coiled, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves.

It produces distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, followed by pod-like fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is notable for its high concentration of alkaloids, particularly mimosine, which can be toxic in large quantities. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used as a protein source.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
