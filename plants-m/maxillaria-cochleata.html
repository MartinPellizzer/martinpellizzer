Maxillaria cochleata

Maxillaria cochleata, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that produce leaves and flower spikes. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with a distinctive spiral pattern on the petals and sepals.

The plant is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
