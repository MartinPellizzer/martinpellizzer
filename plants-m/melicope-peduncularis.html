Melicope peduncularis

Melicope peduncularis, commonly known as the Hawaiian mahogany, is a tropical tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland to montane rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
