Mariscus radians var. floribundus

Mariscus radians var. floribundus, commonly known as the flowering mariscus, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, upright growth with small, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice among gardeners in temperate climates.
