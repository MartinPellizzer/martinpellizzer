Malaxis hirschbergii

Malaxis hirschbergii, commonly known as the Hirschberg's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy, greenish-white flower that has a distinctive lip. Its roots are often clustered and adapted for absorbing nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Malaxis hirschbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
