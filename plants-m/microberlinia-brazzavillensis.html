Microberlinia brazzavillensis

Microberlinia brazzavillensis, commonly known as the Congo bean, is a leguminous tree native to Central and Western Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and supports sustainable agriculture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist lowland forests.

The seeds of Microberlinia brazzavillensis are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
