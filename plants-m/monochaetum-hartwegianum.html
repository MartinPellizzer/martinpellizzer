Monochaetum hartwegianum

Monochaetum hartwegianum, commonly known as the Hartwig's spike rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to New Zealand, this species is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem, supporting various insect and bird life.
