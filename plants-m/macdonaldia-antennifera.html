Macdonaldia antennifera

Macdonaldia antennifera, commonly known as the feathered orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

The plant typically grows in swampy or seasonally flooded areas, where it thrives in the moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its unique feature is the elongated, hair-like structures on its leaves, which give it its common name.
