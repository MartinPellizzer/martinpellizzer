Momordica suringarii

Momordica suringarii, commonly known as the Suringar's bitter melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The fruit is typically elongated, with a spiny exterior and a bitter interior, which is often used in traditional medicine. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and various medicinal properties.
