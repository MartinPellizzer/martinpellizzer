Melochia roseiflora

Melochia roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered melochia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a hairy capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Melochia roseiflora is often grown for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant floral display.
