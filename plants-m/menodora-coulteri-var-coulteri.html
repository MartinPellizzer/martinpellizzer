Menodora coulteri var. coulteri

Menodora coulteri var. coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's menodora, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, brown seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid and semi-arid environments.
