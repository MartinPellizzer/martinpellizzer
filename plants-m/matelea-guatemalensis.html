Matelea guatemalensis

Matelea guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white, night-blooming flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers emit a strong fragrance at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
