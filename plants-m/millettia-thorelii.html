Millettia thorelii

Millettia thorelii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, papery seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

Millettia thorelii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
