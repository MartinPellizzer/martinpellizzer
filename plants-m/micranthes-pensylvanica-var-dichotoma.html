Micranthes pensylvanica var. dichotoma

Micranthes pensylvanica var. dichotoma, commonly known as the Pennsylvania wintergreen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
