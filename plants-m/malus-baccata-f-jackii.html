Malus baccata f. jackii

Malus baccata f. jackii, commonly known as the Jack's crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, tart, yellow or red fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and cooking. The plant has a compact growth habit with attractive, ornamental leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. It is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

The flowers are white and appear in early spring, adding to its aesthetic value.
