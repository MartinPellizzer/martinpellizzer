Malpighia souzae

Malpighia souzae, commonly known as the Souza's acerola, is a species of flowering plant in the Malpighiaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are typically white or pink with five petals.

The botanical features of Malpighia souzae make it an attractive ornamental plant and a valuable source of nutritious fruit.
