Myrcia multiglomerata

Myrcia multiglomerata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often have a strong, spicy flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Myrcia multiglomerata has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
