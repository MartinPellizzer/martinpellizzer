Malvaviscus polakowskyi

Malvaviscus polakowskyi, commonly known as the Brazilian glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds.

Malvaviscus polakowskyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
