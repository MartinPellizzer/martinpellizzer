Myrica arguta var. macrocarpa

Myrica arguta var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited wax myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
