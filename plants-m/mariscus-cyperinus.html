Mariscus cyperinus

Mariscus cyperinus, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and small, clustered flowers. Native to wetland and marshy environments, it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-lobed berries.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in its natural habitat.
