Miltonia spectabilis var. purpureoviolacea

Miltonia spectabilis var. purpureoviolacea, commonly known as the purple viola orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a deep purple or violet hue with contrasting white or cream markings. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, and its leaves are strap-like, arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
