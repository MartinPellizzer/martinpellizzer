Magnolia maxima

Magnolia maxima, commonly known as the Persian magnolia, is a large, deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree can reach heights of up to 30 meters, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are large, ovate, and have a smooth, dark green upper surface.

The bark is thick, flaky, and dark brown, providing a distinctive texture.
