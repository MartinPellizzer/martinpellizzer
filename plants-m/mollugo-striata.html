Mollugo striata

Mollugo striata, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouseear or striped mouseear, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of only a few centimeters. The plant features tiny, paired leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in gardens.

Mollugo striata is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it a common weed in many regions.
