Megastachya aturensis

Megastachya aturensis, commonly known as the golden spike grass, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks. The plant produces distinctive golden-yellow spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
