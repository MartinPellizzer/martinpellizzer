Maesa bengalensis

Maesa bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal maesa, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heather and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences, and it produces berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
