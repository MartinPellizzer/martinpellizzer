Mentha × dossiniana

Mentha × dossiniana, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it forms dense mats.

Mentha × dossiniana is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
