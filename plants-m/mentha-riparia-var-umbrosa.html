Mentha riparia var. umbrosa

Mentha riparia var. umbrosa, commonly known as the river mint or water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, shaded environments.

It typically grows along stream banks and in wetlands, where it thrives in rich, loamy soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and a distinct minty aroma. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue to purple and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Mentha riparia var. umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
