Malaxis chamaeorchis

Malaxis chamaeorchis, commonly known as the ground orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically produces green to reddish stems with few leaves, and its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
