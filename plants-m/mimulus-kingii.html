Mimulus kingii

Mimulus kingii, commonly known as the king's monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features showy, bright orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus kingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
