Melinia peruviana

Melinia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian melinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Melinaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
