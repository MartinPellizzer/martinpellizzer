Mentha × verticillata var. parviflora

Mentha × verticillata var. parviflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved water mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. This variety thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

The plant is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
