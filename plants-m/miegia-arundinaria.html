Miegia arundinaria

Miegia arundinaria, commonly known as the bamboo reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that resemble bamboo, which are often used in traditional crafts. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Miegia arundinaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
