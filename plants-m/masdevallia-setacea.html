Masdevallia setacea

Masdevallia setacea, commonly known as the hairless masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly pubescence. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and delicate beauty, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
