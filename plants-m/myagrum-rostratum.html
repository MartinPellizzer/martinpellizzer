Myagrum rostratum

Myagrum rostratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a deciduous tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a reddish inner layer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Myagrum rostratum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
