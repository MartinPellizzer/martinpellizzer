Maclura pubescens

Maclura pubescens, commonly known as the yellow wood or honey locust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually trifoliate, and its distinctive yellow to orange fruit that resembles a small, hairy gourd. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by the characteristic fruit. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.
