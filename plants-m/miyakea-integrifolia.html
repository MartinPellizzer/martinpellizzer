Miyakea integrifolia

Miyakea integrifolia, commonly known as the Hawaiian tree fern, is a unique species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to several meters.

Miyakea integrifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various native wildlife.
