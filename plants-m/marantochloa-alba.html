Marantochloa alba

Marantochloa alba, commonly known as the white marantchloa, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Marantochloa alba is a popular ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
