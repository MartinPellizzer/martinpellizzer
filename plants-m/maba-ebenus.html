Maba ebenus

Maba ebenus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Maba ebenus is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.
