Malus dasyphylla var. domestica

Malus dasyphylla var. domestica, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, tart fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
