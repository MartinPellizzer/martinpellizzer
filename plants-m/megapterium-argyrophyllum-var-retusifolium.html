Megapterium argyrophyllum var. retusifolium

Megapterium argyrophyllum var. retusifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that produce clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers in the spring. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine silvery-white pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
