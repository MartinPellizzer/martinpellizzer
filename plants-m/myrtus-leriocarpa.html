Myrtus leriocarpa

Myrtus leriocarpa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Myrtus leriocarpa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Its aromatic properties have made it valuable in traditional herbal medicine and perfumery.
