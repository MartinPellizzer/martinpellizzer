Mikaniopsis troupinii

Mikaniopsis troupinii, commonly known as the Cape mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
