Malva colmeiroi var. mariziana

Malva colmeiroi var. mariziana, commonly known as the Mariziana mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes mallows, hibiscus, and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
