Myrcia sessiliflora

Myrcia sessiliflora, commonly known as Brazilian pepper or *Pimenta* in Brazil, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces a strong, aromatic scent.

Myrcia sessiliflora is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its ornate appearance and medicinal properties.
