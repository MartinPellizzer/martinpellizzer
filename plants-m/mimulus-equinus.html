Mimulus equinus

Mimulus equinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright orange to yellow flowers with two lips, typical of the Plantaginaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streambanks and moist meadows.

Mimulus equinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
