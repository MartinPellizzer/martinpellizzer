Malpighia mucronata

Malpighia mucronata, commonly known as the acerola cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. Its fruit is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its nutritional value.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
