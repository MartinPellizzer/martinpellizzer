Mimosa ynga

Mimosa ynga, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its soft, feathery foliage. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often grown for its ornamental value.

Mimosa ynga is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
