Malva lagascae

Malva lagascae, commonly known as the Lagasce mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
