Melastoma calycinum

Melastoma calycinum, commonly known as the purple-stemmed melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its stems are usually woody and covered in fine hairs.

Melastoma calycinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
