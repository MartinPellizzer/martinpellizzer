Marsdenia queirozii

Marsdenia queirozii, commonly known as the Queiroz's Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
