Masdevallia laevis

Masdevallia laevis, commonly known as the smooth masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous flower spikes that bear small, intricate blooms with a distinctive white to pale pink coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its compact, elegant appearance.

Masdevallia laevis is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
