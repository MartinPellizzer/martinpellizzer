Myristica cinnamomea

Myristica cinnamomea, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and woody, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to oval-shaped fruits containing the valuable nutmeg seed and mace, its reddish aril. Myristica cinnamomea thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its essential oils and compounds are widely used in culinary, medicinal, and perfumery applications.
