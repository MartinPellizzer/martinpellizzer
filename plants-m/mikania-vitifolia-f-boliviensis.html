Mikania vitifolia f. boliviensis

Mikania vitifolia f. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
