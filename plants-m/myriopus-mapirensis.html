Myriopus mapirensis

Myriopus mapirensis, commonly known as the Mapire grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Myriopus mapirensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
