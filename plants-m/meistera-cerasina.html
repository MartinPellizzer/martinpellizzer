Meistera cerasina

Meistera cerasina, commonly known as the purple waxy plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, violet-colored leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its unique foliage.
