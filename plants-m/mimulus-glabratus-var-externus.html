Mimulus glabratus var. externus

Mimulus glabratus var. externus, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats like stream banks and meadows. It has smooth, lance-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsule-like, containing numerous small seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
