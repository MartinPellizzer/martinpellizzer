Medicosma exigua

Medicosma exigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves with a distinctive narrow shape. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish-purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Medicosma exigua is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental and ecological value.
