Margaritaria indica f. vestita

Margaritaria indica f. vestita, commonly known as the white-veined mallow, is a variant of the species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive white veins on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation in warm climates.
