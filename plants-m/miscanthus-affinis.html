Miscanthus affinis

Miscanthus affinis, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-tan plumes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more silvery as the plant matures.

Miscanthus affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in various conditions.
