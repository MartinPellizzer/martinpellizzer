Magallana trialata

Magallana trialata, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories. Its botanical name reflects its geographic origin and distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
