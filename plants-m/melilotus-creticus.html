Melilotus creticus

Melilotus creticus, commonly known as Cretan sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Melilotus creticus is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
