Mentha sachalinensis f. pilosa

Mentha sachalinensis f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy Siberian mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular leaves and square stems, typical of the Lamiaceae family. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive minty fragrance.
