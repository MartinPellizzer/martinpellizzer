Melampodium manillense

Melampodium manillense, commonly known as the Manila tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and branching.

Melampodium manillense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
