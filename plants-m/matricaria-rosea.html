Matricaria rosea

Matricaria rosea, commonly known as the rosemary flower or German chamomile, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and serrated, aromatic leaves. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often grown for its ornamental value and medicinal properties. Its essential oils, derived from the flowers, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Matricaria rosea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
