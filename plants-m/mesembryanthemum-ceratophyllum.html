Mesembryanthemum ceratophyllum

Mesembryanthemum ceratophyllum, commonly known as the needleleaf ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, needle-like leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, pink, and white, typically during the winter months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and unique foliage.

Mesembryanthemum ceratophyllum is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
