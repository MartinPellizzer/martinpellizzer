Mimosa kuntzei

Mimosa kuntzei, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in spring, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Mimosa kuntzei is also known for its sensitivity to touch, as its leaves fold when disturbed, a trait shared with other mimosa species.
