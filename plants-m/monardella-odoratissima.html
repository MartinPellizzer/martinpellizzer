Monardella odoratissima

Monardella odoratissima, commonly known as sweet peppergrass, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and emit a subtle, spicy fragrance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.
