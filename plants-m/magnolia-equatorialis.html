Magnolia equatorialis

Magnolia equatorialis, commonly known as the African magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its striking floral display.
