Meconopsis cawdoriana

Meconopsis cawdoriana, commonly known as the Cawdor bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountain forests or alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Meconopsis cawdoriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific soil and climate conditions.
