Microcala filiformis

Microcala filiformis, commonly known as the slender microcala, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features delicate, thread-like leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
