Mandevilla sancta

Mandevilla sancta, commonly known as the sacred mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often blooming throughout the year in warm climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly glossy texture.

Mandevilla sancta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant.
