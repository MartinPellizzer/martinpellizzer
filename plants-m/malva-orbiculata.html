Malva orbiculata

Malva orbiculata, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, round, fuzzy leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Malva orbiculata prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
