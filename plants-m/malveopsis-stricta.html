Malveopsis stricta

Malveopsis stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a branching stem structure.

Malveopsis stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
