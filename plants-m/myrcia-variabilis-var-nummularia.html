Myrcia variabilis var. nummularia

Myrcia variabilis var. nummularia, commonly known as the silverleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. Its evergreen nature makes it suitable for use in ornamental gardening, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and potential therapeutic properties.
