Mespilus amelanchier

Mespilus amelanchier, commonly known as serviceberry or shadbush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn yellow in the fall.

Mespilus amelanchier is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
