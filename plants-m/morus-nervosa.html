Morus nervosa

Morus nervosa, commonly known as the slender mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with prominent veins and a distinctive, pale green bark. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Morus nervosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
