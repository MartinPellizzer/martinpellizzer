Martianthus elongatus

Martianthus elongatus, commonly known as the silverleaf spire, is a unique plant species characterized by its elongated, spiral-shaped leaves that resemble the structure of a helix.

Native to the arid regions of the Martian plains, it has adapted to extreme conditions with a thick waxy cuticle that minimizes water loss. Its leaves are covered in fine, reflective silver hairs that help reflect intense solar radiation. The plant produces small, pale-blue flowers during the brief Martian spring, which are pollinated by specialized winged insects.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Martianthus elongatus is considered a key indicator species for the planet's evolving biosphere.
