Meconopsis simplicifolia

Meconopsis simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive dark green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Meconopsis simplicifolia is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.
