Minuartia hirsuta subsp. asiatica

Minuartia hirsuta subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as the Asiatic sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to regions of Asia. It is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and produces small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in marginal habitats.
