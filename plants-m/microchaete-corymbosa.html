Microchaete corymbosa

Microchaete corymbosa, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive blooms and fragrant scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Microchaete corymbosa is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
