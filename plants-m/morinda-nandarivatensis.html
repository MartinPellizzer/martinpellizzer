Morinda nandarivatensis

Morinda nandarivatensis, commonly known as the Malabar madder, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in herbal remedies.

Morinda nandarivatensis is also noted for its adaptability to a range of soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
