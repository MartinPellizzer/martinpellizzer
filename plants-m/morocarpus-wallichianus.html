Morocarpus wallichianus

Morocarpus wallichianus, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or Himalayan rose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
