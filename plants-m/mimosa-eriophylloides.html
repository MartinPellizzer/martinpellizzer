Mimosa eriophylloides

Mimosa eriophylloides, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold when touched, a trait known as thigmonasty. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in various parts of the world.
