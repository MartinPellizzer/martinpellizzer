Melampodium durandii

Melampodium durandii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow to orange inflorescences.

Melampodium durandii is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value in gardens.
