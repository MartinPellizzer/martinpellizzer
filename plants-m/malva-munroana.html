Malva munroana

Malva munroana, commonly known as the Munro's mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, pale pink to lavender flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
