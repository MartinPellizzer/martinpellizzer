Maxillaria silvana

Maxillaria silvana, commonly known as the Silvan's Maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of flowers that bloom during the summer months.

Maxillaria silvana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a controlled environment.
