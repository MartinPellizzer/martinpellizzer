Medicago lupulina var. glandulosa

Medicago lupulina var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular goat's rue, is a variant of the goat's rue plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it identifiable in ecological and botanical studies.
