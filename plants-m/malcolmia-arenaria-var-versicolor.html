Malcolmia arenaria var. versicolor

Malcolmia arenaria var. versicolor, commonly known as the sand mustache or sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or disturbed areas. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
