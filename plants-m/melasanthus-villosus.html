Melasanthus villosus

Melasanthus villosus, commonly known as the hairy melasanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Melasanthus villosus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
