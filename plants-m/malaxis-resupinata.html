Malaxis resupinata

Malaxis resupinata, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to various regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with a distinct lip that is often marked with purple or red streaks.

Malaxis resupinata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms that appear during the warmer months.
