Myripnois uniflora

Myripnois uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered myripnois, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces a single, showy white or pale blue flower at the tip of each stem, which blooms during the cooler months. Myripnois uniflora is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
