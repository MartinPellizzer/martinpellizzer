Mirabilis odorata

Mirabilis odorata, commonly known as the sweet sylph, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting throat. The plant is known for its fragrant blossoms, which emit a sweet scent, especially during the evening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Mirabilis odorata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
