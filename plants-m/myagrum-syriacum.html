Myagrum syriacum

Myagrum syriacum, commonly known as the Syrian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features small, glossy leaves and produces small white or pink flowers that attract pollinators. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that contain a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
