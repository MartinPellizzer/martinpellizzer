Matthiola dealbata

Matthiola dealbata, commonly known as the white stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it often grows in rocky or dry soils.

Matthiola dealbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in gardens and as a cut flower.
