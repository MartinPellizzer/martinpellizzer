Micranthus serpyllifolius

Micranthus serpyllifolius, commonly known as common yarrow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas due to its adaptability.

Micranthus serpyllifolius is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
