Melothria

Melothria, commonly known as the "little cucumber," is a small, trailing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces small, edible fruits that are typically green and resemble miniature cucumbers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. Melothria is often cultivated for its unique flavor and is used in various culinary applications.

Its botanical classification places it in the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins.
