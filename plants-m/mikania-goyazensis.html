Mikania goyazensis

Mikania goyazensis, commonly known as Brazilian bittersweet, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It is often found in humid, tropical environments and can grow up to several meters in length.

Due to its rapid growth, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
