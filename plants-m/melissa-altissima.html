Melissa altissima

Melissa altissima, commonly known as giant lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features large, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its essential oils, rich in citral and geraniol, are used in perfumery and herbal medicine.
