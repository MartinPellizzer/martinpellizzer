Machilus calcicola

Machilus calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola lauraceous plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to China and is typically found in rocky or calcareous soils, which contribute to its common name. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are usually glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, dark berries.

Machilus calcicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable soil conditions.
