Malaxis paludosa

Malaxis paludosa, commonly known as the marsh malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to its habitat specificity, Malaxis paludosa is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
