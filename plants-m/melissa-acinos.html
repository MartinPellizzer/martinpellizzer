Melissa acinos

Melissa acinos, commonly known as alpine lemon balm, is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and dry grasslands. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its pleasant, lemony scent.

Melissa acinos is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
