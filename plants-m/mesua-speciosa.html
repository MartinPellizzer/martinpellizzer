Mesua speciosa

Mesua speciosa, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Mesua speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
