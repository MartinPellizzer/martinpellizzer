Menisorus blastophorus

Menisorus blastophorus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals and numerous stamens, making it visually striking.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
