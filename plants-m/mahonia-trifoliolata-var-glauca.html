Mahonia trifoliolata var. glauca

Mahonia trifoliolata var. glauca, commonly known as the blue mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a bluish-gray waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its horticultural value lies in its evergreen foliage and long bloom period.
