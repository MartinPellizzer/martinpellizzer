Mentha × pyramidalis

Mentha × pyramidalis, commonly known as hedge mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha viridis and Mentha spicata.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, which bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist, sunny areas such as meadows and along stream banks.

Mentha × pyramidalis is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
