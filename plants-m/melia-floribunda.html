Melia floribunda

Melia floribunda, commonly known as the sweetgum tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves and produces small, spiky fruits that contain seeds. The tree has a tall, slender trunk with a dense crown, and its bark is smooth and grayish-white. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in urban and suburban landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
