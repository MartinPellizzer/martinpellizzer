Mimulus coccineus

Mimulus coccineus, commonly known as the scarlet monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It features striking red-orange flowers with two lips, resembling a monkey's face, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Mimulus coccineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting native pollinator populations.
