Motandra viridiflora

Motandra viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered motandra, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its botanical characteristics make it a unique species within the Apiaceae family.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
