Morphixia grandiflora

Morphixia grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora morphixia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a woody stem and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Morphixia grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
