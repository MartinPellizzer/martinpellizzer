Mespilus stricta

Mespilus stricta, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed, and often spiny bark. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruit that ripens in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

Mespilus stricta is known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
