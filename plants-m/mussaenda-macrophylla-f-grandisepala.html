Mussaenda macrophylla f. grandisepala

Mussaenda macrophylla f. grandisepala, commonly known as the large-sepal Mussaenda, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pinkish-white bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth, glossy surface and a somewhat waxy texture. Its flowers are clustered in terminal panicles, adding to its visual appeal in gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
