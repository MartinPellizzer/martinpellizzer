Mentha × niliaca var. lamarckii

Mentha × niliaca var. lamarckii, commonly known as Lamarck's hybrid mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha × piperita and Mentha × gracilis.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and stems, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive menthol scent. This variety is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses, similar to other mint species. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a popular choice in herbal gardens.
