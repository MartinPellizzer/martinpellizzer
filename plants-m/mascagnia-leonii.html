Mascagnia leonii

Mascagnia leonii, commonly known as the red-flowered trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a woody vine that produces striking red-orange tubular flowers, which attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it is drought-tolerant once established.

Mascagnia leonii is also valued for its ability to climb and cover structures, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
