Meringium holochilum

Meringium holochilum, commonly known as the yellow meringium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular disc florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Meringium holochilum is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
