Meoschium aristatum

Meoschium aristatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is often found in wetland areas and can thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Meoschium aristatum is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a useful plant in certain ecological contexts and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
