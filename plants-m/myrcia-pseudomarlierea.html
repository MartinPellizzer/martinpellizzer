Myrcia pseudomarlierea

Myrcia pseudomarlierea, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain several seeds.

Myrcia pseudomarlierea is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic properties.
