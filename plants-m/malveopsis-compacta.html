Malveopsis compacta

Malveopsis compacta, commonly known as the compact mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
