Mentha × elliptica var. variegata

Mentha × elliptica var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom in late summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild mint scent, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
