Miconia liesneri

Miconia liesneri, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, resembling a starfruit, is edible and has a unique, mildly sweet flavor.

Miconia liesneri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
