Myosotis stolonifera subsp. hirsuta

Myosotis stolonifera subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy forget-me-not, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage, which give it its subspecies name *hirsuta*.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Myosotis genus.
