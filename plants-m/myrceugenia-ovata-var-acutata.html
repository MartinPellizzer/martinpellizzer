Myrceugenia ovata var. acutata

Myrceugenia ovata var. acutata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved myrtle, is a species native to the temperate rainforests of southern Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with lanceolate, leathery leaves that have a distinctive acuminate tip. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are woody drupes that contain a single seed, and the plant is adapted to high humidity and cool temperatures.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
