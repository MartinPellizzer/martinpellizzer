Moenchia glauca var. patula

Moenchia glauca var. patula, commonly known as the silverleaf goosefoot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves that are often described as having a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers in clusters, which are typically arranged in a cup-like structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
