Macreightia oblongifolia

Macreightia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf macreightia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid climates.
