Melissa flava

Melissa flava, commonly known as yellow lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, serrated leaves that emit a lemony scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile garden plant.

Melissa flava is often used in herbal remedies for its calming properties and is also a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
