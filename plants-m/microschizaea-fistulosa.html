Microschizaea fistulosa

Microschizaea fistulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Microschizaea fistulosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
