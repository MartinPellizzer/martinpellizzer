Mimosa cameleopardalis

Mimosa cameleopardalis, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from underground sources. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Mimosa cameleopardalis is also known for its sensitive leaves, which fold shut when touched, a trait that may help reduce water loss.
