Mentha × rotundifolia f. cordifolia

Mentha × rotundifolia f. cordifolia, commonly known as heart-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its rounded, heart-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and mild mint scent.
