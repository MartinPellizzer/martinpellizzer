Miconia plebeia

Miconia plebeia, commonly known as the little miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
