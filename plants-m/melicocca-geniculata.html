Melicocca geniculata

Melicocca geniculata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and oval-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to orange to red fruit, which is edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, astringent flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food for various wildlife species.
