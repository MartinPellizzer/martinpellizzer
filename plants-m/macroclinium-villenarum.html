Macroclinium villenarum

Macroclinium villenarum, commonly known as the Villena's climbing orchid, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor it to rocky substrates in its natural habitat.

The orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
