Mimosa spinisiliqua

Mimosa spinisiliqua, commonly known as the spiny mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by light or touch stimuli. The plant produces small, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its stems are covered in sharp, bristly spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Mimosa spinisiliqua thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
