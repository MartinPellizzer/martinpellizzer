Maxillaria ramiro-medinae

Maxillaria ramiro-medinae, commonly known as the Ramiro-Medina Maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces vibrant, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and blooms in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
