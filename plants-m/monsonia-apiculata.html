Monsonia apiculata

Monsonia apiculata, commonly known as the woolly monsonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, hairy, and often branched stem. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a soft, woolly coating, contributing to its unique appearance.
