Macrochaetium ecklonii

Macrochaetium ecklonii, commonly known as the Cape mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
