Morysia pauciflora

Morysia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered morysia, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or rocky slopes. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in umbrella-shaped clusters, which attract various pollinators.

Morysia pauciflora is noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
