Malacochaete riparia var. chamissoi

Malacochaete riparia var. chamissoi, commonly known as the Chamisso's river sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil along rivers and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. It is often found in temperate regions with a humid climate.

Its ecological importance lies in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial invertebrates.
