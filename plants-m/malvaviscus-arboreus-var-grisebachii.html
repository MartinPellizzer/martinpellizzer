Malvaviscus arboreus var. grisebachii

Malvaviscus arboreus var. grisebachii, commonly known as the gray mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
