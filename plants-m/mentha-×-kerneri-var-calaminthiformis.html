Mentha × kerneri var. calaminthiformis

Mentha × kerneri var. calaminthiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved peppermint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and aromatic, menthol-rich foliage.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with a sprawling or upright habit depending on environmental conditions. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its use in herbal teas, culinary applications, and as a decorative plant in gardens.
