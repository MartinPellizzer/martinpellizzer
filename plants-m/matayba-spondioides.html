Matayba spondioides

Matayba spondioides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or spondias, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by edible, red drupes that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
