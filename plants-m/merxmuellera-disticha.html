Merxmuellera disticha

Merxmuellera disticha, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains one or two seeds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host tree if left unchecked.
