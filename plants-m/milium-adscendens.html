Milium adscendens

Milium adscendens, commonly known as the ascending millet, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Milium adscendens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
