Melochia hirsuta var. regnellii

Melochia hirsuta var. regnellii, commonly known as the hairy melochia, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
