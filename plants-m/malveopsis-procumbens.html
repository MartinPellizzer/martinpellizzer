Malveopsis procumbens

Malveopsis procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate mallows, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
