Mammillaria bussleri

Mammillaria bussleri, commonly known as the Bussler's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and spines that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
