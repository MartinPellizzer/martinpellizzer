Mikania cissampelina

Mikania cissampelina, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a fast-growing vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forest edges. Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
