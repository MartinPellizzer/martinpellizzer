Mucuna aurea

Mucuna aurea, commonly known as golden cowpea, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Mucuna aurea is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
