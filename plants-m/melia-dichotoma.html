Melia dichotoma

Melia dichotoma, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodlands.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
