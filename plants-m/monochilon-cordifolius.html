Monochilon cordifolius

Monochilon cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in dense spikes, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique leaf shape.
