Mauritia intermedia

Mauritia intermedia, commonly known as the golden palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits resembling small coconuts. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping and conservation purposes.
