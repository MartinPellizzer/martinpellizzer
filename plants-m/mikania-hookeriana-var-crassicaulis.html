Mikania hookeriana var. crassicaulis

Mikania hookeriana var. crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed Mikania, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has a robust stem that can become woody with age, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain areas.
