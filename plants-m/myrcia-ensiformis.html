Myrcia ensiformis

Myrcia ensiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often have a strong, sharp flavor.

Myrcia ensiformis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
