Maerua elegans

Maerua elegans, commonly known as the slender maerua, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks. Its fibrous roots help it survive in drought conditions, making it well adapted to harsh climates.

Maerua elegans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
