Micromyrtus minutiflora

Micromyrtus minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered myrtle, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

This species is adapted to salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.
