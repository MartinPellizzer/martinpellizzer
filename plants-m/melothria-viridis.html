Melothria viridis

Melothria viridis, commonly known as the creeping cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, green, cucumber-like fruits that are typically harvested when immature. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit is edible when young and has a tart, slightly sweet flavor.
