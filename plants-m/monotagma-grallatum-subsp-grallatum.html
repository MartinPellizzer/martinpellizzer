Monotagma grallatum subsp. grallatum

Monotagma grallatum subsp.

grallatum, commonly known as the red-stemmed starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has slender, branching stems that are often tinged with red, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
