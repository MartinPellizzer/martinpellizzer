Macaranga sinensis

Macaranga sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its rapid growth rate. Its bark contains high levels of tannins, making it valuable for tanning leather.

The tree also plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
