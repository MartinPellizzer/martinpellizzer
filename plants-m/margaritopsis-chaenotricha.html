Margaritopsis chaenotricha

Margaritopsis chaenotricha, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rosette of silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found along coastal areas.

Its unique morphology and color make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
