Musa africana

Musa africana, commonly known as the African banana, is a member of the Musaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by large, broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, green, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten raw or cooked. Its roots are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Musa africana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
