Mitrasacme glaucescens

Mitrasacme glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, bluish-green coating. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its parasitic nature allows it to absorb water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.
