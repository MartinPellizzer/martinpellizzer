Michelia maudiae var. rubicunda

Michelia maudiae var. rubicunda, commonly known as the red-flowered Michelia, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, rose-like flowers that range from pink to deep red, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and can grow up to 3 meters tall under ideal conditions. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique floral display and aromatic qualities make it a prized addition to botanical collections.
