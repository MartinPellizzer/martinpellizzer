Mimosa lapiazicola

Mimosa lapiazicola, commonly known as the Brazilian mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a response triggered by light or touch. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months.

Mimosa lapiazicola is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
