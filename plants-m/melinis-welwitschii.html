Melinis welwitschii

Melinis welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it flowers during the rainy season.

Melinis welwitschii is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is used in some regions for forage or erosion control.
