Melicopsidium trifoliatum

Melicopsidium trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaved melicopsid, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
