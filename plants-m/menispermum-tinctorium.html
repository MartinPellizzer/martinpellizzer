Menispermum tinctorium

Menispermum tinctorium, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine.
