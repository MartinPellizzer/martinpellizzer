Medicago conica

Medicago conica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and cover cropping.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
