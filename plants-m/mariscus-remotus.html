Mariscus remotus

Mariscus remotus, commonly known as the remote marsh orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific wetland habitats in South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in areas with high humidity and partial shade. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it an important subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.

Due to habitat destruction, Mariscus remotus is considered endangered in many regions.
