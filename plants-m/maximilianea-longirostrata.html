Maximilianea longirostrata

Maximilianea longirostrata, commonly known as the long-beaked bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain the seeds.
