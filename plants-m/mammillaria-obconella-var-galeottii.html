Mammillaria obconella var. galeottii

Mammillaria obconella var. galeottii, commonly known as the Galeotti's mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on the tips of the stems. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts of desert plants.
