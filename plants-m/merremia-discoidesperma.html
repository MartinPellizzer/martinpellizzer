Merremia discoidesperma

Merremia discoidesperma, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
