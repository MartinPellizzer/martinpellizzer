Macromerum

Macromerum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It features large, broad leaves that can span several feet in diameter, with a thick, waxy surface that helps repel water. The plant has showy, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots are anchored in the muddy bottom of shallow waters, while its stems float on the surface.

Macromerum plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
