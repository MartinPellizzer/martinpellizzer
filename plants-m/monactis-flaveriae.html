Monactis flaveriae

Monactis flaveriae, commonly known as the yellow monkey flower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Monactis flaveriae is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
