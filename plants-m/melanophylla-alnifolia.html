Melanophylla alnifolia

Melanophylla alnifolia, commonly known as the alderleaf melanophylla, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its berries are small and dark, containing the seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Melanophylla alnifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian habitats.
