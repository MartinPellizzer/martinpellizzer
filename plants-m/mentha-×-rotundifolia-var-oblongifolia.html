Mentha × rotundifolia var. oblongifolia

Mentha × rotundifolia var. oblongifolia, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Mentha rotundifolia and other Mentha species.

It is a perennial herb with a creeping rhizomatous growth habit, often forming dense mats. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a slightly rounded base, and are covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink, arranged in whorled clusters.

It is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
