Menyanthes excelsa

Menyanthes excelsa, commonly known as the water parsnip, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features a slender, creeping rhizome and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating in shallow water. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several tiny seeds.

Menyanthes excelsa plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
