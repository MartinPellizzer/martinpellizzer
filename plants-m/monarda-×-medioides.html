Monarda × medioides

Monarda × medioides, commonly known as the hybrid bergamot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Monarda × medioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
