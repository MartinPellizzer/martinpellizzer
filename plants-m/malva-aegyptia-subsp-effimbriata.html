Malva aegyptia subsp. effimbriata

Malva aegyptia subsp.

effimbriata, commonly known as the Egyptian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ornamental plant. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The subspecies effimbriata is distinguished by its finely toothed leaves and shorter, more compact growth habit compared to other subspecies.
