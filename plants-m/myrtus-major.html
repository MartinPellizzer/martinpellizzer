Myrtus major

Myrtus major, commonly known as common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fragrant, dark green leaves and produces small, white, five-petaled flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and berries, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Myrtus major thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
