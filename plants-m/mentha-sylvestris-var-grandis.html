Mentha sylvestris var. grandis

Mentha sylvestris var. grandis, commonly known as the large wood mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
