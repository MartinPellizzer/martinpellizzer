Mycetia paniculiformis

Mycetia paniculiformis, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, flame-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, pendulous inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in hairy pods and are dispersed by wind.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
