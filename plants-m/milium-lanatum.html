Milium lanatum

Milium lanatum, commonly known as downy millet, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a small annual herb characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of tiny, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

Milium lanatum is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food for wildlife.
