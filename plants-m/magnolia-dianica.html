Magnolia dianica

Magnolia dianica, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

The flowers have a strong, sweet fragrance and are an important feature in its ecological and ornamental value.
