Mimulus inconspicuus var. acutidens

Mimulus inconspicuus var. acutidens, commonly known as the sharp-toothed monkeyflower, is a delicate annual herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces vibrant orange to yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive species for both ecological studies and native gardens.
