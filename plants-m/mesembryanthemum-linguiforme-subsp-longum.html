Mesembryanthemum linguiforme subsp. longum

Mesembryanthemum linguiforme subsp.

longum, commonly known as the longleaf iceplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
