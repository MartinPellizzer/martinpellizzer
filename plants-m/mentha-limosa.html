Mentha limosa

Mentha limosa, commonly known as marsh mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and moist environments in North America.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its minty scent and medicinal properties.

Mentha limosa is often used in herbal remedies and is valued for its adaptability to wet conditions.
