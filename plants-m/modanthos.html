Modanthos

Modanthos, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small raindrops. The plant has compound leaves that are opposite and pinnately arranged, with oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The Modanthos is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
