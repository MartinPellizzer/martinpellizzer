Melochia parhamii

Melochia parhamii, commonly known as the African shield vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Melochia parhamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
