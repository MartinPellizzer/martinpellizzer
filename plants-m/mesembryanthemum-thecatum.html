Mesembryanthemum thecatum

Mesembryanthemum thecatum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its ability to form dense mats makes it useful for erosion control and as a ground cover.

The species is also popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
