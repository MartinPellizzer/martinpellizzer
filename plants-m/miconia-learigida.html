Miconia learigida

Miconia learigida, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. Miconia learigida is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions, posing a threat to local ecosystems.
