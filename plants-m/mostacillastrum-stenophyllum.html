Mostacillastrum stenophyllum

Mostacillastrum stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
