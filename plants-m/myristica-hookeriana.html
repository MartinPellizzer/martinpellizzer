Myristica hookeriana

Myristica hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that contain valuable seeds. The plant produces a distinctive, fragrant resin known as myrrh, which has been used historically in perfumes and traditional medicine. Its leaves are large, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and typically yellow or green.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the extraction of its essential oils.
