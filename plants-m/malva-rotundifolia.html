Malva rotundifolia

Malva rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy pink or purple flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
