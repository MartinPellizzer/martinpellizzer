Maba quercina

Maba quercina, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and produces small, spiky capsules that contain one to three chestnuts. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive flaky texture.

The plant is valued for its hardy nature and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
