Mentha arvensis var. foliicoma

Mentha arvensis var. foliicoma, commonly known as woolly mint, is a variant of the field mint species.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly pubescence that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a strong, aromatic scent.

Woolly mint is often used in herbal remedies and can be found growing in meadows and along stream banks.
