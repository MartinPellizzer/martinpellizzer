Myrtus punctata

Myrtus punctata, commonly known as the spotted myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and they appear in clusters during the spring.

The fruit is a small, dark blue berry that contains several seeds.
