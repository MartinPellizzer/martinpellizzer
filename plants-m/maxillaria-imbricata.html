Maxillaria imbricata

Maxillaria imbricata, commonly known as the wrinkled maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous growth habit and its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in moisture retention. Its inflorescence typically consists of several flowers arranged in a raceme, with each bloom having a distinctive, intricate pattern of veins.

Maxillaria imbricata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
