Myrceugenia regnelliana f. xanthochlora

Myrceugenia regnelliana f. xanthochlora, commonly known as the yellow-leaved myrtle, is a variant of the myrtle plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellowish-green foliage, which contrasts with the typical green leaves of the species. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antimicrobial properties.

The botanical features of this form make it a unique and valuable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
