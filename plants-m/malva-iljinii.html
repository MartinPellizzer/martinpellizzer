Malva iljinii

Malva iljinii, commonly known as the Iljin's mallows, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Malva iljinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
