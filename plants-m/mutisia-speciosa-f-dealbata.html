Mutisia speciosa f. dealbata

Mutisia speciosa f. dealbata, commonly known as the white mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
