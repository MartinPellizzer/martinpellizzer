Macleania laurina

Macleania laurina, commonly known as the silverleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring glossy, dark green leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming from spring to fall. Its flowers are followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Macleania laurina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
