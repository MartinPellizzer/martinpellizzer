Malaxis brachypoda

Malaxis brachypoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved malaxis, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, erect stem and linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

The species is characterized by its simple morphology and is often found in humid, tropical climates.
