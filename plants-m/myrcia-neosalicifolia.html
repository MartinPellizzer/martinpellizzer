Myrcia neosalicifolia

Myrcia neosalicifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia neosalicifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
