Mucizonia campanulata

Mucizonia campanulata, commonly known as the blue bell flower, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Mucizonia campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
