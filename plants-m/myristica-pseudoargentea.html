Myristica pseudoargentea

Myristica pseudoargentea, commonly known as silver nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic seeds that resemble nutmeg. The plant produces yellowish flowers and has glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release the aromatic seeds.

The plant is valued for its unique fragrance and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
