Moskerion moschatum

Moskerion moschatum, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features long, flat, sword-shaped leaves that float just beneath the water surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Moskerion moschatum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
