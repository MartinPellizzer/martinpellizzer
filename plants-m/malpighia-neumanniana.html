Malpighia neumanniana

Malpighia neumanniana, commonly known as the Neumann's acanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features striking, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, red or orange flowers that attract pollinators. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and floral display.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, edible seeds.
