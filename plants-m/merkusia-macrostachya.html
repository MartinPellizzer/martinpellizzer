Merkusia macrostachya

Merkusia macrostachya, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supported by a dense network of rhizomes. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike-like structure. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often forming a dense mat near the water's edge.

Merkusia macrostachya thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.
