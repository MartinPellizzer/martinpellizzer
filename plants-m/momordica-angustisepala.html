Momordica angustisepala

Momordica angustisepala, commonly known as the narrow-lobed bitter gourd, is a climbing vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fruit that matures to a bright orange color. The fruit is typically elongated and has a distinctive, narrow base, which gives it its specific epithet.

It is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in various traditional cuisines and have medicinal properties.
