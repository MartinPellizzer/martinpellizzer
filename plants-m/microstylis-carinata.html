Microstylis carinata

Microstylis carinata, commonly known as the ridged microstylis, is a small perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, acidic soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters at the base of the plant.

Microstylis carinata plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
