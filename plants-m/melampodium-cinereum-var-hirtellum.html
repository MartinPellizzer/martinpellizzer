Melampodium cinereum var. hirtellum

Melampodium cinereum var. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy sunflower.

This variant is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the southern United States. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
