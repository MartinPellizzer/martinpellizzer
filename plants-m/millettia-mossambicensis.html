Millettia mossambicensis

Millettia mossambicensis, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soils make it an important species for reforestation and soil stabilization.
