Moniera amplexicaulis

Moniera amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping stem fern, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its unique growth habit, with its fronds wrapping around the stem in a clasping manner. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in coastal areas or on rocky outcrops. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, with a leathery texture that helps it retain moisture.

Moniera amplexicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
