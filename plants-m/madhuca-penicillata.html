Madhuca penicillata

Madhuca penicillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lanceolate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in construction and furniture.

Additionally, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
