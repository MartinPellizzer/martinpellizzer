Masdevallia calocalix

Masdevallia calocalix, commonly known as the showy masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting its delicate, strap-like leaves.

The flowers are short-lived but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
