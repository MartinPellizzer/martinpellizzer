Macrosamanea pedicellaris

Macrosamanea pedicellaris, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in the winter months. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in indoor environments with proper care. Its foliage consists of segmented, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a zigzag pattern.

The plant is popular as a decorative plant due to its long blooming period and unique appearance.
