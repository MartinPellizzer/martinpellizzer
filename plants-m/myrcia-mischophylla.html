Myrcia mischophylla

Myrcia mischophylla, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a shrub native to Brazil, characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
