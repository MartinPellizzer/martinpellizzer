Myristica laevis

Myristica laevis, commonly known as the smooth nutmeg tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg tree, Myristica fragrans. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
