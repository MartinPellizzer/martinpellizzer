Miediega procumbens

Miediega procumbens, commonly known as the creeping miediega, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Miediega procumbens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
