Mussaenda pubescens var. yaeyamensis

Mussaenda pubescens var. yaeyamensis, commonly known as the Yaeyama Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, giving it a showy appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal or shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
