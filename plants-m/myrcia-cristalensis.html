Myrcia cristalensis

Myrcia cristalensis, commonly known as the Crystal Bush, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Myrcia cristalensis is also an important species for biodiversity in its native habitat.
