Malus hupehensis var. taishanensis

Malus hupehensis var. taishanensis, commonly known as the Taishan crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental flowers, which bloom in early spring with a range of colors from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and turn vibrant shades of red in autumn.

It is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
