Maripa erecta

Maripa erecta, commonly known as the erect maripa, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central column.

Maripa erecta is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value in gardens.
