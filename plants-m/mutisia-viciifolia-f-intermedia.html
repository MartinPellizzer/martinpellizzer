Mutisia viciifolia f. intermedia

Mutisia viciifolia f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate mutisia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with a vicia-like appearance, giving it its specific epithet. It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
