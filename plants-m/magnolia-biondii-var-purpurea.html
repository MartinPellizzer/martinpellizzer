Magnolia biondii var. purpurea

Magnolia biondii var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, appearing before the leaves in early spring. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the autumn. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
