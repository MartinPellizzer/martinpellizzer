Myrcia splendens var. genuina

Myrcia splendens var. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian beauty or golden myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Myrcia splendens var. genuina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
