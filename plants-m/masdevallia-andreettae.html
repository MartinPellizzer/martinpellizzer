Masdevallia andreettae

Masdevallia andreettae, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate and often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as a small, terrestrial orchid with pseudobulbs and linear leaves. Its flowers are usually fragrant and exhibit a wide range of hues, including purple, pink, and white.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
