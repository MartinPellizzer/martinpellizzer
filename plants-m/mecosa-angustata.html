Mecosa angustata

Mecosa angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf mecosa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its narrow, linear leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

Mecosa angustata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
