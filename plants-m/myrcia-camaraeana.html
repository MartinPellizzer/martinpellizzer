Myrcia camaraeana

Myrcia camaraeana, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and emits a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pink, followed by red, fleshy berries.

The plant is known for its ecological impact, often becoming invasive in non-native environments.
