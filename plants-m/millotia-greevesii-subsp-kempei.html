Millotia greevesii subsp. kempei

Millotia greevesii subsp.

kempei, commonly known as the Kempe's millotia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically lance-shaped.

The plant produces tiny, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique floral structure and subtle morphological differences from other subspecies of Millotia greevesii.
