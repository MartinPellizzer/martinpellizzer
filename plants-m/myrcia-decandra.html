Myrcia decandra

Myrcia decandra, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a fragrant scent.

The flowers are usually pink or red, and the fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.
