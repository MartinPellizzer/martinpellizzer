Malabaila pastinacifolia var. macrocarpa

Malabaila pastinacifolia var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited watercress, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as streams and ponds. The plant has elongated, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with a distinct, peppery aroma. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain the seeds.

The plant is valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and often used in local cuisines.
