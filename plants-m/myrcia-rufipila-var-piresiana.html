Myrcia rufipila var. piresiana

Myrcia rufipila var. piresiana, commonly known as the Pires's myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
