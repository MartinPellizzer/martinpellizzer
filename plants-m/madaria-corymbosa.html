Madaria corymbosa

Madaria corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered madia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing a compact, bushy growth habit. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.
