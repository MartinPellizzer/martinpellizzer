Microlepia protracta

Microlepia protracta, commonly known as the slender chain fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with a creeping underground stem that produces both roots and new shoots. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, rounded pinnae that give it a delicate appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
