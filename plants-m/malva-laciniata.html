Malva laciniata

Malva laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved mallows, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Malva laciniata is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
