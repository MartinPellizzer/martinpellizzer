Malva australiana

Malva australiana, commonly known as the Australian mallows, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture and produces showy, pink or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in coastal regions.

Malva australiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
