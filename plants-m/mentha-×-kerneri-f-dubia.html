Mentha × kerneri f. dubia

Mentha × kerneri f. dubia, commonly known as the slender mint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. It is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan, and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is occasionally used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
