Metabolos rigidus

Metabolos rigidus, commonly known as the rigid metabolos, is a unique plant species characterized by its sturdy, woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to conserve water through its succulent structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in late spring. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
