Macaranga hurifolia

Macaranga hurifolia, commonly known as the silky tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
