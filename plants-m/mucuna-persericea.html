Mucuna persericea

Mucuna persericea, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence covering its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.

Mucuna persericea is also notable for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
