Monnina subspeciosa

Monnina subspeciosa, commonly known as the yellow mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Monnina subspeciosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
