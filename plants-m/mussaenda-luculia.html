Mussaenda luculia

Mussaenda luculia, commonly known as the flame flower or golden flame, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that resemble flames, typically in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Mussaenda luculia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
