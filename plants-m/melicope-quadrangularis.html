Melicope quadrangularis

Melicope quadrangularis, commonly known as the quadrangular melicope, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its quadrangular (four-sided) stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often fragrant. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Melicope quadrangularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some Pacific Island cultures.
