Molina

Molina, commonly known as the molina or the "false dandelion," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are usually lobed or toothed, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk. Molina is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
