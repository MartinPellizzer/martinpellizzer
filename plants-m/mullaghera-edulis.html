Mullaghera edulis

Mullaghera edulis, commonly known as the edible mullaghera, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for food and medicinal purposes. Its adaptability to harsh mountainous environments makes it a resilient species in alpine ecosystems.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of the local biodiversity and a subject of interest for ecological studies.
