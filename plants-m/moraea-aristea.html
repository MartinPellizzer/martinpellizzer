Moraea aristea

Moraea aristea, commonly known as the purple glory flower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant features strap-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, often purple or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in gardens and horticulture.
