Melica

Melica, commonly known as green millet, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It includes over 100 species, many of which are found in temperate and subtropical regions around the world. These plants typically have slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. They produce small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Melica species are often used in ecological restoration and are valued for their ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
