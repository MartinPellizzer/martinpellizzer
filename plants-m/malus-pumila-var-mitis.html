Malus pumila var. mitis

Malus pumila var. mitis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a cultivar of the pear tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, white, sweet fruits that are often used in both culinary and ornamental contexts. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree with a compact, rounded shape and dark green, glossy leaves. It produces fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible fruit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various climates.
