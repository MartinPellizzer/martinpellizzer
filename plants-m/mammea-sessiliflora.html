Mammea sessiliflora

Mammea sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower mammea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both horticultural and traditional uses.
