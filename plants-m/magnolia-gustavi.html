Magnolia gustavi

Magnolia gustavi, commonly known as the Gustav's magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
