Mimosa endlichii

Mimosa endlichii, commonly known as the yellow mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves that are sensitive to touch, curling when disturbed. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mimosa endlichii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
