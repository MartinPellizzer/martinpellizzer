Mantisalca duriaei var. typica

Mantisalca duriaei var. typica, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in survival in dry environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
