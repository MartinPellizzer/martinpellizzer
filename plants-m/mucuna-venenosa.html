Mucuna venenosa

Mucuna venenosa, commonly known as the poison vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, curved pods. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers and is known for its rapid growth habit. Its seeds contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.

Despite its dangers, it has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
