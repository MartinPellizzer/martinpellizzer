Myrciaria peruviana

Myrciaria peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and white with a slight pinkish tint.

The fruit, which resembles a small apple, is rich in vitamin C and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
