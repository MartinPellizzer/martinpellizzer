Myriactis mekongensis

Myriactis mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong myriapod, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a cluster of delicate, thread-like leaves that resemble the legs of a centipede. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of dense rainforests. Its reproductive structures are highly specialized, with small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters.

Due to habitat destruction and limited research, the conservation status of Myriactis mekongensis remains poorly understood.
