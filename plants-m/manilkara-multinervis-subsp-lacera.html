Manilkara multinervis subsp. lacera

Manilkara multinervis subsp.

lacera, commonly known as the lac resin tree, is a member of the Sapotaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense crown.

The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth surface with prominent veins. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed.

The plant is notable for its latex, which is tapped for the production of natural lac resin used in various industrial applications.
