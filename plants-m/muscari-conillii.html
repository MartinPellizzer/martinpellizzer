Muscari conillii

Muscari conillii, commonly known as the alpine grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Muscari conillii is also noted for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and partial shade.
