Melastoma hispidum

Melastoma hispidum, commonly known as the hairy melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or red, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Melastoma hispidum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
