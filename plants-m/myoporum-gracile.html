Myoporum gracile

Myoporum gracile, commonly known as silver leaf myoporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, oval leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Myoporum gracile is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in coastal and dry environments.
