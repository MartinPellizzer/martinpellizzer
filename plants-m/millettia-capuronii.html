Millettia capuronii

Millettia capuronii, commonly known as the Capuron's millettia, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal studies.
