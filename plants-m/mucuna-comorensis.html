Mucuna comorensis

Mucuna comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Comoros Islands.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Mucuna comorensis is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
