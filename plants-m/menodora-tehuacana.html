Menodora tehuacana

Menodora tehuacana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the family Menodoridae and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Menodora tehuacana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting nighttime pollination.
