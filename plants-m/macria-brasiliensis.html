Macria brasiliensis

Macria brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian macria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slight hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
