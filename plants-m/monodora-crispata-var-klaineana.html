Monodora crispata var. klaineana

Monodora crispata var. klaineana, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, starchy fruits that are used in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
