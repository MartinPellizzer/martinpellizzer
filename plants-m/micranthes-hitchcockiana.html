Micranthes hitchcockiana

Micranthes hitchcockiana, commonly known as Hitchcock's wintergreen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense mat. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodland environments.

Micranthes hitchcockiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for early pollinators.
