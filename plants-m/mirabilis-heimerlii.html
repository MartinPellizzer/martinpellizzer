Mirabilis heimerlii

Mirabilis heimerlii, commonly known as the desert four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Nyctaginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

The flowers come in shades of pink, red, and purple, adding vibrant color to desert landscapes.
