Megaphrynium velutinum

Megaphrynium velutinum, commonly known as the velvet leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of starch.
