Maxillaria fuchsii

Maxillaria fuchsii, commonly known as the Fuchs's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink with a distinctive yellow lip.

Maxillaria fuchsii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
