Melastoma brachiatum

Melastoma brachiatum, commonly known as the purple-flowered melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of pink to purple flowers, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Melastoma brachiatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes.
