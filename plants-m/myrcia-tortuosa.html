Myrcia tortuosa

Myrcia tortuosa, commonly known as the zigzag myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its twisted, zigzagging branches and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Myrcia tortuosa is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.
