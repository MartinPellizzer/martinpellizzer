Mammillaria heyderi var. bullingtoniana

Mammillaria heyderi var. bullingtoniana, commonly known as the Bullington's mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant typically produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
