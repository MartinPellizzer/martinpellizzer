Myodocarpus lanceolatus

Myodocarpus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myodocarpus, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that contain one or two seeds, which are typically dispersed by animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm regions.
