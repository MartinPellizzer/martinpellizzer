Matelea inconspicua

Matelea inconspicua, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated primarily by moths, making it an important species for nocturnal pollinators.
