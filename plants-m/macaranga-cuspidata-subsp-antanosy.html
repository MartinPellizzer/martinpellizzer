Macaranga cuspidata subsp. antanosy

Macaranga cuspidata subsp.

antanosy, commonly known as the Antanosy macaranga, is a species native to Madagascar. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces capsules as its fruit type.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
