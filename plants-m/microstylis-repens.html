Microstylis repens

Microstylis repens, commonly known as the creeping microstylis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its stems are prostrate, often forming dense mats, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
