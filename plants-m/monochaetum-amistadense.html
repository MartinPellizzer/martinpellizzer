Monochaetum amistadense

Monochaetum amistadense, commonly known as the Amistad grass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping projects.
