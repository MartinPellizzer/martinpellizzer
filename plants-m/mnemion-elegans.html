Mnemion elegans

Mnemion elegans, commonly known as the elegant kniphofia, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers, which come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
