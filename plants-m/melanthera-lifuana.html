Melanthera lifuana

Melanthera lifuana, commonly known as the Fijian melastoma, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Fiji.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it often grows in shaded, moist environments.

Melanthera lifuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
