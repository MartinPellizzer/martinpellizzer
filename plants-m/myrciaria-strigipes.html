Myrciaria strigipes

Myrciaria strigipes, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, appressed hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. The berries are edible and have a tart, sweet flavor, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
