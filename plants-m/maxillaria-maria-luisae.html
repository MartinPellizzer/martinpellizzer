Maxillaria maria-luisae

Maxillaria maria-luisae, commonly known as the Maria Luisa orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with pseudobulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, often with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria maria-luisae is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
