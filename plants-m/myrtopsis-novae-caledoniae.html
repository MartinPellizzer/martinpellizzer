Myrtopsis novae-caledoniae

Myrtopsis novae-caledoniae, commonly known as the New Caledonia myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features glossy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. It is typically found in moist, shaded forest understories and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Myrtopsis novae-caledoniae is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
