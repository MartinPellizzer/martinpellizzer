Monstera officinalis

Monstera officinalis, commonly known as the Swiss cheese plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop fenestrations as they mature, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its vigorous growth habit, often requiring support as it climbs. Its leaves are typically dark green with a glossy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by black berries.

Monstera officinalis is widely cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
