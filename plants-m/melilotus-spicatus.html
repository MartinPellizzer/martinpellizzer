Melilotus spicatus

Melilotus spicatus, commonly known as spiked sweet clover, is an herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Melilotus spicatus is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
