Melocanna excelsa

Melocanna excelsa, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of over 20 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
