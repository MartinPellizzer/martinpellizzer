Melo persicus

Melo persicus, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of Asia and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and lobed leaves. The plant produces large, green, ribbed fruits with a sweet, aromatic flesh. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

Melo persicus is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is valued for its high water content and refreshing taste.
