Metrosideros collina var. temehaniensis

Metrosideros collina var. temehaniensis, commonly known as the Temehaniensia Christmas Bush, is a unique variant of the Christmas Bush found in specific regions of the South Pacific.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom during the Christmas season. The plant thrives in coastal and forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and humid climates. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural displays, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
