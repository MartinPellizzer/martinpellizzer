Myoxanthus pulvinatus

Myoxanthus pulvinatus, commonly known as the cushion myoxanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub that thrives in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months of the year. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in its arid environment.

Myoxanthus pulvinatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
