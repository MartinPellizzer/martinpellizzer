Melissitus kafirniganicus

Melissitus kafirniganicus, commonly known as the African honeycomb plant, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, honeycomb-like arrangement of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
