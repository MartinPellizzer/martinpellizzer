Menonvillea linearis var. trifida

Menonvillea linearis var. trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features fleshy, linear leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid environments.

Its unique leaf structure and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
