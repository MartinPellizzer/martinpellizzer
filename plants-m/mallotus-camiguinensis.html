Mallotus camiguinensis

Mallotus camiguinensis, commonly known as the Camiguin holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
