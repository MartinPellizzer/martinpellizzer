Mentha haplocalyx subsp. austera

Mentha haplocalyx subsp.

austera, commonly known as Australian peppermint, is a perennial herb native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves that emit a strong menthol scent when crushed.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
