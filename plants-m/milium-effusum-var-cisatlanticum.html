Milium effusum var. cisatlanticum

Milium effusum var. cisatlanticum, commonly known as the eastern marsh millet, is a variant of the marsh millet species native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters.

This variant is adapted to the humid climates of the eastern United States and plays a role in wetland ecosystems.
