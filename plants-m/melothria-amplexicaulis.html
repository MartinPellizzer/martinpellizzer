Melothria amplexicaulis

Melothria amplexicaulis, commonly known as the creeping cucumber, is a vine-like plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It has heart-shaped leaves that often wrap around its stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that resemble cucumbers, which are edible when mature. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female blossoms.

Melothria amplexicaulis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing along stream banks or in dense undergrowth.
