Moquinia lucida

Moquinia lucida, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and spikes of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

Moquinia lucida plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic insects and birds.
