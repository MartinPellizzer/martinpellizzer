Megastachya acutiflora

Megastachya acutiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Megastachya acutiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
