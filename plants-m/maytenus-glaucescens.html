Maytenus glaucescens

Maytenus glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-gray mayten, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or secondary forests. The plant has alternate, simple, and usually ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its small, white flowers are clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals.

Maytenus glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appearance and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
