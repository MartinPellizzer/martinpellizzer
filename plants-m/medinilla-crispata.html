Medinilla crispata

Medinilla crispata, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

This evergreen shrub is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Medinilla crispata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and pleasant fragrance.
