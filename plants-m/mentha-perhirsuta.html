Mentha perhirsuta

Mentha perhirsuta, commonly known as the rough-stemmed mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Mentha perhirsuta is known for its aromatic, minty scent and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
