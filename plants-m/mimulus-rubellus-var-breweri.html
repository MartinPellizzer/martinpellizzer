Mimulus rubellus var. breweri

Mimulus rubellus var. breweri, commonly known as the Brewer's monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and wet meadows. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
