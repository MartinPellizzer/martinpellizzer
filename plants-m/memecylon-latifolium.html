Memecylon latifolium

Memecylon latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf memecylon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
