Melanodiscus africanus

Melanodiscus africanus, commonly known as the African black-eyed bean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its dark purple or black seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to poor soils, making it a hardy species.

Melanodiscus africanus also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
