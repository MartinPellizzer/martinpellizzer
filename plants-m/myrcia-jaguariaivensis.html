Myrcia jaguariaivensis

Myrcia jaguariaivensis, commonly known as the jaguariaivensis myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical conditions. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
