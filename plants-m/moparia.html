Moparia

Moparia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal spikes. Its fibrous roots and robust stems make it a hardy species capable of thriving in a variety of soil types.

Moparia is often found in disturbed areas and can become an invasive weed in certain regions.
