Mentha longifolia subsp. polyadena

Mentha longifolia subsp.

polyadena, commonly known as narrow-leaf peppermint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a strong menthol scent.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for its cooling and invigorating properties.
