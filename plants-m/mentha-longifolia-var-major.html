Mentha longifolia var. major

Mentha longifolia var. major, commonly known as hedge mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Hedge mint is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
