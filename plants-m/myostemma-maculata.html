Myostemma maculata

Myostemma maculata, commonly known as the spotted myostemma, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system, which help it conserve water. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant gardens.
