Mikania racemulosa

Mikania racemulosa, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and twining stems that can cover large areas. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Mikania racemulosa is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates, making it both a common weed and an ornamental plant.
