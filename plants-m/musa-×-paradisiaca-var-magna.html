Musa × paradisiaca var. magna

Musa × paradisiaca var. magna, commonly known as the elephant ear banana, is a large, herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of large, yellow-orange bananas that are typically harvested when fully mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both decorative and culinary purposes.
