Mammillaria ceratophora

Mammillaria ceratophora, commonly known as the horned milk barrel, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
