Monocyclanthus

Monocyclanthus, commonly known as the single-cylinder plant, is a unique genus within the family Cyclanthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which features a single, spirally arranged whorl of bracts. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually broad and alternate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are often small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.
