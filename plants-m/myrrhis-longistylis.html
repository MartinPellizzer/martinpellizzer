Myrrhis longistylis

Myrrhis longistylis, commonly known as the long-styled myrrh, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow flower heads that are composed of numerous small florets.

Myrrhis longistylis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.
