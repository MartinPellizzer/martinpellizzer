Mimosa multiglandulosa

Mimosa multiglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular mimosa, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait called thigmonastic response. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable environments.
