Myrtus brunnea

Myrtus brunnea, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. Myrtus brunnea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
