Messerschmidia velutina

Messerschmidia velutina, commonly known as the velvet leaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are thick and waxy, helping it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are usually pale green to yellowish in color, adding a subtle beauty to the understory of forests.
