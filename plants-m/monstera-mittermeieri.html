Monstera mittermeieri

Monstera mittermeieri, commonly known as the "Swiss cheese plant," is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop fenestrations, or holes, as they mature. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and anchor itself to trees or other structures.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it has become a popular houseplant among indoor gardeners.
