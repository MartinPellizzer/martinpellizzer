Macrocnemum stylocarpum

Macrocnemum stylocarpum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with minimal water.

Macrocnemum stylocarpum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert insects and small animals.
