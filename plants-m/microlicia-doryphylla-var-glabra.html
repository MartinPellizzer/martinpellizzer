Microlicia doryphylla var. glabra

Microlicia doryphylla var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth microlicia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and windy coastal environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in coastal landscaping for erosion control.
