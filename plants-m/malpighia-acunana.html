Malpighia acunana

Malpighia acunana, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a small shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and foliage. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
