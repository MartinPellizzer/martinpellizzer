Marsdenia ludani

Marsdenia ludani, commonly known as the red-flowered Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as birds and insects. Its fruits are follicles that contain seeds with a tuft of hair, aiding in wind dispersal.

Marsdenia ludani is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
