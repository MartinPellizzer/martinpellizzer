Metanemone ranunculoides

Metanemone ranunculoides, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided leaflets, and it produces small, green berries that contain seeds.

Metanemone ranunculoides is often found in clusters, hence its common name.
