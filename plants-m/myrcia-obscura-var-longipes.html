Myrcia obscura var. longipes

Myrcia obscura var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed myrcia, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and clustered in dense inflorescences, producing a distinctive sweet scent.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to red when mature, attracting various bird species.
