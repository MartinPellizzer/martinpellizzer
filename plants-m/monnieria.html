Monnieria

Monnieria, commonly known as the "buttercup fern," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Monnieria species are typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
