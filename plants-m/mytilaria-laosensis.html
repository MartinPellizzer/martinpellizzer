Mytilaria laosensis

Mytilaria laosensis, commonly known as the Laos tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible, sour fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Mytilaria laosensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
