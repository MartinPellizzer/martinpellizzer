Macrochloa gigantea

Macrochloa gigantea, commonly known as the giant grass tree, is a unique plant native to the coastal regions of Western Australia.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are typically bright red or orange in color. It is adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a conservation priority in its native habitat.
