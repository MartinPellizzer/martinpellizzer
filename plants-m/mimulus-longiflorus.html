Mimulus longiflorus

Mimulus longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered monkeyflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mimulus longiflorus is an important species for pollinators such as bees and butterflies due to its nectar-rich flowers.
