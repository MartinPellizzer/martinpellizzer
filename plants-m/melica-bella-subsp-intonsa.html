Melica bella subsp. intonsa

Melica bella subsp.

intonsa, commonly known as the elegant meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks.

The plant features slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which is often slightly drooping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
