Mephitidia apiocarpa

Mephitidia apiocarpa, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, honey-like flavor. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem with a smooth, grayish bark.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of natural flavoring.
