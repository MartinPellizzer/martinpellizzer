Montia nevadensis

Montia nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Valley speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters. It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its delicate appearance and short life cycle.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sensitive species in its native range.
