Mesadenia suaveolens

Mesadenia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Mesadenia suaveolens is native to tropical regions of Africa and is known for its fragrant, aromatic leaves.
