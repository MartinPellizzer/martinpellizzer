Myracrodruon urundeuva

Myracrodruon urundeuva, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
