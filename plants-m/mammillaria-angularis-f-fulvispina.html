Mammillaria angularis f. fulvispina

Mammillaria angularis f. fulvispina, commonly known as the golden spine mammillaria, is a compact cactus with a globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale yellow spines.

It originates from the arid regions of Mexico, where it thrives in full sun and well-draining soil. The plant features a ribbed stem that may develop a reddish or purplish hue when exposed to intense sunlight. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from the areoles in late spring or early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
