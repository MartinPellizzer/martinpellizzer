Medicago tuberculata var. syriaca

Medicago tuberculata var. syriaca, commonly known as Syrian horseshoe vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

This species is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
