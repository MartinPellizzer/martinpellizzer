Mahonia lomariifolia

Mahonia lomariifolia, commonly known as the creeping mahonia, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of leathery, dark green leaves with spiny margins. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.

Mahonia lomariifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
