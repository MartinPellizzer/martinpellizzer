Mariscus tacnensis

Mariscus tacnensis, commonly known as the Andean marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
