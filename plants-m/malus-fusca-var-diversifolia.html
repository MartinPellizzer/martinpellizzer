Malus fusca var. diversifolia

Malus fusca var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variable crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf forms, ranging from elliptic to ovate, with serrated margins and a variety of colors from dark green to reddish bronze. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, tart fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a valuable species for wildlife and garden landscapes.
