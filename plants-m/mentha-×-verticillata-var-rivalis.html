Mentha × verticillata var. rivalis

Mentha × verticillata var. rivalis, commonly known as narrow-leaf water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
