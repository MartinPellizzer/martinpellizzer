Mesosetum wrightii

Mesosetum wrightii, commonly known as Wright's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture and a bluish-green color.

Mesosetum wrightii plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
