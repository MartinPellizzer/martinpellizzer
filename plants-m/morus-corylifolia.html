Morus corylifolia

Morus corylifolia, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry or small-leaved mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fuzzy fruits that are consumed in some cultures. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its foliage and fruit.

The tree is also valued for its role in ecological restoration due to its ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
