Malus doumeri var. formosana

Malus doumeri var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Taiwan.

It features ornamental white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits. The plant has ovate to lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its compact size and seasonal beauty make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
