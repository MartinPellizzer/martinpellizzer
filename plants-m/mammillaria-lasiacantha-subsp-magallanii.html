Mammillaria lasiacantha subsp. magallanii

Mammillaria lasiacantha subsp.

magallanii, commonly known as the Magallanii Hedgehog Cactus, is a compact, spiny cactus native to South America. It features a globular or cylindrical stem with prominent, white or pale areoles that bear clusters of white or pale blue bristles.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental appearance, it is popular among cactus enthusiasts.
