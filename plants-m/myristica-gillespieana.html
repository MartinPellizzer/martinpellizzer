Myristica gillespieana

Myristica gillespieana, commonly known as the Christmas Island nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Christmas Island in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive red, fleshy fruits that enclose a single seed, which is surrounded by a hard, aromatic shell. It thrives in humid, lowland rainforest environments and is known for its unique ecological role on the island.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species.
