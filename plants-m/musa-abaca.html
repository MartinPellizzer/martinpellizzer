Musa abaca

Musa abaca, commonly known as the abaca plant, is a tropical species native to the Philippines.

It is a member of the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and robust stem. The plant is primarily cultivated for its fibrous stalks, which are used to produce natural fibers for textiles and rope. Its growth requires a warm, humid climate with well-drained soil.

Musa abaca is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other tropical regions for agricultural and commercial purposes.
