Melandrium apricum

Melandrium apricum, commonly known as the common sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically thrives in sandy or calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as heaths, grasslands, and coastal areas. The plant features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, usually from April to May. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are prostrate or ascending.

Melandrium apricum is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, clustered flowers and succulent-like foliage.
