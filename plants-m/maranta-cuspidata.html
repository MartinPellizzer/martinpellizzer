Maranta cuspidata

Maranta cuspidata, commonly known as the arrowleaf maranta, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes many ornamental plants known for their striking foliage. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and purple. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which appear in clusters.

Maranta cuspidata is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
