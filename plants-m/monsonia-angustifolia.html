Monsonia angustifolia

Monsonia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved monsonia, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in terminal spikes, and are usually pink or purple in color.

Monsonia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering display.
