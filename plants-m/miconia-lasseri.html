Miconia lasseri

Miconia lasseri, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that eventually develop into bright orange, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in jams and juices.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
