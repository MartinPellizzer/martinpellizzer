Myrrhis occidentalis

Myrrhis occidentalis, commonly known as western sweetflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It features a rhizomatous root system and linear, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Myrrhis occidentalis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
