Metalepis albiflora

Metalepis albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green color.

Metalepis albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
