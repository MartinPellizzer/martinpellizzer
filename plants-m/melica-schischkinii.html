Melica schischkinii

Melica schischkinii, commonly known as Schischkin's meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Melica schischkinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
