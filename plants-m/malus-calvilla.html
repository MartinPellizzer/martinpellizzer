Malus calvilla

Malus calvilla, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, featuring thorny branches and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pink, appearing in clusters, and the fruit is a small, tart apple-like berry.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional Chinese horticulture.
