Medicago lupulina proles cupianiana

Medicago lupulina proles cupianiana, commonly known as the cupianica goat's rue, is a variant of the goat's rue species native to specific regions of China.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments. It has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, though its use remains limited compared to other legumes.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique floral structure and adaptability to harsh climates.
