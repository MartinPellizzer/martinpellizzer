Mentha × schultziana var. carniolica

Mentha × schultziana var. carniolica, commonly known as the Carpathian mint, is a hybrid mint species native to the Carpathian region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomatous stems that allow it to spread vegetatively.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
