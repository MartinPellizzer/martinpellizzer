Malesherbia humilis var. gabrielae

Malesherbia humilis var. gabrielae, commonly known as the Gabriel's malesherbia, is a low-growing shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, woolly texture. The plant produces delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
