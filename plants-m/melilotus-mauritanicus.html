Melilotus mauritanicus

Melilotus mauritanicus, commonly known as the Moroccan sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Melilotus mauritanicus is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
