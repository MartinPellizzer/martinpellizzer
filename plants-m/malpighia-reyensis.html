Malpighia reyensis

Malpighia reyensis, commonly known as the Rey's beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are typically bright red or yellow when mature. These berries are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

Additionally, the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
