Myrcianthes pungens

Myrcianthes pungens, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a strong, spicy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Myrcianthes pungens is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
