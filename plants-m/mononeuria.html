Mononeuria

Mononeuria, commonly known as the "buttercup of the sea," is a unique flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble those of the buttercup, though it is not related to the true buttercups. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in its arid environment. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender stalks and bloom during the summer months.

Mononeuria is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in harsh, saline conditions.
