Millettia glaucescens

Millettia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-leaf millettia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
