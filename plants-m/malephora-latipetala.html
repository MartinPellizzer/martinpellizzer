Malephora latipetala

Malephora latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petalled malephora, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Melanthiaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize open, sunny habitats.

Native to certain regions of South America, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
