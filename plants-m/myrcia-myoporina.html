Myrcia myoporina

Myrcia myoporina, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia myoporina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
