Malmea lucida

Malmea lucida, commonly known as the silverleaf malmea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Malmea lucida is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
