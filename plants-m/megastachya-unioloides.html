Megastachya unioloides

Megastachya unioloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf tawny coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its showy, orange to yellow flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a suitable addition to native gardens.
