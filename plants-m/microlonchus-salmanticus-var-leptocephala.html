Microlonchus salmanticus var. leptocephala

Microlonchus salmanticus var. leptocephala, commonly known as the slender-headed grass vetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, producing a yellowish-green color.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or semi-arid environments.
