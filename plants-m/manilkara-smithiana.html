Manilkara smithiana

Manilkara smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's manilkara, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and smooth, greyish bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, green fruits with a thick, sticky pulp. Its latex, similar to that of the sapodilla, is used in traditional medicine and for making chewing gum.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
