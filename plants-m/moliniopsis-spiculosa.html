Moliniopsis spiculosa

Moliniopsis spiculosa, commonly known as the spiky hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and distinctive spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are inconspicuous, but the plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.

Moliniopsis spiculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions in its native environment.
