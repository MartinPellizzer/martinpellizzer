Mallotus discolor

Mallotus discolor, commonly known as the variegated mallot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that often display variegation. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
