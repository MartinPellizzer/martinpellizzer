Melitella pusilla

Melitella pusilla, commonly known as the small melitella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale blue. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salt spray and drought conditions.

Melitella pusilla is a low-growing species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
