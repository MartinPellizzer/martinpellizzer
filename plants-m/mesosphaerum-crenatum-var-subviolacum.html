Mesosphaerum crenatum var. subviolacum

Mesosphaerum crenatum var. subviolacum, commonly known as the purple-veined mesosphaerum, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and distinctive violet-pink floral hues. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins and a glossy surface. This variant is noted for its unique coloration, particularly the deep purple veins that contrast with its pale green foliage.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
