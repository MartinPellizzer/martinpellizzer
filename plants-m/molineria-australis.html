Molineria australis

Molineria australis, commonly known as the Australian flat sedge, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear yellowish-green flowers. It is often found in dense stands, contributing to the structure and biodiversity of its habitat.

Molineria australis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
