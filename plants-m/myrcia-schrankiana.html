Myrcia schrankiana

Myrcia schrankiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and have a strong, spicy scent, while its fruits are small, capsule-like berries that contain aromatic seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
