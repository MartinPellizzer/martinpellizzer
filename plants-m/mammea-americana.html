Mammea americana

Mammea americana, commonly known as the American mammea, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy fruit.

However, it is also known to have toxic properties, making it important to handle with care.
