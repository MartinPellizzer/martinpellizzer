Medinilla petelotii

Medinilla petelotii, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

It is a flowering plant native to the Philippines, characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble bunches of small, fragrant flowers. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, pinkish-white blooms that are highly ornamental. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Medinilla petelotii is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
