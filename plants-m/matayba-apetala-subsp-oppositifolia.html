Matayba apetala subsp. oppositifolia

Matayba apetala subsp.

oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved matayba, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, which gives it its subspecific name.

The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually oval to lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.
