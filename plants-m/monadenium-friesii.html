Monadenium friesii

Monadenium friesii, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that typically grows up to 30 centimeters in height, characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant's leaves are small, narrow, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Monadenium friesii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
