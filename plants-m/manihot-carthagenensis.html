Manihot carthagenensis

Manihot carthagenensis, commonly known as the Carthage arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces edible tubers resembling those of arrowroot. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The tubers are used as a starchy food source and can be cooked similarly to potatoes or yams.
