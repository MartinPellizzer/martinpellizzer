Melampodium glabribracteatum

Melampodium glabribracteatum, commonly known as the smooth-bracted ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as prairies, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
