Mascagnia ovatifolia f. cordata

Mascagnia ovatifolia f. cordata, commonly known as the cordate mascagnia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically broader at the base, giving them a cordate or heart-shaped appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is dense and leathery, providing a striking visual contrast against its pale green stems.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils in mild climates.
