Melastoma epidendrum

Melastoma epidendrum, commonly known as the red-flowered Melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Melastoma epidendrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
