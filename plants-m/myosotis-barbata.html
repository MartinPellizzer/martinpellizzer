Myosotis barbata

Myosotis barbata, commonly known as the beard sapphire, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Myosotis barbata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
