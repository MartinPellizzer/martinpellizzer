Machaerium paraense

Machaerium paraense, commonly known as the Brazilian bristlepod, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
