Mimosopsis orizabensis

Mimosopsis orizabensis, commonly known as the Mexican mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
