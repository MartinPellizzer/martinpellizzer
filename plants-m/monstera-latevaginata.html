Monstera latevaginata

Monstera latevaginata, commonly known as the Mexican monstera, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop fenestrations as they mature. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach lengths of up to 10 meters when provided with adequate support. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture.

Monstera latevaginata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
