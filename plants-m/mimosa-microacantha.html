Mimosa microacantha

Mimosa microacantha, commonly known as the dwarf sensitive plant, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its delicate appearance and unique movement, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
