Marsea sophiifolia

Marsea sophiifolia, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking aquatic plant native to the high-altitude lakes of South America.

It features large, showy pink to purple flowers that bloom above the water surface, while its leaves are broad and floating. The plant has a complex root system that anchors it in the muddy lake bottoms, and it thrives in nutrient-rich, still waters.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments and its cultural importance to indigenous communities.
