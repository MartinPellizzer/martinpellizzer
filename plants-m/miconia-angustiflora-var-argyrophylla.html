Miconia angustiflora var. argyrophylla

Miconia angustiflora var. argyrophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that often have a silvery or grayish underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, it can become invasive in certain regions.
