Myosotis odorata

Myosotis odorata, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, elliptical seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. Myosotis odorata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
