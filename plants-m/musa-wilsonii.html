Musa wilsonii

Musa wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible fruit, though it is less commonly cultivated for commercial purposes compared to other banana species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Musa wilsonii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes grown in botanical gardens.
