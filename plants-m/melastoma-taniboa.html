Melastoma taniboa

Melastoma taniboa, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens, and they bloom throughout the year in some regions. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.
