Myrtus orbiculata

Myrtus orbiculata, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Myrtus orbiculata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine highlights its significant botanical and cultural importance.
