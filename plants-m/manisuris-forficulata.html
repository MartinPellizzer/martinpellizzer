Manisuris forficulata

Manisuris forficulata, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The plant's compact growth habit and vibrant floral display make it a desirable ornamental species in gardens and landscapes.
