Mentha × phyllostachya

Mentha × phyllostachya, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid mint species that combines traits from various Mentha species.

It is characterized by its square, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in many regions around the world.

Catnip is known for its strong aromatic properties and its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
