Montrichardia arborea

Montrichardia arborea, commonly known as the tree arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and dramatic inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant typically grows as an understory species, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with a woody stem that provides structural support. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is also valued as an ornamental plant in botanical gardens.

The flowers, while not showy, play a crucial role in attracting pollinators such as beetles and flies.
