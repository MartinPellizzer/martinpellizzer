Melastoma coeruleum

Melastoma coeruleum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Melastoma coeruleum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
