Mandevilla leptophylla

Mandevilla leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

Mandevilla leptophylla prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth and blooming.
