Myrcianthes sessilis

Myrcianthes sessilis, commonly known as the sessile myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

The plant is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
