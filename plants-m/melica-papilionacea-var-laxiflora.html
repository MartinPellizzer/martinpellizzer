Melica papilionacea var. laxiflora

Melica papilionacea var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, giving it a feathery appearance. It produces small, two-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or pale blue in color.

This variant is often found in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
