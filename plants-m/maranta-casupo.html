Maranta casupo

Maranta casupo, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, characterized by its unique, often ornamental foliage. The plant features striking, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and variegated patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.
