Melothria grayana

Melothria grayana, commonly known as the grayana wild cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It produces small, green, spiny fruits that mature to a bright orange or red color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and thrives in humid, warm climates. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is edible when ripe but contains seeds that are dispersed by animals.
