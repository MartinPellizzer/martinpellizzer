Mallotus pseudopenangensis

Mallotus pseudopenangensis, commonly known as the Penang mallotus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
