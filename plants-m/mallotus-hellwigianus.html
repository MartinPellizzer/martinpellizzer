Mallotus hellwigianus

Mallotus hellwigianus, commonly known as the Hellwig's mallot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
