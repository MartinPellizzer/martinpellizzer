Monarda punctata subsp. villicaulis

Monarda punctata subsp.

villicaulis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects and wildlife habitats.
