Mocinna heterophylla var. sesseana

Mocinna heterophylla var. sesseana, commonly known as the Sessean coffee plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its alternate, simple, and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its potential use in specialty coffee production due to its unique flavor profile and aroma.
