Miconia pterogona

Miconia pterogona, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia pterogona is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
