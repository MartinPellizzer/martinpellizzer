Magnolia pachyphylla

Magnolia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved magnolia, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the emergence of its foliage. The plant's leaves are thick and waxy, providing a unique texture and helping it retain moisture in its native habitat. It prefers well-drained, acidic soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a desirable addition to gardens in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated for both its beauty and biodiversity benefits.
