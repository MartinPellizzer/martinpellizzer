Mentha × pancicii

Mentha × pancicii, commonly known as the pineapple mint, is a hybrid mint species resulting from the cross between Mentha piperita (peppermint) and Mentha viridis (apple mint).

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly pine-like fragrance and its serrated, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. Mentha × pancicii is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

It is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb due to its unique flavor profile.
