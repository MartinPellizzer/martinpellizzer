Mikania scabra

Mikania scabra, commonly known as creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

Mikania scabra is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
