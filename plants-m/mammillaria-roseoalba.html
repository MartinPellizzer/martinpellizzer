Mammillaria roseoalba

Mammillaria roseoalba, commonly known as the pink-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular shape and a gray-green body covered in white to pinkish tubercles. The plant produces pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise desolate appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
