Mentha × exaltata

Mentha × exaltata, commonly known as the wild mint or purple mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowering spikes that produce small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often a vibrant green with a distinctive minty scent, and it is known for its hardiness and adaptability.

Mentha × exaltata is widely used in herbal remedies and as a decorative plant in gardens.
