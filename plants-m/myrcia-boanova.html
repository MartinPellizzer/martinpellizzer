Myrcia boanova

Myrcia boanova, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia boanova is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
