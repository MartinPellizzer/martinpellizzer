Meconopsis sulphurea subsp. sulphurea

Meconopsis sulphurea subsp.

sulphurea, commonly known as the yellow meconopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific soil and climate conditions.
