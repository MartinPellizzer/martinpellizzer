Myrtus christinae

Myrtus christinae, commonly known as the Christmas myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in winter. Its berries are typically red or black and contain seeds, making it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.

Myrtus christinae is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in coastal and sunny environments.
