Megaclinium zobiaense

Megaclinium zobiaense, commonly known as the Zobia meadowgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed florets. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
