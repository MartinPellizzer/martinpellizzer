Marrubium × bastetanum

Marrubium × bastetanum, commonly known as the hybrid horehound, is a cross between Marrubium vulgare and Marrubium incanum.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
