Myrcia subobliqua

Myrcia subobliqua, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and attract various wildlife. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
