Malapoenna castanea

Malapoenna castanea, commonly known as the red ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark red wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round fruits containing hard, brown seeds.

This species is often found in lowland forests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
