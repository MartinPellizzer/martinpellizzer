Monnina arborescens

Monnina arborescens, commonly known as the redroot false nettle, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color, especially near the base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Monnina arborescens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry conditions.
