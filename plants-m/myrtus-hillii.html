Myrtus hillii

Myrtus hillii, commonly known as the Canary Islands myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries.

Myrtus hillii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
