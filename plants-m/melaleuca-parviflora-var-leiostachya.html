Melaleuca parviflora var. leiostachya

Melaleuca parviflora var. leiostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, red or pink flowers that resemble bottlebrushes. The plant produces small, woody fruits called follicles, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native wildlife.
