Miscanthus hackelii

Miscanthus hackelii, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-tipped plumes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its ornamental value comes from its striking appearance and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.

It is also valued for its potential in bioenergy production due to its high biomass yield.
