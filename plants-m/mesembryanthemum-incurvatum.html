Mesembryanthemum incurvatum

Mesembryanthemum incurvatum, commonly known as the curled iceplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that curl inward when dry, a feature that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to hot, dry climates.

Mesembryanthemum incurvatum is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
