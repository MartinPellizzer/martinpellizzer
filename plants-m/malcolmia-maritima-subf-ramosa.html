Malcolmia maritima subf. ramosa

Malcolmia maritima subf.

ramosa, commonly known as the coastal malcolmia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and northern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its leaves are typically linear to oblanceolate, and the species is noted for its ability to tolerate salt spray and coastal winds.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
