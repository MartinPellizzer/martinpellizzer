Myrtus sintenisii

Myrtus sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenisi myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Sint Eustatius in the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, berry-like structure that turns from green to red as it matures.

Myrtus sintenisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local biodiversity.
