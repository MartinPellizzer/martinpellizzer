Maytenus planifolia

Maytenus planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaved mayten, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered in axillary or terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Maytenus planifolia is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
