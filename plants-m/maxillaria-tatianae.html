Maxillaria tatianae

Maxillaria tatianae, commonly known as the Tatiana's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in nutrient-poor conditions.

The flowers are often clustered in loose inflorescences, making it a visually appealing and sought-after ornamental plant.
