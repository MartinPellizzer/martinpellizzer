Malus halliana var. spontanea

Malus halliana var. spontanea, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its oval, glossy leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sour fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
