Maximilianea regia var. glaberrima

Maximilianea regia var. glaberrima, commonly known as the silverleaf maximilianea, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Mexico.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-water environments.
