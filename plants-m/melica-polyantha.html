Melica polyantha

Melica polyantha, commonly known as the white grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, white to pale blue flowers.

Melica polyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
