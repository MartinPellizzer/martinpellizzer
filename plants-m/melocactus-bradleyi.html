Melocactus bradleyi

Melocactus bradleyi, commonly known as the Bradley's melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces a large, spherical fruit that is typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its flowers are usually yellow or red and bloom during the warmer months.

Melocactus bradleyi is a slow-growing cactus that requires minimal water and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
