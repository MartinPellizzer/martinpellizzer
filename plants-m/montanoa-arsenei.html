Montanoa arsenei

Montanoa arsenei, commonly known as the Mexican mint or mountain mint, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its leaves are aromatic, often with a mint-like scent, and the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Montanoa arsenei is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
