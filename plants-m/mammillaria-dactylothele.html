Mammillaria dactylothele

Mammillaria dactylothele, commonly known as the woolly mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly covering that covers the stems and flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. It produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by pale, spherical fruits.

This species is popular among cactus enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
