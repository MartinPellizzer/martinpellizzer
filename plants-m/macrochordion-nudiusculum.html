Macrochordion nudiusculum

Macrochordion nudiusculum, commonly known as the slender false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white wax layer. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in a loose umbel, and bloom in early spring.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to both humans and animals.
