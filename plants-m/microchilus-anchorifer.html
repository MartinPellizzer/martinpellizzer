Microchilus anchorifer

Microchilus anchorifer, commonly known as the anchor fern, is a small, delicate fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It has slender, dark green fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant features a creeping rhizome with numerous small, clustered spore-producing structures called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Its unique appearance and compact size make it a popular choice for small garden spaces and indoor cultivation.
