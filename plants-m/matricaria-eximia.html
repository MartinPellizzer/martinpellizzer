Matricaria eximia

Matricaria eximia, commonly known as the German chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant produces small, aromatic flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are finely divided and covered in fine hairs, giving them a feathery appearance.

Matricaria eximia is often cultivated for its calming properties and is used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
