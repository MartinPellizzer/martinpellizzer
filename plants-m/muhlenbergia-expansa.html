Muhlenbergia expansa

Muhlenbergia expansa, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tall, slender flower stalks that produce delicate, fluffy plumes. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.

Muhlenbergia expansa is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
