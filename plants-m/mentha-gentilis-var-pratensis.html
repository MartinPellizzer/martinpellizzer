Mentha gentilis var. pratensis

Mentha gentilis var. pratensis, commonly known as meadow mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. It has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Mentha gentilis var. pratensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
