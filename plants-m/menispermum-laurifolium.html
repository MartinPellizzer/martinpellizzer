Menispermum laurifolium

Menispermum laurifolium, commonly known as the American Indian cucumber or laurel-leaf menispermum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble those of laurel trees. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, cucumber-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots contain alkaloids and have been historically used for their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
