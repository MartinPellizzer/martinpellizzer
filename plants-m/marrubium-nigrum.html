Marrubium nigrum

Marrubium nigrum, commonly known as black horehound, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny locations. Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their expectorant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Marrubium nigrum is also used in traditional herbal remedies for respiratory ailments.
