Musa textilis var. amboinensis

Musa textilis var. amboinensis, commonly known as the Amboinese banana, is a variant of the banana plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of small, elongated fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. It is primarily cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a mild, sweet flavor.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is also used in the breeding of modern banana cultivars.
