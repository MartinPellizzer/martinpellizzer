Muraltia thymifolia

Muraltia thymifolia, commonly known as the sweet thyme tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, small leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Muraltia thymifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
