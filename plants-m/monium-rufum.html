Monium rufum

Monium rufum, commonly known as the red bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures used for trapping small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are simple and often submerged, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.
