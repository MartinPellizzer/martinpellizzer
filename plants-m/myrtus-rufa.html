Myrtus rufa

Myrtus rufa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Myrtus rufa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
