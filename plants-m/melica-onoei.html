Melica onoei

Melica onoei, commonly known as the Japanese needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small flowering structures.

Melica onoei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
