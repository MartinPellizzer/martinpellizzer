Mysanthus uleanus var. uleanus

Mysanthus uleanus var. uleanus, commonly known as the Ulean Mysanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
