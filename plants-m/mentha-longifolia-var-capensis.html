Mentha longifolia var. capensis

Mentha longifolia var. capensis, commonly known as Cape mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features aromatic, opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds, making it useful in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
