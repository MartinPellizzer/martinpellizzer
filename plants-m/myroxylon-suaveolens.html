Myroxylon suaveolens

Myroxylon suaveolens, commonly known as the copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and resinous sap. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Its bark and wood are often used in traditional medicine and for producing natural resins.
