Myrcia fawcettii

Myrcia fawcettii, commonly known as the Fawcett's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Myrcia fawcettii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
