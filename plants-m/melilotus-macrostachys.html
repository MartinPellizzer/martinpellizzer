Melilotus macrostachys

Melilotus macrostachys, commonly known as the large-flowered melilot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, bean-like pods containing several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in open fields and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is noted for its aromatic foliage and its historical use in traditional medicine.
