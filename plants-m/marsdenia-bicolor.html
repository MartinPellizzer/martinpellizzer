Marsdenia bicolor

Marsdenia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bi-colored flowers, which typically feature a white base and a yellow or orange center. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Marsdenia bicolor is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments with moderate humidity.
