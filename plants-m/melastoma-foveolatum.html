Melastoma foveolatum

Melastoma foveolatum, commonly known as the small-cup mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
