Melaleuca macronychia

Melaleuca macronychia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in heathland and coastal environments. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, bottle-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in indigenous medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its resilience to coastal conditions.
