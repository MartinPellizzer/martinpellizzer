Medicago caerulea subsp. caspica

Medicago caerulea subsp.

caspica, commonly known as the Caspian blue medic, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are typically clustered in dense spherical inflorescences.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and semi-arid regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
