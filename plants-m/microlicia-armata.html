Microlicia armata

Microlicia armata, commonly known as the red-stemmed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish stems and large, showy flowers that range from white to pink in color. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Microlicia armata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
