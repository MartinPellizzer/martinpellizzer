Myristica punicifolia

Myristica punicifolia, commonly known as the Indian pepper tree or Malabar pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a reddish, peppery fruit that is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its botanical features include opposite, lanceolate leaves and a woody, branched stem.

Myristica punicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
