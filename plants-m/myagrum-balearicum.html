Myagrum balearicum

Myagrum balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant and have a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible berries.

Myagrum balearicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
