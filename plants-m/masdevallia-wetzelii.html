Masdevallia wetzelii

Masdevallia wetzelii, commonly known as the Wetzell's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often colorful flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized epiphyte, anchoring itself to tree trunks or branches. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapted to capture moisture from the humid air.

The orchid's unique floral structure and vibrant hues make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
