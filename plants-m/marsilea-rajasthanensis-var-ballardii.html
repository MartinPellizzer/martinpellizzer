Marsilea rajasthanensis var. ballardii

Marsilea rajasthanensis var. ballardii, commonly known as the Rajasthan water clover, is a aquatic fern species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are divided into four lobes, resembling a clover leaf. The plant typically grows in shallow, still water bodies such as ponds and wetlands. Its reproductive structures include sporocarps that release spores for propagation.

This species plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and providing habitat for microorganisms.
