Myrtus pseudocaryophyllus

Myrtus pseudocaryophyllus, commonly known as the Corsican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark purple berry that contains aromatic seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the essential oils extracted from its leaves and flowers.
