Mosla angustifolia

Mosla angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features slender, aromatic leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
