Mentha viridis var. jaccardii

Mentha viridis var. jaccardii, commonly known as the Jaccard's green mint, is a variant of the green mint plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas or as a decorative plant.
