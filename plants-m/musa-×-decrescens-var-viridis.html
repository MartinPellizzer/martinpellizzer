Musa × decrescens var. viridis

Musa × decrescens var. viridis, commonly known as the green banana plant, is a variety of the banana plant characterized by its striking green leaves and edible fruit.

This plant belongs to the Musaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 2–3 meters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are a vibrant green. The fruit, which is usually harvested when still green, is rich in potassium and has a starchy texture.

It is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
