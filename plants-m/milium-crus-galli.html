Milium crus-galli

Milium crus-galli, commonly known as the common millet or jointed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in temperate regions across Europe, Asia, and North America. Its seeds are edible and have been used historically as a food source by humans and livestock.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional agriculture.
