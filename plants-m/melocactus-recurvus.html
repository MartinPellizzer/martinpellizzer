Melocactus recurvus

Melocactus recurvus, commonly known as the hooked melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and is often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces striking, spiny flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its root system is adapted to absorb water efficiently in its dry environment.

Melocactus recurvus is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
