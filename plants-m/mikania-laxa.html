Mikania laxa

Mikania laxa, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow or white flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. Mikania laxa is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, often found in disturbed habitats.

It is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its competitive nature.
