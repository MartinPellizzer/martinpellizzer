Monvillea

Monvillea, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. Monvillea species are characterized by their climbing habit, with long, wiry stems that often trail or climb over supports. Their flowers are typically small, fragrant, and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

They thrive in warm, humid environments and require well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.
