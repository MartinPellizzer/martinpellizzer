Mutisia taraxacifolia

Mutisia taraxacifolia, commonly known as the dandelion leaf mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-bearing structures. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its native environment, making it a resilient and visually distinctive species.
