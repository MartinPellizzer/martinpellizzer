Melilotus sulcatus var. libanoticus

Melilotus sulcatus var. libanoticus, commonly known as the Lebanon melilot, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
