Manisuris mollicoma

Manisuris mollicoma, commonly known as the soft-hairy manisuris, is a unique plant species characterized by its soft, hairy foliage.

It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, often appearing in clusters along the stems.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and limited distribution.
