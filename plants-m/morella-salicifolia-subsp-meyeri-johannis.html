Morella salicifolia subsp. meyeri-johannis

Morella salicifolia subsp.

meyeri-johannis, commonly known as the Meyer-Johannis willow-leaf inkberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but typically consumed by birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental landscaping.
