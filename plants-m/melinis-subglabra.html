Melinis subglabra

Melinis subglabra, commonly known as slender barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains of seed. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in many regions.
