Mendoncia rabiensis

Mendoncia rabiensis, commonly known as the Rabiensian Bane, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its blooming season occurs during the late spring to early summer months.

Due to its limited habitat and susceptibility to climate change, it is classified as an endangered species.
