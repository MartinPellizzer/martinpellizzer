Malaxis lobbiana

Malaxis lobbiana, commonly known as the Lobb's lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and a long, slender flowering stem that produces a dense cluster of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
