Mastixia clarkeana var. macrophylla

Mastixia clarkeana var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf mastixia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often a deep green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native habitats.
