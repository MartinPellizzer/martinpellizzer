Merremia fulva

Merremia fulva, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often climbing supports as it grows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
