Momordica mixta

Momordica mixta, commonly known as the mixed bitter gourd, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn orange when mature. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a climbing habit with tendrils that help it spread along supports. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is used in various cuisines, particularly in Southeast Asia, and is valued for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
