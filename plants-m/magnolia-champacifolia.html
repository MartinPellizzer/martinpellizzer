Magnolia champacifolia

Magnolia champacifolia, commonly known as the champaca magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces yellowish-white to golden-yellow petals that emit a strong, sweet scent. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

The tree is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
