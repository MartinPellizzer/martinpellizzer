Myrianthus talbotii

Myrianthus talbotii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, flame-like red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces woody pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
