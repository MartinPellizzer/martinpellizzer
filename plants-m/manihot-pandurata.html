Manihot pandurata

Manihot pandurata, commonly known as the arrowleaf manioc, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its starchy roots, which are used in various traditional foods.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical ecosystems.
