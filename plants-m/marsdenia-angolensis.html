Marsdenia angolensis

Marsdenia angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its fibrous, woody stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicle fruits containing numerous seeds. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
