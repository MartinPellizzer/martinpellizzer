Mertensia ciliata var. stomatechoides

Mertensia ciliata var. stomatechoides, commonly known as the coastal bluebells, is a variant of the coastal bluebell native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along coastal areas and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are often covered in fine, hairy hairs.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in coastal climates with moderate to high humidity.
