Milium purpureum

Milium purpureum, commonly known as purple millet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, purple or pinkish flowers that are often clustered in dense spikes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is a hardy annual that thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
