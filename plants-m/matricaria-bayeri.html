Matricaria bayeri

Matricaria bayeri, commonly known as the Bavarian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces yellow flower heads with white ray florets. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in herbal teas.

Matricaria bayeri is valued for its calming properties and is cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
