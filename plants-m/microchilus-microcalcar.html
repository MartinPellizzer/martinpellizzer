Microchilus microcalcar

Microchilus microcalcar, commonly known as the small-lobed rock cress, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant has basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
