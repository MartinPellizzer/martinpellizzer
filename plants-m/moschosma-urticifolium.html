Moschosma urticifolium

Moschosma urticifolium, commonly known as the stinging nettle shrub, is a perennial plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging plants like the common nettle. The plant is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its stems are covered in fine hairs that can cause a stinging sensation when touched.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
