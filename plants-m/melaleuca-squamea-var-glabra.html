Melaleuca squamea var. glabra

Melaleuca squamea var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and its distinctive bottlebrush-like flowers that are typically red or pink. The plant produces small, woody fruits called follicles, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
