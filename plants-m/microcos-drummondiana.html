Microcos drummondiana

Microcos drummondiana, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, featuring slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Microcos drummondiana is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gravelly soils.
