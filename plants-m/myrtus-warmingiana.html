Myrtus warmingiana

Myrtus warmingiana, commonly known as the Maltese myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fragrant, dark green leaves and small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and garden environments.

Myrtus warmingiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
