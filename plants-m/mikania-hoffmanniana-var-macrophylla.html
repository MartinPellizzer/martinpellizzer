Mikania hoffmanniana var. macrophylla

Mikania hoffmanniana var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant Mikania, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic weed in some regions.
