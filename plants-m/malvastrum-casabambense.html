Malvastrum casabambense

Malvastrum casabambense, commonly known as the Andean mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil. It is often found in rocky or mountainous areas, where it adapts well to cooler climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
