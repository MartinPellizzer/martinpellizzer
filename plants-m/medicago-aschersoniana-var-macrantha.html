Medicago aschersoniana var. macrantha

Medicago aschersoniana var. macrantha, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. The plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
