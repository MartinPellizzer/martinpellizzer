Melocactus saxicola

Melocactus saxicola, commonly known as the rock melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from intense sunlight and herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its root system is shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from the dry, rocky soil in which it thrives.

Melocactus saxicola is well-suited for cultivation in dry, well-draining soil and requires minimal water to survive.
