Mammillaria decipiens

Mammillaria decipiens, commonly known as the false moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a covering of white, woolly spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, round fruits.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts.
