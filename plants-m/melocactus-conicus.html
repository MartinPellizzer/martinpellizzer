Melocactus conicus

Melocactus conicus, commonly known as the Easter cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its short, spiny stems that form dense, globular clusters. The plant produces vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically red, orange, or yellow in color. It is well adapted to arid environments, with a shallow root system and thick, fleshy stems for water storage.

Melocactus conicus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and blooming habits.
