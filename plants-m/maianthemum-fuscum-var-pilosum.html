Maianthemum fuscum var. pilosum

Maianthemum fuscum var. pilosum, commonly known as the silky false lily of the valley, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of eastern North America.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters at the base of its grass-like leaves. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies in moist, shaded woodlands. Its leaves are narrow, dark green, and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive texture.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Maianthemum genus, which are often mistaken for true lilies due to their similar appearance.
