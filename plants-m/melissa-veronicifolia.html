Melissa veronicifolia

Melissa veronicifolia, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains essential oils, particularly citral and geraniol, which contribute to its distinctive lemon scent. Melissa veronicifolia thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal medicine.

It is often used in aromatherapy and traditional remedies for its calming properties.
