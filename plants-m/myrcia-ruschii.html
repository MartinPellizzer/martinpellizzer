Myrcia ruschii

Myrcia ruschii, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Myrcia ruschii is often used in traditional medicine and has a pleasant, aromatic scent, making it a popular ornamental plant.
