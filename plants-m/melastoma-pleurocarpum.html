Melastoma pleurocarpum

Melastoma pleurocarpum, commonly known as the broadleaf melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release small, dark seeds.

Melastoma pleurocarpum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
