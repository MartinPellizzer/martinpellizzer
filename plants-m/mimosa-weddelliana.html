Mimosa weddelliana

Mimosa weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's mimosa, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
