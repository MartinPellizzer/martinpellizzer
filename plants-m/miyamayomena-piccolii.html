Miyamayomena piccolii

Miyamayomena piccolii, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its compact size and delicate appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
