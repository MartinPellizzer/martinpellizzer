Morus rubra

Morus rubra, commonly known as red mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn bright red in the fall. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant has historical significance in traditional medicine and as a food source for wildlife.
