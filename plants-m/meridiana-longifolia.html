Meridiana longifolia

Meridiana longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved meridiana, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
