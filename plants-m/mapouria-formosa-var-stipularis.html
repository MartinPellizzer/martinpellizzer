Mapouria formosa var. stipularis

Mapouria formosa var. stipularis, commonly known as the fragrant mapouria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a distinctive stipular structure, giving it its varietal name.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
