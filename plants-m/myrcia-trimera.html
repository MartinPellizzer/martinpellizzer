Myrcia trimera

Myrcia trimera, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and dark, glossy berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The fruit is an important food source for various bird species and can be used in traditional medicine.
