Melothria marginata var. heterophylla

Melothria marginata var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf melon vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, lobed leaves that vary in shape depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, tangy fruits resembling miniature watermelons. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The variability in leaf morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study and horticultural cultivation.
