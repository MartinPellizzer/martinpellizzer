Myristica devogelii

Myristica devogelii, commonly known as the Java nut or West Java nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces oval-shaped fruits that enclose a single, oily seed, which is the source of its aromatic oils. Its botanical features make it valuable in both ornamental horticulture and traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
