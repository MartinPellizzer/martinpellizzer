Masdevallia rhinophora

Masdevallia rhinophora, commonly known as the horned masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia and Ecuador.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flower spikes and its unique, elongated sepals that resemble horns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and delicate, with a variety of color patterns that range from white to pink and purple.

Masdevallia rhinophora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking appearance.
