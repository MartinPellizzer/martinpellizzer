Mesembryanthemum amplectens

Mesembryanthemum amplectens, commonly known as the cushion iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is widely cultivated in regions with Mediterranean climates.
