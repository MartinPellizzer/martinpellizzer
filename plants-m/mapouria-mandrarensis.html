Mapouria mandrarensis

Mapouria mandrarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar maple, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed or serrated. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries containing seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive aromatic scent when cut.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
