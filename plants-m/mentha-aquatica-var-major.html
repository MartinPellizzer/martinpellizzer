Mentha aquatica var. major

Mentha aquatica var. major, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herb native to moist environments in Europe and Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often heart-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in wet soils near streams, ponds, and marshes.

Its aromatic leaves and flowering tops are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
