Myrcia hanoverensis

Myrcia hanoverensis, commonly known as the Hanover's myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a glossy surface, often emitting a subtle, spicy scent.

Myrcia hanoverensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
