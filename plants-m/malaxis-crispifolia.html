Malaxis crispifolia

Malaxis crispifolia, commonly known as the wrinkled orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Malaxis crispifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
