Melica altissima var. atropurpurea

Melica altissima var. atropurpurea, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive purple-tinged leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
