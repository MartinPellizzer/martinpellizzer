Myrceugenia mucronata

Myrceugenia mucronata, commonly known as the velvetleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Myrceugenia mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
