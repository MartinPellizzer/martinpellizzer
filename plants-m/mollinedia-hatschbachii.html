Mollinedia hatschbachii

Mollinedia hatschbachii, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland edges. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

Mollinedia hatschbachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
