Maesa gracilis

Maesa gracilis, commonly known as the slender maesa, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, clustered in small inflorescences.

Maesa gracilis produces small, fleshy berries that are often eaten by birds and other wildlife.
