Masdevallia colibri

Masdevallia colibri, commonly known as the hummingbird orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, colorful flowers that resemble the shape and size of a hummingbird, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a small epiphyte, attaching itself to tree branches in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

Masdevallia colibri is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
