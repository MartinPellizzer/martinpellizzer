Mandevilla pubescens

Mandevilla pubescens, commonly known as the velvet flower or velvet plant, is a flowering vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun.

Mandevilla pubescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and lush foliage.
