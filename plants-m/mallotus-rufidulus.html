Mallotus rufidulus

Mallotus rufidulus, commonly known as the red-leaf mallotus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with reddish veins. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in tropical regions.
