Mimosa acerba var. strigosa

Mimosa acerba var. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and open areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
