Myrodendrum crassifolium

Myrodendrum crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrodendrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white coating. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle scent reminiscent of jasmine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Myrodendrum crassifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
