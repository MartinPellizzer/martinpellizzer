Mentha sylvestris subsp. pellita

Mentha sylvestris subsp.

pellita, commonly known as the alpine mint, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and aromatic. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers appear in whorls along the stems, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
