Medicago sativa var. gigantea

Medicago sativa var. gigantea, commonly known as giant alfalfa, is a variant of the alfalfa plant characterized by its large, robust growth habit.

It is a perennial legume that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its deep root system, which helps in soil stabilization and nutrient uptake. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is valued for its high protein content and is often used as forage for livestock.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
