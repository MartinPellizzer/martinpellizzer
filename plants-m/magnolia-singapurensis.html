Magnolia singapurensis

Magnolia singapurensis, commonly known as the Singapore magnolia, is a small evergreen tree native to Singapore and Peninsular Malaysia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It produces small, round fruits that contain winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
