Microlicia grandiflora

Microlicia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat.
