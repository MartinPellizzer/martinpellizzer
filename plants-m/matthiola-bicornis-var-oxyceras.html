Matthiola bicornis var. oxyceras

Matthiola bicornis var. oxyceras, commonly known as the oxyceratic stock, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched inflorescence and showy, four-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is native to southern Europe and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
