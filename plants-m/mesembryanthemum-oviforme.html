Mesembryanthemum oviforme

Mesembryanthemum oviforme, commonly known as the oval-leaf ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.

Mesembryanthemum oviforme is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in sandy or rocky soils.
