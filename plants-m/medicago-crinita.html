Medicago crinita

Medicago crinita, commonly known as the silky medick, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

As a legume, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
