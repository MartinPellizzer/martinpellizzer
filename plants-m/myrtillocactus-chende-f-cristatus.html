Myrtillocactus chende f. cristatus

Myrtillocactus chende f. cristatus, commonly known as the cristate myrtillocactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, tree-like growth habit, with a central stem that branches into multiple arms. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. Its foliage consists of dense, spiny areoles covered with short, white bristles.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in arid gardens due to its hardy nature.
