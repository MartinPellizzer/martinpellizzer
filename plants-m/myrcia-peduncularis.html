Myrcia peduncularis

Myrcia peduncularis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming an invasive species.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
