Malapoenna longifolia

Malapoenna longifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Malapoenna longifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
