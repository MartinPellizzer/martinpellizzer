Myrrhis iberica

Myrrhis iberica, commonly known as the Iberian myrrh, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Myrrhis iberica is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
