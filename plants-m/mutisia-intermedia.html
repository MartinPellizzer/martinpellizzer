Mutisia intermedia

Mutisia intermedia, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow ray flowers and greenish-yellow disk flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and open habitats, often in regions with a temperate climate. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy, while its stems are woody and can become branched.

Mutisia intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
