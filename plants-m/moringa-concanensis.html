Moringa concanensis

Moringa concanensis, commonly known as the Indian mallow or Malabar mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern India.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow or white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Moringa concanensis is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
