Micranthea hexandra

Micranthea hexandra, commonly known as the broadleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in tropical regions.

It has small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are pollinated by insects. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Micranthea hexandra plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and influencing host tree growth.
