Malaxis truncata

Malaxis truncata, commonly known as the truncated malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

Malaxis truncata is noted for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
