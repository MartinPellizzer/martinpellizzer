Myrcia capivarhyensis

Myrcia capivarhyensis, commonly known as the capivarhyensis myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome.

Myrcia capivarhyensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
