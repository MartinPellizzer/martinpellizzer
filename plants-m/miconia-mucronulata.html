Miconia mucronulata

Miconia mucronulata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. Miconia mucronulata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic in non-native habitats.
