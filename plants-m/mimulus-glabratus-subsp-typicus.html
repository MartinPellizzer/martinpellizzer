Mimulus glabratus subsp. typicus

Mimulus glabratus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the common monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodland areas.

The plant features showy, orange-yellow flowers with five petals, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
