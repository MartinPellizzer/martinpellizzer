Myriopteron

Myriopteron, commonly known as the featherleaf lichen, is a type of foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, leaf-like thallus that ranges in color from green to yellowish-green. The plant consists of a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and algae or cyanobacteria, which together form the lichen's structure. Myriopteron is often found on tree trunks, rocks, and soil in temperate regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator of air quality and ecological health.
