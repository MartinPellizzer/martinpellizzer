Melissa intermedia

Melissa intermedia, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, serrated leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens. Its essential oils, particularly those containing citral, are widely used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.

Melissa intermedia is also valued for its calming effects and is often used in teas and culinary applications.
