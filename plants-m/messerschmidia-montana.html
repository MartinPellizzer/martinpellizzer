Messerschmidia montana

Messerschmidia montana, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky and limestone soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, helping to reduce water loss in cold, windy conditions.

Messerschmidia montana plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of alpine habitats.
