Myosotis chakassica

Myosotis chakassica, commonly known as the Chakassian forget-me-not, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. The plant features slender stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
