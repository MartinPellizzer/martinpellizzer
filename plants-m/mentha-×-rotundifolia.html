Mentha × rotundifolia

Mentha × rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its round, slightly fuzzy leaves and strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple.

Mentha × rotundifolia is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its flavorful essential oils.
