Morus alba var. nodulosa

Morus alba var. nodulosa, commonly known as the nodular mulberry, is a variant of the white mulberry characterized by its unique growth patterns.

This plant exhibits a distinctive nodular structure on its stems, which is a notable botanical feature. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in temperate climates and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
