Mimosa asperata f. glabrescens

Mimosa asperata f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its reduced pubescence.

It is a shrub native to South America, typically found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features compound leaves that are often folded together when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in spherical clusters, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
