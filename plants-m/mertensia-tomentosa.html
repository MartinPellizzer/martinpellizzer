Mertensia tomentosa

Mertensia tomentosa, commonly known as Virginia bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are typically bluish-green in appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia tomentosa is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
