Mycetia gracilis

Mycetia gracilis, commonly known as the slender mycetia, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, stipitate leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a slender, pod-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Mycetia gracilis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
