Mentha × hardeggensis

Mentha × hardeggensis, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha longifolia and Mentha piperita.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters. It is native to Central Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Mentha × hardeggensis is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
