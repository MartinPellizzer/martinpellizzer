Miconia vittata

Miconia vittata, commonly known as the white-veined miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are fleshy, orange-red berries that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal by birds and mammals.

Miconia vittata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
