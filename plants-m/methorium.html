Methorium

Methorium, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant blooms that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers emit a strong, sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Methorium is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and unique blooming pattern.
