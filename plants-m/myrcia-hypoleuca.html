Myrcia hypoleuca

Myrcia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a history of use in traditional medicine.
