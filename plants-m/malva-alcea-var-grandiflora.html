Malva alcea var. grandiflora

Malva alcea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the great mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, gray-green leaves that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
