Myrcianthes limbata

Myrcianthes limbata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are orange-red when ripe, containing several seeds. It is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
