Mimusops balata var. schomburgkii

Mimusops balata var. schomburgkii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural and commercial purposes.
