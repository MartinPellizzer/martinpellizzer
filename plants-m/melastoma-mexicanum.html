Melastoma mexicanum

Melastoma mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican blushing bush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
