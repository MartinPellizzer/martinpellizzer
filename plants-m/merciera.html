Merciera

Merciera, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, trailing vines and clusters of fragrant, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Merciera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
