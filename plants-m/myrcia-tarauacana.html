Myrcia tarauacana

Myrcia tarauacana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous tiny seeds.

Myrcia tarauacana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
