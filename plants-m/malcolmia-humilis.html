Malcolmia humilis

Malcolmia humilis, commonly known as the lesser whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
