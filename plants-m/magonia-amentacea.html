Magonia amentacea

Magonia amentacea, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, giving it a distinctive and elegant look. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers in catkins, which are typical of the Salicaceae family. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, often turning yellow in the fall.

Magonia amentacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to thrive along waterways.
