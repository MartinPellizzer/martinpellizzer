Miltonia × rosina

Miltonia × rosina, commonly known as the Rosina orchid, is a hybrid orchid belonging to the Epidendroideae subfamily.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is prized for its vibrant, showy flowers. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are typically large, with intricate patterns of pink, white, and purple, and are borne on terminal inflorescences.

Miltonia × rosina requires high humidity, bright indirect light, and consistent watering to thrive.
