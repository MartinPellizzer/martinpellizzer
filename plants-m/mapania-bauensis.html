Mapania bauensis

Mapania bauensis, commonly known as the Bauensis orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the air.

Mapania bauensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
