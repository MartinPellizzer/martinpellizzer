Massonia modesta

Massonia modesta, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the spring or summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and drought tolerance.

Massonia modesta prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.
