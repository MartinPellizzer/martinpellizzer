Manilkara kurziana

Manilkara kurziana, commonly known as the Indian mahua, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its latex, which is milky and sticky, has been traditionally used in various applications, including as a natural rubber substitute.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
