Medinilla chapelieri

Medinilla chapelieri, commonly known as the Christmas star or Christmas bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant flowers that resemble miniature stars, typically blooming in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to its showy blossoms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Medinilla chapelieri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its beauty and long-lasting floral display.
