Malvastrum fasciculatum var. typicum

Malvastrum fasciculatum var. typicum, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
