Manihot surinamensis

Manihot surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant's underground stems, or rhizomes, are the primary part used for its starchy, edible roots. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to grow in shaded conditions, making it adaptable to various tropical environments.
