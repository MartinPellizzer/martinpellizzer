Mimulus brachystylis

Mimulus brachystylis, commonly known as the short-styled monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats. The plant features showy, orange-yellow flowers with two lips, typical of the Mimulus genus. Its short floral tube and distinctive staminode are key botanical characteristics.

Mimulus brachystylis plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
