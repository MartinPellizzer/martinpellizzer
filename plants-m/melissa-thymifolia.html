Melissa thymifolia

Melissa thymifolia, commonly known as lemon balm thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Melissa thymifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
