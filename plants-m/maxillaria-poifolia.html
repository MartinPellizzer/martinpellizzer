Maxillaria poifolia

Maxillaria poifolia, commonly known as the Porcupine Orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiky foliage that resembles the bristles of a porcupine, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellow or greenish throat. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria poifolia is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
