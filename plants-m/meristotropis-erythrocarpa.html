Meristotropis erythrocarpa

Meristotropis erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited meristotropis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to drought conditions.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
