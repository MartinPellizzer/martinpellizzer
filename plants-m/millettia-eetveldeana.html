Millettia eetveldeana

Millettia eetveldeana, commonly known as the Eetvelde's millettia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals. It is typically found in open woodlands and savannas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
