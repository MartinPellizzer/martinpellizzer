Menodora coulteri

Menodora coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's menodora, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Menodoridae and is characterized by its woody stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
