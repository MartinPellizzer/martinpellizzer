Momordica luffa

Momordica luffa, commonly known as Chinese horned cucumber, is a climbing vine native to Asia.

It produces large, ridged, green fruits that resemble a cucumber but have a distinctive horn-like protrusion. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The fruit is often used in cooking, particularly in Asian cuisines, and is also valued for its fibrous interior.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucumbers.
