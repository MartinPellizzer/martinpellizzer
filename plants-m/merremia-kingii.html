Merremia kingii

Merremia kingii, commonly known as the King's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to eastern Africa.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.
