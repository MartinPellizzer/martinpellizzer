Moraea ventricosa f. boussardiana

Moraea ventricosa f. boussardiana, commonly known as the Boussard's garden lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant blooms during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its unique appearance and long-lasting flowers make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
