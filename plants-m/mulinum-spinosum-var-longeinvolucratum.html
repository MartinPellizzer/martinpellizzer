Mulinum spinosum var. longeinvolucratum

Mulinum spinosum var. longeinvolucratum, commonly known as the long-involucrate thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a tall, slender stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters at the top of the stem. Its involucres, which are the leaf-like structures surrounding the flower heads, are notably long and reflexed.

This variety is typically found in moist, disturbed soils in woodland or open areas.
