Mokof cuneifolia

Mokof cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mokof, is a distinctive plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem, which contribute to its resilience in tropical environments.

Mokof cuneifolia is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
