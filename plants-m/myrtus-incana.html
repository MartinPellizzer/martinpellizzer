Myrtus incana

Myrtus incana, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue, edible berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.

Myrtus incana is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
