Malva polyantha

Malva polyantha, commonly known as the Chinese malva, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and wild areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
