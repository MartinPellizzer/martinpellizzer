Mauranthe lunata

Mauranthe lunata, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant blossoms that open at night. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid blooming cycle. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth, glossy surface.

The flowers typically close by dawn, making it a popular choice for evening gardens.
