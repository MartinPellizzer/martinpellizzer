Menispermum glabrum

Menispermum glabrum, commonly known as wild turnip, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
