Myrtus guajava

Myrtus guajava, commonly known as the Java myrtle or clove tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, leathery leaves and small, clustered white flowers that give way to dark purple berries. The plant is known for its aromatic essential oils, particularly in its flowers and fruits, which are used in various traditional and commercial applications. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Myrtus guajava is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
