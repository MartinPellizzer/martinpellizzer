Martynia violacea

Martynia violacea, commonly known as the violet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories.
