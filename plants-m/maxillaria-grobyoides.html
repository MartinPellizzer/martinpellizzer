Maxillaria grobyoides

Maxillaria grobyoides, commonly known as the Groby's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers with white and purple petals. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, and its flowers are often described as resembling small, delicate butterflies. Maxillaria grobyoides prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value contribute to its appeal among orchid enthusiasts.
