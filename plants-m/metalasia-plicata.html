Metalasia plicata

Metalasia plicata, commonly known as the pleated metalasia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, pleated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Metalasia plicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to care for.
