Medicago ovalis

Medicago ovalis, commonly known as the oval vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Medicago ovalis is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
