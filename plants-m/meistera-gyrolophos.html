Meistera gyrolophos

Meistera gyrolophos, commonly known as the spiral-veined arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spirally arranged leaves that give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and prefers moderate to high light conditions. Its leaves are typically arrowhead-shaped with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Meistera gyrolophos is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
