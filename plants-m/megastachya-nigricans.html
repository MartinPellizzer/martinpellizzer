Megastachya nigricans

Megastachya nigricans, commonly known as the blackstem stachys, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that are typically dark purple to black in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in whorled clusters at the ends of its stems, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Megastachya nigricans is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.
