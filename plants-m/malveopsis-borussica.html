Malveopsis borussica

Malveopsis borussica, commonly known as the Siberian mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Russia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, adding a splash of color to alpine and tundra environments.

Malveopsis borussica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
