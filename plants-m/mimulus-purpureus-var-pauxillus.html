Mimulus purpureus var. pauxillus

Mimulus purpureus var. pauxillus, commonly known as the purple monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. It has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is part of the Physostegia genus and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
