Myrtus biflora f. subsericea

Myrtus biflora f. subsericea, commonly known as the silky myrtle, is a cultivar of the myrtle plant characterized by its densely packed, fine-textured foliage.

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its leaves are typically a dark green with a silvery-white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The small white flowers that appear in spring are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and aromatic qualities.
