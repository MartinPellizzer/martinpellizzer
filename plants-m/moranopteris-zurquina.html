Moranopteris zurquina

Moranopteris zurquina, commonly known as the Zurquina fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, facilitating spore dispersal.

Moranopteris zurquina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
