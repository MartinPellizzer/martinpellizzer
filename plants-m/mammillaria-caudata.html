Mammillaria caudata

Mammillaria caudata, commonly known as the woolly nipple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that is covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
