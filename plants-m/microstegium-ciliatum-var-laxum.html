Microstegium ciliatum var. laxum

Microstegium ciliatum var. laxum, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows, stream banks, and forest edges. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered with fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
