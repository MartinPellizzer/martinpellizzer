Mimosa invisa subsp. spiciflora

Mimosa invisa subsp.

spiciflora, commonly known as the spiciflora mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces a distinctive, milky sap that can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in horticultural studies and ecological research.
