Meconopsis primulina

Meconopsis primulina, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Meconopsis primulina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders.
