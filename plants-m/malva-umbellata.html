Malva umbellata

Malva umbellata, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, broad leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its fibrous stems and mucilaginous seeds have been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
