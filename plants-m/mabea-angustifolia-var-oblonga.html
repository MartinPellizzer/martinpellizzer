Mabea angustifolia var. oblonga

Mabea angustifolia var. oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf mabea, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its foliage.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in local ecosystems and potential medicinal properties.
