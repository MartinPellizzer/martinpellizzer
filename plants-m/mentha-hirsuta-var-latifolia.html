Mentha hirsuta var. latifolia

Mentha hirsuta var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf hirsute mint, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features hairy, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the mint family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it useful in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
