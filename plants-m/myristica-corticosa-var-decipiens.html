Myristica corticosa var. decipiens

Myristica corticosa var. decipiens, commonly known as the false nutmeg tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruit that resembles the true nutmeg. The plant produces a fruit with a thick, leathery outer layer and a hard, aromatic inner seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The tree is valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.
