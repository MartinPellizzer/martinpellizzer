Macrocarpium mas

Macrocarpium mas, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.
