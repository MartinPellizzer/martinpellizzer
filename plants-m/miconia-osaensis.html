Miconia osaensis

Miconia osaensis, commonly known as the Osa mountain miconia, is a tropical tree native to the Osa Peninsula in Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in humid, warm climates. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, winged seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Miconia osaensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
