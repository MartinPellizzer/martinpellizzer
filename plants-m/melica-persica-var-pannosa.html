Melica persica var. pannosa

Melica persica var. pannosa, commonly known as Persian vernal grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, showcasing a compact, tufted growth form with slender, leafy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
