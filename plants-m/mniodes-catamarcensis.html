Mniodes catamarcensis

Mniodes catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarca orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are often short-lived but attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
