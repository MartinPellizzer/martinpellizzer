Menetho sedifolius

Menetho sedifolius, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in spikes. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural settings. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Menetho sedifolius is often found in wetlands, along riverbanks, and in open woodlands.
