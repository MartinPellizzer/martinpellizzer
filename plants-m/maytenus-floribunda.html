Maytenus floribunda

Maytenus floribunda, commonly known as the flowering mayten, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
