Micrampelis echinata

Micrampelis echinata, commonly known as the spiny climbing plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a vine that typically grows along forest floors or in shaded areas, utilizing its tendrils to climb supports. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a rough texture and dark green coloration. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and are often greenish-yellow in hue.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its dispersal in the natural environment.
