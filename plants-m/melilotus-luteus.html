Melilotus luteus

Melilotus luteus, commonly known as yellow sweet clover, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically in traditional medicine for its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects.
