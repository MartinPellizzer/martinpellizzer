Mosla argyi

Mosla argyi, commonly known as Chinese burnet or wild burnet, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in dense, terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Mosla argyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
