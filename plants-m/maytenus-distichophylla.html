Maytenus distichophylla

Maytenus distichophylla, commonly known as the two-leaf mayten, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, two-lobed fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry or semi-arid regions.

Maytenus distichophylla is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
