Megapterium oklahomense

Megapterium oklahomense, commonly known as the Oklahoma winged bean, is a perennial legume native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and elongated, winged pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its flowers are small and typically white or pale pink, and it produces a moderate amount of biomass.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it has been studied for potential agricultural and forage applications.
