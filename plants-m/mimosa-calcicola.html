Mimosa calcicola

Mimosa calcicola, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry, rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Mimosa calcicola is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
