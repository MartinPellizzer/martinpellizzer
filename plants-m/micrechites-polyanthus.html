Micrechites polyanthus

Micrechites polyanthus, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact rosette of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in early spring. Its flowers are borne on short, sturdy stems and are often fragrant.

Micrechites polyanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil and full sun.
