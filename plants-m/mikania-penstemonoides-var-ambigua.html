Mikania penstemonoides var. ambigua

Mikania penstemonoides var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous Mikania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family, and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
