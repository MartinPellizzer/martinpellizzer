Myosotis palustris subsp. lithuanica

Myosotis palustris subsp.

lithuanica, commonly known as the Lithuanian water forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of eastern Europe. It features delicate blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July.

The plant grows in moist, often boggy soils and is adapted to low-light conditions. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
