Mentha wierzbickiana f. lintiae

Mentha wierzbickiana f. lintiae, commonly known as the Woolly Mint, is a hybrid form of the Mentha genus, characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage.

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cool, moist environments. It produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are aromatic and can be used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.

Its unique appearance and fragrance make it a desirable addition to gardens and botanical collections.
