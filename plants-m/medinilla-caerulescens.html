Medinilla caerulescens

Medinilla caerulescens, commonly known as the blue medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy blue flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Medinilla caerulescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
