Myrtus thyrsoidea

Myrtus thyrsoidea, commonly known as the sweet myrtle or thyrsoid myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

Myrtus thyrsoidea is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value in gardens.
