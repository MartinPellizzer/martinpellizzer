Melissa microphylla

Melissa microphylla, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their calming properties.

Melissa microphylla is also valued in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and ability to attract beneficial insects.
