Monotoca leucantha

Monotoca leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered monotoca, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture.

Monotoca leucantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
