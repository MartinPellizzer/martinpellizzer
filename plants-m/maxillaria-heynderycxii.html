Maxillaria heynderycxii

Maxillaria heynderycxii, commonly known as the Heynderyck's orchid, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its pseudobulbs and fleshy leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, often with a contrasting lip. It prefers warm, humid environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
