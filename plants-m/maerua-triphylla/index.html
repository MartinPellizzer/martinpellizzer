Maerua Triphylla

Red bushwillow, scientifically known as Maerua triphylla, is a hardy shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown bark and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. This plant plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species. Its resilience makes it an important component of local flora in regions with limited water resources. Despite its tough exterior, the Red bushwillow is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
