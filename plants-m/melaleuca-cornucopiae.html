Melaleuca cornucopiae

Melaleuca cornucopiae, commonly known as the golden bottlebrush, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are typically bright red or orange in color. The plant produces small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest regions.

Melaleuca cornucopiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
