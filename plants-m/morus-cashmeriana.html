Morus cashmeriana

Morus cashmeriana, commonly known as Kashmir mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, non-showy flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source for silkworms. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant plant in its native habitat.
