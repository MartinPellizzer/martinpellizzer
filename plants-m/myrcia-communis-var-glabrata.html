Myrcia communis var. glabrata

Myrcia communis var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are typically small, red berries that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
