Mitracarpus polygonifolius

Mitracarpus polygonifolius, commonly known as the African cobra lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with striking, hooded flowers that resemble a cobra's head, hence its common name. The plant features palmately compound leaves with a waxy surface, and its flowers are typically orange or yellow with purple markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
