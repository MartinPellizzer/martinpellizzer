Myosotis discolor subsp. discolor

Myosotis discolor subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a wildflower.
