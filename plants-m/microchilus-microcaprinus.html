Microchilus microcaprinus

Microchilus microcaprinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved microchilus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or pale in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
