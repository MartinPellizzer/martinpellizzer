Mercurialis × longifolia subsp.

malinvaldi, commonly known as the longleaf mercury. This subspecies is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae, characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Its flowers are clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
