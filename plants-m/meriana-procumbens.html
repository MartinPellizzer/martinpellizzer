Meriana procumbens

Meriana procumbens, commonly known as the creeping lily or ground lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple. Its leaves are usually oval or lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair.

Meriana procumbens is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
