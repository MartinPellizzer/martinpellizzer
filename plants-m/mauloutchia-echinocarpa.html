Mauloutchia echinocarpa

Mauloutchia echinocarpa, commonly known as the spiny fruit tree, is a shrub or small tree native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like stem and produces small, spiny fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The fruits are sometimes used in local traditional medicine or as a food source in some communities.
