Merope aretioides

Merope aretioides, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Merope aretioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
