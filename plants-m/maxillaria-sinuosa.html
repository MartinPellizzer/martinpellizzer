Maxillaria sinuosa

Maxillaria sinuosa, commonly known as the Sinuous Maxillaria, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple with a distinctive, sinuous lip. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to its tropical and subtropical habitats.

Maxillaria sinuosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right growing conditions.
