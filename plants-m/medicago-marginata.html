Medicago marginata

Medicago marginata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial forage legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils and withstand drought conditions. It is often used in agricultural settings for soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop for livestock.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
