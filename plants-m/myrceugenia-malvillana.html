Myrceugenia malvillana

Myrceugenia malvillana, commonly known as the Malvillan guava, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, tropical climates. The plant features smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, yellow or orange berry with a sweet and aromatic flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
