Mariscus congestus

Mariscus congestus, commonly known as the clustered mariscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
