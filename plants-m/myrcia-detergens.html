Myrcia detergens

Myrcia detergens, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and showy red or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a food source for various wildlife. It is also known for its strong, spicy scent, which is due to the presence of essential oils in its leaves and stems.

Myrcia detergens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and fragrance.
