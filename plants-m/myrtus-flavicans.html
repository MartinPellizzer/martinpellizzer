Myrtus flavicans

Myrtus flavicans, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or yellow flowers and leathery, dark green leaves. It produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe.

Myrtus flavicans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
