Mesadenia maxima

Mesadenia maxima, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant features a robust rhizome and long, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mesadenia maxima is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
