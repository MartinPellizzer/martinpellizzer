Mephitidia attenuata

Mephitidia attenuata, commonly known as the slender mephit, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive strategy involves insect pollination, which is facilitated by its nectar-rich blooms.

Due to habitat loss, Mephitidia attenuata is considered a rare and endangered species.
