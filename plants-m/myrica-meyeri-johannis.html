Myrica meyeri-johannis

Myrica meyeri-johannis, commonly known as the Japanese wax tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Japan.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and silvery-white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental planting in gardens.

Myrica meyeri-johannis is valued for its hardiness and unique foliage, which adds visual interest throughout the year.
