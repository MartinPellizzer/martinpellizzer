Melinis ruficoma

Melinis ruficoma, commonly known as the red-stemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often turn reddish in color as the plant matures. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically during the dry season.

This plant is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
