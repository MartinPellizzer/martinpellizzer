Mertensia decurrens

Mertensia decurrens, commonly known as bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy, often with a slight waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Mertensia decurrens is a perennial herb that plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
