Myagrum perfoliatum

Myagrum perfoliatum, commonly known as the perfoliate myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. It typically grows in coastal areas and has a shrub-like appearance with rough, dark green foliage.

Myagrum perfoliatum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
