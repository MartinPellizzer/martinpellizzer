Mammillaria beneckei var. multiceps

Mammillaria beneckei var. multiceps, commonly known as the multi-headed mammillaria, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and multiple small, white or pale yellow tubercles that give it a unique, star-like appearance. The plant typically produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
