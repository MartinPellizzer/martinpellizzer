Myrtus rufescens

Myrtus rufescens, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically reddish when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Myrtus rufescens is often used in ornamental gardening and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
