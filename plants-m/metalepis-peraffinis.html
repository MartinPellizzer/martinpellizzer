Metalepis peraffinis

Metalepis peraffinis, commonly known as the slender burrgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its propagation across open habitats. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native range.
