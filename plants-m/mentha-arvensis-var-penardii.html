Mentha arvensis var. penardii

Mentha arvensis var. penardii, commonly known as the Penard's field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are oval to lance-shaped with a serrated margin, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal teas and traditional remedies.
