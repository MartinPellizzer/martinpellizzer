Monanthotaxis pynaertii

Monanthotaxis pynaertii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are borne singly or in pairs and have a strong, sweet fragrance.

Monanthotaxis pynaertii is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.
