Modiolastrum pinnatipartitum

Modiolastrum pinnatipartitum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, pinnately divided leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often the only visible parts of the inflorescence. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is native to parts of southern Africa.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
