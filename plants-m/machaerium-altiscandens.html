Machaerium altiscandens

Machaerium altiscandens, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or silver grass, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants that often form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Machaerium altiscandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
