Mariscus cyperinus var. maximus

Mariscus cyperinus var. maximus, commonly known as the giant sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in areas with standing water or saturated soil. Its flowering spikes are usually green or reddish, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This variant is notable for its height and robust growth, making it a distinctive feature in wetland ecosystems.
