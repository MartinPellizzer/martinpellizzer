Muhlenbergia filipes

Muhlenbergia filipes, commonly known as hairawn muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, feathery panicles of small, purple-tinged flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its fine texture and elegant appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Muhlenbergia filipes is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting local wildlife.
