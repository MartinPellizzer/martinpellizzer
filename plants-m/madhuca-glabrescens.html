Madhuca glabrescens

Madhuca glabrescens, commonly known as the glossy apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green foliage and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
