Myrcia luquillensis

Myrcia luquillensis, commonly known as the Luquillense Myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Myrcia luquillensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
