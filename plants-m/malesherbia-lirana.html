Malesherbia lirana

Malesherbia lirana, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its showy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Malesherbia lirana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
