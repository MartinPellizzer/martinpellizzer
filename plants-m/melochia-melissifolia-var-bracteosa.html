Melochia melissifolia var. bracteosa

Melochia melissifolia var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate melochia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by three-winged, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
