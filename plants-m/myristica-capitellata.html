Myristica capitellata

Myristica capitellata, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive two-seed fruits, with the seeds being the source of the highly valued spices nutmeg and mace. Its botanical features include compound leaves, woody stems, and a unique reproductive system that involves both male and female flowers on the same plant.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its economic value.
