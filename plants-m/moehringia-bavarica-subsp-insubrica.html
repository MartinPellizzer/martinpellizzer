Moehringia bavarica subsp. insubrica

Moehringia bavarica subsp.

insubrica, commonly known as the Alpine moehringia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically forms dense mats of foliage with small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
