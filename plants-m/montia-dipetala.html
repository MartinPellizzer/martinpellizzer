Montia dipetala

Montia dipetala, commonly known as the two-petaled mountain-mint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Montiaceae and is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and typically grows in moist, rocky habitats. Its botanical features include a short, fibrous root system and stems that are often hairy and glandular.

Montia dipetala is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
