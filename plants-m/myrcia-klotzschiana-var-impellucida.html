Myrcia klotzschiana var. impellucida

Myrcia klotzschiana var. impellucida, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
