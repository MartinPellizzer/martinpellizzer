Matthiola fruticulosa var. thessala

Matthiola fruticulosa var. thessala, commonly known as the Thessalian stock, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
