Microcerasus prostrata f. karabastaviensis

Microcerasus prostrata f. karabastaviensis, commonly known as the Karabastavan dwarf cherry, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with short, compact stems that often lie close to the ground. The leaves are small, ovate, and have a glossy dark green surface, while the flowers are white and borne in clusters. This form is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact size and attractive foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
