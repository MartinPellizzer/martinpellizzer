Mentha longifolia subsp. ferdinandii

Mentha longifolia subsp.

ferdinandii, commonly known as Ferdinand's mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other terpenoids, contributing to its use in herbal medicine and aromatherapy.
