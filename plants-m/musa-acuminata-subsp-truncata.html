Musa acuminata subsp. truncata

Musa acuminata subsp.

truncata, commonly known as the dwarf banana, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its compact growth habit and broad, paddle-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear edible berries, though they are typically not cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
