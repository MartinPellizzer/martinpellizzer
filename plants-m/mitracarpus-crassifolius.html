Mitracarpus crassifolius

Mitracarpus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf mitracarpus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a distinctive, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

The species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
