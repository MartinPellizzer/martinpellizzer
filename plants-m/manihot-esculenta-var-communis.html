Manihot esculenta var. communis

Manihot esculenta var. communis, commonly known as cassava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a shrub-like species that produces large, lobed leaves and cylindrical roots. The roots are the primary edible part, valued for their high starch content and versatility in cooking. Cassava is also known as yuca or manioc in different regions.

It is a crucial crop in many tropical countries due to its adaptability and nutritional value.
