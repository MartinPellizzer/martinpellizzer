Macairea viscosa

Macairea viscosa, commonly known as the sticky macairea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that exude a sticky substance, which is a characteristic feature of the genus. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Macairea viscosa is often found in humid forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in shaded garden settings.
