Modecca digitata

Modecca digitata, commonly known as the fingerleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, finger-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Modecca digitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
