Mairia burchellii

Mairia burchellii, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in woodland and savanna habitats. The plant is characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are opposite and usually have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Mairia burchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
