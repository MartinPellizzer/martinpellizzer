Melia azedarach

Melia azedarach, commonly known as the chinaberry tree, is a deciduous tree native to Asia, Africa, and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by ornamental, papery fruits that split open to release seeds. The tree has smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are often hairy when young. Its roots are deep and strong, allowing it to thrive in various soil types and climates.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
