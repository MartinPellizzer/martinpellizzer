Monocera aristata

Monocera aristata, commonly known as the mountain pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its unique appearance and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a notable species in its ecosystem.

Monocera aristata is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens that can accommodate its specific climate requirements.
