Mikania alba

Mikania alba, commonly known as white mikania, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain areas.
