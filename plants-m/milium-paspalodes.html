Milium paspalodes

Milium paspalodes, commonly known as the silky millet, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and disturbed areas.

Milium paspalodes is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in some traditional agricultural systems.
