Melanolepis

Melanolepis, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-forming leaves and showy, tubular flowers that often come in shades of violet, pink, and white. Melanolepis species are typically herbaceous, with some varieties growing as shrubs. They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light to flourish.

The genus is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
