Mycaranthes pannea

Mycaranthes pannea, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

The flowers emit a strong scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
