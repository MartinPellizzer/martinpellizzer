Miconia conomacrantha

Miconia conomacrantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are typically followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia conomacrantha is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it a potential invasive species in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
