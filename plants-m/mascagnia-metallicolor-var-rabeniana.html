Mascagnia metallicolor var. rabeniana

Mascagnia metallicolor var. rabeniana, commonly known as the silverleaf mohavea, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that reflect sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a distinctive addition to xeriscaped gardens.
