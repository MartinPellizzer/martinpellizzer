Mentha × rubra var. wirtgeniana

Mentha × rubra var. wirtgeniana, commonly known as the red mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a combination of characteristics from its parent species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with square stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant produces small, pale purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
