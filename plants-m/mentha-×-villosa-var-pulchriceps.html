Mentha × villosa var. pulchriceps

Mentha × villosa var. pulchriceps, commonly known as the woolly mint or purple mint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its soft, hairy foliage.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
