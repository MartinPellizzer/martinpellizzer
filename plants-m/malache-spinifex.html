Malache spinifex

Malache spinifex, commonly known as spinifex malache, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiky foliage that resembles the appearance of spinifex grass, hence its common name. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
