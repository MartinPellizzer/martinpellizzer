Mearnsia ramiflora

Mearnsia ramiflora, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system and drought-resistant foliage. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mearnsia ramiflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
