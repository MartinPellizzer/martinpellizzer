Menispermum myosotoides

Menispermum myosotoides, commonly known as the blue cohosh, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is known for its medicinal properties historically used by Indigenous peoples.

The berries it produces are dark blue and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.
