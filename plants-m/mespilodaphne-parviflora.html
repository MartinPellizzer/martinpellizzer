Mespilodaphne parviflora

Mespilodaphne parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered mespilodaphne, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
