Machilus velutinus var. longipedunculatus

Machilus velutinus var. longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-pedunculate wax tree, is a variant of the wax tree species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by ellipsoid berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental cultivation.

This variant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
