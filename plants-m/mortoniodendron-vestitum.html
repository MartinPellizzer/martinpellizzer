Mortoniodendron vestitum

Mortoniodendron vestitum, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense forests. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
