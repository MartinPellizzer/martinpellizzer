Memecylon arnhemense

Memecylon arnhemense, commonly known as the Arnhem memecylon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It is often found in shaded, moist understorey environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially vulnerable species.
