Myrciaria leucadendron

Myrciaria leucadendron, commonly known as the white candlewood, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm regions.
