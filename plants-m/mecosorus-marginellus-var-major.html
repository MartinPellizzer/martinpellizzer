Mecosorus marginellus var. major

Mecosorus marginellus var. major, commonly known as the broad-leafed meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with broad, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
