Minthostachys salicifolia

Minthostachys salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a strong minty aroma, and its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their cooling and antiseptic properties.
