Mussaenda elegans

Mussaenda elegans, commonly known as the flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Mussaenda elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
