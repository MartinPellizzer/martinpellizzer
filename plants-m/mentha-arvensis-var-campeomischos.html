Mentha arvensis var. campeomischos

Mentha arvensis var. campeomischos, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Field mint is known for its aromatic leaves and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
