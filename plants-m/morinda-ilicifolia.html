Morinda ilicifolia

Morinda ilicifolia, commonly known as the Indian madder or red sanders, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by bright red or orange berries. Its roots and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Morinda ilicifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant foliage and fruit.
