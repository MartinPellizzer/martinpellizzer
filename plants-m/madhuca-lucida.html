Madhuca lucida

Madhuca lucida, commonly known as the white candlewood tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and large, glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Madhuca lucida is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
