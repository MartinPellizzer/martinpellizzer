Masdevallia argus

Masdevallia argus, commonly known as the Argus orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure with a prominent, eye-like pattern on the lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often clustered at the base.

Masdevallia argus is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
