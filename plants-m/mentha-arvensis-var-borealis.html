Mentha arvensis var. borealis

Mentha arvensis var. borealis, commonly known as northern field mint, is a perennial herb native to arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool climates. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong minty aroma. Its flowering stems bear small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale purple.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
