Myrcia sagotii

Myrcia sagotii, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

Myrcia sagotii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
