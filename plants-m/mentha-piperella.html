Mentha piperella

Mentha piperella, commonly known as horsemint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Horsemint thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties have made it historically valued in traditional herbal remedies.
