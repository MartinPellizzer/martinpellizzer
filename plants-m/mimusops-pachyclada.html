Mimusops pachyclada

Mimusops pachyclada, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often lobed or serrated, providing a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or cream in color, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in furniture and construction.

The plant is also cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
