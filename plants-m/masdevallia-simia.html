Masdevallia simia

Masdevallia simia, commonly known as the monkey orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure that resembles a monkey's face, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually small and come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Masdevallia simia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its distinctive appearance.
