Miconia prasina var. angustifolia

Miconia prasina var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has large, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain environments.
