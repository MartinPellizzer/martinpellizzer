Mentha rugosa var. oblongifolia

Mentha rugosa var. oblongifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fuzzy, glandular surface, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
