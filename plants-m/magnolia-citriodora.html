Magnolia citriodora

Magnolia citriodora, commonly known as the lemon-scented magnolia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that emit a citrus-like aroma. The tree has simple, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
