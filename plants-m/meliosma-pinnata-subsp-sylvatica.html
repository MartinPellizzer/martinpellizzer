Meliosma pinnata subsp. sylvatica

Meliosma pinnata subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as the silverleaf meliosma, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, preferring cool, humid climates.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps reflect light and reduce water loss.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
