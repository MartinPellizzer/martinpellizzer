Mammillaria columbiana var. albescens

Mammillaria columbiana var. albescens, commonly known as the White-Furred Mammillaria, is a small cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
