Musa aurantiaca

Musa aurantiaca, commonly known as the red banana, is a flowering plant in the Musaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking orange-red pseudostems and broad, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of elongated, yellow fruits that ripen to a vibrant red color. It is cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit, which has a sweet and tangy flavor.

Musa aurantiaca thrives in tropical climates and requires warm temperatures and high humidity for optimal growth.
