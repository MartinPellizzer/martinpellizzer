Mandevilla callista

Mandevilla callista, commonly known as the golden mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vigorous climber with thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Mandevilla callista is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and lush foliage.
