Myrrhis claytonii

Myrrhis claytonii, commonly known as Clayton's mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features hairy, basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the spring.

Myrrhis claytonii is typically found in moist, rocky habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
