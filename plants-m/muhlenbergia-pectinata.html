Muhlenbergia pectinata

Muhlenbergia pectinata, commonly known as the pink muhlenbergia, is a native grass species found in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces feathery, pinkish to reddish plumes during the summer months. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that adds ornamental value to landscapes.

Muhlenbergia pectinata is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
