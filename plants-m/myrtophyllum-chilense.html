Myrtophyllum chilense

Myrtophyllum chilense, commonly known as the Chilean myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andes region of Chile.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Myrtophyllum chilense is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
