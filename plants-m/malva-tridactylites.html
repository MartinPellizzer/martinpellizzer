Malva tridactylites

Malva tridactylites, commonly known as the three-toed mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive three-lobed calyx.

Malva tridactylites is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
