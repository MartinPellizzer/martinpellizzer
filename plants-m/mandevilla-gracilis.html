Mandevilla gracilis

Mandevilla gracilis, commonly known as the slender mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vigorous climber with long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade.

Mandevilla gracilis is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
