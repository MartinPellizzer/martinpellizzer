Mogiphanes soratensis

Mogiphanes soratensis, commonly known as the Sora Desert Spurge, is a perennial plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry savannas and semi-desert areas.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing minimal forage for local wildlife.
