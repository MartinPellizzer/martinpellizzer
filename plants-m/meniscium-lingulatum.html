Meniscium lingulatum

Meniscium lingulatum, commonly known as the small-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along riverbanks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bell-shaped, typically blue or violet, and are arranged in loose clusters at the tips of the stems.

Meniscium lingulatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
