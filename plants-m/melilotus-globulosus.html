Melilotus globulosus

Melilotus globulosus, commonly known as globular sweet clover, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, spherical flower heads that resemble small balls, giving it its common name. The plant has trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and produces a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Melilotus globulosus is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
