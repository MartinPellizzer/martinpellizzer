Miconia difficilis

Miconia difficilis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a distinctive glandular texture.

Miconia difficilis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
