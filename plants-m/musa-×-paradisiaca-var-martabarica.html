Musa × paradisiaca var. martabarica

Musa × paradisiaca var. martabarica, commonly known as the Martaban banana, is a cultivated variety of the banana plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, and often curved fruit, which can weigh up to 20 kilograms. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The fruit is typically harvested when it reaches a yellowish color, indicating maturity.
