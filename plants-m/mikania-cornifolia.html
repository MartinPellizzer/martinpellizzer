Mikania cornifolia

Mikania cornifolia, commonly known as the horseweed or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Mikania cornifolia is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, due to its adaptability.

It has been introduced to other regions and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
