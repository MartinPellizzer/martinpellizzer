Millettia macrostachya var. tecta

Millettia macrostachya var. tecta, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces large, edible fruits that resemble breadfruit, which are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation and providing food for various wildlife species.
