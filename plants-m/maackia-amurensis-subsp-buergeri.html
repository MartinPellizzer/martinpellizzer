Maackia amurensis subsp. buergeri

Maackia amurensis subsp.

buergeri, commonly known as the Amur maackia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often pink or white, while its fruits are legumes that split open to release seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
