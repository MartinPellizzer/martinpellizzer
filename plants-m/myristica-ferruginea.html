Myristica ferruginea

Myristica ferruginea, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark red heartwood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

The plant is also known for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.
