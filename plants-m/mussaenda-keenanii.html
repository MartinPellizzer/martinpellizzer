Mussaenda keenanii

Mussaenda keenanii, commonly known as the golden flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow inflorescences that resemble flames, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers that develop into woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

Mussaenda keenanii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
