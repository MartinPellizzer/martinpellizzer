Matricaria partheniifolia

Matricaria partheniifolia, commonly known as the German chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has finely divided, serrated leaves. Its essential oil, derived from the flowers, is widely used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.

Matricaria partheniifolia is also valued for its calming properties and is often used to make teas.
