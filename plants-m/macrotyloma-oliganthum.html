Macrotyloma oliganthum

Macrotyloma oliganthum, commonly known as the small-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
