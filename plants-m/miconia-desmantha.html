Miconia desmantha

Miconia desmantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries. Miconia desmantha is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in disturbed habitats.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a problematic species in certain regions.
