Myrcia lanceolata var. latifolia

Myrcia lanceolata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
