Myrcia gracilis var. opaca

Myrcia gracilis var. opaca, commonly known as the opaca myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually leathery and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
