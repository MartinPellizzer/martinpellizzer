Mimosa pteridifolia var. paucipinnata

Mimosa pteridifolia var. paucipinnata, commonly known as the small-leaf mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It features compound leaves with fewer pairs of leaflets compared to the typical mimosa species, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, arid environments.

Its unique foliage and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
