Menziesia polifolia

Menziesia polifolia, commonly known as the coastal blueberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils along coastal areas and in shaded woodlands. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a bluish-green hue.

Menziesia polifolia plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing coastal soils.
