Micromeria imbricata var. imbricata

Micromeria imbricata var. imbricata, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to hot, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it a hardy, drought-tolerant species well-suited to xerophytic environments.
