Melica ciliata subsp. thuringiaca

Melica ciliata subsp.

thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with finely serrated edges.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
