Marsdenia mayana

Marsdenia mayana, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid forests and along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ornamental flowers make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
