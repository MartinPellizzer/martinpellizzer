Maytenus parviflora

Maytenus parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered mayten, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Maytenus parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
