Mimosa rigescens

Mimosa rigescens, commonly known as the stiff mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
