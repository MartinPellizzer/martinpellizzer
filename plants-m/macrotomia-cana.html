Macrotomia cana

Macrotomia cana, commonly known as the white thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with serrated edges. The plant features a tall, erect stem covered in soft, grayish-white hairs. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Macrotomia cana produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer months.
