Marsdenia sarcoloboides

Marsdenia sarcoloboides, commonly known as the woolly Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by follicles containing seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
