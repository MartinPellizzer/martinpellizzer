Muhlenbergia liebmannii

Muhlenbergia liebmannii, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, silvery-blue leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.

Muhlenbergia liebmannii is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
