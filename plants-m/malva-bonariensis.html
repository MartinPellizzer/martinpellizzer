Malva bonariensis

Malva bonariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mallow, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pale pink to lavender flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Malva bonariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
