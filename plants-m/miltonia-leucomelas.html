Miltonia leucomelas

Miltonia leucomelas, commonly known as the white-leaved orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a lush, green backdrop to its showy blooms.

This orchid is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in greenhouses and gardens.
