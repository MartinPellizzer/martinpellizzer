Morangaya

Morangaya, commonly known as the "Indian bellflower," is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky areas and thrives at high altitudes. Morangaya has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
