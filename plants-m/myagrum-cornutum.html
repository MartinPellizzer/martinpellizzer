Myagrum cornutum

Myagrum cornutum, commonly known as the horned myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Australia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Myagrum cornutum is often found in coastal and swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
