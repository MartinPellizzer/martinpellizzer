Melastoma cuspidatum

Melastoma cuspidatum, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a glossy surface.

The fruit is a small, segmented berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.
