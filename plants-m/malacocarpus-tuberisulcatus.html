Malacocarpus tuberisulcatus

Malacocarpus tuberisulcatus, commonly known as the tuberous sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces tuber-like structures that store nutrients. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female structures.

Malacocarpus tuberisulcatus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
