Mimosa callosa var. longiracemosa

Mimosa callosa var. longiracemosa, commonly known as the long raceme mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and showy, pale yellow flowers that appear in long, drooping clusters. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive, rough, and sometimes hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in regions with warm climates.
