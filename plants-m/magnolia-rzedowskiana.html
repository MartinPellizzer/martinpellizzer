Magnolia rzedowskiana

Magnolia rzedowskiana, commonly known as the Rzedowska magnolia, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white and pink. The plant has broad, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
