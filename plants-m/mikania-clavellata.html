Mikania clavellata

Mikania clavellata, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early morning. Mikania clavellata is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.
