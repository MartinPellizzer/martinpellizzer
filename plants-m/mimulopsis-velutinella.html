Mimulopsis velutinella

Mimulopsis velutinella, commonly known as the velvet mimulopsis, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has a compact growth form with slender stems and is often found in open, disturbed areas.

Mimulopsis velutinella is adapted to arid environments and is considered a rare and specialized species within its range.
