Mentha limnetes

Mentha limnetes, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and pond edges.

Mentha limnetes is known for its strong mint scent and is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
