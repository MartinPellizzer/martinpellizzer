Melilotus indicus subsp. permixtus

Melilotus indicus subsp.

permixtus, commonly known as the Indian melilot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and herbal applications.
