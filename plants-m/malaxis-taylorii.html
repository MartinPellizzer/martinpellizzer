Malaxis taylorii

Malaxis taylorii, commonly known as the Taylor's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are elongated and often clustered at the base, and it is known for its limited distribution and conservation status.

Due to habitat loss, Malaxis taylorii is considered threatened in many parts of its range.
