Mimosa herzogii

Mimosa herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and adaptability to various climates.

Mimosa herzogii is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
