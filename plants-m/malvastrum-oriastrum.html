Malvastrum oriastrum

Malvastrum oriastrum, commonly known as the yellow mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Malvastrum oriastrum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
