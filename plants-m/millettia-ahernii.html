Millettia ahernii

Millettia ahernii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of yellow, pendant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing properties.

Millettia ahernii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
