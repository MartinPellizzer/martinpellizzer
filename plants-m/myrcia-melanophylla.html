Myrcia melanophylla

Myrcia melanophylla, commonly known as black-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

Myrcia melanophylla plays an important role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
