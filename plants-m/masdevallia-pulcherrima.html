Masdevallia pulcherrima

Masdevallia pulcherrima, commonly known as the "Beautiful Masdevallia," is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, intricate flowers clustered in a raceme.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique and vibrant blooms.
