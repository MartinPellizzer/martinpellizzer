Micromeria microphylla var. glabriuscula

Micromeria microphylla var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smoothleaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well adapted to its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
