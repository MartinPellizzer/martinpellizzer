Mascagnia ovatifolia f. oblongifolia

Mascagnia ovatifolia f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblong-leafed mascagnia, is a variant of the Mascagnia ovatifolia species native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically oblong in shape, hence the subspecies name, and often have a glossy appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
