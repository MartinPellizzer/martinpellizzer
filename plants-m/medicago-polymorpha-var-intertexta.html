Medicago polymorpha var. intertexta

Medicago polymorpha var. intertexta, commonly known as the common vetch, is a leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to various regions worldwide. The plant has a fibrous root system and can thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils. It is often used for erosion control and as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
