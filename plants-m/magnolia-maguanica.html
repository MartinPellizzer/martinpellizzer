Magnolia maguanica

Magnolia maguanica, commonly known as the Cuban magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Cuba.

It features large, showy flowers that are typically pink to white in color, with a strong fragrance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are broad and ovate in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish in appearance, and it produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
