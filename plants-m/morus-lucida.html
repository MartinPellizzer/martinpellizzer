Morus lucida

Morus lucida, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically pale green on the underside. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that are often consumed by silkworms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant has been historically significant in sericulture and traditional medicine.
