Maranta hexantha

Maranta hexantha, commonly known as the pink-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes a variety of ornamental and economically important species. The plant is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with pink veins that contrast beautifully against its dark green foliage. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Maranta hexantha is also valued for its starchy rhizomes, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
