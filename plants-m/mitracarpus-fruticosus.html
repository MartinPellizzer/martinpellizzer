Mitracarpus fruticosus

Mitracarpus fruticosus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, spiny fruit pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The plant contains milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
