Malva acutiloba

Malva acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive serrated edge and produces showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Malva acutiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional herbal medicine.
