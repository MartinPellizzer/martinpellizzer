Millettia antsahalanbensis

Millettia antsahalanbensis, commonly known as the Madagascar silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces elongated pods containing flat, fibrous seeds, which are used in traditional practices. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in dry forests and savannas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest for conservation and horticultural studies.
