Mikania laevis

Mikania laevis, commonly known as silky Mikania, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, white seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forests.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some regions.
