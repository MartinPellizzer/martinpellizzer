Mitrasacme galbina

Mitrasacme galbina, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a slender, woody stem with smooth, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mitrasacme galbina is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a shade tree in tropical landscapes.
