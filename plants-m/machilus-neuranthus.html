Machilus neuranthus

Machilus neuranthus, commonly known as the Chinese laurustin, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. Its fruit is a small, spherical drupe that contains a single seed.

Machilus neuranthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
