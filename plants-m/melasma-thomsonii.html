Melasma thomsonii

Melasma thomsonii, commonly known as the Thomson's melasma, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive melasma-like patterns on its leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white and green.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
