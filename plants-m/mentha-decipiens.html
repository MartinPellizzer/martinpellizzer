Mentha decipiens

Mentha decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive minty aroma when crushed.

Mentha decipiens is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and mild medicinal properties.
