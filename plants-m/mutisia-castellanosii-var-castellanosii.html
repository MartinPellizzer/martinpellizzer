Mutisia castellanosii var. castellanosii

Mutisia castellanosii var. castellanosii, commonly known as the Castellano's mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in temperate to cool climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The showy yellow flowers appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
