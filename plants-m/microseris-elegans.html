Microseris elegans

Microseris elegans, commonly known as the slender scabious, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves are adapted to arid conditions.

It produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
