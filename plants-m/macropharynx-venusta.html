Macropharynx venusta

Macropharynx venusta, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, with a distinctive, elongated throat. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Macropharynx venusta is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
