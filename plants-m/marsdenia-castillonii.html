Marsdenia castillonii

Marsdenia castillonii, commonly known as the Castillon's Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to parts of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbellate clusters, which are followed by follicles containing seeds.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories where it can climb trees for support.
