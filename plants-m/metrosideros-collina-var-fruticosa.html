Metrosideros collina var. fruticosa

Metrosideros collina var. fruticosa, commonly known as the Tahitian flame tree, is a shrub-like variant of the native New Zealand species.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salty sea air, making it a common sight in coastal gardens.

This variety is often used for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
