Monodora junodii var. macrantha

Monodora junodii var. macrantha, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Central and East Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and pinnately compound leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by large, fleshy, orange fruits that resemble a breadfruit. The fruit is edible when ripe, with a sweet, starchy pulp that is used in various traditional dishes.

It also has medicinal properties and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
