Melodinus curvinervius

Melodinus curvinervius, commonly known as the zigzag vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive zigzag venation pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
