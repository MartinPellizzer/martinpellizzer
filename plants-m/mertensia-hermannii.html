Mertensia hermannii

Mertensia hermannii, commonly known as Hermann's bluebells, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Mertensia hermannii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
