Myrcia ruiziana

Myrcia ruiziana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy red flowers. The plant produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Myrcia ruiziana is also valued for its aromatic wood and has been used in traditional medicine.
