Mesembryanthemum namibense

Mesembryanthemum namibense, commonly known as the Namibian ice plant, is a succulent plant native to the Namib Desert.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh habitat.

Mesembryanthemum namibense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens.
