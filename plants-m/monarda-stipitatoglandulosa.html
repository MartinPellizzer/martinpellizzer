Monarda stipitatoglandulosa

Monarda stipitatoglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular-stem monarda, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida and Georgia, where it thrives in moist, woodland habitats. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a perennial herb that grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, often forming dense clumps in the understory of forests.
