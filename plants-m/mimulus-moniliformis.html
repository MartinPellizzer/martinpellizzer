Mimulus moniliformis

Mimulus moniliformis, commonly known as the bead lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mimulus moniliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
