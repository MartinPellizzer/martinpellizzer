Monopsis lutea var. ericoides

Monopsis lutea var. ericoides, commonly known as the yellow monopsis, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate partial shade.
