Mascagnia tomentosa

Mascagnia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds inside.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and drought tolerance, making it well adapted to its native habitat.
