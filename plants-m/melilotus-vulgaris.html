Melilotus vulgaris

Melilotus vulgaris, commonly known as yellow sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of North America. It grows in a variety of habitats, including fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Melilotus vulgaris is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural and ecological contexts.
