Melastoma theiforme

Melastoma theiforme, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Melastoma theiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
