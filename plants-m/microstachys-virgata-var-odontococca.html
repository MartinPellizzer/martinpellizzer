Microstachys virgata var. odontococca

Microstachys virgata var. odontococca, commonly known as the toothed bract palm, is a tropical plant characterized by its unique foliage and growth habit.

It features elongated, narrow leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, white flowers. It typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, often found in shaded, humid environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
