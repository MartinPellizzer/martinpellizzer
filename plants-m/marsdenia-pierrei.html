Marsdenia pierrei

Marsdenia pierrei, commonly known as the Philippine star vine, is a climbing plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
