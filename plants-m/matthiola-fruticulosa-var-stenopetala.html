Matthiola fruticulosa var. stenopetala

Matthiola fruticulosa var. stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled stock, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, shrub-like growth with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, colorful plants.
