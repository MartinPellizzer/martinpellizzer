Merostachys argentea

Merostachys argentea, commonly known as silver beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-tomentose leaves and panicle-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive silvery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
