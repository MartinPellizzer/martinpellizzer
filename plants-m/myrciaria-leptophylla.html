Myrciaria leptophylla

Myrciaria leptophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian blackcurrant, is a species of shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinct aromatic scent.

Myrciaria leptophylla is often cultivated for its edible fruits and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
