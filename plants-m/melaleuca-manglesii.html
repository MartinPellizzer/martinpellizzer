Melaleuca manglesii

Melaleuca manglesii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters, often attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant is also known for its ability to tolerate salt spray and is commonly used in coastal landscaping.
