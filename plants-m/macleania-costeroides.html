Macleania costeroides

Macleania costeroides, commonly known as the Costa Rican snowberry, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of Costa Rica and Panama.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact shrub form with glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
