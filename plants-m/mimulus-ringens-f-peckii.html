Mimulus ringens f. peckii

Mimulus ringens f. peckii, commonly known as the Peck's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
