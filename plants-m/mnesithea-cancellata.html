Mnesithea cancellata

Mnesithea cancellata, commonly known as the laceleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely lobed leaves that give it a lace-like appearance. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mnesithea cancellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
