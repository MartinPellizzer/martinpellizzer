Microglossa parvifolia

Microglossa parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved microglossa, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
