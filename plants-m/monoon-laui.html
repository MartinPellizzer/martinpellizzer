Monoon laui

Monoon laui, commonly known as the Java moonflower, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine with large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers at night, which open in the evening and close by dawn. Its flowers have five petals and are known for their strong, sweet scent.

Monoon laui is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
