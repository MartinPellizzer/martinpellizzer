Mesosetum cayennense

Mesosetum cayennense, commonly known as the Cayenne grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, preferring warm and humid climates.

Mesosetum cayennense is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
