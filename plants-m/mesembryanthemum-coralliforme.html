Mesembryanthemum coralliforme

Mesembryanthemum coralliforme, commonly known as the coral flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically rounded and covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant's unique coral-like appearance and drought tolerance make it both ornamental and resilient.
