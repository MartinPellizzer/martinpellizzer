Mallotus baillonianus

Mallotus baillonianus, commonly known as the African wild tea, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive ailments.
