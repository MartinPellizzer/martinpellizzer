Mucuna mairei

Mucuna mairei, commonly known as the Maire's velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by the development of its characteristic seed pods. Mucuna mairei is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural systems.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
