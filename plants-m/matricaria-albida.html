Matricaria albida

Matricaria albida, commonly known as the white chamomile, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has hairy, narrow leaves. Its flowers produce tiny seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation.

Matricaria albida is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
