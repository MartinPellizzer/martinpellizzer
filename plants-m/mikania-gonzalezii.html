Mikania gonzalezii

Mikania gonzalezii, commonly known as the Mexican devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
