Mazus japonicus var. delavayi

Mazus japonicus var. delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's mazus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually in shades of pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded woodland areas.
