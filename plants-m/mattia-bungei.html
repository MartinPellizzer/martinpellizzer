Mattia bungei

Mattia bungei, commonly known as the Bunge's spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the spurge family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Mattia bungei is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
