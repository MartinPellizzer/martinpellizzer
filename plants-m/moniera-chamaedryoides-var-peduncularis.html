Moniera chamaedryoides var. peduncularis

Moniera chamaedryoides var. peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub with small, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
