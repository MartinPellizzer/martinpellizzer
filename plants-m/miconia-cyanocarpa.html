Miconia cyanocarpa

Miconia cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruited miconia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns blue when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Miconia cyanocarpa is often found in humid lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
