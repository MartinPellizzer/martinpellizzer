Mucuna nigricans var. cordata

Mucuna nigricans var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant produces a distinctive, sweet-smelling flower that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a protein source.

The plant also has a rapid growth rate and can become invasive if not managed properly.
