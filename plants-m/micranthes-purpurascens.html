Micranthes purpurascens

Micranthes purpurascens, commonly known as the purplestem sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically thrives in moist, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. Its stems are often tinged with a distinctive purplish hue, which gives it its common name.

Micranthes purpurascens is a member of the Brassicaceae family, known for its characteristic cruciform flowers and aromatic foliage.
