Mimusops huberi

Mimusops huberi, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, containing a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its durable wood.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
