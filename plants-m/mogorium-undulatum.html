Mogorium undulatum

Mogorium undulatum, commonly known as the wavy leaved orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, undulating leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
