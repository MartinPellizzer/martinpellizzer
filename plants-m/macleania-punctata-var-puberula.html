Macleania punctata var. puberula

Macleania punctata var. puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and showy, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
