Mitrophyllum crassifolium

Mitrophyllum crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved mitrophyllum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, succulent-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

The flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters, making it a notable plant in coastal and semi-arid habitats.
