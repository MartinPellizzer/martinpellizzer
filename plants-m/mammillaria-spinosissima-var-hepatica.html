Mammillaria spinosissima var. hepatica

Mammillaria spinosissima var. hepatica, commonly known as the liver-spotted mammillaria, is a compact cactus native to Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly hairs and adorned with small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a distinctive pattern of light and dark spots on its body.

It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.
