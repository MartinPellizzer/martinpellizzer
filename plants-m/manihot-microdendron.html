Manihot microdendron

Manihot microdendron, commonly known as the small-leaf cassava, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a starchy root that is used as a food source in some regions. It is adapted to tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
