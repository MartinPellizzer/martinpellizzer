Mniopsis weddelliana

Mniopsis weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's mniopsis, is a small, perennial herb native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a composite head. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
