Mertensia excelsa

Mertensia excelsa, commonly known as the Eastern bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces striking clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. It is a member of the Boraginaceae family, which is characterized by its hairy stems and showy flowers.
