Michelia caudata

Michelia caudata, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or fragrant michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
