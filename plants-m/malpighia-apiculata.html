Malpighia apiculata

Malpighia apiculata, commonly known as the scarlet pomegranate, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in local cuisines.
