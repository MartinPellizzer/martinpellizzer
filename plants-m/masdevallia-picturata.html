Masdevallia picturata

Masdevallia picturata, commonly known as the Painted Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, colorful patterns on its leaves and flowers, which resemble brushstrokes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized epiphyte, often found in cool, humid cloud forests. Its flowers are usually pale green to white with contrasting red or purple markings, and they bloom in clusters.

Masdevallia picturata is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
