Millettia klainei

Millettia klainei, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and thrives in well-drained soils.
