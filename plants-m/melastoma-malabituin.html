Melastoma malabituin

Melastoma malabituin, commonly known as the Malabar bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually opposite and oval-shaped with a smooth surface.

Melastoma malabituin is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
