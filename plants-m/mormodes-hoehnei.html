Mormodes hoehnei

Mormodes hoehnei, commonly known as the Hoehne's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, intricately patterned flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted to maximize light absorption in its natural habitat.

Due to habitat destruction, Mormodes hoehnei is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
