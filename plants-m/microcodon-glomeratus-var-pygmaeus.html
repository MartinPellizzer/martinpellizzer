Microcodon glomeratus var. pygmaeus

Microcodon glomeratus var. pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf microcodon, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
