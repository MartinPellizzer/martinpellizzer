Melanthera biflora

Melanthera biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered melanthera, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Melanthera biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
