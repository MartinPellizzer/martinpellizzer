Macrorrhynchus elatus

Macrorrhynchus elatus, commonly known as the giant horned orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, featuring vibrant yellow and orange hues. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its humid, shaded habitat. Its unique floral structure includes specialized pollination mechanisms that attract specific insects.

Macrorrhynchus elatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
