Mendoncia aspera

Mendoncia aspera, commonly known as the rough mendoncia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
