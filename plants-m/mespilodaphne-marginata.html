Mespilodaphne marginata

Mespilodaphne marginata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mespilodaphne, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in lowland forests and along riverbanks. The plant has simple, opposite leaves with a distinctive narrow shape and a serrated margin. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that ripens to a yellowish color, making it an important food source for local wildlife.
