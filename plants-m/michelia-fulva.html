Michelia fulva

Michelia fulva, commonly known as the golden michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.

Michelia fulva is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
