Moutabea guianensis

Moutabea guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese moutabea, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed.

It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making, and for its potential medicinal properties.
