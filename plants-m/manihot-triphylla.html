Manihot triphylla

Manihot triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by edible, starchy tubers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible roots, it is cultivated in gardens and for agricultural purposes.
