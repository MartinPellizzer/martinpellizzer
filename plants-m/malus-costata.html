Malus costata

Malus costata, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental flowers, which range from white to pink, and its edible, tart fruits. The plant has a distinctive, rugged bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Malus costata is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of ornamental plants.
