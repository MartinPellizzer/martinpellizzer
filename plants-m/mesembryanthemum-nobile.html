Mesembryanthemum nobile

Mesembryanthemum nobile, commonly known as the noble ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its unique morphology and ability to store water in its leaves make it an attractive ornamental plant.

Mesembryanthemum nobile is also valued for its role in xeriscaping and its ability to attract pollinators.
