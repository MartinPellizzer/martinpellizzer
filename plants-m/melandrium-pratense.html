Melandrium pratense

Melandrium pratense, commonly known as the common honesty, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its fruits are distinctive, consisting of small, bladder-like pods that burst to disperse seeds.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall appearance.
