Myginda myrtifolia var. major

Myginda myrtifolia var. major, commonly known as the large myginda, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
