Merendera caucasica var. eichleri

Merendera caucasica var. eichleri, commonly known as the Caucasian merendera, is a variant of the merendera plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species in botanical collections.
