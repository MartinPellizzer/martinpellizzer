Malva aegyptia subsp. leiocarpa

Malva aegyptia subsp.

leiocarpa, commonly known as the Egyptian mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies leiocarpa is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves compared to other subspecies of Malva aegyptia.
