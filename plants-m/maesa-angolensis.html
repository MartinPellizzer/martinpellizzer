Maesa angolensis

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pink and are followed by small, fleshy berries.
