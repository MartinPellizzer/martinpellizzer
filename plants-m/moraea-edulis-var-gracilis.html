Moraea edulis var. gracilis

Moraea edulis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender edible moraea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming in late summer. It is valued for its edible tubers, which are rich in starch and used in local cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
