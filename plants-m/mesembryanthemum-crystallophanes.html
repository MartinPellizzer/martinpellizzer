Mesembryanthemum crystallophanes

Mesembryanthemum crystallophanes, commonly known as the crystal cushion, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warm season, typically in summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
