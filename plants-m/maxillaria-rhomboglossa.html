Maxillaria rhomboglossa

Maxillaria rhomboglossa, commonly known as the rhomboid-lipped orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its pseudobulbs and fleshy leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive rhomboid shape on the lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Maxillaria rhomboglossa is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
