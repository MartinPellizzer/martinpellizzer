Marica semiaperta

Marica semiaperta, commonly known as the semi-open marica, is a rare and unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, semi-transparent leaves that allow light to pass through, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom during the cooler months. Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it well-suited for shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Marica semiaperta is considered a conservation concern.
