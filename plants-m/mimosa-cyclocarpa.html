Mimosa cyclocarpa

Mimosa cyclocarpa, commonly known as the cyclocarpa mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that are spherical and covered in hooked hairs, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
