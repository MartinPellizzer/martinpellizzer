Medinilla rhodorhachis

Medinilla rhodorhachis, commonly known as the velvet tangerine medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Medinilla rhodorhachis is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
