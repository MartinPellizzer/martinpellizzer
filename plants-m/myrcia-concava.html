Myrcia concava

Myrcia concava, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper or myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia concava is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
