Matthiola valesiaca

Matthiola valesiaca, commonly known as the Valesiaca stock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Matthiola valesiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
