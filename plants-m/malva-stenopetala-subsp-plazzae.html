Malva stenopetala subsp. plazzae

Malva stenopetala subsp.

plazzae, commonly known as the Andean mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and blooms.
