Mentha longifolia var. amphilema

Mentha longifolia var. amphilema, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a variant of the broad-leafed mint species.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. This plant is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color.

The plant is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
