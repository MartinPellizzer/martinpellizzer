Monarda punctata var. leucantha

Monarda punctata var. leucantha, commonly known as white spotted beebalm, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features showy, tubular red or pink flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
