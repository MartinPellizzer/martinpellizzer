Melaleuca ericifolia

Melaleuca ericifolia, commonly known as the silver bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and cylindrical, bottlebrush-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. It produces small, woody fruits called capsules, which release seeds when mature.

Melaleuca ericifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
