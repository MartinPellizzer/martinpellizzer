Myrcia calophylla

Myrcia calophylla, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny, edible seeds.

Myrcia calophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
