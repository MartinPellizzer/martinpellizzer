Machaonia nipensis

Machaonia nipensis, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen holly, is a shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it suitable for both garden and natural environments.
