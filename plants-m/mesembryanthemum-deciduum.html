Mesembryanthemum deciduum

Mesembryanthemum deciduum, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing bright yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
