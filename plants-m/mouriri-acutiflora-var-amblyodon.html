Mouriri acutiflora var. amblyodon

Mouriri acutiflora var. amblyodon, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or sweetshrub, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
