Myrcia oblongifolia

Myrcia oblongifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a strong, spicy scent, while its flowers are borne in clusters and attract various pollinators.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that matures to a dark purple color.
