Maxillaria chicana

Maxillaria chicana, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its compact, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive red or purple lip. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized epiphyte, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base.

Maxillaria chicana is valued for its ornamental flowers and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
