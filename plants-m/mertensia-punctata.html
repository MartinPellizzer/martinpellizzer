Mertensia punctata

Mertensia punctata, commonly known as the spotted bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia punctata is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is often found in woodland habitats.
