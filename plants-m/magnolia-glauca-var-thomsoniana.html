Magnolia glauca var. thomsoniana

Magnolia glauca var. thomsoniana, commonly known as Thomson's magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are elliptical, with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside. The plant has smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age, and its roots are fibrous and shallow.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
