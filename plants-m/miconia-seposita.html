Miconia seposita

Miconia seposita, commonly known as the Hawaiian miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a significant ecological concern in its native habitat.
