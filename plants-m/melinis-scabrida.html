Melinis scabrida

Melinis scabrida, commonly known as rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in full sun and is commonly found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its seeds are small and mature in the fall, contributing to its role in maintaining soil stability.

Melinis scabrida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support local wildlife.
