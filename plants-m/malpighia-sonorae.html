Malpighia sonorae

Malpighia sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-pink bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping in desert landscapes.
