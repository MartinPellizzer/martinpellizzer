Microstachys dasycarpa

Microstachys dasycarpa, commonly known as the rough-fruited microstachys, is a member of the Poaceae family, native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, wiry foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its fruits are distinctive, featuring rough, hairy surfaces that aid in seed dispersal.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
