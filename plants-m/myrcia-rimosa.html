Myrcia rimosa

Myrcia rimosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove.
