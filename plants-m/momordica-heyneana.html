Momordica heyneana

Momordica heyneana, commonly known as the bitter melon or karela, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and ridged fruit, which is typically green when immature and turns orange-red when ripe. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that are followed by the characteristic bitter melon fruit. It is native to Southeast Asia and is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in various cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine systems.
