Merremia tridentata subsp. hastata

Merremia tridentata subsp.

hastata, commonly known as the three-toothed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white.

The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
