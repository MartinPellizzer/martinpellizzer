Millettia brachycarpa

Millettia brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited millettia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its fruit is a pod that matures into a dry, dehiscent capsule, releasing seeds upon ripening.

Millettia brachycarpa is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
