Mertensia tala

Mertensia tala, commonly known as the bluebells of the Andes, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. Mertensia tala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to alpine and woodland gardens.
