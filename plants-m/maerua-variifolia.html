Maerua variifolia

Maerua variifolia, commonly known as the variable leaf maerua, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, elliptical or ovate leaves that vary in shape and size across different parts of the plant. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Maerua variifolia is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
