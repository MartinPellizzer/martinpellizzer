Myrteola microphylla var. glabrata

Myrteola microphylla var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved myrteola, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small shrub or tree characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its bark is smooth and pale, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
