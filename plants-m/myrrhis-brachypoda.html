Myrrhis brachypoda

Myrrhis brachypoda, commonly known as narrow-leaved sweetflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense tufts, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and can reach considerable depth, allowing it to thrive in moist soils.

Myrrhis brachypoda is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
