Mentha arvensis f. nummularia

Mentha arvensis f. nummularia, commonly known as woolly mint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rounded, velvety leaves that are typically silver-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows or along stream banks.

Due to its compact growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
