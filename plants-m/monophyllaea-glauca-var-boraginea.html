Monophyllaea glauca var. boraginea

Monophyllaea glauca var. boraginea, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its foliage is often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
