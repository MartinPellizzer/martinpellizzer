Myrcia clementis

Myrcia clementis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe.

Myrcia clementis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
