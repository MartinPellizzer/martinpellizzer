Masema

Masema, commonly known as the "tree of life," is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, glossy leaves that provide shade and habitat for various organisms. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible fruits resembling small, round oranges. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.

The Masema tree plays a vital role in its ecosystem, supporting biodiversity and contributing to soil stability.
