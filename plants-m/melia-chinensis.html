Melia chinensis

Melia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves and fruits contain toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine.
