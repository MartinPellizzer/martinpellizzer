Myriophyllum callitrichoides subsp. striatum

Myriophyllum callitrichoides subsp.

striatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America. It features slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length.

The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often found near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and rivers.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
