Maranta burchellii

Maranta burchellii, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that often features intricate patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes. Its rhizomes are used to produce a starchy root known as arrowroot, which is used in cooking.

Maranta burchellii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive leaves and ease of care.
