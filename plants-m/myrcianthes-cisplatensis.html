Myrcianthes cisplatensis

Myrcianthes cisplatensis, commonly known as the Paraguay wax tree, is a small evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.
