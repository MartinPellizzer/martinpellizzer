Megaclinium purpureorhachis

Megaclinium purpureorhachis, commonly known as the purple-stemmed flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that display a striking purple hue, particularly in the lower parts. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in loose spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Megaclinium purpureorhachis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in textile production.
