Montia spathulata var. disciformis

Montia spathulata var. disciformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed montia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species with distinctive, broad, spatulate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and streambanks.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a notable species in botanical studies of the Montiaceae family.
