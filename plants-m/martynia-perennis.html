Martynia perennis

Martynia perennis, commonly known as the everlasting flower or red dahlia, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red, orange, or yellow flowers that resemble dahlias, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has a bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Martynia perennis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
