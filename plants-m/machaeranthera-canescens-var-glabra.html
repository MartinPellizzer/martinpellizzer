Machaeranthera canescens var. glabra

Machaeranthera canescens var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has smooth, hairless leaves and is often found in open prairies and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
