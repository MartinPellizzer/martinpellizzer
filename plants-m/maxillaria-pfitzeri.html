Maxillaria pfitzeri

Maxillaria pfitzeri, commonly known as the Pfitzer's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip that is adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maxillaria pfitzeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
