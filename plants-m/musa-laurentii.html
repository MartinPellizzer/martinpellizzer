Musa laurentii

Musa laurentii, commonly known as the African banana, is a species of flowering plant in the Musaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of fruit. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
