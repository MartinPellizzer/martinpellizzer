Monoon clavigerum

Monoon clavigerum, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, tubular flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in full sun.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
