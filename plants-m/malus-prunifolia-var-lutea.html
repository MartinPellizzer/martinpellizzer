Malus prunifolia var. lutea

Malus prunifolia var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow-flowered crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, sour fruits that are typically not edible for humans but are used by wildlife. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn a striking red or orange in the fall.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
