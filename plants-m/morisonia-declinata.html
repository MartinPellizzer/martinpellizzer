Morisonia declinata

Morisonia declinata, commonly known as the creeping morisonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Morisonia declinata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and woodland habitats.
