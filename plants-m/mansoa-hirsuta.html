Mansoa hirsuta

Mansoa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy mansoa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
