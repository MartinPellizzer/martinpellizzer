Myrtus acuminata

Myrtus acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small white flowers and narrow, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Myrtus acuminata thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.
