Mesembryanthemum claviforme

Mesembryanthemum claviforme, commonly known as the diamond flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is well-suited for arid climates and xeriscaping.
