Machaonia subinermis subsp. subinermis

Machaonia subinermis subsp.

subinermis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved machaonia, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit.

The plant features lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This species is often found in rocky or coastal habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
