Mentha × kerneri var. cheuchisensis

Mentha × kerneri var. cheuchisensis, commonly known as the Siberian mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often hairy and have a strong menthol scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other mint species.
