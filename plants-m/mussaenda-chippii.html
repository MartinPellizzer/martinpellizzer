Mussaenda chippii

Mussaenda chippii, commonly known as the golden trumpet bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered yellow flowers that resemble small trumpets, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Mussaenda chippii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
