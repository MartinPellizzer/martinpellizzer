Margaritaria

Margaritaria, commonly known as the margarita plant, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that resemble small margarita glasses. The plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a prominent central stigma.

Margaritaria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
