Manihot congesta

Manihot congesta, commonly known as wild cassava, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to humid tropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
