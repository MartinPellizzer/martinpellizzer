Matricaria tempskyana

Matricaria tempskyana, commonly known as the Siberian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.

Matricaria tempskyana thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
