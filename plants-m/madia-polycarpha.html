Madia polycarpha

Madia polycarpha, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelion blooms. The plant has a taproot system and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Madia polycarpha is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and chaparral habitats.
