Miconia penningtonii

Miconia penningtonii, commonly known as the Pennington miconia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a distinctive red calyx. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
