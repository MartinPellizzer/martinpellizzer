Melanochyla tomentosa var. glabrescens

Melanochyla tomentosa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces purple to violet flowers in late spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
