Myristica millepunctata

Myristica millepunctata, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. Its seeds, known as black pepper, are widely used as a spice and medicinal herb.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and the spice trade.
