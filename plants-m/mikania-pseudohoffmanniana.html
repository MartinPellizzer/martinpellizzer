Mikania pseudohoffmanniana

Mikania pseudohoffmanniana, commonly known as the creeping mikania, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a vigorous climbing vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that are often overlooked due to their size. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a problematic weed in certain ecosystems.
