Mentha longifolia var. gracilis

Mentha longifolia var. gracilis, commonly known as slender wood mint, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
