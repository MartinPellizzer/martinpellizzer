Musa borneensis var. lutea

Musa borneensis var. lutea, commonly known as the golden banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and striking yellow-orange fruit. The plant produces elongated, fleshy berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit coloration.
