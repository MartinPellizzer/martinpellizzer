Mandevilla spruceana

Mandevilla spruceana, commonly known as the spruce mandevilla, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pink. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Mandevilla spruceana is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for those seeking vibrant, flowering plants.
