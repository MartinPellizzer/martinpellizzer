Magnolia vallartensis

Magnolia vallartensis, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. It produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blossoms.
