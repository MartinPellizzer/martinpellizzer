Matricaria coccinea

Matricaria coccinea, commonly known as the red chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright red flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant appearance and easy cultivation requirements.
