Melastoma erostratum

Melastoma erostratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf melastoma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically small, clustered, and have five petals, often displaying a mix of white, pink, and purple hues.

The plant produces berries that are edible and serve as a food source for various wildlife.
