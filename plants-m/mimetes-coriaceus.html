Mimetes coriaceus

Mimetes coriaceus, commonly known as the hard-leaved mimetes, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that help it conserve water in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is an important component of fynbos vegetation in its native region.
