Michelia glauca

Michelia glauca, commonly known as the white-flowered michelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually glossy green on the upper surface and paler underneath. Michelia glauca thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
