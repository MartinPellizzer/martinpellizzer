Mirabilis laevis var. crassifolia

Mirabilis laevis var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers, which attract pollinators such as moths and bats. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
