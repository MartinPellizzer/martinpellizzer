Mimosa bathyrrhena

Mimosa bathyrrhena, commonly known as the desert mimosa, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its bipinnate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in scrublands and along riverbanks.

Mimosa bathyrrhena plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
