Micrampelis fabacea var. agrestis

Micrampelis fabacea var. agrestis, commonly known as the roughleaf poinsettia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety bracts that range in color from red to orange, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are typically rough to the touch, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly during the holiday season.
