Machaonia spinosa

Machaonia spinosa, commonly known as the hairy machaonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate rainforests. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Machaonia spinosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
