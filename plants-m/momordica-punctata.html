Momordica punctata

Momordica punctata, commonly known as the spiny cucumber or horned melon, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, orange-yellow fruits with a thick, waxy rind and a sweet, melon-like interior. Its flowers are yellow with five lobes and are borne in the axils of the leaves.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
