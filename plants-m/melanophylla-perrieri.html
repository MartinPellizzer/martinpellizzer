Melanophylla perrieri

Melanophylla perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's melanic fern, is a rare and unique species of fern native to the humid forests of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are densely covered with a fine, almost metallic sheen, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is known for its slow growth rate. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are clustered on the underside of its fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Melanophylla perrieri is considered a conservation concern.
