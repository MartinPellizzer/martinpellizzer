Myrtus chekenilla

Myrtus chekenilla, commonly known as the Chilean myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant and have a leathery texture. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, dark blue berries.

Myrtus chekenilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
