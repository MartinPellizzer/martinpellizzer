Mentha × ramosissima

Mentha × ramosissima, commonly known as square-stemmed mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha viridis and Mentha spicata.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late summer. It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, shady environments and is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves.

Mentha × ramosissima is valued for its use in culinary applications, herbal remedies, and as a decorative plant.
