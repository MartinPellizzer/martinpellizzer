Mimulus langsdorffii var. californicus

Mimulus langsdorffii var. californicus, commonly known as the California monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features striking orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
