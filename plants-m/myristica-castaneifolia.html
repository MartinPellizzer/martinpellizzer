Myristica castaneifolia

Myristica castaneifolia, commonly known as the African nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and fragrant, reddish-brown seeds. The plant produces a unique fruit that contains a single seed, which is encased in a hard, aromatic shell. The seeds are valued for their aromatic oils and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its economic and medicinal properties.
