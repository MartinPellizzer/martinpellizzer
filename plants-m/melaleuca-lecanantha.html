Melaleuca lecanantha

Melaleuca lecanantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is an evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Melaleuca lecanantha is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
