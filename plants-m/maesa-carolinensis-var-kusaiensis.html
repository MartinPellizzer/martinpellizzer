Maesa carolinensis var. kusaiensis

Maesa carolinensis var. kusaiensis, commonly known as the Kusai Mountain Beauty, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a smooth, dark green surface and a slightly hairy underside. Its flowers are white to pale pink, clustered in terminal panicles, and bloom in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
