Mimosa dolens subsp. callosa

Mimosa dolens subsp.

callosa, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a shrub native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological features, including the presence of hairy stipules along the leaf stems.
