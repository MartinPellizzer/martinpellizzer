Mammillaria pseudorekoi

Mammillaria pseudorekoi, commonly known as the Pseudo-Reko mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant typically produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat, it is a sought-after species among cactus enthusiasts.
