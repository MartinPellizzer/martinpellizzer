Medicago romanica var. canescens

Medicago romanica var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is known for its extensive root system, which helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in agricultural settings as a cover crop.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
