Mimosa cordistipula

Mimosa cordistipula, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique foliage and soft appearance make it a distinctive addition to the botanical landscape.
