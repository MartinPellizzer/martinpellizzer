Myrtus pyrifolia

Myrtus pyrifolia, commonly known as the Persian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a valued plant in horticulture and herbal practices.
