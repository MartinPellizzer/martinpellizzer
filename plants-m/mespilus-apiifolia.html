Mespilus apiifolia

Mespilus apiifolia, commonly known as the wild pear or horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and desserts. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive scent when crushed, and it is often found in moist, woodland environments.

Mespilus apiifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
