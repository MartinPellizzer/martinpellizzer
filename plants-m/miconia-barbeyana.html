Miconia barbeyana

Miconia barbeyana, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or the pink miconia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a bright yellow berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia barbeyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique floral display.
