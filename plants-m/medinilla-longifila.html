Medinilla longifila

Medinilla longifila, commonly known as the long-stemmed Medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil and indirect sunlight.

Medinilla longifila is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and prolonged flowering period.
