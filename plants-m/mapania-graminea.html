Mapania graminea

Mapania graminea, commonly known as the slender grass.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tufted growth habit. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
