Mentha × rotundifolia f. pachystachya

Mentha × rotundifolia f. pachystachya, commonly known as the broad-leaved mint, is a hybrid plant characterized by its rounded, broad leaves and compact growth habit.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is notable for its aromatic foliage and flowering spikes that produce small, tubular flowers. The plant is native to regions in Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its leaves are commonly used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.
