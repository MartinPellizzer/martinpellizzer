Melampyrum pratense var. angustifolium

Melampyrum pratense var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf burningnettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Orobanchaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Melampyrum pratense.
