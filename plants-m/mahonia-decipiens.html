Mahonia decipiens

Mahonia decipiens, commonly known as the false Oregon grape, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with spiny margins and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces blue-black berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant autumn foliage.
