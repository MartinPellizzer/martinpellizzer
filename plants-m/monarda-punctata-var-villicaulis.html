Monarda punctata var. villicaulis

Monarda punctata var. villicaulis, commonly known as the slender horsemint, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that produce showy, tubular flowers in shades of red and violet. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
