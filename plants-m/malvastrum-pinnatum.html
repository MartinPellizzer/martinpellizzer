Malvastrum pinnatum

Malvastrum pinnatum, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry, open habitats.
