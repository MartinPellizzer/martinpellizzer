Musa borneensis var. alutacea

Musa borneensis var. alutacea, commonly known as the Bornean banana, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of fruit. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and requires ample sunlight and moisture. Its fruit, which is often smaller than common bananas, is edible and has a unique flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
