Maxillaria palmifolia

Maxillaria palmifolia, commonly known as the palm-leaf orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that resemble palm fronds, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a velvety texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maxillaria palmifolia is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
