Masdevallia heterotepala

Masdevallia heterotepala, commonly known as the "Wedge-leaf Masdevallia," is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high altitudes, often epiphytic on trees or rocks. Its flowers are usually borne on slender stems and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

The species is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in its native habitat.
