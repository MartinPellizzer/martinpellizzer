Maxillaria tricolor

Maxillaria tricolor, commonly known as the Three-Colored Orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, three-toned flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Maxillaria tricolor is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its striking coloration and relatively easy care requirements.
