Machilus pingii

Machilus pingii, commonly known as the Chinese laurustin, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Machilus pingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
