Munchusia tomentosa

Munchusia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy gray-green foliage and showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and can reach heights of up to 2 feet. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Munchusia tomentosa is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
