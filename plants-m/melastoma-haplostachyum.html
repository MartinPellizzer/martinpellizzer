Melastoma haplostachyum

Melastoma haplostachyum, commonly known as the small-stemmed melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red and purple. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in forest undergrowth.
