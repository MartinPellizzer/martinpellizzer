Mikania lasiopoda

Mikania lasiopoda, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the early morning. Its rapid growth habit makes it an invasive species in many regions, often outcompeting native vegetation.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
