Manihot tenuifolia

Manihot tenuifolia, commonly known as arrowleaf groundcherry, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
