Magnolia sororum

Magnolia sororum, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and aesthetic appeal.
