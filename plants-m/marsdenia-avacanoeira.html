Marsdenia avacanoeira

Marsdenia avacanoeira, commonly known as the Brazilian star vine, is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
