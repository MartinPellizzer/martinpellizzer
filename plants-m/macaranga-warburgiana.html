Macaranga warburgiana

Macaranga warburgiana, commonly known as the Warburg's makara, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of lowland forests.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
