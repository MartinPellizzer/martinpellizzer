Monarda nuttallii

Monarda nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's beebrush, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive minty fragrance.

Monarda nuttallii thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
