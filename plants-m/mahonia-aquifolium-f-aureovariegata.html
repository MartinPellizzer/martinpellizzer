Mahonia aquifolium f. aureovariegata

Mahonia aquifolium f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated mahonia, is a cultivar of the evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features striking, finely toothed leaves with a distinctive golden-yellow variegation that contrasts with its deep green background. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and is well-suited for woodland gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.

This cultivar is prized for its compact growth habit and year-round visual interest.
