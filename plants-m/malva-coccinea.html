Malva coccinea

Malva coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet mallows, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

Malva coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
