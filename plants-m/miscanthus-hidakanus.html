Miscanthus hidakanus

Miscanthus hidakanus, commonly known as the Hidaka muhly, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-green plumes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-gray hue.

Miscanthus hidakanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
