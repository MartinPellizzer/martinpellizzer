Melhania parvifolia

Melhania parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved melhania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Melhania parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
