Morus pyramidalis

Morus pyramidalis, commonly known as the square-leaf mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit and distinctive square-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its historical use in traditional medicine.
