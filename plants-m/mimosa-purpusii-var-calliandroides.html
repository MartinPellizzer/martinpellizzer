Mimosa purpusii var. calliandroides

Mimosa purpusii var. calliandroides, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, fluffy yellow flower clusters that appear in late spring. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, making it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
