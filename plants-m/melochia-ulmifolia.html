Melochia ulmifolia

Melochia ulmifolia, commonly known as the smallleaf melochia, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically pubescent and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in moist woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
