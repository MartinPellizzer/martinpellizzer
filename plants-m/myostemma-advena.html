Myostemma advena

Myostemma advena, commonly known as the wandering stem plant, is a perennial herbaceous species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a slender, branching stem that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often trailing along the ground or climbing over other vegetation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Myostemma advena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
