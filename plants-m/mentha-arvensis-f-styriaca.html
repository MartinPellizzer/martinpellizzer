Mentha arvensis f. styriaca

Mentha arvensis f. styriaca, commonly known as the Styrian mint, is a subspecies of water mint native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin and a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or pink, arranged in whorls along the stem.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
