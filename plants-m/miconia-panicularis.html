Miconia panicularis

Miconia panicularis, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a round, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Miconia panicularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
