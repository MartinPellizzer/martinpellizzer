Mammillaria curvata

Mammillaria curvata, commonly known as the curled mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and dense clusters of white to pale blue flowers. The plant features prominent areoles covered with white, woolly bristles. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
