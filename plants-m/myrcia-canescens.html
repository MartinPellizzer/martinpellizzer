Myrcia canescens

Myrcia canescens, commonly known as the woolly myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

Myrcia canescens is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
