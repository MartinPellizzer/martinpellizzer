Melilotus laxiflorus

Melilotus laxiflorus, commonly known as slender-flowered melilot, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Melilotus laxiflorus is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it serves as a food source for various pollinators.
