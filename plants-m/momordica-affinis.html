Momordica affinis

Momordica affinis, commonly known as the horned melon or African horned melon, is a tropical vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, green to yellowish fruit that resembles a horned melon. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers and has heart-shaped, lobed leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

The fruit's thick, waxy rind contains a sweet, aromatic pulp with a mild melon flavor.
