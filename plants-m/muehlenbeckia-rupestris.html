Muehlenbeckia rupestris

Muehlenbeckia rupestris, commonly known as the rock muehlenbeckia, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It features slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense ground cover. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by small, round, fleshy fruits. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or coastal environments.
