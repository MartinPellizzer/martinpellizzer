Melica jacquemontii f. canescens

Melica jacquemontii f. canescens, commonly known as the hairy meadowgrass, is a botanical variant of the species Melica jacquemontii.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and have a hairy texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
