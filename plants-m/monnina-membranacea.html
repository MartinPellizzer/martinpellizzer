Monnina membranacea

Monnina membranacea, commonly known as velvetleaf monnina, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually hairy and have a velvety texture. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and prominent stamens.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
