Melanosticta burchellii

Melanosticta burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's melastoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for certain bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Melanosticta burchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
