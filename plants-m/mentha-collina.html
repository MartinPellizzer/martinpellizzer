Mentha collina

Mentha collina, commonly known as narrowleaf mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a strong, minty fragrance. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Mentha collina is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its cooling and digestive properties.
