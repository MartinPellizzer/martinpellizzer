Malaxis rupestris

Malaxis rupestris, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and in shaded, moist environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in crevices.

Malaxis rupestris is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
