Medusea patula

Medusea patula, commonly known as the slender ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers and slender, often ephemeral stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on the trunks of trees or in rocky crevices. Its botanical features include a short, fleshy root system and a unique flowering pattern that occurs during the wet season.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Medusea patula is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
