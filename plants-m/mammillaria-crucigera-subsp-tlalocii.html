Mammillaria crucigera subsp. tlalocii

Mammillaria crucigera subsp.

tlalocii, commonly known as the Tlaloc cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico. It features a globular or cylindrical stem with white, woolly hairs that give it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique aesthetic.
