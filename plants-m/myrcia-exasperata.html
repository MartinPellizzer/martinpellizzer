Myrcia exasperata

Myrcia exasperata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but have a strong, spicy flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and traditional uses in medicine.
