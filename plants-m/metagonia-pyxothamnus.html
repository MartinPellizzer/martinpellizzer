Metagonia pyxothamnus

Metagonia pyxothamnus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved metagonia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and compact growth habit, which help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its woody stems are covered in a thick layer of bark, providing protection against drought and herbivory.

Metagonia pyxothamnus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
