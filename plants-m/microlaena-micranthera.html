Microlaena micranthera

Microlaena micranthera, commonly known as the small-flowered microlaena, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and open woodlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikelets. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy conditions.

Microlaena micranthera plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
