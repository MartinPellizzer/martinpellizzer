Myosotis involucrata

Myosotis involucrata, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate or upright depending on the growing conditions.

Myosotis involucrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
