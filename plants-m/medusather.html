Medusather

Medusather, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant that belongs to the nightshade family.

It is native to tropical regions and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant blooms that open at night. The plant has a trailing or climbing habit, with heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers emit a strong scent to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.
