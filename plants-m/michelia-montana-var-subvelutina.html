Michelia montana var. subvelutina

Michelia montana var. subvelutina, commonly known as the velvet-leafed michelia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, giving it the "velvet" appearance in its name. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and scent.
