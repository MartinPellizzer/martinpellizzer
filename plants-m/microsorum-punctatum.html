Microsorum punctatum

Microsorum punctatum, commonly known as the Japanese water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant has a rhizome that anchors it to submerged surfaces, and its leaves are divided into small, rounded lobes. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Microsorum punctatum is also valued for its ability to help maintain water quality in aquariums.
