Malpighia latifolia

Malpighia latifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Natal plum, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.

The botanical features of Malpighia latifolia make it a popular ornamental and edible plant in various regions.
