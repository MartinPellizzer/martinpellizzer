Maxillaria parkeri

Maxillaria parkeri, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically blooms during the holiday season, hence its common name. It has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, and its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Maxillaria parkeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
