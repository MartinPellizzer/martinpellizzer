Myrsiphyllum scandens

Myrsiphyllum scandens, commonly known as the climbing myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its woody stems allow it to climb and twine around other vegetation.

The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains numerous tiny seeds.
