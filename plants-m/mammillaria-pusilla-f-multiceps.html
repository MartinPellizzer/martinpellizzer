Mammillaria pusilla f. multiceps

Mammillaria pusilla f. multiceps, commonly known as the many-headed miniature mammillaria, is a compact cactus species native to Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with numerous small, rounded tubercles that bear white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit, giving it a fluffy appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
