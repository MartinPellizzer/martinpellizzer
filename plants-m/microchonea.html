Microchonea

Microchonea, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. They are small, epiphytic orchids that typically grow on tree branches or rocks. Microchonea species are characterized by their compact size, delicate flowers, and intricate leaf structures.

Due to their limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, they are often considered rare and are of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
