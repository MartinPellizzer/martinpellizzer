Melocactus eburneus f. plurispina

Melocactus eburneus f. plurispina, commonly known as the many-spined white melon cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to a small region in southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant features a dense covering of white, spiny tubercles that give it a unique, almost otherworldly appearance. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom in late summer and are relatively short-lived.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
