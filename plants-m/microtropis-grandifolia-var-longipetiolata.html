Microtropis grandifolia var. longipetiolata

Microtropis grandifolia var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled wild betony, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with long petioles, and its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
