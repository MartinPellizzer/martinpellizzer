Maximowiczia

Maximowiczia, commonly known as the "buttercup grass," is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, yellow or green flowers. Maximowiczia species are often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils.

They play a role in stabilizing soil and are sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
