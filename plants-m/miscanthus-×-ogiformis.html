Miscanthus × ogiformis

Miscanthus × ogiformis, commonly known as silver grass, is a hybrid perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-blue plumes that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are followed by persistent, fluffy seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and ecological restoration projects.

Its ability to grow in a variety of conditions and its aesthetic appeal contribute to its widespread cultivation.
