Microsorum australiense

Microsorum australiense, commonly known as the Australian water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically divided into small, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and wetlands. Its rhizomes form dense mats on the water surface, contributing to water filtration and habitat creation for aquatic organisms.

Microsorum australiense plays a significant role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitats.
