Mentha piperita

Mentha piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its square, hollow stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers in whorled clusters, which are typically borne on erect spikes. Peppermint is known for its strong, aromatic menthol scent, which comes from the essential oils secreted by its leaves and stems.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is widely cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
