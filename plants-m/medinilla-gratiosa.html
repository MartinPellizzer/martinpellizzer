Medinilla gratiosa

Medinilla gratiosa, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or sweet tangerine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits that resemble tangerines, which are edible and have a sweet, citrus-like flavor. Medinilla gratiosa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
