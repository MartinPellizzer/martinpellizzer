Mussaenda lancipetala

Mussaenda lancipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its botanical features include a woody stem, hairy foliage, and a short flowering period during the rainy season.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and gardens.
