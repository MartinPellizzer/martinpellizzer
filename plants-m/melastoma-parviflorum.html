Melastoma parviflorum

Melastoma parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered melastoma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, often pink or white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Melastoma parviflorum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
