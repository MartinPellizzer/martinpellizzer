Micromeria cordata

Micromeria cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf micromeria, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls.

Micromeria cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
