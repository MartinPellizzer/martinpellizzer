Masdevallia expansa

Masdevallia expansa, commonly known as the broad-leafed masdevallia, is a striking orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, spurred flowers, which can range in color from white to pink and purple.

Masdevallia expansa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively compact growth habit.
