Myrrhinium atropurpureum var. octandrum

Myrrhinium atropurpureum var. octandrum, commonly known as the purple-flowered myrrhinium, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of deep purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
