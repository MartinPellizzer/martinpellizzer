Medinilla lorata

Medinilla lorata, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or sweet tangerine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with a red or purple calyx. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value.

Medinilla lorata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and pleasant fragrance.
