Megathyrsus maximus var. pubiglumis

Megathyrsus maximus var. pubiglumis, commonly known as the African grass or elephant grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous stalks and large, broad leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or pale brown in color. It thrives in wet, swampy areas and is often used for livestock fodder and erosion control.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
