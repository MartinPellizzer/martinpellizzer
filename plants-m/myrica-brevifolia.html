Myrica brevifolia

Myrica brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved wax myrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, dark blue berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Myrica brevifolia is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in some traditional herbal remedies.
