Melissa pulegium

Melissa pulegium, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, slightly fuzzy leaves that emit a citrusy scent when crushed. Its flowers are small, pale yellow, and clustered in whorls along the stems.

Melissa pulegium is often cultivated for its calming properties and used in herbal teas and aromatherapy.
