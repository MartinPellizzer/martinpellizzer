Muscari alpanicum

Muscari alpanicum, commonly known as the alpine grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often resembling grapes, hence its common name. The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it survive in harsh, cold climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Muscari alpanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its early spring blooms.
