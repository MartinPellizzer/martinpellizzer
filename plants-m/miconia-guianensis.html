Miconia guianensis

Miconia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Guianas and northern Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It often grows in moist, shaded environments within lowland rainforests.

Miconia guianensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
