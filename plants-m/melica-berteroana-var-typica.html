Melica berteroana var. typica

Melica berteroana var. typica, commonly known as the typical melica, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
