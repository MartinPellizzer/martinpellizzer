Mischocarpus sublaevis

Mischocarpus sublaevis, commonly known as the smooth nutmeg tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myristicaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic seeds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a smooth surface, contributing to its common name.

The tree is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to nutmeg.
