Macrodendron

Macrodendron, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental planting in tropical regions.
