Mentha × abruptifolia

Mentha × abruptifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a hybrid perennial herb in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their menthol-like fragrance.

Mentha × abruptifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
