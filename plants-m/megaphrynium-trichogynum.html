Megaphrynium trichogynum

Megaphrynium trichogynum, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces both male and female flowers on the same inflorescence, a feature known as monoecious reproduction. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used as a starchy food source.

The plant also has medicinal uses in some African cultures.
