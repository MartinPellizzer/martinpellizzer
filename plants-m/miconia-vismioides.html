Miconia vismioides

Miconia vismioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that are followed by large, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
