Magnolia glaucophylla

Magnolia glaucophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, gray-green leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is part of the Magnoliaceae family and is known for its aromatic, fragrant blossoms. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
