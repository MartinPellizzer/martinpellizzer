Memora biternata

Memora biternata, commonly known as the three-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in sets of three, giving it its common name. Its stems are twining and often climb or trail along the ground.

Memora biternata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
