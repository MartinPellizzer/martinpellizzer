Melandrium triste

Melandrium triste, commonly known as the sad bladderpod, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive bluish-green hue.

Melandrium triste is characterized by its inflated, seed-containing pods that resemble bladders, giving it its common name.
