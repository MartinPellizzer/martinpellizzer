Maxillaria bracteata

Maxillaria bracteata, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and produces showy, velvety flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil. Its flowers are borne on terminal racemes and are characterized by their intricate, fragrant structure.

Maxillaria bracteata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
