Myrcia scrobiculata

Myrcia scrobiculata, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and white or pale pink, often arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.
