Marsdenia ulei

Marsdenia ulei, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are opposite and have a velvety texture, which contributes to its ornamental appeal.

Marsdenia ulei is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
