Melandrium apricum f. pubescens

Melandrium apricum f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent honesty, is a cultivated variant of the common honesty plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, cup-shaped flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy or pubescent stems and leaves, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in gardens or wildflower areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in mixed borders or as a cut flower.
