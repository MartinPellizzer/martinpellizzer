Myrsine ferruginea

Myrsine ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty myrsine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrsinaceae.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves with a distinctive reddish-brown underside. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

The fruit is a small, spherical berry that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
