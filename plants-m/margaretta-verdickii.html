Margaretta verdickii

Margaretta verdickii, commonly known as the desert margaret, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and succulent leaves that help it retain moisture. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in the spring. It is well adapted to hot, dry environments and requires minimal water once established.

Margaretta verdickii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
