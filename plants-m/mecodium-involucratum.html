Mecodium involucratum

Mecodium involucratum, commonly known as the woolly mecodium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental appeal.

Mecodium involucratum is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun conditions.
