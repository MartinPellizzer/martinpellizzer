Magnolia allenii

Magnolia allenii, commonly known as the Allen's magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
