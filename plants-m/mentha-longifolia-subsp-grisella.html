Mentha longifolia subsp. grisella

Mentha longifolia subsp.

grisella, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental or culinary purposes.
