Melica callosa

Melica callosa, commonly known as hairy meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks.

Melica callosa is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
