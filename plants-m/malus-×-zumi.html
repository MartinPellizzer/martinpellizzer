Malus × zumi

Malus × zumi, commonly known as the Chinese white pear.

It is a hybrid species native to China and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy white flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

The fruit is a small, yellowish pear-shaped apple that is edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use.
