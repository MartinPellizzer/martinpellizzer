Machaerina flexuosa subsp. laevinux

Machaerina flexuosa subsp.

laevinux, commonly known as the slender machaerina, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are typically visited by insects. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
