Myrcia stemmeriana

Myrcia stemmeriana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or myrcia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
