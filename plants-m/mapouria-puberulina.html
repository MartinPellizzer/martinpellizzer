Mapouria puberulina

Mapouria puberulina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.
