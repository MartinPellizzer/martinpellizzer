Mentha longifolia var. seriata

Mentha longifolia var. seriata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive minty scent.

This variety is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its refreshing flavor and potential health benefits.
