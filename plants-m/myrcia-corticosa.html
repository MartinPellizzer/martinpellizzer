Myrcia corticosa

Myrcia corticosa, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both moist and dry conditions.

Myrcia corticosa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
