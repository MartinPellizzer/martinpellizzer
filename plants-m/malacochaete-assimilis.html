Malacochaete assimilis

Malacochaete assimilis, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, delicate leaves and airy, feather-like inflorescence that gives it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Malacochaete assimilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
