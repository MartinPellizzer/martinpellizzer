Malacothamnus fasciculatus var. laxiflorus

Malacothamnus fasciculatus var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the laxiflorous goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
