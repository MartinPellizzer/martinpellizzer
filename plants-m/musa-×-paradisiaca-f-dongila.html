Musa × paradisiaca f. dongila

Musa × paradisiaca f. dongila, commonly known as the East African plantain.

This variety is a cultivated form of the banana plant, characterized by its large, elongated leaves and robust growth habit. It produces clusters of elongated, yellowish-purple fruits that are typically harvested when mature. The plant is well adapted to tropical climates and requires ample sunlight and moisture.

It is primarily grown for its edible fruits, which are rich in carbohydrates and nutrients.
