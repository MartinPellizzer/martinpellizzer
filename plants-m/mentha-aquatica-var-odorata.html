Mentha aquatica var. odorata

Mentha aquatica var. odorata, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, square stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes, ponds, and stream edges.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers contain essential oils, making it useful in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
