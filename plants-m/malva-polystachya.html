Malva polystachya

Malva polystachya, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in soft, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically arranged in long, branched clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Malva polystachya is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
