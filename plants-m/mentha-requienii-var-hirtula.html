Mentha requienii var. hirtula

Mentha requienii var. hirtula, commonly known as the hairy mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and aromatic, often menthol-like scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
