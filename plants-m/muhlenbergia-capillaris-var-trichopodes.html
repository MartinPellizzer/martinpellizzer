Muhlenbergia capillaris var. trichopodes

Muhlenbergia capillaris var. trichopodes, commonly known as the silky muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
