Melissa officinalis var. graveolens

Melissa officinalis var. graveolens, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, serrated leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves, which contain essential oils rich in compounds like citral and geraniol. It thrives in sunny to partially shaded locations with well-drained soil.

Lemon balm is widely used in herbal medicine for its calming and soothing properties.
