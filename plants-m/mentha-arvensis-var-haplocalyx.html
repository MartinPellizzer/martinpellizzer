Mentha arvensis var. haplocalyx

Mentha arvensis var. haplocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaf field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and flowering spikes that produce small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along waterways. Its essential oils contain menthone and other compounds, making it useful in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

It is also a popular choice for culinary use, particularly in herbal teas and flavoring.
