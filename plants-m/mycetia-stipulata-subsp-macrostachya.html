Mycetia stipulata subsp. macrostachya

Mycetia stipulata subsp.

macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stalked mycetia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger floral stalks compared to other subspecies of Mycetia stipulata.
