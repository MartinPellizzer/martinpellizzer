Matthiola sinuata var. numidica

Matthiola sinuata var. numidica, commonly known as the Algerian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow or orange flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
