Millingtonia dubiosa

Millingtonia dubiosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rutaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central America and northern South America. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
