Mentha arvensis var. argutissima

Mentha arvensis var. argutissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf field mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, slightly minty aroma. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in the summer months.

This variety is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
