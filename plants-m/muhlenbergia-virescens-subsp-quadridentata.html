Muhlenbergia virescens subsp. quadridentata

Muhlenbergia virescens subsp.

quadridentata, commonly known as the purple muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of purple to pinkish flowers that appear in late summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and a distinctive, hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
