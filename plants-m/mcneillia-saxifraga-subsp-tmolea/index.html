Mcneillia Saxifraga Subsp. Tmolea

Mcneillia saxifraga subsp. tmolea is a rare and delicate plant native to the rocky habitats of southern California. It thrives in arid environments where it forms low, mat-like growths to conserve moisture. The species is known for its small, pale green leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in late spring. Due to habitat loss and climate change, it faces increasing threats to its survival. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique subspecies for future generations.
