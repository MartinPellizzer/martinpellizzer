Mesua floribunda

Mesua floribunda, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and dark green in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release flat, winged seeds.

Mesua floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
