Masdevallia biflora

Masdevallia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit and its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically produces two to three flowers per stem, which bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the base.

Masdevallia biflora thrives in cool, humid environments and requires specific light and temperature conditions for optimal growth.
