Mikania venosa

Mikania venosa, commonly known as creeping morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, alternate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Mikania venosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, often becoming invasive in certain regions.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in disturbed areas, woodland edges, and along roadsides.
