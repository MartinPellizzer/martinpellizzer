Melandrium dioicum f. glaberrimum

Melandrium dioicum f. glaberrimum, commonly known as the glabrous catchfly, is a rare and distinctive variant of the common catchfly.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its reduced leaf surface and smooth, glabrous stems, which set it apart from the typical form. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It is native to specific regions in Europe and is often found in disturbed soils or open habitats.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
