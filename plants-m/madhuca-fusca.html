Madhuca fusca

Madhuca fusca, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black, glossy fruits. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or yellowish, arranged in clusters.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible fruit.
