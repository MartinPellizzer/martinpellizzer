Momordica tuberosa

Momordica tuberosa, commonly known as the horned melon or African horned cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, green fruit that resembles a horned melon. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a climbing habit, often growing along fences or shrubs. Its fruit contains a white, fleshy pulp with black seeds, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins.
