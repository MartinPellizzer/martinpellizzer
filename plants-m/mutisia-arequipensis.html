Mutisia arequipensis

Mutisia arequipensis, commonly known as the Arequipa mutisia, is a flowering plant native to the high Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid habitats at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing bright yellow to orange ray florets surrounded by tubular disk florets.
