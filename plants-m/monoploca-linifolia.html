Monoploca linifolia

Monoploca linifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
