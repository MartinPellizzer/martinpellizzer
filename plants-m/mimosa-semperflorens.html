Mimosa semperflorens

Mimosa semperflorens, commonly known as the sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its rapid growth and easy care make it a favored ornamental plant in many regions.
