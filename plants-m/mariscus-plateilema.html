Mariscus plateilema

Mariscus plateilema, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water in harsh environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring or early summer. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access groundwater during dry periods.

Mariscus plateilema is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
