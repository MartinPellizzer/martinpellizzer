Mentha × fenzliana

Mentha × fenzliana, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a cross between Mentha longifolia (spearmint) and Mentha spicata (peppermint), resulting in a plant that exhibits characteristics of both parents. The plant has square stems, opposite leaves, and produces small, pale purple flowers. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Mentha × fenzliana is known for its strong, refreshing scent and is often used in teas, candies, and topical remedies.
