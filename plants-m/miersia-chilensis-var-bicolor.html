Miersia chilensis var. bicolor

Miersia chilensis var. bicolor, commonly known as the Chilean twin-color miersia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored foliage, with leaves that display both green and silver hues. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
