Mesembryanthemum tenellum

Mesembryanthemum tenellum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments.
