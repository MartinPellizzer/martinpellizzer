Merostachys ramosissima

Merostachys ramosissima, commonly known as the slender bamboo grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as swamps and floodplain forests, and is often found in disturbed or swampy soils.

Due to its adaptability and ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
