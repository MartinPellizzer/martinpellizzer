Manihot maguireana

Manihot maguireana, commonly known as the Guiana arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a starch source. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has alternate, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in cup-shaped structures called cyathia.

The plant is valued for its edible roots and has been used traditionally in various regions for culinary and medicinal purposes.
