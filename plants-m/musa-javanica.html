Musa javanica

Musa javanica, commonly known as the Java banana, is a species of flowering plant in the Musaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clustered fruiting structures. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed raw or used in cooking. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Musa javanica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
