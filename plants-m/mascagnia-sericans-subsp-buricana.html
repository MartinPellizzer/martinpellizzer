Mascagnia sericans subsp. buricana

Mascagnia sericans subsp.

buricana, commonly known as the Burkinabe trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to West Africa. It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has woody stems and is typically shrub-like in growth habit. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
