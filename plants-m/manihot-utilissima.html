Manihot utilissima

Manihot utilissima, commonly known as cassava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that produces edible roots and is widely cultivated for its starchy tuberous roots. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellowish flowers. Cassava is adapted to grow in poor soils and is drought-tolerant, making it an important crop in tropical regions.

Its roots are a major source of carbohydrates and are used in various food products worldwide.
