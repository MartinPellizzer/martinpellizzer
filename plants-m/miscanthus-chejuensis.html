Miscanthus chejuensis

Miscanthus chejuensis, commonly known as the Jeju silver grass, is a perennial grass native to the Korean island of Jeju.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silver-gray plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant foliage and seasonal flowering.

Miscanthus chejuensis is also valued for its potential in bioenergy production and ecological restoration projects.
