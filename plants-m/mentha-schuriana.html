Mentha schuriana

Mentha schuriana, commonly known as the Andean mint, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a strong minty scent.

Mentha schuriana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
