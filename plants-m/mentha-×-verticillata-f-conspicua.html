Mentha × verticillata f. conspicua

Mentha × verticillata f. conspicua, commonly known as the showy water mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, whorled arrangement of leaves and its showy, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
