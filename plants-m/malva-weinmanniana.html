Malva weinmanniana

Malva weinmanniana, commonly known as the Mexican mallow, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Malva weinmanniana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
