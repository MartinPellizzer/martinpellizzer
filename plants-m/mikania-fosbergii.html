Mikania fosbergii

Mikania fosbergii, commonly known as the Fosberg's mile-a-minute vine, is a fast-growing perennial vine native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged opposite each other. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its rapid growth rate and ability to spread aggressively make it an invasive species in certain areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is closely related to other species in the Mikania genus.
