Mentha aquatica var. ortmanniana-major

Mentha aquatica var. ortmanniana-major, commonly known as the water mint variant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. The plant features slender, aromatic stems that are often square in cross-section, a characteristic common among the mint family. Its lance-shaped leaves are usually hairy and have a distinct minty fragrance.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in whorls, contributing to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
