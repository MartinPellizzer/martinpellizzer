Mammillaria zepnickii

Mammillaria zepnickii, commonly known as the Zepnick's mammilla, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white, woolly spines. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
