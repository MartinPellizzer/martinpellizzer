Melica ciliata var. pallida

Melica ciliata var. pallida, commonly known as the pale silky grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silvery or pale blue-green coloration on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is characterized by its soft, silky texture and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
