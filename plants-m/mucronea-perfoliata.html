Mucronea perfoliata

Mucronea perfoliata, commonly known as the perfoliate mucronate, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique leaves that have a perfoliate arrangement, with the base of each leaf passing through the stem, creating a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Mucronea perfoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
