Malus strepens

Malus strepens, commonly known as the Chinese white apple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Malus strepens is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
