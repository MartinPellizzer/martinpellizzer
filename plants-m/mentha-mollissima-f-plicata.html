Mentha mollissima f. plicata

Mentha mollissima f. plicata, commonly known as the wrinkled mint, is a variant of the mint genus characterized by its distinctive, wrinkled leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.

The f. plicata form is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology, which sets it apart from other Mentha species.
