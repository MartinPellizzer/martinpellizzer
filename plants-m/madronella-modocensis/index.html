Madronella Modocensis

Modoc madrona, scientifically known as Madronella modocensis, is a unique and rare tree native to the Modoc Plateau in northern California. This plant thrives in the harsh, high-altitude environment characterized by extreme temperature fluctuations and nutrient-poor soils. Its distinctive appearance includes thick, flaky bark and small, evergreen leaves that help it conserve moisture. The tree plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various local wildlife species. Due to its limited range and vulnerability to environmental changes, the Modoc madrona is considered a conservation priority.
