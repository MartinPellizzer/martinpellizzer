Matricaria acutiloba

Matricaria acutiloba, commonly known as the German chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flower heads surrounded by white, velvety petals. The plant is herbaceous, with linear, aromatic leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often cultivated for its calming properties and is used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.

Matricaria acutiloba thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and medicinal cultivation.
