Monotes glandulosus

Monotes glandulosus, commonly known as the hairy monotes, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with vibrant colors that attract pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
