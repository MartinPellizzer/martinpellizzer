Malacothrix glabrata

Malacothrix glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairless. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are tubular and are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

Malacothrix glabrata is often found in dry, sandy soils and is a member of the milkweed family, Asclepiadaceae.
