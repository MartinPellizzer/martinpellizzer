Monnina intermedia

Monnina intermedia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
