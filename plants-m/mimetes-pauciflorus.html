Mimetes pauciflorus

Mimetes pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered mimic, is a South African plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces small, daisy-like flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. It is known for its ability to mimic the appearance of other plants, a trait that may aid in camouflage or pollinator attraction.

Mimetes pauciflorus is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in semi-arid regions.
