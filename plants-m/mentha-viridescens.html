Mentha viridescens

Mentha viridescens, commonly known as green mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue to purple and appear in whorls along the stem.

Mentha viridescens is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
