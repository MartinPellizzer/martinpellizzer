Mentha gentilis f. beckeriana

Mentha gentilis f. beckeriana, commonly known as the Becker's mint, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the mint family.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas or along stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
