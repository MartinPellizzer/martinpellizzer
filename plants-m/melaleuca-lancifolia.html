Melaleuca lancifolia

Melaleuca lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels away in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spikes, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, textured surface.

Melaleuca lancifolia is often found in coastal and wetland areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
