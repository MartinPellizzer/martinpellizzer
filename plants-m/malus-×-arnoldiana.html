Malus × arnoldiana

Malus × arnoldiana, commonly known as the Arnold apple tree, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Malus sylvestris and Malus baccata.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers and vibrant red or orange foliage in autumn. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the winter months. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
