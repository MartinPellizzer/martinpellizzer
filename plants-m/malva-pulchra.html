Malva pulchra

Malva pulchra, commonly known as the pretty mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Malva pulchra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
