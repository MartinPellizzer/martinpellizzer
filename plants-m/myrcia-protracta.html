Myrcia protracta

Myrcia protracta, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, red, fleshy berries. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Myrcia protracta is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world, though it can become invasive in some areas.
