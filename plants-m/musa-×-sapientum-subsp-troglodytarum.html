Musa × sapientum subsp. troglodytarum

Musa × sapientum subsp.

troglodytarum, commonly known as the cave banana, is a unique subspecies of the common banana plant. It is characterized by its robust, elongated leaves and clusters of small, ornamental fruits that resemble berries.

This subspecies is adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments, often found in tropical caves or dense undergrowth. Its fruits are typically not edible but are prized for their aesthetic appeal and ecological role.

The plant plays an important part in the biodiversity of its native habitats, supporting various insect and fungal species.
