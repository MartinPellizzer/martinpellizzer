Magnolia precia

Magnolia precia, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptic leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Magnolia precia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types in its native habitat.
