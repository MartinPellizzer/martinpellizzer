Mentha × frondosa

Mentha × frondosa, commonly known as catnip, is a hybrid mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Mentha × frondosa is known for its aromatic properties, with a strong, camphor-like scent due to the presence of nepetalactone.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
