Myriolepis diffusa

Myriolepis diffusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical perennial plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Myriolepis diffusa is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth rate, making it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
