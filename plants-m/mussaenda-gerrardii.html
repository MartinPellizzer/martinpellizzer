Mussaenda gerrardii

Mussaenda gerrardii, commonly known as the red trumpet bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly glossy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Mussaenda gerrardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
