Malus ioensis var. typica

Malus ioensis var. typica, commonly known as the Iowa serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically pink to red in color, appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and wildlife benefits.

The Iowa serviceberry is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
