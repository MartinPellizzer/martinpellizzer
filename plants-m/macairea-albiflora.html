Macairea albiflora

Macairea albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered macairea, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy as well. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
