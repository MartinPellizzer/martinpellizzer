Morus venassainii

Morus venassainii, commonly known as the Vietnamese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fuzzy fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It plays a significant role in local ecosystems and is also cultivated for its fibrous bark.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as feed for silkworms.
