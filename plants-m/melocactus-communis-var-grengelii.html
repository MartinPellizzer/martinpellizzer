Melocactus communis var. grengelii

Melocactus communis var. grengelii, commonly known as the Grengel's melon cactus, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, ribbed stem that resembles a melon, with a distinctive greenish-gray color and a spiny crown. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warm season, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by collectors and botanists alike.
