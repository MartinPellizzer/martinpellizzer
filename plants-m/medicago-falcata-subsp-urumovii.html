Medicago falcata subsp. urumovii

Medicago falcata subsp.

urumovii, commonly known as the narrowleaf alfalfa, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive narrow shape.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its forage quality and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
