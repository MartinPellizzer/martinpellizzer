Matricaria rigida

Matricaria rigida, commonly known as rigid chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and white ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has a bushy, compact growth habit. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in late summer.

Matricaria rigida is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
