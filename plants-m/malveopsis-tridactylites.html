Malveopsis tridactylites

Malveopsis tridactylites, commonly known as the three-toed mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive three-toed appearance on its leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
