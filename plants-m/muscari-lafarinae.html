Muscari lafarinae

Muscari lafarinae, commonly known as the Farina grape hyacinth, is a species of bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces a spherical bulb that is used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Muscari lafarinae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
