Momordica multicrenulata

Momordica multicrenulata, commonly known as the warty bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces distinctive, warty, pale green fruits that turn yellow when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with wavy edges, and it has tendrils that help it climb.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in traditional cuisines and have medicinal properties.
