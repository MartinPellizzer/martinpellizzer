Milium alternans

Milium alternans, commonly known as the alternate millet, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, white to pale green flowers that are arranged in clusters, typical of the Poaceae family. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
