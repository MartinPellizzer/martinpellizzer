Mammillaria sulcolanata

Mammillaria sulcolanata, commonly known as the crested milk barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered with white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
