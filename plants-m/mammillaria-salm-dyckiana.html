Mammillaria salm-dyckiana

Mammillaria salm-dyckiana, commonly known as the Salm-Dyck mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Mammillaria salm-dyckiana is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
