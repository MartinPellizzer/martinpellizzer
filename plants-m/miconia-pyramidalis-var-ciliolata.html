Miconia pyramidalis var. ciliolata

Miconia pyramidalis var. ciliolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions make it an invasive species in some regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires careful management to prevent ecological disruption.
