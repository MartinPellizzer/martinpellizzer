Melissa grandiflora

Melissa grandiflora, commonly known as lemon balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often fragrant and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Melissa grandiflora is valued for its calming properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
