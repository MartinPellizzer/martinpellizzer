Mammillaria centricirrha f. diadema

Mammillaria centricirrha f. diadema, commonly known as the Diadema mammillaria, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
