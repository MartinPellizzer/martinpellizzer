Microchilus callophylloides

Microchilus callophylloides, commonly known as the red-stemmed greenbriar, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features woody stems that are often reddish in color, covered with small, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
