Mitopetalum plicatum

Mitopetalum plicatum, commonly known as the pleated mitopetalum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pleated leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
