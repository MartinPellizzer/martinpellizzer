Mammillaria irregularis

Mammillaria irregularis, commonly known as the irregular mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in the spring.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
