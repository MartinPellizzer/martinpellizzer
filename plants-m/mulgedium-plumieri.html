Mulgedium plumieri

Mulgedium plumieri, commonly known as the silverleaf mulgedium, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Mulgedium plumieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
