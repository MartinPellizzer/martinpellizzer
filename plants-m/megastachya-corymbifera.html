Megastachya corymbifera

Megastachya corymbifera, commonly known as the clustered spike grass, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to brownish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a wetland indicator species and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
