Monochaetum vulcanicum f. typicum

Monochaetum vulcanicum f. typicum, commonly known as the Vulcan Island grass, is a rare and endemic plant species found exclusively on the island of Vulcano in Italy.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like structure and slender, erect growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a fibrous texture and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Due to its restricted habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts to protect its unique ecological niche.
