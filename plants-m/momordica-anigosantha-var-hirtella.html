Momordica anigosantha var. hirtella

Momordica anigosantha var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy bitter melon, is a variant of the bitter melon species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, oblong fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. Its fruit contains numerous seeds enclosed in a fibrous, ridged rind.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in various traditional cuisines and traditional medicine practices.
