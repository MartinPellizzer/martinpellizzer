Moehringia ciliata f. polygonoides

Moehringia ciliata f. polygonoides, commonly known as the polygon-like moehringia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its finely toothed leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or stream banks. Its leaves are often covered with fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy texture.

The inflorescence consists of clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, contributing to its ornamental value in garden settings.
