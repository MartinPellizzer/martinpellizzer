Mikania phyllopoda

Mikania phyllopoda, commonly known as the giant flame vine, is a fast-growing climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to spread quickly, making it both a popular ornamental and a potential invasive species. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a fibrous root system.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forest edges.
