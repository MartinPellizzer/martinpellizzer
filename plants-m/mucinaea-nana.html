Mucinaea nana

Mucinaea nana, commonly known as the dwarf mucinaea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with delicate, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which appear in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.

Mucinaea nana is known for its low-growing habit and is relatively uncommon in the wild, making it a sought-after species for botanical gardens.
