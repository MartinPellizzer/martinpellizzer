Melica cretica subsp. monticola

Melica cretica subsp.

monticola, commonly known as mountain false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in mountainous and alpine habitats.

The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
