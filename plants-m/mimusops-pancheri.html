Mimusops pancheri

Mimusops pancheri, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests and dry woodlands.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
