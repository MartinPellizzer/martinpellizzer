Mentha longifolia f. candicans

Mentha longifolia f. candicans, commonly known as lemon balm, is a cultivar of the mint family (Lamiaceae) characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers, which appear in clusters during the summer months. Its foliage is used in herbal teas and aromatherapy for its calming properties.

The leaves are also employed in culinary applications, adding a subtle citrus flavor to dishes and beverages.
