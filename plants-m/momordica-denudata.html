Momordica denudata

Momordica denudata, commonly known as the naked fruit cucumber, is a climbing vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to elongated, spiny fruits resembling cucumbers. Its fruit is typically green when immature and turns orange-red when mature, though it is rarely consumed by humans.

The plant is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
