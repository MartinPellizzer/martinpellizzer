Macrocarpaea macrophylla

Macrocarpaea macrophylla, commonly known as the giant macrocarpa, is a striking evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, giving it a dramatic, tropical appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiky red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a specimen plant in temperate climates.
