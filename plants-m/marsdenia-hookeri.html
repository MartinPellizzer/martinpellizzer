Marsdenia hookeri

Marsdenia hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features slender, climbing stems and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Marsdenia hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
