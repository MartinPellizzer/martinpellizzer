Miconia axillaris subsp. urbaniana

Miconia axillaris subsp.

urbaniana, commonly known as the urbaniana miconia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forest environments.

The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that often have a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale pink, and it produces berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The species is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
