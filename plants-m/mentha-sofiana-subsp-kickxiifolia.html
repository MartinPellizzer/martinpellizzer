Mentha sofiana subsp. kickxiifolia

Mentha sofiana subsp.

kickxiifolia, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glandular-dotted.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of white to purple, typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in traditional herbal practices.
