Macroplectrum xylopus

Macroplectrum xylopus, commonly known as the African arrowroot.

This plant is a member of the Costaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces large, paddle-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is valued for its starchy rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
