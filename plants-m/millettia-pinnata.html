Millettia pinnata

Millettia pinnata, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its long, twining stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Millettia pinnata is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
