Musa griersonii

Musa griersonii, commonly known as the Giant Banana Plant, is a rare and striking member of the Musaceae family.

Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it is characterized by its massive, elongated leaves that can reach lengths of over 5 meters. The plant produces large, yellow-orange berries that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use. Its rapid growth and unique appearance make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
