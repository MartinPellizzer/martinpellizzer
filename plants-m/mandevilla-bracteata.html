Mandevilla bracteata

Mandevilla bracteata, commonly known as the flame vine, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from orange to red. The plant produces bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for petals, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a climbing plant or in containers.

Mandevilla bracteata requires well-draining soil and regular watering to flourish.
