Malus roris

Malus roris, commonly known as the cloud apple or snow apple, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible, sweet and tart apples that are typically small in size. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and unique fruiting habit.
