Mariscus neglectus

Mariscus neglectus, commonly known as the neglected mariscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of the Mediterranean.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant features small, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, appearing in clusters during the spring or early summer.

Mariscus neglectus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
