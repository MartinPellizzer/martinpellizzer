Melochia pyramidata var. pseudotomentosa

Melochia pyramidata var. pseudotomentosa, commonly known as the silky melochia, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by fleshy, red or orange berries that are edible. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
