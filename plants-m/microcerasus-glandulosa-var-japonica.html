Microcerasus glandulosa var. japonica

Microcerasus glandulosa var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often glandular-dotted, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture and ecological landscaping.
