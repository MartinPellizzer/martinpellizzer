Myristica simulans

Myristica simulans, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries containing the seeds used for nutmeg and mace. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like myrcene and eugenol, contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and is cultivated for its valuable spice products.
