Mollinedia clavigera

Mollinedia clavigera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mollinedia clavigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
