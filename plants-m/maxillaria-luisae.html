Maxillaria luisae

Maxillaria luisae, commonly known as the Luisa's orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces dark green, velvety leaves and has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers, which can be pink, purple, or white, bloom in clusters and are often adorned with intricate patterns.

Maxillaria luisae is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
