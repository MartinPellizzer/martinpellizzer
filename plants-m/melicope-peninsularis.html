Melicope peninsularis

Melicope peninsularis, commonly known as the Florida melicope, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Bahamas.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal hammocks and wetlands. The plant has simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, white flowers are followed by small, round fruits that are initially green and turn red when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
