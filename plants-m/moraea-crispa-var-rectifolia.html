Moraea crispa var. rectifolia

Moraea crispa var. rectifolia, commonly known as the straight-leaved moraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is a popular choice among horticultural enthusiasts.
