Medinilla balakrishnanii

Medinilla balakrishnanii, commonly known as the Balakrishnan's Medinilla, is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is native to the Philippines.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a distinctive tubular shape and prominent stamens. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
