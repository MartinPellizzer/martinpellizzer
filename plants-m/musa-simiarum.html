Musa simiarum

Musa simiarum, commonly known as the banana plant, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and a pseudostem formed by tightly packed leaf sheaths. The plant produces clusters of flowers that develop into the familiar yellow fruit, which is rich in potassium and other nutrients. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The banana plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been widely cultivated for both food and commercial purposes.
