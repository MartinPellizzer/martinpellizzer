Myrmecodia apoensis

Myrmecodia apoensis, commonly known as the ant plant, is a unique epiphytic species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its swollen, tree-like stems that form a hollow, chamber-like structure. The plant hosts colonies of ants within its hollow cavities, which provide protection and nutrients in exchange for shelter. Its leaves are small, leathery, and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Myrmecodia apoensis is also notable for its ability to produce small, fragrant flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
