Miltitzia scopulina

Miltitzia scopulina, commonly known as the rock miltitzia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and maritime hammocks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture and produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Miltitzia scopulina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
