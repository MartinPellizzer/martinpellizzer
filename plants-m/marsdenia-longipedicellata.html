Marsdenia longipedicellata

Marsdenia longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-fruited Marsdenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, elliptic leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are typically small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a follicle containing numerous seeds.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
