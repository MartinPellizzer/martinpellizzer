Mesembryanthemum abbreviatum

Mesembryanthemum abbreviatum, commonly known as the dwarf ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and yellow, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
