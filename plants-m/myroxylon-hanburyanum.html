Myroxylon hanburyanum

Myroxylon hanburyanum, commonly known as the African teak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
