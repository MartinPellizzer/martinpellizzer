Matucana ritteri

Matucana ritteri, commonly known as the Ritter's matucana, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and clusters of small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green, rugged appearance. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in desert gardens.
