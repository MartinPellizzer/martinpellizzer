Mesembryanthemum pilosum

Mesembryanthemum pilosum, commonly known as the woolly ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves covered in fine, white hairs that give it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Mesembryanthemum pilosum is also known for its ability to store water in its leaves, allowing it to survive in arid environments.
