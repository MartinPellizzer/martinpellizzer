Millingtonia simplicifolia

Millingtonia simplicifolia, commonly known as the white cedar, is a small evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by round, red berries that attract various bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
