Mesembryanthemum glaucoides

Mesembryanthemum glaucoides, commonly known as the silverleaf iceplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to many landscapes.
