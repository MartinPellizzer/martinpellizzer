Mentha × suavis var. maximilianea

Mentha × suavis var. maximilianea, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to gardens. It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it grows in open, sunny areas.

Its foliage is often used in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb due to its pleasant aroma and medicinal properties.
