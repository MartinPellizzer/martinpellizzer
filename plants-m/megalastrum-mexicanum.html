Megalastrum mexicanum

Megalastrum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers, pumpkins, and gourds. The plant produces small, yellow, star-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when they are still immature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
