Medinilla pulleana

Medinilla pulleana, commonly known as the velvet tangerine.

It is a flowering plant native to the Philippines, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, orange fruits that are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Medinilla pulleana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
