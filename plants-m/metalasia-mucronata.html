Metalasia mucronata

Metalasia mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and hardy nature make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
