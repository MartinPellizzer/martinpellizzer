Mascagnia macradena

Mascagnia macradena, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Mascagnia macradena is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.
