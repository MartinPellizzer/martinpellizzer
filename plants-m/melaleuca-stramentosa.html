Melaleuca stramentosa

Melaleuca stramentosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels away in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or yellowish flowers in clusters, which appear during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, contributing to its ornamental value.

Melaleuca stramentosa is typically found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
