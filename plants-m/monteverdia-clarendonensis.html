Monteverdia clarendonensis

Monteverdia clarendonensis, commonly known as the Clarendon mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Caribbean, specifically Jamaica.

It grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus *Piper*, and relies on its host for water and nutrients while conducting photosynthesis. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are dispersed by birds. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Monteverdia clarendonensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution.
