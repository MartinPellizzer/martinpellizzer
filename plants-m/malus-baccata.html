Malus baccata

Malus baccata, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental fruits that range in color from yellow to red. The tree produces white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by the edible berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and turn vibrant shades of red in autumn.

Malus baccata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
