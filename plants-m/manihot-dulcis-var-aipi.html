Manihot dulcis var. aipi

Manihot dulcis var. aipi, commonly known as the aipi cassava, is a variant of the cassava plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its high starch content and relatively low levels of cyanogenic glycosides compared to other cassava varieties. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a root that is typically white or pale yellow in color. It is cultivated for its edible roots, which are used in a variety of culinary preparations.

The plant requires warm, humid conditions and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
