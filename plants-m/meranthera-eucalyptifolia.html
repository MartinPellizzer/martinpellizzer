Meranthera eucalyptifolia

Meranthera eucalyptifolia, commonly known as the eucalyptus leaf meranthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of eucalyptus trees. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its unique foliage and subtle floral display make it an attractive addition to native plant collections.
