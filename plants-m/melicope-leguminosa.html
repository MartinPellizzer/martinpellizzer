Melicope leguminosa

Melicope leguminosa, commonly known as the hairy melicope, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
