Muraltia oxysepala

Muraltia oxysepala, commonly known as the sour fig or wild fig, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically sour and acidic in taste. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Muraltia oxysepala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
