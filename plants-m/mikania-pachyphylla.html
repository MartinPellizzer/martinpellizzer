Mikania pachyphylla

Mikania pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a rapid growth rate and can become invasive in certain environments. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its fibrous stems and persistent foliage make it a notable species in ecological studies.
