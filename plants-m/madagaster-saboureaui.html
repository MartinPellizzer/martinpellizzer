Madagaster saboureaui

Madagaster saboureaui, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It features slender, woody stems that twine around supporting structures as it grows. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and endemic status.
