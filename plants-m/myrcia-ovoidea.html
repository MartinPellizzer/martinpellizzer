Myrcia ovoidea

Myrcia ovoidea, commonly known as the oval-leaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered together. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Myrcia ovoidea is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
