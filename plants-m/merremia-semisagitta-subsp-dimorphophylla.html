Merremia semisagitta subsp. dimorphophylla

Merremia semisagitta subsp.

dimorphophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can vary in size and shape, giving the plant its subspecies name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks and in disturbed soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
