Myriocladus virgatus

Myriocladus virgatus, commonly known as the desert holly, is a succulent shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a compact, sprawling growth habit with fleshy, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring, which are followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Myriocladus virgatus is well adapted to harsh desert environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
