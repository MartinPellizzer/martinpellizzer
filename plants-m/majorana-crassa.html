Majorana crassa

Majorana crassa, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Majorana crassa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
