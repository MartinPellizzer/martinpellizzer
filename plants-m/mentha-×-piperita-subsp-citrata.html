Mentha × piperita subsp. citrata

Mentha × piperita subsp.

citrata, commonly known as lemon balm, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, shady environments.

The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from white to pale yellow.

Lemon balm is widely cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its calming properties.
