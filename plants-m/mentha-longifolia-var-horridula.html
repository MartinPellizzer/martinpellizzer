Mentha longifolia var. horridula

Mentha longifolia var. horridula, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and square, erect stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a strong minty scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.
