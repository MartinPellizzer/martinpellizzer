Maltea

Maltea, commonly known as the Maltese fig, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ficus and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered fruits. The plant produces a unique fruit that is often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a tendency to grow as a shrub or small tree.

The Maltese fig is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
