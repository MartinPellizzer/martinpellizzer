Marattia raddii var. juergensii

Marattia raddii var. juergensii, commonly known as the Juergens' cliff fern, is a rare and striking variety of the marattia family.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
