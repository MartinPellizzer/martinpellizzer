Melica ciliata f. villiculmis

Melica ciliata f. villiculmis, commonly known as the silky beardgrass, is a grass species characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and distinctive inflorescence.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are elongated with a dense covering of short, soft hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.
