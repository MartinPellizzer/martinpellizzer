Mentha × dumetorum var. grantzowii

Mentha × dumetorum var. grantzowii, commonly known as "Grantzow's mint," is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically arranged in whorls along the stem. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
