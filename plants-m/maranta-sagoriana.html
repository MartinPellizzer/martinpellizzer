Maranta sagoriana

Maranta sagoriana, commonly known as the "arrowroot plant," is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaf patterns that often feature red, purple, and green hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch, similar to arrowroot.

Maranta sagoriana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, fertile soil for optimal growth.
