Maxillaria maldonadoensis

Maxillaria maldonadoensis, commonly known as the Maldonado's maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia and Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers with a unique coloration pattern. The plant typically features dark green, velvety leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and fragrance.
