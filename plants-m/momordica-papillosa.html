Momordica papillosa

Momordica papillosa, commonly known as the hairy bitter gourd, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and hairy, elongated fruits that typically measure between 15 to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the fruit has a bitter taste due to the presence of cucurbitacins.

This species is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various traditional cuisines.
