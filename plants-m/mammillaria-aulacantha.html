Mammillaria aulacantha

Mammillaria aulacantha, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring closely spaced, sharp tubercles that give it a spiky, hedgehog-like appearance. The plant typically has pale green to blue-green stems and produces yellow flowers during the spring or early summer.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
