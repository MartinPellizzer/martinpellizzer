Moraea angusta

Moraea angusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf moraea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bloom period occurs in late winter to early spring, producing vibrant purple or pink flowers.

Moraea angusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
