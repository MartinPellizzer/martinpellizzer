Miconia quadrifolia

Miconia quadrifolia, commonly known as the four-leaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers with five petals, which are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous small seeds.

Miconia quadrifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and floral display.
