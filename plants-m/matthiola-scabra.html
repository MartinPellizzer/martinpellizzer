Matthiola scabra

Matthiola scabra, commonly known as the rough stocky mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its branched stems, alternate leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Matthiola scabra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
