Matayba retusa

Matayba retusa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum retusum*, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Erythroxylaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by orange-red berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.

Matayba retusa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
