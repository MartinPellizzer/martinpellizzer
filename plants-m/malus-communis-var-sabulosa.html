Malus communis var. sabulosa

Malus communis var. sabulosa, commonly known as the sand apple or sand pear, is a variant of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is well adapted to sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically not eaten by humans but are used in traditional medicine. It is native to regions with arid or semi-arid climates, such as parts of Central Asia and the Middle East.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
