Machaerium allemanii var. latifolium

Machaerium allemanii var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
