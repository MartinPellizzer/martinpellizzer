Mentha aquatica f. latiovalis

Mentha aquatica f. latiovalis, commonly known as the broad-leaf water mint, is a variant of the water mint plant native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a distinct minty aroma. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in environments with high humidity. Its flowers are small, pale blue or purple, and clustered in whorls along the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
