Matelea aristolochiifolia

Matelea aristolochiifolia, commonly known as the Mexican Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family, characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that resemble a Dutchman's pipe. The plant has heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces showy, hooded flowers that are often greenish or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
