Malva × egarensis

Malva × egarensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pale pink to mauve flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in warm, sunny environments with well-drained soil.

Malva × egarensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
