Mertensia hookeri

Mertensia hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Mertensia hookeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-shaped blooms.
