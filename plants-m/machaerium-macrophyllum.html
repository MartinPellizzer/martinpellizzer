Machaerium macrophyllum

Machaerium macrophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaf machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South and Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features prominently veined, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Machaerium macrophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
