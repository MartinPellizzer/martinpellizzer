Milium paradoxum

Milium paradoxum, commonly known as the common millet, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale green flowers. Its seeds are often used as a grain in traditional cuisines and have been cultivated for thousands of years.

Milium paradoxum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown in gardens for its delicate appearance.
