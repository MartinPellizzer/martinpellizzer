Medicago rotata subsp. bonarotiana

Medicago rotata subsp.

bonarotiana, commonly known as the Bonarotian goat's rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces delicate, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
