Melissa cretica

Melissa cretica, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain essential oils, including nepetalactone, which is responsible for its distinctive scent and effects on animals.

Melissa cretica is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
