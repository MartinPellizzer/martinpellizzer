Muscaria helviensis

Muscaria helviensis, commonly known as the Swiss fly agaric, is a species of psychedelic mushroom native to Europe.

It belongs to the genus *Muscaria* and is characterized by its white, bulbous cap and a distinctive, often reddish stem. The fruiting bodies typically appear in woodlands and grassy areas, often in association with certain trees. Its spores are released in a cloud-like pattern, contributing to its spread.

The mushroom contains psychoactive compounds, making it both a subject of scientific interest and a plant with cultural significance.
