Maripa longifolia

Maripa longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf maripa, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Maripa longifolia is often found in dry forest and savanna habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
