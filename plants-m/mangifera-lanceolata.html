Mangifera lanceolata

Mangifera lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mango, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible mango fruits that are typically smaller and less sweet compared to other mango varieties. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
