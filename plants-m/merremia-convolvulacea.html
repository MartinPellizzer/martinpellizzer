Merremia convolvulacea

Merremia convolvulacea, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Merremia convolvulacea is valued for its ornamental appeal but can become invasive in certain environments.
