Myrica esculenta var. chevalieri

Myrica esculenta var. chevalieri, commonly known as the Chevalier's wax myrtle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, waxy berries that are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in traditional medicine.
