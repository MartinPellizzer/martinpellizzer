Myrcia hernandezii

Myrcia hernandezii, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, with dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Myrcia hernandezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions, though it can become invasive in some areas.
