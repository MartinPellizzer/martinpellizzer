Maclura javanica

Maclura javanica, commonly known as the Javan yellow wood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and yellowish to brownish-gray stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Maclura javanica is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
