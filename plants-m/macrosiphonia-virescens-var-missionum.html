Macrosiphonia virescens var. missionum

Macrosiphonia virescens var. missionum, commonly known as the Mission fig, is a perennial shrub native to southern California.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
