Monotassa secunda

Monotassa secunda, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, fluffy flowers that appear in late spring, resembling small clouds. The plant has pinnately compound leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Monotassa secunda is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
