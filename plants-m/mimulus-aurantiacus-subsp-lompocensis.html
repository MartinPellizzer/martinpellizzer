Mimulus aurantiacus subsp. lompocensis

Mimulus aurantiacus subsp.

lompocensis, commonly known as the Lompoc monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to California. It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers and heart-shaped leaves.

This subspecies thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats. It blooms from spring to summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered plant species.
