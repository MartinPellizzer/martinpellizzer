Mammillaria glassii var. nominis-dulcis

Mammillaria glassii var. nominis-dulcis, commonly known as the sweet name mammillaria, is a compact cactus with a globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale yellow spines.

It originates from the arid regions of Mexico, where it thrives in full sun and well-draining soil. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It is relatively easy to care for, requiring minimal water and moderate light conditions.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
