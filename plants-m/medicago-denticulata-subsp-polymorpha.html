Medicago denticulata subsp. polymorpha

Medicago denticulata subsp.

polymorpha, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant is a legume, capable of nitrogen fixation through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Its ecological importance includes soil enrichment and serving as a forage crop for livestock.
