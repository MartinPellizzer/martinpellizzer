Maytenus tunarina

Maytenus tunarina, commonly known as the Tuna tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, tropical climates. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a small, round drupe that changes color as it matures.

Maytenus tunarina is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
