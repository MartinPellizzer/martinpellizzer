Mitrephora caudata

Mitrephora caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed mitrephora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
