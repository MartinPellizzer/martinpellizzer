Mesosetum pittieri

Mesosetum pittieri, commonly known as the Pittier's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often branched and bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib and a fibrous texture.

Mesosetum pittieri plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
