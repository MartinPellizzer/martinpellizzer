Melandrium transalpinum

Melandrium transalpinum, commonly known as the Transalpine catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Melandrium transalpinum is known for its short flowering period, usually occurring in late spring to early summer.
