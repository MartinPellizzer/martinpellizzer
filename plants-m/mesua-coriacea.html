Mesua coriacea

Mesua coriacea, commonly known as the Indian rose or coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Mesua coriacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
