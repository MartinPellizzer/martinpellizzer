Mentha arvensis var. lancifolia

Mentha arvensis var. lancifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters during the summer months. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it a valuable plant in both traditional medicine and culinary applications.
