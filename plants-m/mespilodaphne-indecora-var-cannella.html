Mespilodaphne indecora var. cannella

Mespilodaphne indecora var. cannella, commonly known as the slender tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
