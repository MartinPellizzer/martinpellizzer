Maschalocorymbus corymbosus

Maschalocorymbus corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered corymbous heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.
