Mimulus lewisii f. tetonensis

Mimulus lewisii f. tetonensis, commonly known as the Teton monkeyflower, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with striking, showy orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
