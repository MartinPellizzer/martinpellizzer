Mammillaria heeseana

Mammillaria heeseana, commonly known as the Heese's nipple cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinct, spiny appearance, featuring short, white spines that give it a unique look. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
