Myrceugenia bracteosa var. fuliginea

Myrceugenia bracteosa var. fuliginea, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a species native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
