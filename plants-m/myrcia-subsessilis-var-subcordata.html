Myrcia subsessilis var. subcordata

Myrcia subsessilis var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. These flowers are typically visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological role in the ecosystem.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional Indigenous practices.
