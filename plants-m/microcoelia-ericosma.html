Microcoelia ericosma

Microcoelia ericosma, commonly known as the red sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to thrive in well-drained sandy soils.

Microcoelia ericosma is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
