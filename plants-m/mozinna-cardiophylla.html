Mozinna cardiophylla

Mozinna cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Mozinna cardiophylla thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
