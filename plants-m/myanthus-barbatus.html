Myanthus barbatus

Myanthus barbatus, commonly known as the bearded myanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, bristly appearance, particularly on its stems and flower clusters.
