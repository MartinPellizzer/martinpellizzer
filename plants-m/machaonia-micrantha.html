Machaonia micrantha

Machaonia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered machaonia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact shrub form. The plant features small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture.

Machaonia micrantha is often found in damp, shaded areas of forest understories, thriving in cool, moist environments.
