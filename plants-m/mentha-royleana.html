Mentha royleana

Mentha royleana, commonly known as Indian mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Mentha royleana is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Its growth habit includes spreading via rhizomes, making it a hardy and adaptable species in various soil types.
