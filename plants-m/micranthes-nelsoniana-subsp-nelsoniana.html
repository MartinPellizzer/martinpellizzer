Micranthes nelsoniana subsp. nelsoniana

Micranthes nelsoniana subsp.

nelsoniana, commonly known as Nelson's micranthes, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and arranged in a basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
