Myrceugenia nothorufa var. major

Myrceugenia nothorufa var. major, commonly known as the greater myrtle, is a hybrid plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and natural habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique botanical features, it is often studied for its ecological and horticultural significance.
