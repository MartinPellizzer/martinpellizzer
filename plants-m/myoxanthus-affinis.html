Myoxanthus affinis

Myoxanthus affinis, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
