Masdevallia filamentosa

Masdevallia filamentosa, commonly known as the filamentous masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescence and intricate, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Masdevallia filamentosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique growth habits.
