Monophyllaea glauca

Monophyllaea glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-blue underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Monophyllaea glauca is often used in native plant gardens for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
