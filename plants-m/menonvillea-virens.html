Menonvillea virens

Menonvillea virens, commonly known as the green-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and a network of rhizomes that help anchor it in moist soil.

Menonvillea virens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
