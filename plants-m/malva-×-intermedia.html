Malva × intermedia

Malva × intermedia, commonly known as the common mallow, is a hybrid species within the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe but has been naturalized in various regions around the world. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and along waterways.

Its fibrous stems and hairy foliage contribute to its distinctive botanical appearance.
