Miconia picinguabensis

Miconia picinguabensis, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Melastomataceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, winged seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
