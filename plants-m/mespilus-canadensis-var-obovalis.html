Mespilus canadensis var. obovalis

Mespilus canadensis var. obovalis, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a yellowish color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat benefits.
