Matthiola alyssifolia

Matthiola alyssifolia, commonly known as the alpine stock or alpine wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Matthiola alyssifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
