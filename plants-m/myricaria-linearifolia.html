Myricaria linearifolia

Myricaria linearifolia, commonly known as the linearleaf myricacia, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Myricaria linearifolia is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
