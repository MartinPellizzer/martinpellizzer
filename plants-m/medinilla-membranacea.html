Medinilla membranacea

Medinilla membranacea, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Medinilla membranacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
