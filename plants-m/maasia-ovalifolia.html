Maasia ovalifolia

Maasia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf maasia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often clustered in whorls, with colors ranging from white to pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
