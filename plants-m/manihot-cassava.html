Manihot cassava

Manihot cassava, commonly known as yuca, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is cultivated for its starchy, edible roots. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green flowers. Cassava is highly adaptable and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its roots are a vital food source in many tropical regions around the world.
