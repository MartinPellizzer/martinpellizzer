Monticalia romeroana

Monticalia romeroana, commonly known as the Colombian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in its native habitat.

Monticalia romeroana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
