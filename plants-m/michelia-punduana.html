Michelia punduana

Michelia punduana, commonly known as the Punduana magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, often peeling in thin flakes.

Michelia punduana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
