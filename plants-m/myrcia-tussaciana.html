Myrcia tussaciana

Myrcia tussaciana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Myrcia tussaciana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
