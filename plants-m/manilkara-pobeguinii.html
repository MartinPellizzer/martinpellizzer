Manilkara pobeguinii

Manilkara pobeguinii, commonly known as the Guianese sapodilla, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a fruit similar to the common sapodilla, with a thick, sweet pulp and a hard, starchy interior. Its latex is used in traditional medicine and for making chewing gum.

The plant is also valued for its durable wood and ecological importance in its native habitats.
