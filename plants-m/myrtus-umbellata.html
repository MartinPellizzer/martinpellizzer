Myrtus umbellata

Myrtus umbellata, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a dark blue berry that develops from the flowers and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
