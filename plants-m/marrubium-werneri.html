Marrubium werneri

Marrubium werneri, commonly known as Werneri horehound, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

It has a strong, aromatic scent and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
