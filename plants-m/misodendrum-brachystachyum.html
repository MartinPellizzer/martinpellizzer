Misodendrum brachystachyum

Misodendrum brachystachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It has small, leathery leaves and produces tiny, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in short inflorescences. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the fleshy berries.

Due to its parasitic nature and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
