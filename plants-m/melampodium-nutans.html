Melampodium nutans

Melampodium nutans, commonly known as the nodding sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with red centers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical name reflects its drooping flower heads, with "nutans" meaning bending or nodding.
