Monochaetum coronatum

Monochaetum coronatum, commonly known as the crowned grass or mountain grass, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive crown of leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at high elevations. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale green flowers.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
