Mirabilis multiflora var. multiflora

Mirabilis multiflora var. multiflora, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly in the use of its seeds for oil extraction.
