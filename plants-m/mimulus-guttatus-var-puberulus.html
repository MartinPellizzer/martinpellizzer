Mimulus guttatus var. puberulus

Mimulus guttatus var. puberulus, commonly known as the hairy yellow water lily, is a variant of the common monkeyflower.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to western North America, typically found in moist or aquatic environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its flowers are bright yellow with a distinctive hooded structure, and they bloom from spring to summer.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
