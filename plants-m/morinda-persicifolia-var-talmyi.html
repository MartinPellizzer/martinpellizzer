Morinda persicifolia var. talmyi

Morinda persicifolia var. talmyi, commonly known as the Talmy's redroot, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are often reddish in color, especially when young. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
