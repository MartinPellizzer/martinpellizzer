Monarda fruticulosa

Monarda fruticulosa, commonly known as scarlet beebrush, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and a square stem, typical of the Lamiaceae family.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.
