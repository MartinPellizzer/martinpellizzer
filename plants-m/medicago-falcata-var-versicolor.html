Medicago falcata var. versicolor

Medicago falcata var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable sickle vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its seed pods are slender and curved, containing several small, dark seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
