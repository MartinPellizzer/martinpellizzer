Mallotus eriocarpoides

Mallotus eriocarpoides, commonly known as the hairy-fruited mallot, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by distinctive, hairy, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
