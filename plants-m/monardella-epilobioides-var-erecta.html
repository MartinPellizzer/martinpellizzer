Monardella epilobioides var. erecta

Monardella epilobioides var. erecta, commonly known as tall false foxglove, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, bearing clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is adapted to grow in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
