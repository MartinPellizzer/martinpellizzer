Mussaenda spinosa

Mussaenda spinosa, commonly known as the spiny Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellowish-white flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Mussaenda spinosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
