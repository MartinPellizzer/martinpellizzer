Malaxis grandiflora

Malaxis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically pale green or yellowish with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Malaxis grandiflora thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
