Morinda titanophylla

Morinda titanophylla, commonly known as the giant leaf morinda, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, fertile soil.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
