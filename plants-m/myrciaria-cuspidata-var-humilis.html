Myrciaria cuspidata var. humilis

Myrciaria cuspidata var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and white, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its fruit, a small drupe, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor, making it valuable in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
