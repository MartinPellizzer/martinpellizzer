Macrozamia eximia

Macrozamia eximia, commonly known as the Exquisite cycad, is a species native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces distinctive cones, with male and female cones typically growing on separate plants. It is a slow-growing, long-lived species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
