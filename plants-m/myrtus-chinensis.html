Myrtus chinensis

Myrtus chinensis, commonly known as Chinese myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant and have a slightly leathery texture. Its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, dark blue berries.

Myrtus chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
