Mimosa sepiaria

Mimosa sepiaria, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it both ecologically and agriculturally significant.
