Mesembryanthemum pilifolium

Mesembryanthemum pilifolium, commonly known as the woolly iceplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant's ability to store water in its leaves helps it survive in arid environments.
