Melandrium eriocalycinum var. persicum

Melandrium eriocalycinum var. persicum, commonly known as Persian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in rocky or arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
