Mimosa scorpioides

Mimosa scorpioides, commonly known as the stinkbush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white seed pods that resemble cotton, which are a distinctive botanical feature. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swamps and marshes.

Mimosa scorpioides is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is released when the leaves are touched.
