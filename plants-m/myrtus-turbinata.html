Myrtus turbinata

Myrtus turbinata, commonly known as the turbinated myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, dark berries that mature in late summer.

Myrtus turbinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
