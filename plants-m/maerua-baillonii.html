Maerua baillonii

Maerua baillonii, commonly known as the African thornbush, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with poor soil conditions.

Maerua baillonii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert animals.
