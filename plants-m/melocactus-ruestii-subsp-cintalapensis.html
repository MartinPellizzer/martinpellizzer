Melocactus ruestii subsp. cintalapensis

Melocactus ruestii subsp.

cintalapensis, commonly known as the Cintalapa melocactus, is a species native to southern Mexico. It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, pale green stem and lack of ribs.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
