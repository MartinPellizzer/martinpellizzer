Melica minuta subsp. latifolia

Melica minuta subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with small, two-ranked flowers.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for grazing animals.
