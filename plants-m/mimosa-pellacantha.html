Mimosa pellacantha

Mimosa pellacantha, commonly known as the woolly mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a distinctive branching structure and is often found in dry, open habitats. It is adapted to arid conditions and can tolerate poor soil, making it resilient in harsh environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
