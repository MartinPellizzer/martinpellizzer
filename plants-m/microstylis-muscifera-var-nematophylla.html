Microstylis muscifera var. nematophylla

Microstylis muscifera var. nematophylla, commonly known as the narrow-styled moss-fruit plant, is a rare and specialized member of the Microstylis genus.

It is characterized by its small, delicate inflorescences and unique floral morphology, which includes reduced stamen and pistil structures. This variant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often associated with specific moss communities. Its flowers are adapted for wind pollination, reflecting its ecological niche in forest understories.

The plant's botanical features make it an important subject for studies on plant evolution and adaptation in tropical ecosystems.
