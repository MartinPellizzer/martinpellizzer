Myrtus punicifolia

Myrtus punicifolia, commonly known as the purple myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are aromatic and have been traditionally used in perfumery and herbal remedies.

Myrtus punicifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental plant in Mediterranean climates.
