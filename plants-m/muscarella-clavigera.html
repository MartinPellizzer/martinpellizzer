Muscarella clavigera

Muscarella clavigera, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to dry climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, contributing to its drought tolerance.
