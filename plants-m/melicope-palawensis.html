Melicope palawensis

Melicope palawensis, commonly known as the Palau pepper or wild pepper, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Caroline Islands.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional contexts.
