Myristica tenuivenia

Myristica tenuivenia, commonly known as the narrow-veined myristica, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Myristicaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the islands of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are alternate and often have a distinct venation pattern. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces an aromatic seed kernel that is used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited commercial use compared to other members of its genus.
