Muhlenbergia presliana

Muhlenbergia presliana, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that add ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a low-maintenance choice for landscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular selection for native plant gardens and erosion control.
