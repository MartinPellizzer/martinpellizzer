Myriocephalus nudus

Myriocephalus nudus, commonly known as the naked-headed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, inflated bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant lacks true leaves and stems, instead relying on its specialized bladders for nutrient absorption. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous adaptation make it an intriguing subject for botanical study.
