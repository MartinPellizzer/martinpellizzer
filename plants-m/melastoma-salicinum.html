Melastoma salicinum

Melastoma salicinum, commonly known as the red-rooted melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Melastoma salicinum is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
