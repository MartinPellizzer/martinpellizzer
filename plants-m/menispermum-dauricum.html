Menispermum dauricum

Menispermum dauricum, commonly known as the Daurian arrowroot, is a climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive red, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
