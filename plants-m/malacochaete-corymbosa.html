Malacochaete corymbosa

Malacochaete corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered corymbous orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
