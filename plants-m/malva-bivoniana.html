Malva bivoniana

Malva bivoniana, commonly known as the Bivona mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as cotton and hibiscus. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy, pink or purple flowers. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas.

Malva bivoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
