Mandevilla obtusifolia

Mandevilla obtusifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It is a vigorous climber that thrives in warm, humid climates with well-draining soil.

Mandevilla obtusifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and lush foliage.
