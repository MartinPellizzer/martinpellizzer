Malvastrum arequipense

Malvastrum arequipense, commonly known as the Arequipa mallow, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of southern Peru.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed edges, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found growing along rocky slopes and in dry grasslands.
