Monarda glabra

Monarda glabra, commonly known as white bergamot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Monarda glabra is also known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
