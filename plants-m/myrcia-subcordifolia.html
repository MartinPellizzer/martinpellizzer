Myrcia subcordifolia

Myrcia subcordifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features dark green, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive subcordate (heart-shaped) base. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and are usually white or pale yellow, while the fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe.

Myrcia subcordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native range.
