Melicope borbonica var. borbonica

Melicope borbonica var. borbonica, commonly known as the Madagascar pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
