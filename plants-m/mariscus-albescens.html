Mariscus albescens

Mariscus albescens, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Mariscus albescens is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
