Moraea namibensis

Moraea namibensis, commonly known as the Namibian glory-of-the-snow, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, white to pale blue flowers that resemble snowflakes. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring succulent leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats across Namibia and South Africa.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
