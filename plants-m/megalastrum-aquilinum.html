Megalastrum aquilinum

Megalastrum aquilinum, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Megalastrum aquilinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
