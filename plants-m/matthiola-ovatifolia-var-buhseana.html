Matthiola ovatifolia var. buhseana

Matthiola ovatifolia var. buhseana, commonly known as the Buhse's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
