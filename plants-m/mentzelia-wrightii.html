Mentzelia wrightii

Mentzelia wrightii, commonly known as Wright's blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mentzelia wrightii is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
