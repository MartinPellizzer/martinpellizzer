Malva villosa

Malva villosa, commonly known as the downy mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a serrated edge, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a prominent central column. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

Malva villosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
