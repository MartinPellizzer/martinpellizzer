Masdevallia pinocchio

Masdevallia pinocchio, commonly known as the "Pinocchio orchid," is a rare and striking species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated petals that resemble a whimsical nose, giving it its playful common name. The plant belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its intricate floral structure and vibrant colors. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to bloom.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
