Miersia minor

Miersia minor, commonly known as the lesser miersia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and range in color from white to pale pink.

Miersia minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
