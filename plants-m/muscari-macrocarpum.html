Muscari macrocarpum

Muscari macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-capsuled grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a white or pinkish tinge. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is a hardy perennial that is often used in gardens for its early spring bloom and ornamental value.

The bulbs are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
