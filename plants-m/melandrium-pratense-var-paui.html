Melandrium pratense var. paui

Melandrium pratense var. paui, commonly known as the purple poppy or meadow catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers are arranged in dense clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and butterflies.
