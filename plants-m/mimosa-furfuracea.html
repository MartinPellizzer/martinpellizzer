Mimosa furfuracea

Mimosa furfuracea, commonly known as the hairy mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its rapid leaf movement in response to touch, a trait known as thigmonastic response. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it often grows in dry, open habitats.

Mimosa furfuracea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
