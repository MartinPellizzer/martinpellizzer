Melastoma fastigiatum

Melastoma fastigiatum, commonly known as the red-rooted melastoma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Melastomataceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, opposite leaves and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The plant has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.
