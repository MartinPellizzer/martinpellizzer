Mentha rubicunda var. langiana

Mentha rubicunda var. langiana, commonly known as the red-stemmed mint, is a variant of the red mint species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
