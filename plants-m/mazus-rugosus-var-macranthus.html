Mazus rugosus var. macranthus

Mazus rugosus var. macranthus, commonly known as the large-flowered mazus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or magenta flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
