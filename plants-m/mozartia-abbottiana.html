Mozartia abbottiana

Mozartia abbottiana, commonly known as the Abbott's grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, drought-prone environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
