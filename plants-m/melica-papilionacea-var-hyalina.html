Melica papilionacea var. hyalina

Melica papilionacea var. hyalina, commonly known as the glaucous beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often have a waxy, bluish-gray coating. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, two-lipped flowers that are typically greenish or pale blue in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

This variety is often found in wetland ecosystems and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
