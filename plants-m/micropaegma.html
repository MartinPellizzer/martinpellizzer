Micropaegma

Micropaegma, commonly known as the dwarf peacock fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, trailing growth habit and its striking, feathery foliage that resembles the plumage of a peacock. The leaves are typically bright green with a waxy texture and often feature a subtle variegation. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Micropaegma requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
