Mucuna comosa

Mucuna comosa, commonly known as the hairy urd or velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being hairy and broad. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by elongated, hairy pods containing dark brown, kidney-shaped seeds. Mucuna comosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural systems.

It also contains compounds with potential medicinal properties.
