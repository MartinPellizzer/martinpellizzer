Myrcia stricta

Myrcia stricta, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in wetlands and disturbed soils.

Myrcia stricta is known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
