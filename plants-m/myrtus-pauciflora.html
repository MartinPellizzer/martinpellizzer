Myrtus pauciflora

Myrtus pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow, evergreen leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Myrtus pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
