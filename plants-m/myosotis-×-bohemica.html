Myosotis × bohemica

Myosotis × bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian forget-me-not, is a hybrid plant species within the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate blue to purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Myosotis × bohemica is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant colors and compact growth habit.

Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
