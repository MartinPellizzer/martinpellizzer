Malus orthocarpa

Malus orthocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved apple, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in cooler climates.
