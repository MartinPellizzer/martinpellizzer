Medinilla nana

Medinilla nana, commonly known as the dwarf medinilla, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Medinilla nana is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
