Myginda

Myginda, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small, citrus-like fruit native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is botanically classified as *Phyllanthus emblica*, a member of the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring simple, alternate leaves and small green flowers. Its fruit develops into a yellow-orange berry with a tart flavor, rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants.

The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
