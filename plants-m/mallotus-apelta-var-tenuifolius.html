Mallotus apelta var. tenuifolius

Mallotus apelta var. tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallow, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
