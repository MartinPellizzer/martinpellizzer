Myrsine citrifolia

Myrsine citrifolia, commonly known as the sour orange or kaffir lime, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow when mature. The fruit's peel is thick and aromatic, containing essential oils used in cooking and traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates.
