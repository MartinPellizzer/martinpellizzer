Melica pyramidalis

Melica pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid grass, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Melica pyramidalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
