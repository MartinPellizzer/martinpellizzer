Merremia umbellata var. occidentalis

Merremia umbellata var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western trumpet vine, is a climbing vine native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains seeds dispersed by wind.
