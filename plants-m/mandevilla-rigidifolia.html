Mandevilla rigidifolia

Mandevilla rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid-stemmed mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in warm climates and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually ovate and have a glossy texture, while its flowers are clustered in terminal panicles.

Mandevilla rigidifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
