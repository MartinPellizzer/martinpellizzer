Marsdenia rostrata

Marsdenia rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and woody stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are often followed by follicles containing seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Marsdenia rostrata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
