Mouriri latihila

Mouriri latihila, commonly known as the Andes mourning fruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of the Andes.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine by local communities. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique floral structure.

Mouriri latihila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
