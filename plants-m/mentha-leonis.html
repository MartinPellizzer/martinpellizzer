Mentha leonis

Mentha leonis, commonly known as the hedgehog mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Mentha leonis is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
