Mussaenda macrophylla var. grandisepala

Mussaenda macrophylla var. grandisepala, commonly known as the large-sepal mussaenda, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, with prominent, broad sepals that give it its common name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
