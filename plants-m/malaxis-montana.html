Malaxis montana

Malaxis montana, commonly known as the mountain malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a slender rhizome with a single, narrow leaf and produces delicate, greenish-white or yellowish flowers. Its flowers are usually clustered in a spike and have a unique structure with a short, narrow column.

Malaxis montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady gardens.
