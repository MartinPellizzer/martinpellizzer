Matricaria nobilis

Matricaria nobilis, commonly known as the noble chamomile, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in sunny locations. Its leaves are finely divided and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers bloom in late summer.

Matricaria nobilis is often used in herbal medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
