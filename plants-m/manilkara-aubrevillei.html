Manilkara aubrevillei

Manilkara aubrevillei, commonly known as the African manilkara, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by smooth, orange-red fruits that are edible. Its latex is milky and used in traditional medicine, while its wood is valued for its durability and workability.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various wildlife species.
