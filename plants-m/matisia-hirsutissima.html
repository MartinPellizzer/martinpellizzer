Matisia hirsutissima

Matisia hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy matisia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Matisia hirsutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
