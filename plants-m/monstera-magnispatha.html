Monstera magnispatha

Monstera magnispatha, commonly known as the giant monstera, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, with a distinctive split that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. The plant is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other popular houseplants like philodendrons and peace lilies. Its stems are thick and can climb or trail, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy texture, and the plant thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.
