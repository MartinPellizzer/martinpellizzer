Malaxis binabayensis

Malaxis binabayensis, commonly known as the Mindanao ghost orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in the Philippines.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers with intricate veining and a unique, ghost-like appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees in the mountainous regions of Mindanao, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include a slender, wiry stem and a single, lance-shaped leaf.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
