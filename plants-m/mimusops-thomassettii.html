Mimusops thomassettii

Mimusops thomassettii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are usually arranged oppositely on the stems. The tree produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests and secondary vegetation.

The plant is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
