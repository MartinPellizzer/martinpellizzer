Memorialis neilgherrensis

Memorialis neilgherrensis, commonly known as the Nilgiri bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often hairy.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators.
