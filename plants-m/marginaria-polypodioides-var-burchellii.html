Marginaria polypodioides var. burchellii

Marginaria polypodioides var. burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's marginal fern, is a species of fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky outcrops. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
