Microlicia euphorbioides var. setosa

Microlicia euphorbioides var. setosa, commonly known as the woolly microlicia, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

The varietal name "setosa" refers to the stiff, bristly hairs that cover its leaves and stems.
