Macropodanthus tridentatus

Macropodanthus tridentatus, commonly known as the three-toothed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The flowers are pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
