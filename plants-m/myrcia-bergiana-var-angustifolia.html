Myrcia bergiana var. angustifolia

Myrcia bergiana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a species native to the humid regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in areas with high humidity and well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant has a dense, aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
