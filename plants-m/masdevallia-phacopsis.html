Masdevallia phacopsis

Masdevallia phacopsis, commonly known as the violet masdevallia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of violet and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on forest floors or among rocks. Its flowers are short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Masdevallia phacopsis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique blooming patterns.
