Mimulus formosa-elegans

Mimulus formosa-elegans, commonly known as the elegant weeping monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, drooping clusters of bright orange or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mimulus formosa-elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also important in supporting pollinator populations.
