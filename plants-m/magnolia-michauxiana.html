Magnolia michauxiana

Magnolia michauxiana, commonly known as the Michaux's magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its flowers are typically 6 to 8 inches in diameter and have numerous sepals that resemble petals.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
