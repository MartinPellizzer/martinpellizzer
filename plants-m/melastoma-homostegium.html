Melastoma homostegium

Melastoma homostegium, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or pink, with five petals and prominent stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.
