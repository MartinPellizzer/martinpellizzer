Mutisia ilicifolia var. truncata

Mutisia ilicifolia var. truncata, commonly known as the truncated mutisia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow or brown, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

The varietal form 'truncata' refers to the distinctive truncate (cut-off) shape of its leaves.
