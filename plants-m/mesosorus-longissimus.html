Mesosorus longissimus

Mesosorus longissimus, commonly known as the long-sporangiate fern, is a species characterized by its distinctive sori arrangement.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, linear fronds with a prominent midrib. Its sori are elongated and linear, often positioned along the margins of the fronds.

The mesosorus, a specialized structure, is elongated and serves as the site of spore production.
