Maxillaria phaeoglossa

Maxillaria phaeoglossa, commonly known as the dark-leafed maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often darker than the rest of the bloom.

Maxillaria phaeoglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
