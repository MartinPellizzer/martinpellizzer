Miconia leacollina

Miconia leacollina, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Miconia leacollina is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it a potential invasive species in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in ecological and horticultural contexts.
