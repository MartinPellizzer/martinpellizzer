Mesosphaerum fruticosum

Mesosphaerum fruticosum, commonly known as the creeping mesosphaerum, is a low-growing shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive and adapted to survive in nutrient-poor, sandy soils.

Mesosphaerum fruticosum is also notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
