Melastoma coccineum

Melastoma coccineum, commonly known as the red melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are elliptical and have a slightly rough texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Melastoma coccineum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
