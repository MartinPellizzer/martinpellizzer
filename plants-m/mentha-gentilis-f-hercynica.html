Mentha gentilis f. hercynica

Mentha gentilis f. hercynica, commonly known as the Hercynian mint, is a subspecies of the catnip plant native to Central and Southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a strong menthol scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
