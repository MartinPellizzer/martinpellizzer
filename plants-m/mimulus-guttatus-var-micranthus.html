Mimulus guttatus var. micranthus

Mimulus guttatus var. micranthus, commonly known as the narrowleaf monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, bright orange or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It is an annual herb that produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in ecological studies.
