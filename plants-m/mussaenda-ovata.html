Mussaenda ovata

Mussaenda ovata, commonly known as the golden glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant has simple, ovate-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Mussaenda ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
