Magnolia conspicua var. rosea

Magnolia conspicua var. rosea, commonly known as the rosea magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
