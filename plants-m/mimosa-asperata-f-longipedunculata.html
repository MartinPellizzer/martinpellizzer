Mimosa asperata f. longipedunculata

Mimosa asperata f. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncled mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its elongated peduncles.

This plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to regions of South America. It features bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
