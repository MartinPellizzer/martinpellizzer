Myrcia lanceolata

Myrcia lanceolata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and seasonal floral display.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
