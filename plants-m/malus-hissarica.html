Malus hissarica

Malus hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tien Shan and Pamir Mountains. The plant is a small deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white and appear in clusters.

The fruit is a small, yellowish apple that is edible but not as widely cultivated as other apple varieties.
