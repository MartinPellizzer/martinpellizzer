Marsdenia longiloba

Marsdenia longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in umbels, which are followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
