Myristica conspersa

Myristica conspersa, commonly known as the hairy nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used for spice. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

The tree is valued not only for its seeds but also for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
