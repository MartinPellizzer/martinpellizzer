Mesadenella pumila

Mesadenella pumila, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. The plant features a low-growing, spreading habit with linear, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Mesadenella pumila plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
