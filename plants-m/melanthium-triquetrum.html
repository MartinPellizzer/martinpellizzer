Melanthium triquetrum

Melanthium triquetrum, commonly known as the three-angled meadow rue, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in loose racemes. Melanthium triquetrum typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

It is an herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
