Myristica simiarum subsp. celebica

Myristica simiarum subsp.

celebica, commonly known as the Celebes nutmeg, is a species of flowering plant in the Myristicaceae family. It is native to the tropical regions of Indonesia, particularly the Moluccas and surrounding islands.

The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into the aromatic, edible seeds used for nutmeg and mace. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The botanical features of this subspecies include its woody structure and the presence of essential oils in its seed kernels.
