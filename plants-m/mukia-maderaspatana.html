Mukia maderaspatana

Mukia maderaspatana, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood or mukia, is a small evergreen tree native to the western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits containing hard, black seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is a valued species in horticulture and conservation efforts.
