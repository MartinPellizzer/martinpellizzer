Machaerium complanatum

Machaerium complanatum, commonly known as the flat-leaf machaerium, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be dispersed by wind or animals.

Machaerium complanatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in nitrogen fixation.
