Miconia serialis

Miconia serialis, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds.

Miconia serialis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in non-native habitats.
