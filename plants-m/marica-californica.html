Marica californica

Marica californica, commonly known as the California marigold, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Marica californica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
