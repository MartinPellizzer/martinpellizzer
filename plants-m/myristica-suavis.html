Myristica suavis

Myristica suavis, commonly known as the sweet myristica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic seeds and bark. The plant produces large, leathery leaves and yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds, known as mace, are encased in a red aril and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The tree also yields a valuable spice called nutmeg, which is derived from its seed kernel.
