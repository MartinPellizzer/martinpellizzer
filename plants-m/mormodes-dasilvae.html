Mormodes dasilvae

Mormodes dasilvae, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its delicate, white, orchid-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to habitat destruction and its elusive nature, Mormodes dasilvae is considered a critically endangered species.
