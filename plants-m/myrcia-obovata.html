Myrcia obovata

Myrcia obovata, commonly known as the wax myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to dark, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and rainforests.

Myrcia obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, supporting various wildlife species.
