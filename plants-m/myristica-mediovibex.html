Myristica mediovibex

Myristica mediovibex, commonly known as the fragrant nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces large, glossy leaves and bears yellowish flowers that develop into drupes containing aromatic seeds. Its most notable botanical feature is the seed, which is encased in a reddish, fibrous aril.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and is often cultivated for its economic and medicinal properties.
