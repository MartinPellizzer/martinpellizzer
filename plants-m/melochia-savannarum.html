Melochia savannarum

Melochia savannarum, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils, often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Melochia savannarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
