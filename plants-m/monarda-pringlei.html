Monarda pringlei

Monarda pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's bee balm, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a minty scent, contributing to its ornamental value.

Monarda pringlei thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to native gardens.
