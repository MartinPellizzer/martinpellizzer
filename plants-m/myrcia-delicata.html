Myrcia delicata

Myrcia delicata, commonly known as the silky myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and leathery, elliptical leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Myrcia delicata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
