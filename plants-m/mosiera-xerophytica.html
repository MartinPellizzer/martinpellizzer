Mosiera xerophytica

Mosiera xerophytica, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its unique adaptations, Mosiera xerophytica is often used in xeriscaping and drought-tolerant garden designs.
