Marlea macrophylla var. velutina

Marlea macrophylla var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet-leaved marlea, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is valued both for ornamental purposes and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
