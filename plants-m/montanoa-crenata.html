Montanoa crenata

Montanoa crenata, commonly known as the Mexican heath or mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at higher elevations. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive crenate margin, giving it its scientific name.

Montanoa crenata produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.
