Malvaviscus arboreus var. sloanei

Malvaviscus arboreus var. sloanei, commonly known as the yellow curlleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
