Mesembryanthemum salicornioides

Mesembryanthemum salicornioides, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It has a rosette growth pattern, with leaves arranged in a tight, compact formation.

Mesembryanthemum salicornioides is well adapted to hot, dry climates and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
