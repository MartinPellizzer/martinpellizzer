Miconia pyxidata

Miconia pyxidata, commonly known as the "Mexican beautyberry," is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Miconia pyxidata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and showy flowers.
