Melampyrum scardicum subsp. serbicum

Melampyrum scardicum subsp.

serbicum, commonly known as the Serbicum touch-me-not, is a member of the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its erect, glabrous stems. It produces small, yellow-orange flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer.

The plant is hemiparasitic, deriving some of its nutrients from the roots of neighboring plants. It is native to Southeastern Europe and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Its ecological role includes supporting pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native range.
