Macfadyena coito

Macfadyena coito, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and pairs of opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, facilitating its spread in suitable habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
