Magnolia mannii

Magnolia mannii, commonly known as the Madagascar magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. Its bark is greyish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
