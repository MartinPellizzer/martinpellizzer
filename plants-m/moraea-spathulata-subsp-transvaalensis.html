Moraea spathulata subsp. transvaalensis

Moraea spathulata subsp.

transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal glory flower, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa. It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

This subspecies is part of the Iridaceae family and is valued for its vibrant floral display.
