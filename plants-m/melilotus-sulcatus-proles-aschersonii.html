Melilotus sulcatus proles aschersonii

Melilotus sulcatus proles aschersonii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spherical heads, which are typical of the genus Melilotus. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
