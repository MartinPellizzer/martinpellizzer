Myrcia pyrifolia

Myrcia pyrifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy red flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
