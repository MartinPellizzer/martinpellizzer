Malapoenna membranifolia

Malapoenna membranifolia, commonly known as the paperbark tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, papery bark that peels off in thin layers, giving it its common name. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red or orange berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
