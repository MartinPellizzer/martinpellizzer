Melilotus caeruleus var. procumbens

Melilotus caeruleus var. procumbens, commonly known as blue melilot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often lie close to the ground, producing clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along woodland edges.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological gardens.
