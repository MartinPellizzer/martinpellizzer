Mentha sylvestris var. simplex

Mentha sylvestris var. simplex, commonly known as wood mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a mild, mint-like fragrance.

The small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.
