Muhlenbergia breviaristata

Muhlenbergia breviaristata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue-gray. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear delicate, thread-like flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a drought-tolerant choice for xeriscaping.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
