Marrubium radiatum

Marrubium radiatum, commonly known as white horehound, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. The plant features grayish-green leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its strong, bitter scent.

Marrubium radiatum has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
