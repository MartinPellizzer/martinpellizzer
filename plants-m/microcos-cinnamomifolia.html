Microcos cinnamomifolia

Microcos cinnamomifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon-leaf microcos, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct cinnamon scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
