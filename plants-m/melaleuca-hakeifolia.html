Melaleuca hakeifolia

Melaleuca hakeifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Melaleuca hakeifolia is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in well-drained environments.
