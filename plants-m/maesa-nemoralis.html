Maesa nemoralis

Maesa nemoralis, commonly known as the creeping maesa, is a low-growing shrub native to the woodlands of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish coating, which helps reduce water loss in its often shady habitat. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are a food source for birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and undergrowth.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
