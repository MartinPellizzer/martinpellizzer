Malpighia guadalajarensis

Malpighia guadalajarensis, commonly known as the Guadalajara acorn vine, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
