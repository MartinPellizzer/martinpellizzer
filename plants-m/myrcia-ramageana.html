Myrcia ramageana

Myrcia ramageana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe.

Myrcia ramageana is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
