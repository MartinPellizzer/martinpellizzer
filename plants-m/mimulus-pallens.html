Mimulus pallens

Mimulus pallens, commonly known as the white monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow throat. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mimulus pallens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
