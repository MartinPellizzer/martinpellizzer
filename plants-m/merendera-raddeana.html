Merendera raddeana

Merendera raddeana, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its straight, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

The plant is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a significant species in its native ecosystems.
