Myrcia toaensis

Myrcia toaensis, commonly known as the Tocantins myrtle, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a fragrant, resinous scent. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Myrcia toaensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
