Monnina pilosa var. glabrescens

Monnina pilosa var. glabrescens, commonly known as silky monnina, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs that give it a soft, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
