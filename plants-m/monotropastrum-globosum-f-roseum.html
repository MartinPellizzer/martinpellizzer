Monotropastrum globosum f. roseum

Monotropastrum globosum f. roseum, commonly known as the roseum form of the common moonflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that produces showy, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant is notable for its unique floral structure, with petals that curl and fold as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
