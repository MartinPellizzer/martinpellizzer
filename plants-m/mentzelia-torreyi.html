Mentzelia torreyi

Mentzelia torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's bluestem, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, contributing to its common name.

Mentzelia torreyi is adapted to arid environments and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
