Mecodium reniforme

Mecodium reniforme, commonly known as the kidneyleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its kidney-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Mecodium reniforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
