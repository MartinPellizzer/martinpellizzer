Masdevallia ariasii

Masdevallia ariasii, commonly known as the Arias' masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers that resemble miniature orchid blooms, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Masdevallia ariasii is valued by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental appeal and relatively compact size.
