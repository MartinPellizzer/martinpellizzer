Mauria

Mauria, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Mauria is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
