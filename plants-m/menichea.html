Menichea

Menichea, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow or golden flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
