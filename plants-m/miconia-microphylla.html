Miconia microphylla

Miconia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. It is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Miconia microphylla is also noted for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
