Maranta geniculata

Maranta geniculata, commonly known as the prayer plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often variegated leaves that fold upwards at night, a behavior known as nyctinasty. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in indoor gardening. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture and can display a range of colors including green, silver, and red.

Maranta geniculata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
