Mexianthus mexicanus

Mexianthus mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.
