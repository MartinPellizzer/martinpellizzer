Melanobatus glaucifolius

Melanobatus glaucifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that thrives on a variety of host trees.

It possesses small, ovate leaves that are covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating, giving it its characteristic glaucifolius name. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange, containing adhesive seeds that aid in dispersal by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
