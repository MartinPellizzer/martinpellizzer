Melo adana

Melo adana, commonly known as the Adana melon, is a species of citrus fruit native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its elongated, yellow-green rind and juicy, sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is typically propagated through seed or grafting, and it blooms with small white flowers before producing its distinctive fruit.

Due to its unique flavor and texture, Melo adana is highly valued in both culinary and commercial contexts.
