Mikania ochroleuca

Mikania ochroleuca, commonly known as whiteleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow as a vine or an upright shrub, depending on environmental conditions. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in some regions.
