Macrosolen oleoides

Macrosolen oleoides, commonly known as the oil-rich macrophyte, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its waxy, oil-producing leaves.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. The plant's leaves are broad and flat, with a thick cuticle that aids in water retention. Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, relying on water currents for seed dispersal.

Due to its high oil content, it has potential applications in biofuel research and ecological restoration projects.
