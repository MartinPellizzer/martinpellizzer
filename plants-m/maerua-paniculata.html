Maerua paniculata

Maerua paniculata, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its showy, white or pink inflorescences that resemble snowballs. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The flowers are pollinated by insects and birds, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
