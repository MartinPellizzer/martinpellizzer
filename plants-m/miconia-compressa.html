Miconia compressa

Miconia compressa, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Miconia compressa is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
