Mulgedium oblongifolium

Mulgedium oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf mulgedium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its elongated, broad leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale in color, which bloom during the spring season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or scrub habitats.

Due to its unique foliage and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
