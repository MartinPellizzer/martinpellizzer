Masdevallia aviceps

Masdevallia aviceps, commonly known as the bird's beak orchid, is a unique epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, bird-like flower spike that gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in cool, humid environments with dappled light.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
