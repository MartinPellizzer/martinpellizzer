Malus baccata var. himalaica

Malus baccata var. himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It features showy white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
