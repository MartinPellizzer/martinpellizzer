Malva thirkeana

Malva thirkeana, commonly known as the African mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is known for its mucilaginous properties in its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
