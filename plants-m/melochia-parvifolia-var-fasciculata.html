Melochia parvifolia var. fasciculata

Melochia parvifolia var. fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered melochia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to tropical landscapes.
