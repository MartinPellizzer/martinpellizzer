Mimulus spinulosus

Mimulus spinulosus, commonly known as the spiked monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Mimulus spinulosus is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
