Myrcia kegeliana var. latifolia

Myrcia kegeliana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
