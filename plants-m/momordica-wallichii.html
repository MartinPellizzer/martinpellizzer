Momordica wallichii

Momordica wallichii, commonly known as the Indian bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces elongated, warty fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn orange when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it has hairy stems.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and cooking, valued for its bitter taste and medicinal properties.
