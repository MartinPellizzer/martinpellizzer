Malus × floribunda f. pendula

Malus × floribunda f. pendula, commonly known as weeping crabapple, is a deciduous ornamental tree characterized by its cascading branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Malus, known for its showy flowers and colorful foliage. The plant typically grows to a moderate height, with slender, drooping branches that give it a graceful, almost ethereal look. Its flowers, which appear in spring, are usually white or pink and are followed by small, ornamental fruits.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
