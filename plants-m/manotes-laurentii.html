Manotes laurentii

Manotes laurentii, commonly known as the Laurentian laurel, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and leathery in texture. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by dark blue berries.

Manotes laurentii is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
