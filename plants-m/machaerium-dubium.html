Machaerium dubium

Machaerium dubium, commonly known as the slender machaerium, is a tropical perennial legume native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, followed by small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Machaerium dubium is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
