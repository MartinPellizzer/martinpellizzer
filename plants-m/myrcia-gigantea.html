Myrcia gigantea

Myrcia gigantea, commonly known as the giant myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

Myrcia gigantea is often found in moist, tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
