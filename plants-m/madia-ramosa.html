Madia ramosa

Madia ramosa, commonly known as the fringed madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy, pinkish to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Madia ramosa is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and disturbed soils.
