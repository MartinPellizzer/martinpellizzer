Mentha longifolia var. incana

Mentha longifolia var. incana, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in culinary and herbal applications.
