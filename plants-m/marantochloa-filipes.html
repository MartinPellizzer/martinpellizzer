Marantochloa filipes

Marantochloa filipes, commonly known as the slender swamp taro, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and broad, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is often found in swamps or along riverbanks. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive white or pale green coloration.

Marantochloa filipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
