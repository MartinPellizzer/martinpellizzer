Mentha aquatica f. aradensis

Mentha aquatica f. aradensis, commonly known as the Arad water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a variant of Mentha aquatica, characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and its preference for moist, shady environments. The plant typically grows along stream banks and in wet meadows, where it thrives in damp, nutrient-rich soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or violet, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
