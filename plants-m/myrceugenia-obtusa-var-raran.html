Myrceugenia obtusa var. raran

Myrceugenia obtusa var. raran, commonly known as the Raran myrtle, is a species native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly in Chile.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically consumed raw or used in local culinary traditions. Botanically, it belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in cooler climates.
