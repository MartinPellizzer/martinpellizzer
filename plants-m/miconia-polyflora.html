Miconia polyflora

Miconia polyflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia polyflora is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in many regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land management.
