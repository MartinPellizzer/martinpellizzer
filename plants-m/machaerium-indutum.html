Machaerium indutum

Machaerium indutum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian needlegrass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with slender, branching stems and trifoliate or pinnate leaves. Its flowers are small and usually clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces legume fruits that contain several seeds.

Machaerium indutum is often found in disturbed sites and can be invasive in some areas.
