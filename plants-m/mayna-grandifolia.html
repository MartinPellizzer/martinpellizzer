Mayna grandifolia

Mayna grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered mayna, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. Mayna grandifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along riverbanks.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important species in its ecosystem.
