Myrocarpus emarginatus

Myrocarpus emarginatus, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or white mangrove, is a small evergreen tree native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in saline and brackish water environments, making it an important species in mangrove ecosystems.

Myrocarpus emarginatus plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
