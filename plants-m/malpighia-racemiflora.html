Malpighia racemiflora

Malpighia racemiflora, commonly known as the tropical cranberry or red mombin, is a member of the Malpighiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
