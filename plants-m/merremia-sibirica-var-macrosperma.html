Merremia sibirica var. macrosperma

Merremia sibirica var. macrosperma, commonly known as the Siberian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to parts of Asia and the Russian Far East.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and its ability to climb by means of tendrils. Its fruit is a capsule that contains large, flat, bean-like seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
