Malpighia oxycocca f. megaphylla

Malpighia oxycocca f. megaphylla, commonly known as the giant granadilla, is a variant of the granadilla plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange in color. The plant belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and the plant produces clusters of small, white flowers.

The fruit is edible, though it is less commonly consumed than its smaller relatives due to its size and texture.
