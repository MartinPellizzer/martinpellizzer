Maackia floribunda var. pubescens

Maackia floribunda var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent maackia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. The leaves are elliptical, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
