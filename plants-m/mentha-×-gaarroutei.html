Mentha × gaarroutei

Mentha × gaarroutei, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a cross between Mentha spicata (spearmint) and Mentha viridis (ovalleaf mint).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually purple or pink, appearing in whorls along the stems.

Mentha × gaarroutei is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
