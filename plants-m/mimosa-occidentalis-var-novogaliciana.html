Mimosa occidentalis var. novogaliciana

Mimosa occidentalis var. novogaliciana, commonly known as the Western mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves that are sensitive to touch, folding shut when disturbed, a characteristic known as thigmonasty. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native range.
