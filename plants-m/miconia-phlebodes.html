Miconia phlebodes

Miconia phlebodes, commonly known as the broadleaf miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain areas.
