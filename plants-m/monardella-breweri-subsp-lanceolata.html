Monardella breweri subsp. lanceolata

Monardella breweri subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf monardella, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
