Meconopsis × cookei

Meconopsis × cookei, commonly known as the hybrid blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a hybrid between Meconopsis cambrica and Meconopsis punicea, resulting in a plant with vibrant blue and purple flowers. The species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are densely packed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
