Masdevallia frilehmannii

Masdevallia frilehmannii, commonly known as the Frilehmann's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flower structure with a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively compact size.
