Meliosma andina

Meliosma andina, commonly known as the Andean meliosma, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes trees and shrubs known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, and its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds.

Meliosma andina is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
