Musa humilis

Musa humilis, commonly known as the dwarf banana plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, edible bananas that are often used in cooking. Its leaves are large, broad, and have a waxy surface.

Musa humilis is known for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
