Mapania longispica

Mapania longispica, commonly known as the long-spined mapania, is a rare and distinctive member of the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces striking, clustered inflorescences with small, white flowers that emit a subtle fragrance. Its fruits are fibrous and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
