Megapterium missouriense

Megapterium missouriense, commonly known as the Missouri winged bean, is a leguminous plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged pods that enclose edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its compound leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a serrated margin.

The flowers are small and usually pale pink or white, contributing to its ornamental value in addition to its nutritional significance.
