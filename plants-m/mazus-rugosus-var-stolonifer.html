Mazus rugosus var. stolonifer

Mazus rugosus var. stolonifer, commonly known as the creeping mazus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread rapidly along the ground. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in ground cover in horticulture.
