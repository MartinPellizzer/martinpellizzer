Mollugo Spathulifolia

Mollugo spathulifolia is a small, flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is commonly known as the cushion moss or the little mouse-ear. This plant is characterized by its compact, mat-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils. It produces tiny white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Mollugo spathulifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
