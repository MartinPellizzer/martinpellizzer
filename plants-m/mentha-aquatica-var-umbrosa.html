Mentha aquatica var. umbrosa

Mentha aquatica var. umbrosa, commonly known as water mint, is a variant of the water mint plant native to moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, stream banks, and other water-rich habitats. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their refreshing and cooling properties.

This variety is closely related to other Mentha species and shares similar medicinal and aromatic qualities.
