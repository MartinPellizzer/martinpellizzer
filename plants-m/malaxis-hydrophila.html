Malaxis hydrophila

Malaxis hydrophila, commonly known as the water orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Malaxis hydrophila is noted for its ability to absorb water through its roots, making it well adapted to humid climates.
