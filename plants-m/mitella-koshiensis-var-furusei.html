Mitella koshiensis var. furusei

Mitella koshiensis var. furusei, commonly known as the Koshi mitella, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are elliptical with a slightly hairy surface, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
