Moluccella acutiloba

Moluccella acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
