Mesembryanthemum uncatum

Mesembryanthemum uncatum, commonly known as the rock rose or the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue or purple leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, or pink, typically blooming in spring. It is a member of the Aizoaceae family, known for its adaptations to hot, dry climates.

Mesembryanthemum uncatum is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
