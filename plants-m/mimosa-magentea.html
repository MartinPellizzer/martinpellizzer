Mimosa magentea

Mimosa magentea, commonly known as the purple mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. It is known for its sensitive leaves that fold when touched, a response called thigmonasty.

Mimosa magentea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
