Mesadenia muehlenbergii

Mesadenia muehlenbergii, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
