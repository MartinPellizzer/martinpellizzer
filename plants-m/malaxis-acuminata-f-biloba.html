Malaxis acuminata f. biloba

Malaxis acuminata f. biloba, commonly known as the bilobed lady's tresses, is a rare and unique orchid species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bilobed flower spikes that bear numerous small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it produces a delicate, orchid-like inflorescence.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often considered a conservation concern.
