Meconella oregana var. californica

Meconella oregana var. californica, commonly known as the California bitterroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, rounded seed pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is also noted for its historical use by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.
