Mentha monticola var. jurana

Mentha monticola var. jurana, commonly known as the alpine mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the European Alps.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
