Melastoma subrepandum

Melastoma subrepandum, commonly known as the small-leaved melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and prominent stamens.

Melastoma subrepandum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
