Micrampelis pubescens

Micrampelis pubescens, commonly known as the hairy climbing plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
