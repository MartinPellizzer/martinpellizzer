Macroglena kalimantanensis

Macroglena kalimantanensis, commonly known as the Kalimantan giant fern, is a unique and rare plant species native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in the undergrowth of dense tropical forests. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of its fronds, and it relies on wind pollination for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss and deforestation, this species is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
