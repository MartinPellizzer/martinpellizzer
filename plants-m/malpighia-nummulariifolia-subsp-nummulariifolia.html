Malpighia nummulariifolia subsp. nummulariifolia

Malpighia nummulariifolia subsp.

nummulariifolia, commonly known as the wax apple or carambola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae. It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly Brazil.

The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The fruit is a distinctive five-lobed berry that turns yellow to orange when ripe.

It is valued for its edible fruit, which has a tart flavor and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
