Marsdenia altissima subsp. faucinuda

Marsdenia altissima subsp.

faucinuda, commonly known as the high mountain Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with silky filaments, aiding in wind dispersal.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
