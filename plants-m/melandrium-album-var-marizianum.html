Melandrium album var. marizianum

Melandrium album var. marizianum, commonly known as the white campion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in racemes along the stems. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile garden plant.

Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a tendency to spread through self-seeding.
