Martynia fallax

Martynia fallax, commonly known as the purple false heather, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

Martynia fallax is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
