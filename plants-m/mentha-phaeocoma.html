Mentha phaeocoma

Mentha phaeocoma, commonly known as black mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Mentha phaeocoma is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
