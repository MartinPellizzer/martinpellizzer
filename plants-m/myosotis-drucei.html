Myosotis drucei

Myosotis drucei, commonly known as the dwarf forget-me-not, is a low-growing perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from September to November. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Myosotis drucei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important species in New Zealand's native flora.
