Melissitus medicaginoideus

Melissitus medicaginoideus, commonly known as the honeybee mimic orchid, is a rare and specialized flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles the appearance of a honeybee, aiding in its pollination by attracting specific bee species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer, often in clusters along its slender stems. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to reduce water loss in its arid habitat.
