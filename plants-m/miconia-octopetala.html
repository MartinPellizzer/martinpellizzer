Miconia octopetala

Miconia octopetala, commonly known as the eight-petaled miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are round, fleshy berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Miconia octopetala is often found in moist, shaded environments within rainforests.
