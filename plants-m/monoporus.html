Monoporus

Monoporus, commonly known as the "single pore" plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single central tube that houses the reproductive organs. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette. Monoporus species are native to various regions in Africa and are adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
