Mentha longifolia subf. leptostachya

Mentha longifolia subf.

leptostachya, commonly known as narrow-stemmed mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
