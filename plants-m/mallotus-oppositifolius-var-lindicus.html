Mallotus oppositifolius var. lindicus

Mallotus oppositifolius var. lindicus, commonly known as the Lind's holly, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
