Macaranga kampotensis

Macaranga kampotensis, commonly known as the Kampot nettle, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological studies and potential applications in phytoremediation.
