Monstera peckoltii

Monstera peckoltii, commonly known as the Swiss cheese plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop fenestrations, or holes, as they mature. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
