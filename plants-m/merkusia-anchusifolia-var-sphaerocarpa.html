Merkusia anchusifolia var. sphaerocarpa

Merkusia anchusifolia var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globular bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. This variant is distinguished by its rounded, fruiting bodies that resemble small spheres, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
