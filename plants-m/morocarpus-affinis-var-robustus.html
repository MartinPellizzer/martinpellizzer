Morocarpus affinis var. robustus

Morocarpus affinis var. robustus, commonly known as the robust morocarpus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It features a compact, rounded stem with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
