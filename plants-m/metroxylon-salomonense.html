Metroxylon salomonense

Metroxylon salomonense, commonly known as the Solomon Islands palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. Its fibrous trunk is valued for its strength and has been historically used in construction and handicrafts.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
