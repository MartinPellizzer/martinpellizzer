Miconia weberbaueri

Miconia weberbaueri, commonly known as the Guianese miconia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are typically followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, it can become invasive in certain regions.
