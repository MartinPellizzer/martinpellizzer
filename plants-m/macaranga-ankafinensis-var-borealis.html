Macaranga ankafinensis var. borealis

Macaranga ankafinensis var. borealis, commonly known as the northern macaranga, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive yellowish-green color, and it relies on insects for pollination.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
