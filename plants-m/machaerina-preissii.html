Machaerina preissii

Machaerina preissii, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Machaerina preissii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
