Myrtus quadrisperma

Myrtus quadrisperma, commonly known as the four-seeded myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Myrtus quadrisperma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use as a hedge or in rock gardens.
