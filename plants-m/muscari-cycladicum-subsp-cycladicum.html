Muscari cycladicum subsp. cycladicum

Muscari cycladicum subsp.

cycladicum, commonly known as the Cycladic musk grape, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge in early spring and wither by late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
