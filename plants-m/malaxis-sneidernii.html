Malaxis sneidernii

Malaxis sneidernii, commonly known as the sneidern's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and specialized growth habits. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and usually greenish or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Malaxis sneidernii is notable for its ability to form mycorrhizal associations, which are essential for its nutrient uptake in nutrient-poor soils.
