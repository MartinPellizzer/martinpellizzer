Melasma physalodes

Melasma physalodes, commonly known as the "sugar maple," is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, pinnately compound leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant has significant ecological value, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
