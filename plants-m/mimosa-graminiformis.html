Mimosa graminiformis

Mimosa graminiformis, commonly known as the grass mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate yellow flowers. The plant produces fluffy, spherical seed pods that burst open when mature, dispersing seeds by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Mimosa graminiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
