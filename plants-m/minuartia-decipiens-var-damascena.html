Minuartia decipiens var. damascena

Minuartia decipiens var. damascena, commonly known as the Damascus rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are borne on short pedicels.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and alpine settings.
