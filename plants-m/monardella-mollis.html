Monardella mollis

Monardella mollis, commonly known as softleaf monardella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Monardella mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a nectar source for bees and other pollinators.
