Maxillaria chapadensis

Maxillaria chapadensis, commonly known as the Chapadense orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and floral structures. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with intricate patterns, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Maxillaria chapadensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both greenhouses and gardens.
