Magnolia coco

Magnolia coco, commonly known as the sweetgum or American sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its star-shaped, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces distinctive, spiky, spherical fruit that contains numerous small, winged seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated edge, turning yellow in the fall.

The bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.
