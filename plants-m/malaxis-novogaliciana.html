Malaxis novogaliciana

Malaxis novogaliciana, commonly known as the Slender Malaxis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
