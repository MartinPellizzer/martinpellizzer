Massonia hirsuta

Massonia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy massonia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that are covered in fine, hairy textures, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and pointed, adapted for water storage and reducing surface area for evaporation.

Massonia hirsuta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience to drought.
