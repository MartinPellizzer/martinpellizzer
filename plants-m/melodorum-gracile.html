Melodorum gracile

Melodorum gracile, commonly known as the slender melody orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil.

Melodorum gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
