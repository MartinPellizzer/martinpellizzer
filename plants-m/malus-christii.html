Malus christii

Malus christii, commonly known as the Christ's crabapple, is a small deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, persistent fruits that provide food for wildlife.

Malus christii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native range.
