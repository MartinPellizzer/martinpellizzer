Manotes racemosa

Manotes racemosa, commonly known as the clustered manroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a raceme, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the roots are thick and fleshy.

Manotes racemosa is often found in moist, swampy habitats and is considered a wetland indicator species.
