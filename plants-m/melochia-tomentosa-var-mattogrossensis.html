Melochia tomentosa var. mattogrossensis

Melochia tomentosa var. mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Its hairy foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological studies.
