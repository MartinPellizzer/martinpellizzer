Malaxis chlorophrys

Malaxis chlorophrys, commonly known as the yellow lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to various regions in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and a long, spiraled inflorescence of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique, intricate flowering structure.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow under suitable conditions.
