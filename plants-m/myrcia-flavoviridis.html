Myrcia flavoviridis

Myrcia flavoviridis, commonly known as the yellow-flowered myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
