Moraea ciliata subsp. cuprina

Moraea ciliata subsp.

cuprina, commonly known as the cup-shaped moraea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy inflorescences that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
