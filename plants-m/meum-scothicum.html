Meum scothicum

Meum scothicum, commonly known as Scotch thistle, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles and parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in stiff, barbed hairs that deter herbivores, while its flowers bloom from July to September.

Scotch thistle is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
