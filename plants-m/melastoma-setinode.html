Melastoma setinode

Melastoma setinode, commonly known as the hairy melastoma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically showy, with five petals and a distinctive calyx, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Melastoma setinode is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks.
