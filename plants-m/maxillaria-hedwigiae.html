Maxillaria hedwigiae

Maxillaria hedwigiae, commonly known as the Hedwig's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Maxillaria hedwigiae is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
