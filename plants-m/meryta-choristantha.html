Meryta choristantha

Meryta choristantha, commonly known as the Christmas tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that resemble a tree's canopy. The plant has a thick, fibrous root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically green and can grow quite large, contributing to its ornamental value.

Meryta choristantha plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
