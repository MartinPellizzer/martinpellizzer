It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Micromeria stricta and Broussonetia papyrifera, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parent species. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, papery fruits.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is often found in regions where the parent species overlap geographically.
