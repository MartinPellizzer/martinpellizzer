Marattia alata

Marattia alata, commonly known as the broad-leafed marattia, is a tropical fern belonging to the family Marattiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, called soruses, are located on the undersides of its fronds.

Marattia alata is valued for its ornamental appearance and plays a role in ecological systems by providing habitat for various small organisms.
