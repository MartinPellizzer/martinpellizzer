Marsdenia floribunda

Marsdenia floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant star vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow blossoms that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its stems are slender and twining.

Marsdenia floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
