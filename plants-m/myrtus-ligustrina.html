Myrtus ligustrina

Myrtus ligustrina, commonly known as common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its compact growth and pleasant scent.

Myrtus ligustrina is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
