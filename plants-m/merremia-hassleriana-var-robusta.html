Merremia hassleriana var. robusta

Merremia hassleriana var. robusta, commonly known as the robust morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. The plant is characterized by its strong, twisting stems and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural spread.
