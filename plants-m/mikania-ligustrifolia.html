Mikania ligustrifolia

Mikania ligustrifolia, commonly known as Mexican devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trailing habit. The plant has heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

Mikania ligustrifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is also considered an invasive species in some regions.
