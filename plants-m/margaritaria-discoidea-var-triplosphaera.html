Margaritaria discoidea var. triplosphaera

Margaritaria discoidea var. triplosphaera, commonly known as the three-spotted margarita, is a unique variant of the margarita plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding an ornamental appeal. Its leaves are typically rounded and can vary in size depending on environmental conditions.

This variant is valued both for its aesthetic qualities and its ability to thrive in challenging climates.
