Myristica discolor

Myristica discolor, commonly known as the two-colored nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruit that contains both nutmeg and mace. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a rough texture.

The tree is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
