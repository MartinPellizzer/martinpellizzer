Maruta cotula

Maruta cotula, commonly known as false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a low-growing herb with small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Maruta cotula is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
