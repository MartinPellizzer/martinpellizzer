Momordica garipensis

Momordica garipensis, commonly known as the Turkish bitter gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a vine that produces elongated, spiny fruits with a distinctive bitter taste. The plant is native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East, where it thrives in warm, arid climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically yellow with five petals.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
