Macaranga mappa

Macaranga mappa, commonly known as the white macaranga, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a fast-growing shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is often smooth and light-colored. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is known for its ability to produce large quantities of seeds, which contribute to its widespread distribution.
