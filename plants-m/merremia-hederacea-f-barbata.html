Merremia hederacea f. barbata

Merremia hederacea f. barbata, commonly known as the Barbata morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a faint scent. Its stems are hairy and can grow up to several meters in length, allowing it to climb trees and structures.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
