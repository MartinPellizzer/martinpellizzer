Mentha × kerneri var. chrysii

Mentha × kerneri var. chrysii, commonly known as the golden mint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers, which distinguish it from other mint varieties. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and has a sprawling, bushy habit. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the aroma is minty with a subtle floral note.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a decorative plant.
