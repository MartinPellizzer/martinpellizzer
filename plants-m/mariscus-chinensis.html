Mariscus chinensis

Mariscus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese marigold, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Mariscus chinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
