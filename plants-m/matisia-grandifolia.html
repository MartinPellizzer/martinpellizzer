Matisia grandifolia

Matisia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved matisia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable timber trees. The plant is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several flat, winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
