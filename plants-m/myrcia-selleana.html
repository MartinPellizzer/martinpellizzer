Myrcia selleana

Myrcia selleana, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and showy red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in moist, shaded environments.

Myrcia selleana is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
