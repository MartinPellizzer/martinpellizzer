Mentha × amaurophylla

Mentha × amaurophylla, commonly known as the black mint, is a hybrid mint species that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant features hairy, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often glandular and aromatic. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters.

Mentha × amaurophylla is notable for its strong, sharp scent and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
