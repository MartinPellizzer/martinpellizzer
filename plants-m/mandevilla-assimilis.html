Mandevilla assimilis

Mandevilla assimilis, commonly known as the flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces showy blooms throughout the growing season, often from spring to fall, depending on the climate. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to the vibrant flowers.

Mandevilla assimilis is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, sunny conditions.
