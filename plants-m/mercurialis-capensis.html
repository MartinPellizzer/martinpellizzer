Mercurialis capensis

Mercurialis capensis, commonly known as the Cape mercury, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
