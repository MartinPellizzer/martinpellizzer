Medicago hispida var. reynieri

Medicago hispida var. reynieri, commonly known as Reynier's goatweed, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with hairy surfaces that give the plant its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
