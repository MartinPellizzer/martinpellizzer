Melochia borbonica

Melochia borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon melochia, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often accompanied by bracts that enhance its ornamental value. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Melochia borbonica is native to Madagascar and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and adaptability to warm, humid climates.
