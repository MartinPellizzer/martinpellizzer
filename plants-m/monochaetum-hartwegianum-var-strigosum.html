Monochaetum hartwegianum var. strigosum

Monochaetum hartwegianum var. strigosum, commonly known as the hairy spike grass, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or swampy areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
