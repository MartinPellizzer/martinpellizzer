Macropetalum filifolium

Macropetalum filifolium, commonly known as the thread-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is characterized by a delicate, thread-like texture, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
