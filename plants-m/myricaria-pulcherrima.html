Myricaria pulcherrima

Myricaria pulcherrima, commonly known as the sacred candleplant, is a flowering shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces tiny, spherical fruits that are often used in traditional ceremonies and rituals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species in desert environments.

Its unique appearance and cultural significance make it an important plant in both ecological and anthropological contexts.
