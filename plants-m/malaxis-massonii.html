Malaxis massonii

Malaxis massonii, commonly known as the Masson's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

Malaxis massonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
