Mitella pacifica

Mitella pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific mitella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Mitella pacifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
