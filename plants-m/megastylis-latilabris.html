Megastylis latilabris

Megastylis latilabris, commonly known as the broad-lipped giant orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a broad, lip-like structure that distinguishes it from other orchid species. Its inflorescence typically consists of several blooms arranged in a loose cluster, and the leaves are broad and strap-like.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
