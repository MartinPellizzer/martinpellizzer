Masdevallia cordeliana

Masdevallia cordeliana, commonly known as the Cordeliana orchid, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with a distinctive bilaterally symmetrical shape.

Masdevallia cordeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
