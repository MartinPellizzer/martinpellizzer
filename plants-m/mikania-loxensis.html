Mikania loxensis

Mikania loxensis, commonly known as the creeping mikania, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, yellow flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces seeds in small, fluffy clusters. Mikania loxensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
