Melothria lobata

Melothria lobata, commonly known as the lobed passionfruit, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits with a distinctive lobed shape and a fragrant, acidic pulp. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The flowers are typically red or orange, with five petals and a crown of filaments.
