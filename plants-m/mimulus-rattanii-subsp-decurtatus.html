Mimulus rattanii subsp. decurtatus

Mimulus rattanii subsp.

decurtatus, commonly known as the decurrent monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental plant.
