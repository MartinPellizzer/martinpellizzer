Medinilla neoebudica

Medinilla neoebudica, commonly known as the Christmas Medinilla, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant, rose-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
