Malouetia tamaquarina var. brasiliensis

Malouetia tamaquarina var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom in the dry season. The plant has elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

The species is also valued for its ability to withstand drought conditions, making it suitable for landscaping in arid regions.
