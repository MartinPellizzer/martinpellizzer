Mentha arvensis var. obornyana

Mentha arvensis var. obornyana, commonly known as the Siberian mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia, particularly Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subarctic climates. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

The variety is distinguished by its smaller, more compact growth habit compared to other Mentha species.
