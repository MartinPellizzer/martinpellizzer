Melastoma maieta

Melastoma maieta, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a tropical berry, often referred to as a "mangosteen," and is known for its vibrant red skin and segmented white flesh.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
