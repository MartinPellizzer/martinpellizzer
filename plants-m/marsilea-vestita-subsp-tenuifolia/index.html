Marsilea Vestita Subsp. Tenuifolia

Water clover, scientifically known as Marsilea vestita subsp. tenuifolia, is a fascinating aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments. With its delicate, clover-like leaves, it adds a unique aesthetic to ponds and wetlands. This species is often mistaken for true clover due to its similar appearance, but it belongs to the water clover family. Its ability to float or grow submerged makes it a versatile addition to aquatic ecosystems. Despite its small size, it plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting aquatic life.
