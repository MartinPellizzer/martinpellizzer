Minuartia eglandulosa

Minuartia eglandulosa, commonly known as the glabrous rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming habit, with succulent, often hairless stems and leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.
