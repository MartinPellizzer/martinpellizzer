Maxillaria wrightii var. imbricata

Maxillaria wrightii var. imbricata, commonly known as the Wright's Maxillaria, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and blooms during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
