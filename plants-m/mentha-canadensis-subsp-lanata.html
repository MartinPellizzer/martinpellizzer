Mentha canadensis subsp. lanata

Mentha canadensis subsp.

lanata, commonly known as Canadian hairy mint, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and square, hairy stems.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain menthol and other volatile oils, making it useful in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
