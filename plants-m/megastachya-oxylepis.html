Megastachya oxylepis

Megastachya oxylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false aster, is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is a perennial herb native to the western United States, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flower heads that resemble daisies. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its flowering heads.

The species is adapted to thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
