Metastelma tomentosum

Metastelma tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly metastelma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a soft, velvety texture.

Metastelma tomentosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full shade.
