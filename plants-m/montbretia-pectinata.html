Montbretia pectinata

Montbretia pectinata, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive red or orange midrib, which adds to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Montbretia pectinata is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, which can make it invasive in certain environments.
