Myrcia cujabensis

Myrcia cujabensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
