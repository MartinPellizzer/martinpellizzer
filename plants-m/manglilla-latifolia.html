Manglilla latifolia

Manglilla latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf manglilla, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils near water bodies. It has a woody stem and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Manglilla latifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
