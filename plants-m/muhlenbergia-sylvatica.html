Muhlenbergia sylvatica

Muhlenbergia sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that are visually striking and often appear in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Muhlenbergia sylvatica is also noted for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
