Myrcia aromatica

Myrcia aromatica, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant produces dark red or black berries that are edible and have a sweet, fragrant flavor. Myrcia aromatica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the essential oils extracted from its leaves.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.
