Mimosa polycephala var. taxifolia

Mimosa polycephala var. taxifolia, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which are often referred to as "mimosas.". It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can improve soil fertility in gardens.
