Machilus rufipes

Machilus rufipes, commonly known as the red-haired laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, typically reddish when mature, and contains a single seed.

Machilus rufipes is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
