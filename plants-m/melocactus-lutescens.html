Melocactus lutescens

Melocactus lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-topped melon cactus, is a species native to northern South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a dense covering of white to yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the morning, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Melocactus lutescens is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.
