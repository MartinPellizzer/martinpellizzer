Maxillaria canarina

Maxillaria canarina, commonly known as the Canary orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Canary Islands.

It is a sympodial orchid with a compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically blooms in winter or early spring, and its flowers are characterized by their intricate, spurred petals. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Maxillaria canarina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to care for in a tropical or subtropical climate.
