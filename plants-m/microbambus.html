Microbambus

Microbambus, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a unique plant species characterized by its compact size and elegant appearance.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is often mistaken for a true bamboo due to its similar growth habit. Microbambus typically grows in clumps, with slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, providing a soft, textured foliage.

This plant is well-suited for small garden spaces and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
