Marattia platybasis

Marattia platybasis, commonly known as the broad-leafed marattia, is a fern belonging to the family Marattiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant typically thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of its fronds, a feature common among ferns.

Marattia platybasis is often found in humid forests and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
