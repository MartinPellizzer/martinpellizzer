Micranthemum indicum

Micranthemum indicum, commonly known as Indian watermilfoil, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. Its flowers are tiny and clustered, often appearing in dense inflorescences.

It is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various water conditions.
