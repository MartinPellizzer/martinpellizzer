Mesua racemosa

Mesua racemosa, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in dense racemes, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are usually green and shed seasonally. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Mesua racemosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
