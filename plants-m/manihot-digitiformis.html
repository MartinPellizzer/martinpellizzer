Manihot digitiformis

Manihot digitiformis, commonly known as the arrowleaf manioc, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the spurge family. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and requires careful processing before use.
