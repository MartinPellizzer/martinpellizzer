Mimosa myriocephala

Mimosa myriocephala, commonly known as the golden mimosa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple stems and a dense, bushy appearance. The plant is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets, and its showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The plant is also known for its sensitive leaves that fold when touched, a trait that adds to its unique botanical appeal.
