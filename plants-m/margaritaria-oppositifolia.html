Margaritaria oppositifolia

Margaritaria oppositifolia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly hairy texture.

Margaritaria oppositifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
