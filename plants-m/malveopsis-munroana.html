Malveopsis munroana

Malveopsis munroana, commonly known as the Munro's mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, pentagonal flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in high-altitude regions and is adapted to cooler, humid climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
