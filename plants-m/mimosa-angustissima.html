Mimosa angustissima

Mimosa angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf sensitiveplant, is a species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Mimosa angustissima is also known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
