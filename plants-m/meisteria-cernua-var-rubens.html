Meisteria cernua var. rubens

Meisteria cernua var. rubens, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins against a glossy green background. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Meisteria cernua var. rubens is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
