Malva cretica

Malva cretica, commonly known as the lesser mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces pink or purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

Malva cretica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional herbal medicine.
