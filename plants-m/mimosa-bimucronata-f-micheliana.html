Mimosa bimucronata f. micheliana

Mimosa bimucronata f. micheliana, commonly known as the Michel's sensitive plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonasty. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a dense, bushy appearance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique leaf response and delicate floral display.
