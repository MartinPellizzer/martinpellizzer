Mentha × pannonica

Mentha × pannonica, commonly known as Hungarian mint, is a hybrid mint species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a strong, minty fragrance. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with square stems and opposite, serrated leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mentha × pannonica is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its cooling and soothing properties.
