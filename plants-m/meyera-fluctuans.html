Meyera fluctuans

Meyera fluctuans, commonly known as the floating meadow plant, is a unique aquatic species characterized by its ability to thrive in shallow, still water environments.

Its leaves are broad and floating, often forming dense mats on the water surface. The plant exhibits a rapid growth rate, which allows it to colonize new areas quickly. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish in color.

Meyera fluctuans plays an important role in stabilizing water bodies by reducing erosion and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
