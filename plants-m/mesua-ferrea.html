Mesua ferrea

Mesua ferrea, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
