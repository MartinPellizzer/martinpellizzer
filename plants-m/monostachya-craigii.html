Monostachya craigii

Monostachya craigii, commonly known as the Craig's monostachya, is a rare and striking member of the Cyperaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive, narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, elongated spike of pale yellow to brownish flowers, which are typically borne on a single, central stem. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.
