Myrciaria caurensis

Myrciaria caurensis, commonly known as the Cauca myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of Colombia, specifically the Cauca Valley, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries.

Myrciaria caurensis is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
