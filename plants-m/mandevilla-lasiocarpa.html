Mandevilla lasiocarpa

Mandevilla lasiocarpa, commonly known as the wooly mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in suitable conditions.
