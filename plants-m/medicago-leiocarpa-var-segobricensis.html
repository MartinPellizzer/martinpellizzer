Medicago leiocarpa var. segobricensis

Medicago leiocarpa var. segobricensis, commonly known as the desert bluebunch broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to arid regions of the southwestern United States. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers and has a fibrous root system that attaches to the roots of its host. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the growth and health of its host plants.
