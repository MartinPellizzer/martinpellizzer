Mimosa acantholoba var. acantholoba

Mimosa acantholoba var. acantholoba, commonly known as the silky mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features compound leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
