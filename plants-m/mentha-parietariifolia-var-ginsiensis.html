Mentha parietariifolia var. ginsiensis

Mentha parietariifolia var. ginsiensis, commonly known as the Korean wall mint, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded areas, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong minty fragrance, while its flowers are small and tubular, usually purple or pink in color.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
