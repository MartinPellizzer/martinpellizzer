Maesa edulis

Maesa edulis, commonly known as the edible maesa, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are also used in local herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Maesa edulis thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.
