Millettia chrysamaryssa

Millettia chrysamaryssa, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a vibrant display during the flowering season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
