Microcerasus microcarpa f. rechingeri

Microcerasus microcarpa f. rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechingeri cherry, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red, edible berries that are popular in traditional Chinese cuisine. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
