Manihot sagittata

Manihot sagittata, commonly known as arrowleaf manioc, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its roots are used in traditional medicine. It is also cultivated for its starchy rootstock, which is processed into a food product similar to cassava.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds that require careful preparation before consumption.
