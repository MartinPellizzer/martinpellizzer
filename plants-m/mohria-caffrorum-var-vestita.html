Mohria caffrorum var. vestita

Mohria caffrorum var. vestita, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting red or purple throat. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
