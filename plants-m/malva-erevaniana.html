Malva erevaniana

Malva erevaniana, commonly known as the Armenian mallows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, pale pink to white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
