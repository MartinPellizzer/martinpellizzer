Mimosa aeschynomenes

Mimosa aeschynomenes, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The leaves are sensitive to touch, curling up when disturbed, a trait that adds to its unique botanical appeal.
