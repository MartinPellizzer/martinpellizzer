Macrotyloma katangense

Macrotyloma katangense, commonly known as the Katanga cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its nutritional seeds and potential use in sustainable agriculture.
