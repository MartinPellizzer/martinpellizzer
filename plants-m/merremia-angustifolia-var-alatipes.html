Merremia angustifolia var. alatipes

Merremia angustifolia var. alatipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct, elongated base. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom in the morning. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a sticky, fleshy coating.

This species is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
