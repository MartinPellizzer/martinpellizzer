Maximowiczia tripartita

Maximowiczia tripartita, commonly known as the three-parted grass, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers, typical of the genus Maximowiczia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
