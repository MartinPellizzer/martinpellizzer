Myosotis krylovii subsp. collucata

Myosotis krylovii subsp.

collucata, commonly known as the Siberian forget-me-not, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July.

The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native environment.
