Mentha aquatica f. aquatica

Mentha aquatica f. aquatica, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands.

Mentha aquatica is often used in herbal remedies and has a strong, minty aroma.
