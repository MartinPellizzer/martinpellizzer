Myrcia multiflora f. glaucescens

Myrcia multiflora f. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that often has a waxy, bluish-gray coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are leathery and have a strong, aromatic scent, which is typical of the Myrtaceae family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
