Myrcia neosmithii

Myrcia neosmithii, commonly known as the Smith's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, often found in moist, shaded forest understories. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Myrcia neosmithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
