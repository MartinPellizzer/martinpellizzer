Muehlenbeckia juncea

Muehlenbeckia juncea, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to form dense mats. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, silvery-gray in color, and covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Muehlenbeckia juncea is often used in landscaping for erosion control due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
