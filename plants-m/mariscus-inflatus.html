Mariscus inflatus

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that aid in buoyancy and trap small aquatic organisms. Its morphology includes small, leafless stems with specialized bladders that function as traps for prey.

Mariscus inflatus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microscopic life.
