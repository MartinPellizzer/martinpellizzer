Mucuna ceramensis

Mucuna ceramensis, commonly known as the Ceram velvet bean, is a climbing leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Mucuna ceramensis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
