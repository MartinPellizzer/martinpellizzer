Mila caespitosa f. albisaetacens

Mila caespitosa f. albisaetacens, commonly known as the white-haired mila, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers in composite inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly encountered in its natural habitat.
