Mentha × kerneri f. thuringiaca

Mentha × kerneri f. thuringiaca, commonly known as Thuringian mint, is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is native to Central Europe and is often found in the region of Thuringia, which gives it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
