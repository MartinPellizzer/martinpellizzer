Moquilea turbinata

Moquilea turbinata, commonly known as the Turbaned Moquilea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, turban-shaped inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the dry season. It has adapted to survive in harsh, saline soils through specialized root systems and water-conserving mechanisms.

Moquilea turbinata is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
