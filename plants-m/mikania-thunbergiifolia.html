Mikania thunbergiifolia

Mikania thunbergiifolia, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive tubular shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
