Mertensia brevistyla var. obtusiloba

Mertensia brevistyla var. obtusiloba, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features lance-shaped, often hairy leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical variety. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers in early spring, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
