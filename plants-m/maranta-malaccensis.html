Maranta malaccensis

Maranta malaccensis, commonly known as the Malay arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are borne on short, erect stalks. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their starchy roots, which are used in traditional cooking.

Maranta malaccensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
