Myrcia velutiflora

Myrcia velutiflora, commonly known as velvet-flowered myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in dense forests or understorey environments.

Myrcia velutiflora is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
