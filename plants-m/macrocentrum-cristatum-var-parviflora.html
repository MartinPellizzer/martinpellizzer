Macrocentrum cristatum var. parviflora

Macrocentrum cristatum var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in dense, conical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The variety parviflora is distinguished by its smaller flowers compared to the typical form of Macrocentrum cristatum.
