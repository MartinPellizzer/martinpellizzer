Myrceugenia bracteosa var. ibitipocensis

Myrceugenia bracteosa var. ibitipocensis, commonly known as the Ibitipoca myrtle, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its lanceolate, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and rocky outcrops.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an endemic species contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
