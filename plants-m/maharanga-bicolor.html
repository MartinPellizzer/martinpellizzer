Maharanga bicolor

Maharanga bicolor, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling trumpets. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 20 meters tall. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
