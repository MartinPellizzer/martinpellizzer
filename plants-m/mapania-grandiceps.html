Mapania grandiceps

Mapania grandiceps, commonly known as the giant mapania, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. This plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. It produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red berries.

Mapania grandiceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
