Myrcia magnoliifolia var. angustifolia

Myrcia magnoliifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its elliptic to lanceolate leaves, which are typically narrow and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or red, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
