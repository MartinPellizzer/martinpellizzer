Mallotus sanguirensis

Mallotus sanguirensis, commonly known as the red-flowered mallotus, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its striking red flowers that appear in clusters, contrasting with its green foliage. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
