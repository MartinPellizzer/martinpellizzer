Micranthes clusii subsp. clusii

Micranthes clusii subsp.

clusii, commonly known as the clustered micranthes, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
