Mentha arvensis var. austriaca

Mentha arvensis var. austriaca, commonly known as Austrian mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Austrian mint is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
