Myrtus elegans

Myrtus elegans, commonly known as the common myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, glossy leaves and produces clusters of tiny white flowers in spring. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and berries, which are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine. Myrtus elegans thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a long lifespan, often reaching up to 30 years in cultivation.
