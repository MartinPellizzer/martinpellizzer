Mystroxylon filiforme

Mystroxylon filiforme, commonly known as the slender screwwood, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, spiral-shaped stems that resemble a helix, which helps it climb and access sunlight. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, adapted for efficient water conservation in humid environments.

Mystroxylon filiforme is often found in the understory of dense forests, where it plays a role in the ecosystem by providing shelter for small animals and insects.
