Myodocarpus crassifolius

Myodocarpus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf myodocarpus, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form and dense foliage.

The species is adapted to the dry, arid conditions of its native habitat, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
