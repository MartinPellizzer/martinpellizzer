Mentha × dumetorum var. lugosiensis

Mentha × dumetorum var. lugosiensis, commonly known as the Hungarian or Hungarian mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy leaves and square, hairy stems typical of the Mentha genus. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its use in herbal teas and traditional medicine due to its potent essential oils.
