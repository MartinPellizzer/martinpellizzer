Merathrepta spicata

Merathrepta spicata, commonly known as the spike-leaved merathrepta, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in its harsh environment. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Merathrepta spicata is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
