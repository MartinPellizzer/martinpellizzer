Masdevallia bicornis

Masdevallia bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated petals that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Masdevallia bicornis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
