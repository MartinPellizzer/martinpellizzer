Muraltia paludosa

Muraltia paludosa, commonly known as swamp acacia, is a deciduous shrub native to wetland habitats in South America.

It typically grows in swampy areas and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features small, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds.

Muraltia paludosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
