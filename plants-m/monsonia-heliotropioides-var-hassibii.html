Monsonia heliotropioides var. hassibii

Monsonia heliotropioides var. hassibii, commonly known as the Cape rose or African rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that resemble those of the rose, blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in Mediterranean climates.
