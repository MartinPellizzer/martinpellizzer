Mimulus guttatus var. platycalyx

Mimulus guttatus var. platycalyx, commonly known as the broad-calyx yellow monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features striking yellow flowers with broad, rounded calyces that enclose the buds before they open. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variant is notable for its distinctive floral morphology and ecological adaptability.
