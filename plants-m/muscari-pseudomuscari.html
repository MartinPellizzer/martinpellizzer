Muscari pseudomuscari

Muscari pseudomuscari, commonly known as the Persian gladiola, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, and its flowers are often arranged in a loose, racemose inflorescence. Muscari pseudomuscari is well-suited for rock gardens and woodland settings due to its compact size and ornamental value.

It is also notable for its ability to naturalize and spread over time in suitable climates.
