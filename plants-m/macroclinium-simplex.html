Macroclinium simplex

Macroclinium simplex, commonly known as the slender macroclinium, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
