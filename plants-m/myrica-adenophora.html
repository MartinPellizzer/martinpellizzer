Myrica adenophora

Myrica adenophora, commonly known as the hairy waxmyrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that develop into small, dark blue berries. These berries are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.

Myrica adenophora is also valued for its aromatic foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
