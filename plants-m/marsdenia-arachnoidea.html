Marsdenia arachnoidea

Marsdenia arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Its fibrous roots and woody stems make it a hardy species often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
