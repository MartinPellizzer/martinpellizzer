Malva lecontei

Malva lecontei, commonly known as Leconte's mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton, hibiscus, and okra. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
