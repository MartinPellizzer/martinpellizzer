Magnolia roraimae

Magnolia roraimae, commonly known as the Roraima magnolia, is a rare and striking plant native to the tepuis of Venezuela and Guyana.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
