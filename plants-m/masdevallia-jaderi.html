Masdevallia jaderi

Masdevallia jaderi, commonly known as the Jader's masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flower structure with a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid air.

Masdevallia jaderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
