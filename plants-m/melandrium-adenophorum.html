Melandrium adenophorum

Melandrium adenophorum, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Melandrium adenophorum is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
