Melocactus acunae

Melocactus acunae, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a crown of spiny, leaf-like structures that are often mistaken for leaves. The plant produces a single, fleshy, red or orange fruit that resembles an apple, which is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor. Melocactus acunae is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and gardeners.
