Mallotus tetracoccus

Mallotus tetracoccus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant has been historically valued for its hard, durable wood used in traditional craftsmanship and furniture.
