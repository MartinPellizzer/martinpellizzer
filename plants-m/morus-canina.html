Morus canina

Morus canina, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its roots are extensive and often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its role in silk production, as its leaves are a primary food source for silkworms.
