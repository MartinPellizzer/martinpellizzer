Mentha arvensis f. tenuifolia

Mentha arvensis f. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a subspecies of Mentha arvensis, characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, slightly hairy stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, fields, and along stream banks.

Its aromatic properties make it a valued plant in both traditional medicine and culinary applications.
