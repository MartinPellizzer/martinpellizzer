Matthiola ovatifolia

Matthiola ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stock, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are narrow and ovate, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Matthiola ovatifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
