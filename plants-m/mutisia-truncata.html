Mutisia truncata

Mutisia truncata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-purple flower heads. The plant features a short, woody stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mutisia truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
