Merremia candei

Merremia candei, commonly known as the yellow bignay, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
