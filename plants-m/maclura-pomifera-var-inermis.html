Maclura pomifera var. inermis

Maclura pomifera var. inermis, commonly known as the naked-fruited gooseberry, is a variant of the common gooseberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to North America, characterized by its thornless stems and yellowish-brown bark. The plant produces small, round, inedible fruits that are typically yellow to orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy species within the genus Maclura.
