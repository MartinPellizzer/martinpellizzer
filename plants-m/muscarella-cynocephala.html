Muscarella cynocephala

Muscarella cynocephala, commonly known as the monkey head orchid, is a unique and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often red or orange-colored pseudobulbs that resemble a monkey's head, giving it its common name. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green with contrasting purple or red markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, Muscarella cynocephala is often cultivated in specialized horticultural collections.
