Massonia bifolia

Massonia bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved massonia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
