Mentha × rotundifolia subsp. macrostachya

Mentha × rotundifolia subsp.

macrostachya, commonly known as round-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its rounded, broad leaves and fragrant, slightly minty aroma.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its use in herbal teas and as a culinary herb.
