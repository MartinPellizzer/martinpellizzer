Maxillaria henrici-gustavi

Maxillaria henrici-gustavi, commonly known as the Gustavus Maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, featuring petals that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
