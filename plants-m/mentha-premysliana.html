Mentha premysliana

Mentha premysliana, commonly known as the Czech mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or blue, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Mentha premysliana is valued for its aromatic leaves, which contain essential oils used in traditional medicine and herbal remedies.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
