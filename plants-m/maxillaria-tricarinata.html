Maxillaria tricarinata

Maxillaria tricarinata, commonly known as the three-ribbed maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its flowers are known for their intricate patterns and strong scent. Maxillaria tricarinata prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
