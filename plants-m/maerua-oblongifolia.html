Maerua oblongifolia

Maerua oblongifolia, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It features small, oblong leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in harsh conditions.

Maerua oblongifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
