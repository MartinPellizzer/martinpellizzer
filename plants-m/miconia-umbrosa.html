Miconia umbrosa

Miconia umbrosa, commonly known as the darkleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Neotropical region.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive, three-lobed berries. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
