Malva nepalensis

Malva nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
