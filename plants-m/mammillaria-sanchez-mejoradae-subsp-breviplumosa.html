Mammillaria sanchez-mejoradae subsp. breviplumosa

Mammillaria sanchez-mejoradae subsp.

breviplumosa, commonly known as the short-stemmed mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to light green tubercles.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
