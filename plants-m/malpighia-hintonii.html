Malpighia hintonii

Malpighia hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's acuba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture and a distinctive scent.

The flowers are small and clustered, often with five petals and a prominent stamen.
