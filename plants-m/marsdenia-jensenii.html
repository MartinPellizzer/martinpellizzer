Marsdenia jensenii

Marsdenia jensenii, commonly known as the Jensen's Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and woody stems. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in its native range.
