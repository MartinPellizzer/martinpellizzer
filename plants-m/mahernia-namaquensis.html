Mahernia namaquensis

Mahernia namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua starling or Namaqua starling plant, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to store water in its fleshy, branching stems. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the winter months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically small, pointed, and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Mahernia namaquensis is well adapted to harsh, dry conditions and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
