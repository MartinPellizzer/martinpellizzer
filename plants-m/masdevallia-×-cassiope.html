Masdevallia × cassiope

Masdevallia × cassiope, commonly known as the hybrid masdevallia, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a hybrid between Masdevallia leucoloma and Masdevallia sanguinea, resulting in a plant with unique floral characteristics. The plant typically produces intricate, colorful flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a velvety texture. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its foliage consists of narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
