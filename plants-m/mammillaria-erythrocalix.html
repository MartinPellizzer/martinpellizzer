Mammillaria erythrocalix

Mammillaria erythrocalix, commonly known as the red-capped mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and bright red flowers that appear at the apex of the plant. The plant has a dense covering of white to pale blue spines, which provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color and bloom during the summer months.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
