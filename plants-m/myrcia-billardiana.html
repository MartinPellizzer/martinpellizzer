Myrcia billardiana

Myrcia billardiana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Myrcia billardiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, posing challenges to local ecosystems.
