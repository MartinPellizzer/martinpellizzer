Melicope savaiensis

Melicope savaiensis, commonly known as the Savaii melicope, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Savaii in Samoa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The tree is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
