Mentha × dalmatica f. thuringiaca

Mentha × dalmatica f. thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian mint, is a hybrid perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its strong menthol scent and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.

Its hybrid origin contributes to its adaptability and resilience in various garden settings.
