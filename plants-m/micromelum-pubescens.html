Micromelum pubescens

Micromelum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, yellowish fruits that are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves and fruits contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in both horticulture and pharmacology.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
