Mentha canadensis var. glabrata

Mentha canadensis var. glabrata, commonly known as smooth mint, is a variant of the American mint species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America, particularly found in moist woodlands and stream banks. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically darker green and less hairy than the typical variety. Its fragrant, tubular flowers are pale blue to purple and bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
