Myrica esculenta var. esculenta

Myrica esculenta var. esculenta, commonly known as the Indian bay leaf or the wild bay tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine and cooking. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and economic purposes.
