Millettia leptocarpa

Millettia leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender-stemmed millettia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
