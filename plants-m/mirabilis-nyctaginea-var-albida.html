Mirabilis nyctaginea var. albida

Mirabilis nyctaginea var. albida, commonly known as the white four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white, tubular flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that are typically pink or red in other varieties but remain white in this variant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

The foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
