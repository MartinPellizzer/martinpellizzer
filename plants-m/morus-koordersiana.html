Morus koordersiana

Morus koordersiana, commonly known as the Koord's mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and exhibits a slow growth rate. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces a distinctive, sweet-smelling scent during flowering.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche.
