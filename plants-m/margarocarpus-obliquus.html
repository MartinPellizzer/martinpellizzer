Margarocarpus obliquus

Margarocarpus obliquus, commonly known as the zigzagging grass, is a unique plant species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging growth pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Margarocarpus obliquus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
