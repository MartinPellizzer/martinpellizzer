Malva phoenicea

Malva phoenicea, commonly known as the Palestine mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate saline conditions.

Malva phoenicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
