Masdevallia calcarata

Masdevallia calcarata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful flower structure. The plant typically produces a single, showy inflorescence with elongated, spurred flowers that are adapted for pollination by specific insects. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette and are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

Masdevallia calcarata thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing on rock faces or in shaded forest areas.
