Miltonioides reichenheimii

Miltonioides reichenheimii, commonly known as the pink velvet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety pink petals and a contrasting white lip, which make it highly ornamental. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, often blooming during the summer months.

Due to its beauty and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice for both indoor and greenhouse settings.
