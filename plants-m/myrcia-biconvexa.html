Myrcia biconvexa

Myrcia biconvexa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and attract various wildlife. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove.

Myrcia biconvexa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
