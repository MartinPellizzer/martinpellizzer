Mentha arvensis subsp. fontana

Mentha arvensis subsp.

fontana, commonly known as field mint or wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are usually oval to lance-shaped with a serrated margin and emit a strong mint aroma.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic and slightly minty flavor.
