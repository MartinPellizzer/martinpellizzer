Malcolmia aegyptiaca var. dasycarpa

Malcolmia aegyptiaca var. dasycarpa, commonly known as Egyptian malcolmia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy, and it produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The fruit is a capsule that is densely hairy, giving the variety its specific name *dasycarpa*, meaning "hairy fruit.".

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
