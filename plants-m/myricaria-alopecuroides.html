Myricaria alopecuroides

Myricaria alopecuroides, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in summer. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access groundwater in harsh conditions.

Myricaria alopecuroides is adapted to saline soils and is often found in coastal or desert areas.
