Menodora pubens

Menodora pubens, commonly known as the hairy menodora, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Menodoridae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems covered in fine, pubescent hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and also bear a hairy texture.

Menodora pubens is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
