Mentha × kerneri var. sabranskyi

Mentha × kerneri var. sabranskyi, commonly known as Siberian mint, is a hybrid plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing scent and flavor.
