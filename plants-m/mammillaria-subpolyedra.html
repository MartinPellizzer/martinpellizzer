Mammillaria subpolyedra

Mammillaria subpolyedra, commonly known as the clustered mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is often covered in white to yellowish spines. The plant features areoles, which are the points from which spines, flowers, and branches emerge. It produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
