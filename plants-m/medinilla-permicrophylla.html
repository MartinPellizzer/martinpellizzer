Medinilla permicrophylla

Medinilla permicrophylla, commonly known as the tiny-leaf medinilla, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is dense and evergreen, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

The flowers bloom during the warmer months, adding ornamental value to the plant.
