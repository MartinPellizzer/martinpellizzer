Margaritopsis paupertina

Margaritopsis paupertina, commonly known as the poor man's margarita, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while its flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
