Mosla japonica f. thymolifera

Mosla japonica f. thymolifera, commonly known as Japanese thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a strong thymol scent, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
