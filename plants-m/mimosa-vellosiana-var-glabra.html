Mimosa vellosiana var. glabra

Mimosa vellosiana var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Brazil.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves that are typically pinnately divided. The plant produces small, yellow, ball-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often in response to touch or light. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its delicate appearance and sensitive flowering response, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
