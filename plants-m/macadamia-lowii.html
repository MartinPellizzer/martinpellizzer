Macadamia lowii

Macadamia lowii, commonly known as the Hawaiian macadamia, is a species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen habit and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces unique, woody capsules that contain the nutrient-rich seeds used in commercial nut products. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and requires a humid environment.

Due to its ornamental value and economic importance, it is cultivated in various regions beyond its native habitat.
