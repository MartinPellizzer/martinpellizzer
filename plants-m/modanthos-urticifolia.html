Modanthos urticifolia

Modanthos urticifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, stinging leaves and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes other stinging plants like common nettle. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
