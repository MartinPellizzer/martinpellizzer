Mohrodendron carolinum var. meehanii

Mohrodendron carolinum var. meehanii, commonly known as the Carolina mohrodendron, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas or along streambanks. It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are a food source for various birds and wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
