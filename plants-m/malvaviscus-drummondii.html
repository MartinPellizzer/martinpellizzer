Malvaviscus drummondii

Malvaviscus drummondii, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with hairy, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
