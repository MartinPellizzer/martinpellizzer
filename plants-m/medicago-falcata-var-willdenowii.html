Medicago falcata var. willdenowii

Medicago falcata var. willdenowii, commonly known as the willdenow's sicklevetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and drought conditions. It is often found in grasslands, disturbed sites, and along roadsides.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
