Malvaviscus arboreus var. sagranus

Malvaviscus arboreus var. sagranus, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a reddish tinge, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade in well-drained soil.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
