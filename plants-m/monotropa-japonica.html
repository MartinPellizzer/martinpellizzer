Monotropa japonica

Monotropa japonica, commonly known as the Japanese ghost plant, is a parasitic flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on fungi to obtain nutrients from the roots of trees and shrubs. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Despite its parasitic nature, it plays a role in forest ecosystems by supporting various insect and fungal life.
