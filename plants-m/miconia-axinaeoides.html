Miconia axinaeoides

Miconia axinaeoides, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or false rubber tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, followed by distinctive, star-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
