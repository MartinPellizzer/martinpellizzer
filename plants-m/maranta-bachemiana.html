Maranta bachemiana

Maranta bachemiana, commonly known as the Bachem's arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important species. The plant is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale yellow, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Maranta bachemiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
