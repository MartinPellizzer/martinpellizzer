Myrcia prunifolia

Myrcia prunifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, cherry-like red fruits that are edible. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a fragrant scent, while its flowers are white or pink and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Myrcia prunifolia is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
