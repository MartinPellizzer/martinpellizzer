Mentha gentilis var. crispa

Mentha gentilis var. crispa, commonly known as the wrinkled mint, is a variant of the Mentha gentilis species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves that give it a unique appearance. This plant is native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. The leaves are aromatic, with a strong minty scent, and the plant produces small, pale blue flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
