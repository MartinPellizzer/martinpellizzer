Myrcia anomala var. multiceps

Myrcia anomala var. multiceps, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some uses in herbal remedies.
