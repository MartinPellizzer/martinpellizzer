Maihuenia tehuelches

Maihuenia tehuelches, commonly known as the Andean ice plant, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid and cold environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the short growing season. It is adapted to rocky soils and can withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Maihuenia tehuelches plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife.
