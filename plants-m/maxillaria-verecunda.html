Maxillaria verecunda

Maxillaria verecunda, commonly known as the "Carnival Orchid," is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, fleshy leaf and produces showy, colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The flowers are typically clustered in racemes and have a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination. This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
