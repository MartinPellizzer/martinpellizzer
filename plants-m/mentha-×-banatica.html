Mentha × banatica

Mentha × banatica, commonly known as banana mint, is a hybrid mint species originating from Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple at the base. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its aromatic leaves and mild mint flavor, it is occasionally used in culinary and medicinal applications.
