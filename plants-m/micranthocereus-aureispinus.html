Micranthocereus aureispinus

Micranthocereus aureispinus, commonly known as the golden-spined cactus, is a species native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered in clusters of spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
