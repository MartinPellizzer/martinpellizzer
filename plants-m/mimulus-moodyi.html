Mimulus moodyi

Mimulus moodyi, commonly known as Moody's monkeyflower, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky soils along streambanks and meadows. The plant features showy, orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds.

Mimulus moodyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
