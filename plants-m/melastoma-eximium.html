Melastoma eximium

Melastoma eximium, commonly known as the red-flowered melastoma, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and showy, clustered red or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
