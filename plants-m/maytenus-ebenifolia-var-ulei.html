Maytenus ebenifolia var. ulei

Maytenus ebenifolia var. ulei, commonly known as the "Ulei Mayten" or "Maytenus ulei," is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a glossy, dark green upper surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health applications.
