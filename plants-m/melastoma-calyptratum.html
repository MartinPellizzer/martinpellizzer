Melastoma calyptratum

Melastoma calyptratum, commonly known as the purple-flowered Melastoma, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often woody and branched. It is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.
