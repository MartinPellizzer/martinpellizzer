Matthiola exigua

Matthiola exigua, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with distinctive four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, featuring small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Matthiola exigua is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its compact growth and prolonged blooming period.
