Meibomia strobilacea

Meibomia strobilacea, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Meibomia strobilacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
