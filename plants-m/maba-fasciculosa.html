Maba fasciculosa

Maba fasciculosa, commonly known as the clustered maba, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in fascicles, giving it its scientific name. Its latex is milky and contains compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Maba fasciculosa is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and traditional uses.
