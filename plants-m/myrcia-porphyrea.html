Myrcia porphyrea

Myrcia porphyrea, commonly known as the purple myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain aromatic seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Myrcia porphyrea is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
