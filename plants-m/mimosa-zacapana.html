Mimosa zacapana

Mimosa zacapana, commonly known as the Mexican sensitive plant, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique sensitivity to touch and light makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
