Madhuca barbata

Madhuca barbata, commonly known as the hairy tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, grey bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
