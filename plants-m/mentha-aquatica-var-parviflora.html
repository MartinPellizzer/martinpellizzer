Mentha aquatica var. parviflora

Mentha aquatica var. parviflora, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in wet soils near streams, marshes, and ponds, and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
