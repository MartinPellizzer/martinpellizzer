Myristica fallax

Myristica fallax, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, fragrant foliage and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its most notable botanical feature is the dual-purpose seed, which contains both the nutmeg and mace, used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its valuable spices.
