Malva africana subsp. bicolor

Malva africana subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the African mallows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features showy, palmate leaves with a velvety texture and produces vibrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive two-colored petals, with the outer part being white and the inner part a deeper hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
