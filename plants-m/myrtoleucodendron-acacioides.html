Myrtoleucodendron acacioides

Myrtoleucodendron acacioides, commonly known as the silverleaf myrtle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and myrtle species. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery-gray waxy coating, providing a distinctive appearance. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the summer months.

The plant is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
