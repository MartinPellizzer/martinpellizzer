Matricaria inodora var. nana

Matricaria inodora var. nana, commonly known as dwarf chamomile, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that emit a mild chamomile scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping and herbal remedies.
