Mazus crassifolius

Mazus crassifolius, commonly known as broadleaf mazus, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features basal rosettes of thick, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its succulent leaves and compact growth habit make it an attractive, low-maintenance plant for gardeners.
