Myodocarpus involucratus var. leratii

Myodocarpus involucratus var. leratii, commonly known as the Lerat's myodocarpus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
