Machaerina iris

Machaerina iris, commonly known as the New Zealand iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream sides. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Machaerina iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
