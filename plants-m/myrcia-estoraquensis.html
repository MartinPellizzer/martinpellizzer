Myrcia estoraquensis

Myrcia estoraquensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest biome of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible berries.

Myrcia estoraquensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
