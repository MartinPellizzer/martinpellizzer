Mentha allionii

Mentha allionii, commonly known as allion's mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated margin and emit a strong minty fragrance.

Mentha allionii is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, though it is less commonly cultivated than other mint species.
