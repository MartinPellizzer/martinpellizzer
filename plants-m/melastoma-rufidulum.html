Melastoma rufidulum

Melastoma rufidulum, commonly known as the red-stemmed melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with reddish veins. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that are typically followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Melastoma rufidulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
