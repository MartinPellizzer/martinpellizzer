Manihot brachyloba

Manihot brachyloba, commonly known as the short-leaf arrowleaf, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its latex is milky and can be toxic, which serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
