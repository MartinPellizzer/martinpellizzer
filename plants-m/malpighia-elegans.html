Malpighia elegans

Malpighia elegans, commonly known as the beauty berry, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red berries that appear in clusters, which are edible but have a tart, slightly acidic flavor. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture.

Malpighia elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
