Millettia harmandii

Millettia harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's sycamore, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
