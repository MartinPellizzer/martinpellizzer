Mentha longifolia subsp. aequifrons

Mentha longifolia subsp.

aequifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a strong mint scent.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other members of the Mentha genus.
