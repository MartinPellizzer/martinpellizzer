Marsdenia amazonica

Marsdenia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or greenish flowers, which are typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along riverbanks or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
