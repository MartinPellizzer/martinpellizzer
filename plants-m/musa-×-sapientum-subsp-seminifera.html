Musa × sapientum subsp. seminifera

Musa × sapientum subsp.

seminifera, commonly known as the seedless banana, is a subspecies of the common banana plant. It is characterized by its large, elongated, and typically yellow or green berries that are rich in nutrients.

Unlike typical bananas, this subspecies does not produce viable seeds, making it primarily cultivated for its fruit rather than propagation. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

It is widely grown for its edible fruit, which is a staple in many cuisines around the world.
