Myrcia pilosa

Myrcia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. Myrcia pilosa typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
