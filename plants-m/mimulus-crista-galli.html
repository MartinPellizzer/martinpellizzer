Mimulus crista-galli

Mimulus crista-galli, commonly known as the crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky inflorescence that resembles a crown, hence its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with a bilabiate structure typical of the Plantaginaceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Mimulus crista-galli is often used in wildflower gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
