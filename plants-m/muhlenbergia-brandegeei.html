Muhlenbergia brandegeei

Muhlenbergia brandegeei, commonly known as the desert muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage that helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread across dry landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is often used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
