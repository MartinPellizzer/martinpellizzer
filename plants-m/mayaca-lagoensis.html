Mayaca lagoensis

Mayaca lagoensis, commonly known as the Amazonian water plant, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems that often grow in water or damp soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and spirally arranged, aiding in its aquatic adaptation.

Mayaca lagoensis is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
