Mentha patrinii

Mentha patrinii, commonly known as Siberian mint, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has a distinctive menthol scent. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs.

Mentha patrinii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its cooling and soothing effects.
