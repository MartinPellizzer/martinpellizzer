Micranthes divaricata

Micranthes divaricata, commonly known as the clustered peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive, slightly heart-shaped base. Its flowers are tiny, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are often pink or purple in color.

It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
