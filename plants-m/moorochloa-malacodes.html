Moorochloa malacodes

Moorochloa malacodes, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice in landscaping.
