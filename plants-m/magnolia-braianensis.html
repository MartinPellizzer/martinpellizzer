Magnolia braianensis

Magnolia braianensis, commonly known as the Brazilian magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are broadly ovate with a smooth margin. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
