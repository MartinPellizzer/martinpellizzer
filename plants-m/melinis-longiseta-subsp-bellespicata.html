Melinis longiseta subsp. bellespicata

Melinis longiseta subsp.

bellespicata, commonly known as the purpletop grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive purple-tinted flower spikes that appear in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it is well adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with the leaves often having a bluish-gray hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
