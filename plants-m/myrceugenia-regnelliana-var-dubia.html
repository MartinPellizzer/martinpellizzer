Myrceugenia regnelliana var. dubia

Myrceugenia regnelliana var. dubia, commonly known as the coastal myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and glossy, and its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal forests, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Myrceugenia regnelliana var. dubia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
