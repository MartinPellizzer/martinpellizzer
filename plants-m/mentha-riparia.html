Mentha riparia

Mentha riparia, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist environments in North America.

It thrives in stream banks, wet meadows, and other waterlogged habitats, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic conditions. The plant has square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers bloom in whorled clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mentha riparia is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies due to its menthol-like compounds.
