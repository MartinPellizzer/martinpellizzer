Medica arborea

Medica arborea, commonly known as the medicinal tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its roots and leaves are traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.
