Mentha pastoris

Mentha pastoris, commonly known as the meadow mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty scent, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Mentha pastoris is often used in traditional medicine for its purported cooling and digestive properties.
