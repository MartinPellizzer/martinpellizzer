Mikania catharinensis

Mikania catharinensis, commonly known as Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and helical twisting stems. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Mikania catharinensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
