Melocactus sergipensis

Melocactus sergipensis, commonly known as the Sergipe melon cactus, is a species native to Brazil, particularly found in the states of Sergipe and Bahia.

It is a small, globular cactus with a thick, ribbed stem that typically grows to a height of about 20 centimeters. The plant produces a single, fleshy, spiny fruit that resembles a melon, which is edible when ripe. Its spines are usually short and white, and the plant is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Melocactus sergipensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
