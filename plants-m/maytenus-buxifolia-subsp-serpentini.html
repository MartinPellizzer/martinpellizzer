Maytenus buxifolia subsp. serpentini

Maytenus buxifolia subsp.

serpentini, commonly known as the Serpent's Tail Mayten, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with dark green leaves that have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its unique leaf shape and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
