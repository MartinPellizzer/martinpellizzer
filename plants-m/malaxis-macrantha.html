Malaxis macrantha

Malaxis macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dark green, linear leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with contrasting purple or brown markings. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as forest undergrowth and rock crevices.

Malaxis macrantha is notable for its intricate floral structure and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
