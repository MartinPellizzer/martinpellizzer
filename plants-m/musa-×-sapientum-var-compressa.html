Musa × sapientum var. compressa

Musa × sapientum var. compressa, commonly known as the Chinese banana, is a cultivar of the banana plant characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, fleshy pseudostems.

It produces small, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green, though some varieties may ripen to a yellow or orange color. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a waxy surface, typical of the Musa genus. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit, which has a sweet, creamy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, fertile soil for optimal growth.
