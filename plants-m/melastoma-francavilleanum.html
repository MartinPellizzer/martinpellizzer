Melastoma francavilleanum

Melastoma francavilleanum, commonly known as the Madagascar melastoma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and purple. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
