Mentha × piperita var. hircina

Mentha × piperita var. hircina, commonly known as peppermint-hircina, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and strong menthol scent.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are widely used in herbal teas, aromatherapy, and as a natural remedy for digestive issues.

This variety is known for its vigorous growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, low-maintenance plant.
