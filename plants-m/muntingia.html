Muntingia

Muntingia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or muntingia calabura, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth upper surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, cherry-like fruits that are edible. The fruits are often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages due to their sweet, tart flavor.

Muntingia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
