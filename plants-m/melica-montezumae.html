Melica montezumae

Melica montezumae, commonly known as the Montezuma grass, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Central America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Melica montezumae is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native habitats.
