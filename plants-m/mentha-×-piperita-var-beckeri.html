Mentha × piperita var. beckeri

Mentha × piperita var. beckeri, commonly known as Becher's peppermint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves and strong menthol scent.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with square stems and opposite, lance-shaped foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Becher's peppermint is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy, herbal remedies, and flavoring.
