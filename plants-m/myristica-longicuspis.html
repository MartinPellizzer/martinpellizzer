Myristica longicuspis

Myristica longicuspis, commonly known as the long-cusped nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is closely related to the nutmeg tree, Myristica fragrans. The plant produces large, oval-shaped fruits that contain a single seed, which is encased in a hard, aromatic shell. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The tree is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.
