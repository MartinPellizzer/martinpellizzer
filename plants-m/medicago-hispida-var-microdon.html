Medicago hispida var. microdon

Medicago hispida var. microdon, commonly known as the narrowleaf sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a hairy stem and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is used in some areas for forage and soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
