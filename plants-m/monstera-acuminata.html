Monstera acuminata

Monstera acuminata, commonly known as the Swiss cheese plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop fenestrations or holes as they mature. The plant has a thick, aerial rhizome that allows it to climb and spread along supports. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a slightly glossy surface.

Monstera acuminata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
