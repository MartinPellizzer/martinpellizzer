Mariscus hassleri

Mariscus hassleri, commonly known as the Hassler's mariscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
