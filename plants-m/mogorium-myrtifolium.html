Mogorium myrtifolium

Mogorium myrtifolium, commonly known as the fragrant myrtle-leaved plant, is a species native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that resemble those of the myrtle plant, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal uses.

Mogorium myrtifolium thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
