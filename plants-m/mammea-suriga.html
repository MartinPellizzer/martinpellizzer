Mammea suriga

Mammea suriga, commonly known as the Philippine mammea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Mammea suriga is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
