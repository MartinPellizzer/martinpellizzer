Machaerium guaremalense

Machaerium guaremalense, commonly known as the Guaremalense Machaerium, is a tropical shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters.

Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
