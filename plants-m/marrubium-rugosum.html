Marrubium rugosum

Marrubium rugosum, commonly known as rough horehound, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Marrubium rugosum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and expectorant qualities.
