Macrochordion vanhoutteanum

Macrochordion vanhoutteanum, commonly known as the Vanhoutte's macrochordion, is a species of aquatic plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for small aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its root system helps stabilize the sediment and improve water quality by filtering impurities.
