Maxillaria crassifolia

Maxillaria crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hair. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically during the summer months. It thrives in humid, well-draining soil and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Maxillaria crassifolia is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
