Magnolia septipetala

Magnolia septipetala, commonly known as the seven-petaled magnolia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has large, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers consist of seven petals, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.

Magnolia septipetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and arboretums.
