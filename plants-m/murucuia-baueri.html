Murucuia baueri

Murucuia baueri, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or golden apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow-orange fruits that resemble star shapes. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruits are edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and are often used in local cuisines.

Murucuia baueri is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for decorative purposes.
