Mannopappus

Mannopappus, commonly known as the "spider plant," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by bracts that resemble petals. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, with a slightly hairy texture.

Mannopappus is known for its adaptability and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
