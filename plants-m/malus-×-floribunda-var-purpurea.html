Malus × floribunda var. purpurea

Malus × floribunda var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple leaf sand cherry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring vibrant purple foliage that contrasts with its white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters, and is native to the United States. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
