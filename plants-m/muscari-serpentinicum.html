Muscari serpentinicum

Muscari serpentinicum, commonly known as the serpent's head squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces ornamental, fragrant blooms in late winter to early spring.

Muscari serpentinicum is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a hardy ornamental plant.
