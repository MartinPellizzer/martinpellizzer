Miconia cubacinerea

Miconia cubacinerea, commonly known as the Cuban miconia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers, which are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are bright red berries that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal by animals.

Miconia cubacinerea is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
