Mertensia pterocarpa

Mertensia pterocarpa, commonly known as the mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves contain volatile oils that contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Mertensia pterocarpa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic benefits.
