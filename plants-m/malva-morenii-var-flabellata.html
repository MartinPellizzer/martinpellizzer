Malva morenii var. flabellata

Malva morenii var. flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate mallows, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
