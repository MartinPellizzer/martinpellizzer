Myrcia lanceolata var. racemosa

Myrcia lanceolata var. racemosa, commonly known as the racemose myrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in racemose inflorescences. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
