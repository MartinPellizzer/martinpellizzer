Myrtus aeruginosa

Myrtus aeruginosa, commonly known as the silver myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. Myrtus aeruginosa is typically found in rocky soils and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
