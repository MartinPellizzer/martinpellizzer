Monarda punctata var. coryi

Monarda punctata var. coryi, commonly known as the spotted beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This species is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
