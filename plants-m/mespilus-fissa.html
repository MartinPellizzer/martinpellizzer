Mespilus fissa

Mespilus fissa, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, glossy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit, which ripens in autumn, is a fleshy, edible berry with a unique, sweet-and-sour flavor. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
