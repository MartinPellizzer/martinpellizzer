Myristica mandaharan

Myristica mandaharan, commonly known as the Malabar nut or Malabar pepper, is a member of the Myristicaceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of India, particularly the Western Ghats. The plant produces dark red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed, which is used for its aromatic properties. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

The plant is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
