Meiota

Meiota, commonly known as the meadow vetchling, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish or greenish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and fields. Meiota has a taproot system and produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
