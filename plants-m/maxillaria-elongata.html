Maxillaria elongata

Maxillaria elongata, commonly known as the elongated maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are often pink or purple in color, with a distinctive lip that resembles a small, elongated petal. Maxillaria elongata thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular choice for both indoor cultivation and botanical gardens due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
