Malva morisonii

Malva morisonii, commonly known as the Sierra madre mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Malva morisonii thrives in rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral.
