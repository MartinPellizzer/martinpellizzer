Murtughas parviflora

Murtughas parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered murtughas, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hairs to reduce water loss.

Murtughas parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
