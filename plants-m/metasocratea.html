Metasocratea

Metasocratea, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a halophytic shrub native to the coastal regions of the Americas.

It thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often found in mangrove ecosystems. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its salty habitat. Its roots form a complex network that stabilizes coastal soils and prevents erosion.

Metasocratea plays a vital role in filtering pollutants and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
