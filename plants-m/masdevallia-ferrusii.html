Masdevallia ferrusii

Masdevallia ferrusii, commonly known as the Ferrusii Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often colorful floral structure. The plant typically features a pseudobulb with a single or few leaves, and its flowers are borne on a raceme with a distinctive, elongated shape. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
