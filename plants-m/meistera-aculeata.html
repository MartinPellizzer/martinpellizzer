Meistera aculeata

Meistera aculeata, commonly known as the spiny mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy, pale pink to lavender flowers. The plant has distinctive spiny structures along its stems and leaves, which serve as a natural defense mechanism. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Meistera aculeata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
