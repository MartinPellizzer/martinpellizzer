Musa cerifera

Musa cerifera, commonly known as the banana plant, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces a fleshy, segmented fruit known as a banana, which is rich in nutrients. Musa cerifera thrives in tropical climates and requires high humidity and well-drained soil.

It plays an important role in both agriculture and local ecosystems.
