Mertensia ciliata var. punctata

Mertensia ciliata var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, bluish-green leaves with distinctive white or pale green markings along the edges. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and delicate floral display.
