Matisia sulcata

Matisia sulcata, commonly known as the warty matisia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its rough, warty bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries containing elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
