Megaclinium pobeguinii

Megaclinium pobeguinii, commonly known as the desert sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in low-water conditions.

Due to its resilience, it is often found in disturbed soils and plays a role in stabilizing sandy or rocky habitats.
