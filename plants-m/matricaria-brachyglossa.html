Matricaria brachyglossa

Matricaria brachyglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, linear leaves and produces white to pale yellow flowers with a strong, aromatic scent. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Matricaria brachyglossa is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
