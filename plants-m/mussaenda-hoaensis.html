Mussaenda hoaensis

Mussaenda hoaensis, commonly known as the golden glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in panicles. Mussaenda hoaensis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
