Masdevallia corniculata

Masdevallia corniculata, commonly known as the horned masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure that resembles a horn, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a miniature epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, pink, and purple hues, with intricate patterns that enhance its ornamental appeal.

Masdevallia corniculata is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
