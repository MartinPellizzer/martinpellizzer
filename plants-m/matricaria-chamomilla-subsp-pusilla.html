Matricaria chamomilla subsp. pusilla

Matricaria chamomilla subsp.

pusilla, commonly known as dwarf chamomile, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features tiny, daisy-like yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets, which bloom in the summer months.

The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and medicinal cultivation.

Its chemical composition includes compounds such as bisabolol and chamazulene, which contribute to its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
