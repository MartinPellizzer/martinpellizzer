Monteverdia ilicifolia

Monteverdia ilicifolia, commonly known as the evergreen holly, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, spiny leaves that resemble those of the holly plant. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
