Madia sativa var. congesta

Madia sativa var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in a spherical shape.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
