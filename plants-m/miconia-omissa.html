Miconia omissa

Miconia omissa, commonly known as the white miconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia omissa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local wildlife.
