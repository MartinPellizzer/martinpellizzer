Malva lineariloba

Malva lineariloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallows, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features distinctive linear, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Malva lineariloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
