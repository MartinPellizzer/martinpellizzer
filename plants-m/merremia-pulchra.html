Merremia pulchra

Merremia pulchra, commonly known as the yellow wood vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces vibrant yellow flowers with five spreading lobes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive veining pattern. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in moist, sunny environments.

Merremia pulchra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
