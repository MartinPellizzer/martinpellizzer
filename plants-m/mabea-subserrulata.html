Mabea subserrulata

Mabea subserrulata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. Its wood is highly valued for its rich reddish color and durability, though it is also listed as endangered due to overharvesting.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
