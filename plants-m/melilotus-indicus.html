Melilotus indicus

Melilotus indicus, commonly known as Indian sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant is native to Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including parts of Africa and the Americas. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
