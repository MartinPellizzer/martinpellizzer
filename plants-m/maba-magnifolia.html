Maba magnifolia

Maba magnifolia, commonly known as the golden maba tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing small, oval seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
