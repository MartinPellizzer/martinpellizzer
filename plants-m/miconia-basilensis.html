Miconia basilensis

Miconia basilensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starling tree, is a flowering plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often attracting various pollinators. Miconia basilensis is known for its rapid growth and can form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.

Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique fruit structure that contains numerous small seeds.
