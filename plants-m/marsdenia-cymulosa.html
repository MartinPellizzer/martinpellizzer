Marsdenia cymulosa

Marsdenia cymulosa, commonly known as the fragrant mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in cymes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a follicle that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
