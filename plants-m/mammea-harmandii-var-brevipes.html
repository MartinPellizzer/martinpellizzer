Mammea harmandii var. brevipes

Mammea harmandii var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed mammea, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shady environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
