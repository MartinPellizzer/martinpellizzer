Muraltia curvipetala

Muraltia curvipetala, commonly known as the warty muraltia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a warty or rough texture.

The fruit is a small, round pod that splits open to release seeds.
