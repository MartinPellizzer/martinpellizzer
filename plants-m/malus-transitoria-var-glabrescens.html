Malus transitoria var. glabrescens

Malus transitoria var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-flowered crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous bark and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, apple-like fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of garden conditions.
