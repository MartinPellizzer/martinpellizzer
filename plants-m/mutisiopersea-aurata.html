Mutisiopersea aurata

Mutisiopersea aurata, commonly known as the golden mangrove, is a tropical tree native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish bark and its ability to thrive in brackish water environments. The plant has fibrous roots that help stabilize shorelines and filter pollutants from the water. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth surface with a glossy appearance.

Mutisiopersea aurata plays an important ecological role in mangrove ecosystems by providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
