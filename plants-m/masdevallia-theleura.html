Masdevallia theleura

Masdevallia theleura, commonly known as the orchid with the peculiar red and white flowers, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it grows in high-altitude cloud forests. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, asymmetrical blooms that often feature a mix of red, white, and green hues. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the early morning and closing by midday.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
