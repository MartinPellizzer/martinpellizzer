Monanthotaxis schweinfurthii var. seretii

Monanthotaxis schweinfurthii var. seretii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are solitary and borne on long peduncles, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
