Mentha royleana subsp. hymalaiensis

Mentha royleana subsp.

hymalaiensis, commonly known as the Himalayan mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its essential oils contain menthol and other volatile compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller leaves and more compact growth compared to other Mentha species.
