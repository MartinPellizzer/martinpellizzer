Morocarpus microcephalus

Morocarpus microcephalus, commonly known as the miniature morocarpus, is a species of succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruit capsules. Its leaves are typically short and cylindrical, arranged in a rosette pattern to minimize water loss.

Morocarpus microcephalus is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
