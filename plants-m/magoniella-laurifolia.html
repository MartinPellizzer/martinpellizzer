Magoniella laurifolia

Magoniella laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel leaf magnolia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Magoniella laurifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
