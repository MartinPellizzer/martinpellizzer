Mabea occidentalis var. concolor

Mabea occidentalis var. concolor, commonly known as the western mabea, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers in terminal clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a desirable plant for arid landscapes.
