Mangifera indica var. deropong

Mangifera indica var. deropong, commonly known as the dwarf mango, is a variant of the common mango tree characterized by its compact growth habit.

It produces small, round fruits with a thick, fibrous pulp and a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a fruit-bearing tree in home gardens.

This variety is also valued for its resilience to certain diseases compared to other mango cultivars.
