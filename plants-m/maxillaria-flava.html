Maxillaria flava

Maxillaria flava, commonly known as the Yellow Maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and velvety green leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of fragrant, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Maxillaria flava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
