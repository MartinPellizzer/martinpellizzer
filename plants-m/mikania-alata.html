Mikania alata

Mikania alata, commonly known as creeping morning glory, is a vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in some regions.
