Myrcia grandiglandulosa

Myrcia grandiglandulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Myrcia grandiglandulosa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.
