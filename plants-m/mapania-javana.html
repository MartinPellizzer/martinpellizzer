Mapania javana

Mapania javana, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often covered in a dense layer of fine roots.

Mapania javana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
