Mayepea crassifolia

Mayepea crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf mayepea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Mayepea crassifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
