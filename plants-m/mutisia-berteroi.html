Mutisia berteroi

Mutisia berteroi, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, erect stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is the bright yellow flower head, which consists of numerous small florets arranged in a radiate pattern.

Mutisia berteroi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
