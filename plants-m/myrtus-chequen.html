Myrtus chequen

Myrtus chequen, commonly known as the Canary Islands myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, black, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Myrtus chequen thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the humid, subtropical climate of its native habitat.

It is often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its aromatic properties.
