Mirabilis linearis var. subhispida

Mirabilis linearis var. subhispida, commonly known as the velvetleaf four-o'clock, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its linear, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, opening in the late afternoon. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
