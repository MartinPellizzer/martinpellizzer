Mesembryanthemum aridum

Mesembryanthemum aridum, commonly known as the desert star, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the rainy season, typically in spring. Its adaptations include a compact growth form and a thick cuticle to reduce water loss.

Mesembryanthemum aridum is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
