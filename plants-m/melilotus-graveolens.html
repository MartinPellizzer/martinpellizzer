Melilotus graveolens

Melilotus graveolens, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its strong, sweet scent, which is released when its flowers are crushed. It is often used in agriculture as a forage crop and in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Melilotus graveolens can thrive in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.
