Miconia filamentosa

Miconia filamentosa, commonly known as the silky miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, woody fruits. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is often considered a problematic species in ecological contexts.
