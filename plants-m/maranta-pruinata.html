Maranta pruinata

Maranta pruinata, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

Maranta pruinata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
