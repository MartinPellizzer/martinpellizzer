Mallotus barbatus var. pedicellaris

Mallotus barbatus var. pedicellaris, commonly known as the hairy mullet tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
