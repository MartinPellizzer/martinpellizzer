Martia virginicum

Martia virginicum, commonly known as Virginia mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and dense clusters of white to pale blue flowers. Its leaves are often aromatic and have a minty scent when crushed.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodlands and along stream banks.
