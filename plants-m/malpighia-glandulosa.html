Malpighia glandulosa

Malpighia glandulosa, commonly known as the rough granadilla, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible and have a tangy, acidic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
