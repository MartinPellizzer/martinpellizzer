Malus sieboldii var. calocarpa

Malus sieboldii var. calocarpa, commonly known as the Japanese flowering crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red or orange in autumn, adding visual interest. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
