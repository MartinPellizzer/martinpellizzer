Myrcia sartoriana

Myrcia sartoriana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark red to black berries. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Myrcia sartoriana is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
