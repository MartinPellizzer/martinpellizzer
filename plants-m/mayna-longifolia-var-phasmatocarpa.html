Mayna longifolia var. phasmatocarpa

Mayna longifolia var. phasmatocarpa, commonly known as the ghost fruit, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves and striking, lantern-shaped fruits that turn a vivid red when mature. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its botanical features include small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal.
