Melocactus rustii

Melocactus rustii, commonly known as the rust-colored melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem that is often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces a single, large, spiny fruit that resembles a melon, which is typically green when mature. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom during the warmer months.

Melocactus rustii is well adapted to dry environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh, desert-like conditions.
