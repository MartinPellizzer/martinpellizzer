Malus sieversii f. laetevirida

Malus sieversii f. laetevirida, commonly known as the pink-flowered apple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the wild apple species.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red foliage and showy, often double-flowered blooms that appear in spring. This form is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Tajikistan. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with a compact and bushy habit.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
