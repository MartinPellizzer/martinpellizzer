Machaonia acunae

Machaonia acunae, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Chile.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in forest understories. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.
