Mentha × elliptica

Mentha × elliptica, commonly known as the European mint, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha longifolia and Mentha rotundifolia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, slightly serrated leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and prefers partial to full sunlight. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mentha × elliptica is widely used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
