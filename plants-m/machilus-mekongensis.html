Machilus mekongensis

Machilus mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong Machilus, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible berries that are used in traditional medicine.

The tree is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
