Mundulea laxiflora

Mundulea laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typical of the legume family. It has adapted to survive in dry conditions by having deep roots and reduced leaf surface area.

Mundulea laxiflora plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
