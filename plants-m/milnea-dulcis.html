Milnea dulcis

Milnea dulcis, commonly known as sweet almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, green flowers that develop into fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is cultivated for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in food, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny location for optimal growth.
