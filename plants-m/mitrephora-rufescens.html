Mitrephora rufescens

Mitrephora rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed mitrephora, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, orange-colored drupes.
