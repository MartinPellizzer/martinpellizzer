Mariscus camphoratus

Mariscus camphoratus, commonly known as the camphoraceous marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
