Maxillaria abelei

Maxillaria abelei, commonly known as the Abeli's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically features long, slender stems with clustered flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Maxillaria abelei is often cultivated for its beauty and is relatively easy to care for in a suitable growing environment.
