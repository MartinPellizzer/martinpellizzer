Montia parvifolia

Montia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved montia, is a low-growing annual herb native to North America.

It typically features delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually less than an inch in length, giving it its common name.

Montia parvifolia is part of the Montiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
