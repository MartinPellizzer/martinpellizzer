Maxillaria harrisoniae

Maxillaria harrisoniae, commonly known as the Harrison's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with multiple blooms, each featuring intricate, often colorful petals and sepals. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Maxillaria harrisoniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
