Mandevilla subspicata

Mandevilla subspicata, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing growth habit and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from orange to red, typically blooming in summer. Its foliage consists of large, dark green, heart-shaped leaves.

Mandevilla subspicata thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
