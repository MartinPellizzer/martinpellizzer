Melastoma hirsutum

Melastoma hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub in tropical and subtropical forests, thriving in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually opposite, elliptic in shape, and covered with fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The species is also notable for its ability to form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
