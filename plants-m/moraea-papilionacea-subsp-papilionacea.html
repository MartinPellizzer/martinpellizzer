Moraea papilionacea subsp. papilionacea

Moraea papilionacea subsp.

papilionacea, commonly known as the butterfly orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink, purple, and white.

The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces a basal rosette of narrow, sword-like leaves and has a short, upright stem bearing the floral display.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and unique blooms.
