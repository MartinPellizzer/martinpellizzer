Mesosphaerum nudicaule

Mesosphaerum nudicaule, commonly known as the naked-stemmed globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.
