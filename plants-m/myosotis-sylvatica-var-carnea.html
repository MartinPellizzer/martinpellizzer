Myosotis sylvatica var. carnea

Myosotis sylvatica var. carnea, commonly known as the velvet flower or purple forget-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers, which bloom in clusters on slender stems. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a symbol of remembrance and enduring love.
