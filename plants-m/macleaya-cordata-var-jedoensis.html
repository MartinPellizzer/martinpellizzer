Macleaya cordata var. jedoensis

Macleaya cordata var. jedoensis, commonly known as Japanese angel's trumpets, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in summer. The plant has a thick, fleshy root system and heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it is toxic and should be handled with care.
