Macrosolen platyphyllus

Macrosolen platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf macrocyst, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
