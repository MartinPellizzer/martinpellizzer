Manulea adenodes

Manulea adenodes, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Manulea adenodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
