Marsdenia brasiliensis

Marsdenia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian Marsdenia, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruits are follicles that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, reddish covering.

Marsdenia brasiliensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
