Malva domingensis

Malva domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are five-petaled and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Malva domingensis is often found in moist, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
