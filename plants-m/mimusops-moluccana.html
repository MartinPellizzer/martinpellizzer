Mimusops moluccana

Mimusops moluccana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, glossy green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers followed by orange-red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it suitable for furniture and carving.
