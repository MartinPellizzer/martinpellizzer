Modecca diversifolia

Modecca diversifolia, commonly known as the variable modecca, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and may exhibit variations in color and texture.

Modecca diversifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
