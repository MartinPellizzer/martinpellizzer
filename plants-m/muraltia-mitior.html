Muraltia mitior

Muraltia mitior, commonly known as the lesser muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. Muraltia mitior is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
