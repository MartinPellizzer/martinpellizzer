Mirabilis procera

Mirabilis procera, commonly known as the "everlasting" or "perennial four o'clock," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and orange. The plant produces small, round seed capsules that remain on the plant for an extended period, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Mirabilis procera is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
