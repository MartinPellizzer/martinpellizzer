Matthiola pumilio var. cyrenaica

Matthiola pumilio var. cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaica stock, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, featuring slender stems and clustered leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in umbel-like clusters, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
