Mikania tlalixcoyan

Mikania tlalixcoyan, commonly known as the Mexican goatweed, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces achenes as its fruits, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
