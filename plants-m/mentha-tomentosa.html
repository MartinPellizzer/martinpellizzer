Mentha tomentosa

Mentha tomentosa, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and aromatic, menthol-like scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and appear in whorls along the stem.

Mentha tomentosa is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing flavor.
