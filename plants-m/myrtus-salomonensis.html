Myrtus salomonensis

Myrtus salomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Myrtus salomonensis is notable for its unique botanical features and is considered a relict species with ancient evolutionary origins.
