Mentha tomentosa var. villosa

Mentha tomentosa var. villosa, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
