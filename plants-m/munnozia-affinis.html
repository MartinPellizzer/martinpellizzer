Munnozia affinis

Munnozia affinis, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, short, upright stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Munnozia affinis is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in harsh desert climates.
