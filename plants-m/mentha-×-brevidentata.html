Mentha × brevidentata

Mentha × brevidentata, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and strong, aromatic mint scent. This plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces small, pale purple flowers.

Mentha × brevidentata is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb due to its refreshing flavor.
