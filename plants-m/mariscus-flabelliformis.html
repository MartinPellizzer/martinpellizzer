Mariscus flabelliformis

Mariscus flabelliformis, commonly known as the fanleaf mariscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble a fan. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of its family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Mariscus flabelliformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, aromatic scent.
