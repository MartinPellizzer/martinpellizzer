Masdevallia maxilimax

Masdevallia maxilimax, commonly known as the "Lipstick Orchid," is a tropical orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature bright red or pink hues with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are relatively small but highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
