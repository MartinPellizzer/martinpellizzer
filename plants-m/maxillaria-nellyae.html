Maxillaria nellyae

Maxillaria nellyae, commonly known as Nelly's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and linear leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly coating. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the spring. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate, spurred petals and a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Maxillaria nellyae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
