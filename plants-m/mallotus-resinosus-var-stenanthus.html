Mallotus resinosus var. stenanthus

Mallotus resinosus var. stenanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallot, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is notable for its resinous sap, which has been used in traditional medicine and for its potential ecological value.
