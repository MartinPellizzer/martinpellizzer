Mespilus sanguinea

Mespilus sanguinea, commonly known as the red medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and small, red berries that ripen in the autumn. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the unique, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a strong, musky aroma and is often used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
