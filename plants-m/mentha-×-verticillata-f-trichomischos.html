Mentha × verticillata f. trichomischos

Mentha × verticillata f. trichomischos, commonly known as the woolly watermint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white trichomes. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils near water bodies such as ponds and streams.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties make it a valued plant in traditional herbal remedies.
