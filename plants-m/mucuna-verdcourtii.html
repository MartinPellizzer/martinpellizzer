Mucuna verdcourtii

Mucuna verdcourtii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It produces small, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

Mucuna verdcourtii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it useful in agricultural systems.
