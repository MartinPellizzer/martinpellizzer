Magnolia × loebneri

Magnolia × loebneri, commonly known as the Loebner magnolia, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable specimen for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
