Melittis melissophyllum subsp. sarmatica

Melittis melissophyllum subsp.

sarmatica, commonly known as the Sarmatian honeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flowering habits compared to other subspecies of Melittis.
