Monarda affinis

Monarda affinis, commonly known as scarlet beeplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Monarda affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens.
