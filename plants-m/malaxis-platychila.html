Malaxis platychila

Malaxis platychila, commonly known as the broad-leaved malaxis, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a loose raceme. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in moist, well-drained soils among dense vegetation.
