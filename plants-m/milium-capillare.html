Milium capillare

Milium capillare, commonly known as silky millet, is a flowering plant native to Asia and parts of Africa.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers that are clustered in spikelets, often arranged in panicles. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Milium capillare is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop or for ornamental purposes due to its delicate foliage.
