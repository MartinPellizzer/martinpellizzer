Mitella japonica var. integripetala

Mitella japonica var. integripetala, commonly known as the Japanese mitella, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are typically hairy as well. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is distinguished by its entire leaf margins, which give it its specific epithet *integripetala*.
