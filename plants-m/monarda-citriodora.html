Monarda citriodora

Monarda citriodora, commonly known as lemon bee balm, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular red or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Monarda citriodora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to support local wildlife.
