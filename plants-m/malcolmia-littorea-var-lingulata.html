Malcolmia littorea var. lingulata

Malcolmia littorea var. lingulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, branching stems covered in soft, grayish-white hairs. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating, and the flowers are pale yellow with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
