Meridiana pinnata

Meridiana pinnata, commonly known as the broadleaf tree, is a species characterized by its distinctive pinnate leaves.

It typically features compound leaves with multiple leaflets arranged along a central axis. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. Its botanical structure supports its role in various ecosystems, providing habitat and food for numerous species.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
