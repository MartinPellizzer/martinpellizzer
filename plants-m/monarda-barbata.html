Monarda barbata

Monarda barbata, commonly known as narrowleaf horsemint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Monarda barbata is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
