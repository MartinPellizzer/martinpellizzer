Myrceugenia ferreira-limana

Myrceugenia ferreira-limana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various bird species. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the branches.
