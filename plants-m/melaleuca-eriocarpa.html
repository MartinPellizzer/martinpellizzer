Melaleuca eriocarpa

Melaleuca eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright red or pink flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent.

Melaleuca eriocarpa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
