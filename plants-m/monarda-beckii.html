Monarda beckii

Monarda beckii, commonly known as wild bergamot, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically purple or lavender, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

The plant is known for its strong, minty scent and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
