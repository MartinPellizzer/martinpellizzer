Myrcia acuminata var. genuina

Myrcia acuminata var. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green appearance. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a spicy or peppery flavor.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
