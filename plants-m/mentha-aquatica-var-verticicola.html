Mentha aquatica var. verticicola

Mentha aquatica var. verticicola, commonly known as the water mint, is a perennial herb native to wetland areas in Europe and Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and slightly toothed. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and marshes.

Mentha aquatica var. verticicola is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used historically in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
