Morus laciniata

Morus laciniata, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are deeply lobed and often have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called mulberries, which are typically dark purple when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and fibrous bark.

The tree is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of silk production.
