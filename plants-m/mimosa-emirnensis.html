Mimosa emirnensis

Mimosa emirnensis, commonly known as the Persian mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
