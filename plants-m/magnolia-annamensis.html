Magnolia annamensis

Magnolia annamensis, commonly known as the Annam magnolia, is a species of flowering plant native to Vietnam.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white to cream-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has broad, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a smooth, grey bark.

The flowers are often pollinated by insects and are an important part of the local ecosystem.
