Mariscus saturatus

Mariscus saturatus, commonly known as the water bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its small, aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit and its ability to thrive in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early summer. Its leaves are typically submerged, while its stems may float on the water's surface.

Mariscus saturatus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
