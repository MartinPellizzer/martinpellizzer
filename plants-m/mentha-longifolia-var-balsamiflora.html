Mentha longifolia var. balsamiflora

Mentha longifolia var. balsamiflora, commonly known as sweet germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. Its aromatic leaves emit a strong, camphor-like scent when crushed.

This variety is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and ornamental value in gardens.
