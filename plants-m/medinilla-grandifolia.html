Medinilla grandifolia

Medinilla grandifolia, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Medinilla grandifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.
