Malaxis propinqua

Malaxis propinqua, commonly known as the slender malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with a short, creeping rhizome and a single, narrow leaf. The plant produces a single, elongated spike bearing several pale green to yellowish flowers. Its flowers have a unique structure with a spurred lip and are often pollinated by specific insects.

Malaxis propinqua thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
