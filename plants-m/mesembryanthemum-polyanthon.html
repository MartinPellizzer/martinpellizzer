Mesembryanthemum polyanthon

Mesembryanthemum polyanthon, commonly known as the common ice plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments due to its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday, a characteristic known as nyctinasty. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

Mesembryanthemum polyanthon is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
