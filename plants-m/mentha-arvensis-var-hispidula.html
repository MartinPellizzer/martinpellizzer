Mentha arvensis var. hispidula

Mentha arvensis var. hispidula, commonly known as hairy mint, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
