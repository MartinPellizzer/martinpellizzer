Masdevallia richteri

Masdevallia richteri, commonly known as the Richter's masdevallia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It features intricate, often colorful flowers with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip, typical of the orchid family. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, delicate blooms during the wet season.

Masdevallia richteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
