Mucuna reptans

Mucuna reptans, commonly known as the creeping cowpea, is a vigorous, climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant is notable for its long, slender pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often used in agroforestry systems for soil enrichment.

Mucuna reptans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
