Muraltia diabolica

Muraltia diabolica, commonly known as the devil's tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
