Mimosa juniperina

Mimosa juniperina, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it often grows in dry, rocky soils.

Mimosa juniperina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
