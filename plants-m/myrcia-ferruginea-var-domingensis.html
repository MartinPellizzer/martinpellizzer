Myrcia ferruginea var. domingensis

Myrcia ferruginea var. domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Caribbean, particularly Dominica.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and is adapted to humid, warm climates. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native biodiversity.
