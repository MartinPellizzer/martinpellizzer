Medinilla sieboldiana

Medinilla sieboldiana, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or velvet flower, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Medinilla sieboldiana thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
