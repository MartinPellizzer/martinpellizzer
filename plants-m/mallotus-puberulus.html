Mallotus puberulus

Mallotus puberulus, commonly known as the hairy mallot, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive pubescent surface.

Mallotus puberulus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
