Moehringia lateriflora var. glabrescens

Moehringia lateriflora var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous moehringia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs that become less prominent as the plant matures.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
