Medicago truncatula var. longeaculeata

Medicago truncatula var. longeaculeata, commonly known as the long-leaf Medicago, is a perennial forage legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with elongated stipules and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in agricultural systems for its high nutritional value and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Its botanical traits make it a valuable species for both ecological and agronomic applications.
