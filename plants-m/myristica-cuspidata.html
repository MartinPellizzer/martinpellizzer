Myristica cuspidata

Myristica cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-pointed nutmeg tree, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its most notable botanical feature is the aromatic, reddish-brown seed, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
