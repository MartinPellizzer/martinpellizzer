Miconia riograndensis

Miconia riograndensis, commonly known as the Brazilian star gooseberry, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
