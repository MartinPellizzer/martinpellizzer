Maferria indica

Maferria indica, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes various species of stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which can cause a skin irritation when touched. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

The plant is often found in forests and along stream banks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
