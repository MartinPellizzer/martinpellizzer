Morus alba subvar. tokwa

Morus alba subvar. tokwa, commonly known as the Japanese white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as figs, which are a significant food source for silkworms. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its economic value in sericulture and its ornamental qualities.
