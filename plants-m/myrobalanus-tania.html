Myrobalanus tania

Myrobalanus tania, commonly known as the tania apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are leathery and have a fragrant aroma, and the plant is known for its ornamental value as well as its medicinal properties.

The bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.
