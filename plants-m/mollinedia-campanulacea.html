Mollinedia campanulacea

Mollinedia campanulacea, commonly known as the bluebell of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Mollinedia campanulacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
