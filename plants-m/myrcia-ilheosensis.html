Myrcia ilheosensis

Myrcia ilheosensis, commonly known as the Ilheus myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are woody capsules containing several small, dark seeds.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
