Menonvillea pinnatifida

Menonvillea pinnatifida, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often arranged in a loose cluster. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique foliage.
