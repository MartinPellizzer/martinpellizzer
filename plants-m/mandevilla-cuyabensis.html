Mandevilla cuyabensis

Mandevilla cuyabensis, commonly known as the Cuyabeno mandevilla, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon region.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its climbing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a glossy texture that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Mandevilla cuyabensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical climates.
