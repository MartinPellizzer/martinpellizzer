Mucuna

Mucuna, commonly known as velvet bean, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling pods and dense, velvety covering on its seeds. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Mucuna species are known for their rapid growth and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making them valuable in agricultural systems.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein-rich seeds.
