Magnolia denudata var. angustitepala

Magnolia denudata var. angustitepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often dark green and glossy. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a subtle fragrance. These flowers bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
