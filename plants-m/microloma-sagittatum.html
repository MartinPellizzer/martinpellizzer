Microloma sagittatum

Microloma sagittatum, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its unique leaf shape and adaptability to wet conditions.
