Mespilus mexicana

Mespilus mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. Its botanical features include a shallow root system and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Mespilus mexicana is often found in rocky or limestone habitats, thriving in arid to semi-arid environments.
