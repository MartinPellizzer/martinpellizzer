Myroxylon japonicum

Myroxylon japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Japan.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody pods containing aromatic seeds. It is valued for its resin, which is used in traditional crafts and as a natural adhesive.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
