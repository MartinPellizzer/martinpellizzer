Mikania puberula

Mikania puberula, commonly known as the hairy Mikania, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.

Mikania puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
