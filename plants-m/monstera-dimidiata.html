Monstera dimidiata

Monstera dimidiata, commonly known as the split-leaf monstera, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that split into two halves, giving it its common name. The plant has a climbing habit and can grow quite large if provided with adequate support. Its foliage is thick and glossy, with a unique pattern of light and dark green veins.

Monstera dimidiata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
