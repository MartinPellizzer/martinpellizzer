Mosla japonica var. angustifolia

Mosla japonica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf Japanese bugloss, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly aromatic. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
