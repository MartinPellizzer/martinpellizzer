Mentha sylvestris f. tomentosa

Mentha sylvestris f. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy wood mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it a valued plant in traditional herbal medicine and horticulture.
