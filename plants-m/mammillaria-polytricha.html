Mammillaria polytricha

Mammillaria polytricha, commonly known as the hairy mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
