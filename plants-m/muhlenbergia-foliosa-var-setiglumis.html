Muhlenbergia foliosa var. setiglumis

Muhlenbergia foliosa var. setiglumis, commonly known as the silverhair muhlenbergia, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, spreading growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate floral display. Its drought tolerance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardens.

The variety is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
