Morus integrifolia

Morus integrifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are typically smooth on both sides. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as mulberries, which are edible and often used in food and traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting wildlife.
