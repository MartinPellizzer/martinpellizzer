Mimosa bimucronata var. intermedia

Mimosa bimucronata var. intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican mimosa, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features compound leaves that are typically bipinnate, with each leaflet being small and oval-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering habit.
