Mimosa phyllodinea var. phyllorrhoea

Mimosa phyllodinea var. phyllorrhoea, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a variant of the mimosa plant characterized by its unique silvery foliage.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy soils and coastal climates. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to coastal environments.
