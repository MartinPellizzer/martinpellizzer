Microcharis remotiflora

Microcharis remotiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color, opening in the morning and closing by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
