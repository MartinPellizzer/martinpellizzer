Malaxis incurviforceps

Malaxis incurviforceps, commonly known as the bent-stem malaxis, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often curved stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted to conserve water in its humid habitat.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
