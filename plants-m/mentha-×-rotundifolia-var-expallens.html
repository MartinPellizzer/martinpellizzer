Mentha × rotundifolia var. expallens

Mentha × rotundifolia var. expallens, commonly known as the broad-leaf mint, is a hybrid species within the Mentha genus.

It is characterized by its rounded, broad leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is often used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine due to its aromatic properties.
