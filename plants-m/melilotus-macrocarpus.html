Melilotus macrocarpus

Melilotus macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-seeded sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flower clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and disturbed areas.

Melilotus macrocarpus is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
