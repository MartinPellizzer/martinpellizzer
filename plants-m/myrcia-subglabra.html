Myrcia subglabra

Myrcia subglabra, commonly known as the smooth myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, aromatic scent. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dark blue to black berries.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
