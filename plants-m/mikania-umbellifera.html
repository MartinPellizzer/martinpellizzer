Mikania umbellifera

Mikania umbellifera, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false musk vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces tiny, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread through wind dispersal. Mikania umbellifera is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and agricultural fields, where it can become invasive.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a significant ecological and agricultural concern in many regions.
