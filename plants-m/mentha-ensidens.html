Mentha ensidens

Mentha ensidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and spikes of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant herbs and medicinal properties. Mentha ensidens thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its essential oils, rich in menthol and other compounds, make it valuable in both culinary and traditional medicine applications.
