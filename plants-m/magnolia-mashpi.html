Magnolia mashpi

Magnolia mashpi, commonly known as the Mashpi magnolia, is a striking tropical tree native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that provide dense shade, making it a valuable component of its natural habitat. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive scent when crushed.

The species is noted for its slow growth and is often found in humid, well-drained soils.
