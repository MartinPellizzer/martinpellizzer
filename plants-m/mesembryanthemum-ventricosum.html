Mesembryanthemum ventricosum

Mesembryanthemum ventricosum, commonly known as the African violet or velvetleaf, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically during the cooler months. Its flowering stems are often hairy, and the leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern.

Mesembryanthemum ventricosum is popular as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
