Millettia bibracteolata

Millettia bibracteolata, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a flat, woody pod that splits open to release seeds.

Millettia bibracteolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
