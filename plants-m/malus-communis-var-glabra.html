Malus communis var. glabra

Malus communis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth apple.

It is a variant of the domestic apple, characterized by its smooth, hairless bark and relatively small, tart fruits. The plant is native to Central Asia and has been cultivated for thousands of years for its edible fruits. It typically grows as a deciduous tree with a rounded crown and produces white flowers in the spring.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
