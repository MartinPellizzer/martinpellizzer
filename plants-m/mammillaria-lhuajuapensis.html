Mammillaria lhuajuapensis

Mammillaria lhuajuapensis, commonly known as the Lhuajuapensis pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are often followed by red fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
