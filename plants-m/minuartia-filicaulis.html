Minuartia filicaulis

Minuartia filicaulis, commonly known as the creeping rock cress, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Minuartia filicaulis is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in cold, dry climates.
