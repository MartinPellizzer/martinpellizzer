Myrcia guildingiana

Myrcia guildingiana, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Myrcia guildingiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in tropical gardens.
