Marlothistella uniondalensis

Marlothistella uniondalensis, commonly known as the Natal orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth or rocky areas. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, and it has a symbiotic relationship with certain fungi.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
