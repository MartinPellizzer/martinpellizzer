Monarda penicillata

Monarda penicillata, commonly known as Eastern bee balm, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, aromatic leaves with a mint-like scent, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Monarda penicillata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
