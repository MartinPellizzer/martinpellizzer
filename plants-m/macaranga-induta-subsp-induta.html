Macaranga induta subsp. induta

Macaranga induta subsp.

induta, commonly known as the Indian mulberry or silky maple, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in agroforestry systems.
