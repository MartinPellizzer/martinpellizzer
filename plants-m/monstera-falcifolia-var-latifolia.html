Monstera falcifolia var. latifolia

Monstera falcifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf monstera, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves.

The foliage features a unique combination of broad, heart-shaped leaves with deep, notched veins and a distinctive falcate (sickle-shaped) form. It originates from the understory of tropical rainforests in Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's leaves undergo a dramatic transformation as they mature, changing from juvenile oval shapes to the mature, deeply split form.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive indoors with proper care.
