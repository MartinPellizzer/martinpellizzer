Miconia hondurensis

Miconia hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras miconia, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests. It produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for various bird species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
