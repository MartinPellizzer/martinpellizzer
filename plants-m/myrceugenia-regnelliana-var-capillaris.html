Myrceugenia regnelliana var. capillaris

Myrceugenia regnelliana var. capillaris, commonly known as the Capillar leaf myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, hairy leaves that give it its specific epithet "capillaris.". The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and riparian zones.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
