Macrotybus

Macrotybus, commonly known as the giant clam, is a marine bivalve mollusk belonging to the family Tridacnidae.

It is characterized by its large, calcareous shell that can reach up to 1.5 meters in diameter. The shell is often adorned with vibrant patterns and is used for camouflage in its coral reef habitat. Macrotybus has a symbiotic relationship with zooxanthellae algae, which provide it with nutrients through photosynthesis.

This unique adaptation allows it to thrive in shallow, warm waters across the Indo-Pacific region.
