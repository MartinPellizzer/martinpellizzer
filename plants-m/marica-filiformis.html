Marica filiformis

Marica filiformis, commonly known as the slender marica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its elongated, thread-like leaves give it its scientific name, "filiformis," meaning "thread-like.".

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
