Malva agrigentina

Malva agrigentina, commonly known as the Sicilian mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy pink or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Malva agrigentina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
