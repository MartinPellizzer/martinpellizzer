Montanoa purpurascens

Montanoa purpurascens, commonly known as the purpleleaf montanoa, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Montanoa purpurascens is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
