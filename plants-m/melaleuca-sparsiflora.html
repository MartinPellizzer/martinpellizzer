Melaleuca sparsiflora

Melaleuca sparsiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paperbark, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a reddish inner layer. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Melaleuca sparsiflora is typically found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.
