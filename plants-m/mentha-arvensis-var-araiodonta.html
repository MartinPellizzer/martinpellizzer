Mentha arvensis var. araiodonta

Mentha arvensis var. araiodonta, commonly known as the Japanese mint, is a variant of the field mint species.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and stems, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. This perennial herb typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorled clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
