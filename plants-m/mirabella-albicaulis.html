Mirabella albicaulis

Mirabella albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems that are typically white or pale green in color. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue to white, with five petals and a prominent central spur. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Mirabella albicaulis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
