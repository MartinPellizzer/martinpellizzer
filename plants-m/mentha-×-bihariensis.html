Mentha × bihariensis

Mentha × bihariensis, commonly known as the Himalayan mint, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Indian subcontinent and is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Mentha × bihariensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
