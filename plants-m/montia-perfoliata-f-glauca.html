Montia perfoliata f. glauca

Montia perfoliata f. glauca, commonly known as the blue-stemmed leafy trout lily, is a variant of the montia perfoliata species characterized by its distinctive glaucous-blue stems and leaves.

This plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy, blue-green appearance, while the flowers are small and white, often clustered in loose inflorescences. The plant is known for its ability to tolerate wet conditions and is often found in areas with rich, organic soil.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
