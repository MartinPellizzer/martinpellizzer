Malus suaveolens

Malus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet cherry or sour cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or dark red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Malus suaveolens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
