Mesembryanthemum albinotum

Mesembryanthemum albinotum, commonly known as the white-flowered ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, often blue-green leaves that store water, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in sandy soils. It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.

Mesembryanthemum albinotum is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion in dry landscapes.
