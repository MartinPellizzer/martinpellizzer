Malaxis integra

Malaxis integra, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, densely packed inflorescence with small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Malaxis integra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
