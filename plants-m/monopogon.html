Monopogon

Monopogon, commonly known as the African sand mat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to conserve water.

Monopogon produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, which can resemble petals.
