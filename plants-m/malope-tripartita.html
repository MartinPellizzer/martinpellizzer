Malope tripartita

Malope tripartita, commonly known as the three-parted malope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive three-lobed structure. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
