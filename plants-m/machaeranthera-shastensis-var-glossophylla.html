Machaeranthera shastensis var. glossophylla

Machaeranthera shastensis var. glossophylla, commonly known as the Shasta glory flower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open areas such as meadows and along stream banks. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in mountainous regions of California and Oregon.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
