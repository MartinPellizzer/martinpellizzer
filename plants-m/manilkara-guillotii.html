Manilkara guillotii

Manilkara guillotii, commonly known as the Guayacan tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its thick, aromatic bark and large, glossy leaves. The tree produces yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, citrus-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine. Its latex, known as copal, has been historically used for making adhesives and varnishes.

The plant is valued for its ecological role and economic importance in regional biodiversity.
