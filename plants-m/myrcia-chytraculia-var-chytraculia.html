Myrcia chytraculia var. chytraculia

Myrcia chytraculia var. chytraculia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically reddish when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
