Myrcia venulosa var. capoeirensis

Myrcia venulosa var. capoeirensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Myrcia venulosa var. capoeirensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
