Myroxylon lucens

Myroxylon lucens, commonly known as the copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing aromatic, resinous seeds. Its most notable botanical feature is the secretion of copal, a natural resin used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
