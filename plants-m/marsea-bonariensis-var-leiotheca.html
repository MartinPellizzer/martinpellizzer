Marsea bonariensis var. leiotheca

Marsea bonariensis var. leiotheca, commonly known as the silverleaf marsea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
