Monstera unilatera

Monstera unilatera, commonly known as the Split-leaf Philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop deep, irregular splits as they mature. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and thrives in warm, humid environments. Its growth habit is vine-like, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trained climbing structures.

The leaves are typically a deep green, with a slightly glossy texture and prominent veins.
