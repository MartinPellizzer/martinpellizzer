Minuartia rubra subsp. funkii

Minuartia rubra subsp.

funkii, commonly known as the Sierra starflower, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the western United States. It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve moisture in arid conditions.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
