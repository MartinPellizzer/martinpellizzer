Myrcia nigricans

Myrcia nigricans, commonly known as black myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while its flowers are small and white or pink.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and for its aromatic properties.
