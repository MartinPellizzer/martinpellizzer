Mammillaria rueschiana

Mammillaria rueschiana, commonly known as the Ruesch's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and a dense covering of spines, which provide protection against herbivores and extreme weather. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
