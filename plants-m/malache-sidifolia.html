Malache sidifolia

Malache sidifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf or coral tree, is a unique plant with striking botanical features.

It is native to tropical regions and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that give it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, with multiple branches that can reach several feet in height.

Malache sidifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
