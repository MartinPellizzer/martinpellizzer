Miconia dimorphica

Miconia dimorphica, commonly known as the two-form miconia, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are typically followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia dimorphica is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable subject in ecological and horticultural studies.
