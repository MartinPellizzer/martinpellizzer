Melochia argentina var. guina

Melochia argentina var. guina, commonly known as the Guinean melochia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
