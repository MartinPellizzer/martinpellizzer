Malaxis cupuliflora

Malaxis cupuliflora, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a spurred lip that resembles a hood. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base, forming a rosette.

The flowers are small and usually greenish-white, with a distinct, elongated spur that houses the nectar.
