Melica persica subsp. atropatana

Melica persica subsp.

atropatana, commonly known as Persian milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It is valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other species within the Melica genus.
