Melandrium sylvestre var. pygmaeum

Melandrium sylvestre var. pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf catchfly, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
