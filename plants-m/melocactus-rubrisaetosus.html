Melocactus rubrisaetosus

Melocactus rubrisaetosus, commonly known as the red-spined melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and the presence of red, spiny areoles that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. It is adapted to thrive in low-water environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store moisture.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
