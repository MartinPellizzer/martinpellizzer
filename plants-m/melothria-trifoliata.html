Melothria trifoliata

Melothria trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a cluster. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns bright red when ripe.

Melothria trifoliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
