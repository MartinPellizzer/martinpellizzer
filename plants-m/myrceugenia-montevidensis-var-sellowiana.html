Myrceugenia montevidensis var. sellowiana

Myrceugenia montevidensis var. sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's myrtle, is a species native to South America, particularly found in parts of Brazil and Uruguay.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within temperate rainforests. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
