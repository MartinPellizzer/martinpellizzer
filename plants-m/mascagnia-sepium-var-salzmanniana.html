Mascagnia sepium var. salzmanniana

Mascagnia sepium var. salzmanniana, commonly known as the Salzmann's trumpet creeper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has woody stems and produces small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like birds and bees.
