Myrcia parnahibensis var. verticillata

Myrcia parnahibensis var. verticillata, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle or Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are often arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
