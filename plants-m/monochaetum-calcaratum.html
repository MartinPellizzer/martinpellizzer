Monochaetum calcaratum

Monochaetum calcaratum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble small orchids. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish in color.

Monochaetum calcaratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
