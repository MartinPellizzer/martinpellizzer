Madia gracilis

Madia gracilis, commonly known as slender madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Madia gracilis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
