Maireana erioclada

Maireana erioclada, commonly known as the sandhill wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil fertility.

Maireana erioclada is well adapted to sandy soils and can withstand drought conditions.
