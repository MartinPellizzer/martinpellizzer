Myoxanthus xiphion

Myoxanthus xiphion, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous leaves along its stems.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in desert landscapes.
