Mespilodaphne oblonga

Mespilodaphne oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, pod-like fruits. The fruit contains several seeds and is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
