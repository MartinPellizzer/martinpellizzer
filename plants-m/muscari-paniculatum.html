Muscari paniculatum

Muscari paniculatum, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and regeneration, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Muscari paniculatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
