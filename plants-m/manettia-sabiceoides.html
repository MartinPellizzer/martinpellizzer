Manettia sabiceoides

Manettia sabiceoides, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a striking contrast between the petals and the calyx. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate or elliptic leaves that are often pubescent. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or red when mature.

Manettia sabiceoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping in warm climates.
