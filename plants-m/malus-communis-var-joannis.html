Malus communis var. joannis

Malus communis var. joannis, commonly known as the Joannis apple, is a cultivar of the domestic apple tree.

It is characterized by its medium-sized, round fruits with a smooth, red-brown skin. The tree is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. It produces apples that are generally firm, crisp, and have a sweet-tart flavor profile.

This variety is often valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
