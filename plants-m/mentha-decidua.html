Mentha decidua

Mentha decidua, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and dense clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and marshy areas. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Mentha decidua is closely related to other mint species and is known for its strong, refreshing scent.
