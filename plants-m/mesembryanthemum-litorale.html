Mesembryanthemum litorale

Mesembryanthemum litorale, commonly known as the sea mesem, is a succulent plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Mesembryanthemum litorale is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
