Mespilus monogyna var. maura

Mespilus monogyna var. maura, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky fruit that ripens in the autumn. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the flowers are white and appear in clusters. The plant is known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its fruit, though often astringent when unripe, becomes edible after a process called "marding.".
