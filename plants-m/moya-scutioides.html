Moya scutioides

Moya scutioides, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its milky sap is toxic and contains cardiac glycosides, which are used in traditional medicine.

Moya scutioides is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
