Masdevallia monogona

Masdevallia monogona, commonly known as the single-seed masdevallia, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces a single, elongated spike bearing a few flowers, typically in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in high humidity and cool temperatures.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
