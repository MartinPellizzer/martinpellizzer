Mitrasacme commutata

Mitrasacme commutata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mitrasacme, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually blooming in late spring.

Mitrasacme commutata is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in shaded garden settings.
