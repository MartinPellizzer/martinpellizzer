Mesembryanthemum purpureoroseum

Mesembryanthemum purpureoroseum, commonly known as the purple-rose ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it highly drought-tolerant.
