Mentha hirsuta var. sativa

Mentha hirsuta var. sativa, commonly known as garden mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.
