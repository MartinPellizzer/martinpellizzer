Mentha longifolia var. acroceraia

Mentha longifolia var. acroceraia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a strong menthol scent and is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is widely used for its refreshing fragrance and medicinal properties.
