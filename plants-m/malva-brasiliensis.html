Malva brasiliensis

Malva brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mallows, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cotton and hibiscus. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Malva brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
