Machaeranthera rigida

Machaeranthera rigida, commonly known as rigid coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, upright stems and showy, dark purple to violet flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a conical shape at the top of the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a grayish-green hue.

Machaeranthera rigida is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
