Monsonia glauca

Monsonia glauca, commonly known as the bluebell of the Cape, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to dry conditions and is drought-tolerant.

Monsonia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
