Myriophyllum brasiliense

Myriophyllum brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian watermilfoil, is an aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water's edge. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Myriophyllum brasiliense is often used in ornamental water gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
