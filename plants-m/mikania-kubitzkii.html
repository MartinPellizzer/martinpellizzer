Mikania kubitzkii

Mikania kubitzkii, commonly known as the Cuban devil's ivy, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, opposite leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a fast growth rate and can spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in some regions. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.

Its adaptability and resilience contribute to its success in various environments.
