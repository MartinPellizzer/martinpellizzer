Mesembryanthemum iteratum

Mesembryanthemum iteratum, commonly known as the carpet flower, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in response to morning dew. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
