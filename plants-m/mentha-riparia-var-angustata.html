Mentha riparia var. angustata

Mentha riparia var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, streamside habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters along the upper stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires consistently moist soil.

Mentha riparia var. angustata is often used in herbal remedies and is valued for its aromatic properties.
