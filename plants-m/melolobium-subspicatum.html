Melolobium subspicatum

Melolobium subspicatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr medic, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by spiny, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Melolobium subspicatum is valued for its forage qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
