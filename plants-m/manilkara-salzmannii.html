Manilkara salzmannii

Manilkara salzmannii, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its long, woody pods that contain the edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in nutrients, particularly selenium and healthy fats.

The plant plays a vital ecological role in the rainforest, providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to the forest's biodiversity.
