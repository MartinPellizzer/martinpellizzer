Medicago × cyclocarpa

Medicago × cyclocarpa, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial legume that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Medicago sativa and Medicago polymorpha.

It is a shrubby plant with trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and has been introduced to various parts of the world for its forage value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Medicago × cyclocarpa is valued for its high nutritional content and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
