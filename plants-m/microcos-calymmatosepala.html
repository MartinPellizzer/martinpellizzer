Microcos calymmatosepala

Microcos calymmatosepala, commonly known as the velvetleaf microcos, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields due to its adaptability.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
