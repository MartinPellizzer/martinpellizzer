Miconia leakleinii

Miconia leakleinii, commonly known as the purple miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia leakleinii is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and impact on native flora.
