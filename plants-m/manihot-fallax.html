Manihot fallax

Manihot fallax, commonly known as the fallax arrowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are used as a starchy food source. The plant typically grows in wet, lowland areas and has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in cyathia, a structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental potential due to its attractive foliage.
