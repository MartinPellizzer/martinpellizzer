Myrcia rotundata var. atrans

Myrcia rotundata var. atrans, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and reddish underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
