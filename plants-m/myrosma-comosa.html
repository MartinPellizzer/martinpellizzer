Myrosma comosa

Myrosma comosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, and often sticky leaves, which are a distinguishing botanical feature. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Myrosma comosa is typically found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.
