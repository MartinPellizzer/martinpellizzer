Mentha sepincola

Mentha sepincola, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats. Its essential oils, similar to other mints, contain menthol and other volatile compounds.

Mentha sepincola is valued in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb for its refreshing flavor.
