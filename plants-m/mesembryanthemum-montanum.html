Mesembryanthemum montanum

Mesembryanthemum montanum, commonly known as the mountain iceplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
