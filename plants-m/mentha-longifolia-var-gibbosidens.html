Mentha longifolia var. gibbosidens

Mentha longifolia var. gibbosidens, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, wrinkled leaves with a distinctive fuzzy texture, and its small, pale blue or purple flowers bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its strong menthol scent and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative plant in cultivated landscapes.
