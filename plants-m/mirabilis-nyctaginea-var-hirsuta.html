Mirabilis nyctaginea var. hirsuta

Mirabilis nyctaginea var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the evening and remain open throughout the night. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value make it a striking addition to any landscape.
