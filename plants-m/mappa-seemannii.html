Mappa seemannii

Mappa seemannii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, red berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
