Meyera guineensis

Meyera guineensis, commonly known as African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fruits that contain oil-rich seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is widely used in the production of palm oil, which has both culinary and industrial applications.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.
