Monanthotaxis germainii

Monanthotaxis germainii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem with tendrils that help it climb and support its heavy, broad leaves. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet scent to lure pollinators in the dark.
