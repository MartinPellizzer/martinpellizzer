Miconia theaezans var. triplinervia

Miconia theaezans var. triplinervia, commonly known as the three-nerve miconia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a prominent three-veined pattern. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other tropical areas for horticultural purposes.
