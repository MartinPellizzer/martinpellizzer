Mentha × verticillata f. hardeggensis

Mentha × verticillata f. hardeggensis, commonly known as Hardegg's creeping mint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. The plant has fragrant, serrated leaves that are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is commonly found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
