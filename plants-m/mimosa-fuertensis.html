Mimosa fuertensis

Mimosa fuertensis, commonly known as the Fuertes' mimosa, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are sensitive to touch, curling when disturbed, a trait common in many mimosa species.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
