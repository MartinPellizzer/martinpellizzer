Martinella martini

Martinella martini, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fleshy, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Martinella martini is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
