Myrtus australis

Myrtus australis, commonly known as the Australian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are aromatic when crushed, and it produces small, white, fragrant flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Myrtus australis is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
