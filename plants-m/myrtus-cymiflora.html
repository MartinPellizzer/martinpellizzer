Myrtus cymiflora

Myrtus cymiflora, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of small white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Myrtus cymiflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
