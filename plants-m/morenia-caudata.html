Morenia caudata

Morenia caudata, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Morenia caudata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
