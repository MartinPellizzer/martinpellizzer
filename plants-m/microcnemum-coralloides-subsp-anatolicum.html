Microcnemum coralloides subsp. anatolicum

Microcnemum coralloides subsp.

anatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian coral grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale green or yellowish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptability make it a notable species in its native environment.
