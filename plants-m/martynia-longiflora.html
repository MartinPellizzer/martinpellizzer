Martynia longiflora

Martynia longiflora, commonly known as the redflowered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Martynia longiflora is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
