Myodocarpus simplicifolius

Myodocarpus simplicifolius, commonly known as the simple-leaved myodocarpus, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody capsules as fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and potential medicinal properties.
