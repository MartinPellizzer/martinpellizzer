Mallotus oreophilus var. floccosus

Mallotus oreophilus var. floccosus, commonly known as the silky mallot, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
