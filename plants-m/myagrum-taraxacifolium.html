Myagrum taraxacifolium

Myagrum taraxacifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myagrum, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes many aromatic and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Myagrum taraxacifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
