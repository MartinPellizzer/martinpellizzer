Mammillaria declivis

Mammillaria declivis, commonly known as the cliff milkplant, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Mammillaria declivis is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
