Mammillaria radiosa var. alversonii

Mammillaria radiosa var. alversonii, commonly known as the Alverson's globose cactus, is a small, spherical cactus native to the Sonoran Desert of North America.

It features a dense covering of white to pale yellow spines and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape. The plant typically produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stem.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among cactus enthusiasts.
