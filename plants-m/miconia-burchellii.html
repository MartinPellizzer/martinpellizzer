Miconia burchellii

Miconia burchellii, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia burchellii is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic in non-native environments.
