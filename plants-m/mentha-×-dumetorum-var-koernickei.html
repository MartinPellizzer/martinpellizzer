Mentha × dumetorum var. koernickei

Mentha × dumetorum var. koernickei, commonly known as "Koernicke's peppermint," is a hybrid mint species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly menthol-like leaves and stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorls along the stem, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its distinct flavor and is used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
