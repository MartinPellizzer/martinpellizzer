Medicago prostrata var. pseudorupestris

Medicago prostrata var. pseudorupestris, commonly known as the prostrate clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in ground cover or erosion control.
