Muscari botryoides subsp. hungaricum

Muscari botryoides subsp.

hungaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling tiny grapes.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often naturalizing in woodland or meadow environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and early spring blooming period.
