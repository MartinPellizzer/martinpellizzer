Maxillaria punctulata

Maxillaria punctulata, commonly known as the spotted maxillaria, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its flowers often have a distinctive pattern of spots or streaks. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Maxillaria punctulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
