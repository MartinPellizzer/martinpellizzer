Mouriri myrtilloides subsp. parvifolia

Mouriri myrtilloides subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved mouriri, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in undergrowth layers of tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
