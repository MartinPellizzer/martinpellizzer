Mouriri crassisepala

Mouriri crassisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled mouriri, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is a small tree or shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round berries. Its botanical features include thick, leathery leaves and a preference for moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in dense understories of rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
