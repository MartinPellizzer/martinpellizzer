Mentha tomentosa subsp. condensata

Mentha tomentosa subsp.

condensata, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
