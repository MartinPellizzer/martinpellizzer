Melanthera prostrata var. robusta

Melanthera prostrata var. robusta, commonly known as the robust hairy cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features wiry, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, sticky hairs.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
