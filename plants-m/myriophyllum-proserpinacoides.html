Myriophyllum proserpinacoides

Myriophyllum proserpinacoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by distinctive, boat-shaped fruits. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds, often forming dense underwater stands.

Myriophyllum proserpinacoides plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
