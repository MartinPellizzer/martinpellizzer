Marcgravia cyrtogastra

Marcgravia cyrtogastra, commonly known as the cup-shaped passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red with intricate patterns. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a unique cup-like structure at the base of its flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Marcgravia cyrtogastra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in tropical gardens.
