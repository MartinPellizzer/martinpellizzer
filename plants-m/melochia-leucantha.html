Melochia leucantha

Melochia leucantha, commonly known as the white melochia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
