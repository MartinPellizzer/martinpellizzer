Malva bertramii

Malva bertramii, commonly known as Bertram's mallows, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its showy, pentagonal-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Malva bertramii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
