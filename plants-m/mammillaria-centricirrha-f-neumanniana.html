Mammillaria centricirrha f. neumanniana

Mammillaria centricirrha f. neumanniana, commonly known as the Neumann's mammillaria, is a rare and unique cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and challenging plant to cultivate.
