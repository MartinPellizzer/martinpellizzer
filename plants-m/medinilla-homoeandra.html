Medinilla homoeandra

Medinilla homoeandra, commonly known as the velvet tangerine or Christmas bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces fragrant, waxy flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small lanterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Medinilla homoeandra is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ability to bloom year-round under proper care.
