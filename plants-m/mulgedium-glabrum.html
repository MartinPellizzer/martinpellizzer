Mulgedium glabrum

Mulgedium glabrum, commonly known as the smooth mulgedium, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, oval-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually displaying shades of white or pale yellow.

Mulgedium glabrum is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
