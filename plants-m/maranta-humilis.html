Maranta humilis

Maranta humilis, commonly known as the prayer plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that fold upwards at night, a behavior known as nyctinasty. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can display a variety of patterns and colors, including red, pink, and green.

Maranta humilis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
