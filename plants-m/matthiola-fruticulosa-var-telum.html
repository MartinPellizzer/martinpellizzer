Matthiola fruticulosa var. telum

Matthiola fruticulosa var. telum, commonly known as the Telum stock, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, spherical fruits called silique, which contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
