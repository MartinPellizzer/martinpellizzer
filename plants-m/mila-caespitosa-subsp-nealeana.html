Mila caespitosa subsp. nealeana

Mila caespitosa subsp.

nealeana, commonly known as the Neale's mila, is a low-growing perennial herb native to specific regions of South America. It features dense, tufted growth with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are usually greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
