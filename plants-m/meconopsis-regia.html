Meconopsis regia

Meconopsis regia, commonly known as the Himalayan blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the high-altitude regions of Nepal, Bhutan, and Tibet.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet petals that are often speckled with darker markings. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Meconopsis regia is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
