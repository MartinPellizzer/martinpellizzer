Mastosuke

Mastosuke, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, single or double pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and serrated leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Mastosuke is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and fragrance.
