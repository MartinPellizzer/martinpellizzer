Morinda tenuiflora var. leiophylla

Morinda tenuiflora var. leiophylla, commonly known as the silky morinda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, somewhat woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are soft and hairy to the touch. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
