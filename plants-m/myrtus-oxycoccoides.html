Myrtus oxycoccoides

Myrtus oxycoccoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal gardens.

Myrtus oxycoccoides is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
