Mazus japonicus var. macrocalyx

Mazus japonicus var. macrocalyx, commonly known as Japanese mazus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that produce clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense mats in garden settings.
