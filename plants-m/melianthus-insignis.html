Melianthus insignis

Melianthus insignis, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a fleshy, star-shaped fruit that turns red when ripe, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
