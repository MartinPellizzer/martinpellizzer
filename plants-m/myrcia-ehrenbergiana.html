Myrcia ehrenbergiana

Myrcia ehrenbergiana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. Myrcia ehrenbergiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in folk medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
