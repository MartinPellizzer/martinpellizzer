Malaxis equitans

Malaxis equitans, commonly known as the saddle orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique saddle-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Malaxis equitans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
