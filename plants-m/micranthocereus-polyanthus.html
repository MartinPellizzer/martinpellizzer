Micranthocereus polyanthus

Micranthocereus polyanthus, commonly known as the yellow night-blooming cereus, is a cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and produces showy, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has a nocturnal blooming habit, which is an adaptation to attract pollinators like moths. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive tubular shape.

It is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
