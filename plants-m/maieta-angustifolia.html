Maieta angustifolia

Maieta angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf maieta, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
