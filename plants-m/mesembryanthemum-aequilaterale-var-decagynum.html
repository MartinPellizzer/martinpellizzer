Mesembryanthemum aequilaterale var. decagynum

Mesembryanthemum aequilaterale var. decagynum, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and distinctive, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
