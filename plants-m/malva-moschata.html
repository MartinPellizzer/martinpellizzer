Malva moschata

Malva moschata, commonly known as the musk mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in traditional herbal remedies.
