Melochia scordiifolia

Melochia scordiifolia, commonly known as the stink vine, is a tropical climbing plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, and the leaves have a distinctive, slightly unpleasant odor.
