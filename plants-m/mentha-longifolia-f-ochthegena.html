Mentha longifolia f. ochthegena

Mentha longifolia f. ochthegena, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a horticultural form of the common mint plant.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in width. This variety produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
