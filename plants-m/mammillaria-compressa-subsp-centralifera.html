Mammillaria compressa subsp. centralifera

Mammillaria compressa subsp.

centralifera, commonly known as the Central Mexican Hedgehog Cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to central Mexico. It features a globular to cylindrical stem that is often ribbed and covered in dense, white to grayish woolly hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its textured surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
