Madhuca chrysocarpa

Madhuca chrysocarpa, commonly known as golden fruit or yellow oil tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in oil. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oil.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
