Mesosetum gibbosum

Mesosetum gibbosum, commonly known as the knobbed bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas and has a distinctive appearance with its swollen nodes along the stem. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Mesosetum gibbosum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
