Maba sudanensis

Maba sudanensis, commonly known as the Sudanese maba, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly Sudan and the Sahel region.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and can thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often with a pink or purple hue.

Maba sudanensis is valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction in local communities.
