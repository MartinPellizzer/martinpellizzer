Millettia usaramensis

Millettia usaramensis, commonly known as the Usaramensis Millettia, is a species of leguminous tree native to the tropical rainforests of West Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive floral structures. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It is often found in secondary growth forests and plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local communities.
