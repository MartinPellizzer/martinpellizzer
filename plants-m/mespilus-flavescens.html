Mespilus flavescens

Mespilus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow medlar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested in late autumn. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The yellow medlar is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
