Mentha × mosoniensis

Mentha × mosoniensis, commonly known as the Hungarian mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha longifolia and Mentha piperita.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. The plant is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.

Its growth habit is typically upright, with a spreading rhizomatous root system that allows it to colonize new areas easily.
