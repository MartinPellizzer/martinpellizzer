Magnolia hypolampra

Magnolia hypolampra, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are ovate and have a smooth, glossy surface, while the bark is gray and fissured. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
