Melandrium sordidum

Melandrium sordidum, commonly known as the stinkcup, is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence.

Melandrium sordidum is known for its distinctive, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the plant is crushed.
