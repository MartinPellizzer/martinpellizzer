Malpighia martinicensis var. jussieuana

Malpighia martinicensis var. jussieuana, commonly known as the red mombin, is a species native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that produces vibrant red berries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant features simple, opposite leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The berries are rich in vitamin C and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
