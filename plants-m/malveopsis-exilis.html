Malveopsis exilis

Malveopsis exilis, commonly known as the slender malveopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
