Mikania arthroclada

Mikania arthroclada, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the morning. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
