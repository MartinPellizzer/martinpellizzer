Mutisia viciifolia var. genuina

Mutisia viciifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the white mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features a woody stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
