Mentha × piperita f. rotundella

Mentha × piperita f. rotundella, commonly known as peppermint, is a hybrid species within the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and strong menthol scent. The plant is perennial, growing up to 60 cm tall with square stems and opposite leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple, appearing in whorls along the stem.
