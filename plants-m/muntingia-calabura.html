Muntingia calabura

Muntingia calabura, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or custard apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. The fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
