Meum athamanticum subsp. nevadense

Meum athamanticum subsp.

nevadense, commonly known as the Nevada thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.
