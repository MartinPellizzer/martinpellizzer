Mentha serrulata

Mentha serrulata, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple.

Mentha serrulata is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, often used in teas and as a natural remedy.
