Melaleuca cowleyae

Melaleuca cowleyae, commonly known as the Cowley's bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the northern regions of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
