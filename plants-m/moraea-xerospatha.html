Moraea xerospatha

Moraea xerospatha, commonly known as the Cape bell, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces showy inflorescences in late spring or early summer.

Moraea xerospatha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
