Mesembryanthemum vescum

Mesembryanthemum vescum, commonly known as the common ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, succulent stems and leaves. It produces a profusion of yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday.

Mesembryanthemum vescum is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
