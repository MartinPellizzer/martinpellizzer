Marsdenia glandulifera

Marsdenia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular Marsdenia, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. Its stems are often covered with glandular dots, which give it its scientific name.

It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
