Myrcia rostrata var. brunea

Myrcia rostrata var. brunea, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green and aromatic. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are borne in clusters.

The fruit is a small, reddish drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.
