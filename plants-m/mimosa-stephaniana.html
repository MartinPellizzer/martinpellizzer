Mimosa stephaniana

Mimosa stephaniana, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and delicate, fern-like leaves that fold shut when touched, a response known as thigmonasty. The plant produces small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a soft, ethereal appearance to its overall form. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and unique characteristics, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
