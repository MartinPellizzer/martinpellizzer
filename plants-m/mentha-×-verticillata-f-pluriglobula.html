Mentha × verticillata f. pluriglobula

Mentha × verticillata f. pluriglobula, commonly known as the globe mint, is a hybrid species within the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant leaves. The plant produces purple or pink flowers that bloom in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.

This variety is well-suited for moist soils and partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
