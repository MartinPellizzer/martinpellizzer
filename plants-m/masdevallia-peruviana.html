Masdevallia peruviana

Masdevallia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often brightly colored flowers that resemble miniature orchids, with intricate patterns and a unique structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high altitudes, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette, adapted to conserve moisture in its native habitat.

Masdevallia peruviana is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
