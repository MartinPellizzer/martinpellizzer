Melica alata var. filiculmis

Melica alata var. filiculmis, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a variant of the meadowgrass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each containing several small, two-lobed flowers.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
