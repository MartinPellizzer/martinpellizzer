Machilus clarkeanus

Machilus clarkeanus, commonly known as the Chinese laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel, avocado, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, dark blue berries.

Machilus clarkeanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
