Mentha ischnostachya

Mentha ischnostachya, commonly known as the African mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Mentha ischnostachya is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream sides.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
