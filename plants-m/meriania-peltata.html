Meriania peltata

Meriania peltata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically velvety to the touch. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
