Melissitus koraiensis

Melissitus koraiensis, commonly known as the Korean melissitus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Melissitus koraiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
