Macropeplus ligustrinus var. schwackeanus

Macropeplus ligustrinus var. schwackeanus, commonly known as the Schwacke's boxwood mite, is a variant of the boxwood mite that primarily affects boxwood plants.

This mite is characterized by its small, elongated body and pale coloration, which helps it blend with the foliage of its host. It feeds on the sap of boxwood leaves, leading to stippling and yellowing of the foliage. The plant's botanical features include dense, compact growth and dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in ornamental landscaping.

Due to its impact on boxwood health, this variant is a subject of study in horticultural and pest management research.
