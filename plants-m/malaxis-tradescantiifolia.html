Malaxis tradescantiifolia

Malaxis tradescantiifolia, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Botanically, it is part of the Orchidaceae family and exhibits typical orchid features such as a specialized root system and a unique method of pollination.
