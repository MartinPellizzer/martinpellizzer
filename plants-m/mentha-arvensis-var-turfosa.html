Mentha arvensis var. turfosa

Mentha arvensis var. turfosa, commonly known as field mint, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a fresh, minty scent.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.
