Myrcia antonia

Myrcia antonia, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, and the plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Myrcia antonia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
