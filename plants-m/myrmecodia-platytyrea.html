Myrmecodia platytyrea

Myrmecodia platytyrea, commonly known as the ant plant, is a unique epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its swollen, tree-like stems that form a hollow structure, providing shelter for ants. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with certain ant species, which in turn protect it from herbivores. Its leaves are small and clustered, and it produces small, white flowers that are often overlooked.

This plant is popular among horticulturists for its unusual appearance and ecological interest.
