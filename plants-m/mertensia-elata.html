Mertensia elata

Mertensia elata, commonly known as bluebells, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has heart-shaped, hairy leaves. It is part of the Violaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Mertensia elata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
