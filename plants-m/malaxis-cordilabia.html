Malaxis cordilabia

Malaxis cordilabia, commonly known as the heart-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in the spring. The plant produces a slender, wiry stem that bears a single, erect flower with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in its native range.
