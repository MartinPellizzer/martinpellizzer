Myrcia humboldtiana var. caribaea

Myrcia humboldtiana var. caribaea, commonly known as the Caribaea myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fragrant foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping.

The fruit, which is a small, fleshy berry, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.
