Mentha viridis var. tenuis

Mentha viridis var. tenuis, commonly known as narrow-leaf mint, is a variant of the green mint species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal teas and culinary applications.
