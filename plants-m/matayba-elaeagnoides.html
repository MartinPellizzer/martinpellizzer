Matayba elaeagnoides

Matayba elaeagnoides, commonly known as the Brazilian silverleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Elaeagnaceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are consumed by various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Matayba elaeagnoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
