Marrubium sivasense

Marrubium sivasense, commonly known as Sivas marrubium, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
