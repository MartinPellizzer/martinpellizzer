Malus subcordata

Malus subcordata, commonly known as the Japanese apple tree, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in early spring and vibrant red or orange fruits in autumn. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly heart-shaped base, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

It is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in Japanese horticulture.
