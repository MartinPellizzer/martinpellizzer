Macrocarpaea opulenta

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their nectar-rich structure and bright coloration.

Macrocarpaea opulenta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
