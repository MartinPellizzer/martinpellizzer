Medinilla assamica

Medinilla assamica, commonly known as the Assam velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its flowers are known to bloom in the early morning and emit a mild, sweet scent.

Medinilla assamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
