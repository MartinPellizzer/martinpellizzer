Melocactus macrocanthus

Melocactus macrocanthus, commonly known as the giant melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its large, globular stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered in thick, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, melon-like fruits. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
