Mentha longifolia var. macilenta

Mentha longifolia var. macilenta, commonly known as slender mint, is a variant of the spearmint species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
