Muhlenbergia pilosa

Muhlenbergia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy muhlenbergia, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and soft, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with purple or brown.

Muhlenbergia pilosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
