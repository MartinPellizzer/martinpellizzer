Miconia calvescens

Miconia calvescens, commonly known as the glorybower, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in many regions. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, contributing to its spread.

Due to its aggressive growth, it poses a significant threat to native ecosystems.
