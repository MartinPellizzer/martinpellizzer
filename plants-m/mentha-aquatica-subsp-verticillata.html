Mentha aquatica subsp. verticillata

Mentha aquatica subsp.

verticillata, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herb native to moist environments in Europe and parts of Asia. It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a serrated edge.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which give it its subspecies name "verticillata," meaning "wheel-like.". Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It thrives in wet soils near streams, ponds, and damp meadows.
