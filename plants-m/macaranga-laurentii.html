Macaranga laurentii

Macaranga laurentii, commonly known as the giant sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive three-lobed capsules containing numerous seeds. The species is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts due to its ecological versatility.
