Maxillaria pastensis

Maxillaria pastensis, commonly known as the Pastens' orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a compact rosette, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Maxillaria pastensis is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and adaptability to various cultivation conditions.
