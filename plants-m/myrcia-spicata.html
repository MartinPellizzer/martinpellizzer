Myrcia spicata

Myrcia spicata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland regions.

Myrcia spicata is also known for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
