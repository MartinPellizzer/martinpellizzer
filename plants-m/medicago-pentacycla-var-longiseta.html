Medicago pentacycla var. longiseta

Medicago pentacycla var. longiseta, commonly known as the long-haired sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and distinctive, feathery leaves with long, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by narrow, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and erosion control due to its hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration and agricultural systems.
