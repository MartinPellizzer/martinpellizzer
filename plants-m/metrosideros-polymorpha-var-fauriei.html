Metrosideros polymorpha var. fauriei

Metrosideros polymorpha var. fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's Hawaiian mahogany, is a variant of the native Hawaiian tree species.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and distinctive red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments on the windward sides of Hawaiian islands. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it has a tendency to form dense thickets.

The species is important for its ecological role in supporting native wildlife and its cultural significance to the Hawaiian people.
