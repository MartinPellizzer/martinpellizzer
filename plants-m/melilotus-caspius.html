Melilotus caspius

Melilotus caspius, commonly known as Caspian sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its strong, sweet scent, which is often used in perfumery and aromatherapy. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Melilotus caspius is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
