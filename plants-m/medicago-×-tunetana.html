Medicago × tunetana

Medicago × tunetana, commonly known as the Algerian clover, is a hybrid perennial legume native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and can be used for forage in certain agricultural settings.

Medicago × tunetana is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility.
