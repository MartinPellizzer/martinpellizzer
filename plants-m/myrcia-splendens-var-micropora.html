Myrcia splendens var. micropora

Myrcia splendens var. micropora, commonly known as the small-fruited myrcia, is a shrub native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, clustered in small inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens for its visual interest.
