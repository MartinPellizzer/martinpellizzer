Merremia hederacea

Merremia hederacea, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread across the ground, often forming dense mats. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing habits.
