Myoxanthus octomeriae

Myoxanthus octomeriae, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Myoxanthus octomeriae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
