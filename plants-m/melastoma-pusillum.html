Melastoma pusillum

Melastoma pusillum, commonly known as the small melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
