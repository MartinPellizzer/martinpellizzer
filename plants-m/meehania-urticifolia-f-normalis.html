Meehania urticifolia f. normalis

Meehania urticifolia f. normalis, commonly known as the common meehania, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of eastern Australia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, stiff hairs, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins and a hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense terminal clusters, which are typically borne during the spring and summer months.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
