Mimosa rhodostachya

Mimosa rhodostachya, commonly known as the red-stemmed mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Mimosa rhodostachya is also known for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait shared with other species in the Mimosa genus.
