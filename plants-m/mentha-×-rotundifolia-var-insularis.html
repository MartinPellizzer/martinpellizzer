Mentha × rotundifolia var. insularis

Mentha × rotundifolia var. insularis, commonly known as the island mint, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Mentha rotundifolia and other Mentha species.

It is a perennial herb with a creeping rhizomatous growth habit, often forming dense mats. The leaves are round or oval in shape, with a slightly hairy surface, and they emit a strong menthol scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stems.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
