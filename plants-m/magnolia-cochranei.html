Magnolia cochranei

Magnolia cochranei, commonly known as the Cochrane magnolia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white to pale pink. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
