Mimosa hapaloclada

Mimosa hapaloclada, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Mimosa hapaloclada is also known for its sensitive leaves, which fold shut when touched, a trait that adds to its unique appeal.
