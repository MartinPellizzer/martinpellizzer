Malus microcarpa turbinata

Malus microcarpa turbinata, commonly known as the Turbinated Apple, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia, particularly the Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small apple-like berry, is edible but not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
