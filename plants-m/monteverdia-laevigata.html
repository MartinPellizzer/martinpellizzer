Monteverdia laevigata

Monteverdia laevigata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Monteverdia laevigata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
