Magnolia kobus f. rosea

Magnolia kobus f. rosea, commonly known as the rosea magnolia, is a cultivar of the magnolia tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive, layered petal structure. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the fall, and it produces fragrant blooms in early spring. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The foliage is broad and dark green, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
