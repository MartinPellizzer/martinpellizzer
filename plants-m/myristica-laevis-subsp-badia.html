Myristica laevis subsp. badia

Myristica laevis subsp.

badia, commonly known as the Banda nutmeg, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia. It is a small evergreen tree that produces distinctive yellow-orange fruits, which contain the aromatic seeds used for nutmeg and mace.

The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

The subspecies is characterized by its slightly different morphology compared to the main species, including variations in leaf shape and fruit size.
