Mascagnia psilophylla var. antifebrilis

Mascagnia psilophylla var. antifebrilis, commonly known as the fever-reducing mescal bean, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive, somewhat heart-shaped appearance.
