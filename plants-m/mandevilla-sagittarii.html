Mandevilla sagittarii

Mandevilla sagittarii, commonly known as the arrowleaf mandevilla, is a flowering vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens or as a houseplant.

Mandevilla sagittarii is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it a low-maintenance choice for gardeners.
