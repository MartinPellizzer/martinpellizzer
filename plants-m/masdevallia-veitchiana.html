Masdevallia veitchiana

Masdevallia veitchiana, commonly known as the Veitch's masdevallia, is a tropical orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers that feature a unique combination of sepals and petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its high-altitude environment.

Masdevallia veitchiana is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively compact size.
