Mantisia

Mantisia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, often pale or white flowers that resemble ghostly shapes, hence its common name. The plants are typically herbaceous, with hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or heart-shaped. Mantisia species are native to various regions in Africa and are known for their ornamental value in gardens.

They thrive in well-drained soil and require full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
