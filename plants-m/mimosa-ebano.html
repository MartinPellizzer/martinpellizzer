Mimosa ebano

Mimosa ebano, commonly known as black mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its sensitive leaves that fold shut when touched, a trait called thigmonastic response. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

The bark and leaves of Mimosa ebano have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
