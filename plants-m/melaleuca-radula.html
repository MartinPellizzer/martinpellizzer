Melaleuca radula

Melaleuca radula, commonly known as the slender honeymyrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Australia, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. It is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes, adapting well to harsh environmental conditions.

Melaleuca radula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
