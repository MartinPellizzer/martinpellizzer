Monoon chloranthum

Monoon chloranthum, commonly known as the white-flowered monoon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Monoonaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences and are visited by various pollinators.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
