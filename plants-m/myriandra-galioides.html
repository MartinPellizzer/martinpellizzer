Myriandra galioides

Myriandra galioides, commonly known as the swamp myriandra, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, often with a waxy coating.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
