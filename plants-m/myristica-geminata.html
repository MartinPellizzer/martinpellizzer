Myristica geminata

Myristica geminata, commonly known as the double nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen belonging to the Myristicaceae family.

It is native to the Moluccas (formerly the Spice Islands) in Indonesia and is closely related to the nutmeg tree, Myristica fragrans. The plant produces two types of seeds, known as "double nuts," which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its aromatic seeds contain essential oils and have been used for centuries in culinary and medicinal applications.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its unique botanical and economic significance.
