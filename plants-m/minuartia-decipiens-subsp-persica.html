Minuartia decipiens subsp. persica

Minuartia decipiens subsp.

persica, commonly known as the Persian rock cress, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to rocky and arid environments in the Middle East and Central Asia. It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
