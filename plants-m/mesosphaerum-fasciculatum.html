Mesosphaerum fasciculatum

Mesosphaerum fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy texture. Mesosphaerum fasciculatum has small, spherical fruits that contain seeds, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
