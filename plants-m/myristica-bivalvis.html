Myristica bivalvis

Myristica bivalvis, commonly known as the two-valved myristica, is a member of the Myristicaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This evergreen tree is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces fragrant, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its unique two-valved capsule fruit, which opens to release its seeds. Myristica bivalvis is also valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
