Monocladus saxatilis var. solidus

Monocladus saxatilis var. solidus, commonly known as the rock monoclad, is a flowering plant native to rocky coastal areas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to saline and well-drained soils, making it resilient to coastal conditions.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for ecological and horticultural study.
