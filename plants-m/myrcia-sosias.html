Myrcia sosias

Myrcia sosias, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are often clustered together, followed by red or orange berries.

Myrcia sosias is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth.
