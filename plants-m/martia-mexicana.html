Martia mexicana

Martia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic to many animals, including mammals and birds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and open woodlands.

Martia mexicana plays an important role in supporting local pollinators, particularly butterflies.
