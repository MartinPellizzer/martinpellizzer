Mentha ulotricha

Mentha ulotricha, commonly known as woolly mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and strongly aromatic, minty scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Mentha ulotricha is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
