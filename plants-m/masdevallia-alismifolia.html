Masdevallia alismifolia

Masdevallia alismifolia, commonly known as the water lily orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble those of water lilies, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, with long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in moist environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, erect spike bearing several fragrant, white or pale pink flowers.

Masdevallia alismifolia thrives in humid, shaded conditions and is prized for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
