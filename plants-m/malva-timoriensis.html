Malva timoriensis

Malva timoriensis, commonly known as the Timor mallow, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cotton and hibiscus.

Malva timoriensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
