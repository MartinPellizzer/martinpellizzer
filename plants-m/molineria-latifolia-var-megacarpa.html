Molineria latifolia var. megacarpa

Molineria latifolia var. megacarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaved molineria, is a striking ornamental plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to white, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
