Melaleuca aquilonia subsp. cinerea

Melaleuca aquilonia subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the grey melaleuca, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its greyish-brown, flaky bark and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in spikes, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or inland sandy areas.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
