Myrcia laxiflora

Myrcia laxiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a problematic species in certain regions.
