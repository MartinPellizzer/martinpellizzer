Mentha longifolia var. falcatodentata

Mentha longifolia var. falcatodentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a variant of the broad-leafed mint species.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often serrated along the edges. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and tubular, usually appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The leaves and flowers are aromatic, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
