Masdevallia acauda

Masdevallia acauda, commonly known as the tailless masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and its striking, intricate flowers that typically feature a combination of purple, pink, and white hues. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with high humidity and indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and delicate floral display.
