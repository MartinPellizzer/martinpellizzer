Massia triseta

Massia triseta, commonly known as the three-branched massia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a slender, upright stem with three distinct branches, giving it its common name. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Massia triseta is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
