Machaerium retusum

Machaerium retusum, commonly known as the broad-leaved machaerium, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves that have a broad, ovate shape. Its flowers are usually small and clustered in racemes, often in shades of white or pale pink.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
