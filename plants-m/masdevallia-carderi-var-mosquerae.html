Masdevallia carderi var. mosquerae

Masdevallia carderi var. mosquerae, commonly known as the Mosquerae Masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often colorful flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically features long, slender pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are usually borne on terminal inflorescences and can exhibit a variety of hues, including red, orange, and yellow.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
