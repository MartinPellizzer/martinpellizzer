Muscari lelievrei

Muscari lelievrei, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from the base of the bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Muscari lelievrei is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to naturalize over time.
