Macaranga borneensis

Macaranga borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean macaranga, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a fast-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with smooth, light green bark. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive venation pattern. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
