Martagon sylvaticum

Martagon sylvaticum, commonly known as the meadow gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Martagon sylvaticum blooms in late summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
