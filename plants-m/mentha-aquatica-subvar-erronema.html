Mentha aquatica subvar. erronema

Mentha aquatica subvar. erronema, commonly known as water mint, is a variant of the water mint species native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the mint family. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and marshes.

Its essential oils contain compounds like menthol and limonene, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.
