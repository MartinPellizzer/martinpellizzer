Myrcia almasensis

Myrcia almasensis, commonly known as the Almasen's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in understory layers of tropical forests.

Myrcia almasensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
