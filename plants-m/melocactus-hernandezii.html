Melocactus hernandezii

Melocactus hernandezii, commonly known as the Hernandez's melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem and a distinctive, spiny areoles. The plant produces a single, large, yellowish-green fruit that is edible and resembles a melon. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
