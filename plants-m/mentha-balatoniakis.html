Mentha balatoniakis

Mentha balatoniakis, commonly known as Hungarian mint, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Its leaves are elongated and serrated, with a strong, refreshing mint scent.

Mentha balatoniakis is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and used in traditional herbal remedies.
