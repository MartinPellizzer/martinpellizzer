Matricaria disciformis

Matricaria disciformis, commonly known as the German chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with a white center, which bloom in the summer months. The plant grows up to 30 cm tall, featuring finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers is widely used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.
