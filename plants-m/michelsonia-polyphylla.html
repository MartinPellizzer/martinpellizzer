Michelsonia polyphylla

Michelsonia polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved michelsonia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive hairy texture.

Michelsonia polyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
