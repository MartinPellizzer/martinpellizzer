Melochia affinis

Melochia affinis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or melochia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Melochia affinis is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
