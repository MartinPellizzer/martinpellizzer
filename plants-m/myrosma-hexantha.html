Myrosma hexantha

Myrosma hexantha, commonly known as the six-angled myrosma, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Myrosma hexantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
