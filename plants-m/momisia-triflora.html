Momisia triflora

Momisia triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered momisia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves composed of three leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Momisia triflora is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
