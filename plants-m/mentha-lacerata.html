Mentha lacerata

Mentha lacerata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Mentha lacerata is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic oils and medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.
