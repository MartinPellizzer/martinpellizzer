Millettia mannii

Millettia mannii, commonly known as African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading canopy and smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are compound, with elongated leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds.

The plant is valued for its hard wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
