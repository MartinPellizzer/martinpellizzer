Malanea ribesioides var. villosula

Malanea ribesioides var. villosula, commonly known as the hairy malanea, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting various pollinators like bees and moths.
