Majorana tomentosa

Majorana tomentosa, commonly known as woolly marjoram, is an aromatic herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces tiny, oval-shaped seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and therapeutic properties.
