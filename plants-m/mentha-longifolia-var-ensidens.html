Mentha longifolia var. ensidens

Mentha longifolia var. ensidens, commonly known as the Spanish mint, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinct minty fragrance.

This variety is often used in culinary and traditional medicinal applications due to its refreshing and cooling properties.
