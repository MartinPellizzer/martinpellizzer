Myrcia mornicola

Myrcia mornicola, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, reddish berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

Myrcia mornicola plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
