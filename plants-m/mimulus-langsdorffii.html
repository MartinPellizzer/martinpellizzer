Mimulus langsdorffii

Mimulus langsdorffii, commonly known as the Langsdorff's monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Mimulus langsdorffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
