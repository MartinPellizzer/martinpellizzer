Medicago caspica

Medicago caspica, commonly known as the Caspian medic, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Medicago caspica is also valued for its potential in forage and as a cover crop.
