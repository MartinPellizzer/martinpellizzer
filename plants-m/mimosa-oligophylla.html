Mimosa oligophylla

Mimosa oligophylla, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Mimosa oligophylla is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
