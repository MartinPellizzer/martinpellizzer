Marica palmifolia

Marica palmifolia, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow waters while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment. Its flowers, which bloom at night, are pale yellow and attract specific pollinators such as beetles.

Marica palmifolia plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in freshwater ecosystems.
