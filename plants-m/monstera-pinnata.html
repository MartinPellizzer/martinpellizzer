Monstera pinnata

Monstera pinnata, commonly known as the giant split-leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that develop deep, irregular splits as the plant matures. The leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy surface, and the plant can grow quite large, reaching heights of up to 30 feet in its natural habitat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Monstera pinnata is a popular houseplant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
