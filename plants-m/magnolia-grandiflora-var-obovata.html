Magnolia grandiflora var. obovata

Magnolia grandiflora var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The leaves are broad, elliptical, and have a smooth, waxy texture. The tree has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

It is valued for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
