Musa × polycarpa

Musa × polycarpa, commonly known as the giant plantain, is a hybrid banana plant characterized by its large, elongated leaves and robust growth habit.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its edible fruits, which are typically larger and less sweet than those of common bananas. The plant produces clusters of berries that contain small, starchy seeds, making it distinct from other banana species. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its hardiness, it is often used in ornamental gardening and as a food source in various regions.
