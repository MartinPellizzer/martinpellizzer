Merremia semisagitta

Merremia semisagitta, commonly known as the zigzag morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain seeds with a hooked appendage, aiding in dispersal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

The botanical features of Merremia semisagitta make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
