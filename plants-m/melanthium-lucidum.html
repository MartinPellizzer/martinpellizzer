Melanthium lucidum

Melanthium lucidum, commonly known as the shining false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible or medicinal properties. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often tinged with red, and its leaves are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. The small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, and the plant produces small, three-lobed fruits.

It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in shaded, wet areas such as stream sides and forest floors.
