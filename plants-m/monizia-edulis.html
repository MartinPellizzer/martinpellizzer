Monizia edulis

Monizia edulis, commonly known as the edible moonflower, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe. Its botanical classification places it in the family Malvaceae, and it is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and nutritional value, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and ethnobotany.
