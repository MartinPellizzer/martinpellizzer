Milla subbiflora

Milla subbiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milla, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
