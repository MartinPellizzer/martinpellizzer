Maxillaria truxillensis

Maxillaria truxillensis, commonly known as the Truxillense Orchid, is a species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces dark green, strap-like leaves and has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with intricate patterns.

Maxillaria truxillensis thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
