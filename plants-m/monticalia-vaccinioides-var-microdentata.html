Monticalia vaccinioides var. microdentata

Monticalia vaccinioides var. microdentata, commonly known as the dwarf wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top with a lighter, sometimes whitish, underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
