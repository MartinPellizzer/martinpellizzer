Mostacillastrum incisum

Mostacillastrum incisum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandreed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in restoration projects due to its hardiness.

The botanical features of Mostacillastrum incisum make it an important species for stabilizing disturbed soils in dry landscapes.
