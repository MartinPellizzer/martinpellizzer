Melocactus pentacanthus

Melocactus pentacanthus, commonly known as the five-spined melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, globular shape and a crown of spines that typically number five, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces a sweet, edible fruit that resembles a small melon, which is often consumed by both humans and animals. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions.

Melocactus pentacanthus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in dry gardens and collections.
