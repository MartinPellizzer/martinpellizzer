Melica ramosa subsp. saxatilis

Melica ramosa subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the saxatile wood millet, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or alpine environments.

The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
