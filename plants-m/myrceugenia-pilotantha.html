Myrceugenia pilotantha

Myrceugenia pilotantha, commonly known as the Chilean blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in temperate rainforests, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers are white and fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The berries, which are rich in antioxidants, are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
