Mucuna montana

Mucuna montana, commonly known as the climbing cowpea, is a leguminous vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and distinctive yellow and purple flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods containing small, edible seeds that are rich in protein. Mucuna montana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular plant in horticulture and traditional medicine.
