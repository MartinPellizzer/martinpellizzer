Myristica longepetiolata

Myristica longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled nutmeg tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves with elongated petioles. The plant produces fragrant, woody fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its economic and medicinal value, it is often cultivated in tropical botanical gardens and agroforestry systems.
