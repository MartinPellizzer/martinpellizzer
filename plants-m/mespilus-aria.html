Mespilus aria

Mespilus aria, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny fruit that ripens in the fall. The plant has alternate, simple, and serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in racemes, typically blooming in early spring.

The medlar is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
