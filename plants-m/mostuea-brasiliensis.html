Mostuea brasiliensis

Mostuea brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ginger, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, leafy stems with large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are typically yellow or orange in color.

The rhizomes of Mostuea brasiliensis are often used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
