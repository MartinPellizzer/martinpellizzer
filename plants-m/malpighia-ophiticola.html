Malpighia ophiticola

Malpighia ophiticola, commonly known as the red mallow, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malpighiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically ovate with a velvety texture, and the fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Malpighia ophiticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
