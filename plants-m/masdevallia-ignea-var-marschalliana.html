Masdevallia ignea var. marschalliana

Masdevallia ignea var. marschalliana, commonly known as the fiery torch orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange inflorescences, which resemble a burning torch, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a dramatic touch to its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouse environments.
