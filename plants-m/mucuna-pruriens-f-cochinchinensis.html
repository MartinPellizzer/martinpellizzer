Mucuna pruriens f. cochinchinensis

Mucuna pruriens f. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the hairy nightshade or cow-itch bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, trifoliate leaves and distinctive, hairy pods containing elongated, curved seeds. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in tropical climates. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in traditional medicine.
