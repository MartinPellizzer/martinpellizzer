Milium vernale subsp. tzvelevii

Milium vernale subsp.

tzvelevii, commonly known as the Caucasian milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered inflorescences.

The plant produces white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are linear and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, alpine environments.
