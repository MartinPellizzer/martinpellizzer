Melilotus balbisianus

Melilotus balbisianus, commonly known as Balbisian sweet clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots have a long history of use in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
