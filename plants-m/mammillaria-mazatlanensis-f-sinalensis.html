Mammillaria mazatlanensis f. sinalensis

Mammillaria mazatlanensis f. sinalensis, commonly known as the Sinalan mammillaria, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It features a globular stem with prominent, white, woolly areoles and spines that are typically yellow to orange in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
