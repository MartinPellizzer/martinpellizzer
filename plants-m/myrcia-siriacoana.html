Myrcia siriacoana

Myrcia siriacoana, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, smooth, black drupe that contains a single seed.

Myrcia siriacoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
