Myrtus axillaris

Myrtus axillaris, commonly known as the creeping myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white wax, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Myrtus axillaris is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
