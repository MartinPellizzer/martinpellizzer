Maxillaria guentheriana

Maxillaria guentheriana, commonly known as the Guenther's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in seasonal environments. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns.

Maxillaria guentheriana prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
