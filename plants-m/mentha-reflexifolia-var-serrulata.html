Mentha reflexifolia var. serrulata

Mentha reflexifolia var. serrulata, commonly known as serrated mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, serrated leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its menthol-rich leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
