Mundulea parvifolia

Mundulea parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf acacia, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has small, pinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Mundulea parvifolia is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to harsh, semi-arid climates.
