Malacantha acutifolia

Malacantha acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved malacantha, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are sharply pointed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
