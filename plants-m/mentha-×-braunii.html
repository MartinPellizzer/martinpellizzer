Mentha × braunii

Mentha × braunii, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Mentha viridiflora and Mentha spicata, combining characteristics of both parent plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in whorled clusters. It is native to the eastern Himalayas and is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds that contribute to its cooling and soothing properties.
