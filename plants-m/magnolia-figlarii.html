Magnolia figlarii

Magnolia figlarii, commonly known as the figleaf magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 inches in length. The tree produces showy, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fleshy, red berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its striking foliage and floral display.
