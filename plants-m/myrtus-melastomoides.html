Myrtus melastomoides

Myrtus melastomoides, commonly known as the Australian myrtle, is a shrub native to Australia, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils and salty air. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
