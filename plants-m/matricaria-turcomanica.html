Matricaria turcomanica

Matricaria turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers surrounded by white petals. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

The essential oil extracted from its flowers is valued for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
