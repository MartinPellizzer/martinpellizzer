Myrcia crocea

Myrcia crocea, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Myrcia crocea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of aromatic oils.
