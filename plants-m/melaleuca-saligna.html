Melaleuca saligna

Melaleuca saligna, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of red or pink flowers that resemble bottlebrushes. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in a powdery white bloom.

Melaleuca saligna is often found in coastal heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
