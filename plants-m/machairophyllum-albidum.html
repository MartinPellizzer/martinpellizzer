Machairophyllum albidum

Machairophyllum albidum, commonly known as the white feathered saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fleshy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a white waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense, compact cluster. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Machairophyllum albidum is often found in alpine meadows and is a member of the Montiaceae family.
