Maxillaria psittacina

Maxillaria psittacina, commonly known as the parrot orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that resemble a parrot's beak, hence its common name. The plant produces pendulous, greenish-yellow inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a waxy coating.

Maxillaria psittacina is a popular choice for both indoor and greenhouse cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
