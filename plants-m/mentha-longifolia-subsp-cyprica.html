Mentha longifolia subsp. cyprica

Mentha longifolia subsp.

cyprica, commonly known as Cyprus mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a strong, minty scent.

The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
