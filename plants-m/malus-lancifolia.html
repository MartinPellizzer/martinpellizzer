Malus lancifolia

Malus lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Malus lancifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
