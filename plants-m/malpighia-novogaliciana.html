Malpighia novogaliciana

Malpighia novogaliciana, commonly known as the Brazilian passionfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It produces small, orange to red berries that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with smooth margins and a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a distinctive crown-like structure.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and has medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
