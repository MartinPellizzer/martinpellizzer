Mentha × barnolae

Mentha × barnolae, commonly known as hybrid mint, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Mentha viridis and Mentha spicata.

It is characterized by its square, erect stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Mentha × barnolae is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in culinary, medicinal, and aromatic applications.
