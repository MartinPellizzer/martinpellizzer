Machaerium floridum

Machaerium floridum, commonly known as Florida machaerium, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, triangular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of white to pale purple flowers, which bloom in summer and attract various pollinators. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
