Mussaenda angustisepala

Mussaenda angustisepala, commonly known as the narrow-lobed Mussaenda, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in terminal panicles, which are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are known to attract various pollinators, including bees and birds, due to their nectar-rich structure.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable ornamental species in tropical gardens.
