Mirabilis campestris

Mirabilis campestris, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically pink, orange, or yellow, and they attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Mirabilis campestris is a herbaceous annual that thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in desert environments.
