Mertensia ciliata var. subpubescens

Mertensia ciliata var. subpubescens, commonly known as the Appalachian bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy underside, and produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in forest understories. Its flowering period typically lasts from March to May, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.

The species is also noted for its ability to naturalize and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
