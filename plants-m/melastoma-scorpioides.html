Melastoma scorpioides

Melastoma scorpioides, commonly known as the scarlet bristle or red bristle plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered inflorescences. The flowers are typically red or pink, with prominent bracts that give the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
