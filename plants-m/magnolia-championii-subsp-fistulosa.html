Magnolia championii subsp. fistulosa

Magnolia championii subsp.

fistulosa, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico. It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes.
