Mesosphaerum pumilum

Mesosphaerum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf mesosphaerum, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has small, clustered leaves and produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme environments.
