Maresia nana var. glabra

Maresia nana var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth dwarf maresia, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
