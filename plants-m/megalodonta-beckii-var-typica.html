Megalodonta beckii var. typica

Megalodonta beckii var. typica, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking plant characterized by its large, compound fronds that can reach several meters in length.

It belongs to the family Osmundaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's fronds are typically bipinnate, with numerous small, lance-shaped pinnae that give it a lush, feathery appearance. It produces spores in sporangia located on the underside of its fronds, a feature typical of ferns.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
