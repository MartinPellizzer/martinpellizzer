Melothria hirsuta

Melothria hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bitterball, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, round, green fruits that turn red when ripe, covered in a fuzzy, hairy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The fruits are typically consumed raw or cooked and have a mildly tart flavor.
