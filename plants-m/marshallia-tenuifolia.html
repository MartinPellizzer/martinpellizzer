Marshallia tenuifolia

Marshallia tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and its flowers are pale yellow to white, often surrounded by bracts that give the appearance of a single bloom.

Marshallia tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
