Melaleuca stipitata

Melaleuca stipitata, commonly known as the slender honeymyrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Australia, where it thrives in sandy soils and humid climates. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its bark is smooth and often peels to reveal a reddish inner layer, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Melaleuca stipitata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
