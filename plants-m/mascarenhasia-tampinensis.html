Mascarenhasia tampinensis

Mascarenhasia tampinensis, commonly known as the Mascarenhasia tree, is a species native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal and dry forest habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
