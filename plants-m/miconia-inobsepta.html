Miconia inobsepta

Miconia inobsepta, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with small, white to pinkish flowers that are typically followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia inobsepta is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land management in invaded areas.
