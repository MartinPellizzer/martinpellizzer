Mastixia trichotoma

Mastixia trichotoma, commonly known as the woolly mastix tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed or moist soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
