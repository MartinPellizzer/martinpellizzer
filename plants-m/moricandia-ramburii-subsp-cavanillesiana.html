Moricandia ramburii subsp. cavanillesiana

Moricandia ramburii subsp.

cavanillesiana, commonly known as the Cavanilles' rocket, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
