Malpighia piauhiensis

Malpighia piauhiensis, commonly known as the Piauhy gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It produces small, bright red, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses.
