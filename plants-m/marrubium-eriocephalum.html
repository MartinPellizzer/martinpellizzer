Marrubium eriocephalum

Marrubium eriocephalum, commonly known as the woolly horehound, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, woolly white hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow, gray-green, and also covered in fine, soft hairs.

Marrubium eriocephalum is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its medicinal properties.
