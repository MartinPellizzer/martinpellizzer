Myrcia fastigiata var. coriacea

Myrcia fastigiata var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with thick, leathery leaves that have a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
