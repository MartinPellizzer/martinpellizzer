Miconia truncata

Miconia truncata, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Miconia truncata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
