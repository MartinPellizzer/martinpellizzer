Mouriri ciliata

Mouriri ciliata, commonly known as the Brazilian passionfruit, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. Its fruit is a hairy, yellowish-orange berry that contains numerous seeds.

Mouriri ciliata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
