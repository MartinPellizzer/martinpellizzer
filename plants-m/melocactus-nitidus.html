Melocactus nitidus

Melocactus nitidus, commonly known as the velvet melon cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, white, velvety spines. The plant produces a large, yellow, fleshy fruit that resembles a melon, which is edible when ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Melocactus nitidus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
