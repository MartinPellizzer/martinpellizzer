Mentha × niliaca var. sapida

Mentha × niliaca var. sapida, commonly known as the sweet catmint, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves that emit a minty fragrance. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile garden plant.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and medicinal uses.
