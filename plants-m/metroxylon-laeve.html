Metroxylon laeve

Metroxylon laeve, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody fruits containing edible seeds. Its trunk is valued for its fibrous material, which is used in making ropes and textiles.

The areca palm is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its economic value in traditional crafts.
