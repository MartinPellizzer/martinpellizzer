Magnolia × pilocarpa var. ellipticifolia

Magnolia × pilocarpa var. ellipticifolia, commonly known as the ellipticifolia magnolia, is a hybrid evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are elliptical in shape, glossy green, and often have a slightly serrated edge. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
