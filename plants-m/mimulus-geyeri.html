Mimulus geyeri

Mimulus geyeri, commonly known as the Geyeri's monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Mimulus geyeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
