Miconia cornuta

Miconia cornuta, commonly known as the horned miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that are highly fragrant. Its fruit is a fleshy, reddish berry that contains numerous seeds.

Miconia cornuta is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some regions.
