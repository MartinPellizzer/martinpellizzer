Muscari amoenocomum

Muscari amoenocomum, commonly known as the Persian grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, resembling small grapes. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

Muscari amoenocomum is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate appearance and pleasant scent.
