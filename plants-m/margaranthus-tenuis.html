Margaranthus tenuis

Margaranthus tenuis, commonly known as the slender margarita, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical structure supports pollination by bees and other insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
