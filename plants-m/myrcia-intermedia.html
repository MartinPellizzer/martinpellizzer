Myrcia intermedia

Myrcia intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in certain regions.
