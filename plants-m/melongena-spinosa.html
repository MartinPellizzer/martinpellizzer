Melongena spinosa

Melongena spinosa, commonly known as the spiny eggplant, is a unique member of the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and dark purple, egg-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring warm climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually yellow with prominent purple stamens, and it produces fruit that can be harvested when fully mature.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
