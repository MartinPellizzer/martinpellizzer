Megastachya panicoides

Megastachya panicoides, commonly known as the panicle grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in a panicle shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
