Myoporum velutinum

Myoporum velutinum, commonly known as the velvet myoporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in winter, attracting native pollinators. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.

Myoporum velutinum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in coastal and dry environments.
