Melanthium inustum

Melanthium inustum, commonly known as the narrowleaf false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible or medicinal properties. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lanceolate leaves that have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and it typically blooms from late spring to early summer.

Melanthium inustum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks.
