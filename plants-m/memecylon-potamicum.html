Memecylon potamicum

Memecylon potamicum, commonly known as the river memecylon, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the wetlands and riverine forests of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like India and Indonesia. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal in aquatic environments.
