Malva americana

Malva americana, commonly known as the American mallows, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
