Marsdenia argentata

Marsdenia argentata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a silvery sheen. It produces small, round berries that are green when immature and turn red when mature.

This plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
