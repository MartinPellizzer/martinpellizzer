Masdevallia collina

Masdevallia collina, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its intricate, often colorful flowers and delicate foliage. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes, where it thrives in shaded, moist conditions. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its challenging habitat.

Masdevallia collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
