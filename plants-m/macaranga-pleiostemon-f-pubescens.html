Macaranga pleiostemon f. pubescens

Macaranga pleiostemon f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy macaranga, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough texture.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, tropical forests.
