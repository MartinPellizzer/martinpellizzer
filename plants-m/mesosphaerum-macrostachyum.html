Mesosphaerum macrostachyum

Mesosphaerum macrostachyum, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant has a robust, woody stem with a fibrous bark, and its leaves are typically large, palmately lobed, providing dense foliage. It produces long, cylindrical inflorescences that bear numerous small, bright orange to red flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and birds.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its vibrant floral display and ability to grow rapidly in suitable conditions.
