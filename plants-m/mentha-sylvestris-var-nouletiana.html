Mentha sylvestris var. nouletiana

Mentha sylvestris var. nouletiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
