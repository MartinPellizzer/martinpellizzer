Mentha aquatica var. longipedunculata

Mentha aquatica var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed water mint, is a variant of the water mint plant native to moist, wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in shallow water or damp soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along riverbanks.

This variant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used historically in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
