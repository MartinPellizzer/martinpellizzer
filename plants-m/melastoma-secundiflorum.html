Melastoma secundiflorum

Melastoma secundiflorum, commonly known as the redroot strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and woody stems, which are typical of the Melastomataceae family. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a glossy surface.

Melastoma secundiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
