Melochia cinerea var. discolor

Melochia cinerea var. discolor, commonly known as the white-veined melochia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green veining. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a fleshy, three-lobed capsule that turns red when mature.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
