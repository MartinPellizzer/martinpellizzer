Miconia krugiana

Miconia krugiana, commonly known as the Krug's miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple bracteate flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release small, winged seeds when mature.

Miconia krugiana is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
