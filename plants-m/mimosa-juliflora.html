Mimosa juliflora

Mimosa juliflora, commonly known as the Jerusalem flower, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, feathery foliage and clusters of small, yellow, pod-like fruits that resemble miniature umbrellas. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers that open in response to touch, a phenomenon known as thigmonastic movement. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
