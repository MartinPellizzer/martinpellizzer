Myrcia teresensis

Myrcia teresensis, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Myrcia teresensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
