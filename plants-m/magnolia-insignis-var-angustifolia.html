Magnolia insignis var. angustifolia

Magnolia insignis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf magnolia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, providing an elegant, foliage-rich appearance throughout the growing season. The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
