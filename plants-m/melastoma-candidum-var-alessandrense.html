Melastoma candidum var. alessandrense

Melastoma candidum var. alessandrense, commonly known as the white-flowered melastoma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. It has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
