Myristica sepicana

Myristica sepicana, commonly known as the nutmeg apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The tree produces an edible fruit that resembles a small apple, containing a single large seed. The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and has a mild, sweet aroma.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
