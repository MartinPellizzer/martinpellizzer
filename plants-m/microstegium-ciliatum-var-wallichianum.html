Microstegium ciliatum var. wallichianum

Microstegium ciliatum var. wallichianum, commonly known as the Himalayan blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with ciliate margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
