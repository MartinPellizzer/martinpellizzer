Malva antofagastana

Malva antofagastana, commonly known as the Antofagasta mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

Malva antofagastana plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing desert soils.
