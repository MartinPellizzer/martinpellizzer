Mercurialis annua var. transsylvanica

Mercurialis annua var. transsylvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian mercury plant, is a variant of the common mercury plant.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its botanical features include hairy stems and small, clustered inflorescences.

It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive in certain environments.
