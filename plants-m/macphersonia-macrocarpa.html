Macphersonia macrocarpa

Macphersonia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant macphersonia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a dense canopy that provides excellent shade.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
