Mouriri torquata

Mouriri torquata, commonly known as the golden sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, orange-colored fruits that are edible but have a sour taste. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.

Mouriri torquata thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
