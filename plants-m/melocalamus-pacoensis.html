Melocalamus pacoensis

Melocalamus pacoensis, commonly known as the Pacoensis bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, particularly found in moist, forested areas. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense clumps of narrow leaves. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Due to its ecological importance, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
