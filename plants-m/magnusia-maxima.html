Magnusia maxima

Magnusia maxima, commonly known as the giant magnolia, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, dark green leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in spring. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
