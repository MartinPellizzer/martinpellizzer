Malus sieboldii var. toringo

Malus sieboldii var. toringo, commonly known as the Toringo apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring attractive, often fragrant, white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically used in traditional Japanese cuisine. Its leaves are ovate, serrated, and turn vibrant red or yellow in autumn.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
