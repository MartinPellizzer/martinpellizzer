Mascagnia riparia

Mascagnia riparia, commonly known as the river mascagnia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows along riverbanks and moist woodlands, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mascagnia riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
