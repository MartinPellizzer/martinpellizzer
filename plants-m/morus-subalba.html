Morus subalba

Morus subalba, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The tree produces small, fuzzy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its bark, leaves, and fruit.

Morus subalba is also valued for its role in silk production, as its leaves are a primary food source for silkworms.
