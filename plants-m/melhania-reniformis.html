Melhania reniformis

Melhania reniformis, commonly known as the kidney bean vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are widely used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and agricultural purposes.
