Mertensia pratensis var. borealis

Mertensia pratensis var. borealis, commonly known as the northern bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open meadow habitats. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinct appearance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
