Macaranga calophylla

Macaranga calophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf paper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used for making paper. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves during the dry season, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
