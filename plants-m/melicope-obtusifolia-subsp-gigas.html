Melicope obtusifolia subsp. gigas

Melicope obtusifolia subsp.

gigas, commonly known as the giant melicope, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are typically leathery and have a glossy surface.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
