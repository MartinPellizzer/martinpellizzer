Mammillaria crinita subsp. painteri

Mammillaria crinita subsp.

painteri, commonly known as the painter's hedgehog cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to Mexico. It features a globular stem covered in white, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring or early summer, adding a striking contrast to its gray-green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and delicate nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
