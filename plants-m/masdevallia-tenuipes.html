Masdevallia tenuipes

Masdevallia tenuipes, commonly known as the slender-leaf masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments, often at high altitudes, and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
