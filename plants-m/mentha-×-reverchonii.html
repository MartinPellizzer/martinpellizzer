Mentha × reverchonii

Mentha × reverchonii, commonly known as the French mint, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Mentha aquatica and Mentha rotundifolia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. Mentha × reverchonii is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
