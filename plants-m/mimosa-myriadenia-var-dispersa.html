Mimosa myriadenia var. dispersa

Mimosa myriadenia var. dispersa, commonly known as the spreading mimosa, is a shrub native to South America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are compound and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are typically branching and can spread outwards, giving it a bushy appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
