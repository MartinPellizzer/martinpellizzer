Myrcia concisa

Myrcia concisa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe, which are an important food source for various birds and small mammals. Myrcia concisa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
