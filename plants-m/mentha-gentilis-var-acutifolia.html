Mentha gentilis var. acutifolia

Mentha gentilis var. acutifolia, commonly known as the acute-leaved mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, serrated leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a sharp, pointed tip. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its strong mint scent and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
