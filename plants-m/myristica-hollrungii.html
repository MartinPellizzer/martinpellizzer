Myristica hollrungii

Myristica hollrungii, commonly known as the Java nut or yellow nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and fragrant, woody fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsules containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and spice trade. Its botanical features include a stout trunk, compound leaves, and a unique seed structure that encases a reddish-brown kernel.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and economic significance.
