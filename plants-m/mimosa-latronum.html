Mimosa latronum

Mimosa latronum, commonly known as the mimic bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that fold shut when touched, a phenomenon known as thigmonastic response. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Mimosa latronum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
