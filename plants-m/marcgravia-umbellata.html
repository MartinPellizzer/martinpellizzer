Marcgravia umbellata

Marcgravia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful inflorescences that resemble an umbrella, hence its common name. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by brightly colored bracts, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Marcgravia umbellata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
