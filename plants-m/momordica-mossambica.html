Momordica mossambica

Momordica mossambica, commonly known as the African bitter melon, is a vine-like plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces elongated, fruit-like structures that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and trailing.

The fruit contains numerous seeds and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
