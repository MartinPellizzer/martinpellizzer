Malva davaei

Malva davaei, commonly known as the Persian mallow, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in soft, velvety hairs.

Malva davaei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
