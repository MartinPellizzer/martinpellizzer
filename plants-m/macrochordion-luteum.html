Macrochordion luteum

Macrochordion luteum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and marshes. Its roots are fibrous and help it anchor in the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

The botanical structure of Macrochordion luteum includes a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.
