Mimulus aurantiacus var. grandiflorus

Mimulus aurantiacus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the Orange Monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features striking orange-red flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
