Myristica sericea

Myristica sericea, commonly known as the velvet nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fruits resembling large, round nuts. The seeds inside the fruit are the source of the aromatic spice known as mace.

This species is valued not only for its economic importance but also for its ecological role in tropical forests.
