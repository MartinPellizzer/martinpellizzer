Marsdenia rhyncholepis

Marsdenia rhyncholepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Marsdenia, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in tropical rainforests.

Marsdenia rhyncholepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
