Melica sabinei

Melica sabinei, commonly known as Sabine's brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, characteristic of the grass family Poaceae.

Melica sabinei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
