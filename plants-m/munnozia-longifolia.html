Munnozia longifolia

Munnozia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf munnozia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax.

Munnozia longifolia is known for its resilience and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
