Matthiola parviflora

Matthiola parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered stock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale purple blooms that appear in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the plant is often grown as an ornamental in gardens due to its delicate appearance.

Matthiola parviflora is also valued for its use in phytotherapy and as a source of essential oils.
