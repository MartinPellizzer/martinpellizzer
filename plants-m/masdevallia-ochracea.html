Masdevallia ochracea

Masdevallia ochracea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, showy flowers. The plant typically features a single, erect stem with several fleshy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. Its blooms are often adorned with intricate patterns and are highly sought after in horticulture for their ornamental value.

Masdevallia ochracea thrives in cool, humid environments and requires specific care to mimic its natural habitat.
