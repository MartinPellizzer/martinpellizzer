Myagrum irregulare

Myagrum irregulare, commonly known as the irregular myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Myagrum irregulare is typically found in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
