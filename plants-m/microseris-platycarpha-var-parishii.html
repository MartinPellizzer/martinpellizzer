Microseris platycarpha var. parishii

Microseris platycarpha var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's narrowleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that disperse its seeds.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal practices.
