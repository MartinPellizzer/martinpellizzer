Melica poecilantha var. umbrosa

Melica poecilantha var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, bearing small, greenish flowers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
