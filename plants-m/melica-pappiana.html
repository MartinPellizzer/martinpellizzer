Melica pappiana

Melica pappiana, commonly known as the common wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Melica pappiana is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
