Merremia wurdackii

Merremia wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it both a popular garden plant and a potential weed in agricultural areas.
