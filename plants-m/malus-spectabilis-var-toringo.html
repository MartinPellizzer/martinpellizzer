Malus spectabilis var. toringo

Malus spectabilis var. toringo, commonly known as the Toringo apple, is a cultivar of the ornamental crabapple tree.

It is native to China and is prized for its showy, double pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically not consumed by humans but are used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8 and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

The Toringo apple is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and landscapes.
