Microstylis atropurpurea

Microstylis atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-stemmed microstylis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves with a distinctive purple hue on the stems and undersides. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets.

Microstylis atropurpurea thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.
