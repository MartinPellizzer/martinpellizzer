Mesospinidium leochilinum

Mesospinidium leochilinum, commonly known as the golden star lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It features a bright yellow to orange thallus with a distinctive star-like pattern on its upper surface. The plant's structure consists of a symbiotic association between a green alga and a fungus, which allows it to absorb nutrients from the air and rain. It is typically found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often used as an indicator species for environmental health.
