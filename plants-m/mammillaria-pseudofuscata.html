Mammillaria pseudofuscata

Mammillaria pseudofuscata, commonly known as the Desert Rose, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to yellow tubercles. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
