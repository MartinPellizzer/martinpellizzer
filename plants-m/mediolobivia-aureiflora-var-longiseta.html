Mediolobivia aureiflora var. longiseta

Mediolobivia aureiflora var. longiseta, commonly known as the golden-flowered long-hair variant, is a unique botanical specimen characterized by its striking golden-yellow blooms and elongated, silky hair-like structures along its stems.

Native to the arid regions of southern Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence, enhancing its ornamental appeal. The flowers, which bloom in late spring, are clustered in loose inflorescences and emit a subtle, sweet fragrance.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
