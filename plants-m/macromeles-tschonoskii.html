Macromeles tschonoskii

Macromeles tschonoskii, commonly known as the Tschonoskii's macaranga, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
