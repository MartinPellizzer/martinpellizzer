Mesembryanthus bicolor

Mesembryanthus bicolor, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink, yellow, and white. The plant thrives in arid environments and is adapted to store water in its thick, succulent stems and leaves. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

The flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.
