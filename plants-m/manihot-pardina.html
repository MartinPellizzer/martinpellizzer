Manihot pardina

Manihot pardina, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its roots are the primary part used, which are starchy and used as a food source.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
