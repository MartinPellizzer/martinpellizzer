Melastoma granulosum

Melastoma granulosum, commonly known as the granular melastoma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, granular bumps. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
