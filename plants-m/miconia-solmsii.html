Miconia solmsii

Miconia solmsii, commonly known as the Solms's miconia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within the rainforest understory. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
