Myrcia elevata

Myrcia elevata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a fragrant aroma.

The fruit is a small, orange-red drupe that contains a single seed.
