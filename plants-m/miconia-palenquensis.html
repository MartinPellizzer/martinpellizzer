Miconia palenquensis

Miconia palenquensis, commonly known as the Panama miconia, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to Central America, particularly found in the tropical rainforests of Panama. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with bright red bracts that surround small, white petals.

Miconia palenquensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
