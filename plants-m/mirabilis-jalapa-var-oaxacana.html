Mirabilis jalapa var. oaxacana

Mirabilis jalapa var. oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan four o'clock, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn, a trait typical of the Mirabilis genus. The plant produces clusters of four-petaled blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation contribute to its popularity in horticulture.
