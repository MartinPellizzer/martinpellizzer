Malpighia urens subsp. ovatifolia

Malpighia urens subsp.

ovatifolia, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean. It is characterized by its small, clustered, bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit and foliage.
