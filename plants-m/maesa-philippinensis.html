Maesa philippinensis

Maesa philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine maesa, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and rocky crevices. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and are clustered in inflorescences, while its fruits are small, red berries that attract birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
