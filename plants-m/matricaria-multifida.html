Matricaria multifida

Matricaria multifida, commonly known as the fringed chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its daisy-like flower heads. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and produces small yellow flowers that develop into white, fluffy seed heads. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and aromatic properties.

Matricaria multifida is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
