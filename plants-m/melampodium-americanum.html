Melampodium americanum

Melampodium americanum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as prairies and roadsides. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Melampodium americanum is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
