Mammillaria machucae

Mammillaria machucae, commonly known as the Machuca mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
