Mischopleura ovalifolia

Mischopleura ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small white or yellowish flowers, followed by berries that contain sticky seeds for dispersal by birds. Its roots form haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to absorb water and nutrients.

The species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain bird species and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
