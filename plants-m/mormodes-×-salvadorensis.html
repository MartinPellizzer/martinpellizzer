Mormodes × salvadorensis

Mormodes × salvadorensis, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a rare and intriguing plant resulting from the cross between Mormodes and Salvadora species.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid environments with well-draining soil. Its botanical significance lies in its unique genetic makeup and potential for horticultural interest.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and researchers for its ornamental value and scientific study.
