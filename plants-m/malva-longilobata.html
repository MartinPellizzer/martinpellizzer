Malva longilobata

Malva longilobata, commonly known as the long-lobed mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its fuzzy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Malva longilobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
