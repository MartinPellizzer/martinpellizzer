Miconia adventitia

Miconia adventitia, commonly known as the velvetleaf miconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Miconia adventitia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an aggressive invader in non-native regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for environmental management.
