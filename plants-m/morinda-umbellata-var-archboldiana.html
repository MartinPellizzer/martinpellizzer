Morinda umbellata var. archboldiana

Morinda umbellata var. archboldiana, commonly known as the Florida starfruit, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to yellow berries. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.

The berries are edible when ripe but can be tart and are sometimes used in local cuisines.
