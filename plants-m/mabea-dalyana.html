Mabea dalyana

Mabea dalyana, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling trumpets. The plant produces elongated, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and simple, with a glossy surface.

Mabea dalyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
