Muehlenbeckia benthamii

Muehlenbeckia benthamii, commonly known as the silver thread plant, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-tinged stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
