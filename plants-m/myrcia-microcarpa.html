Myrcia microcarpa

Myrcia microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Myrcia microcarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
