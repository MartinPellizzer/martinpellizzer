Myodocarpus involucratus

Myodocarpus involucratus, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, clustered leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland forest areas.

Myodocarpus involucratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
