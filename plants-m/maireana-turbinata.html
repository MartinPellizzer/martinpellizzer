Maireana turbinata

Maireana turbinata, commonly known as the turbinated maireana, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Maireana turbinata is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
