Morus parvifolia

Morus parvifolia, commonly known as the Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically pale green and have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fluffy, white or pale pink berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fibrous bark and ornamental value.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine and as a food source for silkworms.
