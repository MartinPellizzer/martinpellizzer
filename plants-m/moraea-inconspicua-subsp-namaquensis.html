Moraea inconspicua subsp. namaquensis

Moraea inconspicua subsp.

namaquensis, commonly known as theNamaqua lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, often with a contrasting throat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
