Medicago apiculata var. confinis

Medicago apiculata var. confinis, commonly known as the Sierra wild vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This variety is also valued for its role in forage and ecological restoration projects.
