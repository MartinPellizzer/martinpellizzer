Machaonia brasiliensis var. intermedia

Machaonia brasiliensis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are bell-shaped, usually blue or violet in color. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
