Mentha longifolia var. mollicoma

Mentha longifolia var. mollicoma, commonly known as narrowleaf spearmint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is often used in culinary and aromatic applications due to its distinct minty fragrance.
