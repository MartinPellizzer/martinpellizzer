Matonia pectinata

Matonia pectinata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of purple or blue flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Matonia pectinata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
