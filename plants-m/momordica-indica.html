Momordica indica

Momordica indica, commonly known as bitter melon, is a vine species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, green fruits that turn orange when mature. The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and pumpkins. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is widely used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
