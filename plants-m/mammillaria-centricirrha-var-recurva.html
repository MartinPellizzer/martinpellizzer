Mammillaria centricirrha var. recurva

Mammillaria centricirrha var. recurva, commonly known as the recurved mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
