Myrcia mollis

Myrcia mollis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or myrtle myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. Myrcia mollis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal regions and disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
