Matthiola conferta

Matthiola conferta, commonly known as the clustered wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known garden plants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Matthiola conferta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
