Macodes megalantha

Macodes megalantha, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
