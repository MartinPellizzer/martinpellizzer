Mariscus javanicus

Mariscus javanicus, commonly known as the Java mariscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and gourds. The plant is characterized by its trailing habit, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that are typically green when mature.

Mariscus javanicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
