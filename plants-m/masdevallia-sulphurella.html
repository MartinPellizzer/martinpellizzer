Masdevallia sulphurella

Masdevallia sulphurella, commonly known as the golden masdevallia, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage and its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and is often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are usually borne on a single stem and can last for several weeks.

Masdevallia sulphurella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
