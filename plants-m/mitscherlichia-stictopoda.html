Mitscherlichia stictopoda

Mitscherlichia stictopoda, commonly known as the white-flowered daphne, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features erect, branching stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
