Melocactus limis

Melocactus limis, commonly known as the desert melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem that often branches into a globular shape, covered with dense, white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its root system is adapted to absorb water efficiently during rare rainfall events.

Melocactus limis is well-suited to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and unique member of its ecosystem.
