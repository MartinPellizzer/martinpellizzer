Mertensia magellanica

Mertensia magellanica, commonly known as Magellan's bluebells, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Mertensia magellanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
