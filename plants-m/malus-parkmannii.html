Malus parkmannii

Malus parkmannii, commonly known as the Chinese crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental flowers, which range from white to pink and bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Malus parkmannii is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
