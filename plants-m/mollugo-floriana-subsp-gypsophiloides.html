Mollugo floriana subsp. gypsophiloides

Mollugo floriana subsp.

gypsophiloides, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to sandy and calcareous soils in Europe. It typically features prostrate stems with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in coastal or dune habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species with ecological significance in its native range.
