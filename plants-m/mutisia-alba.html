Mutisia alba

Mutisia alba, commonly known as the white mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
