Magnolia purpurea var. liliiflora

Magnolia purpurea var. liliiflora, commonly known as the purple magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and have a lily-like appearance, which gives the variety its name. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
