Myanthus barbatus var. immaculatus

Myanthus barbatus var. immaculatus, commonly known as the white myanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
