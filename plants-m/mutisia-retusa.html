Mutisia retusa

Mutisia retusa, commonly known as the silky mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, branched stem with narrow, grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Mutisia retusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
