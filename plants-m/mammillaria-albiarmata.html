Mammillaria albiarmata

Mammillaria albiarmata, commonly known as the white-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and white-tipped tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by pale green fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique features, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
