Muraltia linifolia

Muraltia linifolia, commonly known as the white muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and trifoliate leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Muraltia linifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
