Melocactus inconcinnus subsp. parvus

Melocactus inconcinnus subsp.

parvus, commonly known as the small-spined melocactus, is a cacti species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a dense covering of small, pale spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived. It is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
