Magnolia zotictla

Magnolia zotictla, commonly known as the Mexican magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has smooth, gray bark and elongated, narrow leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
