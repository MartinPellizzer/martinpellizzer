Melaleuca fulgens subsp. corrugata

Melaleuca fulgens subsp.

corrugata, commonly known as the rough-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant has rough, fibrous bark and lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
