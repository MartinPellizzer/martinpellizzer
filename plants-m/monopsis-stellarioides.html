Monopsis stellarioides

Monopsis stellarioides, commonly known as starwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features a low-growing rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in a star-like pattern. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a basal stem that supports the foliage.

Monopsis stellarioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
