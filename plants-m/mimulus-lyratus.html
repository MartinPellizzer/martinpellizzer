Mimulus lyratus

Mimulus lyratus, commonly known as the "Lyre's monkeyflower," is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange or yellow flowers, which are typically spurred at the back, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

Mimulus lyratus plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies due to its nectar-rich flowers.
