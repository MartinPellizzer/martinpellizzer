Mentha viridis var. exquisita

Mentha viridis var. exquisita, commonly known as the slender mint, is a variant of the common mint species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, serrated leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb.
