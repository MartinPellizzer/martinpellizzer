Macaglia tomentosa

Macaglia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly macaglia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
