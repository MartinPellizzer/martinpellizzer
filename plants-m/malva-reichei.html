Malva reichei

Malva reichei, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Malva reichei plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
