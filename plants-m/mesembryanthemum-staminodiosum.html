Mesembryanthemum staminodiosum

Mesembryanthemum staminodiosum, commonly known as the staminodeous ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in spring. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, and the plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive appearance.

Mesembryanthemum staminodiosum is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
