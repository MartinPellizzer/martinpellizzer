Malvastrum flabellatum

Malvastrum flabellatum, commonly known as the Mexican fanflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple bract-like flowers that resemble a fan shape, giving it its common name. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces small, inconspicuous true flowers within the bracts. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance.

Malvastrum flabellatum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
