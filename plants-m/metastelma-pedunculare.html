Metastelma pedunculare

Metastelma pedunculare, commonly known as the hanging mistflower, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
