Malveopsis coromandeliana var. capitatospicata

Malveopsis coromandeliana var. capitatospicata, commonly known as the Coromandel mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
