Microseris cognata

Microseris cognata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, sparsely branched stem topped with a purple or pink composite flower head. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Microseris cognata is also known for its medicinal properties and historical use by indigenous peoples.
