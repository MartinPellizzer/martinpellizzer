Megaleranthis saniculifolia

Megaleranthis saniculifolia, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are upright and often branched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The yellow bellflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
