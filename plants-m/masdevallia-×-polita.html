Masdevallia × polita

Masdevallia × polita, commonly known as the Polka Dot Masdevallia, is a hybrid orchid species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature contrasting patterns resembling polka dots. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact appearance.

Masdevallia × polita requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
