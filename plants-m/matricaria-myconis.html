Matricaria myconis

Matricaria myconis, commonly known as the meadow chamomile, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers and finely divided, aromatic leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves and flowers are used in herbal medicine for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
