Mentha × maximilianea f. willkommii

Mentha × maximilianea f. willkommii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peppermint, is a hybrid mint species characterized by its aromatic, slightly serrated leaves.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its essential oils, rich in menthol, make it valuable for both culinary and medicinal uses.

This variety is often cultivated for its refreshing scent and hardy nature.
