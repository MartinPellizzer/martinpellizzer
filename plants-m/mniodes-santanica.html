Mniodes santanica

Mniodes santanica, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or golden starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research.
