Myrocarpus fastigiatus

Myrocarpus fastigiatus, commonly known as the Brazilian cypress, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, narrow, columnar growth habit. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverine forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
