Malpighia urens subsp. urens

Malpighia urens subsp.

urens, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas. It produces small, yellowish-green fruits that turn orange when ripe, characterized by a rough, warty skin.

The plant is part of the Malpighiaceae family and is known for its edible, aromatic fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
