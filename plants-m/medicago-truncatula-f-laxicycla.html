Medicago truncatula f. laxicycla

Medicago truncatula f. laxicycla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed medick, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, due to its adaptability to various environmental conditions. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Its morphology and ecological niche make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
