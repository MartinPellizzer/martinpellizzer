Mutisia friesiana

Mutisia friesiana, commonly known as the yellow mutisia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, erect stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its most striking feature is the bright yellow flower head, which consists of numerous small florets arranged in a radiate pattern.

Mutisia friesiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
