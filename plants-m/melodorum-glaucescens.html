Melodorum glaucescens

Melodorum glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green melodia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are ovate with a waxy texture, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Melodorum glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
