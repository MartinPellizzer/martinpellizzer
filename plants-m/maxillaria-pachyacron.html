Maxillaria pachyacron

Maxillaria pachyacron, commonly known as the thick-angled maxillaria, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow and purple coloration. The plant typically blooms during the warmer months, with flowers that have a velvety texture and a strong, sweet scent. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value contribute to its appeal among orchid enthusiasts.
