Myrcia crenulata

Myrcia crenulata, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic flavor. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Myrcia crenulata is also known for its aromatic leaves, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
