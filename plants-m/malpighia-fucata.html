Malpighia fucata

Malpighia fucata, commonly known as the Brazilian acerola or West Indian cherry, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, bright red berries. The plant is well adapted to warm, humid climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its berries are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants, making it valuable both botanically and nutritionally.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
