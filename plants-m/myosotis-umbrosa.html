Myosotis umbrosa

Myosotis umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrsinaceae family and is characterized by its small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps reflect light in low-light conditions.

Myosotis umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with partial shade.
