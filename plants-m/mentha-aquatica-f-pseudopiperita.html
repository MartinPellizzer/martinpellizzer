Mentha aquatica f. pseudopiperita

Mentha aquatica f. pseudopiperita, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water mint, is a hybrid form of the water mint plant.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a strong, minty aroma. This plant typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes and stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color.

The foliage is often used in herbal teas and has been historically valued for its medicinal properties.
