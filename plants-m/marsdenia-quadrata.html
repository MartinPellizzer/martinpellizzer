Marsdenia quadrata

Marsdenia quadrata, commonly known as the four-cornered Marsdenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
