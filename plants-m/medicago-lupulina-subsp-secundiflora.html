Medicago lupulina subsp. secundiflora

Medicago lupulina subsp.

secundiflora, commonly known as the secondary-flowered sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with narrow, pointed leaflets and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in forage systems.

Its flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other insects.
