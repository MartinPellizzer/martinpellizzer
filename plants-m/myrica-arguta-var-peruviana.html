Myrica arguta var. peruviana

Myrica arguta var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian wax myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, wax-coated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by dark blue, waxy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

The plant has a distinctive aromatic scent and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
