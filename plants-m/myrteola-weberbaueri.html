Myrteola weberbaueri

Myrteola weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant typically grows in humid, mountainous environments and features small, aromatic leaves with a leathery texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
