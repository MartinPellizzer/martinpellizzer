Marianthus lineatus

Marianthus lineatus, commonly known as the lineated marianthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves with distinct white veins running along the leaf surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
