Malpighia romeroana

Malpighia romeroana, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Malpighiaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, fleshy berries that turn bright red when mature. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has simple, alternate leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often white or pink, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a popular choice for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
