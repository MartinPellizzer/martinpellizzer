Maerua friesii

Maerua friesii, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

The plant is also valued for its hardy nature and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
