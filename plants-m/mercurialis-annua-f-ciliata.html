Mercurialis annua f. ciliata

Mercurialis annua f. ciliata, commonly known as the ciliated mercury plant, is a variant of the common mercury plant.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its hairy, slender stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, with ciliate margins that give it its specific epithet. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas.
