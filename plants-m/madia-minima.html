Madia minima

Madia minima, commonly known as the dwarf madia or small madia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Madia minima is often used in native plant gardens due to its low maintenance and ecological benefits.
