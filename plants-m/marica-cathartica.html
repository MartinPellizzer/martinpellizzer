Marica cathartica

Marica cathartica, commonly known as the yellow poppy, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a delicate appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Marica cathartica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
