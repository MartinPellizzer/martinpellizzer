Megarrhiza muricata

Megarrhiza muricata, commonly known as the spiny leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
