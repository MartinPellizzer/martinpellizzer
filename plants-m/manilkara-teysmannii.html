Manilkara teysmannii

Manilkara teysmannii, commonly known as the Teymann's sapodilla, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, sweet, and starchy fruits. Its latex, which is milky and sticky, is used in traditional medicine and as a natural rubber substitute.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
