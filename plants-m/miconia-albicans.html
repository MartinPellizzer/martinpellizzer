Miconia albicans

Miconia albicans, commonly known as the white miconia, is a species of flowering plant in the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white pubescence. Its flowers are showy, with five white petals and prominent yellow stamens, attracting various pollinators.

Miconia albicans is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.
