Macfadyena dentata

Macfadyena dentata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
