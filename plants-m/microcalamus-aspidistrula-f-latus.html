Microcalamus aspidistrula f. latus

Microcalamus aspidistrula f. latus, commonly known as the broad-leafed bamboo, is a variant of the species Microcalamus aspidistrula, characterized by its wider leaf blades compared to the typical form.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and Thailand. The plant is a clump-forming bamboo with slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves that are often a deep green color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
