Mentha aquatica var. pilosa

Mentha aquatica var. pilosa, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It thrives in wet soils near streams, ponds, and marshes, making it a common sight in riparian habitats.
