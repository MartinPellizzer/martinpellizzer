Monarda punctata var. stanfieldii

Monarda punctata var. stanfieldii, commonly known as the Stanfield's tickseed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, often with a minty fragrance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
