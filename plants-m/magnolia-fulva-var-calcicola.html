Magnolia fulva var. calcicola

Magnolia fulva var. calcicola, commonly known as the limestone magnolia, is a rare and specialized plant adapted to grow in calcareous soils.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in regions with limestone substrates. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
