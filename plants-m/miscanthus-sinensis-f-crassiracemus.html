Miscanthus sinensis f. crassiracemus

Miscanthus sinensis f. crassiracemus, commonly known as the broad-panicle miscanthus, is a ornamental grass native to East Asia.

It features tall, arching stems that produce large, fluffy panicles of silver-white flowers in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are bluish-green in color and turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its dramatic appearance and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental and structural qualities.
