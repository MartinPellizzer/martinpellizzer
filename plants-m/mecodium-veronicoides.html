Mecodium veronicoides

Mecodium veronicoides, commonly known as the silverleaf mecodium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Mecodium veronicoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
