Macaranga maudsleyi

Macaranga maudsleyi, commonly known as the Maudsley's makamaka, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a fast-growing shrub or small tree that typically reaches heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically with five petals.

Macaranga maudsleyi is known for its role in ecological systems, particularly as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
