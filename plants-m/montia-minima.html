Montia minima

Montia minima, commonly known as dwarf watercress, is a small annual herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in length. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale green, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Montia minima is valued for its peppery flavor and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
