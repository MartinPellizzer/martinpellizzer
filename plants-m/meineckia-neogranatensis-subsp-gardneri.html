Meineckia neogranatensis subsp. gardneri

Meineckia neogranatensis subsp.

gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's meineckia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, tubular, and arranged in loose clusters, typically in shades of pink or purple.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
