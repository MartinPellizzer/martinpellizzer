Mixandra butyracea var. pierrei

Mixandra butyracea var. pierrei, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique flowering habit.
