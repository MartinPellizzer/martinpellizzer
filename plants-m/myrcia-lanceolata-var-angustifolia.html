Myrcia lanceolata var. angustifolia

Myrcia lanceolata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its lance-shaped, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical rainforests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and potential therapeutic properties.
