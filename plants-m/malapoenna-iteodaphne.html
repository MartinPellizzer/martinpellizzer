Malapoenna iteodaphne

Malapoenna iteodaphne, commonly known as the fragrant sandalwood.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its aromatic wood and dense foliage. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its healing properties.

The tree is valued for its slow-growing, high-quality timber used in perfumes and furniture.
