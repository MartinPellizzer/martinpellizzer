Magnolia fragrantissima

Magnolia fragrantissima, commonly known as the sweet magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has simple, alternate, dark green leaves that are oval in shape. Its flowers bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge, making it a striking early-season ornamental.

The tree typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 feet and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
