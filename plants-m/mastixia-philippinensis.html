Mastixia philippinensis

Mastixia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine mastix tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The tree is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and pinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that contain seeds.

The plant is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
