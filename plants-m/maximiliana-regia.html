Maximiliana regia

Maximiliana regia, commonly known as the royal maximiliana, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and a distinctive, elongated floral structure. The plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
