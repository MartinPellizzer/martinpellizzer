Myrtus engleriana

Myrtus engleriana, commonly known as the Engler myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fragrant foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and as a hedging plant.

The fruit, which is a small, dark purple berry, is edible but typically not consumed by humans.
