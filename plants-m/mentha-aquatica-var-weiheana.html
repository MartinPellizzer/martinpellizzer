Mentha aquatica var. weiheana

Mentha aquatica var. weiheana, commonly known as water mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist environments in Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly serrated along the edges. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers appear in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late summer.

This variety is known for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
