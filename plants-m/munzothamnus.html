Munzothamnus

Munzothamnus, commonly known as the desert broom, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Munzothamnus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife species.
