Miconia setifera

Miconia setifera, commonly known as the silky miconia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Melastomataceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It has a woody stem and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
