Malva pamiroalaica

Malva pamiroalaica, commonly known as the Pamir mallows, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
