Millettia cubittii

Millettia cubittii, commonly known as the Cuban millettia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by animals.

Due to habitat loss, Millettia cubittii is considered endangered and is of interest for both botanical research and conservation efforts.
