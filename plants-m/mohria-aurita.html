Mohria aurita

Mohria aurita, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, hairy flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Mohria aurita is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
