Myrcia spathulifolia

Myrcia spathulifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Myrcia spathulifolia also has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
