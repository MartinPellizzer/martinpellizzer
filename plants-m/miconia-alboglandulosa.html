Miconia alboglandulosa

Miconia alboglandulosa, commonly known as the white-gland miconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Miconia alboglandulosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
