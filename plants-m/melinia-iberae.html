Melinia iberae

Melinia iberae, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features erect, branched stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by yellowish berries that contain the seeds. Melinia iberae is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
