Mentha × weissenburgensis

Mentha × weissenburgensis, commonly known as the Weissenburg mint, is a hybrid mint species resulting from the cross between Mentha rotundifolia and Mentha spicata.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly menthol-like scent and its serrated, heart-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorls along the stems.

Mentha × weissenburgensis is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its cooling and soothing properties.
