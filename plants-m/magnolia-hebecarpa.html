Magnolia hebecarpa

Magnolia hebecarpa, commonly known as the broad-leafed magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, round, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens for its spring blossoms.
