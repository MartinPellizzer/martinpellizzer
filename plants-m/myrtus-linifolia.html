Myrtus linifolia

Myrtus linifolia, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Myrtus linifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal areas.

Its aromatic properties have made it valuable in traditional medicine and perfumery.
