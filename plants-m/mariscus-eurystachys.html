Mariscus eurystachys

Mariscus eurystachys, commonly known as the broad-fruited tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and its ability to thrive in poor, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Mariscus eurystachys is also known for its resilience to drought and salt, making it a hardy species adapted to extreme conditions.
