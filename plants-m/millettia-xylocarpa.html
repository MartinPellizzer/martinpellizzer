Millettia xylocarpa

Millettia xylocarpa, commonly known as the yellow millettia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are alternate along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody pods containing flat, brown seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
