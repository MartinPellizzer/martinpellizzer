Melocactus flexilis

Melocactus flexilis, commonly known as the flexible melon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that often appears segmented, with a distinctive rib structure that aids in water storage. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. Its spines are relatively short and sparse, giving it a unique appearance compared to other cacti.

Melocactus flexilis is well adapted to harsh desert environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
