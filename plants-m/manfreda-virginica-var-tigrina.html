Manfreda virginica var. tigrina

Manfreda virginica var. tigrina, commonly known as the tiger manfreda, is a striking succulent native to the southwestern United States.

It features thick, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a bluish-gray hue and distinctive red or purple markings. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
