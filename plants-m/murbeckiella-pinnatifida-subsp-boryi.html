Murbeckiella pinnatifida subsp. boryi

Murbeckiella pinnatifida subsp.

boryi, commonly known as the Bory's fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family. It is a small, delicate fern characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive, finely divided structure.

The fronds are typically bright green and have a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
