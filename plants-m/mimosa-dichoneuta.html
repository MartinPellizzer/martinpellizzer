Mimosa dichoneuta

Mimosa dichoneuta, commonly known as the silver mimosa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its leaves are sensitive to touch, folding shut when disturbed, a trait known as thigmonastic response.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
