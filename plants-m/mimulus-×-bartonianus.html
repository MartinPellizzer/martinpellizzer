Mimulus × bartonianus

Mimulus × bartonianus, commonly known as the hybrid monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is a cross between Mimulus cardinalis and Mimulus luteus, resulting in a plant with vibrant orange to yellow flowers. The species exhibits a trailing growth habit, often forming dense mats along the ground. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with two upper petals and three lower petals, typical of the Physopleurineae tribe.

Mimulus × bartonianus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
