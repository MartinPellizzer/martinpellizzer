Melandrium vespertinum

Melandrium vespertinum, commonly known as the evening catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable wildflower.
