Medicago romanica var. viscosa

Medicago romanica var. viscosa, commonly known as sticky medick, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its trifoliate leaves are typically oval to lanceolate in shape.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and pastures.
