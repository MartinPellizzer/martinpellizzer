Montanoa floribunda

Montanoa floribunda, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, white to pale yellow flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

Montanoa floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments.
