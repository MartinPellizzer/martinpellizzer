Monotes rubriglans subsp. upembensis

Monotes rubriglans subsp.

upembensis, commonly known as the Upemba rock fig, is a shrub native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and clustered leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
