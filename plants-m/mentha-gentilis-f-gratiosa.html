Mentha gentilis f. gratiosa

Mentha gentilis f. gratiosa, commonly known as sweet mint, is a hybrid form of the Mentha genus, characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and emit a strong minty scent when crushed.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal in gardens.
