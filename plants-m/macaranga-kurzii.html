Macaranga kurzii

Macaranga kurzii, commonly known as the silverleaf macaranga, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Macaranga kurzii plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
