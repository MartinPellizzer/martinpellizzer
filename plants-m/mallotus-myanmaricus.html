Mallotus myanmaricus

Mallotus myanmaricus, commonly known as the Myanmar holly, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate, elliptic leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
