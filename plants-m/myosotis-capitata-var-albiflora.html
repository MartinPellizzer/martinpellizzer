Myosotis capitata var. albiflora

Myosotis capitata var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered forget-me-not, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers with five petals. The flowers typically bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and streamside habitats.

Its delicate appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
