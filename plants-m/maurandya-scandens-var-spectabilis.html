Maurandya scandens var. spectabilis

Maurandya scandens var. spectabilis, commonly known as the showy moonflower, is a vigorous climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces large, showy flowers that are typically pale purple to white. The flowers open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its sprawling growth habit.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
