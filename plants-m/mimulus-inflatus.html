Mimulus inflatus

Mimulus inflatus, commonly known as the inflated monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red throat. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Mimulus inflatus is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar to various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
