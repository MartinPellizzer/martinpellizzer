Malus pumila f. pendula

Malus pumila f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping apple, is a unique cultivar of the apple tree characterized by its distinctive pendulous branches.

This ornamental tree features clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red or yellow berries in the fall. Its foliage is typically dark green, providing a striking contrast to its cascading form. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
