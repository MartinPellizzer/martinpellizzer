Marsdenia micrantha

Marsdenia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered Marsdenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of milky sap, which is a common feature in the genus. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often pubescent. Marsdenia micrantha is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
