Miconia bolivarensis

Miconia bolivarensis, commonly known as the Bolivian miconia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, it plays a significant role in shaping the understory vegetation in its habitat.
