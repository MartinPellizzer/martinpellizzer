Mikania pteropoda

Mikania pteropoda, commonly known as the giant Mikania, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and extensive foliage. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges due to its adaptability.

Mikania pteropoda is noted for its ability to form dense thickets, which can impact local ecosystems.
