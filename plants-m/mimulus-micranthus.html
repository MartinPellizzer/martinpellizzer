Mimulus micranthus

Mimulus micranthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bright orange or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Mimulus micranthus plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
