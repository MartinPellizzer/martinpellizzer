Meibomia cordistipula

Meibomia cordistipula, commonly known as the hairy meibomia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. Meibomia cordistipula is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forest understories or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
