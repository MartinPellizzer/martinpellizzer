Muraltia flanaganii

Muraltia flanaganii, commonly known as the Flanagan's muraltia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and showy blooms.

Muraltia flanaganii is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
