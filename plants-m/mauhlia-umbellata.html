Mauhlia umbellata

Mauhlia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, showy white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in humid, well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Mauhlia umbellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
