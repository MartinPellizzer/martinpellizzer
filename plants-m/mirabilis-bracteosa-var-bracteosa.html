Mirabilis bracteosa var. bracteosa

Mirabilis bracteosa var. bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that open in the late afternoon and close by morning. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique blooming pattern.
