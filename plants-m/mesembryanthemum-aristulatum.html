Mesembryanthemum aristulatum

Mesembryanthemum aristulatum, commonly known as the arrowhead mesem, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for its diverse and often striking floral displays. The plant features fleshy, ribbed leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often blooming in response to morning dew.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with minimal water.
