Myriophyllum pusillum

Myriophyllum pusillum, commonly known as narrowleaf watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces tiny, reddish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often visible above the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Myriophyllum pusillum is often found in slow-moving rivers, lakes, and ponds with soft, muddy substrates.
