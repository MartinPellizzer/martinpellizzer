Medicago sativa subsp. microcarpa

Medicago sativa subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as small-fruited alfalfa, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in agricultural settings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as forage for livestock.

Due to its hardy nature, it can tolerate drought and is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.
