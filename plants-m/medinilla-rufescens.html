Medinilla rufescens

Medinilla rufescens, commonly known as the red-flowered medinilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Medinilla rufescens is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
