Myrcia parnahibensis var. acutifolia

Myrcia parnahibensis var. acutifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian myrtle, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
