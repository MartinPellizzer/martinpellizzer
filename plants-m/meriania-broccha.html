Meriania broccha

Meriania broccha, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or Brazilian star-apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble star fruits, though they are not related to the common starfruit (Carambola). Meriania broccha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique fruit.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora.
