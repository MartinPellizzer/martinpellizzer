Melastoma succulentum

Melastoma succulentum, commonly known as the succulent melastoma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple stems and dense foliage.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
