Viola scorpiuroides

Viola scorpiuroides, commonly known as the scorpion-like violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet flowers with yellow markings that resemble a scorpion's tail. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

Viola scorpiuroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
