Vernonia rhaponticoides

Vernonia rhaponticoides, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Vernonia rhaponticoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
