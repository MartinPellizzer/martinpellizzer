Vicia amurensis var. pallida

Vicia amurensis var. pallida, commonly known as the pale vetch, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a legume characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are small, typically pink or purple, and appear in clusters. It produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This plant is often found in moist, disturbed habitats and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability.
