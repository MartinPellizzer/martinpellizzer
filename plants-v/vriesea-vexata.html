Vriesea vexata

Vriesea vexata, commonly known as the velvetleaf bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful inflorescence that can range from red to orange, often with a yellow or green central stripe. The plant features a rosette of narrow, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a metallic sheen. Vriesea vexata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.
