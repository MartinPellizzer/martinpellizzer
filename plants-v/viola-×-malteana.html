Viola × malteana

Viola × malteana, commonly known as the Maltean violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often bi-colored flowers that range from purple to violet and white. The plant typically features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Viola × malteana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
