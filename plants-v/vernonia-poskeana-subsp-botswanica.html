Vernonia poskeana subsp. botswanica

Vernonia poskeana subsp.

botswanica, commonly known as the Botswana ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and narrow, leathery leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are arranged in dense clusters, typical of the genus Vernonia. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and savanna habitats.

The species is valued for its hard wood and is used in traditional construction and fuel.
