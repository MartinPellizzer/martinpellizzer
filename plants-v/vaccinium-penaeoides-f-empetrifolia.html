Vaccinium penaeoides f. empetrifolia

Vaccinium penaeoides f. empetrifolia, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are typically edible. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in dune and maritime hammock habitats. Its foliage consists of evergreen, oval-shaped leaves that are densely packed, providing dense cover.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and adaptability to coastal environments.
