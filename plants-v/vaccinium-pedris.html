Vaccinium pedris

Vaccinium pedris, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other acidic-loving plants such as heathers and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black when mature, containing high levels of antioxidants. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Vaccinium pedris thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in high-altitude forests and rocky areas.
