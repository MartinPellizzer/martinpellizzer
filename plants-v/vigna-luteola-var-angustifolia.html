Vigna luteola var. angustifolia

Vigna luteola var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain small, edible seeds. It is often grown for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and fiber.

This variety is well adapted to warm climates and can thrive in poor soils.
