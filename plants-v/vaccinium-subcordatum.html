Vaccinium subcordatum

Vaccinium subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. Its flowers are bell-shaped, pinkish-white in color, and appear in clusters during the spring.

The plant is valued for its edible berries, which are tart and often used in local culinary traditions.
