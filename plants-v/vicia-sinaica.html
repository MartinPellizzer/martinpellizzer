Vicia sinaica

Vicia sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai vetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small oval-shaped leaflets.

Vicia sinaica is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
