Visenia ulmariae

Visenia ulmariae, commonly known as water viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and along streambanks, preferring partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

The fruit is a dark blue to black drupe, which is edible but often has a bitter taste.
