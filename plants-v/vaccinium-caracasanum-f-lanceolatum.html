Vaccinium caracasanum f. lanceolatum

Vaccinium caracasanum f. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate blueberry, is a botanical variant of the Vaccinium caracasanum species.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cooler climates.

This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its edible fruits.
