Vochysia tabascana

Vochysia tabascana, commonly known as the Tabasco beauty or red-veined beauty, is a tropical tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often bright green with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink to red, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.

Vochysia tabascana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
