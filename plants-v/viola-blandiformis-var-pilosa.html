Viola blandiformis var. pilosa

Viola blandiformis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the back. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
