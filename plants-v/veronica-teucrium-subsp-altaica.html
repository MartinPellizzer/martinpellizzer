Veronica teucrium subsp. altaica

Veronica teucrium subsp.

altaica, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with dark green, ovate leaves and blue-violet flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable plant for rock gardens and alpine displays.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native environment.
