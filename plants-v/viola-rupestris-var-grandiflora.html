Viola rupestris var. grandiflora

Viola rupestris var. grandiflora, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy violet-blue flowers with a yellow throat, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
