Valeriana abakanica

Valeriana abakanica, commonly known as the Abakan valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region and parts of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features erect, branched stems covered with soft, hairy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense umbels, and are usually pinkish or white in color.

Valeriana abakanica is known for its aromatic properties and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming effects.
