Verticordia brownii

Verticordia brownii, commonly known as the Geraldton waxplant, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, candle-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Its unique floral structure and seasonal blooming make it a notable species in its native habitat.

Verticordia brownii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
