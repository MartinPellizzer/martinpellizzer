Vachellia ancistroclada

Vachellia ancistroclada, commonly known as the sausage tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, cylindrical pods that resemble sausages, which contain a sticky, milky sap. The tree has bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in savannah and woodland areas.

The plant is also valued for its ecological and medicinal properties.
