Verbascum pyramidatum

Verbascum pyramidatum, commonly known as the yellow mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem covered in soft, velvety leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum pyramidatum is a perennial plant that can grow up to two meters in height, making it a striking addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
