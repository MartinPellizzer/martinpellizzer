Vitis girdiana

Vitis girdiana, commonly known as the Girdled grape, is a deciduous vine native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. Vitis girdiana is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water retention.

It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
