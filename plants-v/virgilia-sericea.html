Virgilia sericea

Virgilia sericea, commonly known as silky virgilia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its soft, silken hairs covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
