Vaccinium cubense var. giganteum

Vaccinium cubense var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant sweet pepper, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant produces edible berries that are prized for their sweet, tangy flavor and high nutritional value. Its foliage consists of broad, glossy leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
