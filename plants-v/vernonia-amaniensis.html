Vernonia amaniensis

Vernonia amaniensis, commonly known as the East African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of branches.

Vernonia amaniensis is often found in woodland and savanna habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
