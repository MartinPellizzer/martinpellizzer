Viola kusanoana f. radicans

Viola kusanoana f. radicans, commonly known as the trailing violet, is a hybrid form of the Japanese violet.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or ground cover. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate gardens.
