Vriesea olmosana var. pachamamae

Vriesea olmosana var. pachamamae, commonly known as the "Cloud Forest Bromeliad," is a striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spathaceous inflorescence that ranges in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple tinge. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central pool that supports various microorganisms. It thrives in high humidity and dappled sunlight, making it well-suited for tropical and subtropical environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in bromeliad diversity.
