Vernicia montana

Vernicia montana, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions as a potential ornamental species.

However, it can become invasive in some areas, posing ecological challenges.
