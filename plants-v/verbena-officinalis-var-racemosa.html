Verbena officinalis var. racemosa

Verbena officinalis var. racemosa, commonly known as hedge verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, bright blue or purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
