Vernonia remotiflora var. divaricata

Vernonia remotiflora var. divaricata, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green to reddish-purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist to wet soils in wetlands or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
