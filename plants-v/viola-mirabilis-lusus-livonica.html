Viola mirabilis lusus livonica

Viola mirabilis lusus livonica, commonly known as the Livonia marvel violet, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
