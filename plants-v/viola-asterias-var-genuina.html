Viola asterias var. genuina

Viola asterias var. genuina, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
