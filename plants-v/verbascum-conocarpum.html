Verbascum conocarpum

Verbascum conocarpum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, fuzzy grayish-white hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a thick layer of soft, woolly hair.

Verbascum conocarpum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
