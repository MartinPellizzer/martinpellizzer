Vatica adenanii

Vatica adenanii, commonly known as the Malay ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, glossy leaves. It thrives in humid, lowland rainforests and plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.

Vatica adenanii is also noted for its fragrant flowers and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
