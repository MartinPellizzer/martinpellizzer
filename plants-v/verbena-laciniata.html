Verbena laciniata

Verbena laciniata, commonly known as the fringed verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
