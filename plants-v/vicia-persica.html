Vicia persica

Vicia persica, commonly known as Persian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, and it typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its compound leaves consist of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a climbing habit due to its slender, twining stems.

Vicia persica is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.
