Veronica sclerophylla

Veronica sclerophylla, commonly known as the rigid leaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid conditions, helping it conserve water. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
