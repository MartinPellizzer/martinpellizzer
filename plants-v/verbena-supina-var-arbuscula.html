Verbena supina var. arbuscula

Verbena supina var. arbuscula, commonly known as creeping verbena, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to southern Europe.

It features small, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has slender, branching stems that lie close to the ground, often rooting at the nodes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
