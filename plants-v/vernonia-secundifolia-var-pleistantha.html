Vernonia secundifolia var. pleistantha

Vernonia secundifolia var. pleistantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is typically gray-green, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.
