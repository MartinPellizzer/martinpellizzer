Viola × doii f. glabra

Viola × doii f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and vibrant, often violet or purple flowers. Native to regions of East Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant's morphology reflects adaptations to its natural habitat, with compact growth and sturdy stems.
