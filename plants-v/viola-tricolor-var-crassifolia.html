Viola tricolor var. crassifolia

Viola tricolor var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive violet or purple coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a ground cover.
