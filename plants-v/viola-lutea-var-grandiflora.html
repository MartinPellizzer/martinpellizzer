Viola lutea var. grandiflora

Viola lutea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered yellow violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive violet throat. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
