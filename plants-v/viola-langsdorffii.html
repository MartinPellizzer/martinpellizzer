Viola langsdorffii

Viola langsdorffii, commonly known as the Langsdorff violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola langsdorffii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
