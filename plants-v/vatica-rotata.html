Vatica rotata

Vatica rotata, commonly known as the golden tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. Vatica rotata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a notable species in tropical flora.
