Valerianella caucasica

Valerianella caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its rosette of trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.

Valerianella caucasica is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
