Vernonia kingii

Vernonia kingii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. Its hard, heavy wood is highly valued for construction and fuel, contributing to its common name.

The plant also plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
