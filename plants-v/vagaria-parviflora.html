Vagaria parviflora

Vagaria parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered vagaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley and carrots, and is characterized by its delicate, umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Vagaria parviflora is often used in herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
