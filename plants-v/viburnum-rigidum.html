Viburnum rigidum

Viburnum rigidum, commonly known as hardy viburnum or American cranberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Viburnum rigidum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also a host plant for several species of butterflies and moths.
