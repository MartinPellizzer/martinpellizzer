Verbascum simplex var. gaetulum

Verbascum simplex var. gaetulum, commonly known as the Gaetulan mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, fuzzy, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is attributed to its soothing properties.
