Viguiera tenuis f. alba

Viguiera tenuis f. alba, commonly known as whiteleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the United States, particularly found in the central and eastern regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, arranged in dense, conical inflorescences.

Viguiera tenuis f. alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
