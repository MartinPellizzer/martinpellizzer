Vernonia gardneri var. nervosa

Vernonia gardneri var. nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and have a distinct venation pattern, contributing to its botanical distinction. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
