Vitex villosa

Vitex villosa, commonly known as velvetleaf vitex, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, purple or white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that releases seeds when mature.

Vitex villosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
