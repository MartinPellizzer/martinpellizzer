Viola epipsila

Viola epipsila, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with purple markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is part of the Violaceae family, which includes other violets and related species.

Viola epipsila is often found in the understory of forests, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
