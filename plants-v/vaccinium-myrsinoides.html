Vaccinium myrsinoides

Vaccinium myrsinoides, commonly known as the dwarf huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes blueberries and cranberries, and is characterized by its acidic soil preferences. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color, often used in local foraging and traditional practices. Its dense foliage provides habitat for various wildlife species, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Vaccinium myrsinoides is valued both for its ecological role and its culinary potential.
