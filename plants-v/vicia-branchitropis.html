Vicia branchitropis

Vicia branchitropis, commonly known as the tropical vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically arranged in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Vicia branchitropis is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.
