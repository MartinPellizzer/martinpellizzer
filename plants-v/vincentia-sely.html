Vincentia sely

Vincentia sely, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom profusely during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
