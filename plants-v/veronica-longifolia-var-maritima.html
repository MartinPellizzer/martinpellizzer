Veronica longifolia var. maritima

Veronica longifolia var. maritima, commonly known as sea hare or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and the Mediterranean.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in salt marshes and coastal dunes. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers. Its adaptability to salty environments makes it a hardy species with ornamental value in coastal gardens.

It plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
