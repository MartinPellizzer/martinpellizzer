Viola nigricans

Viola nigricans, commonly known as the black violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in woodland habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive dark purple hue. It is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
