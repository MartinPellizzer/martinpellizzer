Vancouveria concolor

Vancouveria concolor, commonly known as the common fairybells, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Vancouveria concolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
