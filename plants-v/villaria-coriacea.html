Villaria coriacea

Villaria coriacea, commonly known as roughleaf villaria, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features rough, hairy leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

Villaria coriacea is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
