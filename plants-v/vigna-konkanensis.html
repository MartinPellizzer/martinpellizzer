Vigna konkanensis

Vigna konkanensis, commonly known as Konkan beans, is a leguminous plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a climbing herb that produces small, yellow flowers and elongated, edible pods. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical climates. It is valued for its nutritional content, including high protein and fiber.

Vigna konkanensis is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
