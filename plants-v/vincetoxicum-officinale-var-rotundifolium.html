Vincetoxicum officinale var. rotundifolium

Vincetoxicum officinale var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaved milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features opposite, ovate to rounded leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are slender and can become woody with age, often growing in dense patches.

It is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic to some animals and humans if ingested.
