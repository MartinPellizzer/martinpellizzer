Vitis cantoniensis

Vitis cantoniensis, commonly known as the Canton grape or Chinese fox grape, is a species of flowering vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, sweet-tart berries that are often consumed fresh or used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks and in forested areas. Its vines can climb to considerable heights, making it a common sight in subtropical regions.

The fruit is also used in winemaking and has historical significance in Chinese culture.
