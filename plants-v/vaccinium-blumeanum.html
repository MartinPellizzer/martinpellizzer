Vaccinium blumeanum

Vaccinium blumeanum, commonly known as the Blume's blueberry, is a species of shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture, and the plant is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
