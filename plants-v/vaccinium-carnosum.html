Vaccinium carnosum

Vaccinium carnosum, commonly known as the common cranberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and used in various culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and bogs.

Vaccinium carnosum plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting diverse microbial communities.
