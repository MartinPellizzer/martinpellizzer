Verbesina seemannii

Verbesina seemannii, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is known for its robust growth and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
