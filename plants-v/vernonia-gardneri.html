Vernonia gardneri

Vernonia gardneri, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and fluffy, mist-like flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its ecological value includes providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
