Verbena canescens

Verbena canescens, commonly known as sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers, which range in color from blue to purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Sand verbena is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
