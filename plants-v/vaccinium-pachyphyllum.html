Vaccinium pachyphyllum

Vaccinium pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Vaccinium pachyphyllum plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
