Vigna riukiuensis

Vigna riukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu mung bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its seeds are small and round, often used in local cuisines, while the plant itself is valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties in soil.

Vigna riukiuensis is also noted for its ornamental value due to its delicate foliage and flowering patterns.
