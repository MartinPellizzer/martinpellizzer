Vernonia cardiolepis

Vernonia cardiolepis, commonly known as the heartleaf vernonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open woodlands or disturbed soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped, which gives it its common name, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia cardiolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
