Vaccaria hispanica var. grandiflora

Vaccaria hispanica var. grandiflora, commonly known as Spanish catchfly, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pale green leaves and produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its succulent stems and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
