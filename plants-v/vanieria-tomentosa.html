Vanieria tomentosa

Vanieria tomentosa, commonly known as the silky fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are pinnately divided and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

Vanieria tomentosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
