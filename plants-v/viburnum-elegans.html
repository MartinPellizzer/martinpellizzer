Viburnum elegans

Viburnum elegans, commonly known as the rose-of-Sharon, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features showy, flat clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that ripens in the fall.

Viburnum elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
