Verbesina lineata

Verbesina lineata, commonly known as antelope's horn, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers and distinctive, horn-like seed pods. The plant is an annual herb that grows up to 2 meters tall, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

The genus Verbesina is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
