Vicia sativa subsp. luganensis

Vicia sativa subsp.

luganensis, commonly known as the Laganese vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and historical use in agriculture.
