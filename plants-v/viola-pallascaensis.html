Viola pallascaensis

Viola pallascaensis, commonly known as the Pallas's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola pallascaensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
