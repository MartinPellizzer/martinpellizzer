Veronica buxifolia

Veronica buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite pairs of oval, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Veronica buxifolia is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
