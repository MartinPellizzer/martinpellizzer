Verbascum pseudodigitalis

Verbascum pseudodigitalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of northern Africa.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum pseudodigitalis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects.
