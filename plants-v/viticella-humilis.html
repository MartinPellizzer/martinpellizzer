Viticella humilis

Viticella humilis, commonly known as the creeping grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is a low-growing, trailing vine native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive, glossy appearance.

Viticella humilis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or ground cover.
