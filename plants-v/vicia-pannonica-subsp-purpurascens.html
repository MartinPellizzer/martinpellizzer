Vicia pannonica subsp. purpurascens

Vicia pannonica subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive purple tinge.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet. Its pods are slender and contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.
