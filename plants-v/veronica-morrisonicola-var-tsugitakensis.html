Veronica morrisonicola var. tsugitakensis

Veronica morrisonicola var. tsugitakensis, commonly known as the Japanese speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blossoms.
