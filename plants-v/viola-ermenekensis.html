Viola ermenekensis

Viola ermenekensis, commonly known as the Armenian violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Viola ermenekensis plays an important role in local ecosystems and is a symbol of the biodiversity found in the Caucasus region.
