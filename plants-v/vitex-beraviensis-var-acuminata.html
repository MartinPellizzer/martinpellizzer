Vitex beraviensis var. acuminata

Vitex beraviensis var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chaste tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stem and opposite, lanceolate leaves with acuminate tips. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by woody, bead-like fruits. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or secondary growth habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
