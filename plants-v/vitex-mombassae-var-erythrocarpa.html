Vitex mombassae var. erythrocarpa

Vitex mombassae var. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited chaste tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
