Vigna frutescens

Vigna frutescens, commonly known as the shrub bean, is a tropical legume native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. Vigna frutescens is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
