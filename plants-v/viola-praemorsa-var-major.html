Viola praemorsa var. major

Viola praemorsa var. major, commonly known as the large heartleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a yellow or white throat. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
