Vanda ampullacea subsp. ampullacea

Vanda ampullacea subsp.

ampullacea, commonly known as the giant vanda, is a striking orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and other structures in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native tropical environment.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
