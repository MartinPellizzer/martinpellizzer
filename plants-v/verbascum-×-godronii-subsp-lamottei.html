Verbascum × godronii subsp. lamottei

Verbascum × godronii subsp.

lamottei, commonly known as the Lamotte's mullein, is a hybrid species within the genus Verbascum, characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy leaves. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
