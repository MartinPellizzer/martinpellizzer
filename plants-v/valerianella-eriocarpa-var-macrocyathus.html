Valerianella eriocarpa var. macrocyathus

Valerianella eriocarpa var. macrocyathus, commonly known as the large-capped valerian, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild sedative properties.
