Veronica crassifolia

Veronica crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are broad, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
