Vitis catawba

Vitis catawba, commonly known as the Catawba grape, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its deep green, lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing along riverbanks and woodland edges. Its berries are used in winemaking and have a distinctive musky flavor.

Vitis catawba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Native American cultures.
