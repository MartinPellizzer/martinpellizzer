Vittaria lauana

Vittaria lauana, commonly known as the Brazilian fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that often form dense, compact clusters. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with finely divided segments that contribute to its ornamental appearance.

Vittaria lauana is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
