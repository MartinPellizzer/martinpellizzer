Veronica pannonica

Veronica pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom from June to August.

Veronica pannonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
