Vernonia sessilifolia var. auriculata

Vernonia sessilifolia var. auriculata, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a velvety texture and a slightly serrated edge.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands.
