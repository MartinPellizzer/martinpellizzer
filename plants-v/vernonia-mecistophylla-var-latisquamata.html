Vernonia mecistophylla var. latisquamata

Vernonia mecistophylla var. latisquamata, commonly known as the broad-scale ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, dark purple to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, with prominent veins that contribute to its distinctive appearance. This species is typically found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
