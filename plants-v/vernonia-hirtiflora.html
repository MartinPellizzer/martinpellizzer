Vernonia hirtiflora

Vernonia hirtiflora, commonly known as roughflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands, and it can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are typically blue to purple in color.

Vernonia hirtiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
