Viburnum longistamineum

Viburnum longistamineum, commonly known as the sweet viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant is notable for its showy, red to purple berries that appear in the fall, providing food for birds and wildlife.

Viburnum longistamineum thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
