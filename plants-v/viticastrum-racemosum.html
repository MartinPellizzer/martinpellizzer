Viticastrum racemosum

Viticastrum racemosum, commonly known as the racemose grapevine, is a climbing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and its ability to form dense thickets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Viticastrum racemosum is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
