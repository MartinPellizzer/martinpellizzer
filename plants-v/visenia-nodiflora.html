Visenia nodiflora

Visenia nodiflora, commonly known as the clustered visenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Visenia nodiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
