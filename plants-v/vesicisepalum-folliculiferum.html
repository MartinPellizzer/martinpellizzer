Vesicisepalum folliculiferum

Vesicisepalum folliculiferum, commonly known as the bladder-sepal follicle plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

The plant features showy, inflated sepals that resemble bladders, which are often brightly colored to attract pollinators. Its flowers are typically borne on slender stalks, with the sepals playing a crucial role in the reproductive process. The fruit is a follicle, a dry, dehiscent structure that splits open to release seeds.

This plant is adapted to specific ecological niches, often found in arid or semi-arid environments where it thrives with minimal water.
