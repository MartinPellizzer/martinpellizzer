Vitis hekouensis

Vitis hekouensis, commonly known as the Hekou grapevine, is a species native to China, specifically the Yunnan province.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, dark purple berries. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and its robust, woody stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it has attracted interest among botanists and horticulturists.
