Vaccinium grandibracteatum

Vaccinium grandibracteatum, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acidic-loving plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and pine flatwoods, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its small, edible berries are an important food source for wildlife.

The species is characterized by its dense foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.
