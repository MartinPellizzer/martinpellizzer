Viola × igoschinae

Viola × igoschinae, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Viola tricolor and Viola wittrockiana, resulting in a plant with a wide range of flower colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with heart-shaped leaves and showy, bilaterally symmetric flowers. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and botanical study.
