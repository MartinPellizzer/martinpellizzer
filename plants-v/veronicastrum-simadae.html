Veronicastrum simadae

Veronicastrum simadae, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Veronicastrum simadae plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
