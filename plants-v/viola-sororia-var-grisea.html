Viola sororia var. grisea

Viola sororia var. grisea, commonly known as the gray violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches, featuring heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces violet flowers with a pale purple to almost white color, often with a yellowish throat. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, commonly found in woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
