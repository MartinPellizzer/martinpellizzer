Vulpia sciuroides var. gracilis

Vulpia sciuroides var. gracilis, commonly known as slender vulpia, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. Its botanical features include slender stems and inconspicuous flowers that develop into small, three-angled seed capsules.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
