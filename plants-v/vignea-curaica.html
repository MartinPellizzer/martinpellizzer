Vignea curaica

Vignea curaica, commonly known as the Curaica bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Caribbean region.

It produces small, yellow or green pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Vignea curaica is valued for its nutritional content and is often used in local cuisines.
