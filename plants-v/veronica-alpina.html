Veronica alpina

Veronica alpina, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are often hairy and serrated. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Veronica alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
