Valeriana alypifolia subsp. alypifolia

Valeriana alypifolia subsp.

alypifolia, commonly known as the alpine valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its root system is fibrous and aromatic. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
