Viola ochroleuca

Viola ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow pansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola ochroleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for garden use.
