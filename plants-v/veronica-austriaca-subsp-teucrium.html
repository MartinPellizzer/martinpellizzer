Veronica austriaca subsp. teucrium

Veronica austriaca subsp.

teucrium, commonly known as Austrian speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine white down.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
