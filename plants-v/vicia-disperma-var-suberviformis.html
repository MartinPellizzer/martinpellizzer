Vicia disperma var. suberviformis

Vicia disperma var. suberviformis, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white pea-like flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
