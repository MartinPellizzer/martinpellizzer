Viburnum simonsii

Viburnum simonsii, commonly known as Blackhaw viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are opposite and ovate, with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. Viburnum simonsii is valued for its ornamental qualities, including its showy flowers and autumn foliage.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
