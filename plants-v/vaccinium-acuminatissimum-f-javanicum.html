Vaccinium acuminatissimum f. javanicum

Vaccinium acuminatissimum f. javanicum, commonly known as the Javanese huckleberry, is a compact shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink to white in color. These flowers give way to bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
