Viola nagasawae var. acutilabella

Viola nagasawae var. acutilabella, commonly known as the Japanese violet or Nagasawa's violet, is a species native to Japan.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of violet flowers. The flowers typically bloom in spring and are borne on slender stems. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
