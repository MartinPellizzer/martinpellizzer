Vignea vulpina f. capitulata

Vignea vulpina f. capitulata, commonly known as the fox bean or wild bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, head-like clusters, which give it its specific epithet "capitulata.". Its pods are typically slender and contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas, fields, and along roadsides.
