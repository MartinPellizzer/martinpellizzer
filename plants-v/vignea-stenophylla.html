Vignea stenophylla

Vignea stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, climbing stems and small, compound leaves with a narrow, elongated shape. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and are often pale yellow or white in color.

Vignea stenophylla is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
