Viola gan-chouenensis

Viola gan-chouenensis, commonly known as the Gan-chouen violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola gan-chouenensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
