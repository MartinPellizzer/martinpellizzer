Vriesea maxoniana

Vriesea maxoniana, commonly known as the Maxon's Vriesea, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its striking, recurved inflorescence that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant features broad, grass-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a central cup that collects rainwater. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Vriesea maxoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
