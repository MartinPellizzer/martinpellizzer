Viola pedata var. atropurpurea

Viola pedata var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple viola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to parts of Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of down, while the flowers have five petals with a distinctive violet hue.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a wildflower in natural habitats.
