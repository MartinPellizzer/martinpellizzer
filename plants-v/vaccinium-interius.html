Vaccinium interius

Vaccinium interius, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its berries are edible, though they are usually small and have a tart flavor.

The leaves are narrow and dark green, often with a slightly waxy texture.
