Viola hirta var. japonica

Viola hirta var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
