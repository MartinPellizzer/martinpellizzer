Vicia monantha var. marmorata

Vicia monantha var. marmorata, commonly known as the marbled vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive marbled pattern on its leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in early summer. Its trifoliate leaves and climbing habit make it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.

Vicia monantha var. marmorata is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
