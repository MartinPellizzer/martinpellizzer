Valantia aspera

Valantia aspera, commonly known as the rough valantia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Valantia aspera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
