Vaccinium ellipticum

Vaccinium ellipticum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cranberries and heathers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Vaccinium ellipticum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential as a food source for wildlife.
