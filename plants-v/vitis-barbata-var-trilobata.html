Vitis barbata var. trilobata

Vitis barbata var. trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed grapevine, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, round berries that ripen to a dark purple color.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
