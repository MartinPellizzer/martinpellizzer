Veronica tenuissima

Veronica tenuissima, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has thin, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.

Veronica tenuissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
