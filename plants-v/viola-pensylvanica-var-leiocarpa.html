Viola pensylvanica var. leiocarpa

Viola pensylvanica var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the Pennsylvania violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy underside, and produces showy violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. The plant thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

It is part of the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
