Varronia corymbosa

Varronia corymbosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters, resembling a cascade of golden bells. The plant has pinnate leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Varronia corymbosa is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
