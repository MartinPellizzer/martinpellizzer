Viola canina var. sabulosa

Viola canina var. sabulosa, commonly known as the sand viol, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to sandy soils in Europe.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet or purple flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 cm and thrives in well-drained, dry environments. It is often found in coastal dunes and other sandy habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
