Viola × lehbertiana

Viola × lehbertiana, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a cross between Viola tricolor and Viola lutea, resulting in a plant with distinctive, often variegated foliage. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a spurred shape, and bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
