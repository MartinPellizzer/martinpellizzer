Valeriana capitata subsp. pubicarpa

Valeriana capitata subsp.

pubicarpa, commonly known as the hairy-fruited valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. Its root system is known for containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
