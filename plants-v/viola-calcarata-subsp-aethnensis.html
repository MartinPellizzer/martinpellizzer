Viola calcarata subsp. aethnensis

Viola calcarata subsp.

aethnensis, commonly known as the Aetna violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the volcanic slopes of Mount Etna in Sicily. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow eye.

The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its volcanic habitat. It is often found in open, sunny areas at high altitudes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native environment.
