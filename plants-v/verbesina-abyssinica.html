Verbesina abyssinica

Verbesina abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its leaves are often arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
