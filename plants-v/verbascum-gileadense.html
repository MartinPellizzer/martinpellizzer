Verbascum gileadense

Verbascum gileadense, commonly known as the Gilead mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are large, fuzzy, and gray-green, while its flowers bloom in clusters at the top of the plant.

Verbascum gileadense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
