Viburnum odoratissimum var. odoratissimum

Viburnum odoratissimum var. odoratissimum, commonly known as the sweet viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is valued for its showy floral display and adaptability to various soil conditions.
