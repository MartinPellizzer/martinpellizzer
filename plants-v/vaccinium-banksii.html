Vaccinium banksii

Vaccinium banksii, commonly known as the Banks' blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Vaccinium banksii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
