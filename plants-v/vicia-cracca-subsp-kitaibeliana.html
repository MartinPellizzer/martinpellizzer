Vicia cracca subsp. kitaibeliana

Vicia cracca subsp.

kitaibeliana, commonly known as the Kitaibel's vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish pea-like flowers that are clustered in dense spikes. Its pods are slender and contain several small, elliptical seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
