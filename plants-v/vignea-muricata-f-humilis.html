Vignea muricata f. humilis

Vignea muricata f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf velvet bean, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions.

It features a sprawling growth habit with trifoliate leaves that are hairy and pubescent. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and as a cover crop in agricultural systems.
