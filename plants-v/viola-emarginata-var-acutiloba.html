Viola emarginata var. acutiloba

Viola emarginata var. acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive notched base and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in partial shade.
