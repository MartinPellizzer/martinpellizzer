Vernonia malacophyta

Vernonia malacophyta, commonly known as the Malacophylla Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, conical inflorescences, often displaying shades of yellow or orange.

Vernonia malacophyta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
