Vicia vulcanica

Vicia vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic vetch, is a flowering plant native to volcanic regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in nutrient-poor soils, often found in areas with recent volcanic activity.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important pioneer species in disturbed environments.
