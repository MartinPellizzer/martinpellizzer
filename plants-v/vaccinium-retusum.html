Vaccinium retusum

Vaccinium retusum, commonly known as the rough blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a slightly rough surface.

Vaccinium retusum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.
