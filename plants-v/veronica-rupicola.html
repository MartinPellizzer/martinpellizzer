Veronica rupicola

Veronica rupicola, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, appearing in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Veronica rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
