Valantia hispida f. robusta

Valantia hispida f. robusta, commonly known as the hairy valantia, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its robust growth habit and hairy foliage.

It features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to specific regions in South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
