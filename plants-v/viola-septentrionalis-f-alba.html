Viola septentrionalis f. alba

Viola septentrionalis f. alba, commonly known as the white violet, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, pale green leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The white violet is often found in association with other woodland flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
