Viscum tsiafajavonense

Viscum tsiafajavonense, commonly known as the Madagascar mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is native to Madagascar, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Viscum tsiafajavonense plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food for various bird species.
