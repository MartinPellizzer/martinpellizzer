Viola aubletii

Viola aubletii, commonly known as the Aublet's violet, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland areas. Viola aubletii produces showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
