Vinticena botryantha

Vinticena botryantha, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of bell-shaped, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the warm season. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Vinticena botryantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and attractive foliage.
