Verbena ameghinoi

Verbena ameghinoi, commonly known as the Andean verbena, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing vibrant purple, pink, or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinct appearance.

Verbena ameghinoi is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
