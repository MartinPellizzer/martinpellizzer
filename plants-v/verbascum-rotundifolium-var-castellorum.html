Verbascum rotundifolium var. castellorum

Verbascum rotundifolium var. castellorum, commonly known as the rock mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are rounded at the base and gradually narrow towards the tip. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems that bear yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
