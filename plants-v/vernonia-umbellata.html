Vernonia umbellata

Vernonia umbellata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Vernonia umbellata is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
