Verbena menthifolia

Verbena menthifolia, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

Its essential oil, extracted from the leaves, is used in aromatherapy and natural remedies.
