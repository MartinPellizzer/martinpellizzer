Verbascum × chium

Verbascum × chium, commonly known as the common mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a loose spike at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for respiratory ailments.
