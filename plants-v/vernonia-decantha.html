Vernonia decantha

Vernonia decantha, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes, often forming dense stands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a prominent central disk.

Vernonia decantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
