Viola hidakana f. ciliata

Viola hidakana f. ciliata, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
