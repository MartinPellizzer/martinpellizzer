Vernonia voluta

Vernonia voluta, commonly known as the spiral verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking spiral arrangement of leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique leaf morphology and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Vernonia voluta is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
