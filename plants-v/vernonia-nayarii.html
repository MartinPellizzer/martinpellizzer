Vernonia nayarii

Vernonia nayarii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its upright, branched stems and small, tubular flowers. Vernonia nayarii produces fluffy, white to pale yellow flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
