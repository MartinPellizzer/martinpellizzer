Viola ikedaeana

Viola ikedaeana, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola ikedaeana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
