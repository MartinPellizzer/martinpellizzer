Veronica froelichiana

Veronica froelichiana, commonly known as the Froelich's speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine displays.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of native flora.
