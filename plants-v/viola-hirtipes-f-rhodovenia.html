Viola hirtipes f. rhodovenia

Viola hirtipes f. rhodovenia, commonly known as the Rhodovena violet, is a botanical variant of the viola genus native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet and white, often with a contrasting veining. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, hairy texture, contributing to its overall rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable plant for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
