Vaccaria segetalis

Vaccaria segetalis, commonly known as the field sandwort, is a low-growing annual plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a rough texture, and the stems are prostrate, often rooting at the nodes.

Vaccaria segetalis is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
