Vesalea floribunda var. floribunda

Vesalea floribunda var. floribunda, commonly known as the showy vesalea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters during the warm season. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
