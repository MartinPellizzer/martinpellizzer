Veronica spicata var. vaillantii

Veronica spicata var. vaillantii, commonly known as the Vaillant's speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright, slender stems that bear spikes of small, blue or violet flowers. The leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
