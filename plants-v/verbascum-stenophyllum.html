Verbascum stenophyllum

Verbascum stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, candle-like inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Verbascum stenophyllum is a perennial plant that is well adapted to Mediterranean climates.
