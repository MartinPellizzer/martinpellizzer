Verbascum × nothum

Verbascum × nothum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattifolium.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces tall, hairy stalks that bear large, yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are broad and velvety, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
