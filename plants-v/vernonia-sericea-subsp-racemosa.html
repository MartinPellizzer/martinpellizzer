Vernonia sericea subsp. racemosa

Vernonia sericea subsp.

racemosa, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tall, branching stems that bear clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
