Vicia eriocarpa

Vicia eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are typically covered in fine, white pubescence. Its pea-like flowers are usually violet or purple, blooming in early summer.

Vicia eriocarpa is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration efforts.
