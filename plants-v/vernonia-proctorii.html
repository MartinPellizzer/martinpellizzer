Vernonia proctorii

Vernonia proctorii, commonly known as Proctor's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and pond edges, and is often found in the Carolinas and Florida. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs from late summer into early fall.

Vernonia proctorii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
