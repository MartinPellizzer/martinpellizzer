Verbascum macranthum

Verbascum macranthum, commonly known as the tall mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, woolly, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Verbascum macranthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
