Veronica chamaedrys var. pinnatifida

Veronica chamaedrys var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
