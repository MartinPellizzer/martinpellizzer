Valeriana urbani

Valeriana urbani, commonly known as the urban valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and urban environments, making it a common sight in cities. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Valeriana urbani is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used for its calming effects.
