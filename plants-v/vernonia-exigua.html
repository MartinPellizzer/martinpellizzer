Vernonia exigua

Vernonia exigua, commonly known as the smallflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Vernonia exigua is often found in open woods, prairies, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is a hardy perennial that is relatively low maintenance and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
