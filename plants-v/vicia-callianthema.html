Vicia callianthema

Vicia callianthema, commonly known as the Sierra foothill vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its root system forms nodules with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

Vicia callianthema plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
