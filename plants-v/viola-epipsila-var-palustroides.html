Viola epipsila var. palustroides

Viola epipsila var. palustroides, commonly known as the swamp violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are typically borne on slender stems and are pollinated by bees and other insects.

Viola epipsila var. palustroides plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
