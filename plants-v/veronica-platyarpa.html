Veronica platyarpa

Veronica platyarpa, commonly known as the blueweed or narrowleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Veronica platyarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate flowers and easy care requirements.
