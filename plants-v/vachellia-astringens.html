Vachellia astringens

Vachellia astringens, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in pairs. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by long, pod-like fruits that contain small, hard seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species capable of surviving in poor soil conditions.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.
