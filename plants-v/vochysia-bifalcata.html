Vochysia bifalcata

Vochysia bifalcata, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are usually bright green. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Vochysia bifalcata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
