Virgulus grandiflorus

Virgulus grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorous bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. The plant features a compact growth habit with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Native to temperate regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
