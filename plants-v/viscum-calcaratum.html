Viscum calcaratum

Viscum calcaratum, commonly known as the rock mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It possesses a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant's berries are typically red or orange, containing small seeds that are dispersed by birds. Viscum calcaratum has a woody stem and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
