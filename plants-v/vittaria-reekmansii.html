Vittaria reekmansii

Vittaria reekmansii, commonly known as the Reekman's maidenhair, is a small fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of its leaves, which are often covered in a layer of fine hairs.

Vittaria reekmansii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
