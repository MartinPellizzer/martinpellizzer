Viola acuminata var. austroussuriensis

Viola acuminata var. austroussuriensis, commonly known as the Siberian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
