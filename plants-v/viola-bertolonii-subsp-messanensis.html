Viola bertolonii subsp. messanensis

Viola bertolonii subsp.

messanensis, commonly known as the Southern violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and shaded areas.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It is part of the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to the typical form of V. bertolonii.
