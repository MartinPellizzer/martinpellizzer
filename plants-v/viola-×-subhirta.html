Viola × subhirta

Viola × subhirta, commonly known as the velvetleaf viola, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from purple to violet. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola × subhirta is known for its resilience and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
