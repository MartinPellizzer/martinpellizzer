Verbascum cerinum

Verbascum cerinum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem covered in dense, white, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a thick layer of soft, woolly hairs.

Verbascum cerinum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
