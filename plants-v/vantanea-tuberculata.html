Vantanea tuberculata

Vantanea tuberculata, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its stems are often branched and covered in small, raised bumps, giving it a distinctive texture.

Vantanea tuberculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
