Viola × tzvelevii

Viola × tzvelevii, commonly known as the Tzvelev's violet, is a hybrid species within the viola genus.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, often purple or violet flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
