Vicia glauca var. rerayensis

Vicia glauca var. rerayensis, commonly known as the Reray's vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
