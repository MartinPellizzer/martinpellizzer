Viscum vacciniifolium

Viscum vacciniifolium, commonly known as the creeping mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of white, sticky berries. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it produces its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in temperate forests.

Viscum vacciniifolium plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other wildlife.
