Viola pinetorum subsp. grisea

Viola pinetorum subsp.

grisea, commonly known as the gray violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas, often found in association with coniferous trees.

The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. Its subspecies is distinguished by its grayish-green foliage and shorter, more compact growth habit compared to other viola species.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
