Viola brevistipulata var. laciniata

Viola brevistipulata var. laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow eye at the center. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plant species.
