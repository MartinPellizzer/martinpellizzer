Vernonia thorelii

Vernonia thorelii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Vernonia thorelii is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
