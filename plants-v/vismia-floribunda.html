Vismia floribunda

Vismia floribunda, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its leaves are elliptical with a slightly waxy texture, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Vismia floribunda is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional crafts and furniture.
