Viguiera scabra

Viguiera scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, purple to pinkish-red composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rough, hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Viguiera scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
