Vigna sinensis subsp. sesquipedalis

Vigna sinensis subsp.

sesquipedalis, commonly known as the large-seeded cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with stipules.

The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several large, yellow or brown seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

The subspecies is valued for its adaptability and nutritional benefits in agricultural systems.
