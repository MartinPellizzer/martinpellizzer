Vicia caesarea

Vicia caesarea, commonly known as the azure vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has a trailing growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Vicia caesarea is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
