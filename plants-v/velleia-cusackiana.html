Velleia cusackiana

Velleia cusackiana, commonly known as the Cusack's velleia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually heart-shaped or ovate in form. Its flowers are tiny, white, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Velleia cusackiana is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants, indicating its preference for humid, nutrient-rich soils.
