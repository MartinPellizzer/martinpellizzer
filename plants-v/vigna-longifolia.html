Vigna longifolia

Vigna longifolia, commonly known as the longpod bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, elongated pods. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small white or pale pink flowers. Its seeds are encased in fibrous pods and are often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
