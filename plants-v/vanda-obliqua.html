Vanda obliqua

Vanda obliqua, commonly known as the oblique vanda, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright indirect light for optimal growth. Vanda obliqua is known for its strong, wiry stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
