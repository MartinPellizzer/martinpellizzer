Vaccinium vitis-idaea f. major

Vaccinium vitis-idaea f. major, commonly known as the great cowberry, is a subspecies of the common bilberry.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to boreal and subarctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black in color. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture and remain green throughout the year.

This subspecies is adapted to acidic, nutrient-poor soils and thrives in shaded forest environments.
