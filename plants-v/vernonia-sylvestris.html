Vernonia sylvestris

Vernonia sylvestris, commonly known as the woodland ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, violet-blue composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with rough textures. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia sylvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
