Viola canina lusus riviniana

Viola canina lusus riviniana, commonly known as the water violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soils, often near streams and rivers. Its flowers are short-lived, blooming in spring, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in aquatic gardens.
