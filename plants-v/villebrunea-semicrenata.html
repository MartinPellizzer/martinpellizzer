Villebrunea semicrenata

Villebrunea semicrenata, commonly known as the crested evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and distinctive, rounded growth habit. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a pointed tip and are arranged alternately along the stems.

Villebrunea semicrenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
