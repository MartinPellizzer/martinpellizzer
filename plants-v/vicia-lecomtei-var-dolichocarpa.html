Vicia lecomtei var. dolichocarpa

Vicia lecomtei var. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-seeded vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that bear small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces elongated, curved pods that contain several elliptical seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
