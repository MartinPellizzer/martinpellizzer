Viola mendanca

Viola mendanca, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola mendanca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
