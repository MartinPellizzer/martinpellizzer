Verbena berteroi

Verbena berteroi, commonly known as Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, vibrant blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces showy blooms from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Verbena berteroi is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
