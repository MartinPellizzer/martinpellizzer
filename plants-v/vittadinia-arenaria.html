Vittadinia arenaria

Vittadinia arenaria, commonly known as the sand vittadinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing herb with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the tip of the stem. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Vittadinia arenaria is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
