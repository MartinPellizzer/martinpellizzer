Valvaria ochroleuca

Valvaria ochroleuca, commonly known as the white-leaved valvaria, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, pale green leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Valvaria ochroleuca thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
