Vitis imperialis

Vitis imperialis, commonly known as the Chinese grape or imperial grape, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of sweet, edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Vitis imperialis is also valued for its historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
