Veronica javanica var. nana

Veronica javanica var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with slender, hairy stems and small, rounded leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and compact size, which make it ideal for rock gardens or as a ground cover.
