Vesalea

Vesalea, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent petals that resemble delicate, ghostly shapes, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant belongs to the family Araceae, which includes other ornamental plants like calla lilies. Vesalea thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and exotic plant collections.
