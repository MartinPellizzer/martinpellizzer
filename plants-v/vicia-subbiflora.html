Vicia subbiflora

Vicia subbiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, creeping stems with small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. Its flowers are small, pea-like, and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters.

Vicia subbiflora is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
