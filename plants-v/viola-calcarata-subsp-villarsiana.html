Viola calcarata subsp. villarsiana

Viola calcarata subsp.

villarsiana, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and it produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet and white. Its flowers are typically spurred at the base, a characteristic feature of the Viola genus.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
