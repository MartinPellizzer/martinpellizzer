Veronica barrelieri subsp. andrasovszkyi

Veronica barrelieri subsp.

andrasovszkyi, commonly known as the Andrasovszky's speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands. It features slender, ascending stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the Veronica genus. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
