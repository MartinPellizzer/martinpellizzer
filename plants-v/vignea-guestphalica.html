Vignea guestphalica

Vignea guestphalica, commonly known as the Indian bean or wild bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
