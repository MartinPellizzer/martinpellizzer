Verbascum varians var. trapezunticum

Verbascum varians var. trapezunticum, commonly known as the Trapesuntian mullein, is a perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with soft, velvety gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, fuzzy inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
