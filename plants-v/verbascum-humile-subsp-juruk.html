Verbascum humile subsp. juruk

Verbascum humile subsp.

juruk, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It features tall, erect stems covered in dense, velvety gray-green hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and also covered in soft, fuzzy hairs.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant with ecological significance in its native habitat.
