Valeriana frigidorum

Valeriana frigidorum, commonly known as the Arctic valerian, is a flowering plant native to cold climates of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and arctic regions, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Valeriana frigidorum is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
