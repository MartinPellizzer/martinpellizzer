Vernonia subsquarrosa

Vernonia subsquarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, purple to reddish-purple flower heads. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woods and along roadsides.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
