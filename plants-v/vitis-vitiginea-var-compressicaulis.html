Vitis vitiginea var. compressicaulis

Vitis vitiginea var. compressicaulis, commonly known as the creeping grapevine, is a variant of the common grapevine characterized by its prostrate growth habit.

It is a deciduous vine that produces small, dark green leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in shaded, moist environments. Its berries are typically small and can be green or black when mature.

This variety is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its adaptability and ability to spread across surfaces.
