Viola maderensis

Viola maderensis, commonly known as the Mader's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola maderensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
