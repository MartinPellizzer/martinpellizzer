Vigna unguiculata var. tenuis

Vigna unguiculata var. tenuis, commonly known as slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically pale yellow to white, and the pods are elongated, containing several seeds. This variety is valued for its adaptability to poor soils and its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

It is widely cultivated for both its edible seeds and as a cover crop in agricultural systems.
