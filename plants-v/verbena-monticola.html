Verbena monticola

Verbena monticola, commonly known as mountain verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry environments, thriving in full sun. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Verbena monticola is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
