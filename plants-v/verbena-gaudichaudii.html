Verbena gaudichaudii

Verbena gaudichaudii, commonly known as the Hawaiian verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens. It is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for native pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is also valued as an ornamental plant in horticulture.
