Viola × savourei

Viola × savourei, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. It is native to Europe and has been naturalized in various parts of the world.

Viola × savourei is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
