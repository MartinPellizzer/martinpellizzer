Veronica laevis var. carnosula

Veronica laevis var. carnosula, commonly known as the woolly speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically opposite, narrow, and also covered in a fine, velvety coating. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
