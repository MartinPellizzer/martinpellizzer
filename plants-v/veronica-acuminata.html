Veronica acuminata

Veronica acuminata, commonly known as the sharpleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a pointed tip, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Veronica acuminata is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
