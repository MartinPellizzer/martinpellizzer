Vachellia densiflora

Vachellia densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange spherical flower heads, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Vachellia densiflora is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
