Valeriana albiflora

Valeriana albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.
