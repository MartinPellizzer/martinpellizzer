Veronica linariifolia subsp. cartilaginea

Veronica linariifolia subsp.

cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilaginous speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, upright stems covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs.

The leaves are lance-shaped, often with a slightly wavy margin, and are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This species is adapted to rocky or dry habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
