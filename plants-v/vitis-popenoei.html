Vitis popenoei

Vitis popenoei, commonly known as the Popenoe grape, is a rare and endangered species native to the southwestern United States.

This woody vine belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible berries that are typically consumed by wildlife. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has limited genetic diversity, making it a subject of conservation interest.

Due to its rarity and unique botanical features, it is often studied for its potential ecological and horticultural value.
