Verbascum × breyninum

Verbascum × breyninum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattellum, both native to southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces showy yellow or orange flowers in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
