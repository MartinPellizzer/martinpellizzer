Valeriana magna

Valeriana magna, commonly known as giant valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and can grow up to 2 meters in height, with large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in terminal panicles. Its aromatic foliage contains volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal uses.

Valeriana magna is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional herbal applications.
