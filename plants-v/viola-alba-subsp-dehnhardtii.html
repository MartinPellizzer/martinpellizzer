Viola alba subsp. dehnhardtii

Viola alba subsp.

dehnhardtii, commonly known as the White Violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, pale green leaves and small, delicate white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flowering habits compared to other viola species.
