Veronica tetrasticha

Veronica tetrasticha, commonly known as the four-stemmed speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its flowers are typically arranged in whorls along the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Veronica tetrasticha is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
