Viola × hiyamae

Viola × hiyamae, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola wittrockii and Viola yedoensis.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
