Verbena harbisonii

Verbena harbisonii, commonly known as the Harbison's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces vibrant clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Verbena harbisonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
