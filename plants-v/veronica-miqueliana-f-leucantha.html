Veronica miqueliana f. leucantha

Veronica miqueliana f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The foliage is notable for its striking white or pale green coloration, which distinguishes it from other members of the species. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, with five petals, and bloom during the spring and early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
