Verbascum chrysanthum

Verbascum chrysanthum, commonly known as the yellow mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, velvety, yellow-orange flower clusters. The leaves are broad, oval, and covered in a dense layer of soft, downy hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
