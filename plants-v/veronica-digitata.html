Veronica digitata

Veronica digitata, commonly known as the fingered speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
