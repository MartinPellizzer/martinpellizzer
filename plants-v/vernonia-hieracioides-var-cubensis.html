Vernonia hieracioides var. cubensis

Vernonia hieracioides var. cubensis, commonly known as Cuban ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions make it a notable species in horticulture.
