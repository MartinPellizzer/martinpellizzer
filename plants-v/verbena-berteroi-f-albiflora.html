Verbena berteroi f. albiflora

Verbena berteroi f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its delicate appearance and drought tolerance.
