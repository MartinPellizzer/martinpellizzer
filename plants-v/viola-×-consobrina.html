Viola × consobrina

Viola × consobrina, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is native to Europe and has been naturalized in various parts of the world.

Viola × consobrina is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed sites, where it plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
