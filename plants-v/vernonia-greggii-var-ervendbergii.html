Vernonia greggii var. ervendbergii

Vernonia greggii var. ervendbergii, commonly known as the Gregg's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
