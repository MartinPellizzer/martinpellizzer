Vachellia etbaica subsp. etbaica

Vachellia etbaica subsp.

etbaica, commonly known as the Etbaic acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to spines in mature plants.

The plant produces yellow flowers in compact clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
