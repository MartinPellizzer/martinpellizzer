Verbesina macrophylla

Verbesina macrophylla, commonly known as the giant swamp mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with orange centers, which bloom from late summer to early fall. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it an important species for wetland restoration projects.
