Valota penicilligera

Valota penicilligera, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and its unique, showy flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Valota penicilligera has a complex root system that allows it to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
