Viola godoyae

Viola godoyae, commonly known as the Godoy's violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola godoyae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
