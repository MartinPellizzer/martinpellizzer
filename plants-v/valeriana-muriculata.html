Valeriana muriculata

Valeriana muriculata, commonly known as the rough valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough texture, giving it its common name.
