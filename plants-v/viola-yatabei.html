Viola yatabei

Viola yatabei, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola yatabei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.
