Vitis rotundifolia var. ovata

Vitis rotundifolia var. ovata, commonly known as the sand grape, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to purple in color. The plant thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a robust root system that helps stabilize soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local ecosystems.
