Viburnum rhytidophyllum f. roseum

Viburnum rhytidophyllum f. roseum, commonly known as the roseum viburnum, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and dark green, ovate leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in late spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.

Viburnum rhytidophyllum f. roseum is often used in gardens for its year-round visual interest and wildlife benefits.
