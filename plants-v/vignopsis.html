Vignopsis

Vignopsis, commonly known as the genus of flowering plants in the legume family, is characterized by its small, often erect shrubs.

These plants typically have pinnately compound leaves and produce small, pod-like fruits. They are native to various regions in Africa, South America, and parts of Asia. Vignopsis species are known for their ability to fix nitrogen, making them valuable in soil enrichment.

Many species are cultivated for their ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
