Veronica longifolia subsp. sachalinensis

Veronica longifolia subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as Sachalin speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Far East, including parts of Russia and Japan. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite leaves, which are lance-shaped and often slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
