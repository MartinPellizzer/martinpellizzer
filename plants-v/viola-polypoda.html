Viola polypoda

Viola polypoda, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, often adorned with purple markings. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Viola polypoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
