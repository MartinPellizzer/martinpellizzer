Vicia microphylla subsp. salaminia

Vicia microphylla subsp.

salaminia, commonly known as the Salaminian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and slender, climbing stems that produce pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, and it produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in agricultural settings.
