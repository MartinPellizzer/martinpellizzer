Veronica ceratocarpa

Veronica ceratocarpa, commonly known as the horned speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant features distinctive, sharply pointed bracts that resemble horns, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy, while its flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

Veronica ceratocarpa thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
