Vernonia flexuosa var. uleana

Vernonia flexuosa var. uleana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and wildlife habitats.
