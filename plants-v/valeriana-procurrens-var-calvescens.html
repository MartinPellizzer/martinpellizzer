Valeriana procurrens var. calvescens

Valeriana procurrens var. calvescens, commonly known as the creeping valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features small, fragrant white or pink flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Valeriana procurrens var. calvescens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its calming effects.
