Viola × sublanceolata

Viola × sublanceolata, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are usually violet or purple in color. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, woodland environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola × sublanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
