Valeriana alternifolia var. stubendorfii

Valeriana alternifolia var. stubendorfii, commonly known as the alternate-leaved valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and contains volatile compounds that contribute to its medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
