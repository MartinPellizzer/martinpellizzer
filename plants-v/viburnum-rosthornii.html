Viburnum rosthornii

Viburnum rosthornii, commonly known as the rosthornii viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum rosthornii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its seasonal color changes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
