Vicia hirticalycina f. anguste-pinnata

Vicia hirticalycina f. anguste-pinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hairy vetch, is a variant of the hairy vetch species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, pinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It is a leguminous plant, capable of nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

This form is often cultivated as a cover crop or for forage due to its hardy nature and growth habits.
