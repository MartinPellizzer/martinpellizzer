Viburnum heterophylum

Viburnum heterophylum, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its compact size and seasonal interest make it suitable for gardens and landscape designs.
