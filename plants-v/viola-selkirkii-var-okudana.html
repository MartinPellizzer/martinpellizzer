Viola selkirkii var. okudana

Viola selkirkii var. okudana, commonly known as the Okudana violet, is a variant of the Selkirk violet native to Japan.

It is a small herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped, velvety leaves and delicate purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
