Vaccinium glaucorubrum

Vaccinium glaucorubrum, commonly known as the purple bilberry, is a species of dwarf shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and temperate forest ecosystems.

Vaccinium glaucorubrum plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and small mammals.
