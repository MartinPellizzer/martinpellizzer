Veronica nemorum

Veronica nemorum, commonly known as water speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant features slender stems with opposite leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped. Its flowers are small, blue or violet, and clustered in whorls along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Veronica nemorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
