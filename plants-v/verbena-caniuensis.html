Verbena caniuensis

Verbena caniuensis, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant violet or blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb with slender stems and narrow, opposite leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

Its compact growth habit and showy flowers make it an ornamental addition to landscapes and containers.
