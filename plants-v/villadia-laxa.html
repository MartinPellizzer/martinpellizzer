Villadia laxa

Villadia laxa, commonly known as the slender villadia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, sprawling growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a soft, hairy texture, contributing to its unique appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialty nurseries.
