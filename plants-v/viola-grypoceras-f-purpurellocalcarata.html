Viola grypoceras f. purpurellocalcarata

Viola grypoceras f. purpurellocalcarata, commonly known as the purple-stemmed violet, is a unique variant of the viola genus characterized by its striking purple stems and foliage.

This plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and deep green coloration. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and biodiversity contribution.
