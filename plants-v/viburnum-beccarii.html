Viburnum beccarii

Viburnum beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari viburnum, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy upper surface.

Viburnum beccarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
