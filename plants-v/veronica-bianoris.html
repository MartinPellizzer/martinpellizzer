Veronica bianoris

Veronica bianoris, commonly known as the white veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate blossoms that bloom in late spring to early summer.
