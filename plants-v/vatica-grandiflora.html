Vatica grandiflora

Vatica grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Vatica grandiflora is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
