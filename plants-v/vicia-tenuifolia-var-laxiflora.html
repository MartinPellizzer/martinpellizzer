Vicia tenuifolia var. laxiflora

Vicia tenuifolia var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-yellow and are arranged in loose, racemose inflorescences. This variety is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and can be used in ecological restoration projects.
