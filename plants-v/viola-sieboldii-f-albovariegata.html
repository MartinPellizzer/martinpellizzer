Viola sieboldii f. albovariegata

Viola sieboldii f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-variegated violet, is a hybrid cultivar of the viola genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display white and green patterns, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white, often appearing in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is well-suited for woodland gardens or shaded borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
