Vitis labrusca f. lobata

Vitis labrusca f. lobata, commonly known as the American muscat grape, is a cultivar of the fox grape species.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, round berries that are typically dark purple to black in color.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique flavor and is used in the production of wine and jams.
