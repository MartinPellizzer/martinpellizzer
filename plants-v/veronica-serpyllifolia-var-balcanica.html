Veronica serpyllifolia var. balcanica

Veronica serpyllifolia var. balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive textured appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
