Verbascum ikaricum

Verbascum ikaricum, commonly known as the Ikaric mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are characteristic of the mullein genus. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Verbascum ikaricum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential respiratory benefits.
