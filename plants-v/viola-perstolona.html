Viola perstolona

Viola perstolona, commonly known as the Italian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along streambanks, preferring moist, well-drained soil. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola perstolona is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
