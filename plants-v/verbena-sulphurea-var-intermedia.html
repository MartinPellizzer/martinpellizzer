Verbena sulphurea var. intermedia

Verbena sulphurea var. intermedia, commonly known as the yellow verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a splash of color to gardens. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
