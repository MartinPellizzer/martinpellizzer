Vigna nigerica

Vigna nigerica, commonly known as black gram or urad, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Vigna nigerica thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

It also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility.
