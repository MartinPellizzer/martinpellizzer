Vernonia vohemarensis

Vernonia vohemarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Vernonia vohemarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
