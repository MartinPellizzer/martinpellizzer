Vigna pygmaea var. grandiflora

Vigna pygmaea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the dwarf pigeon pea, is a small leguminous plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its seeds are typically small and yellowish, often used in traditional cuisines.

Vigna pygmaea var. grandiflora is valued for its nutritional content and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
