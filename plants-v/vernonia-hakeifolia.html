Vernonia hakeifolia

Vernonia hakeifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces seeds in small, dry fruits and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Vernonia hakeifolia is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
