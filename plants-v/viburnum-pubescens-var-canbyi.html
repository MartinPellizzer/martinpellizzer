Viburnum pubescens var. canbyi

Viburnum pubescens var. canbyi, commonly known as Canby's snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually covered with fine, grayish-white hairs. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
