Vietorchis furcata

Vietorchis furcata, commonly known as the forked orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive forked or branched flower spike, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Vietorchis furcata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and private collections.
