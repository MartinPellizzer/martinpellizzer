Veronica bodardii

Veronica bodardii, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit, with silver-gray foliage and small blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Veronica bodardii is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
