Vernonia alvimii

Vernonia alvimii, commonly known as the Alvim's ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with gray-green foliage and conical, spiky flower heads that range in color from yellow to brown. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
