Veronica austriaca subvar. platyphylla

Veronica austriaca subvar. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
