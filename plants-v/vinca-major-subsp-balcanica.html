Vinca major subsp. balcanica

Vinca major subsp.

balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan vinca, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit, forming dense mats that can spread aggressively.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in difficult conditions.
