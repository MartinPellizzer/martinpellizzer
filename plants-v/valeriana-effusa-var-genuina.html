Valeriana effusa var. genuina

Valeriana effusa var. genuina, commonly known as the common valerian, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its creeping rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers arranged in terminal clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

It is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
