Viola × egglestoniana

Viola × egglestoniana, commonly known as the Eggleston violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a deep green color. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among gardeners seeking hardy, flowering plants for temperate climates.
