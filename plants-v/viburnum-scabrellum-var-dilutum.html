Viburnum scabrellum var. dilutum

Viburnum scabrellum var. dilutum, commonly known as the roughleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically rough to the touch, with a pale green upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red or purple berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
