Viola drepanophora

Viola drepanophora, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces delicate purple or violet flowers with a distinctive hooded structure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.

Viola drepanophora is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
