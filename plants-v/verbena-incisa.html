Verbena incisa

Verbena incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are distinctive, with a scalloped or lobed appearance, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Verbena incisa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
