Vernonia maxima subsp. pubescens

Vernonia maxima subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance and aid in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.
