Vicia rosani

Vicia rosani, commonly known as the Rosani vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Vicia rosani is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its ecological importance includes supporting pollinators and contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
