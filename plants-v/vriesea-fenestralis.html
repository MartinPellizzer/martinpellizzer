Vriesea fenestralis

Vriesea fenestralis, commonly known as the window leaf bromeliad, is a striking plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spathaceous inflorescence that resembles a flower. The plant features a rosette of elongated, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with green, white, and pink hues. Its foliage is adorned with translucent windows or translucent sections, giving it its distinctive appearance.

Vriesea fenestralis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
