Vaccinium chamissonis var. alpinum

Vaccinium chamissonis var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to mountainous regions of North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine tundra and rocky habitats. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface and are typically dark green.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
