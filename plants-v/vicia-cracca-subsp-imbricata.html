Vicia cracca subsp. imbricata

Vicia cracca subsp.

imbricata, commonly known as the imbricate vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its imbricate (overlapping) leaflets and erect growth habit. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
