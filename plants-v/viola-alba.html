Viola alba

Viola alba, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces showy white or pale purple flowers with a distinctive violet pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its delicate, fragrant blossoms.

Viola alba is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
