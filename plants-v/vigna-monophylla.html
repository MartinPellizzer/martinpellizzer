Vigna monophylla

Vigna monophylla, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces vibrant red or yellow flowers, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Vigna monophylla is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
