Vincetoxicum officinale var. pyrenaicum

Vincetoxicum officinale var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clustered inflorescences, and it produces small, legume-like seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and often endangered species in its native range.
