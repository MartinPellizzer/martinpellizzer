Verbascum phlomoides proles nemorosum

Verbascum phlomoides proles nemorosum, commonly known as the woodland mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features tall, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with soft, velvety leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fibrous roots and hairy foliage are characteristic of the genus Verbascum.
