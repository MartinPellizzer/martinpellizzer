Veronica polita var. dentata

Veronica polita var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features slender, spreading stems that produce clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
