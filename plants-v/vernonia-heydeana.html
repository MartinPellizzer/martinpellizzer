Vernonia heydeana

Vernonia heydeana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in moist woodlands. It serves as a valuable nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
