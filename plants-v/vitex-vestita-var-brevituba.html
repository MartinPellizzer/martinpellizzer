Vitex vestita var. brevituba

Vitex vestita var. brevituba, commonly known as the narrow-tubed chaste tree, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are clustered in terminal panicles. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a dry, two-valved capsule containing small, black seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to well-drained soils.
