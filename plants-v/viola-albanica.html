Viola albanica

Viola albanica, commonly known as the Albanian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, purple-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola albanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
