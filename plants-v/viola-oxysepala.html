Viola oxysepala

Viola oxysepala, commonly known as the pale violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola oxysepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
