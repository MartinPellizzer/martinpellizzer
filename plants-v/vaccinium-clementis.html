Vaccinium clementis

Vaccinium clementis, commonly known as the Clement's blueberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Vaccinium clementis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in home gardens.
