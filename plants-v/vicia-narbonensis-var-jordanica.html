Vicia narbonensis var. jordanica

Vicia narbonensis var. jordanica, commonly known as Jordanian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This variety is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
