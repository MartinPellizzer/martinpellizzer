Vicia pampicola var. burkartii

Vicia pampicola var. burkartii, commonly known as the Burkart's vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
