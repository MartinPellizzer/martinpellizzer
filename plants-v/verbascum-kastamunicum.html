Verbascum kastamunicum

Verbascum kastamunicum, commonly known as the Kastamonu mullein, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
