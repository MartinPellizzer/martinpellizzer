Veronica agrestis var. didyma

Veronica agrestis var. didyma, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with spreading stems and small, paired leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
