Vaccinium macrocarpon f. dahlei

Vaccinium macrocarpon f. dahlei, commonly known as the cranberry bush, is a low-growing shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, red berries. The plant thrives in acidic, sandy soils and is often found in wetland habitats. Its berries are edible and have been used historically for culinary and medicinal purposes.

The f. dahlei form is a natural variant that exhibits distinct morphological traits compared to the typical cranberry species.
