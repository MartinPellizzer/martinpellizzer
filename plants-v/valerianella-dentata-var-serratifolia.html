Valerianella dentata var. serratifolia

Valerianella dentata var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated valerian, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, serrated leaves that resemble those of parsley or other umbelliferous plants. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of its family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Valerianella dentata var. serratifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
