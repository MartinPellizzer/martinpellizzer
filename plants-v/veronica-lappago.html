Veronica lappago

Veronica lappago, commonly known as the blueweed or narrow-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base.

Veronica lappago thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.
