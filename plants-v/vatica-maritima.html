Vatica maritima

Vatica maritima, commonly known as the sea almond, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
