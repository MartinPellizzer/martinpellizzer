Veronica nevadensis

Veronica nevadensis, commonly known as Sierra blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators.

Veronica nevadensis is often found in open habitats such as meadows and along stream banks.
