Viola gmeliniana var. hispida

Viola gmeliniana var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, producing a range of colors from purple to white, and it is often found in meadows and forest edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for garden use in temperate climates.
