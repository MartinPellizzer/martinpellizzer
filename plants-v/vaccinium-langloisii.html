Vaccinium langloisii

Vaccinium langloisii, commonly known as the Langlois huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Vaccinium langloisii plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
