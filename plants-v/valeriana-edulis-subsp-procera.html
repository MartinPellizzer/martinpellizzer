Valeriana edulis subsp. procera

Valeriana edulis subsp.

procera, commonly known as the giant valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
