Veronica dasyphylla var. minor

Veronica dasyphylla var. minor, commonly known as the lesser speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
