Vellozia humilis

Vellozia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf vellozia, is a unique plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Velloziaceae and is characterized by its rosette growth pattern and succulent leaves. The plant has thick, fleshy foliage that stores water, adapting it to arid environments. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in a tight inflorescence.

Vellozia humilis is noted for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
