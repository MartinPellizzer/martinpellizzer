Vigna vexillata var. lobatifolia

Vigna vexillata var. lobatifolia, commonly known as the African snail bean, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value. It has a shallow root system and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for marginal soils.

Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
