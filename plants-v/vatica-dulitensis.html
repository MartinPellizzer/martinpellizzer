Vatica dulitensis

Vatica dulitensis, commonly known as the Malay ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, oval seeds.

Vatica dulitensis typically grows in lowland rainforests and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed sites.
