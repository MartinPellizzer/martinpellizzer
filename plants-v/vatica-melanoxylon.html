Vatica melanoxylon

Vatica melanoxylon, commonly known as black ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
