Vitex neglecta var. puberula

Vitex neglecta var. puberula, commonly known as silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Silver vine is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
