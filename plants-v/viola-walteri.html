Viola walteri

Viola walteri, commonly known as the Walter's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola walteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
