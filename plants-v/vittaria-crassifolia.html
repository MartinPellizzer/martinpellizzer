Vittaria crassifolia

Vittaria crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its robust, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in strobila, a structure typical of many ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Vittaria crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
