Viola rhodopeia var. congesta

Viola rhodopeia var. congesta, commonly known as the Balkan violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
