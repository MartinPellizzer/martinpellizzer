Verbascum natolicum

Verbascum natolicum, commonly known as the Armenian mullein.

This perennial herb is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia. It features tall, hairy stalks that can reach up to two meters in height, supporting large, fuzzy yellow flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
