Viguiera longifolia var. annua

Viguiera longifolia var. annua, commonly known as the annual sunflower or Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow to orange-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 feet, featuring narrow, hairy, and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its botanical features include a taproot system and the ability to self-seed, contributing to its adaptability in various environments.
