Vangueria fulva

Vangueria fulva, commonly known as the yellow vangueria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture.

Vangueria fulva is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
