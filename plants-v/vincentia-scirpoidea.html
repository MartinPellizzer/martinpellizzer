Vincentia scirpoidea

Vincentia scirpoidea, commonly known as the swamp bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive winged calyx. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

The fruit is a dry capsule that releases small, hard seeds when mature.
