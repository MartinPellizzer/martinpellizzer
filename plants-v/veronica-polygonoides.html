Veronica polygonoides

Veronica polygonoides, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Veronica polygonoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its delicate appearance.
