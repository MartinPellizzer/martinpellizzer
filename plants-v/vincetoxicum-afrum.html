Vincetoxicum afrum

Vincetoxicum afrum, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a milky latex sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
