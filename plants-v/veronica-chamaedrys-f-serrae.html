Veronica chamaedrys f. serrae

Veronica chamaedrys f. serrae, commonly known as the serrated rock speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and serrated, giving the plant its distinguishing feature. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky or alpine environments.
