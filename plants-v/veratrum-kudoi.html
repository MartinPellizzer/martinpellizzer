Veratrum kudoi

Veratrum kudoi, commonly known as the Japanese white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, broad leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Due to its medicinal and toxic properties, it is often found in specialized botanical collections.
