Viola superba

Viola superba, commonly known as the noble violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with dark purple markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of downy hair.

Viola superba is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its historical use in traditional medicine.
