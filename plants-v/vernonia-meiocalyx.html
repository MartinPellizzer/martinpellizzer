Vernonia meiocalyx

Vernonia meiocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Vernonia meiocalyx is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
