Vatica havilandii

Vatica havilandii, commonly known as the Haviland's ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Vatica havilandii is valued for its timber and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.
