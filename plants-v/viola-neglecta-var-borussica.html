Viola neglecta var. borussica

Viola neglecta var. borussica, commonly known as the Siberian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
