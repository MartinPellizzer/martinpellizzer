Verbena rigida f. paraguayensis

Verbena rigida f. paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay verbena, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, vibrant blue or violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
