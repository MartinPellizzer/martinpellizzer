Viburnum chingii var. patentiserratum

Viburnum chingii var. patentiserratum, commonly known as the Chinese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it has a compact growth habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
