Vitis vinifera var. apyrena

Vitis vinifera var. apyrena, commonly known as the Muscat of Alexandria, is a grapevine variety native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, green berries that develop a distinctive muscat flavor when fully ripe. The plant is a deciduous vine with compound leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for both wine production and table grapes.
