Vicia sativa var. erythrosperma

Vicia sativa var. erythrosperma, commonly known as crimson vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive red to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods containing small, elliptical seeds that are typically reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agricultural settings for soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
