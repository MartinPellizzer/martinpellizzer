Viola × adulterina var. intricata

Viola × adulterina var. intricata, commonly known as the intricate violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, bi-color flowers that typically feature a mix of purple and yellow. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and as a bedding plant.
