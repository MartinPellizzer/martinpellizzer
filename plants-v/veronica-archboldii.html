Veronica archboldii

Veronica archboldii, commonly known as the Big Bend speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in the United States and Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to arid environments with deep roots for water storage. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and limestone habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
