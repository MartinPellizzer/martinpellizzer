Vernonia andamanica

Vernonia andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia andamanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
