Veronica serpyllifolia subsp. nummularioides

Veronica serpyllifolia subsp.

nummularioides, commonly known as the silverweed veronica, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has dense, mat-forming foliage with silver-gray leaves that are often rounded or oval in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
