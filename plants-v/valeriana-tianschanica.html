Valeriana tianschanica

Valeriana tianschanica, commonly known as the Tian Shan valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its rhizomes contain volatile oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Valeriana tianschanica is valued in traditional medicine for its calming and sedative effects.
