Vernonia petiolaris var. pauciflora

Vernonia petiolaris var. pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy perennial suitable for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
