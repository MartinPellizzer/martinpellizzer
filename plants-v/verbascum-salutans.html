Verbascum salutans

Verbascum salutans, commonly known as the morning glory of the Mediterranean, is a flowering plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often opening in the morning and closing in the afternoon. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. Its leaves are thick and fuzzy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Verbascum salutans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
