Valeriana arkansana

Valeriana arkansana, commonly known as Arkansas valerian, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves. It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.

Valeriana arkansana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
