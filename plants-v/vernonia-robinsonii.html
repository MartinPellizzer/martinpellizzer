Vernonia robinsonii

Vernonia robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Vernonia robinsonii is adapted to moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
