Vernonia agrianthoides

Vernonia agrianthoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Vernonia agrianthoides is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
