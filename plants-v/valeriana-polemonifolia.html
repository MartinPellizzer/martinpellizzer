Valeriana polemonifolia

Valeriana polemonifolia, commonly known as the purple valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky slopes. Its root system is fibrous and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming properties.
