Vigna unguiculata var. brasilica

Vigna unguiculata var. brasilica, commonly known as the Brazilian cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutrient-rich seeds, which are a good source of protein. It also has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.

The flowers are typically pale yellow to white, and the plant is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
