Viola scorpiuroides var. inflata

Viola scorpiuroides var. inflata, commonly known as the inflated violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, inflated spur at the base of the petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
