Verbascum microsepalum

Verbascum microsepalum, commonly known as the narrow-sepal mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features tall, sparsely haired stems that bear large, fuzzy, yellow-orange flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of soft, silky hairs.

It is valued in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.
