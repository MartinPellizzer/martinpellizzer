Veronica teucrium subsp. dentata

Veronica teucrium subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the toothed speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family. It is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive toothed margin, which gives it its subspecific name. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
