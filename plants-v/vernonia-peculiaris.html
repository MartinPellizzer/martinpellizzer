Vernonia peculiaris

Vernonia peculiaris, commonly known as the peculiar ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to reddish-brown.

Vernonia peculiaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
