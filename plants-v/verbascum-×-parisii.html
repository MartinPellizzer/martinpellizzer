Verbascum × parisii

Verbascum × parisii, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species commonly referred to as mulleins. The plant features large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense, showy clusters at the ends of the stems.

Verbascum × parisii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in sunny locations.
