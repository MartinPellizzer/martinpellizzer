Viola calabra

Viola calabra, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white marking at the base. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or woodland environments.

Viola calabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
