Valeriana clarionifolia

Valeriana clarionifolia, commonly known as the clarionleaf valerian, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom during the dry season. Valeriana clarionifolia is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for botanical gardens and private collections.
