Vitis monosperma

Vitis monosperma, commonly known as the African grape or black grapevine, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include a woody stem with tendrils for climbing and small, clustered flowers.

The fruit is typically consumed fresh or processed into jams and wines in some local cultures.
