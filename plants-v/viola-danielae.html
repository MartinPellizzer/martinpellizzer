Viola danielae

Viola danielae, commonly known as the Daniel's violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats, preferring cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural surroundings.

Viola danielae is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
