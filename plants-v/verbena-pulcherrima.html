Verbena pulcherrima

Verbena pulcherrima, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are square and often branched.

Verbena pulcherrima thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
