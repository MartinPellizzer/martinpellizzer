Vigna verdcourtii

Vigna verdcourtii, commonly known as the African cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, and it plays an important role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Vigna verdcourtii is also valued for its adaptability to poor soils and its potential in sustainable agriculture.
