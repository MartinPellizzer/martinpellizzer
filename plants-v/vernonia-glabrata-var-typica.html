Vernonia glabrata var. typica

Vernonia glabrata var. typica, commonly known as the smooth vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
