Veronica scutellata var. angustifolia

Veronica scutellata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
