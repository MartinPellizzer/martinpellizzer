Viola polygalifolia

Viola polygalifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance.
