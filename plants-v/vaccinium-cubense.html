Vaccinium cubense

Vaccinium cubense, commonly known as the Cuban huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and parts of the Greater Antilles.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Vaccinium cubense is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
