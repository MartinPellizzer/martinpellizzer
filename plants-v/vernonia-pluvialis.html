Vernonia pluvialis

Vernonia pluvialis, commonly known as the rain vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant produces upright spikes of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive texture.

Vernonia pluvialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet environments.
