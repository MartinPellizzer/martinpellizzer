Viburnum pubescens var. deamii

Viburnum pubescens var. deamii, commonly known as the Appalachian hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are opposite and hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in panicles during late spring, followed by edible, red drupes in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
