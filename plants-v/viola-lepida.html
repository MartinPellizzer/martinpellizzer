Viola lepida

Viola lepida, commonly known as the slender violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Viola lepida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
