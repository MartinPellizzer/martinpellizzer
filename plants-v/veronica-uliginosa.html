Veronica uliginosa

Veronica uliginosa, commonly known as the marsh speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, often reddish stems that bear opposite pairs of small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are blue to purple, with four petals, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Veronica uliginosa plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various insects and wildlife.
