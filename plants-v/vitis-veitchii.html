Vitis veitchii

Vitis veitchii, commonly known as the Veitchii grape, is a species of flowering vine native to China.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and the production of small, dark blue berries. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Vitis veitchii is also valued in viticulture for its potential as a rootstock due to its resistance to certain soil-borne diseases.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
