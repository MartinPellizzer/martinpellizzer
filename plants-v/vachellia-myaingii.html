Vachellia myaingii

Vachellia myaingii, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces yellow flowers clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, which are followed by distinctive pod fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions.

Vachellia myaingii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in degraded areas.
