Vernonia lingua

Vernonia lingua, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, forming dense, mist-like clouds. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Vernonia lingua is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
