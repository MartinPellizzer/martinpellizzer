Vittaria latipes

Vittaria latipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spikemoss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Vittariaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spore-producing structures called sporangia. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Vittaria latipes is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them.

Its delicate structure and ability to thrive in humid conditions make it a common sight in tropical and subtropical regions.
