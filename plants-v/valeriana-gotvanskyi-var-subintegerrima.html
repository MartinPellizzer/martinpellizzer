Valeriana gotvanskyi var. subintegerrima

Valeriana gotvanskyi var. subintegerrima, commonly known as the Gotvansky valerian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Valeriana gotvanskyi var. subintegerrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild aromatic properties.
