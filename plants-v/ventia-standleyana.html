Ventia standleyana

Ventia standleyana, commonly known as the Malabar mountain vine, is a climbing plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, followed by legume-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
