Vernonia pseudolinearifolia

Vernonia pseudolinearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Vernonia pseudolinearifolia is typically found in moist, open habitats like wetlands and disturbed soils.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
