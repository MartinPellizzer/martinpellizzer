Virgulus patens

Virgulus patens, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are clustered in terminal panicles, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are red when mature.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in coastal and tropical climates.
