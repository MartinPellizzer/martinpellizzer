Verbascum thapsiforme subsp. phlomoides

Verbascum thapsiforme subsp.

phlomoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features tall, erect stems covered in dense, woolly gray-green foliage.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flower clusters in late summer, which are arranged in a cylindrical shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its fibrous roots and thick leaves make it a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
