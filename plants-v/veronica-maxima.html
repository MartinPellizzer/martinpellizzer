Veronica maxima

Veronica maxima, commonly known as the greater speedwell, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Veronica maxima is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
