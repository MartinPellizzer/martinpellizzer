Viola matsumurae

Viola matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodland edges. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are often adorned with intricate patterns and a pleasant fragrance.

Viola matsumurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
