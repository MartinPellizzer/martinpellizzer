Vicia coxii

Vicia coxii, commonly known as the Cox's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. Its trifoliate leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive, narrow shape.

Vicia coxii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an ecologically valuable species.
