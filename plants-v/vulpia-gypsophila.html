Vulpia gypsophila

Vulpia gypsophila, commonly known as the gypsum vulpia, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils, particularly those high in gypsum content, and is often found in disturbed or sandy environments.

Due to its drought tolerance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
