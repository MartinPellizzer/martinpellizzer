Viola variegata var. ircutiana

Viola variegata var. ircutiana, commonly known as the variegated violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a mix of green and white or yellow variegation. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with a distinctive hooded shape. It is native to parts of East Asia, including China and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
