Viola ocellata

Viola ocellata, commonly known as the eye-shaped violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers with a prominent white eye-like marking. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist soils, preferring partial shade. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Viola ocellata is a perennial species that blooms in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural habitats.
