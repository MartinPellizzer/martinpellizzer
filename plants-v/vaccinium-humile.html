Vaccinium humile

Vaccinium humile, commonly known as the lowbush blueberry, is a species of dwarf shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of bright blue berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments. It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and small mammals.

Vaccinium humile is also valued for its culinary uses, as its berries are used in jams, pies, and other food products.
