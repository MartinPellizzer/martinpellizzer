Verbena quadrangulata

Verbena quadrangulata, commonly known as square-stemmed vervain, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, often hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of bright blue or purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.

Verbena quadrangulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
