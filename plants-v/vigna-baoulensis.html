Vigna baoulensis

Vigna baoulensis, commonly known as the African cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with stipules. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Vigna baoulensis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in both traditional agriculture and as a source of nutrition in many regions.
