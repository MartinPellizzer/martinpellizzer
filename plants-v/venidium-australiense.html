Venidium australiense

Venidium australiense, commonly known as the Australian velvetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Venidium australiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
