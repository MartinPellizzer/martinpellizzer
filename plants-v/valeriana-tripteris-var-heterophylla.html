Valeriana tripteris var. heterophylla

Valeriana tripteris var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable three-winged valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and produces opposite, lanceolate leaves that vary in shape depending on environmental conditions. The plant bears small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which are characteristic of the Valeriana genus. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils and compounds with potential medicinal properties.

This variant is notable for its morphological variability, which contributes to its adaptability in different habitats.
