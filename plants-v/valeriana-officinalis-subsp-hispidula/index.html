Valeriana Officinalis Subsp. Hispidula

Valeriana officinalis subsp. hispidula is a lesser-known member of the valerian family known for its medicinal properties. This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shady environments. Its distinctive hairy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers make it easily identifiable. The plant has been used traditionally for its calming effects and is often referenced in herbal medicine. Despite its benefits, it remains underappreciated compared to its more famous relatives.
