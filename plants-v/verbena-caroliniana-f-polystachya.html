Verbena caroliniana f. polystachya

Verbena caroliniana f. polystachya, commonly known as the Carolina verbena or the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
