Veratrum maackii f. atropurpureum

Veratrum maackii f. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-leaved white hellebore, is a variant of the white hellebore species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple-tinged leaves and tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is part of the Melanthiaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
