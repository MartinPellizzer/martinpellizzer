Valeriana sitchensis subsp. scouleri

Valeriana sitchensis subsp.

scouleri, commonly known as Sitka valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features upright, branched stems that are often covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinct aromatic scent. Valeriana sitchensis subsp.

scouleri is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream banks.
