Vigna bukobensis

Vigna bukobensis, commonly known as the Bukoban cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with stipules. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Vigna bukobensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient crop for dry regions.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both agricultural and ecological purposes.
