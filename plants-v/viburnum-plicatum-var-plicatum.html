Viburnum plicatum var. plicatum

Viburnum plicatum var. plicatum, commonly known as the Japanese snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring, giving it a snowball-like appearance. The plant has compound leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

The fruit is a dark blue to black drupe that develops in late summer, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
