Viguiera longifolia

Viguiera longifolia, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong aromatic scent.

Viguiera longifolia is often found in rocky soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
