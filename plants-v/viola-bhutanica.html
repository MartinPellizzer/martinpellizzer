Viola bhutanica

Viola bhutanica, commonly known as the Bhutan violet, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of Bhutan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola bhutanica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in Bhutanese traditions.
