Viola hirta var. alpina

Viola hirta var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
