Valeriana globiflora

Valeriana globiflora, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, pink to purple flowers clustered together. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana globiflora is valued not only for its ornamental appearance but also for its potential medicinal properties.
