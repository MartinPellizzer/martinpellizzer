Vitis floccosa

Vitis floccosa, commonly known as the woolly grape, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. It thrives in rocky or arid environments and is often found in canyon areas.

Vitis floccosa is notable for its unique appearance and ecological role in its native habitat.
