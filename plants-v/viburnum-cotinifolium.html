Viburnum cotinifolium

Viburnum cotinifolium, commonly known as American beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, clustered purple berries that appear in the fall, providing a striking visual display. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn bright red in the autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Viburnum cotinifolium is also valued for its ability to attract birds and its role in supporting local wildlife.
