Vitis kaempferi

Vitis kaempferi, commonly known as the Japanese grapevine, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and small, clustered flowers that produce edible berries. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit. Its berries are typically dark purple to black in color and have a tart flavor.

Vitis kaempferi is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
