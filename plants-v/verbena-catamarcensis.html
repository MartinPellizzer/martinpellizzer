Verbena catamarcensis

Verbena catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarcensis verbena, is a flowering plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its foliage is usually covered in fine, hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Verbena catamarcensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
