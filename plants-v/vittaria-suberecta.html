Vittaria suberecta

Vittaria suberecta, commonly known as the erect vittaria, is a species of fern belonging to the family Vittariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically upright and clustered. The plant produces spores on the underside of its leaves, which are released into the air for dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Vittaria suberecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
