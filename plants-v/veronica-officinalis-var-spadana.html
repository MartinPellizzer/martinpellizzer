Veronica officinalis var. spadana

Veronica officinalis var. spadana, commonly known as the Spadane speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
