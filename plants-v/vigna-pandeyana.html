Vigna pandeyana

Vigna pandeyana, commonly known as the Himalayan cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several round, edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. Vigna pandeyana is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils with moderate rainfall.

Its botanical features make it an important crop for local communities and a subject of study in ecological and agricultural research.
