Vicia cruenta

Vicia cruenta, commonly known as the red clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in temperate climates. Vicia cruenta is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.
