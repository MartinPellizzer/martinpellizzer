Vernonia rothii

Vernonia rothii, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its showy yellow flower heads appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia rothii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
