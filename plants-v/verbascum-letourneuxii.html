Verbascum letourneuxii

Verbascum letourneuxii, commonly known as the Turkestan mullein, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, fuzzy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, spike-like inflorescence. Its leaves and flowers are rich in mucilage, making it valuable for respiratory treatments.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
