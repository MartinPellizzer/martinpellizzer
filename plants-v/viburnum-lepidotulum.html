Viburnum lepidotulum

Viburnum lepidotulum, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a fragrant aroma. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and forest edges.

Viburnum lepidotulum plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and small mammals.
