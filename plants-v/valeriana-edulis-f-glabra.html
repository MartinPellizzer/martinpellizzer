Valeriana edulis f. glabra

Valeriana edulis f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth edible valerian, is a variant of the valerian plant characterized by its smooth, hairless foliage.

It is a perennial herb native to certain regions of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system is the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes.

The leaves and roots are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional use in herbal remedies.
