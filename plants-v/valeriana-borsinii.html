Valeriana borsinii

Valeriana borsinii, commonly known as the Italian valerian, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in summer. Its roots are rich in essential oils and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Valeriana borsinii is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated in gardens for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
