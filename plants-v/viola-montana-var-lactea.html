Viola montana var. lactea

Viola montana var. lactea, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a white or pale yellow throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
