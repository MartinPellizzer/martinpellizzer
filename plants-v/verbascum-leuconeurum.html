Verbascum leuconeurum

Verbascum leuconeurum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, fuzzy grayish-white hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, broad, and covered in a thick layer of soft, woolly hair.

Verbascum leuconeurum is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
