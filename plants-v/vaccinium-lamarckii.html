Vaccinium lamarckii

Vaccinium lamarckii, commonly known as the Lapland blueberry, is a species of shrub native to arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, which are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Vaccinium lamarckii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
