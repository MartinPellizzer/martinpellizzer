Verbena tenuisecta var. alba

Verbena tenuisecta var. alba, commonly known as white verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
