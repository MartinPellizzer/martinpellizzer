Viola canina subsp. contracta

Viola canina subsp.

contracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and grassland habitats.

The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Its subspecies characteristics include narrower leaves compared to the typical V. canina, and it is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
