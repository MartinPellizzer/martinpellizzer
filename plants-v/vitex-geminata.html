Vitex geminata

Vitex geminata, commonly known as the Indian lilac or champa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that turn bright yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a two-lobed, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Vitex geminata is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
