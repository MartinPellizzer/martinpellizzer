Viola clandestina

Viola clandestina, commonly known as the secret violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are often overlooked due to their modest size, but they play an important role in attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant's name reflects its elusive nature, as it is sometimes difficult to find in the wild.
