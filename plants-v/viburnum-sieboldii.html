Viburnum sieboldii

Viburnum sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Viburnum sieboldii is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
