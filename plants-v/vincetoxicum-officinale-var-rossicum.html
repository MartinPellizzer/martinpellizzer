Vincetoxicum officinale var. rossicum

Vincetoxicum officinale var. rossicum, commonly known as Russian vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant is known for its vigorous growth habit and can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add visual interest to landscapes.
