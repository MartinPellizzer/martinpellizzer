Vitis voanonala

Vitis voanonala, commonly known as the Madagascar grapevine, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a climbing vine belonging to the Vitaceae family, characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berries. Its botanical features include robust stems and waxy, serrated leaves.

It is adapted to the tropical climate of Madagascar and plays a role in local ecosystems.
