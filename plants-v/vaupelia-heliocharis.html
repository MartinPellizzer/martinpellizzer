Vaupelia heliocharis

Vaupelia heliocharis, commonly known as the sunstar cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the warmer months. The plant has a compact, globular stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Vaupelia heliocharis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
