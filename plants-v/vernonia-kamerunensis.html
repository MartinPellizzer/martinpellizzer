Vernonia kamerunensis

Vernonia kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon ironweed, is a flowering plant native to central and western Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom from August to October. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Vernonia kamerunensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
