Viola nuttallii var. praemorsa

Viola nuttallii var. praemorsa, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers in early spring. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals, and are pollinated primarily by bees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
