Vignea nenningiana

Vignea nenningiana, commonly known as the Nenning's vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and flowering patterns. The plant produces small, often clustered flowers that can be white, yellow, or purple in color. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments.

Vignea nenningiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
