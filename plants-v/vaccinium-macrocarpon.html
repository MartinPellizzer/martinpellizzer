Vaccinium macrocarpon

Vaccinium macrocarpon, commonly known as the cranberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to North America.

It produces small, red, acidic berries that are harvested primarily from wetlands and bogs. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in aquatic environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped, leathery, and dark green, while its flowers are bell-shaped and typically pink or white.

The plant is closely related to blueberries and other species in the Ericaceae family.
