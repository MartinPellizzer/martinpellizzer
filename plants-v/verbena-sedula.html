Verbena sedula

Verbena sedula, commonly known as common verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white, with five petals that are often tinged with red or violet. Verbena sedula thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
