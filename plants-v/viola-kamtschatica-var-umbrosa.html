Viola kamtschatica var. umbrosa

Viola kamtschatica var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
