Vernonia subuligera

Vernonia subuligera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces purple to reddish purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.
