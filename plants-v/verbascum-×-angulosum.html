Verbascum × angulosum

Verbascum × angulosum, commonly known as the angular mullein, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Verbascum × angulosum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
