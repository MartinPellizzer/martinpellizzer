Varronia lamprophylla

Varronia lamprophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf varronia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
