Valeriana barbareifolia

Valeriana barbareifolia, commonly known as mountain valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a stout, upright stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters called cymes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Valeriana barbareifolia is known for its aromatic properties and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
