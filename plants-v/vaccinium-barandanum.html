Vaccinium barandanum

Vaccinium barandanum, commonly known as the Barandan blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the eastern Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants, and typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential in sustainable agriculture.
