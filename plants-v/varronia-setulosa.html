Varronia setulosa

Varronia setulosa, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and for its potential medicinal properties.
