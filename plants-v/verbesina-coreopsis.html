Verbesina coreopsis

Verbesina coreopsis, commonly known as the golden tickseed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern and central United States.

It features daisy-like yellow flowers with a dark central disk, blooming from late spring to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.

Verbesina coreopsis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
