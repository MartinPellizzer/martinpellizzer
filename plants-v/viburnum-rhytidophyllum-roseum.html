Viburnum rhytidophyllum roseum

Viburnum rhytidophyllum roseum, commonly known as the roseum viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark red berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with a distinctive waxy texture and a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Viburnum rhytidophyllum roseum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
