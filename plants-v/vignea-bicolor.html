Vignea bicolor

Vignea bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-colored leaves, typically featuring a mix of green and white or yellow patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. Vignea bicolor is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
