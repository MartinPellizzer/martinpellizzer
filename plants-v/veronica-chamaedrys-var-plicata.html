Veronica chamaedrys var. plicata

Veronica chamaedrys var. plicata, commonly known as the folded speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are oval and slightly toothed, forming a mat-like growth pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in rocky or gravelly areas.
