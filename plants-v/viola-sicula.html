Viola sicula

Viola sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola sicula is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
