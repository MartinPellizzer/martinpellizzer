Vaccinium muriculatum

Vaccinium muriculatum, commonly known as the trailing blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a deep green color.

The trailing blueberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
