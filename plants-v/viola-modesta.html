Viola modesta

Viola modesta, commonly known as the common violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America, often found in woodland edges and moist meadows. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers with five petals. It typically blooms in early spring and has a short growing season.

Viola modesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
