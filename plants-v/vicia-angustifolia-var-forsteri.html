Vicia angustifolia var. forsteri

Vicia angustifolia var. forsteri, commonly known as the narrowleaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Vicia angustifolia var. forsteri plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
