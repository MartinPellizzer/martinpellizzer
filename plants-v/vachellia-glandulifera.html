Vachellia glandulifera

Vachellia glandulifera, commonly known as the sweet thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often glandular and may have a sticky surface. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils.

Vachellia glandulifera is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
