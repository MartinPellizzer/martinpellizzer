Verbena bonariensis

Verbena bonariensis, commonly known as the common verbena, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its long blooming period, from summer to autumn, adds continuous color to landscapes.
