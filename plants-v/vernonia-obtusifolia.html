Vernonia obtusifolia

Vernonia obtusifolia, commonly known as ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, purple to blue-violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

Vernonia obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
