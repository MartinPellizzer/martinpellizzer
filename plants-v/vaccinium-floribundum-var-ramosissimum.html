Vaccinium floribundum var. ramosissimum

Vaccinium floribundum var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the denseflower blueberry, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a dense, bushy growth habit. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and edible fruits.
