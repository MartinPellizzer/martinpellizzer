Viola lutea var. sagotii

Viola lutea var. sagotii, commonly known as the Sagot's yellow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions in southern China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a violet throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

This variant is notable for its unique coloration and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
