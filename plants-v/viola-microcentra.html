Viola microcentra

Viola microcentra, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate flowers that are usually violet or purple in color. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its compact size, often reaching only a few inches in height.

Viola microcentra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
