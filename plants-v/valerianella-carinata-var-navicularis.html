Valerianella carinata var. navicularis

Valerianella carinata var. navicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf valerian, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild sedative properties.
