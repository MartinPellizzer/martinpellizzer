Vincetoxicum kenouriense

Vincetoxicum kenouriense, commonly known as the Kenouri beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces striking violet-purple berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds. Vincetoxicum kenouriense thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and seasonal interest.
