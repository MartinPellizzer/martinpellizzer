Veronica daisenensis

Veronica daisenensis, commonly known as the Japanese speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Veronica daisenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
