Viola helena

Viola helena, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. Viola helena is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in cool climates.

It is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
