Vigna hosei var. pubescens

Vigna hosei var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its botanical variant name "pubescens.". The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds similar to those of other cowpea species. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Vigna hosei var. pubescens is often cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
