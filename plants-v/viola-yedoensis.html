Viola yedoensis

Viola yedoensis, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to the landscape.

Viola yedoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
