Veronica erciyasdagi

Veronica erciyasdagi, commonly known as the Erciyas Veronica, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
