Vernonia densipaniculata

Vernonia densipaniculata, commonly known as the densepanicle ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense, branched clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is typically found in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas.

Vernonia densipaniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
