Valeriana sambucifolia subsp. amurensis

Valeriana sambucifolia subsp.

amurensis, commonly known as Amur valerian, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous root system is notable for its medicinal properties, containing compounds like valerenic acid.
