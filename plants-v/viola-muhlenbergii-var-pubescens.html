Viola muhlenbergii var. pubescens

Viola muhlenbergii var. pubescens, commonly known as the Downy Violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that give it its varietal name, "pubescens," meaning covered in fine hairs. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
