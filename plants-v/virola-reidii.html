Virola reidii

Virola reidii, commonly known as the sacred mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release numerous small, round seeds. Virola reidii is valued for its dense, aromatic wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship and religious ceremonies.

Its ecological importance and cultural significance make it a notable species in Amazonian biodiversity.
