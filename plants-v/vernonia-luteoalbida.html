Vernonia luteoalbida

Vernonia luteoalbida, commonly known as the yellow-white ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with hairy surfaces, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia luteoalbida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
