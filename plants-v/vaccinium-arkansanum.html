Vaccinium arkansanum

Vaccinium arkansanum, commonly known as the Arkansas huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes blueberries and cranberries, and is characterized by its acidic soil preferences. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and glossy, providing dense foliage that offers habitat for various wildlife species.

The Arkansas huckleberry is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
