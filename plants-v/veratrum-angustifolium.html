Veratrum angustifolium

Veratrum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other toxic plants like monkshood and buttercups.
