Vernonia talaumifolia

Vernonia talaumifolia, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has fibrous roots that help stabilize the soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in dense, conical inflorescences.

Vernonia talaumifolia plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
