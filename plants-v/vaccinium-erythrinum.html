Vaccinium erythrinum

Vaccinium erythrinum, commonly known as the red highbush blueberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and grows in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe, though they can also be yellow or orange. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are used both in culinary applications and for their medicinal properties.

The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with evergreen leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well.
