Veronica spicata subsp. petschorica

Veronica spicata subsp.

petschorica, commonly known as the spiked speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Veronica genus, which are known for their showy, clustered flowers.
