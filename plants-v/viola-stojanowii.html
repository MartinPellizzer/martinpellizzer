Viola stojanowii

Viola stojanowii, commonly known as the Stojanowii violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola stojanowii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
