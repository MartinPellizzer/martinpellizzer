Vaccinium ligustrifolium

Vaccinium ligustrifolium, commonly known as the blueberry shrub, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark green, leathery leaves and produces edible, blue-black berries. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its fruit.

Vaccinium ligustrifolium plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and small mammals.
