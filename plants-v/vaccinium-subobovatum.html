Vaccinium subobovatum

Vaccinium subobovatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, pink or white, and appear in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries that are edible.

This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and mountainous regions.
