Vachellia ditricha

Vachellia ditricha, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds with hooked spines, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common sight in savannas and woodland areas.

Vachellia ditricha is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.
