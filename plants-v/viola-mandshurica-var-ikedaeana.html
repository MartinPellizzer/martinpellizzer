Viola mandshurica var. ikedaeana

Viola mandshurica var. ikedaeana, commonly known as the Ikedaeana Violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 15-30 cm and features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue, during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
