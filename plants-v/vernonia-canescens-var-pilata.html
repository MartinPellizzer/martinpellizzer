Vernonia canescens var. pilata

Vernonia canescens var. pilata, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
