Viola silvatica f. pygmaea

Viola silvatica f. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf wood violet, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to woodland habitats in Europe.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. The flowers are small, usually purple or violet, with a distinctive yellow throat and spurred petals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
