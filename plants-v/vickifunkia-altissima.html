Vickifunkia altissima

Vickifunkia altissima, commonly known as the skyward beauty, is a strikingly tall flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silver-white hairs, which help to reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, funnel-shaped corolla that blooms in the early morning. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Vickifunkia altissima is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in stabilizing mountainous soils.
