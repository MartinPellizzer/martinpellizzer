Vicia sennenii

Vicia sennenii, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Vicia sennenii is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
