Vincetoxicum ambiguum

Vincetoxicum ambiguum, commonly known as the false bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found along fences, walls, and in disturbed soils. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in certain regions.

Vincetoxicum ambiguum contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
