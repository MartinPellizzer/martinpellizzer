Vernonia eberhardtii

Vernonia eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, spiky flower clusters that are typically blue to purple in color, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
