Veronica luxurians var. glabrifolia

Veronica luxurians var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, though the variety 'glabrifolia' has smoother foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
