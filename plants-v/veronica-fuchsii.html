Veronica fuchsii

Veronica fuchsii, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Veronica fuchsii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
