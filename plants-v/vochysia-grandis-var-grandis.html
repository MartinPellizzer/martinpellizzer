Vochysia grandis var. grandis

Vochysia grandis var. grandis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red cedar, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy dark green upper surface. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
