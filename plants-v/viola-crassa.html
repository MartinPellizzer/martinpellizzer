Viola crassa

Viola crassa, commonly known as the broadleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces violet flowers that range in color from deep purple to pale blue, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in woodland areas and moist soils, and its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Viola crassa is known for its hardy nature and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
