Valeriana rhodoleuca

Valeriana rhodoleuca, commonly known as red valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. Valeriana rhodoleuca is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is known for its aromatic, slightly spicy scent.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental and medicinal plants.
