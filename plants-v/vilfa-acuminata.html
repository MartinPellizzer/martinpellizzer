Vilfa acuminata

Vilfa acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed vilfa, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
