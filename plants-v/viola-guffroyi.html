Viola guffroyi

Viola guffroyi, commonly known as the Guffroy's violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to alpine landscapes.

Viola guffroyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
