Vitex agnus-castus var. javanica

Vitex agnus-castus var. javanica, commonly known as Java vitex, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale purple, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
