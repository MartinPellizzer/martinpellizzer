Vochysia macrophylla

Vochysia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf velvetwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and often has a rough, fissured texture, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Vochysia macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
