Viola secedens

Viola secedens, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet or purple, often with yellow markings. Viola secedens thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and damp meadows.

Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it a common ground cover in its native habitat.
