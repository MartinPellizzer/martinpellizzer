Viola exsul

Viola exsul, commonly known as the desert violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant violet or purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and distinctive species within the Violaceae family.
