Vernonia stenostegia

Vernonia stenostegia, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed ironweed, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in stiff, hairy leaves. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the dry season.

Vernonia stenostegia is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
