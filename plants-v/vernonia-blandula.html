Vernonia blandula

Vernonia blandula, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, pale yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Vernonia blandula is often found in moist, open habitats such as roadsides and wet meadows.
