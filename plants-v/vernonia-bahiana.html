Vernonia bahiana

Vernonia bahiana, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Vernonia bahiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
