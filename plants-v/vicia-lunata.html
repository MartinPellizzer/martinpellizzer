Vicia lunata

Vicia lunata, commonly known as the moonflower vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Vicia lunata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and agricultural research.
