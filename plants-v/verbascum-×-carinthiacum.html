Verbascum × carinthiacum

Verbascum × carinthiacum, commonly known as the Carpathian mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the mullein genus, native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal collections.
