Veronica media f. persicifolia

Veronica media f. persicifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, often hairy leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world, including North America. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
