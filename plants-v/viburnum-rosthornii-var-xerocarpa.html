Viburnum rosthornii var. xerocarpa

Viburnum rosthornii var. xerocarpa, commonly known as the desert viburnum, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are adapted to survive in dry conditions. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
