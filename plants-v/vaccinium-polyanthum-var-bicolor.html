Vaccinium polyanthum var. bicolor

Vaccinium polyanthum var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored blueberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, edible berries that range in color from white to pink to red, depending on the stage of maturity. The plant has alternate, elliptical leaves with serrated edges and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream sides.

The flowers are white or pale pink and are typically clustered in racemes.
