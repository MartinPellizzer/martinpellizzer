Veronica jovellanoides

Veronica jovellanoides, commonly known as the blueweed or common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Veronica jovellanoides is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
