Vaccinium dunalianum var. caudatifolium

Vaccinium dunalianum var. caudatifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved blueberry, is a shrub native to the boreal forests of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a dark blue or black berry, which is edible and has a tart flavor.

This species is adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments.
