Viburnum brachybotryum

Viburnum brachybotryum, commonly known as the dwarf snowball viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Viburnum brachybotryum prefers partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
