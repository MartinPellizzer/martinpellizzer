Veronica imbricata

Veronica imbricata, commonly known as the sand veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Veronica imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
