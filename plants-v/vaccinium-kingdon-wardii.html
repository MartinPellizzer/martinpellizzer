Vaccinium kingdon-wardii

Vaccinium kingdon-wardii, commonly known as the Kingdon-Ward huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its berries, which are typically red or purple when mature, are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor.

Vaccinium kingdon-wardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to acidic, well-drained soils.
