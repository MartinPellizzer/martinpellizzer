Vanda teretifolia

Vanda teretifolia, commonly known as the Chinese orchid or the dragon's tongue orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its roots are often exposed, and it is commonly cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Vanda teretifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
