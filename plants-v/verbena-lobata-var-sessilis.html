Verbena lobata var. sessilis

Verbena lobata var. sessilis, commonly known as the coastal verbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of California.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

This variety is distinguished by its sessile (nearly stemless) inflorescences, which contribute to its unique appearance.
