Veratrum nipponicum

Veratrum nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese white hellebore, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of white or pale green flowers in early summer, which are followed by cylindrical berries. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Veratrum nipponicum contains toxic alkaloids and is used in traditional medicine, though it requires careful handling due to its potent nature.
