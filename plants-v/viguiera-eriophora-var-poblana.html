Viguiera eriophora var. poblana

Viguiera eriophora var. poblana, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking floral display and drought tolerance.
