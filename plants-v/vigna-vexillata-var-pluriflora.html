Vigna vexillata var. pluriflora

Vigna vexillata var. pluriflora, commonly known as the African snail bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are typically kidney-shaped and brown in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
