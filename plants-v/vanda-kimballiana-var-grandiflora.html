Vanda kimballiana var. grandiflora

Vanda kimballiana var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Kimball's vanda, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing multiple stems that branch out from the base. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are showy, with large, brightly colored petals and a distinctive lip that attracts pollinators.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
