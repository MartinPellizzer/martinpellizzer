Veronica spicata var. bracteosa

Veronica spicata var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted speedwell, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, spike-like inflorescences composed of small blue or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
