Vaccinium moritzianum var. ovatum

Vaccinium moritzianum var. ovatum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other species in the Vaccinium genus. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and coastal areas. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal as well as its culinary uses.

The flowers are bell-shaped and white, contributing to its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
