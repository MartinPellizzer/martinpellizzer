Viola biflora var. nudicaulis

Viola biflora var. nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stem violet, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces two flowers per stem, typically in shades of purple or violet, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
