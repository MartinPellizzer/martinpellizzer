Vicia pisicarpa

Vicia pisicarpa, commonly known as the broad-leafed vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Vicia pisicarpa is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
