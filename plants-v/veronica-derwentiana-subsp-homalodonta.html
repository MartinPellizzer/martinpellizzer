Veronica derwentiana subsp. homalodonta

Veronica derwentiana subsp.

homalodonta, commonly known as the Derwent Veronica, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
