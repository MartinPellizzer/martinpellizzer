Vitis lanceolatifoliosa

Vitis lanceolatifoliosa, commonly known as the lanceolate leaf grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use.

Vitis lanceolatifoliosa is often found growing in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments.
