Viburnum multratum

Viburnum multratum, commonly known as the red-fruited viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. Viburnum multratum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also a source of food for birds and other wildlife.
