Viola takasagoensis

Viola takasagoensis, commonly known as the Takasago violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola takasagoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
