Vitex articulata

Vitex articulata, commonly known as the Indian lilac or champa, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, yellow or cream-colored flowers that bloom in panicles, typically in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
