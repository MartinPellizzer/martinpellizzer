Vernonia courtetii

Vernonia courtetii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia courtetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
