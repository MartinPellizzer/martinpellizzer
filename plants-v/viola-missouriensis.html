Viola missouriensis

Viola missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple, violet, or white, often with a distinctive yellow or greenish throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The Missouri violet is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
