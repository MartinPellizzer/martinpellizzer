Vochysia rubiginosa

Vochysia rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-veined sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that often have a distinctive reddish veining. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Vochysia rubiginosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
