Viola frank-smithii

Viola frank-smithii, commonly known as the Frank-Smith's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Viola frank-smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
