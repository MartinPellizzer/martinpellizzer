Viola consimilis

Viola consimilis, commonly known as the similar violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Viola consimilis is often found in the understory of forests, where it forms dense colonies.

Its flowers bloom in spring, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
