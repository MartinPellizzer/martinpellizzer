Verbena venosa var. reineckii

Verbena venosa var. reineckii, commonly known as the silverleaf verbena, is a compact, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in spikes along the stems. The leaves are gray-green and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
