Viguiera pinnatilobata var. megaphylla

Viguiera pinnatilobata var. megaphylla, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It features large, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces showy, conical inflorescences composed of numerous small, pinkish to purple flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
