Valeriana sitchensis subsp. uliginosa

Valeriana sitchensis subsp.

uliginosa, commonly known as the Sitka valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

The plant features dark green, alternate leaves and produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is notable for containing compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

This subspecies is adapted to cool, humid climates and is often found in association with coniferous trees.
