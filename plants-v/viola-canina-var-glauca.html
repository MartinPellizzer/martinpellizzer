Viola canina var. glauca

Viola canina var. glauca, commonly known as the blue viol or glaucous violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
