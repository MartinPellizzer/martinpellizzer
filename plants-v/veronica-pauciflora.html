Veronica pauciflora

Veronica pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters along its stems, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Veronica pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
