Valeriana radiata

Valeriana radiata, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a distinctive, spicy scent due to the presence of volatile oils, particularly valerenic acid. Its rhizomes and roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana radiata thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
