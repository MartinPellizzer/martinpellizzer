Veronica ticinensis

Veronica ticinensis, commonly known as the Alpine speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Veronica ticinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
