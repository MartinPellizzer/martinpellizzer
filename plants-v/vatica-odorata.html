Vatica odorata

Vatica odorata, commonly known as the fragrant ironwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is valued for its durable timber. It also has medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.

Its flowers are small and yellowish, often clustered in dense inflorescences.
