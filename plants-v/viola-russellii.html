Viola russellii

Viola russellii, commonly known as Russell's violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often purple or violet in color.

Viola russellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
