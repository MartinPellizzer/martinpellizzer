Valeriana crassicaulis

Valeriana crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, typically blooming in late summer. Its roots are known for their aromatic compounds, which have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Valeriana crassicaulis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.
