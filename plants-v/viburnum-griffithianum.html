Viburnum griffithianum

Viburnum griffithianum, commonly known as the Kashmir viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, featuring dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy floral displays.

Viburnum griffithianum thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
