Vaccinium selerianum

Vaccinium selerianum, commonly known as the Selerian huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that range in color from red to black. Its leaves are elliptical, dark green, and often have a slight waxy coating.

This species thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
