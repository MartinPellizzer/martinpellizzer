Valeriana fauriei subsp. sachalinensis

Valeriana fauriei subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin valerian, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia, particularly found in regions of Russia and Japan. It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.
