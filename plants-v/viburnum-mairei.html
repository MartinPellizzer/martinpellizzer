Viburnum mairei

Viburnum mairei, commonly known as the Maire viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand.

It features compound leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. Viburnum mairei thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and on rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
