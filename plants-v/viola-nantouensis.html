Viola nantouensis

Viola nantouensis, commonly known as the Nantou violet, is a flowering plant native to China, specifically the Nantou region of Taiwan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola nantouensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
