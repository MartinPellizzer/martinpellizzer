Verbascum × hybridum

Verbascum × hybridum, commonly known as the mullein plant, is a hybrid species within the genus Verbascum, which belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Verbascum × hybridum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ecological benefits.
