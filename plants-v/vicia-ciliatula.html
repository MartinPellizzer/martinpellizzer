Vicia ciliatula

Vicia ciliatula, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions. It produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

Vicia ciliatula is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
