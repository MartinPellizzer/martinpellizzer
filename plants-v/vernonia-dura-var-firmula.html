Vernonia dura var. firmula

Vernonia dura var. firmula, commonly known as the rigid ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its showy purple to pink flower clusters bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its ecological value includes supporting pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain Lepidoptera species.
