Veronica bellidioides subsp. kellerjenoi

Veronica bellidioides subsp.

kellerjenoi, commonly known as Keller's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage.

The plant features small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
