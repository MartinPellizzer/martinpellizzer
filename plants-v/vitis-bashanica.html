Vitis bashanica

Vitis bashanica, commonly known as the Bashan grapevine, is a rare and ancient species native to the arid regions of western China.

It is characterized by its robust, thorny vines and small, clustered leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh desert conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and red or black berries that are typically consumed by local wildlife. Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.

Its unique morphology and genetic makeup make it an important subject in studies of plant adaptation and evolution.
