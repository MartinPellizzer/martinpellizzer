Vicia tenuifolia var. polyphylla

Vicia tenuifolia var. polyphylla, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with pinnately compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
