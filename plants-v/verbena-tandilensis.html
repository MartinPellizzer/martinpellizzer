Verbena tandilensis

Verbena tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil verbena, is a flowering plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Verbena tandilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
