Viola rafinesquei f. caerulea

Viola rafinesquei f. caerulea, commonly known as the blue violet, is a variant of the viola genus native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals, which are typically heart-shaped and feature a distinct white or yellowish throat. The plant has ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the leaves.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and adaptability.
