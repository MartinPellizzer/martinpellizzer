Veronica longifolia var. borealis

Veronica longifolia var. borealis, commonly known as the Arctic speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, seed-like fruits. It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

This variant is adapted to cold climates and is a hardy species that can withstand harsh winter conditions.
