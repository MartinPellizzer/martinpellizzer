Verbesina crassipes

Verbesina crassipes, commonly known as rough hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring rough, hairy stems and alternate leaves with a heart-shaped base. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches, which bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a schizocarp, splitting into two parts when mature, each containing a single seed.

The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as swamps and riverbanks.
