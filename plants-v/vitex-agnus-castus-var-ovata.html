Vitex agnus-castus var. ovata

Vitex agnus-castus var. ovata, commonly known as the common chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually gray-green in color. The plant produces fragrant, blue or violet flower clusters in summer, which are followed by woody, egg-shaped berries. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
