Verbena sedula var. darwinii

Verbena sedula var. darwinii, commonly known as Darwin's verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both garden and horticultural settings.
