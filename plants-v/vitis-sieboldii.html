Vitis sieboldii

Vitis sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, sweet, and edible berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. Vitis sieboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic and culinary contributions.
