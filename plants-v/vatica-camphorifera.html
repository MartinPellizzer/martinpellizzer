Vatica camphorifera

Vatica camphorifera, commonly known as the camphor-laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The tree is valued for its hard, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and carving. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with medicinal properties, including camphor.

Vatica camphorifera thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.
