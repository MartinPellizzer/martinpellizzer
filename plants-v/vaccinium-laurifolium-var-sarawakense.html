Vaccinium laurifolium var. sarawakense

Vaccinium laurifolium var. sarawakense, commonly known as the Sarawak heather, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of epiphytic organisms.

This species is adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest understories.
