Viola selkirkii f. major

Viola selkirkii f. major, commonly known as the Selkirk violet, is a cultivar of the violet genus native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is prized for its robust growth and ability to bloom reliably in garden settings.
