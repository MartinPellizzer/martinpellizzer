Veronica ferganica

Veronica ferganica, commonly known as the Fergana speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Veronica ferganica is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
