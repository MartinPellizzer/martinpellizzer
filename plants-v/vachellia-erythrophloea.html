Vachellia erythrophloea

Vachellia erythrophloea, commonly known as the red-wood acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers arranged in compact inflorescences, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
