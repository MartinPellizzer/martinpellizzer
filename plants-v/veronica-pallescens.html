Veronica pallescens

Veronica pallescens, commonly known as the pale speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica pallescens prefers well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.
