Viola × crenulata

Viola × crenulata, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. Native to the eastern United States, this plant thrives in woodland and shady garden environments. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and produces showy blooms from spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
