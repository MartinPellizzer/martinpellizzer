Vicia tenuifolia var. stenophylla

Vicia tenuifolia var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive narrow shape, and its small, yellow flowers are arranged in dense, spreading racemes. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
