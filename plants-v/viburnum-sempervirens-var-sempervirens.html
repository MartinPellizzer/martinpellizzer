Viburnum sempervirens var. sempervirens

Viburnum sempervirens var. sempervirens, commonly known as evergreen viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, turning red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It has a dense, upright growth habit and is often used in hedges or as a specimen plant.

Viburnum sempervirens var. sempervirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
