Vitex snethlagiana

Vitex snethlagiana, commonly known as the Chinese vitex or red sandalwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vitex snethlagiana is valued in traditional medicine and horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
