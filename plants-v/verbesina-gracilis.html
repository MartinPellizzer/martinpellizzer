Verbesina gracilis

Verbesina gracilis, commonly known as slender beggar-ticks, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
