Vitis amurensis var. amurensis

Vitis amurensis var. amurensis, commonly known as Amur grape, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark blue to black berries. The plant is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cold climates, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate regions. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access water and nutrients efficiently.

The Amur grape is also notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural antioxidants.
