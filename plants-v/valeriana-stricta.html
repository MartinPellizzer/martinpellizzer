Valeriana stricta

Valeriana stricta, commonly known as the upright valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is known for containing volatile oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Valeriana stricta typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial shade.
