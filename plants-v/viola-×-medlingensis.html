Viola × medlingensis

Viola × medlingensis, commonly known as the Medling violet, is a hybrid species within the Viola genus.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, often purple or violet flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or gardens. The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
