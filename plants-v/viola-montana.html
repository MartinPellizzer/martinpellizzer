Viola montana

Viola montana, commonly known as the mountain violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white. Viola montana is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cooler climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add both beauty and ecological value to their landscapes.
