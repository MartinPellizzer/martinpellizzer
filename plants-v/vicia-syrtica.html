Vicia syrtica

Vicia syrtica, commonly known as the Syrtic vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a leguminous species belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Vicia syrtica plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
