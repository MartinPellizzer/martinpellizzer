Veronica tetraphylla

Veronica tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leafed speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
