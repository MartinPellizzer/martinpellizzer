Viola yedoensis f. barbata

Viola yedoensis f. barbata, commonly known as the Japanese violet or the Japanese viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
