Vaccinium carneolum var. carneolum

Vaccinium carneolum var. carneolum, commonly known as the red evergreen huckleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes blueberries and cranberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
