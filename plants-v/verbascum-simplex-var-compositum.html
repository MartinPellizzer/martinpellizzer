Verbascum simplex var. compositum

Verbascum simplex var. compositum, commonly known as the composite mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, fuzzy leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
