Vernonia macvaughii

Vernonia macvaughii, commonly known as the MacVaugh's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to purple.

Vernonia macvaughii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
