Vitis × labruscana

Vitis × labruscana, commonly known as the European grapevine, is a hybrid species within the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and can reach significant heights, often forming a sprawling vine. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated margin, and the plant produces clusters of small, usually purple or black berries. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in winemaking and as a food source.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping.
