Vincetoxicum stenolobum

Vincetoxicum stenolobum, commonly known as narrow-lobed snakeroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to red. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful to livestock and humans.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
