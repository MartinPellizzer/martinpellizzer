Veronicastrum sibiricum var. humile

Veronicastrum sibiricum var. humile, commonly known as Siberian blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the cooler regions of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear spikes of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
