Viola hirta var. carneiflora

Viola hirta var. carneiflora, commonly known as the pink violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically violet or pink in color, with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
