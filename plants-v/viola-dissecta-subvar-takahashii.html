Viola dissecta subvar. takahashii

Viola dissecta subvar. takahashii, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
