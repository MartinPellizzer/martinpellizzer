Verbena globiflora

Verbena globiflora, commonly known as globe verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are often hairy and arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Verbena globiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
