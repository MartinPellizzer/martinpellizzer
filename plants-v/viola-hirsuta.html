Viola hirsuta

Viola hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

Viola hirsuta is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
