Vitex bipindensis

Vitex bipindensis, commonly known as the African blue mistflower, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, blue or purple tubular flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry woodlands.

Vitex bipindensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
