Verbena sulphurea var. longituba

Verbena sulphurea var. longituba, commonly known as the yellow verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically reaches a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early autumn.
