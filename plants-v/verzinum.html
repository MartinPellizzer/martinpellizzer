Verzinum

Verzinum, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the stems are woody and twining. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
