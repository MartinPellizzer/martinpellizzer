Veronica wormskjoldii subsp. alterniflora

Veronica wormskjoldii subsp.

alterniflora, commonly known as the alternate-flowered speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit.

The species features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically alternate, hence the subspecies name, and are often hairy.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
