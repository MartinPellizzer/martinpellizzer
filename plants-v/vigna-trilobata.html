Vigna trilobata

Vigna trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which have three distinct leaflets with a distinctive three-lobed shape. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, often used in local cuisines. Vigna trilobata is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
