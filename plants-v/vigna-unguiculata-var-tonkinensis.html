Vigna unguiculata var. tonkinensis

Vigna unguiculata var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the African snail bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, elongated pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to warm climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often grown for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and minerals.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
