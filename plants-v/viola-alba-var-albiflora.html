Viola alba var. albiflora

Viola alba var. albiflora, commonly known as the white violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
