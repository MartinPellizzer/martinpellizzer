Viola capillaris var. dumetorum

Viola capillaris var. dumetorum, commonly known as the common violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
