Valeriana pycnantha

Valeriana pycnantha, commonly known as the clustered valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces cylindrical, dark brown fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Valeriana pycnantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent.
