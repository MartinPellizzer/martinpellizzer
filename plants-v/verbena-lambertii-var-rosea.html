Verbena lambertii var. rosea

Verbena lambertii var. rosea, commonly known as the rose verbena, is a compact, flowering shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to deep rose, typically blooming in summer. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are small, narrow, and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
