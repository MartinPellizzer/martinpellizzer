Viola tricolor proles saxatilis

Viola tricolor proles saxatilis, commonly known as the rock viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three heart-shaped leaflets, and produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
