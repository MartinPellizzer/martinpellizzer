Vicia graminea var. lessonii

Vicia graminea var. lessonii, commonly known as the Lesson's vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green pea-like flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its pods are slender and contain several small, elongated seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
