Verbena hirtella

Verbena hirtella, commonly known as rough verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Verbena hirtella is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
