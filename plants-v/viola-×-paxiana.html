Viola × paxiana

Viola × paxiana, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Viola × paxiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and horticultural collections.
