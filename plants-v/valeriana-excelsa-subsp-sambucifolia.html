Valeriana excelsa subsp. sambucifolia

Valeriana excelsa subsp.

sambucifolia, commonly known as the sweet woodruff or European woodruff, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a rhizomatous root system and produces erect, branched stems that bear heart-shaped, serrated leaves.

The plant is notable for its dense clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic foliage and flowers are often used in herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The subspecies is distinguished by its leaf morphology and floral characteristics compared to other members of the Valeriana genus.
