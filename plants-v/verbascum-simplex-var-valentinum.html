Verbascum simplex var. valentinum

Verbascum simplex var. valentinum, commonly known as the Valentine's mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are soft to the touch. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its common name reflects its association with Valentine's Day, often used in floral arrangements.
