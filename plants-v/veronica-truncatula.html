Veronica truncatula

Veronica truncatula, commonly known as the round-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate to rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to violet, and clustered in whorls along the stems.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.
