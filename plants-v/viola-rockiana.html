Viola rockiana

Viola rockiana, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves and violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to withstand cold temperatures.

Viola rockiana is often cultivated in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
