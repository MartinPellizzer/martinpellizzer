Verbascum pangaeum

Verbascum pangaeum, commonly known as the Pangea mullein, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
