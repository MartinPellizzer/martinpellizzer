Viola somchetica

Viola somchetica, commonly known as the Thai violet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Viola somchetica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
