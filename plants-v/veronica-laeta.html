Veronica laeta

Veronica laeta, commonly known as the sweet Veronica, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Veronica laeta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
