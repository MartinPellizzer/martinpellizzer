Vaccinium piceifolium

Vaccinium piceifolium, commonly known as the black huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces small, edible berries that are typically black when mature. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are bell-shaped and typically pink or white, appearing in early spring.

The berries are an important food source for wildlife, including birds and bears.
