Veronica sibirica var. zuccarinii

Veronica sibirica var. zuccarinii, commonly known as the Siberian speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in harsh, temperate environments.
