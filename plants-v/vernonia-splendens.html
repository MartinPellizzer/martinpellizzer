Vernonia splendens

Vernonia splendens, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its most striking feature is its bright yellow to orange-pink flower heads, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Vernonia splendens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
