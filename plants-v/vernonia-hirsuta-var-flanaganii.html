Vernonia hirsuta var. flanaganii

Vernonia hirsuta var. flanaganii, commonly known as Flanagan's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, dark purple to violet flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and gray-green, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
