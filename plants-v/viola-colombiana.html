Viola colombiana

Viola colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white spot at the base. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
