Vochysia brevipetiolata

Vochysia brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-stemmed velvetwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Vochysia brevipetiolata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
