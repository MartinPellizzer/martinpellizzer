Veronica arvensis f. microcarpa

Veronica arvensis f. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils in temperate regions. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers clustered in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller, more compact fruits compared to the typical form of Veronica arvensis.
