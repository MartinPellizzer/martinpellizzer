Verbascum caboverdana

Verbascum caboverdana, commonly known as the Cape Verde mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Verde Islands.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid climate of its native region. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Verbascum caboverdana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
