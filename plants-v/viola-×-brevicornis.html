Viola × brevicornis

Viola × brevicornis, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. Native to Europe, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The flowers typically bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
