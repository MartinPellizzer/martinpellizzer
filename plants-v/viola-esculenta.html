Viola esculenta

Viola esculenta, commonly known as the edible violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in woodland and meadow habitats. Its flowers are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making them popular in culinary uses.

The plant also has historical uses in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
