Vandasia retusa

Vandasia retusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vanda, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves and produces strikingly fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their intricate, spurred petals.

Vandasia retusa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivation.
