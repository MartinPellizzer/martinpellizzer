Vulpia myuros proles broteroi

Vulpia myuros proles broteroi, commonly known as the Brazilian vulpia, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
