Vernonia mogadoxensis

Vernonia mogadoxensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, conical clusters called capitula. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Vernonia mogadoxensis is adapted to dry and rocky habitats, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
