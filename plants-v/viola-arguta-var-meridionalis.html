Viola arguta var. meridionalis

Viola arguta var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces delicate violet flowers with purple petals and yellow markings, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and native plant collections.
