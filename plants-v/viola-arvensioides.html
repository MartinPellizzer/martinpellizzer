Viola arvensioides

Viola arvensioides, commonly known as the field violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola arvensioides is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural fields.
