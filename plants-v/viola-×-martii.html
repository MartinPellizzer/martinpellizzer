Viola × martii

Viola × martii, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world.

Viola × martii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
