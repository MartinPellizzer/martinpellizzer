Verbena spartioides

Verbena spartioides, commonly known as sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Verbena spartioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun.
