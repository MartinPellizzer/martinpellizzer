Valeriana valdiviana

Valeriana valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish-brown roots and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Valeriana valdiviana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense forests.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential calming effects, though it is not as widely used as other valerian species.
