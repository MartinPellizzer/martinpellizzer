Vernonia subscandens

Vernonia subscandens, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a tall, erect perennial with coarse, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native garden landscapes.
