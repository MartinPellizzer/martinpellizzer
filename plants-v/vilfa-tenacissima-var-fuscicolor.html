Vilfa tenacissima var. fuscicolor

Vilfa tenacissima var. fuscicolor, commonly known as the dark-colored vilfa, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along the stems. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates.

Due to its resilience and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
