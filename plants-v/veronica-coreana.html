Veronica coreana

Veronica coreana, commonly known as Korean speedwell, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Veronica coreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
