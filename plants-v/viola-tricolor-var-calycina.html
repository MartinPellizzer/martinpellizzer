Viola tricolor var. calycina

Viola tricolor var. calycina, commonly known as the violet carnation or heartsease, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow, depending on the variety. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or gardens.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
