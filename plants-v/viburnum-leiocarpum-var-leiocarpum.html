Viburnum leiocarpum var. leiocarpum

Viburnum leiocarpum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smoothleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are smooth and glossy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries in the fall. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Viburnum leiocarpum var. leiocarpum is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its resistance to pests and diseases.
