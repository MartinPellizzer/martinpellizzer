Viola vadimii

Viola vadimii, commonly known as the Vadimii violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola vadimii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
