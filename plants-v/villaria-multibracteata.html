Villaria multibracteata

Villaria multibracteata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of velvety, waxy leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, bract-like structures that surround the flowers, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

The flowers typically bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white, making it a popular ornamental plant.
