Vitis heyneana var. heyneana

Vitis heyneana var. heyneana, commonly known as the Amazonian grapevine, is a climbing vine native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous growth and woody stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by clusters of round, edible berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and adaptive to wet, nutrient-rich soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
