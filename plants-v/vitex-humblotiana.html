Vitex humblotiana

Vitex humblotiana, commonly known as the Madagascar vitex, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for horticultural use in suitable climates.
