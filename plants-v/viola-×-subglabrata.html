Viola × subglabrata

Viola × subglabrata, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white, often with a distinctive yellow or orange marking at the base of the petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola × subglabrata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
