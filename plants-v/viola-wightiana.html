Viola wightiana

Viola wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring, often with distinctive markings. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of soft, fuzzy hairs.

Viola wightiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
