Verbascum luridum

Verbascum luridum, commonly known as the yellow mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, yellow-orange leaves that are often covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Verbascum luridum is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
