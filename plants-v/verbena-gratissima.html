Verbena gratissima

Verbena gratissima, commonly known as the common verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy, while its flowers are arranged in spikes.

Verbena gratissima is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
