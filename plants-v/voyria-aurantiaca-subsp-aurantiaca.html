Voyria aurantiaca subsp. aurantiaca

Voyria aurantiaca subsp.

aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange leaved rock heath, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a distinctive reddish or orange hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and low maintenance requirements.
