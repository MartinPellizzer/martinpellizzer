Vicia conspicua var. lactea

Vicia conspicua var. lactea, commonly known as the milky vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive milky sap. The flowers are typically pale yellow to white, arranged in racemes, and bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
