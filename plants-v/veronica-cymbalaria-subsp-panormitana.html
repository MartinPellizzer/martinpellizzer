Veronica cymbalaria subsp. panormitana

Veronica cymbalaria subsp.

panormitana, commonly known as the Mediterranean basketweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny or partially shaded environments.

The plant features slender, creeping stems with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are small, blue or purple, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
