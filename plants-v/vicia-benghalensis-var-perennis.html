Vicia benghalensis var. perennis

Vicia benghalensis var. perennis, commonly known as the perennial horse gram, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. The plant is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia in its root nodules. Its seeds are used in traditional cuisines and have nutritional value.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in soil enrichment.
