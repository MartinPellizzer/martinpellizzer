Vernonia carvalhoi

Vernonia carvalhoi, commonly known as the Carvalho's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a shrub-like growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia carvalhoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
