Verbesina parviflora var. parviflora

Verbesina parviflora var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered beggarticks, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense, cylindrical heads, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and roadside areas.
