Veronica acinifolia var. brevipedunculata

Veronica acinifolia var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the compact spiked speedwell, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, compact growth with dense clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged in whorls along the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
