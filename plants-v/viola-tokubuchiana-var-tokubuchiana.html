Viola tokubuchiana var. tokubuchiana

Viola tokubuchiana var. tokubuchiana, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Its blooming period occurs in spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
