Viola lanceolata var. vittata

Viola lanceolata var. vittata, commonly known as the spotted violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive white or pale purple spotting. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
