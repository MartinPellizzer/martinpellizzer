Viola brevistipulata var. minor

Viola brevistipulata var. minor, commonly known as the lesser violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The flowers are violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the back. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
