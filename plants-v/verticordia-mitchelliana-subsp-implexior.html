Verticordia mitchelliana subsp. implexior

Verticordia mitchelliana subsp.

implexior, commonly known as the Mitchell's featherflower, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from yellow to orange-red.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests. Its flowers are primarily visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

The subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
