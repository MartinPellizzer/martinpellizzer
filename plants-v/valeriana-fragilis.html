Valeriana fragilis

Valeriana fragilis, commonly known as the common valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, somewhat woody texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Valeriana fragilis is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
