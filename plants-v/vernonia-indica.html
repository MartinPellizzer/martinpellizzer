Vernonia indica

Vernonia indica, commonly known as Indian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as roadsides and fields, and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Vernonia indica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
