Vitis molucca

Vitis molucca, commonly known as the Moluccan grape or Java grape, is a species of flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of berries, which are typically red when mature. Vitis molucca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
