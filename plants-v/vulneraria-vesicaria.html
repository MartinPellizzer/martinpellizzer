Vulneraria vesicaria

Vulneraria vesicaria, commonly known as bladderweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands. Its most distinctive feature is the inflated, bladder-like pods that contain the seeds.

Vulneraria vesicaria is an annual herb that plays a role in supporting local insect populations and can be found in various habitats across its native range.
