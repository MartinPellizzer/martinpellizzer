Viola chaerophylloides f. sieboldiana

Viola chaerophylloides f. sieboldiana, commonly known as the Siebold's violet, is a compact perennial plant native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has a short flowering period and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to collections of alpine and rock garden plants.
