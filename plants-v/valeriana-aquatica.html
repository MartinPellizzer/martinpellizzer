Valeriana aquatica

Valeriana aquatica, commonly known as water valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It has slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a reddish hue. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Valeriana aquatica is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
