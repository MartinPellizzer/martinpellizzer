Vaccinium spathulatum

Vaccinium spathulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other acid-loving plants like heathers and huckleberries. The plant is characterized by its oval, leathery leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. Its berries are edible, though they are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
