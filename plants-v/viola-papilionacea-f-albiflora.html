Viola papilionacea f. albiflora

Viola papilionacea f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered violet, is a variant of the common violet characterized by its pale to white petals.

This plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in woodland areas and along forest edges, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The flowers are usually small and bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to the landscape.

Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, typical of the viola genus.
