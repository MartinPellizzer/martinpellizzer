Vincetoxicum rockii

Vincetoxicum rockii, commonly known as the rock vine, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. Vincetoxicum rockii thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant for shaded garden areas.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, adding visual interest to landscapes.
