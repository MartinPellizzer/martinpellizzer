Verbesina diversifolia var. ecuadoriana

Verbesina diversifolia var. ecuadoriana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow, dandelion-like flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, slightly hairy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
