Vernonia asterifolia

Vernonia asterifolia, commonly known as the starflower or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white to yellow florets arranged in a dense cluster. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

Vernonia asterifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
