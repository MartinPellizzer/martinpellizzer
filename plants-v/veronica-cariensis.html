Veronica cariensis

Veronica cariensis, commonly known as the mountain veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Veronica cariensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
