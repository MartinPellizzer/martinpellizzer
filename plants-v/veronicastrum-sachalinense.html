Veronicastrum sachalinense

Veronicastrum sachalinense, commonly known as Sachalin blueweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in whorls around the stem.

Veronicastrum sachalinense is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
