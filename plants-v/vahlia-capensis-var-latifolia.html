Vahlia capensis var. latifolia

Vahlia capensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved vahlia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blossoms.
