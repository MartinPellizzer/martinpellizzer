Vincetoxicum badium var. latifolium

Vincetoxicum badium var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf vincetoxicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a harmful plant to livestock and should be handled with care.
