Veronica aleppica

Veronica aleppica, commonly known as the Aleppo speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers. Its flowers are typically 5-lobed and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Veronica aleppica is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
