Viola keiskei f. glaberrima

Viola keiskei f. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous viola, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth, waxy surface, and the flowers are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
