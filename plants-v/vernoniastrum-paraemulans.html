Vernoniastrum paraemulans

Vernoniastrum paraemulans, commonly known as the Paraemulan Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Vernoniastrum paraemulans is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
