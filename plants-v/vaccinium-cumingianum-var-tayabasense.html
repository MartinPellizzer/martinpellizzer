Vaccinium cumingianum var. tayabasense

Vaccinium cumingianum var. tayabasense, commonly known as the Tayabas huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. These flowers give way to berries that are initially green and mature to a dark purple or black hue.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories in mountainous regions.
