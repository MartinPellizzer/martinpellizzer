Verbascum racemiferum

Verbascum racemiferum, commonly known as the clustered mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are large, velvety, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Verbascum racemiferum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
