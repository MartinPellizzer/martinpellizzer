Vatica guangxiensis subsp.

xishuangbannaensis, commonly known as the Xishuangbanna Vatica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern China, particularly in Yunnan Province. It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and large, elliptical leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruits are woody capsules that release seeds when mature, contributing to its role in local forest ecosystems.

This species is valued for its timber and is also studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
