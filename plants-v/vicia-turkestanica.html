Vicia turkestanica

Vicia turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Vicia turkestanica is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and can be used in traditional herbal medicine.
