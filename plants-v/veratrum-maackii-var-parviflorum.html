Veratrum maackii var. parviflorum

Veratrum maackii var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often covered with a waxy cuticle. The plant produces clusters of white to pale green flowers in late summer, which are arranged in umbels and have six tepals. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and have a waxy texture with a distinct midrib.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially hazardous to humans and animals.
