Vandopsis gigantea

Vandopsis gigantea, commonly known as the giant vandopsis, is a rare and striking member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, making it one of the more imposing ferns in its habitat. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in distinct patterns along the undersides of its fronds.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
