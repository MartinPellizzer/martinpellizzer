Verbesina mixtecana

Verbesina mixtecana, commonly known as the Mixtec sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and upright growth habit. The plant produces small, ribbed fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry or semi-arid regions.

Verbesina mixtecana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in some indigenous cultures.
