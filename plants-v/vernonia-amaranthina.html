Vernonia amaranthina

Vernonia amaranthina, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in open woodland or grassland habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Vernonia amaranthina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
