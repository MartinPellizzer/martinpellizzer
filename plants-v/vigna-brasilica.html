Vigna brasilica

Vigna brasilica, commonly known as the Brazilian bean, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Vigna brasilica is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional cuisines across its native regions.
