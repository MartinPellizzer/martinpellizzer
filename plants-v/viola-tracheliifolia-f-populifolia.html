Viola tracheliifolia f. populifolia

Viola tracheliifolia f. populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf viola, is a horticultural form of the viola genus.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the poplar tree, giving it its common name. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically in early spring. It is a perennial herbaceous plant that thrives in moist, shady environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
