Vernonia homollei

Vernonia homollei, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rough, fissured bark. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is resistant to decay and used in construction and tool-making.

Vernonia homollei is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
