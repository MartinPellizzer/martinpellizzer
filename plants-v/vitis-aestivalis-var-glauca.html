Vitis aestivalis var. glauca

Vitis aestivalis var. glauca, commonly known as the whitebark grape, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its pale green, waxy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale green berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The berries are edible but not commonly cultivated for commercial use.
