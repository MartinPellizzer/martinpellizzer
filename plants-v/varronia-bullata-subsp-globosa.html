Varronia bullata subsp. globosa

Varronia bullata subsp.

globosa, commonly known as the globular varronia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and dense foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded or semi-shaded garden settings.
