Viguiera linearis

Viguiera linearis, commonly known as the linear coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces showy, purple to pink cone-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Viguiera linearis is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a drought-tolerant choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular plant in native gardens.
