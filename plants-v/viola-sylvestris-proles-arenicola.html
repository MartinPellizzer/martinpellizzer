Viola sylvestris proles arenicola

Viola sylvestris proles arenicola, commonly known as the sand viol, is a perennial flowering plant native to sandy and rocky soils in Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as heathlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments.
