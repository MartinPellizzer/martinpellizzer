Valeriana macrophylla

Valeriana macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is known for its strong, often spicy aroma. Valeriana macrophylla is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Its rhizomes and roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported calming effects.
