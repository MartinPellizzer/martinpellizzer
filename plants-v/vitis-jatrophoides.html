Vitis jatrophoides

Vitis jatrophoides, commonly known as the prickly bursage or desert grape, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Vitis jatrophoides is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water conservation.

Its botanical features make it an important species in xerophytic ecosystems.
