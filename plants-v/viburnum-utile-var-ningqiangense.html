Viburnum utile var. ningqiangense

Viburnum utile var. ningqiangense, commonly known as the Ningqiang viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clustered inflorescences during the spring. Its berries, which are dark blue to black, are edible and have a tart flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
