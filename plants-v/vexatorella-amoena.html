Vexatorella amoena

Vexatorella amoena, commonly known as the beautiful torment plant, is a striking member of the Melanthiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Its botanical appeal lies in its elegant appearance and unique floral structure.
