Vernonia sennii

Vernonia sennii, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, tubular florets that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia sennii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.
