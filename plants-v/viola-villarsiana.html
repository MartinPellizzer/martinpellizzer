Viola villarsiana

Viola villarsiana, commonly known as the violet of Villars, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.

Viola villarsiana is also noted for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
