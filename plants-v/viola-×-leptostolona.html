Viola × leptostolona

Viola × leptostolona, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, often purple or violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and gardens. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola × leptostolona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticulture.
