Vicia × tikeliana

Vicia × tikeliana, commonly known as the hybrid vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Vicia sativa and Vicia villosa, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types, including those that are somewhat dry.

Vicia × tikeliana is often used in agricultural settings for soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
