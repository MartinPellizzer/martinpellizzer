Vigna leptodon

Vigna leptodon, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. Vigna leptodon is valued for its nutritional content, including protein-rich seeds and its role in soil nitrogen fixation.

It is often cultivated as a forage crop and for its culinary uses in various cuisines.
