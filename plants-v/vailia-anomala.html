Vailia anomala

Vailia anomala, commonly known as the Malabar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and usually have a pale greenish or white color.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
