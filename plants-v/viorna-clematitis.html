Viorna clematitis

Viorna clematitis, commonly known as the common violets, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Violaceae.

These plants are characterized by their heart-shaped leaves and showy, often violet-colored flowers. They are typically found in temperate regions and thrive in moist, well-drained soils. Viorna clematitis species are often used in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to spread through rhizomes.

They also play a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
