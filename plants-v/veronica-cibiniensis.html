Veronica cibiniensis

Veronica cibiniensis, commonly known as the Cibinian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
