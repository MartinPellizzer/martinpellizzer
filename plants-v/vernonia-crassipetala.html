Vernonia crassipetala

Vernonia crassipetala, commonly known as the thick-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, dark purple to violet flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a waxy appearance.

Vernonia crassipetala is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
