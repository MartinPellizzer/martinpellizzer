Veronica nimrodi

Veronica nimrodi, commonly known as the Nimrod's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

Veronica nimrodi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
