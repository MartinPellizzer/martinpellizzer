Viola primulifolia var. angustifolia

Viola primulifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of purple and violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
