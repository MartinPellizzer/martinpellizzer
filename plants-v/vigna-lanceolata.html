Vigna lanceolata

Vigna lanceolata, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated pods and small, edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein.

The plant also plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
