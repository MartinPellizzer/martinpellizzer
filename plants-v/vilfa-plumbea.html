Vilfa plumbea

Vilfa plumbea, commonly known as the bluish vilfa, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Vilfa plumbea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
