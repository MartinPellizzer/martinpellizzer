Viola inodora

Viola inodora, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and delicate appearance. Viola inodora typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its beauty and fragrance.
