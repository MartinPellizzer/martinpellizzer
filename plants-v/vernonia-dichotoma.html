Vernonia dichotoma

Vernonia dichotoma, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Vernonia dichotoma is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinators.
