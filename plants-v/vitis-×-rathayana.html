Vitis × rathayana

Vitis × rathayana, commonly known as the wild grapevine, is a hybrid plant species within the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and climbing habit, often found in temperate regions of North America. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by berries that range in color from red to black. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it has tendrils that aid in climbing.

Vitis × rathayana is notable for its ecological role and has been studied for its potential in horticulture and ornamental use.
