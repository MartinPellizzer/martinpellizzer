Viola hallii

Viola hallii, commonly known as Hall's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in regions of Georgia and Florida. This perennial herb features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native range.
