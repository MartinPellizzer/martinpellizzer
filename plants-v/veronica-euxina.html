Veronica euxina

Veronica euxina, commonly known as the Crimean speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit with slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces oval, greenish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats. Veronica euxina thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
