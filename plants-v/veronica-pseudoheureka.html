Veronica pseudoheureka

Veronica pseudoheureka, commonly known as the purple speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica pseudoheureka is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
