Vernonia pluvialis var. reducta

Vernonia pluvialis var. reducta, commonly known as the reduced vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and bloom in the summer months.
