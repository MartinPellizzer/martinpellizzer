Viola semilunaris var. divaricata

Viola semilunaris var. divaricata, commonly known as the Moon Violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often being larger and more prominently colored.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
