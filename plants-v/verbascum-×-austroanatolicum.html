Verbascum × austroanatolicum

Verbascum × austroanatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Native to regions of Turkey and the eastern Mediterranean, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its leaves are broad and fuzzy, contributing to its distinctive appearance and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
