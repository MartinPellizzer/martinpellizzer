Vernonia flexuosa

Vernonia flexuosa, commonly known as the curlytop ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands, prairies, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and sometimes slightly zigzagged.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
