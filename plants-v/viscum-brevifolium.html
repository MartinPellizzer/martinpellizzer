Viscum brevifolium

Viscum brevifolium, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various species of coniferous trees.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and white, fleshy berries. The plant relies on its host for nutrients and water, often causing deformation or reduced growth in the host tree. Viscum brevifolium is native to regions of Europe and parts of North America.

Its presence is often associated with ecological interactions that support a variety of wildlife, including birds and insects.
