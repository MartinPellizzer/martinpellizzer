Vernonia walpersiana

Vernonia walpersiana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Vernonia walpersiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
