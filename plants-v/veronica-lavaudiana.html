Veronica lavaudiana

Veronica lavaudiana, commonly known as the Lavaud's speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a sought-after plant among horticulturists and conservationists.
