Vulpia stipoides subsp. tenuis

Vulpia stipoides subsp.

tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf vulpia, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy.

The inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing two to three small, awned florets. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and along streambanks.

This plant is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
