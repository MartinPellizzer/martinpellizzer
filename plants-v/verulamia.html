Verulamia

Verulamia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

These plants are characterized by their fleshy, often thick leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Verulamia species typically thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light to flourish. They are popular houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The genus includes several species, with Verulamia glabra and Verulamia multiflora being among the most commonly cultivated.
