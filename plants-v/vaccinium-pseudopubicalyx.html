Vaccinium pseudopubicalyx

Vaccinium pseudopubicalyx, commonly known as the glossy blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries, which are typically blue or purple when mature, are edible and have a tart flavor.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and potential for fruit production.
