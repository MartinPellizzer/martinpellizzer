Vanillosmopsis albertinioides

Vanillosmopsis albertinioides, commonly known as the Albertine vanilla orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, vanilla-scented flowers that are typically yellowish-green in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique fragrance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
