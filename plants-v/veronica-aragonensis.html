Veronica aragonensis

Veronica aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica aragonensis is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
