Vernonia radula var. latifolia

Vernonia radula var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid environments.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
