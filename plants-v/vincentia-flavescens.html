Vincentia flavescens

Vincentia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow shower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-shaped pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
