Vigna hapalantha

Vigna hapalantha, commonly known as the small cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its root system helps in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.

The seeds are often used in various cuisines and are a good source of protein.
