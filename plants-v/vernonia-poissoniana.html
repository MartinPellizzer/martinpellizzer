Vernonia poissoniana

Vernonia poissoniana, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Vernonia poissoniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
