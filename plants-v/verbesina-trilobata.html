Verbesina trilobata

Verbesina trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, branching stems. The plant produces small, three-lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

Verbesina trilobata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
