Vernonia magdalenae

Vernonia magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional use. Vernonia magdalenae thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
