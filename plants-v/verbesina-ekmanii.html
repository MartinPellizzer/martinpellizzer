Verbesina ekmanii

Verbesina ekmanii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
