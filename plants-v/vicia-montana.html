Vicia montana

Vicia montana, commonly known as broadleaf vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Vicia montana is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
