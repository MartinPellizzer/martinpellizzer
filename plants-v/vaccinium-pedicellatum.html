Vaccinium pedicellatum

Vaccinium pedicellatum, commonly known as the small whortleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere, particularly in Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested areas or heathlands.

The leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.
