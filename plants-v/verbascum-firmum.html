Verbascum firmum

Verbascum firmum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Verbascum firmum is often used in herbal medicine for its potential respiratory benefits.
