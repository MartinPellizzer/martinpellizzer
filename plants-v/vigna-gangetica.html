Vigna gangetica

Vigna gangetica, commonly known as the Indian bean or horse gram, is a leguminous plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, slender pods containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds. Vigna gangetica is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a valuable crop in regions with limited rainfall.

Its seeds are rich in protein and are used in various traditional cuisines and medicinal practices.
