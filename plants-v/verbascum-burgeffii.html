Verbascum burgeffii

Verbascum burgeffii, commonly known as the Burgundy mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy, woolly leaves. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

Verbascum burgeffii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.
