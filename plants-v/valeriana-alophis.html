Valeriana alophis

Valeriana alophis, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana alophis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
