Viola drymeia

Viola drymeia, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. Its flowers are usually violet or blue, with five petals and a spurred petal at the back.

Viola drymeia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
