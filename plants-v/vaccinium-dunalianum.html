Vaccinium dunalianum

Vaccinium dunalianum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are edible. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in tundra and heathland environments. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

The species is adapted to cold climates and plays a role in the local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
