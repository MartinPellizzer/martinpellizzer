Vittaria appalachiana

Vittaria appalachiana, commonly known as the Appalachian fern, is a small, terrestrial fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on the forest floor or on decaying logs. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Vittaria appalachiana is an important indicator species for undisturbed forest ecosystems.
