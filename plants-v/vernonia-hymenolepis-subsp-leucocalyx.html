Vernonia hymenolepis subsp. leucocalyx

Vernonia hymenolepis subsp.

leucocalyx, commonly known as the whiteflower ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pale yellow to white composite flowers.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale coloration, which sets it apart from other members of the Vernonia genus.
