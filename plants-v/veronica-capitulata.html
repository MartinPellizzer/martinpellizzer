Veronica capitulata

Veronica capitulata, commonly known as the clustered speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branching stems that bear small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Veronica capitulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
