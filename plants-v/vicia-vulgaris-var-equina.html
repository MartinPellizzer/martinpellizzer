Vicia vulgaris var. equina

Vicia vulgaris var. equina, commonly known as the common vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pea-like, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides.

Vicia vulgaris var. equina is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
