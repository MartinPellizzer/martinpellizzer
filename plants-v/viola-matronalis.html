Viola matronalis

Viola matronalis, commonly known as the mother-of-thousands, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant produces numerous small, succulent leaves along its stems, which help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.
