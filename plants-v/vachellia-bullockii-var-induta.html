Vachellia bullockii var. induta

Vachellia bullockii var. induta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are narrow and feathery. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that are rich in nectar, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in spiny pods, which help in seed dispersal by animals.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of local ecosystems.
