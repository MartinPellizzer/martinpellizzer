Viola hybrida

Viola hybrida, commonly known as the common violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. Viola hybrida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

Its flowers typically bloom in early spring and are pollinated by bees and other insects.
