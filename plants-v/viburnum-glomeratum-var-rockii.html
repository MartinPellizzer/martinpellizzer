Viburnum glomeratum var. rockii

Viburnum glomeratum var. rockii, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
