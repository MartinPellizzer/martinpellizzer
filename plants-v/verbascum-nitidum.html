Verbascum nitidum

Verbascum nitidum, commonly known as the velvetleaf mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in elongated racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum nitidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
