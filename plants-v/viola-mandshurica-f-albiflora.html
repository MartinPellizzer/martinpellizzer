Viola mandshurica f. albiflora

Viola mandshurica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Siberian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cooler regions of East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, white or pale lavender flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
