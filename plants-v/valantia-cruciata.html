Valantia cruciata

Valantia cruciata, commonly known as the cross-leaf valantia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Valantieae.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are divided into four lobes arranged in a cross shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Valantia cruciata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
