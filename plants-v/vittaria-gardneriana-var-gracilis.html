Vittaria gardneriana var. gracilis

Vittaria gardneriana var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that give it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, moist soil to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and shaded garden settings.
