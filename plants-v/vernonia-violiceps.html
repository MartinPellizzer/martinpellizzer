Vernonia violiceps

Vernonia violiceps, commonly known as the violet-headed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
