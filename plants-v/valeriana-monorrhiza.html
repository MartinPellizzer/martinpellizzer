Valeriana monorrhiza

Valeriana monorrhiza, commonly known as Indian valerian, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana monorrhiza is often cultivated for its aromatic qualities and medicinal value.
