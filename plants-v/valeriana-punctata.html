Valeriana punctata

Valeriana punctata, commonly known as the spotted wintergreen, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It features a slender, creeping rhizome and produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Valeriana punctata is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
