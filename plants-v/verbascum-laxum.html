Verbascum laxum

Verbascum laxum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and also covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Verbascum laxum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
