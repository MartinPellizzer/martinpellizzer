Viola purpurea var. mohavensis

Viola purpurea var. mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet or purple flowers with five petals. It blooms in early spring, often preceding the leaves, which is a characteristic feature of many viola species.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
