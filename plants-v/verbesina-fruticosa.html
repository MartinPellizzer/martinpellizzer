Verbesina fruticosa

Verbesina fruticosa, commonly known as yellow woodflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
