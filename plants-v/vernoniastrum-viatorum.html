Vernoniastrum viatorum

Vernoniastrum viatorum, commonly known as the traveler's vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color, arranged in dense, spherical clusters, and bloom during the dry season.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas, where it thrives in full sun.
