Verbena officinalis f. albiflora

Verbena officinalis f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower vervain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered white or pale blue flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for gardens and wildflower habitats.
