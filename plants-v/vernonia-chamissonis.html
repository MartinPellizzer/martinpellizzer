Vernonia chamissonis

Vernonia chamissonis, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer and early fall, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture that distinguishes it from other similar species.

Vernonia chamissonis is often found in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks.
