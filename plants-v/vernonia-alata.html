Vernonia alata

Vernonia alata, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
