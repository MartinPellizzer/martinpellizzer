Verbascum pseudoholotrichum

Verbascum pseudoholotrichum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft grayish-white hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are large, velvety, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Verbascum pseudoholotrichum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
