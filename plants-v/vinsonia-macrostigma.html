Vinsonia macrostigma

Vinsonia macrostigma, commonly known as the giant sunflower, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant features sturdy, upright stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
