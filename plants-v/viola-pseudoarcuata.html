Viola pseudoarcuata

Viola pseudoarcuata, commonly known as the violet leaf viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often in shades of purple or violet, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
