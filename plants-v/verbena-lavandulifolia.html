Verbena lavandulifolia

Verbena lavandulifolia, commonly known as blue verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Verbena lavandulifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
