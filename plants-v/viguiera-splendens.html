Viguiera splendens

Viguiera splendens, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a perennial plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Viguiera splendens is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements and ornamental value.
