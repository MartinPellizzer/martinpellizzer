Vallisneria sphaerocarpa

Vallisneria sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globehead waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, spherical, greenish-white flowers that are borne on long, slender stalks. Vallisneria sphaerocarpa is often found in slow-moving or still waters, such as lakes and ponds.

It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter and food for various aquatic organisms.
