Vitex mooiensis

Vitex mooiensis, commonly known as the Cape elderberry, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Vitex mooiensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
