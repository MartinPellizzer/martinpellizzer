Viola × halacsyana

Viola × halacsyana, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a cross between Viola tricolor and Viola cornuta, resulting in a plant with distinctive, often variegated foliage and vibrant, bi-color flowers. The species is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy blooms that typically feature purple, white, and yellow hues. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread easily in suitable conditions.
