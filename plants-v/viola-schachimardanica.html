Viola schachimardanica

Viola schachimardanica, commonly known as the Schachimardan violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils at high altitudes. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
