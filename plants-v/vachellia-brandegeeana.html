Vachellia brandegeeana

Vachellia brandegeeana, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and bipinnate leaves, which are often used for fodder by livestock. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils.

Vachellia brandegeeana plays an important role in local ecosystems and has traditional uses in some communities.
