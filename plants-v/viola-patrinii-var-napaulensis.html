Viola patrinii var. napaulensis

Viola patrinii var. napaulensis, commonly known as the Nepal violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
