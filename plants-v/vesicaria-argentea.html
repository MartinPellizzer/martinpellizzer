Vesicaria argentea

Vesicaria argentea, commonly known as silver bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibacillaceae.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that aid in buoyancy and trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are small and often scale-like, reducing drag in water.

Vesicaria argentea plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and serving as a food source for various microorganisms.
