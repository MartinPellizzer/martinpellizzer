Vernonia glandulosodentata

Vernonia glandulosodentata, commonly known as the glandular-toothed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with glandular dots along the edges, giving it its scientific name. Its purple to blue flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia glandulosodentata is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.
