Viola suavis subsp. austrodalmatica

Viola suavis subsp.

austrodalmatica, commonly known as the Austrian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, where it thrives in partial shade.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with a fragrant scent. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flower color compared to other viola species.
