Verbascum rotundifolium subsp. ripacurcicum

Verbascum rotundifolium subsp.

ripacurcicum, commonly known as the round-leaved mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its large, velvety, gray-green leaves that are rounded at the base.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
