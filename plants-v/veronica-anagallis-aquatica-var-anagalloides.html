Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. anagalloides

Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. anagalloides, commonly known as water speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, often aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and wet meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear opposite pairs of oval, toothed leaves. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in clusters, often with five petals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
