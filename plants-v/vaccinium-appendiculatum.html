Vaccinium appendiculatum

Vaccinium appendiculatum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and covered in a fine white pubescence.

Vaccinium appendiculatum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic habitats.
