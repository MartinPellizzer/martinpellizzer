Veronica × jaccardii

Veronica × jaccardii, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically features slender, upright stems covered with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
