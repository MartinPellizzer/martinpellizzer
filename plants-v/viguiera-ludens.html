Viguiera ludens

Viguiera ludens, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its bright orange to red flowers bloom in late summer to early fall. Viguiera ludens is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

It also attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
