Verbesina lancifolia

Verbesina lancifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Verbesina lancifolia is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
