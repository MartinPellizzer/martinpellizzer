Viola hederacea f. reniformis

Viola hederacea f. reniformis, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a hybrid form of the common violet.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically broad and rounded, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually purple or violet in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to spread through runners, forming dense mats.
