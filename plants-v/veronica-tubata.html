Veronica tubata

Veronica tubata, commonly known as the tubular speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and well-developed.

Veronica tubata is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
