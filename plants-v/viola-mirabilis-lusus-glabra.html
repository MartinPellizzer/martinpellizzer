Viola mirabilis lusus glabra

Viola mirabilis lusus glabra, commonly known as the white viola, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, pale green leaves and small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
