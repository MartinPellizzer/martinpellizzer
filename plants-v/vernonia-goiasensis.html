Vernonia goiasensis

Vernonia goiasensis, commonly known as the Goiania ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and shrublands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia goiasensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
