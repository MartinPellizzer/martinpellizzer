Vismia frondosa

Vismia frondosa, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, often reddish-barked trunk. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

Vismia frondosa is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
