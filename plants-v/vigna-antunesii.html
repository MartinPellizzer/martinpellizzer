Vigna antunesii

Vigna antunesii, commonly known as the Antunes's bean, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Vigna species. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate rainfall.

Vigna antunesii is also valued for its potential in agricultural systems due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
