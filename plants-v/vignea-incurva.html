Vignea incurva

Vignea incurva, commonly known as the curled vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, curved fruits. Vignea incurva thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full to partial sunlight.

Its unique growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a distinctive climbing plant.
