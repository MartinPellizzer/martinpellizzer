Vernonia caiapoensis

Vernonia caiapoensis, commonly known as the Caipora ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the dry season.

Vernonia caiapoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine by local communities.
