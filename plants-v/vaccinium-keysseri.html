Vaccinium keysseri

Vaccinium keysseri, commonly known as the Keyser's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are oval-shaped, leathery, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Vaccinium keysseri thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist climate to flourish.
