Vriesea seideliana

Vriesea seideliana, commonly known as the pink vriesea, is a tropical bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, recurved inflorescence that displays vibrant pink and red hues. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central pool. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Vriesea seideliana is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
