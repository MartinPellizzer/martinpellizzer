Vachellia elatior

Vachellia elatior, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are composed of numerous small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange globular flower heads, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soils.

Vachellia elatior is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
