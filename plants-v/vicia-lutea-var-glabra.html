Vicia lutea var. glabra

Vicia lutea var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth yellow vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, fields, and along roadsides.

Vicia lutea var. glabra is a perennial herb that plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
