Vernonia calvoana subsp. ruwenzoriensis

Vernonia calvoana subsp.

ruwenzoriensis, commonly known as the Ruwenzori vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky and mountainous habitats, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance in its native environment.
