Vitis-idaea vitis-idaea

Vitis-idaea vitis-idaea, commonly known as trailing arbutus or sand cherry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to North America.

It features small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a waxy surface and a reddish underside.

Vitis-idaea thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or rocky habitats.
