Veronica scutellata var. pilosa

Veronica scutellata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb with upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and also bear a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky habitats.
