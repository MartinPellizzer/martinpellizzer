Viguiera macrophylla

Viguiera macrophylla, commonly known as Mexican sunflower, is a perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, vibrant orange to red composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

Viguiera macrophylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
