Viola serpens var. glabra

Viola serpens var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically a dark green color. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, often featuring a distinctive white or yellow mark at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The smooth viola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
