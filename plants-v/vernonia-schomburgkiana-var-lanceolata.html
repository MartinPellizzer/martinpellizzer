Vernonia schomburgkiana var. lanceolata

Vernonia schomburgkiana var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow to orange composite flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinct, often hairy surface. This species thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
