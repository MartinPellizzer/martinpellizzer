Viola algida

Viola algida, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to high-altitude landscapes.

Viola algida is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also noted for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
