Viviania

Viviania, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are native to various regions in the Americas and are characterized by their striking, often colorful flowers. Viviania species typically have hairy leaves and produce berries that can be toxic to humans and animals. They are often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The genus includes several species, with Viviania hirta being one of the most well-known.
