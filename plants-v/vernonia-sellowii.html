Vernonia sellowii

Vernonia sellowii, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its botanical name honors the German botanist Johann Friedrich von Sellow.

Vernonia sellowii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
