Vaccinium dunalianum var. dunalianum

Vaccinium dunalianum var. dunalianum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It thrives in cold, moist environments and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of blue-black berries. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces fruit in late summer to early autumn.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystems, providing food for various wildlife species.
