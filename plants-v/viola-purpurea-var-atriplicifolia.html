Viola purpurea var. atriplicifolia

Viola purpurea var. atriplicifolia, commonly known as the purple violet or the heartleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy violet flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
