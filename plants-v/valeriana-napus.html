Valeriana napus

Valeriana napus, commonly known as common valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in terminal spikes, which bloom in summer. Its roots are often used in herbal medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana napus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
