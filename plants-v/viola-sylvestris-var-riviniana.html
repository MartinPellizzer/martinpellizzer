Viola sylvestris var. riviniana

Viola sylvestris var. riviniana, commonly known as the river violet, is a flowering plant native to moist, shaded environments in Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in riverbanks, meadows, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained, fertile soils. Its blooming period occurs in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
