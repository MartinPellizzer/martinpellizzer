Viola × tristicha

Viola × tristicha, commonly known as the common heartleaf violet, is a hybrid species belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. It has a compact growth habit with upright stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Viola × tristicha is known for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
