Vaginopteris subfalcata

Vaginopteris subfalcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often submerged in water. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Vaginopteris subfalcata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygenation for aquatic life.
