Vicia sativa var. incisa

Vicia sativa var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often used for soil stabilization and as a forage crop for livestock.
