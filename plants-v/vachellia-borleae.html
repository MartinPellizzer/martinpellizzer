Vachellia borleae

Vachellia borleae, commonly known as the Borleae thorn tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It often grows in rocky or sandy soils and is sometimes used for forage by livestock.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
