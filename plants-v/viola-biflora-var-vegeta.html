Viola biflora var. vegeta

Viola biflora var. vegeta, commonly known as the green violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 cm and blooms from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
