Viola vorobievii

Viola vorobievii, commonly known as the Vorobievii violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking violet or purple flowers during the spring season. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species.
