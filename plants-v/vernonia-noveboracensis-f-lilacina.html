Vernonia noveboracensis f. lilacina

Vernonia noveboracensis f. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-leaf ironweed, is a variant of the ironweed species native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking lilac-colored foliage, which contrasts with the typical green of the species. The plant produces dense clusters of small, dark purple to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects and ornamental gardens alike.
