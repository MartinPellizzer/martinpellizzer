Vaccinium poasanum

Vaccinium poasanum, commonly known as the Pacific huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Vaccinium poasanum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
