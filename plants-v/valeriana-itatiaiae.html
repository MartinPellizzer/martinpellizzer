Valeriana itatiaiae

Valeriana itatiaiae, commonly known as the Itatiaia valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to the humid climate of its native habitat.

Its root system is known for containing compounds with potential medicinal properties, though it is less commonly used in traditional medicine compared to other valerian species.
