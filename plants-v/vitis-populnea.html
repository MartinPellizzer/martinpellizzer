Vitis populnea

Vitis populnea, commonly known as the wild grapevine, is a climbing vine native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils that help it climb and spread across surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of small, edible berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Vitis populnea is often found in moist, wooded areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
