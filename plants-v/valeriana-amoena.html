Valeriana amoena

Valeriana amoena, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its root system is known for containing volatile oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its historical use in herbal medicine.

Valeriana amoena thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
