Vernonia arkansana

Vernonia arkansana, commonly known as Arkansas ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of purple to blue-purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland areas and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
