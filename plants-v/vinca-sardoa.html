Vinca sardoa

Vinca sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian vinca, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen nature and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring dark green, glossy leaves. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and purple, bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vinca sardoa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal and rocky environments.
