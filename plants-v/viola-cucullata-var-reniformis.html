Viola cucullata var. reniformis

Viola cucullata var. reniformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of North America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, typical of the Viola genus. It thrives in moist, shady woodland environments and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
