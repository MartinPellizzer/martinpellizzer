Vitex rotundifolia

Vitex rotundifolia, commonly known as the chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to rounded leaves and showy, fragrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, woody fruits called drupes, which contain three seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Vitex rotundifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
