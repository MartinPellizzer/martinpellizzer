Vignea praecox subsp. curvata

Vignea praecox subsp.

curvata, commonly known as the slender winged bean, is a legume native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires moderate water.

Due to its nutritional value, it is cultivated for both food and forage in many parts of the world.
