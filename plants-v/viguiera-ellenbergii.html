Viguiera ellenbergii

Viguiera ellenbergii, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It features showy, bright orange to red composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has a robust root system and can grow up to 3 to 5 feet tall, with lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
