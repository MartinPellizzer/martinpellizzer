Vernonia verbenacea

Vernonia verbenacea, commonly known as ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

The showy purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
