Verbesina pachyphylla

Verbesina pachyphylla, commonly known as the thickstem ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
