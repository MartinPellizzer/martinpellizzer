Verbascum × regelianum

Verbascum × regelianum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum phlomoides.

It is a tall, herbaceous plant that can grow up to 2 meters in height, featuring large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its fibrous stems and leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing properties.
