Vitis × hispanica

Vitis × hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish grapevine, is a hybrid species within the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of climates. The plant produces clusters of small, often sweet berries that are used for both culinary and winemaking purposes. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a glossy surface.

Vitis × hispanica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and vineyards.
