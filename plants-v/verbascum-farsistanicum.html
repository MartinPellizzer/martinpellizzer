Verbascum farsistanicum

Verbascum farsistanicum, commonly known as the Persian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy, woolly leaves. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Verbascum farsistanicum is often used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
