Viburnum pubinerve

Viburnum pubinerve, commonly known as the pubescent viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with a pubescent (hairy) underside, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring. These flowers are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals.

Viburnum pubinerve is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
