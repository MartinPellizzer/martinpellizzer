Verbascum euphraticum

Verbascum euphraticum, commonly known as the Syrian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Verbascum euphraticum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
