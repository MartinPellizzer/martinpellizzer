Viola palustris var. leimonia

Viola palustris var. leimonia, commonly known as the marsh violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge, and it produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding, making it a resilient component of wetland ecosystems.
