Vatica africana var. hypoleuca

Vatica africana var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-veined African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and distinctive white veins on its leaves. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
