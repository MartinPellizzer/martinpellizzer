Viola grypoceras f. chionantha

Viola grypoceras f. chionantha, commonly known as the snow viola, is a frost-tolerant perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features compact, hairy stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet and white, often with a distinctive white or pale pink throat. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This frost-resistant species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
