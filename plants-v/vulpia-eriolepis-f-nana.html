Vulpia eriolepis f. nana

Vulpia eriolepis f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf vulpia, is a low-growing grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that typically reach a height of no more than 10 centimeters. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue when young. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale green to brownish flowers.

This variety is adapted to dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.
