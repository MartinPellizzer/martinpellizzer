Vicia sativa subsp. segetalis

Vicia sativa subsp.

segetalis, commonly known as field vetch, is an annual legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Its pods contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source for livestock.

This species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable cover crop in agricultural settings.
