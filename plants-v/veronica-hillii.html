Veronica hillii

Veronica hillii, commonly known as the Hill's speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Veronica hillii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny habitats.
