Vangueriella rhamnoides, commonly known as the creeping sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is extensive and adapted to poor, sandy soils, allowing it to thrive in harsh environments.
