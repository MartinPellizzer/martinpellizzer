Veronica armena

Veronica armena, commonly known as Armenian speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, bearing opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly serrated. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Veronica armena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
