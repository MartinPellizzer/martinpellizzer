Viola betonicifolia subsp. nova-guineensis

Viola betonicifolia subsp.

nova-guineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of New Guinea. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces vibrant violet to purple flowers with a distinctive spurred petal.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
