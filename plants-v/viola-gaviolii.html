Viola gaviolii

Viola gaviolii, commonly known as the Gavioli violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-20 centimeters and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola gaviolii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
