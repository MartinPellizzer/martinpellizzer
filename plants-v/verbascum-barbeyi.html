Verbascum barbeyi

Verbascum barbeyi, commonly known as the Barbey's mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose panicle. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Verbascum barbeyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
