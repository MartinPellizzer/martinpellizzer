Viola orphanidis subsp. orphanidis

Viola orphanidis subsp.

orphanidis, commonly known as the Orphanides violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific habitat preferences and floral characteristics.
