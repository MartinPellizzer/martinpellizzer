Vitis vinifera var. rozieri

Vitis vinifera var. rozieri, commonly known as the Rozier's grape, is a lesser-known variety of the common grapevine.

It is characterized by its small, dark berries that are often used in winemaking due to their intense flavor profile. The plant is a deciduous vine that thrives in temperate climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sunlight. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to produce high-quality fruit in suitable conditions.

This variety is particularly valued for its unique aromatic qualities and is sometimes used in specialty wines.
