Vernonia dalettiensis

Vernonia dalettiensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Vernonia dalettiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
