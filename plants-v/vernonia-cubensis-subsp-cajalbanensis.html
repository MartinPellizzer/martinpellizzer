Vernonia cubensis subsp. cajalbanensis

Vernonia cubensis subsp.

cajalbanensis, commonly known as the Cuban ironweed, is a shrub native to Cuba and the Bahamas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called corymbs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as coastal areas and limestone formations.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its distinctive floral displays.
