Verbascum × semisplendidum

Verbascum × semisplendidum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in soft, downy hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in clusters at the top of the stems. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Verbascum × semisplendidum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
