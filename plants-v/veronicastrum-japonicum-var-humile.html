Veronicastrum japonicum var. humile

Veronicastrum japonicum var. humile, commonly known as Japanese spirea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy panicles of white or pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of small, fluffy flowers that attract pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
