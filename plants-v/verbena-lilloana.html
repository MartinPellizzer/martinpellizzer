Verbena lilloana

Verbena lilloana, commonly known as the Lillo's verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Verbena lilloana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
