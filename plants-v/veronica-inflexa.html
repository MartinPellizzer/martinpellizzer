Veronica inflexa

Veronica inflexa, commonly known as the narrowleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Veronica inflexa produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its spread.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
