Vittaria loheriana

Vittaria loheriana, commonly known as the Lohr's spikemoss, is a member of the family Vittariaceae and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its creeping stems and clustered, spore-bearing structures called sporangia. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Vittaria loheriana is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in botanical collections.
