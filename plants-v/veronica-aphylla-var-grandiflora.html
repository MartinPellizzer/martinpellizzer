Veronica aphylla var. grandiflora

Veronica aphylla var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora speedwell, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
