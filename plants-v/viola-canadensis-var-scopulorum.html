Viola canadensis var. scopulorum

Viola canadensis var. scopulorum, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in woodland or open areas. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive dark green color and a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are violet-blue, with a spurred petal at the back, and bloom in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
