Vouapa stipulacea

Vouapa stipulacea, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Vouapa stipulacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
