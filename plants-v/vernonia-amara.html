Vernonia amara

Vernonia amara, commonly known as bitterleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical clusters. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Vernonia amara is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
