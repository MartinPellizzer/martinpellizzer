Viburnum dentatum var. deamii

Viburnum dentatum var. deamii, commonly known as the Deam's arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins, and its flowers are typically white or pale green, arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
