Valeriana laevicaulis

Valeriana laevicaulis, commonly known as the smooth valerian, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their sedative properties.

Valeriana laevicaulis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
