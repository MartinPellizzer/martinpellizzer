Verbascum album

Verbascum album, commonly known as white mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its fibrous leaves and stems have been historically used for their medicinal and practical properties.
