Viola × aberrans

Viola × aberrans, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola × wittrockiana.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and a wide range of flower colors, including purple, violet, and white. The plant typically has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Viola × aberrans is known for its ability to produce numerous blooms throughout the growing season.
