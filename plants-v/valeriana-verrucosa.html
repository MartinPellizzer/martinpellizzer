Valeriana verrucosa

Valeriana verrucosa, commonly known as the rough valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative properties.

Valeriana verrucosa thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
