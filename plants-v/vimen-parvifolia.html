Vimen parvifolia

Vimen parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved vimen, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, elliptical leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Vimen parvifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
