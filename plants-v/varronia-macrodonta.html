Varronia macrodonta

Varronia macrodonta, commonly known as the African soursop or African star apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a large, spiny fruit with a thick, green rind and a soft, white, juicy pulp. The fruit is edible and has a flavor similar to a combination of pineapple and banana.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
