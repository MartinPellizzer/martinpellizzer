Vaccinium vacillans var. missouriense

Vaccinium vacillans var. missouriense, commonly known as Missouri blueberry, is a variant of the blueberry plant native to the central United States.

It is a shrub that produces small, edible berries rich in antioxidants and other nutrients. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its foliage is typically dark green, and it blooms with white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
