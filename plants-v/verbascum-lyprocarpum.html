Verbascum lyprocarpum

Verbascum lyprocarpum, commonly known as the Lyprocarpum mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

Verbascum lyprocarpum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
