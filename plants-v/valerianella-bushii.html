Valerianella bushii

Valerianella bushii, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are often narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branched.

Valerianella bushii is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
