Veronica armstrongii var. annulata

Veronica armstrongii var. annulata, commonly known as the ringed speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, blue-to-purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
