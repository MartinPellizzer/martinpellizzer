Viola allchariensis subsp. allchariensis

Viola allchariensis subsp.

allchariensis, commonly known as the Allchar violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and thrives in temperate climates.

The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

The species is also noted for its role in local ecosystems, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
