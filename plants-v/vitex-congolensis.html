Vitex congolensis

Vitex congolensis, commonly known as the African blue bead, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, round, blue-black fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are used for their medicinal properties, including treatments for respiratory and digestive ailments.

Vitex congolensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
