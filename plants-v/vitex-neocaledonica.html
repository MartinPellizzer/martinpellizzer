Vitex neocaledonica

Vitex neocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonia chaste tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of New Caledonia in the South Pacific.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and slightly glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. These flowers are followed by small, woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed.

Vitex neocaledonica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
