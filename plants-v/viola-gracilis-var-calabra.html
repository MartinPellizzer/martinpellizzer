Viola gracilis var. calabra

Viola gracilis var. calabra, commonly known as the slender violet, is a species of flowering plant in the viola family.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it prefers partial shade and well-drained soil.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
