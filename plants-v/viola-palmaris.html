Viola palmaris

Viola palmaris, commonly known as the palmate violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive palmate venation. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. Viola palmaris prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
