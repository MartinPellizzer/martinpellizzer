Veronica lendenfeldii

Veronica lendenfeldii, commonly known as the Lendenfeld's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Veronica lendenfeldii is well adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
