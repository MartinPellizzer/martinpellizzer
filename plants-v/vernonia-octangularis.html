Vernonia octangularis

Vernonia octangularis, commonly known as the square-stemmed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its square-shaped stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Vernonia octangularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
