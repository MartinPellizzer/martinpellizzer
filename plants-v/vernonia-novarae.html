Vernonia novarae

Vernonia novarae, commonly known as the New Guinea Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple or brown center.

Vernonia novarae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
