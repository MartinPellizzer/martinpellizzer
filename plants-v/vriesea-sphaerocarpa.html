Vriesea sphaerocarpa

Vriesea sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, globe-shaped rosette of broad, waxy leaves that can reach up to 3 feet in height. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red bracts that remain for several months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea sphaerocarpa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
