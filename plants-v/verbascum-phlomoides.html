Verbascum phlomoides

Verbascum phlomoides, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are covered in soft, white hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its fuzzy foliage and striking flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
