Viola canina var. pusilla

Viola canina var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf dog violet, is a compact perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, often with a yellow or white throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
