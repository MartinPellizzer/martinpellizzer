Vernonia bealliae

Vernonia bealliae, commonly known as the Beall's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces showy purple to pink flower clusters in the summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Vernonia bealliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
