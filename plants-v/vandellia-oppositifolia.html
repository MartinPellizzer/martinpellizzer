Vandellia oppositifolia

Vandellia oppositifolia, commonly known as the rock wallaby orchid, is a small terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features a slender, hairy stem with opposite, linear leaves. Its flowers are usually small, greenish-white, and clustered in a spike, with a distinctive, narrow labellum.

It is adapted to survive in harsh environments, showcasing the resilience of orchids in challenging botanical settings.
