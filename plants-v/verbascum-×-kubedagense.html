Verbascum × kubedagense

Verbascum × kubedagense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers in the summer, which are clustered in racemes at the top of the stems. It is native to regions in the Middle East and is often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some areas.
