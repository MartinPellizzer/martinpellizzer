Viola averyi

Viola averyi, commonly known as the Avery's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola averyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native habitats.
