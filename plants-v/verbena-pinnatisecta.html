Verbena pinnatisecta

Verbena pinnatisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The leaves are pinnatisect, meaning they are deeply divided into narrow, feather-like segments. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
