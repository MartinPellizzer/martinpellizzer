Viguiera robusta var. confusa

Viguiera robusta var. confusa, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
