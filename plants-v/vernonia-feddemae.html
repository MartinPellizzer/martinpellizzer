Vernonia feddemae

Vernonia feddemae, commonly known as the Fedde's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, spike-like flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
