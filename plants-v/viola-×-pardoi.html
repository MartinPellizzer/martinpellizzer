Viola × pardoi

Viola × pardoi, commonly known as the Dutch viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, often purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Viola × pardoi prefers partial shade and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
