Vincetoxicum mukdenense

Vincetoxicum mukdenense, commonly known as Mukden ironweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
