Viola taltalensis

Viola taltalensis, commonly known as the Taltal violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Chile.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils along the coastline, where it tolerates saline and windy conditions. Viola taltalensis is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall.

Its unique adaptations make it an important part of the local flora in its native habitat.
