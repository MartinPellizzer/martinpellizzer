Vaccinium hirtellum

Vaccinium hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, with a tart flavor. Its leaves are oval-shaped, hairy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Vaccinium hirtellum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.
