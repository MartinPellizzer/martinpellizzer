Viola chamissoniana subsp. robusta

Viola chamissoniana subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the robust viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy violet flowers with yellow markings. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
