Veronica glauca subsp. kavusica

Veronica glauca subsp.

kavusica, commonly known as the Kavusica speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
