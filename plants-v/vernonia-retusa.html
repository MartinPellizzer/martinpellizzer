Vernonia retusa

Vernonia retusa, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, while its flowers are yellow with dark centers.

Vernonia retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
