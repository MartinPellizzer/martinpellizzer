Vernonia bottae

Vernonia bottae, commonly known as the bottlebrush ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, violet to purple flower heads that resemble bottle brushes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Vernonia bottae thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
