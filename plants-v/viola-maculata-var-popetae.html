Viola maculata var. popetae

Viola maculata var. popetae, commonly known as the Popetae violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple-tinged margin and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in association with other woodland flora.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
