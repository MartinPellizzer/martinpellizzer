Vignea angustior

Vignea angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, spiraling stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to white. Vignea angustior is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
