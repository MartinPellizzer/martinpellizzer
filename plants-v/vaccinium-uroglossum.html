Vaccinium uroglossum

Vaccinium uroglossum, commonly known as the purple highbush blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically deep purple in color and have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires cool, moist conditions for optimal growth.

Vaccinium uroglossum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in local culinary traditions.
