Verbascum × gintlii

Verbascum × gintlii, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Verbascum × gintlii prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for dry, rocky gardens.
