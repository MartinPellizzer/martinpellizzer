Vicia setifolia

Vicia setifolia, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

Vicia setifolia is a legume, meaning it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.
