Valeriana coreana subsp. sachalinensis

Valeriana coreana subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as Korean valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Korean Peninsula and parts of northeastern China. It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, terminal spikes, which bloom in summer. Its rhizomatous root system is notable for containing volatile oils and alkaloids, contributing to its medicinal properties.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its calming and sedative effects.
