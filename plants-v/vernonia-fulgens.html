Vernonia fulgens

Vernonia fulgens, commonly known as the blazing goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Vernonia fulgens thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
