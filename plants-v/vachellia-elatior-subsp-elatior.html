Vachellia elatior subsp. elatior

Vachellia elatior subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the giant burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and long, slender pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous covering.

The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, feather-like leaflets and produces a distinctive, spiny fruit. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils. Vachellia elatior subsp.

elatior is adapted to withstand drought and is often used in traditional medicine.
