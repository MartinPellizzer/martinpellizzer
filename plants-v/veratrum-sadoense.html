Veratrum sadoense

Veratrum sadoense, commonly known as the Sado monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the genus Veratrum, which includes several species known for their toxic properties. The plant is characterized by its tall, stout stems and large, hooded flowers that resemble a monk's habit, hence its common name. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
