Vernonia rubricaulis var. denudata

Vernonia rubricaulis var. denudata, commonly known as the red-stemmed ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
