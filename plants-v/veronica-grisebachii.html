Veronica grisebachii

Veronica grisebachii, commonly known as the gray veronica, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
