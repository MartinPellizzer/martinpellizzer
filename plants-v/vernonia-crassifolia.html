Vernonia crassifolia

Vernonia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its botanical characteristics include fibrous roots, woody stems, and a preference for well-drained soils.

Vernonia crassifolia is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in full sun.
