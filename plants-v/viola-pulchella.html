Viola pulchella

Viola pulchella, commonly known as the pretty violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. Viola pulchella is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile garden plant.
