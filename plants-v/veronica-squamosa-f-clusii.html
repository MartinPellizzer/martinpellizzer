Veronica squamosa f. clusii

Veronica squamosa f. clusii, commonly known as the Clusii speedwell, is a compact, mat-forming perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a distinctive silver-gray underside that gives the plant a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
