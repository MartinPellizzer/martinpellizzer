Viburnum carlesii var. syringiflora

Viburnum carlesii var. syringiflora, commonly known as the fragrant snowball viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, resembling snowballs. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not typically consumed by humans.

Viburnum carlesii var. syringiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
