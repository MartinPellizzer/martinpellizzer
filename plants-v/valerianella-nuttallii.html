Valerianella nuttallii

Valerianella nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's valerian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy, alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring.

Valerianella nuttallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
