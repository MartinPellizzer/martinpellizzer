Viola lactea var. minor

Viola lactea var. minor, commonly known as the lesser milkweed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green hue. The flowers are pale violet or white, with five petals and a spurred petal at the back. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
