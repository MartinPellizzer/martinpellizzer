Vitex pubescens var. bicolor

Vitex pubescens var. bicolor, commonly known as the purple-leaf vitex, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored leaves, which are typically green on the upper surface and purple on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of purple or white, which bloom in the summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
