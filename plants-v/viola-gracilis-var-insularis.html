Viola gracilis var. insularis

Viola gracilis var. insularis, commonly known as the slender violet, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a perennial herb with heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is part of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate appearance and adaptability to cool, humid environments.
