Vicia lilacina

Vicia lilacina, commonly known as the lilac vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, clustered purple or lilac-colored flowers. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several elliptical seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
