Viola ticolor var. arvensis

Viola ticolor var. arvensis, commonly known as the field violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces violet or purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
