Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. pseudoanagallioides

Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. pseudoanagallioides, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, often aquatic conditions, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its adaptability to wet soils makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
