Vincentia triflora

Vincentia triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered vincentia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with a distinctive spurred structure at the base of the petals.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
