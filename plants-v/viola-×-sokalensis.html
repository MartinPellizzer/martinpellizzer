Viola × sokalensis

Viola × sokalensis, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola lutea and Viola tricolor, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parent species. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white, often with contrasting veining. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in both garden and conservation settings.
