Vatica philastreana

Vatica philastreana, commonly known as the Philippine ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and glossy in appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Vatica philastreana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
