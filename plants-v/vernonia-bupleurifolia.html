Vernonia bupleurifolia

Vernonia bupleurifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of yellow to orange tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
