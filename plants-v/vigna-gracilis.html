Vigna gracilis

Vigna gracilis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that develop into cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Vigna gracilis is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, improving soil fertility.

It is often cultivated as a forage crop or for its edible seeds.
