Viola caulescens

Viola caulescens, commonly known as the upright violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy, with heart-shaped leaves that are typically pubescent. The plant produces violet flowers that are usually purple or blue, with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Viola caulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
