Verbascum × murbeckii

Verbascum × murbeckii, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety grey-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and ease of growth.
