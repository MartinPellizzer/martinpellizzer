Vitis adenoclada

Vitis adenoclada, commonly known as the African grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that are typically eaten by birds. Vitis adenoclada is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
