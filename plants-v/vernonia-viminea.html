Vernonia viminea

Vernonia viminea, commonly known as slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture, while the flowers are small and yellowish-green.

Vernonia viminea is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
