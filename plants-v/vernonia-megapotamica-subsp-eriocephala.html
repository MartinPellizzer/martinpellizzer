Vernonia megapotamica subsp. eriocephala

Vernonia megapotamica subsp.

eriocephala, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
