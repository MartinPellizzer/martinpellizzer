Valeriana velutina

Valeriana velutina, commonly known as velvet valerian, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Valeriana velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
