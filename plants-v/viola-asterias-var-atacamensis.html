Viola asterias var. atacamensis

Viola asterias var. atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, featuring thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for gardeners in dry climates.
