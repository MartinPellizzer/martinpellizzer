Veronica jacquinii var. virescens

Veronica jacquinii var. virescens, commonly known as the green-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
