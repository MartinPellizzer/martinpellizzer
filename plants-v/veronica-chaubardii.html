Veronica chaubardii

Veronica chaubardii, commonly known as the blueweed or chaubard's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, producing a delicate, airy appearance.

Veronica chaubardii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
