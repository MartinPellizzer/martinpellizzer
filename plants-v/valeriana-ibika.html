Valeriana ibika

Valeriana ibika, commonly known as the African valerian, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves and produces small, oval-shaped berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Valeriana ibika is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
