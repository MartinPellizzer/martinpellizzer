Veronica teucrium var. oxypetala

Veronica teucrium var. oxypetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender stems. The species features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.
