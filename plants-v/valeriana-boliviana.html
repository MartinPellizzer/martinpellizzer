Valeriana boliviana

Valeriana boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and has lance-shaped leaves with a strong, spicy fragrance. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana boliviana is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking floral displays.
