Veronica orsiniana var. verdagueri

Veronica orsiniana var. verdagueri, commonly known as the Canary Islands veronica, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and coastal areas.
