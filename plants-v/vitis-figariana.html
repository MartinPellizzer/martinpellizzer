Vitis figariana

Vitis figariana, commonly known as the figleaf gooseberry, is a deciduous climbing vine native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and the leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often lobed. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to climb using tendrils. Its botanical features include palmate leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
