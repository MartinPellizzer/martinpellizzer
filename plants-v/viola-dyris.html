Viola dyris

Viola dyris, commonly known as the violet orchid or the purple violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers range in color from purple to white.

Viola dyris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
