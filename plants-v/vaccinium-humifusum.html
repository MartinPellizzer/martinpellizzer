Vaccinium humifusum

Vaccinium humifusum, commonly known as the creeping blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and bogs. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a dark green color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
