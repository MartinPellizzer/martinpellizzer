Vernonia isalensis

Vernonia isalensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia isalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
