Vernonia bonapartei

Vernonia bonapartei, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, bark-like stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, it plays a role in traditional medicine and soil stabilization in its native habitats.
