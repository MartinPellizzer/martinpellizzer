Viola confusa f. leucantha

Viola confusa f. leucantha, commonly known as the white viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple or violet hues of its parent species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a soft, hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
