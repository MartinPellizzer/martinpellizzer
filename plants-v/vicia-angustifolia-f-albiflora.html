Vicia angustifolia f. albiflora

Vicia angustifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered narrow-leaf vetch, is a botanical variant of the common vetch.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. This plant is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of temperate climates. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
