Vicia cretica subsp. cretica

Vicia cretica subsp.

cretica, commonly known as the common vetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through a symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.
