Viticella racemosa

Viticella racemosa, commonly known as the common honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellow or white flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its vigorous growth habit.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
