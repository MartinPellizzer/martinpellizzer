Vaccinium brachystachyum

Vaccinium brachystachyum, commonly known as the dwarf huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its small, dark blue berries are edible and often used in local cuisines or for making jams and wines.

The plant's botanical features include evergreen leaves, clustered flowers, and a compact growth habit.
