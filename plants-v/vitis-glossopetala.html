Vitis glossopetala

Vitis glossopetala, commonly known as the Mexican grapevine, is a species of flowering vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. Vitis glossopetala is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
