Vernonia potamophila

Vernonia potamophila, commonly known as the river Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in wetlands, riverbanks, and other moist habitats, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia potamophila plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
