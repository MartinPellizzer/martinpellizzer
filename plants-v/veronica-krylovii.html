Veronica krylovii

Veronica krylovii, commonly known as Krylov's speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Veronica krylovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
