Vaccinium podocarpoideum

Vaccinium podocarpoideum, commonly known as the New Zealand cranberry, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically evergreen. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its berries, which are edible, are small, red, and tart, similar to cranberries.

Vaccinium podocarpoideum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.
