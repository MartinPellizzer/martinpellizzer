Vitex chrysomallum

Vitex chrysomallum, commonly known as the golden vitex, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, which bloom in the summer months. Its woody stems and aromatic foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Vitex chrysomallum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
