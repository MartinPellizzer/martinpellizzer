Veronica cryptomorpha

Veronica cryptomorpha, commonly known as the cryptomorpha speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
