Vernonia walshae

Vernonia walshae, commonly known as the Walsh's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, spiky yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy and somewhat woody.

Vernonia walshae is often found in wetland ecosystems and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
