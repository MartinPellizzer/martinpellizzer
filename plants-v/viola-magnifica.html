Viola magnifica

Viola magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and violet. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Viola magnifica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
