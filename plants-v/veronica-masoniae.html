Veronica masoniae

Veronica masoniae, commonly known as the Masonia veronica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
