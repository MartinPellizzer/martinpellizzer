Viola wightiana var. pubescens

Viola wightiana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded borders.
