Vitmannia polyandra

Vitmannia polyandra, commonly known as the featherleaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized collections.
