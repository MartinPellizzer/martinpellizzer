Veronica kurdica

Veronica kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

Veronica kurdica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
