Veratrum nigrum var. ussuriense

Veratrum nigrum var. ussuriense, commonly known as the Ussuri black hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Far East of Russia and parts of northern China.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white to pale green, bell-shaped flowers in late summer. It is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that make it harmful to humans and animals.

Due to its toxicity and rarity, it is often found in remote, moist habitats such as forests and mountainous regions.
