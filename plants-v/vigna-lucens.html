Vigna lucens

Vigna lucens, commonly known as the shining bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing edible seeds. Vigna lucens is valued for its nutritional content, including protein and essential minerals.

It is often grown as a food crop and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
