Vernonia laxa

Vernonia laxa, commonly known as slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branched stems that often reach heights of up to six feet. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flower heads, which are typically borne on long, slender peduncles. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia laxa is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
