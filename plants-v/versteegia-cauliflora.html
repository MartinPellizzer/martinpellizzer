Versteegia cauliflora

Versteegia cauliflora, commonly known as the cliff-rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom along the branches. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and leathery, providing efficient water retention.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
