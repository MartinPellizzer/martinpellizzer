Valeriana asarifolia

Valeriana asarifolia, commonly known as the fragrant valerian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often spicy scent. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a strong fragrance. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in summer.

Valeriana asarifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its purported calming and sedative properties.
