Vitex cuneata

Vitex cuneata, commonly known as the Chinese vitex or fragrant verbena, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, lavender-blue flowers in the summer months. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.

Vitex cuneata is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and full sun conditions.
