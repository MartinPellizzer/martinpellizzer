Villadia cucullata subsp. apiculata

Villadia cucullata subsp.

apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
