Veronica spicata var. elatior

Veronica spicata var. elatior, commonly known as the tall speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is noted for its height, typically reaching up to 1.5 meters, making it a striking addition to borders and cottage gardens.
