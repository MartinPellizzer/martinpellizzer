Vernonia laevigata

Vernonia laevigata, commonly known as the smooth Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine layer of hair.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
