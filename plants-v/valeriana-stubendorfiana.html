Valeriana stubendorfiana

Valeriana stubendorfiana, commonly known as the Stubendorf valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its root system is known for containing volatile oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Valeriana stubendorfiana is valued in traditional medicine for its potential calming and sedative effects.
