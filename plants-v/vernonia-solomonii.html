Vernonia solomonii

Vernonia solomonii, commonly known as Solomon's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer and are followed by small, dry fruits called achenes.

Vernonia solomonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
