Valeriana dageletiana var. verticillata

Valeriana dageletiana var. verticillata, commonly known as the Alpine valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust root system and erect, branching stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense, whorled clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its mild sedative properties.
