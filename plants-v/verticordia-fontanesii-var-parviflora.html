Verticordia fontanesii var. parviflora

Verticordia fontanesii var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered swamp banksia, is a member of the Proteaceae family native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and its distinctive, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in wetland or swampy habitats, where it forms dense mats on the ground. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

This variety is particularly noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native environment.
