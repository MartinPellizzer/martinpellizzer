Viviania lanceolata

Viviania lanceolata, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
