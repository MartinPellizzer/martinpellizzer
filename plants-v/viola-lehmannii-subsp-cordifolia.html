Viola lehmannii subsp. cordifolia

Viola lehmannii subsp.

cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
