Viola tracheliifolia

Viola tracheliifolia, commonly known as the Sierra viola, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short stem and produces small, spurred petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola tracheliifolia is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
