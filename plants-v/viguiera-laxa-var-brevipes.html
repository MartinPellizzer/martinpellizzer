Viguiera laxa var. brevipes

Viguiera laxa var. brevipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Its ecological value includes providing nectar for pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
