Veronica pseudolongifolia var. taigischensis

Veronica pseudolongifolia var. taigischensis, commonly known as the Taigischenskaya speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
