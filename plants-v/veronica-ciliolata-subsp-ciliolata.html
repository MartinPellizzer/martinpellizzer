Veronica ciliolata subsp. ciliolata

Veronica ciliolata subsp.

ciliolata, commonly known as the hairy veronica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with ciliate margins, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
