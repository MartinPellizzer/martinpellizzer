Verbascum viridissimum

Verbascum viridissimum, commonly known as the greenleaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum viridissimum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and soothing effects.
