Vanieria pubescens

Vanieria pubescens, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery fronds that give it a velvety appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are typically submerged, with some floating on the water surface.

Vanieria pubescens plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
