Verbascum flavidum

Verbascum flavidum, commonly known as the yellow mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Verbascum flavidum is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
