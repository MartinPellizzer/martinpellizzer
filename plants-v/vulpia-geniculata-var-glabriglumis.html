Vulpia geniculata var. glabriglumis

Vulpia geniculata var. glabriglumis, commonly known as the glabrous bentgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted shoots and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairless or nearly so. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along the edges of wetlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
