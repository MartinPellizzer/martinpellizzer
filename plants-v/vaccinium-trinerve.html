Vaccinium trinerve

Vaccinium trinerve, commonly known as the three-veined blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like heathers and cranberries. The plant produces small, acidic berries that are typically blue or black when ripe. Its leaves are elliptical with three distinct veins, giving it its scientific name.

Vaccinium trinerve thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
