Veronica sibirica

Veronica sibirica, commonly known as Siberian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia, including Siberia and the Far East.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems that bear opposite pairs of oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and forest edges.

Veronica sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
