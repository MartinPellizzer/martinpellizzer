Viorna beadlei

Viorna beadlei, commonly known as the beadleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, bead-like texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. Viorna beadlei thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical climates.
