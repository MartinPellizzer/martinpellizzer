Vitis anamalayana

Vitis anamalayana, commonly known as the Anamalai grape, is a species of flowering vine native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. Vitis anamalayana is adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its potential for cultivation and medicinal properties.
