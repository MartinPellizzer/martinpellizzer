Valliera triplinervis

Valliera triplinervis, commonly known as the three-veined valliera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant has slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinct three-veined pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
