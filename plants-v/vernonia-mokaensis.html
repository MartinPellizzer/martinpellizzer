Vernonia mokaensis

Vernonia mokaensis, commonly known as the Moka Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia mokaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
