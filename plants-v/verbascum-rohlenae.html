Verbascum rohlenae

Verbascum rohlenae, commonly known as the Rohlhen's mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants commonly referred to as mulleins. The plant is characterized by its tall, sparsely hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Verbascum rohlenae is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
