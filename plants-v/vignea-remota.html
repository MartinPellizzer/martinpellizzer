Vignea remota

Vignea remota, commonly known as the remote vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees or structures. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.
