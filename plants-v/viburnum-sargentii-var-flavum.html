Viburnum sargentii var. flavum

Viburnum sargentii var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale green, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
