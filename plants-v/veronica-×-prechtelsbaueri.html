Veronica × prechtelsbaueri

Veronica × prechtelsbaueri, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, showy flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, decorative plant.
