Veronica beccabunga var. pallida

Veronica beccabunga var. pallida, commonly known as the pale speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. The plant features small, pale blue or white flowers clustered in pairs, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate appearance.
