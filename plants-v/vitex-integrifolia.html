Vitex integrifolia

Vitex integrifolia, commonly known as the hibiscus syriacus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, fluffy seeds.

Vitex integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
