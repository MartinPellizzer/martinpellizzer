Veronica derwentiana subsp. derwentiana

Veronica derwentiana subsp.

derwentiana, commonly known as the Derwent Speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the British Isles. It typically features clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant has a mat-forming habit with slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland margins and stream sides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
