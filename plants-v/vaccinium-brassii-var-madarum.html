Vaccinium brassii var. madarum

Vaccinium brassii var. madarum, commonly known as the Madarum blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It produces small, dark blue berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full to partial sunlight for optimal growth. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and turn reddish in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice for both landscaping and small-scale cultivation.
