Verbesina saubinetioides

Verbesina saubinetioides, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that are arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
