Virgilia oroboides subsp. oroboides

Virgilia oroboides subsp.

oroboides, commonly known as the white virgilia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces a distinctive, cylindrical seed pod that splits open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
