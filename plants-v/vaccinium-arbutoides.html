Vaccinium arbutoides

Vaccinium arbutoides, commonly known as the highbush blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, rich in antioxidants and nutrients. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Vaccinium arbutoides is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
