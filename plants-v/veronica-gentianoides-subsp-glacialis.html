Veronica gentianoides subsp. glacialis

Veronica gentianoides subsp.

glacialis, commonly known as glacier speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It thrives in cold, moist environments, typically found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapting to the harsh conditions of its high-altitude habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
