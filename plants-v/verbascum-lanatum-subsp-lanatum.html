Verbascum lanatum subsp. lanatum

Verbascum lanatum subsp.

lanatum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft white hairs that give it a distinctive woolly appearance.

The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and also covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall fluffy look.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats.
