Vitis sarcocephala

Vitis sarcocephala, commonly known as the African grape or wild grapevine, is a woody vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red or black when mature. Vitis sarcocephala is often found in forest understories and is adapted to humid, shaded environments.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and has potential for ornamental or agricultural use.
