Vachellia pachyphloia

Vachellia pachyphloia, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions and making it drought-resistant. Its leaves are typically small and bipinnate, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds.

Vachellia pachyphloia is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fuel and fodder.
