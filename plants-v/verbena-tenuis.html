Verbena tenuis

Verbena tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a slightly hairy texture. It produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
