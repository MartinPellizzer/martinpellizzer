Vachellia sieberiana var. villosa

Vachellia sieberiana var. villosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the dry season. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feathery leaflets, and the plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The species is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of forage for wildlife.
