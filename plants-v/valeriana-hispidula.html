Valeriana hispidula

Valeriana hispidula, commonly known as the hairy valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic, often hairy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. Valeriana hispidula is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
