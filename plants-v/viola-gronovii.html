Viola gronovii

Viola gronovii, commonly known as the Greenland violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

Viola gronovii is adapted to cold climates and has a compact growth habit to withstand harsh weather conditions.
