Valeriana locusta var. multifida

Valeriana locusta var. multifida, commonly known as the multifid valerian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that often have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Valeriana locusta var. multifida is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.
