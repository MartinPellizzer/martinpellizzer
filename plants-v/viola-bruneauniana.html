Viola bruneauniana

Viola bruneauniana, commonly known as the purple violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and shaded gardens, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its blooms appear in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola bruneauniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
