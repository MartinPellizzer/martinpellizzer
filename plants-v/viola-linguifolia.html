Viola linguifolia

Viola linguifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are broad and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers bloom in early spring.

Viola linguifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
