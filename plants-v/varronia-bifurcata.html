Varronia bifurcata

Varronia bifurcata, commonly known as the false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Varronia bifurcata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
