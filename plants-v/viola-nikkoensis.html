Viola nikkoensis

Viola nikkoensis, commonly known as the Nikko violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola nikkoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
