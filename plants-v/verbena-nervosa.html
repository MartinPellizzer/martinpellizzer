Verbena nervosa

Verbena nervosa, commonly known as narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically blooms with vibrant blue or purple flowers from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, giving it a distinct appearance.

Verbena nervosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
