Veronica thomsonii var. glabra

Veronica thomsonii var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth veronica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, smooth hairs, giving it its varietal name "glabra," which means smooth in Latin. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
