Veprecella acuminata

Veprecella acuminata, commonly known as the slender leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems covered in small spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its stem to survive in arid environments.

Veprecella acuminata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
