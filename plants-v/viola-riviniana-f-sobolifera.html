Viola riviniana f. sobolifera

Viola riviniana f. sobolifera, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a variant of the common violet that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base of the bloom. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
