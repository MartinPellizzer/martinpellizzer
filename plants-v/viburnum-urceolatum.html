Viburnum urceolatum

Viburnum urceolatum, commonly known as the snowball viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal. Its fruits are small, dark berries that ripen in the fall and are attractive to birds.

Viburnum urceolatum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in a variety of soil conditions.
