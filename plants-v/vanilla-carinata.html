Vanilla carinata

Vanilla carinata, commonly known as Ethiopian coffee bean, is not a vanilla but a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is an annual herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia and grows in cool, temperate climates. The plant has small, green, oval-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into round, brown pods containing seeds. Its primary use is in the production of oilseed for biodiesel and livestock feed.

The botanical features of Vanilla carinata make it an important crop in certain agricultural regions.
