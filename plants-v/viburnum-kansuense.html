Viburnum kansuense

Viburnum kansuense, commonly known as the Kansu viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Viburnum kansuense typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
