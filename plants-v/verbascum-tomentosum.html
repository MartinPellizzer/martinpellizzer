Verbascum tomentosum

Verbascum tomentosum, commonly known as fuzzy mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, woolly gray hairs. The leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a thick layer of soft, fuzzy hairs. It produces yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
