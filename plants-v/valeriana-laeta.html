Valeriana laeta

Valeriana laeta, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, violet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive, pleasant fragrance.

Valeriana laeta is also valued for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional remedies for nervous system ailments.
