Verbascum × decalvans

Verbascum × decalvans, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a hybrid plant in the genus Verbascum, native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in soft, silky hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, candle-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Verbascum × decalvans is a perennial plant that is both ornamental and sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
