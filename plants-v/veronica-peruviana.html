Veronica peruviana

Veronica peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Veronica peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
