Viola adenothrix var. tsugitakaensis

Viola adenothrix var. tsugitakaensis, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the undergrowth.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
