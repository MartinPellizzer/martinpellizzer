Vaccinium impressinerve

Vaccinium impressinerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined blueberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other blueberry species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Vaccinium impressinerve is often found in forest understories and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
