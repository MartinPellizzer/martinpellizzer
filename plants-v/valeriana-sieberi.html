Valeriana sieberi

Valeriana sieberi, commonly known as Siberian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a stout, upright stem with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana sieberi thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
