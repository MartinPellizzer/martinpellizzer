Viola adunca var. longipes

Viola adunca var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters. The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue, and has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both garden use and botanical study.
