Vicia ludoviciana var. occidentalis

Vicia ludoviciana var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western vetch, is a perennial legume native to western North America.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
