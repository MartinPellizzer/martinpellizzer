Viola calthifolia

Viola calthifolia, commonly known as the purple marsh violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces striking violet or purple flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms.

Viola calthifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
