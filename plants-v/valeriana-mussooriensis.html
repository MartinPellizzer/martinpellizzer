Valeriana mussooriensis

Valeriana mussooriensis, commonly known as the Nilgiri valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in the understorey of forests. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana mussooriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
