Viola falcata

Viola falcata, commonly known as the sickle violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive reddish underside. The plant produces violet flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
