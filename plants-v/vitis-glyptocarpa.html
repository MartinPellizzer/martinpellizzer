Vitis glyptocarpa

Vitis glyptocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese grape or wild grape, is a species of flowering vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is often found growing in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Vitis glyptocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
