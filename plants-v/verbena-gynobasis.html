Verbena gynobasis

Verbena gynobasis, commonly known as the narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has slender, branching stems with small, narrow leaves. It produces numerous small flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbena gynobasis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
