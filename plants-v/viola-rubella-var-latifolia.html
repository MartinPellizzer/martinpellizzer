Viola rubella var. latifolia

Viola rubella var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and garden beds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
