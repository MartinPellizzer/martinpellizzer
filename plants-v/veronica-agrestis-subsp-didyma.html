Veronica agrestis subsp. didyma

Veronica agrestis subsp.

didyma, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, linear leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
