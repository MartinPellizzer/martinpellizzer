Varronia tomentosa

Varronia tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and trifoliate leaves with hairy undersides. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, seed-bearing segments. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climatic conditions.
