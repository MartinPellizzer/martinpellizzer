Verbascum asperuloides

Verbascum asperuloides, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves that are covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Verbascum asperuloides is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical uses in traditional herbal remedies.
