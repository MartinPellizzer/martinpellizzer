Verbesina portlandiana

Verbesina portlandiana, commonly known as the Portland goldenrod, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and arid environments. It has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The Portland goldenrod is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
