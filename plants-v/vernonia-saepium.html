Vernonia saepium

Vernonia saepium, commonly known as ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple to pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads. Vernonia saepium thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its foliage is typically hairy, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.
