Vitis zongylioides

Vitis zongylioides, commonly known as the Chinese wild grape, is a woody vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens and botanical collections.
