Veronica novae-hollandiae

Veronica novae-hollandiae, commonly known as the Australian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate or ascending.

Veronica novae-hollandiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
