Veronica wyomingensis

Veronica wyomingensis, commonly known as Wyoming speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
