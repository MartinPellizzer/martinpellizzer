Viguiera wedelioides

Viguiera wedelioides, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine, white to grayish hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often hairy, while the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Viguiera wedelioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
