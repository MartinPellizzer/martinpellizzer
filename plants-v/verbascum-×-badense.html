Verbascum × badense

Verbascum × badense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants in the family Scrophulariaceae. The plant is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its showy yellow or orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
