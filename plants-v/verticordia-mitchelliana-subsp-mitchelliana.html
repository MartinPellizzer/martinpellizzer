Verticordia mitchelliana subsp. mitchelliana

Verticordia mitchelliana subsp.

mitchelliana, commonly known as the Mitchell's featherflower, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal regions. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract nectar-feeding insects such as bees and birds.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
