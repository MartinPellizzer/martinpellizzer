Vicia peregrina subsp. persepolitana

Vicia peregrina subsp.

persepolitana, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
