Vitex trifolia var. simplicifolia

Vitex trifolia var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vitex, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually narrow and slightly serrated. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or white, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils.
