Vernonia nudiflora

Vernonia nudiflora, commonly known as the nakedflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Vernonia nudiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
