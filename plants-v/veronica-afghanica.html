Veronica afghanica

Veronica afghanica, commonly known as Afghan speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer.

Veronica afghanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
