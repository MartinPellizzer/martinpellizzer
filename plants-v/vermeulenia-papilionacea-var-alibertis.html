Vermeulenia papilionacea var. alibertis

Vermeulenia papilionacea var. alibertis, commonly known as the Alibert's spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like flower structure, which features a slender, elongated spur and delicate, petal-like appendages. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of flies, showcasing a specialized ecological relationship.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
