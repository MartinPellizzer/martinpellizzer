Valeriana locusta subsp. dentata

Valeriana locusta subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the toothed valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that have serrated margins.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from June to August. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in meadows and woodland edges.
