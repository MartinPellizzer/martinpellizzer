Verbascum thapsus

Verbascum thapsus, commonly known as common mullein, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Verbascum thapsus is also known for its medicinal properties, including its use in treating respiratory ailments.
