Viola pinnata subsp. multifida

Viola pinnata subsp.

multifida, commonly known as the broad-leafed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive lobed structure, often divided into several segments.

The plant produces clusters of small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine and as ornamental plants.
