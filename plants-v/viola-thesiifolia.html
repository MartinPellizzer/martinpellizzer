Viola thesiifolia

Viola thesiifolia, commonly known as the Greek violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola thesiifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
