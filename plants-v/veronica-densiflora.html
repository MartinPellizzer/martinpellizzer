Veronica densiflora

Veronica densiflora, commonly known as denseflower speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or along streambanks. The plant features upright stems covered in dense, blue to purple flowers that bloom from spring to summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Veronica densiflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
