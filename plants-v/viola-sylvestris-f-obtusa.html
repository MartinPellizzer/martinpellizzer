Viola sylvestris f. obtusa

Viola sylvestris f. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves with a velvety texture and deep green coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, hardy plants.
