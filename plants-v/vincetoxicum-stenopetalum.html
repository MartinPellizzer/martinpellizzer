Vincetoxicum stenopetalum

Vincetoxicum stenopetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled thornapple, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink, often with a distinctive, spurred calyx. Its fruits are follicles that mature into cylindrical, woody pods containing seeds.

Vincetoxicum stenopetalum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
