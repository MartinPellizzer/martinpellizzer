Vincetoxicum hirundinaria subsp. cretaceum

Vincetoxicum hirundinaria subsp.

cretaceum, commonly known as the Cretaceous bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 2 meters in length, often climbing or trailing along the ground.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is often found in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
