Vernonia friisii

Vernonia friisii, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color. Its most striking feature is the bright yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Vernonia friisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
