Viola tricolor var. unguiculata

Viola tricolor var. unguiculata, commonly known as the purple heart violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, colorful flowers that typically display combinations of purple, white, and yellow. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

The variety is distinguished by its heart-shaped markings on the petals, which give it its common name.
