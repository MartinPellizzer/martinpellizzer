Vignea scirpoides

Vignea scirpoides, commonly known as the swamp morning glory, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in wet conditions make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.

Vignea scirpoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
