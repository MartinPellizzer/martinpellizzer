Veronica stenophylla var. hesperia

Veronica stenophylla var. hesperia, commonly known as the Hesperia speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the stems are usually low-growing and sprawling.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
