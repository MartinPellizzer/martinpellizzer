Vernonia martini

Vernonia martini, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wet meadows. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia martini is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
