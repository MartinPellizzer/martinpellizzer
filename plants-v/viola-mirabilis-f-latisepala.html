Viola mirabilis f. latisepala

Viola mirabilis f. latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled violet, is a striking hybrid plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with broad, fleshy sepals that often resemble petals, creating a dramatic visual effect. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland gardens or under forest canopies. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often a deep green.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors and unique floral structure.
