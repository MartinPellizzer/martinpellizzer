Viola notoniana

Viola notoniana, commonly known as the Southern Violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters and features heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, blooming from late winter to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Viola notoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
