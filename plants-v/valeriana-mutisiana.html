Valeriana mutisiana

Valeriana mutisiana, commonly known as the Andean valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in temperate to cold climates. Its root system is known for containing essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Valeriana mutisiana is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and sedative effects.
