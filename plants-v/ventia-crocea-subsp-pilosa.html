Ventia crocea subsp. pilosa

Ventia crocea subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the hairy ventia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.
