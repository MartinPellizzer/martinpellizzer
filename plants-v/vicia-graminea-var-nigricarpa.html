Vicia graminea var. nigricarpa

Vicia graminea var. nigricarpa, commonly known as black-seeded vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruit is a pod containing several dark, rounded seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a forage crop due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
