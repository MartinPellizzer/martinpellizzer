Vaccinium angustifolium f. chiococcum

Vaccinium angustifolium f. chiococcum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a variant of the lowbush blueberry.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and compact growth habit. This plant is native to the northeastern United States and parts of Canada, typically growing in acidic, well-drained soils. It produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and used in culinary applications.

The botanical features of this form make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
