Vagnera trifolia f. bifolia

Vagnera trifolia f. bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved form of the species, is a unique variant characterized by its bifoliate leaves.

This plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a deep green color. It produces small, white flowers during the spring season, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
