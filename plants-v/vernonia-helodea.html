Vernonia helodea

Vernonia helodea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Vernonia helodea is also known for its drought tolerance and adaptability to various soil types.
