Vitis pterophora

Vitis pterophora, commonly known as the grapevine leafminer, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, typically appearing in clusters.

Vitis pterophora is primarily valued for its ecological role as a host plant for various insect species, though it is not commonly cultivated for agricultural purposes.
