Vernonanthura diffusa

Vernonanthura diffusa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
