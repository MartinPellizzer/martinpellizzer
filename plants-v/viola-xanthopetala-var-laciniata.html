Viola xanthopetala var. laciniata

Viola xanthopetala var. laciniata, commonly known as the yellow violet or the cut-leaved viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive violet throat, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
