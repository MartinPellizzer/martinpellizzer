Vernonia pseudoincana

Vernonia pseudoincana, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the southern United States, particularly Texas and Louisiana. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, blue to purple flowers appear in dense, conical clusters, attracting various pollinators.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.
