Vignea vulpinoidea

Vignea vulpinoidea, commonly known as the foxglove vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Vignea vulpinoidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
