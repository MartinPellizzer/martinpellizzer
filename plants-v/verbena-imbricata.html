Verbena imbricata

Verbena imbricata, commonly known as Mexican verbena, is an annual herb native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena imbricata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
