Vernonia veadeiroensis

Vernonia veadeiroensis, commonly known as the Veadeiro vervain, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the dry season.

Vernonia veadeiroensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses among local communities.
