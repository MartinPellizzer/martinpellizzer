Verbascum simplex var. warionis

Verbascum simplex var. warionis, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
