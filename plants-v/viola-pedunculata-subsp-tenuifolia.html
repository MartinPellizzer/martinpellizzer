Viola pedunculata subsp. tenuifolia

Viola pedunculata subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces delicate purple or violet flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully expand.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
