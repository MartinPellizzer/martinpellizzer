Veronica chionantha

Veronica chionantha, commonly known as the snow veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and dense clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces slender, thread-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its delicate blooms appear in late spring to early summer, adding a subtle yet charming presence to the landscape.
