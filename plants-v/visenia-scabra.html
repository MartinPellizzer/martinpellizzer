Visenia scabra

Visenia scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf visenia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round, orange to red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Visenia scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
