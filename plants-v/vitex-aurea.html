Vitex aurea

Vitex aurea, commonly known as the golden helix, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. These flowers are followed by small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Vitex aurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
