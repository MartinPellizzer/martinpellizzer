Vitex yaundensis

Vitex yaundensis, commonly known as the Cameroon chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Cameroon.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to purple, blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Vitex yaundensis is often found in moist forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
