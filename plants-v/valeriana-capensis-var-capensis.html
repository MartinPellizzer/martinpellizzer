Valeriana capensis var. capensis

Valeriana capensis var. capensis, commonly known as the Cape valerian, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Valeriana capensis var. capensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
