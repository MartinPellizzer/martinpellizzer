Verbascum thapsiforme var. laxum

Verbascum thapsiforme var. laxum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in elongated spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and also covered in a thick layer of fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly fields.
