Veronica diversifolia

Veronica diversifolia, commonly known as the variable speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have five petals and are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Veronica diversifolia is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
