Viscaria alpina var. serpentinicola

Viscaria alpina var. serpentinicola, commonly known as the serpentine rockweed, is a variant of the common mouse-ear chickweed.

It is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and rocky habitats, particularly on serpentine soils. This plant has small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of white flowers. It is adapted to nutrient-poor, acidic conditions typical of serpentine substrates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of alpine flora.
