Veratrum versicolor

Veratrum versicolor, commonly known as the purple false hellebore, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant's leaves are large, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals if ingested.
