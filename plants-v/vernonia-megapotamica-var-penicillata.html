Vernonia megapotamica var. penicillata

Vernonia megapotamica var. penicillata, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and dense clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry to moist grasslands and is often found in disturbed areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in folk medicine.
