Vauanthes grammanthoides

Vauanthes grammanthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf papyrus, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clumps, often forming a rosette structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Vauanthes grammanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
