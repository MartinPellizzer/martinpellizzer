Valeriana locusta var. radiata

Valeriana locusta var. radiata, commonly known as the Radiate Valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, aromatic scent.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
