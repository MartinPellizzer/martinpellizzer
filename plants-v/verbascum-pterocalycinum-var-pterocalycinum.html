Verbascum pterocalycinum var. pterocalycinum

Verbascum pterocalycinum var. pterocalycinum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems covered in dense, woolly white hairs. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and also covered in a thick layer of silky hairs.

This species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
