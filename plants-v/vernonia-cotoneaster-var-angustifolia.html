Vernonia cotoneaster var. angustifolia

Vernonia cotoneaster var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironwood, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions. Its woody stems and dense growth habit make it an important component of desert ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
