Valeriana costata

Valeriana costata, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a robust root system and erect, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense spikes, typical of the Valerianaceae family. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a strong, spicy aroma.

Valeriana costata is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its sedative and calming effects.
