Viola parvula var. subarachnoidea

Viola parvula var. subarachnoidea, commonly known as the small violet or lesser violet, is a delicate flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in the eastern United States.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
