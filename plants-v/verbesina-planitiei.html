Verbesina planitiei

Verbesina planitiei, commonly known as the plains beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial that typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and prairies. The plant features yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are opposite, with a rough texture. It has hairy stems and produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Verbesina planitiei is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in the Great Plains region.
