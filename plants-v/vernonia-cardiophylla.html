Vernonia cardiophylla

Vernonia cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaved Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are heart-shaped, hence the common name, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia cardiophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
