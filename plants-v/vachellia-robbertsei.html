Vachellia robbertsei

Vachellia robbertsei, commonly known as the Robberts' acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular heads, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

Vachellia robbertsei is also valued for its hardiness and use in traditional medicine.
