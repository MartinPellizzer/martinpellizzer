Viola rossii var. atropurpurea

Viola rossii var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a purplish tinge. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive spurred petal, which is a characteristic feature of the Viola genus. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, particularly for rock gardens and shaded borders.
