Viola summa

Viola summa, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

Viola summa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
