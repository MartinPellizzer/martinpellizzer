Vigna pseudovenulosa

Vigna pseudovenulosa, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Vigna pseudovenulosa is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
