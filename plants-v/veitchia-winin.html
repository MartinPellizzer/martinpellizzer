Veitchia winin

Veitchia winin, commonly known as the purple-leaf veitchia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that display a vibrant purple hue, especially under full sun exposure. The plant produces large, pendulous inflorescences with fragrant, white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and tolerate occasional drought.

Veitchia winin is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
