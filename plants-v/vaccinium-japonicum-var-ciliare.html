Vaccinium japonicum var. ciliare

Vaccinium japonicum var. ciliare, commonly known as the Japanese huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark blue to black berries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are bell-shaped and usually pink or white, appearing in the spring.
