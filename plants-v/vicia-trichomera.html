Vicia trichomera

Vicia trichomera, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. Vicia trichomera is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration and agriculture.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
