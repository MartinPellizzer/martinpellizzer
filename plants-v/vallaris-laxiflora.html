Vallaris laxiflora

Vallaris laxiflora, commonly known as the slender vallary, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of pink or white.

Vallaris laxiflora is noted for its adaptability to dry conditions and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
