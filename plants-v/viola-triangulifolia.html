Viola triangulifolia

Viola triangulifolia, commonly known as the triangular-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive triangular outline, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or violet, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
