Vernonia baronii

Vernonia baronii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Vernonia baronii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
