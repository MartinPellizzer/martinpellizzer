Vicia leucophaea var. mediocincta

Vicia leucophaea var. mediocincta, commonly known as the pale-winged vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and is characterized by its distinctive pale veins on the foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
