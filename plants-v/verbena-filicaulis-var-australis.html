Verbena filicaulis var. australis

Verbena filicaulis var. australis, commonly known as the Australian verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to Australia.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
