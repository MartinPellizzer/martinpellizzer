Vernonia kuluina

Vernonia kuluina, commonly known as the Kuluina ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive bract structure that gives it a unique appearance.

Vernonia kuluina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
