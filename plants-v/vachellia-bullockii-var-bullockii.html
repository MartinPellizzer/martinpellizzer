Vachellia bullockii var. bullockii

Vachellia bullockii var. bullockii, commonly known as the Bullock's acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures in dry conditions. The plant produces yellow globular inflorescences that attract various pollinators, including bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood and traditional uses in local communities.
