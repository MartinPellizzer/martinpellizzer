Vaccinium malaccense var. lobbii

Vaccinium malaccense var. lobbii, commonly known as the Malacca blueberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by berries that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local horticulture.
