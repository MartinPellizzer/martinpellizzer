Viola semperflorens

Viola semperflorens, commonly known as the common violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet or purple flowers. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated as a garden plant due to its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

Viola semperflorens is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
