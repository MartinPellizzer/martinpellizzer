Vitex bakeri

Vitex bakeri, commonly known as the red flowering vitex, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, which range in color from white to deep purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Vitex bakeri is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
