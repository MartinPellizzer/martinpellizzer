Virectaria angustifolia var. angustifolia

Virectaria angustifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf virectaria, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical or subtropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
