Vernonia sarmentiana

Vernonia sarmentiana, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tall, branched inflorescences that produce small, purple to brown flowers. The plant is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Vernonia sarmentiana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and fall blooms.
