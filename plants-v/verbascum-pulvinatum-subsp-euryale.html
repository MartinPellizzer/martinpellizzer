Verbascum pulvinatum subsp. euryale

Verbascum pulvinatum subsp.

euryale, commonly known as the tall mullein, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered in dense, fuzzy hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters at the top of the stems, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a thick layer of soft, woolly hair.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
