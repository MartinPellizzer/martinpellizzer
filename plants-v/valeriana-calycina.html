Valeriana calycina

Valeriana calycina, commonly known as the pink valerian, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its root system is fibrous and aromatic. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Valeriana calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
