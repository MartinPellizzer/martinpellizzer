Veronica orbelica

Veronica orbelica, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Veronica orbelica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
