Vicia sexajuga

Vicia sexajuga, commonly known as the six-winged vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruit that splits into six segments, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

Vicia sexajuga is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
