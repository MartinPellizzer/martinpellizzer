Viburnum meyer-waldeckii

Viburnum meyer-waldeckii, commonly known as the Meyer-Waldeck viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.

Viburnum meyer-waldeckii is often used in landscaping for its seasonal interest and compact growth habit.
