Vernonia incompta

Vernonia incompta, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and dense, rounded crown. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in heads, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Vernonia incompta is well adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
