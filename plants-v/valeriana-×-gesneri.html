Valeriana × gesneri

Valeriana × gesneri, commonly known as the hybrid valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant that results from the cross between Valeriana officinalis and Valeriana gilliesii.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Valeriana × gesneri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in shaded garden settings.
