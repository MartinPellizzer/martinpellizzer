Verbascum × semivulcanicum

Verbascum × semivulcanicum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a flowering plant in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum wrightii, showcasing characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting a robust, hairy stem and large, woolly leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, clustered in dense, showy inflorescences.

Verbascum × semivulcanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
