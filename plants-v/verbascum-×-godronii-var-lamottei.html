Verbascum × godronii var. lamottei

Verbascum × godronii var. lamottei, commonly known as the Lamotte's mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Verbascum, which includes species commonly referred to as mulleins.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid climates.
