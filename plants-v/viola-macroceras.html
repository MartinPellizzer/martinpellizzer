Viola macroceras

Viola macroceras, commonly known as the large-capsule violet, is a species of flowering plant in the viola genus.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a distinctive, inflated capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Viola macroceras is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
