Viola gracilis f. heterophylla

Viola gracilis f. heterophylla, commonly known as the slender violet variant, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens. Its foliage and flowers are often used in horticultural displays for their ornamental value.

This cultivar is known for its compact growth habit and ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable conditions.
