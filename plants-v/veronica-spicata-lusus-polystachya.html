Veronica spicata lusus polystachya

Veronica spicata lusus polystachya, commonly known as the spiked speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its foliage is typically lance-shaped with a silvery underside, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
