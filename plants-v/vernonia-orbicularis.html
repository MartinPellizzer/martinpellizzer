Vernonia orbicularis

Vernonia orbicularis, commonly known as the globe vervain, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its orbicular, or nearly round, leaves and its spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Vernonia orbicularis is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
