Verbena juniperina

Verbena juniperina, commonly known as the Juniper verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and a compact, shrub-like growth habit.

Verbena juniperina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
