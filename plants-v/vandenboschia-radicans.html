Vandenboschia radicans

Vandenboschia radicans, commonly known as the creeping ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its roots are extensive and help stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control.

Vandenboschia radicans is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
