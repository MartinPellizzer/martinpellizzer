Valeriana virescens var. montteana

Valeriana virescens var. montteana, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features a robust root system and erect, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
