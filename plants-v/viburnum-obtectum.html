Viburnum obtectum

Viburnum obtectum, commonly known as the dwarf snowball viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces dark blue, waxy berries that are an important food source for birds. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and they turn red or orange in the fall.

Viburnum obtectum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
