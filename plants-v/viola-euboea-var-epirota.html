Viola euboea var. epirota

Viola euboea var. epirota, commonly known as the Euboean violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
