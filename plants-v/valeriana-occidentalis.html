Valeriana occidentalis

Valeriana occidentalis, commonly known as western valerian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open areas such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds responsible for its distinctive, often spicy or sweet scent.

Valeriana occidentalis is valued in traditional medicine for its potential calming properties.
