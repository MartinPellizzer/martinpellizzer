Valeriana utahensis

Valeriana utahensis, commonly known as Utah valerian, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often violet-colored flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in areas such as Utah, Nevada, and Arizona. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and have a strong, spicy scent.

Utah valerian is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
