Vicia ervilla

Vicia ervilla, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pea-like, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Vicia ervilla is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
