Viburnum rosaceum

Viburnum rosaceum, commonly known as the roseleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features showy, white to pink flowers that appear in clusters during late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in partial to full shade.

Viburnum rosaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
