Viola lactiflora

Viola lactiflora, commonly known as the milk vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola lactiflora is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
