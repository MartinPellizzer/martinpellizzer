Vicia truncata

Vicia truncata, commonly known as the cut-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Vicia truncata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
