Vitis capensis

Vitis capensis, commonly known as the African grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its fruits, which are small berries, are edible and have a tart flavor.

Vitis capensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
