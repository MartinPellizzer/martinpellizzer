Vigna mensensis var. hastata

Vigna mensensis var. hastata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

The species is also appreciated for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
