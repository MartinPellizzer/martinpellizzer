Vernonia polymorpha

Vernonia polymorpha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Vernonia polymorpha is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
