Vincentia robinsonii

Vincentia robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's vining plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow or white flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its seeds are encased in pods, a typical feature of the Fabaceae family, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
