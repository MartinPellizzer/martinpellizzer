Verbesina megapotamica

Verbesina megapotamica, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in cone-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in ecological gardens.
