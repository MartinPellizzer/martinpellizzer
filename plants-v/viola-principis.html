Viola principis

Viola principis, commonly known as the emperor's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, with five petals arranged in a distinctive pattern. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Viola principis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in herbal medicine.
