Vernonia fuscopilosa

Vernonia fuscopilosa, commonly known as the dark-haired ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, dark hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow with purple-tinted edges, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
