Vochysia rufa subsp. eurufa

Vochysia rufa subsp.

eurufa, commonly known as the red vochysia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy and smooth, reddish-brown bark.

The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic features.
