Viola lechleri

Viola lechleri, commonly known as the Amazonian balsa or "uya" in some indigenous languages, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet flowers. The plant is notable for its latex, which contains compounds with potential medicinal properties. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is also a subject of conservation interest.
