Vaccinium wallichianum

Vaccinium wallichianum, commonly known as the Himalayan blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Its leaves are dark green and leathery, and it is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
