Vaccinium obovalifolium

Vaccinium obovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaved huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible berries that are typically blue or black. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for various wildlife species.
