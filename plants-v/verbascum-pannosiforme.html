Verbascum pannosiforme

Verbascum pannosiforme, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in dense, soft hairs that give it a woolly appearance.

Verbascum pannosiforme is also known for its medicinal properties, having been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
