Vigna grandiflora

Vigna grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.

Vigna grandiflora thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
