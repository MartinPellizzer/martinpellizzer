Verbesina phlebodes

Verbesina phlebodes, commonly known as the showy ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets, typical of the sunflower family. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Verbesina phlebodes thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
