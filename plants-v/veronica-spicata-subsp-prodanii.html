Veronica spicata subsp. prodanii

Veronica spicata subsp.

prodanii, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its erect, spiky inflorescences that bear small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and early spring blooms.
