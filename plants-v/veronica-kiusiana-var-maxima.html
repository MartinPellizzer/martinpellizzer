Veronica kiusiana var. maxima

Veronica kiusiana var. maxima, commonly known as the giant veronica, is a striking perennial plant native to the high altitudes of East Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is valued both for ornamental purposes and ecological significance in its native habitat.
