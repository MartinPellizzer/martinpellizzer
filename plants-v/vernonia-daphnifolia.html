Vernonia daphnifolia

Vernonia daphnifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-blue foliage and clusters of small, yellow-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Vernonia daphnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
