Verbascum pulvinatum

Verbascum pulvinatum, commonly known as the tall mullein, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety gray-green leaves that are covered in soft hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, elongated inflorescence. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Verbascum pulvinatum is known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
