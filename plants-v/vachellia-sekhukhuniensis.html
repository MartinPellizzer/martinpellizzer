Vachellia sekhukhuniensis

Vachellia sekhukhuniensis, commonly known as the Khama thornveld acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate, and its yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as deep root systems. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various animals.

The wood of Vachellia sekhukhuniensis is sometimes used for fuel and construction, though it is not as economically significant as other acacia species.
