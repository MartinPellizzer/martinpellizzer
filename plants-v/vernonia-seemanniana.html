Vernonia seemanniana

Vernonia seemanniana, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to saturated soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
