Veronica tenuis

Veronica tenuis, commonly known as slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica tenuis thrives in rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as slopes and grasslands.
