Viola variegata var. typica

Viola variegata var. typica, commonly known as the variegated violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinct variegation, often featuring white or cream markings. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and showy blooms.
