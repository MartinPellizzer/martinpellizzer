Veronica × lackschewitzii

Veronica × lackschewitzii, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
