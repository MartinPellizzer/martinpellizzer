Viburnum × rhytidocarpum

Viburnum × rhytidocarpum, commonly known as the Japanese snowball bush, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds but not for humans.

Viburnum × rhytidocarpum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
