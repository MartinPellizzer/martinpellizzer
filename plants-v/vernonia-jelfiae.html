Vernonia jelfiae

Vernonia jelfiae, commonly known as the Jelf's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

Vernonia jelfiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
