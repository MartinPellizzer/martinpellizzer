Veronica longifolia var. cordifolia

Veronica longifolia var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
