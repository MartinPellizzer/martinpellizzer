Vilfa articulata

Vilfa articulata, commonly known as the articulated vilfa, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through features such as reduced leaf surface area and deep root systems. Its flowers are typically pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Vilfa articulata is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
