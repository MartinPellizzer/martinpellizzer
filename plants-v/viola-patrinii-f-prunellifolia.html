Viola patrinii f. prunellifolia

Viola patrinii f. prunellifolia, commonly known as the Prunellifolia violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
