Veronica longipedicellata

Veronica longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stemmed speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
