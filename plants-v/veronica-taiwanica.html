Veronica taiwanica

Veronica taiwanica, commonly known as Taiwan speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Veronica taiwanica is often found in rocky or open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
