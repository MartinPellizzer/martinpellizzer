Veronica beccabunga var. minor

Veronica beccabunga var. minor, commonly known as the lesser water speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its habitat. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
