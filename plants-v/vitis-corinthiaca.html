Vitis corinthiaca

Vitis corinthiaca, commonly known as Corinthian grape, is a species of flowering vine native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. Vitis corinthiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in ancient Greek viticulture.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and vineyards in temperate climates.
