Viscaria sartorii

Viscaria sartorii, commonly known as the common viscaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Viscaria sartorii is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
