Veronica brassii

Veronica brassii, commonly known as the Alpine Speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It has slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica brassii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
