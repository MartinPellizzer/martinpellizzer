Vicia sativa var. canariensis

Vicia sativa var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and elongated, pea-like pods. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in Mediterranean climates. It produces small, usually pink or white flowers that bloom in spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
