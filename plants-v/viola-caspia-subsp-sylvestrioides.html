Viola caspia subsp. sylvestrioides

Viola caspia subsp.

sylvestrioides, commonly known as the Eurasian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It features heart-shaped, often hairy leaves and produces delicate violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
