Vetrix purpurea

Vetrix purpurea, commonly known as the purple spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a distinctive purple or reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Vetrix purpurea is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient plant in various habitats.
