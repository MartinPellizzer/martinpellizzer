Viola acuta

Viola acuta, commonly known as the acute viola, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple. Viola acuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape during that season.
