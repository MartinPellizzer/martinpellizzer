Viola domestica

Viola domestica, commonly known as the common violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and gardens. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola domestica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
