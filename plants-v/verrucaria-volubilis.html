Verrucaria volubilis

Verrucaria volubilis, commonly known as the creeping cup lichen, is a species of lichen belonging to the family Verrucariaceae.

It is a foliose lichen, characterized by its leaf-like thallus that is typically greenish to yellowish in color. The plant grows on moist, shaded rock surfaces and is often found in temperate regions. Its structure consists of a cortex, medulla, and hymenium, with the upper surface bearing a network of pores.

Verrucaria volubilis plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for various invertebrates.
