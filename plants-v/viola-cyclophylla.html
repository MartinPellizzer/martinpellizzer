Viola cyclophylla

Viola cyclophylla, commonly known as the cyclophylla violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region, where it thrives in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Viola cyclophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
