Valeriana cornucopiae

Valeriana cornucopiae, commonly known as the horned valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Valeriana cornucopiae is valued both for its aesthetic qualities and its historical use in traditional medicine.
