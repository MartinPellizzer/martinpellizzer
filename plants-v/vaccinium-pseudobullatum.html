Vaccinium pseudobullatum

Vaccinium pseudobullatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and heathers. Its leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a glossy green appearance, and the plant bears edible, dark blue berries in the summer.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in wetland or swampy habitats.
