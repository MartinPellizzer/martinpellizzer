Vincentia kerstingii

Vincentia kerstingii, commonly known as the Kersting's evergreen vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces pods that contain flattened, kidney-shaped seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens in warm climates.
