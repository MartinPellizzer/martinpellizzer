Viola riparia

Viola riparia, commonly known as the river violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows along the banks of rivers and streams, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or blue. Viola riparia is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to periodic flooding.

It plays a role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
