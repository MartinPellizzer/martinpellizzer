Vanilla odorata

Vanilla odorata, commonly known as the orchid vanilla, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by specific bees. Its most notable feature is the vanilla pod, which develops from the flower and is used as a flavoring agent.

The plant requires a humid, warm climate and is often cultivated in shaded environments.
