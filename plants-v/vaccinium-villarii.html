Vaccinium villarii

Vaccinium villarii, commonly known as the Brazilian huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes cranberries, blueberries, and other acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Vaccinium villarii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
