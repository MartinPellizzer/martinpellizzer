Valeriana cumbemayensis

Valeriana cumbemayensis, commonly known as the Cumbemay Valley valerian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in Peru.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in whorls around the stem, while its roots are fibrous and have a strong, spicy scent.

Valeriana cumbemayensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used traditionally in local remedies.
