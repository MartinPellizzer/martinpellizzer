Vernonia abyssinica

Vernonia abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its scientific name reflects its origin and its historical use in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
