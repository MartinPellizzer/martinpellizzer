Vicia pisiformis

Vicia pisiformis, commonly known as the kidney bean vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used as a food source. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Vicia pisiformis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in agricultural settings.
