Vernonia calulu var. calulu

Vernonia calulu var. calulu, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray inflorescences that resemble mist, which appear in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia calulu var. calulu is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
