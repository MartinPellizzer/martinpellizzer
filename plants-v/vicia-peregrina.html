Vicia peregrina

Vicia peregrina, commonly known as the wandering vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 60 cm tall, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Vicia peregrina is adapted to dry and rocky habitats, showcasing its resilience in arid environments.

It is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.
