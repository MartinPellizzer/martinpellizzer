Valeriana weddelliana

Valeriana weddelliana, commonly known as Antarctic valerian, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Antarctica and the sub-Antarctic.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in harsh, icy environments, typically growing in rocky soils and on nunataks. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the short summer season. Its root system is adapted to store nutrients and water, allowing it to survive extreme cold and limited sunlight.

Valeriana weddelliana plays a crucial role in the local ecosystem, providing food for native insects and contributing to the biodiversity of its unique habitat.
