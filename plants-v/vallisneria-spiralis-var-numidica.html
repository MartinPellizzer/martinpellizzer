Vallisneria spiralis var. numidica

Vallisneria spiralis var. numidica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a aquatic plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and dark green. This variety thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth makes it both a valuable addition to water gardens and a potential invasive species in non-native habitats.
