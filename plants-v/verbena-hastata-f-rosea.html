Verbena hastata f. rosea

Verbena hastata f. rosea, commonly known as the rose verbena, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and prairies. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
