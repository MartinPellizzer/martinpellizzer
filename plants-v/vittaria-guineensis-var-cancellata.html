Vittaria guineensis var. cancellata

Vittaria guineensis var. cancellata, commonly known as the cancellate fern, is a rare and delicate epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, thread-like fronds that give it a lace-like appearance, and it often grows on tree trunks or branches in shaded, humid environments. The plant's leaves are usually pale green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
