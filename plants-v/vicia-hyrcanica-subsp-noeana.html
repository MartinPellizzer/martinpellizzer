Vicia hyrcanica subsp. noeana

Vicia hyrcanica subsp.

noeana, commonly known as the Hyrcanian vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, lens-shaped seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
