Vangueriella chlorantha

Vangueriella chlorantha, commonly known as the green vangueriella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually bright green, with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in clusters, and bloom in the spring.

Vangueriella chlorantha is known for its resilience and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in dry landscapes.
