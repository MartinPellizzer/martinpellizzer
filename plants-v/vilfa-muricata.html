Vilfa muricata

Vilfa muricata, commonly known as the spiked velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Vilfa muricata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along riverbanks.
