Verbascum grossum

Verbascum grossum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum grossum is also valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
