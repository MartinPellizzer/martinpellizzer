Viola faurieana

Viola faurieana, commonly known as the Faurie's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola faurieana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
