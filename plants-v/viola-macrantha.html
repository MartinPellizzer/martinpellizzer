Viola macrantha

Viola macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals. The plant typically blooms in spring and early summer, reaching heights of up to 12 inches. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
