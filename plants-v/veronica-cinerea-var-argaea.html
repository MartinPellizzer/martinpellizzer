Veronica cinerea var. argaea

Veronica cinerea var. argaea, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Veronica cinerea var. argaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its visual interest.
