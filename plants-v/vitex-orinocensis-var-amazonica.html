Vitex orinocensis var. amazonica

Vitex orinocensis var. amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian chaste tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically lavender or white in color, arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
