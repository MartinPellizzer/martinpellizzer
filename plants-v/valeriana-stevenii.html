Valeriana stevenii

Valeriana stevenii, commonly known as the Steven's valerian, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and emits a mild, aromatic scent. Valeriana stevenii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
