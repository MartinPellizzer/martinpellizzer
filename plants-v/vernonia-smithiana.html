Vernonia smithiana

Vernonia smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. Vernonia smithiana is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
