Viola sagotii

Viola sagotii, commonly known as the Sagot's violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola sagotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
