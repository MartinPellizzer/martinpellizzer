Vernonia vahliana f. rotundifolia

Vernonia vahliana f. rotundifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green to reddish-brown flowers. The leaves are typically broad and oval, which distinguishes it from other members of the Vernonia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This plant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
