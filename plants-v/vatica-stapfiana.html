Vatica stapfiana

Vatica stapfiana, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and often have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Vatica stapfiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
