Vitex oxycuspis var. mossambicensis

Vitex oxycuspis var. mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or white, which bloom in summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
