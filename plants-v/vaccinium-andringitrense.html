Vaccinium andringitrense

Vaccinium andringitrense, commonly known as the Andringa cranberry, is a species of shrub native to the southern Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many evergreen shrubs and small trees. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are similar in appearance to cranberries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
