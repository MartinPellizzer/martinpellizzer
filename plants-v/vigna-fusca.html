Vigna fusca

Vigna fusca, commonly known as the dark cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, hairy pods containing edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

Vigna fusca is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting subsequent crops.
