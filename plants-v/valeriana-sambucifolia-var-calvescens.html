Valeriana sambucifolia var. calvescens

Valeriana sambucifolia var. calvescens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and lanceolate, often hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils and compounds with potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
