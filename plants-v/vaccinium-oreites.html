Vaccinium oreites

Vaccinium oreites, commonly known as the highbush blueberry, is a shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries that range in color from white to deep blue. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary applications.

Vaccinium oreites thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
