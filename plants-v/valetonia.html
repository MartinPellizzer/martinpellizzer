Valetonia

Valetonia, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that resemble orchids in structure. These plants are typically found in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Valetonia species are known for their compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

They are primarily found in southern Africa, with some species extending into parts of Australia and the Mediterranean.
