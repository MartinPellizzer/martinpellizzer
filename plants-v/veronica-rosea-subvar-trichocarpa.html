Veronica rosea subvar. trichocarpa

Veronica rosea subvar. trichocarpa, commonly known as the hairy veronica, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
