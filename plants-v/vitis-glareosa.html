Vitis glareosa

Vitis glareosa, commonly known as the sand grape, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Vitis glareosa is typically found in sandy or rocky soils along stream banks and woodland edges.
