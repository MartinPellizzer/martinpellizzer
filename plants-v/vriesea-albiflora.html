Vriesea albiflora

Vriesea albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered bromeliad, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports aquatic life. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with bright white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often variegated with silver and green tones, adding visual interest to its appearance.

Vriesea albiflora is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability to low light conditions and minimal water requirements.
