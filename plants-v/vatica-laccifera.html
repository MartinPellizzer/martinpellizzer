Vatica laccifera

Vatica laccifera, commonly known as the lac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, glossy bark. The tree is valued for its ability to produce a valuable resin called lac, which is harvested from its sap. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, warm climates with high rainfall.

Vatica laccifera plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
