Verbena lavandulifolia var. colchaguensis

Verbena lavandulifolia var. colchaguensis, commonly known as the Chilean verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early autumn.
