Viola columnaris

Viola columnaris, commonly known as the upright violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet-green color. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet. Viola columnaris is typically found in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
