Vittaria debilis

Vittaria debilis, commonly known as the slender fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows on tree trunks or branches, forming dense mats of delicate, light green fronds. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces small, spore-bearing leaves called sporangia. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with closely spaced, narrow pinnae.

Vittaria debilis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
