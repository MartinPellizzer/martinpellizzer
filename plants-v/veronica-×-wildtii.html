Veronica × wildtii

Veronica × wildtii, commonly known as the wild thyme or creeping veronica, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, slightly hairy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period and drought tolerance.
