Verbesina virginica

Verbesina virginica, commonly known as yellow beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and roadsides. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom from July to September. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The seeds are small, dry, and dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across various habitats.
