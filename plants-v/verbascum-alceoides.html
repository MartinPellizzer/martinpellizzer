Verbascum alceoides

Verbascum alceoides, commonly known as the alpine mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, with large, gray-green, fuzzy leaves. It produces showy, yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a dense, spike-like inflorescence.

Verbascum alceoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
