Veronica forsteri

Veronica forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense mat on the ground.

Veronica forsteri is often found in coastal and subalpine areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
