Viola scandens var. integristipula

Viola scandens var. integristipula, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet hue and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant has a trailing growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
