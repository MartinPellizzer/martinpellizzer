Vicia lutea subsp. cavanillesii

Vicia lutea subsp.

cavanillesii, commonly known as the Canary Islands vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, followed by lens-shaped pods containing several seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

This subspecies is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, contributing to local biodiversity.
