Veronica cymbalaria

Veronica cymbalaria, commonly known as mouse-ear speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Veronica cymbalaria is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
