Valeriana hirsutissima

Valeriana hirsutissima, commonly known as the rough valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative properties.

Valeriana hirsutissima thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
