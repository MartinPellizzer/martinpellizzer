Viburnum foetidum var. griffithianum

Viburnum foetidum var. griffithianum, commonly known as the Indian hawthorn, is a shrub native to southern India and Sri Lanka.

It features dense, glossy evergreen foliage that provides year-round visual interest. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
