Veronicastrum stenostachyum subsp. stenostachyum

Veronicastrum stenostachyum subsp.

stenostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stalked blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and riverbanks.

The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy blue to purple flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in wetland ecosystems.
