Vernonia pinaster

Vernonia pinaster, commonly known as the ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Vernonia pinaster is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
