Vicia raynaudii

Vicia raynaudii, commonly known as the Raynaud's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, and it has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Vicia raynaudii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
