Vaccinium jacobeanum

Vaccinium jacobeanum, commonly known as the Jacobean huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes blueberries and other heather-like plants. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. Its fruit, a small, dark blue berry, is edible and has a tart flavor.

Vaccinium jacobeanum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats.
