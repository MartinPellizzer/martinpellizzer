Veratrum misae

Veratrum misae, commonly known as the Japanese beetle root, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale green flowers in late summer, which are followed by dark blue berries. Its roots are toxic and contain various alkaloids, making it a plant of both ecological and medicinal interest.

Veratrum misae thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
