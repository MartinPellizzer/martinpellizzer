Veronica azurea

Veronica azurea, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica azurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and meadows.
