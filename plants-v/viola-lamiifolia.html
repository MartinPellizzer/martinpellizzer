Viola lamiifolia

Viola lamiifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, are often purple or violet in color and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are broad and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
