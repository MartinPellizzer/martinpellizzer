Veronica lasiocarpa

Veronica lasiocarpa, commonly known as the wooly speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving at high elevations. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Veronica lasiocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
