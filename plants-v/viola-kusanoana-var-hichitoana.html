Viola kusanoana var. hichitoana

Viola kusanoana var. hichitoana, commonly known as the Hichito's violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
