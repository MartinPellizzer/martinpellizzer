Valeriana calcitrapae

Valeriana calcitrapae, commonly known as the limestone valerian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in calcareous soils, thriving in rocky or limestone-rich environments. The plant features upright, branched stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and are usually pink or white in color.

Valeriana calcitrapae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming properties.
