Vitex gomphophylla

Vitex gomphophylla, commonly known as the African blueberry or African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Vitex gomphophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
