Vigna tenuis

Vigna tenuis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to grow in poor soils and is often used as a cover crop.

Vigna tenuis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
