Viola canina var. pumila

Viola canina var. pumila, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purplish underside and produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its compact growth habit makes it well-suited for ground cover in gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
