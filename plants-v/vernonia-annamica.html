Vernonia annamica

Vernonia annamica, commonly known as the Annam vernonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy yellow flowers are often surrounded by pale green bracts, making it visually distinct.

Vernonia annamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
