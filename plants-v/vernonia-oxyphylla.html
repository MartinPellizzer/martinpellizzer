Vernonia oxyphylla

Vernonia oxyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds. Vernonia oxyphylla is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
