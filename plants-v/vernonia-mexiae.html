Vernonia mexiae

Vernonia mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its tall, erect stems and purple to brownish flower heads. Vernonia mexiae produces small, tubular flowers that are arranged in a dense cluster, often surrounded by bracts.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
