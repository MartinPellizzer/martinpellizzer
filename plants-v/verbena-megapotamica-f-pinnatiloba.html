Verbena megapotamica f. pinnatiloba

Verbena megapotamica f. pinnatiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pinnately lobed leaves and clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
