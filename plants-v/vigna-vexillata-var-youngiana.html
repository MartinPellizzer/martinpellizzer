Vigna vexillata var. youngiana

Vigna vexillata var. youngiana, commonly known as the dwarf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often grown as a drought-tolerant crop.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both agricultural and ornamental purposes.
