Veronica cycnorum

Veronica cycnorum, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, blue to purple flowers clustered in pairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica cycnorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
