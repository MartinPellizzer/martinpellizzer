Vigna mukerjeeanus

Vigna mukerjeeanus, commonly known as the Mukerjee's pigeonpea, is a leguminous plant native to India.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing small, edible seeds that are rich in protein. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown for its nutritional value.

Due to its resilience, it is also used in agroforestry and soil improvement practices.
