Verbesina pichinchensis

Verbesina pichinchensis, commonly known as the Andean ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats at high altitudes, often in the Andes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Verbesina pichinchensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
