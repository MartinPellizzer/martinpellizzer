Valeriana mongolica

Valeriana mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish-brown roots. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and forested areas, thriving in well-drained soil. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and bloom in the summer months.

Valeriana mongolica is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
