Veronica stelleri f. rufescens

Veronica stelleri f. rufescens, commonly known as the rusty veronica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive rust-colored underside, and it produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
