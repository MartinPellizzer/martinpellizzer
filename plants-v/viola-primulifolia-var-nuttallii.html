Viola primulifolia var. nuttallii

Viola primulifolia var. nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland environments and has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with five petals, often marked with white or yellow patterns. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Nuttall's violet is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
