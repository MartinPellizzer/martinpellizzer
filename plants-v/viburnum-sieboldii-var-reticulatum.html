Viburnum sieboldii var. reticulatum

Viburnum sieboldii var. reticulatum, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and a bluish-green hue on top. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and seasonal interest in gardens.
