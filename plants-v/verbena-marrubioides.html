Verbena marrubioides

Verbena marrubioides, commonly known as wild verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Verbena marrubioides is also known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
