Veronica peregrina subsp. typica

Veronica peregrina subsp.

typica, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features small, blue or purple flowers clustered in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
