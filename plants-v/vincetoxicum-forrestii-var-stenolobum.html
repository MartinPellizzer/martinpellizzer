Vincetoxicum forrestii var. stenolobum

Vincetoxicum forrestii var. stenolobum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed glorybower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. These flowers are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods that turn reddish as they mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
