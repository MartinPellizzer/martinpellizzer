Vaccinium × hybridum

Vaccinium × hybridum, commonly known as the highbush blueberry, is a hybrid shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Vaccinium × hybridum is widely cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
