Viola rhodanica

Viola rhodanica, commonly known as the heartsease, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals. Viola rhodanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.

It also has a long history of use in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
