Viola hidakana

Viola hidakana, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola hidakana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Japanese medicine.
