Vernonia nantcianensis

Vernonia nantcianensis, commonly known as the Nantcianensis ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Vernonia nantcianensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
