Vesalea floribunda var. foliacea

Vesalea floribunda var. foliacea, commonly known as the leafy vesalea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the late spring to early summer. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage and floral display.
