Valeriana pavonii

Valeriana pavonii, commonly known as the purple valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
