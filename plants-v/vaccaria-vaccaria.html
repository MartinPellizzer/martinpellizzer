Vaccaria vaccaria

Vaccaria vaccaria, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits called achenes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

Vaccaria vaccaria is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
