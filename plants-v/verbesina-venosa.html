Verbesina venosa

Verbesina venosa, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems, which are covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Verbesina venosa is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas.
