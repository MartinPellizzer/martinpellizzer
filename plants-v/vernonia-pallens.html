Vernonia pallens

Vernonia pallens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Vernonia pallens is a valuable plant for pollinators and is often used in native plant gardens.
