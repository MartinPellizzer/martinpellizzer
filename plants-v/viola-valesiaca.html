Viola valesiaca

Viola valesiaca, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to high-altitude landscapes.

Viola valesiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
