Victoria argentina

Victoria argentina, commonly known as the Argentine water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday, a trait known as nyctinasty. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow waters and absorbing nutrients from the sediment.

Victoria argentina is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
