Vitex flavescens

Vitex flavescens, commonly known as the yellow chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pale yellow to white in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a rough texture with a slightly serrated edge.

Vitex flavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
