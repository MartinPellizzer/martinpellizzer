Veronica thymoides subsp. hasandaghensis

Veronica thymoides subsp.

hasandaghensis, commonly known as the Persian speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey. It features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens seeking drought-tolerant, ornamental plants.
