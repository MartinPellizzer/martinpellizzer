Vataireopsis surinamensis

Vataireopsis surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam vatairea, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellowish-white flowers.

The tree is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
