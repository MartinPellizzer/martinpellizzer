Vitis palmatifida

Vitis palmatifida, commonly known as the broadleaf grape or the palmately lobed grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions of the Appalachian Mountains. The plant is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible berries.

Vitis palmatifida is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its potential for cultivation.
