Vernonia mansoana

Vernonia mansoana, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Vernonia mansoana is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
