Veratrum speciosum

Veratrum speciosum, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the genus Veratrum, which is part of the family Melanthiaceae. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear dense clusters of small, white to greenish-white flowers arranged in a spicate inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, and it typically grows in moist, woodland habitats.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids and has been used historically in traditional medicine, though with caution due to its potent effects.
