Viola patrinii f. glabra

Viola patrinii f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth viol, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, are usually pale violet or white with a distinctive dark purple throat.

The f. glabra form is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, setting it apart from other varieties of Viola patrinii.
