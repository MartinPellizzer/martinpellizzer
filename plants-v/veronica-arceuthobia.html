Veronica arceuthobia

Veronica arceuthobia, commonly known as the sticky geranium, is a small flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tiny, pink or purple flowers and hairy, sticky leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant has a unique seed dispersal mechanism, as its seeds are sticky and adhere to the fur or feathers of animals. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to arid environments and contribute to its ecological significance.
