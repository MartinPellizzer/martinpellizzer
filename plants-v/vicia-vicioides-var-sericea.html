Vicia vicioides var. sericea

Vicia vicioides var. sericea, commonly known as silky vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy, contributing to its overall texture. Its small, pea-like flowers are typically pink to purple in color and appear in clusters.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
