Verbascum thapsus var. bracteatum

Verbascum thapsus var. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracted mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for various garden environments.

Its common name reflects the presence of bracts, which are modified leaves that surround the flower cluster.
