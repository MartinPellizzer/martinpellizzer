Verbascum graecum var. jankaeanum

Verbascum graecum var. jankaeanum, commonly known as the Greek mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose panicle. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is closely related to other species in the genus Verbascum, known for their medicinal and ornamental uses.
