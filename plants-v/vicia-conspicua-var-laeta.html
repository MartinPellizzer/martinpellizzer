Vicia conspicua var. laeta

Vicia conspicua var. laeta, commonly known as the cheerful vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It features trifoliate leaves with a deep green color and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces slender, hairy pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
