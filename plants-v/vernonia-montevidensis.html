Vernonia montevidensis

Vernonia montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo vernonia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and shrublands, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its yellow-orange inflorescences are showy and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia montevidensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
