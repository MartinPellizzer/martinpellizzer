Vernonia pseudocentauropsis

Vernonia pseudocentauropsis, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular florets arranged in a composite head, typical of the sunflower family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
