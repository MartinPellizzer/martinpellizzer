Vicia sojakii

Vicia sojakii, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Vicia sojakii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

It has been used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock in its native regions.
