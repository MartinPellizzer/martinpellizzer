Valentinia ilicifolia

Valentinia ilicifolia, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a faintly aromatic scent. The plant produces clusters of small, red berries that are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. These berries are typically harvested during the winter months, making it a seasonal plant.

Valentinia ilicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
