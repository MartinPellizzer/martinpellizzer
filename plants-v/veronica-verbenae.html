Veronica verbenae

Veronica verbenae, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas, where it contributes to biodiversity and supports pollinators.
