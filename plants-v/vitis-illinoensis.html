Vitis illinoensis

Vitis illinoensis, commonly known as the Illinois grape, is a deciduous vine native to the central United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach lengths of up to 15 feet. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by clusters of round, black berries. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

Vitis illinoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
