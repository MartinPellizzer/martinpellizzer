Voyria angustiflora

Voyria angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved voyria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
