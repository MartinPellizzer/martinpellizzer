Vernonia filigera

Vernonia filigera, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its showy, conical flower heads are usually blue to purple in color and bloom in late summer.

Vernonia filigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
