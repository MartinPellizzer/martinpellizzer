Viola eriocarpa f. leiocarpa

Viola eriocarpa f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved viola, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous, distinguishing it from other varieties of viola. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
