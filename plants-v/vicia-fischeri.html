Vicia fischeri

Vicia fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually purple or violet in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Vicia fischeri is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
