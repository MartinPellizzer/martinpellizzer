Vitis vinifera var. pulchella

Vitis vinifera var. pulchella, commonly known as the noble grape, is a lesser-known variety within the Vitis vinifera species.

It is characterized by its small, intensely flavored berries that are typically used in niche winemaking. The plant exhibits vigorous growth and is often cultivated in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a deep green color with a slightly hairy underside.

This variety is prized for its unique aromatic profile and is rarely seen in commercial vineyards.
