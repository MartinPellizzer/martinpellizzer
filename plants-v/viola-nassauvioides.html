Viola nassauvioides

Viola nassauvioides, commonly known as the Nassau violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with yellow markings. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 6 to 12 inches.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
