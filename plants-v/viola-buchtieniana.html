Viola buchtieniana

Viola buchtieniana, commonly known as the Buchtien violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to high-altitude landscapes.

Viola buchtieniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
