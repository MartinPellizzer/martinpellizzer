Verbena curtisii

Verbena curtisii, commonly known as Curtis's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Curtis's verbena is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
