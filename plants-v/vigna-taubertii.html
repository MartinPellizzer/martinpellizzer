Vigna taubertii

Vigna taubertii, commonly known as the Taubert's bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Vigna taubertii is often cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
