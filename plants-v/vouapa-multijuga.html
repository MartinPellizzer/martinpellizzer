Vouapa multijuga

Vouapa multijuga, commonly known as the multi-lobed fern, is a unique species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its intricate, feather-like fronds that split into multiple lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in dense forests. Its rhizome system allows it to spread slowly across the forest floor.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
