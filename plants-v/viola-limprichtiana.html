Viola limprichtiana

Viola limprichtiana, commonly known as the Limpricht's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, with five petals and a distinctive spur that extends from the bloom.

Viola limprichtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
