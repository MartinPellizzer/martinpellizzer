Vernonia obconica

Vernonia obconica, commonly known as the coneflower or ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of purple to pink tubular flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Vernonia obconica is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
