Viola × kelleri

Viola × kelleri, commonly known as the Keller's violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial shade. It is a short-lived perennial that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
