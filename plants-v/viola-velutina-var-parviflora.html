Viola velutina var. parviflora

Viola velutina var. parviflora, commonly known as the velvet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a soft texture. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white throat. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
