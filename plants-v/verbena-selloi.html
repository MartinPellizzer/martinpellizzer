Verbena selloi

Verbena selloi, commonly known as the Sello's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in spikes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often covered in fine hairs.

Verbena selloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its colorful display.
