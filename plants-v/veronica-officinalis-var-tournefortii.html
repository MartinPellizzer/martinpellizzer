Veronica officinalis var. tournefortii

Veronica officinalis var. tournefortii, commonly known as the tournefort's speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
