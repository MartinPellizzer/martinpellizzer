Vigna oubanguensis

Vigna oubanguensis, commonly known as the Congo bean, is a species of legume native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Vigna oubanguensis is valued for its nutritional content, particularly its high protein and mineral content.

It is often grown as a traditional food crop and has potential for agricultural improvement in tropical regions.
