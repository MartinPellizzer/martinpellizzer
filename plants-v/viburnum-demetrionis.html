Viburnum demetrionis

Viburnum demetrionis, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top and lighter underneath. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in partial to full shade.

Viburnum demetrionis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its seasonal interest.
