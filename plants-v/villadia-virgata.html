Villadia virgata

Villadia virgata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, round seeds.

Villadia virgata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.
