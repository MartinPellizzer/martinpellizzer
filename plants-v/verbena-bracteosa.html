Verbena bracteosa

Verbena bracteosa, commonly known as the desert verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white, with five petals arranged in a whorl. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Verbena bracteosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
