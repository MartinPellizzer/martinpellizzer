Vatica cordifolia

Vatica cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-shaped dipterocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its heavy, durable wood, which is highly valued in the timber industry. Vatica cordifolia typically grows in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, warm climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
