Verticordia oxylepis

Verticordia oxylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved squill, is a striking member of the family Proteaceae native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and clusters of vivid red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in horticulture and conservation efforts.
