Vaccinium dialypetalum

Vaccinium dialypetalum, commonly known as the two-lipped huckleberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, particularly found in moist, acidic soils of forested areas. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The plant's morphology includes evergreen leaves and a shrub-like growth habit.
