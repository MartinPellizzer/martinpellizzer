Vernonia miombicola

Vernonia miombicola, commonly known as the miombo vernonia, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as savannas and grasslands, often growing in full sun.

Vernonia miombicola is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns and is commonly found in regions with nutrient-poor soils.
