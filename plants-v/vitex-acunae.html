Vitex acunae

Vitex acunae, commonly known as the Mexican vitex or velvet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain several seeds.

Vitex acunae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
