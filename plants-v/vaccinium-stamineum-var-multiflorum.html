Vaccinium stamineum var. multiflorum

Vaccinium stamineum var. multiflorum, commonly known as the common blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries that ripen to a bright blue or red color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
