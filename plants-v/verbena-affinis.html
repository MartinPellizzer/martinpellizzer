Verbena affinis

Verbena affinis, commonly known as the common verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, blue, or white, which bloom from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Verbena affinis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
