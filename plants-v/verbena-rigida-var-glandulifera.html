Verbena rigida var. glandulifera

Verbena rigida var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse displays of blue to purple flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with glandular dots, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing its ecological value in native gardens.
