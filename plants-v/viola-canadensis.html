Viola canadensis

Viola canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, toothed leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

Viola canadensis is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
