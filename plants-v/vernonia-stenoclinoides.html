Vernonia stenoclinoides

Vernonia stenoclinoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia stenoclinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
