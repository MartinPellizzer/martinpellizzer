Veronica austriaca subvar. tenuis

Veronica austriaca subvar. tenuis, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and adaptability to various habitats.
