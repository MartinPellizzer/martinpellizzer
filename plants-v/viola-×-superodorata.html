Viola × superodorata

Viola × superodorata, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its flowers come in shades of purple, violet, and white, adding a delicate touch to gardens and landscapes.
