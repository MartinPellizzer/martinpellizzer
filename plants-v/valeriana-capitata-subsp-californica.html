Valeriana capitata subsp. californica

Valeriana capitata subsp.

californica, commonly known as California valerian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters at the top of its stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a distinct, aromatic scent.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its calming properties.
