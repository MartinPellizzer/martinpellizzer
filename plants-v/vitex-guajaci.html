Vitex guajaci

Vitex guajaci, commonly known as the Brazilian lilac, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its woody structure and dense foliage make it a popular ornamental plant in landscaping.

Vitex guajaci is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
