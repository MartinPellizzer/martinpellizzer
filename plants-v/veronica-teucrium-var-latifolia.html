Veronica teucrium var. latifolia

Veronica teucrium var. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized landscapes.
