Viola chaerophylloides f. simplicifolia

Viola chaerophylloides f. simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically simple in structure, distinguishing it from other viola species. The plant produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and early spring blooming period.
