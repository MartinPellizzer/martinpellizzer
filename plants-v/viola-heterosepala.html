Viola heterosepala

Viola heterosepala, commonly known as the large-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically pubescent and have a distinctive violet or purple coloration. The plant produces showy flowers with five petals, often with a dark violet or purple hue and a yellow or white throat. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
