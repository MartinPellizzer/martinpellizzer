Vauquelinia torreyi

Vauquelinia torreyi, commonly known as the torrey's vauquelinia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by elliptical, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Vauquelinia torreyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
