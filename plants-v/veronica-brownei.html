Veronica brownei

Veronica brownei, commonly known as the Hawaiian veronica, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in rocky or coastal areas. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is considered a keystone species in certain habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Veronica brownei is also a subject of conservation interest.
