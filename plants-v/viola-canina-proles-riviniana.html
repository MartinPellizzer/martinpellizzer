Viola canina proles riviniana

Viola canina proles riviniana, commonly known as the Riviera violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, this species thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a short stem and produces small, spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
