Vitex limonifolia

Vitex limonifolia, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, pinnate leaves that are often fragrant and have a lemony scent. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Vitex limonifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in herbal medicine for its aromatic properties.
