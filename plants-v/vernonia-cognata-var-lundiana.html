Vernonia cognata var. lundiana

Vernonia cognata var. lundiana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
