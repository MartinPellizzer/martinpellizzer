Vernonia remotiflora var. aristifera

Vernonia remotiflora var. aristifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to brownish flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a valuable nectar source for pollinators.
