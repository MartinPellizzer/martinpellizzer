Vaccinium vitis-idaea f. microphyllum

Vaccinium vitis-idaea f. microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf cranberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to boreal and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery, elliptical leaves that remain green throughout the year. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is commonly found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its compact form and adaptability make it a hardy plant suitable for garden cultivation in cooler climates.
