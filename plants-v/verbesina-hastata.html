Verbesina hastata

Verbesina hastata, commonly known as the broadleaf beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are covered in stiff, hook-shaped hairs, which can adhere to clothing and animal fur.

The leaves are typically broad, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.
