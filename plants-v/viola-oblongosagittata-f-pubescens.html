Viola oblongosagittata f. pubescens

Viola oblongosagittata f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent viola, is a variant of the oblongosagittata viola species.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture due to its dense pubescence. Its flowers are violet-blue, with five petals, and bloom in early spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
