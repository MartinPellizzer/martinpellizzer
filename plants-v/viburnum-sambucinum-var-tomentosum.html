Viburnum sambucinum var. tomentosum

Viburnum sambucinum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the white-fruited snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly gray pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by spherical, white to pink berries that persist through the winter. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for small garden spaces.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
