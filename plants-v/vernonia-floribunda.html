Vernonia floribunda

Vernonia floribunda, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Vernonia floribunda is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
