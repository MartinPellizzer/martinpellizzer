Verbena scoparia

Verbena scoparia, commonly known as narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
