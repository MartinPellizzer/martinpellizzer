Valeriana coreana subsp. leiocarpa

Valeriana coreana subsp.

leiocarpa, commonly known as the Korean valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea. It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant scent. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.
