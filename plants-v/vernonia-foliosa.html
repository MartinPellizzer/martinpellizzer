Vernonia foliosa

Vernonia foliosa, commonly known as the leafy Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and are accompanied by bracts that add to its ornamental appeal.
