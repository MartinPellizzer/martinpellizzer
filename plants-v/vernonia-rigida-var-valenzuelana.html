Vernonia rigida var. valenzuelana

Vernonia rigida var. valenzuelana, commonly known as the rigid ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in compact heads.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
