Vahea senegalensis

Vahea senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

Vahea senegalensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
