Verbascum microcalycinum

Verbascum microcalycinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a loose, upright spike. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum microcalycinum is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
