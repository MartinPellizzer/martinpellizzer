Vicia thurberi

Vicia thurberi, commonly known as Thurber's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial legume that produces small, yellow pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevents erosion.

Vicia thurberi plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native range.
