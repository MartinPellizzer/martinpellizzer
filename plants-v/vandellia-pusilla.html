Vandellia pusilla

Vandellia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf vandellia, is a small, aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. The plant has delicate, floating leaves that are often less than 1 cm in diameter, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is characterized by its ability to form dense mats on the water surface, which can help in stabilizing the ecosystem.

Vandellia pusilla is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
