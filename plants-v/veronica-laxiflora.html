Veronica laxiflora

Veronica laxiflora, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and fynbos areas. It has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

Veronica laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
