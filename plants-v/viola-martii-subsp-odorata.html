Viola martii subsp. odorata

Viola martii subsp.

odorata, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers.

Native to parts of Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shady environments. The subspecies is distinguished by its more intense fragrance compared to other viola species.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
