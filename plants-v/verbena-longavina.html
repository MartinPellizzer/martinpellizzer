Verbena longavina

Verbena longavina, commonly known as the slender verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Verbena longavina is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
