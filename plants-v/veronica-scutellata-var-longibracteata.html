Veronica scutellata var. longibracteata

Veronica scutellata var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
