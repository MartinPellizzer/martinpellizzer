Vernonia warmingiana

Vernonia warmingiana, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

Vernonia warmingiana is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
