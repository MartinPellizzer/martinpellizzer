Vitex hypoleuca

Vitex hypoleuca, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet. The plant produces showy, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is a smooth, green capsule that turns brown when mature, containing several seeds.
