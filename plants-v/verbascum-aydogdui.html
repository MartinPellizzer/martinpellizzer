Verbascum aydogdui

Verbascum aydogdui, commonly known as the Aydogdu's mullein, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is named after the Turkish botanist who first documented it, highlighting its significance in regional botanical studies.
