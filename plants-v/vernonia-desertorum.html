Vernonia desertorum

Vernonia desertorum, commonly known as the desert ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its fibrous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils and withstand drought conditions.

Vernonia desertorum is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
