Viola trinitatis

Viola trinitatis, commonly known as the Holy Trinity violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola trinitatis is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
