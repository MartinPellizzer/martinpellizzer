Vernonia inconspicua

Vernonia inconspicua, commonly known as the inconspicuous ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems that are often covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough, slightly hairy surface.

Vernonia inconspicua is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist to dry habitats, including woodlands and open fields.
