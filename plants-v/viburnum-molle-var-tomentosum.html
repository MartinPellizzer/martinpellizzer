Viburnum molle var. tomentosum

Viburnum molle var. tomentosum, commonly known as the fuzzy arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has a dense, compact growth habit with greyish-white fuzzy hairs on its stems and leaves. Its foliage turns bright red in autumn, adding ornamental value.

It is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
