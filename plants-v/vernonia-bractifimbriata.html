Vernonia bractifimbriata

Vernonia bractifimbriata, commonly known as the fringed bract vernonia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and woodland edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive appearance that aids in identification.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
