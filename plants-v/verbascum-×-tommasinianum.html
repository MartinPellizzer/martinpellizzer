Verbascum × tommasinianum

Verbascum × tommasinianum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their large, showy flowers. The hybrid mullein typically produces tall, fuzzy stems with clusters of yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are large, velvety, and gray-green, often used in traditional medicine.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
