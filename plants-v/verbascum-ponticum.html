Verbascum ponticum

Verbascum ponticum, commonly known as the Turkish mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in soft, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its common name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
