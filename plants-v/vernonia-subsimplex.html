Vernonia subsimplex

Vernonia subsimplex, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Vernonia subsimplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
