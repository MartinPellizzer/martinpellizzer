Vernonia hilairiana

Vernonia hilairiana, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense heads.

Vernonia hilairiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
