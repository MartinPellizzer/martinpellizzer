Vitex angolensis

Vitex angolensis, commonly known as the African blue mistflower, is a shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale blue, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

Vitex angolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
