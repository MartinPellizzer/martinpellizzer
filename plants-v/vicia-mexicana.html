Vicia mexicana

Vicia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican vetch, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Vicia mexicana is adapted to dry, rocky environments and can thrive in poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional uses in local communities.
