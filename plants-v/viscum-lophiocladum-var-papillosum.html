Viscum lophiocladum var. papillosum

Viscum lophiocladum var. papillosum, commonly known as the papillate mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and gelatinous, sticky berries. The plant derives some of its nutrients from the host through its haustoria, while also performing photosynthesis. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found on species of the genus Ficus.

Its unique appearance and parasitic growth habit make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
