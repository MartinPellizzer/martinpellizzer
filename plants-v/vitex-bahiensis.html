Vitex bahiensis

Vitex bahiensis, commonly known as Brazilian vitex, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in terminal clusters. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Vitex bahiensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
