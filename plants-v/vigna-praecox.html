Vigna praecox

Vigna praecox, commonly known as early cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant produces yellow flowers and is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein. Vigna praecox thrives in warm climates and is drought-tolerant, making it a valuable crop in arid regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional agriculture and ecological studies.
