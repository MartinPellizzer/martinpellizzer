Vitis concinna

Vitis concinna, commonly known as the grape vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. Vitis concinna is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

It is also a member of the Vitaceae family, which includes other well-known grape species.
