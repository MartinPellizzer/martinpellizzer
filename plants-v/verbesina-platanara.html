Verbesina platanara

Verbesina platanara, commonly known as the plains beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and along roadsides. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, which bloom in the summer months.

The plant is known for its sticky, hairy seeds that adhere to clothing and animal fur, aiding in seed dispersal.
