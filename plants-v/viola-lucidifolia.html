Viola lucidifolia

Viola lucidifolia, commonly known as the glossy leaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
