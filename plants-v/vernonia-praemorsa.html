Vernonia praemorsa

Vernonia praemorsa, commonly known as the blackroot vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Vernonia praemorsa is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

Its root system is known for its dark, almost black color, which gives the plant its common name.
