Viola sylvestris f. leucantha

Viola sylvestris f. leucantha, commonly known as the white wood violet, is a frost-tolerant perennial herb native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces pale lavender to white flowers with a distinctive violet center. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

The white variant of this species is notable for its reduced pigmentation compared to the typical violet flower color.
