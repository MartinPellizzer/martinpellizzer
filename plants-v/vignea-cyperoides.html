Vignea cyperoides

Vignea cyperoides, commonly known as the marsh arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

Vignea cyperoides plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic insects and stabilizing soil.
