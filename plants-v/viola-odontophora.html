Viola odontophora

Viola odontophora, commonly known as the toothed violet, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves with toothed edges. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Viola odontophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
