Vernonia mespilifolia

Vernonia mespilifolia, commonly known as the mespilifolia ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia mespilifolia is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
