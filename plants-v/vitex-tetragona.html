Vitex tetragona

Vitex tetragona, commonly known as the four-angle vitex, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, blooming in summer. Its fruit is a dry, four-lobed capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Vitex tetragona is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value in gardens.
