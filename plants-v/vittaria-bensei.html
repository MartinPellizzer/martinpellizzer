Vittaria bensei

Vittaria bensei, commonly known as the Brazilian rock fern, is a terrestrial fern native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates in humid, shaded environments. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sori on the underside of the fronds.

Vittaria bensei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
