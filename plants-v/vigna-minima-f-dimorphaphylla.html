Vigna minima f. dimorphaphylla

Vigna minima f. dimorphaphylla, commonly known as the small cowpea or dwarf cowpea, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as a cover crop or for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both agricultural and ecological studies.
