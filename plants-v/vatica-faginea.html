Vatica faginea

Vatica faginea, commonly known as the yellow ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

Vatica faginea thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.
