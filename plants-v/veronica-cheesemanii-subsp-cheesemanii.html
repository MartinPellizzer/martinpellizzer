Veronica cheesemanii subsp. cheesemanii

Veronica cheesemanii subsp.

cheesemanii, commonly known as the Cheeseman's speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to violet, and arranged in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
