Vernonia paucifolia

Vernonia paucifolia, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Its fibrous roots and sturdy stems make it a hardy species adapted to arid environments.
