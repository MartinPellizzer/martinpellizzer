Verbesina synethes

Verbesina synethes, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and roadside ditches. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
