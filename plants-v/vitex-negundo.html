Vitex negundo

Vitex negundo, commonly known as the common vitex or bush willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, blue or purple flowers in panicles, which bloom in summer. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that split open to release seeds.

Vitex negundo is adaptable to various soil types and is often used in traditional medicine.
