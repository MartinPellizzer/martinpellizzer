Vittaria filiformis

Vittaria filiformis, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Vittariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its sporangia are clustered in sori along the margins of the fronds, a common feature in many ferns.

The plant's small size and intricate structure make it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
