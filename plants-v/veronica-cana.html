Veronica cana

Veronica cana, commonly known as common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica cana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
