Viguiera macrantha

Viguiera macrantha, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Viguiera macrantha thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice in native plant gardens.
