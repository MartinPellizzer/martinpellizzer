Vaccinium hamatidens

Vaccinium hamatidens, commonly known as the roughleaf blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as heathers and other blueberries. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges and a leathery texture, while its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white.

The berries, which are edible, are typically blue or purple when mature and are valued for their flavor and nutritional content.
