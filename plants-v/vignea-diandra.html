Vignea diandra

Vignea diandra, commonly known as the slender legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or green, and it produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds.

Vignea diandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.
