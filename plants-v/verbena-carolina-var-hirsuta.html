Verbena carolina var. hirsuta

Verbena carolina var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy verbena, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name, and are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
