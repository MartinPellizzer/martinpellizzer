Viola × jagellonica

Viola × jagellonica, commonly known as the Queen of the Night viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, violet, and white. The plant is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in temperate climates. It produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Viola × jagellonica is a perennial that prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
