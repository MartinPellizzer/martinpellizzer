Vriesea silvana

Vriesea silvana, commonly known as the forest vriesea, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescence that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers, which come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, are typically borne on a central stem.

Vriesea silvana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
