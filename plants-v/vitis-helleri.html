Vitis helleri

Vitis helleri, commonly known as Heller's grape, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Vitis helleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
