Vetiveria arguta

Vetiveria arguta, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is used in landscaping and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
