Vexillaria

Vexillaria, commonly known as the "flower of the sea," is a unique marine plant found in coastal regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that resemble small sails, hence its name. This plant thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters where it attaches itself to rocky substrates. Its reproductive structures are specialized for dispersal by water currents, ensuring genetic spread across marine environments.

Vexillaria plays a vital role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
