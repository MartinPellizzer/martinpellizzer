Verbascum phlomoides var. australe

Verbascum phlomoides var. australe, commonly known as the Australian mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
