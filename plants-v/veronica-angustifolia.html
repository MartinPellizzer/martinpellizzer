Veronica angustifolia

Veronica angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Veronica angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscaping.
