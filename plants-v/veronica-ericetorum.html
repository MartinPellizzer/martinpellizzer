Veronica ericetorum

Veronica ericetorum, commonly known as the heather speedwell, is a flowering plant native to western Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils, often found in heathlands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its hardy nature.

Veronica ericetorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
