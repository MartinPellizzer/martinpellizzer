Viola tricolor var. arvensioides

Viola tricolor var. arvensioides, commonly known as the common heartleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight purplish tinge, and it produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
