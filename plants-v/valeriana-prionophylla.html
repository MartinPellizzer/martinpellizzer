Valeriana prionophylla

Valeriana prionophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has aromatic leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its medicinal properties.

Valeriana prionophylla is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
