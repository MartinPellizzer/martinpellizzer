Vincentia caffra

Vincentia caffra, commonly known as the Cape rose or wild rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing plant with distinctive pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the plant has a woody stem covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and along roadsides.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
