Vandellia pachypoda

Vandellia pachypoda, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, which float on the water's surface. The plant has a thick, spongy stem that supports its massive foliage and provides buoyancy. Its flowers are typically white or pink and bloom in the early morning, attracting pollinators like beetles.

Vandellia pachypoda plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
