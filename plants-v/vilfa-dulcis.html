Vilfa dulcis

Vilfa dulcis, commonly known as the sweetleaf plant, is a flowering shrub native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its sweet-smelling foliage, which is often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and small, capsule-like fruits.

Vilfa dulcis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
