Veronica aphylla var. nudicaulis

Veronica aphylla var. nudicaulis, commonly known as the nakedstem speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, hairless stems that often lie close to the ground, bearing small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are typically without stipules, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Its simple floral structure and compact growth habit make it a distinctive member of the Plantaginaceae family.
