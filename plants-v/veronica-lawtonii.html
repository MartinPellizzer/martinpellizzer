Veronica lawtonii

Veronica lawtonii, commonly known as the Lawton's speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers and oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
