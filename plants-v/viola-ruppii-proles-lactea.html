Viola ruppii proles lactea

Viola ruppii proles lactea, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are violet-blue in color, typically with five petals. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine meadows. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its delicate nature, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
