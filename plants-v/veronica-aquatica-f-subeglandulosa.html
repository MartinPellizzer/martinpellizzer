Veronica aquatica f. subeglandulosa

Veronica aquatica f. subeglandulosa, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow waters or moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic conditions. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is distinguished by its reduced glandular structures compared to the typical form, contributing to its unique botanical characteristics.
