Viola variegata var. nipponica

Viola variegata var. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
