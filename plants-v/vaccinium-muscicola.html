Vaccinium muscicola

Vaccinium muscicola, commonly known as the mossy blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils found in boreal and subarctic environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, which bloom in late spring. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing dense ground cover.

Vaccinium muscicola is an important component of the ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
