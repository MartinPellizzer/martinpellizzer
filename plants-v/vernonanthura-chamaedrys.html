Vernonanthura chamaedrys

Vernonanthura chamaedrys, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to arid conditions, allowing it to store water efficiently. The plant produces small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
