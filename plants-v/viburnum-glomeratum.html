Viburnum glomeratum

Viburnum glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in late spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum glomeratum typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 6 feet.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
