Verbascum rotundifolium var. numidicum

Verbascum rotundifolium var. numidicum, commonly known as the Numidian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, velvety, gray-green leaves that are rounded and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems with showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots are adapted to retain moisture in dry soils, and it is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
