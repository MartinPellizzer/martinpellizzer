Vallisneria bulbosa

Vallisneria bulbosa, commonly known as the tapegrass, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, making it a striking feature in freshwater habitats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects or water currents. Vallisneria bulbosa is often found in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes.

Its dense growth helps stabilize sediments and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
