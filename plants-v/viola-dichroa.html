Viola dichroa

Viola dichroa, commonly known as the purple violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of North America, particularly in the western United States and southern Canada. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Viola dichroa is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
