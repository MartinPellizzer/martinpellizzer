Verbena caroliniana f. recta

Verbena caroliniana f. recta, commonly known as the Carolina verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to native plant gardens.
