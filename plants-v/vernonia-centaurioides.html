Vernonia centaurioides

Vernonia centaurioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a silver-gray appearance that adds visual interest.

Vernonia centaurioides is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
