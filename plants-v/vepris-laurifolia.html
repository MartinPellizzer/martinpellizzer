Vepris laurifolia

Vepris laurifolia, commonly known as the West African laurier or African laurier, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elliptical, winged fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

Vepris laurifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
