Verbena tenuisecta var. glabrata

Verbena tenuisecta var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface, giving the plant its "glabrata" designation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
