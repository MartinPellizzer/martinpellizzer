Viola macloskeyi subsp. pallens

Viola macloskeyi subsp.

pallens, commonly known as the pale viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are small, usually violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
