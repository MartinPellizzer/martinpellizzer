Viburnum davidi

Viburnum davidi, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves that are opposite and ovate, with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Viburnum davidi is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and floral display.
