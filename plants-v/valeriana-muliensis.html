Valeriana muliensis

Valeriana muliensis, commonly known as the Yunnan valerian, is a flowering plant native to China, particularly in the Yunnan province.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes and roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana muliensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
