Valeriana pilosa

Valeriana pilosa, commonly known as the hairy valerian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana pilosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
