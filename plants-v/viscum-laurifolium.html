Viscum laurifolium

Viscum laurifolium, commonly known as the European mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of trees such as oak and apple.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, and it produces small, white or yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant relies on its host for nutrients and water, while also forming a symbiotic relationship with the host's ecosystem.

It plays a role in traditional medicine and is also associated with cultural and symbolic significance during festive seasons.
