Viola tokubuchiana f. concolor

Viola tokubuchiana f. concolor, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, showy blooms in early spring.

Its foliage is often covered in a fine, velvety texture, which contributes to its ornamental appeal.
