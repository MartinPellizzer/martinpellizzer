Viola arvensis var. latifolia

Viola arvensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces showy violet flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and floral structure.

Its ornamental value and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
