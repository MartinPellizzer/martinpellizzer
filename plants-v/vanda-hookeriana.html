Vanda hookeriana

Vanda hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's vanda, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors including white, yellow, and pink. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Vanda hookeriana is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
