Verbena ephedroides var. entreriensis

Verbena ephedroides var. entreriensis, commonly known as the Entrerian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, while the flowers are usually pink or purple.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
