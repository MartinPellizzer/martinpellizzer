Viola arguta

Viola arguta, commonly known as the smooth violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet, blue, or white. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist meadows, thriving in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola arguta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
