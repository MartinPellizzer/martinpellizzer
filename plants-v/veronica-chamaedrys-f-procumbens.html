Veronica chamaedrys f. procumbens

Veronica chamaedrys f. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens or as ground cover.
