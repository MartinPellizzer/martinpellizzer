Verbena halei f. albiflora

Verbena halei f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered verbena, is a compact, flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its prolonged blooming period and drought tolerance.
