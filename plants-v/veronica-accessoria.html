Veronica accessoria

Veronica accessoria, commonly known as the lesser speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Veronica accessoria is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
