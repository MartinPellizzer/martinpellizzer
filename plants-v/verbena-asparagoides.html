Verbena asparagoides

Verbena asparagoides, commonly known as the purpletop verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, triangular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Verbena asparagoides is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
