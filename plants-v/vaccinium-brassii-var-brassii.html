Vaccinium brassii var. brassii

Vaccinium brassii var. brassii, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It produces small, dark blue berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface, and the plant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its compact size and attractive appearance.

Vaccinium brassii var. brassii is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in coastal environments.
