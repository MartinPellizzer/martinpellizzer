Vitex coriacea

Vitex coriacea, commonly known as the Brazilian lilac, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Vitex coriacea is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
