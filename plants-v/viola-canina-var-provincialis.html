Viola canina var. provincialis

Viola canina var. provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial Violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
