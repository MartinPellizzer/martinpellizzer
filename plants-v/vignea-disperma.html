Vignea disperma

Vignea disperma, commonly known as the scattered vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Vignea disperma thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in forest understories and along riverbanks.
