Verbena barbata

Verbena barbata, commonly known as spreading blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, spike-like clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and open habitats.

Verbena barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
