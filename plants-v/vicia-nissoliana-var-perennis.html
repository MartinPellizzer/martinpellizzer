Vicia nissoliana var. perennis

Vicia nissoliana var. perennis, commonly known as the perennial vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a fibrous root system and produces pods containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration and agricultural contexts.
