Vigna keniensis

Vigna keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing several angular, edible seeds. Vigna keniensis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown as a drought-resistant crop.

It plays an important role in local agriculture and food security in its native region.
