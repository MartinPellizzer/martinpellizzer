Valeriana lindleyana

Valeriana lindleyana, commonly known as the sweet woodruff, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 3 feet tall, featuring heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is known for its pleasant, vanilla-like scent, which comes from the essential oils in its foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Valeriana lindleyana is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat ailments such as insomnia and anxiety.
