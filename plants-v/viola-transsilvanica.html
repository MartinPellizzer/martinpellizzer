Viola transsilvanica

Viola transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Viola transsilvanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
