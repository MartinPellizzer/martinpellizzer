Vitis saccharifera var. saccharifera

Vitis saccharifera var. saccharifera, commonly known as the sugar grape vine, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and the production of small, sweet berries. The plant typically thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a glossy surface, and its vines can climb to considerable heights.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural sweeteners.
