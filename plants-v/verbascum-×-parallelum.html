Verbascum × parallelum

Verbascum × parallelum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a composite plant that results from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattifolium.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
