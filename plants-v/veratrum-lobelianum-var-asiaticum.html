Veratrum lobelianum var. asiaticum

Veratrum lobelianum var. asiaticum, commonly known as the Asian white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
