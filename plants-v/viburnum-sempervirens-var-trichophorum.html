Viburnum sempervirens var. trichophorum

Viburnum sempervirens var. trichophorum, commonly known as the velvetleaf honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that gives it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, berry-like drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
