Verbascum simplex var. dyris

Verbascum simplex var. dyris, commonly known as the dyris mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for both aesthetic and ecological purposes.
