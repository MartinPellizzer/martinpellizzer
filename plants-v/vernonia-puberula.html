Vernonia puberula

Vernonia puberula, commonly known as the rough vervain, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Vernonia puberula is often found in dry, open habitats like woodlands and prairies.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
