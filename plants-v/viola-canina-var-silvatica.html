Viola canina var. silvatica

Viola canina var. silvatica, commonly known as the wood violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in woodland areas and shaded environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive dark purple throat. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
