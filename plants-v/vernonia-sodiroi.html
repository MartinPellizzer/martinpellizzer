Vernonia sodiroi

Vernonia sodiroi, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its thick, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
