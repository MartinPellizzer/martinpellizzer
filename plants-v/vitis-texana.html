Vitis texana

Vitis texana, commonly known as Texas muscadine vine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of black, edible berries. Vitis texana is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its berries are valued for their flavor and are used in local culinary traditions.
