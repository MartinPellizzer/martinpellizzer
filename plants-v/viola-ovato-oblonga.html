Viola ovato-oblonga

Viola ovato-oblonga, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in woodland and meadow environments. The plant features heart-shaped, ovate-oblong leaves with a distinctive waxy texture. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, with five petals and a spurred lower petal.

Viola ovato-oblonga is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
