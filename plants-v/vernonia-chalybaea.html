Vernonia chalybaea

Vernonia chalybaea, commonly known as the ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Vernonia chalybaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by various indigenous cultures.
