Vaccinium membranaceum var. rigidum

Vaccinium membranaceum var. rigidum, commonly known as the Sitka black-currant, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Alaska and the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. The plant has alternate, elliptic leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive glandular surface. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in dense thickets along streams and in coastal areas.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing food for various wildlife species.
