Verbena multifida

Verbena multifida, commonly known as the fernleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is typically herbaceous, growing as an annual or perennial depending on the climate. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
