Viola canina subsp. schultzii

Viola canina subsp.

schultzii, commonly known as the Schultze's dog violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped basal leaves and violet or purple flowers with five petals, arranged in a spurred pattern. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate appearance and ecological significance in supporting pollinators.

This subspecies is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
