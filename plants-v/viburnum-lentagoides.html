Viburnum lentagoides

Viburnum lentagoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green above and lighter beneath, with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, drupes that persist into winter. Viburnum lentagoides thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
