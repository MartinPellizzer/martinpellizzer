Vepris verdoorniana

Vepris verdoorniana, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The fruit, though not commonly eaten, is ornamental and attracts birds and insects.
