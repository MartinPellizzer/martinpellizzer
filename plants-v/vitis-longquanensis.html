Vitis longquanensis

Vitis longquanensis, commonly known as the Longquan grapevine, is a rare and endangered species native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its berries are usually small and can vary in color from red to black, depending on the variety.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Vitis longquanensis is of significant interest for botanical research and conservation efforts.
