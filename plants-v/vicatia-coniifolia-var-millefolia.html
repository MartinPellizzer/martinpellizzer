Vicatia coniifolia var. millefolia

Vicatia coniifolia var. millefolia, commonly known as the featherleaf conifer, is a variant of the coniferous plant Vicatia coniifolia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, such as mountainous regions or shaded forest floors. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in the landscape.

Botanically, it belongs to the Pinaceae family, which includes various conifer species.
