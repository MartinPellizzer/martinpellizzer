Vestia lycioides

Vestia lycioides, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows from 30 to 60 centimeters in height, featuring hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous roots help it survive in dry conditions, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Vestia lycioides is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
