Vicia monadelpha

Vicia monadelpha, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. Vicia monadelpha is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including fields and along roadsides.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
