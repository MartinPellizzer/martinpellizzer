Voacanga angustifolia

Voacanga angustifolia, commonly known as the African apple or African cherry, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have elongated, narrow leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries containing seeds. Its bark and roots contain alkaloids, making it a subject of interest in traditional medicine and pharmacology.

The tree is also valued for its timber and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental features.
