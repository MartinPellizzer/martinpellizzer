Viola arvensis subsp. sublilacina

Viola arvensis subsp.

sublilacina, commonly known as the purple viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
