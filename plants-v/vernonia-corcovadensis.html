Vernonia corcovadensis

Vernonia corcovadensis, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in tropical and subtropical regions.

Vernonia corcovadensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
