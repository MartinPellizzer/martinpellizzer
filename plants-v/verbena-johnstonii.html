Verbena johnstonii

Verbena johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in late summer to early fall. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
