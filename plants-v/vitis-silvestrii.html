Vitis silvestrii

Vitis silvestrii, commonly known as the wild grapevine, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, sour-tasting berries. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.

Vitis silvestrii is valued for its hardiness and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in some regions.
