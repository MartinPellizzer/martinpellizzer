Viguiera gracilis

Viguiera gracilis, commonly known as slender bluemink, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viguiera gracilis is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
