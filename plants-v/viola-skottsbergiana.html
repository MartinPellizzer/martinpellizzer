Viola skottsbergiana

Viola skottsbergiana, commonly known as the Skottsberg's violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, often purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has small, hairy leaves and produces showy flowers that bloom in spring.

Viola skottsbergiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
