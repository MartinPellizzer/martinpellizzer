Vicia glabrescens var. elegantissima

Vicia glabrescens var. elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets. The flowers are typically small, yellowish-green, and borne in clusters. This variety is native to parts of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
