Veronica pinnata

Veronica pinnata, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, often hairy stems that produce small, blue or purple flowers in clusters. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
