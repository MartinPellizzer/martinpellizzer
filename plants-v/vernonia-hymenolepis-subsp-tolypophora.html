Vernonia hymenolepis subsp. tolypophora

Vernonia hymenolepis subsp.

tolypophora, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
