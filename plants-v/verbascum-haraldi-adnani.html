Verbascum haraldi-adnani

Verbascum haraldi-adnani, commonly known as the Harald's mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their woolly leaves and medicinal properties. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential pharmacological applications.
