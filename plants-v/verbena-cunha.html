Verbena cunha

Verbena cunha, commonly known as Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Verbena cunha thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
