Vriesea philippocoburgi

Vriesea philippocoburgi, commonly known as the Philippi's Vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with brightly colored bracts that range from orange to red. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Vriesea philippocoburgi is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
