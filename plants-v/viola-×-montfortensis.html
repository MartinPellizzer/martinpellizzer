Viola × montfortensis

Viola × montfortensis, commonly known as the Montfort violet, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers moist, shady environments such as woodlands or stream banks.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
