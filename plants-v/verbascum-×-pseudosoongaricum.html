Verbascum × pseudosoongaricum

Verbascum × pseudosoongaricum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their fuzzy, velvety leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
