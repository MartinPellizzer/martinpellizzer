Vachellia nilotica

Vachellia nilotica, commonly known as the sickle tree, is a leguminous plant native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and compound leaves that are typically bipinnate. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by long, flat pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species for soil stabilization and livestock forage.

Vachellia nilotica is also valued for its traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
