Vicia peregrina var. albida

Vicia peregrina var. albida, commonly known as the white field vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its pods contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, allowing the plant to spread easily.

Vicia peregrina var. albida is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including fields and woodland edges.
