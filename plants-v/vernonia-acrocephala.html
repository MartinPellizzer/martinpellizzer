Vernonia acrocephala

Vernonia acrocephala, commonly known as the redtop ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wet meadows. It produces showy, purple to red flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia acrocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
