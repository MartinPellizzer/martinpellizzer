Viola blanda var. renifolia

Viola blanda var. renifolia, commonly known as the woolly violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it produces delicate purple or violet flowers with yellow centers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade and well-drained soil.
