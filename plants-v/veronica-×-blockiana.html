Veronica × blockiana

Veronica × blockiana, commonly known as the blueweed or common speedwell, is a flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is a hybrid species that results from the cross between Veronica filiformis and Veronica montana. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions.
