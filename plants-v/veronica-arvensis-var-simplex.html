Veronica arvensis var. simplex

Veronica arvensis var. simplex, commonly known as mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

This species is characterized by its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
