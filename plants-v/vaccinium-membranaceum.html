Vaccinium membranaceum

Vaccinium membranaceum, commonly known as the leatherleaf blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
