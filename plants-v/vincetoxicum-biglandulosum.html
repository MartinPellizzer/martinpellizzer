Vincetoxicum biglandulosum

Vincetoxicum biglandulosum, commonly known as the creeping false bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can spread rapidly through runners, making it both ornamental and potentially invasive. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and garden cultivation.
