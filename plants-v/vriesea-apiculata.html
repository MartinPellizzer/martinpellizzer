Vriesea apiculata

Vriesea apiculata, commonly known as the "Cock's Comb Bromeliad," is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette structure. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with colorful, long-lasting flowers that range from red to orange and yellow. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in both indoor and outdoor gardens.

Vriesea apiculata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
