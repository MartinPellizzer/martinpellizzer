Viola micrantha subsp. shikokuensis

Viola micrantha subsp.

shikokuensis, commonly known as the Shikoku violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the understory vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
