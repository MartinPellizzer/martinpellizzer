Verbascum × bulanikense

Verbascum × bulanikense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant that belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to the Balkans and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
