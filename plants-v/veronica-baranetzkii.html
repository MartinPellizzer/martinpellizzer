Veronica baranetzkii

Veronica baranetzkii, commonly known as the Baranetzki's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing numerous tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
