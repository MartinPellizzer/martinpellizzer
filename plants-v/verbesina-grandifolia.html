Verbesina grandifolia

Verbesina grandifolia, commonly known as the giant ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and riverbanks. The plant features tall, sturdy stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet, adorned with lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
