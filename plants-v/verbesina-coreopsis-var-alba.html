Verbesina coreopsis var. alba

Verbesina coreopsis var. alba, commonly known as white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow ray florets and a central cone-shaped disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
