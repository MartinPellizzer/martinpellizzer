Vitex leucoxylon

Vitex leucoxylon, commonly known as the white woodhoney, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, drupaceous fruits that are typically eaten by birds and small mammals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or along stream banks.
