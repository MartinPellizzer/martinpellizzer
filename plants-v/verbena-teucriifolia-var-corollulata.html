Verbena teucriifolia var. corollulata

Verbena teucriifolia var. corollulata, commonly known as the coral verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a silvery-blue hue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
