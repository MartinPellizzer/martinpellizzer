Vigna brachystachys

Vigna brachystachys, commonly known as the dwarf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. Vigna brachystachys is well adapted to poor soils and can thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient crop.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
