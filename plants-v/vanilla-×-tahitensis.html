Vanilla × tahitensis

Vanilla × tahitensis, commonly known as Tahitian vanilla, is a hybrid vanilla orchid native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like pods that mature to a dark brown color, often with a distinctive wrinkled texture. The plant produces a rich, aromatic vanilla flavor, which is highly valued in the food and fragrance industries. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and requires a sturdy support structure for its climbing vines.

The cultivation of Tahitian vanilla is labor-intensive, involving careful hand-pollination and extended drying periods to develop its unique aroma.
