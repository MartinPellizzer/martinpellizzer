Verbascum lychnitis subvar. aureiflorum

Verbascum lychnitis subvar. aureiflorum, commonly known as the goldenflower mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species known for their woolly foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The subspecies aureiflorum is distinguished by its particularly vibrant yellow floral coloration.
