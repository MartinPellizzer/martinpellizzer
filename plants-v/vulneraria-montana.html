Vulneraria montana

Vulneraria montana, commonly known as mountain vulnerary, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Vulneraria montana is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
