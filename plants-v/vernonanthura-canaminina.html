Vernonanthura canaminina

Vernonanthura canaminina, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red-orange flower heads, which are surrounded by bright yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
