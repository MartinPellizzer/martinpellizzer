Viola sieheana var. oblongifolia

Viola sieheana var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped or ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Violaceae family.
