Viburnum fansipanense

Viburnum fansipanense, commonly known as the Fan Mountain viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are a food source for various birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Viburnum fansipanense is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
