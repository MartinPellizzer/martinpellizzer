Viola tricolor subsp. alpestris

Viola tricolor subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces showy flowers in shades of purple, yellow, and white. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to withstand cold temperatures.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.
