Vigna prainiana

Vigna prainiana, commonly known as the Indian velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers and is often grown for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Vigna prainiana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
