Vriesea sulcata

Vriesea sulcata, commonly known as the Zebra Plant, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, striped leaves that give it its common name, with a central rosette that can hold water. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet and thrives in bright, indirect light. It prefers warm temperatures and moderate humidity, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Vriesea sulcata is known for its long-lasting, showy inflorescence that can last several months.
