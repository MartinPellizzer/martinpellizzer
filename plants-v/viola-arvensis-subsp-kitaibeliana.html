Viola arvensis subsp. kitaibeliana

Viola arvensis subsp.

kitaibeliana, commonly known as the field violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple underside, and it produces small, delicate flowers with five petals. It is part of the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.

The subspecies kitaibeliana is distinguished by its slightly different leaf shape and flower color compared to other viola species.
