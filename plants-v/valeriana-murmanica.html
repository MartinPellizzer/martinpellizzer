Valeriana murmanica

Valeriana murmanica, commonly known as the Murman valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in tundra and alpine environments. The plant has slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette and are covered with fine hairs.

Valeriana murmanica is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
