Vernonia macphersonii

Vernonia macphersonii, commonly known as the MacPherson's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its showy yellow to orange flower heads appear in late summer to early autumn, making it a notable wildflower in its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
