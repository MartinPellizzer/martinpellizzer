Vernonia cephalophora

Vernonia cephalophora, commonly known as the purpletop vervain, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in open woodlands and along roadsides.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
