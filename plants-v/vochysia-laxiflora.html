Vochysia laxiflora

Vochysia laxiflora, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, pinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Vochysia laxiflora thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
