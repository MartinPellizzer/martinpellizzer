Veronica deflectans

Veronica deflectans, commonly known as the bentweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often bent or twisted, giving it its common name.

Veronica deflectans is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
