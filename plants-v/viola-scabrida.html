Viola scabrida

Viola scabrida, commonly known as the rough violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are usually violet or purple in color. Viola scabrida produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
