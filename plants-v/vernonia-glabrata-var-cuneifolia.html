Vernonia glabrata var. cuneifolia

Vernonia glabrata var. cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to native plant collections.
