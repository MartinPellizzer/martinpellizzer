Viola × taradakensis f. variegata

Viola × taradakensis f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated violet, is a hybrid plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring a mix of green and white or yellow patterns. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add visual interest to shaded areas.
