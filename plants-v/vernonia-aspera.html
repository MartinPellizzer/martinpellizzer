Vernonia aspera

Vernonia aspera, commonly known as rough vervain, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Vernonia aspera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
