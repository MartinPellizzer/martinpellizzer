Vernonia philipsoniana

Vernonia philipsoniana, commonly known as the red vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, violet-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

The red vernonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet environments.
