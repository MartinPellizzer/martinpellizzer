Valentinia coriacea

Valentinia coriacea, commonly known as the rough valentine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, waxy leaves with a leathery texture and a distinct, slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. Valentinia coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
