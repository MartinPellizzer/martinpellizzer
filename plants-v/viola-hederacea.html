Viola hederacea

Viola hederacea, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Viola hederacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
