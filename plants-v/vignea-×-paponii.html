Vignea × paponii

Vignea × paponii, commonly known as the hybrid vigna, is a plant species that belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Vignea × paponii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
