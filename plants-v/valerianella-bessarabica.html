Valerianella bessarabica

Valerianella bessarabica, commonly known as Bessarabian valerian, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features small, rounded leaves and produces tiny, pale blue or white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.

Valerianella bessarabica is also a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its compact growth habit and delicate appearance.
