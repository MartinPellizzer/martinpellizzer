Vittaria pauciareolata

Vittaria pauciareolata, commonly known as the sparse-pored fern, is a small epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, slender fronds. The plant has a unique reproductive structure with small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or branches.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for studying fern evolution and ecological adaptations.
