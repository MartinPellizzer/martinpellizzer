Verbascum portae

Verbascum portae, commonly known as the Turkish mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and also covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Verbascum portae is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
