Venatris falcata

Venatris falcata, commonly known as the sickle vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, serpentine stems that coil around supports as it grows. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically have a yellowish or greenish color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique growth habit.
