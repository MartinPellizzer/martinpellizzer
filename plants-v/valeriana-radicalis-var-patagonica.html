Valeriana radicalis var. patagonica

Valeriana radicalis var. patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features aromatic, often hairy leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pink. Its root system is extensive, which contributes to its medicinal properties.

This variety is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
