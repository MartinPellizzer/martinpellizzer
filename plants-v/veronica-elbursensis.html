Veronica elbursensis

Veronica elbursensis, commonly known as the Elburz Veronica, is a flowering plant native to the Elburz Mountains in Iran.

It belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
