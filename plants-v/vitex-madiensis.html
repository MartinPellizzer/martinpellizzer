Vitex madiensis

Vitex madiensis, commonly known as the African blueberry or wild lilac, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to black as it matures.

Vitex madiensis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
