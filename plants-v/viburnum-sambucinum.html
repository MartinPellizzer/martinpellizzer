Viburnum sambucinum

Viburnum sambucinum, commonly known as blackhaw viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and features compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark purple to black berries in the fall. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and an upright growth habit.

Viburnum sambucinum is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
