Vriesea atropurpurea

Vriesea atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple vriesea, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliad family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that displays shades of red, purple, and orange. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that form a rosette, creating a central cup that collects rainwater. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity to flourish.

Vriesea atropurpurea is popular as a decorative plant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
