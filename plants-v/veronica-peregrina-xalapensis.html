Veronica peregrina xalapensis

Veronica peregrina xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is native to Mexico and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy species adaptable to various climates.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
