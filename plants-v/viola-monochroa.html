Viola monochroa

Viola monochroa, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola monochroa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
