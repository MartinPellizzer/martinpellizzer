Verbascum plantagineum

Verbascum plantagineum, commonly known as the common mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose spike at the top of the stem. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Verbascum plantagineum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for respiratory ailments.
