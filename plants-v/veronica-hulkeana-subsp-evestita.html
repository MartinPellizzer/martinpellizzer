Veronica hulkeana subsp. evestita

Veronica hulkeana subsp.

evestita, commonly known as the Hulke's speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers with five petals, typical of the Veronica genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for native gardens and conservation efforts.
