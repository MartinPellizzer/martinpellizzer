Viola lagaipensis

Viola lagaipensis, commonly known as the Lagaip violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of down, which helps to retain moisture in its harsh habitat.

Viola lagaipensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
