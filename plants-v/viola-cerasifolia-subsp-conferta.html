Viola cerasifolia subsp. conferta

Viola cerasifolia subsp.

conferta, commonly known as the clustered violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are violet-blue, with a distinctive white throat and a spurred petal at the back.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
