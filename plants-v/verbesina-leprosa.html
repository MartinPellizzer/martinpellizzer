Verbesina leprosa

Verbesina leprosa, commonly known as the leprose beggar-tick, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
