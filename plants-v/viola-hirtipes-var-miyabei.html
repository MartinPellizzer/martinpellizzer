Viola hirtipes var. miyabei

Viola hirtipes var. miyabei, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically green with a velvety texture. The plant produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive spurred petal, often blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders in horticulture.
