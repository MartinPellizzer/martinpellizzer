Vaccinium ovale

Vaccinium ovale, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other ericaceous plants such as heathers and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or dark purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a leathery texture and a bluish-green hue.

Vaccinium ovale thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.
