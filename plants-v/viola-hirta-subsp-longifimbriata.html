Viola hirta subsp. longifimbriata

Viola hirta subsp.

longifimbriata, commonly known as the long-fringed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, preferring partial to full shade.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and violet or purple flowers that have distinctive, elongated lower petals. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

The subspecies is characterized by its long, fringed petal lobes, which contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.
