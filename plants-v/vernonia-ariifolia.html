Vernonia ariifolia

Vernonia ariifolia, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Vernonia ariifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
