Vincetoxicum officinale var. apodum

Vincetoxicum officinale var. apodum, commonly known as the purple smoke bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of purple, smoky-pink bracts that appear in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fall display.
