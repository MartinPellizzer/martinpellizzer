Vincetoxicum glaucirameum

Vincetoxicum glaucirameum, commonly known as the blue-rayed false bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along riverbanks and in woodland edges.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain regions.
