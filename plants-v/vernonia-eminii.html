Vernonia eminii

Vernonia eminii, commonly known as the highland ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of East Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are sturdy and often branched.

Vernonia eminii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
