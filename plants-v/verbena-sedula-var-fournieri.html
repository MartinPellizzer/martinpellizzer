Verbena sedula var. fournieri

Verbena sedula var. fournieri, commonly known as the Madagascar verbena, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse displays of blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in subtropical and tropical regions.
