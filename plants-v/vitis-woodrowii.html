Vitis woodrowii

Vitis woodrowii, commonly known as Woodrow's grape, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, sweet-tart berries. The plant typically grows along stream banks and in moist woodlands, preferring partial to full shade. Its berries are edible and have been used historically by Native American tribes for food and medicine.

The vine is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
