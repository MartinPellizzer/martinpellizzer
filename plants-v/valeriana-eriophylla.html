Valeriana eriophylla

Valeriana eriophylla, commonly known as the woolly valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in coastal areas. Valeriana eriophylla is part of the Caprifoliaceae family and is known for its aromatic properties.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and resilience.
