Valeriana siifolia

Valeriana siifolia, commonly known as the silvery valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery or grayish down. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, usually pink or purple in color.

Valeriana siifolia is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming effects.
