Veronica polita subsp. polita

Veronica polita subsp.

polita, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant features small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
