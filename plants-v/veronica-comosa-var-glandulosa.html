Veronica comosa var. glandulosa

Veronica comosa var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, glandular hairs, which give the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
