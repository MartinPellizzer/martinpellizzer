Vitex tangensis

Vitex tangensis, commonly known as the Malayan chaste tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and it is known for its ornamental value in horticulture. Vitex tangensis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine.
