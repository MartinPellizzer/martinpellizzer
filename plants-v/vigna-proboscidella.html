Vigna proboscidella

Vigna proboscidella, commonly known as the African bean or wild pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits. The plant is well-adapted to arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. Its seeds are edible and have been used traditionally in various cuisines.

Vigna proboscidella also plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for agricultural systems.
