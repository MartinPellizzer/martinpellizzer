Viola pachysoma

Viola pachysoma, commonly known as the broad-leaf violet, is a flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Viola pachysoma is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
