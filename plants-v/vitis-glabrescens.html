Vitis glabrescens

Vitis glabrescens, commonly known as the Florida grape or sand grape, is a vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Vitis glabrescens is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.

It is considered a valuable plant for wildlife, providing food for birds and small mammals.
