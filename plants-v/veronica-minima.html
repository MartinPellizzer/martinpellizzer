Veronica minima

Veronica minima, commonly known as the lesser speedwell, is a small, flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and along riverbanks. The plant has delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It is a perennial herb that reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters.

Veronica minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
