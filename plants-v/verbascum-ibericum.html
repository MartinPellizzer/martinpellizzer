Verbascum ibericum

Verbascum ibericum, commonly known as the Iberian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

Verbascum ibericum is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
