Veronica cardiocarpa subsp. cardiocarpa

Veronica cardiocarpa subsp.

cardiocarpa, commonly known as the heart-leaved veronica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
