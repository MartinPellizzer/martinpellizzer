Vicia exigua var. hassei

Vicia exigua var. hassei, commonly known as the dwarf vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-30 cm, making it a low-growing species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
