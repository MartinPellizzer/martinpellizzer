Vangueria cana

Vangueria cana, commonly known as the silverleaf vangueria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance and aiding in water retention.

Vangueria cana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
