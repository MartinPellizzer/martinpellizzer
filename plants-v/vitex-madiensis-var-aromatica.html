Vitex madiensis var. aromatica

Vitex madiensis var. aromatica, commonly known as sweet vitex, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils. It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its woody stems and compact growth habit make it suitable for small gardens or as a hedge.
