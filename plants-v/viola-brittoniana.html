Viola brittoniana

Viola brittoniana, commonly known as the Britton's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain several small, dark seeds.

Viola brittoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
