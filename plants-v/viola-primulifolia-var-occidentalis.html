Viola primulifolia var. occidentalis

Viola primulifolia var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western viola, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
