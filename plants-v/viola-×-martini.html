Viola × martini

Viola × martini, commonly known as the Dutch viola or the garden viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, often hairy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from violet to purple. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola × martini is known for its adaptability and is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
