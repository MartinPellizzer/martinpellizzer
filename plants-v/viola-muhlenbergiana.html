Viola muhlenbergiana

Viola muhlenbergiana, commonly known as the Muhlenberg's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy violet flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola muhlenbergiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native gardens.
