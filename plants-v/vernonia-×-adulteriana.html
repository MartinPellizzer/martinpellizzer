Vernonia × adulteriana

Vernonia × adulteriana, commonly known as the hybrid ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia × adulteriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
