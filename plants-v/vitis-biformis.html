Vitis biformis

Vitis biformis, commonly known as the two-lobed grapevine, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a woody vine characterized by its compound leaves with two leaflets, each having a distinct lobed shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of round, edible berries. Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and horticultural research.

Vitis biformis is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
