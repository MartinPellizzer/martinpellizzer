Vernonia proclivicola

Vernonia proclivicola, commonly known as the cliff vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in cliff faces and canyon walls.

Vernonia proclivicola is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant once established.
