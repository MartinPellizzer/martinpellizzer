Vallisneria jacquinii

Vallisneria jacquinii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by the development of elongated, spiral-shaped fruits. Vallisneria jacquinii thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense underwater mats.

Its rapid growth makes it both a valuable aquatic plant for habitat and a potential invasive species in non-native waters.
