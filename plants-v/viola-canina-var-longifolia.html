Viola canina var. longifolia

Viola canina var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved heart violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive yellow center. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
