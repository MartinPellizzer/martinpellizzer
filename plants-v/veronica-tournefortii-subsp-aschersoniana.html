Veronica tournefortii subsp. aschersoniana

Veronica tournefortii subsp.

aschersoniana, commonly known as the ascherson's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
