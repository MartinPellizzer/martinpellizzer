Vicia exstipulata

Vicia exstipulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its pods contain several oval seeds. It is often found in grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Vicia exstipulata is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
