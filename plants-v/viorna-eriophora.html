Viorna eriophora

Viorna eriophora, commonly known as the narrowleaf viorna, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, ascending stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Viorna eriophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
