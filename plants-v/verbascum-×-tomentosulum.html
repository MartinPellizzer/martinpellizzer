Verbascum × tomentosulum

Verbascum × tomentosulum, commonly known as the fuzzy mullein, is a hybrid plant in the genus Verbascum, native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, branched inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
