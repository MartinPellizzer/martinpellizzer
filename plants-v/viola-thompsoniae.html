Viola thompsoniae

Viola thompsoniae, commonly known as Thompson's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to retain moisture.

Viola thompsoniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific climate requirements.
