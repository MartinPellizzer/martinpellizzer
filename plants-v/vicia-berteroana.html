Vicia berteroana

Vicia berteroana, commonly known as the Bertero's vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. It has small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vicia berteroana is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a source of forage for wildlife.
