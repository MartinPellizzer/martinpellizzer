Vernonia reverchonii

Vernonia reverchonii, commonly known as the Arizona tawny coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces striking yellow to orange coneflower-like blooms. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in full sun.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
