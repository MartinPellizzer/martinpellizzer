Vicia persepolitana

Vicia persepolitana, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the spring. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.

Vicia persepolitana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such climates.
