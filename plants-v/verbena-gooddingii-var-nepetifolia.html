Verbena gooddingii var. nepetifolia

Verbena gooddingii var. nepetifolia, commonly known as the blue verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
