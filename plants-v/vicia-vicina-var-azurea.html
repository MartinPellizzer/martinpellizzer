Vicia vicina var. azurea

Vicia vicina var. azurea, commonly known as Azure Field Vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Its blue-violet flowers add a striking color to its natural environment.
