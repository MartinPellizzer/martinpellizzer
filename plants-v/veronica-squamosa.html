Veronica squamosa

Veronica squamosa, commonly known as the squamosa speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, scaly texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Veronica squamosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and easy cultivation.
