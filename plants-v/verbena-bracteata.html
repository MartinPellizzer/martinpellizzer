Verbena bracteata

Verbena bracteata, commonly known as the blue verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with narrow, gray-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Verbena bracteata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
