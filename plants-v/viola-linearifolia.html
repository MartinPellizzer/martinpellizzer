Viola linearifolia

Viola linearifolia, commonly known as the slender violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe, including the British Isles, and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in spring. Viola linearifolia is often found in woodland edges, grassy areas, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for use in gardens and as a decorative plant.
