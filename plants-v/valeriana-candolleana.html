Valeriana candolleana

Valeriana candolleana, commonly known as the Himalayan valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its rhizomes and roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant floral display.
