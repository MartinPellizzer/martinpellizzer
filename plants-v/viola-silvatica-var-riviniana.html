Viola silvatica var. riviniana

Viola silvatica var. riviniana, commonly known as the river viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist woodland habitats.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green and slightly serrated. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white keel. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in riparian zones.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to forest floors.
