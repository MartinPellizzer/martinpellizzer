Verbena lobata var. glabrata

Verbena lobata var. glabrata, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant has hairy, grayish-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and inland areas.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
