Verbena pilosiuscula

Verbena pilosiuscula, commonly known as the woolly verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy spikes of blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
