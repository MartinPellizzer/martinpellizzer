Vitis kanguruh

Vitis kanguruh, commonly known as the Kangaroo Grape, is a species of vine native to Australia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of berries, which are typically dark blue or black when mature. Vitis kanguruh is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Its berries are not typically consumed by humans but are used in traditional Indigenous practices and have potential for ornamental horticulture.
