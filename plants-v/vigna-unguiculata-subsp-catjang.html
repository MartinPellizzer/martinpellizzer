Vigna unguiculata subsp. catjang

Vigna unguiculata subsp.

catjang, commonly known as African cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa. It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with small, hairy stipules.

The plant produces elongated, green pods that contain edible seeds used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is often grown for its nutritional value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The subspecies is valued for its adaptability and is an important crop in many tropical and subtropical regions.
