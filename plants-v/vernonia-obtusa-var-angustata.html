Vernonia obtusa var. angustata

Vernonia obtusa var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, dark purple to violet composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairies and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
