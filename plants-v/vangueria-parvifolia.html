Vangueria parvifolia

Vangueria parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved vangueria, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

Vangueria parvifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
