Viburnum tashiroi

Viburnum tashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Viburnum tashiroi thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in gardens.
