Viola glechomoides

Viola glechomoides, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or white. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in loose, well-drained soils.

Viola glechomoides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
