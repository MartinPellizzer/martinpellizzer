Viburnum wurdackii

Viburnum wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in flat-topped umbels. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds but not for humans.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
