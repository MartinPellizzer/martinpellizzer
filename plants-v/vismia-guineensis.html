Vismia guineensis

Vismia guineensis, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Vismia guineensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
