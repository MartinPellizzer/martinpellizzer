Vaccinium miquelii var. atjehense

Vaccinium miquelii var. atjehense, commonly known as the Atjeh blueberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that range in color from blue to black. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.
