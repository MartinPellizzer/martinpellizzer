Viburnum wrightii f. sylvestre

Viburnum wrightii f. sylvestre, commonly known as the sweet viburnum or wild viburnum, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets, typically arranged oppositely on the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and persistent fruit, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Viburnum wrightii f. sylvestre is also valued for its ecological role, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
