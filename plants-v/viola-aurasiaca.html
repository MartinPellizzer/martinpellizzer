Viola aurasiaca

Viola aurasiaca, commonly known as the Aurasic violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring cool and moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Viola aurasiaca is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
