Vittadinia cervicularis var. occidentalis

Vittadinia cervicularis var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western burr daisy, is a flowering plant native to western Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces a distinctive burr-like structure composed of bracts that enclose the seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
