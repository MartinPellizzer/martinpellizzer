Vepris zambesiaca

Vepris zambesiaca, commonly known as African wild lime, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice. Its aromatic properties are attributed to the presence of essential oils in its leaves and fruits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
