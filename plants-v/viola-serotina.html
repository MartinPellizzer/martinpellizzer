Viola serotina

Viola serotina, commonly known as the late violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often marked by a dark purple spot.

Viola serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
