Viola cerasifolia var. intermedia

Viola cerasifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the purple marsh violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to moist, woodland habitats in North America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural and garden settings.
