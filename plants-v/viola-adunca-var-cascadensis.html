Viola adunca var. cascadensis

Viola adunca var. cascadensis, commonly known as the Cascade violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in cool, humid climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
