Valeriana insignis

Valeriana insignis, commonly known as the Chilean valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Valeriana insignis is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
