Vicia moorei

Vicia moorei, commonly known as the Moore's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Vicia moorei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It also has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
