Veronica macrostachya

Veronica macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stemmed speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged flowering period and attractive foliage.
