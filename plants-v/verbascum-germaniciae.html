Verbascum germaniciae

Verbascum germaniciae, commonly known as the Germanic mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
