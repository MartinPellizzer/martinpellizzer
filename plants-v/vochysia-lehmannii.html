Vochysia lehmannii

Vochysia lehmannii, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets, and it produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable regions.
