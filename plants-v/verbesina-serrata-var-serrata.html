Verbesina serrata var. serrata

Verbesina serrata var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and serrated, contributing to its common name.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides.
