Verbascum gallicum

Verbascum gallicum, commonly known as French mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in herbal medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Verbascum gallicum is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating respiratory conditions.
