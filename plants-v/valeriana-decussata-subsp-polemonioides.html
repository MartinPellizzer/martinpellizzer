Valeriana decussata subsp. polemonioides

Valeriana decussata subsp.

polemonioides, commonly known as the pink valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States. It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and contains aromatic compounds responsible for its characteristic scent.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
