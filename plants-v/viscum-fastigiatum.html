Viscum fastigiatum

Viscum fastigiatum, commonly known as the glossy privet, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with a waxy, glossy appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its berries are small, black, and toxic to humans and animals.

Viscum fastigiatum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
