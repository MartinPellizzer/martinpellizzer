Vernonia harrisii

Vernonia harrisii, commonly known as the Harris's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Vernonia harrisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
