Vernonia yatesii

Vernonia yatesii, commonly known as the Yate's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces small, achene-like fruits.

Vernonia yatesii is adapted to arid environments and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
