Vismia cavalcantei var. arborea

Vismia cavalcantei var. arborea, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
