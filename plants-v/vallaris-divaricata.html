Vallaris divaricata

Vallaris divaricata, commonly known as the variable vallaris, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Vallaris divaricata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
