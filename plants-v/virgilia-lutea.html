Virgilia lutea

Virgilia lutea, commonly known as the yellow virgilia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and shrublands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Virgilia lutea is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
