Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. simplex

Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. simplex, commonly known as water speedwell, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in conditions with abundant sunlight. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in ponds, ditches, and stream margins, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
