Vernonia cordifolia

Vernonia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple-blue composite flower heads. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Vernonia cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
