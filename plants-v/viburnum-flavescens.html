Viburnum flavescens

Viburnum flavescens, commonly known as the sweet pepper shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Viburnum flavescens thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
