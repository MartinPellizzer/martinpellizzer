Vernonia lessingiana

Vernonia lessingiana, commonly known as the lesser ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Vernonia lessingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
