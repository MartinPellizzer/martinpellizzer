Verbena trifurcata

Verbena trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena trifurcata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
