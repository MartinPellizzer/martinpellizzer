Viorna hirsutissima

Viorna hirsutissima, commonly known as the woolly viorna, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture and are covered in fine, white hairs.

Viorna hirsutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
