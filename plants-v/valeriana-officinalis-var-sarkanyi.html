Valeriana officinalis var. sarkanyi

Valeriana officinalis var. sarkanyi, commonly known as the Hungarian valerian, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters called cymes. Its roots are valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine.

This variety is distinguished by its unique chemical composition and ecological adaptations to its native habitat.
