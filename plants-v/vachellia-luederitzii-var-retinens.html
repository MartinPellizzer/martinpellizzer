Vachellia luederitzii var. retinens

Vachellia luederitzii var. retinens, commonly known as the Luederitz acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
