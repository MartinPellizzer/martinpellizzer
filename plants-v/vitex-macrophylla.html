Vitex macrophylla

Vitex macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

The fruit is a dry capsule that releases small, winged seeds when mature.
