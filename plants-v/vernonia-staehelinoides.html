Vernonia staehelinoides

Vernonia staehelinoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist, which are clustered in dense, spherical formations. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for pollinators and a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects.
