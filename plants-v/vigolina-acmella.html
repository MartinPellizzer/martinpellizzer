Vigolina acmella

Vigolina acmella, commonly known as the golden groundcherry, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and broad, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are encased in a fleshy, cup-like calyx. These fruits are typically harvested when they turn a deep orange-red, indicating maturity.

Vigolina acmella is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
