Viguiera longipes

Viguiera longipes, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy purple to pink flower heads bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viguiera longipes is often found in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments.
