Vernonanthura sambrayana

Vernonanthura sambrayana, commonly known as the Sambrayana orchid, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often with a waxy texture to help retain moisture.

Vernonanthura sambrayana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
