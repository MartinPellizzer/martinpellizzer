Verticordia plumosa var. pleiobotrya

Verticordia plumosa var. pleiobotrya, commonly known as the feathered bottlebrush, is a species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in heathland and sandy soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and they appear in late summer to early autumn.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
