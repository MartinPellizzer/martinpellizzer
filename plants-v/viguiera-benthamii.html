Viguiera benthamii

Viguiera benthamii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Viguiera benthamii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
