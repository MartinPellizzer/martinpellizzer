Verbascum valentinum

Verbascum valentinum, commonly known as the Valentine's mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbascum valentinum is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects.
