Veronica demissa

Veronica demissa, commonly known as slender speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Veronica demissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
