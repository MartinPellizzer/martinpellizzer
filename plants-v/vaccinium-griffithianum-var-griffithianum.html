Vaccinium griffithianum var. griffithianum

Vaccinium griffithianum var. griffithianum, commonly known as the Griffith's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark blue or purple in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
