Vouapa venulosa

Vouapa venulosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its stems are slender and can climb or trail, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises.

Vouapa venulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid environments.
