Veronica ciliata subsp. ciliata

Veronica ciliata subsp.

ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf veronica, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae. It is characterized by its dense, hairy leaves that give it a soft, velvety appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to Europe and is often found in moist, grassy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
