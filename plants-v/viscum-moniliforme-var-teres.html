Viscum moniliforme var. teres

Viscum moniliforme var. teres, commonly known as the globular mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Argentina. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that contain toxic seeds. Its leaves are usually rounded and clustered in dense, globular formations.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain birds and insects.
