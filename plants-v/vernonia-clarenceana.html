Vernonia clarenceana

Vernonia clarenceana, commonly known as Clarence's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Vernonia clarenceana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
