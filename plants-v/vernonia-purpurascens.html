Vernonia purpurascens

Vernonia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to reddish-pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The showy flowers bloom in late summer and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
