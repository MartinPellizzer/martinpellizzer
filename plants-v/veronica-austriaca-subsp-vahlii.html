Veronica austriaca subsp. vahlii

Veronica austriaca subsp.

vahlii, commonly known as the Alpine speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
