Vernonia hildebrandtii

Vernonia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces.

Vernonia hildebrandtii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and dry grasslands.
