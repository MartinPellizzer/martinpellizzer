Verbena litoralis var. brasiliensis

Verbena litoralis var. brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian sea verbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Brazil.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky shores.

Its adaptability to salty and windy environments makes it a hardy species suited for maritime climates.
