Vepris macedoi

Vepris macedoi, commonly known as Macedonian laurier or wild laurier, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has pinnate leaves with a fragrant, citrus-like scent, and its bark is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

The fruit, though not widely cultivated, is sometimes used in local remedies and as a spice.
