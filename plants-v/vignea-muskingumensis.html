Vignea muskingumensis

Vignea muskingumensis, commonly known as the Muskingum vine, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to green in color. Vignea muskingumensis is often found in moist, wooded areas and can thrive in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
