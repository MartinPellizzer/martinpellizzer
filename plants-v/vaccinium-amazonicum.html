Vaccinium amazonicum

Vaccinium amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazon huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical environments.

Due to its unique ecological role and potential health benefits, it is of interest to both botanists and conservationists.
