Verbascum × quercetorum

Verbascum × quercetorum, commonly known as the oakleaf mullein, is a hybrid plant that combines characteristics of Verbascum species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, often found in rocky or dry soils. The plant features large, velvety leaves that are lobed and resemble oak leaves, giving it its common name. It produces tall spikes of yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.
