Viola mandshurica f. ciliata

Viola mandshurica f. ciliata, commonly known as the fringed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with ciliate margins. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
