Vicia galilaea

Vicia galilaea, commonly known as the Galilaea vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by wind.

Vicia galilaea is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
