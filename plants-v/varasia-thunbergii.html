Varasia thunbergii

Varasia thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Varasia thunbergii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
