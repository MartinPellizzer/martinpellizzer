Vangueria randii

Vangueria randii, commonly known as the Natal vangueria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Vangueria randii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and aesthetic features.
