Vernonia dura var. megacephala

Vernonia dura var. megacephala, commonly known as the giant ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a grayish-green appearance.

This species is adapted to wetland and moist soil conditions, making it a common sight in swampy areas and along riverbanks.
