Viburnum floccosum

Viburnum floccosum, commonly known as the white-fruited snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green above and grayish-white below. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by fluffy, white berries that persist into the winter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Viburnum floccosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
