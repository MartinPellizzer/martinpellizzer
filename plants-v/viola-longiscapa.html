Viola longiscapa

Viola longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy violet flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep purple. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Viola longiscapa is often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows, where it blooms in early spring.
