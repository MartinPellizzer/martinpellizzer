Veronica raoulii subsp. maccaskillii

Veronica raoulii subsp.

maccaskillii, commonly known as the Maccaskill veronica, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
