Veronicastrum sibiricum var. zuccarinii

Veronicastrum sibiricum var. zuccarinii, commonly known as Siberian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
