Vangueria volkensii var. fyffei

Vangueria volkensii var. fyffei, commonly known as the Red-flowered Vangueria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in terminal clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy areas, preferring full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
