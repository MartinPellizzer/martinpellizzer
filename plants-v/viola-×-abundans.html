Viola × abundans

Viola × abundans, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10–20 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in partial shade. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value.

Viola × abundans is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
