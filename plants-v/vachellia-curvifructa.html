Vachellia curvifructa

Vachellia curvifructa, commonly known as the African broom, is a leguminous shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom throughout the dry season, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties. Its seed pods are curved and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vachellia curvifructa is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
