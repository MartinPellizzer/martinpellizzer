Vaccinium chaetothrix

Vaccinium chaetothrix, commonly known as the rough bilberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant features small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are leathery and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Vaccinium chaetothrix thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and temperate forest regions.
