Vignea rosea

Vignea rosea, commonly known as the rose vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and slender, woody stems that support its lush foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its fruits are small, round pods containing seeds.

Vignea rosea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
