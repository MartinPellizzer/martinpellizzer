Vignea dioica

Vignea dioica, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in groups of three. Vignea dioica is a dicot, belonging to the family Fabaceae, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
