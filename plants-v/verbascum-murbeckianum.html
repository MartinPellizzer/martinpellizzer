Verbascum murbeckianum

Verbascum murbeckianum, commonly known as the Murbeck's mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Verbascum murbeckianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
