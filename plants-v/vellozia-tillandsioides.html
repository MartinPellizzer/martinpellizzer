Vellozia tillandsioides

Vellozia tillandsioides, commonly known as the lace fern, is a unique plant belonging to the family Velloziaceae.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it grows in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its striking appearance and specialized adaptations, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
