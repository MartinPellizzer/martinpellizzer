Veronica multifida var. tenuis

Veronica multifida var. tenuis, commonly known as narrow-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, often hairy stems that grow upright or prostrate, depending on the habitat. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and finely toothed, giving it a distinctive, delicate appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows, rocky areas, and along pathways.
