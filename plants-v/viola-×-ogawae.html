Viola × ogawae

Viola × ogawae, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Japan, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas. The flowers typically have five petals, with the lower one being larger and more prominently marked.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
