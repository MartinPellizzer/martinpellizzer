Vernonia chapmanii

Vernonia chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. Its foliage is gray-green with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
