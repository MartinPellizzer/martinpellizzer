Vaccinium semipersistens var. uniquum

Vaccinium semipersistens var. uniquum, commonly known as the unique blueberry shrub, is a rare variant of the blueberry plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight pubescent texture. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, which give way to small, edible berries that are typically a shade of blue or purple. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool climate with adequate moisture.

Due to its limited distribution and unique genetic traits, it is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
