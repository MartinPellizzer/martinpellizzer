Vaccinium × brunoense

Vaccinium × brunoense, commonly known as the blueberry bush, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Vaccinium corymbosum (blueberry) and Vaccinium angustifolium (lowbush blueberry).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries that range in color from blue to purple.

Vaccinium × brunoense is well adapted to acidic soils and is often cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in antioxidants and used in various culinary applications.
