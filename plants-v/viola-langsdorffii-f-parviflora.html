Viola langsdorffii f. parviflora

Viola langsdorffii f. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered viola, is a flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur. Native to South America, this species thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
