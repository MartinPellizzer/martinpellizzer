Viola odorata var. suavis

Viola odorata var. suavis, commonly known as sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often marked with yellow or white spots. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The fragrance of its flowers is one of its most notable characteristics, making it a popular choice for gardens and perfumery.
