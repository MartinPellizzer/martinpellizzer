Vitis nymphaeifolia

Vitis nymphaeifolia, commonly known as the water grape or waterberry, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn blue when mature. Vitis nymphaeifolia thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
