Vicia godronii

Vicia godronii, commonly known as Godron's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in slender pods that mature into a dark brown color.

Vicia godronii is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
