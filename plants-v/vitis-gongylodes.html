Vitis gongylodes

Vitis gongylodes, commonly known as the Chinese grape or red grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round, red or black berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Vitis gongylodes thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Vitaceae family.
