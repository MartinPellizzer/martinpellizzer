Vigna nyangensis

Vigna nyangensis, commonly known as the Nyangensis cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are edible and rich in protein. Vigna nyangensis is adapted to dry and semi-arid conditions, making it an important crop in regions with limited water resources.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both agricultural and ecological purposes.
