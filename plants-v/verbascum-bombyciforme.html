Verbascum bombyciforme

Verbascum bombyciforme, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem covered in dense, soft, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a tall, upright spike. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a thick layer of silky hairs.

Verbascum bombyciforme is often found in dry, rocky soils and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
