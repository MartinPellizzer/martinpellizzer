Vincetoxicum multinerve var. kiyohikoanum

Vincetoxicum multinerve var. kiyohikoanum, commonly known as the Kiyohiko Japanese honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The leaves are typically ovate with a serrated margin and a hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
