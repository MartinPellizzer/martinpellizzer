Verbesina scabra

Verbesina scabra, commonly known as roughhairy beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features rough, hairy stems and leaves, which help in reducing water loss in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow and produce small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Verbesina scabra is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
