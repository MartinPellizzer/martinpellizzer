Vitis glomerata

Vitis glomerata, commonly known as the cluster grape or swamp grape, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, often white or greenish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically dark purple when mature. It thrives in moist, wetland environments and is often found near streams or in swampy areas.

Vitis glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
