Verbascum speciosum

Verbascum speciosum, commonly known as the showy mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum speciosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
