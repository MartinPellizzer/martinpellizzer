Vicia loweana

Vicia loweana, commonly known as the Lowes vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Vicia loweana is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
