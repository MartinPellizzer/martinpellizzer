Vinca rosea var. albiflora

Vinca rosea var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered periwinkle, is a variant of the Madagascar periwinkle.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Madagascar, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in tropical and subtropical regions.
