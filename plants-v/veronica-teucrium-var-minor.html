Veronica teucrium var. minor

Veronica teucrium var. minor, commonly known as lesser speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
