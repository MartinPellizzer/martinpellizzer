Vicia johannis var. intermedia

Vicia johannis var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate John's vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods are slender, hairy, and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
