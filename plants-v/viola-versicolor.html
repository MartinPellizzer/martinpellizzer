Viola versicolor

Viola versicolor, commonly known as the common violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, colorful flowers that range from purple to white and blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Viola versicolor is known for its ability to thrive in temperate climates and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Its flowers are also popular in gardens for their ornamental value.
