Venidium bellidiastrum

Venidium bellidiastrum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
