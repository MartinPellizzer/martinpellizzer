Veronica caucasica

Veronica caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It is part of the Plantaginaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar characteristics.

Veronica caucasica is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
