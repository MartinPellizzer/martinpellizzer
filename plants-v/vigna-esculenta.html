Vigna esculenta

Vigna esculenta, commonly known as black-eyed pea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa and widely cultivated in warm climates.

It produces pods containing small, round seeds that are rich in protein and dietary fiber. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into the characteristic pod fruits. Vigna esculenta is a hardy annual that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

It is an important crop in many regions for both food security and nutritional value.
