Viola pumilio f. variegata

Viola pumilio f. variegata, commonly known as the dwarf violet or variegated violet, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegation pattern of white and green, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and unique leaf coloration.
