Vitis campestris

Vitis campestris, commonly known as the field grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, and it typically grows in open areas such as fields and along roadsides. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its vines can climb using tendrils.

Vitis campestris is valued for its ornamental qualities and its historical use in winemaking.
