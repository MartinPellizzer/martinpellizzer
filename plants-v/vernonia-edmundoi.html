Vernonia edmundoi

Vernonia edmundoi, commonly known as the Edmund's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are purple to brown in color.

Vernonia edmundoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
