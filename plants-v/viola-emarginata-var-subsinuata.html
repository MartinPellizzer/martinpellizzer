Viola emarginata var. subsinuata

Viola emarginata var. subsinuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive notched base and finely toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and moist meadows, preferring well-drained, loamy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
