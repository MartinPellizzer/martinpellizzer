Vitis vinifera var. purpurea

Vitis vinifera var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple grapevine, is a cultivar of the common grapevine known for its distinctive deep purple foliage and berries.

It is a deciduous vine that produces clusters of small, sweet grapes with a rich, complex flavor. The plant is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and for its fruit, which can be used in winemaking or culinary applications. Its leaves turn vibrant red and orange in the fall, adding visual interest to gardens.

This variety thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
