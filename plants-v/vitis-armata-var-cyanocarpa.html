Vitis armata var. cyanocarpa

Vitis armata var. cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blue grape vine, is a rare and ornamental species native to parts of China.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, dark blue to black berries that mature in late summer. The leaves are typically lobed and have a glossy texture, adding visual interest to the plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its limited distribution and unique coloration, it is highly valued in botanical collections.
