Vernonia teitensis

Vernonia teitensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to tropical climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually blue to purple in color.

Vernonia teitensis plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
