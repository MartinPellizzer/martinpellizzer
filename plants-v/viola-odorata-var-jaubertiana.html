Viola odorata var. jaubertiana

Viola odorata var. jaubertiana, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular garden plant.

The fragrance of its flowers is often described as sweet and honey-like, contributing to its ornamental value.
