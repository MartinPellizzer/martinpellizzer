Viscum cruciatum

Viscum cruciatum, commonly known as the European mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has a distinctive appearance with smooth, waxy leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in late winter. The plant produces small, sticky berries that are a food source for birds, which in turn aid in seed dispersal. Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis.

Viscum cruciatum is often associated with Christmas traditions and has cultural significance in many regions.
