Veronica pilocarpa

Veronica pilocarpa, commonly known as the woolly speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Veronica pilocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy and drought-resistant nature.
