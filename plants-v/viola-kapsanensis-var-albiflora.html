Viola kapsanensis var. albiflora

Viola kapsanensis var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are predominantly white with a faint violet hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
