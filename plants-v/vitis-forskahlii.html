Vitis forskahlii

Vitis forskahlii, commonly known as the Kansu grape, is a species of flowering vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, sweet berries. Vitis forskahlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
