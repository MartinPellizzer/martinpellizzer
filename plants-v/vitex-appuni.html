Vitex appuni

Vitex appuni, commonly known as the chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually gray-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a dry, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Vitex appuni is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
