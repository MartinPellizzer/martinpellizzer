Vernonia helferi

Vernonia helferi, commonly known as the Helfer's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Vernonia helferi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
