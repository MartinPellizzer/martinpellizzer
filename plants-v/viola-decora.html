Viola decora

Viola decora, commonly known as the showy viola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Viola decora is a perennial herb that produces showy flowers from late spring to early summer.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
