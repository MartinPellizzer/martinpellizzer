Verticordia setigera

Verticordia setigera, commonly known as the red-stemmed featherflower, is a striking Western Australian plant belonging to the family Proteaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clusters of narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces strikingly ornate, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathland and coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
