Vicia luganensis

Vicia luganensis, commonly known as the Laganese vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Vicia luganensis is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and forest edges.
