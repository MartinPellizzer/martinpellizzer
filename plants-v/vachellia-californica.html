Vachellia californica

Vachellia californica, commonly known as the California acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and Baja California.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in drought conditions.

Vachellia californica is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
