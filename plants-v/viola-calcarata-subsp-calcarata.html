Viola calcarata subsp. calcarata

Viola calcarata subsp.

calcarata, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
