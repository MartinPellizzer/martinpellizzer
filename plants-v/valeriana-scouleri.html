Valeriana scouleri

Valeriana scouleri, commonly known as Douglas's valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. The plant features a stout, upright stem with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Valeriana scouleri is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
