Viola muriculata

Viola muriculata, commonly known as the purple heart viola, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet or purple flowers with a distinctive dark center. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola muriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
