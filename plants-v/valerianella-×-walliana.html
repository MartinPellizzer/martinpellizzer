Valerianella × walliana

Valerianella × walliana, commonly known as garden sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Valerianella × walliana is often cultivated as a decorative ground cover or for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
