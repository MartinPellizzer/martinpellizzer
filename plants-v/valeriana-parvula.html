Valeriana parvula

Valeriana parvula, commonly known as the small valerian, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative properties.
