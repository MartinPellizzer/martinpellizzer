Vernonia namnaoensis

Vernonia namnaoensis, commonly known as the Namnao ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest clearings. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia namnaoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
