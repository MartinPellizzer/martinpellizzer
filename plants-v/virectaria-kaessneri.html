Virectaria kaessneri

Virectaria kaessneri, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
