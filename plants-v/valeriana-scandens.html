Valeriana scandens

Valeriana scandens, commonly known as climbing valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Valeriana scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
