Vernonia baldwinii f. alba

Vernonia baldwinii f. alba, commonly known as the white vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
