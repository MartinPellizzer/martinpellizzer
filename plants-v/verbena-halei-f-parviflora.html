Verbena halei f. parviflora

Verbena halei f. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf verbena, is a compact, flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet or blue. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely appreciated in horticultural settings.
