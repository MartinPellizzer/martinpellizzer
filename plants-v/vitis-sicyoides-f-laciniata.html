Vitis sicyoides f. laciniata

Vitis sicyoides f. laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaf grape, is a unique variant of the grapevine species Vitis sicyoides.

This plant is characterized by its finely lobed and serrated leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

While it is not widely cultivated for fruit, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
