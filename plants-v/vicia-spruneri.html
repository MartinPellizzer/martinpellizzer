Vicia spruneri

Vicia spruneri, commonly known as the alpine vetch, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Vicia spruneri is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
