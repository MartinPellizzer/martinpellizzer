Vitex rigens

Vitex rigens, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 feet tall, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vitex rigens is also known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and aromatic foliage.
