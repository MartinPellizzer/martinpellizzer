Vernonia spixiana

Vernonia spixiana, commonly known as the Spix's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Vernonia spixiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
