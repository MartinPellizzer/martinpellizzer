Vaccinium japonicum var. fauriei

Vaccinium japonicum var. fauriei, commonly known as Japanese huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that range in color from red to black. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Japanese cuisine.
