Viola tripartita

Viola tripartita, commonly known as the three-lobed violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in woodland edges and moist meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, and its flowers are typically violet or purple in color. Viola tripartita is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
