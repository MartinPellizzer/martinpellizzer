Viola pseudosuavis

Viola pseudosuavis, commonly known as the false sweet violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals, often featuring a distinctive dark spot at the base. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Viola pseudosuavis is valued for its beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
