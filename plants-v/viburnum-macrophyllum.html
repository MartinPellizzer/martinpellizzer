Viburnum macrophyllum

Viburnum macrophyllum, commonly known as Pacific dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a mottled appearance with patches of gray and red.

Viburnum macrophyllum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to wildlife.
