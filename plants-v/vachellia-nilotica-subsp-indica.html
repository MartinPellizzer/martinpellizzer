Vachellia nilotica subsp. indica

Vachellia nilotica subsp.

indica, commonly known as the Indian screw tree, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its straight, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets.

The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
