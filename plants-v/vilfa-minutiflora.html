Vilfa minutiflora

Vilfa minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a small flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its small size and ornamental flowers, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
