Viola apetala

Viola apetala, commonly known as the pale violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, pale violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Viola apetala is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
