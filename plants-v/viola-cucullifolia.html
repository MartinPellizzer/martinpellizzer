Viola cucullifolia

Viola cucullifolia, commonly known as the hooded violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hood-like structure covering its flowers, which are typically violet or purple in color. The plant blooms in spring, producing small, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique floral morphology.
