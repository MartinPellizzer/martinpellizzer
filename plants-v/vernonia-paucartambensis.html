Vernonia paucartambensis

Vernonia paucartambensis, commonly known as the Andean ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to purple.

Vernonia paucartambensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
