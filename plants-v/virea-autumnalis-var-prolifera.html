Virea autumnalis var. prolifera

Virea autumnalis var. prolifera, commonly known as autumn virea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the fall, producing pale blue to violet flowers that attract pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its varietal form, prolifera, is noted for its vigorous growth and ability to produce multiple shoots, enhancing its ornamental value.
