Verbascum taygeteum

Verbascum taygeteum, commonly known as the Greek mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
