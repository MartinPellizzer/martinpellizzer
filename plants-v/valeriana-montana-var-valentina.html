Valeriana montana var. valentina

Valeriana montana var. valentina, commonly known as the Alpine valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland edges.

Valeriana montana var. valentina is valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
