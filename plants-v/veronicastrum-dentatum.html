Veronicastrum dentatum

Veronicastrum dentatum, commonly known as blue mist spirea, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open areas such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronicastrum dentatum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
