Vernonia trichotoma

Vernonia trichotoma, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and dense, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Vernonia trichotoma is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in traditional construction and as a source of charcoal.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in various African cultures.
