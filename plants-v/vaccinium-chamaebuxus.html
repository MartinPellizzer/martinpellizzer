Vaccinium chamaebuxus

Vaccinium chamaebuxus, commonly known as the common blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, blue-black berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and is often found in heathlands and acidic forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife and ecosystems.
