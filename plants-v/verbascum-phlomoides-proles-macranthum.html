Verbascum phlomoides proles macranthum

Verbascum phlomoides proles macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered in soft, fuzzy gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and densely pubescent, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, meadows, and rocky habitats.
