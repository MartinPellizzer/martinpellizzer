Vignea cespitosa var. polygama

Vignea cespitosa var. polygama, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste grounds.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
