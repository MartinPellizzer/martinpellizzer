Viola binayensis

Viola binayensis, commonly known as the Philippine violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, purple-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Viola binayensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
