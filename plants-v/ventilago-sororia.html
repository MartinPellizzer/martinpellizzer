Ventilago sororia

Ventilago sororia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant has smooth, pale grey bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Ventilago sororia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
