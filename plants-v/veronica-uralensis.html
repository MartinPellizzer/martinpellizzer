Veronica uralensis

Veronica uralensis, commonly known as the Siberian speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite pairs of oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Veronica uralensis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
