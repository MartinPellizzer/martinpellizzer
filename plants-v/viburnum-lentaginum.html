Viburnum lentaginum

Viburnum lentaginum, commonly known as the blackhaw viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves that turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. Viburnum lentaginum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also known for its hardiness and adaptability to different climates.
