Vicia lomensis

Vicia lomensis, commonly known as the Lomond vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Vicia lomensis is primarily of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential horticultural value.
