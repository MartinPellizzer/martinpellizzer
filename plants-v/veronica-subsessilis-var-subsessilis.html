Veronica subsessilis var. subsessilis

Veronica subsessilis var. subsessilis, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and native landscaping.
