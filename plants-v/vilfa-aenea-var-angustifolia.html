Vilfa aenea var. angustifolia

Vilfa aenea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple to pink flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological and ornamental purposes.
