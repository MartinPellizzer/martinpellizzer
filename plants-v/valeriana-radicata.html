Valeriana radicata

Valeriana radicata, commonly known as valerian, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright stems, lance-shaped leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is known for its strong, often unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom. Valerian is widely cultivated for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects on the nervous system.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens and herbal collections.
