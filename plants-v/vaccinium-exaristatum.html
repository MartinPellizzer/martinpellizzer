Vaccinium exaristatum

Vaccinium exaristatum, commonly known as the spiked blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits are small, tart berries that ripen to a dark blue or purple color.

This species is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and mountainous areas.
