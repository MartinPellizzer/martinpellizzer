Vernonia speiracephala

Vernonia speiracephala, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
