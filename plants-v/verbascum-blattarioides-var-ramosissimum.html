Verbascum blattarioides var. ramosissimum

Verbascum blattarioides var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a similar fuzzy coating.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry environments.
