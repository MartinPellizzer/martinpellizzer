Viola hookeri

Viola hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking violet or purple flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
