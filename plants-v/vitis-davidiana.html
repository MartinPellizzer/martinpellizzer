Vitis davidiana

Vitis davidiana, commonly known as the David's grape, is a woody vine native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible berries. The plant is often found in forested areas and can climb using tendrils to reach sunlight. Its berries are typically dark purple or black and are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Vitis davidiana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
