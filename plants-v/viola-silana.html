Viola silana

Viola silana, commonly known as the Italian violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in rocky or woodland habitats. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with five petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Viola silana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
