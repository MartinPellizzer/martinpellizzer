Verbesina veronicifolia

Verbesina veronicifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, disturbed soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

Verbesina veronicifolia is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
