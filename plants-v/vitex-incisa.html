Vitex incisa

Vitex incisa, commonly known as the fragrant verbena or the Indian lilac, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in summer. Vitex incisa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and ornamental value have contributed to its cultivation in various parts of the world.
