Veronica ciliata subsp. zhongdianensis

Veronica ciliata subsp.

zhongdianensis, commonly known as the Zhongdian speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of China. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features slender stems that are often hairy, with small, blue to violet flowers clustered in racemes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
