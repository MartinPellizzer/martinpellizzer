Vernonia lasiopus var. grandiceps

Vernonia lasiopus var. grandiceps, commonly known as the tall ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by narrow, greenish-blue ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped, opposite leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

The flowers bloom from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
