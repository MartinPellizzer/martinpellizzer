Vernonia interior

Vernonia interior, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands, and it thrives in full sun. Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators like butterflies and bees, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
