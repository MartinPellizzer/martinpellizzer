Vincetoxicum assadii

Vincetoxicum assadii, commonly known as the Assadi's glorybower, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple hue at the base. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden cultivation.

Vincetoxicum assadii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
