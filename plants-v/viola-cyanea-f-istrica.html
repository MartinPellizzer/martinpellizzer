Viola cyanea f. istrica

Viola cyanea f. istrica, commonly known as the Istrian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the stems are hairy and upright.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
