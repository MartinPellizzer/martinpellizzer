Veronica × liabaudii

Veronica × liabaudii, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Veronica × liabaudii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
