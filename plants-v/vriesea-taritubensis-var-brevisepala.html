Vriesea taritubensis var. brevisepala

Vriesea taritubensis var. brevisepala, commonly known as the short-lobed vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to the humid forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spathaceous inflorescence that displays a range of colors from red to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant features broad, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette, creating a compact and visually appealing form. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
