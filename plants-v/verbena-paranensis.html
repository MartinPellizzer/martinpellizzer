Verbena paranensis

Verbena paranensis, commonly known as the Parana verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of blue, purple, and pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in horticulture.
