Verbena officinalis var. orientalis

Verbena officinalis var. orientalis, commonly known as oriental verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
