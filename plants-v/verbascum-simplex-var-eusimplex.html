Verbascum simplex var. eusimplex

Verbascum simplex var. eusimplex, commonly known as the common mullein.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a cylindrical spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its leaves and flowers have been used historically for their medicinal properties.
