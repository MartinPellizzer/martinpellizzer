Viola kutzingiana

Viola kutzingiana, commonly known as the Chinese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to China and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.
