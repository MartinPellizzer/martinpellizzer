Viola allardii

Viola allardii, commonly known as the Allard's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur.

Viola allardii is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
