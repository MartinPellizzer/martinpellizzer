Vigna hispida

Vigna hispida, commonly known as hairy vigna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Vigna hispida is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.
