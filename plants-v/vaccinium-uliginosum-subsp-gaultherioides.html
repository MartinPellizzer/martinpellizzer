Vaccinium uliginosum subsp. gaultherioides

Vaccinium uliginosum subsp.

gaultherioides, commonly known as the alpine blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to alpine and subarctic regions. It produces small, blue-black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor.

The plant thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in wet, mossy environments. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

The species is adapted to cold climates and can withstand frost and snow cover.
