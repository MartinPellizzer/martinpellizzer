Verbena venosa var. lilacina

Verbena venosa var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to lilac, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage is narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly fuzzy texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
