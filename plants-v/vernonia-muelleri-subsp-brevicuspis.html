Vernonia muelleri subsp. brevicuspis

Vernonia muelleri subsp.

brevicuspis, commonly known as the narrow-ciliate vernonia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its inflorescence consists of yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow to brown ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
