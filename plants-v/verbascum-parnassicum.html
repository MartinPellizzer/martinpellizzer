Verbascum parnassicum

Verbascum parnassicum, commonly known as the Parnassian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Verbascum parnassicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
