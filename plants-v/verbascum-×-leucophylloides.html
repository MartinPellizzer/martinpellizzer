Verbascum × leucophylloides

Verbascum × leucophylloides, commonly known as the silverleaf mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its large, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
