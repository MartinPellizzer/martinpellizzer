Vaccinium mandarinorum

Vaccinium mandarinorum, commonly known as the Chinese huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright red berries that are edible. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor, making them popular in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
