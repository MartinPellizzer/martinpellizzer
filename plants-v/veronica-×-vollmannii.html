Veronica × vollmannii

Veronica × vollmannii, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. Veronica × vollmannii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
