Verbascum plantaginifolium

Verbascum plantaginifolium, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, white hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry environments.

Verbascum plantaginifolium is often used in traditional medicine for its purported soothing properties.
