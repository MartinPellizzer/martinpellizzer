Viola voliotisii

Viola voliotisii, commonly known as the Maltese violet, is a flowering plant native to the island of Malta.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained conditions. Viola voliotisii is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
