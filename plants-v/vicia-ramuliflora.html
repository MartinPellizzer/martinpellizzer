Vicia ramuliflora

Vicia ramuliflora, commonly known as the clustered vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, along roadsides, and in waste areas. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by wind.

Vicia ramuliflora is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
