Vigna parkeri subsp. parkeri

Vigna parkeri subsp.

parkeri, commonly known as the African wing bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and essential nutrients. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Vigna parkeri subsp.

parkeri is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
