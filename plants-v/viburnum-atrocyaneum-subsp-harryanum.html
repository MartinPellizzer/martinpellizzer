Viburnum atrocyaneum subsp. harryanum

Viburnum atrocyaneum subsp.

harryanum, commonly known as the black-fruited viburnum, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by the distinctive berries in the fall.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
