Vernonia simplex var. angustifolia

Vernonia simplex var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple composite flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
