Viola gmeliniana

Viola gmeliniana, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring cool, moist conditions. Viola gmeliniana is known for its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to cultivate hardy, decorative plants.
