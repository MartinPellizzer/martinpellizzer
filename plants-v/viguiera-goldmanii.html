Viguiera goldmanii

Viguiera goldmanii, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Viguiera goldmanii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements contribute to its ecological and ornamental value in arid landscapes.
