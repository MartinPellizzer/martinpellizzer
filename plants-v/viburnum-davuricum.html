Viburnum davuricum

Viburnum davuricum, commonly known as the Chinese snowball or dwarf snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Viburnum davuricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
