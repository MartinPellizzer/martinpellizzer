Viola odorata var. subcarnea

Viola odorata var. subcarnea, commonly known as the purple viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a deep green hue and produces fragrant, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
