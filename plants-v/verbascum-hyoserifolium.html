Verbascum hyoserifolium

Verbascum hyoserifolium, commonly known as the woolly flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its fuzzy texture and vibrant blooms make it an attractive addition to gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
