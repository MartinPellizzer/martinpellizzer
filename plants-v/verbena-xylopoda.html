Verbena xylopoda

Verbena xylopoda, commonly known as the flame verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with slender, branching stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

Verbena xylopoda thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
