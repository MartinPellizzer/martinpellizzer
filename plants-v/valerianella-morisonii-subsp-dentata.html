Valerianella morisonii subsp. dentata

Valerianella morisonii subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the toothed valerian, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in open habitats such as slopes, cliffs, and woodland edges. Valerianella morisonii subsp.

dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
