Veronica alpina var. wormskioldii

Veronica alpina var. wormskioldii, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Its botanical features include narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit adapted to harsh, cold environments.
