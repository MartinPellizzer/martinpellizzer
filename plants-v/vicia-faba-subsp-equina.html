Vicia faba subsp. equina

Vicia faba subsp.

equina, commonly known as the horse bean, is a subspecies of the broad bean native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used as a food source.

Botanically, it is characterized by its robust growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
