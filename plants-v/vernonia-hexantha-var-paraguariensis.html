Vernonia hexantha var. paraguariensis

Vernonia hexantha var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in full sun.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
