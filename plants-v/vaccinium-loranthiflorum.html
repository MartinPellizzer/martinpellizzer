Vaccinium loranthiflorum

Vaccinium loranthiflorum, commonly known as the Indian whortleberry, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its berries are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor, making them useful in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
