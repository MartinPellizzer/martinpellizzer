Valeriana scorpioides

Valeriana scorpioides, commonly known as the scorpion root or narrow-leaved valerian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic root system.

Valeriana scorpioides is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its sedative and calming effects.
