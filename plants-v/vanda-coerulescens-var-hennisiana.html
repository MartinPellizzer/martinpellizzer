Vanda coerulescens var. hennisiana

Vanda coerulescens var. hennisiana, commonly known as the Hennisian vanda, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant stems and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking blooms.
