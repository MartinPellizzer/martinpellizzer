Viburnum disjunctum

Viburnum disjunctum, commonly known as the dwarf arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. Viburnum disjunctum thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
