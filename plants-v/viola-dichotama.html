Viola dichotama

Viola dichotama, commonly known as the twinleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically divided into two lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or white, often with a distinctive yellow eye. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola dichotama is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
