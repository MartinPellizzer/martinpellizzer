Vellozia crispata

Vellozia crispata, commonly known as the zigzag veldt, is a unique member of the family Velloziaceae, characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves.

Native to southern Africa, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are typically rolled or crinkled, forming a dense rosette that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered near the base of the stem.

Due to its striking appearance and specialized growth habits, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
