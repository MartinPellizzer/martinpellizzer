Viola begoniifolia

Viola begoniifolia, commonly known as the begonia-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and deep green coloration. The plant produces delicate violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique leaf morphology resembles that of begonias, giving it its common name.
