Viola asterias

Viola asterias, commonly known as the star violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and upright.

Viola asterias is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
