Vernonia subtilis

Vernonia subtilis, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in clusters.

Vernonia subtilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
