Valerianella olitoria var. lasiocarpa

Valerianella olitoria var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in gardens, lawns, and along woodland edges.

Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly peppery flavor, making it a useful plant in salads and as a garnish.
