Vernonia arabica

Vernonia arabica, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to reddish-brown.

Vernonia arabica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
