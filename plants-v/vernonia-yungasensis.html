Vernonia yungasensis

Vernonia yungasensis, commonly known as the Yungas Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Yungas region of Bolivia and northern Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in humid, subtropical environments, often in shrubland or along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Vernonia yungasensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
