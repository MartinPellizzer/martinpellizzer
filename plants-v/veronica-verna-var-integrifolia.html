Veronica verna var. integrifolia

Veronica verna var. integrifolia, commonly known as the spring speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, dark green leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.
