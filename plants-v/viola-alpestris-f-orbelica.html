Viola alpestris f. orbelica

Viola alpestris f. orbelica, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a botanical variant of the common violet, characterized by its compact growth habit and striking purple-pink flowers.

It is native to alpine regions of Europe and thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to rocky slopes and meadows.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in garden settings.
