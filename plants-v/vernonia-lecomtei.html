Vernonia lecomtei

Vernonia lecomtei, commonly known as the Lecomte's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, purple to reddish-brown flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia lecomtei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
