Vernonia kaessneri

Vernonia kaessneri, commonly known as the Cape ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and heat resistance. Its woody structure makes it a hardy species, often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.

The leaves are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture, contributing to its drought-resistant traits.
