Verbesina scabriuscula

Verbesina scabriuscula, commonly known as roughleaf beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often found in dry, open habitats like fields and roadsides.
