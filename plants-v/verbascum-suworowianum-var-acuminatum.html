Verbascum suworowianum var. acuminatum

Verbascum suworowianum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the slender mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, velvety leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower clusters in late summer, which are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
