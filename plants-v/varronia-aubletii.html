Varronia aubletii

Varronia aubletii, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-brown bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by fruit resembling a star, hence its common name. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and contains a single large, starchy seed.

It is valued for its edible fruit and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
