Viburnum burmanicum var. motoense

Viburnum burmanicum var. motoense, commonly known as the Burmese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, often grayish bark. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.
