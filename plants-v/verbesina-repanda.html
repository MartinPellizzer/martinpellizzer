Verbesina repanda

Verbesina repanda, commonly known as the spreading beggarticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom from late summer to early autumn.

Verbesina repanda is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
