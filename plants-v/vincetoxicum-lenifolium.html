Vincetoxicum lenifolium

Vincetoxicum lenifolium, commonly known as the slenderleaf milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks.

Vincetoxicum lenifolium is noted for its ability to attract various pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
