Verbena teucriifolia

Verbena teucriifolia, commonly known as creeping verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces vibrant blue to purple flowers throughout the summer. The plant has slender, trailing stems and small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena teucriifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
