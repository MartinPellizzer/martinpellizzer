Vouapa explicans

Vouapa explicans, commonly known as the Madagascar flame vine, is a striking climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vivid orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb and spread along supports.

Vouapa explicans thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
