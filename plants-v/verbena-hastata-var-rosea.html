Verbena hastata var. rosea

Verbena hastata var. rosea, commonly known as the showy verbena, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This species thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
