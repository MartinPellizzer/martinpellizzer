Valeriana officinalis subsp. versifolia

Valeriana officinalis subsp.

versifolia, commonly known as blue valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and aromatic foliage, which contain compounds responsible for its calming properties.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Valeriana officinalis subsp.

versifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its sedative and anxiolytic effects.
