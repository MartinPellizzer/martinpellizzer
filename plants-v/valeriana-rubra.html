Valeriana rubra

Valeriana rubra, commonly known as red valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Valeriana rubra is also valued for its fragrant properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
