Viburnum laxum

Viburnum laxum, commonly known as the looseflower viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum laxum thrives in partial to full sun and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
