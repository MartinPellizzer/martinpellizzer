Valeriana pilosissima

Valeriana pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Valeriana pilosissima is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
