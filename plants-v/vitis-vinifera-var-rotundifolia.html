Vitis vinifera var. rotundifolia

Vitis vinifera var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the Muscat of Alexandria, is a grapevine species native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, round, aromatic berries that are typically green to yellow when mature. The plant is known for its vigorous growth and is often cultivated for its wine and table grape varieties. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are heart-shaped, which is a distinguishing feature of the variety.
