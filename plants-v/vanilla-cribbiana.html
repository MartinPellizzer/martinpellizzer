Vanilla cribbiana

Vanilla cribbiana, commonly known as the Cribb's vanilla, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the production of fragrant, vanilla-like pods. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The vanilla flavor comes from the aromatic compounds found in its mature pods.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
