Viola guestphalica

Viola guestphalica, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Viola guestphalica is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh weather conditions.

Its blooms usually appear in spring, adding a splash of color to high-altitude landscapes.
