Viola tricolor f. turgescens

Viola tricolor f. turgescens, commonly known as the edible violet or sweet violet, is a cultivar of the common violet.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant violet, white, and yellow flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of down, giving them a soft texture. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild, sweet flavor, making it suitable for culinary use.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade.
