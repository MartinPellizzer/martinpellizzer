Verbena lasiostachys var. septentrionalis

Verbena lasiostachys var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern verbena, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is well-suited for gardens and landscapes due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
