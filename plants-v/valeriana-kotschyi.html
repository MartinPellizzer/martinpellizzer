Valeriana kotschyi

Valeriana kotschyi, commonly known as the Ethiopian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its erect stem and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of the genus Valeriana. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

The roots of Valeriana kotschyi are used in traditional medicine for their purported sedative and antispasmodic properties.
