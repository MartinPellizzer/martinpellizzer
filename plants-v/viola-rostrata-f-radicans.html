Viola rostrata f. radicans

Viola rostrata f. radicans, commonly known as the trailing violet, is a cultivated form of the common violet.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or ground cover. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet flowers with yellow markings. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
