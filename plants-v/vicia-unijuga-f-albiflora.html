Vicia unijuga f. albiflora

Vicia unijuga f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered narrow-leaf vetch, is a rare and distinctive variant of the vetch genus.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant is native to specific regions in Central Asia and is adapted to rocky or arid environments. Its pale flowers contrast with the green foliage, making it visually striking.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
