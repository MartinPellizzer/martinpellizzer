Verbena officinalis var. illyrica

Verbena officinalis var. illyrica, commonly known as the Illyrian verbena, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The Illyrian verbena is valued for its ornamental qualities and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
