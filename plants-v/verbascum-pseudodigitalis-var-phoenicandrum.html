Verbascum pseudodigitalis var. phoenicandrum

Verbascum pseudodigitalis var. phoenicandrum, commonly known as the Phoenician mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its common name reflects its historical association with the Phoenicians, who may have used it for medicinal purposes.
