Verbascum haenseleri var. hieronymi

Verbascum haenseleri var. hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymi mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their showy, often fuzzy flowers. The plant typically features tall, erect stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, and its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
