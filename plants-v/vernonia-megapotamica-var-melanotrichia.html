Vernonia megapotamica var. melanotrichia

Vernonia megapotamica var. melanotrichia, commonly known as black-haired vernonia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in dense, dark brown to black hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and also hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
