Veitchia vitiensis var. microcarpa

Veitchia vitiensis var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to the Pacific islands.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its short, stout trunk and dense, feather-like foliage. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
