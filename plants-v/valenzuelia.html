Valenzuelia

Valenzuelia, commonly known as the Mexican lily, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plants typically feature showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive red or purple stripe. Valenzuelia species are herbaceous perennials, often growing up to 1 meter in height, with strap-like leaves.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are commonly cultivated in gardens and as houseplants.
