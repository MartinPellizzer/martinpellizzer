Vernonia nyassae

Vernonia nyassae, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its rough, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Vernonia nyassae is adapted to various soil types and can thrive in both moist and dry conditions.
