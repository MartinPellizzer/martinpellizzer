Verbascum barnadesii subsp. valentinum

Verbascum barnadesii subsp.

valentinum, commonly known as the Valentin's mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, branched inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
