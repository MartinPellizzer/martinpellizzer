Vernonia polycephala

Vernonia polycephala, commonly known as the clustered ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters at the ends of the branches.

Vernonia polycephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
