Verbascum pseudodigitalis var. chrysandrum

Verbascum pseudodigitalis var. chrysandrum, commonly known as the golden mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its common name reflects the golden hue of its blooms and its resemblance to the traditional mullein plant.
