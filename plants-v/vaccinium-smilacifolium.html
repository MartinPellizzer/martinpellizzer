Vaccinium smilacifolium

Vaccinium smilacifolium, commonly known as the smilacifolium blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or swampy areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
