Vagaria ollivieri var. gattefossei

Vagaria ollivieri var. gattefossei, commonly known as the Gattefosse's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on oak trees in the Mediterranean region.

It has a dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are usually clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by berries that are consumed by birds. It plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its host trees.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
