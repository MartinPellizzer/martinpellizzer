Vaccinium macgillivrayi

Vaccinium macgillivrayi, commonly known as the Macgillivray huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal rainforests. Its small, dark blue berries are edible and are used in local cuisine and traditional practices.
