Viola × subodorata

Viola × subodorata, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Viola tricolor and Viola papilionacea, resulting in a plant with distinctive, often fragrant flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers typically display a combination of purple, violet, and white hues. This species is commonly found in moist, shaded areas of woodlands and gardens across Europe.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
