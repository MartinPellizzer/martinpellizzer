Vulneraria heterophylla var. hirsutissima

Vulneraria heterophylla var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy vulneraria, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented seed pods. The plant is notable for its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
