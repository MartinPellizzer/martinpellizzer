Vitis labrusca f. aestivalis

Vitis labrusca f. aestivalis, commonly known as the June grape, is a botanical variant of the fox grape.

It is characterized by its early ripening habit, typically producing fruit in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous vine with compound leaves and tendrils typical of the Vitaceae family. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in winemaking.

This cultivar is valued for its adaptability to various climates and its role in both ornamental and agricultural settings.
