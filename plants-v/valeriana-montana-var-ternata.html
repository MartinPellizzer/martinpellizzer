Valeriana montana var. ternata

Valeriana montana var. ternata, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often pink or white flowers. The plant features a creeping rhizome system and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, fragrant scent. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Valeriana montana var. ternata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
