Valeriana pulchella

Valeriana pulchella, commonly known as the showy valerian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.
