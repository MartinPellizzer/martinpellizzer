Vandellia alsinoides

Vandellia alsinoides, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It typically forms dense mats of linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise subdued appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological studies in dryland ecosystems.
