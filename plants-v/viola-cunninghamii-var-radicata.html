Viola cunninghamii var. radicata

Viola cunninghamii var. radicata, commonly known as the purple violets, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers in early spring. It has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in rich, organic soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
