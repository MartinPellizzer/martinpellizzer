Vitis hardyi

Vitis hardyi, commonly known as the Hardy grapevine, is a species native to eastern North America.

It is a vigorous, climbing vine that produces small, sweet berries. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various climates.

Vitis hardyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential for fruit production.
