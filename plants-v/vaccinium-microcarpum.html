Vaccinium microcarpum

Vaccinium microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are oval-shaped, leathery, and have a dense, compact growth habit.

Vaccinium microcarpum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in bogs and heathlands.
