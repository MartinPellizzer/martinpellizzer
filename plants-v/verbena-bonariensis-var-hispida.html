Verbena bonariensis var. hispida

Verbena bonariensis var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, vividly colored flowers, typically in shades of purple, blue, or pink. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, textured appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its long blooming period, from late spring to early autumn, adds continuous color to gardens.
