Verbascum × ordymnense

Verbascum × ordymnense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their large, showy flowers and fuzzy leaves. The hybrid exhibits characteristics of its parent species, often displaying tall stems and yellow to orange flowers. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
