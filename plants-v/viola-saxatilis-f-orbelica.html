Viola saxatilis f. orbelica

Viola saxatilis f. orbelica, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a variant of the common violet that thrives in high-altitude environments.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and harsh weather conditions. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it forms dense mats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
