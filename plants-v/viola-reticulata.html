Viola reticulata

Viola reticulata, commonly known as the Chinese violet, is a species of flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
