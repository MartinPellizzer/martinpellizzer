Vaccinium schlechterianum

Vaccinium schlechterianum, commonly known as the Schlechter's blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Vaccinium schlechterianum is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential culinary and medicinal uses.
