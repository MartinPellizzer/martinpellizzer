Veronica tournefortii subsp. restrictior

Veronica tournefortii subsp.

restrictior, commonly known as the narrowleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
