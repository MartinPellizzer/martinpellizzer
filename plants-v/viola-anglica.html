Viola anglica

Viola anglica, commonly known as the English violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and gardens. Viola anglica is known for its ability to thrive in cool climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape during the transition from winter to summer.
