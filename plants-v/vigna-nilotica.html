Vigna nilotica

Vigna nilotica, commonly known as the African honey bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces small, yellowish-green pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. The plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional cuisines across Africa and Asia. Vigna nilotica thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate rainfall.

Its growth habit is typically annual, making it a valuable crop for sustainable agriculture.
