Viola calcarata var. rotundifolia

Viola calcarata var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the rounded-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are rounded at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and streambanks.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
