Viola grandiflora subsp. lutea

Viola grandiflora subsp.

lutea, commonly known as the yellow pansy, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
