Viola crassa var. borealis

Viola crassa var. borealis, commonly known as the northern hardy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in cooler climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
