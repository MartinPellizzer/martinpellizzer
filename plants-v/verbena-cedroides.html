Verbena cedroides

Verbena cedroides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Verbena cedroides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
