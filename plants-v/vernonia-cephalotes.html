Vernonia cephalotes

Vernonia cephalotes, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellow to orange flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Vernonia cephalotes is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
