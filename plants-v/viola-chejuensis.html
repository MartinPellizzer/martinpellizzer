Viola chejuensis

Viola chejuensis, commonly known as the Cheju violet, is a flowering plant native to the island of Jeju in South Korea.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the local flora.

Viola chejuensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
