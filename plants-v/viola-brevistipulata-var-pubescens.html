Viola brevistipulata var. pubescens

Viola brevistipulata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Violaceae family.
