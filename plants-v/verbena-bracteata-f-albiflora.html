Verbena bracteata f. albiflora

Verbena bracteata f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower verbena, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
