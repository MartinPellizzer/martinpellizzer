Vicia atropurpurea f. alba

Vicia atropurpurea f. alba, commonly known as the white sweet clover, is a variant of the common vetch.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, sparsely branched stems. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it valuable in agricultural settings.
