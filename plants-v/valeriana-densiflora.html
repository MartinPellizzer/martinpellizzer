Valeriana densiflora

Valeriana densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, round fruits called drupes, which contain the seeds.

Valeriana densiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, aromatic scent.
