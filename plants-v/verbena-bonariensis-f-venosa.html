Verbena bonariensis f. venosa

Verbena bonariensis f. venosa, commonly known as the variegated verbena, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and distinctive foliage.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is native to South America, specifically Argentina. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and often have a variegated pattern of white or yellow margins, adding visual interest to gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade conditions.
