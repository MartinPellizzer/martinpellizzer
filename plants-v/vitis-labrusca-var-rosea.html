Vitis labrusca var. rosea

Vitis labrusca var. rosea, commonly known as the pink muscat grape, is a cultivar of the American grapevine known for its distinctive pink to red coloration.

This variety is characterized by its medium-sized, oval-shaped berries that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. The leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is deciduous, shedding its foliage in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
