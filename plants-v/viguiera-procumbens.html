Viguiera procumbens

Viguiera procumbens, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange to yellow flowers that bloom from summer to fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features include showy composite flowers and a fibrous root system.
