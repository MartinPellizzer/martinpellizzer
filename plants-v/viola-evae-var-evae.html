Viola evae var. evae

Viola evae var. evae, commonly known as the Eaves violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters and thrives in cool, moist environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
