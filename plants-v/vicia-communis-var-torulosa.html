Vicia communis var. torulosa

Vicia communis var. torulosa, commonly known as the curled vetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling stipules that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are typically hairy and have a soft, velvety texture.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
