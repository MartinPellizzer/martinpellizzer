Viola × florariensis

Viola × florariensis, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Viola × florariensis is valued for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
