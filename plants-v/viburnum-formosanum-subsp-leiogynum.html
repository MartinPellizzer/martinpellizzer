Viburnum formosanum subsp. leiogynum

Viburnum formosanum subsp.

leiogynum, commonly known as the smoothleaf viburnum, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are smooth on both surfaces.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
