Viola × savatieri var. multifida

Viola × savatieri var. multifida, commonly known as the multifid viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of delicate, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as a decorative garden plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage and the long-lasting nature of its blooms.
