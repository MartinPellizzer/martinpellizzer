Veltheimia glauca

Veltheimia glauca, commonly known as the blue veltheimia, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, bottlebrush-shaped inflorescences with bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Veltheimia glauca is relatively low maintenance and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
