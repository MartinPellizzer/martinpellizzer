Vilfa virginica

Vilfa virginica, commonly known as the Virginia violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate purple to blue flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Vilfa virginica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
