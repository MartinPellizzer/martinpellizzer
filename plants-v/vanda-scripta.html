Vanda scripta

Vanda scripta, commonly known as the sacred vanda, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with intricate patterns resembling script or writing. Vanda scripta is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
