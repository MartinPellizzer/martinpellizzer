Vernonia calvoana subsp. usambarensis

Vernonia calvoana subsp.

usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarensis Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to East Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are typical of the genus Vernonia. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
