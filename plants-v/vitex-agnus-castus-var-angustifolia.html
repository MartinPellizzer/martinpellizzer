Vitex agnus-castus var. angustifolia

Vitex agnus-castus var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, violet-blue flowers in terminal clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release small, brown seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
