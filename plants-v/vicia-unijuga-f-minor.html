Vicia unijuga f. minor

Vicia unijuga f. minor, commonly known as the lesser narrow-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green pea-like flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This ferny-stemmed perennial is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
