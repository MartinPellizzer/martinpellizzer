Veratrum maackii var. latifolium

Veratrum maackii var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing large, broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale green flowers in late summer, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
