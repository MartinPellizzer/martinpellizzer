Vernonia davalliaefolia

Vernonia davalliaefolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Vernonia davalliaefolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
