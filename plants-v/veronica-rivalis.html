Veronica rivalis

Veronica rivalis, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are borne in dense, terminal clusters.

Veronica rivalis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
