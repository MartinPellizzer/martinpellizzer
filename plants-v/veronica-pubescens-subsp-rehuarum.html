Veronica pubescens subsp. rehuarum

Veronica pubescens subsp.

rehuarum, commonly known as the New Zealand veronica, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
