Veronica daurica subsp. pyrethrina

Veronica daurica subsp.

pyrethrina, commonly known as Daurian speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It features basal rosettes of dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in the summer.

The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
