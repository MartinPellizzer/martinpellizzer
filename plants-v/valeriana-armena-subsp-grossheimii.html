Valeriana armena subsp. grossheimii

Valeriana armena subsp.

grossheimii, commonly known as Armenian valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and surrounding areas. It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant produces aromatic, often fragrant leaves that are used in traditional medicine for their calming properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
