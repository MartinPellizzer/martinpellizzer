Vaccinium craspedotum var. brevipes

Vaccinium craspedotum var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stem bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other acid-loving plants such as blueberries and heathers. The plant features small, dark green leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, and it produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Its berries, which are usually red or black, are edible and often used in local cuisines or for medicinal purposes.

The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in open, rocky or sandy habitats.
