Vernonia lanceolaris

Vernonia lanceolaris, commonly known as the lanceleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wetlands, and it thrives in full sun. Its lance-shaped leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and the flowers are usually purple or pink, appearing in clusters.

Vernonia lanceolaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
