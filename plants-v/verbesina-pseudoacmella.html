Verbesina pseudoacmella

Verbesina pseudoacmella, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
