Verbascum × vidavense

Verbascum × vidavense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy, woolly leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the ends of stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for medicinal purposes.
