Vernonia loretensis

Vernonia loretensis, commonly known as the Loretto ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia loretensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
