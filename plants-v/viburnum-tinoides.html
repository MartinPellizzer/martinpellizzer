Viburnum tinoides

Viburnum tinoides, commonly known as arrowwood viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant is noted for its ornamental value, with showy red berries that appear in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Viburnum tinoides is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
