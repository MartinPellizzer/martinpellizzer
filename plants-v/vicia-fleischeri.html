Vicia fleischeri

Vicia fleischeri, commonly known as the Fleischer's vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves, typical of many vetch species, and its pods contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Vicia fleischeri is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh climates.
