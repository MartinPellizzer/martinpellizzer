Vatica lamponga

Vatica lamponga, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and striking red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
