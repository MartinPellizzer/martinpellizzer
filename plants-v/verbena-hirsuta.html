Verbena hirsuta

Verbena hirsuta, commonly known as hairy verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena hirsuta is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
