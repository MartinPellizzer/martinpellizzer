Vernonanthura Sinclairii

Sinclair's vernonia, scientifically known as Vernonanthura sinclairii, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the deserts of southern California and northern Baja California. This unique species is named after the botanist Henry Sinclair, who first documented its discovery in the early 20th century. With its vibrant yellow flowers and distinctive, spiny leaves, it stands out in its arid environment. Despite its beauty, Vernonanthura sinclairii is highly threatened by habitat loss and climate change. Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this endangered plant and preserve its ecological role in the region.
