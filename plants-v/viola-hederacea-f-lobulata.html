Viola hederacea f. lobulata

Viola hederacea f. lobulata, commonly known as the lobed violet, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are typically velvety in texture. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue or purple flowers with five petals, often featuring a distinctive dark throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
