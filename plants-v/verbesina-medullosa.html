Verbesina medullosa

Verbesina medullosa, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its branches, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its scientific name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine, and it is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
