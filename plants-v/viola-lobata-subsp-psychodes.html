Viola lobata subsp. psychodes

Viola lobata subsp.

psychodes, commonly known as the ghost violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its delicate, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, often along stream banks or in thickets. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and erect.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its subtle beauty.
