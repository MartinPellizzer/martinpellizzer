Viola variegata var. viridis

Viola variegata var. viridis, commonly known as the green-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a subtle variegation pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
