Vulpia bromoides subsp. broteroi

Vulpia bromoides subsp.

broteroi, commonly known as the narrowleaf foxtail, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its small, wind-dispersed seeds contribute to its adaptability and spread in various habitats.
