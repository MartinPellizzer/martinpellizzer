Vesper macrorhizus

Vesper macrorhizus, commonly known as the large-rooted evening bell, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive root system and flowering pattern.

It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon, attracting specific pollinators. Its roots are notably thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water and nutrients.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
