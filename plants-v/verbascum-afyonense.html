Verbascum afyonense

Verbascum afyonense, commonly known as Afghan mullein, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its thick, fuzzy, gray-green leaves and tall flowering stems. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Verbascum afyonense is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
