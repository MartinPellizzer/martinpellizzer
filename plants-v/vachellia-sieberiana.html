Vachellia sieberiana

Vachellia sieberiana, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Vachellia sieberiana is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
