Vigna mendesii

Vigna mendesii, commonly known as the African bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to warm climates and can thrive in poor soils, making it a resilient crop. Vigna mendesii is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are rich in protein and essential minerals.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
