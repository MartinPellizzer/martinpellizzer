Veronica cymbalariifolia var. longipes

Veronica cymbalariifolia var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on short pedicels along the upper stem. Its flowering period makes it a valuable addition to gardens, particularly in regions with Mediterranean climates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
