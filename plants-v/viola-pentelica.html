Viola pentelica

Viola pentelica, commonly known as the Greek violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola pentelica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
