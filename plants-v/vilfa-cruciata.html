Vilfa cruciata

Vilfa cruciata, commonly known as the cross-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a hairy surface, and its flowering stems bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Vilfa cruciata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
