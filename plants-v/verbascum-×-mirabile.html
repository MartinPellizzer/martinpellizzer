Verbascum × mirabile

Verbascum × mirabile, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, Verbascum × mirabile is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
