Vriesea gladioliflora var. purpurascens

Vriesea gladioliflora var. purpurascens, commonly known as the Crimson Vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a dense, water-holding central cup. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences with bright red bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular as a decorative plant due to its bold color and ease of care.
