Veronica iranica

Veronica iranica, commonly known as Persian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Iran and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer.

Veronica iranica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
