Vitellaria salicifolia

Vitellaria salicifolia, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to the savannas of West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a fatty seed kernel. The kernel is the source of shea butter, a valuable natural product used in cosmetics and cooking.

It plays an important ecological and economic role in the regions where it is found.
