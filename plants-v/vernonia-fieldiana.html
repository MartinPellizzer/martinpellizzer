Vernonia fieldiana

Vernonia fieldiana, commonly known as the field vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia fieldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
