Veronica vitifolia

Veronica vitifolia, commonly known as the grape-leaved speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Veronica vitifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings.
