Vicia abyssinica

Vicia abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian vetch, is a perennial legume native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Vicia abyssinica is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
