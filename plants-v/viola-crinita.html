Viola crinita

Viola crinita, commonly known as the curly top viola, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to native plant collections.
