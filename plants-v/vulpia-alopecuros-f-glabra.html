Vulpia alopecuros f. glabra

Vulpia alopecuros f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth hairgrass, is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is typically found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its glabrous (smooth) appearance is a distinguishing feature of this particular form.

This plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in various environmental conditions.
