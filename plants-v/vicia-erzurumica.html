Vicia erzurumica

Vicia erzurumica, commonly known as the Erzurum vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the spring. Vicia erzurumica is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in soil stabilization.
