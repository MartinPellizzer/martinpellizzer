Vernonia petersii

Vernonia petersii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Vernonia petersii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
