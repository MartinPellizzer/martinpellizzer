Vaccinium macgregorii

Vaccinium macgregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Vaccinium macgregorii plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
