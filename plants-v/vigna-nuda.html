Vigna nuda

Vigna nuda, commonly known as the naked bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Vigna nuda thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
