Veronica buxbaumii var. kochiana

Veronica buxbaumii var. kochiana, commonly known as the Buxbaum's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces tubular flowers that are typically clustered in whorls along the stems, and it has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to dry climates.
