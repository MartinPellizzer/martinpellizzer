Valeriana elongata var. polygama

Valeriana elongata var. polygama, commonly known as the long-stemmed valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching towards the top. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Valeriana elongata var. polygama is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
