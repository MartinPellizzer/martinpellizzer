Vitis rotundifolia var. pygmaea

Vitis rotundifolia var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf muscadine grape, is a compact, vigorous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, round leaves and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and fruit.

The berries are edible and have a sweet, musky flavor, making them suitable for fresh consumption or winemaking.
