Vernonia cistifolia

Vernonia cistifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Vernonia cistifolia is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
