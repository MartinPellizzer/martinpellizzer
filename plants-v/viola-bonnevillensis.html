Viola bonnevillensis

Viola bonnevillensis, commonly known as the Bonneville violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, typical of many viola species.

The Bonneville violet is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
