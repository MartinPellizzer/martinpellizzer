Vernonia gerberiformis var. hockii

Vernonia gerberiformis var. hockii, commonly known as the Hockii ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
