Vittaria spartimorpha

Vittaria spartimorpha, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that often grow in dense clusters. The plant typically has a creeping rhizome and produces spores in specialized structures called sorus. Its leaves are usually divided into segments, giving it a feathery appearance.

Vittaria spartimorpha thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests.
