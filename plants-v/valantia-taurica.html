Valantia taurica

Valantia taurica, commonly known as the Tauric valantia, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny, arid environments. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Valantia taurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements.
