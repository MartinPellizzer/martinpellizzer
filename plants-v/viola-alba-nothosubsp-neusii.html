Viola alba nothosubsp. neusii

Viola alba nothosubsp.

neusii, commonly known as the white violet, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species. It is a herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped leaves and delicate, white or pale violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle floral display to the landscape.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
