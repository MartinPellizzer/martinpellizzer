Vigna schottii

Vigna schottii, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. It is often used as a forage crop and for its edible seeds.

Vigna schottii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in agricultural systems.
