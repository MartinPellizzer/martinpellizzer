Volkameria orientalis var. indica

Volkameria orientalis var. indica, commonly known as the Indian velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
