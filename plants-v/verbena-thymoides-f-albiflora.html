Verbena thymoides f. albiflora

Verbena thymoides f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The whiteflower verbena is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
