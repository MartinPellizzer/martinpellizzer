Viola × insignis

Viola × insignis, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers cool, moist environments. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Viola × insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
