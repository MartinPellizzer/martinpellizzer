Vernonia plumbaginifolia

Vernonia plumbaginifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and slightly woody.

Vernonia plumbaginifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
