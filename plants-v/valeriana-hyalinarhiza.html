Valeriana hyalinarhiza

Valeriana hyalinarhiza, commonly known as the translucent root valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fibrous, pale-colored roots that are often harvested for medicinal purposes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Valeriana hyalinarhiza is valued in traditional medicine for its purported calming and sedative properties.
