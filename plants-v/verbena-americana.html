Verbena americana

Verbena americana, commonly known as American verbena, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden use.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
