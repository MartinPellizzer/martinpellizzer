Vaccinium meridionale

Vaccinium meridionale, commonly known as the southern highbush blueberry, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub that produces small, edible berries rich in antioxidants. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its botanical characteristics include alternate, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring.

This species is widely cultivated for both ornamental and commercial purposes due to its hardiness and fruit quality.
