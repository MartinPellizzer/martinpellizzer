Vellozia glauca var. genuina

Vellozia glauca var. genuina, commonly known as the blue vellozia, is a striking succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, cushion-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and rockeries.
