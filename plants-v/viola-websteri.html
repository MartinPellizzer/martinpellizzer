Viola websteri

Viola websteri, commonly known as the Webster's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in areas of the Appalachian region. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded habitats and features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base.

Viola websteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
