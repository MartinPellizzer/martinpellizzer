Valeriana turuchanica

Valeriana turuchanica, commonly known as Turuchan valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Russian Far East.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a stout, upright stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering occurs in summer, producing small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers arranged in loose clusters.

Valeriana turuchanica is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
