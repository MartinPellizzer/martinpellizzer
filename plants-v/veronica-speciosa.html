Veronica speciosa

Veronica speciosa, commonly known as the showy speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The showy speedwell is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
