Valeriana alternifolia subsp. pseudocoreana

Valeriana alternifolia subsp.

pseudocoreana, commonly known as Korean valerian, is a perennial herb native to East Asia. It features a rhizomatous root system and lanceolate, opposite leaves with a distinct aromatic scent.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its chemical composition includes volatile oils and alkaloids, making it valued in traditional medicine.
