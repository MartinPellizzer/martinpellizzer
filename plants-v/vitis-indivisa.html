Vitis indivisa

Vitis indivisa, commonly known as the red sandalwood vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its distinctive red or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Vitis indivisa is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
