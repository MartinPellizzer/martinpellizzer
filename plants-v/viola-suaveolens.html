Viola suaveolens

Viola suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is often found in woodland areas and meadows. Its flowers have a sweet, aromatic scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola suaveolens is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in herbal remedies.
