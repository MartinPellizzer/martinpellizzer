Viola holubyi

Viola holubyi, commonly known as the Holub's violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola holubyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
