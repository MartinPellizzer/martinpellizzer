Velvitsia calycina

Velvitsia calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf calycina, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
