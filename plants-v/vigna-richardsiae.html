Vigna richardsiae

Vigna richardsiae, commonly known as the Richards cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often grown for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein. Vigna richardsiae has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in regions where it is cultivated for both food and agricultural purposes.
