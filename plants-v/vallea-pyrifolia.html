Vallea pyrifolia

Vallea pyrifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets that are arranged in a feathery pattern. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Vallea pyrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
