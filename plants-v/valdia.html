Valdia

Valdia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Valdia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types make it both a useful plant in some contexts and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
