Viola tricolor subsp. olyssiponensis

Viola tricolor subsp.

olyssiponensis, commonly known as the Portuguese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of violet, blue, and white.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in spring and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
