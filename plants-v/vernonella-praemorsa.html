Vernonella praemorsa

Vernonella praemorsa, commonly known as the death's-head hawkmoth plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, tubular shape that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Vernonella praemorsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
