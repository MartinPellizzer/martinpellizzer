Veronica kamtchatica

Veronica kamtchatica, commonly known as Kamchatka speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and parts of northeastern Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and forest edges. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. It belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped blooms.

Veronica kamtchatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
