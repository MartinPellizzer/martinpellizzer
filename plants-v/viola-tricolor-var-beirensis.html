Viola tricolor var. beirensis

Viola tricolor var. beirensis, commonly known as the Chinese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is used in both garden landscapes and traditional medicine.
