Viola × beebyi

Viola × beebyi, commonly known as the Beeby's violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil. It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, delicate flowers, often with intricate patterns on the petals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
