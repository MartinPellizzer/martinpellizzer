Verbesina richardsonii

Verbesina richardsonii, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet soils along streambanks and wetlands. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
