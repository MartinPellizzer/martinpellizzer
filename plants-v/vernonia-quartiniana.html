Vernonia quartiniana

Vernonia quartiniana, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a hardy shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with a distinctive scent.

Vernonia quartiniana is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
