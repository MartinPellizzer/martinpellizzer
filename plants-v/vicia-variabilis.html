Vicia variabilis

Vicia variabilis, commonly known as variable vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow-pink flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.

Vicia variabilis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
