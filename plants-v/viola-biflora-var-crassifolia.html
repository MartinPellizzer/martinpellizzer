Viola biflora var. crassifolia

Viola biflora var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a compact, low-growing habit. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a versatile choice for shaded garden beds.
