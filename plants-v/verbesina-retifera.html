Verbesina retifera

Verbesina retifera, commonly known as the velvetleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. It grows as an annual herb, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

Verbesina retifera is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
