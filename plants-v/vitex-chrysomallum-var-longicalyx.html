Vitex chrysomallum var. longicalyx

Vitex chrysomallum var. longicalyx, commonly known as the long-leaf chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in the summer, which are followed by woody, elongated fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
