Valarum officinale

Valarum officinale, commonly known as the noble herb, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like carrots and parsley. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbrella-shaped clusters. Valarum officinale is often cultivated for its aromatic qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Its essential oils are valued for their calming and digestive properties.
