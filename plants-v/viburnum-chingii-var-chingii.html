Viburnum chingii var. chingii

Viburnum chingii var. chingii, commonly known as the Chinese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in clustered inflorescences during the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.

The species is also notable for its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
