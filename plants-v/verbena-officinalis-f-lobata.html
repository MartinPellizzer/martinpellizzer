Verbena officinalis f. lobata

Verbena officinalis f. lobata, commonly known as the lobate vervain, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with lobed margins, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
