Verbascum × kozaniense

Verbascum × kozaniense, commonly known as the Kozani mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Verbascum, which is part of the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Greece and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
