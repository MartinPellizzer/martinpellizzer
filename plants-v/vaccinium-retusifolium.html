Vaccinium retusifolium

Vaccinium retusifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, with a smooth margin, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.
