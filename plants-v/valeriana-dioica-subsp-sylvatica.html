Valeriana dioica subsp. sylvatica

Valeriana dioica subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as wood valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in woodland areas and has slender, aromatic stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose panicles, blooming in late summer.

Wood valerian is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its calming effects.
