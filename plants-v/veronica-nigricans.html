Veronica nigricans

Veronica nigricans, commonly known as black-eyed Veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
