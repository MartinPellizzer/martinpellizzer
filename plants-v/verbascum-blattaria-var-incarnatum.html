Verbascum blattaria var. incarnatum

Verbascum blattaria var. incarnatum, commonly known as the crimson mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, fuzzy leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a panicle-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The species is noted for its medicinal uses and ornamental value in gardens.
