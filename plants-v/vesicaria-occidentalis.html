Vesicaria occidentalis

Vesicaria occidentalis, commonly known as the western bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor wetlands and ponds. Its leaves are often submerged, while its stems and flowers emerge above the water surface.

Vesicaria occidentalis plays a unique role in its ecosystem by supplementing its nutrient intake through predation.
