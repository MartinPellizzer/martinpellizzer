Viola × iselensis

Viola × iselensis, commonly known as the Isel violet, is a hybrid species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. Viola × iselensis is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its hybrid origin contributes to its genetic diversity and adaptability to various growing conditions.
