Vernonia pycnostachya

Vernonia pycnostachya, commonly known as the dense-stemmed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of dark purple to violet tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
