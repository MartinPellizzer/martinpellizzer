Verbesina holwayi

Verbesina holwayi, commonly known as the Holway's beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and it produces small, prickly fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Verbesina holwayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
