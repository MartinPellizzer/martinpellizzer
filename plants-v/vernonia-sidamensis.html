Vernonia sidamensis

Vernonia sidamensis, commonly known as the Sidamense Ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia sidamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
