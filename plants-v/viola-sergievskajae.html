Viola sergievskajae

Viola sergievskajae, commonly known as the Sergeyevskaya violet, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and delicate, violet-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the high-altitude landscape.

Viola sergievskajae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
