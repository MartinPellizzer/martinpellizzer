Viorna coccinea

Viorna coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. Viorna coccinea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long flowering period and vibrant color.
