Viola sikkimensis

Viola sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim violet, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. It has a short stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
