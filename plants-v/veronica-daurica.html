Veronica daurica

Veronica daurica, commonly known as Daurian speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite pairs of ovate, toothed leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
