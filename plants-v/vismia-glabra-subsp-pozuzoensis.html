Vismia glabra subsp. pozuzoensis

Vismia glabra subsp.

pozuzoensis, commonly known as the Pozuzo vismia, is a shrub native to the humid lowland forests of eastern South America. It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and elliptical, dark green leaves with a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It is often found in secondary growth and disturbed habitats, thriving in warm, moist conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptability.
