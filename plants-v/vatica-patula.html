Vatica patula

Vatica patula, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant typically grows in lowland evergreen forests and can reach heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
