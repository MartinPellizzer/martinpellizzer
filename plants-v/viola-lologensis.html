Viola lologensis

Viola lologensis, commonly known as the Lolo violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola lologensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
