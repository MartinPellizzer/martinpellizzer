Veronica arganthera

Veronica arganthera, commonly known as the silverleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Veronica arganthera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
