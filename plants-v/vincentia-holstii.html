Vincentia holstii

Vincentia holstii, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
