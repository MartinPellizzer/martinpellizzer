Vigna venulosa var. lathyroides

Vigna venulosa var. lathyroides, commonly known as the climbing peanut or lathyroid peanut, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by pods containing edible, peanut-like seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and oils.

This variety is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in soil nitrogen fixation.
