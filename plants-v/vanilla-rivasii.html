Vanilla rivasii

Vanilla rivasii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre vanilla, is a climbing orchid native to Mexico.

It produces fragrant, dark purple pods that are harvested for their aromatic qualities. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically yellow with a deep violet throat, and it requires a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi for proper development.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Vanilla rivasii is considered a rare and endangered species.
