Verbena azorelloides

Verbena azorelloides, commonly known as the Azorean verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Azores, a group of islands in the North Atlantic Ocean.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often covered in fine hairs, and it is known for its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.

Verbena azorelloides is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
