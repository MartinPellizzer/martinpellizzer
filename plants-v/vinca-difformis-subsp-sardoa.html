Vinca difformis subsp. sardoa

Vinca difformis subsp.

sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian vinca, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial that produces vibrant blue to purple flowers throughout the summer.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
