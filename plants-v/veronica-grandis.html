Veronica grandis

Veronica grandis, commonly known as the tall speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, making it a striking addition to gardens. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica grandis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
