Vernonia custodiana

Vernonia custodiana, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged oppositely on the stems. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the rainy season.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
