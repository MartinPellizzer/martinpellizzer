Veronica ovata f. canescens

Veronica ovata f. canescens, commonly known as the hairy everlasting, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a grayish appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and gravelly fields.
