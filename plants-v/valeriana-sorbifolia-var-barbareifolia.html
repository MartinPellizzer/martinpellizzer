Valeriana sorbifolia var. barbareifolia

Valeriana sorbifolia var. barbareifolia, commonly known as the alpine valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it blooms during the summer months.

Valeriana sorbifolia var. barbareifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.
