Vanilla pauciflora

Vanilla pauciflora, commonly known as the low-flower vanilla, is a climbing orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, pale green flowers that are typically not showy but are essential for its reproductive cycle. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its orchid family classification indicates it has the typical orchid structure, including a single ovary and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Despite its modest appearance, it is valued for its potential in horticulture and as a source of aromatic compounds.
