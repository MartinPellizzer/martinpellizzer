Vigna juncea

Vigna juncea, commonly known as urad dal or split green gram, is an annual leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is widely cultivated for its edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits containing 2–4 kidney-shaped seeds. The seeds are rich in protein and are a staple in many Indian cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an important crop for both nutritional and agricultural purposes.
