Vepris louvelii

Vepris louvelii, commonly known as the African pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elliptical, orange-red fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its essential oils.
