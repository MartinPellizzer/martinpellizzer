Vangueria chariensis

Vangueria chariensis, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves. The leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
