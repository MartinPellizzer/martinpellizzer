Vaccinium bolivianum

Vaccinium bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, acidic berries that are typically dark blue or black in color. These berries are often consumed by local wildlife and have been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Its botanical characteristics include dense, leathery leaves and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.
