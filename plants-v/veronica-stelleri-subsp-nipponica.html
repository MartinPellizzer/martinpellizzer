Veronica stelleri subsp. nipponica

Veronica stelleri subsp.

nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.
