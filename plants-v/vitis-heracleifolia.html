Vitis heracleifolia

Vitis heracleifolia, commonly known as the grape ivy, is a climbing vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Vitis heracleifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and easy maintenance.
