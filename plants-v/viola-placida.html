Viola placida

Viola placida, commonly known as the common violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. Viola placida is often found in woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
