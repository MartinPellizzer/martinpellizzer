Verbena leucanthemifolia

Verbena leucanthemifolia, commonly known as the white verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Verbena leucanthemifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
