Verbascum × moravicum

Verbascum × moravicum, commonly known as the Moravian mullein, is a hybrid plant native to Central Europe.

It is a member of the mullein genus, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Verbascum × moravicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
