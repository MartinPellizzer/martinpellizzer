Veronica pubescens subsp. sejuncta

Veronica pubescens subsp.

sejuncta, commonly known as the hairy speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in dense clusters, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
