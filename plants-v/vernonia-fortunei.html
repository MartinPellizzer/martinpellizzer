Vernonia fortunei

Vernonia fortunei, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Vernonia fortunei is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
