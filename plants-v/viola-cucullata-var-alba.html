Viola cucullata var. alba

Viola cucullata var. alba, commonly known as the white hound's tongue, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically white or pale lavender in color. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical structure includes a short, stout stem and a basal rosette of leaves, making it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.
