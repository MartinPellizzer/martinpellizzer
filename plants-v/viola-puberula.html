Viola puberula

Viola puberula, commonly known as the downy violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or violet, often with yellow markings. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Viola puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
