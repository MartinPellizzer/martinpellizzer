Vereia crenata

Vereia crenata, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that have wavy edges. Its flowers are small, blue or purple, and are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Vereia crenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
