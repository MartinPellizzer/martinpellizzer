Valeriana montana subsp. tripteris

Valeriana montana subsp.

tripteris, commonly known as the Alpine valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink.

The plant has aromatic leaves and stems, which contain volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils at high altitudes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
