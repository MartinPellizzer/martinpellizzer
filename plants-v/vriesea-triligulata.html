Vriesea triligulata

Vriesea triligulata, commonly known as the Three-lobed Vriesea, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, recurved inflorescence that displays multiple tiers of bright orange to red flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that form a rosette, creating a compact and visually appealing structure. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea triligulata is popular as a decorative plant due to its long-lasting blooms and elegant appearance.
