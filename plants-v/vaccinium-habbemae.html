Vaccinium habbemae

Vaccinium habbemae, commonly known as the Habbema blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

The leaves are elliptical, dark green, and covered in a fine layer of hair, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
