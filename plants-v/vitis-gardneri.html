Vitis gardneri

Vitis gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's grape, is a species of flowering vine native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, palmate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, sweet berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Vitis gardneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
