Vernonia congolensis subsp. longiflora

Vernonia congolensis subsp.

longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered vernonia, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish bracteate flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions.
