Vaccinium pahalae

Vaccinium pahalae, commonly known as the Himalayan blueberry, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
