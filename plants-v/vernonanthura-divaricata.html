Vernonanthura divaricata

Vernonanthura divaricata, commonly known as the variable vernonberry, is a species of flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and open forests, characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a red color, providing food for native wildlife.

Vernonanthura divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
