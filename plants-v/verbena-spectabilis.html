Verbena spectabilis

Verbena spectabilis, commonly known as the showy verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
