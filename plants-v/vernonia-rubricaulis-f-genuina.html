Vernonia rubricaulis f. genuina

Vernonia rubricaulis f. genuina, commonly known as the red-stemmed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and prominent ray florets. The plant is distinguished by its reddish-brown stems and lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

This variety is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands.
