Vernonia cupularis var. oligocephala

Vernonia cupularis var. oligocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, dark purple to brownish-purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
