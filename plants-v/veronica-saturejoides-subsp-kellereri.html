Veronica saturejoides subsp. kellereri

Veronica saturejoides subsp.

kellereri, commonly known as the Kellerer's speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodland or along stream banks.

The plant features small, blue or purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ornamental value in garden settings.
