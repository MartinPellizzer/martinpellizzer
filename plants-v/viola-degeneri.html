Viola degeneri

Viola degeneri, commonly known as the Johnny-jump-up, is a delicate annual or biennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, often violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-20 centimeters and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects.

Viola degeneri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.
