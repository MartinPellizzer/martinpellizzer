Vitis apiifolia

Vitis apiifolia, commonly known as theApiifolia grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red or black berries. Its leaves are often divided into lobes, resembling those of the carrot, hence its common name.

Vitis apiifolia is typically found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
