Vincetoxicum tsaii

Vincetoxicum tsaii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fleshy, violet-blue berries that appear in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces showy, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
