Viticella maculata

Viticella maculata, commonly known as the spotted ivy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Vitaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Viticella maculata is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated as a decorative vine due to its ornamental foliage.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
