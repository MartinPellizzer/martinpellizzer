Volvulus dahuricus

Volvulus dahuricus, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are hairy and often branched. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in late summer. Volvulus dahuricus is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
