Vernonia biafrae var. tufnelliae

Vernonia biafrae var. tufnelliae, commonly known as the Nigerian ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for construction and fuel. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also considered a potential candidate for reforestation projects.
