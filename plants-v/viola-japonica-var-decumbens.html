Viola japonica var. decumbens

Viola japonica var. decumbens, commonly known as the Japanese violet or creeping violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple or violet, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover in horticulture.
