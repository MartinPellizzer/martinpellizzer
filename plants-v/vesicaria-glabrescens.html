Vesicaria glabrescens

Vesicaria glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairless stems and small, bladder-like structures called vesicles, which are used for trapping small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, still freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. It is a carnivorous plant, relying on the nutrients obtained from the prey it captures.

Its simple morphology and specialized adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
