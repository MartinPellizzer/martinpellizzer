Viola maculata f. fimbriata

Viola maculata f. fimbriata, commonly known as the spotted violet, is a hybrid species of the viola genus native to Europe.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive white or pale green mottling. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with a white or pale yellow throat and finely frayed edges, giving it the species name *fimbriata*. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
