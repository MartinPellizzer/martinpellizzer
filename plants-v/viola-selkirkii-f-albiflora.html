Viola selkirkii f. albiflora

Viola selkirkii f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered violet, is a rare and striking variant of the viola genus.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which contrast with its dark green, heart-shaped leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. The foliage is typically pubescent, adding a soft texture to its appearance.

It is prized by botanists and gardeners for its unique coloration and ornamental value.
