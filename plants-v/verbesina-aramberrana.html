Verbesina aramberrana

Verbesina aramberrana, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats like wetlands and along stream banks.

Its hairy stems and leaves, along with its burr-like seeds, contribute to its distinctive appearance and ecological role.
