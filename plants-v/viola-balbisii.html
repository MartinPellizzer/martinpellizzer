Viola balbisii

Viola balbisii, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white eye at the center. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Viola balbisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
