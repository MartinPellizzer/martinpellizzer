Viola rodriguezii

Viola rodriguezii, commonly known as the Rodriguez's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
