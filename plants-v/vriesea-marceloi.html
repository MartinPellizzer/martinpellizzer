Vriesea marceloi

Vriesea marceloi, commonly known as the Marcelo's Vriesea, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that typically features vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant has a rosette-shaped leaf structure with waxy, often bluish-green foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea marceloi is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
