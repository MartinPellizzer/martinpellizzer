Veratrum lobelianum var. obovatum

Veratrum lobelianum var. obovatum, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing large, showy white flowers arranged in umbels. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant is characterized by its toxic alkaloids, which make it harmful to both humans and animals.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
