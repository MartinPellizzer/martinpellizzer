Volkameria aggregata

Volkameria aggregata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and garden design.
