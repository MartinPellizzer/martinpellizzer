Viola × hallieri

Viola × hallieri, commonly known as the purple heart viola, is a hybrid species in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with upright stems that bear clusters of showy blooms. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in moist, shady woodland environments.

Viola × hallieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful display.
