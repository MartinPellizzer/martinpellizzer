Vicia tibetica

Vicia tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan vetch, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in arid and cold environments, often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Vicia tibetica plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
