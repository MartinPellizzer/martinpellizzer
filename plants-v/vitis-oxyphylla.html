Vitis oxyphylla

Vitis oxyphylla, commonly known as the Chinese foxglove vine, is a climbing plant native to China and neighboring regions.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not widely cultivated for consumption. Vitis oxyphylla is often grown for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
