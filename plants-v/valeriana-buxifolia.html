Valeriana buxifolia

Valeriana buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.

Valeriana buxifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
