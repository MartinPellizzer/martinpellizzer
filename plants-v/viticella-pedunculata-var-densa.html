Viticella pedunculata var. densa

Viticella pedunculata var. densa, commonly known as the dense-flowered ivy, is a vigorous climbing plant characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing an attractive visual contrast to its flowering display. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in moist, shady environments, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to cover walls or trellises. Its rapid growth habit requires regular pruning to maintain shape and prevent it from becoming invasive.

The dense inflorescences attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing its ecological value in the garden.
