Veratrum caudatum var. tenuipetaloides

Veratrum caudatum var. tenuipetaloides, commonly known as the narrow-petaled white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of white to pale blue flowers. The plant has large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of cardiac glycosides.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower petals compared to other varietal forms of Veratrum caudatum.
