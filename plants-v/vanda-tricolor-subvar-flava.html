Vanda tricolor subvar. flava

Vanda tricolor subvar. flava, commonly known as the yellow vanda, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers, which typically feature a combination of yellow, white, and green hues. The plant has erect, hairy pseudobulbs and long, leathery leaves that are adapted to its warm, humid environment. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Vanda tricolor subvar. flava is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant and long-lasting blooms.
