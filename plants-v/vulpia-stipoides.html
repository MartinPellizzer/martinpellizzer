Vulpia stipoides, commonly known as the slender vulpia, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a small annual grass that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it often blooms from late spring to early autumn.
