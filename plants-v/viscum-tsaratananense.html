Viscum tsaratananense

Viscum tsaratananense, commonly known as the Tsaratanana mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and typically grows on the branches of host trees, such as those in the genus Erythrophleum. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. Its berries are usually red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Viscum tsaratananense plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food for various bird species.
