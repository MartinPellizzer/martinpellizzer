Viola violacea f. variegata

Viola violacea f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated violet, is a cultivar of the common violet.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring white or yellow markings on the leaves. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in cool, moist environments.

This cultivar is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and shaded landscapes.
