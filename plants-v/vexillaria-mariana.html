Vexillaria mariana

Vexillaria mariana, commonly known as the Florida flag flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, flag-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.

Vexillaria mariana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
