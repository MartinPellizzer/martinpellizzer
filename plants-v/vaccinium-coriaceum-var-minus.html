Vaccinium coriaceum var. minus

Vaccinium coriaceum var. minus, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically reaches heights of less than 30 centimeters, with small, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that transition into bright blue or red berries, depending on the variety. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and is often found in boreal and subarctic environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide food for wildlife.
