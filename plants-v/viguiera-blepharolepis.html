Viguiera blepharolepis

Viguiera blepharolepis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a grayish-green hue. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Viguiera blepharolepis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
