Veronica macrocalyx var. humilis

Veronica macrocalyx var. humilis, commonly known as the low-growing speedwell, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite pairs of ovate to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers with five petals, typical of the Veronica genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and attractive floral display make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
