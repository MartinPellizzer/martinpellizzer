Verbena pulchella var. gracilior

Verbena pulchella var. gracilior, commonly known as slender verbena, is a compact, flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems and small, vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is prized for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
