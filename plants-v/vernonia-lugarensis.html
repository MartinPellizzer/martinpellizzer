Vernonia lugarensis

Vernonia lugarensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Vernonia lugarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
