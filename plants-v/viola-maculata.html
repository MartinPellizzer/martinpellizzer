Viola maculata

Viola maculata, commonly known as the spotted violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple or reddish spot on the underside. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a spurred petal at the back.

Viola maculata is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows.
