Vanda testacea var. parviflora

Vanda testacea var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant has thick, fleshy roots adapted for absorbing moisture from the air and rain. Its leaves are typically broad and leathery, providing structural support and aiding in water retention.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
