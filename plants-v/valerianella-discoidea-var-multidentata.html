Valerianella discoidea var. multidentata

Valerianella discoidea var. multidentata, commonly known as the toothed valerian, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
