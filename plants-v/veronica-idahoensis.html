Veronica idahoensis

Veronica idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. This perennial herb typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The plant is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
