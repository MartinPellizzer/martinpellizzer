Verbena junciformis

Verbena junciformis, commonly known as swamp verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and moist soils. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense tufts.

Verbena junciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
