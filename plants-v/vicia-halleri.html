Vicia halleri

Vicia halleri, commonly known as Haller's vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Vicia halleri is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in certain habitats.
