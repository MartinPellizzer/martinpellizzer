Veronica calycina var. parviflora

Veronica calycina var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant produces clusters of tiny, five-petaled blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often forming a dense mat on the ground.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
