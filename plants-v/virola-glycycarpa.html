Virola glycycarpa

Virola glycycarpa, commonly known as the sweet wood or yellow wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Virola glycycarpa is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in folk medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.
