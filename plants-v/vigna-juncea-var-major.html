Vigna juncea var. major

Vigna juncea var. major, commonly known as yellow gram or sesban, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and elongated, yellowish pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its high-protein seeds used in food and animal feed. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in agricultural systems across Asia and Africa.
