Viola nephrophylla var. arizonica

Viola nephrophylla var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The species is part of the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
