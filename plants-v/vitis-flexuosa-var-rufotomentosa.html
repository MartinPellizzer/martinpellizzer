Vitis flexuosa var. rufotomentosa

Vitis flexuosa var. rufotomentosa, commonly known as the red-tomentose grapevine, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, white to greenish flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rough, hairy texture on the underside. This variety is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its berries, though not commonly cultivated for wine, are used in local foraging and traditional practices.
