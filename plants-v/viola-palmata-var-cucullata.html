Viola palmata var. cucullata

Viola palmata var. cucullata, commonly known as the hooded violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive hood-like structure over the upper petal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
