Vernonia ehrenbergiana

Vernonia ehrenbergiana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, daisy-like blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with a silvery appearance and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

The plant's structure includes a branching stem and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
