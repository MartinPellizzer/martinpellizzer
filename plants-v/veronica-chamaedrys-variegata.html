Veronica chamaedrys variegata

Veronica chamaedrys variegata, commonly known as the variegated speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features small, blue or white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
