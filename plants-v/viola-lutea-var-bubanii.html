Viola lutea var. bubanii

Viola lutea var. bubanii, commonly known as the Balkan violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
