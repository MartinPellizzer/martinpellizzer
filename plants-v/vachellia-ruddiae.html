Vachellia ruddiae

Vachellia ruddiae, commonly known as Rudd's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions with its deep root system and ability to fix nitrogen. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Vachellia ruddiae is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and forage.
