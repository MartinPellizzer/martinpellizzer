Vanilla poitaei

Vanilla poitaei, commonly known as the Madagascar vanilla orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is a climbing vine that produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark brown pods. The plant's botanical features include fleshy roots and elongated, narrow leaves. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is closely related to the commercially cultivated vanilla orchid, Vanilla planifolia.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Vanilla poitaei is considered a conservation concern.
