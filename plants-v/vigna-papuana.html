Vigna papuana

Vigna papuana, commonly known as the Papuan bean, is a climbing legume native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces edible seeds that are used in local cuisines.

Vigna papuana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
