Viola mandshurica f. crassa

Viola mandshurica f. crassa, commonly known as the broad-leaved viola, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive dark purple or black spot at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Violaceae family.
