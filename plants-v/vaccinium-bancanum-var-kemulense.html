Vaccinium bancanum var. kemulense

Vaccinium bancanum var. kemulense, commonly known as the Kemulense blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the highland regions of South America.

It produces small, dark blue berries that are typically harvested in the late summer and early autumn. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool climate with moderate rainfall. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a deep green color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential as a source of edible berries in specific ecological conditions.
