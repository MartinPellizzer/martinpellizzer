Viola declinata

Viola declinata, commonly known as the drooping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces violet-blue or purple flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive white or yellow mark near the base. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.

Viola declinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
