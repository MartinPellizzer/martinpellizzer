Vaccinium floridanum var. revolutum

Vaccinium floridanum var. revolutum, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes blueberries and cranberries, and is characterized by its acidic soil preference. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are dark red to black in color. Its leaves are elliptic to ovate, with a leathery texture and a waxy coating, and they remain green throughout the year.

This variety is often found in sandy or pine flatwood habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
