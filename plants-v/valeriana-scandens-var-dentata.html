Valeriana scandens var. dentata

Valeriana scandens var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or white in color.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported calming and sedative properties.
