Valeriana pseudofficinalis

Valeriana pseudofficinalis, commonly known as false valerian, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clustered inflorescences. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its sedative properties.

Valeriana pseudofficinalis is closely related to other members of the Valerianaceae family, sharing similar chemical compositions and ecological preferences.
