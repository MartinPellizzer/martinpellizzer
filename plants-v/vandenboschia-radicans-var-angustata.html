Vandenboschia radicans var. angustata

Vandenboschia radicans var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are narrow and pointed, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
