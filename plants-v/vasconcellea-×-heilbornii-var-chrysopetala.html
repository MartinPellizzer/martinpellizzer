Vasconcellea × heilbornii var. chrysopetala

Vasconcellea × heilbornii var. chrysopetala, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a hybrid plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the stems are woody and somewhat fibrous. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
