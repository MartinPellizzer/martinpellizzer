Valeriana serratifolia

Valeriana serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Valeriana serratifolia thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.
