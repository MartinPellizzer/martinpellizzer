Viola selkirkii var. subbarbata

Viola selkirkii var. subbarbata, commonly known as the Selkirk violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a short stem with opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.
