Viola Lanceolata Subsp. Occidentalis

Viola lanceolata subsp. occidentalis is a lesser-known member of the violet family native to western North America. This subspecies is characterized by its slender leaves and delicate purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated and often overlooked by gardeners and botanists alike. Understanding and preserving this plant is crucial for maintaining biodiversity in its natural habitat.
