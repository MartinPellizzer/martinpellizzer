Vitex elmeri

Vitex elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant produces showy, fragrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with a strong, aromatic scent.

Vitex elmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral displays.
