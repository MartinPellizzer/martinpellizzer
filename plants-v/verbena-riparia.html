Verbena riparia

Verbena riparia, commonly known as the river verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows near water sources such as rivers and streams. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Verbena riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist, sandy soils.
