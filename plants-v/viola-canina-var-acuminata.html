Viola canina var. acuminata

Viola canina var. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
