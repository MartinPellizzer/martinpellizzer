Valeriana robertianifolia

Valeriana robertianifolia, commonly known as the Sierra Valley valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has aromatic, finely divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the cool, temperate climate of its native habitat.

Valeriana robertianifolia plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
