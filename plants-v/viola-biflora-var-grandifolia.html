Viola biflora var. grandifolia

Viola biflora var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white throat. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
