Vaccinium leptanthum f. malayanum

Vaccinium leptanthum f. malayanum, commonly known as the Malayan huckleberry, is a variant of the Vaccinium genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it bears edible, small berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
