Viola minae

Viola minae, commonly known as the Mina's violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola minae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
