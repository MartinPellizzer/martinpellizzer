Verbena selaginoides var. illapelina

Verbena selaginoides var. illapelina, commonly known as the Illapel verbena, is a flowering plant native to Chile.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
