Veronica carsei

Veronica carsei, commonly known as the Sicilian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Veronica carsei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
