Viola squamulosa

Viola squamulosa, commonly known as the squamulose violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy or scaly appearance, giving it its common name. Its flowers are violet-blue in color, with five petals and a spurred petal at the back.

Viola squamulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
