Vitiphoenix petiolata

Vitiphoenix petiolata, commonly known as the slender palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that are often feather-like in appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance.

Vitiphoenix petiolata is relatively slow-growing and requires moderate to bright indirect light for optimal growth.
