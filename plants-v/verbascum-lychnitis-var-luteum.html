Verbascum lychnitis var. luteum

Verbascum lychnitis var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
