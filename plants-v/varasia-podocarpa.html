Varasia podocarpa

Varasia podocarpa, commonly known as the mountain pine, is a coniferous tree native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Varasia podocarpa thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potentially medicinal purposes.
