Valeriana tripteris subsp. heterophylla

Valeriana tripteris subsp.

heterophylla, commonly known as the variable valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape, giving it its subspecies name. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pink or purple in color.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
