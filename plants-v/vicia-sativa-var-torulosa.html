Vicia sativa var. torulosa

Vicia sativa var. torulosa, commonly known as the curled vetch, is a variant of the common vetch.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its distinctive curled foliage and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
