Viola tokubuchiana f. lactiflora

Viola tokubuchiana f. lactiflora, commonly known as the milkflower viola, is a cultivar of the violet plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a creamy or milky appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
