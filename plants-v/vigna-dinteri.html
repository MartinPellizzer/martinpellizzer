Vigna dinteri

Vigna dinteri, commonly known as the Desert Pea, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple or pink pea-like flowers. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. Vigna dinteri is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
