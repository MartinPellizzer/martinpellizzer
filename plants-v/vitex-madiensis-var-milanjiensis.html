Vitex madiensis var. milanjiensis

Vitex madiensis var. milanjiensis, commonly known as the Cape rose or redbrush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
