Vleckia anisata

Vleckia anisata, commonly known as the Indian clover, is a herbaceous plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale blue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Vleckia anisata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
