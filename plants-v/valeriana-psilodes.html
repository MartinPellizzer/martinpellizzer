Valeriana psilodes

Valeriana psilodes, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, and it produces pink to white flowers in late summer.

Valeriana psilodes is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
