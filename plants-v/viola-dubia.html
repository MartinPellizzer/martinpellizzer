Viola dubia

Viola dubia, commonly known as the western violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet hue and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white. Viola dubia is part of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its short, creeping stems.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
