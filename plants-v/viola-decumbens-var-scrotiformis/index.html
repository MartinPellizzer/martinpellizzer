Viola Decumbens Var. Scrotiformis

Viola decumbens var. scrotiformis is a rare and peculiar variant of the violet family known for its unusual appearance. This plant is characterized by its elongated, flattened leaves that resemble a scrotum, giving it its distinctive common name. Native to specific regions in North America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a botanical curiosity. Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this fascinating and often overlooked species.
