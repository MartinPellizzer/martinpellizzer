Viola × mistura

Viola × mistura, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors, including purple, violet, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and gardens, where it spreads through rhizomes and self-seeding.

Viola × mistura is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in horticulture.
