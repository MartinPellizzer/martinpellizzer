Verbena livermorensis

Verbena livermorensis, commonly known as Livermore verbena, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white, producing small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Verbena livermorensis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in native plant landscapes.
