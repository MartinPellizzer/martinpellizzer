Viscum semiplanum

Viscum semiplanum, commonly known as the flatleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in small inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain toxic seeds.

Viscum semiplanum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain birds and insects.
