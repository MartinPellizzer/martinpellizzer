Viburnum kerrii

Viburnum kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Viburnum kerrii is valued for its attractive foliage, floral display, and seasonal fruit, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
