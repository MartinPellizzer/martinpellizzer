Virola bicuhyba

Virola bicuhyba, commonly known as the "Amazonian ironwood," is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark wood and thick, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing numerous seeds. Virola bicuhyba is valued for its durable timber and has cultural significance among indigenous communities.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various forest species.
