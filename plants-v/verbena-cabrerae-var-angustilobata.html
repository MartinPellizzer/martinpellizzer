Verbena cabrerae var. angustilobata

Verbena cabrerae var. angustilobata, commonly known as narrow-leaved verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and the plant produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
