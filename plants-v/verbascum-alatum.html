Verbascum alatum

Verbascum alatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves that are covered in soft, downy hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. Verbascum alatum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
