Vitex barorum

Vitex barorum, commonly known as the Indian vitex, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that turn red or orange in autumn. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom in summer. Its fruit is a dry, two-valved capsule that releases small, winged seeds.

Vitex barorum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
