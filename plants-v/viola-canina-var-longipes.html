Viola canina var. longipes

Viola canina var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed heartleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and slightly pubescent. The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Violaceae family, valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
