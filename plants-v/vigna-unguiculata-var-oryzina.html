Vigna unguiculata var. oryzina

Vigna unguiculata var. oryzina, commonly known as black-eyed pea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing 3-5 kidney-shaped seeds, which are typically black or brown in color. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its high protein content.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important crop in both agriculture and nutrition.
