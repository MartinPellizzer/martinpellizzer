Vernonia subsessilis var. macrophylla

Vernonia subsessilis var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, blue to purple composite flowers. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinct central vein. This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
