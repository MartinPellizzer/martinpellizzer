Viola umbellata

Viola umbellata, commonly known as the clustered violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower one often being larger and more showy.

Viola umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
