Viola formosana var. tozanensis

Viola formosana var. tozanensis, commonly known as the Tozan violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
