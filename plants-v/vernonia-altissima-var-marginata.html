Vernonia altissima var. marginata

Vernonia altissima var. marginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple composite flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive silver-gray margin, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinators.
