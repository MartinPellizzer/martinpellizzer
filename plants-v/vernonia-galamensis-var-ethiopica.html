Vernonia galamensis var. ethiopica

Vernonia galamensis var. ethiopica, commonly known as the Ethiopian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
