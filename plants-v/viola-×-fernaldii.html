Viola × fernaldii

Viola × fernaldii, commonly known as the Fernald's violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, often purple or violet flowers. This plant is native to the eastern United States, particularly in woodland habitats. It typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Viola × fernaldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
