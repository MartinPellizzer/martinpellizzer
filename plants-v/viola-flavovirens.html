Viola flavovirens

Viola flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive yellow-green coloration. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with five petals, typically in shades of yellow and green. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Viola flavovirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
