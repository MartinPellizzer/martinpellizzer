Vernonia glomeruliflora

Vernonia glomeruliflora, commonly known as the clustered ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia glomeruliflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
