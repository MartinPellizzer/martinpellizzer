Vignea × haussknechtii

Vignea × haussknechtii, commonly known as the hybrid vigna, is a rare and less-studied plant species within the legume family.

It exhibits morphological characteristics intermediate between its parent species, Vigna unguiculata and Vigna vexillata. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and efficient nitrogen fixation.

Due to its limited distribution and unique genetic makeup, it holds potential for agricultural and botanical research.
