Vigna juncea var. juncea

Vigna juncea var. juncea, commonly known as cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa and widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cowpea is a staple food in many parts of the world, providing a rich source of protein and nutrients.
