Vernonia exaltata

Vernonia exaltata, commonly known as the tall ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Vernonia exaltata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
