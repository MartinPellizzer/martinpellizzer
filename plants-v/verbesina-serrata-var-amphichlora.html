Verbesina serrata var. amphichlora

Verbesina serrata var. amphichlora, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, giving the plant its species name. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in open woods or along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
