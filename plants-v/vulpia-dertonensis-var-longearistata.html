Vulpia dertonensis var. longearistata

Vulpia dertonensis var. longearistata, commonly known as the long-haired vulpia, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like structures. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and resilience in various environmental conditions.
