Viburnum disjunctum var. mendax

Viburnum disjunctum var. mendax, commonly known as the Mendacum viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, fragrant flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and supporting local biodiversity.
