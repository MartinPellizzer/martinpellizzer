Viola acuminata f. shikokuensis

Viola acuminata f. shikokuensis, commonly known as the Shikoku violet, is a botanical variant of the viola genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers with a yellow throat and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
