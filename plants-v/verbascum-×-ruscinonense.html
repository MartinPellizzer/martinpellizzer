Verbascum × ruscinonense

Verbascum × ruscinonense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. This hybrid plant is a cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum thapsus, resulting in a plant with traits from both parents. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in gardens and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
