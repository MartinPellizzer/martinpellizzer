Viola tricolor var. trimestris

Viola tricolor var. trimestris, commonly known as the three-color violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
