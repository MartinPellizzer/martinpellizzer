Verbascum × selimense

Verbascum × selimense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum thapsus.

It is a tall, herbaceous plant characterized by its large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Native to regions of Central Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments. The plant is noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies for respiratory ailments.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful and sometimes invasive species in its native habitat.
