Viola eximia subsp. eximia

Viola eximia subsp.

eximia, commonly known as the showy viola, is a species native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces vibrant violet or purple flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a short, upright stem and a preference for well-drained, loamy soil.

It is often found in association with other woodland flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
