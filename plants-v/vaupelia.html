Vaupelia

Vaupelia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, velvety leaves that reflect sunlight, reducing water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody pods containing seeds. Vaupelia is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep taproots that allow it to access groundwater.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species in its ecological niche.
