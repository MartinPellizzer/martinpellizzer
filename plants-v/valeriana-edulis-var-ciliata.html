Valeriana edulis var. ciliata

Valeriana edulis var. ciliata, commonly known as the fringed valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with finely toothed margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its root system is highly aromatic and contains volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.
