Vriesea glaucophylla

Vriesea glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed vriesea, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescence that typically displays vibrant orange or red hues. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish bloom, giving it its common name. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea glaucophylla is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
