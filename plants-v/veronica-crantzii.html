Veronica crantzii

Veronica crantzii, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Veronica crantzii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
