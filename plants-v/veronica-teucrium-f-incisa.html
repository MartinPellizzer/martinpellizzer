Veronica teucrium f. incisa

Veronica teucrium f. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
