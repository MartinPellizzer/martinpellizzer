Vitis macrophylla

Vitis macrophylla, commonly known as the Canadian grape or swamp grape, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found along stream banks and in wetlands.

Vitis macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
