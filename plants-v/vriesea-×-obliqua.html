Vriesea × obliqua

Vriesea × obliqua, commonly known as the rainbow bromeliad, is a hybrid plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that resembles a rainbow. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that can be green, blue, or variegated, often with a metallic sheen. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea × obliqua is popular as a decorative plant due to its long-lasting blooms and ornamental foliage.
