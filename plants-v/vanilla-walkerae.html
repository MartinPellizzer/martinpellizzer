Vanilla walkerae

Vanilla walkerae, commonly known as the Walkera's vanilla, is a climbing orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of its distinctive vanilla-like pods. These pods are initially green and mature to a dark brown, containing a rich, aromatic pulp.

Vanilla walkerae is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential use in the production of natural vanilla flavoring.
