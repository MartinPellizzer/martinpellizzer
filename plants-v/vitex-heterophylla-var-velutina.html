Vitex heterophylla var. velutina

Vitex heterophylla var. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces woody, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common ornamental in warm climates.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
