Viguiera flava var. genuina

Viguiera flava var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, silver-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and also covered in a fine layer of hair.

Viguiera flava var. genuina is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
