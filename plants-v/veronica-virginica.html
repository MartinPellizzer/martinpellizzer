Veronica virginica

Veronica virginica, commonly known as Virginia speedwell, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Veronica virginica is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
