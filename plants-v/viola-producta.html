Viola producta

Viola producta, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola producta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in wildflower gardens.
