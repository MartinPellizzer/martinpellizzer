Vicia pseudo-orobus

Vicia pseudo-orobus, commonly known as the false orobrush vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink to purple in color and are arranged in dense clusters.

Vicia pseudo-orobus is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
