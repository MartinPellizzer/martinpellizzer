Vandenboschia serratifolia

Vandenboschia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf vandenboschia, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette growth form and fleshy, serrated leaves that are adapted to store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
