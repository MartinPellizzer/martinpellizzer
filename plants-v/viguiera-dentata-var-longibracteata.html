Viguiera dentata var. longibracteata

Viguiera dentata var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

Viguiera dentata var. longibracteata is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
