Veronica pinnatifida

Veronica pinnatifida, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streams.

Veronica pinnatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
