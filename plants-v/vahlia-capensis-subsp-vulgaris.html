Vahlia capensis subsp. vulgaris

Vahlia capensis subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the Cape vahlia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky, coastal habitats and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom in late spring. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in coastal gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
