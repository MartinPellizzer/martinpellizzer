Viola arvensis subsp. olyssiponensis

Viola arvensis subsp.

olyssiponensis, commonly known as the Portuguese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow spot near the base of the petals. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more robust growth compared to the typical V. arvensis.
