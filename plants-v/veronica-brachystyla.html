Veronica brachystyla

Veronica brachystyla, commonly known as the dwarf speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Veronica brachystyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
