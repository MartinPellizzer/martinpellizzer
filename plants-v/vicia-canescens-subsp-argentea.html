Vicia canescens subsp. argentea

Vicia canescens subsp.

argentea, commonly known as silver vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale green to silver-blue leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Vicia canescens subsp.

argentea is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
