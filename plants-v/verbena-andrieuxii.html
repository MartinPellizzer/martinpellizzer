Verbena andrieuxii

Verbena andrieuxii, commonly known as the Andrieux's verbena, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Verbena andrieuxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
