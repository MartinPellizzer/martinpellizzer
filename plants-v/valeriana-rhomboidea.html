Valeriana rhomboidea

Valeriana rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its rhombic-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Valeriana rhomboidea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.

Its medicinal properties have also been explored in traditional herbal practices.
