Veronica linastrum

Veronica linastrum, commonly known as silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name.

Veronica linastrum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
