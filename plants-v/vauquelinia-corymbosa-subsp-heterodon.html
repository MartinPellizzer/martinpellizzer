Vauquelinia corymbosa subsp. heterodon

Vauquelinia corymbosa subsp.

heterodon, commonly known as the Mexican hill evergreen, is a shrub native to central Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense thickets in its native habitat.
