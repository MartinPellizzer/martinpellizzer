Viguiera glandulosa

Viguiera glandulosa, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features showy orange to red composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves with glandular dots, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Viguiera glandulosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
