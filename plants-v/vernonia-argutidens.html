Vernonia argutidens

Vernonia argutidens, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it its common name, while its showy purple flowers bloom in late summer.

Vernonia argutidens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
