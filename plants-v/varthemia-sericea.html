Varthemia sericea

Varthemia sericea, commonly known as silky flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
