Viguiera tenuis

Viguiera tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Viguiera tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
