Vernonia hecatantha

Vernonia hecatantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Vernonia hecatantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
