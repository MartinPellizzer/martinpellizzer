Viola ebracteolata

Viola ebracteolata, commonly known as the leafless violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are often reduced or absent, giving it its common name. The plant produces delicate violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Viola ebracteolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
