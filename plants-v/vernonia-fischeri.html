Vernonia fischeri

Vernonia fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its showy purple or pink flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
