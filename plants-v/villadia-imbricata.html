Villadia imbricata

Villadia imbricata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained and sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually violet or purple in color.

Villadia imbricata is often found in alpine or subalpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist climates.
