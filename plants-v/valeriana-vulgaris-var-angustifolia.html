Valeriana vulgaris var. angustifolia

Valeriana vulgaris var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds responsible for its sedative properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.
