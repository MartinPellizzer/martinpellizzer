Viburnum involucratum

Viburnum involucratum, commonly known as snowball bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, which is typically eaten by birds.

Viburnum involucratum prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to native gardens.
