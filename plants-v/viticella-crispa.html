Viticella crispa

Viticella crispa, commonly known as the curly ivy, is a climbing plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often curled leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by blue-black berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Viticella crispa is a member of the Vitaceae family, which includes other climbing plants like grapes.

It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.
