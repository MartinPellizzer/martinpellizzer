Vaccinium hirtum

Vaccinium hirtum, commonly known as the hairy blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, which are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, forested areas.

Vaccinium hirtum is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
