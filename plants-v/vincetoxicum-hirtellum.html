Vincetoxicum hirtellum

Vincetoxicum hirtellum, commonly known as hairy false bindweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist woodland areas and along stream banks.

Vincetoxicum hirtellum is known for its ability to spread rapidly and can become invasive in certain environments.
