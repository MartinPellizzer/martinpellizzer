Veronica teucrium var. dentata

Veronica teucrium var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite pairs of oval, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and prolonged flowering period.
