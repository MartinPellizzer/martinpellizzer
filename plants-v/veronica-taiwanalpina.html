Veronica taiwanalpina

Veronica taiwanalpina, commonly known as the Taiwan speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Taiwan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica taiwanalpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
