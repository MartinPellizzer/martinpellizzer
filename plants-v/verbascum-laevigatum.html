Verbascum laevigatum

Verbascum laevigatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Verbascum laevigatum is a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
