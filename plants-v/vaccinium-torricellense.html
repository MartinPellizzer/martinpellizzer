Vaccinium torricellense

Vaccinium torricellense, commonly known as the Torricella blueberry, is a species of dwarf shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many ericaceous plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a deep green color.

Vaccinium torricellense thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.
