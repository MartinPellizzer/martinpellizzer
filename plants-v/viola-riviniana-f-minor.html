Viola riviniana f. minor

Viola riviniana f. minor, commonly known as the lesser violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically purple or blue, often with a yellow or white throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
