Verbesina connata

Verbesina connata, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems that bear small, leaf-like bracts. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Verbesina connata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
