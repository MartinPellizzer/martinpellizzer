Vernonia larensis

Vernonia larensis, commonly known as the Laren's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as coastal areas and disturbed sites. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer to fall, are usually purple to pink in color and attract various pollinators.

Vernonia larensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
