Viola silvatica var. adunca

Viola silvatica var. adunca, commonly known as the slender wood violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in shaded woodland areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are violet-blue, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
