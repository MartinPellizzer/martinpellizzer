Vitex balbi

Vitex balbi, commonly known as the Balbi's chaste tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to purple, often blooming in summer. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed capsules containing small seeds.

Vitex balbi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
