Vignea muricata subsp. lamprocarpa

Vignea muricata subsp.

lamprocarpa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its palmately compound leaves and yellow flowers. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

The plant has a woody stem and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
