Verbesina guianensis

Verbesina guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
