Viola komarovii

Viola komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Viola komarovii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
