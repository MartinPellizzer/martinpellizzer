Viola Canadensis Subsp. Rydbergii

Viola canadensis subsp. rydbergii is a lesser-known member of the violet family native to the northern United States and southern Canada. This subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and pale purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated compared to more common violet species. Understanding this plant can enhance our appreciation for the biodiversity of native flora.
