Verbena plicata var. degeneri

Verbena plicata var. degeneri, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring through early autumn.
