Viola × montivaga

Viola × montivaga, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been naturalized in various parts of the world. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola × montivaga is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.
