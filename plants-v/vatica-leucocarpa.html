Vatica leucocarpa

Vatica leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood and long, narrow leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its durable timber and ecological importance.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a threatened species in its native range.
