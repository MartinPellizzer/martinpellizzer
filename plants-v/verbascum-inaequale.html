Verbascum inaequale

Verbascum inaequale, commonly known as the unequal mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum inaequale is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
