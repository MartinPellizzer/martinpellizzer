Vachellia turnbulliana

Vachellia turnbulliana, commonly known as the Turnbull acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

The seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often dispersed by animals or through mechanical means.
