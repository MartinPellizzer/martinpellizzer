Viola canina

Viola canina, commonly known as the dog violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces violet flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, often with yellow markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and grassy areas.

Viola canina is often found in the wild and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
