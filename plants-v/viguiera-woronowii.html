Viguiera woronowii

Viguiera woronowii, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Viguiera woronowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to attract pollinators.
