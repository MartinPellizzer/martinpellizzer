Viola tricolor var. kitaibeliana

Viola tricolor var. kitaibeliana, commonly known as the heartleaf viola, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a deep green color with a velvety texture. The flowers are typically violet, yellow, and white, arranged in a distinctive three-petaled pattern. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
