Vitis hissarica

Vitis hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar grape, is a species of flowering vine native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of small, sweet-tasting berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a deep green color.

Vitis hissarica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its fruits and its use in landscaping.
