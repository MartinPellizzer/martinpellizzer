Villaresia paniculata

Villaresia paniculata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in panicles. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to different soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
