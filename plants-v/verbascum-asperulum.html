Verbascum asperulum

Verbascum asperulum, commonly known as the rough mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Verbascum asperulum is a perennial herb that is also known for its medicinal uses throughout history.
