Viola scaturiginosa

Viola scaturiginosa, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive purple throat. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is often found in the eastern United States.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
