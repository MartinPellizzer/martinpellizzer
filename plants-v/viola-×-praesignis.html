Viola × praesignis

Viola × praesignis, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Viola × praesignis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
