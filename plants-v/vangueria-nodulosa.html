Vangueria nodulosa

Vangueria nodulosa, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems with distinctive nodules and produces clusters of yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Vangueria nodulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
