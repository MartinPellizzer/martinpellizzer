Villebrunea frutescens var. nivea

Villebrunea frutescens var. nivea, commonly known as the white-flowered velvetwood, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical regions.
