Vittaria planipes

Vittaria planipes, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that often grow in dense clusters. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces small, spore-bearing sporangia on the undersides of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
