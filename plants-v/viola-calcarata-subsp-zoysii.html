Viola calcarata subsp. zoysii

Viola calcarata subsp.

zoysii, commonly known as the Zoysii violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers during the spring.

The plant is typically found in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flower structure compared to the main species.
