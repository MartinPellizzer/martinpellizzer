Vallisneria spiralis subvar. michelii

Vallisneria spiralis subvar. michelii, commonly known as the Mexican Vallisneria, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with moderate to high light levels.

This subvariety is often used in aquatic gardens and artificial ponds due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
