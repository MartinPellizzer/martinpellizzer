Viola glauca

Viola glauca, commonly known as the blue violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Viola glauca is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
