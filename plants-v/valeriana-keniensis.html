Valeriana keniensis

Valeriana keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan valerian, is a flowering plant native to East Africa, particularly Kenya.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the dry season. Valeriana keniensis is often found in rocky or wooded areas and is adapted to thrive in semi-arid conditions.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties, used traditionally for its calming effects.
