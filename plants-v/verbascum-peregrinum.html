Verbascum peregrinum

Verbascum peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves that are covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats like meadows and hillsides.

Verbascum peregrinum is a perennial plant that is relatively easy to grow in gardens with well-drained soil.
