Veronica biloba var. minima

Veronica biloba var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, decorative plant.
