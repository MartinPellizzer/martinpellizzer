Verticordia lepidophylla

Verticordia lepidophylla, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a species of flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valuable component of the local flora.
