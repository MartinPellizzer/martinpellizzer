Vachellia erioloba

Vachellia erioloba, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by its long, drooping branches. The plant has small, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Vachellia erioloba plays an important role in local ecosystems and is used in traditional medicine.
