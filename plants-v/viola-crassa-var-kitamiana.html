Viola crassa var. kitamiana

Viola crassa var. kitamiana, commonly known as the Kitamiana Violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas. It has small, hairy stems and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific climate conditions.
