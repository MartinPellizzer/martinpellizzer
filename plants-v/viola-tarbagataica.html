Viola tarbagataica

Viola tarbagataica, commonly known as the Tarbagatai violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Viola tarbagataica is also of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological adaptations.
