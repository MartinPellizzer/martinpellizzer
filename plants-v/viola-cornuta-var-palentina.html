Viola cornuta var. palentina

Viola cornuta var. palentina, commonly known as the palentine violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
