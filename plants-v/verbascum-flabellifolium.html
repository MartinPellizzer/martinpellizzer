Verbascum flabellifolium

Verbascum flabellifolium, commonly known as the fan-leaved mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, velvety, gray-green leaves that are often fan-shaped. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, upright spike. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Verbascum flabellifolium is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
