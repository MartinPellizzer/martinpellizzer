Vernonia galamensis subsp. gibbosa

Vernonia galamensis subsp.

gibbosa, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some regions.
