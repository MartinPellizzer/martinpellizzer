Viola orientalis var. conferta

Viola orientalis var. conferta, commonly known as the clustered violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple and violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and shaded woodland areas.
