Vernonia desertorum var. campestris

Vernonia desertorum var. campestris, commonly known as desert ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
