Viola grypoceras f. trifolia

Viola grypoceras f. trifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved violet, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaf structure.

This form exhibits three leaflets arranged in a rosette, giving it a unique appearance compared to typical viola species. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or violet, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical study.
