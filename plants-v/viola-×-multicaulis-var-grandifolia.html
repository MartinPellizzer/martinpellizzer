Viola × multicaulis var. grandifolia

Viola × multicaulis var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet hue and its showy, spurred flowers that range in color from purple to deep violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
