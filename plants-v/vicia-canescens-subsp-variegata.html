Vicia canescens subsp. variegata

Vicia canescens subsp.

variegata, commonly known as the variegated vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive variegation pattern.

The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats. Vicia canescens subsp.

variegata is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
