Vernonia mariana

Vernonia mariana, commonly known as the American ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant features upright stems covered in rough, hairy texture, and it produces clusters of small, yellow to brownish flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a serrated edge.

Vernonia mariana is known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various traditional healing purposes.
