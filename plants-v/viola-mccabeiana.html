Viola mccabeiana

Viola mccabeiana, commonly known as the Mexican violet, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola mccabeiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
