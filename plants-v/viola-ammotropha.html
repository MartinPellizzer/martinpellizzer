Viola ammotropha

Viola ammotropha, commonly known as the purple violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals, and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
