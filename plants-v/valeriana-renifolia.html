Valeriana renifolia

Valeriana renifolia, commonly known as the kidney-leaf valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are kidney-shaped, which gives it its common name, and it produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers in late summer.

Valeriana renifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
