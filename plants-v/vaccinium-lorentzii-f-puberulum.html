Vaccinium lorentzii f. puberulum

Vaccinium lorentzii f. puberulum, commonly known as the pubescent blueberry, is a variant of the lowbush blueberry.

It is a shrub native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada, typically found in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are white to pale pink, and it produces small, tart blue berries that are edible.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
