Viola × cebennensis

Viola × cebennensis, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola lutea and Viola verecunda.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the northeastern United States, this plant thrives in moist, woodland habitats. It is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
