Viola babunensis

Viola babunensis, commonly known as the Babun's violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Viola babunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
