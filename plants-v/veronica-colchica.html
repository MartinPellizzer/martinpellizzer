Veronica colchica

Veronica colchica, commonly known as the Caucasian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Veronica colchica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
