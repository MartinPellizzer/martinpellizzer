Verbena lipozygioides

Verbena lipozygioides, commonly known as the African verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with narrow, often hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
