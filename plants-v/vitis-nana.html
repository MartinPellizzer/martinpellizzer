Vitis nana

Vitis nana, commonly known as the dwarf grapevine, is a small, woody vine native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, with slender, trailing stems that produce small, clustered leaves. The plant bears tiny, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that ripen to a dark blue or black color. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and streambanks.

Vitis nana is a member of the Vitaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
