Vignea lucorum

Vignea lucorum, commonly known as the common vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Vignea lucorum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
