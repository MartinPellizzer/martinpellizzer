Vitis vinifera var. morveda

Vitis vinifera var. morveda, commonly known as the Morveda grape, is a lesser-known variety of Vitis vinifera, primarily cultivated in specific regions of Spain.

It is a red grape variety characterized by its small, thick-skinned berries and high acidity. The plant exhibits typical Vitis vinifera traits, including a vigorous growth habit and deep root system. It is often used in local wine production, though its cultivation remains limited due to its specialized requirements.

The Morveda grape is valued for its ability to produce complex, aromatic wines with unique flavor profiles.
