Valantia hirta

Valantia hirta, commonly known as the hairy valantia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and also covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive textured appearance.

Valantia hirta is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
