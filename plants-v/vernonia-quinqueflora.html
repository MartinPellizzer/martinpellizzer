Vernonia quinqueflora

Vernonia quinqueflora, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green color.

Vernonia quinqueflora is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
