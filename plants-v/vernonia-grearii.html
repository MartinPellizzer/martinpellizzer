Vernonia grearii

Vernonia grearii, commonly known as the Grear's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

Vernonia grearii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
