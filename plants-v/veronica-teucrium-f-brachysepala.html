Veronica teucrium f. brachysepala

Veronica teucrium f. brachysepala, commonly known as the short-sepaled speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
