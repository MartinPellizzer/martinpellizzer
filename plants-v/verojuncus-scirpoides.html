Verojuncus scirpoides

Verojuncus scirpoides, commonly known as the African bulrush, is a perennial aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and rivers. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like. It produces small, clustered flower heads that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Verojuncus scirpoides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
