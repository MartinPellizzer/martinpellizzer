Vulpia danthonii var. tripolitania

Vulpia danthonii var. tripolitania, commonly known as the Tripolitania vulpia, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly rolled, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
