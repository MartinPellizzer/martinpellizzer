Verbesina pseudoclaussenii

Verbesina pseudoclaussenii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
