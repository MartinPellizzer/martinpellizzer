Valeriana trinervis

Valeriana trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined valerian, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana trinervis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.
