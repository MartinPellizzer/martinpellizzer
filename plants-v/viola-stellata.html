Viola stellata

Viola stellata, commonly known as the star violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive violet-blue coloration. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically violet or blue, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Viola stellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
