Viola × baltica

Viola × baltica, commonly known as the Baltic violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. Viola × baltica is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
