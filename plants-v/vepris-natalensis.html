Vepris natalensis

Vepris natalensis, commonly known as Natal lemonwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic foliage.
