Vernonia andersonii

Vernonia andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its fibrous root system helps it retain moisture, making it drought-tolerant.

Vernonia andersonii is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
