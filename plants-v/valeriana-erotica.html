Valeriana erotica

Valeriana erotica, commonly known as the sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinkish-white flowers and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana erotica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
