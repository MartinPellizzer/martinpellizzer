Veronica bastardii

Veronica bastardii, commonly known as the bastard speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

Veronica bastardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
