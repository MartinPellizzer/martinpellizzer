Verbesina neriifolia

Verbesina neriifolia, commonly known as the blackleaf beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
