Vicia villosa subsp. eriocarpa

Vicia villosa subsp.

eriocarpa, commonly known as hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and distinctive yellow pea-like flowers.

The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields. Vicia villosa subsp.

eriocarpa is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a useful plant in soil enrichment.
