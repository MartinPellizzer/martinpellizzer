Viola arvensis var. ruralis

Viola arvensis var. ruralis, commonly known as the field violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration and produces small, delicate flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The species is also an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
