Vernonia wendtiana

Vernonia wendtiana, commonly known as Wendt's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
