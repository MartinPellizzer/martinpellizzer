Vaccinium roraimense

Vaccinium roraimense, commonly known as the Roraima huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the tepuis of Venezuela and Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans alike. It thrives in the nutrient-poor soils of high-altitude regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

Its presence is an indicator of the unique ecological conditions found in these isolated mountainous areas.
