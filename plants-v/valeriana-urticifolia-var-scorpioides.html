Valeriana urticifolia var. scorpioides

Valeriana urticifolia var. scorpioides, commonly known as the Scorpioid Valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, pungent odor. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported sedative properties.
