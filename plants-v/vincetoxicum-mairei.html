Vincetoxicum mairei

Vincetoxicum mairei, commonly known as Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by its showy fruit.

Vincetoxicum mairei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.
