Vernonia keniensis

Vernonia keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish or purplish in color.

Vernonia keniensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local communities.
