Veratrum nigrum f. atropurpureum

Veratrum nigrum f. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-leaved death-cup, is a striking variant of the death-cup plant.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, characterized by its dark purple to black flowers and foliage. The plant produces large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a reddish or purplish tinge. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dramatic appearance.
