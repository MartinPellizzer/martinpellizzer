Valerianella amblyotis

Valerianella amblyotis, commonly known as the small valerian, is a low-growing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically forms a dense mat of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is part of the Caprifoliaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage.
