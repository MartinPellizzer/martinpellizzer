Verbesina guaranitica

Verbesina guaranitica, commonly known as Guarani goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Verbesina guaranitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
