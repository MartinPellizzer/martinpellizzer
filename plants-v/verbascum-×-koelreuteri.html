Verbascum × koelreuteri

Verbascum × koelreuteri, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
