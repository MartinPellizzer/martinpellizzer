Viola palmata var. sororia

Viola palmata var. sororia, commonly known as the sororal viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of violet or lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
