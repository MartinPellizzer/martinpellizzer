Verbesina texana

Verbesina texana, commonly known as Texas beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are hairy and alternate, with a distinctive shape that aids in identification.
