Vanheerdia angusta

Vanheerdia angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant is part of the Proteaceae family, which is known for its showy floral displays and adaptations to nutrient-poor soils. Vanheerdia angusta is often found in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
