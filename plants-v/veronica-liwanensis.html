Veronica liwanensis

Veronica liwanensis, commonly known as the Liwan veronica, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and arranged in dense spikes.

Veronica liwanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local biodiversity.
