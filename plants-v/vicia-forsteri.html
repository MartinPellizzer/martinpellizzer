Vicia forsteri

Vicia forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Vicia forsteri is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in dry and sunny environments.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
