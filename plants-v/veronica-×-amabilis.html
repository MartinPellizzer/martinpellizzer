Veronica × amabilis

Veronica × amabilis, commonly known as the sweet veronica, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically reaches a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions for its attractive appearance and ease of care.
