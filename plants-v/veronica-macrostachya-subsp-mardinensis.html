Veronica macrostachya subsp. mardinensis

Veronica macrostachya subsp.

mardinensis, commonly known as the Mardin veronica, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Turkey. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
