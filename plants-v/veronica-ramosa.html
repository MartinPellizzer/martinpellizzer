Veronica ramosa

Veronica ramosa, commonly known as the clustered speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Veronica ramosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
