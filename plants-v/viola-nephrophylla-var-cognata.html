Viola nephrophylla var. cognata

Viola nephrophylla var. cognata, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a range of colors from pale blue to deep purple.

The Sierra violet is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
