Viola perpusilla

Viola perpusilla, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a small flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the forest floor.

Viola perpusilla is noted for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
