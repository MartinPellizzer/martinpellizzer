Viola caucasica

Viola caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola caucasica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
