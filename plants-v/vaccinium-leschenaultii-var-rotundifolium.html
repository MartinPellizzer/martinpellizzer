Vaccinium leschenaultii var. rotundifolium

Vaccinium leschenaultii var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its rounded, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black when mature. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
