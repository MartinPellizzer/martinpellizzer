Viola micrantha lusus austroussuriensis

Viola micrantha lusus austroussuriensis, commonly known as the Southern Cape violet, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, often found in grassy slopes and rocky outcrops.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
