Verbascum serpenticola

Verbascum serpenticola, commonly known as the serpent's tail mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum serpenticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
