Vicia sativa f. melanosperma

Vicia sativa f. melanosperma, commonly known as black-seeded vetch, is a variant of the common vetch, characterized by its dark-colored seeds.

This plant is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. It typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pods containing the distinctive black seeds.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed in cultivated areas.
