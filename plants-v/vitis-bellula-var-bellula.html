Vitis bellula var. bellula

Vitis bellula var. bellula, commonly known as the dwarf grapevine, is a compact, deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green during the growing season. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit for wildlife.

Vitis bellula var. bellula is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
