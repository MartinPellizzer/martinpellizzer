Viguiera multiflora var. macrocephala

Viguiera multiflora var. macrocephala, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
