Veronica × grisea

Veronica × grisea, commonly known as the blueweed or silverweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its delicate appearance.
