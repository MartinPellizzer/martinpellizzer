Vriesea bicolor

Vriesea bicolor, commonly known as the pink vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette structure, which holds water to support its epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored bracts, often in shades of pink and red, which attract pollinators. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea bicolor is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
