Valeriana samolifolia

Valeriana samolifolia, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana samolifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
