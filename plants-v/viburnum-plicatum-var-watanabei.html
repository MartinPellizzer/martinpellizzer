Viburnum plicatum var. watanabei

Viburnum plicatum var. watanabei, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, cascading clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
