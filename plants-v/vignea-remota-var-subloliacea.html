Vignea remota var. subloliacea

Vignea remota var. subloliacea, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, pale yellow flowers that develop into elongated, narrow pods containing several seeds. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in agricultural systems where nitrogen fixation and forage value are beneficial.
