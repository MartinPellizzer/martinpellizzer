Viola canina var. acaulis

Viola canina var. acaulis, commonly known as the slender violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features upright, slender stems that are often hairless, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy surface. Its flowers are violet-blue, with five petals and a distinctive white or yellow throat.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens.
