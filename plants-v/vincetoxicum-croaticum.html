Vincetoxicum croaticum

Vincetoxicum croaticum, commonly known as the Croatian milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.

It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with well-drained soil.
