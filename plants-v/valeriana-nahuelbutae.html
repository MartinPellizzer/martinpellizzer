Valeriana nahuelbutae

Valeriana nahuelbutae, commonly known as the Andean valerian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its roots are valued for their medicinal properties, containing compounds with sedative and calming effects.

Valeriana nahuelbutae is an important species in traditional Andean medicine and is also studied for its potential in modern pharmacology.
