Vicoa kotschyi

Vicoa kotschyi, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick stems and leaves. It typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Vicoa kotschyi is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
