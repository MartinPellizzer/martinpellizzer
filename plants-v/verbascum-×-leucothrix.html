Verbascum × leucothrix

Verbascum × leucothrix, commonly known as the white flax or silverleaf mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the mullein genus.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Verbascum × leucothrix is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow.
