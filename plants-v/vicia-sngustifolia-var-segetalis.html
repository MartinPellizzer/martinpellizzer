Vicia sngustifolia var. segetalis

Vicia sngustifolia var. segetalis, commonly known as the field vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets and produces slender, yellowish-green pods containing lens-shaped seeds. The plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in agricultural fields and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation.

Its botanical structure supports its use as a forage crop and in ecological restoration projects.
