Valeriana catacata

Valeriana catacata, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb with upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a strong, aromatic scent.

Valeriana catacata is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects and use in traditional herbal remedies.
