Viola canina subsp. lucorum

Viola canina subsp.

lucorum, commonly known as the English violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It produces small, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a white or pale purple hue.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
