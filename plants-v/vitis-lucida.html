Vitis lucida

Vitis lucida, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, round, green berries that turn red when mature. Vitis lucida is often found in moist, wooded areas and is adapted to partial to full shade.

It has historical uses in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
