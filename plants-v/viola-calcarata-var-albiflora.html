Viola calcarata var. albiflora

Viola calcarata var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly wrinkled texture. The plant produces showy, white or pale violet flowers with five petals, often borne on slender stems. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun to partial shade conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
