Vernonia teucrioides

Vernonia teucrioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Vernonia teucrioides is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
