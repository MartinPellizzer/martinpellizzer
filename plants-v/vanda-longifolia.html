Vanda longifolia

Vanda longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered vanda, is a epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 cm in length. The plant has long, slender pseudobulbs and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light.

Vanda longifolia is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
