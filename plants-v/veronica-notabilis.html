Veronica notabilis

Veronica notabilis, commonly known as the mountain veronica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy as well.

Veronica notabilis is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
