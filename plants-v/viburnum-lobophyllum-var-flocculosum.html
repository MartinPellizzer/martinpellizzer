Viburnum lobophyllum var. flocculosum

Viburnum lobophyllum var. flocculosum, commonly known as the clouded viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white flower clusters that appear in early spring, giving it a soft, cloud-like appearance. The plant has oval to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter green underneath. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Viburnum lobophyllum var. flocculosum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
