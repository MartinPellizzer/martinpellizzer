Vitex sakondriensis

Vitex sakondriensis, commonly known as Sakondri's vitex, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lanceolate leaves and showy, violet-blue flower clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
