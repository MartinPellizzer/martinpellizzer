Viola leucoceras

Viola leucoceras, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually white or pale purple, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola leucoceras is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
