Vulpia myuros var. dertonensis

Vulpia myuros var. dertonensis, commonly known as the Dertonese vulpia, is a variant of the common vulpia grass.

It is a low-growing, annual grass that typically thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it a useful indicator species for soil type and environmental conditions.
