Veronica ponae

Veronica ponae, commonly known as the mountain veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and arranged in dense spikes.

Veronica ponae is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
