Viola striata f. albiflora

Viola striata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinct purple underside. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers with five petals, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
