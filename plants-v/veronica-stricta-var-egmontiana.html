Veronica stricta var. egmontiana

Veronica stricta var. egmontiana, commonly known as the Egmont violet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
