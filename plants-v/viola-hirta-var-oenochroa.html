Viola hirta var. oenochroa

Viola hirta var. oenochroa, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant thrives in moist, shady woodland environments and is often found in rich, loamy soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to natural landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
