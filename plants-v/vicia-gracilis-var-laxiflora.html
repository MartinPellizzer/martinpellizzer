Vicia gracilis var. laxiflora

Vicia gracilis var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods contain several small, rounded seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
