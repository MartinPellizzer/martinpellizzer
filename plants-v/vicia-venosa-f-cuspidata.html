Vicia venosa f. cuspidata

Vicia venosa f. cuspidata, commonly known as the cuspidate vetch, is a variant of the common vetch, characterized by its distinctive cuspidate (pointed) leaflets.

It is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, and it produces small, elongated pods containing seeds.

This plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and soil-enriching properties.
