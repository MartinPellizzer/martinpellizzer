Vigna trinervia var. bourneae

Vigna trinervia var. bourneae, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender, climbing stems that often reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and role in sustainable agriculture due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
