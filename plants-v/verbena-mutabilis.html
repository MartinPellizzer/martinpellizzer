Verbena mutabilis

Verbena mutabilis, commonly known as the common verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena mutabilis is also known for its ability to thrive in coastal areas due to its salt tolerance.
