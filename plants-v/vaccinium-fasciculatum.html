Vaccinium fasciculatum

Vaccinium fasciculatum, commonly known as the lowbush blueberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that develop into berries, which are typically blue or purple when mature. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and bogs.

Vaccinium fasciculatum is an important species for both ecological and commercial purposes, particularly in the blueberry industry.
