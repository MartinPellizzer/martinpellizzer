Vernonia sordidopapposa

Vernonia sordidopapposa, commonly known as the dark-haired ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dark, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, grayish-white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is known for its hardy nature and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
