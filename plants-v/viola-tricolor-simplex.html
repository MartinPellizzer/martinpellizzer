Viola tricolor simplex

Viola tricolor simplex, commonly known as the three-color violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which typically display three different colors—purple, white, and yellow—on a single leaf. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of violet, white, and yellow. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Viola tricolor simplex prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a versatile addition to woodland gardens.
