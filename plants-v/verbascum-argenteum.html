Verbascum argenteum

Verbascum argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in the summer, typically in clusters at the top of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry environments.

Verbascum argenteum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
