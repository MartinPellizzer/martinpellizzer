Verbascum adenophorum

Verbascum adenophorum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, gray-green, fuzzy leaves that are covered in soft hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, upright inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum adenophorum is a perennial herb that is both ornamental and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
