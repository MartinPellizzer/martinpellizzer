Verbena hystrix

Verbena hystrix, commonly known as the Mexican heather, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Verbena hystrix is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
