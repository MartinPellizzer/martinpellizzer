Vicia caucasica

Vicia caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, and it is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Vicia caucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
