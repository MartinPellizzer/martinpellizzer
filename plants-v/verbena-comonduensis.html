Verbena comonduensis

Verbena comonduensis, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Verbena comonduensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
