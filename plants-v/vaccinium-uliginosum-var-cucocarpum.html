Vaccinium uliginosum var. cucocarpum

Vaccinium uliginosum var. cucocarpum, commonly known as the bog bilberry, is a variant of the common bilberry found in acidic, wet environments.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by dark blue berries. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and culinary applications.

It plays an important role in the ecosystems of bogs and heathlands, supporting various wildlife species.
