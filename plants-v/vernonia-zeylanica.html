Vernonia zeylanica

Vernonia zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in summer. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Vernonia zeylanica is valued for its hard, durable wood and is used in traditional medicine and construction.
