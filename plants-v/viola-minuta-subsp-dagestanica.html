Viola minuta subsp. dagestanica

Viola minuta subsp.

dagestanica, commonly known as the Dagestani viola, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows to a height of about 5-10 centimeters, with delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, violet-blue flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle yet vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
