Veronica viscosula

Veronica viscosula, commonly known as sticky speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its sticky texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Veronica viscosula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
