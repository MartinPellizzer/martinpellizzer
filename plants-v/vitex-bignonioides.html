Vitex bignonioides

Vitex bignonioides, commonly known as the American vitex or chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often gray-green in color and have a rough texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of lavender or purple, which bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Vitex bignonioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
