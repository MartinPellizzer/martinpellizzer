Viola × ruprechtiana

Viola × ruprechtiana, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a cross between Viola lutea and Viola tricolor, resulting in a plant with a diverse range of flower colors, including shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant features heart-shaped, toothed leaves and produces showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for its beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
