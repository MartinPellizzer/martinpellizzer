Vernonia gofensis

Vernonia gofensis, commonly known as the Gofen's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia gofensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some communities.
