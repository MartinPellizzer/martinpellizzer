Vincentia turbinata

Vincentia turbinata, commonly known as the turban vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its rapid growth and attractive foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Vincentia turbinata is also known for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardening.
