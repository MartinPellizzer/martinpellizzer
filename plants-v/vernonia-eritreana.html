Vernonia eritreana

Vernonia eritreana, commonly known as Eritrean ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Eritrea and parts of Ethiopia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences. Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora, often found in rocky and well-drained soils.

Vernonia eritreana is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
