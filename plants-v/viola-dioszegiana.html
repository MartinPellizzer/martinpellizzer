Viola dioszegiana

Viola dioszegiana, commonly known as the Hungarian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola dioszegiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
