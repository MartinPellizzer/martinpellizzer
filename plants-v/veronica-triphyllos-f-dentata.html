Veronica triphyllos f. dentata

Veronica triphyllos f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in early summer, which are arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
