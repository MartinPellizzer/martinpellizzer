Vernonia chlorolepis

Vernonia chlorolepis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with a soft, mist-like appearance that gives it its common name.

Vernonia chlorolepis is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and woodland edges.
