Vernonia urbaniana

Vernonia urbaniana, commonly known as the urban vernonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy yellow or orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Vernonia urbaniana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
