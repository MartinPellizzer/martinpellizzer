Vasconcellea microcarpa subsp. pilifera

Vasconcellea microcarpa subsp.

pilifera, commonly known as the hairy passionfruit, is a member of the Passifloraceae family. It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions with warm, humid climates.

The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, hairy stems, and showy, five-petaled flowers. Its fruits are typically small, yellow or orange, and have a thick, aromatic rind.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
