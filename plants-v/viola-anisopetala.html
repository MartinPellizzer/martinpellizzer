Viola anisopetala

Viola anisopetala, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from purple to white, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Viola anisopetala is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
