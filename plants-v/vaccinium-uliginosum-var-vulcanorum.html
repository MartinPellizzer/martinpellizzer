Vaccinium uliginosum var. vulcanorum

Vaccinium uliginosum var. vulcanorum, commonly known as the volcanic cowberry, is a variant of the common bilberry found in nutrient-poor, acidic soils.

It is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. This plant thrives in wet, boggy environments and is often found in peatlands and heathlands. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and oval-shaped, providing dense foliage.

The species is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecological community.
