Viola × canninefas

Viola × canninefas, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a cross between Viola tricolor and Viola cornuta, resulting in a plant that exhibits characteristics of both parent species. The hybrid is known for its vibrant, often bi-colored flowers that come in shades of purple, blue, and white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is commonly found in woodland areas or gardens.

Botanically, it has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.
