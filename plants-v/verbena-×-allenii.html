Verbena × allenii

Verbena × allenii, commonly known as the hybrid verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Verbena rigida and Verbena bonariensis, resulting in a plant with distinctive ornamental features. The species is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Verbena × allenii is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
