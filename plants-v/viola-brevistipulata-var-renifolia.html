Viola brevistipulata var. renifolia

Viola brevistipulata var. renifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are reniform, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces violet-blue or purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

This variety is part of the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
