Valeriana densiflora var. affinis

Valeriana densiflora var. affinis, commonly known as the dense-flowered valerian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features wiry, branching stems that often grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and aromatic, containing compounds responsible for its medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
