Viola vallicola

Viola vallicola, commonly known as the valley violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in cool, humid environments. Viola vallicola produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
