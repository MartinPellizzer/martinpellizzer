Viguiera cronquistii

Viguiera cronquistii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its showy, purple-pink flower heads bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viguiera cronquistii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
