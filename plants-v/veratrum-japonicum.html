Veratrum japonicum

Veratrum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese white helleborine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, stout stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers with prominent yellow stamens, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous root system is thick and fleshy, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Veratrum japonicum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
