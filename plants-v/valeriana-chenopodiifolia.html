Valeriana chenopodiifolia

Valeriana chenopodiifolia, commonly known as sand valerian, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in loose, dry environments.

Valeriana chenopodiifolia is often found in steppes and deserts, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
