Vachellia paolii subsp. paucijuga

Vachellia paolii subsp.

paucijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr acacia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits.

The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring small, narrow leaves that reduce water loss. It produces yellow flowers followed by long, twisted pods that contain seeds.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
