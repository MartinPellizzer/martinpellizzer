Viola tricolor var. alpestris

Viola tricolor var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often covered in a fine white or pale violet bloom. The flowers are typically violet, white, and yellow, arranged in a distinctive trifoliate pattern. This variety thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
