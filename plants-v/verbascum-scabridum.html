Verbascum scabridum

Verbascum scabridum, commonly known as the rough mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Verbascum scabridum is a perennial plant that is both ornamental and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
