Vernonia myrsinites

Vernonia myrsinites, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

Vernonia myrsinites is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
