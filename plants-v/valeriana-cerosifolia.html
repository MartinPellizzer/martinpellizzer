Valeriana cerosifolia

Valeriana cerosifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a waxy, whitish coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Valeriana cerosifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, spicy scent.
