Vincentia botryantha

Vincentia botryantha, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers that hang in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with slender, woody stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
