Verbesina perymenioides

Verbesina perymenioides, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its upright stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal panicles. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
