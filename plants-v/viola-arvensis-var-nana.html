Viola arvensis var. nana

Viola arvensis var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf common violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, with heart-shaped, hairy leaves and violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant prefers moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and meadows, and it is often found in disturbed soils. Its flowers are usually borne on short stems and are characterized by their distinctive markings.

Viola arvensis var. nana is a member of the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
