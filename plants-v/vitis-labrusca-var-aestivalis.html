Vitis labrusca var. aestivalis

Vitis labrusca var. aestivalis, commonly known as the summer grape, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is often used in ornamental landscaping and as a food source for wildlife.
