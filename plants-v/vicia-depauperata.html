Vicia depauperata

Vicia depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial legume characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically pale yellow to brownish, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early summer. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

It has a fibrous root system and produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds.
