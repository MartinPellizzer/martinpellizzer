Viola arvensis subsp. arvensis

Viola arvensis subsp.

arvensis, commonly known as the field violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and thrives in moist, temperate climates.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and it produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, violet, or white. It is characterized by its erect stems and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
