Veronica gentianoides subsp. gentianoides

Veronica gentianoides subsp.

gentianoides, commonly known as the gentian-like speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers, which typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
