Vismia laurifolia

Vismia laurifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white, waxy flowers that resemble snowballs, which bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement and provide a striking contrast to its showy inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Vismia laurifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
