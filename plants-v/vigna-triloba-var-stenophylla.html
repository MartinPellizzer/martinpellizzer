Vigna triloba var. stenophylla

Vigna triloba var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to thrive in poor soils. It is often used as a forage crop and for soil nitrogen fixation.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological and agricultural systems.
