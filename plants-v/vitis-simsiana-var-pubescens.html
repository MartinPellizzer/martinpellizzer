Vitis simsiana var. pubescens

Vitis simsiana var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent grapevine, is a lesser-known variant of the vitis genus native to specific regions of Asia.

This plant is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves and stems, which give it its botanical name. It typically grows as a climbing vine, producing small, clustered berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires a support structure for its growth.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
