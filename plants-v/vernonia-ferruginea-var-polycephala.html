Vernonia ferruginea var. polycephala

Vernonia ferruginea var. polycephala, commonly known as the rusty Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, rusty-brown hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and seasonal blooms.
