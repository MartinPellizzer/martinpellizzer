Vaccinium viscifolium var. trichostylum

Vaccinium viscifolium var. trichostylum, commonly known as the hairy leathery blueberry, is a variant of the Vaccinium genus native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark blue to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it the common name, and it is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
