Vignea × fussii

Vignea × fussii, commonly known as the wild bean or wild cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional agriculture. Vignea × fussii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable cover crop.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
