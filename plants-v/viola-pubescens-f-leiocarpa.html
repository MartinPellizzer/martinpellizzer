Viola pubescens f. leiocarpa

Viola pubescens f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the silky viola, is a cultivar of the viola genus characterized by its soft, velvety foliage.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters. The flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.

It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens or shaded borders.
