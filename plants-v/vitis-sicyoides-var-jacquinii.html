Vitis sicyoides var. jacquinii

Vitis sicyoides var. jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacquin's muscadine grape, is a vine species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

The berries are used in local cuisines and have potential for commercial cultivation due to their unique flavor and nutritional value.
