Viguiera molinae

Viguiera molinae, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its showy purple to pink flower heads appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viguiera molinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
