Vaccinium woodianum

Vaccinium woodianum, commonly known as the Hawaiian highland blueberry, is a shrub native to the islands of Hawaii.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and montane habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
