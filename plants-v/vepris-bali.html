Vepris bali

Vepris bali, commonly known as Balinese wild laurier, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The fruit, though not commonly consumed, is sometimes used in local remedies.
