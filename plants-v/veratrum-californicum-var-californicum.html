Veratrum californicum var. californicum

Veratrum californicum var. californicum, commonly known as California aconite, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers in late spring to early summer. Its roots and stems contain toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous plant.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in forested or meadow habitats.
