Veronica spicata subsp. crassifolia

Veronica spicata subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
