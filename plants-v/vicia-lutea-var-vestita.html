Vicia lutea var. vestita

Vicia lutea var. vestita, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically found in disturbed soils and is often used for soil stabilization due to its extensive root system. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates.

Vicia lutea var. vestita is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
