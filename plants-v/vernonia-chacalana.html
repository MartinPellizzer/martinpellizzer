Vernonia chacalana

Vernonia chacalana, commonly known as the Chacalana ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its striking blue to violet flower heads appear in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia chacalana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
