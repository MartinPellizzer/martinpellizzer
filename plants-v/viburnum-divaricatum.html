Viburnum divaricatum

Viburnum divaricatum, commonly known as the creeping viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a sprawling, ground-hugging growth habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green above and lighter beneath, with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Viburnum divaricatum produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
