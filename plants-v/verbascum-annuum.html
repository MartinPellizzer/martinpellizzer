Verbascum annuum

Verbascum annuum, commonly known as the common mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the second year of growth, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Verbascum annuum has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly for respiratory ailments.
