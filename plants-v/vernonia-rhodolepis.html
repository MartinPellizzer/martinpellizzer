Vernonia rhodolepis

Vernonia rhodolepis, commonly known as the redstem ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, often forming dense stands in swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to reddish-brown in color.

Vernonia rhodolepis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
