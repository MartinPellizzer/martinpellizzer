Vanillosmopsis brasiliensis

Vanillosmopsis brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian vanilla tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, vanilla-like pods that are used in aromatherapy and as a flavoring agent. Its botanical significance lies in its unique aromatic compounds and its role in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
