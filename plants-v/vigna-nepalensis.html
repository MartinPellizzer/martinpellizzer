Vigna nepalensis

Vigna nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like pods that contain edible seeds. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Vigna nepalensis is also valued for its nutritional content, including protein and essential minerals.
