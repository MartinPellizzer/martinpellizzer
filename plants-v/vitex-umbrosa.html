Vitex umbrosa

Vitex umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow vine, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, which bloom in shades of purple or blue. Its fruit is a dry, woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Vitex umbrosa is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
