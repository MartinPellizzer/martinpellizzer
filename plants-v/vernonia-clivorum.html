Vernonia clivorum

Vernonia clivorum, commonly known as cliff vervain, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers make it visually distinctive.

Vernonia clivorum is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
