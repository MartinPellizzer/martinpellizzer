Vernonia muelleri

Vernonia muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular florets that are typically clustered in dense, spherical or cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia muelleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
