Vaccinium laurifolium var. pensile

Vaccinium laurifolium var. pensile, commonly known as the hanging huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
