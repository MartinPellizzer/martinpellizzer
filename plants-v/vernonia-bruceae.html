Vernonia bruceae

Vernonia bruceae, commonly known as Bruce's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pale blue to purple, arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia bruceae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
