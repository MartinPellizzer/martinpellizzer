Verbena macrostachya

Verbena macrostachya, commonly known as the giant verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, five-petaled blue or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Verbena macrostachya is known for its long blooming period, which extends from late spring through early autumn.
