Valerianopsis foliosa

Valerianopsis foliosa, commonly known as the leafy valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, small white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a strong, spicy scent.

Valerianopsis foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.
