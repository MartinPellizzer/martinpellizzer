Vitaliana primuliflora subf. cinerea

Vitaliana primuliflora subf.

cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf vitalia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, silky hairs, giving the plant its distinctive silvery appearance.

The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically have five petals with a pale yellow to greenish hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
