Vernonia boliviana

Vernonia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Argentina.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy blue to violet flowers.

Vernonia boliviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
