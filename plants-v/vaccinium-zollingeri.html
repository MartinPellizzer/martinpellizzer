Vaccinium zollingeri

Vaccinium zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger huckleberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Andes mountains in South America, specifically in countries like Peru and Ecuador. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its berries are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
