Viola tonkinensis

Viola tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Viola tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
