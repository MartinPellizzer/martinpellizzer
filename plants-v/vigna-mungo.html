Vigna mungo

Vigna mungo, commonly known as black gram, is an annual leguminous plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect growth habit. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Vigna mungo thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for both its seeds and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
