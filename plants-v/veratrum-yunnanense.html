Veratrum yunnanense

Veratrum yunnanense, commonly known as Yunnan aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, stout stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots and rhizomes contain toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both medicinal and cautionary interest.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.
