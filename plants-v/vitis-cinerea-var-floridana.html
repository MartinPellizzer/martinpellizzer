Vitis cinerea var. floridana

Vitis cinerea var. floridana, commonly known as Florida grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a fuzzy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by clusters of edible, sweet berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streams.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
