Vitex brittoniana

Vitex brittoniana, commonly known as the Britton's chaste tree, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, clustered purple to pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Vitex brittoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
