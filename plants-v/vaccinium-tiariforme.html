Vaccinium tiariforme

Vaccinium tiariforme, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark red to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and pine flatwoods.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
