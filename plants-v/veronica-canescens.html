Veronica canescens

Veronica canescens, commonly known as the downy speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to violet in color, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Veronica canescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
