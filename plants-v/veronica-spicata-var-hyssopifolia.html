Veronica spicata var. hyssopifolia

Veronica spicata var. hyssopifolia, commonly known as hyssop-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear whorled clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
