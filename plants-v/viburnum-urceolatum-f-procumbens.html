Viburnum urceolatum f. procumbens

Viburnum urceolatum f. procumbens, commonly known as the creeping snowberry, is a compact, low-growing shrub characterized by its prostrate growth habit.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. The plant has ovate to elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly toothed margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value lies in its colorful berries and adaptability to various garden conditions.
