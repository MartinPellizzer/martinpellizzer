Vismia sellowiana

Vismia sellowiana, commonly known as the Natal ebony, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy wood and smooth, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by round, orange-red fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
