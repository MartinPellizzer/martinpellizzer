Viola × joannis-wagneri

Viola × joannis-wagneri, commonly known as the Wagner's violet, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, bi-colored flowers that typically display shades of purple and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded gardens. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in horticulture for its attractive foliage and flowers.
