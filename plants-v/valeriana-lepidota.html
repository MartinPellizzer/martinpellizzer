Valeriana lepidota

Valeriana lepidota, commonly known as the smooth valerian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a creeping rhizome and erect, branched stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its aromatic foliage contains volatile oils, which contribute to its use in herbal remedies.

Valeriana lepidota thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
