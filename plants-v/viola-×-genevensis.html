Viola × genevensis

Viola × genevensis, commonly known as the Genevan violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm and prefers cool, moist environments. It is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers often have a distinctive yellow or white throat, adding to its visual appeal.
