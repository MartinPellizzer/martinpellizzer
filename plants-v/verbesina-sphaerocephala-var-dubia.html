Verbesina sphaerocephala var. dubia

Verbesina sphaerocephala var. dubia, commonly known as the globehead beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spherical, yellow-orange flower heads that are surrounded by bracts. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and roadside ditches.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
