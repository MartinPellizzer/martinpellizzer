Viburnum opulus f. sterile

Viburnum opulus f. sterile, commonly known as the European Guelder Rose, is a hybrid form of the European guelder rose.

It is characterized by its sterile flowers, which lack the ability to produce seeds. The plant typically features showy, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that turn red in the autumn.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
