Vernonia tombadorensis

Vernonia tombadorensis, commonly known as the Tombador goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange ray flowers and greenish-brown disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
