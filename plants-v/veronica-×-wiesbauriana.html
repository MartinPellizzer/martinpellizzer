Veronica × wiesbauriana

Veronica × wiesbauriana, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and along stream banks.

Veronica × wiesbauriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
