Vincentia grevei

Vincentia grevei, commonly known as the green spider-orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant greenish-yellow flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns resembling spider webs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its moist habitat.

Vincentia grevei is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native ecosystem.
