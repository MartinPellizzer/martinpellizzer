Vitis incarnata

Vitis incarnata, commonly known as the swamp grape, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Vitis incarnata thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
