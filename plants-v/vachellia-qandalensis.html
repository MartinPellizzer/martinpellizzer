Vachellia qandalensis

Vachellia qandalensis, commonly known as the Socotra thorn tree, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is a leguminous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny stems and distinctive, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Vachellia qandalensis plays a significant ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
