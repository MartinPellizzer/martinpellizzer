Viola × bessarabica

Viola × bessarabica, commonly known as the Bessarabian violet, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola × bessarabica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
