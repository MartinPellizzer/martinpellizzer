Valeriana spathulata

Valeriana spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf valerian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, herbaceous stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late summer. Valeriana spathulata is often found in woodland margins and moist meadows, thriving in partial shade.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its calming effects.
