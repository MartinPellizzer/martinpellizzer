Vernonia anceps

Vernonia anceps, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Vernonia anceps is often found in open woods, meadows, and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
