Vicia dadianorum

Vicia dadianorum, commonly known as the Dadiano vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Vicia dadianorum is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and is often found in rocky or arid habitats.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
