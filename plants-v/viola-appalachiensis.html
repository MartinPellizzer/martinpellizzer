Viola appalachiensis

Viola appalachiensis, commonly known as the Appalachian violet, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a threatened species in some regions.
