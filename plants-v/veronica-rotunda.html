Veronica rotunda

Veronica rotunda, commonly known as roundleaf speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has opposite, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

Veronica rotunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes for its delicate floral display.
