Verbascum densifolium

Verbascum densifolium, commonly known as the dense-leaved mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and broad, velvety leaves that are densely packed. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum densifolium is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
