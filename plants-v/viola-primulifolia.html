Viola primulifolia

Viola primulifolia, commonly known as the spring viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of violet and white. Its blooming period occurs in early spring, making it one of the first wildflowers to appear after winter.

Viola primulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
