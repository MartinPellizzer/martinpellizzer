Vitex waterlotii

Vitex waterlotii, commonly known as the African vitex, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, blooming in shades of purple or white. Its fruit is a dry, two-valved capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Vitex waterlotii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.
