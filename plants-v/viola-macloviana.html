Viola macloviana

Viola macloviana, commonly known as the Maclovian violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of violet and white. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

Viola macloviana is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
