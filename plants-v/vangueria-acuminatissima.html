Vangueria acuminatissima

Vangueria acuminatissima, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, featuring woody stems and small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, sour-tasting berries.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
