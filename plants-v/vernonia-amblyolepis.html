Vernonia amblyolepis

Vernonia amblyolepis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and the ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.

Vernonia amblyolepis is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
