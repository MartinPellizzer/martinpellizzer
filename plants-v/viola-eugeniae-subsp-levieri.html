Viola eugeniae subsp. levieri

Viola eugeniae subsp.

levieri, commonly known as the Sierra Madre violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
