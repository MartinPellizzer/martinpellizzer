Viguiera brevifolia

Viguiera brevifolia, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Viguiera brevifolia is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its ecological value includes providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
