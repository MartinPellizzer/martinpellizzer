Vernonia zollingeriana

Vernonia zollingeriana, commonly known as the Zollinger's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, dark purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Vernonia zollingeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
