Vallaris lancifolia

Vallaris lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vallaris, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Vallaris lancifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
