Vicia amphicarpos

Vicia amphicarpos, commonly known as the twin-seeded vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing two seeds. Vicia amphicarpos is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
