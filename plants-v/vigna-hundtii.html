Vigna hundtii

Vigna hundtii, commonly known as the African wing bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is typically shrub-like, with hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein.

Vigna hundtii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable farming systems.
