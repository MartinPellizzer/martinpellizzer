Vernonia liatroides subsp. ehrenbergiana

Vernonia liatroides subsp.

ehrenbergiana, commonly known as the Ehrenberg's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a distinctive blue-green coloration.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
