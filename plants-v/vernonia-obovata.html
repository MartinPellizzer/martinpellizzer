Vernonia obovata

Vernonia obovata, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a rough texture and a gray-green color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy perennial suitable for native gardens.

This plant is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
