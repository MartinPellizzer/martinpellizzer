Viola pseudaetolica

Viola pseudaetolica, commonly known as the false pansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its blooms appear in spring, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes.

Viola pseudaetolica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
