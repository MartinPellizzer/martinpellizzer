Vepris araliopsioides

Vepris araliopsioides, commonly known as the African cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that resembles cinnamon. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round, yellowish fruits.

The bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
