Vernonia eupatorifolia

Vernonia eupatorifolia, commonly known as ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
