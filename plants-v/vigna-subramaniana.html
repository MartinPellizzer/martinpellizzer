Vigna subramaniana

Vigna subramaniana, commonly known as the Indian bean or wild pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid conditions. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its nutritional content and potential agricultural use.

Vigna subramaniana is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
