Verbascum × bastardii var. racemosum

Verbascum × bastardii var. racemosum, commonly known as the racemose mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in soft, downy hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and ecological benefits.
