Vitex lutea

Vitex lutea, commonly known as the yellow vitex, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, blooming in summer. Its fruits are dry, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Vitex lutea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
