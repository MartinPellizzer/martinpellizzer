Viola cerasifolia subsp. typica

Viola cerasifolia subsp.

typica, commonly known as the cherry-leaved violet, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in calcareous soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
