Viola watsonii

Viola watsonii, commonly known as Watson's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the viola family, Violaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in California and Nevada. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, where it thrives in cooler, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive lower petal that is often spurred.

Viola watsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
