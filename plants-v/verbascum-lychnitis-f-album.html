Verbascum lychnitis f. album

Verbascum lychnitis f. album, commonly known as the white mullein, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Its fibrous roots and fuzzy foliage make it well adapted to arid environments.
