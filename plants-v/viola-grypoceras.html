Viola grypoceras

Viola grypoceras, commonly known as the violet bell, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest undergrowth. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are often clustered in racemes.

Viola grypoceras is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
