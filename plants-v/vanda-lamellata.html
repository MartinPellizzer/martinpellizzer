Vanda lamellata

Vanda lamellata, commonly known as the flat-leaf vanda, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and often flat leaves that are adapted for efficient light capture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, typically blooming during the rainy season. Vanda lamellata thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, highlighting its cultural and practical significance.
