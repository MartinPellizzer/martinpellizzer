Vaccinium floridanum var. caesium

Vaccinium floridanum var. caesium, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible, dark blue to black berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and pine flatwoods.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
