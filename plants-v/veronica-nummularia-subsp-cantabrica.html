Veronica nummularia subsp. cantabrica

Veronica nummularia subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
