Veronica bellidifolia

Veronica bellidifolia, commonly known as the blueweed or bellflower veronica, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica bellidifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
