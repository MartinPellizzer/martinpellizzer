Viola nevadensis

Viola nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola nevadensis is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to cooler, temperate climates.
