Veronica fruticulosa subsp. saxatilis

Veronica fruticulosa subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the rock everlasting, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has woody stems and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

This subspecies is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
