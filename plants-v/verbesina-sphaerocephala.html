Verbesina sphaerocephala

Verbesina sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globehead beggar-ticks, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features distinctive spherical, yellow-orange flower heads that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. The plant has hairy, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom from late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its aggressive growth habit and can become invasive in certain regions.
