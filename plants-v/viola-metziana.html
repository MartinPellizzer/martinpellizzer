Viola metziana

Viola metziana, commonly known as the Metziana violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola metziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
