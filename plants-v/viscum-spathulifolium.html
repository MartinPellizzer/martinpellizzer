Viscum spathulifolium

Viscum spathulifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It possesses a distinctive appearance with broad, ovate leaves that are often waxy and glossy. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are usually clustered together, followed by small, fleshy berries that are a source of food for birds. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the berries, allowing the plant to spread to new hosts.

Viscum spathulifolium plays an important ecological role in its habitat, contributing to biodiversity and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
