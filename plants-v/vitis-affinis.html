Vitis affinis

Vitis affinis, commonly known as the Virginia creeper, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which typically consist of five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, palmate appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by red to purple berries that are attractive to birds. Vitis affinis thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it both a popular garden plant and an invasive species in some regions.
