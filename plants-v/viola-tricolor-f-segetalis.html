Viola tricolor f. segetalis

Viola tricolor f. segetalis, commonly known as the garden violet, is a cultivar of the common violet, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 15-20 centimeters. The flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with three petals and a distinctive yellow center. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
