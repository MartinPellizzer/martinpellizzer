Vigna subterranea var. spontanea

Vigna subterranea var. spontanea, commonly known as the wild cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
