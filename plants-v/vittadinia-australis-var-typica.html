Vittadinia australis var. typica

Vittadinia australis var. typica, commonly known as the Australian everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant produces numerous small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across arid and semi-arid regions. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The species is well adapted to poor soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
