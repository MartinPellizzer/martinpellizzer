Viguiera laciniata

Viguiera laciniata, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features erect, sparsely branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. Its showy, yellow to orange flower heads appear in late summer and are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Viguiera laciniata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
