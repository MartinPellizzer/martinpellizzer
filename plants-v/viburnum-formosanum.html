Viburnum formosanum

Viburnum formosanum, commonly known as the Taiwan arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum formosanum thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
