Vepris teitensis

Vepris teitensis, commonly known as the Madagascar laurier or sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's aromatic compounds contribute to its use in perfumery and as a flavoring agent.
