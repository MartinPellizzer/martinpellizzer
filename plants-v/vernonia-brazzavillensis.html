Vernonia brazzavillensis

Vernonia brazzavillensis, commonly known as the Congo ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as along riverbanks and in swampy areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Vernonia brazzavillensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
