Vernonia cinerea var. lentii

Vernonia cinerea var. lentii, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

The flowers are arranged in paniculate inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value in native gardens.
