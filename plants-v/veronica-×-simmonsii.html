Veronica × simmonsii

Veronica × simmonsii, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant flowers and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
