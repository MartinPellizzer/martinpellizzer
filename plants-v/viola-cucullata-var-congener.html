Viola cucullata var. congener

Viola cucullata var. congener, commonly known as the hooded violets, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet flowers with a hood-like structure over the reproductive organs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers range in color from purple to blue.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
