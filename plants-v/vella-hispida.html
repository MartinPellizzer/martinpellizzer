Vella hispida

Vella hispida, commonly known as the rough veldt grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and can thrive in a variety of soil types. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to dry and semi-arid environments.
