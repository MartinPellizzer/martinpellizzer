Vanilla lutea

Vanilla lutea, commonly known as the yellow vanilla, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow pods that mature into a yellowish-orange color, which are used for their aromatic properties. Its leaves are ovate and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Vanilla lutea is often cultivated for its fragrance and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
