Vitis multijugata

Vitis multijugata, commonly known as the Chinese grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically consumed by birds and other animals. Vitis multijugata is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
