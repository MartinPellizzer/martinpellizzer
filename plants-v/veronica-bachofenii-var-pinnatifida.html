Veronica bachofenii var. pinnatifida

Veronica bachofenii var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with appropriate growing conditions.
