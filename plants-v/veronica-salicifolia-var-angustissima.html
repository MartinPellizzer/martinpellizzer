Veronica salicifolia var. angustissima

Veronica salicifolia var. angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a desirable plant for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
