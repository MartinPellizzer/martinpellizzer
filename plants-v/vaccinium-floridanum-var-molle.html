Vaccinium floridanum var. molle

Vaccinium floridanum var. molle, commonly known as Florida huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, bright red berries in the summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and pine flatwoods.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
