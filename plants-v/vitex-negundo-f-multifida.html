Vitex negundo f. multifida

Vitex negundo f. multifida, commonly known as the fragrant verbena or Indian bead tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often deeply lobed and resemble the shape of a fan. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically lavender or white in color, blooming in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and showy flowers.
