Viola subdimidiata

Viola subdimidiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions such as Virginia and North Carolina. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple bloom. Its flowers are usually violet or blue-violet, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Viola subdimidiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
