Vernonia fournetii

Vernonia fournetii, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stature and showy yellow blooms. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. Vernonia fournetii thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
