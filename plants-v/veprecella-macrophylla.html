Veprecella macrophylla

Veprecella macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf veprecella, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
