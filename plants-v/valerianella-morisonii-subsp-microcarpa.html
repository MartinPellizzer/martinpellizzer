Valerianella morisonii subsp. microcarpa

Valerianella morisonii subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as dwarf valerian, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features small, clustered leaves and tiny, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers. Valerianella morisonii subsp.

microcarpa is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like butterflies and bees.
