Viburnum tengyuehense var. tengyuehense

Viburnum tengyuehense var. tengyuehense, commonly known as the Tengyuehense Viburnum, is a shrub native to China.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its foliage provides ornamental value throughout the growing season, and it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

The species is also valued for its potential ecological benefits, including supporting local wildlife.
