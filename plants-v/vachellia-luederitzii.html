Vachellia luederitzii

Vachellia luederitzii, commonly known as the Luederitz thornveld acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions.

Vachellia luederitzii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in nutrient-poor areas.
