Vulpia ulochaeta

Vulpia ulochaeta, commonly known as the narrowleaf foxtail, is a grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of bristly, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish in color. Vulpia ulochaeta thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is adapted to temperate climates and can tolerate a range of soil conditions.
