Viola fontana

Viola fontana, commonly known as the Fontana violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. Viola fontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in partial shade.

It also has historical uses in herbal medicine for its mild sedative properties.
