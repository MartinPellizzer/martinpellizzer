Viola canina var. pubescens

Viola canina var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy heart violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and grasslands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its flowers are violet-blue, with five petals and a distinctive yellow center.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
