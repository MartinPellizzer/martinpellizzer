Vanda limbata

Vanda limbata, commonly known as the orchid with the "butterfly" appearance, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in length, supporting clusters of showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces large, butterfly-like petals that are typically white or pale pink with a contrasting lip. Vanda limbata thrives in humid, warm climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
