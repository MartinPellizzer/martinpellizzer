Viola × discors

Viola × discors, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and garden beds. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola × discors is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
