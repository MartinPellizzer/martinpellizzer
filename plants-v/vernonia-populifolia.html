Vernonia populifolia

Vernonia populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine. Vernonia populifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, drought-resistant plants.
