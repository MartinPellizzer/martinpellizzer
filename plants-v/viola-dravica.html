Viola dravica

Viola dravica, commonly known as the Dravina violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces violet-blue or purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Viola dravica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
