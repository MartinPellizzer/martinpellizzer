Vicia taquetii

Vicia taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's vetch, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Vicia taquetii is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for certain insect species.
