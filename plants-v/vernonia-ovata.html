Vernonia ovata

Vernonia ovata, commonly known as the ovalleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, conical clusters.

Vernonia ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
