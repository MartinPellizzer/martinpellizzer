Verbena lobata var. lobata

Verbena lobata var. lobata, commonly known as spreading blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant has hairy, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its drought tolerance makes it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping and native gardens.
