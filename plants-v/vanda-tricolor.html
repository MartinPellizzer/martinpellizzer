Vanda tricolor

Vanda tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid that thrives on tree trunks and branches, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers with three distinct sections, each displaying a different hue. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Vanda tricolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
