Viola extremiorientalis

Viola extremiorientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola extremiorientalis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
