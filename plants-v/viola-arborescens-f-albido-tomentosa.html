Viola arborescens f. albido-tomentosa

Viola arborescens f. albido-tomentosa, commonly known as the white-tomentose tree violet, is a unique variant of the tree violet species.

It is characterized by its soft, white pubescence that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in woodland areas. Its flowers are usually pale violet or white, with a prominent violet throat.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
