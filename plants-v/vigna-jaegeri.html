Vigna jaegeri

Vigna jaegeri, commonly known as the yellow winged bean, is a legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Vigna jaegeri is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
