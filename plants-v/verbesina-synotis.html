Verbesina synotis

Verbesina synotis, commonly known as yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
