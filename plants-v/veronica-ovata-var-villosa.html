Veronica ovata var. villosa

Veronica ovata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy veronica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with a similar fuzzy coating.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
