Vanclevea stylosa

Vanclevea stylosa, commonly known as the slender-flowered vanclevea, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in whorls around the stem.

Vanclevea stylosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
