Vicia sericocarpa subsp. microphylla

Vicia sericocarpa subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved silky vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a silvery-white underside.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
