Viola variegata

Viola variegata, commonly known as the variegated violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive variegation patterns. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade.

Viola variegata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
