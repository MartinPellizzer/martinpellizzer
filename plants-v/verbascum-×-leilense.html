Verbascum × leilense

Verbascum × leilense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattifolium.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Verbascum × leilense is often used in landscaping and as a naturalized plant in suitable climates.
