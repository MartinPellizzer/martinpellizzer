Veronica pseudodivaricata

Veronica pseudodivaricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
