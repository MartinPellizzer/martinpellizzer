Viola adunca f. albiflora

Viola adunca f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered velvetleaf violet, is a botanical variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with its deep green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals, and it produces small, round seed pods.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
