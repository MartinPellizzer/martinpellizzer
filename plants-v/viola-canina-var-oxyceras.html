Viola canina var. oxyceras

Viola canina var. oxyceras, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
