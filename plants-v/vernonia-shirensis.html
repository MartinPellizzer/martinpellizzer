Vernonia shirensis

Vernonia shirensis, commonly known as the Shire violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Vernonia shirensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
