Viola xanthopotamica

Viola xanthopotamica, commonly known as the yellow marsh violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows and riverbanks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive purple throat. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola xanthopotamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
