Vernonia schomburgkiana var. elliptica

Vernonia schomburgkiana var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic ironweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening.

The species is valued for its attractive foliage and long-lasting floral display.
