Vandenboschia titibuensis

Vandenboschia titibuensis, commonly known as the Titibuensis ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
