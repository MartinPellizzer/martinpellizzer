Viola aspera

Viola aspera, commonly known as the rough violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola aspera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
