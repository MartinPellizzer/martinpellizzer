Vernonia angolensis

Vernonia angolensis, commonly known as the Angola ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The genus Vernonia is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
