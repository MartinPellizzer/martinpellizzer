Vitis persica

Vitis persica, commonly known as the Persian grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to clusters of berries, which are typically red or black when mature. Vitis persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its fruit and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Vitis.
