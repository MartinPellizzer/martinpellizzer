Vaccinium altomontanum

Vaccinium altomontanum, commonly known as the Altomontan blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, with five petals, and it blooms in the spring, producing edible berries that are rich in antioxidants.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
