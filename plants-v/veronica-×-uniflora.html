Veronica × uniflora

Veronica × uniflora, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, blue or purple flowers clustered in pairs. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is native to Europe, though it has been naturalized in various parts of the world. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, gardens, and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, low-maintenance plant.
