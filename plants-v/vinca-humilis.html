Vinca humilis

Vinca humilis, commonly known as smallflower vinca, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has a trailing habit, making it suitable for ground cover or hanging baskets. Its leaves are typically ovate and slightly hairy, with a dark green color.

Vinca humilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is known for its drought tolerance.
