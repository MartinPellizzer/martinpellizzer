Veronica austriaca var. tenuifolia

Veronica austriaca var. tenuifolia, commonly known as slender leaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, shade-tolerant plant.
