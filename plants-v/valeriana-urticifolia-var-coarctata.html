Valeriana urticifolia var. coarctata

Valeriana urticifolia var. coarctata, commonly known as narrow-leaved valerian, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its rhizomatous root system is notable for its aromatic compounds, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
