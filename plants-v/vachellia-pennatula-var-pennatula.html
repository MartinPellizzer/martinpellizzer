Vachellia pennatula var. pennatula

Vachellia pennatula var. pennatula, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It has slender, often twisted branches and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like leaflets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

The wood of this species is used locally for fuel and construction.
