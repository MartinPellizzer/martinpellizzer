Vicia agerii

Vicia agerii, commonly known as the lesser vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink. Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which benefits soil fertility.

Vicia agerii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
