Vernonia angustifolia var. pumila

Vernonia angustifolia var. pumila, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
