Vaccinium myrtillus var. leucocarpum

Vaccinium myrtillus var. leucocarpum, commonly known as the white-fruited blueberry, is a variant of the common blueberry.

It is a low-growing shrub native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. The plant produces small, white or pale blue berries that are distinct from the typical blue color of the species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool climate with ample sunlight.

This variety is valued for its unique fruit color and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental or culinary purposes.
