Verbascum nitidum var. alopecuros

Verbascum nitidum var. alopecuros, commonly known as the silky mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, erect stems covered in soft, silvery-gray hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, gradually reducing in size toward the top of the plant. It produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

This plant is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
