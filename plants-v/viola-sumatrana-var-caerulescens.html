Viola sumatrana var. caerulescens

Viola sumatrana var. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue viol, is a perennial flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Its flowers are also pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
