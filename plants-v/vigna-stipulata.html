Vigna stipulata

Vigna stipulata, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in various cuisines. Vigna stipulata thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting subsequent crops.
