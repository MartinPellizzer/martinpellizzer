Vanda subulifolia

Vanda subulifolia, commonly known as the needle-leaf vanda, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

Vanda subulifolia is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.
