Vincetoxicum japonicum var. purpurascens

Vincetoxicum japonicum var. purpurascens, commonly known as Japanese snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

While it is ornamental, it can be invasive in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
