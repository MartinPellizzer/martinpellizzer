Verbascum decurrens

Verbascum decurrens, commonly known as the creeping mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features gray-green, fuzzy leaves that are lance-shaped and gradually tapering towards the base. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, forming clusters at the ends of the stems.

Verbascum decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
