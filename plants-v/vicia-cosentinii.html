Vicia cosentinii

Vicia cosentinii, commonly known as the Cosentin's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by wind.

Vicia cosentinii is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential as a forage plant.
