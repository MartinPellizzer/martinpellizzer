Vitex syringifolia

Vitex syringifolia, commonly known as the Chinese lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
