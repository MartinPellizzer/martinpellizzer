Vernonia sublutea

Vernonia sublutea, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange flower heads.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
