Vernonia persericea

Vernonia persericea, commonly known as the ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia persericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
