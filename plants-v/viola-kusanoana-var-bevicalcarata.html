Viola kusanoana var. bevicalcarata

Viola kusanoana var. bevicalcarata, commonly known as the Kusano's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
