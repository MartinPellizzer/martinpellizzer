Vachellia ormocarpoides

Vachellia ormocarpoides, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and bipinnate leaves that are feathery in appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Vachellia ormocarpoides is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of browse for livestock.
