Viburnum chunii

Viburnum chunii, commonly known as the Chinese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Viburnum chunii is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its seasonal interest.
