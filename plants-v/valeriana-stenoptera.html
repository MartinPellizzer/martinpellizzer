Valeriana stenoptera

Valeriana stenoptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, usually pink or purple in color. Valeriana stenoptera has a strong, aromatic scent, which is characteristic of the Valeriana genus.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential calming and sedative properties.
