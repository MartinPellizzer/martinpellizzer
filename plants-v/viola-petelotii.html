Viola petelotii

Viola petelotii, commonly known as the Mexican violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Viola petelotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
