Vachellia biaciculata

Vachellia biaciculata, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of small, paired leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The bark and leaves have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
