Vernonia vulturina

Vernonia vulturina, commonly known as the vulture's eye, is a striking plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is known for its distinctive, eye-like appearance due to its dense, rounded flower heads. Vernonia vulturina thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in open woodlands or grasslands.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some regions.
