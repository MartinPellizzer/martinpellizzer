Vernonia diversifolia subsp. bechioides

Vernonia diversifolia subsp.

bechioides, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dark, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

This subspecies is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
