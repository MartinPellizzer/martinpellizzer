Valantia ciliata

Valantia ciliata, commonly known as the feathered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are often tinged with white. Valantia ciliata thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and borders due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
