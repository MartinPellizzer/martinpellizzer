Vicia larissae

Vicia larissae, commonly known as the Larissa vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Vicia larissae is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
