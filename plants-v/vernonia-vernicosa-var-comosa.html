Vernonia vernicosa var. comosa

Vernonia vernicosa var. comosa, commonly known as the hairy vernonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

The hairy vernonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
