Veronica takedana

Veronica takedana, commonly known as Japanese speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate or creeping.

Veronica takedana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
