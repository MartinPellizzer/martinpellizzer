Vismia alternifolia

Vismia alternifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a prominent midrib. Its most striking botanical feature is its dense clusters of white, waxy flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
