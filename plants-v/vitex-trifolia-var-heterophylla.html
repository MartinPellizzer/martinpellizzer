Vitex trifolia var. heterophylla

Vitex trifolia var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable-leaf chaste tree, is a variant of the chaste tree species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves that can be trifoliate or simple depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite or alternate, with serrated edges and a glossy green upper surface.

The flowers are usually clustered in terminal panicles, displaying shades of purple or white, and are followed by small, woody fruits.
