Veronica rotunda var. petiolata

Veronica rotunda var. petiolata, commonly known as the broad-leaved speedwell, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rounded, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its compact growth habit.
