Veronica poppelwellii

Veronica poppelwellii, commonly known as the New Zealand veronica, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Veronica poppelwellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
