Vladimiria crispoundulata

Vladimiria crispoundulata, commonly known as the velvetleaf vladimiria, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it a soft texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique foliage and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
