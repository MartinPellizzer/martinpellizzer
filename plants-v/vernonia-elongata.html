Vernonia elongata

Vernonia elongata, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces fluffy, pale yellow to brownish flower clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Vernonia elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
