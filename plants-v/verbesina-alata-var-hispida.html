Verbesina alata var. hispida

Verbesina alata var. hispida, commonly known as rough hound's tongue, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in conical heads. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and has a distinctive, rugged appearance.

It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
