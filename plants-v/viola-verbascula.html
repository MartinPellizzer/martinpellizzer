Viola verbascula

Viola verbascula, commonly known as the violet beardtongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers with a long, narrow lower lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and hairy.

Viola verbascula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
