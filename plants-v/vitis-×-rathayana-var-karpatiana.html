Vitis × rathayana var. karpatiana

Vitis × rathayana var. karpatiana, commonly known as the Karpatian grapevine, is a hybrid plant native to the Carpathian region of Europe.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and climbing habit, with dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its berries are usually small and can range in color from red to black, though they are not typically consumed by humans.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in cooler climates.
