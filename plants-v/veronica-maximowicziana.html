Veronica maximowicziana

Veronica maximowicziana, commonly known as the Tibetan speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in alpine and rocky environments, often growing at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
