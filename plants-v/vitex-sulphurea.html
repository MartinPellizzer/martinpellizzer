Vitex sulphurea

Vitex sulphurea, commonly known as golden helix, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to botanical gardens and landscapes.
