Vaccinium floribundum var. polystachyum

Vaccinium floribundum var. polystachyum, commonly known as the clustered blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 feet and features dense clusters of small, dark blue berries. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
