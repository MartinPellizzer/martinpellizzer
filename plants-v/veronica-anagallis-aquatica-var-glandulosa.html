Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. glandulosa

Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. glandulosa, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and ponds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The variety glandulosa is distinguished by the presence of glandular dots on its leaves and stems, which contribute to its unique botanical appearance.
