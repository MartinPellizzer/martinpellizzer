Vimen × russeliana

Vimen × russeliana, commonly known as the hybrid boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is a cross between the American boxwood (Buxus sempervirens) and the Japanese boxwood (Buxus microphylla). The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes due to its ability to be pruned into various shapes.

Vimen × russeliana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
