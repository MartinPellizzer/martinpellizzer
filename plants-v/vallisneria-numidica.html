Vallisneria numidica

Vallisneria numidica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, followed by the development of cylindrical, ribbon-shaped fruits. Vallisneria numidica plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is often used in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
