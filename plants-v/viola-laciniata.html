Viola laciniata

Viola laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant features heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically violet-blue in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base.

Viola laciniata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
