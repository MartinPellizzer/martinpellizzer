Vicia noeana

Vicia noeana, commonly known as the Balkan vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks. Vicia noeana is an annual or biennial plant that produces pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
