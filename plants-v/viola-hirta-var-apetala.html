Viola hirta var. apetala

Viola hirta var. apetala, commonly known as the hairleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
