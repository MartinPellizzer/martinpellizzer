Verbascum laetum

Verbascum laetum, commonly known as the yellow mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and along fences.

Verbascum laetum is a hardy perennial that is sometimes used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
