Viola brevistipulata var. brevistipulata

Viola brevistipulata var. brevistipulata, commonly known as the short-stippled violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with short, blunt lobes and a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
