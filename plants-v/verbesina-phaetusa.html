Verbesina phaetusa

Verbesina phaetusa, commonly known as the golden beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow blooms. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

Verbesina phaetusa is also known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
