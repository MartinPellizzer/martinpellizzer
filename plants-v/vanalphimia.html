Vanalphimia

Vanalphimia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale, almost translucent petals that appear to shimmer in low light, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant's leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat. Vanalphimia flowers at night, releasing a faint, sweet fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is highly sought after by plant enthusiasts.
