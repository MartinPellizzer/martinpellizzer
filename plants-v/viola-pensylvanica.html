Viola pensylvanica

Viola pensylvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a range of purple to blue hues.

The Pennsylvania violet is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
