Vaccinium loranthifolium

Vaccinium loranthifolium, commonly known as the red-berried huckleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate and elliptical, with a leathery texture and dark green color.

Vaccinium loranthifolium thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
