Vigna caesia

Vigna caesia, commonly known as the pink bean or blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink or purple flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain round, yellow seeds used in various cuisines. Vigna caesia is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its nutritional benefits.

Its growth habit includes climbing or trailing vines, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.
