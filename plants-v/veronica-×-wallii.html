Veronica × wallii

Veronica × wallii, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers, typically arranged in whorls along the stems. It is native to Europe and has been naturalized in various parts of the world, including North America.

Veronica × wallii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.
