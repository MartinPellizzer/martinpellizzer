Valeriana alpina var. exaltata

Valeriana alpina var. exaltata, commonly known as the alpine valerian, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pink or white in color.

Valeriana alpina var. exaltata is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
