Viscaria sigeri

Viscaria sigeri, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in the early morning. Viscaria sigeri thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
