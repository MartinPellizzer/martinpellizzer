Veronica fruticans var. diversifolia

Veronica fruticans var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variable speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, blue or violet flowers in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with some variation in shape among individuals, contributing to its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
