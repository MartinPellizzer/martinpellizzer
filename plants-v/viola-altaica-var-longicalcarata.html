Viola altaica var. longicalcarata

Viola altaica var. longicalcarata, commonly known as the long-beaked viola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive long, narrow beak-like structure at the base. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a spurred shape.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
