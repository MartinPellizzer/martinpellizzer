Verbena officinalis f. roseiflora

Verbena officinalis f. roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered vervain, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and historical use in herbal medicine.
