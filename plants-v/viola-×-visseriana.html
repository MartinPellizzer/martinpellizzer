Viola × visseriana

Viola × visseriana, commonly known as the Dutch viola, is a hybrid species belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm and prefers cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a bedding plant.

Viola × visseriana is known for its adaptability and ability to thrive in partial shade.
