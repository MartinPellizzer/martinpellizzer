Vicia menziesii

Vicia menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' vetch, is a perennial legume native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its pods are slender and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vicia menziesii thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.
