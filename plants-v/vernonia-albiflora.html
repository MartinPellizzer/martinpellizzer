Vernonia albiflora

Vernonia albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Vernonia albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
