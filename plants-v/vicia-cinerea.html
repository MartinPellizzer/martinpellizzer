Vicia cinerea

Vicia cinerea, commonly known as hoary vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its pale green, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Vicia cinerea is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environments, including roadsides and meadows.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
