Vitex compressa f. angustifolia

Vitex compressa f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf chaste tree, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
