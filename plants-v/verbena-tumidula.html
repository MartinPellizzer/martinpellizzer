Verbena tumidula

Verbena tumidula, commonly known as the woolly verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its clusters of small, showy blue or violet flowers. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Verbena tumidula is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
