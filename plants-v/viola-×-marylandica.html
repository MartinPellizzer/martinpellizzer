Viola × marylandica

Viola × marylandica, commonly known as the Maryland violet, is a hybrid species native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 10–20 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
