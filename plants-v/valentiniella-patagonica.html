Valentiniella patagonica

Valentiniella patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian valerian, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the harsh climate of Patagonia. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces tiny, white or pale pink blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
