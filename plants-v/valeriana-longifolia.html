Valeriana longifolia

Valeriana longifolia, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lavender-colored flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has aromatic leaves and stems, which contain compounds responsible for its distinctive scent. Valeriana longifolia is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its historical use in herbal medicine.
