Veronica daghestanica

Veronica daghestanica, commonly known as the Daghestan speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region, particularly Georgia and Russia.

It belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
