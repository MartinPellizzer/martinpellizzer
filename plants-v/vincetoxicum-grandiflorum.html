Vincetoxicum grandiflorum

Vincetoxicum grandiflorum, commonly known as the greatflower false bindweed, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its rapid growth and invasive tendencies make it a challenging weed in certain ecosystems.
