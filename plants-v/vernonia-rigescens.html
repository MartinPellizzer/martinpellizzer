Vernonia rigescens

Vernonia rigescens, commonly known as the rigid ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Vernonia rigescens is often found in open woodlands and prairies, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
