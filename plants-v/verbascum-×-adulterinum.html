Verbascum × adulterinum

Verbascum × adulterinum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant that belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in its natural habitat.
