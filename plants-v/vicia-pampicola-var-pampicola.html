Vicia pampicola var. pampicola

Vicia pampicola var. pampicola, commonly known as the Pampas vetch, is a perennial legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets and has small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
