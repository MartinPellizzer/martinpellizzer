Viola kunawarensis var. angustifolia

Viola kunawarensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are narrower than those of the typical species. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
