Vitis mannii

Vitis mannii, commonly known as the African grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to sub-Saharan Africa and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend trees and shrubs. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

The fruit, a small, round berry, is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
