Viola macroceras subsp. purpurea

Viola macroceras subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple hound's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features heart-shaped, violet-blue leaves with a distinctive purple underside, and its flowers are spurred and typically purple or violet in color.

The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its robust root system and is often found in calcareous soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
