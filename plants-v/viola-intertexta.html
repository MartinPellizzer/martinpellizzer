Viola intertexta

Viola intertexta, commonly known as the common violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas, meadows, and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola intertexta is also known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
