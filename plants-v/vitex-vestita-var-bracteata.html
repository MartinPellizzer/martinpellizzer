Vitex vestita var. bracteata

Vitex vestita var. bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf vitex, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a dry capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
