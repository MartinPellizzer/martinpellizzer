Verbascum collinsii

Verbascum collinsii, commonly known as Collins' mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Collins' mullein is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
