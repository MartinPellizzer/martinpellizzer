Vignea wallichiana

Vignea wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan climbing bean, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Vignea wallichiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential agricultural use.
