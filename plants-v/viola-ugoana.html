Viola ugoana

Viola ugoana, commonly known as the Ugandan violet, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola ugoana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
