Viola hirsutula

Viola hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are violet-blue in color, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat.

Viola hirsutula is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
