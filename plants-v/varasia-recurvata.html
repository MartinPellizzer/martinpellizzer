Varasia recurvata

Varasia recurvata, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream sides. The plant features thin, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

Varasia recurvata is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its low maintenance requirements.
