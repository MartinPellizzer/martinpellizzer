Viola mirabilis lusus caulescens

Viola mirabilis lusus caulescens, commonly known as the showy violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, purple-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and moist meadows. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and early blooming habit.
