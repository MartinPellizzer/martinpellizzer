Verticordia chrysostachys var. chrysostachys

Verticordia chrysostachys var. chrysostachys, commonly known as the golden club, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.
