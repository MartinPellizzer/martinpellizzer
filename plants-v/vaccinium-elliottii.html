Vaccinium elliottii

Vaccinium elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture and are often covered in fine hairs.
