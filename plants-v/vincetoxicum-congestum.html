Vincetoxicum congestum

Vincetoxicum congestum, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in rocky and arid environments.

Vincetoxicum congestum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
