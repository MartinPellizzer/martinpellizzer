Viola punicea

Viola punicea, commonly known as the scarlet violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Viola punicea has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom in early spring.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
