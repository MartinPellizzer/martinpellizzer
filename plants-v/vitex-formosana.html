Vitex formosana

Vitex formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces fragrant, blue or purple flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its fruit is a dry, two-valved capsule that releases small, hairy seeds.

Vitex formosana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
