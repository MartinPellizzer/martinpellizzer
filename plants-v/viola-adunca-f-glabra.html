Viola adunca f. glabra

Viola adunca f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth viol, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 10-30 cm, featuring heart-shaped, glabrous (smooth) leaves that are often slightly hairy beneath. The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, usually in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in woodland or shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
