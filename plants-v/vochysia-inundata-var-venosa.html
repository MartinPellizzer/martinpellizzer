Vochysia inundata var. venosa

Vochysia inundata var. venosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf vochysia, is a tropical tree species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
