Vitis dunniana

Vitis dunniana, commonly known as the Chinese fox grape, is a woody vine native to China.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of edible, seedless berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Vitaceae family.
