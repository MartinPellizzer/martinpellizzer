Vernonia pteropoda

Vernonia pteropoda, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Vernonia pteropoda is often found in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various pollinators.
