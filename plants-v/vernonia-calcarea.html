Vernonia calcarea

Vernonia calcarea, commonly known as limestone ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
