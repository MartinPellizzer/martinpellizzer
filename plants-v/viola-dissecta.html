Viola dissecta

Viola dissecta, commonly known as the dissected violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a velvety texture. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals that are often marked with darker veins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
