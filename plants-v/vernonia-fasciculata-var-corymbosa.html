Vernonia fasciculata var. corymbosa

Vernonia fasciculata var. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.
