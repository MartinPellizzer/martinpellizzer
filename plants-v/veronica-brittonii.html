Veronica brittonii

Veronica brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
