Veronica austriaca

Veronica austriaca, commonly known as Austrian speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Veronica austriaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
