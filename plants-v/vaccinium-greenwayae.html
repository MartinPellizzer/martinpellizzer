Vaccinium greenwayae

Vaccinium greenwayae, commonly known as the Greenway huckleberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swamps.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
