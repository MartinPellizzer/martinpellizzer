Vernonia fasciculata

Vernonia fasciculata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and can reach heights of up to 6 feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery or grayish underside, adding to its ornamental value.

Vernonia fasciculata is valued in gardens for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
