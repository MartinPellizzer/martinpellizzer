Vaccinium corymbiferum

Vaccinium corymbiferum, commonly known as the lowbush blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Vaccinium corymbiferum plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is a valuable component of native plant ecosystems.
