Verbena guaranitica

Verbena guaranitica, commonly known as the Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Verbena guaranitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
