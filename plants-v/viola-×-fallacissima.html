Viola × fallacissima

Viola × fallacissima, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with heart-shaped leaves and showy, often bi-colored flowers that range from purple to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Viola × fallacissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
