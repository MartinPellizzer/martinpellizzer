Viola × wiesbaurii

Viola × wiesbaurii, commonly known as the violet hybrid, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing shades of violet, purple, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and blooms.

Viola × wiesbaurii is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread through rhizomes, creating dense ground cover in suitable conditions.
