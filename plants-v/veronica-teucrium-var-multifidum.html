Veronica teucrium var. multifidum

Veronica teucrium var. multifidum, commonly known as the multifid blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, sky-blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often deeply divided, giving the plant a delicate, feathery appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.
