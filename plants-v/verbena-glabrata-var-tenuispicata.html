Verbena glabrata var. tenuispicata

Verbena glabrata var. tenuispicata, commonly known as narrow-spurred verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green hue. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for drought-tolerant landscaping.
