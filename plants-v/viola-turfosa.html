Viola turfosa

Viola turfosa, commonly known as the meadow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, usually in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in early spring. Viola turfosa is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and is often found in woodland understories.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
