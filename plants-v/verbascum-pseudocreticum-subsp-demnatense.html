Verbascum pseudocreticum subsp. demnatense

Verbascum pseudocreticum subsp.

demnatense, commonly known as the Demnat's mullein, is a perennial herb native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are broad and woolly, adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

It is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes other members known for their medicinal properties.
