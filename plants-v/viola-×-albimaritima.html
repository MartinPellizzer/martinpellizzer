Viola × albimaritima

Viola × albimaritima, commonly known as the coastal viola, is a hybrid species native to the coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with heart-shaped, hairy leaves and violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize dune environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many areas.
