Vulneraria rustica

Vulneraria rustica, commonly known as the rough vulnerary, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its scientific name reflects its historical use in treating wounds and skin ailments.
