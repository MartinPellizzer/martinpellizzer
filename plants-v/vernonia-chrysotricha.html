Vernonia chrysotricha

Vernonia chrysotricha, commonly known as golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is known for its bright yellow flowers, which are surrounded by silky, white bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils.

Vernonia chrysotricha is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
