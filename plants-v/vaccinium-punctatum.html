Vaccinium punctatum

Vaccinium punctatum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, with a tart flavor. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are evergreen, providing year-round interest.

Vaccinium punctatum plays an important ecological role by supporting local wildlife and contributing to coastal biodiversity.
