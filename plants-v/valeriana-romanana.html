Valeriana romanana

Valeriana romanana, commonly known as the Roman valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often purple or pink flowers. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots are rich in essential oils and have been traditionally used for their calming properties.

Valeriana romanana thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
