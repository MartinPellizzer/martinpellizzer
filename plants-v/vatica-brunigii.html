Vatica brunigii

Vatica brunigii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Vatica brunigii is often found in lowland and hill forests, playing a significant role in the ecological structure of its native habitats.
