Vittadinia cervicularis

Vittadinia cervicularis, commonly known as the candle bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, candle-like yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a sprawling, shrub-like habit. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Vittadinia cervicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
