Vaccinium kansaiense var. pubescens

Vaccinium kansaiense var. pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese huckleberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is distinguished by its hairy foliage, which gives it its scientific name "pubescens.".
