Vernonia tricephala

Vernonia tricephala, commonly known as the three-headed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flower heads consist of three distinct clusters of bracts.

Vernonia tricephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
