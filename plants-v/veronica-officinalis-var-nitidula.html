Veronica officinalis var. nitidula

Veronica officinalis var. nitidula, commonly known as the glossy speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, glossy coating. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
