Veronica consolatae

Veronica consolatae, commonly known as the Consolation Speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.
