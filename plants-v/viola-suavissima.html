Viola suavissima

Viola suavissima, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola suavissima is also valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
