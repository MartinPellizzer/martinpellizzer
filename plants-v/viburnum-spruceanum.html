Viburnum spruceanum

Viburnum spruceanum, commonly known as Spruceleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, persistent berries. Viburnum spruceanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a versatile and visually appealing addition to native plant gardens.
