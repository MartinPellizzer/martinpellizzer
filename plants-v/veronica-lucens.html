Veronica lucens

Veronica lucens, commonly known as the shining speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Veronica lucens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
