Vernonia niederleinii

Vernonia niederleinii, commonly known as the Niederlein's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The plant features tall, slender stems covered with fine hairs and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The botanical characteristics of Vernonia niederleinii make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
