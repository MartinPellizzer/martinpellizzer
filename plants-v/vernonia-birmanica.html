Vernonia birmanica

Vernonia birmanica, commonly known as Burmese ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically arranged in clusters. Vernonia birmanica is often found in open woodland areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
