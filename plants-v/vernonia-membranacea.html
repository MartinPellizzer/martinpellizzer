Vernonia membranacea

Vernonia membranacea, commonly known as the paperflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its foliage is typically gray-green, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Vernonia membranacea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
