Verbena calliantha

Verbena calliantha, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Verbena calliantha is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
