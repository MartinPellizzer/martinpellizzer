Viola kunawarensis

Viola kunawarensis, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing shades of purple and white. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded gardens.

Viola kunawarensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for garden use.
