Vaccinium lancifolium

Vaccinium lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like heathers and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal or temperate forest ecosystems.

Vaccinium lancifolium plays an important role in local wildlife habitats and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
