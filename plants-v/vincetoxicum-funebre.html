Vincetoxicum funebre

Vincetoxicum funebre, commonly known as black bamboo, is a striking perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and leaves that range from green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it requires careful management to prevent ecological disruption.
