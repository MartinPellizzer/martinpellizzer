Ventilago tinctoria

Ventilago tinctoria, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is highly valued for its rich reddish color and durability, making it a significant resource in traditional craftsmanship.

The plant also has medicinal uses in some African cultures, where various parts of the tree are used in herbal remedies.
