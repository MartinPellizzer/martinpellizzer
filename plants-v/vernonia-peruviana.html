Vernonia peruviana

Vernonia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Vernonia peruviana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
