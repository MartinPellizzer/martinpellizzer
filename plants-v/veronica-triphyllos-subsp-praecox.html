Veronica triphyllos subsp. praecox

Veronica triphyllos subsp.

praecox, commonly known as early common speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a useful indicator species in ecological studies.
