Vaccinium salicinum

Vaccinium salicinum, commonly known as the swamp blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to wetland areas of North America.

It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in jams and pies. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that remain green throughout the year. It thrives in acidic, sandy soils and requires consistent moisture.

Vaccinium salicinum plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and small mammals.
