Viola tokubuchiana f. austroyezoensis

Viola tokubuchiana f. austroyezoensis, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a rare and unique variant of the viola genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. This fern-like plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forested areas with rich, well-drained soil. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with specific humidity and light conditions.
