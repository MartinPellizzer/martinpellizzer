Veronicastrum sibiricum f. candidum

Veronicastrum sibiricum f. candidum, commonly known as the White Meadow Gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This ornamental plant is valued for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
