Viola acuminata subsp. grandistipulata

Viola acuminata subsp.

grandistipulata, commonly known as the large-stippled violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and violet flowers that range from purple to blue. It has distinctive, elongated stipules that are often larger than the leaves themselves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
