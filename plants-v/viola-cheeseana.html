Viola cheeseana

Viola cheeseana, commonly known as the cheeseplant violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and Florida. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the back.

Viola cheeseana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
