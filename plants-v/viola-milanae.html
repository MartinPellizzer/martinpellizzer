Viola milanae

Viola milanae, commonly known as the Milan violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe, including parts of Italy and the Balkans. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Viola milanae thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
