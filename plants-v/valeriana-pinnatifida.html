Valeriana pinnatifida

Valeriana pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved valerian, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana pinnatifida is known for its distinctive pinnately divided leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
