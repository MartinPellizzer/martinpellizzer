Verbascum leiocladum

Verbascum leiocladum, commonly known as the silverleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum leiocladum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
