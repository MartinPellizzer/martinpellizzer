Visnaga daucoides

Visnaga daucoides, commonly known as the greater celandine, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and rough, hairy leaves. The plant has a taproot that is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny habitats.

Visnaga daucoides is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain areas.
