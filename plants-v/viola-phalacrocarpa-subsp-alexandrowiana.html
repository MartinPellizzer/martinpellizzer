Viola phalacrocarpa subsp. alexandrowiana

Viola phalacrocarpa subsp.

alexandrowiana, commonly known as the Alexandrian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
