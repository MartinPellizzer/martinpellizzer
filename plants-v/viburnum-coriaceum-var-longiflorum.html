Viburnum coriaceum var. longiflorum

Viburnum coriaceum var. longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered leatherleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features dense, elliptical leaves that are thick and leathery, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in late spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and supporting local biodiversity.
