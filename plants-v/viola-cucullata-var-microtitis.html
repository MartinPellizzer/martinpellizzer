Viola cucullata var. microtitis

Viola cucullata var. microtitis, commonly known as the dwarf pansy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive hooded petal structure. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
