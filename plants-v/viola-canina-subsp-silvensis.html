Viola canina subsp. silvensis

Viola canina subsp.

silvensis, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or violet. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
