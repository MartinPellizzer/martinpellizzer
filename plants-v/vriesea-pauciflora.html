Vriesea pauciflora

Vriesea pauciflora, commonly known as the silver vriesea, is a species of bromeliad native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or white powdery coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, sword-shaped inflorescences that can reach several feet in height, bearing small, colorful flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea pauciflora is a popular ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
