Vicia douglassii

Vicia douglassii, commonly known as Douglas's vetch, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Vicia douglassii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
