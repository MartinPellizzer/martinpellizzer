Vicia lecomtei var. embergeri

Vicia lecomtei var. embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds, and its trifoliate leaves are typically hairy and have a distinct, serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
