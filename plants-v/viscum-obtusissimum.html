Viscum obtusissimum

Viscum obtusissimum, commonly known as the bluntleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy, with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are followed by berries containing sticky seeds. Its parasitic nature allows it to derive moisture and nutrients from its host, while still performing some photosynthesis.

Viscum obtusissimum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other animals that consume its berries.
