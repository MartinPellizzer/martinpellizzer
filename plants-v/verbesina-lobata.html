Verbesina lobata

Verbesina lobata, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lobed leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters and are followed by small, prickly fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Verbesina lobata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and open woods.
