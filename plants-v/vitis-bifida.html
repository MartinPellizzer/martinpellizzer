Vitis bifida

Vitis bifida, commonly known as the split grape or two-lobed grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America, particularly found in the southeastern United States. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves that are divided into two lobes, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of berries.

The berries are typically edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making it a valuable plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
