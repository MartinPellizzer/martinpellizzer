Vellozia stipitata

Vellozia stipitata, commonly known as the stalked vellozia, is a unique member of the family Velloziaceae, native to South America.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and distinctive, branched stems. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. Vellozia stipitata produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in desert botany and adaptation strategies.
