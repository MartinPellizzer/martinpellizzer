Viola beraudii var. pubescens

Viola beraudii var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces delicate purple or violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and brief flowering period.
