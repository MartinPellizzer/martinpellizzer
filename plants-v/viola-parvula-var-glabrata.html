Viola parvula var. glabrata

Viola parvula var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf viola, is a small perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, where it thrives in rich, loamy soils. The plant features heart-shaped, glabrous (smooth) leaves and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with five petals. Its blooming period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other woodland wildflowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
