Vicia disperma var. subuniflora

Vicia disperma var. subuniflora, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-yellow, arranged in loose racemes. This variety is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
