Viola grypoceras f. variegata

Viola grypoceras f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow markings on its leaves. The plant produces small, typically purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders in horticulture.
