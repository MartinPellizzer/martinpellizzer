Vitex alata

Vitex alata, commonly known as the chaste tree or velvetleaf, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. These flowers are followed by small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Vitex alata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
