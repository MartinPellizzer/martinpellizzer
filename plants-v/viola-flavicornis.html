Viola flavicornis

Viola flavicornis, commonly known as the yellow-horned violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
