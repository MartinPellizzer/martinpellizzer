Viburnum wrightii f. kaiense

Viburnum wrightii f. kaiense, commonly known as the Kaienese arrowwood, is a compact shrub native to the southern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its seasonal color changes and attractive foliage.
