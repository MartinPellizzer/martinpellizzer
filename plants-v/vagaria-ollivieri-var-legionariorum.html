Vagaria ollivieri var. legionariorum

Vagaria ollivieri var. legionariorum, commonly known as the Roman soldier's plant, is a rare and ornamental species native to southern Europe.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and glossy, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its rarity and specific cultivation requirements, it is often grown in botanical gardens and by specialty horticulturists.
