Vernonia apocynifolia

Vernonia apocynifolia, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, rough bark and its dense, fibrous wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia apocynifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
