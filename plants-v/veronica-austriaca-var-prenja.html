Veronica austriaca var. prenja

Veronica austriaca var. prenja, commonly known as the Austrian speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit with spreading stems. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in garden landscaping for its hardiness and visual interest.
