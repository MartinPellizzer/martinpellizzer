Verbascum phlomoides subsp. thapsiforme

Verbascum phlomoides subsp.

thapsiforme, commonly known as the thapsiforme mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in elongated clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is closely related to other mullein species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
