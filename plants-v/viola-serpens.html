Viola serpens

Viola serpens, commonly known as the snake's head violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

Viola serpens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
