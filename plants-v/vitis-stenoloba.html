Vitis stenoloba

Vitis stenoloba, commonly known as the Chinese hardy grape, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five lobes and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Vitis stenoloba is also noted for its hardiness, making it suitable for colder climates.
