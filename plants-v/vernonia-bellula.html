Vernonia bellula

Vernonia bellula, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Vernonia bellula is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
