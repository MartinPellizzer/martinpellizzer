Viguiera parkinsonii f. flaviflora

Viguiera parkinsonii f. flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central cluster of tubular disc florets, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

Viguiera parkinsonii f. flaviflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
