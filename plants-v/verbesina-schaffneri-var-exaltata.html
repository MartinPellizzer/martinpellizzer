Verbesina schaffneri var. exaltata

Verbesina schaffneri var. exaltata, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its showy inflorescences make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
