Volutaria lippii var. ramosissima

Volutaria lippii var. ramosissima, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glabrous, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by oval-shaped capsules containing seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative means make it an invasive species in some regions.
