Viburnum hoanglienense

Viburnum hoanglienense, commonly known as the Hoang Lien viburnum, is a species native to Vietnam.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with cool, humid climates. The plant features opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, blooming in the spring.

The fruit is a dark blue to black berry that ripens in the fall and is often consumed by birds and other wildlife.
