Vatica congesta

Vatica congesta, commonly known as the dense vatica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its straight, cylindrical trunk and dense foliage. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and are borne in clusters.

Vatica congesta plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
