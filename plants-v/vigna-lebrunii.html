Vigna lebrunii

Vigna lebrunii, commonly known as the wild cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Vigna lebrunii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in agricultural systems.
