Valeriana papilla var. andicola

Valeriana papilla var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their calming and sedative properties.

Valeriana papilla var. andicola is valued both for its ecological role and its cultural significance in local communities.
