Viola granulosa

Viola granulosa, commonly known as the granular violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves with a distinctive granular texture. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola granulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
