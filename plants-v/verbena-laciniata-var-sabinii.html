Verbena laciniata var. sabinii

Verbena laciniata var. sabinii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, vibrant blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its compact growth habit and long blooming season from summer to autumn add ornamental value to gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
