Vanilla cristatocallosa

Vanilla cristatocallosa, commonly known as the Mexican vanilla orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a climbing vine that produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark brown pods. The plant is characterized by its unique, waxy leaves and intricate floral structure. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Despite its ornamental value, it is not widely cultivated for vanilla extract due to its low yield and specific growing requirements.
