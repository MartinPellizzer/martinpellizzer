Valeriana brunoniana

Valeriana brunoniana, commonly known as Brunon's valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana brunoniana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.
