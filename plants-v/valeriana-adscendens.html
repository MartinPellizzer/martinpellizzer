Valeriana adscendens

Valeriana adscendens, commonly known as the ascending valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana adscendens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
