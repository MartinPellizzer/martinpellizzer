Viola gracilis var. munbyana

Viola gracilis var. munbyana, commonly known as the slender violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, usually purple or violet, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
