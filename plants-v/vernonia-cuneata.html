Vernonia cuneata

Vernonia cuneata, commonly known as ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, conical clusters of yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Vernonia cuneata is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
