Vernonia bakeri

Vernonia bakeri, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow to reddish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as forest edges and coastal areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia bakeri is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
