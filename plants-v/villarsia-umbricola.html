Villarsia umbricola

Villarsia umbricola, commonly known as the shadow vine, is a climbing plant native to the humid forests of tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
