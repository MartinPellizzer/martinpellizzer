Viola alisoviana

Viola alisoviana, commonly known as the alisovian violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola alisoviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
