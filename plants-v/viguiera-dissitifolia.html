Viguiera dissitifolia

Viguiera dissitifolia, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. Its showy yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viguiera dissitifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
