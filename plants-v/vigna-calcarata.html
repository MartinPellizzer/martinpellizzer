Vigna calcarata

Vigna calcarata, commonly known as the rock bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Vigna calcarata is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils, making it resilient in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it both a useful forage crop and a subject of botanical study.
