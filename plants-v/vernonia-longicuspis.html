Vernonia longicuspis

Vernonia longicuspis, commonly known as the long-cusped ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its upright stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Vernonia longicuspis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
