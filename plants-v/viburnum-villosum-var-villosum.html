Viburnum villosum var. villosum

Viburnum villosum var. villosum, commonly known as the silky viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Viburnum villosum var. villosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
