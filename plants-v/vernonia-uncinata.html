Vernonia uncinata

Vernonia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked vervain, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Vernonia uncinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
