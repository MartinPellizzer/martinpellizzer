Vernonia usafuensis

Vernonia usafuensis, commonly known as the Usafuensis Ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia usafuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
