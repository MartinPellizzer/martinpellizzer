Vepris reflexa

Vepris reflexa, commonly known as the roughleaf stinkwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
