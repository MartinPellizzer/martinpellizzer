Vepris madagascarica

Vepris madagascarica, commonly known as the Madagascar cinnamon tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-yellow fruits that contain a single seed, and its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
