Verbascum × erraticum

Verbascum × erraticum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their large, showy leaves and flowers. The hybrid is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
