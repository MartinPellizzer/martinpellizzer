Veronica palmatiloba

Veronica palmatiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
