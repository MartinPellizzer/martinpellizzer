Veronica americana var. crassula

Veronica americana var. crassula, commonly known as the thick-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has thick, succulent-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
