Viola aethnensis subsp. calabra

Viola aethnensis subsp.

calabra, commonly known as the Calabrian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and broader leaves compared to other viola species.
