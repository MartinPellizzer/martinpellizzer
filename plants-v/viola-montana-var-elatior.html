Viola montana var. elatior

Viola montana var. elatior, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors and hardy nature.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
