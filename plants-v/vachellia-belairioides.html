Vachellia belairioides

Vachellia belairioides, commonly known as the Belair acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are compound and feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

Vachellia belairioides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
