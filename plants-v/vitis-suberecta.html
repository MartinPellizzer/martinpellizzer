Vitis suberecta

Vitis suberecta, commonly known as the creeping grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and red to purple berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Vitis suberecta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
