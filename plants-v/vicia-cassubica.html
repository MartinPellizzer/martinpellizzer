Vicia cassubica

Vicia cassubica, commonly known as the Cassubian vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. Vicia cassubica has small, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges.
