Veronica cordata var. minor

Veronica cordata var. minor, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, upright leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders.
