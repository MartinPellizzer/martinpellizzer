Vaccinium chamissonis

Vaccinium chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many species of heather and blueberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
