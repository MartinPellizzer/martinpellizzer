Viola sylvestris

Viola sylvestris, commonly known as the common violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along paths, thriving in partial shade. Viola sylvestris is known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating respiratory ailments.

Its delicate appearance and early spring blooming make it a notable wildflower in temperate regions.
