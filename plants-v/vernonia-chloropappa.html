Vernonia chloropappa

Vernonia chloropappa, commonly known as the greenhead ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct yellowish or greenish hue, giving it its common name.

Vernonia chloropappa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
