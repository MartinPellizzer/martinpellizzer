Viola suavis subsp. barceloi

Viola suavis subsp.

barceloi, commonly known as the sweet violet of Barcelona, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to the typical sweet violet.
