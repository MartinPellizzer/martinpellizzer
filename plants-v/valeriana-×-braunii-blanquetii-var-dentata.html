Valeriana × braunii-blanquetii var. dentata

Valeriana × braunii-blanquetii var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated valerian, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

The serrated leaf margins and compact growth habit make it a distinctive species within the valerian genus.
