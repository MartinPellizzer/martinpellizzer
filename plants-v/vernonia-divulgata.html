Vernonia divulgata

Vernonia divulgata, commonly known as the spreading ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along creek banks. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple-tipped central disk.

Vernonia divulgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
