Vaccinium ligustrinum

Vaccinium ligustrinum, commonly known as the common blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by blue-black berries in the summer. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shady woodlands.
