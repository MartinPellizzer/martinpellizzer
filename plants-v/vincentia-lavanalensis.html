Vincentia lavanalensis

Vincentia lavanalensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spider flower, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and compound leaves.

Native to Madagascar, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. It produces striking, spider-like blooms that are typically violet or purple in color, attracting various pollinators. The plant's leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, Vincentia lavanalensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
