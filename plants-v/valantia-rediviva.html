Valantia rediviva

Valantia rediviva, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a unique species of moss that exhibits remarkable desiccation tolerance.

It can survive extreme dehydration by entering a state of cryptobiosis, where its metabolic processes nearly cease. When rehydrated, the plant rapidly regains its turgid structure and vitality, appearing to "come back to life.". Botanically, it belongs to the family Pottiaceae and is characterized by its small, acicular leaves and ability to thrive in arid environments.

This plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and moisture retention.
