Vernonia squarrosa var. angustifolia

Vernonia squarrosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-gray underside. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy perennial suitable for native gardens.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
