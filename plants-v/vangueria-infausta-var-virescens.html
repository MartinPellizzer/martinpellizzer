Vangueria infausta var. virescens

Vangueria infausta var. virescens, commonly known as the green vangueria, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
