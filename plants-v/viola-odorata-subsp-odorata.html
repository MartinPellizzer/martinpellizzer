Viola odorata subsp. odorata

Viola odorata subsp.

odorata, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers that emit a sweet, fragrant aroma.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
