Viola tokubuchiana var. crassicornis

Viola tokubuchiana var. crassicornis, commonly known as the broad-horned violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture and distinctive violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often marked by a yellow or white spur.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
