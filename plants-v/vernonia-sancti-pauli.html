Vernonia sancti-pauli

Vernonia sancti-pauli, commonly known as the Saint Paul's Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Vernonia sancti-pauli is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
