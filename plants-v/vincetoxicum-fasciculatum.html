Vincetoxicum fasciculatum

Vincetoxicum fasciculatum, commonly known as false bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces cylindrical, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it is considered a potential invasive species in some regions.
