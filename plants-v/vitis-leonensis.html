Vitis leonensis

Vitis leonensis, commonly known as the Leonese grape or the Spanish grape, is a species of flowering vine native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces small, often clustered, greenish-white flowers. The plant's leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and typically have serrated edges. Vitis leonensis is adapted to Mediterranean climates and can thrive in rocky or dry soils.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional winemaking.
