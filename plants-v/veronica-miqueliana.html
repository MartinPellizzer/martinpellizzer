Veronica miqueliana

Veronica miqueliana, commonly known as the Miquel's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Veronica miqueliana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
