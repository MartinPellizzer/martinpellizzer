Vaccinium angustifolium var. nigrum

Vaccinium angustifolium var. nigrum, commonly known as black huckleberry, is a shrub native to North America, particularly found in the Pacific Northwest.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other acid-loving plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
