Viola gracilis var. pinifolia

Viola gracilis var. pinifolia, commonly known as the slender violet or pineleaf violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically appearing in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Violaceae family.
