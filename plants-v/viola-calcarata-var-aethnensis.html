Viola calcarata var. aethnensis

Viola calcarata var. aethnensis, commonly known as the Etna violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the volcanic slopes of Mount Etna in Sicily.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of violet-blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, high-altitude environment of its habitat. It is a member of the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.

The species is also noted for its resilience to cold temperatures and seasonal fluctuations.
