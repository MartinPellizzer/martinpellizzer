Viguiera sonorae

Viguiera sonorae, commonly known as desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Viguiera sonorae produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
