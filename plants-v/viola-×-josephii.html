Viola × josephii

Viola × josephii, commonly known as the Josephine violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from purple to white. Native to Europe, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. It typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds via the wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
