Vachellia nilotica subsp. nilotica

Vachellia nilotica subsp.

nilotica, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, followed by long, curved pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
