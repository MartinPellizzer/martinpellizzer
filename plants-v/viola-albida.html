Viola albida

Viola albida, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of blossoms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola albida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
