Viburnum molle f. leiophyllum

Viburnum molle f. leiophyllum, commonly known as the smoothleaf viburnum, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and glossy, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
