Vernonia albocinerascens

Vernonia albocinerascens, commonly known as the white-haired ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its grayish-white, woolly foliage and clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. It typically grows in open woodland or grassland habitats, often in well-drained soils.

Vernonia albocinerascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
