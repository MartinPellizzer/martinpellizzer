Vigna sinensis var. spontanea

Vigna sinensis var. spontanea, commonly known as the wild cowpea, is a species of legume native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a flowering period that usually occurs during the rainy season.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
