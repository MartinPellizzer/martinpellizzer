Viola tricolor subsp. erecta

Viola tricolor subsp.

erecta, commonly known as the upright violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive three-lobed structure and produces clusters of violet, yellow, and white flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.

The subspecies is distinguished by its upright growth habit compared to the more spreading form of V. tricolor.
