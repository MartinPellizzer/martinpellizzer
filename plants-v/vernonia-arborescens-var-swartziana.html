Vernonia arborescens var. swartziana

Vernonia arborescens var. swartziana, commonly known as the Swartz's ironweed, is a shrub-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fluffy yellow to brown flowers in compact, terminal clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
