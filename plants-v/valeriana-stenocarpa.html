Valeriana stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. Valeriana stenocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
