Valeriana belonantha

Valeriana belonantha, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its rhizomatous root system is notable for containing compounds used in traditional medicine.
