Viola canina var. pygmaea

Viola canina var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf dog violet, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with heart-shaped, hairy leaves and delicate violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in association with other woodland flora.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
