Vitellaria grandiflora

Vitellaria grandiflora, commonly known as shea tree, is a tropical tree native to Africa, particularly found in the Sahel region.

It is a member of the Sapotaceae family and is valued for its fruit, which contains a nutritious seed used to produce shea butter. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The fruit is a smooth, yellowish drupe that matures over several months.
