Verbesina clarkiae

Verbesina clarkiae, commonly known as Clark's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, erect growth habit. The plant features showy yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Verbesina clarkiae is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
