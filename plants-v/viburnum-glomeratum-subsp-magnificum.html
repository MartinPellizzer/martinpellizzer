Viburnum glomeratum subsp. magnificum

Viburnum glomeratum subsp.

magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent snowball viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its dense clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Viburnum glomeratum subsp.

magnificum is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
