Veratrum bosniacum

Veratrum bosniacum, commonly known as the Bosnian false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers during the summer months. It contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
