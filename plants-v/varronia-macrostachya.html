Varronia macrostachya

Varronia macrostachya, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Varronia macrostachya is valued for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
