Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. neilreichii

Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. neilreichii, commonly known as the Neilreich's water speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often near water bodies such as streams and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
