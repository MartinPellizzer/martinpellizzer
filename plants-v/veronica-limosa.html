Veronica limosa

Veronica limosa, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged oppositely along the stem.

Veronica limosa is a perennial species that spreads by rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable environments.
