Viscum pedicellatum

Viscum pedicellatum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are adapted for attachment to the host. The plant produces small, white or yellowish berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. Viscum pedicellatum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often associated with Christmas traditions due to its cultural significance.

Its unique growth habit and ecological role make it an interesting subject in botany.
