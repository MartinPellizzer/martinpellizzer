Valeriana tuberculata

Valeriana tuberculata, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its roots are aromatic and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their sedative properties.

Valeriana tuberculata thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.
