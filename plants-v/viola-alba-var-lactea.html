Viola alba var. lactea

Viola alba var. lactea, commonly known as the milk viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale yellow flowers with a distinctive white or cream coloration. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and botanical collections.
