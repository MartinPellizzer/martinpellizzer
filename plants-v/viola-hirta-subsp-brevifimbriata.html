Viola hirta subsp. brevifimbriata

Viola hirta subsp.

brevifimbriata, commonly known as the short-fringed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its subspecies is distinguished by its short, fringed petals and compact flower structure.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and genetic characteristics.
