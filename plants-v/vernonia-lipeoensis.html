Vernonia lipeoensis

Vernonia lipeoensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical or conical flower clusters. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to reddish-brown.

Vernonia lipeoensis is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
