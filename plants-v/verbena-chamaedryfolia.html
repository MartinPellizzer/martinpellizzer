Verbena chamaedryfolia

Verbena chamaedryfolia, commonly known as the rock verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular, blue or violet flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Verbena chamaedryfolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
