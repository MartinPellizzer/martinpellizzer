Veronica hederifolia subsp. insularis

Veronica hederifolia subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the island speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to certain island regions of the Mediterranean. It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, ovate leaves that are often slightly lobed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
