Viscum longiarticulatum

Viscum longiarticulatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It is native to parts of Asia and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of forest environments.

Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis.
