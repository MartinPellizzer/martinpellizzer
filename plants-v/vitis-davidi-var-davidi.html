Vitis davidi var. davidi

Vitis davidi var. davidi, commonly known as the David grapevine, is a deciduous woody vine native to China.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and distinctive palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, yellowish berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in horticulture for its adaptability to various soil types.

The species is also valued for its potential in hybrid grape breeding programs.
