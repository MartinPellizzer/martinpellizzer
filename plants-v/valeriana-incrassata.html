Valeriana incrassata

Valeriana incrassata, commonly known as the rough valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Valeriana incrassata is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.

It is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
