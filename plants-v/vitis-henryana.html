Vitis henryana

Vitis henryana, commonly known as Henry's grape, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, doubly serrated leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, round berries that turn from green to dark purple as they ripen. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Vitis henryana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in horticulture and ecological restoration.
