Vitex peduncularis

Vitex peduncularis, commonly known as the Indian vitex or champa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, dark blue or black berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions of India.
