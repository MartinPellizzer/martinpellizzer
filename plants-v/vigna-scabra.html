Vigna scabra

Vigna scabra, commonly known as rough sickle bean, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its small, yellow flowers give way to elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds.

Vigna scabra is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
