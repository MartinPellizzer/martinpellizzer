Vernonia arborea var. javanica

Vernonia arborea var. javanica, commonly known as the Java Vernonia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, appressed hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a rough texture.

The flowers are yellow, arranged in dense, terminal clusters, and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.
