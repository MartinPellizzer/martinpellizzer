Viburnum foetidum var. rectangulatum

Viburnum foetidum var. rectangulatum, commonly known as the rectangular-leaved sweet viburnum, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and seasonal color changes.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
