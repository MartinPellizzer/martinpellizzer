Vicia onobrychioides

Vicia onobrychioides, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in agricultural settings for soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Vicia onobrychioides is also valued in ecological restoration projects for its hardy nature and ability to colonize degraded areas.
