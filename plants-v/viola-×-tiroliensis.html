Viola × tiroliensis

Viola × tiroliensis, commonly known as the Viennese violet, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola wittrockiana.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Viola × tiroliensis is well-suited for partial shade and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
