Veronica arcuata

Veronica arcuata, commonly known as the bentwillow herb, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching stems that often grow along the ground. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica arcuata is often found in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
