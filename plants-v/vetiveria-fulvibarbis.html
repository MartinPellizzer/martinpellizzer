Vetiveria fulvibarbis

Vetiveria fulvibarbis, commonly known as the golden beard grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with golden hues. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for soil stabilization due to its extensive root network. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Vetiveria fulvibarbis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience to environmental stress.
