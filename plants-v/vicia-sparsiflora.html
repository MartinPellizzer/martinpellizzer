Vicia sparsiflora

Vicia sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or through animal activity. Vicia sparsiflora is often found in rocky or disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its growth habit and floral structure make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
