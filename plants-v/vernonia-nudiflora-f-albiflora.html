Vernonia nudiflora f. albiflora

Vernonia nudiflora f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
