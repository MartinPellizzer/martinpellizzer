Viola segobricensis

Viola segobricensis, commonly known as the Segobric violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.

Viola segobricensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
