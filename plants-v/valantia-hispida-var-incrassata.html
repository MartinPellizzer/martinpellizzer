Valantia hispida var. incrassata

Valantia hispida var. incrassata, commonly known as the rough-hairy valantia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Valantia hispida var. incrassata is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
