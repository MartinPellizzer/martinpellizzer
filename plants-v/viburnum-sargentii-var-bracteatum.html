Viburnum sargentii var. bracteatum

Viburnum sargentii var. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteated sargent viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces red to black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and it can grow up to 6 feet tall.

Viburnum sargentii var. bracteatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
