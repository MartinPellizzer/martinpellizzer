Veronica kurdica var. longiracemosa

Veronica kurdica var. longiracemosa, commonly known as the long raceme speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Turkey and the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged opposite along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is distinguished by its elongated inflorescences, which give it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
