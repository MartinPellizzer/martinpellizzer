Viola × greenei

Viola × greenei, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is native to the western United States, particularly in California. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
