Vitex thomasii

Vitex thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's vitex, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Vitex thomasii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
