Viola × pacheri

Viola × pacheri, commonly known as the violet carnation, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola wittrockii.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of violet, purple, and white. The plant typically has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Viola × pacheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
