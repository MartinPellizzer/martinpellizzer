Verbesina aspilioides

Verbesina aspilioides, commonly known as the Mexican beggarticks, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
