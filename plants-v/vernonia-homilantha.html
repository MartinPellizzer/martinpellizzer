Vernonia homilantha

Vernonia homilantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is known for its long blooming period, often extending from late summer into autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
