Verbesina nicaraguensis

Verbesina nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, rough, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Verbesina nicaraguensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
