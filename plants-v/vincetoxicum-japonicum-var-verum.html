Vincetoxicum japonicum var. verum

Vincetoxicum japonicum var. verum, commonly known as Japanese vincetoxicum, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves that are typically trifoliate, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are dark blue to black when mature and are often consumed by birds.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
