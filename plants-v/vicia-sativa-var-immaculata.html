Vicia sativa var. immaculata

Vicia sativa var. immaculata, commonly known as the white vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
