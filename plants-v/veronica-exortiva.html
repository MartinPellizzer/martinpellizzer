Veronica exortiva

Veronica exortiva, commonly known as the upright speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica exortiva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
