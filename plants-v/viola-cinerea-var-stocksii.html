Viola cinerea var. stocksii

Viola cinerea var. stocksii, commonly known as the gray violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a grayish-green upper surface and a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are violet-blue in color, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
