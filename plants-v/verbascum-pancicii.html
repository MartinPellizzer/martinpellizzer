Verbascum pancicii

Verbascum pancicii, commonly known as Pancici's mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes over 300 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a spike-like cluster, and bloom in late summer.

Pancici's mullein is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
