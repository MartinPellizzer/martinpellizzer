Verbascum atlanticum

Verbascum atlanticum, commonly known as common mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that form a rosette in the first year. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the second year, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in dry, open areas.

Verbascum atlanticum is also known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
