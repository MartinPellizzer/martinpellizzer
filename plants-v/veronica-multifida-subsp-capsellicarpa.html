Veronica multifida subsp. capsellicarpa

Veronica multifida subsp.

capsellicarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
