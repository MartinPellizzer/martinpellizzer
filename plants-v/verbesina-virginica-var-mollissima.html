Verbesina virginica var. mollissima

Verbesina virginica var. mollissima, commonly known as velvetleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with prominent orange centers, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
