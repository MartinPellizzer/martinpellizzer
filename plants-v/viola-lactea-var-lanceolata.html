Viola lactea var. lanceolata

Viola lactea var. lanceolata, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers with a distinctive violet-tinged throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
