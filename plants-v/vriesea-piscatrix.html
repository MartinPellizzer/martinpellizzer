Vriesea piscatrix

Vriesea piscatrix, commonly known as the "Piscatorial Vriesea," is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that can range from red to orange, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silver-green hairs that help reduce water loss in its humid, tropical habitat.

Vriesea piscatrix is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
