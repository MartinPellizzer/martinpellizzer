Viola peduncularis

Viola peduncularis, commonly known as the common pansy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The flowers typically come in shades of purple, violet, and white, with a distinctive yellow or orange marking on the lower petal. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant is also known for its ability to self-seed and spread readily in suitable conditions.
