Viola aethnensis subsp. aethnensis

Viola aethnensis subsp.

aethnensis, commonly known as the Etna violets, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic slopes of Mount Etna in Sicily. It is a perennial herb with heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with five petals.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, high-altitude environment of its habitat. It is part of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive blooms.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
