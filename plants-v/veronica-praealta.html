Veronica praealta

Veronica praealta, commonly known as the tall speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically reaches heights of up to 1.5 meters, making it a striking addition to gardens and natural landscapes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica praealta thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.
