Valeriana bracteata

Valeriana bracteata, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters and has a compact, bushy appearance. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Valeriana bracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with partial shade.
