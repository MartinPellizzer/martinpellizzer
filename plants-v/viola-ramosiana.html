Viola ramosiana

Viola ramosiana, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with five petals. Viola ramosiana produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
