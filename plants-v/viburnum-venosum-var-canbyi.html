Viburnum venosum var. canbyi

Viburnum venosum var. canbyi, commonly known as Canby's arrowwood, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a distinctive venation pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside habitats.

Viburnum venosum var. canbyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
