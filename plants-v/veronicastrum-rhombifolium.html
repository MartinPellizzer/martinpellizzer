Veronicastrum rhombifolium

Veronicastrum rhombifolium, commonly known as blue mist spirea, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Veronicastrum rhombifolium is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
