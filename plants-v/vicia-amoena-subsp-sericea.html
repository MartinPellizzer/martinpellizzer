Vicia amoena subsp. sericea

Vicia amoena subsp.

sericea, commonly known as the silky vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and trifoliate leaves with a silky, hairy texture.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
