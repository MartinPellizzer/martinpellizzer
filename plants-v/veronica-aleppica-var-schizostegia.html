Veronica aleppica var. schizostegia

Veronica aleppica var. schizostegia, commonly known as the Aleppo speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
