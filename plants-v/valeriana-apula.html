Valeriana apula

Valeriana apula, commonly known as the Apulian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish-brown roots. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in southern Italy, particularly in the Apulia region. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and bloom in late summer.

Valeriana apula is valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used for its calming and sedative effects.
