Viola adunca subsp. ashtonae

Viola adunca subsp.

ashtonae, commonly known as Ashton's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet or purple flowers with five petals. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in gardens and naturalized areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
