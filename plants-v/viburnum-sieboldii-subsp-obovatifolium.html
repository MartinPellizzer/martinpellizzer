Viburnum sieboldii subsp. obovatifolium

Viburnum sieboldii subsp.

obovatifolium, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It features opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
