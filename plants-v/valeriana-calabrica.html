Valeriana calabrica

Valeriana calabrica, commonly known as the Mediterranean valerian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Valeriana calabrica is known for its aromatic foliage and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
