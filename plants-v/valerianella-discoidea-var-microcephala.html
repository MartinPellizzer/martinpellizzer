Valerianella discoidea var. microcephala

Valerianella discoidea var. microcephala, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences, and the plant is known for its delicate appearance and ability to spread vegetatively.

Valerianella discoidea var. microcephala is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and easy cultivation.
