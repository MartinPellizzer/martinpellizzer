Viola tidestromii

Viola tidestromii, commonly known as the Tidestrom's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or blue. It is part of the Violaceae family, which includes other violets and related species.

Viola tidestromii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
