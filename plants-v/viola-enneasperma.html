Viola enneasperma

Viola enneasperma, commonly known as the nine-seeded violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing showy petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola enneasperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
