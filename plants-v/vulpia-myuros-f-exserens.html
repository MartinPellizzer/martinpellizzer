Vulpia myuros f. exserens

Vulpia myuros f. exserens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vulpia, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, two-lobed flowers. It is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
