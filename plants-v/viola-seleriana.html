Viola seleriana

Viola seleriana, commonly known as the common violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. Viola seleriana is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
