Vaccinium rubrum

Vaccinium rubrum, commonly known as the common blueberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces small, edible berries rich in antioxidants. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires cool, moist conditions for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically bell-shaped and white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The berries are harvested in the summer and are widely cultivated for their flavor and nutritional value.
