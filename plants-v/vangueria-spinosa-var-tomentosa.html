Vangueria spinosa var. tomentosa

Vangueria spinosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly vangueria, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, fleshy leaves covered in a fine, white pubescence that gives it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a distinctive species within the Aizoaceae family.
