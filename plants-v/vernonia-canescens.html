Vernonia canescens

Vernonia canescens, commonly known as grayironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and showy yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting a variety of pollinators including bees and butterflies.

Vernonia canescens is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
