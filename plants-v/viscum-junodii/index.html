Viscum Junodii

Viscum junodii is a rare and intriguing plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family. It is commonly known as the Junod's mistletoe and is native to the tropical regions of Africa. This parasitic plant attaches itself to the branches of its host trees, drawing nutrients and water from them. Viscum junodii is often found in humid, shaded environments where it can thrive without direct sunlight. Its unique ecological role and limited distribution make it a subject of scientific interest and conservation concern.
