Valantia intricata

Valantia intricata, commonly known as the laceleaf orchid, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, finely divided foliage that resembles lace, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often arranged in clusters along the stems. Valantia intricata thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and botanical collections.
