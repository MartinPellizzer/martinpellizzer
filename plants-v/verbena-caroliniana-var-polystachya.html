Verbena caroliniana var. polystachya

Verbena caroliniana var. polystachya, commonly known as the showy verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and grayish-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
