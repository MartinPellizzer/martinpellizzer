Vigna unguiculata var. huillensis

Vigna unguiculata var. huillensis, commonly known as the Huillense cowpea, is a variety of the cowpea species native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, with small, pale green pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, improving soil fertility.

This variety is well adapted to dry conditions, making it a resilient crop in agricultural systems.
