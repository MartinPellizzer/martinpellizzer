Vigna haumaniana var. pedunculata

Vigna haumaniana var. pedunculata, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender, hairy stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

This variety is valued for its nutritional seeds and is often cultivated for both food and forage purposes.
