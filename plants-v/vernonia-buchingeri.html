Vernonia buchingeri

Vernonia buchingeri, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. Vernonia buchingeri typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
