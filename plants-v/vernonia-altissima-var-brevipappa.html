Vernonia altissima var. brevipappa

Vernonia altissima var. brevipappa, commonly known as the tall ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy purple to pinkish-purple composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
