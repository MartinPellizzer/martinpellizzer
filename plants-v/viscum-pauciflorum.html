Viscum pauciflorum

Viscum pauciflorum, commonly known as the lesser mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse its seeds. Viscum pauciflorum is typically found in temperate regions and can thrive in a variety of host species, including oaks and pines.

Its growth is often limited by the availability of suitable host trees and environmental conditions.
