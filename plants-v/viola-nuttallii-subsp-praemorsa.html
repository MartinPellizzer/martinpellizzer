Viola nuttallii subsp. praemorsa

Viola nuttallii subsp.

praemorsa, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy violet flowers with distinctive dark markings. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and early spring blooming period.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flowers and more elongated petals compared to other viola species.
