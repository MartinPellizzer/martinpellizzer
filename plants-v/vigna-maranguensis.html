Vigna maranguensis

Vigna maranguensis, commonly known as the Marangua bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect growth habit. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry and semi-arid conditions, making it an important crop in regions with limited rainfall.

The plant's botanical features make it suitable for both traditional and sustainable agricultural practices.
