Vachellia farnesiana f. armata

Vachellia farnesiana f. armata, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a variant of the common acacia species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense, fine pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in compact, cylindrical spikes, typically during the dry season. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and extreme temperatures.

The plant is often used for ornamental purposes and in traditional medicine due to its hardy nature and unique appearance.
