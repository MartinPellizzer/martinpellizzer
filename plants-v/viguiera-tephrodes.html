Viguiera tephrodes

Viguiera tephrodes, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy, purple to pink flower clusters in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Viguiera tephrodes is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
