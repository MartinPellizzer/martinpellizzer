Veronica × sapiehae

Veronica × sapiehae, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Veronica × sapiehae prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
