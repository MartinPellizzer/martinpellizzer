Valeriana × braunii-blanquetii var. pulevicii

Valeriana × braunii-blanquetii var. pulevicii, commonly known as the Balkan valerian, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Native to the Balkan Peninsula, this species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for studies on hybridization and ecological adaptation.
