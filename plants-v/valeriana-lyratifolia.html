Valeriana lyratifolia

Valeriana lyratifolia, commonly known as the lyratic valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its root system is fibrous and is known for containing volatile oils, which contribute to its distinctive aroma.

Valeriana lyratifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
