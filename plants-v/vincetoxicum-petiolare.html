Vincetoxicum petiolare

Vincetoxicum petiolare, commonly known as the red-spined antirrhinum, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a reddish midrib, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Vincetoxicum petiolare is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display and moderate growth requirements.
