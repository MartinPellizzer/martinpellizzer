Vasconcellea boissieri

Vasconcellea boissieri, commonly known as the "Cuban wax apple," is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Caricaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red or orange when mature. The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
