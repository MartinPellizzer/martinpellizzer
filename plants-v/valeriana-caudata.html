Valeriana caudata

Valeriana caudata, commonly known as wild valerian, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic leaves and is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks. Valeriana caudata is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.

Its roots and leaves have been used traditionally for their calming effects.
