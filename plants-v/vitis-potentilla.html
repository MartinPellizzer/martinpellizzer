Vitis potentilla

Vitis potentilla, commonly known as trailing buffalo grape, is a deciduous vine native to North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb or trail along the ground. Its berries are typically dark purple to black and are edible, though often tart.

Vitis potentilla is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
