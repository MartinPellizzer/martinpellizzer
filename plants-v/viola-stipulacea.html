Viola stipulacea

Viola stipulacea, commonly known as the stipule violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola stipulacea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
