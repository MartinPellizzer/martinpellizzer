Vachellia lasiopetala

Vachellia lasiopetala, commonly known as the hairy leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Vachellia lasiopetala is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
