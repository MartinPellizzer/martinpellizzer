Veronicastrum virginicum

Veronicastrum virginicum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands.

Blue mistflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
