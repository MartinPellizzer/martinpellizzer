Vatica flavida

Vatica flavida, commonly known as the yellow vatica, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its fruit is a capsule containing winged seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and potential medicinal properties.

The species plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
