Valeriana eupatoria

Valeriana eupatoria, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often pink or white flowers and its distinctive, fragrant leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its roots contain compounds such as valerenic acid, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

Valeriana eupatoria is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in phytotherapy.
