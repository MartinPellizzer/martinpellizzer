Verbascum boissieri

Verbascum boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
