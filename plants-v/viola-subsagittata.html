Viola subsagittata

Viola subsagittata, commonly known as the sagittate violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola subsagittata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
