Vaccinium chamissonis var. subcoriaceum

Vaccinium chamissonis var. subcoriaceum, commonly known as the rough huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically thrives in moist, acidic soils found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive rough texture, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
