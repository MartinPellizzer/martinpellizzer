Veronica anagalloides subsp. heureka

Veronica anagalloides subsp.

heureka, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
