Vincetoxicum sibiricum f. hypopsilum

Vincetoxicum sibiricum f. hypopsilum, commonly known as Siberian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, temperate climates. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This subspecies is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
