Viguiera aspilioides

Viguiera aspilioides, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its striking purple to reddish-pink flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viguiera aspilioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
