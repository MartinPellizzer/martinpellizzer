Vitis mexicana

Vitis mexicana, commonly known as Mexican grapevine, is a species of flowering vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into round, fleshy berries. Its berries are edible and have been used traditionally for their medicinal and culinary properties.

Vitis mexicana thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
