Valerianella morisonii proles microcarpa

Valerianella morisonii proles microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf valerian, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, clustered leaves and tiny, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically less than 5 millimeters in diameter. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded conditions.

Valerianella morisonii proles microcarpa is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it forms dense mats.
