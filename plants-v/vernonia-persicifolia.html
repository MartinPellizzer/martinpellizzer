Vernonia persicifolia

Vernonia persicifolia, commonly known as the peachleaf veronica, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a peacheskin-like texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
