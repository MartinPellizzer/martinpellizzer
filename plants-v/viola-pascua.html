Viola pascua

Viola pascua, commonly known as the common violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, often purple or violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in partial shade to full sun. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and self-seeding.

Viola pascua is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
