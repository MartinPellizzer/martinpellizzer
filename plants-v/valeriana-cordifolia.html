Valeriana cordifolia

Valeriana cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf valerian, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces a strong, often disagreeable odor, which is a common trait among species in the Valerianaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Valeriana cordifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its purported sedative and antispasmodic properties.
