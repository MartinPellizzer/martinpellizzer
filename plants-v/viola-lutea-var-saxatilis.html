Viola lutea var. saxatilis

Viola lutea var. saxatilis, commonly known as the yellow violet or mountain violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
