Verbascum nigrum var. verum

Verbascum nigrum var. verum, commonly known as common mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that form a rosette near the ground. The plant produces tall, erect stems with showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in wastelands.

Verbascum nigrum var. verum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for respiratory ailments.
