Verbascum × neilreichii

Verbascum × neilreichii, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Verbascum × neilreichii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens with full sun exposure.
