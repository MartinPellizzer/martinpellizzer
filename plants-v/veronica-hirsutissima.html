Veronica hirsutissima

Veronica hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers and hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Veronica hirsutissima is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
