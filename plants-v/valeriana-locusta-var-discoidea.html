Valeriana locusta var. discoidea

Valeriana locusta var. discoidea, commonly known as the clustered valerian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its root system is known for containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal remedies.
