Veronica pilosa

Veronica pilosa, commonly known as the hairy speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Veronica pilosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
