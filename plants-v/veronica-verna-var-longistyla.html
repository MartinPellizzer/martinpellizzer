Veronica verna var. longistyla

Veronica verna var. longistyla, commonly known as the early speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it an important species in ecological habitats.
