Veronica glauca subsp. peloponnesiaca

Veronica glauca subsp.

peloponnesiaca, commonly known as the Peloponnesian speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens.
