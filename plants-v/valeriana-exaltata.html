Valeriana exaltata

Valeriana exaltata, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces violet-blue or white flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Valeriana exaltata is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
