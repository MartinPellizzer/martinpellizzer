Veronica spectabilis

Veronica spectabilis, commonly known as the showy veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from early summer to late summer.
