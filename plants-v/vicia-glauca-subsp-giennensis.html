Vicia glauca subsp. giennensis

Vicia glauca subsp.

giennensis, commonly known as the Sicilian vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually violet or blue in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
