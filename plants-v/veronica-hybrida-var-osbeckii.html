Veronica hybrida var. osbeckii

Veronica hybrida var. osbeckii, commonly known as the Osbeck's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are arranged in whorls along the stems.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
