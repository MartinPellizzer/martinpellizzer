Verbascum mucronatum

Verbascum mucronatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies.
