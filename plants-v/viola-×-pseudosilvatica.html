Viola × pseudosilvatica

Viola × pseudosilvatica, commonly known as the purple wood violet, is a hybrid perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola × pseudosilvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
