Viola fimbriatula

Viola fimbriatula, commonly known as the fringed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive fringed edge, and its flowers are violet-blue with five petals and a spurred petal at the back. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola fimbriatula is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a pollinator plant.
