Viola lutea var. multicaulis

Viola lutea var. multicaulis, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with purple veins and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and early spring blooming period.

The varietal name "multicaulis" refers to its multiple stems, which contribute to its bushy growth habit.
