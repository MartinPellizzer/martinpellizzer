Vatica thwaitesii

Vatica thwaitesii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Vatica thwaitesii thrives in humid, lowland rainforest environments and is often found in secondary growth areas.
