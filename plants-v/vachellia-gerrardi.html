Vachellia gerrardi

Vachellia gerrardi, commonly known as the gum arabic tree, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, bipinnate leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds surrounded by a sticky, resinous substance known as gum arabic. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Vachellia gerrardi is also valued for its economic contribution through the harvesting of gum arabic, which is used in food, pharmaceuticals, and industrial applications.
