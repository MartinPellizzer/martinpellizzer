Verticordia multiflora subsp. solox

Verticordia multiflora subsp.

solox, commonly known as the yellow-veined globemallow, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange flower heads, which are often clustered in dense, globular formations.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with prominent veins that give it its common name.
