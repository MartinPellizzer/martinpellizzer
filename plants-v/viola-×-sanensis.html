Viola × sanensis

Viola × sanensis, commonly known as the San Francisco violet, is a hybrid species native to California.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces showy flowers with five petals, often featuring a distinctive yellow or white center.

Viola × sanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
