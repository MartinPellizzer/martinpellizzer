Viola parvula var. villosa

Viola parvula var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety, hairy texture, which is characteristic of the varietal name "villosa.". Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive yellow lower petal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in native plant gardens.
