Verbascum celsioides

Verbascum celsioides, commonly known as the alpine mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing at high altitudes. The plant features large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, yellowish-green in color, and appear in clusters at the end of the stems.

Verbascum celsioides is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in meadows and scree slopes.
