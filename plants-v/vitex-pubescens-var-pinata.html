Vitex pubescens var. pinata

Vitex pubescens var. pinata, commonly known as the Mexican lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent, giving it a fuzzy texture. The plant produces panicles of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of purple or white, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Vitex pubescens var. pinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral displays.
