Veronica pseudoincana

Veronica pseudoincana, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Veronica pseudoincana is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
