Viburnum betulifolium f. aurantiacum

Viburnum betulifolium f. aurantiacum, commonly known as the orangeleaf viburnum, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, ovate leaves that turn a striking orange-red color in the fall, adding vibrant seasonal interest. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark red berries that are attractive to birds. Its foliage and fruit make it a valuable ornamental plant in gardens and natural landscapes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
