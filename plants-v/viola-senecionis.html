Viola senecionis

Viola senecionis, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola senecionis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
