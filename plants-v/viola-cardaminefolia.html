Viola cardaminefolia

Viola cardaminefolia, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale purple, bloom in spring, adding a touch of color to high-altitude landscapes.

Viola cardaminefolia is often found in association with other alpine flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
