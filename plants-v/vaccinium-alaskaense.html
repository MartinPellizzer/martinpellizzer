Vaccinium alaskaense

Vaccinium alaskaense, commonly known as Alaskan blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Vaccinium alaskaense plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization in its harsh environment.
