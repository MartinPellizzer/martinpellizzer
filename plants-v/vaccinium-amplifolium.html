Vaccinium amplifolium

Vaccinium amplifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cranberries and azaleas. The plant is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries, which are typically blue or purple, are edible and have a tart flavor.

Vaccinium amplifolium thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
