Valeriana wallrothii subsp. intermedia

Valeriana wallrothii subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a perennial herb with a robust rhizomatous root system and aromatic foliage.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive, pungent odor. Valeriana wallrothii subsp.

intermedia is valued in traditional medicine for its sedative and calming properties.
