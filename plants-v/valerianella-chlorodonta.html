Valerianella chlorodonta

Valerianella chlorodonta, commonly known as the green valerian or sweet valerian, is a flowering plant in the family Valerianaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant features small, fragrant white or greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Valerianella chlorodonta is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
