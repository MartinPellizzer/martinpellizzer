Viola odorata subsp. dehnhardtii

Viola odorata subsp.

dehnhardtii, commonly known as the Dehnhardt's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky soils, preferring partial shade to full sun. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
