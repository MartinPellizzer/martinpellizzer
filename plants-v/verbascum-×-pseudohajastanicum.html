Verbascum × pseudohajastanicum

Verbascum × pseudohajastanicum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum hajastanicum and Verbascum blattaria.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to the Caucasus region and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and ease of growth.
