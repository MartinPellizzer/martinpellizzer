Viola palustris var. rubra

Viola palustris var. rubra, commonly known as the red marsh violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that range in color from purple to red, often with a distinctive red throat. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
