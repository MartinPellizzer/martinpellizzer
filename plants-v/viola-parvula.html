Viola parvula

Viola parvula, commonly known as the small viola, is a delicate flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Viola parvula is a perennial herb that produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
