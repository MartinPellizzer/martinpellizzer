Vatica javanica subsp. javanica

Vatica javanica subsp.

javanica, commonly known as the Javan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability.

The plant has simple, opposite, and elliptic leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
