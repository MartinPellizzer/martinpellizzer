Veronica multifida var. valida

Veronica multifida var. valida, commonly known as the hairy veronica, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Veronica multifida var. valida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
