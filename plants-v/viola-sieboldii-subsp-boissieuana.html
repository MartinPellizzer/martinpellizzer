Viola sieboldii subsp. boissieuana

Viola sieboldii subsp.

boissieuana, commonly known as the Boissieu's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the underbrush.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flower coloration compared to the typical Viola sieboldii.
