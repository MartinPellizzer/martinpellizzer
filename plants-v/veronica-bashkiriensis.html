Veronica bashkiriensis

Veronica bashkiriensis, commonly known as Bashkir speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Veronica bashkiriensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
