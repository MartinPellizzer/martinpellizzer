Verojuncus phaeocephalus

Verojuncus phaeocephalus, commonly known as the black-headed veldt grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, terminal inflorescence, which contrasts with its green foliage. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowering stems are usually erect and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Verojuncus phaeocephalus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
