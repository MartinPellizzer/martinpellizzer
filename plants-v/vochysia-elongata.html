Vochysia elongata

Vochysia elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed velvet tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spindle-shaped fruits containing several seeds.

Vochysia elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
