Vaccinium piliferum

Vaccinium piliferum, commonly known as the hairy blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other berry-producing plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of pale blue or white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, which is typically small and acidic, is consumed by various wildlife species.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or coastal regions.
