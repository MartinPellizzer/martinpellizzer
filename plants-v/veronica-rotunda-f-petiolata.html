Veronica rotunda f. petiolata

Veronica rotunda f. petiolata, commonly known as the petiolate speedwell, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, round, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically rounded and slightly hairy, with petioles that give it its distinguishing name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and easy maintenance in garden settings.
