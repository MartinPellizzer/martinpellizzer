Vitex axillariflora

Vitex axillariflora, commonly known as the axillary-flowered vitex, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Vitex axillariflora is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
