Viola tricolor var. vulgaris

Viola tricolor var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common heartsease, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy flowers that typically feature purple, white, and yellow petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas, gardens, and along stream banks.

Viola tricolor var. vulgaris is a perennial species that blooms from spring to early summer.
