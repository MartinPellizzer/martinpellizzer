Vicia communis var. nemoralis

Vicia communis var. nemoralis, commonly known as the wood vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in woodland and hedgerow habitats. It has a climbing habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various insects.
