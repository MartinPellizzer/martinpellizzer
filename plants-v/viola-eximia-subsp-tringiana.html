Viola eximia subsp. tringiana

Viola eximia subsp.

tringiana, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. It has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soil.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and broader leaves compared to other viola species.
