Vilfa commutata

Vilfa commutata, commonly known as the variable violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, which can range from ovate to lanceolate shapes. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of soft hair.

Vilfa commutata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
