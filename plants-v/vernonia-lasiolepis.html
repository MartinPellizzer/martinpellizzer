Vernonia lasiolepis

Vernonia lasiolepis, commonly known as the woolly ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Vernonia lasiolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
