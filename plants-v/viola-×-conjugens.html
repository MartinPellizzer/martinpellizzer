Viola × conjugens

Viola × conjugens, commonly known as the common heartleaf violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. It produces showy flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
