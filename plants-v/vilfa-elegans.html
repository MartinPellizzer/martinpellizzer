Vilfa elegans

Vilfa elegans, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a similar silvery coating, contributing to its ornamental value.

Vilfa elegans is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is adapted to well-drained, rocky soils.
