Viola tricolor f. luteoroseola

Viola tricolor f. luteoroseola, commonly known as the yellow-rose viola, is a cultivar of the common violet.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy flowers that display a combination of white, yellow, and violet hues. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade.

This variety is known for its compact growth habit and ability to bloom early in the spring.
