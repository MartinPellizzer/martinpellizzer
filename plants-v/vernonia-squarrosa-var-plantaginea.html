Vernonia squarrosa var. plantaginea

Vernonia squarrosa var. plantaginea, commonly known as the woolly ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, woolly foliage and conical, spiky flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are usually a pale yellow to brownish color.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
