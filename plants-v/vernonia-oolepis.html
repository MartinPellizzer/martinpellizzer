Vernonia oolepis

Vernonia oolepis, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia oolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
