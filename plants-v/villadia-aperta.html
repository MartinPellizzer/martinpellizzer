Villadia aperta

Villadia aperta, commonly known as the open-villain plant, is a rare species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, sprawling growth habit with elongated, waxy leaves that often curl under dry conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its leaves contain a high concentration of alkaloids, making it a subject of interest in pharmacological research.

Due to habitat loss, Villadia aperta is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
