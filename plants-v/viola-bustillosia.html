Viola bustillosia

Viola bustillosia, commonly known as the Mexican violet, is a species native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters, with showy violet or purple blossoms that appear in early spring. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower one often being larger and more prominent.

Viola bustillosia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.
