Vulpia octoflora var. glauca

Vulpia octoflora var. glauca, commonly known as the blue foxgrain, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that typically grow up to 30 cm in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale blue to whitish florets. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.
