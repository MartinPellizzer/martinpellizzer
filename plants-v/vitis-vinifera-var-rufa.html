Vitis vinifera var. rufa

Vitis vinifera var. rufa, commonly known as the red grapevine, is a less commonly cultivated variety of the common grapevine.

It is characterized by its reddish-purple foliage and clusters of small, dark berries. This variety is often found in temperate climates and is valued for its unique flavor profile in winemaking. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and viticultural experimentation.
