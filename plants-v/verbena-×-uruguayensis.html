Verbena × uruguayensis

Verbena × uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay verbena, is a hybrid perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant floral displays. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom profusely from summer to fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies add to its horticultural value.
