Vitis taurina

Vitis taurina, commonly known as the Caucasian grapevine, is a species native to the Caucasus region and parts of southern Europe.

It is a woody vine that typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, often climbing on trees or shrubs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by small, sour-tasting berries. Vitis taurina is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of wild grapes.
