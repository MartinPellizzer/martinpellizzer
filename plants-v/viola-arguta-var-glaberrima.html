Viola arguta var. glaberrima

Viola arguta var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped, dark green leaves and violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often marked by a distinctive yellowish marking.

The species is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, giving it its common name.
