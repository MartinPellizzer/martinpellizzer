Vicia atropurpurea var. punicea

Vicia atropurpurea var. punicea, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of purple or pink pea-like flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
