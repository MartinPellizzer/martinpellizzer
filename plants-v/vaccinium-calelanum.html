Vaccinium calelanum

Vaccinium calelanum, commonly known as the Calelan huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black when mature. These berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its dense foliage and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
