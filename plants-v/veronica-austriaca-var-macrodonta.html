Veronica austriaca var. macrodonta

Veronica austriaca var. macrodonta, commonly known as the large-flowered speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers, which are typically larger than those of the typical species. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive floral display.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate wet conditions, making it suitable for water garden settings.
