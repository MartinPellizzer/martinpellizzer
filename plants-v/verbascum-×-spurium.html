Verbascum × spurium

Verbascum × spurium, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant that belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Verbascum × spurium is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
