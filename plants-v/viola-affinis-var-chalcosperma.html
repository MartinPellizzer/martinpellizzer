Viola affinis var. chalcosperma

Viola affinis var. chalcosperma, commonly known as the copper-spurred violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of violet and blue, often with a distinctive copper-colored spur at the base of each petal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
