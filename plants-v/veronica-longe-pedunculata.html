Veronica longe-pedunculata

Veronica longe-pedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems that bear clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy surface.

Veronica longe-pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
