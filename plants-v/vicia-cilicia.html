Vicia cilicia

Vicia cilicia, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, often found in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Vicia cilicia is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

This characteristic makes it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
