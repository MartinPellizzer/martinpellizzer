Viburnum euryphyllum

Viburnum euryphyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink, appearing in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds and small mammals. Viburnum euryphyllum typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

Its foliage is often dark green with a slightly glossy appearance, adding ornamental value to gardens.
