Veronica linnaeoides

Veronica linnaeoides, commonly known as the blueweed or narrowleaf veronica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Veronica linnaeoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
