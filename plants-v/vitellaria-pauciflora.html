Vitellaria pauciflora

Vitellaria pauciflora, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by large, hairy fruits containing a valuable seed kernel. The seeds are used to produce shea butter, an important commodity in cosmetics and food industries.

It plays a significant ecological and economic role in the regions where it grows.
