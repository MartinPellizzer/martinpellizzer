Vernonia scorpioides var. subrepanda

Vernonia scorpioides var. subrepanda, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and bottomlands, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically pale yellow to brownish in color.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental potential in wetland landscapes.
