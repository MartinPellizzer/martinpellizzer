Verbesina rugosa

Verbesina rugosa, commonly known as roughleaf beggar-ticks, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, sticky hairs. The leaves are heart-shaped with a rough, textured surface and serrated edges. It produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.
