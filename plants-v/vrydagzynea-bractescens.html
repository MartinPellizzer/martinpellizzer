Vrydagzynea bractescens

Vrydagzynea bractescens, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by spiny, woody fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
