Viola sacchalinensis f. alpina

Viola sacchalinensis f. alpina, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
