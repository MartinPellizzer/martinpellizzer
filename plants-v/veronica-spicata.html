Veronica spicata

Veronica spicata, commonly known as spiked speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features upright, spike-like clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense, low-growing mat.

Veronica spicata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
