Viola rupestris var. himalayensis

Viola rupestris var. himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, violet-colored flowers with yellow markings. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Due to its specific environmental requirements, it is relatively rare in cultivation outside its native range.
