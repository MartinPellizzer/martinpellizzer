Vitis vinifera var. maurillona

Vitis vinifera var. maurillona, commonly known as the Mauritius grape, is a rare and ancient variety of grape native to the island of Mauritius.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple berries and is typically grown in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant has a vigorous vine structure with heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fruit that are often used for winemaking or culinary purposes.

Due to its limited cultivation and genetic uniqueness, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
