Verbesina arnottii f. scabriuscula

Verbesina arnottii f. scabriuscula, commonly known as the scabrous beggar's ticks, is a variant of the species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a rough, scabrous texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.
