Veronica spicata lusus caerulea

Veronica spicata lusus caerulea, commonly known as the blue tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for borders and rock gardens.

Its foliage is typically a vibrant green, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
