Vincetoxicum pubiflorum

Vincetoxicum pubiflorum, commonly known as the hairyflower agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that are often covered with soft, hairy hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Vincetoxicum pubiflorum is often found in moist, woodland habitats and can spread through rhizomes.
