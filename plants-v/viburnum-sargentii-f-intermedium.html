Viburnum sargentii f. intermedium

Viburnum sargentii f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate sargent viburnum, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that appear in early spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that turn reddish in autumn, adding ornamental value. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest in garden settings.
