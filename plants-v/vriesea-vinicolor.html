Vriesea vinicolor

Vriesea vinicolor, commonly known as the flame violet, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spathaceous inflorescence that resembles a flaming flower, typically in shades of red and orange. The plant features a rosette of narrow, dark green leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Vriesea vinicolor is popular as a decorative plant due to its long-lasting, showy blooms.
