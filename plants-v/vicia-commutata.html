Vicia commutata

Vicia commutata, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. Vicia commutata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration and agriculture.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.
