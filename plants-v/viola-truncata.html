Viola truncata

Viola truncata, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive truncate (notched) base, and its flowers are typically violet-blue with yellow markings. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Viola truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
