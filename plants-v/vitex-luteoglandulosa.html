Vitex luteoglandulosa

Vitex luteoglandulosa, commonly known as the yellow gland verbena, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have glandular dots, giving the plant its scientific name. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
