Vitis crassiuscula

Vitis crassiuscula, commonly known as the Canadian grape or wild grape, is a deciduous vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color in the fall. It is often found in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Vitis crassiuscula is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
