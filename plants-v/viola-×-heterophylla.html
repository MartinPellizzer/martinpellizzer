Viola × heterophylla

Viola × heterophylla, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, typically purple or violet flowers. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola × heterophylla is known for its ability to self-seed and spread in gardens, making it a popular choice for ground cover.
