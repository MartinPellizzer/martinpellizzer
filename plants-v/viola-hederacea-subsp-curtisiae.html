Viola hederacea subsp. curtisiae

Viola hederacea subsp.

curtisiae, commonly known as the curly violets, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy or curled margin, and it produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet. It is often found in association with other woodland flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
