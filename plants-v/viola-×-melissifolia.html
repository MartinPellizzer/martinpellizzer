Viola × melissifolia

Viola × melissifolia, commonly known as the honeyleaf violet, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Viola sororia and Viola tricolor.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive white or pale green underside. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with a yellow center, and bloom in early spring. This species is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shady environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
