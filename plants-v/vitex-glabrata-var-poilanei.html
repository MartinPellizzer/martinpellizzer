Vitex glabrata var. poilanei

Vitex glabrata var. poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar vitex, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and pairs of opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed capsules that release seeds when mature.

This species is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
