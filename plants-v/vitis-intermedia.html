Vitis intermedia

Vitis intermedia, commonly known as the red river grape, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, sweet-tart berries. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. Its berries are used in winemaking and are also a food source for wildlife.

Vitis intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
