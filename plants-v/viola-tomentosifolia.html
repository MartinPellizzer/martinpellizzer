Viola tomentosifolia

Viola tomentosifolia, commonly known as the woolly violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves covered in a dense, woolly layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Violaceae family.
