Viola × pragensis

Viola × pragensis, commonly known as the Prague violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. Native to Central Europe, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers typically bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
