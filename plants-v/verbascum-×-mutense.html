Verbascum × mutense

Verbascum × mutense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a flowering plant in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is a hybrid between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattellum, resulting in a plant with distinctive fuzzy, woolly leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is native to southern Europe. It produces tall, erect stems with showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer.

Verbascum × mutense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
