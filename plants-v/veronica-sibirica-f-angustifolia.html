Veronica sibirica f. angustifolia

Veronica sibirica f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes for its delicate appearance.
