Viola rupestris var. bolosii

Viola rupestris var. bolosii, commonly known as the Bolosii's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry, sunny conditions.
