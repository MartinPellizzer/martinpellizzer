Vernonia leucolepis

Vernonia leucolepis, commonly known as the white-stemmed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Vernonia leucolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
