Viola uniflora subsp. lasczinskyi

Viola uniflora subsp.

lasczinskyi, commonly known as the Lasczinsky's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or blue. Its flowers are typically solitary, emerging from the leaf axils, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors and compact growth habit.
