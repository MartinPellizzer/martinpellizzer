Vernonia diffusa var. ferruginea

Vernonia diffusa var. ferruginea, commonly known as rusty ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flower heads. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
