Vignea straminea

Vignea straminea, commonly known as the slender legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines.

Vignea straminea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
