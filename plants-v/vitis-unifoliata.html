Vitis unifoliata

Vitis unifoliata, commonly known as the single-leaf grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its single leaflet per leaf and produces small, sweet berries that are often used in local cuisines. The plant has a trailing growth habit and can climb using tendrils, making it adaptable to various garden settings. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a robust root system that helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Vitis unifoliata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for cultivation in horticulture.
