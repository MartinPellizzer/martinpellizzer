Viola × chinoi

Viola × chinoi, commonly known as Chinese violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola sylvatica.

It is a perennial flowering plant native to China and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is known for its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.

The flowers bloom in spring and are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
