Vernonella subaphylla

Vernonella subaphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vervain, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of ponds.

Vernonella subaphylla is an important plant for pollinators and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
