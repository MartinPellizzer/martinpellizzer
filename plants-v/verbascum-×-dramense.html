Verbascum × dramense

Verbascum × dramense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattellum.

It is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and easy care requirements.
