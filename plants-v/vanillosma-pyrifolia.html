Vanillosma pyrifolia

Vanillosma pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often lobed leaves. The plant produces orange to reddish fruits that are rich in sugars and tannins, which soften as they ripen. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow in color.
