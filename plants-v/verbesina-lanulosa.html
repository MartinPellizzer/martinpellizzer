Verbesina lanulosa

Verbesina lanulosa, commonly known as the woolly beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides.

Verbesina lanulosa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
