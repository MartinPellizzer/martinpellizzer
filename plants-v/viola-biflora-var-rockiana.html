Viola biflora var. rockiana

Viola biflora var. rockiana, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet and white. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, and it is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
