Verbesina lottiana

Verbesina lottiana, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and riverbanks. The plant features upright, hairy stems and produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
