Vernonia cubensis var. cajalbanensis

Vernonia cubensis var. cajalbanensis, commonly known as the Cuban ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Cuba.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and woody.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
