Vicia sativa f. leucosperma

Vicia sativa f. leucosperma, commonly known as the white-flowered vetch, is a botanical variant of the common vetch.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The most distinguishing feature is its white or pale yellow flowers, which appear in small clusters. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climates.
