Verbascum × pseudobalcanicum

Verbascum × pseudobalcanicum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a flowering plant in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum nigrum, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is known for its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats across parts of Europe and western Asia.
