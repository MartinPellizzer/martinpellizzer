Viola primulifolia var. villosa

Viola primulifolia var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its flowers are violet-blue in color, with five petals and yellow markings at the base.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
