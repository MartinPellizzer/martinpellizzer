Vitex obovata subsp. wilmsii

Vitex obovata subsp.

wilmsii, commonly known as the Wilmsii chaste tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It features opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces showy, fragrant, blue to purple flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
