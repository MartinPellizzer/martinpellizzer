Vicia afghanica

Vicia afghanica, commonly known as Afghan vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Vicia afghanica is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
