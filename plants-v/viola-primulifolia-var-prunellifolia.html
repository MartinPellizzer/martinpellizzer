Viola primulifolia var. prunellifolia

Viola primulifolia var. prunellifolia, commonly known as the purple violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows to a height of about 15-30 cm, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from violet to purple, often with a distinctive yellow eye. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
