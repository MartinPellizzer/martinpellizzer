Veronica polita subsp. thellungiana

Veronica polita subsp.

thellungiana, commonly known as the Swedish speedwell, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting a variety of pollinators.
