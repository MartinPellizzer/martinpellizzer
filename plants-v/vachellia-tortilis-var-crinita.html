Vachellia tortilis var. crinita

Vachellia tortilis var. crinita, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and extreme climates.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
