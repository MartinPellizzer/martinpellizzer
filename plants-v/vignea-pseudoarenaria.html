Vignea pseudoarenaria

Vignea pseudoarenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.
