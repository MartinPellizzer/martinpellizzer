Veronica irrigans

Veronica irrigans, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

Veronica irrigans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
