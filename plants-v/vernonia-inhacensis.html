Vernonia inhacensis

Vernonia inhacensis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Vernonia inhacensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
