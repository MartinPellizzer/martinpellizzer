Vaccinium thibaudifolium

Vaccinium thibaudifolium, commonly known as the Thibaud's blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are bell-shaped, pinkish-white, and appear in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in local ecosystems and horticulture.
