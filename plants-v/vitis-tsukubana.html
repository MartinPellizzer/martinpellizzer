Vitis tsukubana

Vitis tsukubana, commonly known as the Tsukubana grape, is a rare and endangered species native to Japan.

It is a woody vine that produces small, dark purple berries. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to habitat loss, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to survive.
