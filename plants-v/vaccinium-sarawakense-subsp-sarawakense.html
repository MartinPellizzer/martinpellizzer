Vaccinium sarawakense subsp. sarawakense

Vaccinium sarawakense subsp.

sarawakense, commonly known as the Sarawak heather, is a shrub native to the Bornean region. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in montane forest ecosystems.
