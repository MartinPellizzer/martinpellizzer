Viscago baccifera

Viscago baccifera, commonly known as the common dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Viscago baccifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
