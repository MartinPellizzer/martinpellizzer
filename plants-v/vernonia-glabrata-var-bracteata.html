Vernonia glabrata var. bracteata

Vernonia glabrata var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a distinct grayish-green color.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
