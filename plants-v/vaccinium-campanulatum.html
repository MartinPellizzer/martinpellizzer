Vaccinium campanulatum

Vaccinium campanulatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped blueberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its erect, woody stems and evergreen leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a small, edible berry that is usually blue or purple in color, similar to other Vaccinium species.

This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.
