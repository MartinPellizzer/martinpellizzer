Verbena adulterina

Verbena adulterina, commonly known as the common verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Verbena adulterina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
