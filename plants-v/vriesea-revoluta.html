Vriesea revoluta

Vriesea revoluta, commonly known as the velvetleaf bromeliad, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety layer of trichomes. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful, long-lasting bracts that can range from red to orange or yellow. Vriesea revoluta thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
