Vernicia cordata

Vernicia cordata, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and usually elliptical leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, lowland areas.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate urban environments and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.
