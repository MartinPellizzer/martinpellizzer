Viola pinetorum var. pinetorum

Viola pinetorum var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple hue, and its flowers are usually violet or blue with yellow markings. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, humid conditions.
