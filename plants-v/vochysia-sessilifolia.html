Vochysia sessilifolia

Vochysia sessilifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or yellow bell, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Vochysia sessilifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and timber.
