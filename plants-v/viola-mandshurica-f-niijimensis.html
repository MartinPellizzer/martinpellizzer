Viola mandshurica f. niijimensis

Viola mandshurica f. niijimensis, commonly known as the Niijima viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northeastern Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are often pale violet or white, with a distinctive pattern of darker markings.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
