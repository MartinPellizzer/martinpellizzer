Vigna anomala

Vigna anomala, commonly known as the wild cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into cylindrical pods. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Vigna anomala is valued for its edible seeds and has traditional uses in various cultures.
