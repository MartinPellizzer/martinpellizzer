Viola tenuicornis

Viola tenuicornis, commonly known as the slender-horned violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate, purple-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in partial to full shade.

Viola tenuicornis is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
