Verbascum pyramidale

Verbascum pyramidale, commonly known as the upright mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, heart-shaped leaves covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a pyramid-like arrangement, giving it its species name "pyramidale.". Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Verbascum pyramidale thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.
