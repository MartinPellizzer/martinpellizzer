Viburnum phlebotrichum

Viburnum phlebotrichum, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its stems are distinctive for their bright red coloration, especially during the winter months.

Viburnum phlebotrichum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
