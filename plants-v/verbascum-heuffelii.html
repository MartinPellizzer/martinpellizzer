Verbascum heuffelii

Verbascum heuffelii, commonly known as the Heuffel's mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its fibrous leaves and stems have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
