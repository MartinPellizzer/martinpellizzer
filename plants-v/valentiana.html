Valentiana

Valentiana, commonly known as the Valentine's flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems. These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and as cut flowers.
