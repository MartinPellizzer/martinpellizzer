Viguiera zaluzanioides

Viguiera zaluzanioides, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late summer and early fall.

Viguiera zaluzanioides is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
