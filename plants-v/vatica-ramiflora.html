Vatica ramiflora

Vatica ramiflora, commonly known as the ramiflorous tamanu, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, grayish bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in local medicine.
