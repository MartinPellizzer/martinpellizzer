Veronica ovata var. kitadakemontana

Veronica ovata var. kitadakemontana, commonly known as the Japanese speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and blue-violet flowers. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered blooms throughout the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
