Viola × wilhelmii

Viola × wilhelmii, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Viola × wilhelmii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
