Viola × neglectiformis

Viola × neglectiformis, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world.

Viola × neglectiformis is often found in gardens and wildflower meadows due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
