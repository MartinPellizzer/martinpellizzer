Valeriana interjecta

Valeriana interjecta, commonly known as the meadow valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana interjecta is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat anxiety and sleep disorders.
