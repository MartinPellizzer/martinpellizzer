Vitex secundiflora

Vitex secundiflora, commonly known as the Mexican heliotrope, is a deciduous shrub native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It features aromatic, opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered purple or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its fragrant foliage and vibrant floral displays.
