Vernonia mbalensis

Vernonia mbalensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Vernonia mbalensis is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or along roadsides.
