Vaccinium secundum

Vaccinium secundum, commonly known as the lowbush blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically harvested in late summer, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Vaccinium secundum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and wildlife.
