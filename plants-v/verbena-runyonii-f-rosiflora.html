Verbena runyonii f. rosiflora

Verbena runyonii f. rosiflora, commonly known as the Rosyflower verbena, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, slightly hairy, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to lavender. The plant blooms from late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
