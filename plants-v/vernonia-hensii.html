Vernonia hensii

Vernonia hensii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Vernonia hensii is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
