Veronica arvensis subsp. rubricaulis

Veronica arvensis subsp.

rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, often reddish stems that distinguish it from other members of the genus.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.
