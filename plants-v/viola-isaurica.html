Viola isaurica

Viola isaurica, commonly known as the Isaurian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola isaurica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens with well-draining soil.
