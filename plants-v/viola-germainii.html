Viola germainii

Viola germainii, commonly known as the Germain violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and violet. Viola germainii is known for its ability to thrive in cool climates and is often found in regions with high humidity.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
