Vangueria bowkeri

Vangueria bowkeri, commonly known as the Cape reed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, exhibiting a creeping growth habit with erect flowering stems. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Vangueria bowkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
