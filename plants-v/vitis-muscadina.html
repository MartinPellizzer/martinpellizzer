Vitis muscadina

Vitis muscadina, commonly known as the scuppernong grape, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and its ability to produce both seeded and seedless fruits. The plant typically has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, green, and clustered in panicles. The fruit is usually a dark purple to black berry, with a sweet and sometimes musky flavor.

It is often cultivated for its culinary and wine-making potential.
