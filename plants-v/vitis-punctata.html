Vitis punctata

Vitis punctata, commonly known as the spotted grapevine, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white to greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red or black when mature. Vitis punctata is often found in moist, wooded areas and can thrive in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Vitaceae family.
