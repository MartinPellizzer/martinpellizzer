Vernonia glabrata var. parvifolia

Vernonia glabrata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered ironweed, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green appearance. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
