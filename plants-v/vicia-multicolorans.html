Vicia multicolorans

Vicia multicolorans, commonly known as the multicolored vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Vicia multicolorans is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for soil stabilization and ecological restoration projects.
