Viola palmata f. variegata

Viola palmata f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated pansy, is a cultivar of the common pansy, characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

This plant is native to Europe but is widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It features heart-shaped leaves with white and green markings, adding visual interest to gardens. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with a distinctive pattern of darker hues.

It thrives in cool climates and is often grown as an annual or biennial in temperate regions.
