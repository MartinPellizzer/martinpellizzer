Vigna gazensis

Vigna gazensis, commonly known as the Ganzari bean, is a climbing legume native to Africa.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional cuisines.

Vigna gazensis is also important in agricultural systems due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
