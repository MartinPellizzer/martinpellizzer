Viola macounii

Viola macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola macounii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
