Vigna minima var. minor

Vigna minima var. minor, commonly known as dwarf cowpea, is a small, annual legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, typically reaching only a few inches in height. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, similar to other members of the legume family. It thrives in warm climates and is often grown as a cover crop or for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for use in sustainable agriculture and soil enrichment.
