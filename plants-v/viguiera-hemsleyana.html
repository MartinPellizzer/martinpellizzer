Viguiera hemsleyana

Viguiera hemsleyana, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with purple to pink florets that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viguiera hemsleyana is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.
