Vassobia

Vassobia, commonly known as the yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers and hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed areas and is native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. Vassobia produces small, red berries that contain toxic compounds, making the plant potentially harmful to animals and humans.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
