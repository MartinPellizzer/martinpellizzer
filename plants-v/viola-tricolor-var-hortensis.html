Viola tricolor var. hortensis

Viola tricolor var. hortensis, commonly known as the heartleaf viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of violet, yellow, and white flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and traditional herbal use.
