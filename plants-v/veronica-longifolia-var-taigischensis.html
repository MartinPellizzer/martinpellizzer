Veronica longifolia var. taigischensis

Veronica longifolia var. taigischensis, commonly known as the Taigischense speedwell, is a variant of the speedwell plant native to specific regions of Russia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.
