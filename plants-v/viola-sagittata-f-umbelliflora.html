Viola sagittata f. umbelliflora

Viola sagittata f. umbelliflora, commonly known as the umbellate violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin, and the flowers are usually purple or violet in color.

This fern-like variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
