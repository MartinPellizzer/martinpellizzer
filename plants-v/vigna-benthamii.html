Vigna benthamii

Vigna benthamii, commonly known as the African honey bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and climbing habit, often growing as a vine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Vigna benthamii is valued for its nutritional seeds and is cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
