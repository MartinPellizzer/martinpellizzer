Viola canina var. collina

Viola canina var. collina, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple or violet during the spring. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic value.
