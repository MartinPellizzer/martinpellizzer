Vernonia guadalupensis

Vernonia guadalupensis, commonly known as Guadalupe ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and its clusters of small, spiky, blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal and ornamental value. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Vernonia guadalupensis is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
