Verbena connatibracteata f. normalis

Verbena connatibracteata f. normalis, commonly known as the common verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy spikes of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
