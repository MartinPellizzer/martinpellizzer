Vaccinium varingiifolium var. orientale

Vaccinium varingiifolium var. orientale, commonly known as the oriental huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in traditional cuisines.
