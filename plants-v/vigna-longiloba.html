Vigna longiloba

Vigna longiloba, commonly known as the long pod bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated pods that contain small, round seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

Vigna longiloba is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.
