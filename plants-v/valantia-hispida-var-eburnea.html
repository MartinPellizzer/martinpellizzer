Valantia hispida var. eburnea

Valantia hispida var. eburnea, commonly known as the silverleaf valantia, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive silvery-white foliage.

Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
