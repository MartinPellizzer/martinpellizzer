Viola himalayensis

Viola himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola himalayensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
