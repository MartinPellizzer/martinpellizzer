Viguiera excelsa var. genuina

Viguiera excelsa var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
