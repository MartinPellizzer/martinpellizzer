Vernonia bahiensis

Vernonia bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia bahiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
