Vangueria caffra

Vangueria caffra, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Vangueria caffra is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance.

Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.
