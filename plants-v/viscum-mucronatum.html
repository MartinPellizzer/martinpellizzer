Viscum mucronatum

Viscum mucronatum, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various coniferous trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually evergreen and lack stipules. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by small, berry-like fruits. Viscum mucronatum relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent.

It plays an important role in the ecological interactions of forest ecosystems.
