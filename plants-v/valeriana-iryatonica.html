Valeriana iryatonica

Valeriana iryatonica, commonly known as the Irian Jaya valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian region of Irian Jaya.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often spicy-smelling leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous areas. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.

However, due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species.
