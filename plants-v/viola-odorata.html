Viola odorata

Viola odorata, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities. Viola odorata thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine highlights its cultural and botanical significance.
