Verbena elegans var. asperata

Verbena elegans var. asperata, commonly known as the roughleaf verbena, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage is narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly textured surface.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
