Verbesina fragrans

Verbesina fragrans, commonly known as the sweetroot or fragrant beggar-ticks, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Verbesina fragrans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
