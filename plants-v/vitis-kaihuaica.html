Vitis kaihuaica

Vitis kaihuaica, commonly known as the Kaihua grape, is a rare and endemic species native to China.

It is a woody vine belonging to the Vitaceae family, characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by berries that range in color from green to red. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
