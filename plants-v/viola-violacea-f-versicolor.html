Viola violacea f. versicolor

Viola violacea f. versicolor, commonly known as the variable violet, is a fascinating botanical variant of the common violet.

This plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its diverse flower colors, ranging from purple to white and pink. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of down, giving them a soft texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce multiple blooms throughout the growing season.
