Vigna khandalensis

Vigna khandalensis, commonly known as Khandal pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

Vigna khandalensis is valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
