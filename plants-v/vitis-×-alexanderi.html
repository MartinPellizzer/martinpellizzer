Vitis × alexanderi

Vitis × alexanderi, commonly known as Alexander's grape, is a hybrid vine species native to the western United States.

It is a cross between Vitis vinifera and Vitis berlandieri, resulting in a plant that exhibits characteristics of both parents. The vine produces small, sweet berries that are often used in winemaking and culinary applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its botanical features include heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing and spreading.
