Vaccinium warburgii

Vaccinium warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Vaccinium warburgii is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
