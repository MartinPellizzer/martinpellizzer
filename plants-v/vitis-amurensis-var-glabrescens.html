Vitis amurensis var. glabrescens

Vitis amurensis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Amur grape, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically lance-shaped and hairy when young. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. It is adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for cultivation in gardens and vineyards.
