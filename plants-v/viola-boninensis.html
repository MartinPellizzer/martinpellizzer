Viola boninensis

Viola boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin violet, is a rare and endangered species native to the Bonin Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped leaves and violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near coastal areas. Its flowers are characterized by their delicate petals and a distinctive purple hue.

Due to habitat loss and invasive species, it is critically threatened in its natural range.
