Vicia iberica

Vicia iberica, commonly known as the Iberian vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Vicia iberica produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
