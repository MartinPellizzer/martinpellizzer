Veronica baylyi

Veronica baylyi, commonly known as the Bayly's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Veronica baylyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
