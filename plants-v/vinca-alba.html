Vinca alba

Vinca alba, commonly known as white vinca, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a soft, velvety texture and produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Vinca alba is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
