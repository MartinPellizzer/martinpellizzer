Vigna brachycarpa

Vigna brachycarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, round pods that contain several seeds, typically yellow or brown in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

Vigna brachycarpa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
