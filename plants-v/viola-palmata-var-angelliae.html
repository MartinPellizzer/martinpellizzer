Viola palmata var. angelliae

Viola palmata var. angelliae, commonly known as the Angell's violet, is a rare and beautiful flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the forest understory.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a threatened species in some regions.
