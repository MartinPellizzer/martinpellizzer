Vitex harveyana f. geminata

Vitex harveyana f. geminata, commonly known as the twinleaf vitex, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along streambanks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite and usually have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its unique habit of producing two leaves per node, giving it its "geminata" designation.
