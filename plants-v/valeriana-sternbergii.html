Valeriana sternbergii

Valeriana sternbergii, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features wiry stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous fruits known as caryopses.

Valeriana sternbergii is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
