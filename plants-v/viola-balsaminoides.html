Viola balsaminoides

Viola balsaminoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a deep green hue and produces striking, spurred flowers in shades of violet and purple. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, downward-facing blooms. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
