Viola × maxima

Viola × maxima, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is native to Europe and has been naturalized in various parts of the world.

Viola × maxima is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
