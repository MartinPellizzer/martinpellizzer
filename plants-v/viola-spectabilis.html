Viola spectabilis

Viola spectabilis, commonly known as the showy viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding color to forest floors and gardens.

Viola spectabilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to support local pollinators.
