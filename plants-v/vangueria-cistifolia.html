Vangueria cistifolia

Vangueria cistifolia, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, fig-like fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Vangueria cistifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
