Vitis sicyoides

Vitis sicyoides, commonly known as the Sicilian grape or wild grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its climbing vines, palmate leaves, and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its berries are typically dark blue to black when mature and are often used in local cuisines or traditional medicine.

Vitis sicyoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in viticulture.
