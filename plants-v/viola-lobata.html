Viola lobata

Viola lobata, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, are often purple or blue and are attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola lobata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
