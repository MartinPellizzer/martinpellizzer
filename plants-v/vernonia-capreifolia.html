Vernonia capreifolia

Vernonia capreifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.

The plant's ability to thrive in partial shade and its drought tolerance contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
