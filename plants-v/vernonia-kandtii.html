Vernonia kandtii

Vernonia kandtii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads. Vernonia kandtii thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and landscapes.
