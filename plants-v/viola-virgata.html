Viola virgata

Viola virgata, commonly known as the Virginia violet, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Viola virgata produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
