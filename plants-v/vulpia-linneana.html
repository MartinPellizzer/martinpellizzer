Vulpia linneana

Vulpia linneana, commonly known as redgrass, is a species of grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing, perennial plant that typically forms dense mats on dry, disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish flowers. It is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and other open areas with poor soil conditions.

Vulpia linneana is adapted to withstand drought and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
