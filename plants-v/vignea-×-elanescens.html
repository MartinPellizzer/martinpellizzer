Vignea × elanescens

Vignea × elanescens, commonly known as the hybrid cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible, nutrient-rich seeds similar to those of common cowpeas. Vignea × elanescens is valued for its adaptability to poor soils and its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen.

It is often cultivated for both its seeds and as a forage crop in agricultural systems.
