Viola muhlenbergiana var. minor

Viola muhlenbergiana var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in woodland gardens for its ornamental value.
