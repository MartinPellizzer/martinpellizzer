Virola amistadensis

Virola amistadensis, commonly known as the Amistad mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its large, spreading canopy and aromatic wood. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in some regions.
