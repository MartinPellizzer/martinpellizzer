Vernonia polyphylla

Vernonia polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and is often found in the southeastern states. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Vernonia polyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
