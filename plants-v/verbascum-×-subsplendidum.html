Verbascum × subsplendidum

Verbascum × subsplendidum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a plant in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is a hybrid between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum wulfenii, resulting in a plant with unique characteristics. The leaves are large, fuzzy, and gray-green, while the flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a spike. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This plant is cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
