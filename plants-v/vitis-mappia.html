Vitis mappia

Vitis mappia, commonly known as the Japanese grapevine, is a woody vine native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red or black when mature. Vitis mappia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
