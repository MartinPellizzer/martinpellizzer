Vernonia nandensis

Vernonia nandensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
