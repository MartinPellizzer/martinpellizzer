Verbesina perfoliata

Verbesina perfoliata, commonly known as the common beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Verbesina perfoliata is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas.
