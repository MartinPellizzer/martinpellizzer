Viola mirabilis var. vulgaris

Viola mirabilis var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common heartleaf violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple bloom, typically appearing in early spring. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
