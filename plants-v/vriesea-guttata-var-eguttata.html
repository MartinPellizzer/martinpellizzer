Vriesea guttata var. eguttata

Vriesea guttata var. eguttata, commonly known as the spotted vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spiky inflorescence that typically displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are often adorned with distinctive white or yellow spots. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
