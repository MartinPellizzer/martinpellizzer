Veronica biggarii

Veronica biggarii, commonly known as the Alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Veronica biggarii is often used in rock gardens and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
