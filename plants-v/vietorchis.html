Vietorchis

Vietorchis, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Vietorchis, is a group of flowering plants native to Southeast Asia.

These plants are typically epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of trees and other structures rather than in soil. They possess long, narrow leaves and produce intricate, showy flowers that often come in shades of white, pink, and purple. Vietorchis species are valued for their ornamental qualities and are often cultivated in greenhouses or as houseplants.

Their growth habits and floral structures reflect adaptations to their humid, tropical environments.
