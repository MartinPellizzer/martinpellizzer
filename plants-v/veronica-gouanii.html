Veronica gouanii

Veronica gouanii, commonly known as the mountain veronica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically produces opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Veronica gouanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
