Vigna paraguariensis f. latifolia

Vigna paraguariensis f. latifolia, commonly known as the Amazonian passionflower, is a variant of the passionflower genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that resemble a heart shape. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to attach to supports. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with a distinctive crown of stamens that resembles a crown of thorns.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
