Venidium canescens

Venidium canescens, commonly known as the white prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in open prairies and dry woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Venidium canescens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
