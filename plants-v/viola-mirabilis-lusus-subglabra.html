Viola mirabilis lusus subglabra

Viola mirabilis lusus subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved wonder violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and vibrant purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often marked with a distinctive yellow or white spur.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
