Viola rostrata f. trirostrata

Viola rostrata f. trirostrata, commonly known as the three-beaked violet, is a botanical variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-beaked flower structure, which gives it its scientific name. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces small, violet-colored flowers during the spring season.
