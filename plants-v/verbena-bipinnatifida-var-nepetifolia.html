Verbena bipinnatifida var. nepetifolia

Verbena bipinnatifida var. nepetifolia, commonly known as the purple verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular, violet or purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are finely toothed, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
