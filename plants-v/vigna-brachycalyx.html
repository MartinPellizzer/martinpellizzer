Vigna brachycalyx

Vigna brachycalyx, commonly known as the short calyx cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

Vigna brachycalyx is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
