Viola levieri

Viola levieri, commonly known as the Levieri's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet. Viola levieri is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its blooming period usually occurs in spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape during that time.
