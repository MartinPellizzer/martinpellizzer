Verbesina macrophylla var. macrophylla

Verbesina macrophylla var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved beggar-tick, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its fibrous roots and hairy stems make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
