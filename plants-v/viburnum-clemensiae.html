Viburnum clemensiae

Viburnum clemensiae, commonly known as the Mexican snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Viburnum clemensiae typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.
