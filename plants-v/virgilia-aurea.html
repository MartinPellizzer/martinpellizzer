Virgilia aurea

Virgilia aurea, commonly known as the golden virgilia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in spring and are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight reddish tinge.

Virgilia aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
