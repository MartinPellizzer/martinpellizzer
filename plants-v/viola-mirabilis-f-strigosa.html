Viola mirabilis f. strigosa

Viola mirabilis f. strigosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf viola, is a hybrid cultivar of the viola genus characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

It typically features heart-shaped leaves with a thick, fuzzy texture that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, downward-facing flowers in shades of violet or purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
