Vernonia verbascifolia var. laxa

Vernonia verbascifolia var. laxa, commonly known as the laxa vernonia, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
