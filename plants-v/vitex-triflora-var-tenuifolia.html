Vitex triflora var. tenuifolia

Vitex triflora var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is valued in traditional medicine and horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability.
