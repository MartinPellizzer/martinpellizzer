Vanieria cochinchinensis var. gerontogea

Vanieria cochinchinensis var. gerontogea, commonly known as the golden bamboo or golden grass, is a variant of the bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescences, which contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and exhibits a clump-forming growth habit. Its unique coloration makes it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
