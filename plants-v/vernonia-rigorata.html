Vernonia rigorata

Vernonia rigorata, commonly known as the rigid ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture, and its stems are covered in fine, stiff hairs.

Vernonia rigorata is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
