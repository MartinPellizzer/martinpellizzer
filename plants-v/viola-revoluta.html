Viola revoluta

Viola revoluta, commonly known as the purple violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shady woodland environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive revolute margin, and its flowers are typically violet or purple in color. Viola revoluta produces capsule fruits that contain several small, dark seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
