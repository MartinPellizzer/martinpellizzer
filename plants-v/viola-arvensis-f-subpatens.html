Viola arvensis f. subpatens

Viola arvensis f. subpatens, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, often with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically violet or blue, with five petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
