Veronica longifolia var. grandis

Veronica longifolia var. grandis, commonly known as the tall speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its extended blooming period, which can last from spring through early summer.
