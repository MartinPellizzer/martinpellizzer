Verbena regnelliana

Verbena regnelliana, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and often hairy.

Verbena regnelliana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
