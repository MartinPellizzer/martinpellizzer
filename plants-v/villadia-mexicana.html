Villadia mexicana

Villadia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican thornapple, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
