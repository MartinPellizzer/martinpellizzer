Viburnum ichangense var. atratocarpum

Viburnum ichangense var. atratocarpum, commonly known as the black-fruited viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark purple to black berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
