Vicia glauca var. montisferrati

Vicia glauca var. montisferrati, commonly known as the Montisferrati vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in racemes, and bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.
