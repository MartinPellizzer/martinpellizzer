Viola × einseleana

Viola × einseleana, commonly known as the velvetleaf viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and vibrant, spurred flowers that come in a range of colors, including purple, blue, and white. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and garden beds. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
