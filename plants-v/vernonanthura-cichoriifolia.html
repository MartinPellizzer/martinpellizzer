Vernonanthura cichoriifolia

Vernonanthura cichoriifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
