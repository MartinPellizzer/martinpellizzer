Vernonia psilophylla

Vernonia psilophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
