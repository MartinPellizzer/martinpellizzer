Viola lewisiana

Viola lewisiana, commonly known as Lewis's violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from purple to white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It is often found in regions with cool climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
