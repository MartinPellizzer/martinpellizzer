Verbena setosa

Verbena setosa, commonly known as hairy verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, hairy leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Verbena setosa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
