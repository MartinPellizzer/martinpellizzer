Verbena urticifolia var. simplex

Verbena urticifolia var. simplex, commonly known as the smooth verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and ecological adaptations.
