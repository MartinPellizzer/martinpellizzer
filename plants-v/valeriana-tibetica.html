Valeriana tibetica

Valeriana tibetica, commonly known as Tibetan valerian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its rhizomes and roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana tibetica is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its delicate foliage and showy blooms.
