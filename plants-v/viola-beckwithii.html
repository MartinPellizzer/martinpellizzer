Viola beckwithii

Viola beckwithii, commonly known as the Beckwith's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high elevations. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Viola beckwithii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
