Vangueria canthioides

Vangueria canthioides, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features oval, leathery leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
