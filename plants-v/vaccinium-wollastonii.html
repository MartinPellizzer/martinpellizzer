Vaccinium wollastonii

Vaccinium wollastonii, commonly known as the Wollaston huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage consists of oval, dark green leaves that remain on the plant year-round.

Vaccinium wollastonii thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
