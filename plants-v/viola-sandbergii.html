Viola sandbergii

Viola sandbergii, commonly known as the sandberg's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces showy violet flowers with a yellow center. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Viola sandbergii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
