Viola prenja

Viola prenja, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola prenja is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
