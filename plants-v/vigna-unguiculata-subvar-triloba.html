Vigna unguiculata subvar. triloba

Vigna unguiculata subvar. triloba, commonly known as the African cowpea or sickle bean, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and elongated, curved pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a valuable crop in regions with limited water resources. It fixes nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, improving soil fertility.

The seeds are rich in protein and are widely cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed.
