Vicia alba

Vicia alba, commonly known as the white vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. Vicia alba is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in fields.

It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
