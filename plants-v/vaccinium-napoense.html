Vaccinium napoense

Vaccinium napoense, commonly known as the Napo huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acidic-tolerant plants. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features include evergreen leaves and a compact growth habit.
