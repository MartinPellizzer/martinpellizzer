Vernonia altissima f. alba

Vernonia altissima f. alba, commonly known as white ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

It serves as a valuable nectar source for various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
