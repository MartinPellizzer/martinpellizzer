Viola albida var. suavis

Viola albida var. suavis, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the woodlands of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.

Its delicate appearance and pleasant aroma make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
