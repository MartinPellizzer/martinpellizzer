Vanilla aromatica var. bicolor

Vanilla aromatica var. bicolor, commonly known as the bicolor vanilla orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a climbing orchid that produces fragrant, yellow-green flowers with distinctive bicolor patterns. The plant's leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy texture and a slightly serrated margin. Its inflorescences are clustered and emit a sweet, vanilla-like aroma, which is characteristic of the genus Vanilla.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its unique fragrance, making it a popular choice in horticultural collections.
