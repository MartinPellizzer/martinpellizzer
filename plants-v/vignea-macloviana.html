Vignea macloviana

Vignea macloviana, commonly known as the Maclovian vine, is a flowering plant native to the humid regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red upon maturity. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Vignea macloviana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
