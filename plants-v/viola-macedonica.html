Viola macedonica

Viola macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola macedonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
