Valeriana elata

Valeriana elata, commonly known as the blue valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides. It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.

Valeriana elata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
