Verbesina encelioides var. cana

Verbesina encelioides var. cana, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are rough to the touch and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period typically occurs from July to September, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
