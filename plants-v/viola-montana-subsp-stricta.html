Viola montana subsp. stricta

Viola montana subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet or blue. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to withstand cold temperatures.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is often considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
