Vernonia scapigera

Vernonia scapigera, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces showy blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Vernonia scapigera thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
