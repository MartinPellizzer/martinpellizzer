Viola × convicta

Viola × convicta, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from purple to violet. The plant is native to Europe and has been naturalized in many parts of the world. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola × convicta is often found in woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these habitats.
