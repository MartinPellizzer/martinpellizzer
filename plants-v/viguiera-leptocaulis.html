Viguiera leptocaulis

Viguiera leptocaulis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed bluestem, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It features narrow, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer.

Viguiera leptocaulis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
