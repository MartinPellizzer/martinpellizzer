Valeriana mandonii subsp. andina

Valeriana mandonii subsp.

andina, commonly known as Andean valerian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the summer months.

The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade. Valeriana mandonii subsp.

andina is valued for its medicinal uses and its ability to adapt to harsh alpine environments.
