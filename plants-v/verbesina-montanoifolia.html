Verbesina montanoifolia

Verbesina montanoifolia, commonly known as the mountain verbena, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a desirable plant in native gardens.
