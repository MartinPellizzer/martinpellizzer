Vaccinium cruentum

Vaccinium cruentum, commonly known as the red huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a bluish-green color.

Vaccinium cruentum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions and open woodlands.
