Volkameria verticillata

Volkameria verticillata, commonly known as the mountain glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Volkameria verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
