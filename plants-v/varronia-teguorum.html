Varronia teguorum

Varronia teguorum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptic leaves with a glossy green surface. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
