Viola eriocarpa

Viola eriocarpa, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Viola eriocarpa is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
