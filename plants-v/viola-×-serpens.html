Viola × serpens

Viola × serpens, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a hybrid species in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces showy flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola × serpens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
