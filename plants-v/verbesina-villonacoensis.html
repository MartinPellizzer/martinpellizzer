Verbesina villonacoensis

Verbesina villonacoensis, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Puerto Rico and the Virgin Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as coastal areas and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and erect.

Verbesina villonacoensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
