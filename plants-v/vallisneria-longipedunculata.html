Vallisneria longipedunculata

Vallisneria longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, making it a prominent feature in freshwater ecosystems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, which help stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Vallisneria longipedunculata is often used in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
