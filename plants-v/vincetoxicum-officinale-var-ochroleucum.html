Vincetoxicum officinale var. ochroleucum

Vincetoxicum officinale var. ochroleucum, commonly known as white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
