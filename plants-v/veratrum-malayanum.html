Veratrum malayanum

Veratrum malayanum, commonly known as the Malayan buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.
