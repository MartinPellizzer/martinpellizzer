Vicia argentea subsp. serinica

Vicia argentea subsp.

serinica, commonly known as silver vetch, is a perennial legume native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its slender, erect stems that produce clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods are slender, hairy, and contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Vicia argentea subsp.

serinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
