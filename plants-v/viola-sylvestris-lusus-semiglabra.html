Viola sylvestris lusus semiglabra

Viola sylvestris lusus semiglabra, commonly known as the hairy wood violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodland areas and has a compact growth habit. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.
