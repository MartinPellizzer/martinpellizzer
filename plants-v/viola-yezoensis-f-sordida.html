Viola yezoensis f. sordida

Viola yezoensis f. sordida, commonly known as the sordid violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage and flowers are notable for their muted, subdued coloration, which gives it its common name.
