Vateria indica

Vateria indica, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic heartwood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark and lanceolate, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces round, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Vateria indica is often cultivated for its essential oils and is also used in traditional medicine.
