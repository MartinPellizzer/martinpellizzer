Vauquelinia californica subsp. pauciflora

Vauquelinia californica subsp.

pauciflora, commonly known as the California vauquelinia, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to coastal areas of California. It features small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and dry summers.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
