Veronica austriaca proles jacquinii

Veronica austriaca proles jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems that give it a ladder-like appearance, hence its common name. Native to central and southern Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially sunny environments.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
