Veronica verna var. succulenta

Veronica verna var. succulenta, commonly known as the spring speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, early-blooming ground cover.
