Vigna comosa subsp. comosa

Vigna comosa subsp.

comosa, commonly known as the hairy pinto bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several round, yellow to brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often grown for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

The subspecies is noted for its adaptability to various climatic conditions, making it a valuable crop in agricultural systems.
