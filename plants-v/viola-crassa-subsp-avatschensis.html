Viola crassa subsp. avatschensis

Viola crassa subsp.

avatschensis, commonly known as the Avatschen violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its subspecific traits, including leaf morphology and floral structure, which set it apart from other members of the Viola genus.
