Vernonia mutimushii

Vernonia mutimushii, commonly known as the Kikuyu Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, often in semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
