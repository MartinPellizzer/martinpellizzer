Verticordia chrysantha var. preissii

Verticordia chrysantha var. preissii, commonly known as the golden wattle or yellow verticordia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native region.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
