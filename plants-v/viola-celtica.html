Viola celtica

Viola celtica, commonly known as the Celtic violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, fragrant blossoms that bloom in early spring.

Viola celtica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
