Vitex polygama var. hirsuta

Vitex polygama var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy chaste tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and flowering display.
