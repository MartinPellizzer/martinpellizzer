Viola mandshurica f. macrantha

Viola mandshurica f. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and botanical collections.
