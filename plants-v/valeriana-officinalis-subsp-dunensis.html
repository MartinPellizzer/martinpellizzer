Valeriana officinalis subsp. dunensis

Valeriana officinalis subsp.

dunensis, commonly known as the Scottish valerian, is a perennial herb native to the British Isles. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

The plant features aromatic, dark green leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in terminal clusters. It is characterized by its strong, pleasant scent, which is attributed to the presence of volatile oils.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
