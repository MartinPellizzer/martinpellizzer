Veronica spicata subsp. velutina

Veronica spicata subsp.

velutina, commonly known as velvet veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name and contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
