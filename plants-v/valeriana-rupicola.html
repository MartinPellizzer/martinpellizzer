Valeriana rupicola

Valeriana rupicola, commonly known as the rock valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it produces a distinctive, sometimes spicy aroma.

Valeriana rupicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
