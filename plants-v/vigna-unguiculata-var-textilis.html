Vigna unguiculata var. textilis

Vigna unguiculata var. textilis, commonly known as the African cowpea, is a legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and distinctive yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, fibrous pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
