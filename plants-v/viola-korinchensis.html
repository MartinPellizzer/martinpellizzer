Viola korinchensis

Viola korinchensis, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to alpine and rocky habitats.

Viola korinchensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
