Verbascum × horakii

Verbascum × horakii, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, woolly leaves and tall, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
