Viola calcarata var. grandiflora

Viola calcarata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
