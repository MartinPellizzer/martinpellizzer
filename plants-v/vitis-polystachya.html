Vitis polystachya

Vitis polystachya, commonly known as the Chinese grapevine, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Vitis polystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
