Veronica avromanica

Veronica avromanica, commonly known as the blueweed or wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica avromanica is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
