Viola odorata f. sulfurea

Viola odorata f. sulfurea, commonly known as the sulfur-yellow violet, is a rare and striking cultivar of the common violet.

It is characterized by its vibrant, sulfur-yellow flowers that stand out against its deep green foliage. This hybrid exhibits a unique coloration due to specific genetic traits that enhance the pigmentation of its petals. The plant is typically compact, with heart-shaped leaves and a short, bushy growth habit.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
