Vadulia longipes

Vadulia longipes, commonly known as the long-footed veldt grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. Vadulia longipes is typically found in grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
