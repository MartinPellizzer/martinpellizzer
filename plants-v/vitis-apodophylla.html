Vitis apodophylla

Vitis apodophylla, commonly known as the leafless grapevine, is a unique species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, which is an adaptation to extreme drought conditions. Instead of leaves, it has modified stems that perform photosynthesis. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are typically green when mature.

It is a climbing vine that thrives in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
