Vierhapperia hieraciphylla

Vierhapperia hieraciphylla, commonly known as the golden tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like structures called cypselae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.
