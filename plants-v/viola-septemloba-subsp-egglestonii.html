Viola septemloba subsp. egglestonii

Viola septemloba subsp.

egglestonii, commonly known as the Eggleston's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with seven lobes, which give it its scientific name.

The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species within its native habitat.
