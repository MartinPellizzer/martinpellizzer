Vinca mixta

Vinca mixta, commonly known as the common periwinkle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant typically grows in a trailing or spreading habit, making it suitable for ground cover or hanging baskets. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while its stems are woody and slightly succulent.

Vinca mixta is native to the Mediterranean region and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
