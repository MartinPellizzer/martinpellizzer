Vigna repens var. lutea

Vigna repens var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow prostrate pigeonpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows in a sprawling, vine-like manner, often covering the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by lens-shaped pods containing several seeds. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible seeds.
