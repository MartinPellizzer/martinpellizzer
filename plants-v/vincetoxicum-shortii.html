Vincetoxicum shortii

Vincetoxicum shortii, commonly known as the Himalayan beauty or Himalayan false bryony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
