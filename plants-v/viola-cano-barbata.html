Viola cano-barbata

Viola cano-barbata, commonly known as the canyon beardtongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive beard-like structure on the lower lip. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Viola cano-barbata is adapted to arid environments and is typically found in open, sunny habitats.
