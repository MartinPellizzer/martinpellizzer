Viola jacquiniana

Viola jacquiniana, commonly known as the Jacob's ladder violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often arranged in a cascading pattern. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
