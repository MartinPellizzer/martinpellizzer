Vigna debilis

Vigna debilis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clustered flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used in various culinary applications. Vigna debilis is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
