Veronica

Veronica, commonly known as speedwell, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Plantaginaceae.

These plants are characterized by their small, often blue or purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves. They are typically found in temperate regions and thrive in well-drained soils. Veronica species are often used in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Some species are also cultivated for their medicinal properties.
