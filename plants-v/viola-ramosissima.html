Viola ramosissima

Viola ramosissima, commonly known as the spreading violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive violet or purple hue, and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of violet, purple, or white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.

Viola ramosissima is also a host plant for several species of butterflies and moths.
