Vitis mossambicensis

Vitis mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly Mozambique, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. Vitis mossambicensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and potential for use in horticulture and conservation efforts.
