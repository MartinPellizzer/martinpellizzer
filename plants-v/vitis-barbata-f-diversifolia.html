Vitis barbata f. diversifolia

Vitis barbata f. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated grapevine, is a unique cultivar of the grapevine species Vitis barbata.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant is a deciduous vine that typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

While it is not widely cultivated for fruit production, it is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
