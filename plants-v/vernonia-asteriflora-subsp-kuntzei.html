Vernonia asteriflora subsp. kuntzei

Vernonia asteriflora subsp.

kuntzei, commonly known as the Kuntze's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
