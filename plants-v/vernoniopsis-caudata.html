Vernoniopsis caudata

Vernoniopsis caudata, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in disturbed soils. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and has small, composite flower heads.
