Viola montana subsp. schultzii

Viola montana subsp.

schultzii, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. It has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology and floral structure, making it a notable species in its native habitat.
