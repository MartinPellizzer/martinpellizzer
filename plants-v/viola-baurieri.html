Viola baurieri

Viola baurieri, commonly known as the Sierra Madre viola, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soil, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Viola baurieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
