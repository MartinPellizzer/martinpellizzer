Vernonia katangensis

Vernonia katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its yellow flower heads consist of numerous small florets, and it is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Vernonia katangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
