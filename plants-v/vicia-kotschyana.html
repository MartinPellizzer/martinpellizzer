Vicia kotschyana

Vicia kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschy's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, trailing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom in early spring. Vicia kotschyana is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically valuable in soil enrichment.
