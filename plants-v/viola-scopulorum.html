Viola scopulorum

Viola scopulorum, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, such as cliffs, walls, and dry meadows. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet or purple flowers with five petals. It is often found in sunny, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Viola scopulorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
