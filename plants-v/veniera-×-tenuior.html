Veniera × tenuior

Veniera × tenuior, commonly known as the slender veniera, is a hybrid plant species resulting from the cross between Veniera rubra and Veniera montana.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forest regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
