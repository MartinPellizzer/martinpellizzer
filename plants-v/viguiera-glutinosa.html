Viguiera glutinosa

Viguiera glutinosa, commonly known as sticky blanketweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, sticky stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

Viguiera glutinosa is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
