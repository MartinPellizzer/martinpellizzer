Viburnum opulus f. pygmaeum

Viburnum opulus f. pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf European snowball, is a compact shrub native to Europe.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in autumn. The plant has ovate, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

Viburnum opulus f. pygmaeum prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
