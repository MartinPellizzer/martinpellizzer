Vitex gabunensis var. gabunensis

Vitex gabunensis var. gabunensis, commonly known as the Gabon vitex, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by smooth, spherical berries that turn from green to black as they mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist forested areas and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
