Vitis hastata

Vitis hastata, commonly known as the prickly vine or sand grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the central and eastern United States, where it typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and small, five-lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible but have a tart flavor.

Vitis hastata is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
