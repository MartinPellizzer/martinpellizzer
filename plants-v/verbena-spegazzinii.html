Verbena spegazzinii

Verbena spegazzinii, commonly known as the pink verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, vibrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, four-lobed leaves that are typically hairy and grayish-green in appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena spegazzinii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
