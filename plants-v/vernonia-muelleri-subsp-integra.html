Vernonia muelleri subsp. integra

Vernonia muelleri subsp.

integra, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer and early fall.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
