Vicia nipponica

Vicia nipponica, commonly known as Japanese vetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vicia nipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration.
