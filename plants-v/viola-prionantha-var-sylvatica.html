Viola prionantha var. sylvatica

Viola prionantha var. sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the forests of eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, often with a yellow or white throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
