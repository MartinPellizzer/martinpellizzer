Viola × skofitziana

Viola × skofitziana, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
