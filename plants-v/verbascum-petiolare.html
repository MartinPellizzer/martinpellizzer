Verbascum petiolare

Verbascum petiolare, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers clustered in terminal spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum petiolare is a perennial plant that is often used in herbal medicine and ornamental gardening.
