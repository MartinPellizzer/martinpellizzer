Vernonia gossweileri

Vernonia gossweileri, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a tall, woody shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong aromatic scent.

Vernonia gossweileri is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
