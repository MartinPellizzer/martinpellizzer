Viola septempunctata

Viola septempunctata, commonly known as the seven-spot violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple-tinged underside and produces showy violet flowers with seven dark purple spots. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 cm, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
