Vigna ornata

Vigna ornata, commonly known as the ornate cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. Vigna ornata is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are a good source of protein and essential nutrients.

Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms, depending on the climate and variety.
