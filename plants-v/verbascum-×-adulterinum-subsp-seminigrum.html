Verbascum × adulterinum subsp. seminigrum

Verbascum × adulterinum subsp.

seminigrum, commonly known as the dark-seed mullein, is a hybrid plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in dense, branching inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Its common name refers to the dark coloration of its seeds, which are small and oblong.
