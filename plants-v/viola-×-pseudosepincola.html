Viola × pseudosepincola

Viola × pseudosepincola, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a cross between *Viola sororia* and *Viola sparsa*.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America, typically growing to a height of 10–20 cm. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
