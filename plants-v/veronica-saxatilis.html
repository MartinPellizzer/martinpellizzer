Veronica saxatilis

Veronica saxatilis, commonly known as the stone vetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are blue or purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Veronica saxatilis is often found in alpine or coastal environments, where it withstands harsh conditions.
