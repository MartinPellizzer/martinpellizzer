Vernonia exserta

Vernonia exserta, commonly known as the tall ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are often subtended by bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. Vernonia exserta is typically found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun.

Its fibrous roots and robust stems make it a hardy species well adapted to various environmental conditions.
