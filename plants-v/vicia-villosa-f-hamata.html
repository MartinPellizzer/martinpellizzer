Vicia villosa f. hamata

Vicia villosa f. hamata, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including those that are poor in nutrients. It is often used as a cover crop due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Additionally, it has been cultivated for its forage value and ornamental appeal.
