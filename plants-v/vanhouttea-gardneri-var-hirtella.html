Vanhouttea gardneri var. hirtella

Vanhouttea gardneri var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy vanhouttea, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

The flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale yellow, and the plant produces small, woody fruits.
