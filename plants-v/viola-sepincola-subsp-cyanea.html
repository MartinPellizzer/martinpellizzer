Viola sepincola subsp. cyanea

Viola sepincola subsp.

cyanea, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and its flowers are bell-shaped, usually violet or blue in hue. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
