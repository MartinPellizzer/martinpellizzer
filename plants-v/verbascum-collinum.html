Verbascum collinum

Verbascum collinum, commonly known as the mountain mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, panicle-like arrangement. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and covered with a dense layer of silky hairs.

Verbascum collinum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
