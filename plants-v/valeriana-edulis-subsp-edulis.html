Valeriana edulis subsp. edulis

Valeriana edulis subsp.

edulis, commonly known as the edible valerian, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant is notable for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and tubular, pink or white flowers that attract pollinators.
