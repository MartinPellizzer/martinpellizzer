Viorna scottii

Viorna scottii, commonly known as the Scottish viorna, is a rare and striking member of the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Scottish Highlands, where it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, contributing to its evergreen nature.

Due to habitat loss, Viorna scottii is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
