Vicia litvinovii

Vicia litvinovii, commonly known as the Litvinov vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Vicia litvinovii is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential for use in restoration projects.
