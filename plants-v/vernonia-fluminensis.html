Vernonia fluminensis

Vernonia fluminensis, commonly known as the river vernonia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments, often near rivers or in wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia fluminensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
