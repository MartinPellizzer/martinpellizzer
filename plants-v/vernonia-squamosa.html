Vernonia squamosa

Vernonia squamosa, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features gray-green, fuzzy leaves that give it its common name, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Vernonia squamosa is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
