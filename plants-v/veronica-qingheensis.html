Veronica qingheensis

Veronica qingheensis, commonly known as the Qingheen Veronica, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
