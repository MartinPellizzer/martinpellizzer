Vachellia luederitzii var. luederitzii

Vachellia luederitzii var. luederitzii, commonly known as the Luederitz acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that appear in terminal clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
