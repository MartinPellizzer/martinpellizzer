Veronica kindlii

Veronica kindlii, commonly known as the desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, sky-blue to violet flowers clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Veronica kindlii is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy species in desert ecosystems.
