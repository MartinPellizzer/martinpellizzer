Veronica byzantina

Veronica byzantina, commonly known as the Byzantine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that features bright blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, attractive ground cover.
