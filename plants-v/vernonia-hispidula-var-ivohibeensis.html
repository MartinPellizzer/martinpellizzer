Vernonia hispidula var. ivohibeensis

Vernonia hispidula var. ivohibeensis, commonly known as the Madagascar ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or scrubland habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
