Veronica minor

Veronica minor, commonly known as lesser speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It features slender, creeping stems that produce small, blue or violet flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and gardens.

Veronica minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
