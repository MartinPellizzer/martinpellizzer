Vaccinium contractum

Vaccinium contractum, commonly known as the small blue huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its berries are edible, though they are often described as having a tart and slightly bitter flavor.

The shrub thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
