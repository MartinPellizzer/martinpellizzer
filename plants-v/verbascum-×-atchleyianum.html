Verbascum × atchleyianum

Verbascum × atchleyianum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
