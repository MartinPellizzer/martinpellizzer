Vitex madiensis var. angustifolia

Vitex madiensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf vitex, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves that are typically less than 2 cm wide. The plant produces small, fragrant, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid environments.
