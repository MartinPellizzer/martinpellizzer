Vangueria loranthifolia subsp. loranthifolia

Vangueria loranthifolia subsp.

loranthifolia, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that belongs to the family Loranthaceae. It is native to southern Africa and typically grows on the branches of host trees such as acacias and eucalyptus.

The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by forming symbiotic relationships with its host plants.
