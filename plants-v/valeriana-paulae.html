Valeriana paulae

Valeriana paulae, commonly known as the Pauline valerian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Valeriana paulae is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
