Vitis rubiginosa

Vitis rubiginosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its roots and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Vitis rubiginosa is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
