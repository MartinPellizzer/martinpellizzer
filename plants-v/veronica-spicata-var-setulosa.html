Veronica spicata var. setulosa

Veronica spicata var. setulosa, commonly known as the spiked speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
