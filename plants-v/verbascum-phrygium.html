Verbascum phrygium

Verbascum phrygium, commonly known as the Phrygian mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Verbascum phrygium is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
