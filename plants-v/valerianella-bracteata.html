Valerianella bracteata

Valerianella bracteata, commonly known as the common valerian, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and hairy leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

Valerianella bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
