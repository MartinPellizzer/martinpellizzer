Viola tricolor var. machadiana

Viola tricolor var. machadiana, commonly known as the Mexican violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Mexico and parts of Central America, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade. The flowers typically have three petals, with two larger ones and one smaller, spurred petal.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
