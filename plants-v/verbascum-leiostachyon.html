Verbascum leiostachyon

Verbascum leiostachyon, commonly known as the silky mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are soft to the touch. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats like roadsides and fields.

Verbascum leiostachyon is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory ailments.
