Vernonia senecionea var. adenocarpa

Vernonia senecionea var. adenocarpa, commonly known as the腺果铁线莲, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its fruits are follicles that are inflated and covered with glandular hairs, which aid in seed dispersal.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
