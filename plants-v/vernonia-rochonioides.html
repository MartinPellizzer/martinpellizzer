Vernonia rochonioides

Vernonia rochonioides, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is well adapted to dry environments and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
