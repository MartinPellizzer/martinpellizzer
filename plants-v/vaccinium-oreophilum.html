Vaccinium oreophilum

Vaccinium oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain blueberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

The leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture, and the plant is known for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
