Viola × chinoi f. sordida

Viola × chinoi f. sordida, commonly known as the gloomy violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black foliage and small, pale violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodlands or rocky crevices. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the flowers often appear in early spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to woodland gardens.
