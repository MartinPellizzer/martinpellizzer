Veronica rosea var. maroccana

Veronica rosea var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, upright stems and small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable species suitable for rock gardens and borders.
