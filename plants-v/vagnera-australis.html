Vagnera australis

Vagnera australis, commonly known as the Southern Vagnera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Vagnera australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
