Verbascum × fridae

Verbascum × fridae, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum phlomoides.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens and landscapes.
