Veronica verdagueri var. angustifolia

Veronica verdagueri var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, creeping stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, mat-like growths.
