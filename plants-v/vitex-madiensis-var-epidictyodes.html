Vitex madiensis var. epidictyodes

Vitex madiensis var. epidictyodes, commonly known as the African blueberry, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, which bloom in summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
