Vismia cayennensis subsp. tenuinervia

Vismia cayennensis subsp.

tenuinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined vismia, is a member of the Clusiaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves with prominent, parallel veins.

The flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in clusters, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
