Viola deseglisei

Viola deseglisei, commonly known as the violet of Deseglise, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a garden plant.
