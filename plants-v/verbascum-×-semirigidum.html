Verbascum × semirigidum

Verbascum × semirigidum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes various species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy leaves and showy flowers. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that are often covered in soft, hairy textures. Its flowers, typically yellow or orange, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
