Vaccinium laurifolium var. ellipticum

Vaccinium laurifolium var. ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic leatherleaf, is a variant of the leatherleaf viburnum.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region. The plant features elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by blue-black berries in the fall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
