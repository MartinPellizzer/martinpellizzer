Villaria marinduquensis

Villaria marinduquensis, commonly known as the Marinduque spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
