Viola brevistipulata f. incisa

Viola brevistipulata f. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive narrow, serrated margin. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native garden cultivation.
