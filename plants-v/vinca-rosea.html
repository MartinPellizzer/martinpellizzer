Vinca rosea

Vinca rosea, commonly known as the Madagascar jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has woody stems and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Vinca rosea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly used in horticulture and landscaping.
