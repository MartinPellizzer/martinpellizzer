Vicia pannonica var. purpurascens

Vicia pannonica var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple flowering vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
