Vitis labordei

Vitis labordei, commonly known as the Laborde's grapevine, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of southern France.

It is a climbing vine belonging to the Vitaceae family, characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. Vitis labordei is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and historical significance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
