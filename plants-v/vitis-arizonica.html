Vitis arizonica

Vitis arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that range in color from green to deep purple. Vitis arizonica thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along rocky slopes and in desert scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in regions with limited water availability.
