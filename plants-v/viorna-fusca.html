Viorna fusca

Viorna fusca, commonly known as the black alder, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins that hang from the branches, typical of the alder genus. Viorna fusca thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and wetlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
