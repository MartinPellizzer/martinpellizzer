Vincetoxicum cordifolium

Vincetoxicum cordifolium, commonly known as heartleaf false bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in moist, shady areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Vincetoxicum cordifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal but can be invasive in certain regions.
