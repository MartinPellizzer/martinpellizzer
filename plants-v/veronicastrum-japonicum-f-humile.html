Veronicastrum japonicum f. humile

Veronicastrum japonicum f. humile, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
