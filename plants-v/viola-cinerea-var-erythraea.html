Viola cinerea var. erythraea

Viola cinerea var. erythraea, commonly known as the purple violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple hue, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals and a yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
