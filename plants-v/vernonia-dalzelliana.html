Vernonia dalzelliana

Vernonia dalzelliana, commonly known as the red vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its foliage is narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces vibrant orange to red flower heads in the summer months.

Vernonia dalzelliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
