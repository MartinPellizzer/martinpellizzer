Viguiera drymonia

Viguiera drymonia, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring upright stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing pink to purple ray florets surrounding yellow disk florets.

Viguiera drymonia is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
