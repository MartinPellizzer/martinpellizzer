Viola hastata var. tripartita

Viola hastata var. tripartita, commonly known as the three-lobed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive three-lobed structure. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
