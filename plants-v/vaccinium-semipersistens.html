Vaccinium semipersistens

Vaccinium semipersistens, commonly known as the red-berried huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, while its small, red berries are edible and favored by both wildlife and humans.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various species.
