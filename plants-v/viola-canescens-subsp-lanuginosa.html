Viola canescens subsp. lanuginosa

Viola canescens subsp.

lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft pubescence.

The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white throat. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular species for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
