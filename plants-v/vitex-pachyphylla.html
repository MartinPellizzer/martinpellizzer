Vitex pachyphylla

Vitex pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved chaste tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, blooming in shades of purple or white. Its fruit is a dry, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Vitex pachyphylla is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
