Vitex doniana

Vitex doniana, commonly known as the African pride of Barbados, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to violet. The plant produces small, dry fruits called drupes, which contain a single seed. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
