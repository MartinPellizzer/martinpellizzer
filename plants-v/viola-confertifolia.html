Viola confertifolia

Viola confertifolia, commonly known as the clustered violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native garden landscaping.
