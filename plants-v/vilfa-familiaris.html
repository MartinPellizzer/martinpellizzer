Vilfa familiaris

Vilfa familiaris, commonly known as the common woodland violet, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and features heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Vilfa familiaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
