Valeriana florifera

Valeriana florifera, commonly known as the flowering valerian, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Valeriana florifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
