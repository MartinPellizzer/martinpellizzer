Viola buchaniana

Viola buchaniana, commonly known as the Himalayan violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola buchaniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
