Viola massonii

Viola massonii, commonly known as the Chinese violet, is a species of flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often adorned with distinctive markings.

Viola massonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
