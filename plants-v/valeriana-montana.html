Valeriana montana

Valeriana montana, commonly known as mountain valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana montana is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
