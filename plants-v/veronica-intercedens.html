Veronica intercedens

Veronica intercedens, commonly known as the blueweed or common speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are borne in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Veronica intercedens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
