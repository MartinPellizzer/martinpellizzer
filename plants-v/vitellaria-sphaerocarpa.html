Vitellaria sphaerocarpa

Vitellaria sphaerocarpa, commonly known as shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, woody fruits containing a single seed. The seeds are the source of shea butter, a valuable natural product used in cosmetics and food.

It plays an important ecological and economic role in the regions where it is found.
