Vernonia wakefieldii

Vernonia wakefieldii, commonly known as Wakefield's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and conical, purple to brown flower clusters. Its flowering period occurs in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia wakefieldii is often found in moist, open habitats like wetlands and prairies.
