Vanilla acuminata

Vanilla acuminata, commonly known as the black vanilla orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces fragrant, dark purple or black vanilla pods, which are used in both culinary and perfumery applications. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires a warm climate with consistent moisture. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-yellow, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific bee species.

The vanilla pods, when mature, develop a rich, aromatic flavor that is highly valued in the food and fragrance industries.
