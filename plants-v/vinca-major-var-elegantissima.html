Vinca major var. elegantissima

Vinca major var. elegantissima, commonly known as the Persian shield, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, shield-shaped leaves that come in a variety of colors, including green, silver, and red. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to form dense mats.
