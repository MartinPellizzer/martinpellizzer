Vitex annamensis

Vitex annamensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to purple, which bloom in summer. Its fruit is a dry, two-valved capsule that releases tiny seeds.

Vitex annamensis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
