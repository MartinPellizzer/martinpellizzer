Viburnum brachyandrum

Viburnum brachyandrum, commonly known as the dwarf snowball viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries that persist into winter. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Viburnum brachyandrum is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
