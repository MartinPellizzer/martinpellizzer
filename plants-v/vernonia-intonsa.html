Vernonia intonsa

Vernonia intonsa, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Vernonia intonsa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
