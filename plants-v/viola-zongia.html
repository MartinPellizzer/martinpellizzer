Viola zongia

Viola zongia, commonly known as the Chinese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats and prefers cool, moist environments. Viola zongia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It also has traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
