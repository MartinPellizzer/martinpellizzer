Vitex pubescens var. lilacina

Vitex pubescens var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive violet-blue flower cluster that blooms in late spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically dark purple when mature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its showy flowers.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
