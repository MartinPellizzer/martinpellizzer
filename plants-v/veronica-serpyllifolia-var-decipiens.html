Veronica serpyllifolia var. decipiens

Veronica serpyllifolia var. decipiens, commonly known as the false sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species well adapted to harsh, dry conditions.
