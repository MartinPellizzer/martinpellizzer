Vaccinium angustifolium f. nigrum

Vaccinium angustifolium f. nigrum, commonly known as black huckleberry, is a compact shrub native to North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas. Its leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
