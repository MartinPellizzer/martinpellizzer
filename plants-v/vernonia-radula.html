Vernonia radula

Vernonia radula, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

Vernonia radula is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
