Veronica tetragona subsp. subsimilis

Veronica tetragona subsp.

subsimilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant features small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, with four petals and a distinctive spurred shape. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
