Verbesina pedunculosa

Verbesina pedunculosa, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open woods.
