Vigna unguiculata subsp. protracta

Vigna unguiculata subsp.

protracta, commonly known as the protracted cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, prostrate growth habit and small, yellow flowers.

The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable crop for soil enrichment.
