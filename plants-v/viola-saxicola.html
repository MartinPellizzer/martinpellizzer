Viola saxicola

Viola saxicola, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from violet to white, often with a distinctive yellow or orange eye. Viola saxicola thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in shaded conditions.
