Vitis poiretiana

Vitis poiretiana, commonly known as the Chinese fox grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that are typically red or black when mature. Vitis poiretiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
