Vesicaria purpurea

Vesicaria purpurea, commonly known as the purple bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibacillaceae.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures that aid in trapping small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, while the bladders are the primary photosynthetic organs.

Vesicaria purpurea plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for microorganisms.
