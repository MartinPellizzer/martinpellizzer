Vepris mbamensis

Vepris mbamensis, commonly known as African wild ginger, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green fruits that contain several seeds, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
