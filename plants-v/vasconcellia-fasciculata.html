Vasconcellia fasciculata

Vasconcellia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered vasconcellia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Vasconcellia fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
