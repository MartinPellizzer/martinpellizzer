Veronica albiflora

Veronica albiflora, commonly known as the white vervain, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Veronica albiflora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant qualities.
