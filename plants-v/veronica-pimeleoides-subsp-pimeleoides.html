Veronica pimeleoides subsp. pimeleoides

Veronica pimeleoides subsp.

pimeleoides, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, branched stems and small, blue to violet flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
