Vaccinium praestans

Vaccinium praestans, commonly known as the highbush blueberry, is a species of shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible, tart berries that are harvested in the summer months. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Vaccinium praestans is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
