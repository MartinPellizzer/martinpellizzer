Viscum taenioides

Viscum taenioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is native to regions of Africa, including South Africa and Madagascar. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain sticky seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Viscum taenioides plays a role in its ecosystem by providing food for certain bird species and contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.
