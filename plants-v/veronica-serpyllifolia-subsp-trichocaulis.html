Veronica serpyllifolia subsp. trichocaulis

Veronica serpyllifolia subsp.

trichocaulis, commonly known as the hairy speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
