Vaccinium crassiflorum

Vaccinium crassiflorum, commonly known as the broadflower blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black when ripe. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Vaccinium crassiflorum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
