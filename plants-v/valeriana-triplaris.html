Valeriana triplaris

Valeriana triplaris, commonly known as the three-lobed valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, sometimes fragrant, leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that have three distinct lobes. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

Valeriana triplaris is often found in rocky or woodland habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
