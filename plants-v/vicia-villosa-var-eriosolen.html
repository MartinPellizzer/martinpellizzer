Vicia villosa var. eriosolen

Vicia villosa var. eriosolen, commonly known as hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink, and it is often cultivated as a cover crop or for forage. The plant is native to Europe and Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It has a deep root system that helps improve soil structure and fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Vicia villosa var. eriosolen is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
