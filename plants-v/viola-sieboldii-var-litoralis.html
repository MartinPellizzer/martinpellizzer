Viola sieboldii var. litoralis

Viola sieboldii var. litoralis, commonly known as the coastal violet, is a variant of the viola genus native to coastal regions of Japan.

This plant is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. The species is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in similar climates.
