Vernonia towaensis

Vernonia towaensis, commonly known as the Towaensis ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers in late summer.

Vernonia towaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
