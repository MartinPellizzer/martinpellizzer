Valeriana kirilovii

Valeriana kirilovii, commonly known as Kirilov's valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinct, spicy scent. Its root system is notable for containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

Valeriana kirilovii is valued for its potential calming properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
