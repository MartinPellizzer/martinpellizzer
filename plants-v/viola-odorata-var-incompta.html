Viola odorata var. incompta

Viola odorata var. incompta, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in woodland edges and shaded areas. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its medicinal and ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
