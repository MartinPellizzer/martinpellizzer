Vaccinium calycinum

Vaccinium calycinum, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in wetland or swampy habitats. Its small, leathery leaves are dark green and glossy, and it produces edible, red berries that are often consumed by wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its ornamental appeal in wetland restoration projects.
