Viola × vaudensis

Viola × vaudensis, commonly known as the Vaud violet, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Switzerland.

It is a herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped, dark green leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in shaded woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also notable for its role in pollination by bees and other insects.
