Veronica taurica subsp. bordzilowskii

Veronica taurica subsp.

bordzilowskii, commonly known as the Caucasian speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features small, blue to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in rocky or mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
