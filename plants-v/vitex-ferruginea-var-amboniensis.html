Vitex ferruginea var. amboniensis

Vitex ferruginea var. amboniensis, commonly known as the Ambon ironwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are clustered in dense panicles and typically bloom in shades of violet or purple.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its ecological and horticultural significance in its native habitat.
