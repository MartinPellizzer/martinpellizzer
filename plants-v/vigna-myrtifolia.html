Vigna myrtifolia

Vigna myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its nutritious seeds and ornamental value.

Vigna myrtifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial plant in agricultural systems.
