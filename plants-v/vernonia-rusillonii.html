Vernonia rusillonii

Vernonia rusillonii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces vibrant yellow to orange flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
