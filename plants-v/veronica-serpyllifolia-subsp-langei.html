Veronica serpyllifolia subsp. langei

Veronica serpyllifolia subsp.

langei, commonly known as the Lange's speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from late winter to early summer.

The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in open, rocky areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
