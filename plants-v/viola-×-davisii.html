Viola × davisii

Viola × davisii, commonly known as the Davis violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It is native to the eastern United States and is often found in the Appalachian region.

Viola × davisii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
