Viscum obscurum var. decurrens

Viscum obscurum var. decurrens, commonly known as the creeping mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white or pale green berries that are typically toxic to animals. The plant has a unique ability to absorb water and nutrients from its host through its haustoria, which are specialized root-like structures. It is often found in temperate regions and can form dense thickets on its host.

Its presence can have significant ecological impacts on the health of its host trees.
