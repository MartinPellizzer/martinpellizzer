Viburnum erosum var. ichangense

Viburnum erosum var. ichangense, commonly known as the Chinese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
