Vincetoxicum umbrosum

Vincetoxicum umbrosum, commonly known as black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that appear in clusters, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Vincetoxicum umbrosum is toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
