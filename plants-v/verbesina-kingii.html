Verbesina kingii

Verbesina kingii, commonly known as the King's velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with prominent orange centers. Its seeds are encased in a spiny, three-lobed capsule that splits open when mature.

Verbesina kingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
