Valeriana montana var. tarraconensis

Valeriana montana var. tarraconensis, commonly known as the Pyrenean valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often pinkish or white flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
