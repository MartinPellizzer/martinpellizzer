Vitis sola

Vitis sola, commonly known as the solitary grapevine, is a lesser-known species within the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are typically green when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a coarsely toothed margin.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued in some regions for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
