Veronica reuteriana

Veronica reuteriana, commonly known as the silver germander, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Veronica reuteriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
