Vulpia ligustica subsp. hispidula

Vulpia ligustica subsp.

hispidula, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet bearing two to three small, bracteate flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in stabilizing soil in various habitats.
