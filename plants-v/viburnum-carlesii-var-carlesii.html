Viburnum carlesii var. carlesii

Viburnum carlesii var. carlesii, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Its foliage is typically dark green, turning reddish in the fall, adding ornamental value throughout the growing season.

Viburnum carlesii var. carlesii thrives in partial shade to full sun and is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils.
