Veronica vendettadeae

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs.
