Vernonia myriantha

Vernonia myriantha, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and roadsides, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Vernonia myriantha is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
