Viola hirtipes f. grisea

Viola hirtipes f. grisea, commonly known as the gray viol, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. This subspecies is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in woodland and shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
