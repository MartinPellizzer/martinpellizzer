Vernonia calyculata

Vernonia calyculata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and streambanks. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads. Its blooms appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia calyculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
