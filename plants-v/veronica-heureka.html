Veronica heureka

Veronica heureka, commonly known as the blueweed or European speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue or purple, and arranged in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
