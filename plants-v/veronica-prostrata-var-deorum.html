Veronica prostrata var. deorum

Veronica prostrata var. deorum, commonly known as the creeping speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.
