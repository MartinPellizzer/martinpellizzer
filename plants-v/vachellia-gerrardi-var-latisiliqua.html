Vachellia gerrardi var. latisiliqua

Vachellia gerrardi var. latisiliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in poor soils.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock.
