Viola silvatica subsp. nemoralis

Viola silvatica subsp.

nemoralis, commonly known as the wood violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe. It typically grows in woodland understories and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple and violet. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals, and it is pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
