Vaccinium Laurifolium Var. Ellipticum

Laurifolium blueberry, scientifically known as Vaccinium laurifolium var. ellipticum, is a unique and lesser-known variety of blueberry native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. This plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, edible berries that range in color from blue to purple. Unlike common blueberries, it thrives in cooler, more humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish. Its distinctive appearance and flavor make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and small-scale cultivation. Due to its limited distribution and specific growing conditions, it remains a rare and intriguing subject for botanists and horticulturists alike.
