Valerianella cupulifera

Valerianella cupulifera, commonly known as the "lawn buttercup," is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive cup-like structure at the base, giving it its scientific name. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically rounded and hairy. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in cultivated areas.

Valerianella cupulifera is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread easily, making it both a common weed and a garden plant.
