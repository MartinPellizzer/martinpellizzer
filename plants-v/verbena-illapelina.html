Verbena illapelina

Verbena illapelina, commonly known as the Illapel verbena, is a flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Verbena illapelina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
