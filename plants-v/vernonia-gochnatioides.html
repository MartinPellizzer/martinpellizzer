Vernonia gochnatioides

Vernonia gochnatioides, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Vernonia gochnatioides thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry to moist habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
