Vernonia neogleasoniana

Vernonia neogleasoniana, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It is adapted to the tropical climate and is often found in dry, well-drained soils.

Vernonia neogleasoniana plays a role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
