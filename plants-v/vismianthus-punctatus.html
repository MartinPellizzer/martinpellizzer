Vismianthus punctatus

Vismianthus punctatus, commonly known as the spotted sandarac tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. Its leaves and bark contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
