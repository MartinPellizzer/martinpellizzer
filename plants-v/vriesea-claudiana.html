Vriesea claudiana

Vriesea claudiana, commonly known as the "Brazillian flame" or "red vriesea," is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that displays a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central pool that supports small aquatic life. Vriesea claudiana thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance make it a favored ornamental plant in both home and commercial settings.
