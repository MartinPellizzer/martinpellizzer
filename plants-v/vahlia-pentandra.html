Vahlia pentandra

Vahlia pentandra, commonly known as the Indian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring simple, alternate, and ovate leaves. The plant produces showy flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a woody stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
