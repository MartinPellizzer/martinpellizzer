Viola adriatica

Viola adriatica, commonly known as the Adriatic violet, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola adriatica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
