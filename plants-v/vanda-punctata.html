Vanda punctata

Vanda punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces pendulous, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, with a distinctive pattern of spots on the petals. Vanda punctata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
