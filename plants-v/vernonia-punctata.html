Vernonia punctata

Vernonia punctata, commonly known as the dotted ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its showy, yellow-to-orange flower heads appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
