Vitis quinquefolia var. hirsuta

Vitis quinquefolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy fiveleaf grape, is a wild vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with five distinct leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of edible berries. These berries are typically dark purple to black when ripe and are used in winemaking and culinary applications.

It thrives in woodland and moist environments, often climbing trees or shrubs for support.
