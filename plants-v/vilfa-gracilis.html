Vilfa gracilis

Vilfa gracilis, commonly known as the slender vilfa, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It features delicate, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to efficiently capture moisture from the air. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Vilfa gracilis thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among botanists and gardeners alike.
