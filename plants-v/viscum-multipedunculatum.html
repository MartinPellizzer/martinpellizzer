Viscum multipedunculatum

Viscum multipedunculatum, commonly known as the two-branched mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds, which in turn aid in seed dispersal. Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis.

Viscum multipedunculatum is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained conditions.
