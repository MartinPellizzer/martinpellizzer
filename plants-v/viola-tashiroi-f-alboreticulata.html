Viola tashiroi f. alboreticulata

Viola tashiroi f. alboreticulata, commonly known as the white-veined viola, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and pale purple to white flowers with distinctive veining. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle beauty to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
