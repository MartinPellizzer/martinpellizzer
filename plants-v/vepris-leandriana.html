Vepris leandriana

Vepris leandriana, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain smooth, black seeds. Its wood is valued for its scent and is used in traditional crafts and incense.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
