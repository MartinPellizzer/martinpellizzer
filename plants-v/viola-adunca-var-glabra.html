Viola adunca var. glabra

Viola adunca var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
