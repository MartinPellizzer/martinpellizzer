Viola guangzhouensis

Viola guangzhouensis, commonly known as Guangzhou violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in spring, producing a delicate fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
