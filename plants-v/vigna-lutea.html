Vigna lutea

Vigna lutea, commonly known as cowpea, is an annual legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and elongated, slender pods that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Vigna lutea is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and essential minerals.
