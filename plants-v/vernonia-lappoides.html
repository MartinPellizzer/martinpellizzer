Vernonia lappoides

Vernonia lappoides, commonly known as the blueweed or lapp's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its showy blue to violet flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has a fibrous root system and can spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
