Viola canina var. riviniana

Viola canina var. riviniana, commonly known as the river viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and woodland edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
