Vitex congolensis var. thomasii

Vitex congolensis var. thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's vitex, is a shrub native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in panicles. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
