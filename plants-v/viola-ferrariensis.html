Viola ferrariensis

Viola ferrariensis, commonly known as the Ferrari's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, soft pubescence.

Viola ferrariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
