Vitex sellowiana var. parviflora

Vitex sellowiana var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf chaste tree, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
