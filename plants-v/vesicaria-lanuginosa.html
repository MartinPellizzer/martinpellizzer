Vesicaria lanuginosa

Vesicaria lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibacillaceae.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures called vesicles, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. It has delicate, hair-like leaves that help it float and absorb nutrients from the water.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
