Verbesina polyanthes

Verbesina polyanthes, commonly known as the golden beggar's-ticks, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it grows in moist, sunny habitats like meadows and wetlands. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Verbesina polyanthes is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
