Vernonia petiolaris var. appendiculata

Vernonia petiolaris var. appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures, and the species is known for its hardy nature and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
