Verbesina tamaunuevana

Verbesina tamaunuevana, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical heads.

Verbesina tamaunuevana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
