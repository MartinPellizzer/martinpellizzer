Vernonia cronquistii

Vernonia cronquistii, commonly known as the Cronquist's ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, spires of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia cronquistii is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
