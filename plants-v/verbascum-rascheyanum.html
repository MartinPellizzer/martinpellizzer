Verbascum rascheyanum

Verbascum rascheyanum, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of soft, fuzzy hairs.

Verbascum rascheyanum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
