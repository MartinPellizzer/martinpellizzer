Valeriana rotundifolia

Valeriana rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaved valerian, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its rounded, aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana rotundifolia is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
