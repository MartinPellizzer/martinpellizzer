Vachellia davyi

Vachellia davyi, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Vachellia davyi plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used for soil stabilization.
