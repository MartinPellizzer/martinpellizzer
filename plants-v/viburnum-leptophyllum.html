Viburnum leptophyllum

Viburnum leptophyllum, commonly known as sand viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries that attract birds. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and turn yellow in the fall.

Viburnum leptophyllum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
