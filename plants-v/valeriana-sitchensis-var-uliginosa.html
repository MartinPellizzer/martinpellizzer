Valeriana sitchensis var. uliginosa

Valeriana sitchensis var. uliginosa, commonly known as the bog valerian, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of western North America.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, acidic soils, often found in bogs and swampy areas. The plant features slender, aromatic stems that bear small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Valeriana sitchensis var. uliginosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
