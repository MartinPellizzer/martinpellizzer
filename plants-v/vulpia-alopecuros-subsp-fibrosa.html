Vulpia alopecuros subsp. fibrosa

Vulpia alopecuros subsp.

fibrosa, commonly known as the fibrous wild oat, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Its fibrous structure and adaptability make it a resilient species in various ecological settings.
