Verbascum thapsus var. valentinum

Verbascum thapsus var. valentinum, commonly known as the Valentine's mullein, is a variant of the common mullein plant.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, fuzzy gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
