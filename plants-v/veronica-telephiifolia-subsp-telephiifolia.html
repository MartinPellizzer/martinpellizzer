Veronica telephiifolia subsp. telephiifolia

Veronica telephiifolia subsp.

telephiifolia, commonly known as the rock everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or sandy habitats. Veronica telephiifolia subsp.

telephiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
