Veronica urticifolia f. latifolia

Veronica urticifolia f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant is native to regions of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is typically hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
