Vachellia drepanolobium

Vachellia drepanolobium, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often reduced to spines, a feature that helps it conserve water in arid environments. It plays an important role in providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Its seeds are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological significance in savanna ecosystems.
