Verbascum longibracteatum

Verbascum longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its common name reflects the long, bract-like leaves that surround the flowering stems.
