Veronica fridericae

Veronica fridericae, commonly known as the Austrian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of flowers in early to mid-summer.

Veronica fridericae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
