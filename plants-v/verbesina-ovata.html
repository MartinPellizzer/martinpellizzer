Verbesina ovata

Verbesina ovata, commonly known as yellow crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small crowns. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbesina ovata is often found in open woods, prairies, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
