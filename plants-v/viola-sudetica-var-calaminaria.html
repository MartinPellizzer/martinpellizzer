Viola sudetica var. calaminaria

Viola sudetica var. calaminaria, commonly known as the Calaminaria violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in woodland edges and rocky areas. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are usually pale purple or white with a distinctive yellowish throat.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
