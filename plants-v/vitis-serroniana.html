Vitis serroniana

Vitis serroniana, commonly known as the Siberian grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Vitis serroniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in horticulture and ecological restoration.
