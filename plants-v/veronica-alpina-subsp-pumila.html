Veronica alpina subsp. pumila

Veronica alpina subsp.

pumila, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems.

The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
