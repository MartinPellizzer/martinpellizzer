Viola × egregia

Viola × egregia, commonly known as the showy violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to violet flowers, which are typically heart-shaped and often have a distinctive white or yellow eye. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola × egregia is known for its ability to spread through runners, making it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.
