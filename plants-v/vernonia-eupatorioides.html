Vernonia eupatorioides

Vernonia eupatorioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, misty appearance.

Vernonia eupatorioides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
