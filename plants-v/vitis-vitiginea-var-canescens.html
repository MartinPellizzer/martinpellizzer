Vitis vitiginea var. canescens

Vitis vitiginea var. canescens, commonly known as the sand grape or prairie grape, is a deciduous vine native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, slender stems and small, clustered leaves with a distinctive grayish-green color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are typically sour and tart. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
