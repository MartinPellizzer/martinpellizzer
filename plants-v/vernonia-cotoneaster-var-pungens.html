Vernonia cotoneaster var. pungens

Vernonia cotoneaster var. pungens, commonly known as the Pungent Vernonia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for xeriscaping.
