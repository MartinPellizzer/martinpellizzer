Vernonia hassleriana

Vernonia hassleriana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Vernonia hassleriana is valued in native gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
