Vellozia angustifolia

Vellozia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vellozia, is a striking member of the Velloziaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental value. Vellozia angustifolia is adapted to arid environments, showcasing succulent features that help it conserve water.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in regions with low rainfall.
