Viorna ochroleuca

Viorna ochroleuca, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds.

Viorna ochroleuca is often used in traditional medicine and is also a popular ornamental plant.
