Viola minima

Viola minima, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet, blue, or white, often with a distinctive yellow or white marking at the base of the petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Viola minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
