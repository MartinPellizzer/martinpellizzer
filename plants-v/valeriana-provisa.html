Valeriana provisa

Valeriana provisa, commonly known as the creeping valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a sprawling growth habit with slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Valeriana provisa is known for its aromatic foliage and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its calming properties.
