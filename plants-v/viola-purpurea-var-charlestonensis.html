Viola purpurea var. charlestonensis

Viola purpurea var. charlestonensis, commonly known as the Charleston violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive purple coloration, often with a yellow or white eye. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and pollinator habitats.
