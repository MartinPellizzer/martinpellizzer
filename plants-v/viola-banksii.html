Viola banksii

Viola banksii, commonly known as the Banks' violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, often with a white or pale margin. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Viola banksii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
