Viola litoralis subsp. litvinovii

Viola litoralis subsp.

litvinovii, commonly known as the coastal violet, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of eastern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and rocky areas.

The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals. It is adapted to salt-laden air and can tolerate periodic flooding.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and genetic distinctiveness.
