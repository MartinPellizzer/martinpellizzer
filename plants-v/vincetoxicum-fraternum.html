Vincetoxicum fraternum

Vincetoxicum fraternum, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
