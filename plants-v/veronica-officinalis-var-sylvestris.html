Veronica officinalis var. sylvestris

Veronica officinalis var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wood speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland areas and moist meadows, reaching a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small blue or violet flowers. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and bloom from June to August.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
