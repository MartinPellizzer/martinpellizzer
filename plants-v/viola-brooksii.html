Viola brooksii

Viola brooksii, commonly known as the Brooks' violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to forest floors.

Viola brooksii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
