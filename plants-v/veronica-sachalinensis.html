Veronica sachalinensis

Veronica sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and parts of eastern Siberia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
