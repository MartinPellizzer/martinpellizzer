Verbena intercedens

Verbena intercedens, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Verbena intercedens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
