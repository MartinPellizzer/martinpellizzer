Viola eugeniae

Viola eugeniae, commonly known as the Eugenia violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Viola eugeniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
