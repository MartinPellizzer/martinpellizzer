Viola albida f. viridis

Viola albida f. viridis, commonly known as the green viola, is a variant of the common violet characterized by its distinctive greenish foliage.

This plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually pale purple or white.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
