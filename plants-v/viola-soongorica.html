Viola soongorica

Viola soongorica, commonly known as the Karakorum violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet and blue. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the high-altitude landscape.

Viola soongorica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
