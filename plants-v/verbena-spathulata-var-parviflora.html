Verbena spathulata var. parviflora

Verbena spathulata var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf verbena, is a compact, flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies further enhance its ornamental value.
