Verbena megapotamica var. tweedieana

Verbena megapotamica var. tweedieana, commonly known as the Tweedie's verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse displays of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its foliage is covered in fine, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
