Vatica cinerea

Vatica cinerea, commonly known as the pale vatica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elongated leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It is often found in lowland and hill forests, thriving in humid, warm climates.

The tree is valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
