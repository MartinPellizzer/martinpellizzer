Valeriana cephalantha

Valeriana cephalantha, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces oval, ribbed berries that contain the seeds, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often aromatic. Valeriana cephalantha is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
