Valeriana germainii

Valeriana germainii, commonly known as Germain's valerian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana germainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
