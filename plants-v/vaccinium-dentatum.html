Vaccinium dentatum

Vaccinium dentatum, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other heath and blueberry plants. The plant has small, oval leaves with serrated edges and produces edible, dark red berries in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and pine flatwoods.

The berries are a food source for various wildlife, including birds and mammals.
