Verbascum orbicularifolium

Verbascum orbicularifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Verbascum orbicularifolium is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating respiratory ailments.
