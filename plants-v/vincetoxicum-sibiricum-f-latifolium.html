Vincetoxicum sibiricum f. latifolium

Vincetoxicum sibiricum f. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved Siberian bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are broad and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found along riverbanks and in forested areas.

Its growth habit is somewhat invasive, making it a notable species in ecological studies.
