Vernonia thomsoniana

Vernonia thomsoniana, commonly known as Thomson's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for gardens.

Vernonia thomsoniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
