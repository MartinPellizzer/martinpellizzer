Viola manaslensis

Viola manaslensis, commonly known as the Manaslu violet, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas, particularly in Nepal and India.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, purple-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
