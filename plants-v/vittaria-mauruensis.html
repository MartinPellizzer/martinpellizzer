Vittaria mauruensis

Vittaria mauruensis, commonly known as the Mauruensian fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually small and pinnately divided, with a distinctive pattern of veins.

Vittaria mauruensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
