Vitis ibuensis

Vitis ibuensis, commonly known as the Izu grapevine, is a species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous vine belonging to the Vitaceae family, characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
