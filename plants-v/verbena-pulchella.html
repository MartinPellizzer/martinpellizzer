Verbena pulchella

Verbena pulchella, commonly known as the scarlet verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. It produces small, four-lobed leaves and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant blooms.

Verbena pulchella is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
