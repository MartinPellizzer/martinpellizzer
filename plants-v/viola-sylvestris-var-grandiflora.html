Viola sylvestris var. grandiflora

Viola sylvestris var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple or violet coloration, and its flowers are typically violet or blue with yellow markings. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing nectar that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
