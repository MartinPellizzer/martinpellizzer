Verbena comberi

Verbena comberi, commonly known as the woolly verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbena comberi is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
