Viola resupinata

Viola resupinata, commonly known as the downy violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with yellow markings, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Viola resupinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
