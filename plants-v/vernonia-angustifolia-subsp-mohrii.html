Vernonia angustifolia subsp. mohrii

Vernonia angustifolia subsp.

mohrii, commonly known as Mohri's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and conical, spike-like flower heads that range in color from yellow to brown.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the dry season, making it an important source of nectar for local pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
