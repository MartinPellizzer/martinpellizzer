Vatica najibiana

Vatica najibiana, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is known for its tall, straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also considered a threatened species in its natural habitat.
