Vachellia quintanilhae

Vachellia quintanilhae, commonly known as the Quintanilha acacia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically small and delicate. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular heads, which are visited by various pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils and in savanna regions.

Vachellia quintanilhae is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its ornamental appeal.
