Veronica montbretii

Veronica montbretii, commonly known as the Alpine speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand cold and harsh alpine conditions.

Veronica montbretii is often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows, where it thrives in full sun.
