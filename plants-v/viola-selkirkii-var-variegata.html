Viola selkirkii var. variegata

Viola selkirkii var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
