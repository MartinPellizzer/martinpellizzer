Viburnum macrocephalum var. indutum

Viburnum macrocephalum var. indutum, commonly known as the velvetleaf snowball viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The leaves are typically ovate with a velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
