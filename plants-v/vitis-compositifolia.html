Vitis compositifolia

Vitis compositifolia, commonly known as the composite-leaf grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique compound leaves, which consist of multiple leaflets arranged in a distinctive pattern. The plant is native to certain regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its vines can climb using tendrils, and it produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible berries.

Vitis compositifolia is of interest to botanists due to its morphological features and potential ecological roles.
