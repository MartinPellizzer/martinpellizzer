Vilfa triniana

Vilfa triniana, commonly known as the desert violet, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to blue, often resembling violets. The plant has succulent leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. It produces small, round fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Vilfa triniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
