Verbena scordioides

Verbena scordioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, four-lobed leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Verbena scordioides is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
