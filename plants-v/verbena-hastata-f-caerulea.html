Verbena hastata f. caerulea

Verbena hastata f. caerulea, commonly known as blue verbena, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and prairies, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its slender stems are often branched, and it produces small, narrow leaves that are opposite in arrangement.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
