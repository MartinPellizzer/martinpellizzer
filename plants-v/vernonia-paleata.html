Vernonia paleata

Vernonia paleata, commonly known as slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn.

Vernonia paleata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
