Veronica rigidula var. sulcata

Veronica rigidula var. sulcata, commonly known as the serrated speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
