Vitis turbinata

Vitis turbinata, commonly known as the turban grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is often found in forested areas. The plant is characterized by its tendrils, compound leaves, and small, clustered flowers that develop into berries. Its vines can grow quite long, and the berries are typically dark purple or black when mature.

Vitis turbinata is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
