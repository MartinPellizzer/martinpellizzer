Vitex holocalyx

Vitex holocalyx, commonly known as the Java hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink or violet, often with a prominent yellow or orange center. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Vitex holocalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
