Veronica × obovata

Veronica × obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
