Vittaria ensata

Vittaria ensata, commonly known as the Japanese moss or the creeping forest moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Vittariaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in two rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. Vittaria ensata is often found in temperate forests, particularly in regions of East Asia.

Its ecological role includes contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
