Valeriana papilla

Valeriana papilla, commonly known as the papillate valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its distinctive papillate (bump-like) structures on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Valeriana papilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential calming properties.
