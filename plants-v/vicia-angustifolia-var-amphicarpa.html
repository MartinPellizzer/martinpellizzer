Vicia angustifolia var. amphicarpa

Vicia angustifolia var. amphicarpa, commonly known as narrowleaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and long, cylindrical pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, disturbed areas, and along roadsides.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for livestock and supporting pollinators.
