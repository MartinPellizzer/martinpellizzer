Viola takesimana

Viola takesimana, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces delicate violet flowers with purple petals and yellow centers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.

Viola takesimana prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or rock gardens.
