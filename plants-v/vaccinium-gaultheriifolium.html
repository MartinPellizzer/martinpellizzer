Vaccinium gaultheriifolium

Vaccinium gaultheriifolium, commonly known as the Alaska cranberry bush, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. This plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves and produces bright red berries that are edible. It thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in tundra and boreal forest environments.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
