Verbesina abietifolia

Verbesina abietifolia, commonly known as the mountain beggar-ticks, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Verbesina abietifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for various health purposes.
