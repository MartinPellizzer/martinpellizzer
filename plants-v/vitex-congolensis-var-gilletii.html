Vitex congolensis var. gilletii

Vitex congolensis var. gilletii, commonly known as the Congo vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white or pale blue, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are cylindrical, woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

This species is often found in moist forests and along riverbanks, contributing to local biodiversity.
