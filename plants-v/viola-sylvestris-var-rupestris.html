Viola sylvestris var. rupestris

Viola sylvestris var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodland or along stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet-blue or purple flowers with yellow markings. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to harsh weather.
