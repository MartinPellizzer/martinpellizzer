Viola × akirae

Viola × akirae, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola philippica and Viola yedoensis.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.
