Verbascum aureiforme

Verbascum aureiforme, commonly known as the goldenleaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy leaves that are covered in soft, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum aureiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
