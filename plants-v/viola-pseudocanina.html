Viola pseudocanina

Viola pseudocanina, commonly known as the slender violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding color to the landscape during the cooler months.
