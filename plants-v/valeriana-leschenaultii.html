Valeriana leschenaultii

Valeriana leschenaultii, commonly known as the Leschenault's valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Valeriana leschenaultii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
