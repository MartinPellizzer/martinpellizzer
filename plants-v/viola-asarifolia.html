Viola asarifolia

Viola asarifolia, commonly known as the asarifolia violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola asarifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
