Verbesina textitlana

Verbesina textitlana, commonly known as Mexican beggarticks, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 to 2 meters tall, featuring upright stems covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
