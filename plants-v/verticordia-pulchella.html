Verticordia pulchella

Verticordia pulchella, commonly known as the pink fairy or the pink wattle, is a striking evergreen shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, pink to magenta flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
