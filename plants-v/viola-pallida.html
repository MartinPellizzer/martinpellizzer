Viola pallida

Viola pallida, commonly known as the pale violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, pale green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in spring. Viola pallida thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
