Viola yezoensis var. discolor

Viola yezoensis var. discolor, commonly known as the Japanese pansy, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, colorful flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for garden borders and rock gardens.

Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, yellow, and white, adding visual interest to the landscape.
