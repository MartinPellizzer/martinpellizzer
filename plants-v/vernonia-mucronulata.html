Vernonia mucronulata

Vernonia mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-pointed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, dark purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Vernonia mucronulata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
