Vaccinium viscifolium var. minus

Vaccinium viscifolium var. minus, commonly known as the lesser trailing blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal or subarctic environments. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and adaptability to cold climates.
