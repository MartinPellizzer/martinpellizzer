Vernonia hirsutiflora

Vernonia hirsutiflora, commonly known as the hairyflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is known for its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, hairy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia hirsutiflora is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
