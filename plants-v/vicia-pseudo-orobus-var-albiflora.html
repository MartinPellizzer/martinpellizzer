Vicia pseudo-orobus var. albiflora

Vicia pseudo-orobus var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
