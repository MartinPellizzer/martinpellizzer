Verbascum aliciae

Verbascum aliciae, commonly known as the Alice's mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants commonly referred to as mulleins. The plant is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Verbascum aliciae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
