Viola lancifolia

Viola lancifolia, commonly known as the lancifoliate violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with sharply pointed tips and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and shaded environments, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola lancifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blossoms.
