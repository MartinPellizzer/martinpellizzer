Veronica macrantha

Veronica macrantha, commonly known as the largeflower speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, with clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.

Veronica macrantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its role in supporting local pollinators.
