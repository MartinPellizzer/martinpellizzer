Valeriana alba

Valeriana alba, commonly known as white valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its rhizomatous root system makes it adaptable to various soil types and environments.

Valeriana alba is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its sedative and calming effects.
