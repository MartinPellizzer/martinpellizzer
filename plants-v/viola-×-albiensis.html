Viola × albiensis

Viola × albiensis, commonly known as the white violet, is a hybrid species in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale or white petals, which distinguish it from other viola species. The plant typically has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola × albiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
