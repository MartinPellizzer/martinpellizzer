Veronica pseudocatenata

Veronica pseudocatenata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Veronica pseudocatenata is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
