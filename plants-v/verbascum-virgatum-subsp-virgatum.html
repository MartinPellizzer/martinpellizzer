Verbascum virgatum subsp. virgatum

Verbascum virgatum subsp.

virgatum, commonly known as the common mullein, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, silky white hairs.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose spike. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in soft, fuzzy hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.
