Vernonia kirungae

Vernonia kirungae, commonly known as the Kirungae ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia kirungae is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
