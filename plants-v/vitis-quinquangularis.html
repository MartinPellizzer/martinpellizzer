Vitis quinquangularis

Vitis quinquangularis, commonly known as the fox grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its distinctive five-angled stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and red or black berries that are edible but often tart. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.

Vitis quinquangularis is also valued for its potential in hybrid grape breeding programs.
