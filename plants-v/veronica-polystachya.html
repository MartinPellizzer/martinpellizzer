Veronica polystachya

Veronica polystachya, commonly known as the clustered speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, wiry stems that bear dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
