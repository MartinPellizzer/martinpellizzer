Veronica didyma f. pusilla

Veronica didyma f. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, clustered blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its delicate form and attractive flowers, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
