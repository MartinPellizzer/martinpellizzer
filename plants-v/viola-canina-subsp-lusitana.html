Viola canina subsp. lusitana

Viola canina subsp.

lusitana, commonly known as the Iberian violets, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly Portugal and Spain. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive floral display.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
