Vernonia flavescens

Vernonia flavescens, commonly known as yellow ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of yellow to orange-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a rough texture. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads.

Vernonia flavescens is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
