Viola brachyphylla

Viola brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stemmed violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to woodland gardens.

Viola brachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
