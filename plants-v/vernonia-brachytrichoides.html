Vernonia brachytrichoides

Vernonia brachytrichoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
