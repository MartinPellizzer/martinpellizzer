Viscum ochroleucum

Viscum ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-flowered mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pale green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange, containing sticky seeds that adhere to birds for dispersal.

Viscum ochroleucum is native to parts of Europe and is often found in temperate forests.
