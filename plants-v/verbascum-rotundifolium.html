Verbascum rotundifolium

Verbascum rotundifolium, commonly known as round-leaved mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, velvety leaves that are rounded and slightly lobed. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
