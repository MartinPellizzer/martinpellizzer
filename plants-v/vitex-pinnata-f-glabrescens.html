Vitex pinnata f. glabrescens

Vitex pinnata f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth vitex or velvetwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue or purple, which are followed by woody, bead-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
