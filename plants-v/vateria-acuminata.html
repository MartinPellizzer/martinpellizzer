Vateria acuminata

Vateria acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed vateria, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
