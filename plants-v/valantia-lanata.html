Valantia lanata

Valantia lanata, commonly known as the hairy valantia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Valantia lanata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
