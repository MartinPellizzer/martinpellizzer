Valeriana umbilicata

Valeriana umbilicata, commonly known as the umbilical valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana umbilicata is often found in moist, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
