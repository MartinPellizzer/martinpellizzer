Verbascum elongatum

Verbascum elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that are often covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose raceme. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Verbascum elongatum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
