Viola macloskeyi

Viola macloskeyi, commonly known as Macloskey's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Viola macloskeyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
