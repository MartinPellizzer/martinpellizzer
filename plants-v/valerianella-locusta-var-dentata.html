Valerianella locusta var. dentata

Valerianella locusta var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed mouse-ear or toothed valerian, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, gardens, and along pathways.

Valerianella locusta var. dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
