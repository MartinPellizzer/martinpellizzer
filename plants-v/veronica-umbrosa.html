Veronica umbrosa

Veronica umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, four-petaled flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. Its root system is fibrous and helps it anchor in rocky or loamy soils.

Veronica umbrosa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
