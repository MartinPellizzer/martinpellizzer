Vernonia macropoda

Vernonia macropoda, commonly known as the large-headed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive large flower heads, which are composed of numerous tiny florets.

Vernonia macropoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
