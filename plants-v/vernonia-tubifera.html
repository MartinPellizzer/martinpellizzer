Vernonia tubifera

Vernonia tubifera, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blossoms. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Vernonia tubifera is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
