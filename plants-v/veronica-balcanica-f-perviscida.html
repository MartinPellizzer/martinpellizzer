Veronica balcanica f. perviscida

Veronica balcanica f. perviscida, commonly known as the Balkan speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
