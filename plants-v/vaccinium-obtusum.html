Vaccinium obtusum

Vaccinium obtusum, commonly known as the broadleaf blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cranberries and heathers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are bell-shaped and typically pink or white, and it bears edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or swampy habitats.
