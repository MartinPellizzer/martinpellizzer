Vicia japonica var. laxiracemis

Vicia japonica var. laxiracemis, commonly known as the Japanese vetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
