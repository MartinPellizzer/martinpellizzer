Veronica ciliolata var. fiordensis

Veronica ciliolata var. fiordensis, commonly known as the Fiord bentweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping growth habit and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant has finely toothed leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, high-altitude conditions. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous fiords.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
