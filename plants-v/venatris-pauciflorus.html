Venatris pauciflorus

Venatris pauciflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered venation, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves with intricate venation patterns that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for ecological and botanical research.
