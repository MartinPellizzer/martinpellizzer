Vitex discoideoglandulosa

Vitex discoideoglandulosa, commonly known as the broad-lobed chaste tree, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with glandular dots. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Vitex discoideoglandulosa is often found in dry to moist forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
