Vicia loiseleurii

Vicia loiseleurii, commonly known as the Loiseleur's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, trifoliate leaves and produces yellowish to reddish-purple pea-like flowers. Its pods are narrow and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vicia loiseleurii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
