Vernonia interjecta

Vernonia interjecta, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branching stems. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Vernonia interjecta is often found in moist, open areas such as meadows and woodland edges.
