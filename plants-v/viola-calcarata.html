Viola calcarata

Viola calcarata, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces violet or purple flowers with five petals. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Viola calcarata is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
