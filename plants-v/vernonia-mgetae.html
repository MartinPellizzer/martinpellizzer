Vernonia mgetae

Vernonia mgetae, commonly known as the East African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Vernonia mgetae is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
