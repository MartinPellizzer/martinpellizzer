Viola ovato-oblonga f. albiflora

Viola ovato-oblonga f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered heartleaf violet, is a hybrid viola species characterized by its ovate-oblong leaves and pale to white flowers.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, shaded woodland habitats. The plant produces small, showy flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive dark violet or purple blotch at the base. Its foliage is heart-shaped and covered in fine, downy hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
