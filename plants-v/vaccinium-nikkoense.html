Vaccinium nikkoense

Vaccinium nikkoense, commonly known as the Nikko huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
