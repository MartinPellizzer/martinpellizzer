Valeriana excelsa subsp. procurrens

Valeriana excelsa subsp.

procurrens, commonly known as the creeping valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features upright, branched stems that often lie prostrate, with small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Valeriana excelsa subsp.

procurrens is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
