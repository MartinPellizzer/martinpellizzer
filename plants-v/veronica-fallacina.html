Veronica fallacina

Veronica fallacina, commonly known as the false speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica fallacina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
