Veronica koelzii

Veronica koelzii, commonly known as the Koelz's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
