Vernonia blanchetiana

Vernonia blanchetiana, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Vernonia blanchetiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
