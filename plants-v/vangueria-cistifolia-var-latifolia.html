Vangueria cistifolia var. latifolia

Vangueria cistifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf vangueria, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are broad and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its leaves and stems.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
