Vallea calomala

Vallea calomala, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.
