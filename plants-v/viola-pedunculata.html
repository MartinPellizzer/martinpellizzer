Viola pedunculata

Viola pedunculata, commonly known as the purple violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
