Vicia venosa var. fauriei

Vicia venosa var. fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to pink. The plant produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
