Vitis mairei

Vitis mairei, commonly known as the Maire's grape, is a species of flowering vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. Vitis mairei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
