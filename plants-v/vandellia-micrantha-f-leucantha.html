Vandellia micrantha f. leucantha

Vandellia micrantha f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered vandellia, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and sepals, which contrast beautifully with its green foliage. Native to the humid forests of South America, this plant thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its unique floral structure suggests a specialized pollination mechanism, likely involving specific insect pollinators.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
