Vesicaria physiphora

Vesicaria physiphora, commonly known as the bladder wort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its bladder-like structures called vesicles.

These vesicles are specialized air-filled sacs that help the plant float and facilitate gas exchange. The plant is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes, where it attaches to submerged surfaces. Its morphology includes slender stems and small, inconspicuous leaves.

Vesicaria physiphora plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and contributing to water oxygenation.
