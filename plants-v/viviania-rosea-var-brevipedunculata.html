Viviania rosea var. brevipedunculata

Viviania rosea var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the dwarf rose ivy, is a compact, trailing plant native to South America.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

This variety is popular for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in both indoor and outdoor settings.
