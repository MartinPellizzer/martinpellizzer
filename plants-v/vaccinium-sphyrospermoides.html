Vaccinium sphyrospermoides

Vaccinium sphyrospermoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the high elevations of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. The berries, which are edible, are dark blue to black in color and have a tart flavor.

This species is well adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.
