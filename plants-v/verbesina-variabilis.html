Verbesina variabilis

Verbesina variabilis, commonly known as the variable beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have hairy surfaces.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
