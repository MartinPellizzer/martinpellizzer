Verbena brevibracteata

Verbena brevibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bract verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for flowers, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Verbena brevibracteata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
