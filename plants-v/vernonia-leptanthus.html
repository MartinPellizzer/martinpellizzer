Vernonia leptanthus

Vernonia leptanthus, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands, often forming dense clusters. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow-orange flower heads make it a distinctive member of the genus Vernonia.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
