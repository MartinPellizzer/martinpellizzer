Viola tricolor var. polygonata

Viola tricolor var. polygonata, commonly known as the purple violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy flowers that typically display a combination of purple, yellow, and white petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape before the leaves fully emerge.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
