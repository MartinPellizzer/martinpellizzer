Villarsia grandifolia

Villarsia grandifolia, commonly known as the giant villarsia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has woody stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
