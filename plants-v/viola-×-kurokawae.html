Viola × kurokawae

Viola × kurokawae, commonly known as the Kurokawa violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, bi-colored flowers that typically feature a deep violet or purple upper lip and a lighter, often yellow or white lower lip. The plant is native to Japan and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often grown in shaded or partially shaded conditions.

Viola × kurokawae is prized for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in containers or as part of a woodland garden.
