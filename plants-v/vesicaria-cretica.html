Vesicaria cretica

Vesicaria cretica, commonly known as the Cretan bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures called vesicles, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are thin and ribbon-like, often floating on the water surface.

Vesicaria cretica plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
