Verbascum × versiflorum

Verbascum × versiflorum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are characteristic of the genus Verbascum. The plant produces showy, yellow-to-orange flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and easy maintenance, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
