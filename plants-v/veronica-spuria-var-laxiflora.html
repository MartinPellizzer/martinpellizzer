Veronica spuria var. laxiflora

Veronica spuria var. laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, often prostrate stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
