Vernonia nitidula var. florida

Vernonia nitidula var. florida, commonly known as the Florida ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy structures, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces clusters of small, dark purple to brown flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to wetland environments.
