Vinticena nossibeensis

Vinticena nossibeensis, commonly known as the Congo orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the bark of trees in its humid, shaded habitat. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
