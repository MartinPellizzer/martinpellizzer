Veronica telephiifolia var. pilosula

Veronica telephiifolia var. pilosula, commonly known as the silky veronica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense clusters of small, sky-blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky layer of hair, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
