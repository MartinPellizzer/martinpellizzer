Viburnum blandum var. vulcanicum

Viburnum blandum var. vulcanicum, commonly known as the Vulcan's viburnum, is a rare and striking shrub native to the volcanic soils of the Pacific Northwest.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. Its fruit is a dark blue to black drupe, which ripens in late summer and is edible for both humans and wildlife.

Viburnum blandum var. vulcanicum thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full to partial sunlight.
