Verticordia densiflora var. cespitosa

Verticordia densiflora var. cespitosa, commonly known as the denseflowered squill, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to Western Australia.

It features wiry, prostrate stems that bear clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often densely packed to form a compact mat. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun and dry conditions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
