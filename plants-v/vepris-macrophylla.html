Vepris macrophylla

Vepris macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. Its wood is highly valued for its rich, aromatic scent and is used in furniture and musical instrument making.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
