Vitis cuspidifera

Vitis cuspidifera, commonly known as the Brazilian grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its vigorous growth and climbing habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a pointed tip, and the fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature.

Vitis cuspidifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
