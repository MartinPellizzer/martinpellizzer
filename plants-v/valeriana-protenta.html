Valeriana protenta

Valeriana protenta, commonly known as the purple valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Valeriana protenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.
