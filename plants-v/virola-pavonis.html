Virola pavonis

Virola pavonis, commonly known as the golden virola, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and large, glossy leaves. The tree produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local economies.

The plant also holds cultural significance among indigenous communities in the region.
