Verbascum pyroliforme subsp. pyroliforme

Verbascum pyroliforme subsp.

pyroliforme, commonly known as the pyroliform hollyhock, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
