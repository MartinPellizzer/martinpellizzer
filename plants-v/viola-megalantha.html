Viola megalantha

Viola megalantha, commonly known as the giant violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often being more prominent and adorned with distinctive markings.

Viola megalantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
