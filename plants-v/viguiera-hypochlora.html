Viguiera hypochlora

Viguiera hypochlora, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are hairy and grayish-green, while the flowers are blue to violet in color.

Viguiera hypochlora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
