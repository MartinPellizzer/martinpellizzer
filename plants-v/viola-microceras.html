Viola microceras

Viola microceras, commonly known as the miniature violet, is a small annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, typically purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, making it one of the smallest species in the viola genus. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects.
