Vandellia micrantha

Vandellia micrantha, commonly known as the rock wallaby orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and features small, hairy leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
