Viola balansae

Viola balansae, commonly known as the Balansae violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple. It belongs to the Violaceae family, which includes various species of violets and pansies.

Viola balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
