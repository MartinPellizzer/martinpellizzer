Vernonia missurica f. swinkii

Vernonia missurica f. swinkii, commonly known as the Missouri ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of violet-blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
