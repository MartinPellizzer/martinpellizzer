Verbascum blattarioides

Verbascum blattarioides, commonly known as the blattaria thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, silver-green, woolly leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
