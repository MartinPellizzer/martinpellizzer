Vernonia scabrifoliata

Vernonia scabrifoliata, commonly known as roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are rough to the touch, giving it its common name, and its purple to pinkish flowers bloom in late summer.

Vernonia scabrifoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
