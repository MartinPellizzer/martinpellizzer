Vicia leyboldii

Vicia leyboldii, commonly known as the Leybold's vetch, is a species of legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its pods are slender and contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family.

Vicia leyboldii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
