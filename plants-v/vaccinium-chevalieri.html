Vaccinium chevalieri

Vaccinium chevalieri, commonly known as the Corsican bilberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or coastal environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a dense canopy that helps retain moisture.

The flowers are bell-shaped and typically pink or white, blooming in early spring.
