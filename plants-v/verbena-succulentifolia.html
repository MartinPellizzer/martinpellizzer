Verbena succulentifolia

Verbena succulentifolia, commonly known as the silver verbena, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
