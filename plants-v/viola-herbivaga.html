Viola herbivaga

Viola herbivaga, commonly known as the herbivorous violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. Viola herbivaga typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
