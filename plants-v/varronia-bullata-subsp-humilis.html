Varronia bullata subsp. humilis

Varronia bullata subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the slender varronia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often borne at the leaf axils. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that dehisce to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to grow in humid, low-light conditions.
