Viburnum × rhytidophylloides

Viburnum × rhytidophylloides, commonly known as the American beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, violet-blue berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
