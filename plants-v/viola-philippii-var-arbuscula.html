Viola philippii var. arbuscula

Viola philippii var. arbuscula, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in meadows and forested areas at high altitudes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
