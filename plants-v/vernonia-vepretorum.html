Vernonia vepretorum

Vernonia vepretorum, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
