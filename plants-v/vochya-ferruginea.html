Vochya ferruginea

Vochya ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Vochya ferruginea is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
