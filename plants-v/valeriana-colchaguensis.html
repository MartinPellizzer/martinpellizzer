Valeriana colchaguensis

Valeriana colchaguensis, commonly known as the Chilean valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. It has a long taproot and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
