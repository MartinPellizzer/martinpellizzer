Vernonia salicifolia

Vernonia salicifolia, commonly known as silky ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local pollinators.
