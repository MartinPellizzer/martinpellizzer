Valeriana officinalis var. capensis

Valeriana officinalis var. capensis, commonly known as African valerian, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and contains volatile oils responsible for its aromatic properties.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
