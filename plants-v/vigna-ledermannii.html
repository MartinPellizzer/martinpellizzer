Vigna ledermannii

Vigna ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to elongated, slender pods. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in poor soils. It is often grown for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

Vigna ledermannii is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
