Veronicastrum virginicum f. villosa

Veronicastrum virginicum f. villosa, commonly known as hairy blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant's foliage is characterized by its soft, hairy texture, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woodlands or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
