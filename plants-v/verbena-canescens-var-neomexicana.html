Verbena canescens var. neomexicana

Verbena canescens var. neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
