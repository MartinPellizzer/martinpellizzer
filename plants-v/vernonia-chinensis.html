Vernonia chinensis

Vernonia chinensis, commonly known as Chinese ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange inflorescences. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia chinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
