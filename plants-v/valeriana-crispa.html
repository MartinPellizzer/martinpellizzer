Valeriana crispa

Valeriana crispa, commonly known as the crisp valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often branched and somewhat woody. Valeriana crispa is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade.

Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.
