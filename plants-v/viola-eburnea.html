Viola eburnea

Viola eburnea, commonly known as the ivory viola, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Viola eburnea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
