Velaea glauca var. purpurascens

Velaea glauca var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a faint purple tint. Its hairy foliage and rapid growth make it a common weed in agricultural areas.

This variant is notable for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
