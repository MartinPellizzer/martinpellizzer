Verbesina nudicaulis

Verbesina nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Verbesina nudicaulis is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
