Viola hispida

Viola hispida, commonly known as the hispid violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Viola hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
