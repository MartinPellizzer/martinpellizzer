Vicia pyrenaica var. angustifolia

Vicia pyrenaica var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to other forms of Vicia pyrenaica.
