Viola turkestanica var. rupestris

Viola turkestanica var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock viol, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in well-drained environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant violet or purple flowers. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to harsh, arid conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xerophytic habitats and rock gardens.
