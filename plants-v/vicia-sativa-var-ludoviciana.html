Vicia sativa var. ludoviciana

Vicia sativa var. ludoviciana, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, trailing stems that produce clusters of pea-like, violet-blue flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used for soil stabilization and as a forage crop.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for improving soil fertility.
