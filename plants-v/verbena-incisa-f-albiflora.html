Verbena incisa f. albiflora

Verbena incisa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs. Native to South America, this plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate blooms and drought tolerance.
