Veronica × edinensis

Veronica × edinensis, commonly known as the Edinburgh speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely grown in gardens across temperate regions.
