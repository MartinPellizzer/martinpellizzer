Veronica ovata subsp. ovata

Veronica ovata subsp.

ovata, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite leaves which are lance-shaped and hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscaping.
