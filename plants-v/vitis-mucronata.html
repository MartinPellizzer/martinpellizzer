Vitis mucronata

Vitis mucronata, commonly known as the bluntleaf grape, is a species of flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with rounded tips and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red or black when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Vitis mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
