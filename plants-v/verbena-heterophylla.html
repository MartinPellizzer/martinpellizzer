Verbena heterophylla

Verbena heterophylla, commonly known as the variable verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, which can range from lance-shaped to nearly round, depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces vibrant clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
