Vernonia amboinensis

Vernonia amboinensis, commonly known as the Amboina ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
