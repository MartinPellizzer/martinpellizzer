Vaccinium oxycoccos var. ovalifolium

Vaccinium oxycoccos var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaved cranberry, is a variant of the cranberry plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and bright red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant thrives in acidic, wet soils and is often found in bogs and swamps. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a system of underground runners that help it spread.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
