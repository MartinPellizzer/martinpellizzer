Vitis piasezkii var. baroniana

Vitis piasezkii var. baroniana, commonly known as the Baron's grape, is a rare and less-studied species within the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its robust vines, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, dark blue berries. Its botanical features include a strong woody stem, tendrils for climbing, and a complex floral structure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a botanically significant but under-researched species.
