Viola herminii

Viola herminii, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including Iran and Turkey.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

Viola herminii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
