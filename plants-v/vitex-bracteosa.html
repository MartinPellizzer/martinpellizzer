Vitex bracteosa

Vitex bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces distinctive bracts, which are modified leaves that often resemble petals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Vitex bracteosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
