Veronica kiusiana

Veronica kiusiana, commonly known as the mountain veronica, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Veronica kiusiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
