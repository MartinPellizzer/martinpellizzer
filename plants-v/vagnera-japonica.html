Vagnera japonica

Vagnera japonica, commonly known as the Japanese vagnera, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Vagnera japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
