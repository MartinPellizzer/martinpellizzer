Viola canadensis var. corymbosa

Viola canadensis var. corymbosa, commonly known as the Canadian violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and thrives in moist, shady environments. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often marked by a distinctive yellow or white spur.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
