Viola granatensis

Viola granatensis, commonly known as the granate violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly velvety texture. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white mark near the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Viola granatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
