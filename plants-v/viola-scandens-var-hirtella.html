Viola scandens var. hirtella

Viola scandens var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant has hairy stems and foliage, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
