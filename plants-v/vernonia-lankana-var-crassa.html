Vernonia lankana var. crassa

Vernonia lankana var. crassa, commonly known as the broad-leaved vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
