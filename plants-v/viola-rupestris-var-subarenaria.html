Viola rupestris var. subarenaria

Viola rupestris var. subarenaria, commonly known as the sand viol, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear heart-shaped, gray-green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
