Viola keiskei

Viola keiskei, commonly known as the Japanese violet or the ghost orchid, is a small, flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant is often found growing on rocks or in shaded, moist environments. It is a member of the Violaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates.

Due to its unique appearance and limited habitat, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species.
