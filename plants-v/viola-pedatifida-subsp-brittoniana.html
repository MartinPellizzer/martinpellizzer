Viola pedatifida subsp. brittoniana

Viola pedatifida subsp.

brittoniana, commonly known as the Britton's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive lobed structure, and its flowers are typically violet or purple in color.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
