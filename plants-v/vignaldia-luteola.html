Vignaldia luteola

Vignaldia luteola, commonly known as the yellow vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, woody stems that can twine around supports, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Vignaldia luteola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
