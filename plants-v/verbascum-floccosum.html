Verbascum floccosum

Verbascum floccosum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft, white hairs that give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a thick layer of silky hairs.

Verbascum floccosum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
