Veronica denudata

Veronica denudata, commonly known as the naked-stem speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, leafless stems that rise from a basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Veronica denudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
