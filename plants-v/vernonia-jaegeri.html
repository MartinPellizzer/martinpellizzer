Vernonia jaegeri

Vernonia jaegeri, commonly known as the jaeger's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flower heads that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Vernonia jaegeri is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
