Verbena polycnemoides

Verbena polycnemoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Verbena polycnemoides is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
