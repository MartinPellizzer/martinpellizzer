Valeriana kryloviana

Valeriana kryloviana, commonly known as the Siberian valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features a robust rhizome system and produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct, somewhat aromatic scent.

Valeriana kryloviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
