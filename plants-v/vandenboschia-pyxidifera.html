Vandenboschia pyxidifera

Vandenboschia pyxidifera, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy tissues.

Vandenboschia pyxidifera is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appearance.
