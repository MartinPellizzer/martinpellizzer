Viola grypoceras f. pubescens

Viola grypoceras f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent viola, is a botanical variant of the viola grypoceras species.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "pubescens.". The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to garden settings.
