Viola rostrata var. japonica

Viola rostrata var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is noted for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
