Viola chrysantha var. nevadensis

Viola chrysantha var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with heart-shaped, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are characterized by their five petals, with a distinctive yellow coloration.

The Sierra violet is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in native plant gardens.
