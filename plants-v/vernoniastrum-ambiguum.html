Vernoniastrum ambiguum

Vernoniastrum ambiguum, commonly known as the swamp verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is adapted to tolerate saturated soil conditions. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The showy purple to blue flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
