Vitis vinifera var. nicaensis

Vitis vinifera var. nicaensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan grape, is a lesser-known variety native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple berries and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates. The plant typically grows as a vine, with compound leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a high tolerance for humid conditions.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique genetic makeup and potential for cultivation in warm regions.
