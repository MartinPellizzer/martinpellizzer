Vandenboschia angustata

Vandenboschia angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, narrow leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its growth habit is prostrate, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Vandenboschia angustata is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
