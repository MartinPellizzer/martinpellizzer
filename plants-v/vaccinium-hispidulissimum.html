Vaccinium hispidulissimum

Vaccinium hispidulissimum, commonly known as the rough leaved huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant features small, oval leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisine.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
