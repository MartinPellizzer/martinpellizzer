Verbena abramsii

Verbena abramsii, commonly known as Abrams' verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves and has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Abrams' verbena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
