Viola ulleungdoensis

Viola ulleungdoensis, commonly known as the Ulleungdo violet, is a rare flowering plant native to the Korean island of Ulleungdo.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it plays a significant role in the local ecosystem.
