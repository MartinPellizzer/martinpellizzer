Viola collina subsp. propera

Viola collina subsp.

propera, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. It is part of the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
