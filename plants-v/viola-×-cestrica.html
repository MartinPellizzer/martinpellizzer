Viola × cestrica

Viola × cestrica, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Viola genus, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy flowers.

It typically features violet or purple petals with a distinctive yellow or white center, and its foliage is usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, thriving in moist, well-drained soils in partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Viola × cestrica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a wildflower in natural habitats.
