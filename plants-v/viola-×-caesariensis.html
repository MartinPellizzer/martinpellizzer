Viola × caesariensis

Viola × caesariensis, commonly known as the English violet, is a hybrid perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers well-drained, moist soil in partial shade. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping and floral arrangements.
