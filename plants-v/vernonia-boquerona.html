Vernonia boquerona

Vernonia boquerona, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia boquerona is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
