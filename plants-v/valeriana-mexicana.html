Valeriana mexicana

Valeriana mexicana, commonly known as Mexican valerian, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive, fragrant scent. Its rhizomatous root system is used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.

Valeriana mexicana plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal uses.
