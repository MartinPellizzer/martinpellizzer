Veronica austriaca subsp. macrodonta

Veronica austriaca subsp.

macrodonta, commonly known as the large-flowered speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
