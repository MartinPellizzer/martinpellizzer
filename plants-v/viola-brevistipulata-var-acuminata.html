Viola brevistipulata var. acuminata

Viola brevistipulata var. acuminata, commonly known as the acute-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often marked with a yellow or white spur.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
