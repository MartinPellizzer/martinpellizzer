Vitex rubra

Vitex rubra, commonly known as the red verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, violet to red flowers that bloom in summer. Vitex rubra is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for coastal and dry climates.
