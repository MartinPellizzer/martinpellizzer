Viola canina var. sylvestris

Viola canina var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild violet or woodland violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture and produces showy violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas, meadows, and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
