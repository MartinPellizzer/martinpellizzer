Viola hirtipes var. rhodovenia

Viola hirtipes var. rhodovenia, commonly known as the Rhodovena violet, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking violet to purple flowers with yellow markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
