Vaccinium haemanthum

Vaccinium haemanthum, commonly known as the red-berried heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Vaccinium haemanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
