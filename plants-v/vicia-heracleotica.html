Vicia heracleotica

Vicia heracleotica, commonly known as the broad-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces legumes, or pods, that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Vicia heracleotica is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
