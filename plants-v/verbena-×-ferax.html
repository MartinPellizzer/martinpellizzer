Verbena × ferax

Verbena × ferax, commonly known as the common verbena, is a hybrid plant in the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse displays of vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves. Its flowers are borne in dense clusters at the ends of the stems, and it is known for its adaptability to various soil types.

Verbena × ferax is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
