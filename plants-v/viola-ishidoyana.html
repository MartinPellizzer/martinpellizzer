Viola ishidoyana

Viola ishidoyana, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has small, velvety leaves and produces flowers that bloom in early spring.

Viola ishidoyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of humility in Japanese culture.
