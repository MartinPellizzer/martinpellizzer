Vigna racemosa f. glabrescens

Vigna racemosa f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth urdbean, is a botanical variant of the urdbean species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its glabrous or nearly hairless leaves and pods, distinguishing it from the typical form of Vigna racemosa. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds. It has a climbing habit and produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content, making it a useful crop in various agricultural systems.
