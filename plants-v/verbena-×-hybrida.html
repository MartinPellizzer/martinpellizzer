Verbena × hybrida

Verbena × hybrida, commonly known as the common verbena, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, pink, and red. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial, depending on the climate, and features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Verbena × hybrida thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
