Vandenboschia collariata

Vandenboschia collariata, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear vibrant orange-red flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually blue-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Vandenboschia collariata is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
