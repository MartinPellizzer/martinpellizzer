Viguiera calva

Viguiera calva, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Viguiera calva thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ecological value includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
