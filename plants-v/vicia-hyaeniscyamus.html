Vicia hyaeniscyamus

Vicia hyaeniscyamus, commonly known as the hyena vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or through animal activity. Native to regions of southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
