Vaccinium moupinense

Vaccinium moupinense, commonly known as the Yunnan blueberry, is a species of shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in early spring. Its fruits are edible, appearing as dark blue berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor.

Vaccinium moupinense thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.
