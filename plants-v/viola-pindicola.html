Viola pindicola

Viola pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Viola pindicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to alpine landscapes.
