Viola × wilibaldii

Viola × wilibaldii, commonly known as the Wilibald's violet, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola lutea.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bi-colored flowers that typically display shades of purple, violet, and yellow. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Viola × wilibaldii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can be found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural displays and ecological studies.
