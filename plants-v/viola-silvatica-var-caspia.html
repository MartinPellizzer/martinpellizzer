Viola silvatica var. caspia

Viola silvatica var. caspia, commonly known as the Caspian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, often along stream banks or in mountainous areas. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy violet flowers with five petals, usually in shades of purple or blue. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
