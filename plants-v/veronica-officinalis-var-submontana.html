Veronica officinalis var. submontana

Veronica officinalis var. submontana, commonly known as mountain speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features upright stems with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
