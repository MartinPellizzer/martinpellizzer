Vicia ochroleuca subsp. atlantica

Vicia ochroleuca subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic vetch, is a perennial legume native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast. It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

This subspecies is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
