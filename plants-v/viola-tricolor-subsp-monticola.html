Viola tricolor subsp. monticola

Viola tricolor subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and delicate, trifoliate flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet, white, and yellow.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to flourish in challenging mountainous habitats.
