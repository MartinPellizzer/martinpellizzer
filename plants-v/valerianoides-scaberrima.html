Valerianoides scaberrima

Valerianoides scaberrima, commonly known as the rough valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it produces small, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
