Vigna unguiculata var. spontanea

Vigna unguiculata var. spontanea, commonly known as wild cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It has a climbing or trailing growth habit and produces small white or purple flowers.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes used in traditional agriculture and herbal medicine.
