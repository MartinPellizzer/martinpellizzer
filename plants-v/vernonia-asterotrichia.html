Vernonia asterotrichia

Vernonia asterotrichia, commonly known as the woolly ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Vernonia asterotrichia is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands.

Its hairy foliage and compact growth habit make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
