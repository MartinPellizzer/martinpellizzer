Vernonia calycina

Vernonia calycina, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine, silky hairs. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia calycina is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
