Vateria macrocarpa

Vateria macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic life. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its showy white flowers bloom at night, attracting pollinators such as moths.

Vateria macrocarpa plays a significant role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water quality through its natural filtration processes.
