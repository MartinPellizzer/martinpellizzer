Viola primulifolia var. boscii

Viola primulifolia var. boscii, commonly known as the Bosci's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and produces delicate violet flowers with purple petals and yellow throats. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in woodland or rocky habitats. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate cooler climates and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
