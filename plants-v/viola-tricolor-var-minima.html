Viola tricolor var. minima

Viola tricolor var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a compact variety of the common violet.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight purplish tinge. The flowers are trifoliate, with three petals in shades of violet, blue, and white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
