Valeriana laevigata

Valeriana laevigata, commonly known as smooth valerian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Valeriana laevigata thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
