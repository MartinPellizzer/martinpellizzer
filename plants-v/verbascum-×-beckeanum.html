Verbascum × beckeanum

Verbascum × beckeanum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their large, fuzzy leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of elongated, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its fibrous leaves and flowers have historically been used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
