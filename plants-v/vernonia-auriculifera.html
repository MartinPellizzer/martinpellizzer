Vernonia auriculifera

Vernonia auriculifera, commonly known as the ear-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and prominent, often showy, inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive ear-like base, and its flowers are usually blue to purple in color.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
