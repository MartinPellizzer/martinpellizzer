Valeriana crassiscaposa

Valeriana crassiscaposa, commonly known as the broad-capped valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Valeriana crassiscaposa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
