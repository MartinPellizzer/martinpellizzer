Vitex langundi

Vitex langundi, commonly known as African blue mistflower, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
