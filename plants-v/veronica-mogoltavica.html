Veronica mogoltavica

Veronica mogoltavica, commonly known as the Mongolian speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense mats.

Veronica mogoltavica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic value.
