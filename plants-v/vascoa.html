Vascoa

Vascoa, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture.

Vascoa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
