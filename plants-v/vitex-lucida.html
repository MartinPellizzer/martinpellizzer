Vitex lucida

Vitex lucida, commonly known as the silverleaf vitex, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically silver-white underneath, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually purple or blue in color. These flowers bloom in the summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vitex lucida is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
