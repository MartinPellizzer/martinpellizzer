Velaea cordata

Velaea cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a decorative element to its appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in moist tropical forests.

Velaea cordata is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
