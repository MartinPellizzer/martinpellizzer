Veronica fruticans f. glandulifera

Veronica fruticans f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular veronica, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "glandulifera.". It is native to southern Europe and thrives in dry, sandy soils.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
