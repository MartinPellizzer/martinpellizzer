Villocuspis januariensis

Villocuspis januariensis, commonly known as the January's velvet cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, globular stem covered in dense, soft spines that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the early spring months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This species is adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient addition to xerophytic gardens.
