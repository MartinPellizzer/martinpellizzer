Vincetoxicum mucronatum

Vincetoxicum mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release silky, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its invasive potential, it can become a problematic weed in certain regions.
