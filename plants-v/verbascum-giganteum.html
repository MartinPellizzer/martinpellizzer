Verbascum giganteum

Verbascum giganteum, commonly known as the giant mullein, is a striking plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, woolly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered in dense, soft hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, upright spike. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in a thick layer of downy hairs.

Verbascum giganteum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
