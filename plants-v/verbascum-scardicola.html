Verbascum scardicola

Verbascum scardicola, commonly known as the alpine mullein, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Verbascum scardicola is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes other notable plants such as snapdragons and foxgloves.
