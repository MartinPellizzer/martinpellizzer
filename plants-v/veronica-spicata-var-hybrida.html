Veronica spicata var. hybrida

Veronica spicata var. hybrida, commonly known as the spiked speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense spikes of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 12 to 24 inches and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
