Vicia disperma var. approximata

Vicia disperma var. approximata, commonly known as the clustered vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale purple or pink in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration efforts.
