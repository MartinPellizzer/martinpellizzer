Vernonia subverticillata

Vernonia subverticillata, commonly known as the clustered ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is typically found in open areas such as fields and woodland edges.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
