Vincetoxicum wallichii

Vincetoxicum wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright blue berries in the fall. These berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
