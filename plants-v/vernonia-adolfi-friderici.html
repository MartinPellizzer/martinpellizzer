Vernonia adolfi-friderici

Vernonia adolfi-friderici, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
