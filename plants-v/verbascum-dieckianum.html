Verbascum dieckianum

Verbascum dieckianum, commonly known as the Desert mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant features large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. It produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for small organisms.
