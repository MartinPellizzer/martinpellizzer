Vallaris clavata

Vallaris clavata, commonly known as the clustered vallaris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Vallaris clavata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
