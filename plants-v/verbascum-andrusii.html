Verbascum andrusii

Verbascum andrusii, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, fuzzy, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a cylindrical shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its fibrous roots and hairy leaves are characteristic of the mullein genus, which is part of the Scrophulariaceae family.
