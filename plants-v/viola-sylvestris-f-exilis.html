Viola sylvestris f. exilis

Viola sylvestris f. exilis, commonly known as the slender wood violet, is a botanical variant of the common violet, characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more delicate flowers.

It typically features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are usually pale violet or white, with a distinctive spur. This form is often found in woodland habitats, thriving in moist, shaded environments. It is a perennial herbaceous plant that blooms in early spring, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.

The plant's morphology reflects adaptations to its specific ecological niche, making it a notable species in botanical studies.
