Verbena canescens var. roemeriana

Verbena canescens var. roemeriana, commonly known as the silver verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, hairy stems and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The leaves are narrow, grayish-green, and covered in fine white hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
