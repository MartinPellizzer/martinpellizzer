Viola vandasii

Viola vandasii, commonly known as the Vanda violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from violet to blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Viola vandasii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.

It prefers well-draining soil and indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
