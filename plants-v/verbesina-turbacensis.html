Verbesina turbacensis

Verbesina turbacensis, commonly known as the mountain beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
