Viola vectensis

Viola vectensis, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola vectensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
