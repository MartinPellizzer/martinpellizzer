Vernonia albicaulis subsp. longistylis

Vernonia albicaulis subsp.

longistylis, commonly known as the longstyle ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its erect, sparsely leafy stems.

The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.
