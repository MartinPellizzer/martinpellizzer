Vitis rhombifolia

Vitis rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombifoliate grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, woodland habitats. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, black berries that ripen in the fall.

Vitis rhombifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
