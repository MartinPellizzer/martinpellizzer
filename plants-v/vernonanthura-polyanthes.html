Vernonanthura polyanthes

Vernonanthura polyanthes, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are borne on long, slender stalks.

Vernonanthura polyanthes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
