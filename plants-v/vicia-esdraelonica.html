Vicia esdraelonica

Vicia esdraelonica, commonly known as the Eshel's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Vicia esdraelonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
