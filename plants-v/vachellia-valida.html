Vachellia valida

Vachellia valida, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are followed by long, thin pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Vachellia valida is valued for its ability to withstand drought and its role in preventing soil erosion.
