Viola pringlei

Viola pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Viola pringlei is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for garden use.

Its botanical features include a short stem, trifoliate leaves, and a compact growth habit.
