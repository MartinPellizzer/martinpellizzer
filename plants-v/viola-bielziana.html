Viola bielziana

Viola bielziana, commonly known as the Bielziana violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often preceding the foliage, and it is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Viola bielziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
