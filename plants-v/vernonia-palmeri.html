Vernonia palmeri

Vernonia palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to reddish-brown.

Vernonia palmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
