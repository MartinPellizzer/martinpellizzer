Viola indica f. barbata

Viola indica f. barbata, commonly known as the Barbata violet, is a variant of the common violet with distinctive botanical features.

It is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Violaceae family, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 cm, with hairy stems and serrated foliage. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing showy, five-petaled blossoms that attract pollinators.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in moist, partially shaded environments.
