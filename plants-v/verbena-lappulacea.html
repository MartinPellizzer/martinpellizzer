Verbena lappulacea

Verbena lappulacea, commonly known as hoary verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is drought-tolerant and has a compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
