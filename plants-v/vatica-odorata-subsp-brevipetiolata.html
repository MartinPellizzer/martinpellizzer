Vatica odorata subsp. brevipetiolata

Vatica odorata subsp.

brevipetiolata, commonly known as the fragrant ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown wood and dense foliage.

The leaves are typically elliptical, with a short petiole, giving it its subspecies name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round fruits.

It is valued for its hard, durable timber and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.
