Vachellia lutea f. leptocarpa

Vachellia lutea f. leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its bark is smooth and pale, and it is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
