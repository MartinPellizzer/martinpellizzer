Vitex patula

Vitex patula, commonly known as Mexican vitex, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its strong, aromatic scent and is often used in traditional medicine. Vitex patula thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and attractive foliage.
