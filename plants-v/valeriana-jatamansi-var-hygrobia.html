Valeriana jatamansi var. hygrobia

Valeriana jatamansi var. hygrobia, commonly known as Himalayan valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant has upright stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The root system is valued for its medicinal properties, containing compounds like valerenic acid and sesquiterpenes.
