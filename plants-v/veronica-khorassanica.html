Veronica khorassanica

Veronica khorassanica, commonly known as the Persian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica khorassanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
