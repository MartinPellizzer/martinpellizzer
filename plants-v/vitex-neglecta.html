Vitex neglecta

Vitex neglecta, commonly known as the desert blueberry, is a small shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, blue-black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, pale purple to white flowers in summer. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Vitex neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
