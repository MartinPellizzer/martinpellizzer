Viviania laxa

Viviania laxa, commonly known as the slender waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, thin, ribbon-like leaves that are usually submerged, with small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge above the water surface. It is characterized by its flexible stems and ability to spread through both vegetative growth and seed dispersal.

Viviania laxa plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
