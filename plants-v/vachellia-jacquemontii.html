Vachellia jacquemontii

Vachellia jacquemontii, commonly known as the jacquemont's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of the local flora in these regions.

Vachellia jacquemontii is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
