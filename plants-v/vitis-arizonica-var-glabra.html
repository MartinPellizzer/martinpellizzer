Vitis arizonica var. glabra

Vitis arizonica var. glabra, commonly known as Arizona grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that range in color from green to red. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and canyons.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use by indigenous peoples for food and medicine.
