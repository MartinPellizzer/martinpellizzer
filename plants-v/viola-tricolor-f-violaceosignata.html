Viola tricolor f. violaceosignata

Viola tricolor f. violaceosignata, commonly known as the purple-flowered violet, is a botanical variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its striking violet and white petals, which are more pronounced than in the typical form. This plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is native to Europe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.
