Valeriana amplexicaulis

Valeriana amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features a stout, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often branching near the top. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite and clasping, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Valeriana amplexicaulis is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
