Viola adenothrix

Viola adenothrix, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple and white. Viola adenothrix is known for its ability to tolerate harsh climates and is often found in high-altitude habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
