Vitis glaberrima

Vitis glaberrima, commonly known as the wild grapevine, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces small, sour-tasting berries. The plant has a woody stem with tendrils that allow it to climb and attach to supports. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Vitis glaberrima is often found in woodland areas and is considered a wild relative of cultivated grapevines.
