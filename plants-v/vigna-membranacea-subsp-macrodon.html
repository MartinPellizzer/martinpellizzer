Vigna membranacea subsp. macrodon

Vigna membranacea subsp.

macrodon, commonly known as the large-seeded winged bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia. It features compound leaves with three leaflets and produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small white to pinkish flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

The subspecies is notable for its larger seed size compared to other members of the Vigna genus.
