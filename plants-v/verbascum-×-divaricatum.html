Verbascum × divaricatum

Verbascum × divaricatum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattellum.

It is a tall, biennial or short-lived perennial plant characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves and showy yellow flowers. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Its hairy leaves and tall flower stalks make it a distinctive and easily recognizable species in its native range.
