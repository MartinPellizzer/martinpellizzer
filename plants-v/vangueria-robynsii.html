Vangueria robynsii

Vangueria robynsii, commonly known as the dwarf vangueria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Vangueria robynsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact size and delicate blooms.
