Vernonia barclayi

Vernonia barclayi, commonly known as Barclay's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and is often found in the southeastern states. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy and sturdy.

Vernonia barclayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
