Viola dissecta f. viridis

Viola dissecta f. viridis, commonly known as the green-leaved viola, is a variant of the common violet characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant features heart-shaped leaves that are finely dissected, giving it a feathery appearance. The leaves are typically a vibrant green, which sets it apart from other viola species. It produces delicate purple or violet flowers with a spurred petal, typical of the Violaceae family.

This cultivar is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
