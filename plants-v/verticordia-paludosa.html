Verticordia paludosa

Verticordia paludosa, commonly known as the swamp squill, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often reddish or pinkish flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and has a low, sprawling habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are known for their intricate, spurred petals.

Verticordia paludosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important role in the ecological balance of its wetland habitats.
