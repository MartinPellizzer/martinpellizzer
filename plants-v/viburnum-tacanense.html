Viburnum tacanense

Viburnum tacanense, commonly known as the Tacana viburnum, is a species native to Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Its berries, which are dark blue to black, are an important food source for various bird species.

Viburnum tacanense is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
