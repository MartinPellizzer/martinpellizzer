Veronica tournefortii

Veronica tournefortii, commonly known as the rock everlasting or blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry and sunny locations. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.

Veronica tournefortii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
