Viburnum amatenangense

Viburnum amatenangense, commonly known as the Mexican viburnum, is a species native to Central America and Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually white or pale green, blooming in the spring.

The fruit is a small, dark blue to black drupe that ripens in the fall.
