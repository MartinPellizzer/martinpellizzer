Vibones auratus

Vibones auratus, commonly known as the golden flame bellflower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Vibones auratus is known for its hardiness and ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
