Viburnum adenophorum

Viburnum adenophorum, commonly known as the腺毛 Viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Its foliage provides habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Viburnum adenophorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
