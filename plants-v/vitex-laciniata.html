Vitex laciniata

Vitex laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a dry, two-valved capsule containing several small, brown seeds.

Vitex laciniata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
