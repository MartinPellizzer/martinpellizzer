Voyria araguensis

Voyria araguensis, commonly known as the Guanabara orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
