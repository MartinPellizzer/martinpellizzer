Votomita orinocensis

Votomita orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the Orinoco River basin in South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrates of slow-moving water bodies.

Votomita orinocensis plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native wetland environments.
