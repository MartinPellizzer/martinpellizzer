Viola × castillensis

Viola × castillensis, commonly known as the Spanish violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the Iberian Peninsula, this plant thrives in woodland and shady garden environments. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is valued for its beauty and adaptability, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
