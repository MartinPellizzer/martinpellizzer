Viscum menyharthii

Viscum menyharthii, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in the regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds to new hosts.

Viscum menyharthii has a symbiotic relationship with its host, deriving some of its nutrients through its haustorium, while also potentially affecting the host's growth and health.
