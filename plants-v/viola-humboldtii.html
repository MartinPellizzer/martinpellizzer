Viola humboldtii

Viola humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy violet or purple flowers with distinctive yellow markings. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola humboldtii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
