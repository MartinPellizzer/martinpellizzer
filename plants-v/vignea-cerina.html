Vignea cerina

Vignea cerina, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into round, fleshy berries. These berries are typically red when mature and contain several seeds.

Vignea cerina is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
