Vulpiella incrassata

Vulpiella incrassata, commonly known as the thick-stemmed foxglove, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
