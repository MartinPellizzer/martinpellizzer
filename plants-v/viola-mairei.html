Viola mairei

Viola mairei, commonly known as the Maire violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
