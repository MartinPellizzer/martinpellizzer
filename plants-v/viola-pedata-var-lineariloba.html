Viola pedata var. lineariloba

Viola pedata var. lineariloba, commonly known as the slender violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet hue and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is characterized by its linear, lance-shaped leaves. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
