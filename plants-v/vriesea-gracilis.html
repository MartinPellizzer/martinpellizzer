Vriesea gracilis

Vriesea gracilis, commonly known as the slender vriesea, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with brightly colored bracts that range from red to orange. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea gracilis is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
