Viola selkirkii

Viola selkirkii, commonly known as Selkirk violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Botanically, it belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy blooms.
