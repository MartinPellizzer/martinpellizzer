Viola × conturbata

Viola × conturbata, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola × conturbata is often used in gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.
