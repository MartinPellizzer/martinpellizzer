Vernonia cognata

Vernonia cognata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Vernonia cognata is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
