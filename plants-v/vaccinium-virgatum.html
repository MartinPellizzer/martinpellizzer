Vaccinium virgatum

Vaccinium virgatum, commonly known as sheep laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes blueberries and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Vaccinium virgatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
