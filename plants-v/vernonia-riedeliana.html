Vernonia riedeliana

Vernonia riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. Its scientific name honors the German botanist Hermann Riedel, who contributed to the study of South American flora.

Vernonia riedeliana is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
