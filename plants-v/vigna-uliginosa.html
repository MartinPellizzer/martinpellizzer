Vigna uliginosa

Vigna uliginosa, commonly known as swamp cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with small, hairy stipules. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, and the plant produces small, round pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in lowland areas.

Vigna uliginosa is valued for its nutritional seeds and is used in traditional cuisines.
