Vatica lowii

Vatica lowii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a broad, buttressed trunk and dark green, glossy leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Vatica lowii plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
