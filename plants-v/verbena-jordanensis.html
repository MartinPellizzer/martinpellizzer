Verbena jordanensis

Verbena jordanensis, commonly known as Jordanian verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East, particularly Jordan.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Verbena jordanensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular plant in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
