Vernonia homblei

Vernonia homblei, commonly known as the Hombli's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia homblei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
