Valeriana kurtziana

Valeriana kurtziana, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, sometimes medicinal aroma.

Valeriana kurtziana is often found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, well-drained soils.
