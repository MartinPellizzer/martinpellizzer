Vitis africara

Vitis africara, commonly known as the African grapevine, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clustered berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. Vitis africara is adapted to warm climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
