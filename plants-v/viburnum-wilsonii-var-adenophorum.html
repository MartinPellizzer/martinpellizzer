Viburnum wilsonii var. adenophorum

Viburnum wilsonii var. adenophorum, commonly known as the腺毛 Viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
