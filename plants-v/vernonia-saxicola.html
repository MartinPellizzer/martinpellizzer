Vernonia saxicola

Vernonia saxicola, commonly known as the rock Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical heads. It is well adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in full sun with minimal water requirements.

Vernonia saxicola is often found in crevices and on dry slopes, making it a hardy and resilient species in harsh environments.
