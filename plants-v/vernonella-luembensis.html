Vernonella luembensis

Vernonella luembensis, commonly known as the Congo ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Vernonella luembensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
