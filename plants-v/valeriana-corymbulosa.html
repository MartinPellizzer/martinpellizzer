Valeriana corymbulosa

Valeriana corymbulosa, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, creeping stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, cylindrical fruit capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and emit a sweet, spicy odor when crushed.

Valeriana corymbulosa is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soil.
