Vaccinium kruhsianum

Vaccinium kruhsianum, commonly known as the Kruhsian huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bright red berries that are edible. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to humid, maritime climates.
