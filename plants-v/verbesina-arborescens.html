Verbesina arborescens

Verbesina arborescens, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring erect stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.
