Vernonia milleri

Vernonia milleri, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Vernonia milleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
