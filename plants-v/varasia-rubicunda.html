Varasia rubicunda

Varasia rubicunda, commonly known as the red-flowered varasia, is a striking perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals that contrast beautifully with its deep green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-50 centimeters and thrives in partial shade to full sun conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and botanical collections.
