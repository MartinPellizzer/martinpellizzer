Verbena tampensis

Verbena tampensis, commonly known as the Tampas verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Verbena tampensis is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
