Vaccinium ovatum var. saporosum

Vaccinium ovatum var. saporosum, commonly known as the coastal blueberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It thrives in coastal environments, preferring sandy or gravelly soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
