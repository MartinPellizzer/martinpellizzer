Vernonia poskeana subsp. bractifimbriata

Vernonia poskeana subsp.

bractifimbriata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark purple to brownish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
