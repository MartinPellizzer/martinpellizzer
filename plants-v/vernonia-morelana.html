Vernonia morelana

Vernonia morelana, commonly known as the Morelana ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia morelana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
