Verbascum siculum

Verbascum siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, downy hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Verbascum siculum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
