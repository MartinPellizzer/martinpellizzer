Viola palmata var. triloba

Viola palmata var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, often covered in a fine, downy layer. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with a white throat, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
