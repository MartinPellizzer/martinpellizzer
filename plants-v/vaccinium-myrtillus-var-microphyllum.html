Vaccinium myrtillus var. microphyllum

Vaccinium myrtillus var. microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved bilberry, is a variant of the bilberry plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its small, dark blue berries and elliptical leaves. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories. It plays an important role in ecosystems as a food source for wildlife and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it distinct from the typical bilberry, with reduced leaf size and a more compact growth habit.
