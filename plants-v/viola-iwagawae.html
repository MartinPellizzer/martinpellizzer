Viola iwagawae

Viola iwagawae, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Viola iwagawae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in the study of temperate flora.
