Vernonia peguensis

Vernonia peguensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its rough, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Vernonia peguensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
