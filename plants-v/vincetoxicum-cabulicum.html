Vincetoxicum cabulicum

Vincetoxicum cabulicum, commonly known as the Afghan snakeroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Vincetoxicum cabulicum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
