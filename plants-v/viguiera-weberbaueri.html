Viguiera weberbaueri

Viguiera weberbaueri, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Viguiera weberbaueri has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
