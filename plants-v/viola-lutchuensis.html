Viola lutchuensis

Viola lutchuensis, commonly known as the Lutchu violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Lutchu in the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Viola lutchuensis is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
