Vignantha rigida

Vignantha rigida, commonly known as the African spinach or Malabar spinach, is a tropical vine native to Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and rapid growth habit, often climbing or sprawling along the ground. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are edible and rich in nutrients, making it a valuable vegetable in many cuisines.

Vignantha rigida is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
