Ventilago brunnea

Ventilago brunnea, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
