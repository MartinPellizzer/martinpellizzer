Viola serpens var. pilosa

Viola serpens var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with five petals arranged symmetrically. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The hairy violet is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
