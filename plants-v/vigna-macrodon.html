Vigna macrodon

Vigna macrodon, commonly known as the large-seeded cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with small, hairy stipules. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several large, oval seeds. Vigna macrodon is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in many parts of Africa and Asia.
