Viola kawakamii

Viola kawakamii, commonly known as the Kawakami violet, is a rare and endemic species native to Japan.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its small, five-petaled flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Viola kawakamii is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation and display.
