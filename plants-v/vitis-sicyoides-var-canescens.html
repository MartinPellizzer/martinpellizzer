Vitis sicyoides var. canescens

Vitis sicyoides var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf grapevine, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, clustered leaves that often have a silvery or grayish underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by small, round berries. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

The leaves and fruits have been used traditionally in some regions for medicinal and culinary purposes.
