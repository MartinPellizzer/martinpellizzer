Varronia bullulata

Varronia bullulata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a leathery texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its role in traditional medicine.
