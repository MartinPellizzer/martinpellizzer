Vouapa simira

Vouapa simira, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the island of Madagascar.

It features delicate, star-shaped flowers with a vibrant mix of purple and white hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the rainforests of Madagascar. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
