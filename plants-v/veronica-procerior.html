Veronica procerior

Veronica procerior, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces seed capsules that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and easy care requirements.
