Vitex sellowiana var. lundiana

Vitex sellowiana var. lundiana, commonly known as the Brazilian vitex or Brazilian chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, clustered panicles of purple or pink flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a two-valved capsule that releases small, winged seeds.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
