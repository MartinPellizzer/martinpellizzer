Viburnum wrightii var. stipellatum

Viburnum wrightii var. stipellatum, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces showy, dark purple berries that persist through the winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant red in the fall.

Viburnum wrightii var. stipellatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
