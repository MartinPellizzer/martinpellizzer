Vernonia lankesteri

Vernonia lankesteri, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to reddish-brown flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody and erect.

Vernonia lankesteri plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
