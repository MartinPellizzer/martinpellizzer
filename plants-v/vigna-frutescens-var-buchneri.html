Vigna frutescens var. buchneri

Vigna frutescens var. buchneri, commonly known as the small cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its protein-rich seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Botanically, it is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful crop in sustainable agriculture.
