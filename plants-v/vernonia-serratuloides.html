Vernonia serratuloides

Vernonia serratuloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
