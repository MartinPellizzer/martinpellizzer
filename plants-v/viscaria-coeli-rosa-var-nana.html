Viscaria coeli-rosa var. nana

Viscaria coeli-rosa var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf cushion carnation, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming from spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
