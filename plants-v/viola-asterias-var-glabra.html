Viola asterias var. glabra

Viola asterias var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

The smooth, glabrous texture of its leaves distinguishes this variety from others in the species.
