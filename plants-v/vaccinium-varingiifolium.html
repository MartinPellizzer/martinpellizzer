Vaccinium varingiifolium

Vaccinium varingiifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaved blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cranberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its common name. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by blue or purple berries.

This species thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.
