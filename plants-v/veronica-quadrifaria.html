Veronica quadrifaria

Veronica quadrifaria, commonly known as the four-leafed speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Veronica quadrifaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings for its delicate appearance.
