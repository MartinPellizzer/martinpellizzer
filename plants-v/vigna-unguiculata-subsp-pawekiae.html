Vigna unguiculata subsp. pawekiae

Vigna unguiculata subsp.

pawekiae, commonly known as the Pawekia cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its edible seeds.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological and agricultural significance in regions where it is cultivated.
