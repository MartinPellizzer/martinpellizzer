Verbascum stevenii

Verbascum stevenii, commonly known as Steven's mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a dense, upright spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Verbascum stevenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
