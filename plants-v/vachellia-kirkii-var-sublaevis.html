Vachellia kirkii var. sublaevis

Vachellia kirkii var. sublaevis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and narrow, leathery leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the branches. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular inflorescences, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing flattened, angular seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is often used for soil stabilization and provides forage for livestock in its native habitat.
