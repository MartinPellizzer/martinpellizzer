Viola kusanoana var. pubescens

Viola kusanoana var. pubescens, commonly known as the Kusano's violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant features hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
