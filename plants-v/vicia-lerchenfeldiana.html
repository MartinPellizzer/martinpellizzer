Vicia lerchenfeldiana

Vicia lerchenfeldiana, commonly known as the Lerchenfeld's vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Vicia lerchenfeldiana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
