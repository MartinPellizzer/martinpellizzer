Vaccinium yakushimense

Vaccinium yakushimense, commonly known as the Yakushima huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the subtropical forests of Yakushima Island, Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in humid, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested environments.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and valuable species in its native habitat.
