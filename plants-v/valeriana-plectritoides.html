Valeriana plectritoides

Valeriana plectritoides, commonly known as the Sierra Valley valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, with a distinct, sometimes pungent odor.

Valeriana plectritoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and fragrance.
