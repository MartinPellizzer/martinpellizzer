Viola mongolica

Viola mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in mountainous regions and has adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Viola mongolica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
