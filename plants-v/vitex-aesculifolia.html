Vitex aesculifolia

Vitex aesculifolia, commonly known as the sweetwood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy underneath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, blue or violet flowers in the summer, which are followed by small, woody fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive, aromatic scent when crushed.

Vitex aesculifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
