Vernonia gymnoclada

Vernonia gymnoclada, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive dark center.

Vernonia gymnoclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for pollinators.
