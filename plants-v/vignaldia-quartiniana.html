Vignaldia quartiniana

Vignaldia quartiniana, commonly known as the Quartinian thornveld, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Vignaldia quartiniana is valued for its hardy nature and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
