Valeriana tuberosa proles lusitanica

Valeriana tuberosa proles lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly Portugal and Spain.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are tuberous and contain essential oils, contributing to its medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along riverbanks.
