Volkameria angustifolia

Volkameria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf volkameria, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring aromatic, narrow leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and a woody stem, making it a distinctive member of the Rutaceae family.

Volkameria angustifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
