Verbena orubica

Verbena orubica, commonly known as redstem verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
