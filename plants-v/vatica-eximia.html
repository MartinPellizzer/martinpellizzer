Vatica eximia

Vatica eximia, commonly known as the Malay ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and can grow up to 40 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and glossy in appearance. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds.

Vatica eximia is valued for its hardiness and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.
