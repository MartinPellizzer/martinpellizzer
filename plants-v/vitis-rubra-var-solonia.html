Vitis rubra var. solonia

Vitis rubra var. solonia, commonly known as Solonia grape.

It is a cultivar of the European grapevine, characterized by its compact growth habit and high productivity. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are often used for winemaking. It is resistant to powdery mildew and thrives in temperate climates.

Its vine structure makes it suitable for both table grapes and vineyard cultivation.
