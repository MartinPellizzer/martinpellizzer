Vernonia ornata

Vernonia ornata, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow flower clusters that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are sturdy and often hairy.

Vernonia ornata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
