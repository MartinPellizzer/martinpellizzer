Verbascum simplex

Verbascum simplex, commonly known as the common mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, spiky inflorescence. Verbascum simplex thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

It is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating respiratory ailments.
