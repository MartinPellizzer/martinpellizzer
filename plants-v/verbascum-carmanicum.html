Verbascum carmanicum

Verbascum carmanicum, commonly known as the Carmarathen mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum carmanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
