Vernonia antanala

Vernonia antanala, commonly known as the Madagascar ironwood, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. Vernonia antanala typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
