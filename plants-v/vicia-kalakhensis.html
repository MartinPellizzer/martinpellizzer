Vicia kalakhensis

Vicia kalakhensis, commonly known as the Kalakh wild vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Vicia kalakhensis is of interest in ecological and agricultural studies due to its resilience and potential for land rehabilitation.
