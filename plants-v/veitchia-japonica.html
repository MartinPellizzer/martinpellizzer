Veitchia japonica

Veitchia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese veitchia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Japan.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in a clumping form, with a crown of elongated, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for both landscaping and indoor cultivation.
