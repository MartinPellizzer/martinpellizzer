Valeriana peltata

Valeriana peltata, commonly known as the broadleaf valerian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its sedative properties.

Valeriana peltata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.
