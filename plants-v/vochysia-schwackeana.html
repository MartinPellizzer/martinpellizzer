Vochysia schwackeana

Vochysia schwackeana, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its dispersal.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
