Vauquelinia retherfordii

Vauquelinia retherfordii, commonly known as the Texas mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are arranged in whorls along the stems, and its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid climates. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds.

Vauquelinia retherfordii is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
