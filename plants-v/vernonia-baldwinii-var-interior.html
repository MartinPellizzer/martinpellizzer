Vernonia baldwinii var. interior

Vernonia baldwinii var. interior, commonly known as the interior ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green to reddish-purple flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species for ecological diversity.
