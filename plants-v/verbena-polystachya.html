Verbena polystachya

Verbena polystachya, commonly known as the mountain verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Verbena polystachya is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
