Viola tricolor var. bellioides

Viola tricolor var. bellioides, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
