Vitis davidi var. hispida

Vitis davidi var. hispida, commonly known as the Chinese grape or hairy grape, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by round, dark blue berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

This variety is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
