Verbena micrantha

Verbena micrantha, commonly known as small-flowered verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Verbena micrantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
