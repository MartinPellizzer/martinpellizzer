Viola mucuzii

Viola mucuzii, commonly known as the Mucuza violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola mucuzii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
