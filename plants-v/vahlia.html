Vahlia

Vahlia, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It consists of several species, primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plants are typically shrubs or small trees, characterized by their showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep red. Vahlia species often have hairy stems and leaves, and their flowers are often visited by birds and insects.

The genus is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and its potential uses in traditional medicine.
