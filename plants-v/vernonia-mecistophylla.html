Vernonia mecistophylla

Vernonia mecistophylla, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in appearance, and it produces small, hard, black seeds. Vernonia mecistophylla is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
