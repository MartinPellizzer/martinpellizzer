Verbesina guatemalensis var. glabrata

Verbesina guatemalensis var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
