Vangueria brachytricha

Vangueria brachytricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vangueria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, woody stem with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for study in arid and semi-arid regions.
