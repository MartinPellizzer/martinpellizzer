Viola nujiangensis

Viola nujiangensis, commonly known as the Nujiang violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola nujiangensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
