Viola × curnowii

Viola × curnowii, commonly known as the New Zealand violet, is a hybrid species native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant typically reaches a height of about 15–30 cm and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in spring and are characterized by their five petals and a distinctive nectar guide.

Viola × curnowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
