Vernonia cubensis

Vernonia cubensis, commonly known as Cuban ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia cubensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to attract pollinators.
