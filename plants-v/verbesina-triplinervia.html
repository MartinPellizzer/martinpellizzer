Verbesina triplinervia

Verbesina triplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have three distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

The plant is known for its hairy foliage and is often found in disturbed soils.
