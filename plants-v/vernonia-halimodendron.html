Vernonia halimodendron

Vernonia halimodendron, commonly known as the desert ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Vernonia halimodendron is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy plant in desert ecosystems.
