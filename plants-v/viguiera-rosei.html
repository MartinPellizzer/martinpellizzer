Viguiera rosei

Viguiera rosei, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. Its showy, pinkish-purple flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viguiera rosei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
