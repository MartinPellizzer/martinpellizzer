Viola uniquissima

Viola uniquissima, commonly known as the rare violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. Native to specific regions in Europe, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
