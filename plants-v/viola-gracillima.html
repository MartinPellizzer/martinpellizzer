Viola gracillima

Viola gracillima, commonly known as the slender violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and violet. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Viola gracillima thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.
