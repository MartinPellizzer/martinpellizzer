Viola calceolaria

Viola calceolaria, commonly known as the pansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, often colorful flowers that resemble small faces. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens. Viola calceolaria thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for spring bedding.

Its flowers bloom in early spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
