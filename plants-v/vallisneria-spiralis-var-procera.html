Vallisneria spiralis var. procera

Vallisneria spiralis var. procera, commonly known as the giant vallisneria, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds, lakes, and canals. Its dense foliage provides habitat and shelter for various aquatic organisms.

Vallisneria spiralis var. procera is also valued in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to improve water quality.
