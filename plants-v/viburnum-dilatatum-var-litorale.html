Viburnum dilatatum var. litorale

Viburnum dilatatum var. litorale, commonly known as the coastal honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to salt spray, making it a hardy plant for coastal environments.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for coastal landscaping.
