Veronica grandiflora

Veronica grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Veronica grandiflora prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and natural habitats.
