Vicia lens subsp. subexasperma

Vicia lens subsp.

subexasperma, commonly known as the narrow-leafed vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
