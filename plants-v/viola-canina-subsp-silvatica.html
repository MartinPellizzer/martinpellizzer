Viola canina subsp. silvatica

Viola canina subsp.

silvatica, commonly known as the wood violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in woodland habitats, thriving in moist, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces violet flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep purple. Its flowers are often borne on slender stems and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
