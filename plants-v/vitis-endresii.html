Vitis endresii

Vitis endresii, commonly known as the Endres' grape, is a species of flowering vine native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces small, clustered flowers that develop into berries. The plant is notable for its adaptability to high-altitude environments and its preference for well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a glossy appearance, while its fruit is often consumed by local wildlife.

Vitis endresii is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique genetic makeup and potential for cultivation in temperate climates.
