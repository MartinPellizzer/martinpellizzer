Vitex gabunensis

Vitex gabunensis, commonly known as Gabon vitex, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy purple or blue flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are clustered in panicles.

Vitex gabunensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
