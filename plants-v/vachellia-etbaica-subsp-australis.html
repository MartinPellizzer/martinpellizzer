Vachellia etbaica subsp. australis

Vachellia etbaica subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Ethiopian acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Horn of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often thorned branches and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures.

The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its hardiness and traditional uses, including as a source of timber and forage.
