Vaccinium duclouxii var. pubipes

Vaccinium duclouxii var. pubipes, commonly known as the pubescent huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, pubescent hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its berries, which are typically red or black when mature, are edible and have a tart flavor.

This species thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates.
