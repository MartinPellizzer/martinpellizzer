Veronica schreberiana

Veronica schreberiana, commonly known as the Schreber's speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Veronica schreberiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
