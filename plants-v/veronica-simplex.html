Veronica simplex

Veronica simplex, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in whorls along the stem, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Veronica simplex is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.
