Vriesea haematina

Vriesea haematina, commonly known as the blood leaf bromeliad, is a striking plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, forming a central cup that collects water and nutrients.

Vriesea haematina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique and colorful appearance.
