Viola prionantha var. latifolia

Viola prionantha var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive violet or purple hue. The plant produces small, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of violet or blue. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in native plant gardens.
