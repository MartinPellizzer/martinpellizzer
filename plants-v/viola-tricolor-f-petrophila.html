Viola tricolor f. petrophila

Viola tricolor f. petrophila, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a variant of the common violet that thrives in high-altitude environments.

This perennial plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking bicolor petals, typically featuring violet and white hues. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions. The species is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh weather conditions.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.
