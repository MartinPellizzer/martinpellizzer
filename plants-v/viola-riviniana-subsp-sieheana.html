Viola riviniana subsp. sieheana

Viola riviniana subsp.

sieheana, commonly known as the Siehe's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with five petals. It is part of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its erect stem and showy blooms that appear in spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
