Vittaria gueinzii

Vittaria gueinzii, commonly known as the Gueinz's spikemoss, is a species of fern ally native to South America.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and creeping growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in strobila, which are borne on the underside of the leaves.

Vittaria gueinzii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
