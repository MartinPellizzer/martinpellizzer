Viola acuminata var. dentata

Viola acuminata var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive toothed margin, which gives it its botanical name. The plant produces violet flowers that range in color from purple to blue, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady woodland environments and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
