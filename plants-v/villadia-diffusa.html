Villadia diffusa

Villadia diffusa, commonly known as the spreading mistflower, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features a fibrous root system and upright, branching stems that often grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, slightly hairy, and have a mild, aromatic scent.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded woodland areas.
