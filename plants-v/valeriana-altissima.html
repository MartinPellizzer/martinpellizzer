Valeriana altissima

Valeriana altissima, commonly known as sweet violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, violet-colored flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas and can reach heights of up to 60 cm. Its root system is known for containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

Valeriana altissima is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
