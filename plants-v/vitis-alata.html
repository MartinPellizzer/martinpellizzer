Vitis alata

Vitis alata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, silver-veined leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by black, grape-like berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Vitis alata is also known for its ability to climb using tendrils and its adaptability to various soil types.
