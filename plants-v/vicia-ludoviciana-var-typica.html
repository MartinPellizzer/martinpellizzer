Vicia ludoviciana var. typica

Vicia ludoviciana var. typica, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

The seeds are typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
