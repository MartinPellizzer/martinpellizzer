Viscum cuneifolium var. grandifolium

Viscum cuneifolium var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its berries are orange-red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This variety is native to parts of Europe and is often found in forests with suitable host trees.
