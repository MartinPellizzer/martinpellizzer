Virola urbaniana

Virola urbaniana, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are large, simple, and alternate, often with a glossy surface.

Virola urbaniana plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
