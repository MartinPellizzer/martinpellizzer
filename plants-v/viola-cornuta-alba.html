Viola cornuta alba

Viola cornuta alba, commonly known as the white horned violets, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped petals that are typically white or pale purple in color. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Viola cornuta alba is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
