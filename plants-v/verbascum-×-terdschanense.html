Verbascum × terdschanense

Verbascum × terdschanense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their large, fuzzy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves covered in dense, soft hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, erect spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid environments.
