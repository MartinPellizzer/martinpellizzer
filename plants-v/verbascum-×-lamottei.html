Verbascum × lamottei

Verbascum × lamottei, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves that are often covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
