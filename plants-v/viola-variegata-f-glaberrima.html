Viola variegata f. glaberrima

Viola variegata f. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth-leaved viola, is a compact perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a smooth, glabrous surface, distinguishing it from other viola varieties. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive white or yellowish throat. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
