Veronica caucasica var. pallida

Veronica caucasica var. pallida, commonly known as the Caucasian speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender stems that often lie prostrate or ascend, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
