Vicia magellanica var. andicola

Vicia magellanica var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Andes region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock.

Its seeds are also used in traditional medicine in some South American communities.
