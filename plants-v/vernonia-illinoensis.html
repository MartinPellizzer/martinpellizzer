Vernonia illinoensis

Vernonia illinoensis, commonly known as Illinois ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. Its showy purple to pink flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
