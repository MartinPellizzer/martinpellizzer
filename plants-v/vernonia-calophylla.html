Vernonia calophylla

Vernonia calophylla, commonly known as the white vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pale yellow to white in color.

Vernonia calophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
