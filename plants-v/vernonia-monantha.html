Vernonia monantha

Vernonia monantha, commonly known as the clustered ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils along stream banks and in swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Vernonia monantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
