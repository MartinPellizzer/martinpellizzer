Veronica sennenii

Veronica sennenii, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Veronica sennenii is often used in alpine gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
