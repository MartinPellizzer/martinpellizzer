Verbascum basianicum

Verbascum basianicum, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
