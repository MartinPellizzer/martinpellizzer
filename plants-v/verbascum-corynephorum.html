Verbascum corynephorum

Verbascum corynephorum, commonly known as the tall mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in soft hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and rocky areas.

Verbascum corynephorum is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
