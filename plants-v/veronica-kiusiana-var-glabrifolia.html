Veronica kiusiana var. glabrifolia

Veronica kiusiana var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically smooth and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
