Votomita pubescens

Votomita pubescens, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often opening in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Votomita pubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in gardens or as a decorative plant in shaded areas.
