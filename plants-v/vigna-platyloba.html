Vigna platyloba

Vigna platyloba, commonly known as the flatpod bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Vigna platyloba is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in various culinary traditions.
