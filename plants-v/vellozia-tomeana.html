Vellozia tomeana

Vellozia tomeana, commonly known as the red-flowered vellozia, is a striking member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family native to South Africa.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
