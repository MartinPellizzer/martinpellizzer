Verbascum leiocaulon

Verbascum leiocaulon, commonly known as the white mullein.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, woolly leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Verbascum leiocaulon is a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
