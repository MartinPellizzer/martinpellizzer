Viminaria denudata

Viminaria denudata, commonly known as the naked viminal, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Viminaria denudata is adapted to grow in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed areas.
