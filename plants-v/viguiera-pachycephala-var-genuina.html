Viguiera pachycephala var. genuina

Viguiera pachycephala var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features fibrous roots and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
