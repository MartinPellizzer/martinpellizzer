Viola adunca var. kirkii

Viola adunca var. kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest understories. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.

The species is relatively hardy and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full shade.
