Verbena racemosa

Verbena racemosa, commonly known as marsh verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and moist soils. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer.

Verbena racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet environments.
