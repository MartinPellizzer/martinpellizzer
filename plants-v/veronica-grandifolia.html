Veronica grandifolia

Veronica grandifolia, commonly known as the grandiflorous speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer, are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Veronica grandifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
