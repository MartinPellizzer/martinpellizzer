Vitis rupestris

Vitis rupestris, commonly known as the rock grape, is a woody vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries are typically black or blue when mature and are often used in local winemaking.

Vitis rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
