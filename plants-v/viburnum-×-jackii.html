Viburnum × jackii

Viburnum × jackii, commonly known as the Chinese snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible for birds but generally unpalatable to humans. Viburnum × jackii thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
