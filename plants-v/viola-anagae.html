Viola anagae

Viola anagae, commonly known as the Anaga violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in coastal and mountainous regions. It has small, hairy stems and produces fruit capsules that release tiny seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
