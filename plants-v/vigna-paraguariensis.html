Vigna paraguariensis

Vigna paraguariensis, commonly known as the passion flower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that have five petals, five sepals, and a crown-like structure at the center. The plant produces small, edible pods containing seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is also known for its historical and cultural significance in various indigenous communities.
