Villadia jimulcensis

Villadia jimulcensis, commonly known as the Jimulcense orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers with a mix of purple and white hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky substrates or in dense forest understories. Its root system is adapted to absorb nutrients from the limited soil available in its habitat.

Due to its restricted range and habitat vulnerability, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
