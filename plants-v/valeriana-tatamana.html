Valeriana tatamana

Valeriana tatamana, commonly known as the Japanese valerian, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana tatamana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.
