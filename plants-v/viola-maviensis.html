Viola maviensis

Viola maviensis, commonly known as the Maviensian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola maviensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
