Veronica szechuanica subsp. sikkimensis

Veronica szechuanica subsp.

sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, Bhutan, and India. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of blue to violet flowers with five petals. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
