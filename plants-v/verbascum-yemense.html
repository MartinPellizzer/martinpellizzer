Verbascum yemense

Verbascum yemense, commonly known as the Ethiopian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Verbascum yemense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
