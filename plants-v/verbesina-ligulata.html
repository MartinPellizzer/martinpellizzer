Verbesina ligulata

Verbesina ligulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggar-ticks, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has slender, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, hairy fruits called achenes.

Verbesina ligulata is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
