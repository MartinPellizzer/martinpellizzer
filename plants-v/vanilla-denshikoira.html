Vanilla denshikoira

Vanilla denshikoira, commonly known as the Japanese vanilla orchid, is a species native to Japan and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, lip-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and emit a sweet, vanilla-like aroma.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
