Veronica alpina subsp. australis

Veronica alpina subsp.

australis, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Australia. It typically features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The leaves are linear to lanceolate, often hairy, and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native insect populations.
