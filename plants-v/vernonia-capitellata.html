Vernonia capitellata

Vernonia capitellata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small, blue-gray clouds. The plant typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a bushy growth habit with lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia capitellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
