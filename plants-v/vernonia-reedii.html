Vernonia reedii

Vernonia reedii, commonly known as Reed's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Vernonia reedii plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
