Vernonia aldabrensis

Vernonia aldabrensis, commonly known as Aldabra vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and occur in dense clusters, often surrounded by pale green bracts.

Vernonia aldabrensis is adapted to coastal and rocky habitats, showing resilience to saline and arid conditions.
