Vicia glauca var. aurasiaca

Vicia glauca var. aurasiaca, commonly known as the aurasiaca sweet clover, is a variant of the sweet clover species native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves with a pale blue or grayish hue. The flowers are small, pea-like, and typically appear in clusters, ranging in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This plant is notable for its potential use in forage and as a cover crop due to its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability.
