Valerianella praecox

Valerianella praecox, commonly known as common valerian, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and streambanks.

Valerianella praecox is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
