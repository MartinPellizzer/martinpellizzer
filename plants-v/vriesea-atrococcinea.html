Vriesea atrococcinea

Vriesea atrococcinea, commonly known as the blood-leaf vriesea, is a striking epiphytic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant features a rosette of narrow, waxy leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Vriesea atrococcinea thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity to flourish.
