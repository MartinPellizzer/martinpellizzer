Veronica odora

Veronica odora, commonly known as sweet germander, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Veronica odora is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
