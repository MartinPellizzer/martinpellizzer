Viola odontocalycina

Viola odontocalycina, commonly known as the toothed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with a distinctive tooth-like structure at the base of the calyx. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola odontocalycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
