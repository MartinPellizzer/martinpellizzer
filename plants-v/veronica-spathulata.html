Veronica spathulata

Veronica spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped or spatulate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wet meadows.

Veronica spathulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
