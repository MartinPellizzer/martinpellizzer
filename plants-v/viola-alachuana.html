Viola alachuana

Viola alachuana, commonly known as the Alachua violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, often found in forest understories.

The Alachua violet is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
