Vaccinium elongatum

Vaccinium elongatum, commonly known as the longleaf blueberry, is a species of dwarf shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its small, elliptical leaves are evergreen, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit, a small, dark blue berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
