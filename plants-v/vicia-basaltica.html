Vicia basaltica

Vicia basaltica, commonly known as the basaltic vetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and the Middle East.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia makes it ecologically significant in soil enrichment.

Vicia basaltica is often found in open, sunny areas such as cliffs and slopes.
