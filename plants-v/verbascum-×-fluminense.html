Verbascum × fluminense

Verbascum × fluminense, commonly known as the yellow mullein.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Verbascum, characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the coastal regions of southern Europe and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Verbascum × fluminense is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking appearance.
