Vernonia argyrophylla

Vernonia argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its tall, erect stem that bears clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, also known as the daisy or sunflower family, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads. Vernonia argyrophylla thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
