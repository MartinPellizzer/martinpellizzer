Vouarana

Vouarana, commonly known as the "Brazilian rosewood," is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and is highly valued for its use in musical instruments. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered an endangered species.
