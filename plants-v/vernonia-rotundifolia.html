Vernonia rotundifolia

Vernonia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for native gardens.

Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
