Veronica missurica subsp. major

Veronica missurica subsp.

major, commonly known as the Great Blue Lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, clustered inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
