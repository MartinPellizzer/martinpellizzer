Vernonia klattii

Vernonia klattii, commonly known as Klatt's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and riverbanks, and can reach heights of up to six feet. Its showy purple to reddish-purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in challenging environments.
