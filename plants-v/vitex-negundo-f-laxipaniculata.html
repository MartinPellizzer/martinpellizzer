Vitex negundo f. laxipaniculata

Vitex negundo f. laxipaniculata, commonly known as the fragrant vitex or Chinese vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are opposite and often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, dry berries that mature in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional medicine.
