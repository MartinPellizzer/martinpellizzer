Vitex petitia

Vitex petitia, commonly known as the small-leaved chaste tree, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Vitex petitia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
