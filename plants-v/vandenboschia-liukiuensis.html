Vandenboschia liukiuensis

Vandenboschia liukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu sand grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is well adapted to coastal and dune environments, playing a role in stabilizing sand dunes.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
