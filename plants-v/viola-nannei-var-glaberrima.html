Viola nannei var. glaberrima

Viola nannei var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive white or yellowish throat. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its lack of pubescence on both leaves and stems.
