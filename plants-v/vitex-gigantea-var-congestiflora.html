Vitex gigantea var. congestiflora

Vitex gigantea var. congestiflora, commonly known as the giant vitex or clustered chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy inflorescences and adaptability to various soil conditions.
