Vandellia grandiflora

Vandellia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered vandellia, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces pendulous, fragrant blooms in shades of white and pink, often with a subtle greenish tinge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its unique beauty.
