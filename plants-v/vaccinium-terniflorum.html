Vaccinium terniflorum

Vaccinium terniflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to western North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are oval and dark green, and the plant bears edible berries that are typically red or black when mature.

Vaccinium terniflorum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky habitats.
