Veronica bellidioides var. bulgarica

Veronica bellidioides var. bulgarica, commonly known as the Balkan speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
