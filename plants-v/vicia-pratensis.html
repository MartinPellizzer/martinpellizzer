Vicia pratensis

Vicia pratensis, commonly known as the meadow vetchling, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to heart-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in racemes. Vicia pratensis has a fibrous root system and produces lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and damp woodlands.
