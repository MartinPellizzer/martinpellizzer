Vitis spicifera

Vitis spicifera, commonly known as the Japanese grape or spicate grape, is a species of flowering vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. Vitis spicifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

It is also used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
