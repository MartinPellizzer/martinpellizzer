Vitis vulpina subsp. riparia

Vitis vulpina subsp.

riparia, commonly known as the riverbank grape, is a vigorous vine native to North America. It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five lobes and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wetlands.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
