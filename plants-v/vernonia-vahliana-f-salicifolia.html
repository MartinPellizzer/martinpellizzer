Vernonia vahliana f. salicifolia

Vernonia vahliana f. salicifolia, commonly known as the silky Vernonia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads that are typically arranged in clusters. It is native to regions of Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in dry, sunny environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
