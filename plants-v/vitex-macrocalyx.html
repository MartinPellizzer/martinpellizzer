Vitex macrocalyx

Vitex macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-leaved chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a dry, woody capsule that splits open to release small, oblong seeds.

Vitex macrocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
