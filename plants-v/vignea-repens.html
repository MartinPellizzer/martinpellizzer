Vignea repens

Vignea repens, commonly known as the prostrate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in a sprawling, vine-like manner along the ground. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in the morning. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Vignea repens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a ground cover.
