Varasia

Varasia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. Native to regions in Asia and the Mediterranean, Varasia species often thrive in well-drained soils and full sun. They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Some species are also used in traditional herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.
