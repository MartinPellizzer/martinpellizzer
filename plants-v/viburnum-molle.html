Viburnum molle

Viburnum molle, commonly known as sweet pepper shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract birds.

Viburnum molle prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
