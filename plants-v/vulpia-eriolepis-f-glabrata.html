Vulpia eriolepis f. glabrata

Vulpia eriolepis f. glabrata, commonly known as the glabrous veldt grass, is a variant of the species Vulpia eriolepis, which is a member of the Poaceae family.

This subspecies is characterized by its reduced hair coverage, giving it a smoother appearance compared to the typical form. It is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is well adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a common sight in such regions.
