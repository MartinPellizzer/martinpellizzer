Vaccinium chinense

Vaccinium chinense, commonly known as Chinese huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Vaccinium chinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in traditional medicine.
