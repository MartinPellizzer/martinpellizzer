Vaccinium emarginatum

Vaccinium emarginatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf huckleberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and parts of Canada, typically found in moist, acidic soils of coniferous forests. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. Its leaves are elliptical, with a pointed tip and serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various species.
