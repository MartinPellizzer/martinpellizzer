Vicia durbrowii

Vicia durbrowii, commonly known as the Durbrow vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Vicia durbrowii is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
