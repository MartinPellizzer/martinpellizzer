Viguiera nelsonii

Viguiera nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces vibrant purple to pink flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem.

Viguiera nelsonii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.
