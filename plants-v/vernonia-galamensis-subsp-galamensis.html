Vernonia galamensis subsp. galamensis

Vernonia galamensis subsp.

galamensis, commonly known as the Galamensian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
