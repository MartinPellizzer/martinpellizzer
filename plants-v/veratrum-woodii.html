Veratrum woodii

Veratrum woodii, commonly known as the western white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stem and large, broad leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of white to pale green flowers in late summer, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

Veratrum woodii contains toxic alkaloids, making it poisonous to both animals and humans.
