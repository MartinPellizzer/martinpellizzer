Viguiera ciliata var. hispida

Viguiera ciliata var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid golden coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a robust root system that helps it thrive in arid conditions. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its nectar attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
