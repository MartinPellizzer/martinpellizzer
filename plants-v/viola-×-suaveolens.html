Viola × suaveolens

Viola × suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola × suaveolens is known for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
