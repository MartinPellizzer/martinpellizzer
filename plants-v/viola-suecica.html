Viola suecica

Viola suecica, commonly known as the Swedish violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

Viola suecica is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory and skin conditions.
