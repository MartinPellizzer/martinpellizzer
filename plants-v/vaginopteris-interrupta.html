Vaginopteris interrupta

Vaginopteris interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are often submerged in water. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomes are creeping and help anchor the plant in the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Vaginopteris interrupta plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
