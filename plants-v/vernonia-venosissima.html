Vernonia venosissima

Vernonia venosissima, commonly known as the veiny ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely veined, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a grayish pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Vernonia venosissima is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
