Vigna incana

Vigna incana, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Vigna incana is often grown for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary preparations.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial plant in agricultural settings.
