Vitex pubescens var. pantjarensis

Vitex pubescens var. pantjarensis, commonly known as the Pantjaran vitex, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of violet or purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its woody stems are typically greyish-brown and become more branched with age.

This species is adapted to drought and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
