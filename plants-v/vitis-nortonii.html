Vitis nortonii

Vitis nortonii, commonly known as Norton's grape, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring. The plant's berries are typically small, dark purple, and contain a single seed. It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is adapted to thrive in partial shade.

Vitis nortonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in the region.
