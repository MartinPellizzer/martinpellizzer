Viola canina var. degeneri

Viola canina var. degeneri, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a low-growing perennial species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
