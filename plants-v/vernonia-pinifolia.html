Vernonia pinifolia

Vernonia pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and pine flatwoods. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and appear in dense clusters.

Vernonia pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
