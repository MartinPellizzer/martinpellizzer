Viola × variifrons

Viola × variifrons, commonly known as the variable violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Viola × variifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
