Vernonia extranea

Vernonia extranea, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, and it produces small, cylindrical fruits called cypsela.

Vernonia extranea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
