Verbascum × rostafinskii

Verbascum × rostafinskii, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that resemble those of the common mullein. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to Central Asia and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
