Veronica ovata var. diamantiaca

Veronica ovata var. diamantiaca, commonly known as the Diamantina speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it can tolerate saline conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
