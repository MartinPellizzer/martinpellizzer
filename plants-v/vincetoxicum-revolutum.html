Vincetoxicum revolutum

Vincetoxicum revolutum, commonly known as the creeping false bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces toxic seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in certain regions. Its roots can become extensive, making it difficult to eradicate once established.

Vincetoxicum revolutum is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, where it thrives in partial shade.
