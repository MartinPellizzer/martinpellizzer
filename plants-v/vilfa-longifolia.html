Vilfa longifolia

Vilfa longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf vilfa, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Vilfa longifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
