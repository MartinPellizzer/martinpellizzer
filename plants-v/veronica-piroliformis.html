Veronica piroliformis

Veronica piroliformis, commonly known as the woolly speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
