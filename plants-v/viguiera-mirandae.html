Viguiera mirandae

Viguiera mirandae, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Viguiera mirandae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
