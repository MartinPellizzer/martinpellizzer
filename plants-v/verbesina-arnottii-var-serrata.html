Verbesina arnottii var. serrata

Verbesina arnottii var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
