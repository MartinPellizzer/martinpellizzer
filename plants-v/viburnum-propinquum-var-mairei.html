Viburnum propinquum var. mairei

Viburnum propinquum var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated margin and turn yellow in the fall.

Viburnum propinquum var. mairei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
