Verbascum faurei

Verbascum faurei, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Verbascum faurei is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
