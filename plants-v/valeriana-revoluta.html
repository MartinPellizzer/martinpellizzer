Valeriana revoluta

Valeriana revoluta, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, toothed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. Valeriana revoluta is known for its aromatic properties, with a scent reminiscent of vanilla and spices.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland edges.
