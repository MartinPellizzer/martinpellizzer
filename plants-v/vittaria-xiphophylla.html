Vittaria xiphophylla

Vittaria xiphophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green and slightly glossy. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves, which are protected by a kidney-shaped indusium. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in rocky crevices.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant, though it requires careful attention to moisture and light conditions.
