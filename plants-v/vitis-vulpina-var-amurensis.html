Vitis vulpina var. amurensis

Vitis vulpina var. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur grape, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
