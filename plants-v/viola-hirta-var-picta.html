Viola hirta var. picta

Viola hirta var. picta, commonly known as the painted violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with a distinctive pattern of darker markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
