Vulpia tenuis

Vulpia tenuis, commonly known as slender vulpia, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing, perennial plant that typically forms dense mats on disturbed soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue.

Vulpia tenuis is adapted to thrive in dry, nutrient-poor environments, making it a common weed in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
