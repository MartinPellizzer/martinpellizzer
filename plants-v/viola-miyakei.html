Viola miyakei

Viola miyakei, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in early spring. Viola miyakei prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments make it a popular choice among gardeners.
