Vagnera trifolia f. unifolia

Vagnera trifolia f. unifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vagnera, is a unique variant of the Vagnera trifolia species.

It is characterized by its reduced leaf structure, with only one leaflet per leaf, distinguishing it from the typical trifoliate form. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are not highly showy.

This botanical feature makes it a subject of interest for researchers studying plant morphology and adaptation.
