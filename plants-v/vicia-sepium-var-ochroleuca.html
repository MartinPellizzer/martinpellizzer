Vicia sepium var. ochroleuca

Vicia sepium var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow hopper vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

It has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
