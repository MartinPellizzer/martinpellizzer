Viola mandshurica f. villosa

Viola mandshurica f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple or violet, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
