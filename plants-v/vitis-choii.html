Vitis choii

Vitis choii, commonly known as the desert grape, is a perennial vine native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to edible, round berries. Vitis choii is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
