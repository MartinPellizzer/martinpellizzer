Veronica sempervirens

Veronica sempervirens, commonly known as evergreen speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small blue or purple flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value contribute to its widespread cultivation in horticulture.
