Viscum schottii

Viscum schottii, commonly known as the Schott's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Viscaceae family.

It grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, leathery leaves and small white or yellowish flowers. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing sticky seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Viscum schottii plays a role in its ecosystem by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.
