Venidium ramosissimum

Venidium ramosissimum, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and branched, woody stems that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue-black berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

Venidium ramosissimum is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
