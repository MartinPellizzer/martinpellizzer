Veronica upsaliensis

Veronica upsaliensis, commonly known as the Upsala speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to northern Europe.

It typically features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along its stems. The plant has thin, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
