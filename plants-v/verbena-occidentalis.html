Verbena occidentalis

Verbena occidentalis, commonly known as western verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its spikes of blue, purple, or white flowers. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
