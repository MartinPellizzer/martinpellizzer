Vignea glareosa

Vignea glareosa, commonly known as the yellow vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. Vignea glareosa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
