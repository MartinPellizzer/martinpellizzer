Vignea cinerea

Vignea cinerea, commonly known as the pale morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or white, opening in the early morning. Vignea cinerea is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in partial to full sun.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in gardens and natural habitats across its range.
