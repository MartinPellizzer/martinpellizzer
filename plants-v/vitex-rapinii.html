Vitex rapinii

Vitex rapinii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vitex, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in summer. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Vitex rapinii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
