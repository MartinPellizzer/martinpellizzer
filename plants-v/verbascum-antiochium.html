Verbascum antiochium

Verbascum antiochium, commonly known as the Antioch poppy, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, upright stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has soft, fuzzy leaves that are gray-green in color, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
