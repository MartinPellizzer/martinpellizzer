Veronica pseudolongifolia subsp. borealis

Veronica pseudolongifolia subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the Arctic speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of North America and Eurasia. It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons. It is often found in tundra and subarctic habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species well-suited to extreme environmental conditions.
