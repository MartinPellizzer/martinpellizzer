Vitex zambesiaca

Vitex zambesiaca, commonly known as the Zambesi chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually smooth and dark green. The plant produces fragrant, blue to purple flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Vitex zambesiaca is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
