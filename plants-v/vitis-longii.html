Vitis longii

Vitis longii, commonly known as the longleaf grapevine, is a lesser-known species within the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and slender, woody stems that often twine around supporting structures. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly pubescent underside. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish-white in color, and the berries are small, round, and dark purple when mature.

This plant is native to parts of China and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
