Valeriana collina var. stolonifera

Valeriana collina var. stolonifera, commonly known as the creeping valerian, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with rhizomatous stolons that allow it to form dense mats. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are borne in terminal spikes and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
