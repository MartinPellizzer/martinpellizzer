Verbesina sordescens var. glabrata

Verbesina sordescens var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are typically smooth or slightly pubescent. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, producing small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as marshes and along stream banks.
