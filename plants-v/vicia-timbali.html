Vicia timbali

Vicia timbali, commonly known as the Timbali vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its pods contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Vicia timbali is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
