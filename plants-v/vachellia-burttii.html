Vachellia burttii

Vachellia burttii, commonly known as the Burtt's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures in mature plants. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, cylindrical inflorescences, which attract various pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep taproots and a waxy coating on its leaves to reduce water loss.

Vachellia burttii is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used traditionally for fuel and construction.
