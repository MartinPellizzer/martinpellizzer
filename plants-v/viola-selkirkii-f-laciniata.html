Viola selkirkii f. laciniata

Viola selkirkii f. laciniata, commonly known as the Selkirk ivy violet, is a unique hybrid form of the Selkirk violet.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble those of ivy. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover in humid climates.
