Viola emarginata

Viola emarginata, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive notched or scalloped edge, and its flowers are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces small, delicate blossoms that bloom in early spring, often in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Viola emarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
