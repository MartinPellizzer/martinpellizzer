Viola variegata var. primorskajensis

Viola variegata var. primorskajensis, commonly known as the Primorsky viola, is a rare and unique variant of the viola genus native to the Primorsky Krai region of Russia.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring a mix of green and white or yellow markings. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. The flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
