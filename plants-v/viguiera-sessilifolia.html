Viguiera sessilifolia

Viguiera sessilifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
