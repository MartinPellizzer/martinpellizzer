Viola grypoceras var. imberbis

Viola grypoceras var. imberbis, commonly known as the hairless violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions in Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, hairless surface, distinguishing it from other viola species. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, often with a white or yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable subject for ecological and horticultural study.
