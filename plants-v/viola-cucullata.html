Viola cucullata

Viola cucullata, commonly known as the hooded violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hood-like structure over its flowers. Its violet to purple flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to the forest floor.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
