Valeriana tarbagataica

Valeriana tarbagataica, commonly known as the Siberian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown roots and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana tarbagataica is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
