Vicia unijuga f. venusta

Vicia unijuga f. venusta, commonly known as the showy vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to violet in color, arranged in elongated clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Vicia unijuga f. venusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
