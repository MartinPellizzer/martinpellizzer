Vigna hosei

Vigna hosei, commonly known as the small cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped pods that contain edible seeds, often used in various cuisines. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small white or purple flowers. Vigna hosei thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both nutritional and agricultural purposes.
