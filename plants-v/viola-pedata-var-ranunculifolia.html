Viola pedata var. ranunculifolia

Viola pedata var. ranunculifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are typically violet or purple with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas. It has a short flowering period in spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
