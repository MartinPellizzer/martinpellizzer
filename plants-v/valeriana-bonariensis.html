Valeriana bonariensis

Valeriana bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is known for containing volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Valeriana bonariensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in herbal remedies.
