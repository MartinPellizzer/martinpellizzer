Vernonia herbacea

Vernonia herbacea, commonly known as slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color and bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia herbacea is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
