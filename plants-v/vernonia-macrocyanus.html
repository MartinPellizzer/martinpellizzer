Vernonia macrocyanus

Vernonia macrocyanus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and fluffy, pale blue or white flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower structure. Vernonia macrocyanus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
