Viola hispida subsp. cryana

Viola hispida subsp.

cryana, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with five petals. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
