Viburnum opulus var. trilobum

Viburnum opulus var. trilobum, commonly known as three-lobed elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five ovate lobes, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like drupes in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
