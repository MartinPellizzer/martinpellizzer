Veronica × mauksii

Veronica × mauksii, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers on slender stems, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture, and it prefers well-drained soils in partial shade to full sun.

Veronica × mauksii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
