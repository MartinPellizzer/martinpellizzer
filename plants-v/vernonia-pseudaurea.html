Vernonia pseudaurea

Vernonia pseudaurea, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia pseudaurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
