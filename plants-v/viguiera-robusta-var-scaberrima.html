Viguiera robusta var. scaberrima

Viguiera robusta var. scaberrima, commonly known as roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-green flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and ecologically valuable plant for pollinators.
