Vernonia thodei

Vernonia thodei, commonly known as the Natal ironwood, is a tall, evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, hard wood and dense foliage, which makes it valuable for timber and ornamental purposes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged oppositely along the stems. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

The tree is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
