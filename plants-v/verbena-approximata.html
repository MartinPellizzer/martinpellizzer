Verbena approximata

Verbena approximata, commonly known as narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Verbena approximata is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
