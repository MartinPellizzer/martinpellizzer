Vicia canescens subsp. leucomala

Vicia canescens subsp.

leucomala, commonly known as the white-flowered vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, elongated clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in slender pods and are typically dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
