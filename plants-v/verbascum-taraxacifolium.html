Verbascum taraxacifolium

Verbascum taraxacifolium, commonly known as the dandelion-leaved mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves and yellow, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
