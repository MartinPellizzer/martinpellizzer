Viola × luciae

Viola × luciae, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers cool, moist environments. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world.

Viola × luciae is often found in woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
