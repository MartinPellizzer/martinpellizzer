Veratrum maackii

Veratrum maackii, commonly known as Maack's white hellebore, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, stout stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white or pale green flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Veratrum maackii contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
