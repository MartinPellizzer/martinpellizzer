Vernonia salviifolia

Vernonia salviifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Vernonia salviifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
