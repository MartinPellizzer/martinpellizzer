Veronica tournefortii var. albiflora

Veronica tournefortii var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
