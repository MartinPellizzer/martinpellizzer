Viscaria taenioides

Viscaria taenioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, compound leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, which are borne in clusters. Viscaria taenioides is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
