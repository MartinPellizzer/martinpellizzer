Vitis trifida

Vitis trifida, commonly known as the three-leaf grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and red to black berries that are edible. Vitis trifida is often found in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
