Vicia fimbriata

Vicia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive fringed appearance on the leaflets. Its small, pea-like flowers are typically pale blue or purple, and it produces elongated pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

Vicia fimbriata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in certain habitats.
