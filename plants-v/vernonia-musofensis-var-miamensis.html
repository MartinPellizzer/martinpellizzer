Vernonia musofensis var. miamensis

Vernonia musofensis var. miamensis, commonly known as the Miamensis ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and it produces clusters of pale yellow to brownish flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
