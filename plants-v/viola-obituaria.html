Viola obituaria

Viola obituaria, commonly known as the funeral violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola obituaria is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
