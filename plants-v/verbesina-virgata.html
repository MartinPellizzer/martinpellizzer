Verbesina virgata

Verbesina virgata, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant features upright stems covered with stiff, bristly hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its showy yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, are clustered in dense, spherical heads.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for various health purposes.
