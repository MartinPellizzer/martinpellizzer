Vaccinium longicaudatum

Vaccinium longicaudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that are typically blue or purple when mature. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and have a distinctive elongated shape.

Vaccinium longicaudatum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
