Viola acuminata subsp. austroussuriensis

Viola acuminata subsp.

austroussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces violet or purple flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy blooms.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
