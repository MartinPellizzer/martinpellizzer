Viguiera cordifolia

Viguiera cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Viguiera cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
