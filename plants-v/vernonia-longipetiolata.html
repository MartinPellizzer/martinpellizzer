Vernonia longipetiolata

Vernonia longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled ironweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with long petioles, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Vernonia longipetiolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
