Verbascum × bastardii subsp. pilosum

Verbascum × bastardii subsp.

pilosum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.
