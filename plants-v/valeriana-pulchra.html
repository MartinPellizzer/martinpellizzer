Valeriana pulchra

Valeriana pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
