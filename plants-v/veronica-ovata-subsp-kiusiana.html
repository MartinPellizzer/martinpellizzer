Veronica ovata subsp. kiusiana

Veronica ovata subsp.

kiusiana, commonly known as the Kiusian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems. This subspecies is often found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
