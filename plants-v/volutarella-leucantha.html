Volutarella leucantha

Volutarella leucantha, commonly known as the white spiral orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, spiral-shaped flowers that bloom in a striking white color. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that help it absorb moisture from the air.

Volutarella leucantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
