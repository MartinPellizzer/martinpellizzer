Vaccinium jesoense

Vaccinium jesoense, commonly known as the Jesso blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested areas.

Vaccinium jesoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
