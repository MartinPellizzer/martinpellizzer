Vernonia coriifolia

Vernonia coriifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features rough, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Vernonia coriifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
