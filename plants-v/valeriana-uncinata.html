Valeriana uncinata

Valeriana uncinata, commonly known as the hooked valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a stout, aromatic rootstock and erect, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense umbels, and are usually pinkish or white in color.

Valeriana uncinata is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects due to the presence of volatile oils and alkaloids.
