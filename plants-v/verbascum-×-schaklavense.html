Verbascum × schaklavense

Verbascum × schaklavense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and ease of care.
