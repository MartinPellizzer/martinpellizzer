Viola elegans

Viola elegans, commonly known as the elegant violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
