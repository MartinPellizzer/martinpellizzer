Veronicastrum brunonianum subsp. brunonianum

Veronicastrum brunonianum subsp.

brunonianum, commonly known as the purple loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged opposite along the stem. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it an important plant in wetland ecosystems.
