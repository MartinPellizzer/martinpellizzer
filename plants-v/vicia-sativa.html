Vicia sativa

Vicia sativa, commonly known as common vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used as a food source for livestock. Vicia sativa is a hardy annual or biennial plant that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
