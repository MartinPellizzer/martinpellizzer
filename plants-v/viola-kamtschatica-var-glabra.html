Viola kamtschatica var. glabra

Viola kamtschatica var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine habitats and thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy violet or purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
