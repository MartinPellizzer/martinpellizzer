Veronica thymoides subsp. thymoides

Veronica thymoides subsp.

thymoides, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
