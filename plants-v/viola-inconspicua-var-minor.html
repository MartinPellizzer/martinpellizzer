Viola inconspicua var. minor

Viola inconspicua var. minor, commonly known as the slender violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or violet. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found growing in clusters along the ground.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
