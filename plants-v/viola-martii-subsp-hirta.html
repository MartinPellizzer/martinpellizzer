Viola martii subsp. hirta

Viola martii subsp.

hirta, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions.

The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are violet to purple in color, with five petals and a spurred structure at the base.

The subspecies is notable for its hirsute foliage and is often found in high-altitude environments.
