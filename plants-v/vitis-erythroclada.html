Vitis erythroclada

Vitis erythroclada, commonly known as the red-stemmed grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue to black berries. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional Chinese medicine.

Vitis erythroclada thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
