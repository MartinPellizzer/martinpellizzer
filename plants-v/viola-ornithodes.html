Viola ornithodes

Viola ornithodes, commonly known as the bird's eye violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and violet-colored flowers with a distinctive yellow center. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist soils. Viola ornithodes is often found in shaded environments, where it thrives under the canopy of trees.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
