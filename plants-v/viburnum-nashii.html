Viburnum nashii

Viburnum nashii, commonly known as the Japanese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and features clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used as ornamental features. Viburnum nashii has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil.
