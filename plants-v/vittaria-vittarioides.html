Vittaria vittarioides

Vittaria vittarioides, commonly known as the creeping polypody, is a small evergreen fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It has slender, arching fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length, with a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces primarily through spore dispersal.

Vittaria vittarioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
