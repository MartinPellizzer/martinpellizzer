Vicia uncinata

Vicia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is native to Europe and western Asia. The plant features slender, ascending stems with pinnately compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small, pea-like, and usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters.

Vicia uncinata is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
