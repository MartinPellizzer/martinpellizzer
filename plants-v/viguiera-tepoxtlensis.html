Viguiera tepoxtlensis

Viguiera tepoxtlensis, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Viguiera tepoxtlensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
