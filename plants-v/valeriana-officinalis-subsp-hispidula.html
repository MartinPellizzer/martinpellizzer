Valeriana officinalis subsp. hispidula

Valeriana officinalis subsp.

hispidula, commonly known as hairy valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Its rhizomes and roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.
