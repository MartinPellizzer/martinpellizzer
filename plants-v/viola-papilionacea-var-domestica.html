Viola papilionacea var. domestica

Viola papilionacea var. domestica, commonly known as the domestic viola, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers that come in shades of violet, blue, and white. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in well-drained soil with partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant indoors.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
