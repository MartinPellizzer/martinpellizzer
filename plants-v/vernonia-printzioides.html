Vernonia printzioides

Vernonia printzioides, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include fibrous roots and a robust stem system adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Vernonia printzioides is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
