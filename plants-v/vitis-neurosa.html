Vitis neurosa

Vitis neurosa, commonly known as the black grapevine, is a woody vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its roots can form symbiotic relationships with fungi, enhancing nutrient uptake.

Vitis neurosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
