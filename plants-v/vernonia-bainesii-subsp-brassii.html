Vernonia bainesii subsp. brassii

Vernonia bainesii subsp.

brassii, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and bright yellow inflorescences.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
