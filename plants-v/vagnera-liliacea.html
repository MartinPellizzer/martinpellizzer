Vagnera liliacea

Vagnera liliacea, commonly known as the pink lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Vagnera liliacea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
