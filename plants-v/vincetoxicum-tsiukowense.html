Vincetoxicum tsiukowense

Vincetoxicum tsiukowense, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume that contains several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils.
