Vitis javanica

Vitis javanica, commonly known as the Javan grape, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. The plant's leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a waxy coating. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to dark purple when ripe.

Vitis javanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
