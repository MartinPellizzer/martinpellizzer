Veronica longifolia f. grandis

Veronica longifolia f. grandis, commonly known as the grandiflora speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
