Viola brevistipulata f. laciniata

Viola brevistipulata f. laciniata, commonly known as the dissected violet, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely lobed leaves that give it a lace-like appearance. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive pattern of markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
