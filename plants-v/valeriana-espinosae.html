Valeriana espinosae

Valeriana espinosae, commonly known as the spiny valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Paraguay.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers that bloom during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Valeriana espinosae is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential calming properties.
