Viola palustris var. pensylvanica

Viola palustris var. pensylvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and early spring blooming period.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
