Vignea lithophila

Vignea lithophila, commonly known as the rock vine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in rocky or coastal environments, often growing on cliffs or among stones. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery hair.

Vignea lithophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
