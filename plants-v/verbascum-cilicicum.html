Verbascum cilicicum

Verbascum cilicicum, commonly known as the Cilician mullein, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes over 200 species of flowering plants commonly referred to as mulleins. The plant is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which appear in late summer, are typically clustered in dense, elongated spikes.

Verbascum cilicicum is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
