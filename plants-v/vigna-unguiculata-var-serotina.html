Vigna unguiculata var. serotina

Vigna unguiculata var. serotina, commonly known as cowpea or black-eyed pea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to warm climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions. It plays a significant role in agricultural systems due to its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen.

The seeds are widely used in various cuisines and are also valued for their nutritional content.
