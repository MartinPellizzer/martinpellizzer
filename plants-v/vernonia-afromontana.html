Vernonia afromontana

Vernonia afromontana, commonly known as African ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of East Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Vernonia afromontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
