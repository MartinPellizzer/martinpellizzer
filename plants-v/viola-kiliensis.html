Viola kiliensis

Viola kiliensis, commonly known as the Kili Mountain violet, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountain meadows. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola kiliensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
