Vaccinium eberhardtii var. pubescens

Vaccinium eberhardtii var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acidic, evergreen shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are typically covered in a whitish bloom. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its varietal name.

This species thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
