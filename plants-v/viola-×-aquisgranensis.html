Viola × aquisgranensis

Viola × aquisgranensis, commonly known as the European violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile choice for horticultural use.
