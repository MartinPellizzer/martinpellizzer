Verbascum coeruleum

Verbascum coeruleum, commonly known as blue flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has soft, fuzzy leaves that are gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbascum coeruleum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
