Viburnum utile

Viburnum utile, commonly known as the American hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by edible, nut-like fruits. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Viburnum utile thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
