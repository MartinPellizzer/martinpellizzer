Vulpia inops

Vulpia inops, commonly known as the slender vulpia, is a small annual grass native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short life cycle, which allows it to thrive in environments with frequent disturbances.

Vulpia inops is often considered a weed but plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
