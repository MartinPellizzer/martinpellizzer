Verbascum degenii

Verbascum degenii, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Verbascum degenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
