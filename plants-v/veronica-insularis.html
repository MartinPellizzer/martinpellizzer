Veronica insularis

Veronica insularis, commonly known as the island speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Azores and Madeira islands.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to coastal environments.

Its flowers bloom from spring to summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
