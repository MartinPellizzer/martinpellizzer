Viola pubescens var. peckii

Viola pubescens var. peckii, commonly known as the Peck's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy violet flowers with five petals. Its flowers are often pinkish or purple in color, and they bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
