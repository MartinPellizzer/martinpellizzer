Verbena erinoides f. sabinii

Verbena erinoides f. sabinii, commonly known as the silverleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy foliage and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is known for its compact growth habit and drought tolerance. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a distinctive powdery coating that helps reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
