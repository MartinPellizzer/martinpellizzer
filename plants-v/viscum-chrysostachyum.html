Viscum chrysostachyum

Viscum chrysostachyum, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers that are often fragrant. The plant's berries are small, red, and contain sticky seeds that adhere to birds, facilitating seed dispersal. Viscum chrysostachyum relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it provides food for certain bird species.

Its growth is slow, and it can live for several decades, forming dense foliage on its host.
