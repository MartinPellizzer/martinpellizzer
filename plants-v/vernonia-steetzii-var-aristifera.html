Vernonia steetzii var. aristifera

Vernonia steetzii var. aristifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
