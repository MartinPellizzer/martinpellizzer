Vassobia dichotoma

Vassobia dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched vassobia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its branching habit and opposite leaf arrangement. The leaves are simple, ovate, and have a rough texture, often with a waxy coating. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom during the wet season.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
