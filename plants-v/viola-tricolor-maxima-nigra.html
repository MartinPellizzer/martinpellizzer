Viola tricolor maxima-nigra

Viola tricolor maxima-nigra, commonly known as the heartsease or three-color violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture. The flowers are typically violet, white, and yellow, arranged in a distinctive triangular pattern. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
