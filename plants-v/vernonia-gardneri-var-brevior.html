Vernonia gardneri var. brevior

Vernonia gardneri var. brevior, commonly known as the short-stemmed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, dark purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
