Vernonia × dissimilis

Vernonia × dissimilis, commonly known as the hybrid ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. It produces small, oblong seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

The foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance.
