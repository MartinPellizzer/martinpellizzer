Viola betonicifolia subsp. australiensis

Viola betonicifolia subsp.

australiensis, commonly known as the Australian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as undergrowth in forests or along stream banks. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure and flowering habits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of Australian flora.
