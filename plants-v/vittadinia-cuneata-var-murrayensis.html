Vittadinia cuneata var. murrayensis

Vittadinia cuneata var. murrayensis, commonly known as the Murray's cuneate everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
