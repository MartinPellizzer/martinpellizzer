Viola pedatifida

Viola pedatifida, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola pedatifida is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
