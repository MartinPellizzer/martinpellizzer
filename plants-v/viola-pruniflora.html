Viola pruniflora

Viola pruniflora, commonly known as the cherry-leaved violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive cherry-red underside. The plant produces showy violet flowers that range in color from purple to white, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Viola pruniflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
