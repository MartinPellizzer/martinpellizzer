Vicia peregrina subsp. curvituba

Vicia peregrina subsp.

curvituba, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are pinnate with small, oval leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by slender, curved pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for certain insect species.
