Viola × sedunensis

Viola × sedunensis, commonly known as the violet-leafed viola, is a hybrid plant that combines characteristics of several viola species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and violet. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom in partial shade.

Viola × sedunensis is also valued for its resilience and adaptability to various soil types.
