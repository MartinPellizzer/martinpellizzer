Veronica cretacea

Veronica cretacea, commonly known as the Cretan speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica cretacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
