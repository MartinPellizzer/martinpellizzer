Viola silvatica var. nemoralis

Viola silvatica var. nemoralis, commonly known as the wood violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and along forest edges, thriving in partial to full shade. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
