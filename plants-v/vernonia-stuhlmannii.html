Vernonia stuhlmannii

Vernonia stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Vernonia stuhlmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
