Velleia hispida

Velleia hispida, commonly known as the hairy velleia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Velleia hispida is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in garden settings.
