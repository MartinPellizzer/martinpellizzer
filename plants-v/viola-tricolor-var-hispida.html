Viola tricolor var. hispida

Viola tricolor var. hispida, commonly known as the Hispid Violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, or hispid, stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of violet, blue, and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
