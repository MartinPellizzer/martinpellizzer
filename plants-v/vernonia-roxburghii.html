Vernonia roxburghii

Vernonia roxburghii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to termites. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually opposite and covered in fine hairs. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.
