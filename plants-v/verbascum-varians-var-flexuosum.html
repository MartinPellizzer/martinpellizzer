Verbascum varians var. flexuosum

Verbascum varians var. flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stems, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and also covered in fine, velvety hairs.

This species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
