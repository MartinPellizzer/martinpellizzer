Verticordia nitens

Verticordia nitens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved featherflower, is a striking member of the family Myrtaceae native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for rock gardens and native landscapes.

Verticordia nitens is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
