Viola canina subsp. pumila

Viola canina subsp.

pumila, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Violaceae family.
