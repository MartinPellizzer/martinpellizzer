Vernonia megapotamica var. argentina

Vernonia megapotamica var. argentina, commonly known as the Argentine ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy yellow to orange flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy, and it produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
