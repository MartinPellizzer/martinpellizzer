Veronica wormskjoldii var. stelleri

Veronica wormskjoldii var. stelleri, commonly known as Steller's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This species is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a rare and endemic plant in its native range.
