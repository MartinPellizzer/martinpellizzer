Viola elata

Viola elata, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola elata is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
