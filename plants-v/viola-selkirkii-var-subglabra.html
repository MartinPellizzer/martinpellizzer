Viola selkirkii var. subglabra

Viola selkirkii var. subglabra, commonly known as the Selkirk's violets, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with five petals. It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves, which distinguish it from other varieties of Viola selkirkii.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
