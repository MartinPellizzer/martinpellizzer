Viola phalacrocarpa

Viola phalacrocarpa, commonly known as the water violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, pond edges, and other moist habitats, where it thrives in saturated soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are violet-blue with yellow markings. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Viola phalacrocarpa plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
