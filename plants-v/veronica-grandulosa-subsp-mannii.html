Veronica grandulosa subsp. mannii

Veronica grandulosa subsp.

mannii, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
