Vaccinium virgatum var. ozarkense

Vaccinium virgatum var. ozarkense, commonly known as Ozark blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for cultivation in suitable climates.
