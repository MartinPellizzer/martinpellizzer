Vaccinium saxicola

Vaccinium saxicola, commonly known as the saxifrage bilberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and vibrant blue or purple berries. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and the plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.

Vaccinium saxicola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
