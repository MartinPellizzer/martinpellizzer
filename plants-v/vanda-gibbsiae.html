Vanda gibbsiae

Vanda gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' vanda, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and yellow. The plant typically has long, strap-like leaves and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Vanda gibbsiae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
