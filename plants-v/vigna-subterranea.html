Vigna subterranea

Vigna subterranea, commonly known as the underground bean, is a legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its unique growth habit, with the main stem growing underground and the leaves and flowers emerging above ground. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate rainfall.

Vigna subterranea is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional cuisines across its native range.
