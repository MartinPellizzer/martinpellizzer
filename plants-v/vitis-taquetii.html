Vitis taquetii

Vitis taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Mexico. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and other structures. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a glossy appearance, while its flowers are small and greenish-white in color.

Vitis taquetii is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
