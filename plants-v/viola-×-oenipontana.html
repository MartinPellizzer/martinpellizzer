Viola × oenipontana

Viola × oenipontana, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a member of the Violaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.

The hybrid nature of Viola × oenipontana results from crosses between different viola species, contributing to its genetic diversity and adaptability.
