Vigna triloba var. somalensis

Vigna triloba var. somalensis, commonly known as the Somali cluster bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval to oblong in shape. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Vigna triloba var. somalensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful crop in sustainable agriculture.
