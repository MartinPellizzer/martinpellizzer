Verbesina fraseri var. nelsonii

Verbesina fraseri var. nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's beggarticks, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its serrated leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
