Vernonia gagnepainiana

Vernonia gagnepainiana, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a striking flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, often aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions.

Vernonia gagnepainiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
