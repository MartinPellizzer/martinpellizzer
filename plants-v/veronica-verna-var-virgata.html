Veronica verna var. virgata

Veronica verna var. virgata, commonly known as the early blueweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs, with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, blooming from early spring to late summer.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
