Viola calcarata subsp. cavillieri

Viola calcarata subsp.

cavillieri, commonly known as the Sicilian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet-blue hue and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and blue.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
