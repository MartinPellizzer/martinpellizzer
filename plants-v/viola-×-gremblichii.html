Viola × gremblichii

Viola × gremblichii, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Viola tricolor and Viola lutea, resulting in a plant with distinctive, colorful flowers. The species is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, often purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along shaded paths.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
