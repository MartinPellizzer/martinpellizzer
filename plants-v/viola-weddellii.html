Viola weddellii

Viola weddellii, commonly known as the Weddell's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Viola weddellii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
