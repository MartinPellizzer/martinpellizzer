Vouapa suaveolens

Vouapa suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, emitting a sweet, aromatic scent. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Vouapa suaveolens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
