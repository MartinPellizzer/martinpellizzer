Verbascum phlomoides var. adulterinum

Verbascum phlomoides var. adulterinum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems covered in dense, soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a thick layer of silky hairs.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
