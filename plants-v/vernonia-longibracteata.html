Vernonia longibracteata

Vernonia longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of up to six feet. Its showy, purple-to-yellow flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
