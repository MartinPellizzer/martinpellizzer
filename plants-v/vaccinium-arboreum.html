Vaccinium arboreum

Vaccinium arboreum, commonly known as the evergreen huckleberry, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes blueberries and cranberries, and is characterized by its acidic soil preferences. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and oval-shaped, providing dense foliage.

Vaccinium arboreum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
