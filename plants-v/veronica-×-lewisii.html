Veronica × lewisii

Veronica × lewisii, commonly known as Lewis's speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. It produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Veronica × lewisii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
