Ventilago gladiata

Ventilago gladiata, commonly known as the swordleaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in shrubland and woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions make it a notable species in its native range.
