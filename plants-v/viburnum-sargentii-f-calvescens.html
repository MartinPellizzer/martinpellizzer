Viburnum sargentii f. calvescens

Viburnum sargentii f. calvescens, commonly known as the Sargent viburnum, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in autumn, adding ornamental value. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for landscape design.
