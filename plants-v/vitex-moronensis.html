Vitex moronensis

Vitex moronensis, commonly known as the African blueberry or blue hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and compact growth habit.
