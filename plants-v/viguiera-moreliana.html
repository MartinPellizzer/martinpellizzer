Viguiera moreliana

Viguiera moreliana, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its showy, purple to pink flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viguiera moreliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
