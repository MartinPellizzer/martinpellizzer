Viola dissecta var. albida

Viola dissecta var. albida, commonly known as the white viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola dissecta var. albida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
