Viburnum nudum var. cassinoides

Viburnum nudum var. cassinoides, commonly known as the swamp viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green above and lighter green beneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
