Vitis vinifera var. verlantina

Vitis vinifera var. verlantina, commonly known as the Verlantina grape, is a rare and ancient variety of European grapevine.

It is characterized by its small, dark-colored berries and is typically grown in cooler climates. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and produces clusters of fruit that are often used in traditional winemaking. Its botanical features include thick, waxy leaves and a robust root system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor profile and historical significance in viticulture.
