Viola lutea var. hispida

Viola lutea var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid yellow violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with a distinctive violet throat, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
