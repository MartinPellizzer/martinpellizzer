Vicia nissoliana subsp. sojuchica

Vicia nissoliana subsp.

sojuchica, commonly known as the Siberian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its role in nitrogen fixation and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
