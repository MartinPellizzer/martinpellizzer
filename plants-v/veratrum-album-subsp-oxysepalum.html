Veratrum album subsp. oxysepalum

Veratrum album subsp.

oxysepalum, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of the United States and Canada. It is characterized by its tall, smooth, green stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are large, broad, and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture.

This species contains toxic alkaloids and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.
