Viola micrantha proles grandistipulata

Viola micrantha proles grandistipulata, commonly known as the large-stipule viola, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive, elongated stipules. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
