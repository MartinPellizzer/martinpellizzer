Veronica anagallis-aquatica subsp. transiens

Veronica anagallis-aquatica subsp.

transiens, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite pairs of ovate to lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of the Veronica genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and streams.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
