Vinca speciosa

Vinca speciosa, commonly known as the common periwinkle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that produces clusters of bright blue or purple flowers in the spring. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and a trailing growth habit, making it popular for ground cover. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, often used in gardens and landscapes.

Vinca speciosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to suppress weeds in shaded areas.
