Vochysia citrifolia

Vochysia citrifolia, commonly known as the citric tree or yellow tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
