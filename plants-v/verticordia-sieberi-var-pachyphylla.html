Verticordia sieberi var. pachyphylla

Verticordia sieberi var. pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved feather flower, is a unique species native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences that produce vivid red or orange flowers, often resembling feathers in appearance. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native habitat. Its leaves are thick and leathery, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

This variety is particularly notable for its striking floral display and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
