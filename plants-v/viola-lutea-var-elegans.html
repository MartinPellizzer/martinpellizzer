Viola lutea var. elegans

Viola lutea var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, toothed leaves and delicate, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
