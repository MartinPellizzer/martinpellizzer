Vitex madiensis var. gossweileri

Vitex madiensis var. gossweileri, commonly known as the Cape leadwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. These flowers are followed by small, woody fruits containing a single seed.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
