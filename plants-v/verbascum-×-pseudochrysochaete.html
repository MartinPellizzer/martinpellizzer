Verbascum × pseudochrysochaete

Verbascum × pseudochrysochaete, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves and showy yellow flowers.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which is part of the family Scrophulariaceae. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and ease of care.
