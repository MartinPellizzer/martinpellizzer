Vachellia pacensis

Vachellia pacensis, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Vachellia pacensis plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
