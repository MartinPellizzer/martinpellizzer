Vatica obtusifolia

Vatica obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leafed ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The tree typically grows in lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with a blunt tip, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
