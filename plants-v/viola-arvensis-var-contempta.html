Viola arvensis var. contempta

Viola arvensis var. contempta, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white. It has a fibrous root system that allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

The species is known for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
