Vaccinium carmesinum

Vaccinium carmesinum, commonly known as the red evergreen huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are broad and leathery, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Vaccinium carmesinum plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
