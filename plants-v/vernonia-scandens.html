Vernonia scandens

Vernonia scandens, commonly known as the creeping ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows along streambanks and moist woodlands. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow-orange flower heads appear in late summer and are arranged in loose clusters.

Vernonia scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded to sunny conditions.
