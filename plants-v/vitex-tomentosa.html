Vitex tomentosa

Vitex tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Vitex tomentosa is often used in traditional medicine and ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
