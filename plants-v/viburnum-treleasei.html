Viburnum treleasei

Viburnum treleasei, commonly known as the Trelise viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its seasonal interest.

Viburnum treleasei prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
