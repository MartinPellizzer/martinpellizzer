Visenia serrata

Visenia serrata, commonly known as the serrated vicia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant is native to various regions in Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along woodland edges. Its seed pods develop into elongated, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

Visenia serrata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
