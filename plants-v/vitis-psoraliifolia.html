Vitis psoraliifolia

Vitis psoraliifolia, commonly known as the Chinese grape or psoraliifolia grape, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, often fragrant, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. It is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Vitis psoraliifolia is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
