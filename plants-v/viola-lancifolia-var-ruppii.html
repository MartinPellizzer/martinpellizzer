Viola lancifolia var. ruppii

Viola lancifolia var. ruppii, commonly known as the rock viol, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky habitats in temperate regions.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive notched margin, and produces clusters of violet-blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
