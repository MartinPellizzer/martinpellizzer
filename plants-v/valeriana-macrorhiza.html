Valeriana macrorhiza

Valeriana macrorhiza, commonly known as American valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a rhizomatous root system and erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from June to September. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Valeriana macrorhiza is often found in moist meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.
