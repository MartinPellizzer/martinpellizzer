Viburnum utile var. elaegnifolium

Viburnum utile var. elaegnifolium, commonly known as the American cranberrybush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red, edible berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
