Vicia ambigua

Vicia ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges. Vicia ambigua is an annual or biennial plant that produces pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
