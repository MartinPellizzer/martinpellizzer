Viola tricolor subsp. machadiana

Viola tricolor subsp.

machadiana, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family. It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy purple, white, and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest understories. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
