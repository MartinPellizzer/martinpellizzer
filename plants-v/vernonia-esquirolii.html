Vernonia esquirolii

Vernonia esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its striking blue to purple flower clusters appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia esquirolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
