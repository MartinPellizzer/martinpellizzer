Viburnum oxycoccos var. subintegrifolium

Viburnum oxycoccos var. subintegrifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackhaw, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, waxy berries in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark and dense, rounded growth habit.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
