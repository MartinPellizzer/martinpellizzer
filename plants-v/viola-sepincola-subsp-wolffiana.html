Viola sepincola subsp. wolffiana

Viola sepincola subsp.

wolffiana, commonly known as the Wolff's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with five petals. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different floral characteristics compared to other viola species.
