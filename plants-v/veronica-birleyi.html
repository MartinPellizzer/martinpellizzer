Veronica birleyi

Veronica birleyi, commonly known as the New Zealand veronica, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded areas and is often found in forest understories. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces seed capsules that release tiny seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
