Vitis heyneana subsp. ficifolia

Vitis heyneana subsp.

ficifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian grapevine, is a climbing vine native to South America. It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant bears clusters of small, sweet-tart berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
