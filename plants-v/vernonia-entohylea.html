Vernonia entohylea

Vernonia entohylea, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia entohylea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
