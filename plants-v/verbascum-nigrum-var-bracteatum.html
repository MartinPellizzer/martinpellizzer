Verbascum nigrum var. bracteatum

Verbascum nigrum var. bracteatum, commonly known as the black mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and rocky areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically for respiratory ailments and skin conditions.
