Vernonia salzmannii

Vernonia salzmannii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia salzmannii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
