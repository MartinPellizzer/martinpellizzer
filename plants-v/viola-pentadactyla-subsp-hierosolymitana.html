Viola pentadactyla subsp. hierosolymitana

Viola pentadactyla subsp.

hierosolymitana, commonly known as the Jerusalem violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It is often found in open habitats such as slopes and cliffs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the typical V. pentadactyla.
