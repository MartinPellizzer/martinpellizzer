Veronica yezo-alpina

Veronica yezo-alpina, commonly known as the Japanese speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Veronica yezo-alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
