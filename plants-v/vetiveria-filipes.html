Vetiveria filipes

Vetiveria filipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf vetiver, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often used in soil stabilization due to its extensive root system.

Vetiveria filipes thrives in moist, tropical environments and is adaptable to various soil types.
