Veronica nova

Veronica nova, commonly known as the New Zealand veronica, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate or upright depending on the habitat.

Veronica nova is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
