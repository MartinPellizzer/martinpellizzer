Valeriana triplinervis f. latifolia

Valeriana triplinervis f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved valerian, is a variant of the valerian plant characterized by its wide, lance-shaped leaves.

This perennial herb belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is native to certain regions of Asia. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically have a mild, sometimes aromatic scent. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
