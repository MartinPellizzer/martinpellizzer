Vinicia tomentosa

Vinicia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that are covered in a dense, white, woolly pubescence. The plant produces clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that are followed by green, spiny fruits. The fruit has a rough, leathery exterior and contains a juicy, segmented interior with a strong, aromatic flavor.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
