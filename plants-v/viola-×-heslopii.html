Viola × heslopii

Viola × heslopii, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, often bi-colored flowers.

It typically features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers can range in color from purple to white, often with a distinctive yellow eye. This plant is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.

Viola × heslopii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
