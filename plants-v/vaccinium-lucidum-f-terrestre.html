Vaccinium lucidum f. terrestre

Vaccinium lucidum f. terrestre, commonly known as the lowbush blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces edible, bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with partial shade.

This variety is particularly valued for its hardiness and adaptability to cooler climates.
