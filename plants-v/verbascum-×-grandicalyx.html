Verbascum × grandicalyx

Verbascum × grandicalyx, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that resemble those of the common mullein. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is native to regions in Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Verbascum × grandicalyx prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
