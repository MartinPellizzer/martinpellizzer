Vernonia schomburgkiana

Vernonia schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to red, depending on the species.

Vernonia schomburgkiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
