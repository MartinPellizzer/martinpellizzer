Vulpia bromoides

Vulpia bromoides, commonly known as slender veldtgrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense mats. The leaves are narrow, flat, and have a rough, hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in panicles, which are often hidden beneath the foliage.

This plant thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand drought and grazing.
