Vicia venosa var. baicalensis

Vicia venosa var. baicalensis, commonly known as the Baical vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Russia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
