Verbena platensis

Verbena platensis, commonly known as the Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Verbena platensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun.
