Verbascum pseudocreticum subsp. pseudocreticum

Verbascum pseudocreticum subsp.

pseudocreticum, commonly known as the Cretan mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with dense, fuzzy hairs.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, arranged in a loose, terminal spike. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and also covered with a thick layer of soft, woolly hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
