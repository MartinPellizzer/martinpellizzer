Vitex sumatrana var. urceolata

Vitex sumatrana var. urceolata, commonly known as the broad-leafed chaste tree, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or white, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are drupes, containing a single seed, and it is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
