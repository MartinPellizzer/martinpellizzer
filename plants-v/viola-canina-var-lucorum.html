Viola canina var. lucorum

Viola canina var. lucorum, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces showy flowers in shades of yellow and white. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often being larger and more prominently marked.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
