Veronica delicatula

Veronica delicatula, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, decorative plant.
