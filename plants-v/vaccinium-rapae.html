Vaccinium rapae

Vaccinium rapae, commonly known as the purple cranberry shrub, is a species of evergreen shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically purple or red in color, often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Vaccinium rapae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
