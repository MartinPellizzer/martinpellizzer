Vicia leavenworthii var. typica

Vicia leavenworthii var. typica, commonly known as the Leavenworth vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and prairies.

This species is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for wildlife.
