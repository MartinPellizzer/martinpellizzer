Vitis subciliata

Vitis subciliata, commonly known as the silky grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters, followed by small, round berries that ripen to a dark purple color.

Vitis subciliata is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological benefits.
