Veronica debilis subsp. aksekiensis

Veronica debilis subsp.

aksekiensis, commonly known as the Akseki speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Turkey. It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of around 10-20 centimeters.

The plant features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in rocky or arid environments.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
