Vicia narbonensis var. ecirrhosa

Vicia narbonensis var. ecirrhosa, commonly known as the narrow-crested vetch, is a variant of the common vetch.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to parts of Europe and the Mediterranean region. The plant features slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow leaflets. Its small, yellow flowers are typically arranged in racemes, and it produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
