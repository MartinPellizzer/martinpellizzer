Viguiera brevipes

Viguiera brevipes, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and full sun. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Viguiera brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
