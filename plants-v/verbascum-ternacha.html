Verbascum ternacha

Verbascum ternacha, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and also covered in a fuzzy texture.

Verbascum ternacha is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
