Vilfa alba var. sylvatica

Vilfa alba var. sylvatica, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green coloration, often adorned with a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, delicate white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its pale appearance and woodland habitat.
