Valeriana iganciana

Valeriana iganciana, commonly known as the Brazilian valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often purple or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system is notable for its medicinal properties, which have been traditionally used in herbal remedies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong, sometimes disagreeable scent when crushed.
