Vangueria fuscosetulosa

Vangueria fuscosetulosa, commonly known as the black-hairy vangueria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, branched stems covered in fine, dark brown hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Vangueria fuscosetulosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
