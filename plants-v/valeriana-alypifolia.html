Valeriana alypifolia

Valeriana alypifolia, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is known for its strong, often pleasant fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Valeriana alypifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
