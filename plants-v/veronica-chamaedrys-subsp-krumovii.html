Veronica chamaedrys subsp. krumovii

Veronica chamaedrys subsp.

krumovii, commonly known as the Balkan speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July.

The plant has hairy, creeping stems and thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils. It is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats, growing at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
