Verbena cuneifolia

Verbena cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Verbena cuneifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
