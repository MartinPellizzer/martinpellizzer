Verbascum majale

Verbascum majale, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, gray-green, velvety leaves that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Verbascum majale is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects.
