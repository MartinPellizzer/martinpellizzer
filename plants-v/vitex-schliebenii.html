Vitex schliebenii

Vitex schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's chaste tree, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Vitex schliebenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
