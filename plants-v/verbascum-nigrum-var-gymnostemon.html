Verbascum nigrum var. gymnostemon

Verbascum nigrum var. gymnostemon, commonly known as black mullein, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and along roadsides.

Its leaves and flowers have been used historically for their medicinal properties.
