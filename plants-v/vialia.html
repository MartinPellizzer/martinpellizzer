Vialia

Vialia, commonly known as the glasswort, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, often salt-tolerant leaves and small, clustered flowers. Vialia species are typically found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and saline soils due to their ability to thrive in high-salinity environments. The plant's name is derived from the glass-like appearance of its stems and leaves.

Vialia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
