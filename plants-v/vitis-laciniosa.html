Vitis laciniosa

Vitis laciniosa, commonly known as the giant grapevine or wild grapevine, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth, with long, trailing stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn from green to red or black as they mature.

Vitis laciniosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
