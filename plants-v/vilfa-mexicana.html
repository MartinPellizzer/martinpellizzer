Vilfa mexicana

Vilfa mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican violet, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Vilfa mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
