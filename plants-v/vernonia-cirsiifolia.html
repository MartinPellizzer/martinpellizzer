Vernonia cirsiifolia

Vernonia cirsiifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces fluffy blue or purple seed heads that resemble mist. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Vernonia cirsiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
