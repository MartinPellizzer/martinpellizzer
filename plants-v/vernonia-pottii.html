Vernonia pottii

Vernonia pottii, commonly known as the desert ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring deep root systems and waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Vernonia pottii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
