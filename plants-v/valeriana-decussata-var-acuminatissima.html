Valeriana decussata var. acuminatissima

Valeriana decussata var. acuminatissima, commonly known as the sharp-pointed valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant, leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its sedative and calming properties.
