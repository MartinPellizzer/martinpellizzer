Viola sieboldii var. pseudoselkirkii

Viola sieboldii var. pseudoselkirkii, commonly known as the Selkirk violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
