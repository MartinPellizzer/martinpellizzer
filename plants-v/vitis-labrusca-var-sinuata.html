Vitis labrusca var. sinuata

Vitis labrusca var. sinuata, commonly known as the sand grape, is a variety of the fox grape native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often climbing vines and small, dark purple berries. The leaves are typically lobed with a distinctive sinuate (wavy) margin, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local winemaking.
