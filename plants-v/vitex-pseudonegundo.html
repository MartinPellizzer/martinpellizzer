Vitex pseudonegundo

Vitex pseudonegundo, commonly known as the false chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually grayish-green in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a cylindrical, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Vitex pseudonegundo is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
