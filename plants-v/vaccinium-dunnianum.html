Vaccinium dunnianum

Vaccinium dunnianum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Vaccinium dunnianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
