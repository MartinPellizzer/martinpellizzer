Vicia venosa var. albiflora

Vicia venosa var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are trifoliate with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, elongated clusters called racemes. Its pods are slender and contain several elliptical seeds.

Vicia venosa var. albiflora is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
