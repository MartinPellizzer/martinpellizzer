Verbascum graecum

Verbascum graecum, commonly known as Greek mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Verbascum graecum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory conditions.
