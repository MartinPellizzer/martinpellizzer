Velleia rosea

Velleia rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf velleia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with small, delicate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Velleia rosea thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native garden landscapes.
