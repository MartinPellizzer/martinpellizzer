Vitis inconstans

Vitis inconstans, commonly known as the variable grapevine, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Vitis inconstans is often found in moist, wooded areas and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
