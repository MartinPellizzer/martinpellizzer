Verbascum ovatum

Verbascum ovatum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, fuzzy gray-green hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a dense layer of silky hairs.

Verbascum ovatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
