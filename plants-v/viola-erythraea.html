Viola erythraea

Viola erythraea, commonly known as the red violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola erythraea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its beauty in gardens.
