Vachellia abyssinica subsp. abyssinica

Vachellia abyssinica subsp.

abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often thorned branches and compound leaves that are trifoliate or pinnate.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, narrow pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

This species is valued for its role in local ecosystems and traditional uses, including as a source of timber and fodder.
