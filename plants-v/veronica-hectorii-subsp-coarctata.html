Veronica hectorii subsp. coarctata

Veronica hectorii subsp.

coarctata, commonly known as the narrowleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters.

The plant features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert environments.
