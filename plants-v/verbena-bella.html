Verbena bella

Verbena bella, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the southern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Verbena bella thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
