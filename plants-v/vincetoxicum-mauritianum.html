Vincetoxicum mauritianum

Vincetoxicum mauritianum, commonly known as the false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic in non-native habitats.
