Vernonia sordida

Vernonia sordida, commonly known as the dirty ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to brown, depending on the stage of bloom. Vernonia sordida thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
