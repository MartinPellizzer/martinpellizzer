Vaccinium urophyllum

Vaccinium urophyllum, commonly known as the red evergreen huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal regions and mountainous areas. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Vaccinium urophyllum plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
