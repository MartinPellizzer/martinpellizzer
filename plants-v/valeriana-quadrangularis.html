Valeriana quadrangularis

Valeriana quadrangularis, commonly known as the square valerian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its square, angular stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a slightly rough texture.

Valeriana quadrangularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
