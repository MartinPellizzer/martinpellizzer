Vernonia filisquama

Vernonia filisquama, commonly known as the silky Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, conical flower heads that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
