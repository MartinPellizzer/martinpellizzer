Viola septentrionalis

Viola septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola septentrionalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
