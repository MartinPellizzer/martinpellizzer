Valeriana briquetiana

Valeriana briquetiana, commonly known as the Brazilian valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes contain essential oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine.

Valeriana briquetiana is valued for its potential calming effects and is sometimes used as a natural remedy for anxiety and sleep disorders.
