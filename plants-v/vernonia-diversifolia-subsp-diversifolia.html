Vernonia diversifolia subsp. diversifolia

Vernonia diversifolia subsp.

diversifolia, commonly known as the diverse Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow or orange flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

It is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
