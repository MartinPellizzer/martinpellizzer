Vernonia piovani

Vernonia piovani, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its most notable botanical feature is its bright yellow flower heads, which consist of numerous small tubular florets.

Vernonia piovani typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
