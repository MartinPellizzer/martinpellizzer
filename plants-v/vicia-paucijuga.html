Vicia paucijuga

Vicia paucijuga, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Vicia paucijuga is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
