Vitis brevipedunculata

Vitis brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, round berries that ripen to a dark blue or black color. Vitis brevipedunculata is typically found in moist woodlands and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying native flora and biodiversity.
