Viburnum cassinoides var. harbisonii

Viburnum cassinoides var. harbisonii, commonly known as Harbison's arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green above and lighter green beneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a desirable choice for garden landscapes.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
