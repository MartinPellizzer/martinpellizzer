Vitis retordii

Vitis retordii, commonly known as the Chinese grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its climbing habit and woody stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, round berries. Vitis retordii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
