Viburnum matudae

Viburnum matudae, commonly known as the Matudae viburnum, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in forest understories, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Viburnum matudae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
