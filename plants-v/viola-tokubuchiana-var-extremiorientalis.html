Viola tokubuchiana var. extremiorientalis

Viola tokubuchiana var. extremiorientalis, commonly known as the Korean violet, is a species native to East Asia, particularly Korea and northeastern China.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
