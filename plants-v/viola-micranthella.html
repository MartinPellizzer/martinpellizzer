Viola micranthella

Viola micranthella, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a small herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often clustered in a raceme. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its compact size and ornamental value in gardens.

Viola micranthella is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators.
