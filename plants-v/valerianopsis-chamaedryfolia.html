Valerianopsis chamaedryfolia

Valerianopsis chamaedryfolia, commonly known as the rock valerian, is a perennial herb native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It is often found growing in crevices and on slopes, thriving in well-drained soils.

Valerianopsis chamaedryfolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
