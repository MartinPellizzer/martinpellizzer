Veronica teucrium var. minima

Veronica teucrium var. minima, commonly known as the lesser speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from spring to early summer. The plant has slender, spreading stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
