Veronica nivea

Veronica nivea, commonly known as the snow veronica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a soft appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

Veronica nivea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
