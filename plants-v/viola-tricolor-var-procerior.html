Viola tricolor var. procerior

Viola tricolor var. procerior, commonly known as the purple heart viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, white, and yellow. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy and visually striking plant.
