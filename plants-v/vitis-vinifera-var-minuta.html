Vitis vinifera var. minuta

Vitis vinifera var. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf grapevine, is a compact variety of the common grapevine.

It is characterized by its smaller size and vigorous growth habit, making it suitable for smaller spaces. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are typically used for making wine or as ornamental features. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its adaptability and resilience to certain environmental conditions.
