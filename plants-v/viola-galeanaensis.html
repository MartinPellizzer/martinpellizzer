Viola galeanaensis

Viola galeanaensis, commonly known as the Mexican violet, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
