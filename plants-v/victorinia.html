Victorinia

Victorinia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical regions of Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Victorinia species are typically evergreen and thrive in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. They are popular houseplants due to their ornamental value and ease of care.

The genus includes several species, such as Victorinia sanguinea and Victorinia hirsuta, which are prized for their vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
