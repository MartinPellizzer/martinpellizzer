Vignea spicata

Vignea spicata, commonly known as the spiked vigna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Vignea spicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
