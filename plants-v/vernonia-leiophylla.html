Vernonia leiophylla

Vernonia leiophylla, commonly known as the smoothleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Vernonia leiophylla is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
