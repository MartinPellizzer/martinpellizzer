Viola subdelavayi

Viola subdelavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China.

It typically grows in forested areas and rocky habitats at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals. It is part of the Violaceae family, known for its ornamental and medicinal value.

Viola subdelavayi is valued for its resilience and aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
