Vernonia rampans

Vernonia rampans, commonly known as the spreading ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces fibrous, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its spread across open habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Vernonia rampans is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in full sun conditions.
