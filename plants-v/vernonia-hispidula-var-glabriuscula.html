Vernonia hispidula var. glabriuscula

Vernonia hispidula var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the silky vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The yellow to orange flower heads appear in late summer and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
