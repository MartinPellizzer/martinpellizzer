Verbena sagittalis

Verbena sagittalis, commonly known as arrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a pointed tip, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
