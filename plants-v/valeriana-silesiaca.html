Valeriana silesiaca

Valeriana silesiaca, commonly known as Silesian valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often purple or pink flowers. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Valeriana silesiaca is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
