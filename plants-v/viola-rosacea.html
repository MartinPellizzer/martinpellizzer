Viola rosacea

Viola rosacea, commonly known as the heartleaf viola, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of down, giving it a soft appearance. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern.

Viola rosacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
