Veronica macropoda

Veronica macropoda, commonly known as the large-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Veronica macropoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
