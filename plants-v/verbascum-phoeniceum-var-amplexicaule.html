Verbascum phoeniceum var. amplexicaule

Verbascum phoeniceum var. amplexicaule, commonly known as the purple mullein, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, clustered purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often clasping the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
