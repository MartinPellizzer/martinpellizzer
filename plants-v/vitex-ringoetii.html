Vitex ringoetii

Vitex ringoetii, commonly known as the redbrush or redbrush vitex, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Vitex ringoetii is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and pine flatwoods.
