Valerianella hirsutissima

Valerianella hirsutissima, commonly known as hairy valerian, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Valerianella hirsutissima is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats.
