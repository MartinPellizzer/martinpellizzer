Viburnum reticulatum

Viburnum reticulatum, commonly known as arrowwood viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern, and its flowers are typically white or pale green, arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant produces red drupes that ripen in the fall, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
