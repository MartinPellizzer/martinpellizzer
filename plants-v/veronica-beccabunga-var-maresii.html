Veronica beccabunga var. maresii

Veronica beccabunga var. maresii, commonly known as the marsh speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and damp meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
