Viola verecunda var. yakusimana

Viola verecunda var. yakusimana, commonly known as the Yakusimana violet, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and pale violet to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle beauty to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare species with conservation concerns.
