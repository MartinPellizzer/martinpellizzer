Valeriana locusta subsp. olitoria

Valeriana locusta subsp.

olitoria, commonly known as garden valerian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically pink or white, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and contains essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal remedies.
