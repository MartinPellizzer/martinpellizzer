Vicia nataliae

Vicia nataliae, commonly known as Natal vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its seed pods are cylindrical and contain several elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vicia nataliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
