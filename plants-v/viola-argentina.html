Viola argentina

Viola argentina, commonly known as the Argentine violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and forest floors. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Viola argentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
