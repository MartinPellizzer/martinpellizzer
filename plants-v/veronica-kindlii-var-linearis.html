Veronica kindlii var. linearis

Veronica kindlii var. linearis, commonly known as the linear leaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. This perennial species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. The plant's compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable addition to native plant gardens.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
