Vernonia aronifolia

Vernonia aronifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Vernonia aronifolia is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
