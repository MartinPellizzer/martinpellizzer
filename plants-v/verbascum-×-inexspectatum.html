Verbascum × inexspectatum

Verbascum × inexspectatum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein.

This plant is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum phlomoides. It is a tall, biennial or short-lived perennial herb with large, gray-green, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a cylindrical spike.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.
