Viola affinis

Viola affinis, commonly known as the common violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, typically purple or violet flowers. Native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is often found in woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Viola affinis is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
