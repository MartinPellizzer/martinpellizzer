Veronicastrum sibiricum f. albiflorum

Veronicastrum sibiricum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower blueweed, is a cultivar of the Veronicastrum sibiricum species, which is native to eastern Asia.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white flowers. The foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, providing a delicate texture to the plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The whiteflower variant stands out for its pale, almost porcelain-like blooms that contrast with its green leaves.
