Vernonia stachyanthus

Vernonia stachyanthus, commonly known as the purpletop vervain, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
