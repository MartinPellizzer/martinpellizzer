Viola × celinae

Viola × celinae, commonly known as the Celina violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola wittrockiana.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments with partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola × celinae is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
