Vigna nakashimae

Vigna nakashimae, commonly known as the Nakashima cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Vigna nakashimae is adapted to temperate climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible seeds.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both horticultural and agricultural studies.
