Verbena seriphioides var. glabra

Verbena seriphioides var. glabra, commonly known as smooth verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has smooth, hairless leaves that are opposite and oval in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Smooth verbena is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
