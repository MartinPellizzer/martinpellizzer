Veronica tetragona subsp. tetragona

Veronica tetragona subsp.

tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled veronica, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, often hairy stems.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinct four-angled stem structure.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.
