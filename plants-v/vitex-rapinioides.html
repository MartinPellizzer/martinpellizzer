Vitex rapinioides

Vitex rapinioides, commonly known as the false chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to purple, blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a dry, two-valved capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

Vitex rapinioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
