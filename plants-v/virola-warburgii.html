Virola warburgii

Virola warburgii, commonly known as the "Amazonian ironwood," is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning, followed by the development of woody, spiky fruits. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its bark is thick and often fissured.

Virola warburgii plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
