Vernonia eminens

Vernonia eminens, commonly known as the tall ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows to heights of up to 6 feet, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and dark purple to brown flowers. Its sturdy stalks and showy blooms make it a notable wildflower in moist to wet habitats.

Vernonia eminens is often found in swamps, marshes, and along creek banks, where it thrives in the humid, nutrient-rich environment.
