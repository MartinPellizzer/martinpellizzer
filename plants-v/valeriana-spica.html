Valeriana spica

Valeriana spica, commonly known as spike valerian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant is native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often growing in woodland or meadow environments. Its roots contain essential oils and compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Valeriana spica is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
