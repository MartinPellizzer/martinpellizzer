Vochysia acuminata subsp. quadrangulata

Vochysia acuminata subsp.

quadrangulata, commonly known as the quadrangular vochysia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular (four-sided) stems and compound leaves with elongated leaflets.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
