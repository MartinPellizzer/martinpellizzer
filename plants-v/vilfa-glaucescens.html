Vilfa glaucescens

Vilfa glaucescens, commonly known as the blue vilfa, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a bluish sheen. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Vilfa glaucescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
