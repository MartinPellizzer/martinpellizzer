Vernonia pineticola

Vernonia pineticola, commonly known as the mountain ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, hairy stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a rough texture.

The small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
