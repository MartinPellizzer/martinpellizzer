Vicia varia subsp. ambigua

Vicia varia subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the variable vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep violet.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia. It has a climbing habit and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its variability in morphology and flower color, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
