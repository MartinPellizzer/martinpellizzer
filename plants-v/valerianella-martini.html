Valerianella martini

Valerianella martini, commonly known as the creeping valerian, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, fragrant flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Valerianella martini is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for ground cover.
