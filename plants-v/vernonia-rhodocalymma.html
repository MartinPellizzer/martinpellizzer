Vernonia rhodocalymma

Vernonia rhodocalymma, commonly known as the red-stemmed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn.

Vernonia rhodocalymma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
