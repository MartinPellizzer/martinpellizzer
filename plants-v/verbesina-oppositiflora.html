Verbesina oppositiflora

Verbesina oppositiflora, commonly known as the showy beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. Verbesina oppositiflora is typically found in moist, open habitats like woodland edges and prairies.
