Vitis labrusca var. dissecta

Vitis labrusca var. dissecta, commonly known as the Concord grape vine, is a cultivar of the American grape species Vitis labrusca.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically used for making jams, jellies, and wines. The plant is a vigorous vine with compound leaves that have a distinctive lobed or dissected appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and requires a chilling period to break dormancy and initiate flowering.

This variety is also noted for its high sugar content and aromatic qualities.
