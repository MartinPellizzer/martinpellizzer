Vernonia coronata

Vernonia coronata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and fluffy, white to blue-pink flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. Its upright growth habit and showy blooms make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Vernonia coronata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
