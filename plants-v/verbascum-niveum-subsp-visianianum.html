Verbascum niveum subsp. visianianum

Verbascum niveum subsp.

visianianum, commonly known as the white mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, white pubescence.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically arranged in a terminal spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
