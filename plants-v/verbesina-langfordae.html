Verbesina langfordae

Verbesina langfordae, commonly known as Langford's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces showy yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
