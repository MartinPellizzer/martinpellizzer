Veronica squamosa var. barrelieri

Veronica squamosa var. barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its upright, branching stems. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring and early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine, scale-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
