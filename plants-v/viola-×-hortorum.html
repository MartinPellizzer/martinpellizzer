Viola × hortorum

Viola × hortorum, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. It is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value.

Viola × hortorum is often used in gardens and as a cut flower due to its delicate appearance and fragrance.
