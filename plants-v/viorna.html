Viorna

Viorna, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds. Viorna is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and unique growth habit.

However, it requires support structures to grow properly and thrives in warm, humid environments.
