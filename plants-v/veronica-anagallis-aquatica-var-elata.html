Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. elata

Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. elata, commonly known as the tall water speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, often wet environments such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is known for its ability to spread via runners, forming dense mats in suitable habitats.
