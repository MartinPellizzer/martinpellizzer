Vernonia ruboides

Vernonia ruboides, commonly known as the red ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright red to orange-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Vernonia ruboides is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a food source for various pollinators.
