Vicia orbelica

Vicia orbelica, commonly known as the Orbelica vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Vicia orbelica is also valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation, contributing to ecological balance.
