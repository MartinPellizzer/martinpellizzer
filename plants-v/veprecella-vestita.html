Veprecella vestita

Veprecella vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in small, three-lobed pods that split open when mature.

Veprecella vestita is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
