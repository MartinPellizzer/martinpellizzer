Vicia salisii

Vicia salisii, commonly known as Salisii vetch, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. Vicia salisii has a fibrous root system and produces legumes that contribute to soil nitrogen fixation.

It is generally a short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
