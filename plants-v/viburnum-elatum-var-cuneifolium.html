Viburnum elatum var. cuneifolium

Viburnum elatum var. cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching branches and small, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy underneath. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.

This variety is valued for its compact growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
