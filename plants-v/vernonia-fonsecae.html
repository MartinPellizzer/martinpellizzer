Vernonia fonsecae

Vernonia fonsecae, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to brownish in color.

Vernonia fonsecae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
