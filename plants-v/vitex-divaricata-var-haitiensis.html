Vitex divaricata var. haitiensis

Vitex divaricata var. haitiensis, commonly known as the Haitian chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically purple or white, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a dry, two-valved capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
