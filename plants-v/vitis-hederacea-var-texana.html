Vitis hederacea var. texana

Vitis hederacea var. texana, commonly known as Texas grapevine, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the southern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge and produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and full sun conditions. Its berries are typically small and turn red or black when mature, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability.
