Valeriana arizonica

Valeriana arizonica, commonly known as Arizona valerian, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a strong, aromatic scent.

Valeriana arizonica is part of the Caprifoliaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
