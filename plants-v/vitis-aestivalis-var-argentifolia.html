Vitis aestivalis var. argentifolia

Vitis aestivalis var. argentifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf muscadine, is a variant of the muscadine grape native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It is a vigorous vine that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Botanically, it is noted for its robust growth habit and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
