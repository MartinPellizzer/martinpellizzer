Venidium speciosum

Venidium speciosum, commonly known as the showy venidium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Venidium speciosum is typically found in moist, open habitats like woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
