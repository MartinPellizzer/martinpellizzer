Vitis serjaniifolia var. aconitifolia

Vitis serjaniifolia var. aconitifolia, commonly known as the aconite-leaved grapevine, is a climbing vine native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble the foliage of aconite plants, giving it its distinctive common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in loose panicles. Its berries are small, round, and change from green to dark purple as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and moderate growth habit.
