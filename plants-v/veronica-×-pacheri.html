Veronica × pacheri

Veronica × pacheri, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Veronica × pacheri is often cultivated for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soil conditions.
