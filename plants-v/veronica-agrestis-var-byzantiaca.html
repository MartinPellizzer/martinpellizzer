Veronica agrestis var. byzantiaca

Veronica agrestis var. byzantiaca, commonly known as the Byzantine speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a drought-tolerant, decorative plant.
