Viola buxifolia

Viola buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of violet or purple during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for both its beauty and ecological benefits.
