Viola lovelliana

Viola lovelliana, commonly known as the Lovell's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the forest floor.

Viola lovelliana is also noted for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
