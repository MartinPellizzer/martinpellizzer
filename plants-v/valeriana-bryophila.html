Valeriana bryophila

Valeriana bryophila, commonly known as the moss valerian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white or pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Valeriana bryophila is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
