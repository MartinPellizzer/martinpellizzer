Verbesina purpusii

Verbesina purpusii, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flower heads, which consist of numerous small tubular florets surrounded by pale green bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody and branched.

Verbesina purpusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
