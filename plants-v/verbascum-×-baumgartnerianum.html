Verbascum × baumgartnerianum

Verbascum × baumgartnerianum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their large, showy flowers and fuzzy, woolly leaves. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
