Viola kusanoana f. pubescens

Viola kusanoana f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and vibrant purple petals.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is native to certain regions of East Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
