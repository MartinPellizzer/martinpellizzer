Valeriana descolei

Valeriana descolei, commonly known as the Madagascar valerian, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant foliage. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a distinctive, spicy scent.

Valeriana descolei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
