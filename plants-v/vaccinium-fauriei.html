Vaccinium fauriei

Vaccinium fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded environments, often found in coastal forests. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a deep green color.

Vaccinium fauriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
