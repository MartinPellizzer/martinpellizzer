Viola bangiana

Viola bangiana, commonly known as the Bangla violet, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant with heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-20 centimeters and prefers well-drained, moist soil. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture for their ornamental value.

Viola bangiana is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
