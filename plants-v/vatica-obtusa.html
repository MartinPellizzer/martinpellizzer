Vatica obtusa

Vatica obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and sturdy, hardy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Vatica obtusa is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
