Viola mandshurica var. glabripetala

Viola mandshurica var. glabripetala, commonly known as the Siberian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of pubescence. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the back.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
