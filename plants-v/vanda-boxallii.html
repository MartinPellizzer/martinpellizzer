Vanda boxallii

Vanda boxallii, commonly known as the Boxall's vanda, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it often grows on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Vanda boxallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
