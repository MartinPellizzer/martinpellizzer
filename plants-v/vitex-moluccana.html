Vitex moluccana

Vitex moluccana, commonly known as the Java glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple, pink, or white, which bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a cylindrical, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Vitex moluccana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
