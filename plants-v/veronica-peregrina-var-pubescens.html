Veronica peregrina var. pubescens

Veronica peregrina var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its "hairy" designation. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
