Viola arborescens f. integrifolia

Viola arborescens f. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf woodsorrel, is a hybrid form of the wood sorrel genus.

It is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit and heart-shaped, smooth-edged leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, often appearing in spring. It thrives in woodland habitats and is adapted to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
