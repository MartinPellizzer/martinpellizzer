Vernonia ugandensis

Vernonia ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia ugandensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
