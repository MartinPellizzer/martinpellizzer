Vanilla claviculata

Vanilla claviculata, commonly known as the Mexican vanilla orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a climbing vine that produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers followed by long, thin pods. The plant is native to Central America and parts of the southern United States. Its pods, which resemble vanilla beans, contain a rich, aromatic flavor used in culinary applications.

Due to its unique scent and limited cultivation, it is less commonly used than the more well-known vanilla planifolia.
