Vicia sativa var. angustifolia

Vicia sativa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, compound leaves with a narrow, elongated shape. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
