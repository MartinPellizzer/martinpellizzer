Viviania rosea var. alba

Viviania rosea var. alba, commonly known as the white flame-of-the-forest, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal gardens. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

The varietal name 'alba' refers to its distinctive white coloration, which sets it apart from other varieties of Viviania rosea.
