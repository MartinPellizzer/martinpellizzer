Vitis vinifera var. denticulata

Vitis vinifera var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed grapevine, is a lesser-known cultivar of the common grapevine.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaves and small, often unripe berries. This variety is native to parts of Europe and is typically grown in cooler climates. The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in marginal soil conditions.

While not widely cultivated for wine, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in viticulture.
