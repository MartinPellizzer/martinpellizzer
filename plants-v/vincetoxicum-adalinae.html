Vincetoxicum adalinae

Vincetoxicum adalinae, commonly known as the Adalina's beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the wetlands of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of dark purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, dark green upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

Vincetoxicum adalinae is valued for its ornamental berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
