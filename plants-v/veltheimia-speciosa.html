Veltheimia speciosa

Veltheimia speciosa, commonly known as the Natal yellow fern, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feather-like foliage that forms a compact, fountain-like shape. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract. Veltheimia speciosa thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires partial to full shade.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
