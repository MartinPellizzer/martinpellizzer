Valeriana domingensis

Valeriana domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana domingensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
