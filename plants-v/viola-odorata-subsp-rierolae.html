Viola odorata subsp. rierolae

Viola odorata subsp.

rierolae, commonly known as the Rierolae violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a deep green hue and produces clusters of delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of violet and white.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
