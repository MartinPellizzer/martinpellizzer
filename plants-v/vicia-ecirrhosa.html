Vicia ecirrhosa

Vicia ecirrhosa, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock. Vicia ecirrhosa thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable in soil enrichment and ecological restoration efforts.
