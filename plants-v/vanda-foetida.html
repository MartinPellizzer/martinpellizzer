Vanda foetida

Vanda foetida, commonly known as the stinking vanda, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, dark green leaves and long, wiry stems that often bear clusters of small, pale green to yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is believed to attract specific pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Vanda foetida is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological significance in its native habitat.
