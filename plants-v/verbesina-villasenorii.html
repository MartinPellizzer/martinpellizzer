Verbesina villasenorii

Verbesina villasenorii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, hairy leaves that are typically alternate along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in dry, open habitats.

Verbesina villasenorii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
