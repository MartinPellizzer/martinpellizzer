Veronica hederifolia subsp. hederifolia

Veronica hederifolia subsp.

hederifolia, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
