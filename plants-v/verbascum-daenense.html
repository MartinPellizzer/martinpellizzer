Verbascum daenense

Verbascum daenense, commonly known as the Persian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are broad and woolly, which help reduce water loss in dry climates.

Verbascum daenense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
