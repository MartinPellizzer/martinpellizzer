Vanda himalaica

Vanda himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan vanda, is an epiphytic orchid native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing on trees or rocks in humid, cool climates. The plant features long, slender stems with fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its arid habitats. Its flowers are showy, with vibrant colors and a complex structure typical of the Vanda genus.

Vanda himalaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking blooms.
