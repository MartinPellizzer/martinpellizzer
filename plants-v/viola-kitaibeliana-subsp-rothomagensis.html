Viola kitaibeliana subsp. rothomagensis

Viola kitaibeliana subsp.

rothomagensis, commonly known as the Rothomagens violet, is a species native to the Alps and surrounding regions. It is a herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas, preferring well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pale violet to almost white, with a distinctive dark purple throat.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
