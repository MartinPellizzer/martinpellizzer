Veronica sternbergiana

Veronica sternbergiana, commonly known as the Sierra veronica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.
