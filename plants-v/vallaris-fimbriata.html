Vallaris fimbriata

Vallaris fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed vallaris, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Vallaris fimbriata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
