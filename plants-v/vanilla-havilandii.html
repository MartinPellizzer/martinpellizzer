Vanilla havilandii

Vanilla havilandii, commonly known as the Mexican vanilla or vanilla orchid, is a tropical vine native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dark brown pods that contain the aromatic vanilla essence used in culinary and fragrance industries. Its growth requires high humidity, indirect light, and well-draining soil to thrive.

Vanilla havilandii is prized for its unique scent and is often cultivated for both ornamental and commercial purposes.
