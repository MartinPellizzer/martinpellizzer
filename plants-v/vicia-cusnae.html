Vicia cusnae

Vicia cusnae, commonly known as the narrowleaf vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in early summer. Vicia cusnae is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

It has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
