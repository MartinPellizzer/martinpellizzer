Virgularia lanceolata

Virgularia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf horseweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in roadsides, fields, and waste areas. It has a taproot system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Virgularia lanceolata is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and common weed in many regions.
