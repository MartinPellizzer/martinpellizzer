Vigna bequaertii

Vigna bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods. The plant produces edible seeds that are rich in protein and are used in various culinary traditions. Vigna bequaertii is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its adaptability to diverse environmental conditions makes it an important crop in agricultural systems.
