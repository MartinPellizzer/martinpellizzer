Viola rossii

Viola rossii, commonly known as the Ross's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Viola rossii is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It also plays a role in supporting local pollinators, particularly bees and butterflies.
