Vernonia galamensis var. gibbosa

Vernonia galamensis var. gibbosa, commonly known as the waxy Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow-centered composite flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with a fine layer of white wax, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
