Viburnum cylindricum

Viburnum cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum cylindricum typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage, flowers, and berries, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
