Viola tricolor var. arvensis

Viola tricolor var. arvensis, commonly known as the common heartsease, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy violet or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
