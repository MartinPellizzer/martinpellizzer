Valeriana alternifolia subsp. stubendorfiana

Valeriana alternifolia subsp.

stubendorfiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its aromatic foliage and historical use in herbal medicine contribute to its ecological and cultural significance.
