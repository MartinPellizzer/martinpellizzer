Vignaldia schimperiana

Vignaldia schimperiana, commonly known as the Schimper's vigna, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
