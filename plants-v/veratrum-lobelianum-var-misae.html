Veratrum lobelianum var. misae

Veratrum lobelianum var. misae, commonly known as the Japanese white hellebore, is a variant of the tall white hellebore native to Japan.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
