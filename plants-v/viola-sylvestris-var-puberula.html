Viola sylvestris var. puberula

Viola sylvestris var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy wood violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and shaded soils, thriving in moist, well-drained conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Viola sylvestris var. puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
