Viola alba subsp. cretica

Viola alba subsp.

cretica, commonly known as the Cretan white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate white or pale purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
