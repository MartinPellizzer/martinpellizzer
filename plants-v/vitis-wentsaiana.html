Vitis wentsaiana

Vitis wentsaiana, commonly known as the Wentsaian grapevine, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Myanmar and Thailand.

It is a climbing vine that belongs to the Vitaceae family, characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. Vitis wentsaiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in horticulture and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological research and botanical classification.
