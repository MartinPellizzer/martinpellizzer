Veronica chantavica

Veronica chantavica, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Veronica chantavica is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
