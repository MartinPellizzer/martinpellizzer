Veronica subincanovelutina

Veronica subincanovelutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The leaves are generally hairy and slightly velvety, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland margins or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
