Veitchia sessilifolia

Veitchia sessilifolia, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, trunk-like stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, providing a distinctive visual appearance. It flowers infrequently, producing large, cone-like structures that are pollinated by insects.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
