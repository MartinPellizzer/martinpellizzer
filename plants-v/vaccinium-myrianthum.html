Vaccinium myrianthum

Vaccinium myrianthum, commonly known as the blueberry shrub, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other acidic-loving plants such as heather and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, often with a slight wax coating.

Vaccinium myrianthum thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
