Viridantha uniflora

Viridantha uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower green vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
