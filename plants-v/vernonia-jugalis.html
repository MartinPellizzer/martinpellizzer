Vernonia jugalis

Vernonia jugalis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its erect, branching structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Vernonia jugalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
