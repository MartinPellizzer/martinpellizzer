Viola borchersii

Viola borchersii, commonly known as the Borchers' violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet to purple flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Viola borchersii is a perennial herb that is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native environment.
