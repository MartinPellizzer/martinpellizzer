Valeriana fauriei f. coreana

Valeriana fauriei f. coreana, commonly known as Korean valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often purple or white flowers. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in herbal remedies for their calming and sedative effects.
