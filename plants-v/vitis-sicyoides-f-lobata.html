Vitis sicyoides f. lobata

Vitis sicyoides f. lobata, commonly known as the lobed grape or lobate fox grape, is a rare and unique variant of the vitis genus.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that distinguish it from other grape species. The plant is native to specific regions in Africa, particularly South Africa, where it grows in rocky and arid environments. Its small, dark berries are typically consumed by local wildlife rather than humans.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a botanically significant and ecologically important species.
