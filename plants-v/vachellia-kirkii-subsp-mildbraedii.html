Vachellia kirkii subsp. mildbraedii

Vachellia kirkii subsp.

mildbraedii, commonly known as the Kirk's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often thorny branches and compound leaves that are typically bipinnate.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences, which are followed by long, slender pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

The species is well adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
