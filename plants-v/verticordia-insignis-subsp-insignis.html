Verticordia insignis subsp. insignis

Verticordia insignis subsp.

insignis, commonly known as the pink wattle-leaf, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
