Valeriana moravica

Valeriana moravica, commonly known as Moravian valerian, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish-brown roots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in summer, producing small, clustered white or pinkish flowers.

Valeriana moravica is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
