Vernonia vinhae

Vernonia vinhae, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia vinhae is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
