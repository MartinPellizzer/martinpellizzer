Veronica austriaca subsp. pseudochamaedrys

Veronica austriaca subsp.

pseudochamaedrys, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August.

The plant has wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
