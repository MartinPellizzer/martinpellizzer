Valeriana phitosiana

Valeriana phitosiana, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing white to pinkish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana phitosiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential calming properties.
