Vitex rufescens var. abludens

Vitex rufescens var. abludens, commonly known as the red chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, panicle-like inflorescences with purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
