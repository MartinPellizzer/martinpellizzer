Verbascum sevanense

Verbascum sevanense, commonly known as the Sevan mullein, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Armenia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
