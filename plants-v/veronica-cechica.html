Veronica cechica

Veronica cechica, commonly known as the Cech's speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Veronica cechica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
