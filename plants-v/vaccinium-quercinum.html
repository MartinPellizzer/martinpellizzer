Vaccinium quercinum

Vaccinium quercinum, commonly known as the oakleaved blueberry, is a shrub native to the northern United States and Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like heathers and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are leathery and oval-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

Vaccinium quercinum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
