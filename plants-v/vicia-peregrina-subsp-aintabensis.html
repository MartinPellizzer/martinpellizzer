Vicia peregrina subsp. aintabensis

Vicia peregrina subsp.

aintabensis, commonly known as the Aintab vetch, is a perennial legume native to Turkey. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple.

The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods containing several seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance in supporting pollinators and contributing to soil nitrogen fixation.
