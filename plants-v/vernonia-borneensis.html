Vernonia borneensis

Vernonia borneensis, commonly known as the Borneo ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the wet season.

Vernonia borneensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential medicinal uses in traditional practices.
