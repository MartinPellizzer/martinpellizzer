Vanhouttea fruticulosa

Vanhouttea fruticulosa, commonly known as the African velvetbean, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, round pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Its morphology includes compound leaves and a woody stem, typical of many shrubs in its native habitat.
