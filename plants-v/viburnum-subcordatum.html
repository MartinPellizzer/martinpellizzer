Viburnum subcordatum

Viburnum subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Viburnum subcordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
