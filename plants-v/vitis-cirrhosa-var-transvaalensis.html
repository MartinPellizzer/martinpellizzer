Vitis cirrhosa var. transvaalensis

Vitis cirrhosa var. transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal grape, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are typically edible. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in woodland or riverine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
