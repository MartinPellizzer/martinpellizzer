Vicia laxiflora

Vicia laxiflora, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Vicia laxiflora is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
