Viola ruppii var. lusitana

Viola ruppii var. lusitana, commonly known as the Portuguese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland areas, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

The species is also noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
