Viscum tuberculatum f. ovatifolium

Viscum tuberculatum f. ovatifolium, commonly known as the ovate-leaved European mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in Europe.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along its stems. The plant produces small, white, waxy berries that contain sticky seeds, which adhere to the bark of host trees for germination. It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds, which disperse its seeds.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations for parasitic growth and seed dispersal.
