Verbascum × trolanderi

Verbascum × trolanderi, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their large, showy flowers. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
