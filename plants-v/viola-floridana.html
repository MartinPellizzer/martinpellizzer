Viola floridana

Viola floridana, commonly known as the Florida violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a yellowish throat. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Viola floridana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
