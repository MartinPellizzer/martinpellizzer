Vachellia leucophloea var. leucophloea

Vachellia leucophloea var. leucophloea, commonly known as the white-wood acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by elongated, woody pods. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
