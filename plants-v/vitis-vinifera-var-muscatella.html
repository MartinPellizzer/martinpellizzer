Vitis vinifera var. muscatella

Vitis vinifera var. muscatella, commonly known as the muscat grape, is a variety of the common grapevine characterized by its aromatic and sweet fruit.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is known for producing small, clustered berries with a distinctive musky fragrance. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a glossy green surface. This variety is widely cultivated for both wine production and table grapes, valued for its unique flavor profile.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
