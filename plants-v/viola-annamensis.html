Viola annamensis

Viola annamensis, commonly known as the Annam violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals. It is often found in forest undergrowth and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
