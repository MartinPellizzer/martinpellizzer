Vernonia cyanopidea

Vernonia cyanopidea, commonly known as blue false heather, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in sunny conditions. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
