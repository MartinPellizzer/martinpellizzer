Vangueria schumanniana

Vangueria schumanniana, commonly known as the Schumann's vangueria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
