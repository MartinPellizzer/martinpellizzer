Verbesina atriplicifolia

Verbesina atriplicifolia, commonly known as roughhollow or roughleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has alternate, rough, and hairy leaves that are often triangular in shape. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and open areas, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
