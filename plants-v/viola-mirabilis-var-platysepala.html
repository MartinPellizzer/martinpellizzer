Viola mirabilis var. platysepala

Viola mirabilis var. platysepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its sepals are broader and more prominent than those of typical violas, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
