Verbena chubutensis

Verbena chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Verbena chubutensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
