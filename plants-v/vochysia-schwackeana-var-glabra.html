Vochysia schwackeana var. glabra

Vochysia schwackeana var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth vochysia, is a deciduous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.
