Vallisneria anhuiensis

Vallisneria anhuiensis, commonly known as the Anhui Vallisneria, is a species of aquatic plant native to China.

It is a submerged herb that typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and rivers. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that can reach several meters in length, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Vallisneria anhuiensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.

It is also used in ornamental aquaculture due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
