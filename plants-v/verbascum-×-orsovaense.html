Verbascum × orsovaense

Verbascum × orsovaense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their large, fuzzy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The hybrid form results from crosses between Verbascum phlomoides and Verbascum blattaria, leading to unique morphological characteristics. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
