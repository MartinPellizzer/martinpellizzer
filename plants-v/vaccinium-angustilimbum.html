Vaccinium angustilimbum

Vaccinium angustilimbum, commonly known as narrowleaf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like heather and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, followed by edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or pine forests.

Its foliage is dense and dark green, providing valuable habitat for various wildlife species.
