Veronica undulata

Veronica undulata, commonly known as the wavy Veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly wavy margin, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
