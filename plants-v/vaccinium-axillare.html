Vaccinium axillare

Vaccinium axillare, commonly known as the axillary blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors.

The leaves are elliptical, dark green, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
