Vaccinium macrophyllum

Vaccinium macrophyllum, commonly known as the Oregon grape, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue-black berries. The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other acid-loving plants like blueberries and heathers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal regions and mountainous areas.

The berries are used in jams and wines, and the plant also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
