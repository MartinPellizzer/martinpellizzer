Viola × doii f. kisokomana

Viola × doii f. kisokomana, commonly known as the Kisoskomana violet, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Japan, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

The species is noted for its genetic diversity, which contributes to its adaptability and resilience in different climates.
