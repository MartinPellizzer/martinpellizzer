Viburnum mullaha var. tashiroi

Viburnum mullaha var. tashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
