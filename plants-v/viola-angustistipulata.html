Viola angustistipulata

Viola angustistipulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola angustistipulata is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
