Viola taltalensis var. glaberrima

Viola taltalensis var. glaberrima, commonly known as the Taltal violets, is a rare and endemic species native to the coastal regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils along the coastline, where it is adapted to the harsh, arid climate. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
