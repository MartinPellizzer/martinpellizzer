Valeriana glacialis

Valeriana glacialis, commonly known as the alpine valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Valeriana glacialis is known for its aromatic foliage and is often found in mountainous and tundra environments.
