Verbena officinalis var. gracilescens

Verbena officinalis var. gracilescens, commonly known as slender verbena, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
