Vignea fluviatilis

Vignea fluviatilis, commonly known as the river bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pod-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its twining stems and is often found growing along riverbanks and moist soils. It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems and agricultural practices.
