Viburnum dentatum var. semitomentosum

Viburnum dentatum var. semitomentosum, commonly known as the grayleaf arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, hairy undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by dark red berries in the fall. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the autumn, adding ornamental value.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and wildlife value.
