Viola frutescens

Viola frutescens, commonly known as the common violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola frutescens is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
