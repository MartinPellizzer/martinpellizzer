Vernonia theophrastifolia var. richardiana

Vernonia theophrastifolia var. richardiana, commonly known as the Richard's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, dark blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a grayish-green appearance, while its stems are sturdy and often reddish in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
