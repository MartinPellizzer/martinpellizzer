Vernonia tragiifolia

Vernonia tragiifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact, upright growth habit. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and wetland restoration projects.
