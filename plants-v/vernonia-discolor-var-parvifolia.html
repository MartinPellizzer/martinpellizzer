Vernonia discolor var. parvifolia

Vernonia discolor var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
