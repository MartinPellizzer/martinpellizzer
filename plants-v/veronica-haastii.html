Veronica haastii

Veronica haastii, commonly known as the Haast's veronica, is a striking plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
