Veronica macrostemonoides

Veronica macrostemonoides, commonly known as the broad-stemmed speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue or purple, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and bloom in spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
