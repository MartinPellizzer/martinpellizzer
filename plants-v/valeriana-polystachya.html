Valeriana polystachya

Valeriana polystachya, commonly known as the clustered valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Valeriana polystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
