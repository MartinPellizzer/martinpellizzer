Vernonia ammophila var. vestita

Vernonia ammophila var. vestita, commonly known as the white vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in dune environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine, white pubescence, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to withstand coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
