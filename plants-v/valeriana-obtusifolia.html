Valeriana obtusifolia

Valeriana obtusifolia, commonly known as blunt-leaved valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom from June to August. Its leaves are oval to lance-shaped with rounded tips, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Valeriana obtusifolia is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects, and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
