Valerianella coronata f. pumila

Valerianella coronata f. pumila, commonly known as "pumila valerian," is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces tiny, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or garden beds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
