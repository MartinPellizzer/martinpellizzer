Verbascum coromandelicum

Verbascum coromandelicum, commonly known as the Coromandel mullein, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
