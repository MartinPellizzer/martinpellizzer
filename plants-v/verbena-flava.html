Verbena flava

Verbena flava, commonly known as yellow verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and slender, branched stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Verbena flava is a member of the Verbenaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Its small, tubular flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
