Viola reichei f. gunckelii

Viola reichei f. gunckelii, commonly known as the Gunckel's violet, is a member of the Violaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

This perennial herb features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy violet or purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous areas of Argentina and Chile.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
