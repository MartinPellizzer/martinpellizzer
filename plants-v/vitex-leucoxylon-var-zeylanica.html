Vitex leucoxylon var. zeylanica

Vitex leucoxylon var. zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon vitex, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and medicinal purposes.
