Viola × inouei

Viola × inouei, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, butterfly-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10–20 cm and thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers, which range in color from white to purple, bloom in early spring, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Viola × inouei is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care in garden settings.
