Vandopsis praealta

Vandopsis praealta, commonly known as the tall vandopsis, is a rare and striking member of the fern family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach impressive lengths. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
