Veronica pectinata

Veronica pectinata, commonly known as the upright speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Veronica pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
