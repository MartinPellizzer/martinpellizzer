Viscum ambiguum

Viscum ambiguum, commonly known as the common European mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various trees and shrubs.

It has a smooth, glossy, evergreen foliage with elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white, waxy flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its berries are red, sticky, and toxic to humans and livestock.

Viscum ambiguum plays a role in traditional folklore and is also used in herbal medicine.
