Verticordia plumosa var. vassensis

Verticordia plumosa var. vassensis, commonly known as the Wimmera featherflower, is a striking shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that appear in spring, producing clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species within its native range.
