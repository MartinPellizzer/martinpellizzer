Vitex quadrangula

Vitex quadrangula, commonly known as the four-angle vitex, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often four-angled, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant, blue or purple flowers in panicles, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain several seeds.

Vitex quadrangula is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
