Vicia anguste-pinnata

Vicia anguste-pinnata, commonly known as narrow-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, pinnate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Vicia anguste-pinnata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
