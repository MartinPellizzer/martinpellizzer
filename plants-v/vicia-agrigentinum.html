Vicia agrigentinum

Vicia agrigentinum, commonly known as the Sicilian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes, which are followed by slender, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Vicia agrigentinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
