Vriesea tessellata

Vriesea tessellata, commonly known as the zigzag ginger plant, is a tropical bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette and are often covered in a waxy, blue-green coating. The plant produces vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer or early fall. Vriesea tessellata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
