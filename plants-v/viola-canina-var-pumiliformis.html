Viola canina var. pumiliformis

Viola canina var. pumiliformis, commonly known as the dwarf dog violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from purple to violet, often with yellow markings. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and grassy areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and attractive addition to gardens and natural habitats.
