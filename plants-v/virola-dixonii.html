Virola dixonii

Virola dixonii, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Virola dixonii plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
