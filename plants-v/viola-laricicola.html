Viola laricicola

Viola laricicola, commonly known as the mountain violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the forest floor.

Viola laricicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
