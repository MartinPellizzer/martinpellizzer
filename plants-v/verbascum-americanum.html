Verbascum americanum

Verbascum americanum, commonly known as American mullein, is a biennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a cylindrical spike. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along fences.

American mullein is also known for its medicinal uses, particularly in respiratory treatments.
