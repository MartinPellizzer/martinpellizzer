Vaccinium duclouxii var. hirticaule

Vaccinium duclouxii var. hirticaule, commonly known as the hairy blueberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible blue berries. Its leaves are oval and covered with fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive texture.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential as a food source for wildlife.
