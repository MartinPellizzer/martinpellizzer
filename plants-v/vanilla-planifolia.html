Vanilla planifolia

Vanilla planifolia, commonly known as the vanilla orchid, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It produces a unique, fragrant pod known as the vanilla bean, which is used extensively in flavoring foods and beverages. The plant requires a tropical climate with high humidity and partial shade to thrive. Its flowers are pollinated by a specific species of bee, the melipona, which is essential for fruit development.

The vanilla bean undergoes a complex curing process to develop its characteristic aroma and flavor.
