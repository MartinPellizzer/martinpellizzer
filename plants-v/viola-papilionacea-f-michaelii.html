Viola papilionacea f. michaelii

Viola papilionacea f. michaelii, commonly known as the Michael's violet, is a unique horticultural form of the common violet.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from pale purple to white. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant is also valued for its historical significance in botanical gardens and its role in hybridization efforts.
