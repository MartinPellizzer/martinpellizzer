Veronicastrum virginicum f. roseum

Veronicastrum virginicum f. roseum, commonly known as the pink giant hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial that produces spikes of showy, pink to purple flowers in late summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
