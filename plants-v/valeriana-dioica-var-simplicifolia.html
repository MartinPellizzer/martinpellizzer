Valeriana dioica var. simplicifolia

Valeriana dioica var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are simple and alternate, often with a slightly hairy surface. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its calming effects.
