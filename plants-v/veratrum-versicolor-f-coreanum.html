Veratrum versicolor f. coreanum

Veratrum versicolor f. coreanum, commonly known as the Korean white hellebore, is a variant of the Veratrum genus native to Korea.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers at the top of the stem, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

This species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its alkaloid content.
