Viola grypoceras f. rosipetala

Viola grypoceras f. rosipetala, commonly known as the pink-leaved violet, is a rare and ornamental variant of the viola genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple petal coloration, which sets it apart from typical viola species. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, such as woodland floors or shaded gardens. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

This cultivar is valued for its unique floral display and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in horticulture.
