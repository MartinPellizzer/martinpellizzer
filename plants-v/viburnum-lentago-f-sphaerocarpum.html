Viburnum lentago f. sphaerocarpum

Viburnum lentago f. sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the globular arrowwood, is a botanical variant of the arrowwood viburnum.

It is characterized by its unique, rounded, fruiting heads that distinguish it from the typical elongated clusters of the species. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by dark red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various wildlife species.
