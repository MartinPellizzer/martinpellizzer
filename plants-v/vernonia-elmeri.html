Vernonia elmeri

Vernonia elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are dark purple to black, giving it a striking appearance.

Vernonia elmeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
