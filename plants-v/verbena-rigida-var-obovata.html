Verbena rigida var. obovata

Verbena rigida var. obovata, commonly known as the rigid verbena or narrow-leaf verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and produces spikes of small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
