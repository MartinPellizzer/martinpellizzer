Vitex guanahacabibensis

Vitex guanahacabibensis, commonly known as the Guanahacabib vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Bahamas.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to purple. The plant produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps, which split into two parts when mature.

It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in dry, sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to tropical environments.
