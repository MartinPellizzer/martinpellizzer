Viola boliviana

Viola boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Viola boliviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
