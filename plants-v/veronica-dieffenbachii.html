Veronica dieffenbachii

Veronica dieffenbachii, commonly known as the silver-leafed veronica, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and container planting.
