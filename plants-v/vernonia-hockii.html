Vernonia hockii

Vernonia hockii, commonly known as the Hockii ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and can reach heights of up to six feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually a vibrant blue or purple.

Vernonia hockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
