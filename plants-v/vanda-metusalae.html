Vanda metusalae

Vanda metusalae, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth. Vanda metusalae is known for its ability to climb using its aerial roots, often found growing on trees or rocky surfaces.

Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
