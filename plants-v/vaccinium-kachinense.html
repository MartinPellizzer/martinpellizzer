Vaccinium kachinense

Vaccinium kachinense, commonly known as the Kachin huckleberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Myanmar and Thailand. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued both for its ecological role and its potential use in local cuisines.
