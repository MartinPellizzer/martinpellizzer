Vladimiria mirabilis

Vladimiria mirabilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is often found growing along riverbanks and in moist, shaded environments. Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Vladimiria mirabilis has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, though its exact chemical composition remains under investigation.
