Vicia lens subsp. lamottei

Vicia lens subsp.

lamottei, commonly known as the Lamotte vetch, is a subspecies of the common vetch. It is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its pods contain small, lens-shaped seeds, which are characteristic of the genus Vicia.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
