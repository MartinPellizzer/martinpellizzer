Vicia graminea var. grata

Vicia graminea var. grata, commonly known as the sweet vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
