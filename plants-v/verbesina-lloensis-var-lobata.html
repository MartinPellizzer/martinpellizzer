Verbesina lloensis var. lobata

Verbesina lloensis var. lobata, commonly known as the Lloentis verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and adaptation to high-altitude conditions.
