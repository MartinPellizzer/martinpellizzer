Vaccinium versicolor

Vaccinium versicolor, commonly known as the variable blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like heathers and cranberries. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish tinge. Its flowers are white or pink and bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that range in color from blue to red.

Vaccinium versicolor thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.
