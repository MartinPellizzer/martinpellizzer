Vernonia aschersonioides

Vernonia aschersonioides, commonly known as the silver vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
