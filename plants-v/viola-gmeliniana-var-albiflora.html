Viola gmeliniana var. albiflora

Viola gmeliniana var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
