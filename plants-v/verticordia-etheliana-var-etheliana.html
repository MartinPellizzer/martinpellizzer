Verticordia etheliana var. etheliana

Verticordia etheliana var. etheliana, commonly known as the Ethel's feathered banksia, is a striking plant native to the southwestern region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red, depending on the season. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, often in coastal or near-coastal environments. Its flowers attract a variety of native pollinators, including bees and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
