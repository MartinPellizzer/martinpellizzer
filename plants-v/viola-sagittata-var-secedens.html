Viola sagittata var. secedens

Viola sagittata var. secedens, commonly known as the narrowleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive dark green color and a serrated edge. Its flowers are violet-blue in color, with five petals and a spurred structure at the base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
