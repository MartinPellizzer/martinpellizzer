Veronica teucrium subsp. prostrata

Veronica teucrium subsp.

prostrata, commonly known as creeping speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.
