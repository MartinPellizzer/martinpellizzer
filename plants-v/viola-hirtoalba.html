Viola hirtoalba

Viola hirtoalba, commonly known as the rough violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and produces showy flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Viola hirtoalba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
