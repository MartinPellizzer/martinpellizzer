Viburnum × carlocephalum

Viburnum × carlocephalum, commonly known as the rose-of-Sharon viburnum, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern North America.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds but not recommended for human consumption. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Viburnum × carlocephalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
