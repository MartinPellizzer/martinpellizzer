Viola mirabilis var. strigosa

Viola mirabilis var. strigosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a deep green color and a distinctive fuzzy texture. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and native plant collections.
