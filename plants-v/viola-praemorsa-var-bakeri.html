Viola praemorsa var. bakeri

Viola praemorsa var. bakeri, commonly known as Baker's Violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
