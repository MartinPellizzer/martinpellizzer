Viola biflora var. akaishiensis

Viola biflora var. akaishiensis, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully develop.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
