Valeriana scandens var. genuina

Valeriana scandens var. genuina, commonly known as the creeping valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features spreading stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to form dense mats or colonies. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Valeriana scandens var. genuina is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
