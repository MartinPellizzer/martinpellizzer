Ventilago bombaiensis

Ventilago bombaiensis, commonly known as the Bangalore velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Ventilago bombaiensis is often found in moist, disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
