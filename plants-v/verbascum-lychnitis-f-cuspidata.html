Verbascum lychnitis f. cuspidata

Verbascum lychnitis f. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a flowering plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its more compact growth and sharply pointed leaf tips compared to other forms of Verbascum lychnitis.
