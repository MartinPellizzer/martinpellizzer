Verticordia sieberi var. curta

Verticordia sieberi var. curta, commonly known as the short-stemmed featherflower, is a small shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, feathery inflorescences that bloom in shades of pink and mauve. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate. Its flowers are highly ornamental and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and threatened species.
