Veronica spicata subsp. transcaucasica

Veronica spicata subsp.

transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant features upright, spiky stems that bear clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
