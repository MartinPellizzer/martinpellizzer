Vigna occidentalis

Vigna occidentalis, commonly known as the West African cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Vigna occidentalis is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and essential nutrients.
