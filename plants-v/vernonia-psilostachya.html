Vernonia psilostachya

Vernonia psilostachya, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Vernonia psilostachya is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
