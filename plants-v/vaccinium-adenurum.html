Vaccinium adenurum

Vaccinium adenurum, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are bell-shaped and usually pink or white, appearing in clusters.

The berries are edible, though they are small and often used in local foraging or as a food source for wildlife.
