Viola tricolor var. maritima

Viola tricolor var. maritima, commonly known as the coastal violet, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three heart-shaped leaflets, and vibrant flowers that come in shades of purple, white, and yellow. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in moist, well-drained environments near the shore. Its blooms appear in early spring, adding color to coastal landscapes.

This variety is well adapted to salty air and can tolerate light coastal winds.
