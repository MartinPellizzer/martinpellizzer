Vaccinium penaeoides

Vaccinium penaeoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia, where it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit, a small, dark blue berry, is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a potential crop for local agriculture.

The plant's leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in landscaping.
