Viticella parviflora var. austinae

Viticella parviflora var. austinae, commonly known as Austina's grapevine, is a compact, vigorous vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, round berries that range in color from green to black, depending on the variety and ripening stage. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

This variety is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.
