Viola × excerpta

Viola × excerpta, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the viola genus.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 centimeters and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value.

Viola × excerpta prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
