Veronica austriaca var. emarginata

Veronica austriaca var. emarginata, commonly known as the Austrian speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
