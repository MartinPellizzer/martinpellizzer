Veronica prostrata var. virens

Veronica prostrata var. virens, commonly known as the green prostrate speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage make it a versatile choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
