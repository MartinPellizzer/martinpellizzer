Vernonia petiolaris

Vernonia petiolaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months.

Vernonia petiolaris is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
