Vanda curvifolia

Vanda curvifolia, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spirally arranged leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. Vanda curvifolia thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
