Vitex involucratus

Vitex involucratus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, followed by woody, bean-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
