Vincetoxicum chihuahuense

Vincetoxicum chihuahuense, commonly known as Mexican thornapple, is a perennial shrub native to the Chihuahua region of Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds, and the plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.
