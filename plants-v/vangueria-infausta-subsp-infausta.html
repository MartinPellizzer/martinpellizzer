Vangueria infausta subsp. infausta

Vangueria infausta subsp.

infausta, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The species is noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
