Veronica lanisepala

Veronica lanisepala, commonly known as the silverleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery coating, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative ground cover due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
