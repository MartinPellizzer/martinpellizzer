Viola chillanensis

Viola chillanensis, commonly known as the Chilean violet, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, fragrant blossoms in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Viola chillanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
