Vitex leucoxylon f. saligna

Vitex leucoxylon f. saligna, commonly known as the silky ash or silky vitex, is a compact, shrub-like form of the vitex genus.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost cloud-like appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a velvety texture that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
