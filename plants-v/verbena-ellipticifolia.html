Verbena ellipticifolia

Verbena ellipticifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Verbena ellipticifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
