Verbena kuntzeana

Verbena kuntzeana, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, hairy leaves. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Verbena kuntzeana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
