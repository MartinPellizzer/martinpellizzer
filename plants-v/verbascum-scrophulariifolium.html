Verbascum scrophulariifolium

Verbascum scrophulariifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hair. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
