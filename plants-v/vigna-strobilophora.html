Vigna strobilophora

Vigna strobilophora, commonly known as the African horsebean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant has a climbing habit and can thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions. It is valued for its nutritional content, as the seeds are rich in protein and can be used in traditional cuisines.

Additionally, Vigna strobilophora plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
