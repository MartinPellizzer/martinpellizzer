Viburnum erubescens var. neurophyllum

Viburnum erubescens var. neurophyllum, commonly known as the redstem viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its seasonal color changes and attractive fruit display.
