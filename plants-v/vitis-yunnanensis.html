Vitis yunnanensis

Vitis yunnanensis, commonly known as Yunnan grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to China, specifically the Yunnan province, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by berries that ripen to a dark purple color. Vitis yunnanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in viticulture and horticulture.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
