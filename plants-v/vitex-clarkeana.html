Vitex clarkeana

Vitex clarkeana, commonly known as the Clarke's chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vitex clarkeana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
