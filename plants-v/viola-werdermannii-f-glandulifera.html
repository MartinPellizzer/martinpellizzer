Viola werdermannii f. glandulifera

Viola werdermannii f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular viola, is a horticultural form of the viola genus characterized by its distinctive glandular structures on its leaves and stems.

This plant typically features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered with small, sticky glands. The flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a spurred lower petal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is prized for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
