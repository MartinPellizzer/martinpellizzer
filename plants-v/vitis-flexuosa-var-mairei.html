Vitis flexuosa var. mairei

Vitis flexuosa var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to clusters of small, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
