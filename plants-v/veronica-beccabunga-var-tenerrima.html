Veronica beccabunga var. tenerrima

Veronica beccabunga var. tenerrima, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as ponds and wet meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are typically upright and sturdy.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
