Vigna marina subsp. oblonga

Vigna marina subsp.

oblonga, commonly known as the marine bean, is a leguminous plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant thrives in saline environments, making it well-adapted to coastal and tidal areas. Its seeds are often used in traditional cuisines and have nutritional value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and exhibits features typical of legumes, such as nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
