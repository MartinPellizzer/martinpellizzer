Verhuellia

Verhuellia, commonly known as the waxplant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and features small, waxy, white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy appearance. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
