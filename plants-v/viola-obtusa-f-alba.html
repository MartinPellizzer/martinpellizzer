Viola obtusa f. alba

Viola obtusa f. alba, commonly known as the white viola, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including Japan and China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, white or pale violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
