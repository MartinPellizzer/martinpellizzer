Veronica hispidula subsp. hispidula

Veronica hispidula subsp.

hispidula, commonly known as the hispid mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically features hairy, spreading stems and small, clustered flowers with five blue or purple petals.

The leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
