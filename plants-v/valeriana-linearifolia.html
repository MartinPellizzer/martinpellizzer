Valeriana linearifolia

Valeriana linearifolia, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a grass-like appearance.

Valeriana linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in shady conditions.
