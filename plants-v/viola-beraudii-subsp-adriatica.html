Viola beraudii subsp. adriatica

Viola beraudii subsp.

adriatica, commonly known as the Adriatic violet, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
