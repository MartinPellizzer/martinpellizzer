Valeriana gracilipes

Valeriana gracilipes, commonly known as slender valerian, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Valeriana gracilipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
