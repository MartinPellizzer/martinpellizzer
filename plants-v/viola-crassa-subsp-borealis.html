Viola crassa subsp. borealis

Viola crassa subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as tundra and rocky slopes.

The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are violet to purple, with five petals and are often clustered in racemes.

It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season, blooming in early summer.
