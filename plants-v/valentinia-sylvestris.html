Valentinia sylvestris

Valentinia sylvestris, commonly known as the wild valentine flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in early spring. Valentinia sylvestris thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in folklore.
