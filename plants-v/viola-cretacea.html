Viola cretacea

Viola cretacea, commonly known as the Crete violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, reflecting its preference for alkaline environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet-blue coloration, and it produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white. It is often found in open habitats such as cliffs, slopes, and grasslands.

Viola cretacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
