Verbena setacea

Verbena setacea, commonly known as the hairy verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Verbena setacea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to many botanical gardens.
