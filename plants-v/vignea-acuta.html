Vignea acuta

Vignea acuta, commonly known as the acute vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with slender stems and trifoliate leaves. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually purple or white, and it produces elongated pods containing several seeds.

Vignea acuta is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
