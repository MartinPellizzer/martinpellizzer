Vicia gemella proles laxiflora

Vicia gemella proles laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods are slender and contain several small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This species is notable for its role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
