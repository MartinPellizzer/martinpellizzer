Veronica sintenisii

Veronica sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis' speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat endangered species.
