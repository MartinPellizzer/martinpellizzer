Valeriana schachristanica

Valeriana schachristanica, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, somewhat woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system is often used in traditional medicine for its sedative properties.

Valeriana schachristanica thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
