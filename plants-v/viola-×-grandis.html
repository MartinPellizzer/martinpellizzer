Viola × grandis

Viola × grandis, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. Viola × grandis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to both ecological and aesthetic benefits in cultivated landscapes.
