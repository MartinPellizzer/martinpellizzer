Vaccinium myrtifolium

Vaccinium myrtifolium, commonly known as the red evergreen huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. Its leaves are oval-shaped, leathery, and have a fragrant, slightly acidic aroma.

Vaccinium myrtifolium thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
