Valeriana klementzii

Valeriana klementzii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley lady's-tongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana klementzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
