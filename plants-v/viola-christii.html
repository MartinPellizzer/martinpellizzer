Viola christii

Viola christii, commonly known as the Ethiopian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Viola christii has small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value has made it a subject of interest in botanical gardens and horticultural studies.
