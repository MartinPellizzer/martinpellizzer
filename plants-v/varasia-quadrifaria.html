Varasia quadrifaria

Varasia quadrifaria, commonly known as the four-leafed tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow-orange flowering heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly fields.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antimicrobial properties.
