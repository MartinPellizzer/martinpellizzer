Veronica anagalloides brachypetala

Veronica anagalloides brachypetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
