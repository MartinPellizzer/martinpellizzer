Veronica dilatata

Veronica dilatata, commonly known as the broad-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Veronica dilatata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
