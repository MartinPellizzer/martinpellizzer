Vellozia clavata

Vellozia clavata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vellozia, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its rosette growth pattern and fleshy, narrow leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
