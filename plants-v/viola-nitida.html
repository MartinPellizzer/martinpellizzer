Viola nitida

Viola nitida, commonly known as the slender violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, pale purple or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Viola nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle beauty to the landscape.
