Valota insularis

Valota insularis, commonly known as the island valota, is a flowering plant native to specific island regions in the Pacific.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for coastal and rocky habitats. Its foliage is typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Valota insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
