Vangueria pygmaea

Vangueria pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf vangueria, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compact, rounded leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is popular in gardens and landscapes.
