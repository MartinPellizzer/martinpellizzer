Vriesea speciosa

Vriesea speciosa, commonly known as the "Iron Cross" bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It features a rosette of narrow, dark green leaves with striking white and yellow markings that form a cross-like pattern. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear bright orange to red flowers, which bloom for several weeks. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea speciosa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
