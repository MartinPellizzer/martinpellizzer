Vernonia deflexa

Vernonia deflexa, commonly known as the flexible ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flower heads. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Vernonia deflexa is typically found in moist, open habitats such as roadsides and wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
