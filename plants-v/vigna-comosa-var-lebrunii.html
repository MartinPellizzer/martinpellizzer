Vigna comosa var. lebrunii

Vigna comosa var. lebrunii, commonly known as the hairy pinto bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several round, edible seeds. These seeds are often used in traditional cuisines and are valued for their nutritional content.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
