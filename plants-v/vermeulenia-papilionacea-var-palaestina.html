Vermeulenia papilionacea var. palaestina

Vermeulenia papilionacea var. palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, trifoliate leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of Mediterranean flora.
