Vigna wightii

Vigna wightii, commonly known as the Indian cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Vigna wightii is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It is often cultivated for both its edible seeds and its ornamental value.
