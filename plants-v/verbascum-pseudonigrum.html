Verbascum pseudonigrum

Verbascum pseudonigrum, commonly known as common mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, velvety leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in waste areas.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
