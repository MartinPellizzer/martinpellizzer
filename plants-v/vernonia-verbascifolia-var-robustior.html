Vernonia verbascifolia var. robustior

Vernonia verbascifolia var. robustior, commonly known as the robust ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered in dense, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces showy, conical flower heads that are typically pale purple to pink in color, appearing in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This species is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.
