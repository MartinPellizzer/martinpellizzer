Veronica leiophylla

Veronica leiophylla, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
