Viguiera scandens

Viguiera scandens, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial shrub or subshrub that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring woody stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces bright orange to red composite flowers, which are composed of ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Viguiera scandens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
