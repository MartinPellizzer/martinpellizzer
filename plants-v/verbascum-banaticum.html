Verbascum banaticum

Verbascum banaticum, commonly known as the Banat mullein, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, hairy stems that bear yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Verbascum banaticum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
