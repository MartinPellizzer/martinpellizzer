Viguiera parkinsonii f. typica

Viguiera parkinsonii f. typica, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fluffy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.
