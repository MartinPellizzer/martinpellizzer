Vignea paniculata

Vignea paniculata, commonly known as the climbing bean or winged bean, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive winged seeds that resemble small beans. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods containing edible seeds. Vignea paniculata is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and essential minerals.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
