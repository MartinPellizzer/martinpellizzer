Varronia lanata

Varronia lanata, commonly known as the hairy varronia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, hairy textures. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

Varronia lanata is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
