Vignea chordorrhiza

Vignea chordorrhiza, commonly known as the thread-root morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its roots are fibrous and have a unique, thread-like appearance, which gives it its scientific name.

Vignea chordorrhiza is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and vigorous growth.
