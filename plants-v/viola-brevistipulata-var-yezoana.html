Viola brevistipulata var. yezoana

Viola brevistipulata var. yezoana, commonly known as the Yezo violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are typically purple or violet, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
