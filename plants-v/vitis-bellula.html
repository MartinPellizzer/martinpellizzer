Vitis bellula

Vitis bellula, commonly known as the dwarf grape or miniature grapevine, is a deciduous woody vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, grape-like berries and its ability to grow in a compact form, making it suitable for small spaces. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

Vitis bellula is also valued for its potential in horticultural research and as a parent plant for breeding programs.
