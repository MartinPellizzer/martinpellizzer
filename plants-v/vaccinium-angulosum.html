Vaccinium angulosum

Vaccinium angulosum, commonly known as the angular blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge.

Vaccinium angulosum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic environments.
