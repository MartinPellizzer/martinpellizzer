Vigna hirtella

Vigna hirtella, commonly known as hairy vigna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Vigna hirtella is valued for its nutritional content, including proteins and essential minerals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
