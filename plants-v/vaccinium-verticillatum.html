Vaccinium verticillatum

Vaccinium verticillatum, commonly known as the whorled blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are tart and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.
