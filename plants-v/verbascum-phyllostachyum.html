Verbascum phyllostachyum

Verbascum phyllostachyum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, velvety gray-green leaves that are covered in dense, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in elongated spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum phyllostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
