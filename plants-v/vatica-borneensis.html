Vatica borneensis

Vatica borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Vatica borneensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
