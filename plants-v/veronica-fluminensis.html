Veronica fluminensis

Veronica fluminensis, commonly known as the river veronica, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as riverbanks and forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica fluminensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
