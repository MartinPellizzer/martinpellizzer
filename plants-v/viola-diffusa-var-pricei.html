Viola diffusa var. pricei

Viola diffusa var. pricei, commonly known as the Purple Heart Violet, is a flowering plant native to the Appalachian region of the United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
