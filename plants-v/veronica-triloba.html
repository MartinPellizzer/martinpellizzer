Veronica triloba

Veronica triloba, commonly known as the three-leaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine white down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

Veronica triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
