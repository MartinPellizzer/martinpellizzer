Viola × chevalieri

Viola × chevalieri, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, often bi-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-20 cm and thrives in partial shade to full sun. It prefers well-drained, moist soil and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by bees and other insects.
