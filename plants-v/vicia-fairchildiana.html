Vicia fairchildiana

Vicia fairchildiana, commonly known as Fairchild's vetch, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Vicia fairchildiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
