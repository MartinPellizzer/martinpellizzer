Viola patrinii f. subsagittata

Viola patrinii f. subsagittata, commonly known as the sagittate violet, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Violaceae family.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking violet flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant's botanical characteristics include a short, hairy stem and trifoliate leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
