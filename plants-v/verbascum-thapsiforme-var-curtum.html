Verbascum thapsiforme var. curtum

Verbascum thapsiforme var. curtum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters at the top of the stems, typically blooming from summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a fuzzy surface, while its flowers are arranged in a raceme.

This species is well adapted to dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
