Vernonia bishopii

Vernonia bishopii, commonly known as Bishop's vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

Vernonia bishopii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
