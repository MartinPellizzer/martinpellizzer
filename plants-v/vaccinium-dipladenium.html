Vaccinium dipladenium

Vaccinium dipladenium, commonly known as the Brazilian huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color, containing high levels of antioxidants. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
