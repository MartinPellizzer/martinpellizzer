Valeriana nitida

Valeriana nitida, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
