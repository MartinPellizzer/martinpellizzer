Viola biflora var. hirsuta

Viola biflora var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained, moist soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland areas.

Its hairy foliage and showy flowers make it a distinctive and ornamental species in native gardens.
