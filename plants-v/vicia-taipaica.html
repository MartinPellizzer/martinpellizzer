Vicia taipaica

Vicia taipaica, commonly known as the Taipaica vetch, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Vicia taipaica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
