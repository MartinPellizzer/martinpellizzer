Viola heterophylla var. ovatifolia

Viola heterophylla var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive dark green color. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or violet, during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
