Verbena officinalis var. anatolica

Verbena officinalis var. anatolica, commonly known as Turkish verbena, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
