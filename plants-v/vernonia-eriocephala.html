Vernonia eriocephala

Vernonia eriocephala, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray flower heads that resemble mist, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Vernonia eriocephala is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
