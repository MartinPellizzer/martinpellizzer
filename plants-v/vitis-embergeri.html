Vitis embergeri

Vitis embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's grape, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible berries that are typically black or purple in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in some regions.

Vitis embergeri thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
