Vigna unguiculata var. sulphurea

Vigna unguiculata var. sulphurea, commonly known as yellow cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow pods and small, edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, tropical climates. Its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers contribute to its ornamental value.

The botanical features of this variety make it both a valuable food crop and a potential ornamental plant in gardens.
