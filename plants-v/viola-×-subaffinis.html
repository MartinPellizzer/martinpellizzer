Viola × subaffinis

Viola × subaffinis, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the viola genus.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Viola × subaffinis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
