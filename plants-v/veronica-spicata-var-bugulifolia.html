Veronica spicata var. bugulifolia

Veronica spicata var. bugulifolia, commonly known as the spiked speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and long blooming period.
