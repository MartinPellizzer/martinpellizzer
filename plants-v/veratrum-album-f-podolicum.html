Veratrum album f. podolicum

Veratrum album f. podolicum, commonly known as the Podolic white hellebore, is a rare and striking botanical variant of the white hellebore.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a unique visual element to its appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is typically found in forested areas across its native range.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is often protected in its natural habitats.
