Vaccinium confertum

Vaccinium confertum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically a deep blue or black color when mature. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a glossy appearance.

Vaccinium confertum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
