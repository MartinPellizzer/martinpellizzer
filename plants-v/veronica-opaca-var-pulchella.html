Veronica opaca var. pulchella

Veronica opaca var. pulchella, commonly known as the purple veronica, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features slender stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
