Vigna unguiculata

Vigna unguiculata, commonly known as cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to warm climates and is drought-tolerant, making it a valuable crop in arid regions. Its flowers are typically white or purple, and it produces a high yield of protein-rich seeds.

Vigna unguiculata plays an important role in agricultural systems due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
