Viola japonica var. pusilla

Viola japonica var. pusilla, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a small herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple or violet, often with a white or yellow center. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
