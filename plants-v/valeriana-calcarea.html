Valeriana calcarea

Valeriana calcarea, commonly known as limestone valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana calcarea is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming effects.
