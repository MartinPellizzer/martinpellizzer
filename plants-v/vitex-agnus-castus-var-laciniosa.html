Vitex agnus-castus var. laciniosa

Vitex agnus-castus var. laciniosa, commonly known as the laceleaf chaste tree, is a variant of the chaste tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lacy foliage that gives it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming and hormonal balancing properties.
