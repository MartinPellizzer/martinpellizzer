Viscum microphyllum

Viscum microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Viscaceae family.

It attaches itself to the branches of various coniferous trees, such as pines, firs, and spruces, using a specialized structure called a haustellum to extract nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in seed dispersal by birds. Viscum microphyllum is native to the northern hemisphere and is often found in temperate forest ecosystems.

Its growth is slow, and it can live for many years, forming dense clusters on its host trees.
