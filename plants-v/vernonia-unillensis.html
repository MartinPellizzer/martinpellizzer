Vernonia unillensis

Vernonia unillensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces striking purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Vernonia unillensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
