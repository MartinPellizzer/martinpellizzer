Viola molisana

Viola molisana, commonly known as the Molisana violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola molisana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
