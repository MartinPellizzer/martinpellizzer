Vella pseudocytisus

Vella pseudocytisus, commonly known as the false Scotch broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with thorn-like branches, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Vella pseudocytisus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
