Vicia grandiflora var. scopoliana

Vicia grandiflora var. scopoliana, commonly known as the Scopolian vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, elongated clusters. Its pods are slender and contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family.

Vicia grandiflora var. scopoliana is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
