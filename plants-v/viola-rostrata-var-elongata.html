Viola rostrata var. elongata

Viola rostrata var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive elongated petiole, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with a characteristic spurred petal, typical of the Violaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in horticulture.
