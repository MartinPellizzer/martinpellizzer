Vitekorchis obryzata

Vitekorchis obryzata, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a sweet, fragrant aroma. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of dense forests. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from decaying organic matter, a common trait among orchids.

Vitekorchis obryzata is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
