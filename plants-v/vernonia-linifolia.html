Vernonia linifolia

Vernonia linifolia, commonly known as silky Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia linifolia is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
