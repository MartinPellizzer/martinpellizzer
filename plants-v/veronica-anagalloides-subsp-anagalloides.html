Veronica anagalloides subsp. anagalloides

Veronica anagalloides subsp.

anagalloides, commonly known as the stonecrop veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers on slender stems, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, with a rough, hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
