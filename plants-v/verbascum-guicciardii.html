Verbascum guicciardii

Verbascum guicciardii, commonly known as the desert mullein, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stalks that bear large, showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open landscapes. Its leaves are large, broad, and covered in dense, soft hairs that help reduce water loss.

Verbascum guicciardii is a hardy perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
