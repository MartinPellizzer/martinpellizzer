Viola vaginata subsp. alata

Viola vaginata subsp.

alata, commonly known as the winged violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive winged base, and it produces violet-blue flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Viola vaginata subsp.

alata is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators.
