Vincetoxicum × sakaianum

Vincetoxicum × sakaianum, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, so caution is advised when handling or consuming them.
