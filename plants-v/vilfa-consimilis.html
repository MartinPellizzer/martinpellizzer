Vilfa consimilis

Vilfa consimilis, commonly known as the similar vetch, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its slender, climbing stems that often twine around other plants. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Vilfa consimilis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
