Veronica beccabunga subsp. abscondita

Veronica beccabunga subsp.

abscondita, commonly known as the hidden veronica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are small, blue to violet, and arranged in dense spikes, attracting various pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
