Vatica canaca

Vatica canaca, commonly known as the yellow or white ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Vatica canaca is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
