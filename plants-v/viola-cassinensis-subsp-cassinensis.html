Viola cassinensis subsp. cassinensis

Viola cassinensis subsp.

cassinensis, commonly known as the Cassin's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet-blue coloration and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of purple and violet. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it spreads through rhizomes and seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
