Viola tenuicornis subsp. primorskajensis

Viola tenuicornis subsp.

primorskajensis, commonly known as the Primorsky viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Primorsky Krai region of Russia. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet-colored flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky slopes. It has small, spurred petals and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
