Viola tricolor var. machadeana

Viola tricolor var. machadeana, commonly known as the violet tricolor, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and produces heart-shaped petals in shades of purple, white, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
