Valantia cordata

Valantia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf valantia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its leaves are typically broad and have a glossy surface, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Valantia cordata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
