Vicia narbonensis subsp. johannis

Vicia narbonensis subsp.

johannis, commonly known as the Johann's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
