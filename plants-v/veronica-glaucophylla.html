Veronica glaucophylla

Veronica glaucophylla, commonly known as the blueleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers and hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
