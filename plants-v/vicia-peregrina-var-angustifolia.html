Vicia peregrina var. angustifolia

Vicia peregrina var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three narrow, elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in racemes. Its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruit capsules are characteristic of the Fabaceae family.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in sunny, open habitats.
