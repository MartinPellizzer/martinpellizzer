Valeriana niphobia

Valeriana niphobia, commonly known as the alpine valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called cymes. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Valeriana niphobia is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
