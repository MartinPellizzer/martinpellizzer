Vesquella oblongifolia

Vesquella oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed vesquella, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, oblong leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often blooming during the warmer months. Vesquella oblongifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
