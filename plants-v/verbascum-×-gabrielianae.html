Verbascum × gabrielianae

Verbascum × gabrielianae, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes various species of mullein known for their fuzzy, woolly leaves. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
