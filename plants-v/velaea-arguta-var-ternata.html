Velaea arguta var. ternata

Velaea arguta var. ternata, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in some areas.
