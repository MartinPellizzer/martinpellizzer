Viola livonica

Viola livonica, commonly known as the Siberian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Viola livonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
