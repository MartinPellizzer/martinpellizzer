Vernonia georgiana

Vernonia georgiana, commonly known as George's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, dark violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a distinctive blue-green color.

Vernonia georgiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for pollinators and wildlife.
