Vitis miqueliana var. linearifolia

Vitis miqueliana var. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved grapevine, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and vigorous climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of small, edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential for fruit production.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
