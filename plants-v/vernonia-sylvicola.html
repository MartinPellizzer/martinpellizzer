Vernonia sylvicola

Vernonia sylvicola, commonly known as the swamp ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Vernonia sylvicola plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
