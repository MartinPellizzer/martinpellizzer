Vouacapoua parvifolia

Vouacapoua parvifolia, commonly known as the pink velvet tamarind, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive pinkish-white flowers. The fruit is a woody pod that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
