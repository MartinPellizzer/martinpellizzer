Viola alba var. picta

Viola alba var. picta, commonly known as the white violet or painted violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
