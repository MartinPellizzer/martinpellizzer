Villanova bipinnatifida

Villanova bipinnatifida, commonly known as the two-lobed fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its intricate, bipinnatifid fronds.

The fronds are divided into small, finely toothed segments, giving the plant a delicate and feathery appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests or along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor in soil and absorb nutrients.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical study.
