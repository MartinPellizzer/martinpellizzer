Vitis floridana

Vitis floridana, commonly known as the Florida grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by wildlife. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Vitis floridana is valued for its ecological role and historical use by Native American communities.
