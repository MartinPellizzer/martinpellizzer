Vernonia verbascifolia

Vernonia verbascifolia, commonly known as the woolly ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, woolly appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
