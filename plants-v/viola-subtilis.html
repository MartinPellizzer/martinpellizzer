Viola subtilis

Viola subtilis, commonly known as the delicate violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It prefers partial shade and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.
