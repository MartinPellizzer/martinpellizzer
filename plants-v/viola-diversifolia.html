Viola diversifolia

Viola diversifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland areas. Its leaves are usually broad and have a slightly rough texture, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

Viola diversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
