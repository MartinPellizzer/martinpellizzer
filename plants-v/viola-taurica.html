Viola taurica

Viola taurica, commonly known as the Caucasian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-30 cm and thrives in moist, shady environments. Its flowers have five petals and are often adorned with a distinctive yellowish spot at the base.

Viola taurica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
