Vaccinium bracteatum var. longitubum

Vaccinium bracteatum var. longitubum, commonly known as the long-tube huckleberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
