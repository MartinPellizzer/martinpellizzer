Valeriana ficariifolia var. paripinnata

Valeriana ficariifolia var. paripinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in umbels. Its root system is fibrous and often contains aromatic compounds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
