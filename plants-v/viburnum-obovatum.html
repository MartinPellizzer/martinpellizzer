Viburnum obovatum

Viburnum obovatum, commonly known as the northern arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, spherical berries. Viburnum obovatum is often found in moist, wooded areas and can grow up to 6 feet tall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and supporting local wildlife.
