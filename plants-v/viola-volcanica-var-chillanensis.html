Viola volcanica var. chillanensis

Viola volcanica var. chillanensis, commonly known as the Chillan violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is a member of the Violaceae family, which includes other violets and related species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
