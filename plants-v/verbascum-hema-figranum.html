Verbascum hema-figranum

Verbascum hema-figranum, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Verbascum hema-figranum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
