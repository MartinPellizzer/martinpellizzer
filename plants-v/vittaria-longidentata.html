Vittaria longidentata

Vittaria longidentata, commonly known as the long-toothed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Vittariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green and have a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, a feature typical of many ferns.

Vittaria longidentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
