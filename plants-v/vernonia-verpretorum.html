Vernonia verpretorum

Vernonia verpretorum, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind. Vernonia verpretorum is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
