Veronica darwiniana

Veronica darwiniana, commonly known as the Darwin's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica darwiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
