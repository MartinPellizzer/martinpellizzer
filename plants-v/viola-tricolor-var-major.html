Viola tricolor var. major

Viola tricolor var. major, commonly known as the heartsease or common violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, white, and yellow, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola tricolor var. major is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
