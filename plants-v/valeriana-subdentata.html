Valeriana subdentata

Valeriana subdentata, commonly known as the serrated valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves and roots that contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Valeriana subdentata is valued for its calming effects and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for centuries.
