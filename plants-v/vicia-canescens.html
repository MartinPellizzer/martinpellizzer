Vicia canescens

Vicia canescens, commonly known as the hoary vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. Its showy, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple and appear in clusters.

Vicia canescens is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
