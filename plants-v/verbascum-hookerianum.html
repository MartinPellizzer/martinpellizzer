Verbascum hookerianum

Verbascum hookerianum, commonly known as Hooker's mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are broad and velvety, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Verbascum hookerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
