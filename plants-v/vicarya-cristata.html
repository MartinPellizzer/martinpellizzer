Vicarya cristata

Vicarya cristata, commonly known as the crested zamioculcas, is a striking member of the Zamioculcas genus.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant features a short, thick stem with a distinctive crest-like pattern on its surface. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering.

Vicarya cristata is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
