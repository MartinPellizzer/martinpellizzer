Vernonia dupuisii

Vernonia dupuisii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is a tall, perennial shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its sturdy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to brownish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Vernonia dupuisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
