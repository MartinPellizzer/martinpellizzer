Verbascum × sublyratum

Verbascum × sublyratum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a composite plant belonging to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green, lobed leaves that resemble those of the common mullein. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Verbascum × sublyratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
