Veronica angustifolia var. dilatata

Veronica angustifolia var. dilatata, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
