Vicia cassubica var. orobus

Vicia cassubica var. orobus, commonly known as the alpine vetch, is a perennial legume native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, followed by slender, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
