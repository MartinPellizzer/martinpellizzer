Veronica arvensis var. microphylla

Veronica arvensis var. microphylla, commonly known as the lesser speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common indicator plant in certain ecological habitats.
