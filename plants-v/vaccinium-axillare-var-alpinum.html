Vaccinium axillare var. alpinum

Vaccinium axillare var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine huckleberry, is a shrub native to mountainous regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants adapted to poor soils. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically evergreen, with a dense, compact growth habit.

It thrives in cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
