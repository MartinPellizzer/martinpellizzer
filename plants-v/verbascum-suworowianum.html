Verbascum suworowianum

Verbascum suworowianum, commonly known as the Woolly Mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Verbascum suworowianum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
