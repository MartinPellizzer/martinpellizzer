Veronica stelleri var. glabrescens

Veronica stelleri var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silverleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, blue-violet flowers. The leaves are typically gray-green and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
