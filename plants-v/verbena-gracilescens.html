Verbena gracilescens

Verbena gracilescens, commonly known as slender verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soils. Its flowers, which range in color from blue to purple, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbena gracilescens is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
