Viola mandshurica f. pubigera

Viola mandshurica f. pubigera, commonly known as the Siberian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and dark green coloration. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
