Verbena perennis var. johnstonii

Verbena perennis var. johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and prolonged blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
