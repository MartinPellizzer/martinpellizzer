Veronica simulans

Veronica simulans, commonly known as the blueweed or blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant features small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

Veronica simulans is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful display.
