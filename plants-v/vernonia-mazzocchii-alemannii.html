Vernonia mazzocchii-alemannii

Vernonia mazzocchii-alemannii, commonly known as the Alemanian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia mazzocchii-alemannii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils.
