Viola canina var. arenaria

Viola canina var. arenaria, commonly known as the sand viol, is a low-growing perennial herb native to sandy and coastal regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces violet or purple flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white spot at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, coastal environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
