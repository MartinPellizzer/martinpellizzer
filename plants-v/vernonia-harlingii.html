Vernonia harlingii

Vernonia harlingii, commonly known as the Harling's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Vernonia harlingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
