Vulpia membranacea var. longiseta

Vulpia membranacea var. longiseta, commonly known as the long-haired fescue, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, soft foliage. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, with each spikelet containing two to three flowering lemmas. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a bluish-green hue, with a prominent midrib.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a range of environmental conditions.
