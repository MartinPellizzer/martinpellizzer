Visenia spicata

Visenia spicata, commonly known as the spiked elder, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, dark berries in the autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Visenia spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
