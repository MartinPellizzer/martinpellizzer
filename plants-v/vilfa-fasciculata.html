Vilfa fasciculata

Vilfa fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered vilfa, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Vilfa fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
