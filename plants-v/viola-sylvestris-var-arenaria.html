Viola sylvestris var. arenaria

Viola sylvestris var. arenaria, commonly known as the sand violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open woodland or along paths. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
