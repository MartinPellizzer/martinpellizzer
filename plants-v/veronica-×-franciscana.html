Veronica × franciscana

Veronica × franciscana, commonly known as the Franciscan speedwell, is a hybrid plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
