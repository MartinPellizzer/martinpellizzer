Vitis × koberi

Vitis × koberi, commonly known as the koberi grape, is a hybrid vine species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces small, often clustered berries that are typically used in winemaking. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it is known for its adaptability to different climates.

Vitis × koberi is also valued for its resistance to certain pests and diseases, making it a useful cultivar in viticulture.
