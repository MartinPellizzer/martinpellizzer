Viscum laxum subsp. pini

Viscum laxum subsp.

pini, commonly known as the pine mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various coniferous trees, primarily pines. It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually yellow or orange, containing one or two seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to regions of Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
