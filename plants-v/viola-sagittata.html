Viola sagittata

Viola sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped or arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive dark green color. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Viola sagittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
