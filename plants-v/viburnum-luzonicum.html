Viburnum luzonicum

Viburnum luzonicum, commonly known as the Luzon viburnum, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Viburnum luzonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
