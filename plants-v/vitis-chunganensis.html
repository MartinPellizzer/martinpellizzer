Vitis chunganensis

Vitis chunganensis, commonly known as the Chungan grapevine, is a species native to China.

It is a deciduous climbing vine that belongs to the Vitaceae family. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it has tendrils that help it climb.

Vitis chunganensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in viticulture and horticulture.
