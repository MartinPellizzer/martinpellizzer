Valeriana caucasica

Valeriana caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often pink or white flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its root system is extensively branched and contains the main medicinal compounds.

Valeriana caucasica is valued for its sedative properties and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
