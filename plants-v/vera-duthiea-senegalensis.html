Vera-duthiea senegalensis

Vera-duthiea senegalensis, commonly known as the African wild bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is often found in dry savanna and woodland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a shallow root system, allowing it to adapt to arid conditions.

Vera-duthiea senegalensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
