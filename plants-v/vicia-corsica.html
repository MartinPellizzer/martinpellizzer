Vicia corsica

Vicia corsica, commonly known as Corsican vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. Vicia corsica thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration efforts.
