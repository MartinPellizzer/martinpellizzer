Verbena simplex f. eggertii

Verbena simplex f. eggertii, commonly known as the silverleaf verbena, is a compact, low-growing perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-gray foliage that provides a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
