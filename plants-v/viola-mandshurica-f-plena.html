Viola mandshurica f. plena

Viola mandshurica f. plena, commonly known as the full-flowered Chinese violet, is a hybrid form of the viola genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, double flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a compact growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and covered in fine hair, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.

This variety is valued for its resilience and ability to flourish in partial shade.
