Viola negulosa

Viola negulosa, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, blue, or white. Viola negulosa is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cold climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in natural landscapes.
