Viola pedata var. inornata

Viola pedata var. inornata, commonly known as the unadorned violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
