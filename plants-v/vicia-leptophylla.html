Vicia leptophylla

Vicia leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, this plant often grows in grassy areas and along roadsides. It has a climbing habit, using its twining stems to ascend supports, and produces lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
