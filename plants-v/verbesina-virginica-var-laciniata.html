Verbesina virginica var. laciniata

Verbesina virginica var. laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, palmately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The varietal name 'laciniata' refers to the finely cut, laciniate appearance of its leaves.
