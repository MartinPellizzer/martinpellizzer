Veronica connata subsp. glaberrima

Veronica connata subsp.

glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth veronica, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy but can be smooth in this subspecies. Its flowers are blue to purple, arranged in dense spike-like clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
