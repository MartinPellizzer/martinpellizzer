Vernonia sutepensis

Vernonia sutepensis, commonly known as the Andean ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in cooler, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to reddish-brown.

Vernonia sutepensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
