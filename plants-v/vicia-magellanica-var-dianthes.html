Vicia magellanica var. dianthes

Vicia magellanica var. dianthes, commonly known as the Magellan pea or Magellan vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually purple or pink in color. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
