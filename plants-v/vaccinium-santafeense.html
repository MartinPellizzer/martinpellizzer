Vaccinium santafeense

Vaccinium santafeense, commonly known as the Santa Fe blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many other shrubs and small trees. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in acidic soils and requires a cool, humid climate for optimal growth.
