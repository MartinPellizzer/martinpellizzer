Veronica cusickii

Veronica cusickii, commonly known as the Cusick's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, blue to violet flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
