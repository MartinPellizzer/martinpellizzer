Vaccinium glandulosissimum

Vaccinium glandulosissimum, commonly known as the glandular blueberry, is a species native to the southern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetland areas, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in acidic, sandy soils. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. Its leaves are covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various species.
