Verbascum thapsus var. albiflora

Verbascum thapsus var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered mullein, is a variant of the common mullein plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
