Veratrum mairei

Veratrum mairei, commonly known as Maire's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its stems are smooth and often have a milky sap, while its roots are thick and fleshy.

Veratrum mairei is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
