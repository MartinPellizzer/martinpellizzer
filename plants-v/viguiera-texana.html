Viguiera texana

Viguiera texana, commonly known as Texas ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Viguiera texana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
