Viola lactea var. ruppii

Viola lactea var. ruppii, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces showy flowers in shades of white to pale purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific care requirements.
