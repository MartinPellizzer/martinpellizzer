Verbascum officinarum

Verbascum officinarum, commonly known as common mullein, is a biennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that form a rosette in the first year. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in the second year, typically in late summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields.

Verbascum officinarum has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly for respiratory ailments.
