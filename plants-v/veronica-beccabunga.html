Veronica beccabunga

Veronica beccabunga, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica beccabunga is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions, making it a valuable plant for wetland restoration projects.
