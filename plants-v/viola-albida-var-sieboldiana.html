Viola albida var. sieboldiana

Viola albida var. sieboldiana, commonly known as Siebold's white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate white flowers with a pale violet center. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
