Viola brittoniana var. pectinata

Viola brittoniana var. pectinata, commonly known as the pectinate viola, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pectinate (toothed) margin, and it produces showy violet flowers with five petals. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, often with a yellow or white keel.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
