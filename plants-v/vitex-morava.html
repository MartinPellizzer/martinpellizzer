Vitex morava

Vitex morava, commonly known as the Moravian vitex, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blue or purple, which bloom in summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Vitex morava is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
