Viguiera pedunculata

Viguiera pedunculata, commonly known as Mexican sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces fluffy, seed-filled capsules that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
