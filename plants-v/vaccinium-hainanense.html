Vaccinium hainanense

Vaccinium hainanense, commonly known as the Hainan blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark blue or black in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in its native habitat.
