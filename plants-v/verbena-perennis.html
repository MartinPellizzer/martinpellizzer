Verbena perennis

Verbena perennis, commonly known as perennial verbena, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue, purple, or pink, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn. This plant prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
