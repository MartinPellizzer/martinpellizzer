Vernonia diffusa var. macrocephala

Vernonia diffusa var. macrocephala, commonly known as the broad-headed ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of dark purple to brownish-red flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
