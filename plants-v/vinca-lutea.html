Vinca lutea

Vinca lutea, commonly known as the yellow vinca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Vinca lutea is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
