Vicia andicola

Vicia andicola, commonly known as the Andean vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its trifoliate leaves are compound, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

Vicia andicola is adapted to cold and arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
