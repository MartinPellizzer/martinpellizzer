Vernonia lampropappa

Vernonia lampropappa, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage and flowers are often used in native gardens for their ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Vernonia lampropappa is also an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
