Viola canina var. alba

Viola canina var. alba, commonly known as the white dog violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, showy white or pale violet flowers with a distinctive yellow marking on the lower lip. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
