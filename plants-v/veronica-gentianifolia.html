Veronica gentianifolia

Veronica gentianifolia, commonly known as the gentianleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Veronica gentianifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
