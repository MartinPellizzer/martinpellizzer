Vaccinium costaricense

Vaccinium costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican huckleberry, is a shrub native to the humid forests of Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

Vaccinium costaricense thrives in shaded, moist environments and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
