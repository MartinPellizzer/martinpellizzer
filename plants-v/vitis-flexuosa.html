Vitis flexuosa

Vitis flexuosa, commonly known as the river grape, is a deciduous vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from green to black. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Vitis flexuosa thrives in moist, riparian habitats and is an important species for ecological studies.
