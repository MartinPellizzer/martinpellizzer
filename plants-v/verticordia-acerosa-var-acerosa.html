Verticordia acerosa var. acerosa

Verticordia acerosa var. acerosa, commonly known as the hard-leaved bottlebrush, is a striking plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from red to orange, which bloom during late winter to early spring. The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are typically grey-green in color and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and seasonal flowering make it a distinctive addition to native plant collections.
