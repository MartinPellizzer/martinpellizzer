Valeriana rotundiloba

Valeriana rotundiloba, commonly known as the round-leaved valerian, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features a robust rhizome system and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant has opposite, rounded leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Valeriana rotundiloba is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
