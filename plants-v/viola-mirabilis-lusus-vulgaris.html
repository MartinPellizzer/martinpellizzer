Viola mirabilis lusus vulgaris

Viola mirabilis lusus vulgaris, commonly known as the common violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

The common violet is also known for its medicinal uses and has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
