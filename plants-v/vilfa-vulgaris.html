Vilfa vulgaris

Vilfa vulgaris, commonly known as the common vilfa, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy. The flowers are small, clustered in loose inflorescences, and range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
