Viola rostrata f. phelpsiae

Viola rostrata f. phelpsiae, commonly known as Phelps' violet, is a rare and unique variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue petals with a yellowish throat and a narrow, elongated spur. This plant is native to the eastern United States, particularly in moist woodland habitats. It typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters and produces small, delicate flowers in early spring.

The foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.
