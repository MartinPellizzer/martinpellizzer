Vignea × kuekenthalii

Vignea × kuekenthalii, commonly known as the hybrid cowpea, is a cross between Vigna unguiculata and Vigna angularis.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the pods are slender, containing edible seeds. This plant is valued for its nutritional content, including protein and essential minerals, and is cultivated in various regions for both food and forage.

Its botanical features make it an important species in agricultural and ecological contexts.
