Valeriana lancifolia

Valeriana lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved valerian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana lancifolia is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
