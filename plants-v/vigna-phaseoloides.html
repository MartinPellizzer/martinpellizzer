Vigna phaseoloides

Vigna phaseoloides, commonly known as the cowpea or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into elongated pods. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable crop for soil enrichment. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as a forage crop or for its edible beans.

Vigna phaseoloides is also used in traditional medicine and has ecological benefits due to its nitrogen-fixing properties.
