Vignea × pseudovulpina

Vignea × pseudovulpina, commonly known as the hybrid vigna, is a cross between Vigna unguiculata and Vigna villosa.

It is a legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

The plant is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various traditional cuisines.
