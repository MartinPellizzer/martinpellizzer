Vernonia flaccidifolia var. angustifolia

Vernonia flaccidifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and native plant gardens.
