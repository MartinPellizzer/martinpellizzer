Vitis hyemalis

Vitis hyemalis, commonly known as the winter grape, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sour-tasting berries that ripen in the fall. Vitis hyemalis is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
