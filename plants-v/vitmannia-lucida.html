Vitmannia lucida

Vitmannia lucida, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1.5 meters in height, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Vitmannia lucida thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
