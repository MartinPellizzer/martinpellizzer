Vincetoxicum cissoides

Vincetoxicum cissoides, commonly known as the creeping false bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and roadsides. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Its roots are tuberous and can spread aggressively, making it a potential weed in certain environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value but requires careful management due to its invasive tendencies.
