Veronica sibirica f. pubescens

Veronica sibirica f. pubescens, commonly known as the Siberian speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a similar pubescent coating.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
