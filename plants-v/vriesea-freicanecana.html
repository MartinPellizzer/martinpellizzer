Vriesea freicanecana

Vriesea freicanecana, commonly known as the Freicanecana Vriesea, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that can range from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves is covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention.

Vriesea freicanecana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
