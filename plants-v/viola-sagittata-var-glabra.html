Viola sagittata var. glabra

Viola sagittata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet-blue coloration and produces small, delicate flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its smooth, hairless stems and leaves are characteristic of the varietal name "glabra.".

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
