Vicia angustifolia var. segetalis

Vicia angustifolia var. segetalis, commonly known as narrow-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along field margins. Its seeds are encased in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

This species is also valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
