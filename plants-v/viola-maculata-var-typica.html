Viola maculata var. typica

Viola maculata var. typica, commonly known as the common pansy, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive purple or violet marking on the underside. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, often found in woodland areas and along paths.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
