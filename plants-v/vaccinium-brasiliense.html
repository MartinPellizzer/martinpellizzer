Vaccinium brasiliense

Vaccinium brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian huckleberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to acidic soils makes it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
