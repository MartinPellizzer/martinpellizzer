Veronica spicata subsp. vulgaris

Veronica spicata subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as common speedwell, is a flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae. It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This species is often used in gardens for its ability to attract pollinators and its hardy nature.
