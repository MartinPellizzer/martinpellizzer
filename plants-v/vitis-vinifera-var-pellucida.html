Vitis vinifera var. pellucida

Vitis vinifera var. pellucida, commonly known as the "pearl grape," is a rare and less commonly cultivated variety of the common grapevine.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy skin that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically produces small, sweet berries with a unique flavor profile. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity, it is often found in specialized botanical gardens or conservation programs.
