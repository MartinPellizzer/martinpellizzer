Verbesina virgata var. oreopola

Verbesina virgata var. oreopola, commonly known as the mountain ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with serrated edges, and its fibrous root system helps it thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in open meadows and alpine environments, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
