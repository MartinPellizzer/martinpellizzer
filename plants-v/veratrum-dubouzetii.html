Veratrum dubouzetii

Veratrum dubouzetii, commonly known as the Dubouzet's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, stout stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and often have a waxy coating.

Veratrum dubouzetii contains various toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
