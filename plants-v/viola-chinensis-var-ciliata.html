Viola chinensis var. ciliata

Viola chinensis var. ciliata, commonly known as the Chinese violet or the purple violet, is a species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with hairy margins and clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
