Vrydagzynea kerintjiensis

Vrydagzynea kerintjiensis, commonly known as the Kerintjiensis cycad, is a unique cycad species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of stiff, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants, a feature common in cycads. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, sunny climate to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
