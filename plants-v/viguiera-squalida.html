Viguiera squalida

Viguiera squalida, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States and Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, while its flowers are typically pink to purple in color.

Viguiera squalida is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
