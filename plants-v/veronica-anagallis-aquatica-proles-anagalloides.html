Veronica anagallis-aquatica proles anagalloides

Veronica anagallis-aquatica proles anagalloides, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating on water surfaces. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including ponds, streams, and marshes. It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats in its aquatic environment.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird life.
