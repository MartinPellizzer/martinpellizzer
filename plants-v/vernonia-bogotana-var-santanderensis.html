Vernonia bogotana var. santanderensis

Vernonia bogotana var. santanderensis, commonly known as the Santander Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure. The plant is known for its upright growth habit and produces small, pale yellow to brownish flowers. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in grasslands and open slopes.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a component of the unique flora of the Andes, contributing to local biodiversity.
