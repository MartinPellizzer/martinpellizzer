Verbascum × harknessii

Verbascum × harknessii, commonly known as the Harkness mullein, is a hybrid plant in the genus Verbascum, which belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, velvety hairs, and large, lobed leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to the southwestern United States and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
