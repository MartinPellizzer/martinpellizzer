Verbena × engelmannii

Verbena × engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's verbena, is a hybrid flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It is drought-tolerant and resistant to pests, which contributes to its popularity among gardeners.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
