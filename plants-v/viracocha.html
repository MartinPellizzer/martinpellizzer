Viracocha

Viracocha, commonly known as the "Great Creator" in Incan mythology, is not a botanical species but a mythological figure.

However, in some Andean cultures, certain plants are associated with Viracocha due to their symbolic or sacred significance. These plants are often used in rituals and traditional medicine. Examples include quinoa, coca, and maca, which are revered for their nutritional and spiritual properties.

The connection between Viracocha and these plants reflects the deep interweaving of botany, religion, and culture in Andean traditions.
