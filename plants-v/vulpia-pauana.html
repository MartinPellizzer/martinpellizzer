Vulpia pauana

Vulpia pauana, commonly known as the Brazilian silky grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that are clustered along the stems, which are often wind-pollinated. Vulpia pauana thrives in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, supporting various invertebrates and birds.
