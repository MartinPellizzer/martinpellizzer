Viola vaginata f. albescens

Viola vaginata f. albescens, commonly known as the white viola, is a variant of the common violet characterized by its pale to white flowers.

This plant is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Violaceae family, native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy texture, and the flowers are usually solitary, with five petals.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants with a delicate, early spring bloom.
