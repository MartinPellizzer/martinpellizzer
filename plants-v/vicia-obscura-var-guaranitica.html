Vicia obscura var. guaranitica

Vicia obscura var. guaranitica, commonly known as the dark vetch, is a perennial legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
