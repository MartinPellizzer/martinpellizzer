Vernonanthura serratuloides

Vernonanthura serratuloides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed veldt grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny flowers. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to semi-arid climates, making it a hardy component of local vegetation.

The species plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
