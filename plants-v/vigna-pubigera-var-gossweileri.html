Vigna pubigera var. gossweileri

Vigna pubigera var. gossweileri, commonly known as the African cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in regions with tropical and subtropical conditions.
