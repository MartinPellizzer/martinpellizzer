Vernonia carinata

Vernonia carinata, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland areas and along stream banks. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive central ridge, and its flowers are violet to purple in color.

Vernonia carinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
