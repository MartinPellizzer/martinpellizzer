Vernonia kawakamii

Vernonia kawakamii, commonly known as Kawakami's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Hawaii.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia kawakamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
