Vincetoxicum hirundinaria subsp. contiguum

Vincetoxicum hirundinaria subsp.

contiguum, commonly known as the silver weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically silver-white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red during late spring. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
