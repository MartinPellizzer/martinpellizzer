Viola distans var. fimbriata

Viola distans var. fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive fringed edge, and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with a white throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas, preferring well-drained soil. It is part of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its short, upright stems.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
