Verbesina donnell-smithii

Verbesina donnell-smithii, commonly known as the Donnell-Smith beggar-ticks, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant has rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks.

Its common name reflects its ability to spread via seeds and its prickly, seed-producing structures.
