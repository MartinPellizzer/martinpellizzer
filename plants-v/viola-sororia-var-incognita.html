Viola sororia var. incognita

Viola sororia var. incognita, commonly known as the unknown sororia violet, is a lesser-known variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, oval-shaped seed pods.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
