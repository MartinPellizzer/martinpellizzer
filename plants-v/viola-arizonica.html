Viola arizonica

Viola arizonica, commonly known as Arizona violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its native habitat.

The species is well adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
