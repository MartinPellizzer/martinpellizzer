Vernonia rosmarinifolia

Vernonia rosmarinifolia, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and roadside ditches. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its showy, conical flower heads are usually blue to purple in color and bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia rosmarinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
