Viburnum molinae

Viburnum molinae, commonly known as Molina's viburnum, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Viburnum molinae typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as chaparral and woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
