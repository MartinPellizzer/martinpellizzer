Vismia baccifera subsp. baccifera

Vismia baccifera subsp.

baccifera, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and large, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a segmented aril that encases a single seed, making it distinctive in appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
