Vernonia lanceolata

Vernonia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
