Verbascum × haynaldianum

Verbascum × haynaldianum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves and tall spikes of yellow flowers. The plant is a hybrid between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattellum, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
