Vepris pilosa

Vepris pilosa, commonly known as hairy vepris, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves and stems are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
