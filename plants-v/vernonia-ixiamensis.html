Vernonia ixiamensis

Vernonia ixiamensis, commonly known as the ixiamensis ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Vernonia ixiamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
