Viola tricolor var. pesneaui

Viola tricolor var. pesneaui, commonly known as the Purple Heart Viola, is a hybrid cultivar of the common violet.

It is characterized by its striking violet, purple, and white petals, which create a distinctive color contrast. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 cm, with heart-shaped leaves that are velvety in texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

This variety is particularly noted for its prolonged flowering period, making it a popular choice for spring displays.
