Viguiera greggii

Viguiera greggii, commonly known as Gregg's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces cylindrical seed heads that are often used in native garden landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid environments.

Viguiera greggii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
