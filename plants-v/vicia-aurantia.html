Vicia aurantia

Vicia aurantia, commonly known as yellow vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a trailing growth habit and produces small, lens-shaped pods containing several seeds.

Vicia aurantia is a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
