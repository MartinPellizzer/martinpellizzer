Viola volcanica var. truncata

Viola volcanica var. truncata, commonly known as the truncated violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is notable for its compact growth habit and unique leaf morphology, making it a distinctive member of the Violaceae family.
