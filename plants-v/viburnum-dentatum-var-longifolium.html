Viburnum dentatum var. longifolium

Viburnum dentatum var. longifolium, commonly known as the flowering arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and elongated leaflets, giving it its varietal name "longifolium.". The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
