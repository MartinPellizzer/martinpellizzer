Vitis seguinii

Vitis seguinii, commonly known as the Seguin's grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, typically growing in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are usually green to red in color. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it is known for its adaptability to harsh climates.

Vitis seguinii is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential for cultivation in drought-prone regions.
