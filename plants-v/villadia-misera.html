Villadia misera

Villadia misera, commonly known as the bitterleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both traditional medicine and ecological studies.
