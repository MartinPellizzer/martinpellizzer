Viola chrysantha var. glaberrima

Viola chrysantha var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glaberrima violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive dark center. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a velvety texture. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
