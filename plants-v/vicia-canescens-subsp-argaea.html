Vicia canescens subsp. argaea

Vicia canescens subsp.

argaea, commonly known as the Argentinian vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
