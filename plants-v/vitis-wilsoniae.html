Vitis wilsoniae

Vitis wilsoniae, commonly known as Wilson's grape, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn from green to purple as they mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
