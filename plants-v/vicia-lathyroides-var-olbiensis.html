Vicia lathyroides var. olbiensis

Vicia lathyroides var. olbiensis, commonly known as the Olbiensis vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
