Verbena alternifolia

Verbena alternifolia, commonly known as the alternateleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena alternifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
