Vernonia greggii subsp. schaffneri

Vernonia greggii subsp.

schaffneri, commonly known as Schaffner's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow to orange composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments with minimal water requirements.
