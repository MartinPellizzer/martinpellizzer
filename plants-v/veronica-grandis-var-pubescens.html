Veronica grandis var. pubescens

Veronica grandis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy speedwell, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
