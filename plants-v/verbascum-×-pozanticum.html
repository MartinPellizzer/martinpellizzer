Verbascum × pozanticum

Verbascum × pozanticum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species commonly referred to as mulleins. The plant is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. Its flowers, which typically appear in late summer, are yellow and clustered in elongated spikes.

Verbascum × pozanticum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun.
