Vernonia arnottiana

Vernonia arnottiana, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy purple or pink flower heads that resemble coneflowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in a dense, spiky head at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
