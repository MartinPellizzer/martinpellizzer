Vincetoxicum officinale var. alpicola

Vincetoxicum officinale var. alpicola, commonly known as alpine milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to cold and nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude habitats.
