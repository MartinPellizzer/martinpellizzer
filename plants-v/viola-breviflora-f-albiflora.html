Viola breviflora f. albiflora

Viola breviflora f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered viola, is a compact perennial herb native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with white petals and yellow centers, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
