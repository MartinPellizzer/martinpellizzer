Vernonia henryi

Vernonia henryi, commonly known as Henry's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia henryi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
