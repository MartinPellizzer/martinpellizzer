Viola schulzeana

Viola schulzeana, commonly known as the Schulze's violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the undergrowth.

Viola schulzeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
