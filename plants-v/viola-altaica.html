Viola altaica

Viola altaica, commonly known as the Altai violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Viola altaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its beauty in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
