Vaccinium atrococcum var. longianum

Vaccinium atrococcum var. longianum, commonly known as the dark huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and persist through the winter, providing evergreen cover.

The berries are an important food source for various wildlife species, including birds and bears.
