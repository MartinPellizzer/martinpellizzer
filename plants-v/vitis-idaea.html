Vitis-idaea

Vitis-idaea, commonly known as trailing arbutus, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces clusters of pink or white flowers in early spring, which are often fragrant. Its leaves are evergreen, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Vitis-idaea thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
