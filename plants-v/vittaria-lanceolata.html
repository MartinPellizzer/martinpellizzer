Vittaria lanceolata

Vittaria lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Vittariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and have a finely toothed margin. The plant produces sporangia in clusters on the underside of its fronds, a feature typical of many ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Vittaria lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
