Valeriana procurrens

Valeriana procurrens, commonly known as creeping valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a sprawling growth habit with slender, branching stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically pink or white, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties.

Valeriana procurrens is known for its medicinal uses, particularly for its sedative and calming effects.
