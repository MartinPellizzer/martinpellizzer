Vateria reticulata

Vateria reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers with intricate veining patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface to retain moisture.

Vateria reticulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
