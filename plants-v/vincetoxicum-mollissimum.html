Vincetoxicum mollissimum

Vincetoxicum mollissimum, commonly known as velvet leaf bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, often velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, and it is known for its vigorous growth habit. Vincetoxicum mollissimum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it both a notable ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain environments.
