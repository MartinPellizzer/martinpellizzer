Veronica vestiana

Veronica vestiana, commonly known as the blueweed or slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Veronica vestiana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
