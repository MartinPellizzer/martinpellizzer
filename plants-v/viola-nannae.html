Viola nannae

Viola nannae, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a small flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals with a distinctive violet color.

Viola nannae is a rare species, often found in specific microhabitats, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
