Vriesea rhodostachys

Vriesea rhodostachys, commonly known as the red-flowered vriesea, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, torch-like inflorescence that displays a deep red color, often with contrasting yellow or white bracts. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea rhodostachys is popular as a decorative plant due to its long-lasting blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
