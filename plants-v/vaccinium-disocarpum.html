Vaccinium disocarpum

Vaccinium disocarpum, commonly known as the two-seed cranberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Chile and Argentina. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor similar to other cranberry species. Its leaves are typically elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
