Ventilago malaccensis

Ventilago malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca thornapple, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its thorny branches and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
