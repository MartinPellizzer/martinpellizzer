Virgulus × amethystinus

Virgulus × amethystinus, commonly known as the amethystine orchid, is a hybrid species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking violet and purple flower spikes, which often feature intricate patterns resembling amethyst crystals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers bloom in clusters, producing a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique botanical features.
