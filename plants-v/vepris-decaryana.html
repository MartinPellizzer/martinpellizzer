Vepris decaryana

Vepris decaryana, commonly known as the African pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, round, orange berries that are edible. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils and have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant properties.
