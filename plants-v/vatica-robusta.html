Vatica robusta

Vatica robusta, commonly known as the robust nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing edible seeds. Vatica robusta is valued for its hard timber and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
