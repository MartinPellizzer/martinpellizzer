Veronica austriaca f. cana

Veronica austriaca f. cana, commonly known as the Austrian speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It features basal leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a hairy surface, and produces small blue or purple flowers in racemes. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
