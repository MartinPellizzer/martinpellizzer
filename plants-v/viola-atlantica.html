Viola atlantica

Viola atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in areas with high salt exposure, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple, which bloom in early spring. Viola atlantica is known for its ability to thrive in coastal conditions, making it a hardy species for garden cultivation in similar climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
