Viburnum betulifolium var. flocculosum

Viburnum betulifolium var. flocculosum, commonly known as the woollyleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white, fluffy inflorescences that resemble clouds, which appear in early spring. The plant has oval, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and persistent berries that attract wildlife.
