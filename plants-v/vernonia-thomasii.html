Vernonia thomasii

Vernonia thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are rigid and often branched.

Vernonia thomasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
