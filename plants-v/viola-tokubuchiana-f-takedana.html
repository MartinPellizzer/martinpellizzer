Viola tokubuchiana f. takedana

Viola tokubuchiana f. takedana, commonly known as the Takeda's violet, is a rare and distinctive botanical variant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with dark markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of down, contributing to its unique appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
