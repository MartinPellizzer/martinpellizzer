Vernonia brachiata

Vernonia brachiata, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Vernonia brachiata is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
