Vincetoxicum tenuipedunculatum

Vincetoxicum tenuipedunculatum, commonly known as narrow-stemmed snakeroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its roots contain toxic compounds, which have historically been used in traditional medicine.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil.
