Viola verecunda f. variegata

Viola verecunda f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated violet, is a cultivar of the viola genus characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

This plant typically features leaves with alternating shades of green and white, creating a visually appealing contrast. It produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet, often blooming in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique leaf patterns and vibrant floral display.
