Vernonia pichinchensis

Vernonia pichinchensis, commonly known as the Pichincha Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia pichinchensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
