Vanieria fruticosa

Vanieria fruticosa, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a halophytic shrub that thrives in coastal and estuarine environments.

It is characterized by its dense, knotted root system that forms a network beneath the water, aiding in soil stabilization and providing habitat for marine life. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in saline conditions. It produces yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by small, round fruits that disperse via water.

Vanieria fruticosa plays a crucial role in protecting shorelines from erosion and maintaining the ecological balance of mangrove ecosystems.
