Veronica teucrium subsp. vahlii

Veronica teucrium subsp.

vahlii, commonly known as the Persian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
