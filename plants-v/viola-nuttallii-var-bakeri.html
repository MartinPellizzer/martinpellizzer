Viola nuttallii var. bakeri

Viola nuttallii var. bakeri, commonly known as Baker's violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and thrives in partial shade. It has small, hairy stems and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
