Vernonia villosissima

Vernonia villosissima, commonly known as the woolly ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and prairies. The plant is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia villosissima is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinators.
