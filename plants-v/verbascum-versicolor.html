Verbascum versicolor

Verbascum versicolor, commonly known as the variable mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are soft to the touch. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Verbascum versicolor is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
