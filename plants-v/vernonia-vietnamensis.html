Vernonia vietnamensis

Vernonia vietnamensis, commonly known as Vietnamese ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

Vernonia vietnamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
