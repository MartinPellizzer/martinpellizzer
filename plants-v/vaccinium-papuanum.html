Vaccinium papuanum

Vaccinium papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the highland regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that are typically blue or purple in color when mature. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Vaccinium papuanum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, moist climates.
