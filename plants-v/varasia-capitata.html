Varasia capitata

Varasia capitata, commonly known as the clustered varasia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
