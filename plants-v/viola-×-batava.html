Viola × batava

Viola × batava, commonly known as the Dutch violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be found in various horticultural varieties.

The flowers bloom in early spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
