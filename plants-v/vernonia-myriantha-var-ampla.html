Vernonia myriantha var. ampla

Vernonia myriantha var. ampla, commonly known as the broad-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, with lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its inflorescence is a dense, cylindrical spike that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
