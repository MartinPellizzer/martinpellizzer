Vernonia scapiformis

Vernonia scapiformis, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Vernonia scapiformis typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
