Veronica scutellata var. velutina

Veronica scutellata var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet veronica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
