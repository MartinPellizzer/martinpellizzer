Viburnum lancifolium

Viburnum lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum lancifolium is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It also provides food and habitat for wildlife, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
