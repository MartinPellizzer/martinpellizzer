Vernonia pachyclada var. pachyclada

Vernonia pachyclada var. pachyclada, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its stems are typically woody and branched, with a rough texture.

Vernonia pachyclada var. pachyclada is often found in moist to wet soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
