Veronica besseya

Veronica besseya, commonly known as the Sierra veronica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Veronica besseya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
