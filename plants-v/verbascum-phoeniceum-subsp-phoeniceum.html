Verbascum phoeniceum subsp. phoeniceum

Verbascum phoeniceum subsp.

phoeniceum, commonly known as the Phoenix mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its common name reflects its striking appearance and its historical use in traditional medicine.
