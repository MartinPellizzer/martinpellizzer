Vicia americana var. oregana

Vicia americana var. oregana, commonly known as the Oregon vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and riparian habitats.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
