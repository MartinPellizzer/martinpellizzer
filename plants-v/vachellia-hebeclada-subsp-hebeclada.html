Vachellia hebeclada subsp. hebeclada

Vachellia hebeclada subsp.

hebeclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and glandular.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are followed by long, slender pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
