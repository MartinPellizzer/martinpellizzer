Viola modestula

Viola modestula, commonly known as the common pansy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-20 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola modestula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
