Valeriana polybotrya

Valeriana polybotrya, commonly known as the African valerian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Valeriana polybotrya is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and sedative properties.
