Vitis austrokoreana

Vitis austrokoreana, commonly known as the Korean foxglove vine, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family, which includes grapes and other climbing plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. It typically grows as a vine, using its tendrils to climb supports in its natural habitat.

Vitis austrokoreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
