Vernonia filifolia

Vernonia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its slender, thread-like leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads make it a distinctive ornamental plant.

Vernonia filifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
