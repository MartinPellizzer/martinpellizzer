Verbena pulchra

Verbena pulchra, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with slender stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

Verbena pulchra thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
