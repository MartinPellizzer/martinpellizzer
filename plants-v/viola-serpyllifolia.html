Viola serpyllifolia

Viola serpyllifolia, commonly known as creeping violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine white down. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from violet to white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in grassy areas or along woodland edges.

Its foliage and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.
