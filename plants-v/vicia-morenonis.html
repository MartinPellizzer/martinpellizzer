Vicia morenonis

Vicia morenonis, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Vicia morenonis is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open woodland areas.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
