Vigna grahamiana

Vigna grahamiana, commonly known as the Graham's bean, is a species of legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically green and serrated. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines. Vigna grahamiana thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
