Verbascum × commutatum

Verbascum × commutatum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their fuzzy, woolly leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The hybrid nature of Verbascum × commutatum results from crossbreeding between different Verbascum species, leading to a variety of morphological characteristics. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
