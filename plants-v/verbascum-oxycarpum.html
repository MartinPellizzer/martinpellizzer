Verbascum oxycarpum

Verbascum oxycarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a raceme. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Verbascum oxycarpum is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat respiratory ailments.
