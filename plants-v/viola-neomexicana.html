Viola neomexicana

Viola neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy violet flowers with five petals. It is part of the Violaceae family, which includes other violets and related species.

Viola neomexicana is known for its adaptability to arid environments and is often found in elevations ranging from 1,500 to 7,000 feet.
