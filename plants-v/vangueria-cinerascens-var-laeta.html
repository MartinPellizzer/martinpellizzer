Vangueria cinerascens var. laeta

Vangueria cinerascens var. laeta, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom during the warmer months. Its botanical characteristics include a preference for well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
