Viola replicata

Viola replicata, commonly known as the violet orchid or the violet violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with five petals arranged symmetrically. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Viola replicata is valued for both its aesthetic qualities and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
