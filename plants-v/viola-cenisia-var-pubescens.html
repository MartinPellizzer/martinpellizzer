Viola cenisia var. pubescens

Viola cenisia var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically in early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
