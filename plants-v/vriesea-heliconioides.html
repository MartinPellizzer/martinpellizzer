Vriesea heliconioides

Vriesea heliconioides, commonly known as the rainbow bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that collect water, forming a central pool. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with vibrant, long-lasting flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are typically broad, glossy, and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Vriesea heliconioides thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
