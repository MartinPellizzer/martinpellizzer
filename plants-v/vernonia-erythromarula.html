Vernonia erythromarula

Vernonia erythromarula, commonly known as the red-veined vernonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, with prominent red veins. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Vernonia erythromarula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
