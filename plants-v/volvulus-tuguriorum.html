Volvulus tuguriorum

Volvulus tuguriorum, commonly known as the spiral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its helical twisting stems that coil around supports, giving it a unique growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Volvulus tuguriorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
