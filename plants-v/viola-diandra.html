Viola diandra

Viola diandra, commonly known as the violet of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Viola diandra is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its unique appearance and fragrance.
