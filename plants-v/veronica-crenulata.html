Veronica crenulata

Veronica crenulata, commonly known as the scalycup veronica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow, serrated, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica crenulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
