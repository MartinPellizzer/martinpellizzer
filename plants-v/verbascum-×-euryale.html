Verbascum × euryale

Verbascum × euryale, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their large, velvety leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The hybrid is characterized by its robust stems and fuzzy, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
