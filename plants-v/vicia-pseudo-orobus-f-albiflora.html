Vicia pseudo-orobus f. albiflora

Vicia pseudo-orobus f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that are predominantly white in color. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods contain several seeds. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
