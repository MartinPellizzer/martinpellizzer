Vernonia laurifolia

Vernonia laurifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense, conical clusters. Vernonia laurifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
