Vaccinium pulchellum

Vaccinium pulchellum, commonly known as the showy blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. Its fruits are edible, small berries that are typically blue or purple in color, and are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
