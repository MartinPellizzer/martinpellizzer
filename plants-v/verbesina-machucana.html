Verbesina machucana

Verbesina machucana, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Verbesina machucana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
