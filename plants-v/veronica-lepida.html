Veronica lepida

Veronica lepida, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, silver-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers in clustered sprays, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a soft, woolly coating.

Veronica lepida thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
