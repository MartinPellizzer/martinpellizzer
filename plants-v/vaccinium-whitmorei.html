Vaccinium whitmorei

Vaccinium whitmorei, commonly known as the Malayan huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
