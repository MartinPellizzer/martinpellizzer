Vicia glareosa

Vicia glareosa, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are yellow and pea-like, arranged in spikes, and it produces small, hairy pods containing several seeds.

Vicia glareosa is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
