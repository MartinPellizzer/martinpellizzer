Veronica saturejoides

Veronica saturejoides, commonly known as the blueweed or narrow-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Veronica saturejoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
