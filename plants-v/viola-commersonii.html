Viola commersonii

Viola commersonii, commonly known as the Commerson's violet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Viola commersonii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
