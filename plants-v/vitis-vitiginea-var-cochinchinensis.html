Vitis vitiginea var. cochinchinensis

Vitis vitiginea var. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese grapevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries, which are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
