Veronicastrum villosulum var. hirsutum

Veronicastrum villosulum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue flowers. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This species is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
