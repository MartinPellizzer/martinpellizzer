Valeriana dioscoridis

Valeriana dioscoridis, commonly known as Dioscorides' valerian, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana dioscoridis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
