Viola nephrophylla f. bicolor

Viola nephrophylla f. bicolor, commonly known as the twin-color violet, is a striking perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, bi-colored flowers that range from purple to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
