Viola grayi var. glabra

Viola grayi var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth viola, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, smooth-leaved foliage. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
