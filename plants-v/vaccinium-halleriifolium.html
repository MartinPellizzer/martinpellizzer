Vaccinium halleriifolium

Vaccinium halleriifolium, commonly known as the Haller's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a small blue-black berry, is edible and has a tart, acidic flavor.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.
