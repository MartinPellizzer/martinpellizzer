Viola abyssinica var. ulugurensis

Viola abyssinica var. ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru violet, is a flowering plant native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It is a perennial herb characterized by heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It has small, lanceolate leaves and produces showy blooms during the rainy season.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
