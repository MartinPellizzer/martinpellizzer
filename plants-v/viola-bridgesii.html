Viola bridgesii

Viola bridgesii, commonly known as the Bridgess' violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a deep green color and produces striking violet to purple flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters and blooms in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and butterflies, making it an important species in local ecosystems.
