Viola palustris proles juressi

Viola palustris proles juressi, commonly known as the marsh violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in certain regions.
