Vaccinium formosum

Vaccinium formosum, commonly known as the Taiwan huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
