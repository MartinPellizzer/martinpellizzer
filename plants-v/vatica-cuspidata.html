Vatica cuspidata

Vatica cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and elliptical leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

Vatica cuspidata is often found in lowland evergreen forests and is valued for its timber and ecological role.
