Veronica simensis

Veronica simensis, commonly known as the Eastern Veronica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Veronica simensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
