Veronica ovata subsp. maritima

Veronica ovata subsp.

maritima, commonly known as sea veronica, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and western Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces blue to purple flowers in clusters.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in salty, windy environments. It is often found growing on rocky shores and coastal dunes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
