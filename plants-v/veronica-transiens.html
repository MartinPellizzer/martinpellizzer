Veronica transiens

Veronica transiens, commonly known as the wandering jew, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Veronica transiens is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.
