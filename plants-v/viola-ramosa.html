Viola ramosa

Viola ramosa, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola ramosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
