Vicia ochroleuca proles dinara

Vicia ochroleuca proles dinara, commonly known as the Dinara yellow vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green trifoliate leaves. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, often found in the Balkan region.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
