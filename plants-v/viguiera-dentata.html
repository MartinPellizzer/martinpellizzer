Viguiera dentata

Viguiera dentata, commonly known as the Mexican coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers range in color from pale yellow to orange. Viguiera dentata thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
