Verbascum tenuicaule

Verbascum tenuicaule, commonly known as narrowleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, upright spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
