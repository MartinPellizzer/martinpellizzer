Veronica serpyllifolia var. nummulariifolia

Veronica serpyllifolia var. nummulariifolia, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features dense clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant's leaves are typically rounded and silver-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
