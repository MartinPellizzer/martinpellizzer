Vaccinium bigibbum

Vaccinium bigibbum, commonly known as the two-fruited blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Vaccinium bigibbum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal or subarctic climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and contributing to forest undergrowth diversity.
