Viola yedoensis f. pallescens

Viola yedoensis f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale viola, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, pale violet to white flowers with yellow markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
