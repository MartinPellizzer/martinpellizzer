Vigna decipiens

Vigna decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its seeds are typically enclosed in hairy, elongated pods. Vigna decipiens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in certain agricultural contexts.
