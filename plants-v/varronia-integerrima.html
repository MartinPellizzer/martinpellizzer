Varronia integerrima

Varronia integerrima, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dark, smooth bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits.

Varronia integerrima is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.
