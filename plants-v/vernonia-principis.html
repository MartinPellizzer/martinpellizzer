Vernonia principis

Vernonia principis, commonly known as the royal ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its sturdy stems and showy inflorescences make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Vernonia principis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
