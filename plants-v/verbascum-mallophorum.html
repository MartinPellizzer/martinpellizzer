Verbascum mallophorum

Verbascum mallophorum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, lobed leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, upright spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum mallophorum is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
