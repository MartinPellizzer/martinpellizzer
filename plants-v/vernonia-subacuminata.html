Vernonia subacuminata

Vernonia subacuminata, commonly known as the narrow-pointed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The botanical features of Vernonia subacuminata make it a distinctive member of the sunflower family, Asteraceae.
