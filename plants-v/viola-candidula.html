Viola candidula

Viola candidula, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety hair. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with five white petals and a distinctive yellowish-green throat. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Viola candidula is often found in calcareous soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
