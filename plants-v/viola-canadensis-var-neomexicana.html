Viola canadensis var. neomexicana

Viola canadensis var. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in the wild along the edges of forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
