Viola phalacrocarpa var. glaberrima

Viola phalacrocarpa var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smoothleaf viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and damp meadows.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
