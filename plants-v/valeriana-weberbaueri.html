Valeriana weberbaueri

Valeriana weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool and moist conditions. The plant features a fibrous root system and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often pink or white in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, aromatic scent, which is characteristic of the Valeriana genus.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
