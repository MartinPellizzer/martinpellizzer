Viminaria

Viminaria, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species, primarily native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plants are characterized by their woody stems, trifoliate leaves, and pods that contain edible seeds. Viminaria species are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in some cultures.

They play an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil.
