Vernonia flexuosa var. microcephala

Vernonia flexuosa var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flower heads arranged in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a rough texture.

This species is typically found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and woodland edges.
