Vernonia chromolepis

Vernonia chromolepis, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most striking feature is its bright yellow flower heads, which consist of numerous small, tubular florets surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

Vernonia chromolepis is typically found in moist, open habitats such as swamps and woodland edges.
