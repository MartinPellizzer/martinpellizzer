Vitis aralioides

Vitis aralioides, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Vitis aralioides thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or trees.
