Vigna lasiocarpa

Vigna lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy vigna, is a species of legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is often grown as a forage crop due to its nutritional value for livestock.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
