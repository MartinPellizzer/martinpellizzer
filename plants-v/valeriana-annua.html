Valeriana annua

Valeriana annua, commonly known as annual valerian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Valeriana annua is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
