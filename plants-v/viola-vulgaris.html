Viola vulgaris

Viola vulgaris, commonly known as the common violet, is a flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from purple to blue. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa, and it thrives in woodland and grassland habitats. Viola vulgaris is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can grow in partial to full shade.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
