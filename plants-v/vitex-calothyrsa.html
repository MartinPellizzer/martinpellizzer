Vitex calothyrsa

Vitex calothyrsa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers in the summer, which are followed by distinctive, woody, elongated fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Vitex calothyrsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
