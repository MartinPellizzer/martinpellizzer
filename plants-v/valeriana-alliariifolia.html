Valeriana alliariifolia

Valeriana alliariifolia, commonly known as wild chervil, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Valeriana alliariifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
