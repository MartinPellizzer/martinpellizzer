Viola dactyloides var. calcicola

Viola dactyloides var. calcicola, commonly known as the desert pansy, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is notable for its ability to survive in harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
