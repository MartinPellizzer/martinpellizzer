Vitex trifolia var. variegata

Vitex trifolia var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings, adding visual interest to the plant. The plant produces small, fragrant, purple flowers in panicles during the summer months. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.

The fruits are small, woody capsules that mature in the fall.
