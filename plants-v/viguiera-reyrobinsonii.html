Viguiera reyrobinsonii

Viguiera reyrobinsonii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers in the summer months. It is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils.

Viguiera reyrobinsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
