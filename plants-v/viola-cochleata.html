Viola cochleata

Viola cochleata, commonly known as the spiral violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spiraled petals that form a helical shape. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom in early spring.

Viola cochleata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
