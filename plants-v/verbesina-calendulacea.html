Verbesina calendulacea

Verbesina calendulacea, commonly known as the golden beggar's-ticks, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its common name reflects its prickly, seed-bearing structures that cling to clothing and animals.
