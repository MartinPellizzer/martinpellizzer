Vernonia jodopappa

Vernonia jodopappa, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow to orange-red flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique climate and soil conditions of Hawaii. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody and branching.

Vernonia jodopappa plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for native pollinators.
