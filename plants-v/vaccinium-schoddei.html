Vaccinium schoddei

Vaccinium schoddei, commonly known as the Schodde's blueberry, is a species of dwarf shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The plant's fruit, a small, dark blue berry, is edible and has a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Vaccinium schoddei plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various birds and small mammals.
