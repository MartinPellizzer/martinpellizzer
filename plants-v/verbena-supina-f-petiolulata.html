Verbena supina f. petiolulata

Verbena supina f. petiolulata, commonly known as the trailing verbena, is a flowering plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is native to regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are typically covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and hanging baskets.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
