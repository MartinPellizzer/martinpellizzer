Vaccinium jevidalianum

Vaccinium jevidalianum, commonly known as the Jevidalian blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that are typically blue or purple in color, containing a single seed. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
