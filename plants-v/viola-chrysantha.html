Viola chrysantha

Viola chrysantha, commonly known as the golden viola, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive orange throat, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Viola chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
