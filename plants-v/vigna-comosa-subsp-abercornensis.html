Vigna comosa subsp. abercornensis

Vigna comosa subsp.

abercornensis, commonly known as the Abercorn cowpea, is a herbaceous annual legume native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pale green trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its drought tolerance and nutritional content.
