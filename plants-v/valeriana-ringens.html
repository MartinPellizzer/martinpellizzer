Valeriana ringens

Valeriana ringens, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana ringens thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
