Veronica peregrina subsp. asiatica

Veronica peregrina subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as the Asian speedwell, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit with opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
