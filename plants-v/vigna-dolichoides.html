Vigna dolichoides

Vigna dolichoides, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods. The plant is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Vigna dolichoides also plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
