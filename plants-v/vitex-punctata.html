Vitex punctata

Vitex punctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually narrow and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in panicles. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Vitex punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
