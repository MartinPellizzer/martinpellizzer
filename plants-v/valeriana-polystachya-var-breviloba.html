Valeriana polystachya var. breviloba

Valeriana polystachya var. breviloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and erect, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with fine hairs.

This species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
