Vaccinium sororium

Vaccinium sororium, commonly known as the common blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or red in color, depending on the variety. Vaccinium sororium thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
