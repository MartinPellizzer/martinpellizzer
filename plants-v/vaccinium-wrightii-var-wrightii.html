Vaccinium wrightii var. wrightii

Vaccinium wrightii var. wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's blueberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark blue berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and pine flatwoods.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
