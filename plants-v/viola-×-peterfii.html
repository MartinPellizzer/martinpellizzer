Viola × peterfii

Viola × peterfii, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom from early spring to late summer.
