Vernonia maxima

Vernonia maxima, commonly known as the giant ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Vernonia maxima is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and resilience.
