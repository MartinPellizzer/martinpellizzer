Vulpia tenuicula

Vulpia tenuicula, commonly known as slender vulpia, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in open fields. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Vulpia tenuicula is an annual plant that produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.
