Veronica spicata var. bulgarica

Veronica spicata var. bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarica speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear dense clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, giving the plant a distinctive texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its delicate blooms.
