Vernonia morilloi

Vernonia morilloi, commonly known as Morillo's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Vernonia morilloi is known for its hardy nature and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
