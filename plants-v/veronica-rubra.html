Veronica rubra

Veronica rubra, commonly known as red veronica, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its clusters of small, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Veronica rubra is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
