Vitis repandospinulosa

Vitis repandospinulosa, commonly known as the spinulose grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of spinose tendrils, which aid in its attachment to supports. The leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and greenish-yellow in color. Its berries are usually small and can vary in color from green to black depending on maturity.

This species is native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan, and is often found in woodland and shrubland habitats.
