Valeriana obovata

Valeriana obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate valerian, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are ovate to broadly ovate, with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in summer. Its roots are aromatic and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Valeriana obovata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
