Viola pseudomunbyana

Viola pseudomunbyana, commonly known as the New Zealand violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in spring, producing a range of colors from pale purple to deep violet.

Viola pseudomunbyana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
