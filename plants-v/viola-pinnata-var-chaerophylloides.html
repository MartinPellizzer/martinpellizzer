Viola pinnata var. chaerophylloides

Viola pinnata var. chaerophylloides, commonly known as the purple viola, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and produces clusters of violet flowers with yellow centers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
