Verbesina hypomalaca var. hypochlora

Verbesina hypomalaca var. hypochlora, commonly known as the paleleaf beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically pale green in color. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

The species is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.
