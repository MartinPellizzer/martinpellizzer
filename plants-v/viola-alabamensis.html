Viola alabamensis

Viola alabamensis, commonly known as the Alabama violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola alabamensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
