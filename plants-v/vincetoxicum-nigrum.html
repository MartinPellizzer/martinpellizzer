Vincetoxicum nigrum

Vincetoxicum nigrum, commonly known as black nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has palmate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
