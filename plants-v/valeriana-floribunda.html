Valeriana floribunda

Valeriana floribunda, commonly known as the clustered valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often purple or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and often have a strong, spicy scent.

Valeriana floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.
