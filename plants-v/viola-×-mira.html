Viola × mira

Viola × mira, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers well-drained soil in partial shade. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value.

Viola × mira is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
