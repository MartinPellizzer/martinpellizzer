Viburnum prunifolium var. globosum

Viburnum prunifolium var. globosum, commonly known as the globesweet viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green above and lighter beneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its compact growth habit and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and supporting local wildlife.
