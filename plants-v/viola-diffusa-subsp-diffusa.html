Viola diffusa subsp. diffusa

Viola diffusa subsp.

diffusa, commonly known as the common violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its showy blooms and aromatic foliage. Viola diffusa subsp.

diffusa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
