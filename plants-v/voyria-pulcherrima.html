Voyria pulcherrima

Voyria pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange, often with contrasting yellow or white markings. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its upright growth habit and long-lasting blooms. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green.

Voyria pulcherrima is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
