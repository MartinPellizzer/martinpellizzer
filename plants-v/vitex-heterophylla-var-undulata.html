Vitex heterophylla var. undulata

Vitex heterophylla var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf chaste tree, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with undulating leaf edges that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of violet or blue. Its leaves are often alternate and can vary in shape from ovate to lanceolate.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
