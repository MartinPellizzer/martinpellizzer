Vernonia amygdalina

Vernonia amygdalina, commonly known as bitter leaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that have a distinctive bitter taste. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a conical cluster, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Vernonia amygdalina is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional African medicine to treat various ailments.
