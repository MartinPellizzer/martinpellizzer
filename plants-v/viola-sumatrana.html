Viola sumatrana

Viola sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola sumatrana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential ecological and medicinal significance.
