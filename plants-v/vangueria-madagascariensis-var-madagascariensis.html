Vangueria madagascariensis var. madagascariensis

Vangueria madagascariensis var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar vangueria, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, characterized by its woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the dry season. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system to access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
