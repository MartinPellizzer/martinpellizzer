Vitis jaegeriana

Vitis jaegeriana, commonly known as the Jaeger's grape, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and shrubs. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

The small, dark blue berries are edible but are not widely cultivated due to the plant's limited distribution and challenging growing conditions.
