Veronica urvilleana

Veronica urvilleana, commonly known as the sand veronica, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or mountainous areas. The plant features slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Veronica urvilleana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
