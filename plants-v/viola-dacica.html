Viola dacica

Viola dacica, commonly known as the Dacian violet, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its flowers bloom in spring, producing a range of colors from pale purple to deep violet.

Viola dacica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
