Viola tricolor var. orophila

Viola tricolor var. orophila, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers display a striking combination of purple, yellow, and white petals, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant.

Viola tricolor var. orophila is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness in cooler climates.
