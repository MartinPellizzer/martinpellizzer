Vaccinium sednense

Vaccinium sednense, commonly known as the Seward huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the Aleutian Islands of Alaska.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
