Viola palmensis

Viola palmensis, commonly known as the palmleaf violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which resemble the shape of a palm tree's fronds. Its flowers are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals arranged symmetrically.

Viola palmensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments.
