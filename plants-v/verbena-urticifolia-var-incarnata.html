Verbena urticifolia var. incarnata

Verbena urticifolia var. incarnata, commonly known as red verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.

Its botanical features include showy inflorescences and a compact growth habit, contributing to its ornamental value.
