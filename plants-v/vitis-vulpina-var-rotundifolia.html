Vitis vulpina var. rotundifolia

Vitis vulpina var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf muscadine grape, is a cultivated variety of the muscadine grape native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, round, thick-skinned berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is a vigorous vine with compound leaves that have three to five leaflets, often with a waxy texture. It produces clusters of small, sweet berries that are prized for their flavor and used in jams, wines, and other culinary applications.

The variety is well adapted to warm climates and requires a long growing season to mature its fruit.
