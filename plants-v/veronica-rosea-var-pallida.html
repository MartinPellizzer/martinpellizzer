Veronica rosea var. pallida

Veronica rosea var. pallida, commonly known as the pale veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to almost white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early summer blooms.
