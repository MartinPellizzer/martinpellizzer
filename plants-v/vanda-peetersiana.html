Vanda peetersiana

Vanda peetersiana, commonly known as Peeters' vanda, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a contrasting lip. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

Vanda peetersiana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
