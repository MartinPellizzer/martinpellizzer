Verticordia vicinella

Verticordia vicinella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and a compact, shrub-like growth habit. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathland and coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
