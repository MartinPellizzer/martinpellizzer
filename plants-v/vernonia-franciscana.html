Vernonia franciscana

Vernonia franciscana, commonly known as the Franciscan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, dark purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is hairy and often reddish in color.

Vernonia franciscana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
