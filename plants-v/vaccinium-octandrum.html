Vaccinium octandrum

Vaccinium octandrum, commonly known as the eight-stamen huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries, which are typically red or black when mature, are edible and often used in local cuisine.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
