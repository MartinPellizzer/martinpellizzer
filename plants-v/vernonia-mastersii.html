Vernonia mastersii

Vernonia mastersii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. It is often found in the understory of forests and can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

Vernonia mastersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
