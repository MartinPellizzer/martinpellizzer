Viola philippiana

Viola philippiana, commonly known as the Philippine violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers come in various colors, including purple, violet, and white, and bloom from spring to early summer.

Viola philippiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
