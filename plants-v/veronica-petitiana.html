Veronica petitiana

Veronica petitiana, commonly known as the small blue speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Veronica petitiana is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these specialized environments.
