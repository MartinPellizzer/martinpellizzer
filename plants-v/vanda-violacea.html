Vanda violacea

Vanda violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that display a range of violet and purple hues. The plant has strap-like, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Vanda violacea is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the year.
