Viola tricolor subsp. curtisii

Viola tricolor subsp.

curtisii, commonly known as the violet wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a fibrous root system and spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
