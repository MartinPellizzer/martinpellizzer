Vernonia thyrsoidea

Vernonia thyrsoidea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Vernonia thyrsoidea is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
