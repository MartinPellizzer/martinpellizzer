Verbena subpetiolata

Verbena subpetiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant blue to purple flowers in spikes. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
