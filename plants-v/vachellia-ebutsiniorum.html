Vachellia ebutsiniorum

Vachellia ebutsiniorum, commonly known as the Eburu tree, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, which benefits surrounding vegetation.

Additionally, it is used in traditional medicine and for fuelwood in some regions.
