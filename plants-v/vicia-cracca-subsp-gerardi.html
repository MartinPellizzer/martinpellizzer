Vicia cracca subsp. gerardi

Vicia cracca subsp.

gerardi, commonly known as the red clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop for livestock.
