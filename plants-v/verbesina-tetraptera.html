Verbesina tetraptera

Verbesina tetraptera, commonly known as the four-winged beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant's distinctive feature is its four-winged seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.
