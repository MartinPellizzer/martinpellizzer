Vriesea andaraiensis

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its striking, spathaceous inflorescence that displays vibrant colors, often in shades of red and orange. The plant features a rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes, giving them a fuzzy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Vriesea andaraiensis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated as a decorative plant in many botanical gardens and private collections.
