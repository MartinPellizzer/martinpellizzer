Vicia macraei

Vicia macraei, commonly known as the Macrae's vetch, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Vicia macraei is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
