Vaccinium timorense

Vaccinium timorense, commonly known as the Timor huckleberry, is a shrub native to the island of Timor in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue or black in color. These berries are often used in local cuisines and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

Vaccinium timorense thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
