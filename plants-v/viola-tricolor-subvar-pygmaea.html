Viola tricolor subvar. pygmaea

Viola tricolor subvar. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
