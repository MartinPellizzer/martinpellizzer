Villarsia humboldtiana

Villarsia humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's villarsia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests and rocky areas. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and usually have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions and its limited distribution.
