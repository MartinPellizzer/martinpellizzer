Vitis heyneana

Vitis heyneana, commonly known as the Guyana grape or Amazonian grape, is a species of flowering vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and climbing nature. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy surface, while the fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Vitis heyneana is often found in moist, tropical environments and is adapted to thrive in dense forest understories.
