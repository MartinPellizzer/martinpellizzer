Vanilla anomala

Vanilla anomala, commonly known as the mountain vanilla, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, slender pods. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant pods that are used in both culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires a trellis or support for its vine-like growth.

The botanical features of Vanilla anomala make it a unique and valuable species in the vanilla family.
