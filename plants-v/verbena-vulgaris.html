Verbena vulgaris

Verbena vulgaris, commonly known as common vervain, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and wildflower meadows.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
