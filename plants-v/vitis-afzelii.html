Vitis afzelii

Vitis afzelii, commonly known as the wild grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to North America.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible berries that range in color from green to black. Vitis afzelii is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in woodland edges, stream banks, and disturbed areas across the eastern United States.
