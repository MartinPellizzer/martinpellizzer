Vernoniastrum nestor

Vernoniastrum nestor, commonly known as the Mexican coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, grayish hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often hairy. Its showy, yellow to orange flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernoniastrum nestor is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
