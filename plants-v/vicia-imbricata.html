Vicia imbricata

Vicia imbricata, commonly known as the common vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Vicia imbricata is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry grasslands.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.
