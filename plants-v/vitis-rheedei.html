Vitis rheedei

Vitis rheedei, commonly known as the Rhede's grape, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Vitis rheedei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its unique foliage and fruit.
