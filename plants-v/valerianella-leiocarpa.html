Valerianella leiocarpa

Valerianella leiocarpa, commonly known as the white valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Valerianella leiocarpa is often found in temperate regions and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
