Veronica fontinalis

Veronica fontinalis, commonly known as water speedwell, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and woodland floors. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring, often accompanied by narrow, grass-like leaves. Veronica fontinalis is characterized by its delicate appearance and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.

It is often found in calcareous soils and is a common sight in mountainous and coastal areas.
