Valeriana sanguisorbae

Valeriana sanguisorbae, commonly known as bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, reddish-brown leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears a cluster of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is characterized by its striking, blood-red sap.
