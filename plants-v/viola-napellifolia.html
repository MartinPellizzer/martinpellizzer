Viola napellifolia

Viola napellifolia, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, velvety green leaves with a distinctive violet or purple bloom that appears in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental species. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, often found in woodland settings.

Its flowers are known for their delicate appearance and are often cultivated for their aesthetic value in gardens.
