Verbascum sulcatum

Verbascum sulcatum, commonly known as the wrinkled mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply wrinkled. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Verbascum sulcatum is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
