Viola praemorsa var. praemorsa

Viola praemorsa var. praemorsa, commonly known as the death violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces striking violet-blue flowers with dark veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in clusters.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
