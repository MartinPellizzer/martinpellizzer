Verbascum thapsus var. pseudothapsiforme

Verbascum thapsus var. pseudothapsiforme, commonly known as the hybrid mullein.

This variant is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum thapsus and Verbascum blattaria. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer.

It is native to southern Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
