Viola polymorpha

Viola polymorpha, commonly known as the common violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet hue and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and blue. Viola polymorpha is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
