Vitex orinocensis

Vitex orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Vitex orinocensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
