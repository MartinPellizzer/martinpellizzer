Vernonia hypoleuca

Vernonia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its showy, pale yellow to white flower heads bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia hypoleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
