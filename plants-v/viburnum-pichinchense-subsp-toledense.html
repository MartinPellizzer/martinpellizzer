Viburnum pichinchense subsp. toledense

Viburnum pichinchense subsp.

toledense, commonly known as the Toledan honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America. It typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in humid and cool climates.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and range in color from white to pink.

The berries, which are usually red or black, are edible and attract various bird species.
