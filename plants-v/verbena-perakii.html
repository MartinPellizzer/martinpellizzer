Verbena perakii

Verbena perakii, commonly known as the Perak verbena, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, specifically Malaysia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Verbena perakii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
