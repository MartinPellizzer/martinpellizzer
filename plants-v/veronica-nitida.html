Veronica nitida

Veronica nitida, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows, rocky areas, and along pathways.

Veronica nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
