Verbascum leptophyllum

Verbascum leptophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves and tall spikes of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Verbascum leptophyllum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory conditions.
