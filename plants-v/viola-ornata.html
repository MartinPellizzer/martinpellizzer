Viola ornata

Viola ornata, commonly known as the showy viola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. Viola ornata is often found in woodlands and rocky slopes, where it prefers partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
