Vaccinium minuticalcaratum f. glabrum

Vaccinium minuticalcaratum f. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved trailing blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous, distinguishing it from other forms of the species. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and moist, acidic soils.
