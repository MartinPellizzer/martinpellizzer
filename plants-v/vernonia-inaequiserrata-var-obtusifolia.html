Vernonia inaequiserrata var. obtusifolia

Vernonia inaequiserrata var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with rounded tips. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to attract pollinators.
