Veronica elongata

Veronica elongata, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

Veronica elongata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
