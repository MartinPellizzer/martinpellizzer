Viola sikkimensis var. acuminatifolia

Viola sikkimensis var. acuminatifolia, commonly known as the Sikkim violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of purple and violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
