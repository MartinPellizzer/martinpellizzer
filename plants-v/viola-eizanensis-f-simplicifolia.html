Viola eizanensis f. simplicifolia

Viola eizanensis f. simplicifolia, commonly known as the Eizan violet, is a rare and distinctive variant of the viola genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its simple, heart-shaped leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet or blue. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in shaded forest floors or along stream banks. Its foliage is notable for its smooth, waxy texture and vibrant green color.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
