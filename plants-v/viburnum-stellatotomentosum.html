Viburnum stellatotomentosum

Viburnum stellatotomentosum, commonly known as the starry-tomentose viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
