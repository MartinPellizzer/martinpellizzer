Vicia caroliniana var. texana

Vicia caroliniana var. texana, commonly known as Texas vetch, is a perennial legume native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and prairies. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
