Vicia nummularia var. macrophylla

Vicia nummularia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It is typically found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
