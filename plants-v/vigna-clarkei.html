Vigna clarkei

Vigna clarkei, commonly known as Clarke's pigeon pea, is a tropical legume native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a climbing or shrub-like plant with trifoliate leaves and produces pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility. Vigna clarkei thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in regions with tropical and subtropical conditions.
