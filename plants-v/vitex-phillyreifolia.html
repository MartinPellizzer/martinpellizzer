Vitex phillyreifolia

Vitex phillyreifolia, commonly known as the sweetwood tree, is a species native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
