Verbascum mykales

Verbascum mykales, commonly known as the Greek mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in soft, velvety hairs.

Verbascum mykales is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.
