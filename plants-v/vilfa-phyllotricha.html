Vilfa phyllotricha

Vilfa phyllotricha, commonly known as the hairy leaf violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces upright stems that bear small, violet-colored flowers with five petals. Its leaves and stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Vilfa phyllotricha is adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
