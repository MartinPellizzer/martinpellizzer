Viviania rosea var. ovata

Viviania rosea var. ovata, commonly known as the pink sandalwood tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its bark is smooth and can be harvested for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.
