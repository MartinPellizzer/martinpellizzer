Verbesina walteri

Verbesina walteri, commonly known as Walter's beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its stems are hairy and often have a reddish tinge, while its leaves are typically covered with fine, white hairs.

Walter's beggar-ticks is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open woods, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
