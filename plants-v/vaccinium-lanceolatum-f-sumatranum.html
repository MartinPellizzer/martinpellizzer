Vaccinium lanceolatum f. sumatranum

Vaccinium lanceolatum f. sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran huckleberry, is a botanical variant of the Vaccinium genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, dark blue berries that are typically edible. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife.

This form is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential ornamental value.
