Verbascum atroviolaceum

Verbascum atroviolaceum, commonly known as the dark violet mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Verbascum atroviolaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
