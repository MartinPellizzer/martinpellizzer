Vaseyochloa multinervosa

Vaseyochloa multinervosa, commonly known as the multivenined grass, is a species of grass native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with distinct parallel veins. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along riverbanks. Its flowering structures consist of spikelets that bear small, two-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Vaseyochloa multinervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
