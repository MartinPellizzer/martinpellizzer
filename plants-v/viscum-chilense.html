Viscum chilense

Viscum chilense, commonly known as the Chilean mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its berries are usually red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Viscum chilense plays an important ecological role by providing food for birds and other wildlife.
