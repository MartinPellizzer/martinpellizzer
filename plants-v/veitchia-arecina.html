Veitchia arecina

Veitchia arecina, commonly known as the coconut palm, is a tall, slender tree native to the tropical regions of the Pacific and Indian Oceans.

It is characterized by its pinnate leaves, which can grow up to 5 meters in length, and its large, clustered inflorescences that produce both male and female flowers. The plant produces oval, fibrous fruits that contain a single seed, which is used to propagate new plants. Its extensive root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it ecologically significant in coastal areas.

The coconut palm is also valued for its economic contributions, including the production of coconuts, palm oil, and other by-products.
