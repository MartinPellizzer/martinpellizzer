Verbascum reiseri

Verbascum reiseri, commonly known as the Reiser's mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy, woolly leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy yellow flowers. It is a perennial herb that produces copious amounts of nectar, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
