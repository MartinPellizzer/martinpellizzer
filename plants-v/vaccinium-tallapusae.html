Vaccinium tallapusae

Vaccinium tallapusae, commonly known as the Tallapusae blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are evergreen, with a leathery texture and a dark green color.

Vaccinium tallapusae thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.
