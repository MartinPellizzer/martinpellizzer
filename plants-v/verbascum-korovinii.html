Verbascum korovinii

Verbascum korovinii, commonly known as the Korovin's mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in soft hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose raceme. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Verbascum korovinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
