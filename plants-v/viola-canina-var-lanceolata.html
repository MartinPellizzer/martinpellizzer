Viola canina var. lanceolata

Viola canina var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate dog violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and showy blooms.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
