Vicia caroliniana

Vicia caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina vetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, trailing stems that produce small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

The seeds are typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in various habitats.
