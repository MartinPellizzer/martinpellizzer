Verbascum drymophyloides

Verbascum drymophyloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a cylindrical spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum drymophyloides is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
