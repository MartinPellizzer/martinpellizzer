Vitis prunisapida

Vitis prunisapida, commonly known as the Chinese dragon vine, is a climbing plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and distinctive foliage. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and greenish-white. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly cultivated for fruit.

It is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
