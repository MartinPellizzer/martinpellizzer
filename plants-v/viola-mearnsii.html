Viola mearnsii

Viola mearnsii, commonly known as the Mexican viola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Viola mearnsii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
