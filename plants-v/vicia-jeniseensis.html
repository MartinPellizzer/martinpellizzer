Vicia jeniseensis

Vicia jeniseensis, commonly known as the Jenisean vetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Vicia jeniseensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
