Viola ericetorum

Viola ericetorum, commonly known as the common violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy violet or purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade.

Viola ericetorum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
