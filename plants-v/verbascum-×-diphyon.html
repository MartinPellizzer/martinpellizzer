Verbascum × diphyon

Verbascum × diphyon, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
