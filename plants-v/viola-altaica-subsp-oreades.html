Viola altaica subsp. oreades

Viola altaica subsp.

oreades, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its flowers are typically borne on slender stems and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Viola altaica subsp.

oreades is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
