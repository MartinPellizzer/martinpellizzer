Vitis pullaria

Vitis pullaria, commonly known as the Japanese grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Vitis pullaria thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural research.
