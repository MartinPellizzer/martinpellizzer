Vateria cordifolia

Vateria cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf vateria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens for its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
