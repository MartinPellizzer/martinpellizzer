Vigna aristata

Vigna aristata, commonly known as the mountain pea, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, and the pods are slender with hairy textures. The plant produces small, round seeds that are often used in local cuisines.

Vigna aristata is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various soil conditions.
