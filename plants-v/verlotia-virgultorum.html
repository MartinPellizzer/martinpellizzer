Verlotia virgultorum

Verlotia virgultorum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture and a distinct reddish hue when young.

Verlotia virgultorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also recognized for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
