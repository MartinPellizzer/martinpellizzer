Vicia garinensis

Vicia garinensis, commonly known as Garin's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to alpine climates. Vicia garinensis is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
