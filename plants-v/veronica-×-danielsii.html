Veronica × danielsii

Veronica × danielsii, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Veronica × danielsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
