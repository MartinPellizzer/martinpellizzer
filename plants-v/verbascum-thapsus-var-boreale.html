Verbascum thapsus var. boreale

Verbascum thapsus var. boreale, commonly known as the northern mullein, is a variant of the common mullein plant.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to the northern regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features large, velvety, gray-green leaves that are often covered in soft hairs. It produces tall, erect flowering stems with yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variety is adapted to cooler climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
