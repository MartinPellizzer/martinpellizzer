Veratrum album var. albiflorum

Veratrum album var. albiflorum, commonly known as white hellebore, is a perennial plant native to the northern regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

However, it is highly toxic, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
