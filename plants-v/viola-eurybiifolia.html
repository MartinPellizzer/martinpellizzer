Viola eurybiifolia

Viola eurybiifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive yellow center.

Viola eurybiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
