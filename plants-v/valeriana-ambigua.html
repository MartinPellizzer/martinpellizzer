Valeriana ambigua

Valeriana ambigua, commonly known as the Brazilian valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana ambigua is part of the Caprifoliaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like honeysuckle.
