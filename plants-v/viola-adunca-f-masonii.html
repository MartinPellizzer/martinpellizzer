Viola adunca f. masonii

Viola adunca f. masonii, commonly known as the Mason's violet, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. It is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and rarity.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened in the wild, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
