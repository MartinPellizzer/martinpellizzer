Viola kamtschadalorum var. parviflora

Viola kamtschadalorum var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered Kamtschadal violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Russia and northern China.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to cold climates and its role in local ecosystems.
