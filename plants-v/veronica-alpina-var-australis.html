Veronica alpina var. australis

Veronica alpina var. australis, commonly known as the Australian speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in clusters along slender stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
