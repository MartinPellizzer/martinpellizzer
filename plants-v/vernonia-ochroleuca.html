Vernonia ochroleuca

Vernonia ochroleuca, commonly known as yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia ochroleuca is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
