Vilfa subpyramidata

Vilfa subpyramidata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed veldtgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
