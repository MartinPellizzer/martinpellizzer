Virola carinata

Virola carinata, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in humid environments. Virola carinata is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
