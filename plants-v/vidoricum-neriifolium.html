Vidoricum neriifolium

Vidoricum neriifolium, commonly known as the black-leafed orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique foliage and floral structure make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
