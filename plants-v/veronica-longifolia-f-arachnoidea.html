Veronica longifolia f. arachnoidea

Veronica longifolia f. arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider-hair veronica, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is native to southern Europe. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. It produces small, blue to purple flowers clustered in spikes, which bloom in early summer.

This cultivar is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
