Veronica bombycina subsp. bolkardaghensis

Veronica bombycina subsp.

bolkardaghensis, commonly known as the Bolkardagh speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Balkans. It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Veronica genus.
