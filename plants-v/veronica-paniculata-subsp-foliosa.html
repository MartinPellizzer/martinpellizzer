Veronica paniculata subsp. foliosa

Veronica paniculata subsp.

foliosa, commonly known as the leafy speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are blue to purple, arranged in paniculate inflorescences, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
