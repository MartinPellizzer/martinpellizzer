Vigna unguiculata subvar. deflexa

Vigna unguiculata subvar. deflexa, commonly known as the cowpea or yardlong bean, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and elongated, bean-like pods that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers and has trifoliate leaves with a distinctive, narrow leaflet shape. It thrives in warm climates and is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

The subvariety is often cultivated for both its nutritional benefits and ornamental appeal.
