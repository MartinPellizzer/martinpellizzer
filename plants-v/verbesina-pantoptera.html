Verbesina pantoptera

Verbesina pantoptera, commonly known as the yellow crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy bracts. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy. Its most distinctive feature is the bright yellow, fluffy bracts that surround the small, pale yellow flowers, creating a striking visual display.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.
