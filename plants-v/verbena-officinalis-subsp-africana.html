Verbena officinalis subsp. africana

Verbena officinalis subsp.

africana, commonly known as African verbena, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
