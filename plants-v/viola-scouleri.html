Viola scouleri

Viola scouleri, commonly known as the Pacific violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy violet flowers that range in color from pale lavender to deep purple. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Viola scouleri is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
