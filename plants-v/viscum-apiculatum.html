Viscum apiculatum

Viscum apiculatum, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Viscaceae family.

It typically grows on the branches of coniferous trees such as pines and firs, forming dense, shrub-like structures. The plant produces small, white or yellowish berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its life cycle involves the release of sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of host trees.

Viscum apiculatum plays a significant ecological role by influencing the growth and structure of its host trees.
