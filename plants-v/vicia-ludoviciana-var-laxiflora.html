Vicia ludoviciana var. laxiflora

Vicia ludoviciana var. laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower vetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
