Valeriana malvacea

Valeriana malvacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Malvaceae family member, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its showy, often pink or purple flowers. The plant typically has broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces capsules as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Valeriana malvacea is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
