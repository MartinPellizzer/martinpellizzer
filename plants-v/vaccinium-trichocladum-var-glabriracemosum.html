Vaccinium trichocladum var. glabriracemosum

Vaccinium trichocladum var. glabriracemosum, commonly known as the glabrous racemose huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, edible berries that are usually red or black when mature.

This variety is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and is often found in moist, acidic soils in forested areas.
