Viscaria asterias

Viscaria asterias, commonly known as the starry groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which consist of a central yellow disk surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Viscaria asterias blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing numerous small flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
