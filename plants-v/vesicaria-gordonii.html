Vesicaria gordonii

Vesicaria gordonii, commonly known as the Gordon's bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain seeds, a feature typical of the mustard family. Vesicaria gordonii thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and desert margins.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
