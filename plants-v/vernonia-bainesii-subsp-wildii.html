Vernonia bainesii subsp. wildii

Vernonia bainesii subsp.

wildii, commonly known as the Cape York vernonia, is a shrub native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in native habitats.
