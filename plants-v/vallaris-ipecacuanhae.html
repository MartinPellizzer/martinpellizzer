Vallaris ipecacuanhae

Vallaris ipecacuanhae, commonly known as the Brazilian ipecac, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. Its roots are traditionally used in herbal medicine for their expectorant properties.

However, the plant contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
