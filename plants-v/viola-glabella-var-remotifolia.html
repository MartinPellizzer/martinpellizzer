Viola glabella var. remotifolia

Viola glabella var. remotifolia, commonly known as the remoteleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
