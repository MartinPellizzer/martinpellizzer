Vitis vinifera var. labrusca

Vitis vinifera var. labrusca, commonly known as the Concord grape, is a variety of grape native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple berries with a thick skin and a distinctive musky aroma. The plant is a vigorous vine that produces clusters of fruit, which are often used in making jams, jellies, and wines. It thrives in temperate climates and requires a chilling period for proper fruit development.

The Concord grape is also valued for its use in producing grape juice and as a rootstock for other grape varieties.
