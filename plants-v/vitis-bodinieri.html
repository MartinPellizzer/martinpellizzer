Vitis bodinieri

Vitis bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier grape, is a species of flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and clusters of small, dark purple berries. The plant produces both male and female flowers, with the female flowers being essential for fruit development. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Vitis bodinieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
