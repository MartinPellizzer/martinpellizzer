Verbascum calvum

Verbascum calvum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft white hairs. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a thick layer of silky hair.

Verbascum calvum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
