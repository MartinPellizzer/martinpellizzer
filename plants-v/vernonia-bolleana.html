Vernonia bolleana

Vernonia bolleana, commonly known as the Bolleana ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the islands of Cuba and Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with rough, dark green leaves. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the dry season.

Vernonia bolleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
