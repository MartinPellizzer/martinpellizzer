Viola maximowicziana

Viola maximowicziana, commonly known as the Siberian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats, preferring cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its hardy nature.

Viola maximowicziana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
