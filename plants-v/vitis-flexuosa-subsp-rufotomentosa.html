Vitis flexuosa subsp. rufotomentosa

Vitis flexuosa subsp.

rufotomentosa, commonly known as the rough-haired grapevine, is a perennial woody vine native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
