Verbena officinalis f. anomala

Verbena officinalis f. anomala, commonly known as the lesser verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of blue, purple, and pink. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, narrow leaves, and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
