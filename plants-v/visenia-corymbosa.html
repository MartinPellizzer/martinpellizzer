Visenia corymbosa

Visenia corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered violets, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Visenia corymbosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
