Verbena scoparia var. puberula

Verbena scoparia var. puberula, commonly known as the rough verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with a fine layer of hair, giving the plant a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
