Viburnum coriaceum

Viburnum coriaceum, commonly known as leatherleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the winter, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum coriaceum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for wildlife.
