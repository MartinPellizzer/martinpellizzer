Veronica longifolia subsp. pseudolongifolia

Veronica longifolia subsp.

pseudolongifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and grassland habitats.

The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in dense spikes, attracting various pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
