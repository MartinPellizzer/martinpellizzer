Vignea × ludibunda

Vignea × ludibunda, commonly known as the hybrid grapevine, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Vigna sinensis and Vigna umbellata.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and production of small, sweet, edible fruits. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval and serrated.

The fruit, which is a small, round berry, contains a single seed and is often used in culinary applications.
