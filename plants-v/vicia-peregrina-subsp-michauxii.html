Vicia peregrina subsp. michauxii

Vicia peregrina subsp.

michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, erect stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for certain insect species.
