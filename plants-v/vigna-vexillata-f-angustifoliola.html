Vigna vexillata f. angustifoliola

Vigna vexillata f. angustifoliola, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a botanical variant of the cowpea species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of about 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
