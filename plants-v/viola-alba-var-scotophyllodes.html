Viola alba var. scotophyllodes

Viola alba var. scotophyllodes, commonly known as the moon violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its pale violet to white flowers, which bloom in early spring. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture. This variety thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
