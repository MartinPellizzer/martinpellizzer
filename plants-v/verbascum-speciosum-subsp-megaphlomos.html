Verbascum speciosum subsp. megaphlomos

Verbascum speciosum subsp.

megaphlomos, commonly known as the giant flax or woolly mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
