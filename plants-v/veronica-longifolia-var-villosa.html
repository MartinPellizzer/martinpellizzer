Veronica longifolia var. villosa

Veronica longifolia var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy veronica, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its varietal name, and are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
