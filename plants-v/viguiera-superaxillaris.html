Viguiera superaxillaris

Viguiera superaxillaris, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its root system is fibrous and deep. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Viguiera superaxillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
