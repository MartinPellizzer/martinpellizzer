Vernonia ledocteana

Vernonia ledocteana, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wetlands. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
