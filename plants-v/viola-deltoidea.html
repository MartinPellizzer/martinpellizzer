Viola deltoidea

Viola deltoidea, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and is often found in the understory of forests.

Viola deltoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
