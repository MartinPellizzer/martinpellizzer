Viola rauliniana

Viola rauliniana, commonly known as the Raulin's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in Guatemala and El Salvador. The plant produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive purple hue, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Viola rauliniana is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual charm.
