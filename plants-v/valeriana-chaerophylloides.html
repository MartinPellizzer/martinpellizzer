Valeriana chaerophylloides

Valeriana chaerophylloides, commonly known as the creeping valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana chaerophylloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its mild sedative properties.
