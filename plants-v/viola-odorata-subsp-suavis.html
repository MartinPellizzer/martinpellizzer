Viola odorata subsp. suavis

Viola odorata subsp.

suavis, commonly known as sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent, especially noticeable when crushed. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The subspecies is noted for its strong fragrance, which has been historically used in perfumes and traditional medicine.
