Verbena tecticaulis

Verbena tecticaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a grayish-green hue.

Verbena tecticaulis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive flowers and drought tolerance.
