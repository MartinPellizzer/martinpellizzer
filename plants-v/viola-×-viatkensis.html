Viola × viatkensis

Viola × viatkensis, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda, resulting in a plant with diverse morphological characteristics. The species is known for its vibrant, often bi-colored flowers that range from purple to white and yellow. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
