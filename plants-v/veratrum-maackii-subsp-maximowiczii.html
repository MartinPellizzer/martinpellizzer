Veratrum maackii subsp. maximowiczii

Veratrum maackii subsp.

maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Eastern Asia. It features a tall, slender stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and the plant contains various toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.
