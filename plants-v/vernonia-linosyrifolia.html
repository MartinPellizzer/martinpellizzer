Vernonia linosyrifolia

Vernonia linosyrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. It is often found in open woodlands, savannas, and along stream banks.

Vernonia linosyrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
