Vachellia barahonensis

Vachellia barahonensis, commonly known as the Barahon acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the dry regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, thin pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in savannas and rocky soils.

Vachellia barahonensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for wildlife.
