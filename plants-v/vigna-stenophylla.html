Vigna stenophylla

Vigna stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing small, edible seeds that are rich in protein. Vigna stenophylla is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in various culinary traditions.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
