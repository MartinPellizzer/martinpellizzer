Vicia truncatula subsp. balansae

Vicia truncatula subsp.

balansae, commonly known as the Balansae vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its seeds are encased in small, hairy pods and are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
