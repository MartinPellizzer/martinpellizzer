Vignaldia

Vignaldia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. Vignaldia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and ability to withstand poor soil conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
