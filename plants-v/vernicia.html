Vernicia

Vernicia, commonly known as the lac tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It comprises several species, many of which are native to Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. Some species of Vernicia are cultivated for their latex, which is used in the production of shellac.

Additionally, certain Vernicia species are valued for their ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
