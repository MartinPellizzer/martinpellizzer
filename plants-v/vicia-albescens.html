Vicia albescens

Vicia albescens, commonly known as the white vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in elongated clusters, which are typical of the vetch genus. Vicia albescens is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
