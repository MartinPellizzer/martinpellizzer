Veronica officinalis var. caespitosa

Veronica officinalis var. caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact, tuft-like appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
