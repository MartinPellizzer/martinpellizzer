Vaccaria sessilifolia

Vaccaria sessilifolia, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Vaccaria sessilifolia is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
