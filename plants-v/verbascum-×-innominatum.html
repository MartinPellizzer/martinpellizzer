Verbascum × innominatum

Verbascum × innominatum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
