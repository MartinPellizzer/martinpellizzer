Vernonia wrightii

Vernonia wrightii, commonly known as Wright's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, dark purple to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
