Viola mandshurica var. albescens

Viola mandshurica var. albescens, commonly known as the White Viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale violet, with five petals and a distinctive spurred petal at the back. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
