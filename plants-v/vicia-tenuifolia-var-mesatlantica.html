Vicia tenuifolia var. mesatlantica

Vicia tenuifolia var. mesatlantica, commonly known as the narrowleaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-green pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
