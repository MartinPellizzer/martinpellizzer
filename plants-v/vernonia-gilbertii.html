Vernonia gilbertii

Vernonia gilbertii, commonly known as Gilbert's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy, yellow to orange composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
