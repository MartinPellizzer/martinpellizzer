Veronica nevadensis var. nevadensis

Veronica nevadensis var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its adaptability in harsh climates.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
