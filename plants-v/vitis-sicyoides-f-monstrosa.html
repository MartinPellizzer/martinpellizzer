Vitis sicyoides f. monstrosa

Vitis sicyoides f. monstrosa, commonly known as the giant grapevine, is a unique horticultural form of the grapevine species Vitis sicyoides.

This variety is characterized by its exceptionally vigorous growth and large, often twisted vines that can reach significant lengths. It produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers and grape-like fruits that are typically not suitable for consumption but are valued for ornamental purposes. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
