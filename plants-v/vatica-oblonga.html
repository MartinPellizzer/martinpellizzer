Vatica oblonga

Vatica oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its hard, dense wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant has elliptic to oblong leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, and its fruits are woody capsules containing several seeds.

Vatica oblonga is often found in lowland rainforests and plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
