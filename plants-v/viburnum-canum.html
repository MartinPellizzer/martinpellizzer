Viburnum canum

Viburnum canum, commonly known as blackhaw viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white flowers that appear in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Viburnum canum is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
