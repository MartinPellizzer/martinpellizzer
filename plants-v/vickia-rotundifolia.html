Vickia rotundifolia

Vickia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf vickia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has rounded, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Vickia rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
