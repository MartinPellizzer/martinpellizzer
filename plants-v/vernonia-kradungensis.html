Vernonia kradungensis

Vernonia kradungensis, commonly known as the Thai ironwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its dense, fibrous bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The wood of Vernonia kradungensis is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it a significant resource in local communities.
