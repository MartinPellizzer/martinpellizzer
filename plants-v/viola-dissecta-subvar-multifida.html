Viola dissecta subvar. multifida

Viola dissecta subvar. multifida, commonly known as the multifid viola, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
