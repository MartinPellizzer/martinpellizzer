Vernonia armerioides

Vernonia armerioides, commonly known as roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and riverbanks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its showy, purple to pinkish-blue flowers appear in clusters at the tips of the stems, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland ecosystems.
