Viola purpurea subsp. aurea

Viola purpurea subsp.

aurea, commonly known as the golden violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy flowers that range from yellow to orange in color.

The species is part of the Violaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

The golden violet is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
