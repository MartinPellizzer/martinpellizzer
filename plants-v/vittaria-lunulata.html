Vittaria lunulata

Vittaria lunulata, commonly known as the moon's moss, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Vittariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant has a unique morphology with a stalk-like structure called a stipe, which supports the fertile leaves. Vittaria lunulata thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its reproductive structures, called sorus, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
