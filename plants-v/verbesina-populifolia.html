Verbesina populifolia

Verbesina populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of a poplar tree, hence its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, also known as the daisy or sunflower family.

Verbesina populifolia is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks.
