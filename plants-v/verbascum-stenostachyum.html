Verbascum stenostachyum

Verbascum stenostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stalked mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters at the top of the stems, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum stenostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
