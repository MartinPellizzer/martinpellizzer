Viola × burnatii

Viola × burnatii, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Viola tricolor and Viola lutea, resulting in a plant with distinctive purple and yellow flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps to reduce water loss. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
