Veratrum nigrum subsp. maackii

Veratrum nigrum subsp.

maackii, commonly known as black hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It features tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often covered with a white, waxy coating.

The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters during late summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

This species contains toxic alkaloids and is part of the Melanthiaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
