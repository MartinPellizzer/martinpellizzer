Veronica bibarbata

Veronica bibarbata, commonly known as the bearded veronica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, blue or purple flowers clustered in dense spikes. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Veronica bibarbata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
