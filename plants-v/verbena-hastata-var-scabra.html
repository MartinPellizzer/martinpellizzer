Verbena hastata var. scabra

Verbena hastata var. scabra, commonly known as rough verbena, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Rough verbena is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
