Vaccinium uliginosum var. japonicum

Vaccinium uliginosum var. japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese blueberry or Japanese cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. The plant thrives in acidic, moist soils and is typically found in shaded, woodland environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are bell-shaped and pink or white.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Vaccinium genus, which includes blueberries and cranberries.
