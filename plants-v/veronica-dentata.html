Veronica dentata

Veronica dentata, commonly known as the toothed speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves with finely toothed edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Veronica dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
