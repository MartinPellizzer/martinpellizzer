Venidium scabrum

Venidium scabrum, commonly known as rough venidium, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-brown, rough, and often hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture.

Venidium scabrum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert regions.
