Viola bolivari

Viola bolivari, commonly known as the Bolivian violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces delicate, showy flowers in shades of purple and violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Viola bolivari is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
