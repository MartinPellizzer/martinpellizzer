Veronica didyma var. lilacina

Veronica didyma var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac flowering veronica, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to lilac. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
