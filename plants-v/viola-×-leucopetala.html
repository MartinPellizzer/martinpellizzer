Viola × leucopetala

Viola × leucopetala, commonly known as the white violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a companion plant.

The species is known for its ability to hybridize with other viola species, contributing to the diversity within the genus.
