Viscum laxum f. angustifolium

Viscum laxum f. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical Viscum laxum. The plant produces white, waxy berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to host plants. It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly in countries like South Africa and Namibia.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant ecological impacts on its host trees.
