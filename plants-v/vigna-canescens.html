Vigna canescens

Vigna canescens, commonly known as the hairy bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods containing edible seeds. Vigna canescens is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary applications.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil also makes it a valuable crop in sustainable agriculture.
