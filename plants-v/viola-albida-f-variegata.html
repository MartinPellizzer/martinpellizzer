Viola albida f. variegata

Viola albida f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated white violet, is a cultivar of the viola genus native to Europe.

It is characterized by its pale white petals with distinctive variegation, featuring contrasting green and yellow markings. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 cm, with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is known for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
