Viburnum erosum f. xanthocarpum

Viburnum erosum f. xanthocarpum, commonly known as the yellow-fruited snowberry, is a variant of the snowberry plant characterized by its distinctive yellow berries.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, white flowers in clustered inflorescences. Its fruits, which are yellow and persistent into winter, provide food for birds and other wildlife.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in garden settings.
