Verbesina laevifolia

Verbesina laevifolia, commonly known as smoothleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, alternate leaves that are typically smooth along their margins. Its flowers are yellow and produce small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Verbesina laevifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, open habitats.
