Viola primulifolia var. cordata

Viola primulifolia var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with yellow centers during the spring season. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
