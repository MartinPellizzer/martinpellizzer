Viburnum rufotomentosum

Viburnum rufotomentosum, commonly known as the red-tomentose viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for small garden spaces.

Viburnum rufotomentosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
