Vernonia kroneana

Vernonia kroneana, commonly known as Krone's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical herbaceous structure. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense spikes.

Vernonia kroneana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
