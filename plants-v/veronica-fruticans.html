Veronica fruticans

Veronica fruticans, commonly known as the sand veronica, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species suitable for xeriscaping.
