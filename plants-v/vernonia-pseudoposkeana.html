Vernonia pseudoposkeana

Vernonia pseudoposkeana, commonly known as the Kew vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs during the dry season.

Vernonia pseudoposkeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
