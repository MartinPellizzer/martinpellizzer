Vicia unguiculata subsp. atropurpurea

Vicia unguiculata subsp.

atropurpurea, commonly known as purple vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with a distinctive purple or reddish hue on the underside.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
