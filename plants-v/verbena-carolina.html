Verbena carolina

Verbena carolina, commonly known as swamp verbena, is a native flowering plant of the United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Verbena carolina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
