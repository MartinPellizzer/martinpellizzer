Veronica micromera

Veronica micromera, commonly known as the dwarf speedwell, is a small flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and clusters of tiny blue or violet flowers. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Veronica micromera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
