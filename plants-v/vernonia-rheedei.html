Vernonia rheedei

Vernonia rheedei, commonly known as the yellow ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its dense, woody structure. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant is known for its hardiness in tropical and subtropical climates.

Vernonia rheedei is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
