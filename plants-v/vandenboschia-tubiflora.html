Vandenboschia tubiflora

Vandenboschia tubiflora, commonly known as the woolly bush, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant typically grows in shrub form, with thick, fleshy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

Vandenboschia tubiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
