Veratrum coreanum var. trichothyrsum

Veratrum coreanum var. trichothyrsum, commonly known as the Korean white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a waxy surface, while its roots contain toxic alkaloids.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
