Viola × fragrans

Viola × fragrans, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodlands and meadows.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
