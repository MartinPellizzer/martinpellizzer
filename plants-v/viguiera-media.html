Viguiera media

Viguiera media, commonly known as Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange to yellow flowers that bloom from summer to fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 10.

Viguiera media is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
