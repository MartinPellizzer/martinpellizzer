Vigna oahuensis

Vigna oahuensis, commonly known as the Oahu bean, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native range. Vigna oahuensis is notable for its ecological significance in supporting local wildlife and its potential for use in sustainable agriculture.

However, it is considered endangered due to habitat loss and invasive species.
