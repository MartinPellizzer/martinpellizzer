Volvulus silvaticus

Volvulus silvaticus, commonly known as the forest volvulus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its spiral-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in dense, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a smooth surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers.

Volvulus silvaticus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
