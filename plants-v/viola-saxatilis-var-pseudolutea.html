Viola saxatilis var. pseudolutea

Viola saxatilis var. pseudolutea, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained, acidic environments. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces small, violet-blue flowers in early spring. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and have five petals with a distinctive yellow keel.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
