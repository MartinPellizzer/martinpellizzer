Viburnum corymbiflorum subsp. corymbiflorum

Viburnum corymbiflorum subsp.

corymbiflorum, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa. It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
