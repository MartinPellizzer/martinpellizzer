Vigna micrantha var. lebrunii

Vigna micrantha var. lebrunii, commonly known as the small-seeded cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows in warm climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content, making it an important crop in many agricultural systems.
