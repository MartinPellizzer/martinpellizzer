Vernonia burtonii

Vernonia burtonii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the late summer and early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.

Vernonia burtonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
