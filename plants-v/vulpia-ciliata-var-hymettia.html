Vulpia ciliata var. hymettia

Vulpia ciliata var. hymettia, commonly known as the Hymettian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and hairy leaf blades, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of spikelets.

Vulpia ciliata var. hymettia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
