Viola × badensis

Viola × badensis, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola × badensis is often used in gardens for its ability to naturalize and its attractive, fragrant blooms.
