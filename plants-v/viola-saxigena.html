Viola saxigena

Viola saxigena, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet or purple flowers with five petals. It is known for its ability to withstand cold temperatures and high altitudes.

Viola saxigena is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
