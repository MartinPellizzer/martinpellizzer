Vernonia augustiniana

Vernonia augustiniana, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Vernonia augustiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
