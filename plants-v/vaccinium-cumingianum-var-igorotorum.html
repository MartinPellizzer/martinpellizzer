Vaccinium cumingianum var. igorotorum

Vaccinium cumingianum var. igorotorum, commonly known as the Igorot blueberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are blue-black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential as a food source.
