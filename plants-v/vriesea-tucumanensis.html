Vriesea tucumanensis

Vriesea tucumanensis, commonly known as the Tucuman flame, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a central pool that collects rainwater. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Vriesea tucumanensis is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
