Vernonia sandemanii

Vernonia sandemanii, commonly known as the sandeman's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Vernonia sandemanii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
