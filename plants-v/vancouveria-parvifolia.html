Vancouveria parvifolia

Vancouveria parvifolia, commonly known as the Vancouver crocus, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Vancouveria parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
