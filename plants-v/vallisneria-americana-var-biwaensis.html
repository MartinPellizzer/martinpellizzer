Vallisneria americana var. biwaensis

Vallisneria americana var. biwaensis, commonly known as the Japanese waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found near the water surface. It is often used in aquaculture and as a water filtration plant due to its ability to absorb nutrients.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an effective indicator of water quality and a potential invasive species in non-native environments.
