Valeriana dioica

Valeriana dioica, commonly known as wild valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
