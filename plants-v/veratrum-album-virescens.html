Veratrum album virescens

Veratrum album virescens, commonly known as the greenish white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It features tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often adorned with pale green to white flowers. The plant has large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
