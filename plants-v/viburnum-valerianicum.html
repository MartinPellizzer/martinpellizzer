Viburnum valerianicum

Viburnum valerianicum, commonly known as sweet viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels. The plant produces dark blue, waxy berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Viburnum valerianicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
