Viola thomasiana

Viola thomasiana, commonly known as the Thomas's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola thomasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
