Vriesea pulverulentolineata

Vriesea pulverulentolineata, commonly known as the powdered line vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that displays intricate patterns of pink and white. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery, waxy coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea pulverulentolineata is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
