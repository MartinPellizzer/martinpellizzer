Valeriana acutiloba var. pubicarpa

Valeriana acutiloba var. pubicarpa, commonly known as the rough-fruited valerian, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia and Africa.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy fruits, which are distinctive in appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Valeriana acutiloba var. pubicarpa is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
