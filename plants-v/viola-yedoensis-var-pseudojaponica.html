Viola yedoensis var. pseudojaponica

Viola yedoensis var. pseudojaponica, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy violet or purple flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
