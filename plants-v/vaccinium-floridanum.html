Vaccinium floridanum

Vaccinium floridanum, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a waxy coating, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Vaccinium floridanum plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
