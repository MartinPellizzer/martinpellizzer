Viola cucullata var. hispidula

Viola cucullata var. hispidula, commonly known as the hispid violet, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Violaceae family.
