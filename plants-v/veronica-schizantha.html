Veronica schizantha

Veronica schizantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive zigzag pattern along the edges.

Veronica schizantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
