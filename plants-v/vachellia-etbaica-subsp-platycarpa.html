Vachellia etbaica subsp. platycarpa

Vachellia etbaica subsp.

platycarpa, commonly known as the Ethiopian carob tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Ethiopia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which have been traditionally used as a food source.

It is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and nitrogen-fixing capabilities through its root nodules.
