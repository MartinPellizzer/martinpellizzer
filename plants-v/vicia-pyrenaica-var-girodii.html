Vicia pyrenaica var. girodii

Vicia pyrenaica var. girodii, commonly known as the Pyrenean vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive shape and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its pods contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in soil stabilization.
