Vernonia argyropappa

Vernonia argyropappa, commonly known as the silverleaf vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to the dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.
