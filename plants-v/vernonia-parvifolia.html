Vernonia parvifolia

Vernonia parvifolia, commonly known as the smallflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Vernonia parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
