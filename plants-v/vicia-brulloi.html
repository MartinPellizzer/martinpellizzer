Vicia brulloi

Vicia brulloi, commonly known as the Brullo's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic regions. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Vicia brulloi is adapted to harsh environmental conditions and is often found in mountainous areas across Europe and Asia.
