Valeriana dioica f. nana

Valeriana dioica f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf valerian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. The plant has aromatic, medicinal leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. Its compact form makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Valeriana dioica f. nana thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, preferring cooler climates.
