Verbena delicatula

Verbena delicatula, commonly known as the slender verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Verbena delicatula is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
