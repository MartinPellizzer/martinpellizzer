Verbascum nigrum var. parisiense

Verbascum nigrum var. parisiense, commonly known as the Paris poppy, is a variant of the common mullein.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range from pale yellow to deep orange, bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
