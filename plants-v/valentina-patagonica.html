Valentina patagonica

Valentina patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian valerian, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Valerianaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, thread-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous or forested areas. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Valentina patagonica is valued both for its ecological role and its potential medicinal applications.
