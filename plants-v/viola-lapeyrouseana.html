Viola lapeyrouseana

Viola lapeyrouseana, commonly known as the Lapeyrouse's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Viola lapeyrouseana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
