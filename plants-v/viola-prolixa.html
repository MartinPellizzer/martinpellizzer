Viola prolixa

Viola prolixa, commonly known as the long-stalked violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces delicate violet flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
