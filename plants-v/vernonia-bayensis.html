Vernonia bayensis

Vernonia bayensis, commonly known as the Bay Islands Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bay Islands of Honduras.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to salt spray and sandy soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Vernonia bayensis is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
