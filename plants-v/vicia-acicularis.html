Vicia acicularis

Vicia acicularis, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vicia acicularis is often found in grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
