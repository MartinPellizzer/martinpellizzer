Viola chamissoniana var. olokelensis

Viola chamissoniana var. olokelensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. It has small, hairy stems and produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is notable for its unique adaptations to the island's climate and its role in local ecosystems.
