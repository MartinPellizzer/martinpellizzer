Vernoniastrum camporum

Vernoniastrum camporum, commonly known as the plains vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and prairies, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernoniastrum camporum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
