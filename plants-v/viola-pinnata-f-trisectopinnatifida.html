Viola pinnata f. trisectopinnatifida

Viola pinnata f. trisectopinnatifida, commonly known as the trisect leaf violet, is a variant of the common violet with distinctive foliage.

It features trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a unique and intricate appearance. The plant produces small, heart-shaped flowers in shades of purple or violet, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
