Vernonia linarifolia

Vernonia linarifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Vernonia linarifolia is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it is known for its hardy nature.

It is often found in open woods, thickets, and along roadsides, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
