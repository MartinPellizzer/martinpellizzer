Vaccinium roylei

Vaccinium roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Vaccinium roylei is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential in local food systems.
