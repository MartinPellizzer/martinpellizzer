Vernonia tigrensis

Vernonia tigrensis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Vernonia tigrensis is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
