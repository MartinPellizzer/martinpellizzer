Vriesea psittacina var. psittacina

Vriesea psittacina var. psittacina, commonly known as the parrot's beak bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It features a rosette of elongated, waxy leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, spathiflorous inflorescences with brightly colored bracts that resemble a parrot's beak. Its foliage is often variegated with yellow and green patterns, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.
