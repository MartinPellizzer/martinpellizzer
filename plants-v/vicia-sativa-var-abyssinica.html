Vicia sativa var. abyssinica

Vicia sativa var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian vetch, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and surrounding regions.

It is a leguminous species characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, often arranged in clusters. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, typical of the Fabaceae family.

Vicia sativa var. abyssinica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging climates.
