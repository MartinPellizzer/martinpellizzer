Viola danubialis

Viola danubialis, commonly known as the Danube violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and riverbanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola danubialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
