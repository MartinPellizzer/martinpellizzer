Vernonia pilgeriana

Vernonia pilgeriana, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind. Vernonia pilgeriana thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
