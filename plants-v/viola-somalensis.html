Viola somalensis

Viola somalensis, commonly known as the Somali violet, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from violet to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
