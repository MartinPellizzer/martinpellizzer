Veronica gracillima

Veronica gracillima, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and attractive flowers make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
