Viola × marcetii

Viola × marcetii, commonly known as the marsh violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola palustris and Viola tricolor.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with heart-shaped, hairy leaves and violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are typically five-petaled, with a distinctive violet coloration and a yellow center.

Viola × marcetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
