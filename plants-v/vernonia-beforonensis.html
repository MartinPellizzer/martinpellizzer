Vernonia beforonensis

Vernonia beforonensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its botanical name honors the Swedish botanist Carl Peter Thunberg.

Vernonia beforonensis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
