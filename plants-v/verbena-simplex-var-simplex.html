Verbena simplex var. simplex

Verbena simplex var. simplex, commonly known as common verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a compact, bushy annual or perennial that produces vibrant clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
