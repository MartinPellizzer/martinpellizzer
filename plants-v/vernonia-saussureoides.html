Vernonia saussureoides

Vernonia saussureoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include a sturdy stem and a fibrous root system, which help it thrive in wet conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
