Viola palustris subsp. palustris

Viola palustris subsp.

palustris, commonly known as the marsh violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and its flowers are usually pale purple to white with a darker throat. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
