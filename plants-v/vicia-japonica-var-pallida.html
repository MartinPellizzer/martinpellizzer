Vicia japonica var. pallida

Vicia japonica var. pallida, commonly known as the Japanese vetch or pale vetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its pods are slender and contain several seeds, and it is often found in woodland or open areas.

Vicia japonica var. pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
