Vernonia forbesii

Vernonia forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple composite flowers. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia forbesii is often found in open woods, roadsides, and disturbed areas, making it a common sight in the region.
