Veronica gebhardiana

Veronica gebhardiana, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Veronica gebhardiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
