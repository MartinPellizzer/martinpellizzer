Viola palmaensis

Viola palmaensis, commonly known as the Palm Island violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to humid, tropical climates and its role in local ecosystems.

Viola palmaensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
