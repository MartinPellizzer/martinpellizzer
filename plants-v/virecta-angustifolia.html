Virecta angustifolia

Virecta angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf vireya, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Virecta angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
