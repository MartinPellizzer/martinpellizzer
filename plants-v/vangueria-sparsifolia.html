Vangueria sparsifolia

Vangueria sparsifolia, commonly known as the African star grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Vangueria sparsifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
