Viola cascadensis

Viola cascadensis, commonly known as the cascade violet, is a delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along streams. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola cascadensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
