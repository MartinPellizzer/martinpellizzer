Verbascum creticum

Verbascum creticum, commonly known as the Cretan mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Verbascum creticum is a perennial species that is relatively easy to grow in gardens with suitable conditions.
