Viola sororia var. missouriensis

Viola sororia var. missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue or purple flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive dark throat. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding color to the landscape during the transition from winter to summer.
