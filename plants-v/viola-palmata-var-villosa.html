Viola palmata var. villosa

Viola palmata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, which gives it its varietal name. The plant produces violet flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The hairy violet is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in native plant gardens.
