Viola eriocarpa var. leiocarpa

Viola eriocarpa var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or violet. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
