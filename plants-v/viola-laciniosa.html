Viola laciniosa

Viola laciniosa, commonly known as the laceflower violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin, and its flowers are typically violet or purple with a yellow center. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Viola laciniosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
