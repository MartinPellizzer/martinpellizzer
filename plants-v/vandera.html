Vandera

Vandera, commonly known as the vanda orchid, is a genus of epiphytic and lithophytic orchids native to Southeast Asia.

These plants are characterized by their striking, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including pink, yellow, and white. Vandera species typically have thick, fleshy roots adapted for absorbing moisture from the air. They thrive in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Their unique botanical features make them popular among orchid enthusiasts for their beauty and resilience.
