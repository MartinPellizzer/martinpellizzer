Vernonia arborescens var. divaricata

Vernonia arborescens var. divaricata, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom from late summer to fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

This species is adapted to moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.
