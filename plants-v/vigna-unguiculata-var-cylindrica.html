Vigna unguiculata var. cylindrica

Vigna unguiculata var. cylindrica, commonly known as cowpea or yardlong bean, is a legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its long, cylindrical pods that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically has compound leaves with three leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
