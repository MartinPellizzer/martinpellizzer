Vaccinium pullei

Vaccinium pullei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like heathers and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist climate for optimal growth.

The leaves are narrow and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in horticultural settings.
