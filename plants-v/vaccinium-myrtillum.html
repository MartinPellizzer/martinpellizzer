Vaccinium myrtillum

Vaccinium myrtillum, commonly known as the blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that range in color from white to deep blue, depending on the variety. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes.

Vaccinium myrtillum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
