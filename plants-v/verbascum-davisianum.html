Verbascum davisianum

Verbascum davisianum, commonly known as Davis' mullein, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Davis' mullein is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
