Viola purpurea subsp. integrifolia

Viola purpurea subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the purple violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, entire margin, which distinguishes it from other viola species.

The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically arranged in a spurred pattern. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
