Vaccinium glandulosum

Vaccinium glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like heathers and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its flowers are typically bell-shaped and pink or white, producing small, edible berries that are usually dark blue or purple in color.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.
