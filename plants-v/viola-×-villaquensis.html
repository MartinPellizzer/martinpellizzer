Viola × villaquensis

Viola × villaquensis, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola lutea and Viola tricolor, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parent species. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Viola × villaquensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
