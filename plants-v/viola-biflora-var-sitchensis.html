Viola biflora var. sitchensis

Viola biflora var. sitchensis, commonly known as the Sitka violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

The Sitka violet is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
