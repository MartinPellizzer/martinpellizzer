Vitaliana primuliflora subsp. praetutiana

Vitaliana primuliflora subsp.

praetutiana, commonly known as the Praetutian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny or partially shaded environments.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in late spring. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to local biodiversity.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
