Vernonia bainesii

Vernonia bainesii, commonly known as the Baines' ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellow to brownish-green composite flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its hardy nature, often growing in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia bainesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
