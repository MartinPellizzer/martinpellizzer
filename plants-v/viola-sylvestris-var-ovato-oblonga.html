Viola sylvestris var. ovato-oblonga

Viola sylvestris var. ovato-oblonga, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped or ovate-oblong leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet or blue, often with yellow markings near the base of the petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and conservation efforts.
