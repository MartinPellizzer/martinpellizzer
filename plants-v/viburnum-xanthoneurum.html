Viburnum xanthoneurum

Viburnum xanthoneurum, commonly known as the yellow-leafed viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically yellowish-green, turning bright yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a visually appealing addition to landscapes.
