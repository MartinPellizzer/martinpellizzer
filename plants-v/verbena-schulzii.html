Verbena schulzii

Verbena schulzii, commonly known as the purple verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple or blue flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in spikes.

Verbena schulzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
