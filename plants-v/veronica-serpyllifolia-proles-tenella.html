Veronica serpyllifolia proles tenella

Veronica serpyllifolia proles tenella, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
