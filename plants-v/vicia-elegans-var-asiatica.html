Vicia elegans var. asiatica

Vicia elegans var. asiatica, commonly known as the Asiatic slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Vicia elegans var. asiatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological and agricultural purposes.
