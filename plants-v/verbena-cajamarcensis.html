Verbena cajamarcensis

Verbena cajamarcensis, commonly known as the Peruvian verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Verbena cajamarcensis is often cultivated for its vibrant flowers and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
