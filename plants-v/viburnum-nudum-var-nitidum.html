Viburnum nudum var. nitidum

Viburnum nudum var. nitidum, commonly known as shining arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is adaptable to various soil types.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
