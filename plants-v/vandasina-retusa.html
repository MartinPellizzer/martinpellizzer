Vandasina retusa

Vandasina retusa, commonly known as the slender vanda, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and long, slender stems that often bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces pale yellow to orange flowers with darker markings, which bloom during the wet season. Vandasina retusa thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture.

It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
