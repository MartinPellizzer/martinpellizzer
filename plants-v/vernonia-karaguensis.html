Vernonia karaguensis

Vernonia karaguensis, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and as a source of fuel. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Vernonia karaguensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
