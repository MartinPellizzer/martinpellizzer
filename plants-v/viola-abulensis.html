Viola abulensis

Viola abulensis, commonly known as the Abulian violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, particularly in countries like Georgia and Russia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Viola abulensis typically grows in rocky soils and shaded forest environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
