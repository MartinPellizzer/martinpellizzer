Vaccinium profusum

Vaccinium profusum, commonly known as the common blueberry, is a species of shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic soils and requires partial shade to full sun for optimal growth.

Vaccinium profusum is an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
