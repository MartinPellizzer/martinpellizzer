Vernonia rhodesiana

Vernonia rhodesiana, commonly known as Rhodesian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery-gray underside, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia rhodesiana is known for its resilience in dry conditions and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
