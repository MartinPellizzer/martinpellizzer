Verbena melindroides

Verbena melindroides, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Verbena melindroides is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
