Vernonia vogeliana

Vernonia vogeliana, commonly known as Vogel's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia vogeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
