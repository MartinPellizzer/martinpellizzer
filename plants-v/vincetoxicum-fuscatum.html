Vincetoxicum fuscatum

Vincetoxicum fuscatum, commonly known as black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, followed by dark blue or black berries. Its botanical features include hairy stems, glandular leaves, and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

It is often found in woodland edges, disturbed soils, and along roadsides.
