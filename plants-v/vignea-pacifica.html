Vignea pacifica

Vignea pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific vine, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Vignea pacifica thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
