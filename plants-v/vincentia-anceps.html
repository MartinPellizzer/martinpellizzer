Vincentia anceps

Vincentia anceps, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The vine is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
