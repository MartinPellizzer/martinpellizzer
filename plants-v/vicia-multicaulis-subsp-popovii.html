Vicia multicaulis subsp. popovii

Vicia multicaulis subsp.

popovii, commonly known as the Popov's vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, upright clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically significant in nutrient-poor soils.
