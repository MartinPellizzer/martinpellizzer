Viola tricolor subsp. vulgaris

Viola tricolor subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common heartsease, is a flowering plant in the family Violaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, typically with three heart-shaped leaflets, and vibrant flowers that range from violet to white and yellow.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wildflower displays.
