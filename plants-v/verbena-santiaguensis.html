Verbena santiaguensis

Verbena santiaguensis, commonly known as the Santiago verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in coastal or disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in tropical regions.
