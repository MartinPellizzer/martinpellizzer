Valeriana hookeriana

Valeriana hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late summer. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana hookeriana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.
