Viguiera cordata var. websteri

Viguiera cordata var. websteri, commonly known as the Webster's blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
