Veronica saxatilis var. microcalyx

Veronica saxatilis var. microcalyx, commonly known as the dwarf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe.

It features small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for alpine and xeriscaping gardens.
