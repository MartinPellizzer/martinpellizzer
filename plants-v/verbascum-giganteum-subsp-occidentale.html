Verbascum giganteum subsp. occidentale

Verbascum giganteum subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as the western mullein, is a tall, erect biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its large, fuzzy, velvety leaves that are covered in soft, downy hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

The plant is known for its medicinal uses and has been traditionally used for its soothing properties.
