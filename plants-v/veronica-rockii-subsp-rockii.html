Veronica rockii subsp. rockii

Veronica rockii subsp.

rockii, commonly known as the rock speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
