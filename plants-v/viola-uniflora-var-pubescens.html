Viola uniflora var. pubescens

Viola uniflora var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
