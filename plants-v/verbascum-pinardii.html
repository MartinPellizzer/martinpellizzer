Verbascum pinardii

Verbascum pinardii, commonly known as the Turkish mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thick, woolly stems and large, broad leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, grayish hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, upright spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Verbascum pinardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
