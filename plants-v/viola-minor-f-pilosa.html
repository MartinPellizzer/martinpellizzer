Viola minor f. pilosa

Viola minor f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a compact perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, delicate purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
