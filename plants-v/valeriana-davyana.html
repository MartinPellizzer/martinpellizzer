Valeriana davyana

Valeriana davyana, commonly known as the Siberian valerian, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in the summer months. Its root system is extensively branched and contains essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Valeriana davyana is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and sedative effects.
