Vitis cavaleriei

Vitis cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Vitis cavaleriei is also valued for its potential in viticulture and horticulture.
