Vignea intermedia

Vignea intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate vigna, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Vignea intermedia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful crop in sustainable agriculture.

Its adaptability to various soil types contributes to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
