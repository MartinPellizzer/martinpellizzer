Vincetoxicum indicum var. indicum

Vincetoxicum indicum var. indicum, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its medicinal properties have been explored in traditional medicine, though it contains toxic compounds that require caution in use.
