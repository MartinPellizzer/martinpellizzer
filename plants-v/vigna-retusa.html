Vigna retusa

Vigna retusa, commonly known as the slender pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Vigna retusa is often grown for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
