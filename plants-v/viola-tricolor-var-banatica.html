Viola tricolor var. banatica

Viola tricolor var. banatica, commonly known as the Banat violet, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is a perennial species belonging to the Violaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of colorful flowers. The plant typically produces violet, white, and yellow petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its presence in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
