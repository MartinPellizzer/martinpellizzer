Vaccinium chapaense

Vaccinium chapaense, commonly known as Chapa huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. These berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to acidic soils and cold climates makes it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
