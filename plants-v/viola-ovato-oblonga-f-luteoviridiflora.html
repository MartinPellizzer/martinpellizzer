Viola ovato-oblonga f. luteoviridiflora

Viola ovato-oblonga f. luteoviridiflora, commonly known as the yellow-green viola, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped to oval-oblong leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The flowers are notable for their unique yellow-green petals, which give the plant its common name. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

It thrives in cool, moist conditions and prefers partial shade.
