Vitex glabrata f. bombacifolia

Vitex glabrata f. bombacifolia, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, fluffy seed pods that resemble those of the Bombacaceae family, giving it its specific epithet "bombacifolia.". Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
