Vaccinium leptanthum

Vaccinium leptanthum, commonly known as the slender blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, though they are less commonly consumed by humans compared to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
