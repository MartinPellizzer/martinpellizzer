Vernonia glabrata var. angustifolia

Vernonia glabrata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers are typically purple or pink, appearing in late summer to early fall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
