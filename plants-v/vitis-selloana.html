Vitis selloana

Vitis selloana, commonly known as the Sello's grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries such as Argentina, Brazil, and Uruguay. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and other structures. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-white in color.

The berries produced by Vitis selloana are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making it a potential candidate for cultivation in certain regions.
