Vernonia damazioi

Vernonia damazioi, commonly known as the Damazo's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the savannas and grasslands of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. It is often found in open habitats with well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal droughts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
