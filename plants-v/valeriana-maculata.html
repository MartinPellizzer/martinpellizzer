Valeriana maculata

Valeriana maculata, commonly known as spotted centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or red spot at the base of each flower. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

Valeriana maculata thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
