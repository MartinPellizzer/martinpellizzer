Viola canina var. macrantha

Viola canina var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered dog violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy violet flowers with yellow markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in spring, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
