Vulpia membranacea

Vulpia membranacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf foxtail, is a species of grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a small, annual grass that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to spread in various environments.

Vulpia membranacea is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides, where it can become a weed.
