Verbena × perturbata

Verbena × perturbata, commonly known as the hybrid verbena, is a flowering plant in the Verbenaceae family.

It is a cross between Verbena hastata and Verbena officinalis, resulting in a plant with distinctive, showy flowers. The species is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance blooms.
