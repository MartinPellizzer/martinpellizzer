Veronica pedunculata

Veronica pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Veronica pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscaping.
