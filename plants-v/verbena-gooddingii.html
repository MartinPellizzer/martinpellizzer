Verbena gooddingii

Verbena gooddingii, commonly known as Goodding's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms from summer to fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
