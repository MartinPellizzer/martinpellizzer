Viola willkommii proles parvifolia

Viola willkommii proles parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved viola, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically smaller than those of other viola species. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
