Vaccinium berberidifolium

Vaccinium berberidifolium, commonly known as the red mountain cranberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by dark red or black berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
