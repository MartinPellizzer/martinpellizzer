Viola collina var. intramongolica

Viola collina var. intramongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate habitats such as meadows and forest edges. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy violet or purple flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
