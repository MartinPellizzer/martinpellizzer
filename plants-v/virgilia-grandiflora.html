Virgilia grandiflora

Virgilia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered virgilia, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
