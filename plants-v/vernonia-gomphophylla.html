Vernonia gomphophylla

Vernonia gomphophylla, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Vernonia gomphophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
