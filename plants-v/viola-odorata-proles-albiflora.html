Viola odorata proles albiflora

Viola odorata proles albiflora, commonly known as the sweet violet with white flowers, is a botanical variant of the sweet violet species.

It is characterized by its pale, almost white petals that contrast with its deep green foliage. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
