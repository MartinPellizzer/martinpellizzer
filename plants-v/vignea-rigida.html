Vignea rigida

Vignea rigida, commonly known as the rigid vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by pods containing several seeds. Vignea rigida is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and agricultural studies.
