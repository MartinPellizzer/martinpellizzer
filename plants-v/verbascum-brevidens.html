Verbascum brevidens

Verbascum brevidens, commonly known as the dwarf mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, candle-like inflorescence. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Verbascum brevidens is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
