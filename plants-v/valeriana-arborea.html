Valeriana arborea

Valeriana arborea, commonly known as tree valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and clustered leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called corymbs. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana arborea is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
