Vernonia glabrata var. lanata

Vernonia glabrata var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to brownish-red flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or open woodlands.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral displays.
