Vernonia shannonii

Vernonia shannonii, commonly known as Shannon's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pale blue to purple in color.

Vernonia shannonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
