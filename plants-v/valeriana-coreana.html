Valeriana coreana

Valeriana coreana, commonly known as Korean valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes and roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana coreana is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal remedies.
