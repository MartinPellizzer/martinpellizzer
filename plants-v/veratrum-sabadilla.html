Veratrum sabadilla

Veratrum sabadilla, commonly known as the sacred or blessed datura, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or purple flowers and hairy, ridged stems. The plant contains various alkaloids, including scopolamine and hyoscyamine, which have medicinal and toxic properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its potent compounds, it is used cautiously in traditional medicine and has a long history in indigenous practices.
