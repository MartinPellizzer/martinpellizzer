Verbesina rupestris

Verbesina rupestris, commonly known as the rock coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with prominent central disks, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Verbesina rupestris is valued in xeriscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
