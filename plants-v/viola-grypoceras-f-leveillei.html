Viola grypoceras f. leveillei

Viola grypoceras f. leveillei, commonly known as the Leveille's violet, is a rare and ornamental plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodland areas or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in some regions.
