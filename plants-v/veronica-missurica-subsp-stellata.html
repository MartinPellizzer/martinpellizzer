Veronica missurica subsp. stellata

Veronica missurica subsp.

stellata, commonly known as the starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
