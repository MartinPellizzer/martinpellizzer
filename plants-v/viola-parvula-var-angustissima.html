Viola parvula var. angustissima

Viola parvula var. angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are small, usually purple or violet, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
