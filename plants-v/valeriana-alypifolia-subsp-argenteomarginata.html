Valeriana alypifolia subsp. argenteomarginata

Valeriana alypifolia subsp.

argenteomarginata, commonly known as silver-edged valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which have a distinctive silver or white margin, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild medicinal properties.
