Valeriana dentata

Valeriana dentata, commonly known as toothed valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with toothed edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

Valeriana dentata is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat nervous disorders and sleep issues.
