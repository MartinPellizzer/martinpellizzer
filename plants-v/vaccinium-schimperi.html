Vaccinium schimperi

Vaccinium schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian highland blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark blue when mature. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Vaccinium schimperi is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential as a food source in its native region.
