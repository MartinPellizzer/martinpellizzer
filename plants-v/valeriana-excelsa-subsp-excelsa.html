Valeriana excelsa subsp. excelsa

Valeriana excelsa subsp.

excelsa, commonly known as the tall valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a robust root system and erect, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically pink or white, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a strong, aromatic odor. Valeriana excelsa subsp.

excelsa is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
