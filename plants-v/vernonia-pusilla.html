Vernonia pusilla

Vernonia pusilla, commonly known as dwarf ironweed, is a small annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of less than 12 inches and features narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
