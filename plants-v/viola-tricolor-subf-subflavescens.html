Viola tricolor subf. subflavescens

Viola tricolor subf.

subflavescens, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from yellow to purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its lighter floral coloration compared to the typical Viola tricolor.
