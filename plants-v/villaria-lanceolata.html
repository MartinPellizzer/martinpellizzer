Villaria lanceolata

Villaria lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf villaria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hence the species name, and are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.
