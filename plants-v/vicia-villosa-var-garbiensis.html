Vicia villosa var. garbiensis

Vicia villosa var. garbiensis, commonly known as the Garbienskaya vicia, is a variant of the common vetch.

It is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia, characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, twisted pods containing several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides.

This variety is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
