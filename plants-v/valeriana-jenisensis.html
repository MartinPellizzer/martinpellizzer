Valeriana jenisensis

Valeriana jenisensis, commonly known as the Java valerian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana jenisensis is valued both for its ecological role and its potential pharmacological applications.
