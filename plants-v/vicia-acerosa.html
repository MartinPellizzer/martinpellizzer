Vicia acerosa

Vicia acerosa, commonly known as steel-vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features slender, erect stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small, pea-like, and usually range in color from purple to pink, appearing in clusters.

Vicia acerosa is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
