Verbascum lusitanicum

Verbascum lusitanicum, commonly known as the Portuguese mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are broad and velvety, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Verbascum lusitanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
