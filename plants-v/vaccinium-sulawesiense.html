Vaccinium sulawesiense

Vaccinium sulawesiense, commonly known as the Sulawesi blueberry, is a species native to the Indonesian island of Sulawesi.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its fruit, which is edible, is a bright blue or purple berry that contains a high concentration of antioxidants and is valued for its nutritional properties.

This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical forest environments.
