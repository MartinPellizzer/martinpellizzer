Vernonia conzattii

Vernonia conzattii, commonly known as the Conzattii ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, dark purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia conzattii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
