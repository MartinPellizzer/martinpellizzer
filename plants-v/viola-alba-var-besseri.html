Viola alba var. besseri

Viola alba var. besseri, commonly known as the Bessera violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces delicate, pale violet flowers with yellow markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
