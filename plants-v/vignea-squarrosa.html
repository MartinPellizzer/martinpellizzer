Vignea squarrosa

Vignea squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from purple to blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a distinctive, spiral-shaped seedpod. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Vignea squarrosa is cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
