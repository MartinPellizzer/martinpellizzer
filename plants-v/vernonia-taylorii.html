Vernonia taylorii

Vernonia taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

Vernonia taylorii is often found in dry grasslands, rocky slopes, and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
