Viola cenisia var. vestita

Viola cenisia var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in early spring. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
