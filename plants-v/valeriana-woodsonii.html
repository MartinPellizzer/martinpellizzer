Valeriana woodsonii

Valeriana woodsonii, commonly known as the Appalachian valerian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Valeriana woodsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
