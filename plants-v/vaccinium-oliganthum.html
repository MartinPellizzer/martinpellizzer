Vaccinium oliganthum

Vaccinium oliganthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.

Vaccinium oliganthum plays an important ecological role, providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
