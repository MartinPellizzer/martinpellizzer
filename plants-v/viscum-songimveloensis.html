Viscum songimveloensis

Viscum songimveloensis, commonly known as the Songimvelo mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees, primarily in the region of South Africa.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and white, gelatinous berries that contain toxic seeds. The plant derives some of its nutrients from its host through specialized roots called haustoria, while also performing photosynthesis.

Viscum songimveloensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain bird species that consume its berries.
