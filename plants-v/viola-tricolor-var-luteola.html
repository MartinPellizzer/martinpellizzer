Viola tricolor var. luteola

Viola tricolor var. luteola, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, three-colored flowers that typically display purple, white, and yellow. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola tricolor var. luteola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
