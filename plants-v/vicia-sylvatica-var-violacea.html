Vicia sylvatica var. violacea

Vicia sylvatica var. violacea, commonly known as the violet vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive violet hue. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
