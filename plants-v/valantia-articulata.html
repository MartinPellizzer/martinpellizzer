Valantia articulata

Valantia articulata, commonly known as the articulated valantia, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its segmented, branching structure.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant features elongated, flexible stems with small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water resistance. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with floating seeds that facilitate dispersal across water surfaces.

Valantia articulata plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
