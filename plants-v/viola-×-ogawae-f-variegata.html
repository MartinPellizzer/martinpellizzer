Viola × ogawae f. variegata

Viola × ogawae f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated violet, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which often display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in early spring. Native to Japan, it thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a visually appealing and easy-to-care-for plant.
