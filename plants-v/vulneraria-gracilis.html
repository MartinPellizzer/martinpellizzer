Vulneraria gracilis

Vulneraria gracilis, commonly known as the slender woundwort, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and often have a glandular surface.

Vulneraria gracilis is known for its medicinal properties, historically used for treating wounds and skin ailments.
