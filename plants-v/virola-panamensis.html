Virola panamensis

Virola panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in panicles, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing several seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, though it is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
