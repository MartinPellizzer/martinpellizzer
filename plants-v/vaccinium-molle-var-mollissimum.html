Vaccinium molle var. mollissimum

Vaccinium molle var. mollissimum, commonly known as the soft blueberry, is a variant of the lowbush blueberry species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, bright blue berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential for cultivation in gardens and commercial settings.
