Vitis flexuosa var. flexuosa

Vitis flexuosa var. flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible grapevine, is a vine species native to North America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to climb using tendrils. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by black or purple berries in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
