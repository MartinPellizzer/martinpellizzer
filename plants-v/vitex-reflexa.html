Vitex reflexa

Vitex reflexa, commonly known as the Indian Vitex or velvetwood, is a shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Vitex reflexa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
