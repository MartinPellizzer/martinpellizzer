Veltheimia pumila

Veltheimia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf veltheimia, is a small, evergreen bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette, and it produces delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It is relatively low maintenance and thrives in mild climates, though it may require protection in colder regions.

Its compact size and elegant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
