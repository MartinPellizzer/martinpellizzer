Vernonia herbertii

Vernonia herbertii, commonly known as Herbert's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia herbertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
