Vereia ceratophylla

Vereia ceratophylla, commonly known as the horned violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, slightly hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Its habitat includes moist meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, where it thrives in partial shade.

Vereia ceratophylla is often found in calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
