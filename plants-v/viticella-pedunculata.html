Viticella pedunculata

Viticella pedunculata, commonly known as the grape ivy, is a vigorous climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in partial to full sun and is often used in vertical gardening due to its fast growth and ability to cover trellises or walls.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor ornamental purposes.
