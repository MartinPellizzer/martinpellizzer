Veronica × handelii

Veronica × handelii, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10–20 cm and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Veronica × handelii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
