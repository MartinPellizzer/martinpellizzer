Virola elongata var. punctata

Virola elongata var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted virola, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark with distinctive white or light-colored spots. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

Its fruits are large, woody capsules that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.
