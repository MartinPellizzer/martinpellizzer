Vittaria curvidentata

Vittaria curvidentata, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a rare and delicate epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, zigzagging fronds that give it its common name. The plant has small, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a distinctive curvature, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and indirect light.

Vittaria curvidentata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare plants.
