Vaccinium santanense

Vaccinium santanense, commonly known as the Santa Ana huckleberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, typically found in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Vaccinium santanense plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
