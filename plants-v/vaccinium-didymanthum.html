Vaccinium didymanthum

Vaccinium didymanthum, commonly known as the dwarf huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants, and it typically thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local culinary traditions. Its foliage consists of oval, dark green leaves that are densely packed along its stems.

Vaccinium didymanthum is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
