Viguieranthus densinervus var. densinervus

Viguieranthus densinervus var. densinervus, commonly known as the dense-veined viguiera, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with prominent, dense veins, giving the plant its scientific name. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

The plant is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
