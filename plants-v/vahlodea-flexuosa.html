Vahlodea flexuosa

Vahlodea flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible vahlodea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and slender, often twisting stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural applications.
