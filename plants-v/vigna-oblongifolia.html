Vigna oblongifolia

Vigna oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and the seeds are edible, often used in culinary applications.

Vigna oblongifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
