Viburnum nitidum

Viburnum nitidum, commonly known as the glossyleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that remain glossy throughout the growing season. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Viburnum nitidum is also valued for its adaptability and resistance to pests and diseases.
