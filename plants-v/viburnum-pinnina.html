Viburnum pinnina

Viburnum pinnina, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red berries that are attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage, flowers, and fruit, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
