Vernonia calvoana var. mokaensis

Vernonia calvoana var. mokaensis, commonly known as Moka vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the highland regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellowish-green, often arranged in dense clusters at the ends of branches.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
