Verbascum acuminatum

Verbascum acuminatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Verbascum acuminatum is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for respiratory ailments.
