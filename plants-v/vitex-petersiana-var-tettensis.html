Vitex petersiana var. tettensis

Vitex petersiana var. tettensis, commonly known as the Tettens' vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
