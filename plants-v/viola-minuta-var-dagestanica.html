Viola minuta var. dagestanica

Viola minuta var. dagestanica, commonly known as the Dagestani dwarf violet, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the Caucasus region.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental species. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
