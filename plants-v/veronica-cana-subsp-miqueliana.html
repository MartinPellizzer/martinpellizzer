Veronica cana subsp. miqueliana

Veronica cana subsp.

miqueliana, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to violet, and borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
