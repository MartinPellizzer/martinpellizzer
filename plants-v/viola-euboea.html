Viola euboea

Viola euboea, commonly known as the Euboean violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the island of Euboea in Greece.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded areas. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Viola euboea is also noted for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional remedies.
