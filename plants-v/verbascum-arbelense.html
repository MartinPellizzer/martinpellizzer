Verbascum arbelense

Verbascum arbelense, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or semi-arid environments.

Verbascum arbelense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
