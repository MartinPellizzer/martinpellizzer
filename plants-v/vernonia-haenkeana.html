Vernonia haenkeana

Vernonia haenkeana, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with gray-green, hairy leaves. Its showy blue to purple flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia haenkeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
