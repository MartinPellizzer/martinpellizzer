Vicia amoena var. angusta

Vicia amoena var. angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods contain several small, dark brown seeds. Native to Europe, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This variety is well-suited for gardens and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
