Viscago fimbriata

Viscago fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest aquatic invertebrates. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor waters, where it relies on its specialized adaptations to obtain essential nutrients. Its leaves are thin and often covered in tiny, hair-like structures that aid in capturing prey.

Viscago fimbriata plays an important ecological role in its environment by controlling insect populations and contributing to the aquatic food web.
