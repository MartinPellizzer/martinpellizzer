Vachellia nilotica subsp. tomentosa

Vachellia nilotica subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has a woody, thorny stem and produces small, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and along riverbanks.

The silky acacia is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
