Viola nipponica

Viola nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola nipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
