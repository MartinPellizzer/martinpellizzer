Viola × bezdelevae

Viola × bezdelevae, commonly known as the Bezdeleva violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers often have distinctive markings. Native to Central and Eastern Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
