Veronica humile

Veronica humile, commonly known as the small blueweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
