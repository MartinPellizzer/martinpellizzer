Vitis landuk

Vitis landuk, commonly known as the Philippine grapevine, is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils that help it climb supports. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its vines can grow quite long.

Vitis landuk is often found in forested areas and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
