Viola × intricata

Viola × intricata, commonly known as the Dutch violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from purple to white. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Viola × intricata is often used in gardens and as a bedding plant due to its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
