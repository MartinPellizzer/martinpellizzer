Viola × valentiana

Viola × valentiana, commonly known as the heartleaf viola, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or gardens. It produces small, seed-filled pods that mature in late summer.

Viola × valentiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its vibrant blooms and foliage.
