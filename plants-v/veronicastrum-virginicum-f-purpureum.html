Veronicastrum virginicum f. purpureum

Veronicastrum virginicum f. purpureum, commonly known as purple giant hyssop, is a striking perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spires of lavender-pink to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are opposite along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
