Viola japonica f. albida

Viola japonica f. albida, commonly known as the Japanese violet or white viola, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and striking white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodland areas or gardens. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The f. albida form is distinguished by its predominantly white petals, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
