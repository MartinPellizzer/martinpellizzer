Vernonia obtusa subsp. parkeri

Vernonia obtusa subsp.

parkeri, commonly known as the Narrowleaf Ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to brownish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
