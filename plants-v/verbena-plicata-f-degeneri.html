Verbena plicata f. degeneri

Verbena plicata f. degeneri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf verbena, is a compact, semi-evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
