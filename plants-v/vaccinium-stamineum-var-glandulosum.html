Vaccinium stamineum var. glandulosum

Vaccinium stamineum var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular blueberry, is a variant of the blueberry plant native to North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in forested areas. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate and have a slightly hairy texture, which is characteristic of the glandular variant.

The flowers are white and bell-shaped, typical of the Ericaceae family.
