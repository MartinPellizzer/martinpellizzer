Vernonia pseudobirmanica

Vernonia pseudobirmanica, commonly known as the false birmanica ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the dry season.

Vernonia pseudobirmanica is often found in moist, open habitats such as forest edges and grasslands.
