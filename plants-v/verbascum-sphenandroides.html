Verbascum sphenandroides

Verbascum sphenandroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, woolly leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Verbascum sphenandroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
