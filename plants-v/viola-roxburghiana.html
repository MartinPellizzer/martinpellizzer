Viola roxburghiana

Viola roxburghiana, commonly known as the Himalayan violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows. Viola roxburghiana is part of the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.
