Vernonia phlomoides

Vernonia phlomoides, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy foliage and tall, spiky flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Vernonia phlomoides is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
