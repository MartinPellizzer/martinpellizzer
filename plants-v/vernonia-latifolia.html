Vernonia latifolia

Vernonia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Vernonia latifolia is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
