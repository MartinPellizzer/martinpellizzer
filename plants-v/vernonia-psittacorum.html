Vernonia psittacorum

Vernonia psittacorum, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands, and it produces bright yellow blooms in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
