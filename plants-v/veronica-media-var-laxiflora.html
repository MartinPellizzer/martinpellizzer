Veronica media var. laxiflora

Veronica media var. laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are slender and often branched. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
