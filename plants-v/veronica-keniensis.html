Veronica keniensis

Veronica keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan speedwell, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Veronica keniensis is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
