Viscum cryptophlebium var. lanceolatum

Viscum cryptophlebium var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its berries are orange-red and contain sticky seeds that adhere to birds, facilitating seed dispersal.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is notable for its ecological role in supporting birdlife.
