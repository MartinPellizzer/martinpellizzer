Viola philippica f. intermedia

Viola philippica f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
