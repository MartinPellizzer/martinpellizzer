Vepris aralioides

Vepris aralioides, commonly known as the African laurier or African cinnamon, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and aromatic. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
