Viburnum opulus f. hydrangeoides

Viburnum opulus f. hydrangeoides, commonly known as the hydrangea-leaf snowball bush, is a compact shrub characterized by its showy, spherical inflorescences.

It features large, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and a pale underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red, persistent berries. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its foliage and floral display make it a valuable addition to ornamental gardens.
