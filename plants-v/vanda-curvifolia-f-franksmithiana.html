Vanda curvifolia f. franksmithiana

Vanda curvifolia f. franksmithiana, commonly known as the Frank Smith's vanda, is a hybrid orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters of 3 to 5. The plant has long, strap-like leaves and is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
