Vincetoxicum sieboldii

Vincetoxicum sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that appear in the spring, followed by bright purple berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are toxic to humans and livestock, so care should be taken when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
