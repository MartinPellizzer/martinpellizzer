Viola × tenuis

Viola × tenuis, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, typically purple or violet flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland and meadow habitats. Viola × tenuis is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
