Vernonia sagasteguii

Vernonia sagasteguii, commonly known as the Peruvian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in summer, and it is known for its hardy, woody stems.

Vernonia sagasteguii is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological significance in its native range.
