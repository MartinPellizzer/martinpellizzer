Vicia sativa var. linearis

Vicia sativa var. linearis, commonly known as narrowleaf vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It has a taproot system and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
