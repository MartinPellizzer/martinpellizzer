Viola scanica

Viola scanica, commonly known as the Swedish violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with yellow markings. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola scanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
