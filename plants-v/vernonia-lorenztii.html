Vernonia lorenztii

Vernonia lorenztii, commonly known as the Lorentz's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia lorenztii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
