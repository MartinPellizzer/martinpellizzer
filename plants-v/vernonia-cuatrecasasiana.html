Vernonia cuatrecasasiana

Vernonia cuatrecasasiana, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas's ironweed, is a flowering plant species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia cuatrecasasiana is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
