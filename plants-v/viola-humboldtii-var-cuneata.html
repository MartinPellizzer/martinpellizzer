Viola humboldtii var. cuneata

Viola humboldtii var. cuneata, commonly known as the Humboldt violet, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and vibrant violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often featuring a distinctive yellow or white marking.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
