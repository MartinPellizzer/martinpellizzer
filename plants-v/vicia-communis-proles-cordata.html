Vicia communis proles cordata

Vicia communis proles cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides. It is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with slender, hairy stems.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread in various habitats.
