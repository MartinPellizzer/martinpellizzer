Verbesina petrophila

Verbesina petrophila, commonly known as the rock coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Verbesina petrophila is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.
