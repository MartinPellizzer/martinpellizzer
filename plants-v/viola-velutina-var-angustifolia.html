Viola velutina var. angustifolia

Viola velutina var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with five petals, often marked with yellow or white markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
