Vaccaria hispanica subsp. grandiflora

Vaccaria hispanica subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the Spanish catchfly, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, branching stems and produces small, round, red or black seeds in its capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
