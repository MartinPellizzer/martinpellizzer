Vernonia greggii subsp. faustiana

Vernonia greggii subsp.

faustiana, commonly known as Gregg's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in rocky soils and desert habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for pollinators and a valuable component of native plant landscapes.
