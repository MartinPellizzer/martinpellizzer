Verbascum ergin-hamzaoglui

Verbascum ergin-hamzaoglui, commonly known as the Ergin-Hamzaoglui mullein, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes many species of mullein known for their fuzzy leaves and showy flowers. This particular species is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
