Valeriana cusickii

Valeriana cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant, leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, such as meadows and woodlands. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and are usually pink or white in color.

Valeriana cusickii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.
