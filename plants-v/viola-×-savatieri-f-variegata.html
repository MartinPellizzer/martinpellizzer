Viola × savatieri f. variegata

Viola × savatieri f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated violets, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegation pattern of white and green. The plant produces showy violet flowers in shades of purple and blue, typically in spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
