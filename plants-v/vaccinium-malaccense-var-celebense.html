Vaccinium malaccense var. celebense

Vaccinium malaccense var. celebense, commonly known as the Celebes huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cuisines.
