Viburnum rafinesqueanum

Viburnum rafinesqueanum, commonly known as the Allegheny blackhaw, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that turn bright red in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. Viburnum rafinesqueanum typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
