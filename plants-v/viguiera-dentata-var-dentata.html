Viguiera dentata var. dentata

Viguiera dentata var. dentata, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a grayish-white fuzzy layer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and native gardens.

Viguiera dentata var. dentata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
