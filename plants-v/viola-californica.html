Viola californica

Viola californica, commonly known as the California violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, violet-colored flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It has a fibrous root system and spreads via rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

Viola californica is adapted to mild, temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions and mountainous areas.
