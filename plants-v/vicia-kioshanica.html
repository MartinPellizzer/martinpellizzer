Vicia kioshanica

Vicia kioshanica, commonly known as the Kioshan vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Vicia kioshanica is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and can thrive in temperate climates.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
