Viola canina var. subacaulis

Viola canina var. subacaulis, commonly known as the slender violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and subshrubby structure.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and conservation efforts.
