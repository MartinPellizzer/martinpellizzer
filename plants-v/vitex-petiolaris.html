Vitex petiolaris

Vitex petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chaste tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or white, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a dry, two-valved capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Vitex petiolaris is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
