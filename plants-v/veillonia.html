Veillonia

Veillonia, commonly known as the veillonia tree, is a rare and ancient plant species found in specific tropical regions.

It is characterized by its unique, waxy leaves that reflect sunlight, helping it thrive in high-light environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is known for its ability to absorb and store water, making it resilient in dry conditions.

Botanists study Veillonia for its potential ecological significance and medicinal properties.
