Vicia americana var. linearis

Vicia americana var. linearis, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in open fields, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, lens-shaped seed pods that contain several seeds.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
