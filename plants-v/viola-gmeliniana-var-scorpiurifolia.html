Viola gmeliniana var. scorpiurifolia

Viola gmeliniana var. scorpiurifolia, commonly known as the scorpiurifolia violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky habitats. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
