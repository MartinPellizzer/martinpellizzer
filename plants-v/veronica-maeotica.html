Veronica maeotica

Veronica maeotica, commonly known as the Crimean speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the steppes of Eastern Europe and western Siberia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of blue or violet flowers during the summer months. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Veronica maeotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
