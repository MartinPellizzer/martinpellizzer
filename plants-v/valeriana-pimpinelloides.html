Valeriana pimpinelloides

Valeriana pimpinelloides, commonly known as the lesser valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often strongly scented leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes and roots contain volatile oils and compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Valeriana pimpinelloides is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
