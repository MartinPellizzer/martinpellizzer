Verojuncus ensifolius

Verojuncus ensifolius, commonly known as the swordleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Verojuncus ensifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
