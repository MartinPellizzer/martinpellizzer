Vitex triflora

Vitex triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically violet or purple in color and bloom in the summer months. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
