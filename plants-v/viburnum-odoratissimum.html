Viburnum odoratissimum

Viburnum odoratissimum, commonly known as the sweet viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Viburnum odoratissimum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
