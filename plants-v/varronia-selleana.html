Varronia selleana

Varronia selleana, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Varronia selleana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
