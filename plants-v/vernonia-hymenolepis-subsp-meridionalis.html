Vernonia hymenolepis subsp. meridionalis

Vernonia hymenolepis subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa. It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, pale yellow to brownish flowers.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
