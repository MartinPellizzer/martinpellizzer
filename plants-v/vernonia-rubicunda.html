Vernonia rubicunda

Vernonia rubicunda, commonly known as the red Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its showy, violet-to-red flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia rubicunda is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
