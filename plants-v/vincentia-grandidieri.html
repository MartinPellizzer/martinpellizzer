Vincentia grandidieri

Vincentia grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar star bean, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the island's unique climate. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.
