Verbesina pinnata

Verbesina pinnata, commonly known as the golden beggar's-ticks, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a pinnate leaflet arrangement, giving it a unique appearance. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbesina pinnata is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
