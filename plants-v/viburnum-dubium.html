Viburnum dubium

Viburnum dubium, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. Viburnum dubium typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal conditions.
