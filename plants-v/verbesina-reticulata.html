Verbesina reticulata

Verbesina reticulata, commonly known as the yellow woodflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive reticulate pattern, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, native plant species.
