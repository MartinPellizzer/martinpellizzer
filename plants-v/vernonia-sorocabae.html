Vernonia sorocabae

Vernonia sorocabae, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its yellow to orange flower heads are composed of many small florets, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
