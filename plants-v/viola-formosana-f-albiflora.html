Viola formosana f. albiflora

Viola formosana f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Chinese violet, is a rare and striking cultivar of the viola genus.

It is characterized by its pale white to almost ivory-colored flowers, which contrast with its deep green, heart-shaped leaves. Native to China, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate beauty to gardens and natural landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated by horticulturists and botanists.
