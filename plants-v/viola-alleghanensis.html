Viola alleghanensis

Viola alleghanensis, commonly known as the Allegheny violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and rocky soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and its flowers are usually violet or blue-violet in hue. Viola alleghanensis produces showy, five-petaled blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in naturalized gardens and conservation areas.
