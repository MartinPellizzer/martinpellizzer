Vilfa atrorubens

Vilfa atrorubens, commonly known as the red-brown vilfa, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with a rosette of dark green, hairy leaves in its first year. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers in its second year. Its foliage and inflorescence often exhibit a reddish hue, which intensifies with age.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
