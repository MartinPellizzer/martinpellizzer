Vernonia pseudomyrtus

Vernonia pseudomyrtus, commonly known as the false myrtle, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
