Valeriana ferax

Valeriana ferax, commonly known as wild valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Valeriana ferax is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat insomnia and anxiety.
