Vicia obcordata

Vicia obcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It has a climbing habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Vicia obcordata is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
