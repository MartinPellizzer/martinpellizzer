Veronica spicata var. barrelieri

Veronica spicata var. barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's speedwell, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, spike-like inflorescences composed of small blue or violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its compact growth habit.
