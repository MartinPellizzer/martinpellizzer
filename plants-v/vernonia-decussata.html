Vernonia decussata

Vernonia decussata, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The botanical characteristics of Vernonia decussata make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
