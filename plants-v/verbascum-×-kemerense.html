Verbascum × kemerense

Verbascum × kemerense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions in the Mediterranean and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Verbascum × kemerense thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
