Vilfa vinealis

Vilfa vinealis, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a climbing plant characterized by its slender, flexible stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in the spring. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
