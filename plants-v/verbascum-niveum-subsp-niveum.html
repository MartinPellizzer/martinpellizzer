Verbascum niveum subsp. niveum

Verbascum niveum subsp.

niveum, commonly known as the white mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for respiratory and skin ailments.
