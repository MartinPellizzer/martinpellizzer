Viola aethnensis subsp. messanensis

Viola aethnensis subsp.

messanensis, commonly known as the Sicilian violet, is a perennial herb native to southern Italy. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture and conservation efforts.
