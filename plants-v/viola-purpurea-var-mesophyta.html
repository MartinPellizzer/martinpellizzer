Viola purpurea var. mesophyta

Viola purpurea var. mesophyta, commonly known as the common violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often being more prominent and spurred.

Viola purpurea var. mesophyta is native to Europe and has been naturalized in many parts of North America.
