Vernonia alboviridis

Vernonia alboviridis, commonly known as the white-veined ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a preference for well-drained soils in full sun.

Vernonia alboviridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
