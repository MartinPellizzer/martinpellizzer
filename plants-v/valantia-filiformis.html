Valantia filiformis

Valantia filiformis, commonly known as the slender valantia, is a rare flowering plant native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant adaptation.

Due to habitat loss, Valantia filiformis is considered endangered and is often found in conservation programs.
