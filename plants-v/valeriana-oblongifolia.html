Valeriana oblongifolia

Valeriana oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in terminal spikes, which bloom in late summer. Valeriana oblongifolia is known for its aromatic foliage and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to rocky and arid environments.
