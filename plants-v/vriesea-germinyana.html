Vriesea germinyana

Vriesea germinyana, commonly known as the Guiana Skyflower, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for the plant's nourishment. Its flowers are typically red or orange with yellow stripes, adding a dramatic visual impact to its habitat.

Vriesea germinyana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
