Vitis muricata

Vitis muricata, commonly known as the bristly grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its hairy, bristly stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers give way to berries that are typically edible and used in local cuisines.

Vitis muricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in tropical regions.
