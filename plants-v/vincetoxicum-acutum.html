Vincetoxicum acutum

Vincetoxicum acutum, commonly known as the sharpleaf false bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, lobed leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces cylindrical, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain regions.
