Vriesea edmundoi

Vriesea edmundoi, commonly known as the Edmund's Vriesea, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescence that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.

Vriesea edmundoi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
