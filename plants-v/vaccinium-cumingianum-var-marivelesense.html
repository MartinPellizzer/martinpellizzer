Vaccinium cumingianum var. marivelesense

Vaccinium cumingianum var. marivelesense, commonly known as the Mariveles blueberry, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its berries are edible, with a tart flavor, and are often used in local cuisines.

This variety thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
