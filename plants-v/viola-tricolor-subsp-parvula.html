Viola tricolor subsp. parvula

Viola tricolor subsp.

parvula, commonly known as the small-flowered violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, colorful flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet, blue, and white.

The plant prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
