Vernonia purpurea

Vernonia purpurea, commonly known as purpletop vervain, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Vernonia purpurea is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
