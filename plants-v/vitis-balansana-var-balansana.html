Vitis balansana var. balansana

Vitis balansana var. balansana, commonly known as the Balansan grapevine, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, often sour-tasting berries. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is characterized by its vigorous growth habit. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in humid environments with ample sunlight.

The fruit of this species is sometimes used in local cuisines or for traditional medicinal purposes.
