Viola pulla

Viola pulla, commonly known as the black violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black flowers that bloom in early spring. Viola pulla typically grows in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
