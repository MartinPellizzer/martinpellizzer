Veronica friesii

Veronica friesii, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in cold, high-altitude environments.

Veronica friesii is often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
