Vincetoxicum suberosum

Vincetoxicum suberosum, commonly known as the rough false bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread across various habitats.
