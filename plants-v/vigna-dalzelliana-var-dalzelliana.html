Vigna dalzelliana var. dalzelliana

Vigna dalzelliana var. dalzelliana, commonly known as the Dalzell's pigeon pea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features pinnately compound leaves with oval, serrated leaflets and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant bears pods that contain several round, yellow seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to poor soils.

This species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
