Vaccinium setipes

Vaccinium setipes, commonly known as the slender blueberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, typical of many Ericaceae species.

The berries, which are edible, are usually dark blue or black when mature and are valued for their flavor and nutritional content.
