Vernonia acutangula

Vernonia acutangula, commonly known as the sharp-angled ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for native plant gardens.

Vernonia acutangula is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
