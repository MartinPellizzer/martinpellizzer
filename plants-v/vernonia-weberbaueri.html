Vernonia weberbaueri

Vernonia weberbaueri, commonly known as Weberbauer's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a grayish-green hue, while its stems are woody and branched.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
