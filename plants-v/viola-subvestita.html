Viola subvestita

Viola subvestita, commonly known as the velvet leaf violet, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola subvestita is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
