Vaccinium pratense

Vaccinium pratense, commonly known as sheep laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries.

Vaccinium pratense is often found in acidic soils in woodlands and heathlands, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
