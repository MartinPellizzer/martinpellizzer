Vitis erythrophylla

Vitis erythrophylla, commonly known as the redleaf grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered leaves that are typically reddish in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round berries.

Vitis erythrophylla is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soils in harsh conditions.
