Veronica sparsiflora

Veronica sparsiflora, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed capsules that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Veronica sparsiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
