Vitex vestita f. quinquefoliolata

Vitex vestita f. quinquefoliolata, commonly known as the five-leaf chaste tree, is a compact shrub native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are distinctive, with five leaflets arranged in a pinnate pattern, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
