Vernonia pellegrinii

Vernonia pellegrinii, commonly known as the Pellegrin's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces vibrant purple to reddish-orange flower clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Vernonia pellegrinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
