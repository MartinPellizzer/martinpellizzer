Vitis kaempferi var. glabrescens

Vitis kaempferi var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Japanese grape or glabrous grape, is a vine species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, sweet, edible berries. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and its flowers are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a fruit-bearing plant.

The variety 'glabrescens' is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to other varieties of Vitis kaempferi.
