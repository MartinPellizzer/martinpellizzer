Valeriana ledoides

Valeriana ledoides, commonly known as the swamp valerian, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, herbaceous stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in marshes and along stream banks.

Valeriana ledoides is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
