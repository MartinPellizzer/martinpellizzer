Vaccinium viburnoides

Vaccinium viburnoides, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other ericaceous plants like heathers and blueberries. The plant features small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. Its berries, which are typically blue or black when mature, are edible and have a tart flavor.

The shrub thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, woodland environments.
