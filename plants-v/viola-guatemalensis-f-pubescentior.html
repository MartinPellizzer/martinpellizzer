Viola guatemalensis f. pubescentior

Viola guatemalensis f. pubescentior, commonly known as the pubescent viola, is a variant of the Guatemalan violet characterized by its dense pubescence.

This plant is native to Central America and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with yellow centers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
