Vitex grandidiana

Vitex grandidiana, commonly known as the Mexican lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves and showy, fragrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Vitex grandidiana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
