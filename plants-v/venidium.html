Venidium

Venidium, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species native to the Americas, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions. The plants are characterized by their shrub-like growth habit, with small, often yellow or golden flowers. Their leaves are typically compound, and the pods that develop from the flowers are usually hairy and contain seeds.

Venidium species are adapted to dry environments and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
