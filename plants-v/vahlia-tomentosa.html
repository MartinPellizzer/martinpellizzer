Vahlia tomentosa

Vahlia tomentosa, commonly known as the silky vahlia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, white to greyish pubescence covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Vahlia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
