Viola pinnata var. angustisecta

Viola pinnata var. angustisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are deeply lobed and narrow, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in rich soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
