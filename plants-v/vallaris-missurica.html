Vallaris missurica

Vallaris missurica, commonly known as the Missourian vallaris, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polemoniaceae family.

It is native to the central United States, particularly Missouri, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Vallaris missurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
