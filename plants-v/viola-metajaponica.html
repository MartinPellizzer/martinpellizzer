Viola metajaponica

Viola metajaponica, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Viola.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan and Korea, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or mountainous regions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola metajaponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
