Vitex novae-pommeraniae

Vitex novae-pommeraniae, commonly known as the New Pommerania vitex, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Germany and northern France.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to violet, blooming in the summer months. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Vitex novae-pommeraniae is often found in sandy or calcareous soils along the coastline.
