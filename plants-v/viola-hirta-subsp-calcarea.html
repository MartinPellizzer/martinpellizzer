Viola hirta subsp. calcarea

Viola hirta subsp.

calcarea, commonly known as the limestone violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and calcareous soils in Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, often with a distinctive yellowish or white throat. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
