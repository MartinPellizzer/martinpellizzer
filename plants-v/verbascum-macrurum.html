Verbascum macrurum

Verbascum macrurum, commonly known as the tall mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety gray-green leaves that are covered in dense, soft hairs. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Verbascum macrurum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory conditions.
