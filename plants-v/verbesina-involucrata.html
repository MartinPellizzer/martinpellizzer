Verbesina involucrata

Verbesina involucrata, commonly known as golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The plant produces fluffy, seed-filled spheres that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.
