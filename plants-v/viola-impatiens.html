Viola impatiens

Viola impatiens, commonly known as the common violet, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as a garden ornamental and is popular for its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

Viola impatiens is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
