Veronica aberdarica

Veronica aberdarica, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette.

Veronica aberdarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine plantings.
