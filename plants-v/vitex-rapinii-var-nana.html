Vitex rapinii var. nana

Vitex rapinii var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf chaste tree, is a compact shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and as a decorative plant in temperate climates.
