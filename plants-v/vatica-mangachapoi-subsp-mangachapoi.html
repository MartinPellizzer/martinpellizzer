Vatica mangachapoi subsp. mangachapoi

Vatica mangachapoi subsp.

mangachapoi, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and evergreen foliage.

The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas, reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in spikes, and it produces elliptical, woody fruits.

The species is valued for its durable timber but is also threatened by habitat loss and overharvesting.
