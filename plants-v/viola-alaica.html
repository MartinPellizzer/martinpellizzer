Viola alaica

Viola alaica, commonly known as the Alaskan violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Alaska and northern Canada.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola alaica is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
