Viola sororia f. priceana

Viola sororia f. priceana, commonly known as the Price's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its foliage is often adorned with distinctive markings, and it produces showy, spurred petals typical of the Violaceae family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
