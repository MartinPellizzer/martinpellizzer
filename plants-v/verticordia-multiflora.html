Verticordia multiflora

Verticordia multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive, clustered inflorescences that produce numerous small, waxy flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant typically blooms from late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to the landscape during this period. It prefers well-drained sandy soils and full sun, making it well-suited to coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
