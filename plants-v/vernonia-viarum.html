Vernonia viarum

Vernonia viarum, commonly known as the rough vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are rough to the touch and often have a grayish-green color.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late summer and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
