Valeriana volubilis

Valeriana volubilis, commonly known as creeping valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit, producing clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its sedative properties.

Valeriana volubilis is also an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
