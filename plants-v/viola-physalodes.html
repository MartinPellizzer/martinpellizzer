Viola physalodes

Viola physalodes, commonly known as the Crown Vetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a creeping habit, with stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread aggressively. Its fibrous root system makes it effective for soil stabilization and erosion control.

Viola physalodes is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
