Viridivia

Viridivia, commonly known as the emerald leaf vine, is a tropical plant characterized by its glossy, deep green foliage.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is native to regions of South America. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Viridivia is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
