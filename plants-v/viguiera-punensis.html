Viguiera punensis

Viguiera punensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features sturdy, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

Viguiera punensis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
