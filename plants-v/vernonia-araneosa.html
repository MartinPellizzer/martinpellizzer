Vernonia araneosa

Vernonia araneosa, commonly known as the spider vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a spider's web, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.
