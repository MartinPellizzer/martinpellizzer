Vernonia tephrodoides

Vernonia tephrodoides, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinctive grayish-green color.

Vernonia tephrodoides is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
