Vepris noldeae

Vepris noldeae, commonly known as the Madagascar tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
