Vigna katangensis

Vigna katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several round, edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

Vigna katangensis is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional cuisines across its native regions.
