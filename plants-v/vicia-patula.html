Vicia patula

Vicia patula, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Vicia patula is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.

Its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules also contributes to soil fertility.
