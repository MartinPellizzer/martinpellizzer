Vitis deliciosa

Vitis deliciosa, commonly known as the muscadine grape, is a vigorous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, dark purple or black berries. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often climbing supports such as fences or trellises. Its berries are known for their high antioxidant content and distinct flavor.

Vitis deliciosa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in both commercial and home gardening settings.
