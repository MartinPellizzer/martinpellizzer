Viola linifolia

Viola linifolia, commonly known as the slender violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, showy violet flowers in shades of purple and blue. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola linifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
