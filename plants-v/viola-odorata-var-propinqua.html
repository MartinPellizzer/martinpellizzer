Viola odorata var. propinqua

Viola odorata var. propinqua, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland areas. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
