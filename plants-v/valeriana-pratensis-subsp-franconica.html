Valeriana pratensis subsp. franconica

Valeriana pratensis subsp.

franconica, commonly known as Franconian valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white. Its roots contain volatile oils and are used in traditional medicine for their sedative properties.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
