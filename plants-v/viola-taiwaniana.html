Viola taiwaniana

Viola taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan violet, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola taiwaniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
