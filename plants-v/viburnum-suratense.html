Viburnum suratense

Viburnum suratense, commonly known as the Surat viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant produces dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Viburnum suratense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
