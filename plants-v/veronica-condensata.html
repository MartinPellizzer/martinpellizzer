Veronica condensata

Veronica condensata, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and typically grows in moist, calcareous soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually blue to purple in color.

Veronica condensata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
