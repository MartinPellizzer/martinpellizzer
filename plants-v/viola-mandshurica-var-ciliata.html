Viola mandshurica var. ciliata

Viola mandshurica var. ciliata, commonly known as the Siberian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with hairy margins, and produces delicate violet flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its short, creeping stems and rhizomatous growth habit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
