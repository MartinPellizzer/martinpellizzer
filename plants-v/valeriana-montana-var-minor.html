Valeriana montana var. minor

Valeriana montana var. minor, commonly known as lesser mountain valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, adorned with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spike-like inflorescences. Its roots are rich in volatile oils and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their sedative properties.

Valeriana montana var. minor thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
