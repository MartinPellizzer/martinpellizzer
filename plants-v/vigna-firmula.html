Vigna firmula

Vigna firmula, commonly known as the small cowpea, is a species of legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, round, and edible seeds that are often used in various cuisines. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. Its pods are usually slender and contain several seeds.

Vigna firmula is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed.
