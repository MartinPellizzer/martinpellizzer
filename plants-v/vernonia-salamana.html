Vernonia salamana

Vernonia salamana, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow to reddish in color.

Vernonia salamana is known for its hardy nature and is often used in traditional medicine.
