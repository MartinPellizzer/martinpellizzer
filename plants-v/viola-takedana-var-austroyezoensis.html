Viola takedana var. austroyezoensis

Viola takedana var. austroyezoensis, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the spring season.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
