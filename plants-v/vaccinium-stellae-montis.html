Vaccinium stellae-montis

Vaccinium stellae-montis, commonly known as the starry mountain blueberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It produces small, dark blue berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, while its flowers are bell-shaped and typically pink or white.

This species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential use in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
