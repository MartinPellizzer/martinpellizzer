Vigna kirkii

Vigna kirkii, commonly known as the African bean or Kikuyu bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with pinnate leaves and small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Vigna kirkii is valued for its nutritional seeds and is sometimes cultivated for both food and traditional medicine.
