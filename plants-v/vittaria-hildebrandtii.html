Vittaria hildebrandtii

Vittaria hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or branches. The plant has delicate, pinnately divided leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Vittaria hildebrandtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
