Vernonia arborescens

Vernonia arborescens, commonly known as the tree ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of purple to pink bracteate flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Vernonia arborescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
