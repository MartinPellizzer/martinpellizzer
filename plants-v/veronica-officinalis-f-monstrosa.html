Veronica officinalis f. monstrosa

Veronica officinalis f. monstrosa, commonly known as the strange veronica, is a horticultural form of the common speedwell.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often twisted or curled foliage, which gives it a striking, unusual appearance. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically grown as a ornamental plant in gardens due to its unique foliage.

This form is a naturally occurring variant of the species, showcasing genetic mutations that result in its peculiar morphology.
