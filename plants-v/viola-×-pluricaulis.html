Viola × pluricaulis

Viola × pluricaulis, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the violet family, Violaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola × pluricaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in horticulture.
