Viola odorata f. albiflora

Viola odorata f. albiflora, commonly known as the sweet violet's white-flowered form, is a variant of the sweet violet characterized by its pale or white flowers instead of the typical purple.

This plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist, shady woodland areas. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its fragrant, heart-shaped leaves. The flowers typically bloom in early spring and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal remedies.
