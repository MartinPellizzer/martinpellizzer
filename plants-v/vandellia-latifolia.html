Vandellia latifolia

Vandellia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf vandellia, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
