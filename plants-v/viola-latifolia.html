Viola latifolia

Viola latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white, often with a distinctive yellow eye. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
