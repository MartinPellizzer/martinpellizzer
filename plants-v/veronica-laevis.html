Veronica laevis

Veronica laevis, commonly known as the smooth veronica, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
