Viola philippica subsp. malesica

Viola philippica subsp.

malesica, commonly known as the Philippine violet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and violet. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct morphology and is part of the Violaceae family.
