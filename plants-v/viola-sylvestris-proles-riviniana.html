Viola sylvestris proles riviniana

Viola sylvestris proles riviniana, commonly known as the river violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and woodland edges. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with a yellow center. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its short stature and early blooming period.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
