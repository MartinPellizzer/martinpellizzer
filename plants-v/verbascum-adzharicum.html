Verbascum adzharicum

Verbascum adzharicum, commonly known as the Caucasian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a terminal spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum adzharicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
