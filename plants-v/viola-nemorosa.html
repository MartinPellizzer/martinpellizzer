Viola nemorosa

Viola nemorosa, commonly known as the spring viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. It thrives in woodland environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Viola nemorosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and natural landscapes.
