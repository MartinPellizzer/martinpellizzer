Verbesina cinerascens

Verbesina cinerascens, commonly known as the ashleaf beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant has hairy, ash-gray leaves that are often lobed or divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

Verbesina cinerascens is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to diverse environments.
