Viola teplouchovii

Viola teplouchovii, commonly known as the Teplovich violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to high-altitude landscapes.

Viola teplouchovii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
