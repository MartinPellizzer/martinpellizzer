Vernonia bruceana

Vernonia bruceana, commonly known as Bruce's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia bruceana is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
