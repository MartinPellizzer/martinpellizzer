Veronica virginica f. purpurea

Veronica virginica f. purpurea, commonly known as purple speedwell, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its more intense purple flower color compared to the typical form of Veronica virginica.
