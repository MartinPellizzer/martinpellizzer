Vaccinium myrtilloides var. rigidum

Vaccinium myrtilloides var. rigidum, commonly known as the rigid blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

This variety is characterized by its stiff, upright stems, which distinguish it from other species within the Vaccinium genus.
