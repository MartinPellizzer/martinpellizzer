Vanhouttea mollis

Vanhouttea mollis, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
