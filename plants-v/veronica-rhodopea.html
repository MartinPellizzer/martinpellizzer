Veronica rhodopea

Veronica rhodopea, commonly known as the Rhodope speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Veronica rhodopea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
