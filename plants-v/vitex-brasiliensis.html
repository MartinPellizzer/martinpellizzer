Vitex brasiliensis

Vitex brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian vitex or Brazilian lilac, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, blue or purple flowers in panicles. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Vitex brasiliensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
