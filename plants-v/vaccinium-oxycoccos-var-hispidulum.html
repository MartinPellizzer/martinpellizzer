Vaccinium oxycoccos var. hispidulum

Vaccinium oxycoccos var. hispidulum, commonly known as the rough bilberry, is a low-growing shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in boreal and tundra environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a dense covering of fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
