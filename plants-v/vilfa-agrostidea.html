Vilfa agrostidea

Vilfa agrostidea, commonly known as the slender grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and green. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that contain both male and female flowers, typical of grass species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
