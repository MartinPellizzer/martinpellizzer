Vignea mucronata

Vignea mucronata, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Vignea mucronata is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.
