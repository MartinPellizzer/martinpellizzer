Vicia abbreviata subsp. balansae

Vicia abbreviata subsp.

balansae, commonly known as the Balansae pea, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along woodland edges. Vicia abbreviata subsp.

balansae is known for its ecological value, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivorous insects.
