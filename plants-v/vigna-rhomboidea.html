Vigna rhomboidea

Vigna rhomboidea, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

Vigna rhomboidea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
