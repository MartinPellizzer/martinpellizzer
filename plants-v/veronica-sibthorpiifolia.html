Veronica sibthorpiifolia

Veronica sibthorpiifolia, commonly known as the Sibthorpiifolia speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
