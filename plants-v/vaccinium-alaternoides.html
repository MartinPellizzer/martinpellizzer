Vaccinium alaternoides

Vaccinium alaternoides, commonly known as the alpine blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, blue-black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic value.
