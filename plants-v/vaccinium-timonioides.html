Vaccinium timonioides

Vaccinium timonioides, commonly known as the Timonioid highbush blueberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features include alternate, oval-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit.
