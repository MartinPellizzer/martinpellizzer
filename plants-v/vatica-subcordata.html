Vatica subcordata

Vatica subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas, often reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
