Vernonia rubricaulis var. plantaginoides

Vernonia rubricaulis var. plantaginoides, commonly known as the red-stemmed ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, dark purple to brownish-red flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically reddish or purple in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variant is distinguished by its compact growth and dense flowering heads, making it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
