Viola obtusa f. hemileuca

Viola obtusa f. hemileuca, commonly known as the pale viola, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
