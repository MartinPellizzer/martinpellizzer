Vernonia nebularum

Vernonia nebularum, commonly known as cloudflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its pale blue to violet flower heads. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia nebularum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
