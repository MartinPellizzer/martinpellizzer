Verbascum × praetutianum

Verbascum × praetutianum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their fuzzy, velvety leaves and showy yellow flowers. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, soft leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
