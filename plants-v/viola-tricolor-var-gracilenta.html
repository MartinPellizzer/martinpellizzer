Viola tricolor var. gracilenta

Viola tricolor var. gracilenta, commonly known as the slender violet, is a variant of the common violet with distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-20 cm, with stems that are slender and often hairy. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in garden settings.
