Vandellia anagallis var. verbenifolia

Vandellia anagallis var. verbenifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, pink or red flowers. The leaves are typically fleshy and covered in a fine, velvety texture, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
