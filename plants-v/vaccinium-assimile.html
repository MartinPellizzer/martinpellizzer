Vaccinium assimile

Vaccinium assimile, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like heather and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and remain green throughout the year, making it a desirable ornamental plant.
