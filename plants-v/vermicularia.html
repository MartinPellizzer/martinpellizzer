Vermicularia

Vermicularia, commonly known as the "liverworts," is a group of non-vascular plants that thrive in moist, shaded environments.

These plants typically have a simple structure with a thallus, which is a leaf-like body that lacks true roots, stems, or leaves. Vermicularia species are often found growing on soil, rocks, or decaying wood, and they play a vital role in nutrient cycling in their ecosystems. They reproduce through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporophytes.

Due to their small size and often cryptic growth habits, they are less noticeable but ecologically significant in many habitats.
