Vernonia vagans

Vernonia vagans, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in soft, blue-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its showy, blue-violet flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Vernonia vagans is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
