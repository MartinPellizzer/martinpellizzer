Vaccinium paddywoodsii

Vaccinium paddywoodsii, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy coating, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Vaccinium paddywoodsii is often found in wetlands, pine flatwoods, and other moist habitats.
