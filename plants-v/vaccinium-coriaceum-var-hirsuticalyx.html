Vaccinium coriaceum var. hirsuticalyx

Vaccinium coriaceum var. hirsuticalyx, commonly known as the hirsute trailing blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California.

It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "hirsuticalyx.". It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
