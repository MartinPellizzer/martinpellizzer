Veronica melincolica

Veronica melincolica, commonly known as the melancholy verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica melincolica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a nectar source for pollinators.
