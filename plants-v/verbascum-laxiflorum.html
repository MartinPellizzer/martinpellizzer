Verbascum laxiflorum

Verbascum laxiflorum, commonly known as the softleaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose, panicle-like arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Verbascum laxiflorum is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
