Vernonia trinervis

Vernonia trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped with three distinct veins, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
