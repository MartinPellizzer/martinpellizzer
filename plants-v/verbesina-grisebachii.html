Verbesina grisebachii

Verbesina grisebachii, commonly known as the gray verbesina, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features erect, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in cone-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Verbesina grisebachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
