Verbascum lydium

Verbascum lydium, commonly known as Lydian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum lydium is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
