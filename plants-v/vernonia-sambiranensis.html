Vernonia sambiranensis

Vernonia sambiranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its pale yellow to orange flowers appear in clusters, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Vernonia sambiranensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native environment.
