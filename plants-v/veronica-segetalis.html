Veronica segetalis

Veronica segetalis, commonly known as field speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue or violet, and are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Veronica segetalis is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils.
