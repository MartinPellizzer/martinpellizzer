Valeriana montana subsp. nemorensis

Valeriana montana subsp.

nemorensis, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is known for containing volatile oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming effects.
