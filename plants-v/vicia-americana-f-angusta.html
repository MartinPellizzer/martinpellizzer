Vicia americana f. angusta

Vicia americana f. angusta, commonly known as narrow-leaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep violet. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets, and its pods contain several small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ecological benefits, including nitrogen fixation and habitat for pollinators.
