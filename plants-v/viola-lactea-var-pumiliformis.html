Viola lactea var. pumiliformis

Viola lactea var. pumiliformis, commonly known as the dwarf milk violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically feature a white or pale violet color with a yellow center. The plant grows low to the ground, often forming a dense mat of foliage, and is native to certain regions of Europe. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, while its stems are prostrate and hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
