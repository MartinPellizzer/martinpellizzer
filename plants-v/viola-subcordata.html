Viola subcordata

Viola subcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, showy blooms in early spring.

Viola subcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
