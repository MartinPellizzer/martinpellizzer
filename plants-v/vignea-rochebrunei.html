Vignea rochebrunei

Vignea rochebrunei, commonly known as the Madagascar bean, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Vignea rochebrunei is valued for its nutritional content and is used in local cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
