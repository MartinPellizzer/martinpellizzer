Viola tricolor subsp. kitaibeliana

Viola tricolor subsp.

kitaibeliana, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped, and its clusters of violet, yellow, and white flowers.

Native to Central and Eastern Europe, this perennial herb thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
