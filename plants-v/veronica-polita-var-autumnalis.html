Veronica polita var. autumnalis

Veronica polita var. autumnalis, commonly known as autumn veronica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
