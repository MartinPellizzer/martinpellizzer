Vitis aegirophylla

Vitis aegirophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or grayish underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

The plant is adapted to warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
