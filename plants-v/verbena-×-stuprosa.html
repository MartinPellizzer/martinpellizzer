Verbena × stuprosa

Verbena × stuprosa, commonly known as the hybrid verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is a cross between Verbena rigida and Verbena bonariensis, resulting in a plant that combines the characteristics of both parents. The species is known for its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple and pink, often appearing in spikes. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
