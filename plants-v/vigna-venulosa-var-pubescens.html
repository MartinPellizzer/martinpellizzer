Vigna venulosa var. pubescens

Vigna venulosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy mung bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, green pods that contain edible seeds used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both nutritional and agricultural purposes.
