Vitis lanceolaria var. glycosmoides

Vitis lanceolaria var. glycosmoides, commonly known as the sweetleaf vine, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a woody stem with a rough texture.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
