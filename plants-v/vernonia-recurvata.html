Vernonia recurvata

Vernonia recurvata, commonly known as the zigzag ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, zigzag-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Vernonia recurvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
