Viola × porteriana

Viola × porteriana, commonly known as the Dutch violet, is a hybrid species in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from violet to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and gardens, preferring partial shade.

Viola × porteriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated for its beauty and ease of care.
