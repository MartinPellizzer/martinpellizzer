Verbesina guatemalensis

Verbesina guatemalensis, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange composite flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Verbesina guatemalensis is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed habitats.
