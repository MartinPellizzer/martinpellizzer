Viola jordanii

Viola jordanii, commonly known as Jordan's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola jordanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
