Vignea virens

Vignea virens, commonly known as the green vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Vignea virens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.
