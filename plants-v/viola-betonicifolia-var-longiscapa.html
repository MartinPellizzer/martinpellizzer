Viola betonicifolia var. longiscapa

Viola betonicifolia var. longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 30 cm in height, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy surface. The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
