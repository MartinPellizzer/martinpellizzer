Valeriana fonkii var. spiciformis

Valeriana fonkii var. spiciformis, commonly known as the spicate valerian, is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in spiky inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its extensive root system, which is often harvested for its medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its calming effects in traditional herbal medicine.

The flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
