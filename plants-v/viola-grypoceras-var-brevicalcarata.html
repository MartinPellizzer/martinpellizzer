Viola grypoceras var. brevicalcarata

Viola grypoceras var. brevicalcarata, commonly known as the short-spurred violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet. The flowers have short, spurred petals that distinguish it from other viola species. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
