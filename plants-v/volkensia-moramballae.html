Volkensia moramballae

Volkensia moramballae, commonly known as the Moramballa orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the tropical rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks and branches in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a complex inflorescence with numerous fragrant blossoms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
