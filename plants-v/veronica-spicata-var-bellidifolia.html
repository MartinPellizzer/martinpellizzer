Veronica spicata var. bellidifolia

Veronica spicata var. bellidifolia, commonly known as the spiked speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often has a bluish-green hue, adding visual interest to gardens.

It is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
