Veronica tournefortii var. aschersoniana

Veronica tournefortii var. aschersoniana, commonly known as the Ascherson's speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that bear small, blue to violet flowers clustered in whorls along the stem. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and early spring blooming period.
