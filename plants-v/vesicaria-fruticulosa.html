Vesicaria fruticulosa

Vesicaria fruticulosa, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It features a thick, fleshy stem covered in spiny areoles, which bear clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are widely consumed in various cuisines. Its ability to store water in its stems makes it highly adapted to drought conditions.

Vesicaria fruticulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
