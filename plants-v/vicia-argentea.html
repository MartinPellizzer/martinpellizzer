Vicia argentea

Vicia argentea, commonly known as silver vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in dry, open habitats.

Vicia argentea is a legume, meaning it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.
