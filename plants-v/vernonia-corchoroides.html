Vernonia corchoroides

Vernonia corchoroides, commonly known as wild sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
