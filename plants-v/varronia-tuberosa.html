Varronia tuberosa

Varronia tuberosa, commonly known as the rubber tree or yellow wood, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. The plant is noted for its rubbery latex, which has been historically used in various industrial applications. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Varronia tuberosa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
