Verticordia sieberi var. sieberi

Verticordia sieberi var. sieberi, commonly known as the Geraldton Wax, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, red or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in heathland and sandy soils, often in coastal regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The Geraldton Wax is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
