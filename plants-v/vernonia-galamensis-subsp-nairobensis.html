Vernonia galamensis subsp. nairobensis

Vernonia galamensis subsp.

nairobensis, commonly known as the Nairobi Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, often in regions with moderate to high rainfall. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
