Vaccinium tanjungii

Vaccinium tanjungii, commonly known as the Bornean blueberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the island of Borneo and typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are evergreen, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Vaccinium tanjungii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
