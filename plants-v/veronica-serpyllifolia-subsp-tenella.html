Veronica serpyllifolia subsp. tenella

Veronica serpyllifolia subsp.

tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas, rocky slopes, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
