Vriesea imbricata

Vriesea imbricata, commonly known as the "Rainbow Vriesea," is a tropical bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that features multiple tiers of bracts in varying shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has rosette-shaped leaves that are stiff and covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. Its roots are relatively small, and it primarily absorbs water and nutrients through the leaf axils.

Vriesea imbricata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in indoor environments.
