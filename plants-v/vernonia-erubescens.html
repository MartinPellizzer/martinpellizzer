Vernonia erubescens

Vernonia erubescens, commonly known as the red Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open woods and prairies, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
