Veronica longifolia subsp. longifolia

Veronica longifolia subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or coastal environments.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue or violet, and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, attracting various pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
