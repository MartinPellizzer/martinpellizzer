Verbascum brevipedicellatum

Verbascum brevipedicellatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum in the family Scrophulariaceae, which includes other well-known species like common mullein. The plant is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Verbascum brevipedicellatum is also noted for its medicinal properties, historically used for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
