Viola takedana var. variegata

Viola takedana var. variegata, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of white and green. The plant produces delicate purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage and flowers are also used in traditional Japanese herbal medicine.
