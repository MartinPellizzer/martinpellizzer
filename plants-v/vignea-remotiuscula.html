Vignea remotiuscula

Vignea remotiuscula, commonly known as the small-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in poor soils.

Vignea remotiuscula is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
