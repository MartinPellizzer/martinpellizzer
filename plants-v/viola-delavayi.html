Viola delavayi

Viola delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often adorned with intricate patterns of purple and white.

Viola delavayi is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
