Viola hastata

Viola hastata, commonly known as the purple violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and it produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and blue. Viola hastata is part of the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
