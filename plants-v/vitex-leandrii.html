Vitex leandrii

Vitex leandrii, commonly known as the leander vitex, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in tropical and subtropical climates.

Vitex leandrii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
