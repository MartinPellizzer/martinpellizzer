Viscum

Viscum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white or yellowish berries. Viscum species are found in temperate regions across the Northern Hemisphere. The plant absorbs water and nutrients from its host through specialized structures called haustoria.

Its berries are often associated with holiday traditions, particularly in Christmas celebrations.
