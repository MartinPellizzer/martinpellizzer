Verbena ferreyrae

Verbena ferreyrae, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

Verbena ferreyrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
