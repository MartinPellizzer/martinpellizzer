Viburnum koreanum

Viburnum koreanum, commonly known as Korean viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea and parts of China.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture.

Viburnum koreanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
