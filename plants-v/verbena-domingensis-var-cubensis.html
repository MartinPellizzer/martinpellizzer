Verbena domingensis var. cubensis

Verbena domingensis var. cubensis, commonly known as Cuban verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically violet or blue in color, with five petals and a distinctive tubular throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
