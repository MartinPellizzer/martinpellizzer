Valeriana locusta var. coronata

Valeriana locusta var. coronata, commonly known as the crown valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and emits a distinctive, sometimes spicy scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in woodland edges or meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
