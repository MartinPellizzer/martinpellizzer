Vicia atlantica var. mesatlantica

Vicia atlantica var. mesatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
