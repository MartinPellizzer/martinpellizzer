Viola lavrenkoana

Viola lavrenkoana, commonly known as the Lavrenko's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Viola lavrenkoana is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
