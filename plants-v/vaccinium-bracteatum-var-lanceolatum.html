Vaccinium bracteatum var. lanceolatum

Vaccinium bracteatum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces small, edible berries that are typically red or purple in color. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and pine flatwoods. It plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
