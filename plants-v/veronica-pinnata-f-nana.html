Veronica pinnata f. nana

Veronica pinnata f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its compact stature and dense foliage.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground cover in gardens.
