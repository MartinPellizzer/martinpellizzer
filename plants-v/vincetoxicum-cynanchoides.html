Vincetoxicum cynanchoides

Vincetoxicum cynanchoides, commonly known as the blue cohosh, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features compound leaves with three leaflets and produces showy, blue-violet flowers in late spring. Its berries are dark red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Vincetoxicum cynanchoides is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
