Vicia macrocarpa

Vicia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces large, showy purple or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Vicia macrocarpa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
