Viguiera subincisa

Viguiera subincisa, commonly known as Mexican fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in shades of pink to purple.

Viguiera subincisa is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
