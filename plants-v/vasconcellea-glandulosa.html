Vasconcellea glandulosa

Vasconcellea glandulosa, commonly known as the hairy waxplant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a waxy texture.

Vasconcellea glandulosa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
