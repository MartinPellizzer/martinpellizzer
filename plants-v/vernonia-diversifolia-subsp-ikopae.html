Vernonia diversifolia subsp. ikopae

Vernonia diversifolia subsp.

ikopae, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant is notable for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
