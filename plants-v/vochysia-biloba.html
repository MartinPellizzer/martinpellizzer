Vochysia biloba

Vochysia biloba, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that are clustered in bell-shaped inflorescences, which are a striking feature of the species. Vochysia biloba thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticulture in suitable climates.
