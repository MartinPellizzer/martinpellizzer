Verbena chamaedryfolia f. strigosa

Verbena chamaedryfolia f. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy verbena, is a compact, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged flowering period and drought tolerance.
