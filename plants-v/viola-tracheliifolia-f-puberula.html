Viola tracheliifolia f. puberula

Viola tracheliifolia f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent viola, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves that give it its specific epithet "puberula," meaning softly hairy. The plant produces violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
