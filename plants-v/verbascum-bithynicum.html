Verbascum bithynicum

Verbascum bithynicum, commonly known as the Bithynian mullein, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and the eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Verbascum bithynicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
