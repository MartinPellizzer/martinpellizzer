Viola minor var. ciliata

Viola minor var. ciliata, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Viola minor var. ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
