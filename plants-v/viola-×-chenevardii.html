Viola × chenevardii

Viola × chenevardii, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with stems that are often hairy and upright. Its flowers, which range in color from purple to white, bloom in early spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Viola × chenevardii is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil.
