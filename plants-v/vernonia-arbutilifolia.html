Vernonia arbutilifolia

Vernonia arbutilifolia, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and erect.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a host for several butterfly species.
