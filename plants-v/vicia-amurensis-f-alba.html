Vicia amurensis f. alba

Vicia amurensis f. alba, commonly known as the white Siberian vetch, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, white flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
