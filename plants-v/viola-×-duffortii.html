Viola × duffortii

Viola × duffortii, commonly known as the Dutch viola, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often bicolor flowers that range from purple to white. The plant typically produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Viola × duffortii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its early spring blooms.
