Viola torresii

Viola torresii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit.

Viola torresii is adapted to cool, temperate climates and is often found in high-elevation regions.
