Viola sororia var. novae-angliae

Viola sororia var. novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and violet. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and thrives in partial to full shade. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
