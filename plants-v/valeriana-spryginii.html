Valeriana spryginii

Valeriana spryginii, commonly known as the Siberian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often pinkish or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a strong, pleasant scent. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana spryginii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its medicinal significance.
