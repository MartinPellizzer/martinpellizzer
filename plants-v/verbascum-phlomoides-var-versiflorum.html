Verbascum phlomoides var. versiflorum

Verbascum phlomoides var. versiflorum, commonly known as the blueleaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

This variety is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
