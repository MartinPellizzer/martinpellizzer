Vernonia cinerea var. glabriuscula

Vernonia cinerea var. glabriuscula, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and erect, branched stems that bear small, clustered purple flowers. The plant is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is typically found in open woodlands and disturbed sites. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
