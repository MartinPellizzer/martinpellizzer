Veronica wormskjoldii subsp. wormskjoldii

Veronica wormskjoldii subsp.

wormskjoldii, commonly known as the dwarf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring and summer.

The plant has slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
