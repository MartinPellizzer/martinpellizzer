Vatica schouteniana

Vatica schouteniana, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, often with a leathery texture.

Vatica schouteniana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
