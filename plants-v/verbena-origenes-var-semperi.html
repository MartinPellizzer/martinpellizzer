Verbena origenes var. semperi

Verbena origenes var. semperi, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to pollinator-friendly gardens.
