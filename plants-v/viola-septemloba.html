Viola septemloba

Viola septemloba, commonly known as the seven-lobed violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with seven distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive dark purple throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
