Vernonia sinclairii

Vernonia sinclairii, commonly known as the Sinclair's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its showy blue to purple flower heads bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia sinclairii is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
