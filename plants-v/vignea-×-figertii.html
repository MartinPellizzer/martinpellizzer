Vignea × figertii

Vignea × figertii, commonly known as the hybrid vigna, is a botanical species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of many leguminous plants. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. Vignea × figertii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is of interest in botanical research and agricultural breeding programs.
