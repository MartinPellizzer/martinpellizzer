Viola canina var. pyrenaica

Viola canina var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, delicate violet flowers in shades of purple and blue. It is part of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit.

This variety is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
