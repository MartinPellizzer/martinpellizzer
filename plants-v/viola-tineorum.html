Viola tineorum

Viola tineorum, commonly known as the velvetleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from purple to violet, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Viola tineorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
