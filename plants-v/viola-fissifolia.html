Viola fissifolia

Viola fissifolia, commonly known as the fissured violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy or notched edge, and its flowers are usually violet or blue, with five petals. Viola fissifolia produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
