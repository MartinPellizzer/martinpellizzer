Vaccinium bicolor

Vaccinium bicolor, commonly known as the twinflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, and it produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
