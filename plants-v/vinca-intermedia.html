Vinca intermedia

Vinca intermedia, commonly known as the periwinkle or Madagascar vine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a perennial shrub that produces vibrant blue to purple flowers in the spring. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and a dense growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, preferring moderate to cool climates.

Vinca intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
