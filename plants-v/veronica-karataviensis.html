Veronica karataviensis

Veronica karataviensis, commonly known as the Karatavi speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica karataviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
