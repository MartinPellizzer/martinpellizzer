Veronica glabriuscula

Veronica glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Veronica glabriuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
