Viola longistipulata

Viola longistipulata, commonly known as the long-stalked violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet hue and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Viola longistipulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
