Viola pumila var. orientalis

Viola pumila var. orientalis, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5 to 10 centimeters, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and prefers partial to full shade.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
