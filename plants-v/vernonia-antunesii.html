Vernonia antunesii

Vernonia antunesii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, hard wood and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
