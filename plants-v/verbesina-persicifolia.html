Verbesina persicifolia

Verbesina persicifolia, commonly known as the peachleaf false sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that resemble peach leaves, hence its common name. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
