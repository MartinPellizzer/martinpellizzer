Vernonia tomentosa

Vernonia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, woolly foliage and conical flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are usually pink to purple in color and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
