Viola × sourekii

Viola × sourekii, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant that belongs to the Violaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Viola tricolor and Viola lutea, resulting in a plant with diverse color variations in its flowers. The foliage is typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the flowers can range from purple to yellow and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
