Verbesina rueppellii

Verbesina rueppellii, commonly known as the desert beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its rough, hairy stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain seeds with hooked spines, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Verbesina rueppellii is adapted to hot, dry environments and plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
