Valeriana officinalis subsp. procurrens

Valeriana officinalis subsp.

procurrens, commonly known as creeping valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

It is characterized by its spreading growth habit and strong, sometimes unpleasant, aromatic scent.
