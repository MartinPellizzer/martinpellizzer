Vaccinium anthonyi

Vaccinium anthonyi, commonly known as Anthony's blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many other heath and blueberry species. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which later develop into bright red, edible berries. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Vaccinium anthonyi thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as pine woods and swamps.
