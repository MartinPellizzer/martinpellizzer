Vaccinium donianum var. laetum

Vaccinium donianum var. laetum, commonly known as the highbush blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, bright blue berries in the summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal forests and heathlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in both culinary and commercial blueberry cultivation.
