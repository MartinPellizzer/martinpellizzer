Valeriana oaxacana

Valeriana oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Oaxaca, Mexico.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Valeriana oaxacana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
