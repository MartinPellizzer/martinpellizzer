Vaccinium sintenisii

Vaccinium sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenisi blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that are typically blue or purple in color when mature. These berries are often used in local traditional medicine and are valued for their flavor and nutritional content.

Vaccinium sintenisii thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist climate for optimal growth.
