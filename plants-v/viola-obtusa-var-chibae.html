Viola obtusa var. chibae

Viola obtusa var. chibae, commonly known as the Chiba viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in spring, producing a showy display of blue to purple petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
