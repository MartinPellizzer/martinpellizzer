Vachellia gerrardi var. calvescens

Vachellia gerrardi var. calvescens, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for sandy soils. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for wildlife.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
