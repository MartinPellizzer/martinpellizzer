Viola peregrina

Viola peregrina, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from violet to white, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Viola peregrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
