Veronica austriaca var. bipinnatifida

Veronica austriaca var. bipinnatifida, commonly known as the Austrian speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite pairs of lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
