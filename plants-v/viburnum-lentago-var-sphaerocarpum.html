Viburnum lentago var. sphaerocarpum

Viburnum lentago var. sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the spherical-fruited arrowwood, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The fruit is distinctive, consisting of small, round drupes that turn from white to red as they mature. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and wildlife habitat benefits.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
