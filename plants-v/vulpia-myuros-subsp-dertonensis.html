Vulpia myuros subsp. dertonensis

Vulpia myuros subsp.

dertonensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved veldt grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing forage for grazing animals.
