Vitis blumeana

Vitis blumeana, commonly known as the Blume's grapevine, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of berries. Its berries are typically edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

Vitis blumeana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fruit.
