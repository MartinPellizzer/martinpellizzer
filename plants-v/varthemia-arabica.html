Varthemia arabica

Varthemia arabica, commonly known as the Persian sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has silver-gray foliage, which helps it conserve water in its dry habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky areas.

Varthemia arabica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
