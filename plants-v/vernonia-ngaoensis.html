Vernonia ngaoensis

Vernonia ngaoensis, commonly known as the Ngao Vernonia, is a flowering plant species native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia ngaoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local communities.
