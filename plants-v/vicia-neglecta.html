Vicia neglecta

Vicia neglecta, commonly known as the common vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its pods contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vicia neglecta is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
