Viguiera shrevei

Viguiera shrevei, commonly known as Shreve's coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to pink petals and a prominent yellow center, blooming from late spring to early fall. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Viguiera shrevei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
