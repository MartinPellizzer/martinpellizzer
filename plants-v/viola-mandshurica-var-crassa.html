Viola mandshurica var. crassa

Viola mandshurica var. crassa, commonly known as the broad-leaved viola, is a perennial herb native to northeastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are broad and often hairy, with a distinctive violet-blue flower that blooms in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal being spurred, a characteristic feature of the Viola genus.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
