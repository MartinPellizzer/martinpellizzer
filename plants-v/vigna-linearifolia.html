Vigna linearifolia

Vigna linearifolia, commonly known as cowpea or black-eyed pea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is often grown for its high-protein seeds, which are used in various cuisines worldwide. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Vigna linearifolia also has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
