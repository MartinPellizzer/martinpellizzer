Vitis rhomboidea var. transvaalensis

Vitis rhomboidea var. transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal grape, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and can grow quite long, often reaching several meters in length. Its berries are typically small and dark, with a somewhat tart flavor.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal interest.
