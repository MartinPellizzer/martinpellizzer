Viola × neilreichii

Viola × neilreichii, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola wittrockii.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
