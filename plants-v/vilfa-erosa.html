Vilfa erosa

Vilfa erosa, commonly known as the rough vilfa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Vilfa erosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
