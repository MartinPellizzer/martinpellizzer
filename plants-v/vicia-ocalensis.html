Vicia ocalensis

Vicia ocalensis, commonly known as the Ocalens vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Vicia ocalensis is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
