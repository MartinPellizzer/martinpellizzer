Vitex triflora var. quinquefoliolata

Vitex triflora var. quinquefoliolata, commonly known as the five-leaf chaste tree, is a variant of the vitex genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring leaves with five lobes, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or white, which bloom during the summer months. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with a dense, compact form that makes it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
