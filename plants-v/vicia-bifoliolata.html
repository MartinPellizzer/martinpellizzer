Vicia bifoliolata

Vicia bifoliolata, commonly known as the two-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure, featuring two small leaflets that give it its specific epithet "bifoliolata.". The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates.

Vicia bifoliolata is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator plant for certain ecological conditions.
