Vriesea carinata var. wawra

Vriesea carinata var. wawra, commonly known as the Wawra's Vriesea, is a bromeliad native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, recurved, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, and its inflorescence features vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its ornate appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Vriesea carinata var. wawra is also appreciated for its ability to thrive in humid environments, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
