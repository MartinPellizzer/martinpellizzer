Vitis microdonta

Vitis microdonta, commonly known as the dwarf grape or small grape vine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia, where it grows in moist, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its small, trailing vines and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature.

Vitis microdonta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
