Viola nuttallii var. venosa

Viola nuttallii var. venosa, commonly known as the spotted violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves with a distinctive purplish hue and a serrated margin. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a spurred lip, characteristic of the Violaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
