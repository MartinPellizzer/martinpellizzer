Viola dirphya

Viola dirphya, commonly known as the purple violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive purple throat, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Viola dirphya is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
