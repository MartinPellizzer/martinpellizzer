Viola arguta subsp. meridionalis

Viola arguta subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern hardy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady woodland habitats and has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple and violet, usually with five petals and a distinctive spur. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and its preference for well-drained soil.
