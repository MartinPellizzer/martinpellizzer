Vittaria anodontolepis

Vittaria anodontolepis, commonly known as the toothless fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually small, with a distinctive, finely divided structure.

Vittaria anodontolepis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
