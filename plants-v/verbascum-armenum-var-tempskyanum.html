Verbascum armenum var. tempskyanum

Verbascum armenum var. tempskyanum, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and adjacent areas.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a dense layer of silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
