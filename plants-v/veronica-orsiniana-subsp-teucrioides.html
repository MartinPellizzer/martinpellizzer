Veronica orsiniana subsp. teucrioides

Veronica orsiniana subsp.

teucrioides, commonly known as the Alpine speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often forms dense mats to protect itself from strong winds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
