Vernonia calerana

Vernonia calerana, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens in arid climates.

Its common name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for treating ailments such as wounds and infections.
