Vernonia galamensis subsp. mutomoensis

Vernonia galamensis subsp.

mutomoensis, commonly known as the Mutomo vernonia, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typical of the genus Vernonia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadside verges and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
