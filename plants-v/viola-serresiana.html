Viola serresiana

Viola serresiana, commonly known as the Sierra viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and features showy violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Viola serresiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and its preference for well-drained soil.
