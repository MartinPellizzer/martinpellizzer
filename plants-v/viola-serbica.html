Viola serbica

Viola serbica, commonly known as the Serbian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist soils, preferring partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape.

Viola serbica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
