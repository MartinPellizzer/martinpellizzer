Varronia paraguariensis

Varronia paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina, Brazil, and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its serrated leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is most notable for its caffeine-rich leaves, which are used to prepare a traditional infusion. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
