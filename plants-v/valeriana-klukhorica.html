Valeriana klukhorica

Valeriana klukhorica, commonly known as the Siberian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Russia.

It features a thick, creeping rhizome and produces clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers during the summer months. The plant has aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or alpine regions.

Valeriana klukhorica is valued for its medicinal uses and its ability to adapt to harsh environmental conditions.
