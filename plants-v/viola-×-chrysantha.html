Viola × chrysantha

Viola × chrysantha, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant that combines traits from both viola and pansy species.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners. Its flowers typically feature a combination of yellow and purple hues, with a distinctive yellow center.

Viola × chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
