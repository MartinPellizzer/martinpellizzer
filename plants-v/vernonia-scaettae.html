Vernonia scaettae

Vernonia scaettae, commonly known as the scarlet Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright orange to red tubular flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value in arid environments.
