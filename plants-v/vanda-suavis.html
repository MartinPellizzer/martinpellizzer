Vanda suavis

Vanda suavis, commonly known as the sweet orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, making it well-suited for climbing on trees or other structures.

Vanda suavis is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and distinctive aroma.
