Veronica teucrium proles pseudochamaedrys

Veronica teucrium proles pseudochamaedrys, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species often found in mountainous landscapes.
