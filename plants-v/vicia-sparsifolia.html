Vicia sparsifolia

Vicia sparsifolia, commonly known as the sparseleaf vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features slender stems with alternate, trifoliate leaves composed of three small, ovate leaflets. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, arranged in clusters along the stems.

Vicia sparsifolia is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in certain habitats.
