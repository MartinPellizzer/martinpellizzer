Vitis rubrifolia

Vitis rubrifolia, commonly known as the redleaf grapevine, is a deciduous woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn a striking red or orange color in the fall, making it a notable ornamental plant. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by clusters of small, edible grapes. Its berries are typically dark purple to black and are used in winemaking and culinary applications.

Vitis rubrifolia is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil types.
