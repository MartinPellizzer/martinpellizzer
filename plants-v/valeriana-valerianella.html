Valeriana valerianella

Valeriana valerianella, commonly known as lesser valerian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, often strongly scented leaves that contain volatile oils, contributing to its distinctive fragrance. Its root system is fibrous and can produce a variety of secondary metabolites.

Valerianella valerianella thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
