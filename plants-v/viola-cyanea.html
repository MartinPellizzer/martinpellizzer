Viola cyanea

Viola cyanea, commonly known as the blue violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in woodland habitats. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Viola cyanea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important food source for certain pollinators.
