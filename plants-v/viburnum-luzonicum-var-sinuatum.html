Viburnum luzonicum var. sinuatum

Viburnum luzonicum var. sinuatum, commonly known as the Sinuous Viburnum, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The leaves are typically ovate with a slightly wavy or sinuous margin, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
