Vicarya

Vicarya, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns resembling the wings of a butterfly. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. Vicarya is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional Andean medicine.

Its unique appearance and cultural significance make it a notable botanical species in the region.
