Viola miersii

Viola miersii, commonly known as Miers' violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a range of colors from pale purple to deep violet.

Viola miersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
