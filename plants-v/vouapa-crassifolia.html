Vouapa crassifolia

Vouapa crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed vouapa, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, indicating its ability to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, adding to its ornamental value. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

Vouapa crassifolia is valued in horticulture for its resilience and distinctive foliage.
