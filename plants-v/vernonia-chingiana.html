Vernonia chingiana

Vernonia chingiana, commonly known as the Chinese ironweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its showy, yellow-orange flower heads appear in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia chingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long-lasting blooms.
