Viola canina var. calcarea

Viola canina var. calcarea, commonly known as the limestone violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in calcareous soils, thriving in rocky or limestone-rich environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces delicate violet flowers with purple petals. It is often found in open woodland areas and along rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to alkaline conditions and poor soil.
