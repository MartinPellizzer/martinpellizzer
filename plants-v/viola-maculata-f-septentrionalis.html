Viola maculata f. septentrionalis

Viola maculata f. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern spotted violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves with distinctive purple spots, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, often with a yellow center. The plant grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It is part of the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial to full shade.
