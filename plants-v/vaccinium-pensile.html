Vaccinium pensile

Vaccinium pensile, commonly known as trailing blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a deep green color, while its flowers are bell-shaped and typically pink or white.

Vaccinium pensile is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
