Vochysia elegans

Vochysia elegans, commonly known as the golden glory tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet, yellow-orange fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Vochysia elegans is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
