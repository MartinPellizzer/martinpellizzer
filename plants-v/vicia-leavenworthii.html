Vicia leavenworthii

Vicia leavenworthii, commonly known as Leavenworth vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged along a central stem.

Vicia leavenworthii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
