Viburnum stenocalyx

Viburnum stenocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Viburnum stenocalyx is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
