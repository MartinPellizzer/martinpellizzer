Verbascum thapsus var. condensatum

Verbascum thapsus var. condensatum, commonly known as the denseleaf mullein, is a variant of the common mullein plant.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils. The plant features dense, velvety gray-green leaves that are often clustered at the base. Its showy yellow flowers appear in late summer, forming a tall, upright spike.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
