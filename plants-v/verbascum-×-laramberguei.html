Verbascum × laramberguei

Verbascum × laramberguei, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattifolium.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, silver-green leaves that are typical of the genus Verbascum. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ease of growth.

Verbascum × laramberguei prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to xeriscaped landscapes.
