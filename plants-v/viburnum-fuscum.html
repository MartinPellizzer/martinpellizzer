Viburnum fuscum

Viburnum fuscum, commonly known as blackhaw viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that appear in clusters, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape. It produces fragrant, white, clustered flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viburnum fuscum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
