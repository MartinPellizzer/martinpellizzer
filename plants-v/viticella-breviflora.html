Viticella breviflora

Viticella breviflora, commonly known as the dwarf grapevine, is a compact, trailing plant native to South Africa.

It features small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability.

Its berries, while not typically eaten by humans, are attractive to birds and can be used in some traditional herbal applications.
