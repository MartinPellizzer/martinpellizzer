Viola canina var. ericetorum

Viola canina var. ericetorum, commonly known as the heath violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils, often found in heathlands and woodland edges. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces violet-blue or purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often used in native plant landscaping.
