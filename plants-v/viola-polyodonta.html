Viola polyodonta

Viola polyodonta, commonly known as the purple viola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola polyodonta is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local pollinators.
