Veronica cymbalaria f. glandulifera

Veronica cymbalaria f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular common speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "glandulifera.". It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in moist grasslands and woodland edges.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
