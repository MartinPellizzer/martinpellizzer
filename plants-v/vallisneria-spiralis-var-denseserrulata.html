Vallisneria spiralis var. denseserrulata

Vallisneria spiralis var. denseserrulata, commonly known as the dense-serrated waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves with a distinctive serrated edge, which contributes to its ornamental value in aquariums. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments with soft, nutrient-rich substrates. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.

Its rapid growth rate makes it both a popular choice for aquatic landscaping and a potential invasive species in non-native waters.
