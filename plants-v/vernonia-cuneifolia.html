Vernonia cuneifolia

Vernonia cuneifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of purple to pinkish-blue flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Vernonia cuneifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
