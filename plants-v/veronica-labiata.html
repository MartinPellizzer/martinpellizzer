Veronica labiata

Veronica labiata, commonly known as the blueweed or common speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Veronica labiata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
