Viola obliqua var. crenulata

Viola obliqua var. crenulata, commonly known as the zigzag violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy or zigzagged margin, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue or purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plant species.
