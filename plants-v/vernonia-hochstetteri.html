Vernonia hochstetteri

Vernonia hochstetteri, commonly known as the New Zealand iron vine, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It is adapted to coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Vernonia hochstetteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
