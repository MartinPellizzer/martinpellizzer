Vernonia affinis

Vernonia affinis, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Vernonia affinis is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
