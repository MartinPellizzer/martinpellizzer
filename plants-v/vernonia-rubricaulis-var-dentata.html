Vernonia rubricaulis var. dentata

Vernonia rubricaulis var. dentata, commonly known as the red-stemmed ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, dark purple to reddish purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving the plant its "dentata" variety name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
