Verbascum humile subsp. rhodopaeum

Verbascum humile subsp.

rhodopaeum, commonly known as the Greek mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and tall spikes of yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are adaptations to its arid environment.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
