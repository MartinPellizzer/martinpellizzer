Vroedea maritima

Vroedea maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it withstand salty air and harsh coastal conditions. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. Vroedea maritima thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Its resilience to salt spray and drought makes it a popular choice for maritime environments.
