Veronica latifolia var. dubia

Veronica latifolia var. dubia, commonly known as the broadleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters. The leaves are broad and oval, often with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
