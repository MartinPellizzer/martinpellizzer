Verbascum × fallax

Verbascum × fallax, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in soft hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. Verbascum × fallax is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
