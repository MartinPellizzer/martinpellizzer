Verbesina pentalobifolia

Verbesina pentalobifolia, commonly known as the five-lobed beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with five distinct lobes. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is known for its prickly, bristly fruits.
