Verbena phlogiflora var. mucilenta

Verbena phlogiflora var. mucilenta, commonly known as the mucilaginous verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in summer. The plant produces mucilage-rich leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
