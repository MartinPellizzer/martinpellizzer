Verbascum cicekdagensis

Verbascum cicekdagensis, commonly known as the Turkish mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or dry environments.

Verbascum cicekdagensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
