Vitis pergamacea

Vitis pergamacea, commonly known as the Persian grape or Persian vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth, producing tendrils that allow it to climb and spread over surfaces. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruits are small, round berries that ripen to a dark blue or black color.

Vitis pergamacea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
