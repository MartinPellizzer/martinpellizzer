Vetrix helix

Vetrix helix, commonly known as the spiral rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy areas in temperate regions.

It features a cylindrical, spirally twisted stem that gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering spikes appear in late summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Vetrix helix plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
