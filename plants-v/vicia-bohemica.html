Vicia bohemica

Vicia bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in early summer. Vicia bohemica has small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.
