Viola alba var. violacea

Viola alba var. violacea, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a white throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for garden cultivation and ecological studies.
