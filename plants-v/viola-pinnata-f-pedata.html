Viola pinnata f. pedata

Viola pinnata f. pedata, commonly known as the broadleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
