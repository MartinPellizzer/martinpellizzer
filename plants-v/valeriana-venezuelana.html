Valeriana venezuelana

Valeriana venezuelana, commonly known as the Venezuelan valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana venezuelana is valued not only for its medicinal uses but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
