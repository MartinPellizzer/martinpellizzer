Vernonia mikumiensis

Vernonia mikumiensis, commonly known as Mikumi Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in regions with moderate to high rainfall. Its foliage is usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia mikumiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some local communities.
