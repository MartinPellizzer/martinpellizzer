Viola tricolor var. minor

Viola tricolor var. minor, commonly known as the lesser violet, is a compact perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, colorful flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a beneficial addition to ecological landscapes.
