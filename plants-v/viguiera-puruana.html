Viguiera puruana

Viguiera puruana, commonly known as the Puruana mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, sunny conditions. Its showy yellow to orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viguiera puruana is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to withstand harsh mountain climates.
