Vallaris angustifolia

Vallaris angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf vallaris, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters wide. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Vallaris angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
