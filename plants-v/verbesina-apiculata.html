Verbesina apiculata

Verbesina apiculata, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in open fields and along roadsides.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
