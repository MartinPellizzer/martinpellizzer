Veronica gaubae

Veronica gaubae, commonly known as the Cape Verge, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its foliage is lance-shaped with a silvery-blue hue, adding visual interest to the landscape.

Veronica gaubae is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
