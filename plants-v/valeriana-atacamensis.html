Valeriana atacamensis

Valeriana atacamensis, commonly known as Atacama valerian, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom during the cooler months, adapting to the extreme conditions of its environment. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in the dry climate.

Valeriana atacamensis is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
