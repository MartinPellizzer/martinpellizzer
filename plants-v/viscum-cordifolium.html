Viscum cordifolium

Viscum cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in temperate regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers followed by berries that are often red or yellow in color. Viscum cordifolium is known for its ability to form dense clusters on its host, which can affect the growth and health of the tree.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other wildlife.
