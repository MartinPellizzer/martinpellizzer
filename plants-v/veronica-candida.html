Veronica candida

Veronica candida, commonly known as the white speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica candida is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in cool, humid conditions.
