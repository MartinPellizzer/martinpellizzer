Vigna indica

Vigna indica, commonly known as the horsegram or kidney bean, is a leguminous plant native to India and widely cultivated for its edible seeds.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several angular, dark brown seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Vigna indica thrives in warm climates and is often grown in dry regions due to its drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it an important crop for both nutritional and agricultural purposes.
