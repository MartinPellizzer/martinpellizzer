Vitex columbiensis

Vitex columbiensis, commonly known as the Columbia vitex, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Vitex columbiensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
