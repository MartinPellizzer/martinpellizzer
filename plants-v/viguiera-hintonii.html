Viguiera hintonii

Viguiera hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Viguiera hintonii is valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
