Vitex negundo var. thyrsoides

Vitex negundo var. thyrsoides, commonly known as the Chinese vitex or fragrant vitex, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in summer. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional medicine.
