Vaccinium smithianum

Vaccinium smithianum, commonly known as Smith's blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late spring. Its berries, which are edible, are usually a dark blue or black color when mature.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.
