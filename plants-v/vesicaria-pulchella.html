Vesicaria pulchella

Vesicaria pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures called vesicles, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are slender and often submerged, while its flowers are small and usually pink or white.

Vesicaria pulchella plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by serving as both a predator and a food source for various invertebrates.
