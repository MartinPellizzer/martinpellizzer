Verbena lucanensis

Verbena lucanensis, commonly known as the Lucanian verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Italy.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant spikes of blue, purple, or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Verbena lucanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens for its color and ability to attract pollinators.
