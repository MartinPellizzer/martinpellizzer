Viburnum microcarpum var. evanescens

Viburnum microcarpum var. evanescens, commonly known as the fading viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring. Its berries are dark blue to black and are typically consumed by birds.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
