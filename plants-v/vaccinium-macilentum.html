Vaccinium macilentum

Vaccinium macilentum, commonly known as the slender blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in spring. Its berries, which are edible, are usually small and dark blue when mature, containing a high concentration of antioxidants.

Vaccinium macilentum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for native wildlife.
