Verbascum pterocalycinum

Verbascum pterocalycinum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem covered in dense, soft grayish-white hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a thick layer of fuzzy hairs.

Verbascum pterocalycinum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
