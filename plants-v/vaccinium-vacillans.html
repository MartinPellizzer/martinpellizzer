Vaccinium vacillans

Vaccinium vacillans, commonly known as the wavering blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are bell-shaped and usually pink or white, blooming in late spring.

The fruit, a small, tart berry, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
