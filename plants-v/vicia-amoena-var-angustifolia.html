Vicia amoena var. angustifolia

Vicia amoena var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet pea, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are pinnate, composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is typically found in meadows and woodland edges. It produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
