Vicia subserrata

Vicia subserrata, commonly known as the serrated vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several small, round seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry, disturbed soils.

Vicia subserrata is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
