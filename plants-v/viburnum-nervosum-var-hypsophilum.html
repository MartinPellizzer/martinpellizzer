Viburnum nervosum var. hypsophilum

Viburnum nervosum var. hypsophilum, commonly known as the highland viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe that attracts birds and other wildlife.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
