Vernonia mashonica

Vernonia mashonica, commonly known as the Mashonaland ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices. Vernonia mashonica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and grassland habitats.

Its fibrous roots and robust growth make it an important component of local ecosystems.
