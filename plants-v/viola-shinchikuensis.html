Viola shinchikuensis

Viola shinchikuensis, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing violet-blue or white petals that attract pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in horticulture for its beauty and ecological benefits.
