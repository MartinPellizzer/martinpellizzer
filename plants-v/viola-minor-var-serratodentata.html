Viola minor var. serratodentata

Viola minor var. serratodentata, commonly known as the serrated-toothed violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Viola minor var. serratodentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
