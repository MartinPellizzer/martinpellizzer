Victoria reginae

Victoria reginae, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to three feet in diameter, providing a unique floating habitat for various aquatic life. The plant's showy white flowers bloom at night, releasing a sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators such as beetles. Its leaves have a waxy surface that repels water, allowing them to remain buoyant on the water's surface.

The Victoria reginae is a symbol of the botanical wonders found in the world's wetlands.
