Vernonia jelfiae var. albida

Vernonia jelfiae var. albida, commonly known as the white vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are pale yellow to white in color.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
