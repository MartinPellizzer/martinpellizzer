Viburnum subpubescens

Viburnum subpubescens, commonly known as the red chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are edible and commonly used in jams and wines.

Viburnum subpubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
