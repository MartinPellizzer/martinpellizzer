Vicia candolleana

Vicia candolleana, commonly known as the Candollean vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils. It produces slender, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vicia candolleana is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
