Varasia linoides

Varasia linoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf varasia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color, blooming during the cooler months. It is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme environments.
