Vilfa truncatella

Vilfa truncatella, commonly known as the narrow-leafed violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are small, violet-blue, and arranged in a loose raceme. Vilfa truncatella has a fibrous root system and produces capsules as its fruit, containing several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
