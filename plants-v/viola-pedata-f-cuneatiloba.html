Viola pedata f. cuneatiloba

Viola pedata f. cuneatiloba, commonly known as the narrow-lobed violet, is a horticultural form of the common violet.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to its aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
