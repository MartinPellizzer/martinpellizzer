Vitis cumingiana

Vitis cumingiana, commonly known as Cuming's grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its climbing habit and woody vines. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its berries are usually small and black when mature, containing seeds.

Vitis cumingiana is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its potential for cultivation and its unique botanical features.
