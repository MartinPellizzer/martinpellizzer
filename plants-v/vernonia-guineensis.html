Vernonia guineensis

Vernonia guineensis, commonly known as African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Vernonia guineensis is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional African medicine.
