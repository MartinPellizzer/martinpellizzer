Vigna polytricha

Vigna polytricha, commonly known as the hairy bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect growth habit. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Vigna polytricha is often cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
