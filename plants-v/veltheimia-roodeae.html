Veltheimia roodeae

Veltheimia roodeae, commonly known as the Roodea cycad, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces distinctive, cone-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
