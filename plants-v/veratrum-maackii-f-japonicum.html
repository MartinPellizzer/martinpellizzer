Veratrum maackii f. japonicum

Veratrum maackii f. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese white helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Melanthiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers with prominent, reflexed tepals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a persistent component of its native habitat.
