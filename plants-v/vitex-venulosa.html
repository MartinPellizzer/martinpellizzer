Vitex venulosa

Vitex venulosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
