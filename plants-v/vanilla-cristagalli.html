Vanilla cristagalli

Vanilla cristagalli, commonly known as the yellow vanilla, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are followed by long, slender pods. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and woody stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires a support structure to climb.

The pods, which mature to a dark brown color, are used in perfumery and as a flavoring agent.
