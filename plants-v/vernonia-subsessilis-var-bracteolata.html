Vernonia subsessilis var. bracteolata

Vernonia subsessilis var. bracteolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
