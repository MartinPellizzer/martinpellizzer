Vernonia grevei

Vernonia grevei, commonly known as the Cape ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rigid, often thorny branches and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Vernonia grevei is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
