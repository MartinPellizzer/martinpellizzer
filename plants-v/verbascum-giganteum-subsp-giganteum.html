Verbascum giganteum subsp. giganteum

Verbascum giganteum subsp.

giganteum, commonly known as the giant mullein, is a striking plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, supporting large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its fibrous leaves and stems have been historically used for medicinal and practical purposes.
