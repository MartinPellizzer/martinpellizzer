Vitex neglecta var. glabrior

Vitex neglecta var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth vitex or lesser chaste tree, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are often hairy but become smoother in the varietal form. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of violet or blue, which bloom in summer. Its fruits are dry, woody capsules that release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
