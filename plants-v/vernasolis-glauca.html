Vernasolis glauca

Vernasolis glauca, commonly known as the blue-flowered verna, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Vernasolis glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual beauty.
