Vaccinium smallii var. minus

Vaccinium smallii var. minus, commonly known as the dwarf huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark blue to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a bluish-green hue.

This variety is often found in coastal forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
