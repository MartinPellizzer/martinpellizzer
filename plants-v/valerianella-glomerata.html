Valerianella glomerata

Valerianella glomerata, commonly known as mouse whiskers, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright green, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and wet meadows.

Valerianella glomerata is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
