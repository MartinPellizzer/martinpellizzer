Viola thomsonii

Viola thomsonii, commonly known as the Thomson's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a range of colors from pale blue to deep purple.

Viola thomsonii is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
