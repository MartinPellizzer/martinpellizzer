Viola canina subsp. jordanii

Viola canina subsp.

jordanii, commonly known as Jordan's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, showy violet flowers in shades of purple or blue. It is often found in mountainous regions and has a compact growth habit.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
