Volutaria abyssinica

Volutaria abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian violets, is a member of the Violaceae family and is native to Ethiopia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. It has small, hairy stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Volutaria abyssinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
