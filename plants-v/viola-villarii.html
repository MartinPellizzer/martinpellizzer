Viola villarii

Viola villarii, commonly known as the Italian violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white, often with a distinctive yellow eye-like marking. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of downy hair.

Viola villarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
