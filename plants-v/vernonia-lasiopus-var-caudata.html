Vernonia lasiopus var. caudata

Vernonia lasiopus var. caudata, commonly known as the purpletop vervain, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The varietal form 'caudata' distinguishes it from other varieties of Vernonia lasiopus by its elongated, tail-like flower spikes.
