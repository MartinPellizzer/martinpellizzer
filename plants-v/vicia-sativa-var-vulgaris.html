Vicia sativa var. vulgaris

Vicia sativa var. vulgaris, commonly known as common vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.

Vicia sativa var. vulgaris is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
