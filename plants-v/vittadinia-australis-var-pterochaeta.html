Vittadinia australis var. pterochaeta

Vittadinia australis var. pterochaeta, commonly known as the hairy vittadinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in a loose cluster and bloom during the spring and summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
