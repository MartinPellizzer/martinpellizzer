Viola odorata var. leucantha

Viola odorata var. leucantha, commonly known as the white viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, pale green leaves and showy white or pale violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
