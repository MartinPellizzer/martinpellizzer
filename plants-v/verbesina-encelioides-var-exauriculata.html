Verbesina encelioides var. exauriculata

Verbesina encelioides var. exauriculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and tall, branching stems that produce clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
