Vernonia noveboracensis var. tomentosa

Vernonia noveboracensis var. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and tall, spiky inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
