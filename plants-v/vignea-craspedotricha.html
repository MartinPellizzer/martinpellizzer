Vignea craspedotricha

Vignea craspedotricha, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Vignea craspedotricha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
