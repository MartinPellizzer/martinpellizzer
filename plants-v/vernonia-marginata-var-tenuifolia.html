Vernonia marginata var. tenuifolia

Vernonia marginata var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its flowering heads are typically surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Vernonia marginata var. tenuifolia is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
