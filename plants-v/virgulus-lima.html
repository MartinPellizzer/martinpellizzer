Virgulus lima

Virgulus lima, commonly known as the Lima bean plant, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are widely used in various cuisines. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain the seeds. Its growth habit includes a climbing or trailing vine that can reach several meters in length.

The botanical characteristics of Virgulus lima make it an important crop in agriculture due to its nutritional value and adaptability to different climates.
