Veronica teucrium var. major

Veronica teucrium var. major, commonly known as greater speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers throughout the summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile garden plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
