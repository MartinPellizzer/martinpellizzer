Vanda roxburghii var. wrightiana

Vanda roxburghii var. wrightiana, commonly known as the Wright's vanda, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without being parasitic. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a yellow lip. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
