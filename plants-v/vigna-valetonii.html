Vigna valetonii

Vigna valetonii, commonly known as the Valeton's pigeonpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems that produce clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several round, edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. Vigna valetonii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and resilience to drought.

It plays an important role in agricultural systems due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
