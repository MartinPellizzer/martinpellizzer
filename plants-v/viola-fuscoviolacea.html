Viola fuscoviolacea

Viola fuscoviolacea, commonly known as the dark violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Viola fuscoviolacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
