Verbesina pellucida

Verbesina pellucida, commonly known as slender beggar-ticks, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features slender, branched stems covered with fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in spherical heads, which are surrounded by leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It is commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in sunny, open habitats.
