Verbascum haussknechtianum var. fallacinum

Verbascum haussknechtianum var. fallacinum, commonly known as the autumn mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, fuzzy, grey-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer to autumn, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its fibrous roots and robust growth make it adaptable to various ecological conditions.
