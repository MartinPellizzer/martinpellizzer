Vigna sepiaria

Vigna sepiaria, commonly known as the purple wing bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged, winged pods and small, edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

Vigna sepiaria is valued for its nutritional content, including protein and minerals, making it an important crop in some parts of the world.
