Verbena hirta

Verbena hirta, commonly known as hairy verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Verbena hirta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
