Veronica atlantica

Veronica atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the British Isles and northern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky, sandy, or gravelly soils along sea cliffs and coastal dunes. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue or purple, and arranged in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Veronica atlantica is well adapted to harsh maritime conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
