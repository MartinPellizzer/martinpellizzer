Vepris montisbambutensis

Vepris montisbambutensis, commonly known as the Cameroon citrus or mountain citron, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Cameroon.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are rich in essential oils and have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
