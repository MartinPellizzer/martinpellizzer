Verbena ballsii

Verbena ballsii, commonly known as purpletop verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and clusters of vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Verbena ballsii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the summer season.
