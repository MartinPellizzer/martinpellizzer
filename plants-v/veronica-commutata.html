Veronica commutata

Veronica commutata, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Veronica commutata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
