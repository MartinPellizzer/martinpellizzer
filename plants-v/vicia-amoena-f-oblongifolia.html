Vicia amoena f. oblongifolia

Vicia amoena f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblong-leaf sweet clover, is a variant of the sweet clover species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves with oblong, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its ecological and medicinal properties.
