Valeriana selerorum

Valeriana selerorum, commonly known as the Seler's valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana selerorum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
