Verbena filicaulis

Verbena filicaulis, commonly known as the Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of purple or violet flowers. Its botanical name reflects its leaf structure, with "filicaulis" referring to the fern-like appearance of its foliage.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
