Vaccinium cavendishioides

Vaccinium cavendishioides, commonly known as the purple highbush blueberry, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and turn red in the fall.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit.
