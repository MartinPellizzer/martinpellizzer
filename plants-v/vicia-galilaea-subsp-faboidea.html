Vicia galilaea subsp. faboidea

Vicia galilaea subsp.

faboidea, commonly known as the Galilean vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial legume that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
