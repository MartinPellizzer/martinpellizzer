Vaccinium costeroides

Vaccinium costeroides, commonly known as the dwarf huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color, often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are evergreen, oval-shaped, and have a leathery texture.

Vaccinium costeroides thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.
