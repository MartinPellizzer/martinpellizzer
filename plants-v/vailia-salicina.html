Vailia salicina

Vailia salicina, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of silver-white hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
