Viguiera goebelii

Viguiera goebelii, commonly known as Mexican blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces showy, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Viguiera goebelii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.
