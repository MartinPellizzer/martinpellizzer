Viola × oreliensis

Viola × oreliensis, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Viola tricolor and Viola sororia, resulting in a plant that exhibits a combination of traits from both parents. The species is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, colorful flowers that typically bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Viola × oreliensis is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread through self-seeding.
