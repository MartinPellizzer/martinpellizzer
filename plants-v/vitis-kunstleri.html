Vitis kunstleri

Vitis kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstler's grape, is a species of flowering vine native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. Vitis kunstleri is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its potential in viticulture.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and genetic research.
