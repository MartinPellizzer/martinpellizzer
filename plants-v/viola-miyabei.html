Viola miyabei

Viola miyabei, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola miyabei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
