Vernonia larseniae

Vernonia larseniae, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and sturdy.

Vernonia larseniae plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is a symbol of Hawaii's unique flora.
