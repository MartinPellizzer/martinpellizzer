Viola × eamesii

Viola × eamesii, commonly known as the Eames violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola sororia and Viola pubescens.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to violet. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to shaded landscapes.

The Eames violet is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
