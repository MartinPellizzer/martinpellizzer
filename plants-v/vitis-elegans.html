Vitis elegans

Vitis elegans, commonly known as the noble grape or sweet white grape, is a species of flowering vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth, trailing stems, and heart-shaped leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into clusters of small, sweet, and often edible berries. Vitis elegans is commonly cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in certain regions.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
