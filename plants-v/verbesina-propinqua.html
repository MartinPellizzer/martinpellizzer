Verbesina propinqua

Verbesina propinqua, commonly known as the common beggar-ticks, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
