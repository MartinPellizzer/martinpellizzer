Viola grandifolia

Viola grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which bloom in early spring. Viola grandifolia is part of the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
