Viola × greenmanii

Viola × greenmanii, commonly known as the Greenman's violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola wittrockii.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors including purple, blue, and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize readily.

Viola × greenmanii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
