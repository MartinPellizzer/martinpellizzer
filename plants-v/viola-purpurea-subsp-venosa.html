Viola purpurea subsp. venosa

Viola purpurea subsp.

venosa, commonly known as the purple viola or violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple-tinged underside, and its flowers are typically violet or purple with yellow markings.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
