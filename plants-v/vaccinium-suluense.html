Vaccinium suluense

Vaccinium suluense, commonly known as the Sulu huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its small, dark blue to black berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisine.

The plant is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
