Vigna radiata var. grandiflora

Vigna radiata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered mung bean, is a variant of the common mung bean.

It is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features compound leaves and produces showy, pink or white flowers. It is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Botanically, it is characterized by its indeterminate growth habit and ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
