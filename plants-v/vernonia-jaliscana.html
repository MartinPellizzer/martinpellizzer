Vernonia jaliscana

Vernonia jaliscana, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

Vernonia jaliscana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
