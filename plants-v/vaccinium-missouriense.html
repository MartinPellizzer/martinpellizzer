Vaccinium missouriense

Vaccinium missouriense, commonly known as Missouri huckleberry, is a shrub native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands and along stream banks. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces edible berries that are dark blue to black in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
