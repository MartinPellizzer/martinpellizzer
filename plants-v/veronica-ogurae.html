Veronica ogurae

Veronica ogurae, commonly known as the Sierra Madre speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Veronica ogurae has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
