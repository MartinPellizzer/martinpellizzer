Vernonia zumbadorensis

Vernonia zumbadorensis, commonly known as the Zumbadorensis ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open areas such as grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Vernonia zumbadorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
