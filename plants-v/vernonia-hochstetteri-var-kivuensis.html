Vernonia hochstetteri var. kivuensis

Vernonia hochstetteri var. kivuensis, commonly known as the Kivu Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the East African region, particularly Rwanda and the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in montane forests, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
