Viola ramiflora

Viola ramiflora, commonly known as the branched violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to forest floors.

Viola ramiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
