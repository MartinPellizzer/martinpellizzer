Viola adunca f. candida

Viola adunca f. candida, commonly known as the white violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, toothed leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
