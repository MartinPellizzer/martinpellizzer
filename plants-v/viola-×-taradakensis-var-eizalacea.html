Viola × taradakensis var. eizalacea

Viola × taradakensis var. eizalacea, commonly known as the Armenian violet, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and semi-shaded environments, thriving in well-drained soil. It produces showy, spurred petals that are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
