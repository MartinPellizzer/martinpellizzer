Viola pinnata var. multifida

Viola pinnata var. multifida, commonly known as the multifid leaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a fern-like appearance. The plant produces violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants for ecological and aesthetic purposes.
