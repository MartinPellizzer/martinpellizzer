Vesicarpa potentilloides

Vesicarpa potentilloides, commonly known as the bladderpod, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its inflated, pod-like fruits that resemble bladders, which is a key botanical feature. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in arid and semi-arid regions. Its small, white or yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and the leaves are usually linear or lance-shaped.

Vesicarpa potentilloides is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and its drought tolerance.
