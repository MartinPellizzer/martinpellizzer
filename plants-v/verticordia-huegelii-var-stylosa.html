Verticordia huegelii var. stylosa

Verticordia huegelii var. stylosa, commonly known as the pink-stemmed bottlebrush, is a distinctive member of the Myrtaceae family native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, often pink or red-stemmed inflorescences that resemble bottlebrushes, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are usually grey-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
