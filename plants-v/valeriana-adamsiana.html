Valeriana adamsiana

Valeriana adamsiana, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant, flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Valeriana adamsiana is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
