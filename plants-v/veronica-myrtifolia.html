Veronica myrtifolia

Veronica myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Veronica myrtifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
