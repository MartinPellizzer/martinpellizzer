Verbascum × freynianum

Verbascum × freynianum, commonly known as the Freyn's mullein, is a hybrid plant in the genus Verbascum, which belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are typical of the mullein genus. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to the western United States and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky areas.

Verbascum × freynianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
