Viola tricolor subf. albescens

Viola tricolor subf.

albescens, commonly known as the white violets, is a botanical variant of the common violet. It is characterized by its pale or white flowers, which distinguish it from the typical purple-flowered Viola tricolor.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate blooms.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
