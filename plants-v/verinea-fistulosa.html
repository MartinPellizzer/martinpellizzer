Verinea fistulosa

Verinea fistulosa, commonly known as the hollow vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

Due to its unique morphology and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
