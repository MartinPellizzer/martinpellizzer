Vulpia gaudiniana

Vulpia gaudiniana, commonly known as the tufted fescue, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and sandy soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Vulpia gaudiniana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
