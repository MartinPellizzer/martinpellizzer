Viola laurifolia

Viola laurifolia, commonly known as the Florida violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has a short, thick rhizome that helps it survive in wet conditions.

Viola laurifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in swampy soils and is often found near water sources.
