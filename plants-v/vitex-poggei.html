Vitex poggei

Vitex poggei, commonly known as the Madagascar vitex, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Vitex poggei is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
