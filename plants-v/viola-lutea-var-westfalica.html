Viola lutea var. westfalica

Viola lutea var. westfalica, commonly known as the Westphalian yellow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, yellowish flowers with a distinctive violet hue. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
