Valeriana ulei

Valeriana ulei, commonly known as the oil valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its distinctive scent, which is attributed to the presence of volatile oils in its leaves and stems. Valeriana ulei typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both traditional medicine and horticultural settings.
