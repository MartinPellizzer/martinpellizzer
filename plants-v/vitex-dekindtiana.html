Vitex dekindtiana

Vitex dekindtiana, commonly known as the African blueberry or Kudu tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flower clusters. The plant produces small, round, blue-black berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Vitex dekindtiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
