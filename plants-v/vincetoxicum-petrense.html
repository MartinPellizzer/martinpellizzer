Vincetoxicum petrense

Vincetoxicum petrense, commonly known as the rock vine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Vincetoxicum petrense is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
