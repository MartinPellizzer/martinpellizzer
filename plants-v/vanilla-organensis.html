Vanilla organensis

Vanilla organensis, commonly known as the Madagascar vanilla, is a climbing vine native to the island of Madagascar.

It produces fragrant, dark brown pods that are harvested and processed to yield the highly valued vanilla extract. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and delicate white flowers. Vanilla organensis thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Its cultivation is closely associated with the traditional methods used in vanilla production.
