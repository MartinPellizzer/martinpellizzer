Veronica spicata var. alternifolia

Veronica spicata var. alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate leaf speedwell, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
