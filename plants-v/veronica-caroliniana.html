Veronica caroliniana

Veronica caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
