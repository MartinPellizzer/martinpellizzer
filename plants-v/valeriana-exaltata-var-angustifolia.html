Valeriana exaltata var. angustifolia

Valeriana exaltata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, dry fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
