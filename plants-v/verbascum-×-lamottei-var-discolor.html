Verbascum × lamottei var. discolor

Verbascum × lamottei var. discolor, commonly known as the white mullein.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. It produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of traditional medicinal use.
