Viola taischanensis

Viola taischanensis, commonly known as the Taischan violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often pollinated by bees and other small insects.

Viola taischanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
