Vicia platensis

Vicia platensis, commonly known as the broad-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Vicia platensis is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, where it can colonize quickly.
