Verbena cayennensis

Verbena cayennensis, commonly known as the scarlet verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or purple flowers that bloom in spikes, typically during the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and tropical climates.

Verbena cayennensis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
