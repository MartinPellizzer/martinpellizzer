Vernonia ekmanii

Vernonia ekmanii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Vernonia ekmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
