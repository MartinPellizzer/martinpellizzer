Veronica sutchuenensis

Veronica sutchuenensis, commonly known as the Szechuan speedwell, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Veronica sutchuenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
