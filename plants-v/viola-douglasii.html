Viola douglasii

Viola douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a range of colors from pale lavender to deep purple.

Viola douglasii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
