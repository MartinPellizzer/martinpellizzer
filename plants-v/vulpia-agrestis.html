Vulpia agrestis

Vulpia agrestis, commonly known as the common vulpia, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual plant that typically reaches a height of about 20 to 50 centimeters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually smooth and green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, two-rowed spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

Vulpia agrestis is commonly found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
