Vernonia garrettiana

Vernonia garrettiana, commonly known as the Garrett's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, dark purple to violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a grayish-green hue.

Vernonia garrettiana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry landscapes.
