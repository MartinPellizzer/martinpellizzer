Viola calycina

Viola calycina, commonly known as the California viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola calycina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
