Viola violacea f. pictifolia

Viola violacea f. pictifolia, commonly known as the painted violet, is a striking cultivar of the violet plant known for its vivid, colorful foliage.

This hybrid exhibits variegated leaves with combinations of green, white, and sometimes red or yellow, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant typically produces small, purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value lies in both its foliage and its delicate floral display.
