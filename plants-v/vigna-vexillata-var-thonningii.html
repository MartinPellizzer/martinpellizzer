Vigna vexillata var. thonningii

Vigna vexillata var. thonningii, commonly known as the African bean or thonning's cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with small, hairy stipules. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful companion plant in agricultural systems.
