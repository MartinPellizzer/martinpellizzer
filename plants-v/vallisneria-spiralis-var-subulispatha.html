Vallisneria spiralis var. subulispatha

Vallisneria spiralis var. subulispatha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrower than those of the standard V. spiralis, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, followed by the development of spirally arranged, ribbon-shaped fruits. It thrives in shallow, still waters with soft sediment and is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers.

This variant is valued in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
