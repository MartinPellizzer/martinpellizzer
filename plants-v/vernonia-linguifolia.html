Vernonia linguifolia

Vernonia linguifolia, commonly known as the tongueleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Vernonia linguifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
