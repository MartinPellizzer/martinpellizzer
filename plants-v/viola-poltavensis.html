Viola poltavensis

Viola poltavensis, commonly known as the Poltavinskaya violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from violet to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Viola poltavensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
