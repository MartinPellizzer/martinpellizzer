Vulpia dertonensis f. violacea

Vulpia dertonensis f. violacea, commonly known as the violet false brome, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive violet-colored inflorescence.

It is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in rocky and calcareous soils. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands, where it forms dense, low-growing vegetation. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the flowers are arranged in spike-like clusters.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
