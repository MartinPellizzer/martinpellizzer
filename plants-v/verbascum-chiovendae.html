Verbascum chiovendae

Verbascum chiovendae, commonly known as the Sardinian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their fuzzy, velvety leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and unique characteristics, it is considered a rare and endemic species within the region.
