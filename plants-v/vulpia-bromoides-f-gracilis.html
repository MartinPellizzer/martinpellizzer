Vulpia bromoides f. gracilis

Vulpia bromoides f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender veldt grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged along the stem, typical of many grass species. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which develop into glabrous, three-angled seeds.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, contributing to ecological stability in grassland ecosystems.
