Veronica pseudolongifolia var. angustata

Veronica pseudolongifolia var. angustata, commonly known as narrow-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in whorls along the stems, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
