Vicia consobrina

Vicia consobrina, commonly known as the common vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to seven oval leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its seeds are often used as a food source for birds and small mammals.

Vicia consobrina thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.
