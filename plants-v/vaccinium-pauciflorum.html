Vaccinium pauciflorum

Vaccinium pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to white in color, which develop into the characteristic blue-black berries. Its leaves are oval-shaped, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.
