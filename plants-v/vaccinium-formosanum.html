Vaccinium formosanum

Vaccinium formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
