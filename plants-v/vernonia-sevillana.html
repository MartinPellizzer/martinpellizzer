Vernonia sevillana

Vernonia sevillana, commonly known as the Spanish ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

Vernonia sevillana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
