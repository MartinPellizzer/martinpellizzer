Vigna rotundifolia

Vigna rotundifolia, commonly known as the cowpea or African bean, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in warm climates, making it a valuable crop in arid regions. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Vigna rotundifolia is widely cultivated for its protein-rich seeds, which are used in various cuisines around the world.
