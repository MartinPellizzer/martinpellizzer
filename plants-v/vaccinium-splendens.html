Vaccinium splendens

Vaccinium splendens, commonly known as the shining blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which develop into edible berries that are rich in antioxidants. Vaccinium splendens thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or swampy areas.

Its ornamental value, combined with its ecological importance, makes it a notable species in native plant gardens.
