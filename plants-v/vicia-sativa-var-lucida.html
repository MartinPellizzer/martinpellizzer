Vicia sativa var. lucida

Vicia sativa var. lucida, commonly known as the shining vetch, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The leaves are pinnate, with three to five leaflets that are often hairy and slightly serrated. This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
