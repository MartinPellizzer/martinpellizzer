Viola pallens var. subreptans

Viola pallens var. subreptans, commonly known as the pale violet or creeping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, pale violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its stems are prostrate, allowing it to spread along the ground, and it is often found in colonies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
