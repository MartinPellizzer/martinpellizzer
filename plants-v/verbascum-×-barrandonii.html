Verbascum × barrandonii

Verbascum × barrandonii, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that resemble those of the common mullein. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
