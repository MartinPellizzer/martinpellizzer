Viola matsudae

Viola matsudae, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Viola matsudae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
