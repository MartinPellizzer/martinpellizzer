Verbascum × subcymosum

Verbascum × subcymosum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy, velvety leaves. The plant produces tall, erect stems with large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are thick and covered in soft, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Verbascum × subcymosum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
