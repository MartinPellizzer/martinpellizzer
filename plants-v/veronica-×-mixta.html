Veronica × mixta

Veronica × mixta, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Veronica × mixta is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a resilient, flowering ground cover.
