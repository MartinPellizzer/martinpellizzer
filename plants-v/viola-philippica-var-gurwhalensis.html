Viola philippica var. gurwhalensis

Viola philippica var. gurwhalensis, commonly known as the Gurwhalensis violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern India, particularly in the states of Assam and West Bengal. This variant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
