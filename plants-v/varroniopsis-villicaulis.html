Varroniopsis villicaulis

Varroniopsis villicaulis, commonly known as the hairy stalked bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features slender, hairy stems that support its showy, bell-shaped flowers, which are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Varroniopsis villicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
