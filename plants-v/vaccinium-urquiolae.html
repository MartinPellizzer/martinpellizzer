Vaccinium urquiolae

Vaccinium urquiolae, commonly known as the Andean huckleberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple or black in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil conditions.
