Vilfa minima

Vilfa minima, commonly known as the dwarf violet, is a small flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact stature and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Vilfa minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
