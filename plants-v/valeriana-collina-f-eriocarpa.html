Valeriana collina f. eriocarpa

Valeriana collina f. eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana collina f. eriocarpa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
