Veronica michauxii

Veronica michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite pairs of ovate to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Veronica michauxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
