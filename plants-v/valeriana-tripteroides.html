Valeriana tripteroides

Valeriana tripteroides, commonly known as the Three-lobed Valerian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, three-lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or white and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Valeriana tripteroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential sedative properties.
