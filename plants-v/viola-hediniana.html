Viola hediniana

Viola hediniana, commonly known as the Hedin's violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the high-altitude landscape.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Viola hediniana is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
