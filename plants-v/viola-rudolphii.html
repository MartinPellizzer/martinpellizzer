Viola rudolphii

Viola rudolphii, commonly known as the Rudolph's violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Viola rudolphii is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding color to the landscape during the transition from winter to summer.
