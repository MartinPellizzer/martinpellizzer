Viola arcuata f. radicans

Viola arcuata f. radicans, commonly known as the trailing violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in spring. It has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover or hanging baskets.

Botanically, it belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous root system that aids in its spreading growth.
