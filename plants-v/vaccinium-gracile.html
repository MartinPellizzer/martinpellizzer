Vaccinium gracile

Vaccinium gracile, commonly known as the slender blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like rhododendrons and heathers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or black in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Vaccinium gracile is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and swamps.
