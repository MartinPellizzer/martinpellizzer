Vitis thunbergii var. mairei

Vitis thunbergii var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's grapevine, is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, sweet berries that are popular in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and growth habit.

The species is also valued for its potential in viticulture and traditional medicine.
