Viola acanthophylla var. tontalensis

Viola acanthophylla var. tontalensis, commonly known as the Tontalensis violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
