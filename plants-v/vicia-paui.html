Vicia paui

Vicia paui, commonly known as the Paui vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Vicia paui is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
