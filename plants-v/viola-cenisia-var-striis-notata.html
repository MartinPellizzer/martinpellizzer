Viola cenisia var. striis-notata

Viola cenisia var. striis-notata, commonly known as the spotted violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with distinctive dark green veins and a pale yellowish-green underside. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers with a characteristic violet color and a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
