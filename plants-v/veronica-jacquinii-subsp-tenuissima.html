Veronica jacquinii subsp. tenuissima

Veronica jacquinii subsp.

tenuissima, commonly known as slender speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America. It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, spreading stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.
