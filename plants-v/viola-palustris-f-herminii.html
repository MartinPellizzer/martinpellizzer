Viola palustris f. herminii

Viola palustris f. herminii, commonly known as the marsh violet, is a botanical variant of the marsh violet species.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland environments in North America. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and marshes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
