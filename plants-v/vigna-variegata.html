Vigna variegata

Vigna variegata, commonly known as the horsegram or cluster bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits. The plant is valued for its seeds, which are used in various cuisines and traditional medicine. Vigna variegata thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate sunlight.

It also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
