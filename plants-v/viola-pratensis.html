Viola pratensis

Viola pratensis, commonly known as the meadow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, often with five petals. Viola pratensis produces small, showy flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
