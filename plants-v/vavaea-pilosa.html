Vavaea pilosa

Vavaea pilosa, commonly known as the hairy vavaea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its hairy foliage helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

Vavaea pilosa is often found in open grasslands and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
