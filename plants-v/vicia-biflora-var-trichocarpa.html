Vicia biflora var. trichocarpa

Vicia biflora var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and its distinctive, elongated pods that contain small, round seeds. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Vicia biflora var. trichocarpa is often used in agricultural settings as a cover crop or for forage.
