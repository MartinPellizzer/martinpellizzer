Valeriana hoppii

Valeriana hoppii, commonly known as the Hoppi valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana hoppii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
