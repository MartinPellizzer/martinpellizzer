Veronica × schmakovii

Veronica × schmakovii, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
