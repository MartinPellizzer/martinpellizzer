Vanilla guianensis

Vanilla guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese vanilla, is a climbing orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing the highly prized vanilla beans. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with rich, well-drained soil. Its vanilla beans are harvested when mature and dried to develop their characteristic aroma and flavor.

This species is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
