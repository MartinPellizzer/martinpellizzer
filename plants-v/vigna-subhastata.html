Vigna subhastata

Vigna subhastata, commonly known as the Indian bean or dwarf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and essential nutrients.

Vigna subhastata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
