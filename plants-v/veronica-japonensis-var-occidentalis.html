Veronica japonensis var. occidentalis

Veronica japonensis var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western speedwell, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
