Vepris gossweileri

Vepris gossweileri, commonly known as the African wild lime, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often dotted with oil glands, contributing to its strong scent. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are borne in clusters, while the fruit is a small, rounded drupe with a thick, leathery rind.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has traditional uses in medicine and as a flavoring agent.
