Valerianella radiata f. parviflora

Valerianella radiata f. parviflora, commonly known as dwarf valerian, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact habit with dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Valerianella radiata f. parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes for its delicate appearance.
