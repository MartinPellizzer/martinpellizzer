Vauquelinia potosina

Vauquelinia potosina, commonly known as the Mexican rocknettle, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its rocky or sandy habitat. Vauquelinia potosina is adapted to arid environments, demonstrating resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
