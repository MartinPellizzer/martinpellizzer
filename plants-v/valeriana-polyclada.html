Valeriana polyclada

Valeriana polyclada, commonly known as clustered valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish or purple flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its rhizomatous root system is notable for its medicinal properties, containing compounds like valerenic acid.

Valeriana polyclada is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in herbal medicine.
