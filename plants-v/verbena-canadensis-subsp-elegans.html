Verbena canadensis subsp. elegans

Verbena canadensis subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the slender verbena, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
