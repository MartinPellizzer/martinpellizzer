Verbascum urobracteum

Verbascum urobracteum, commonly known as the desert mullein, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Verbascum urobracteum is often found in dry grasslands, sandy soils, and along roadsides.
