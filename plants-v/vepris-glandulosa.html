Vepris glandulosa

Vepris glandulosa, commonly known as the African spicebush, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers. Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems and a source of traditional medicinal uses.
