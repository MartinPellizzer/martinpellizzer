Vaccinium retifolium

Vaccinium retifolium, commonly known as the red-berry huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bright red berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as an important food source for wildlife.
