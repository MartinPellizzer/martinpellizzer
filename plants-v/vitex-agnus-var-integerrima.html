Vitex agnus var. integerrima

Vitex agnus var. integerrima, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a perennial shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually pubescent, giving the plant a velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in terminal clusters, followed by woody, bead-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
