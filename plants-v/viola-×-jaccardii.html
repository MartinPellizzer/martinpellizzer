Viola × jaccardii

Viola × jaccardii, commonly known as the common heartleaf violet, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. Native to Europe, this species thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 centimeters, with a compact, spreading habit.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
