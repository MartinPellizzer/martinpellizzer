Vernonia denudata

Vernonia denudata, commonly known as the naked vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Vernonia denudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
