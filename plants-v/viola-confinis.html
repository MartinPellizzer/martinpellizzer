Viola confinis

Viola confinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and violet. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola confinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
