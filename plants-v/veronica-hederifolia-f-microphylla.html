Veronica hederifolia f. microphylla

Veronica hederifolia f. microphylla, commonly known as the creeping speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a dense, mat-like formation. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.
