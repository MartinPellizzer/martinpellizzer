Verbesina hexantha

Verbesina hexantha, commonly known as the six-angled beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring slender stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its seeds are enclosed in small, spiny capsules that aid in wind dispersal.

Verbesina hexantha is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, making it a common weed in various regions.
