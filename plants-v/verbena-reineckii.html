Verbena reineckii

Verbena reineckii, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Verbena reineckii is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
