Viola crassa subsp. alpicola

Viola crassa subsp.

alpicola, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and frost-resistant nature.

Due to its habitat, it is often found in mountainous areas and is considered a rare and specialized species.
