Vitis amurensis var. dissecta

Vitis amurensis var. dissecta, commonly known as the Amur grape or dissected Amur grape, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are deeply lobed and often resemble those of a maple tree. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by berries that range from yellow to red in color. It is adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
