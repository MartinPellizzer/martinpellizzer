Valeriana alternifolia f. filisecta

Valeriana alternifolia f. filisecta, commonly known as the filisecta valerian, is a botanical variant of the common valerian.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided leaves that give it a feathery appearance. This plant belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.
