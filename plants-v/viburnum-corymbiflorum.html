Viburnum corymbiflorum

Viburnum corymbiflorum, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy green surface.

Viburnum corymbiflorum thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
