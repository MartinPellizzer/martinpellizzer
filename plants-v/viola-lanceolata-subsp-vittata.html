Viola lanceolata subsp. vittata

Viola lanceolata subsp.

vittata, commonly known as the spotted violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive violet petals with white or pale yellow markings, which help in attracting pollinators.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its overall appearance.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
