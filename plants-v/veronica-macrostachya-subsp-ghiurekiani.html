Veronica macrostachya subsp. ghiurekiani

Veronica macrostachya subsp.

ghiurekiani, commonly known as the Ghiurekian's speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
