Vatica roxburghiana

Vatica roxburghiana, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a broad, rounded crown and dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, clustered in spikes, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Vatica roxburghiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
