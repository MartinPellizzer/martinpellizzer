Vernonia vialis

Vernonia vialis, commonly known as smallleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are typically purple to pink, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.
