Viola grahamii

Viola grahamii, commonly known as Graham's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Viola grahamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
