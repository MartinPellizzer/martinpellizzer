Vulpia geniculata f. breviglumis

Vulpia geniculata f. breviglumis, commonly known as the short-awned bentgrass, is a grass species characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive floral structures.

It is a perennial plant native to temperate regions, often found in disturbed soils and grassy habitats. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically shorter than those of the typical species, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in low-maintenance landscaping.
