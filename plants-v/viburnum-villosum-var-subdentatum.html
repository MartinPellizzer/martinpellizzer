Viburnum villosum var. subdentatum

Viburnum villosum var. subdentatum, commonly known as the silky viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are elliptical with finely toothed margins, and it typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet.

Viburnum villosum var. subdentatum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
