Vernonia sublanata var. angustata

Vernonia sublanata var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along streambanks. The plant features slender, erect stems covered with fine, silky hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its showy, yellow flower heads appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
