Vatica teysmanniana

Vatica teysmanniana, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.

Vatica teysmanniana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests.
