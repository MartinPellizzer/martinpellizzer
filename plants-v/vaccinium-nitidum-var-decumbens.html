Vaccinium nitidum var. decumbens

Vaccinium nitidum var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by edible berries that are blue or purple in color. Its leaves are oval, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and potential for fruit production.
