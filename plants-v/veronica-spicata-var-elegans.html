Veronica spicata var. elegans

Veronica spicata var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces spikes of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.
