Verbascum × heterophlomos

Verbascum × heterophlomos, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum phlomoides.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is often found in rocky or dry soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
