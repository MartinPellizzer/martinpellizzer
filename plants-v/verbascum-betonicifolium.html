Verbascum betonicifolium

Verbascum betonicifolium, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum betonicifolium is a perennial species that is both ornamental and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
