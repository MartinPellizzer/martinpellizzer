Vitis tuberosa

Vitis tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and its flowers are small, greenish-white in color.

The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly cultivated for commercial use.
