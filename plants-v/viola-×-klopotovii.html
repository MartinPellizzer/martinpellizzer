Viola × klopotovii

Viola × klopotovii, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola lutea.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of violet, blue, and yellow. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers typically bloom in spring, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Viola × klopotovii is also noted for its ability to hybridize readily with other viola species, contributing to its genetic diversity.
