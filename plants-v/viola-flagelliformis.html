Viola flagelliformis

Viola flagelliformis, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features a creeping stem that can spread up to several meters, with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine down. Its flowers are violet or purple, with five petals, and bloom in early spring.

Viola flagelliformis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
