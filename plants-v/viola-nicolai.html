Viola nicolai

Viola nicolai, commonly known as the Nicolai violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a striking touch of color to woodland gardens.

Viola nicolai is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
