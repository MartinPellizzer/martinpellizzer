Verbena scabridoglandulosa

Verbena scabridoglandulosa, commonly known as rough-hairy verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The leaves are typically hairy and have a rough texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
