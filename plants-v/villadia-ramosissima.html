Villadia ramosissima

Villadia ramosissima, commonly known as the branched villadia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and dense, branched stems that often bear small, clustered leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
