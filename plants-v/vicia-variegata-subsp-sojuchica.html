Vicia variegata subsp. sojuchica

Vicia variegata subsp.

sojuchica, commonly known as the variable vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with variable leaflet patterns, often featuring a mix of green and white or yellow markings.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep purple. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Vicia variegata subsp.

sojuchica is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.
