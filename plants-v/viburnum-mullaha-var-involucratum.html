Viburnum mullaha var. involucratum

Viburnum mullaha var. involucratum, commonly known as the clustered snowball viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green above and lighter below, and it produces persistent, showy fruits that attract birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and aesthetic purposes in gardens.
