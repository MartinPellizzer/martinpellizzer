Vernonia kassneri

Vernonia kassneri, commonly known as the Kassner's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its striking violet to blue-purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.
