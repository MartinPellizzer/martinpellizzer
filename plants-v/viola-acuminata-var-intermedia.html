Viola acuminata var. intermedia

Viola acuminata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves with a distinctive violet or purple hue. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a yellow or brown throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
