Vriesea psittacina var. truffautiana

Vriesea psittacina var. truffautiana, commonly known as the Truffaut's Vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a dense, water-holding structure at the center. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright orange to red flowers, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
