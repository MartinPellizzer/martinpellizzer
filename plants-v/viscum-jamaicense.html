Viscum jamaicense

Viscum jamaicense, commonly known as the Jamaican mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and has a unique structure with a hemispherical shape and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by the development of fleshy, berry-like fruits. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume the fruits, facilitating its spread to new hosts.

Viscum jamaicense is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
