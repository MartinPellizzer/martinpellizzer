Vesicaria cochlearioides

Vesicaria cochlearioides, commonly known as the bladder fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated sporangia that resemble bladders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually pinnately divided, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Vesicaria cochlearioides is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
