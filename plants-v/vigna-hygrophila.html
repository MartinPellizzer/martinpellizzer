Vigna hygrophila

Vigna hygrophila, commonly known as water bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways. It has a climbing habit and can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

Vigna hygrophila is an important species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in nitrogen fixation.
