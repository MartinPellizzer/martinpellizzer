Vernonia oxylepis

Vernonia oxylepis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Vernonia oxylepis is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
