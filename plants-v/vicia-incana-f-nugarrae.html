Vicia incana f. nugarrae

Vicia incana f. nugarrae, commonly known as the Nugarra vetch, is a subspecies of the common vetch.

It is a herbaceous plant native to Australia, characterized by its hairy, slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits are typical of the Fabaceae family.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
