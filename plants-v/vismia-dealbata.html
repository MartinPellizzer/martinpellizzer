Vismia dealbata

Vismia dealbata, commonly known as the white vismia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests or savannas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
