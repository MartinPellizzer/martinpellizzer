Vernonia calongensis

Vernonia calongensis, commonly known as the Calonge ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are usually yellow to brown in color and bloom during the rainy season.

Vernonia calongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
