Vernonia missurica f. carnea

Vernonia missurica f. carnea, commonly known as the pink coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, sunny habitats like wetlands and prairies.

The cultivar 'carnea' is noted for its vibrant floral color and is often used in ornamental gardening.
