Vaccinium stenophyllum

Vaccinium stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The berries, which are typically red or purple, are edible and contain a high concentration of antioxidants.
