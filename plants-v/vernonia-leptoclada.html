Vernonia leptoclada

Vernonia leptoclada, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

Vernonia leptoclada is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
