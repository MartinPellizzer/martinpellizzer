Vilfa pyramidalis

Vilfa pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

Vilfa pyramidalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
