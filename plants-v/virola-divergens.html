Virola divergens

Virola divergens, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical conditions. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

Virola divergens is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in indigenous medicine.
