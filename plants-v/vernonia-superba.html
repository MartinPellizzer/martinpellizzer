Vernonia superba

Vernonia superba, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant produces striking blue to purple flower clusters that appear in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Vernonia superba is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
