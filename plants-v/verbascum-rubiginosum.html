Verbascum rubiginosum

Verbascum rubiginosum, commonly known as the red velvetleaf or red mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Verbascum rubiginosum is a perennial species that can reach heights of up to two meters, making it a striking addition to wildflower gardens.
