Vania kurdica

Vania kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Vania kurdica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
