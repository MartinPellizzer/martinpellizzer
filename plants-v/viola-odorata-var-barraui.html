Viola odorata var. barraui

Viola odorata var. barraui, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, typically growing to a height of about 15-30 cm. Its flowers emit a sweet, fragrant aroma, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
