Valvaria gentianoides

Valvaria gentianoides, commonly known as the Sierra foothill gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valvaria gentianoides plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
