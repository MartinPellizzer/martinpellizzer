Viola mirabilis var. glaberrima

Viola mirabilis var. glaberrima, commonly known as the velvetleaf viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety texture. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
