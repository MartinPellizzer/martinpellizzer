Vellozia alata

Vellozia alata, commonly known as the winged vellozia, is a unique member of the Velloziaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, wing-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to drought conditions. Vellozia alata produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish in color.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and evolutionary botany.
