Verbascum glabratum subsp. bosnense

Verbascum glabratum subsp.

bosnense, commonly known as the Bosnian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, grey-green leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
