Viola calcarata f. grandiflora

Viola calcarata f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered rock violet, is a horticultural form of the rock violet species.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with distinctive yellow markings on the petals. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy coating.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
