Vachellia abyssinica

Vachellia abyssinica, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, and its yellow, globular inflorescences that attract various pollinators. The plant produces small, flat, pod-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous, spiky covering. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Vachellia abyssinica also has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber and fuelwood.
