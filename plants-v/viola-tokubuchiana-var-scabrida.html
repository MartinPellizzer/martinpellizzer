Viola tokubuchiana var. scabrida

Viola tokubuchiana var. scabrida, commonly known as the rough viol, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough, hairy texture that gives it its common name. The plant produces showy violet flowers with purple petals and yellow markings, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Violaceae family.
