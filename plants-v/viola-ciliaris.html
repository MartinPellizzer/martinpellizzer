Viola ciliaris

Viola ciliaris, commonly known as the fringed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive fringed edges. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive hooded structure. Viola ciliaris thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
