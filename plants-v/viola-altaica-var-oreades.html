Viola altaica var. oreades

Viola altaica var. oreades, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to harsh climates and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
