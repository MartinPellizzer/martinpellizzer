Valeriana alternifolia f. verticillata

Valeriana alternifolia f. verticillata, commonly known as the clustered valerian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite or whorled leaves, which give it its scientific name, and it is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Valeriana alternifolia f. verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.
