Verbesina monticola

Verbesina monticola, commonly known as mountain beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. Verbesina monticola is typically found in dry, open habitats like woodlands and prairies.
