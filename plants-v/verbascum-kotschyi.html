Verbascum kotschyi

Verbascum kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants commonly referred to as mulleins. The plant is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in soft, velvety hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
