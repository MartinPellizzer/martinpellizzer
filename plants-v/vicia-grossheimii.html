Vicia grossheimii

Vicia grossheimii, commonly known as the Grossheim's vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-pink flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Vicia grossheimii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically significant in nutrient-poor environments.
