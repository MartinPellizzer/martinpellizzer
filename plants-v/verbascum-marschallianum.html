Verbascum marschallianum

Verbascum marschallianum, commonly known as the marshmallow weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. The plant's common name reflects its fuzzy texture, which resembles the texture of marshmallows.
