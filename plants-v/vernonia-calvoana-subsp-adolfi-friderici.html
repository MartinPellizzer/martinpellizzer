Vernonia calvoana subsp. adolfi-friderici

Vernonia calvoana subsp.

adolfi-friderici, commonly known as the Adolph-Friedrich vernonia, is a perennial shrub native to Central America. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
