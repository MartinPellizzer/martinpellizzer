Viguiera cordifolia var. genuina

Viguiera cordifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
