Vaccinium taliense

Vaccinium taliense, commonly known as the Taliense blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like heather and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool climate with adequate rainfall.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
