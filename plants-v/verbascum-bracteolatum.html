Verbascum bracteolatum

Verbascum bracteolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its attractive appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
