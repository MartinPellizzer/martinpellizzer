Viola nesiotica

Viola nesiotica, commonly known as the Neotropical violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of violet and purple. Viola nesiotica is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small insects.
