Verbesina virgata var. virgata

Verbesina virgata var. virgata, commonly known as yellow beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is an annual or biennial herb with erect, branched stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from summer to fall. Its seeds are encased in small, spiny structures that aid in wind dispersal.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological role.
