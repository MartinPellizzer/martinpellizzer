Verbascum biebersteinii

Verbascum biebersteinii, commonly known as the Kashmir mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a cylindrical spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid garden environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for respiratory ailments.
