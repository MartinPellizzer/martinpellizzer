Vigna unguiculata var. protracta

Vigna unguiculata var. protracta, commonly known as the protracta cowpea, is a variant of the cowpea species native to Africa.

It is an annual legume characterized by its slender, elongated pods and small, edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein.

The botanical features of this variety make it well-suited for both traditional farming and sustainable agriculture practices.
