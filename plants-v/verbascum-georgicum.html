Verbascum georgicum

Verbascum georgicum, commonly known as Georgian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies. Its leaves are thick and fuzzy, often used in poultices for respiratory ailments.

Verbascum georgicum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
