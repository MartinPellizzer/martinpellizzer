Verbascum stachydifolium var. adspersum

Verbascum stachydifolium var. adspersum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, silky white hairs. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and also densely hairy, giving the plant a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a spiky inflorescence.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
