Vitex mossambicensis

Vitex mossambicensis, commonly known as the African blueberry or Cape ivy, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, which are followed by blue or black berries. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environments.

Vitex mossambicensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
