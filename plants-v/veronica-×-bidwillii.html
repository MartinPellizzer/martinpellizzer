Veronica × bidwillii

Veronica × bidwillii, commonly known as the Bidwill's speedwell, is a hybrid plant in the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period, which extends from spring to autumn.
