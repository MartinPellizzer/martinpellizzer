Verbena rigida f. lilacina

Verbena rigida f. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of lilac-pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is narrow and gray-green, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
