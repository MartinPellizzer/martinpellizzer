Viola pseudasterias

Viola pseudasterias, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a deep green color and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of violet or blue. Its flowers are typically spurred at the base, a characteristic feature of the Violaceae family.

Viola pseudasterias is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
