Vitex rapinii f. dentata

Vitex rapinii f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed vitex, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Vitex rapinii f. dentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
