Varronia urticifolia

Varronia urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing only leaves and the second year producing flowers and fruits. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Varronia urticifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
