Viola hederacea subsp. sieberiana

Viola hederacea subsp.

sieberiana, commonly known as the Sieber's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow markings.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include a basal rosette structure and a short, creeping stem.
