Viguiera triquetra

Viguiera triquetra, commonly known as the three-lobed ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green to reddish-purple flower heads. The plant produces small, three-lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Viguiera triquetra is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
