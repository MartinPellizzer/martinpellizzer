Vernonia lualabaensis

Vernonia lualabaensis, commonly known as the Lualaba ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Vernonia lualabaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
