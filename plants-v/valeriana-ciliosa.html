Valeriana ciliosa

Valeriana ciliosa, commonly known as the silky valerian, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. Valeriana ciliosa thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a common sight in meadows and woodland edges.

Its aromatic properties have made it a popular choice in herbal remedies for its calming effects.
