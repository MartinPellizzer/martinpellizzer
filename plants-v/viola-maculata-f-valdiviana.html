Viola maculata f. valdiviana

Viola maculata f. valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive dark purple or reddish markings. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, often with a yellow or white center. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders in horticulture.
