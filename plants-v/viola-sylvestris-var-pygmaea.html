Viola sylvestris var. pygmaea

Viola sylvestris var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf wood violet, is a small, flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in association with other woodland flora.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
