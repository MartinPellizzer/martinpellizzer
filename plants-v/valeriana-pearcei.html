Valeriana pearcei

Valeriana pearcei, commonly known as the Sierra Valley valerian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces tiny, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Valeriana pearcei is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
