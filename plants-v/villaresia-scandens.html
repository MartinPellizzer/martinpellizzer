Villaresia scandens

Villaresia scandens, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, adapted for climbing and photosynthesis in shaded environments.

Villaresia scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is found in humid, warm climates.
