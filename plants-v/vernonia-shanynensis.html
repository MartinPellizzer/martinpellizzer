Vernonia shanynensis

Vernonia shanynensis, commonly known as the Shanyn's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces vibrant yellow to orange blooms during the late summer and early autumn.

Vernonia shanynensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
