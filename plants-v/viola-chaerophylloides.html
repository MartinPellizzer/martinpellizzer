Viola chaerophylloides

Viola chaerophylloides, commonly known as the rock viol, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate drought conditions.

Viola chaerophylloides is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a wildflower in garden landscapes.
