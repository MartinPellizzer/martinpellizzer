Viola calcarata subsp. grandiflora

Viola calcarata subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
