Viola yedoensis f. candida

Viola yedoensis f. candida, commonly known as the white violet or Japanese white violet, is a hybrid form of the viola yedoensis species.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which contrast with its dark green, heart-shaped leaves. This plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to woodland gardens.
