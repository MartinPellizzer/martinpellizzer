Valerianella umbilicata f. patellaria

Valerianella umbilicata f. patellaria, commonly known as the "pea leaf sorrel," is a cultivated form of the wild plant Valerianella umbilicata.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, succulent leaves that resemble pea leaves, giving it its common name. The plant is typically grown as a leafy vegetable, often used in salads or as a garnish due to its mild, tangy flavor. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, preferring partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use, making it a popular choice in home gardens.
