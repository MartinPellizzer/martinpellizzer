Valetonia brasiliensis

Valetonia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazil orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Valetonia brasiliensis is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
