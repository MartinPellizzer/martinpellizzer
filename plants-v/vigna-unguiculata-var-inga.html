Vigna unguiculata var. inga

Vigna unguiculata var. inga, commonly known as cowpea or black-eyed pea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is highly drought-tolerant. It plays a significant role in agricultural systems due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both food and forage purposes.
