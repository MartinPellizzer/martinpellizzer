Viscum longipetiolatum

Viscum longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and gelatinous berries. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded into the host's bark. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Viscum longipetiolatum is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its host trees.
