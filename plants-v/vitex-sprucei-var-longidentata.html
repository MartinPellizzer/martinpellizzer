Vitex sprucei var. longidentata

Vitex sprucei var. longidentata, commonly known as the long-toothed vitex, is a shrub native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are typically lanceolate with serrated margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
