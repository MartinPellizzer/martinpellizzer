Viola grypoceras var. grayi

Viola grypoceras var. grayi, commonly known as the Gray's viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from violet to white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
