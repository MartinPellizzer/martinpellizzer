Viola dehnhardtii var. atlantica

Viola dehnhardtii var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the Atlantic coast, thriving in partially shaded to sunny conditions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, violet-colored flowers with yellow markings. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to stabilize dune ecosystems.

Due to its limited range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
