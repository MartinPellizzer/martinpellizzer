Viola × insidiosa

Viola × insidiosa, commonly known as the common violets, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Viola × insidiosa is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade.
