Verbesina oerstediana

Verbesina oerstediana, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescence that resembles a crown, which is a key botanical feature. The plant has opposite, hairy, ovate leaves that are often gland-dotted, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, typically found in open woods and along roadsides.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
