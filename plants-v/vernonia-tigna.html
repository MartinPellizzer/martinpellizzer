Vernonia tigna

Vernonia tigna, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the cooler months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Vernonia tigna is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging climates.
