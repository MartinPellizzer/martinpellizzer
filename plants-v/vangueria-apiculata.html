Vangueria apiculata

Vangueria apiculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf vangueria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

Vangueria apiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
