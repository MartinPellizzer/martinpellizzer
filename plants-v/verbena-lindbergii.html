Verbena lindbergii

Verbena lindbergii, commonly known as Lindberg's verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms during the warmer months, producing clusters of small, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both garden and horticultural settings.
