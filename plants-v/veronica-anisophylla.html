Veronica anisophylla

Veronica anisophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Veronica anisophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
