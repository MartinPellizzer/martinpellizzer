Vernonia obtusata

Vernonia obtusata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces fluffy, blue-gray flower clusters that resemble mist, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Vernonia obtusata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
