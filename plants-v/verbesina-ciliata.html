Verbesina ciliata

Verbesina ciliata, commonly known as the silkyleaf beggarticks, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, ovate leaves with soft, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Verbesina ciliata is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.
