Vangueria cinnamomea

Vangueria cinnamomea, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils near water bodies. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Vangueria cinnamomea is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
