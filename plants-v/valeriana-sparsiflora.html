Valeriana sparsiflora

Valeriana sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds responsible for its aromatic properties.

Valeriana sparsiflora is valued in traditional medicine for its sedative and calming effects.
