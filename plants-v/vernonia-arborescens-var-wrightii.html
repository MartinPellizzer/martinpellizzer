Vernonia arborescens var. wrightii

Vernonia arborescens var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a tall, erect perennial that typically grows up to 6 feet in height, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, spiky, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its fibrous roots and sturdy stems make it a hardy species adapted to various soil types.

It is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and streambanks.
