Viola segobricensis var. leiocarpa

Viola segobricensis var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, pale yellow or white flowers. Its flowers have five petals, with the upper one often being larger and more prominent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
