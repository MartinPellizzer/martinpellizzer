Verbena salviifolia

Verbena salviifolia, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Verbena salviifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
