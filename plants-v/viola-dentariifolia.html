Viola dentariifolia

Viola dentariifolia, commonly known as the toothed violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinct toothed edges and delicate purple to blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola dentariifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
