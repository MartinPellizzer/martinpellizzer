Vernonia oligolepis

Vernonia oligolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Vernonia oligolepis is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies.

Its foliage and flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
