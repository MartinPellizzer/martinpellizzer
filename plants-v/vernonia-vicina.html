Vernonia vicina

Vernonia vicina, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with purple centers.

Vernonia vicina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
