Veronica olympica

Veronica olympica, commonly known as the Olympic speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its resilience in harsh climates.

Veronica olympica is often found in meadows and open slopes at high altitudes.
