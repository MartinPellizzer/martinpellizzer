Vanasushava

Vanasushava, commonly known as the "wild licorice," is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots are valued for their sweet, aromatic flavor and are used in herbal remedies.

Vanasushava thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
