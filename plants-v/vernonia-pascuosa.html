Vernonia pascuosa

Vernonia pascuosa, commonly known as tall ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of dark purple to reddish violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia pascuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a food source for various pollinators.
