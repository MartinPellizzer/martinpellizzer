Vernonia multiflora

Vernonia multiflora, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in a compact, bushy form. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Vernonia multiflora is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
