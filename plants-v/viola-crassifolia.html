Viola crassifolia

Viola crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, velvety hair, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
