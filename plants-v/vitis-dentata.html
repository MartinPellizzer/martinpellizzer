Vitis dentata

Vitis dentata, commonly known as the river grape, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. Vitis dentata is often found growing along streams, rivers, and moist woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in winemaking.
