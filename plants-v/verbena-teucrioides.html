Verbena teucrioides

Verbena teucrioides, commonly known as narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom from summer into early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Verbena teucrioides is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.
