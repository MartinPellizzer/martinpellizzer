Vicia johannis var. ecirrhosa

Vicia johannis var. ecirrhosa, commonly known as the John's vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods are slender and curved. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
