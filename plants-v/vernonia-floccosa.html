Vernonia floccosa

Vernonia floccosa, commonly known as the woolly ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, woolly foliage and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Vernonia floccosa typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and thrives in full sun.

Its unique appearance and ecological value make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
