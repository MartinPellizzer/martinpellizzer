Veronica spicata subsp. paczoskiana

Veronica spicata subsp.

paczoskiana, commonly known as the Pacific Veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
