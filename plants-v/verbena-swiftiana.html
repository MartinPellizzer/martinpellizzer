Verbena swiftiana

Verbena swiftiana, commonly known as the desert verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of pink, purple, and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Verbena swiftiana is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
