Veronica anagallis-aquatica subsp. pallidiflora

Veronica anagallis-aquatica subsp.

pallidiflora, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist or aquatic habitats. It features slender stems that often grow from water or damp soil, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped.

The flowers are small, blue or purple, and arranged in clusters, giving it a delicate appearance. It is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other waterlogged environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
