Vernonia ruficoma var. neriifolia

Vernonia ruficoma var. neriifolia, commonly known as the blackleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red, depending on the stage of bloom. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside.

This species is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
