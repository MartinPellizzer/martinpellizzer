Viola arborescens f. latifolia

Viola arborescens f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved tree violet, is a unique variant of the tree violet species.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland habitats. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

The plant's ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms.
