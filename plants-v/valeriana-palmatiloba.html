Valeriana palmatiloba

Valeriana palmatiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved valerian, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are typically dark green and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Valeriana palmatiloba is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands or along streambanks.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
