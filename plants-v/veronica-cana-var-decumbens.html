Veronica cana var. decumbens

Veronica cana var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
