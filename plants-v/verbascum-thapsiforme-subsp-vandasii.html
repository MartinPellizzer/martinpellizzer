Verbascum thapsiforme subsp. vandasii

Verbascum thapsiforme subsp.

vandasii, commonly known as the Kashmir sunhemp, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has soft, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It is often found growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the genus Verbascum.
