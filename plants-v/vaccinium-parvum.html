Vaccinium parvum

Vaccinium parvum, commonly known as the lowbush blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and other nutrients. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn red in the fall.

The plant is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
