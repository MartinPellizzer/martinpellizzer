Veronica sublaciniata

Veronica sublaciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in alpine and coastal regions, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
