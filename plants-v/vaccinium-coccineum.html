Vaccinium coccineum

Vaccinium coccineum, commonly known as the red huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Vaccinium coccineum plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
