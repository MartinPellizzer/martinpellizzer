Vaccinium angulatum

Vaccinium angulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, rich in antioxidants. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial shade to full sun.

Its botanical features include alternate, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring.
