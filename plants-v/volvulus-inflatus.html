Volvulus inflatus

Volvulus inflatus, commonly known as the inflated volvulus, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, balloon-like inflated leaves.

These leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Volvulus inflatus is native to certain regions of Africa and thrives in dry, sunny conditions.
