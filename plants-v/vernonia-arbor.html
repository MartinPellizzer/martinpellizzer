Vernonia arbor

Vernonia arbor, commonly known as the tree verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Vernonia arbor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and garden design.
