Veronica prostrata

Veronica prostrata, commonly known as creeping speedwell, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
