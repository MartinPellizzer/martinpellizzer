Vernonia altissima var. taeniotricha

Vernonia altissima var. taeniotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, dark purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for native plant gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects.
