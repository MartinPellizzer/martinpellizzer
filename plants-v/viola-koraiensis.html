Viola koraiensis

Viola koraiensis, commonly known as the Korean violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola koraiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
