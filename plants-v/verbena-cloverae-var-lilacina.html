Verbena cloverae var. lilacina

Verbena cloverae var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, lilac-pink flowers. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
