Vulpia geniculata subsp. monanthera

Vulpia geniculata subsp.

monanthera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vulpia, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-flowered spikelets. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
