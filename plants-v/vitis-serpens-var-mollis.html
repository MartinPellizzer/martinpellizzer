Vitis serpens var. mollis

Vitis serpens var. mollis, commonly known as the smoothleaf grapevine, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of edible berries. These berries are typically dark purple to black when ripe and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
