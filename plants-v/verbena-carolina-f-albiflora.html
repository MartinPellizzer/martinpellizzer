Verbena carolina f. albiflora

Verbena carolina f. albiflora, commonly known as the white verbena, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, tubular white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and pollinator habitats.
