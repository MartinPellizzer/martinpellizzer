Viola raddeana var. japonica

Viola raddeana var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded gardens.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a houseplant.
