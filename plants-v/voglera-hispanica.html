Voglera hispanica

Voglera hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Voglera hispanica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
