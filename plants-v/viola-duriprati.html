Viola duriprati

Viola duriprati, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including parts of Iran and Central Asia. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, downy coating.

Viola duriprati is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions.
