Viola barhalensis

Viola barhalensis, commonly known as the Barhal violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola barhalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
