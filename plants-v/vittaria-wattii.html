Vittaria wattii

Vittaria wattii, commonly known as the slender spikemoss, is a species of moss plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Vittaria wattii reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of its leaves.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
