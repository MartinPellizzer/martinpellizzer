Viguiera guerrerana

Viguiera guerrerana, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open areas and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Viguiera guerrerana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
