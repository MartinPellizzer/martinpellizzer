Viola purpurea subsp. quercetorum

Viola purpurea subsp.

quercetorum, commonly known as the oakleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive oak-like lobed pattern, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces violet flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
