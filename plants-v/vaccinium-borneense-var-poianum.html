Vaccinium borneense var. poianum

Vaccinium borneense var. poianum, commonly known as the Bornean huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark red or black in color, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and montane forest habitats.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential economic value.
