Viola silvatica var. rostrata

Viola silvatica var. rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the forest floor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
