Veronica subspicata

Veronica subspicata, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its characteristic spiculate inflorescence, which consists of dense, spike-like clusters of flowers.
