Vanhouttea salviifolia var. parviflora

Vanhouttea salviifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered sterculia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features elliptical to lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release small, smooth seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
