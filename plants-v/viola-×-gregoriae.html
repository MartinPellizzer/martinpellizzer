Viola × gregoriae

Viola × gregoriae, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade.

Viola × gregoriae is often used in gardens for its attractive appearance and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
