Verbesina coahuilensis var. coahuilensis

Verbesina coahuilensis var. coahuilensis, commonly known as the Coahuiltecan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, providing some drought resistance.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
