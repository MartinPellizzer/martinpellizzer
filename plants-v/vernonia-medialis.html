Vernonia medialis

Vernonia medialis, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The showy purple to pink flower heads appear in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
