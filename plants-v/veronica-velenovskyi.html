Veronica velenovskyi

Veronica velenovskyi, commonly known as the Sierra veronica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
