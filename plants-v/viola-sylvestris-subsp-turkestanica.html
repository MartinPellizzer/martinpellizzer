Viola sylvestris subsp. turkestanica

Viola sylvestris subsp.

turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet or purple flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.

This subspecies is closely related to other viola species and shares similar botanical features such as a sympetalous corolla and a retuse apex.
