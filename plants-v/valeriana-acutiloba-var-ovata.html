Valeriana acutiloba var. ovata

Valeriana acutiloba var. ovata, commonly known as the ovate valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is thick and fleshy, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.
