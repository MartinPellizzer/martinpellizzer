Verbascum thapsus subsp. crassifolium

Verbascum thapsus subsp.

crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that are broad and lance-shaped.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in a loose spike, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

Due to its hairy leaves and medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies.
