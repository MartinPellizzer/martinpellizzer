Verbascum × ambigens

Verbascum × ambigens, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their large, showy flowers. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, erect stems and fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It typically blooms with yellow or orange flowers in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower meadows.
