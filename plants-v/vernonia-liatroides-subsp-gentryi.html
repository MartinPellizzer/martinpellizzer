Vernonia liatroides subsp. gentryi

Vernonia liatroides subsp.

gentryi, commonly known as the Gentry's ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. Its small, blue to purple flower clusters appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
