Verbascum × keklikolukense

Verbascum × keklikolukense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum denscanthum.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and surrounding areas. The plant features large, fuzzy, silver-green leaves that are characteristic of the mullein genus. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a dense spike, and bloom in late summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
