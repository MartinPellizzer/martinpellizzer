Vatovaea pseudolablab

Vatovaea pseudolablab, commonly known as the Madagascar bean, is a leguminous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees. Its seeds are often used in local cuisines and have nutritional value.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.
