Viola lutea var. curtisii

Viola lutea var. curtisii, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive violet center. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
