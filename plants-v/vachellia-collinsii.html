Vachellia collinsii

Vachellia collinsii, commonly known as the Collins' acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and phyllodes that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular spikes, which are a notable feature during the flowering season. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky soils.

Vachellia collinsii is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
