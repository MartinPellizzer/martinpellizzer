Valeriana tripteris subsp. austriaca

Valeriana tripteris subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as Austrian valerian, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, somewhat hairy leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, berry-like fruits that are dispersed by birds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
