Verbena officinalis var. gaudichaudii

Verbena officinalis var. gaudichaudii, commonly known as Gaudichaud's vervain, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
