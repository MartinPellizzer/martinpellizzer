Vicia sativa subsp. cosentinii

Vicia sativa subsp.

cosentinii, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its slender, trailing growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green pea-like flowers followed by narrow, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in dry grasslands.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
