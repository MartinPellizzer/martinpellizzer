Viola triloba f. annjoae

Viola triloba f. annjoae, commonly known as the Annjoa viola, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the common violet.

It is characterized by its unique, trifoliate leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. Native to specific regions in the United States, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. Its foliage is often covered in a fine layer of pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is valued for its horticultural significance and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
