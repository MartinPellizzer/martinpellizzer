Viola reidiae

Viola reidiae, commonly known as the Reida's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe, particularly in the Carpathian Mountains. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Viola reidiae is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
