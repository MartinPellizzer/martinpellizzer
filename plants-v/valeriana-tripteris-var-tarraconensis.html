Valeriana tripteris var. tarraconensis

Valeriana tripteris var. tarraconensis, commonly known as the Iberian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and lanceolate, slightly hairy leaves arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its historical use in traditional medicine.
