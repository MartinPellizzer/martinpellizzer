Viola tricolor proles cryana

Viola tricolor proles cryana, commonly known as the three-color violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often displaying a mix of purple, yellow, and white hues. The flowers are typically spurred and come in a range of colors, including violet, blue, and white, depending on the variety. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or garden settings.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
