Vilfa minuta

Vilfa minuta, commonly known as the dwarf veldt grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Vilfa minuta is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in well-drained soils.
