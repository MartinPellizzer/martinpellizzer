Vernonia lanata

Vernonia lanata, commonly known as the woolly ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Vernonia lanata is often found in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in full sun.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and drought-tolerant species within the Asteraceae family.
