Vulpia geniculata subsp. pauana

Vulpia geniculata subsp.

pauana, commonly known as the Brazilian mouse-tail grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
