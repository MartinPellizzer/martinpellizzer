Vitis lanata

Vitis lanata, commonly known as the hairy grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color. Vitis lanata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun.

It is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
