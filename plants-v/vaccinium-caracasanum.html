Vaccinium caracasanum

Vaccinium caracasanum, commonly known as the Caracas huckleberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the tropical Andes of South America, specifically in regions of Ecuador and Peru. This evergreen shrub typically grows at high elevations, often in cloud forests, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and are used both for food and traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native range.
