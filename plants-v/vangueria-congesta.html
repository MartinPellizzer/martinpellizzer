Vangueria congesta

Vangueria congesta, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Vangueria congesta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
