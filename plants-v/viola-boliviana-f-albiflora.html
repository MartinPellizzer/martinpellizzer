Viola boliviana f. albiflora

Viola boliviana f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, white flowers with a distinctive violet hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
