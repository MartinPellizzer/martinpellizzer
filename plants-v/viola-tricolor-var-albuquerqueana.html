Viola tricolor var. albuquerqueana

Viola tricolor var. albuquerqueana, commonly known as the Albuquerque violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet, white, and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid climates.
