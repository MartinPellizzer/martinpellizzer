Verbesina aligera

Verbesina aligera, commonly known as the yellow beggar-ticks, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds with tiny hooks. It thrives in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and along stream banks.

Verbesina aligera is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
