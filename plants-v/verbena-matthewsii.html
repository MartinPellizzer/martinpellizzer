Verbena matthewsii

Verbena matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthews' verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
