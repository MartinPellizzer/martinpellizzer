Verbesina alata var. genuina

Verbesina alata var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow ray flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces numerous elongated, slender stems that can grow up to several feet in height. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
