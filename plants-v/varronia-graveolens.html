Varronia graveolens

Varronia graveolens, commonly known as the West Indian cherry or the Brazilian cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its flowers are fragrant and white, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit, while edible, is typically used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural antioxidants.
