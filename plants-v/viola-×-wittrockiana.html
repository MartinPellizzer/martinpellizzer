Viola × wittrockiana

Viola × wittrockiana, commonly known as the modern garden viola, is a hybrid species resulting from crosses between species of the genus Viola.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and an abundance of showy, heart-shaped flowers in a wide range of colors. The plant typically produces flowers in early spring and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and containers. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for various garden settings.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, Viola × wittrockiana is widely used in horticulture.
