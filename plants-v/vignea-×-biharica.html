Vignea × biharica

Vignea × biharica, commonly known as the Bihar bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to the plant.
