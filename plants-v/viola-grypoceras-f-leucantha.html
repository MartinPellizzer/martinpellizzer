Viola grypoceras f. leucantha

Viola grypoceras f. leucantha, commonly known as the white viola, is a rare and ornamental cultivar of the viola genus.

It is characterized by its pale to white petals, which contrast with its deep green foliage. This hybrid originates from a cross between Viola grypoceras and another viola species, resulting in unique floral characteristics. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade.

The plant is valued for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
