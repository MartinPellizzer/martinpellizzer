Velleia macrophylla var. foliosa

Velleia macrophylla var. foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf velleia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.
