Vaccinium moritzianum

Vaccinium moritzianum, commonly known as the Moritz blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
