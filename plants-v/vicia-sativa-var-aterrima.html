Vicia sativa var. aterrima

Vicia sativa var. aterrima, commonly known as black vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agricultural settings for soil enrichment.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
