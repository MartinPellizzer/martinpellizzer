Viola utchinensis

Viola utchinensis, commonly known as the Utsunomiya violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola utchinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
