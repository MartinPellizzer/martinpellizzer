Vitis vinifera var. columellae

Vitis vinifera var. columellae, commonly known as the Columella grape, is a rare and ancient variety of grapevine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple berries and is typically grown in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and produces clusters of fruit that are often used for making wine or jams. It is valued for its unique flavor profile and historical significance in viticulture.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized vineyards or botanical gardens.
