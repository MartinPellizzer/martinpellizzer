Viola ilvensis

Viola ilvensis, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Viola ilvensis is often found in meadows and forested areas at high altitudes.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
