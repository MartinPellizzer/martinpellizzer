Verbena alopecurus

Verbena alopecurus, commonly known as mouse-ear verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Verbena alopecurus is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.
