Vitis crenata

Vitis crenata, commonly known as the Japanese grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, including Japan, Korea, and China, and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when ripe. Vitis crenata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

It has been used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
