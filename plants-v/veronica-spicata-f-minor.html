Veronica spicata f. minor

Veronica spicata f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf loosestrife, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wet meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, shade-tolerant plant.
