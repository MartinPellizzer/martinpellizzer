Vaccaria parviflora

Vaccaria parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming habit with numerous prostrate stems and small, fleshy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
