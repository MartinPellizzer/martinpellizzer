Vigna unguiculata subsp. burundiensis

Vigna unguiculata subsp.

burundiensis, commonly known as the Burundi cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive dark green color.

The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used as a food source. It is adapted to grow in poor soils and is drought-tolerant, making it an important crop in regions with limited agricultural resources.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
