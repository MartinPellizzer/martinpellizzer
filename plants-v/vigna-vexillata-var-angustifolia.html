Vigna vexillata var. angustifolia

Vigna vexillata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its protein-rich seeds, which are used in various cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in sustainable agriculture due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
