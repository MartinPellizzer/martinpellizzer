Viola leucocentra

Viola leucocentra, commonly known as the white violet, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, pale violet or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in calcareous soils.

Viola leucocentra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
