Vicoa rupicola

Vicoa rupicola, commonly known as the rock vicoa, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its adaptability in harsh environments.

Vicoa rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
