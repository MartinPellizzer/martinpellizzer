Viola langeana

Viola langeana, commonly known as the Lange's violet, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola langeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
