Veronica rotundifolia

Veronica rotundifolia, commonly known as roundleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, succulent leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
