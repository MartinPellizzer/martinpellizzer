Viola canina var. kamtschatica

Viola canina var. kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamtschatka violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Far East and parts of Siberia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet, often with yellow markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
