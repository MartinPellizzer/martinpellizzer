Viola hemsleyana

Viola hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high elevations. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola hemsleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
