Valentinia

Valentinia, commonly known as the Valentine's rose, is a flowering plant prized for its vibrant, heart-shaped blooms.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, are often used in floral arrangements and as symbols of love.

Valentinia thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
