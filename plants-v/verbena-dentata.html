Verbena dentata

Verbena dentata, commonly known as the toothed verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated edges, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woods and along stream banks.

The plant is known for its showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
