Veronica vaillantii

Veronica vaillantii, commonly known as the Sierra veronica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Veronica vaillantii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
