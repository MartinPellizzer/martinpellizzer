Vaccinium insigne

Vaccinium insigne, commonly known as the noble blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are highly prized for their flavor and nutritional value. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and remain green throughout the year, providing ornamental value.

Vaccinium insigne is also valued for its ecological role, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
