Vriesea roberto-seidelii

Vriesea roberto-seidelii, commonly known as the Roberto Seidel Vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette structure, which helps collect rainwater for the plant's roots. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
