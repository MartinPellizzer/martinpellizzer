Verbena simplex var. orcuttiana

Verbena simplex var. orcuttiana, commonly known as the Orcutt's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
