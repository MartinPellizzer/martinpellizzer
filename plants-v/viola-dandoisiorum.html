Viola dandoisiorum

Viola dandoisiorum, commonly known as the violet of the Dandois, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, typically found in moist, woodland habitats. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of down.

Viola dandoisiorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
