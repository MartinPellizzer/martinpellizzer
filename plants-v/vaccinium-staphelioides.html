Vaccinium staphelioides

Vaccinium staphelioides, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant typically features small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in the spring. Its berries, which are edible, are usually red or black and have a tart, acidic flavor.

The species is adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and pine flatwoods.
