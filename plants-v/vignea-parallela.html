Vignea parallela

Vignea parallela, commonly known as the parallel vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Vignea parallela thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
