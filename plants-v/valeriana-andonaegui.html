Valeriana andonaegui

Valeriana andonaegui, commonly known as the Andean valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, slightly medicinal scent and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

Valeriana andonaegui is valued for its potential therapeutic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
