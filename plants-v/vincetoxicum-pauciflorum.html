Vincetoxicum pauciflorum

Vincetoxicum pauciflorum, commonly known as the lesser bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in waste areas.

Vincetoxicum pauciflorum is known for its ornamental value but can be considered invasive in some regions.
