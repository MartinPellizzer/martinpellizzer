Vaccinium tenerellum

Vaccinium tenerellum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Vaccinium tenerellum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
