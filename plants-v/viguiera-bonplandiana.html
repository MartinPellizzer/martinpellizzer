Viguiera bonplandiana

Viguiera bonplandiana, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its showy, pale purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viguiera bonplandiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.
