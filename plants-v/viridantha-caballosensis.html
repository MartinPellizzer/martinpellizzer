Viridantha caballosensis

Viridantha caballosensis, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale green flowers that emit a subtle fragrance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery down that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
