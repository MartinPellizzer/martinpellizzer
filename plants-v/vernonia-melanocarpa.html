Vernonia melanocarpa

Vernonia melanocarpa, commonly known as blackstem ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia melanocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
