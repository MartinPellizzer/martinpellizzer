Vernonia popayanensis

Vernonia popayanensis, commonly known as the Popayan ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and is known for its striking blue to purple flower clusters. Vernonia popayanensis has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
