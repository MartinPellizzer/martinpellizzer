Velezia hispida

Velezia hispida, commonly known as the rough velezia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Velezia hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
