Vignea × pseudoaxillaris

Vignea × pseudoaxillaris, commonly known as the hybrid passion flower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble those of the genus Passiflora, with intricate patterns of red, blue, and white. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Vignea × pseudoaxillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
