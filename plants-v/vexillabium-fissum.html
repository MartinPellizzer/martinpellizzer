Vexillabium fissum

Vexillabium fissum, commonly known as the Split-leaf Bannerflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Vexillabium fissum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
