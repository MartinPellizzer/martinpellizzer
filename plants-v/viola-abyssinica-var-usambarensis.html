Viola abyssinica var. usambarensis

Viola abyssinica var. usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarensis Violet, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant violet flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are usually borne in terminal clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique botanical features, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of alpine flora.
