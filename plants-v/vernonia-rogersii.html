Vernonia rogersii

Vernonia rogersii, commonly known as Rogers' ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall.

Vernonia rogersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
