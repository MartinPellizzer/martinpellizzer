Vernonia bambilorensis

Vernonia bambilorensis, commonly known as the Bambilo ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Vernonia bambilorensis is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in traditional construction and tool-making.
