Vitex pearsonii

Vitex pearsonii, commonly known as the pearson's vitex, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, rich soils along stream banks and in forest edges. The plant produces fragrant, lavender-colored flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
