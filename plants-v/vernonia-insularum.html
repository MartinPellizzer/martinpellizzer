Vernonia insularum

Vernonia insularum, commonly known as the island ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, violet-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique environmental conditions of Hawaii. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia insularum plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
