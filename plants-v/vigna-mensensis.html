Vigna mensensis

Vigna mensensis, commonly known as the Mense bean, is a flowering plant species native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and the seeds are enclosed within hairy, elongated pods.

Vigna mensensis is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed.
