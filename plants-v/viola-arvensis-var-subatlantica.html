Viola arvensis var. subatlantica

Viola arvensis var. subatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast.

It typically grows in moist, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as dunes, cliffs, and coastal grasslands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and it produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white. It is adapted to tolerate salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of coastal ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
