Vachellia fischeri

Vachellia fischeri, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like crown of thorny branches and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers and long, flat pods that contain seeds. It has small, compound leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss.

Vachellia fischeri plays an important role in providing shade and habitat for wildlife in its native ecosystems.
