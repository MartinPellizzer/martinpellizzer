Vicia biflora subsp. carthaginensis

Vicia biflora subsp.

carthaginensis, commonly known as the Carthaginian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, elongated clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
