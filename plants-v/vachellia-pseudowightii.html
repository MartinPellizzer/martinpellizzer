Vachellia pseudowightii

Vachellia pseudowightii, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually trifoliate or pinnate, and its distinctive yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces long, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species often found in savannas and woodland areas.
