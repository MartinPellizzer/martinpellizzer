Vernonia thwaitesii

Vernonia thwaitesii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-to-orange flower heads, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Vernonia thwaitesii is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in savanna and woodland habitats.
