Verbascum × maeandriforme

Verbascum × maeandriforme, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum sinuatum.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, velvety leaves that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
