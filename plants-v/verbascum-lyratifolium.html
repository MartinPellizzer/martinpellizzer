Verbascum lyratifolium

Verbascum lyratifolium, commonly known as the lyrated mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a panicle-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the genus Verbascum.
