Viola palustris var. moupinensis

Viola palustris var. moupinensis, commonly known as the Moupin violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or blue. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and adaptability to woodland gardens.
