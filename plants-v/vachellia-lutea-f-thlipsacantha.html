Vachellia lutea f. thlipsacantha

Vachellia lutea f. thlipsacantha, commonly known as the yellow fever tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange-pink flowers that bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

The species is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
