Viburnum lasiophyllum

Viburnum lasiophyllum, commonly known as the snowberry viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Viburnum lasiophyllum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and wildlife benefits.
