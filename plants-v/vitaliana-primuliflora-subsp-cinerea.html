Vitaliana primuliflora subsp. cinerea

Vitaliana primuliflora subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf vitalia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-gray, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
