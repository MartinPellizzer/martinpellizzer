Verbena delticola

Verbena delticola, commonly known as saltbush verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage and is adapted to saline and arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal areas.

Verbena delticola is a perennial shrub that plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local wildlife.
