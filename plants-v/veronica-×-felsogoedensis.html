Veronica × felsogoedensis

Veronica × felsogoedensis, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens with alpine or rockery features.
