Vitex vestita var. siamica

Vitex vestita var. siamica, commonly known as the Siam vitex, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or white, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often found in moist, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
