Viola sandrasea

Viola sandrasea, commonly known as the sandalwood violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are pollinated by bees and other insects.

Viola sandrasea is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade.
