Vernonia leptospermoides

Vernonia leptospermoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Vernonia leptospermoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
