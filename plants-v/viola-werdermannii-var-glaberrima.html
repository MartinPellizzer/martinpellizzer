Viola werdermannii var. glaberrima

Viola werdermannii var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, glabrous surface, and produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals. Its floral structure includes a distinctive lower petal that is often spurred, a characteristic feature of the Violaceae family.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
