Verbascum capusii

Verbascum capusii, commonly known as the Turkish mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum capusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
