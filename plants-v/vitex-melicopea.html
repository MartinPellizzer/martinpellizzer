Vitex melicopea

Vitex melicopea, commonly known as the golden vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
