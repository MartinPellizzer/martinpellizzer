Viola tridentata

Viola tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed violet, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Viola tridentata is a perennial herb with a short stem and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
