Vaccinium korinchense

Vaccinium korinchense, commonly known as the Korinchen huckleberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. This evergreen shrub typically grows in high-altitude forests and grasslands, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
