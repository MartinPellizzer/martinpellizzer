Verbesina mexicana

Verbesina mexicana, commonly known as Mexican beggarticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
