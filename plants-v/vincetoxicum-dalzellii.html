Vincetoxicum dalzellii

Vincetoxicum dalzellii, commonly known as the red bower vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to China.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, red flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by bright orange berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 10 meters in length.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
