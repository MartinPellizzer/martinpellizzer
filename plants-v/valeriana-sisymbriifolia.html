Valeriana sisymbriifolia

Valeriana sisymbriifolia, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called schizocarps, which split into two one-seeded parts upon maturity. Valeriana sisymbriifolia typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in shaded environments and is known for its aromatic foliage.
