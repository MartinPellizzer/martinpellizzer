Vulpia sciuroides

Vulpia sciuroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf vulpia, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in dry, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas easily.

Vulpia sciuroides is often found in open habitats and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
