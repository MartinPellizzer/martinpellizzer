Vernonia bababudensis

Vernonia bababudensis, commonly known as the Bababudan Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow to orange in color, arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Vernonia bababudensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
