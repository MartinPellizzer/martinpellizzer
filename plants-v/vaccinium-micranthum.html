Vaccinium micranthum

Vaccinium micranthum, commonly known as dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. Vaccinium micranthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
