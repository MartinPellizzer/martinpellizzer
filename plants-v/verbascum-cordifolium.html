Verbascum cordifolium

Verbascum cordifolium, commonly known as heartleaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are soft to the touch. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its common name reflects the distinct shape of its leaves, which resemble a heart.
