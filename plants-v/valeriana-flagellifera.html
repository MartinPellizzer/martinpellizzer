Valeriana flagellifera

Valeriana flagellifera, commonly known as the flagellate valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and possesses opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense terminal spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are often harvested for their aromatic compounds, which have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Valeriana flagellifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
