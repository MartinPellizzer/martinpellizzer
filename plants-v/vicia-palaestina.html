Vicia palaestina

Vicia palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. Vicia palaestina is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in regions with limited water availability.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
