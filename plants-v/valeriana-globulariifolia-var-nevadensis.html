Valeriana globulariifolia var. nevadensis

Valeriana globulariifolia var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Nevada.

It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic leaves and stems, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in alpine and rocky habitats.

This variety is notable for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
