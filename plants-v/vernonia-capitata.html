Vernonia capitata

Vernonia capitata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows up to 3 feet tall, featuring slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Vernonia capitata is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
