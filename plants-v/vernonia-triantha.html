Vernonia triantha

Vernonia triantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and fluffy, pale blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia triantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
