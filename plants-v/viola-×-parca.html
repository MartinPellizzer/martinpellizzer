Viola × parca

Viola × parca, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Viola × parca is known for its adaptability and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
