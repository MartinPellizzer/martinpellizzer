Verbascum thapsiforme subsp. cuspidatum

Verbascum thapsiforme subsp.

cuspidatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in soft hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
