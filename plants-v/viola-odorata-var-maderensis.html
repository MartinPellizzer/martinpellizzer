Viola odorata var. maderensis

Viola odorata var. maderensis, commonly known as the Mader's sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white marking at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a common sight in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
