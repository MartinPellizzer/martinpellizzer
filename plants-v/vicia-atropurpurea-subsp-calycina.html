Vicia atropurpurea subsp. calycina

Vicia atropurpurea subsp.

calycina, commonly known as purple vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish-purple in color, especially when mature.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink, arranged in dense spikes. Its trifoliate leaves are pinnately compound, with each leaflet being oval to lance-shaped.

This species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration and as a forage plant.
