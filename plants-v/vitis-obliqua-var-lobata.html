Vitis obliqua var. lobata

Vitis obliqua var. lobata, commonly known as the Mexican wild grape, is a perennial vine native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with lobed margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are often used in traditional cuisine. Its vines are robust and can grow up to several meters in length, making it a common sight in tropical and subtropical regions.

Vitis obliqua var. lobata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
