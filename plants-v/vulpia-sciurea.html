Vulpia sciurea

Vulpia sciurea, commonly known as the hairy vulpia, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a low-growing annual grass that typically thrives in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Vulpia sciurea is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
