Viola maculata var. pubescens

Viola maculata var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its hairy (pubescent) stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cool, humid conditions.
