Vincetoxicum gonocarpos

Vincetoxicum gonocarpos, commonly known as the purple false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, oval-shaped leaflets that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces showy, clustered purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
