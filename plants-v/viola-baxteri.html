Viola baxteri

Viola baxteri, commonly known as the Baxter's violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky streambanks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola baxteri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
