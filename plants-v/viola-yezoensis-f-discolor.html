Viola yezoensis f. discolor

Viola yezoensis f. discolor, commonly known as the Japanese pansy, is a frost-tolerant perennial belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is notable for its ability to bloom early in the spring and its adaptability to temperate regions.
