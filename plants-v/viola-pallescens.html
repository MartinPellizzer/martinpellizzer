Viola pallescens

Viola pallescens, commonly known as the pale violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its pale violet to white flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Viola pallescens is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
