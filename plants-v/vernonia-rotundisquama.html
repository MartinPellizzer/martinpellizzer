Vernonia rotundisquama

Vernonia rotundisquama, commonly known as the round-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, round-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, spiky yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.
