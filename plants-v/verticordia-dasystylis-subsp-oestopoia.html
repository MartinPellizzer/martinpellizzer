Verticordia dasystylis subsp. oestopoia

Verticordia dasystylis subsp.

oestopoia, commonly known as the Geraldton waratah, is a striking member of the Myrtaceae family native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in heathland and sandplain environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
