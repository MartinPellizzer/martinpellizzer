Veronica pareora

Veronica pareora, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica pareora is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive flowers and ability to thrive in various conditions.
