Vaccinium forrestii

Vaccinium forrestii, commonly known as the Forrester huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas.

Vaccinium forrestii is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
