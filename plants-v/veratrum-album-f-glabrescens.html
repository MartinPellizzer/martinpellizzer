Veratrum album f. glabrescens

Veratrum album f. glabrescens, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a variant of the white hellebore plant native to Europe.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glabrous, giving it its name. Its flowers are white and arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer.

The plant is characterized by its toxic alkaloids and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
