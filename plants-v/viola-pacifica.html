Viola pacifica

Viola pacifica, commonly known as the peace violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Violaceae family, which includes many species of violets and pansies. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from purple to white. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Viola pacifica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
