Viola sirinica

Viola sirinica, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola sirinica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
