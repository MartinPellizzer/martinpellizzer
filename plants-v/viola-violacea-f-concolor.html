Viola violacea f. concolor

Viola violacea f. concolor, commonly known as the violet-leaf violet, is a botanical variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet-tinged leaves and flowers that typically range from purple to blue. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually five-petaled and bloom in early spring.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
