Viola austriaca

Viola austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Viola austriaca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
