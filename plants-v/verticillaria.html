Verticillaria

Verticillaria, commonly known as the mouse-ear cress, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the mustard family, Brassicaceae.

These plants are characterized by their small, radially symmetrical flowers and compound leaves that often resemble a mouse's ears. They typically grow in moist, temperate environments and are found across Europe, Asia, and North America. The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Verticillaria species are also valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
