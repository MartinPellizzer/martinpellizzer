Vriesea luschnathii

Vriesea luschnathii, commonly known as the Luschnath's Vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette structure, which helps collect rainwater. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with clusters of colorful, long-lasting flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea luschnathii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
