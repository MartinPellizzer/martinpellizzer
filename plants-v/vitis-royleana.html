Vitis royleana

Vitis royleana, commonly known as the Indian grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical berries that ripen to a dark purple color. Vitis royleana is often found in moist, shady areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
