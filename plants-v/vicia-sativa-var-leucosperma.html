Vicia sativa var. leucosperma

Vicia sativa var. leucosperma, commonly known as white blossom vetch, is a variant of the common vetch that is characterized by its white flowers.

This plant is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and soil-enriching properties.
