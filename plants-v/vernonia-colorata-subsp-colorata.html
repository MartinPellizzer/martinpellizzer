Vernonia colorata subsp. colorata

Vernonia colorata subsp.

colorata, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
