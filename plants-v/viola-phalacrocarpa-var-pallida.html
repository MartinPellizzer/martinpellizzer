Viola phalacrocarpa var. pallida

Viola phalacrocarpa var. pallida, commonly known as the pale violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or pale violet. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
