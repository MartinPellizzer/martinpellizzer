Vernonia crassa

Vernonia crassa, commonly known as rough vervain, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and woodland edges. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, rough hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its showy, blue to purple flower heads appear in late summer and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia crassa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
