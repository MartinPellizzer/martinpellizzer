Valeriana celtica

Valeriana celtica, commonly known as Celtic valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a strong, somewhat medicinal aroma.

Valeriana celtica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.
