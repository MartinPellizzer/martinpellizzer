Valeriana mairei

Valeriana mairei, commonly known as Maire's valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Valeriana mairei is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
