Veronica cymbalaria subsp. glandulosa

Veronica cymbalaria subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted, giving it its subspecies name. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in whorls along the stem.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
