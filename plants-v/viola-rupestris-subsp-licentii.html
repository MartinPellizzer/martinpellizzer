Viola rupestris subsp. licentii

Viola rupestris subsp.

licentii, commonly known as the rock violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
