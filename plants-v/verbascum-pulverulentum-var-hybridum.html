Verbascum pulverulentum var. hybridum

Verbascum pulverulentum var. hybridum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its fuzzy leaves and medicinal properties have made it historically valued in traditional herbal remedies.
