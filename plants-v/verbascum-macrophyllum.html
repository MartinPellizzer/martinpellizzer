Verbascum macrophyllum

Verbascum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved mullein, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose raceme. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy environments.

Verbascum macrophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
