Vatica zollingeriana

Vatica zollingeriana, commonly known as the Zollinger's ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and can grow up to 40 meters tall. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.
