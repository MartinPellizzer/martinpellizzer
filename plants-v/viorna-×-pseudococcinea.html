Viorna × pseudococcinea

Viorna × pseudococcinea, commonly known as the hybrid pink-flowered viorna, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Viorna coccinea and Viorna × rubra, characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
