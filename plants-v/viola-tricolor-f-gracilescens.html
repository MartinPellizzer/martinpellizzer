Viola tricolor f. gracilescens

Viola tricolor f. gracilescens, commonly known as the slender violet, is a compact, herbaceous perennial belonging to the Violaceae family.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive three-lobed structure, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy clusters of violet, yellow, and white flowers, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
