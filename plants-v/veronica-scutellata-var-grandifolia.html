Veronica scutellata var. grandifolia

Veronica scutellata var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that can reach up to 30 cm in height.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and borders.
