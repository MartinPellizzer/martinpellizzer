Verbascum visianianum

Verbascum visianianum, commonly known as the Persian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its common name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
