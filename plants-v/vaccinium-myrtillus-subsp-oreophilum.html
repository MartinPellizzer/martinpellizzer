Vaccinium myrtillus subsp. oreophilum

Vaccinium myrtillus subsp.

oreophilum, commonly known as the alpine blueberry, is a subspecies of the bilberry native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are rich in antioxidants.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and alpine tundra. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a bluish-green hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
