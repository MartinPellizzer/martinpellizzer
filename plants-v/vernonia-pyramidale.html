Vernonia pyramidale

Vernonia pyramidale, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its showy, daisy-like blooms appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia pyramidale is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
