Vitis bronneri

Vitis bronneri, commonly known as Bronner's grape, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in regions where it is native.
