Vaccinium auriculatum

Vaccinium auriculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like heathers and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its berries, which are typically dark blue to black when mature, are edible and often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.
