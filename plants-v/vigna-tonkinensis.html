Vigna tonkinensis

Vigna tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese bean or Tonkin bean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines.

Vigna tonkinensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
