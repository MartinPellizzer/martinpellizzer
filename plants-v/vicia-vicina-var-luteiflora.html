Vicia vicina var. luteiflora

Vicia vicina var. luteiflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. It is a perennial herb that thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

Vicia vicina var. luteiflora is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in certain habitats.
