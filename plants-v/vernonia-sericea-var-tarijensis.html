Vernonia sericea var. tarijensis

Vernonia sericea var. tarijensis, commonly known as the Tarijensis Ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
