Verbascum bulgaricum

Verbascum bulgaricum, commonly known as the Balkan mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Verbascum bulgaricum is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
