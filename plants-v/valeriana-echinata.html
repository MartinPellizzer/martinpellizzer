Valeriana echinata

Valeriana echinata, commonly known as the spiked valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana echinata is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
