Vernonia bracteolata

Vernonia bracteolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pale yellow to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia bracteolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
