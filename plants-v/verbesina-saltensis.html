Verbesina saltensis

Verbesina saltensis, commonly known as Saltgrass or Saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has adapted to saline soils and can thrive in environments with limited water availability. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss.

Verbesina saltensis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing food for wildlife.
