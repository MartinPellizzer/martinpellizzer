Valeriana pratensis subsp. angustifolia

Valeriana pratensis subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and elongated, with a slightly hairy surface, and small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters. Its root system is fibrous and contains essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties. Valeriana pratensis subsp.

angustifolia is valued for its calming effects and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
