Vouapa pendula

Vouapa pendula, commonly known as the hanging vouapa, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous growth habit, with long, drooping stems that support its foliage. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, which helps in capturing sunlight efficiently. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
