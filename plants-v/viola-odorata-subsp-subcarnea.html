Viola odorata subsp. subcarnea

Viola odorata subsp.

subcarnea, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often dark green with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces fragrant, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
