Veronica agrestis var. polita

Veronica agrestis var. polita, commonly known as the silverweed veronica, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
