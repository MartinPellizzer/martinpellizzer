Viola nemoralis

Viola nemoralis, commonly known as the common violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from purple to white, often with a yellow or greenish throat. It thrives in woodland areas and shaded environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Viola nemoralis is known for its adaptability and is often found in disturbed soils and along forest edges.
