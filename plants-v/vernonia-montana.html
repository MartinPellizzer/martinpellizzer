Vernonia montana

Vernonia montana, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its most striking feature is the dense clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Vernonia montana is often found in open woodlands and prairies, where it thrives in full sun.
