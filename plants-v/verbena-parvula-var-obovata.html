Verbena parvula var. obovata

Verbena parvula var. obovata, commonly known as the narrowleaf verbena, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine, hairy texture. The leaves are opposite, obovate in shape, and have a bluish-green hue with a slightly waxy coating. Its small, clustered flowers are typically blue or purple, blooming in summer, and are tubular with five lobes.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
