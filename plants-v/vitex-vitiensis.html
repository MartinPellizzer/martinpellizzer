Vitex vitiensis

Vitex vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific islands, particularly Vanuatu.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually pubescent, and it produces fragrant, blue to purple flowers in panicles. The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Vitex vitiensis is often found in coastal areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
