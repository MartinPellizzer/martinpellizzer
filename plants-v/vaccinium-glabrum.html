Vaccinium glabrum

Vaccinium glabrum, commonly known as the smooth bilberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy surface, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Vaccinium glabrum plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food for various wildlife species.
