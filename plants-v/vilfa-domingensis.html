Vilfa domingensis

Vilfa domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican violet, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its violet-colored flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It has small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss, Vilfa domingensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
