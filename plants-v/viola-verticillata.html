Viola verticillata

Viola verticillata, commonly known as the clustered violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves arranged in a whorl at the base of the plant, giving it its common name. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive dark purple throat. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
