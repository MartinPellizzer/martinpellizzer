Vernonia pacchensis

Vernonia pacchensis, commonly known as the Pachic ponytail, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Vernonia pacchensis thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in open, sunny areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
