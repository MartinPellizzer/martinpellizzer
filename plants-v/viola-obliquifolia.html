Viola obliquifolia

Viola obliquifolia, commonly known as the zigzag violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from purple to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Viola obliquifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
