Viguiera pringlei

Viguiera pringlei, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Viguiera pringlei is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
