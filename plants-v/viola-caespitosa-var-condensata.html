Viola caespitosa var. condensata

Viola caespitosa var. condensata, commonly known as the dense violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features clustered, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and velvety in texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple or violet, often with a yellow or white throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a valuable nectar source for pollinators.
