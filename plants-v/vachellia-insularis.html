Vachellia insularis

Vachellia insularis, commonly known as the Socotra cactus, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is adapted to the arid and coastal environments of its habitat. Its structure resembles a cactus, despite not being a true cactus, due to its succulent-like appearance.

Vachellia insularis plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystem, providing shelter and food for local wildlife.
