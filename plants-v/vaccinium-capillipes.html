Vaccinium capillipes

Vaccinium capillipes, commonly known as the slender blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are usually blue or purple in color. Vaccinium capillipes thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic environments.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
