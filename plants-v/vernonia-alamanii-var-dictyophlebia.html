Vernonia alamanii var. dictyophlebia

Vernonia alamanii var. dictyophlebia, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and conical flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
