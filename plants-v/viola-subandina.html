Viola subandina

Viola subandina, commonly known as the Andean violet, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to mountainous regions.

Viola subandina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued for its resilience in challenging climates.
