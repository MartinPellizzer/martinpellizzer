Viburnum discolor f. integrum

Viburnum discolor f. integrum, commonly known as the white-fruited viburnum, is a cultivar of the viburnum plant characterized by its unique white berries.

It is native to North America and is often found in woodland areas. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by the distinctive white fruits that persist into the winter.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
