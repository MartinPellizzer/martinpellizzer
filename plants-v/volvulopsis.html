Volvulopsis

Volvulopsis, commonly known as the spiral-shaped alga, is a genus of green algae characterized by its unique, coiled morphology.

These organisms typically form intricate, spiral-shaped colonies that can be observed under a microscope. Volvulopsis species are often found in freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where they thrive in nutrient-rich waters. Their cellular structure is simple, consisting of a single layer of cells that form the spiral pattern.

Due to their distinctive appearance, they are sometimes used as indicators of water quality in ecological studies.
