Vernonia hymenolepis

Vernonia hymenolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Vernonia hymenolepis is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
