Vulpiella stipoides subsp. letourneuxii

Vulpiella stipoides subsp.

letourneuxii, commonly known as the Letourneux's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along riverbanks and in swampy areas. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in spikelets, and it produces tiny, three-lobed seeds.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
