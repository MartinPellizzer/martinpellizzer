Verbascum longifolium

Verbascum longifolium, commonly known as the longleaf mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum longifolium is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its soothing properties.
