Verbesina pallens

Verbesina pallens, commonly known as the white crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces fluffy, white seed heads that resemble a crown. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Verbesina pallens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
