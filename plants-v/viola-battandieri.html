Viola battandieri

Viola battandieri, commonly known as the violet of the Alps, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola battandieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
