Vernonia eitenii

Vernonia eitenii, commonly known as the Eiten's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Vernonia eitenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
