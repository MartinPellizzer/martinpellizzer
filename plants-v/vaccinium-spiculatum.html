Vaccinium spiculatum

Vaccinium spiculatum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other acidic-loving plants like heathers and cranberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Vaccinium spiculatum is often found in boreal forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
