Vepris peraperta

Vepris peraperta, commonly known as the Malabar boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically followed by yellowish-orange fruits. Its dense foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and hedges.

The leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and have been used in traditional medicine.
