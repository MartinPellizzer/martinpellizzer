Veronica media var. persicifolia

Veronica media var. persicifolia, commonly known as the Persian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
