Vaccinium alvarezii var. maisense

Vaccinium alvarezii var. maisense, commonly known as the Maisen huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are evergreen, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by supporting local wildlife and contributing to biodiversity.
