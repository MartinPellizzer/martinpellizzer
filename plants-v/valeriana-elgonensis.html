Valeriana elgonensis

Valeriana elgonensis, commonly known as Elgon valerian, is a perennial herb native to the highland regions of East Africa, particularly around Mount Elgon.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic leaves and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.

Valeriana elgonensis is valued in traditional medicine for its calming and sedative effects.
