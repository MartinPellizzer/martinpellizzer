Vernonia pyrrhopappa

Vernonia pyrrhopappa, commonly known as the rough vernonia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Vernonia pyrrhopappa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
