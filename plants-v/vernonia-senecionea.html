Vernonia senecionea

Vernonia senecionea, commonly known as yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Vernonia senecionea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
