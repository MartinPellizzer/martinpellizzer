Verbena paulensis

Verbena paulensis, commonly known as the Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spike-like clusters of small, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Verbena paulensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
