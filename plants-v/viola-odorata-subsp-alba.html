Viola odorata subsp. alba

Viola odorata subsp.

alba, commonly known as the white violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly toothed margin.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale purple, with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
