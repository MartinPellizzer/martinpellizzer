Vaccinium symplocifolium

Vaccinium symplocifolium, commonly known as the alpine blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, adapted to withstand cold and harsh alpine conditions.

The berries, which are usually dark blue or black, are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.
