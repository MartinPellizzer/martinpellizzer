Vilfa tricholepis

Vilfa tricholepis, commonly known as the hairy leaf veldtgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside, contributing to its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle, with small, two-lipped flowers that are often pale green or brown.

Vilfa tricholepis is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
