Veronica cymbalaria f. panormitana

Veronica cymbalaria f. panormitana, commonly known as the Panormitan speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting biodiversity in its habitat.
