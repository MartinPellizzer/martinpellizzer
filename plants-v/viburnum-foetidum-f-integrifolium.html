Viburnum foetidum f. integrifolium

Viburnum foetidum f. integrifolium, commonly known as the fragrant viburnum, is a hybrid plant that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are typically entire, giving it a more refined appearance compared to the typical form. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark red berries in the fall. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with a compact form that makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

This cultivar is often valued for its aromatic foliage and seasonal floral display.
