Viola kurilensis var. pubescens

Viola kurilensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the Kuril violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Kuril Islands.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a dense covering of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Violaceae family, valued for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
