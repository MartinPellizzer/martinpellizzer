Veronica triquetra

Veronica triquetra, commonly known as the three-angled speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive three-angled cross-section, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.

This perennial plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting a variety of pollinators.
