Viola arisanensis

Viola arisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its flowers bloom in spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Viola arisanensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
