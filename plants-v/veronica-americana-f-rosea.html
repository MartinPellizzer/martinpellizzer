Veronica americana f. rosea

Veronica americana f. rosea, commonly known as the pink speedwell, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate appearance.
