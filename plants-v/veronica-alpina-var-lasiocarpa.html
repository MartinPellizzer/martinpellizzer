Veronica alpina var. lasiocarpa

Veronica alpina var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its alpine habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
