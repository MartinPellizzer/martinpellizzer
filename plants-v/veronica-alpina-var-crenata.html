Veronica alpina var. crenata

Veronica alpina var. crenata, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and harsh alpine conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
