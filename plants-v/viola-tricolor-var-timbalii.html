Viola tricolor var. timbalii

Viola tricolor var. timbalii, commonly known as the Italian viola, is a species of flowering plant in the violaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy violet, yellow, and white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant colors and ability to bloom throughout the spring season.

The variety timbalii is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and slightly different flower coloration compared to the standard Viola tricolor.
