Verbena chamissonis

Verbena chamissonis, commonly known as blue verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Verbena chamissonis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
