Verbascum × hayekii

Verbascum × hayekii, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes various species of mullein known for their large, fuzzy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The hybrid nature of Verbascum × hayekii results from crosses between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum thapsus, leading to a unique combination of traits. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant's flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
