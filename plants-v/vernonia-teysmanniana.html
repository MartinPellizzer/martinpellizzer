Vernonia teysmanniana

Vernonia teysmanniana, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of Hawaii.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a vulnerable species.
