Vicia chianschanensis

Vicia chianschanensis, commonly known as the Chinese vetch, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It is often found in grasslands and mountainous regions, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
