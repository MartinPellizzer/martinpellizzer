Valeriana locusta var. pumila

Valeriana locusta var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf valerian, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, featuring slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, emitting a mild, herbaceous scent.

This variety is often found in grassy areas, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
