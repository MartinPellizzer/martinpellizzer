Verbascum sinuatum var. adenosepalum

Verbascum sinuatum var. adenosepalum, commonly known as the woolly flaxflower, is a variant of the flaxflower plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green foliage and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a herbaceous perennial that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
