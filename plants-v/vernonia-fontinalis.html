Vernonia fontinalis

Vernonia fontinalis, commonly known as waterleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, terminal spikes, which bloom in late summer.

Vernonia fontinalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
