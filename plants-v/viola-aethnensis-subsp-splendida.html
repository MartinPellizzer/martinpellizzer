Viola aethnensis subsp. splendida

Viola aethnensis subsp.

splendida, commonly known as the Splendid Violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.

It is also valued for its role in local ecosystems, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
