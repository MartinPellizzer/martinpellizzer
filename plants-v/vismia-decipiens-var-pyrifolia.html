Vismia decipiens var. pyrifolia

Vismia decipiens var. pyrifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small white flowers that appear in winter, giving it a snow-like appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
