Vitex orinocensis var. orinocensis

Vitex orinocensis var. orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco vitex, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, blooming in the summer months. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds, and it is often found in tropical dry forests and savannas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
