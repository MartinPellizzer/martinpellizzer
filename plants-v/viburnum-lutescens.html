Viburnum lutescens

Viburnum lutescens, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets, and its flowers are small, fragrant, and white to pale yellow in color. The plant produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.

Viburnum lutescens is valued for its ornamental value and ability to provide seasonal interest.
