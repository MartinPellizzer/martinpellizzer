Viola confusa f. serratodentata

Viola confusa f. serratodentata, commonly known as the serrated-toothed viola, is a horticultural form of the viola genus characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

The leaves are serrated along the margins, giving the plant a unique and ornamental appearance. This variety typically produces clusters of small, delicate violet flowers in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

The plant is a perennial, preferring partial shade and well-drained soil.
