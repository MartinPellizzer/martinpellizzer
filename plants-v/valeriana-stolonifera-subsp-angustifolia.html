Valeriana stolonifera subsp. angustifolia

Valeriana stolonifera subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features a creeping rhizomatous growth habit, with slender, branched stems that often lie close to the ground.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, with a slightly rough texture, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Valeriana stolonifera subsp.

angustifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
