Vaccinium benthamianum

Vaccinium benthamianum, commonly known as the Bentham huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and heathers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or on rocky slopes. Its leaves are small, dark green, and leathery, while its flowers are bell-shaped and typically pink or white.

The berries, which are edible, are small, dark purple, and contain a single seed, making it a valuable plant for both ecological and culinary purposes.
