Veronica rosea subvar. eriocarpa

Veronica rosea subvar. eriocarpa, commonly known as the hairy everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
