Vilmorinia plumieri

Vilmorinia plumieri, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Vilmorinia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and slightly fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in its native habitat.

Vilmorinia plumieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
