Veronica orbiculata var. celakovskyana

Veronica orbiculata var. celakovskyana, commonly known as the Celakovsky's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are typically rounded and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Plantaginaceae family.
