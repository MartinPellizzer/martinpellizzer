Vaccinium costerifolium

Vaccinium costerifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, acidic berries that are typically dark blue to black in color when ripe. Its leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a leathery texture and a bluish-green hue.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, forested areas.
