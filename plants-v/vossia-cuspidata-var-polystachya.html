Vossia cuspidata var. polystachya

Vossia cuspidata var. polystachya, commonly known as the clustered vossia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue due to dense pubescence.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
