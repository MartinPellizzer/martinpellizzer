Vignea × suziella

Vignea × suziella, commonly known as the hybrid passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive, showy flowers that often have five petals and a crown-like structure. The plant produces orange or yellow fruits that are typically edible and contain numerous seeds. Vignea × suziella thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
