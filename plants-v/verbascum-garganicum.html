Verbascum garganicum

Verbascum garganicum, commonly known as the Garganica mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Verbascum garganicum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported respiratory benefits.
