Vicioides lutea

Vicioides lutea, commonly known as the yellow vicia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Vicioides lutea is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.
