Vernonia imeriensis

Vernonia imeriensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Vernonia imeriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
