Vitis hyrcanica

Vitis hyrcanica, commonly known as the Hyrcanian grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region and parts of Central Asia, including countries like Iran and Azerbaijan. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth, climbing habit, and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers and red to black berries that are edible.

Vitis hyrcanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
