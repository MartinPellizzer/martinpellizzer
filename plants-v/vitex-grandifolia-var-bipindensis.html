Vitex grandifolia var. bipindensis

Vitex grandifolia var. bipindensis, commonly known as the African vitex or red vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. These flowers are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
