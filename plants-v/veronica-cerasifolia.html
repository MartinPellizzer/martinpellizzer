Veronica cerasifolia

Veronica cerasifolia, commonly known as the cherryleaf speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Veronica cerasifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
