Vernonia senecioides

Vernonia senecioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands and along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Vernonia senecioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
