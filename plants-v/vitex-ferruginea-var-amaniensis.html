Vitex ferruginea var. amaniensis

Vitex ferruginea var. amaniensis, commonly known as the Amani vitex, is a shrub native to the East African highlands.

It is characterized by its aromatic, grayish-green leaves and clusters of small, purple to pink flowers. The plant produces woody capsules containing small, brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
