Viola tricolor subsp. trimestris

Viola tricolor subsp.

trimestris, commonly known as the three-color violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, colorful flowers that typically display three different hues.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and botanical collections.
