Viola tricolor var. derelicta

Viola tricolor var. derelicta, commonly known as the Purple Heart Violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy flowers that typically display a combination of purple, yellow, and white hues. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape during the cooler months.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
