Valeriana trichiata

Valeriana trichiata, commonly known as the hairy valerian, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or a climbing vine, with opposite leaves that are usually hairy or pubescent. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and come in shades of white, pink, or purple.

Valeriana trichiata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its traditional medicinal uses.
