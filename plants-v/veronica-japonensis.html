Veronica japonensis

Veronica japonensis, commonly known as Japanese speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, blue to violet flowers clustered in pairs. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged opposite along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Veronica japonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
