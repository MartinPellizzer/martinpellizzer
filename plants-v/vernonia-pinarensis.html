Vernonia pinarensis

Vernonia pinarensis, commonly known as the Cuban ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Bahamas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads that are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Vernonia pinarensis is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
