Vernonia johannis

Vernonia johannis, commonly known as the John's Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia johannis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
