Viburnum tinoides f. turumiquirense

Viburnum tinoides f. turumiquirense, commonly known as the Turumiquirense snowberry, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that provide attractive foliage throughout the growing season. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

The cultivar is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
