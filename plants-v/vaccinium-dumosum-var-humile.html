Vaccinium dumosum var. humile

Vaccinium dumosum var. humile, commonly known as dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and strong winds. It has small, leathery leaves and typically blooms with white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
