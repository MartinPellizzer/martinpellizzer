Vitis vinifera var. nigra

Vitis vinifera var. nigra, commonly known as the Black Muscat grape, is a variety of the common grapevine characterized by its dark purple to black berries.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is known for its high sugar content and aromatic profile. The plant produces clusters of small, intensely flavored grapes that are often used in winemaking. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires a significant amount of sunlight.

The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a glossy appearance.
