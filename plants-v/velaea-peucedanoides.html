Velaea peucedanoides

Velaea peucedanoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Velaea peucedanoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local pollinators.
