Viscum grossum

Viscum grossum, commonly known as the common mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant has a distinctive appearance, with glossy evergreen leaves and small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries, which are toxic to humans, are a food source for birds that help disperse its seeds.

Viscum grossum plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by supporting a variety of wildlife.
