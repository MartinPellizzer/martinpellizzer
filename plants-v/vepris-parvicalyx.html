Vepris parvicalyx

Vepris parvicalyx, commonly known as the small-leaved laurier or African laurier, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue-black fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
