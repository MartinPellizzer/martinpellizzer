Veronica wulingensis

Veronica wulingensis, commonly known as the Wuling speedwell, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Veronica wulingensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
