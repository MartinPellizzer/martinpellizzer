Vrieseida

Vrieseida, commonly known as the Vriesea, is a genus of flowering plants in the bromeliad family, Bromeliaceae.

These plants are native to South America, particularly Brazil, and are characterized by their striking, often colorful inflorescences. Vrieseida species typically feature rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that store water, a feature adapted to their tropical and subtropical habitats. The flowers, which can range from yellow to red, are often borne on tall, branching stems.

Vrieseida plants are popular as ornamental houseplants due to their vibrant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
