Vitis wenchowensis

Vitis wenchowensis, commonly known as the Wenchow grape, is a rare species native to China.

It is a deciduous vine that produces small, dark purple berries. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
