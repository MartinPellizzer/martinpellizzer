Viola yubariana

Viola yubariana, commonly known as the Yubari violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, particularly in the Yubari area of Japan. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Viola yubariana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
