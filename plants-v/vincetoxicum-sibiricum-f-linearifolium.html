Vincetoxicum sibiricum f. linearifolium

Vincetoxicum sibiricum f. linearifolium, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
