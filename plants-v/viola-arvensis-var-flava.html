Viola arvensis var. flava

Viola arvensis var. flava, commonly known as the yellow field violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive purple throat. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

The yellow field violet is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
