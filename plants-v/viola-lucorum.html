Viola lucorum

Viola lucorum, commonly known as the evening violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola lucorum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional use in herbal medicine.
