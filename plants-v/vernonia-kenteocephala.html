Vernonia kenteocephala

Vernonia kenteocephala, commonly known as the Kente-headed Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, while its ecological role supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
