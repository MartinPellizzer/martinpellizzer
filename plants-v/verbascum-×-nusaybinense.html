Verbascum × nusaybinense

Verbascum × nusaybinense, commonly known as the Nusaybin mullein, is a hybrid plant species native to Turkey.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their thick, fuzzy leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. This particular species is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered in soft hairs. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
