Vitex madiensis subsp. milanjiensis

Vitex madiensis subsp.

milanjiensis, commonly known as the Millet tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its pinnate leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a fibrous coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
