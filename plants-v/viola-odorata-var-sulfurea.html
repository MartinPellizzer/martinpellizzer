Viola odorata var. sulfurea

Viola odorata var. sulfurea, commonly known as the sulfur yellow violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking sulfur-yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
