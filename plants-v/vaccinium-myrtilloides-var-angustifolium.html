Vaccinium myrtilloides var. angustifolium

Vaccinium myrtilloides var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueberry, is a shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 2 centimeters in length. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and temperate forest ecosystems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
