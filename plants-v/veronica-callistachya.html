Veronica callistachya

Veronica callistachya, commonly known as the mountain veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Veronica callistachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
