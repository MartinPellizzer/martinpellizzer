Vicia benghalensis var. atropurpurea

Vicia benghalensis var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often appearing in clusters. It is native to regions of Asia, including India and Nepal, and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for soil enrichment.
