Veronica opaca

Veronica opaca, commonly known as narrow-leaved speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features slender stems and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along pathways.

Veronica opaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
