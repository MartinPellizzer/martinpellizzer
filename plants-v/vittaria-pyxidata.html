Vittaria pyxidata

Vittaria pyxidata, commonly known as the pyxidate fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often used in horticultural displays. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in small, cup-like formations on the underside of its leaves.

Vittaria pyxidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
