Vaccinium daphniphyllum

Vaccinium daphniphyllum, commonly known as the wintergreen huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and have a sharp, tangy flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Vaccinium daphniphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
