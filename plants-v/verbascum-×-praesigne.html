Verbascum × praesigne

Verbascum × praesigne, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattellum, resulting in a plant with distinctive, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature contribute to its cultivation in gardens across temperate climates.
