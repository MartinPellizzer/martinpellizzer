Verbascum virgatum subsp. lusitanicum

Verbascum virgatum subsp.

lusitanicum, commonly known as the Portuguese mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It features thick, woolly stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its common name reflects its historical use in folk remedies for respiratory ailments.
