Viola kusanoana f. bevicalcarata

Viola kusanoana f. bevicalcarata, commonly known as the Kusano's violet, is a rare and distinctive botanical variant of the viola genus.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a unique, waxy texture and a distinctive calyx that is broader than the typical form. This fern-like perennial thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in shaded woodland areas. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, velvety hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is highly valued in horticulture for its rare beauty and adaptability to specific ecological niches.
