Viola gracilis var. pubescens

Viola gracilis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, ornamental plants.
