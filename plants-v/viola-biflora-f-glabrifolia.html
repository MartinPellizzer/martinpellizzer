Viola biflora f. glabrifolia

Viola biflora f. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and hairless, giving it its specific epithet "glabrifolia.". The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, often with a yellow center. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
