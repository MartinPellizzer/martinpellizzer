Viscum bagshawei

Viscum bagshawei, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus *Pseudotsuga*.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its leaves, which are adapted for photosynthesis. The plant produces small, white or yellowish berries that contain toxic seeds, which can be dispersed by birds. Viscum bagshawei is often found in temperate regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

Its presence can have both ecological and medicinal significance in traditional practices.
