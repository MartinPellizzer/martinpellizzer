Vaccinium thibaudioides

Vaccinium thibaudioides, commonly known as the Thibaudioides huckleberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bright red berries that ripen in the autumn. Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
