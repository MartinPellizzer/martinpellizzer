Vigna macrorhyncha

Vigna macrorhyncha, commonly known as the large-seeded cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits containing several round, edible seeds. Vigna macrorhyncha is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed.

It also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
