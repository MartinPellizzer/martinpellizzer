Vernonia tristis

Vernonia tristis, commonly known as the weeping vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its drooping, pale yellow to orange flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

Vernonia tristis is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of streams.
