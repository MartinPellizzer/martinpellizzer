Verbascum apiculatum var. apiculatum

Verbascum apiculatum var. apiculatum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thick, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces tall, upright stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is deep, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
