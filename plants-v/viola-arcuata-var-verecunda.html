Viola arcuata var. verecunda

Viola arcuata var. verecunda, commonly known as the purple viola, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
