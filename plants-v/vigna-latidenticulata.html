Vigna latidenticulata

Vigna latidenticulata, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, and it has a fibrous root system that helps in soil stabilization.

Vigna latidenticulata is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
