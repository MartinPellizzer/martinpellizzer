Viola pubescens var. brevistipulata

Viola pubescens var. brevistipulata, commonly known as the short-stippled violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves with short, stiff hairs along the veins. Its flowers are violet to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
