Valeriana saxatilis subsp. saxatilis

Valeriana saxatilis subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the saxatile valerian, is a perennial herb native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as gravelly soils and limestone outcrops.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Valeriana saxatilis subsp.

saxatilis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
