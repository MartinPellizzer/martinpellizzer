Viburnum pallidum

Viburnum pallidum, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Viburnum pallidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
