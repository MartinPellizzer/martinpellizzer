Veronica rubella

Veronica rubella, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple in color, and arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Veronica rubella thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
