Vigna mungo var. silvestris

Vigna mungo var. silvestris, commonly known as wild mung bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces elongated, slender pods containing small, round seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Vigna mungo var. silvestris is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
