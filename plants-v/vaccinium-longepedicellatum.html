Vaccinium longepedicellatum

Vaccinium longepedicellatum, commonly known as the long pedicellate blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist climate for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
