Verbascum × paphlagonicum

Verbascum × paphlagonicum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their large, fuzzy leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clustered flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
