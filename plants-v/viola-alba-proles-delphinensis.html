Viola alba proles delphinensis

Viola alba proles delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinian white violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its pale lavender to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often sprawling.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
