Vincetoxicum hortulorum

Vincetoxicum hortulorum, commonly known as the garden nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its lobed leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or garden settings.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
