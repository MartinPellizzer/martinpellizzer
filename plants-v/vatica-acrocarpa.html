Vatica acrocarpa

Vatica acrocarpa, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Vatica acrocarpa plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
