Verbena officinalis var. spuria

Verbena officinalis var. spuria, commonly known as the lesser verbena, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces vibrant blue or violet flowers. The plant has slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is noted for its purported calming and diuretic properties.
