Vanilla bampsiana

Vanilla bampsiana, commonly known as the giant vanilla orchid, is a tropical plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing orchid that produces long, fragrant vanilla pods, similar to the more well-known vanilla planifolia. The plant's botanical features include heart-shaped leaves and a complex root system adapted to its epiphytic growth habit. It thrives in warm, moist environments with high humidity and indirect sunlight.

The vanilla pods, which take several months to mature, are prized for their aromatic flavor and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
