Viscum decurrens

Viscum decurrens, commonly known as the creeping mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically yellow or red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Viscum decurrens plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
