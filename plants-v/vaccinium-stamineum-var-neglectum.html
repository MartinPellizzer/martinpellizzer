Vaccinium stamineum var. neglectum

Vaccinium stamineum var. neglectum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features small, dark blue berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter, sometimes whitish underside. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
