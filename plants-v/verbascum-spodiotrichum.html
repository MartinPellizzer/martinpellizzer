Verbascum spodiotrichum

Verbascum spodiotrichum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
