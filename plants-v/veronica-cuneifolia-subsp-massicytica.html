Veronica cuneifolia subsp. massicytica

Veronica cuneifolia subsp.

massicytica, commonly known as the Massicys mountain speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, blue to purple in color, and are arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
