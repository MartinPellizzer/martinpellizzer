Viola auricolor

Viola auricolor, commonly known as the golden violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, including parts of Italy and the Alps. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Viola auricolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
