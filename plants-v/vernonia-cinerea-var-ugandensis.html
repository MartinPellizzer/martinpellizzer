Vernonia cinerea var. ugandensis

Vernonia cinerea var. ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-green foliage and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to drier conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
