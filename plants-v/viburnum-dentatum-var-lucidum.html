Viburnum dentatum var. lucidum

Viburnum dentatum var. lucidum, commonly known as shining arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically shiny and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
