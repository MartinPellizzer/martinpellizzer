Vaccinium iteophyllum var. hispidum

Vaccinium iteophyllum var. hispidum, commonly known as the rough-leaved huckleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and the fruit is a dark blue to black berry.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
