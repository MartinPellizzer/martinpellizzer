Vicia hirticalycina

Vicia hirticalycina, commonly known as the hairy calyx vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a trifoliate structure. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its root system fixes nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Vicia hirticalycina is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is used in ecological restoration projects.
