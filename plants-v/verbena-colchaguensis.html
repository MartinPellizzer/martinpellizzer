Verbena colchaguensis

Verbena colchaguensis, commonly known as the Andes verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly the Andes region.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Verbena colchaguensis is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
