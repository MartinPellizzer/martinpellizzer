Vernonia echioides

Vernonia echioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring slender, branching stems. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green appearance.

Vernonia echioides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
