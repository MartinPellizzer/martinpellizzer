Vitis assamica

Vitis assamica, commonly known as the Assam grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, including regions in India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth, climbing habit, and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible berries.

Vitis assamica is valued for its potential in viticulture and as a source of medicinal compounds.
