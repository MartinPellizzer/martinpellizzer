Viola ganderi

Viola ganderi, commonly known as Gander's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola ganderi is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
