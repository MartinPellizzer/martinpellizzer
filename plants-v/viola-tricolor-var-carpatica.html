Viola tricolor var. carpatica

Viola tricolor var. carpatica, commonly known as the heart-shaped viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive dark green color and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize easily in suitable conditions.

The species is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
