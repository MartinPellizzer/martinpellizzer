Veronica miyabei var. hondoensis

Veronica miyabei var. hondoensis, commonly known as the Hondo Veronica, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is noted for its compact size and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
