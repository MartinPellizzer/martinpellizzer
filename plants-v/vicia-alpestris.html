Vicia alpestris

Vicia alpestris, commonly known as the alpine vetch, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Vicia alpestris has small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This plant is adapted to harsh alpine environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in such habitats.
