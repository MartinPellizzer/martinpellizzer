Viola × kotschyana

Viola × kotschyana, commonly known as the Kashmir violet, is a hybrid species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a herbaceous perennial with heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous areas with cool climates. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Viola × kotschyana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
