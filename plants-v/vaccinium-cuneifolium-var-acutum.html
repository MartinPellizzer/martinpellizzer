Vaccinium cuneifolium var. acutum

Vaccinium cuneifolium var. acutum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueberry, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
