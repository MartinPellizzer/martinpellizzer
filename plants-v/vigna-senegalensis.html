Vigna senegalensis

Vigna senegalensis, commonly known as cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with yellow or white flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to warm climates and is drought-tolerant, making it a valuable crop in arid regions. Its seeds are rich in protein and are widely used in cuisines across Africa and the Americas.

Additionally, Vigna senegalensis has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia, improving soil fertility.
