Verbascum × geminatum

Verbascum × geminatum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy, velvety leaves. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
