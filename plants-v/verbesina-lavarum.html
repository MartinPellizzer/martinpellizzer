Verbesina lavarum

Verbesina lavarum, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as achenes.

Verbesina lavarum is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
