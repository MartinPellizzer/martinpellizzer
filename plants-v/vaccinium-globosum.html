Vaccinium globosum

Vaccinium globosum, commonly known as the blueberry shrub, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. The plant has alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Vaccinium globosum plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
