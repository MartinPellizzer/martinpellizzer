Verbascum × subcheiranthifolium

Verbascum × subcheiranthifolium, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves and tall spikes of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its hybrid nature results from crossbreeding between Verbascum species, leading to unique morphological traits.
