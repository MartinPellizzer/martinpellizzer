Verbascum calaurium

Verbascum calaurium, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a thick layer of silky hair.

Verbascum calaurium is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
