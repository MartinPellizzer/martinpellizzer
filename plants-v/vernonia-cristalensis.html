Vernonia cristalensis

Vernonia cristalensis, commonly known as the crystal crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking, spiky purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
