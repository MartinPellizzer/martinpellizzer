Villaria uniflora

Villaria uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower villaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has slender, ascending stems. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The single, showy flower at the tip of each stem is usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
