Viola × consona

Viola × consona, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors, including purple, violet, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas, meadows, and along stream banks.

Viola × consona is known for its adaptability and is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes.
