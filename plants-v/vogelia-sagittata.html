Vogelia sagittata

Vogelia sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf veldtgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, pale green to brownish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Vogelia sagittata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is often found in grasslands and along rocky outcrops, contributing to the ecological stability of these habitats.
