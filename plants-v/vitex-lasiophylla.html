Vitex lasiophylla

Vitex lasiophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf chaste tree, is a species of flowering plant in the verbena family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Mexico and Central America. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. Its showy, fragrant flowers are typically blue or purple and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule that contains several small, round seeds.
