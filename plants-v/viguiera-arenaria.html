Viguiera arenaria

Viguiera arenaria, commonly known as sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Viguiera arenaria is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
