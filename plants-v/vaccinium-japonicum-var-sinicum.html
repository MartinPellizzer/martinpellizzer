Vaccinium japonicum var. sinicum

Vaccinium japonicum var. sinicum, commonly known as the Chinese huckleberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
