Valeriana ficariifolia var. glabra

Valeriana ficariifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its root system is often harvested for its aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
