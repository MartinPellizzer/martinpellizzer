Vadia jotolana

Vadia jotolana, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, drooping clusters of golden yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree has a fast growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs.

Vadia jotolana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade in warm climates.
