Vicia remrevillensis

Vicia remrevillensis, commonly known as the Revillensis vetch, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually a pale yellow or greenish-white in color.

The seeds are enclosed in a pod and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in soil enrichment and biodiversity.
