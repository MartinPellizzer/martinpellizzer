Vriesea racinae

Vriesea racinae, commonly known as the banana leaf bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, banana-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a central reservoir to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences with colorful bracts that can range from red to orange and yellow. Vriesea racinae thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance and air-purifying qualities make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
