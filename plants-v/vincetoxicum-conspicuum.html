Vincetoxicum conspicuum

Vincetoxicum conspicuum, commonly known as the showy false bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Vincetoxicum conspicuum is valued for its ornamental appeal but can be invasive in certain regions.
