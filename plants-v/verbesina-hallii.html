Verbesina hallii

Verbesina hallii, commonly known as the Hall's beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its hairy stems and leaves are adapted to thrive in moist, sunny environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Verbesina hallii is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
