Viola patrinii var. suaveolens

Viola patrinii var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers emit a mild, sweet fragrance, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
