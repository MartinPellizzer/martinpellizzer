Viola cenisia subsp. valderia

Viola cenisia subsp.

valderia, commonly known as the Valderia violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae. It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

The plant is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects.
