Vernonia francavillana

Vernonia francavillana, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along riverbanks and in forest understories. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
