Veronica pusanensis

Veronica pusanensis, commonly known as the Korean speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Veronica pusanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
