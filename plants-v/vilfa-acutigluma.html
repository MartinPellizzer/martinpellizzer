Vilfa acutigluma

Vilfa acutigluma, commonly known as the sharp-leafed violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate purple flowers that bloom in the spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Vilfa acutigluma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
