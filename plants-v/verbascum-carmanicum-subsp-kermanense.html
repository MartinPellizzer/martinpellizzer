Verbascum carmanicum subsp. kermanense

Verbascum carmanicum subsp.

kermanense, commonly known as the Kerman mullein, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Iran. It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, woolly hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in semi-desert environments.
