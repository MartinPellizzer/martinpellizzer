Vernonia sceptrum

Vernonia sceptrum, commonly known as the royal blue ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and can reach heights of up to six feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Vernonia sceptrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
