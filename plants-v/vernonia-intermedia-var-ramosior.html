Vernonia intermedia var. ramosior

Vernonia intermedia var. ramosior, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with reddish-brown bracts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
