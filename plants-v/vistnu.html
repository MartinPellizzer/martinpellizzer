Vistnu

Vistnu, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a tropical aquatic plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, winding stems emerging from water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate. Vistnu is known for its symbolic significance in various cultures, often representing purity and spiritual enlightenment.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Nelumbonaceae and is closely related to water lilies.
