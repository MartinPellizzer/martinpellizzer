Viola hederacea subsp. seppeltiana

Viola hederacea subsp.

seppeltiana, commonly known as the Seppelt's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Australia. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with a yellow throat.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
