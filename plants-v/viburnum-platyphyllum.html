Viburnum platyphyllum

Viburnum platyphyllum, commonly known as the flatleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum platyphyllum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
