Villebrunea sylvatica var. integrifolia

Villebrunea sylvatica var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed sylvatic villebrunea, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically entire, lacking serrations or lobes.

The leaves are usually dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual contrast against the plant's woody stems. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. The plant is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
