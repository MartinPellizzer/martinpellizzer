Verbesina aurea

Verbesina aurea, commonly known as golden beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems, which are covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a reddish or purplish tinge on the underside.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
