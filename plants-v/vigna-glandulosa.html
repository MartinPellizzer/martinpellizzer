Vigna glandulosa

Vigna glandulosa, commonly known as the hairy bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several round, yellowish seeds. Vigna glandulosa is often grown for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
