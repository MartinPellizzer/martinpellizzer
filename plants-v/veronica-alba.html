Veronica alba

Veronica alba, commonly known as white speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Veronica alba is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
