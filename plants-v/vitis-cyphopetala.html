Vitis cyphopetala

Vitis cyphopetala, commonly known as the Mexican grapevine, is a woody vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically followed by clusters of berries. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Vitis cyphopetala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
