Viola phalacrocarpa var. pallescens

Viola phalacrocarpa var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are violet-blue in color, with five petals and a distinctive downward-curled lower petal.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.
