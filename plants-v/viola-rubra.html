Viola rubra

Viola rubra, commonly known as the red violet, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas, meadows, and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Viola rubra is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
