Viola pedata var. alba

Viola pedata var. alba, commonly known as the white edible violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, pale purple to white flowers with a distinct yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often used in culinary applications due to its mild, sweet flavor.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
