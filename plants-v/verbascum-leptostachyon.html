Verbascum leptostachyon

Verbascum leptostachyon, commonly known as the slender mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum leptostachyon is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
