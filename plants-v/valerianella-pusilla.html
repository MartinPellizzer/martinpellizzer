Valerianella pusilla

Valerianella pusilla, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a small, annual plant belonging to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with tiny, rounded leaves that resemble mouse ears, and small, pink or white flowers. Native to Europe, it is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows. The plant is known for its mild peppery flavor, making it a popular ingredient in salads and herbal teas.

It also has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
