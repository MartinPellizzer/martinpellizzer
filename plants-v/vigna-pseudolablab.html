Vigna pseudolablab

Vigna pseudolablab, commonly known as horsegram or kulthi dal, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect growth habit. The plant produces small, cylindrical pods containing several kidney-shaped seeds. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Vigna pseudolablab is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines and traditional medicines.
