Viola × najadum

Viola × najadum, commonly known as the water violet, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various viola species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in wetland habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces violet or purple flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in water gardens or as a decorative plant in ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticulture and ecological studies.
