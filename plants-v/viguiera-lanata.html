Viguiera lanata

Viguiera lanata, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and showy yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Viguiera lanata is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is often found in grasslands and chaparral habitats.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
