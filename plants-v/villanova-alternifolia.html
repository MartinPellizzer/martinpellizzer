Villanova alternifolia

Villanova alternifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive, feather-like fronds.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, slender stems that spread horizontally along the soil surface. The leaves are alternate, meaning they grow singly on either side of the stem, and are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, moist soil to flourish.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
