Veratrum stamineum var. glabrum

Veratrum stamineum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing large, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has smooth, glabrous leaves that are lanceolate in shape and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
