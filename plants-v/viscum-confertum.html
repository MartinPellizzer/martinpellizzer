Viscum confertum

Viscum confertum, commonly known as the dense mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often eaten by birds, which then disperse the seeds. Viscum confertum is native to various regions in Europe and is often found in temperate climates.

Its botanical structure includes a spongy root system that allows it to absorb nutrients from its host.
