Vitis oppositifolia

Vitis oppositifolia, commonly known as the riverbank grape or swamp grape, is a deciduous vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, sandy soils near rivers and wetlands.

Vitis oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
