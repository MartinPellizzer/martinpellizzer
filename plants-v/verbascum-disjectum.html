Verbascum disjectum

Verbascum disjectum, commonly known as the scattered mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and fuzzy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Verbascum disjectum is a perennial plant that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
