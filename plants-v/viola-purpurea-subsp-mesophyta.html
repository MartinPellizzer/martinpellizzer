Viola purpurea subsp. mesophyta

Viola purpurea subsp.

mesophyta, commonly known as the common violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet or purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its rhizomatous root system, which allows it to spread vegetatively.
