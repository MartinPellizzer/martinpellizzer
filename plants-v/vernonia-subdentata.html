Vernonia subdentata

Vernonia subdentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, and it produces showy purple to pink flowers in late summer.

Vernonia subdentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
