Verbesina arborescens var. argentea

Verbesina arborescens var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf false beggar-ticks, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
