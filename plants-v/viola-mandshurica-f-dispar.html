Viola mandshurica f. dispar

Viola mandshurica f. dispar, commonly known as the Siberian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle ornamental value to its natural habitat.

The species is noted for its adaptability to cool climates and its preference for well-drained, loamy soils.
