Vepris ebolowensis

Vepris ebolowensis, commonly known as the Congo sandarac tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The plant is also valued for its resin, which has been used historically in various applications.
