Veronica adamsii

Veronica adamsii, commonly known as Adams' speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
