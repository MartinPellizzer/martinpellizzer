Viola heterophylla subsp. messanensis

Viola heterophylla subsp.

messanensis, commonly known as the Southern Violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate purple or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other viola species.
