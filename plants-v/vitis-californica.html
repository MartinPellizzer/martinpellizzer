Vitis californica

Vitis californica, commonly known as the California grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that range in color from green to black, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Vitis californica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
