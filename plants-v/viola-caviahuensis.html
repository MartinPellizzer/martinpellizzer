Viola caviahuensis

Viola caviahuensis, commonly known as the Cahuenga viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of violet-blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola caviahuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
