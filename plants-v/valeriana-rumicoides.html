Valeriana rumicoides

Valeriana rumicoides, commonly known as the rough valerian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a rough texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and are usually pink or purple in color.

Valeriana rumicoides is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.
