Valeriana nivalis

Valeriana nivalis, commonly known as the snow valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pinkish, and arranged in dense, terminal clusters, blooming in late summer.

Valeriana nivalis is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
