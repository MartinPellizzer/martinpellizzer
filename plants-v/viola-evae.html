Viola evae

Viola evae, commonly known as Eave's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in Italy and the Balkans. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate purple flowers with yellow markings. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Viola evae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
