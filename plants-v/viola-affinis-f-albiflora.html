Viola affinis f. albiflora

Viola affinis f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of small, white or pale lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cool, humid conditions.
