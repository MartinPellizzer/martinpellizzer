Villaria philippinensis

Villaria philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
