Vernonia merana

Vernonia merana, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for treating various ailments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
