Viola pubescens var. eriocarpon

Viola pubescens var. eriocarpon, commonly known as the downy violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, softly hairy leaves that are typically pubescent, giving it its common name. The plant produces delicate purple to violet flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white keel. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to natural habitats.
