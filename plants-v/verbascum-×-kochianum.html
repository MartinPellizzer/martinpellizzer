Verbascum × kochianum

Verbascum × kochianum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their fuzzy, woolly leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The hybrid form, V. × kochianum, is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating respiratory ailments.
