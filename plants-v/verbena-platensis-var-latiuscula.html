Verbena platensis var. latiuscula

Verbena platensis var. latiuscula, commonly known as the broad-leaved vervain, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
