Veronica pentasepala

Veronica pentasepala, commonly known as the five-lobed speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Veronica pentasepala is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
