Verbena litoralis f. angustifolia

Verbena litoralis f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved beach verbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, vividly blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal winds. It is often found in dune environments and plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
