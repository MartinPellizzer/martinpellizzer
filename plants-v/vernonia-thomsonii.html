Vernonia thomsonii

Vernonia thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern and central Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its striking purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
