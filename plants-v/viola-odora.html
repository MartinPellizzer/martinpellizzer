Viola odora

Viola odora, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Viola odora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fragrant blossoms.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
