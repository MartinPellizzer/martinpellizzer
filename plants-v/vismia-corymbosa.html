Vismia corymbosa

Vismia corymbosa, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-bark and its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Vismia corymbosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some cultures.
