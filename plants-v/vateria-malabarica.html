Vateria malabarica

Vateria malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland and hill forests.

The plant is valued for its timber and has cultural significance in certain regions.
