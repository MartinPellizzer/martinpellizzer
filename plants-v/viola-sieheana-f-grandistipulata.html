Viola sieheana f. grandistipulata

Viola sieheana f. grandistipulata, commonly known as the large-stipule viola, is a horticultural form of the viola genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with prominent stipules, which are leaf-like structures at the base of the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers come in a range of colors, including shades of violet, purple, and white, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
