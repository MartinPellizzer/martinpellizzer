Valeriana boelckei

Valeriana boelckei, commonly known as the African valerian, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

The species is part of the Caprifoliaceae family, which includes other plants known for their aromatic compounds.
