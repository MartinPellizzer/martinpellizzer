Veronica flavida

Veronica flavida, commonly known as the golden everlet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Veronica flavida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and compact size.
