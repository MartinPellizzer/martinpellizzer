Viola papillaris

Viola papillaris, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the back. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Viola papillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
