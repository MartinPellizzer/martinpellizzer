Vitex sellowiana var. grandiflora

Vitex sellowiana var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian vitex or sweet vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, violet-blue or purple flowers in panicles, which bloom in the summer months. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

The fruit is a globose capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.
