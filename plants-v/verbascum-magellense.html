Verbascum magellense

Verbascum magellense, commonly known as the Magellanic mullein, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, soft, velvety leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Verbascum magellense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
