Veronica stamatiadae

Veronica stamatiadae, commonly known as the Stamatiadis speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the island of Crete.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
