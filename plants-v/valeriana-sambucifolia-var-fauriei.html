Valeriana sambucifolia var. fauriei

Valeriana sambucifolia var. fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
