Vicia lutea var. muricata

Vicia lutea var. muricata, commonly known as the rough yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its pods are typically hairy and contain several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in erosion control due to its vigorous growth.
