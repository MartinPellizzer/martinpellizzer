Viola odorata subsp. sarotina

Viola odorata subsp.

sarotina, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet hue and produces fragrant, violet-blue flowers during the spring.

The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The subspecies is known for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
