Vicia galloprovincialis

Vicia galloprovincialis, commonly known as the Mediterranean vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume that grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. Its pods are slender and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vicia galloprovincialis is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
