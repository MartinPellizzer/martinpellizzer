Verbena pinnatiloba

Verbena pinnatiloba, commonly known as the showy verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking spikes of violet or purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Verbena pinnatiloba is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
