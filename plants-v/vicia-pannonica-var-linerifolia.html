Vicia pannonica var. linerifolia

Vicia pannonica var. linerifolia, commonly known as the Hungarian vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features slender, erect stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in nitrogen fixation in agricultural systems.
