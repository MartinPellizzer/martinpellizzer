Vernonia appendiculata

Vernonia appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy yellow to orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

Vernonia appendiculata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
