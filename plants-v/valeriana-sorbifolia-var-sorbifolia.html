Valeriana sorbifolia var. sorbifolia

Valeriana sorbifolia var. sorbifolia, commonly known as the sorb leaf valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in traditional herbal medicine.
