Vernonia elegans

Vernonia elegans, commonly known as the elegant ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Vernonia elegans is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
