Vanillosma bicolor

Vanillosma bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored vanilla orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bi-colored flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, fragrant blossoms that are often used in perfumery and aromatherapy. Its roots contain compounds similar to vanilla, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native regions.
