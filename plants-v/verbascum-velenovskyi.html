Verbascum velenovskyi

Verbascum velenovskyi, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
