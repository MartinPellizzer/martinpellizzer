Verbesina cumingii

Verbesina cumingii, commonly known as Cuming's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and showy yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Verbesina cumingii is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in wetlands and along stream banks.
