Vernonia homilocephala

Vernonia homilocephala, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia homilocephala is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
