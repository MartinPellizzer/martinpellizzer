Viridantha atroviridipetala

Viridantha atroviridipetala, commonly known as the emerald-veined green-petaled orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vivid green petals with dark green veins, creating a unique visual contrast. Native to the humid cloud forests of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a delicate, sweet fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is listed as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
