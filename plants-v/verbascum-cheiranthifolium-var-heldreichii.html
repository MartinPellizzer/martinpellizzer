Verbascum cheiranthifolium var. heldreichii

Verbascum cheiranthifolium var. heldreichii, commonly known as the Heldreich's mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in soft hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in a clustered inflorescence, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is closely related to other mullein species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
