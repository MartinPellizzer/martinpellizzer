Vitex agnus-castus f. latifolia

Vitex agnus-castus f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf chaste tree, is a cultivar of the chaste tree species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are broader than those of the typical species, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of lavender and violet, typically blooming in summer. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

The broadleaf form is valued for its unique foliage and floral display, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
