Vaccinium camphorifolium

Vaccinium camphorifolium, commonly known as the camphorleaf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, camphor-scented leaves and small, edible berries that range in color from white to red. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like heathers and rhododendrons.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
