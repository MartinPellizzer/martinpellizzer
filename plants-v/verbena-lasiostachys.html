Verbena lasiostachys

Verbena lasiostachys, commonly known as the woolly verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost woolly appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Verbena lasiostachys is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
