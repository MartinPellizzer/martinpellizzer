Vitex pachyclada

Vitex pachyclada, commonly known as the giant vitex or mountain vitex, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, which bloom in summer. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Vitex pachyclada is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
