Vanilla chamissonis var. brevifolia

Vanilla chamissonis var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved vanilla.

It is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a faint fragrance, and it produces fragrant vanilla-like pods that are used in culinary applications.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and potential economic value.
