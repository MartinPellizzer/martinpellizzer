Viola arvensis var. anglica

Viola arvensis var. anglica, commonly known as the common heartsease, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces showy violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a hairy texture, while the plant is generally low-growing and herbaceous.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
