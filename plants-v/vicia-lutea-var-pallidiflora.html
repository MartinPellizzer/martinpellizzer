Vicia lutea var. pallidiflora

Vicia lutea var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered vetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers with pale markings. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Vicia lutea var. pallidiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
