Vernonia pannosa

Vernonia pannosa, commonly known as the silky Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Vernonia pannosa thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
