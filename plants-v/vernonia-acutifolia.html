Vernonia acutifolia

Vernonia acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Vernonia acutifolia is often found in moist to wet soils in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to native plant gardens.
