Valeriana latifolia

Valeriana latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish-brown roots and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Valeriana latifolia is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects.
