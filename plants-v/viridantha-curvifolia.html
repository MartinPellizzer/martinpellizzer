Viridantha curvifolia

Viridantha curvifolia, commonly known as the curl-leaved fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that curve gracefully, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and colonize moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This unique adaptation makes it a rare and fascinating subject in botanical studies.
