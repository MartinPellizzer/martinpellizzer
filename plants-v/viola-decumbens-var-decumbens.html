Viola decumbens var. decumbens

Viola decumbens var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It typically features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple or violet. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it spreads via rhizomes, forming dense mats.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate wet conditions and is often found in moist meadows and swampy areas.
