Vernonia schultzii

Vernonia schultzii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late summer to autumn. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its bark is thick and fissured.

Vernonia schultzii is known for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used for tool-making and construction.
