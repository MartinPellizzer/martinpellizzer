Veitchia pickeringii

Veitchia pickeringii, commonly known as the Pickering's areca, is a species of palm native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are similar to dates. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
