Viburnum calvum var. kwapiense

Viburnum calvum var. kwapiense, commonly known as the Kwapien viburnum, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for local wildlife.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
