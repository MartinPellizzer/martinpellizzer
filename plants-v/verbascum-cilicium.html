Verbascum cilicium

Verbascum cilicium, commonly known as the Cilician mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Verbascum cilicium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
