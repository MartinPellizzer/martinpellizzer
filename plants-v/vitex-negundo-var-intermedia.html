Vitex negundo var. intermedia

Vitex negundo var. intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese vitex or fragrant vitex, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are often fragrant and have a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale purple, blooming in summer. Its fruit is a dry, woody capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Vitex negundo var. intermedia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
