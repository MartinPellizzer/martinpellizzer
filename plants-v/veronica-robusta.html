Veronica robusta

Veronica robusta, commonly known as the robust speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Veronica robusta is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
