Verbena aristigera

Verbena aristigera, commonly known as the Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of purple, blue, and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Verbena aristigera is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
