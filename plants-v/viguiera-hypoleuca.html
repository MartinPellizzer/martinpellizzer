Viguiera hypoleuca

Viguiera hypoleuca, commonly known as Mexican sunflower, is a perennial plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, daisy-like blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Viguiera hypoleuca is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
