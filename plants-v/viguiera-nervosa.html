Viguiera nervosa

Viguiera nervosa, commonly known as Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its showy, purple to pink flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viguiera nervosa is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
