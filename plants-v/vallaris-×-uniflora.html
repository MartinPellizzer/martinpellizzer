Vallaris × uniflora

Vallaris × uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower vallaris, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Oleaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally grown as a decorative plant in temperate regions.
