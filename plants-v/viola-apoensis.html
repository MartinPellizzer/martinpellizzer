Viola apoensis

Viola apoensis, commonly known as the Appalachian violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola apoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
