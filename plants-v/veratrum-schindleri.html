Veratrum schindleri

Veratrum schindleri, commonly known as Schindler's aconite, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine meadows.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
