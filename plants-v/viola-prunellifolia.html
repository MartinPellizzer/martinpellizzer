Viola prunellifolia

Viola prunellifolia, commonly known as the purple heart viola, is a species of flowering plant in the viola family, Violaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly the Alps and the Carpathians. This perennial herb has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
