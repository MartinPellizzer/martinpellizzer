Vernonia incanescens

Vernonia incanescens, commonly known as the white ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to six feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Vernonia incanescens is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
