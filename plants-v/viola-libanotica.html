Viola libanotica

Viola libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10-20 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
