Verbascum × thermiense

Verbascum × thermiense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
