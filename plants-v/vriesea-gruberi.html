Vriesea gruberi

Vriesea gruberi, commonly known as the Schomburgk's Vriesea, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a central tank for collecting rainwater. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with bright orange flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Vriesea gruberi is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
