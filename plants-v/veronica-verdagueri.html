Veronica verdagueri

Veronica verdagueri, commonly known as the Spanish speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Veronica verdagueri prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in Mediterranean climates.
