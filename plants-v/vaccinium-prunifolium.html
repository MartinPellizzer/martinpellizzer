Vaccinium prunifolium

Vaccinium prunifolium, commonly known as wild blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Vaccinium prunifolium plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and ecosystems.
