Viola pinnata

Viola pinnata, commonly known as the slender violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pinnate pattern, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Viola pinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
