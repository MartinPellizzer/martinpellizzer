Vriesea serranegrensis

Vriesea serranegrensis, commonly known as the serranegrensis bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spathate inflorescence that can display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. Its foliage is often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention in its natural habitat.

Vriesea serranegrensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
