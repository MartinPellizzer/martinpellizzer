Vella pseudocytisus subsp. paui

Vella pseudocytisus subsp.

paui, commonly known as the Sierra Madre broom, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, often in elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and appear in clusters, while its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments.
