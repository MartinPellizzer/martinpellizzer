Verbena crithmifolia

Verbena crithmifolia, commonly known as sea verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments and well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or sandy areas. Its fragrance is reminiscent of thyme and is used in perfumery and herbal medicine.

Verbena crithmifolia is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
