Verbesina mutica

Verbesina mutica, commonly known as the hairy beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, which give it a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a fuzzy surface.

Verbesina mutica is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.
