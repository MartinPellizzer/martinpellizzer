Vatica bancana

Vatica bancana, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with long, lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. Vatica bancana is also known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it suitable for shaded forest environments.

Its seeds are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in forest regeneration.
