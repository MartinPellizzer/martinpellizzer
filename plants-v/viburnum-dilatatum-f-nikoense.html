Viburnum dilatatum f. nikoense

Viburnum dilatatum f. nikoense, commonly known as the Nikoense honeysuckle, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its growth habit is typically rounded and bushy. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in the garden.
