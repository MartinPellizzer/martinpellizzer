Vernonanthura hintoniorum

Vernonanthura hintoniorum, commonly known as the Hinton's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with translucent, waxy leaves and delicate, pale pink or white flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them, relying instead on atmospheric moisture and organic debris. Its habitat is highly specialized, requiring specific humidity and light conditions, which contribute to its endangered status.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
