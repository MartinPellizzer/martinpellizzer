Vernonia tenoreana

Vernonia tenoreana, commonly known as the Tenerife ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are characteristic of the Vernonia genus. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant traits.

Vernonia tenoreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climates.
