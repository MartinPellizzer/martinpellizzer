Viola leunisii

Viola leunisii, commonly known as the Leunis' violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces showy violet flowers that range in color from deep purple to pale blue. Viola leunisii is known for its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates and is often found in calcareous soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
