Vitex agnus-castus

Vitex agnus-castus, commonly known as the chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, clustered purple or blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, woody fruits called drupes, which contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Vitex agnus-castus has been historically used in herbal medicine for its potential hormonal and calming effects.
