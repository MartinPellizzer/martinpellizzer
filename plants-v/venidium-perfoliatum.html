Venidium perfoliatum

Venidium perfoliatum, commonly known as the perfoliate venidium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique leaves that appear to be perforated, as the leaf blades pass through the stem, creating a distinctive perfoliate structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Venidium perfoliatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
