Vaccinium bracteatum var. bracteatum

Vaccinium bracteatum var. bracteatum, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color.
