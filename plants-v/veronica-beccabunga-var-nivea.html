Veronica beccabunga var. nivea

Veronica beccabunga var. nivea, commonly known as the white speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is often found in moist, calcareous soils, such as along stream banks and in meadows.
