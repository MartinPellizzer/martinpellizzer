Veronica × amabilis var. blanda

Veronica × amabilis var. blanda, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate to lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This versatile plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
