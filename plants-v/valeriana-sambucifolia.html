Valeriana sambucifolia

Valeriana sambucifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spherical heads.

Valeriana sambucifolia is typically found in moist meadows and woodland edges, and it thrives in temperate climates.
