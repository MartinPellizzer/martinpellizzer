Vigna multiflora

Vigna multiflora, commonly known as the African bean or wild pigeon pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. Vigna multiflora is often grown as a forage crop and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural systems.
