Vernonia missurica

Vernonia missurica, commonly known as the Missouri ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a tall, erect perennial that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, featuring coarse, hairy leaves and clusters of yellow to orange-red flower heads. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its robust growth and showy inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The genus Vernonia is known for its diverse species, many of which are valued for their ornamental and ecological benefits.
