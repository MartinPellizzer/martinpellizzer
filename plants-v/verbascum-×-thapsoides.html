Verbascum × thapsoides

Verbascum × thapsoides, commonly known as the Russian mullein, is a hybrid plant in the genus Verbascum, which belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, typically blooming in late summer. It is native to southern Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, where it often thrives in disturbed soils.

Verbascum × thapsoides is a perennial herb that is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
