Vitis × doaniana

Vitis × doaniana, commonly known as the Doan's grape, is a hybrid vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is a cross between Vitis labrusca and Vitis riparia, resulting in a plant with robust growth and adaptability to various soil conditions. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, grape-like fruits that are often used in winemaking.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in humid climates.
