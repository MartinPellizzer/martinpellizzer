Vilfa monandra

Vilfa monandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Vilfa monandra thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
