Viola biflora var. cuspidata

Viola biflora var. cuspidata, commonly known as the cuspidate violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
