Veronica anagallis-aquatica subsp. divaricata

Veronica anagallis-aquatica subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, spreading stems that often grow in wet or marshy environments.

The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
