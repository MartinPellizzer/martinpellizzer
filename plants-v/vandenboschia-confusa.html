Vandenboschia confusa

Vandenboschia confusa, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the rainy season, typically in winter. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Vandenboschia confusa is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
