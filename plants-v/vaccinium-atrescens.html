Vaccinium atrescens

Vaccinium atrescens, commonly known as the red evergreen huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in local culinary traditions. The plant has elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen, providing year-round foliage. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and strong winds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
