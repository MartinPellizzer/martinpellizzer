Vepris suaveolens

Vepris suaveolens, commonly known as sweet shrub, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, simple, and aromatic leaves with a distinctive sweet scent. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often displaying yellow or greenish-white petals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
