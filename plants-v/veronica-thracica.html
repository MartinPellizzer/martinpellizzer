Veronica thracica

Veronica thracica, commonly known as the Thrace speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit with hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica thracica is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
