Viola × dissena

Viola × dissena, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, bi-colored flowers that typically display shades of purple, violet, and white. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it forms dense colonies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
