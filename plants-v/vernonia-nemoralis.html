Vernonia nemoralis

Vernonia nemoralis, commonly known as the common ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, yellow-orange flower heads appear in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia nemoralis is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various habitats.
