Vulpia myuros var. tenella

Vulpia myuros var. tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vulpia, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Vulpia myuros var. tenella is an important component of grassland ecosystems, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
