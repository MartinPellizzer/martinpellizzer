Vicia sativa f. erythrosperma

Vicia sativa f. erythrosperma, commonly known as the red-seeded vetch, is a variant of the common vetch, characterized by its distinctive red-colored seeds.

This plant is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. It typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range from purple to pink in color.

Its seeds are not only ornamental but also have historical uses in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
