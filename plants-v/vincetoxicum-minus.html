Vincetoxicum minus

Vincetoxicum minus, commonly known as lesser greenbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems with thorny branches. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a serrated margin, while its flowers are small and clustered in umbels. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are dark blue to black in color.

It is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
