Verbena angustifolia

Verbena angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Verbena angustifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
