Vitis rufotomentosa

Vitis rufotomentosa, commonly known as the red-hairy grapevine, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to berries that are typically red or black when mature. It is a climbing vine that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens and botanical collections.
