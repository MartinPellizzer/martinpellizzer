Vernonia acuminata

Vernonia acuminata, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in dense, spherical clusters, and bloom during the dry season.

Vernonia acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
