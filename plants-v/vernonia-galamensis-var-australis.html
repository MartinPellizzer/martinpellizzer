Vernonia galamensis var. australis

Vernonia galamensis var. australis, commonly known as the southern ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. These flowers are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in open woodland or along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
