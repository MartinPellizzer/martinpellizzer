Viola × zapalowiczii

Viola × zapalowiczii, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola wittrockii.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bi- or tri-colored flowers that typically display shades of purple, violet, and white. The plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Viola × zapalowiczii is a short-lived perennial that blooms in early spring and is relatively easy to care for.
