Vernonia simplex var. latifolia

Vernonia simplex var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Vernonia simplex var. latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
