Veronica osiliensis

Veronica osiliensis, commonly known as the Italian speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Veronica osiliensis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
