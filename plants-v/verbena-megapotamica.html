Verbena megapotamica

Verbena megapotamica, commonly known as the Andean verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Verbena megapotamica is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in arid climates.
