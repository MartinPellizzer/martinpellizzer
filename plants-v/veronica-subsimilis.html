Veronica subsimilis

Veronica subsimilis, commonly known as the common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and typically grows in moist, grassy areas. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica subsimilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its delicate appearance.
