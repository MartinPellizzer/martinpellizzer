Valeriana sambucifolia var. angustifolia

Valeriana sambucifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved valerian, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often tinged with red at the edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their sedative properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
