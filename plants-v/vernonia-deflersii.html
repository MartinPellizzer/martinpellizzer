Vernonia deflersii

Vernonia deflersii, commonly known as the desert ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping.

The genus Vernonia is part of the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is known for its ornamental and ecological value.
