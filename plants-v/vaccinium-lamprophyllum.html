Vaccinium lamprophyllum

Vaccinium lamprophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are blue-black in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential as a food source for wildlife.
