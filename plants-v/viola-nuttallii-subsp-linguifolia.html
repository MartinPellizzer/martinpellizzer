Viola nuttallii subsp. linguifolia

Viola nuttallii subsp.

linguifolia, commonly known as the western violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet hue and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and blue. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
