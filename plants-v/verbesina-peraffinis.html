Verbesina peraffinis

Verbesina peraffinis, commonly known as the smooth beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairless or only slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

It is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
