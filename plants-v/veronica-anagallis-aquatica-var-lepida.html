Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. lepida

Veronica anagallis-aquatica var. lepida, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial native to moist environments in Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, trailing stems with small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wet soils and is often found near streams, ditches, and marshy areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
