Vitis rotundifolia var. munsoniana

Vitis rotundifolia var. munsoniana, commonly known as the Munson's musk grape, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elliptical, waxy leaves and clusters of small, dark purple berries. The plant is adapted to wetland and swampy habitats, often growing in association with other wetland species. Its berries are aromatic and have been used historically for their medicinal and culinary properties.

The vine is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils.
