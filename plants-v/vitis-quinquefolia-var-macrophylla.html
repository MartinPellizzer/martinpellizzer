Vitis quinquefolia var. macrophylla

Vitis quinquefolia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant muscadine grape, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories. Its berries are edible, though typically consumed raw or used in jams and wines.

The vine is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
