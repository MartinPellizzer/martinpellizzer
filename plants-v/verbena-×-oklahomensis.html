Verbena × oklahomensis

Verbena × oklahomensis, commonly known as Oklahoma verbena, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early fall, producing clusters of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and garden borders.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements contribute to its adaptability in various garden settings.
