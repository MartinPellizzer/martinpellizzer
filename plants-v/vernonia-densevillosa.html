Vernonia densevillosa

Vernonia densevillosa, commonly known as the dense-hairy ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant has dense, soft hairs covering its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
