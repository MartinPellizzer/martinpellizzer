Vriesea philippocoburgi var. vagans

Vriesea philippocoburgi var. vagans, commonly known as the Vriesea hybrid, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with colorful, long-lasting flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care.
