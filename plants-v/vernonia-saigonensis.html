Vernonia saigonensis

Vernonia saigonensis, commonly known as Vietnamese ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as riverbanks and forest edges.

Vernonia saigonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
