Viburnum opulus var. xanthocarpum

Viburnum opulus var. xanthocarpum, commonly known as the golden-fruited elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn bright red in the fall and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant produces distinctive, yellowish-orange berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
