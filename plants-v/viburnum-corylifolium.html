Viburnum corylifolium

Viburnum corylifolium, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, giving it a distinctive, palmately lobed appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Viburnum corylifolium thrives in partial shade to full sun and is valued for its ornamental foliage and seasonal interest.

It is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
