Vitis juncea

Vitis juncea, commonly known as the hybrid grape or Japanese grape, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of berries, typically yellow or red in color. Vitis juncea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.
