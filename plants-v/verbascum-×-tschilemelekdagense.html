Verbascum × tschilemelekdagense

Verbascum × tschilemelekdagense, commonly known as the Tschiilemelekdag mullein, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, fuzzy leaves that are often covered in soft, silky hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, elongated inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
