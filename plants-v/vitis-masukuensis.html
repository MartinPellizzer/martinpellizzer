Vitis masukuensis

Vitis masukuensis, commonly known as the African fox grape, is a woody vine native to tropical Africa.

It produces small, clustered berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and is characterized by its vigorous growth habit. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream sides.

The berries are edible but are not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
