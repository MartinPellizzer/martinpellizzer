Vitis cirrhosa

Vitis cirrhosa, commonly known as the hairy grapevine, is a species of flowering vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its berries are typically small and sour, with a thick, leathery skin.

Vitis cirrhosa is often found in woodland areas and is adapted to moist, temperate climates.
