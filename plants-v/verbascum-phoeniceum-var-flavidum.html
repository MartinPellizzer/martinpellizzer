Verbascum phoeniceum var. flavidum

Verbascum phoeniceum var. flavidum, commonly known as the yellow mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
