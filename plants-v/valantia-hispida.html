Valantia hispida

Valantia hispida, commonly known as the rough valantia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Valantia hispida thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
