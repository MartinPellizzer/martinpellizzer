Vaccinium microcarpum f. kirigaminense

Vaccinium microcarpum f. kirigaminense, commonly known as the Kirigamine huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in early summer. The berries, which are dark blue to black when mature, are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to acidic, well-drained soils.
