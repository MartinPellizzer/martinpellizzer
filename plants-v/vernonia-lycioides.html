Vernonia lycioides

Vernonia lycioides, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its silver-gray foliage gives it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

Vernonia lycioides is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinators.
