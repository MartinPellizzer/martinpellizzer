Viola orphanidis

Viola orphanidis, commonly known as the Greek violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland soils and thrives in temperate climates. Viola orphanidis is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its blooming period occurs in spring, adding a touch of color to alpine and subalpine habitats.
