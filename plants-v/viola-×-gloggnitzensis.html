Viola × gloggnitzensis

Viola × gloggnitzensis, commonly known as the Gloggnitz violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet-blue petals and heart-shaped leaves, typical of many viola species. The plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, showy flowers that bloom in early spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
