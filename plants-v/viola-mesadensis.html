Viola mesadensis

Viola mesadensis, commonly known as the desert violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
