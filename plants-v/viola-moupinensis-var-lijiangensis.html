Viola moupinensis var. lijiangensis

Viola moupinensis var. lijiangensis, commonly known as the Lijiang viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in forest understories. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
