Viburnum bracteatum

Viburnum bracteatum, commonly known as the Chinese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Viburnum bracteatum is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest in gardens.
