Viola triloba f. dilatata

Viola triloba f. dilatata, commonly known as the broad-leafed violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive broad lobing that sets it apart from other viola species. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in shady, moist woodland habitats and is often found in moist soils along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in native plant gardens.
