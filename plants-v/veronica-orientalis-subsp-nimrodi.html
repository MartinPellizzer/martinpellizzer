Veronica orientalis subsp. nimrodi

Veronica orientalis subsp.

nimrodi, commonly known as the Eastern Speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, usually from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
