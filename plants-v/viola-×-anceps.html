Viola × anceps

Viola × anceps, commonly known as the common pansy, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, often violet or purple flowers. The plant is typically herbaceous, with a short stem and clusters of petals that resemble a pansy. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Viola × anceps is native to Europe but has been widely introduced to other regions around the world.
