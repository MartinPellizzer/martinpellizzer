Viola striis-notata

Viola striis-notata, commonly known as the spotted violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and purple-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
