Viburnum plicatum f. plenum

Viburnum plicatum f. plenum, commonly known as the doublefile viburnum, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy, double-flowered inflorescences that appear in early spring. The leaves are dark green and glossy, providing attractive foliage throughout the growing season. This variety is particularly noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its tolerance to a range of soil conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and structural form.
