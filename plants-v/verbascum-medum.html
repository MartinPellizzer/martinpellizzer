Verbascum medum

Verbascum medum, commonly known as the common mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Verbascum medum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory ailments.
