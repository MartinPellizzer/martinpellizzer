Verbena pumila f. albida

Verbena pumila f. albida, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a desirable addition to native plant collections.
