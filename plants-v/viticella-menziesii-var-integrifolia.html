Viticella menziesii var. integrifolia

Viticella menziesii var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Coast Redbud, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth-edged and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers in early spring, which are followed by elongated, bean-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests and riparian zones.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
