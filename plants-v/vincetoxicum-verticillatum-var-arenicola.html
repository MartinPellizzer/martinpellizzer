Vincetoxicum verticillatum var. arenicola

Vincetoxicum verticillatum var. arenicola, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas where it can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
