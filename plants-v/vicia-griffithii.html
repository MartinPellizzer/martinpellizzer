Vicia griffithii

Vicia griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's vetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Vicia griffithii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
