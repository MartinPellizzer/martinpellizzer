Viola × columbiana

Viola × columbiana, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value.

Viola × columbiana is often found in gardens and naturalized areas, where it spreads through both seeds and rhizomes.
