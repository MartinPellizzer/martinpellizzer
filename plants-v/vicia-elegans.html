Vicia elegans

Vicia elegans, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, climbing stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive pale green color. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, arranged in clusters along the stems.

Vicia elegans is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in certain environments.
