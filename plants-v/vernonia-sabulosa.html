Vernonia sabulosa

Vernonia sabulosa, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Vernonia sabulosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
