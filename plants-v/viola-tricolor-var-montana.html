Viola tricolor var. montana

Viola tricolor var. montana, commonly known as the mountain violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive three-lobed pattern and produces vibrant violet, yellow, and white flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade to full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
