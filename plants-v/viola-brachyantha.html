Viola brachyantha

Viola brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. Its flowers are small, usually violet or blue, and bloom in early spring.

Viola brachyantha is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
