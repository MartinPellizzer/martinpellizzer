Viola lutea subsp. sudetica

Viola lutea subsp.

sudetica, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive violet throat. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
