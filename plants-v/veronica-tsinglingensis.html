Veronica tsinglingensis

Veronica tsinglingensis, commonly known as the Tsingling speedwell, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
