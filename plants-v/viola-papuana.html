Viola papuana

Viola papuana, commonly known as the Papuan violet, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Viola papuana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
