Viola guadalupensis

Viola guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupan violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky soils and open woodlands, preferring partial shade to full sun. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple and violet. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola guadalupensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
