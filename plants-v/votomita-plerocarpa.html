Votomita plerocarpa

Votomita plerocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces clusters of yellow-orange fruits that are edible. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in inflorescences at the base of the leaves.

The fruit has a thick, fibrous rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp that is popular in local cuisines.
