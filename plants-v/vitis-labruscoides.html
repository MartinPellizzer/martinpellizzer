Vitis labruscoides

Vitis labruscoides, commonly known as the wild grape or sand grape, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. Its leaves are alternate and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Vitis labruscoides is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.
