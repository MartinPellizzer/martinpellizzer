Veronica hederifolia subsp. triloba

Veronica hederifolia subsp.

triloba, commonly known as the creeping veronica, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features dense, mat-forming foliage with rounded, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens and natural landscapes.
