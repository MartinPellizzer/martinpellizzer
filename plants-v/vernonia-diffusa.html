Vernonia diffusa

Vernonia diffusa, commonly known as blue vervain, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value.

Vernonia diffusa is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
