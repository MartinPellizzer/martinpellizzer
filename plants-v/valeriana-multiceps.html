Valeriana multiceps

Valeriana multiceps, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. Its root system is notable for containing volatile compounds, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine.

Valeriana multiceps is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to diverse soil conditions.
