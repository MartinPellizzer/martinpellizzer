Veratrum bracteatum var. tibeticum

Veratrum bracteatum var. tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan white helleborine, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has large, broad leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of alpine and subalpine zones.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Tibetan medicine for various ailments.
