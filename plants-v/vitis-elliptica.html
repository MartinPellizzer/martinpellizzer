Vitis elliptica

Vitis elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is often found in forested areas. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. Its small, greenish-white flowers give way to berries that are typically yellow or red when mature.

Vitis elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
