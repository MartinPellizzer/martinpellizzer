Veronica cymbalaria var. freyniana

Veronica cymbalaria var. freyniana, commonly known as the Freyn's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
