Valeriana × ekmanii

Valeriana × ekmanii, commonly known as the Swedish valerian, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Valeriana officinalis and Valeriana sambucifolia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its aromatic, sometimes hairy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks, where it forms dense colonies.

Valeriana × ekmanii is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming effects, and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
