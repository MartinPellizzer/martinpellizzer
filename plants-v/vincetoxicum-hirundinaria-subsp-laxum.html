Vincetoxicum hirundinaria subsp. laxum

Vincetoxicum hirundinaria subsp.

laxum, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale pink.

The subspecies laxum is distinguished by its more loosely arranged inflorescences compared to other subspecies of Vincetoxicum.
