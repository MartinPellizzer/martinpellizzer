Vignea canariensis

Vignea canariensis, commonly known as the Canary vine, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing along coastal areas. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Vignea canariensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
