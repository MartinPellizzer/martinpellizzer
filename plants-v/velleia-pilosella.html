Velleia pilosella

Velleia pilosella, commonly known as the hairy velleia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features tiny, opposite leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in groups of three, and bloom in the spring.

Velleia pilosella is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its delicate, ephemeral beauty.
