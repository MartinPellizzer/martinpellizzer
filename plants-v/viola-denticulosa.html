Viola denticulosa

Viola denticulosa, commonly known as the toothed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with finely toothed edges. The plant produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Viola denticulosa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
