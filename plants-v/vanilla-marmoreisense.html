Vanilla marmoreisense

Vanilla marmoreisense, commonly known as the marble vanilla, is a climbing orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant, cream-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces vanilla pods, which are harvested and processed to yield the highly valued vanilla extract. These pods have a unique marbled appearance, giving the species its common name.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, Vanilla marmoreisense is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
