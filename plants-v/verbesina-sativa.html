Verbesina sativa

Verbesina sativa, commonly known as golden beggar's ticks, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, branching stems. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural areas.
