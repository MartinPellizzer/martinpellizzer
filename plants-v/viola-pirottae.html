Viola pirottae

Viola pirottae, commonly known as the Pyrenean violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, preferring cool and moist conditions. Viola pirottae is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to alpine landscapes.
