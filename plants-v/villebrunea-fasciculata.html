Villebrunea fasciculata

Villebrunea fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and fragrant, leathery leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered, often producing a sweet, resinous scent.
