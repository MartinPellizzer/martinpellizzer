Veronica incana

Veronica incana, commonly known as hoary veronica, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Veronica incana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
