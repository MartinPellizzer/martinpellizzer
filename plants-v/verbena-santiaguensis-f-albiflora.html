Verbena santiaguensis f. albiflora

Verbena santiaguensis f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, tubular, white or pale pink blooms that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture and appearance.
