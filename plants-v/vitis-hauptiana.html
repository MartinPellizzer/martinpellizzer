Vitis hauptiana

Vitis hauptiana, commonly known as the Hauptian grape, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous climbing vine characterized by its thick, woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of round, dark blue berries. Vitis hauptiana is valued for its ornamental potential and has limited horticultural use due to its rarity and specific growing requirements.

Its conservation status highlights the need for habitat protection and propagation efforts.
