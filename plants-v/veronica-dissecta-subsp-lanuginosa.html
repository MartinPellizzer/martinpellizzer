Veronica dissecta subsp. lanuginosa

Veronica dissecta subsp.

lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered blue or purple flowers.

The subspecies lanuginosa is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
