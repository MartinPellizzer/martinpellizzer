Verbena peruviana

Verbena peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Verbena peruviana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
