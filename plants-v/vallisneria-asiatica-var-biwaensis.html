Vallisneria asiatica var. biwaensis

Vallisneria asiatica var. biwaensis, commonly known as the Japanese waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by water currents. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with moderate to high light levels.

Vallisneria asiatica var. biwaensis is often used in aquaculture and aquatic gardening for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
