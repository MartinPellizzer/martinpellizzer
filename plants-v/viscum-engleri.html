Viscum engleri

Viscum engleri, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is native to regions of Africa, including South Africa, Kenya, and Tanzania. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers and red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are evergreen, and it can form dense clusters on its host, potentially affecting the host's health.

Viscum engleri is also used in some cultures for ornamental purposes and as a source of natural adhesives.
