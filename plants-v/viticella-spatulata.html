Viticella spatulata

Viticella spatulata, commonly known as the spatulate honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to spatulate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, followed by small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Viticella spatulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
