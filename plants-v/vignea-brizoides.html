Vignea brizoides

Vignea brizoides, commonly known as the slender vine mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are slender and often hairy as well. Vignea brizoides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
