Viscaria vulgaris subsp. helvetica

Viscaria vulgaris subsp.

helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and is relatively easy to cultivate.
