Veronica rotunda subsp. subintegra

Veronica rotunda subsp.

subintegra, commonly known as the rounded speedwell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters.

The leaves are rounded and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
