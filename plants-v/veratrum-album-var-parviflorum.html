Veratrum album var. parviflorum

Veratrum album var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, stout stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, white, bell-shaped flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and have a waxy texture, while its roots are thick and fleshy, containing toxic alkaloids.

This species is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appearance and historical medicinal use.
