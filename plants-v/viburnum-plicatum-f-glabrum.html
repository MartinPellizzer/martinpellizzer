Viburnum plicatum f. glabrum

Viburnum plicatum f. glabrum, commonly known as the laceleaf viburnum, is a compact, deciduous shrub characterized by its finely cut, lace-like foliage.

It features ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and have a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are followed by showy red or pink berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Viburnum plicatum f. glabrum is relatively low maintenance and resistant to common pests and diseases.
