Veronica schmidtiana f. pilosa

Veronica schmidtiana f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy veronica, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, compact foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and prolonged flowering period.
