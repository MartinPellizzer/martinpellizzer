Vicia angustifolia var. arvensis

Vicia angustifolia var. arvensis, commonly known as narrowleaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces elongated pods containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Vicia angustifolia var. arvensis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
