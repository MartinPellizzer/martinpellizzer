Viola pubescens var. scabriuscula

Viola pubescens var. scabriuscula, commonly known as the rough-stemmed violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves with a distinctive rough texture, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Violaceae family.
