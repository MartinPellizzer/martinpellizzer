Viola serpens var. hamiltoniana

Viola serpens var. hamiltoniana, commonly known as Hamilton's violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild sedative properties.
