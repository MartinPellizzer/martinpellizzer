Viola × mollis

Viola × mollis, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial to full shade. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in woodland gardens and as a ground cover in shaded areas.
