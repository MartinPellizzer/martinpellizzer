Viola coreana

Viola coreana, commonly known as the Korean violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola coreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
