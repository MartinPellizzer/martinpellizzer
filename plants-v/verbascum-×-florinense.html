Verbascum × florinense

Verbascum × florinense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a flowering plant in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattellum, both native to southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used medicinally. It produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer.

Verbascum × florinense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
