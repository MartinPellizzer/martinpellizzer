Vellozia pumila

Vellozia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf vellozia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Vellozia pumila is a hardy species that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
