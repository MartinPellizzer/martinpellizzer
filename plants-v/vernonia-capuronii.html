Vernonia capuronii

Vernonia capuronii, commonly known as the Capuron's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
