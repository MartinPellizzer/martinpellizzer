Ventilago calyculata var. trichoclada

Ventilago calyculata var. trichoclada, commonly known as the hairy calyx ventilago, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
