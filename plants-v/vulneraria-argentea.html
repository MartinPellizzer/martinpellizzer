Vulneraria argentea

Vulneraria argentea, commonly known as silver vulnerary, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers and hairy, silver-green leaves. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

Vulneraria argentea is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional herbal remedies.
