Verbena arequipensis

Verbena arequipensis, commonly known as the Arequipa verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of blue, purple, and pink. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean climates.

Verbena arequipensis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
