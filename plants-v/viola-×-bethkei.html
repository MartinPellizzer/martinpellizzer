Viola × bethkei

Viola × bethkei, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, often purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and wildflower habitats.
