Vicia willdenowiana

Vicia willdenowiana, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by long, slender pods containing several seeds.

Vicia willdenowiana is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
