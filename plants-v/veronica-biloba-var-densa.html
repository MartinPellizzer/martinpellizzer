Veronica biloba var. densa

Veronica biloba var. densa, commonly known as the dense Veronica, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its ability to thrive in partial shade.
