Verbena hookeriana

Verbena hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow throat. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Verbena hookeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
