Viola × merkensteiniensis

Viola × merkensteiniensis, commonly known as the Merkensteiniensis violet, is a hybrid species within the Viola genus, characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant flower colors.

It typically produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and displays flowers in shades of purple, violet, and white. This plant is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates, often found in woodland edges and moist soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to partial shade and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
