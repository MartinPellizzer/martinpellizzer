Vincetoxicum hirundinaria subsp. lusitanicum

Vincetoxicum hirundinaria subsp.

lusitanicum, commonly known as the Lusitanian milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface. It produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
