Viguiera kunthiana

Viguiera kunthiana, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue-violet composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed areas, preferring full sun. Its foliage is lance-shaped and gray-green, with hairy textures that help reduce water loss.

Viguiera kunthiana is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
