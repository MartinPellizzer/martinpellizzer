Verbascum durmitoreum

Verbascum durmitoreum, commonly known as the sleepy mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Verbascum durmitoreum is a perennial species that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
