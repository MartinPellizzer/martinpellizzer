Viola sempervirens

Viola sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen violet, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of violet or blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies, and the flowers are popular in garden cultivation.
