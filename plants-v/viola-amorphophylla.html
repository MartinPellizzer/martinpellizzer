Viola amorphophylla

Viola amorphophylla, commonly known as the ghost violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of pale violet or white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers have five petals, with the lower petal often being larger and more showy.

Viola amorphophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
