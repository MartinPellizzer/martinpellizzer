Veronica conferta var. glanduligera

Veronica conferta var. glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular Veronica, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
