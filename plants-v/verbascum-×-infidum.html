Verbascum × infidum

Verbascum × infidum, commonly known as the common mullein.

This plant is a hybrid species within the genus Verbascum, which belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The flowers are typically yellow or orange and are borne in clusters at the top of the plant.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in various regions around the world.
