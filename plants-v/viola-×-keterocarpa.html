Viola × keterocarpa

Viola × keterocarpa, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is native to parts of Europe and has been introduced to other regions as an ornamental species.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
