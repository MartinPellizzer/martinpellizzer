Viola lunaria

Viola lunaria, commonly known as the moon violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pale purple or white, with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and streambanks.

Viola lunaria is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
