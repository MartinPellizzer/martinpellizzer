Viola concordifolia var. hirtipedicellata

Viola concordifolia var. hirtipedicellata, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its stems are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, which gives it its specific variety name.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to forest understories.
