Viola verecunda

Viola verecunda, commonly known as the shy violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of delicate, violet-colored flowers. Viola verecunda is known for its modest appearance and is often found growing in shaded, forested areas.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
