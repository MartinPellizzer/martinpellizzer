Valeriana crassifolia

Valeriana crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved valerian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Valeriana crassifolia is typically found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is a perennial herb that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
