Verbesina aristata

Verbesina aristata, commonly known as the arrowleaf coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Verbesina aristata is also known for its ability to grow in disturbed habitats, making it a hardy and resilient species.
