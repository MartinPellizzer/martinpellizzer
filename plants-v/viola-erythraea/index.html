Viola Erythraea

Erythraea violet, scientifically known as Viola erythraea, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. This species is distinguished by its striking violet petals and heart-shaped leaves that reflect its botanical family, Violaceae. Despite its beauty, Viola erythraea is critically endangered due to habitat loss and climate change. Conservation efforts are underway to protect and propagate this rare species. Its unique characteristics make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
