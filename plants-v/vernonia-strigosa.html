Vernonia strigosa

Vernonia strigosa, commonly known as the rough vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are hairy and grayish-green, while its flowers are yellow with purple centers, adding visual interest to the landscape.

Vernonia strigosa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
