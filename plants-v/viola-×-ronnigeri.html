Viola × ronnigeri

Viola × ronnigeri, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a popular ornamental plant resulting from crosses between species of the genus Viola.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and vibrant, often purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade. It is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

Viola × ronnigeri is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize, making it a favored choice for woodland gardens and rockeries.
