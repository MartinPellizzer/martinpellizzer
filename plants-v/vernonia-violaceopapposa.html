Vernonia violaceopapposa

Vernonia violaceopapposa, commonly known as the violet pappous vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, violet-tinged flower heads. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its propagation. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and covered in a powdery coating.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
