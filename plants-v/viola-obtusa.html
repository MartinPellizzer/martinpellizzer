Viola obtusa

Viola obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaf violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is often found in woodland and grassy areas. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces violet or purple flowers with five petals. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Viola obtusa is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
