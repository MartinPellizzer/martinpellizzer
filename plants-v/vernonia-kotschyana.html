Vernonia kotschyana

Vernonia kotschyana, commonly known as the Egyptian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. Vernonia kotschyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.

Its fibrous roots and sturdy structure make it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.
