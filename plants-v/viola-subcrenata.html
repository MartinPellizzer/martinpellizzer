Viola subcrenata

Viola subcrenata, commonly known as the silver-leaf violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in moist, shady woodlands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are typically violet-blue, with five petals and a yellow throat, blooming in early spring.

Viola subcrenata is a perennial herb that spreads by rhizomes, forming dense colonies in its natural habitat.
