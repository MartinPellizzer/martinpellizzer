Vitex divaricata var. cubensis

Vitex divaricata var. cubensis, commonly known as Cuban vitex or Cuban chaste tree, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or white, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
