Viola oetolica

Viola oetolica, commonly known as the Greek violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters and features heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white marking at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola oetolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
