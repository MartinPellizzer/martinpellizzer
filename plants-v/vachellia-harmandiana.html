Vachellia harmandiana

Vachellia harmandiana, commonly known as the Harmand's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures in mature plants. The plant produces yellow globular flowers, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is well adapted to drought conditions. Vachellia harmandiana plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance is significant in desert ecosystems where it provides habitat and forage for wildlife.
