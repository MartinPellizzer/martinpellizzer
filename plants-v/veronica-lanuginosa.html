Veronica lanuginosa

Veronica lanuginosa, commonly known as woolly speedwell, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Veronica lanuginosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil.
