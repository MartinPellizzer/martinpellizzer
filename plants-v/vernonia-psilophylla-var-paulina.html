Vernonia psilophylla var. paulina

Vernonia psilophylla var. paulina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
