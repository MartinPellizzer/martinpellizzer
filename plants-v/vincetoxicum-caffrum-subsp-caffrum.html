Vincetoxicum caffrum subsp. caffrum

Vincetoxicum caffrum subsp.

caffrum, commonly known as the African bittersweet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that can reach lengths of up to 5 meters.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
