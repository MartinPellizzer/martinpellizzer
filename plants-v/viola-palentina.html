Viola palentina

Viola palentina, commonly known as the white violets, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Viola palentina is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical uses in traditional herbal remedies.
