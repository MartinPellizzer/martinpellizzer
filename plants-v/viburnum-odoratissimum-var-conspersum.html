Viburnum odoratissimum var. conspersum

Viburnum odoratissimum var. conspersum, commonly known as the fragrant viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and fragrance.
