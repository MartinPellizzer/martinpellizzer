Vicia montevidensis

Vicia montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Vicia montevidensis is valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
