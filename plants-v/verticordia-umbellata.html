Verticordia umbellata

Verticordia umbellata, commonly known as the Christmas lily, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, urn-shaped pink or red flowers that bloom during the winter months. The plant typically grows in heathland and coastal regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.
