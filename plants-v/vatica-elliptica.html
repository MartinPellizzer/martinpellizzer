Vatica elliptica

Vatica elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and lanceolate, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Vatica elliptica is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its durable timber.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
