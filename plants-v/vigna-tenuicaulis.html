Vigna tenuicaulis

Vigna tenuicaulis, commonly known as slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces pods that contain small, edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Vigna tenuicaulis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting other plants in agricultural settings.
