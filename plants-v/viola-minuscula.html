Viola minuscula

Viola minuscula, commonly known as the small viola, is a delicate flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Viola minuscula is often found in rocky or clay soils and prefers cool, temperate climates.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
