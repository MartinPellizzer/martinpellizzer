Viola tashiroi

Viola tashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Viola tashiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
