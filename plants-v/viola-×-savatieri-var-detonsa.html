Viola × savatieri var. detonsa

Viola × savatieri var. detonsa, commonly known as the violet-leaved viola, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet or purple foliage, which sets it apart from other viola varieties. The plant typically produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple, blue, or white, often with dark veins. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to bloom reliably in temperate climates.
