Valeriana acuminata

Valeriana acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its roots contain compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Valeriana acuminata is part of the Caprifoliaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like honeysuckle.
