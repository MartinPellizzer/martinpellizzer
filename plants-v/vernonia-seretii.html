Vernonia seretii

Vernonia seretii, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia seretii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
