Vagaria

Vagaria, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Apiaceae.

These plants are typically found in coastal areas and are adapted to saline and sandy environments. Vagaria species are characterized by their distinctive, often spiny leaves and showy, umbellate flowers. They are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.

The genus includes several species, such as Vagaria tinctoria, which has historical uses in dye production.
