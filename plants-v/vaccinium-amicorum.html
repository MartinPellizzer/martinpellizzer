Vaccinium amicorum

Vaccinium amicorum, commonly known as the friendship blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, with a tart flavor. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a dense ground cover.

Vaccinium amicorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
