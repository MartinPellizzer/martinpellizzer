Vieraea laevigata

Vieraea laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf everlet, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, smooth, evergreen leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vieraea laevigata is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils near the shoreline.
