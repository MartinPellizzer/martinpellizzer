Veronica linariifolia f. dilatata

Veronica linariifolia f. dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
