Verbascum rugulosum

Verbascum rugulosum, commonly known as the roughleaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum rugulosum is a hardy perennial that is relatively easy to grow in gardens with full sun.
