Veronica spicata var. japonica

Veronica spicata var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
