Vicia singarensis

Vicia singarensis, commonly known as the Singarensis vetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Vicia singarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in sandy or disturbed soils.
