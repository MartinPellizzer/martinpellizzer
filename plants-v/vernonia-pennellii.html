Vernonia pennellii

Vernonia pennellii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
