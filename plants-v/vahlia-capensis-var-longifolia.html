Vahlia capensis var. longifolia

Vahlia capensis var. longifolia, commonly known as the Cape vahlia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
