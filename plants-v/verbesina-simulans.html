Verbesina simulans

Verbesina simulans, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy texture, and its most striking characteristic is its clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
