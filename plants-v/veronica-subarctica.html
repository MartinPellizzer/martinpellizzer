Veronica subarctica

Veronica subarctica, commonly known as Arctic speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats, with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Veronica subarctica is often found in tundra and rocky habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
