Viola gracilis var. euboea

Viola gracilis var. euboea, commonly known as the Euboean violet, is a delicate perennial species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, violet-blue flowers during the spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous root system.
