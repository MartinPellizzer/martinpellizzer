Veronica maccaskillii

Veronica maccaskillii, commonly known as the Macaskill's speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged opposite along its slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Veronica maccaskillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native gardens.
