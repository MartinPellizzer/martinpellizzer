Vernonia tortuosa

Vernonia tortuosa, commonly known as the spiral verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia tortuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
