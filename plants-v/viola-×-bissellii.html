Viola × bissellii

Viola × bissellii, commonly known as the Bissell violet, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola wittrockii.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shady environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded borders.
