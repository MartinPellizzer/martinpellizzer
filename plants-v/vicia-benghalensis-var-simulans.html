Vicia benghalensis var. simulans

Vicia benghalensis var. simulans, commonly known as the Indian vetch, is a leguminous plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.
