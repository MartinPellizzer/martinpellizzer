Villebrunea crenulata

Villebrunea crenulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lily, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting pollinators like moths and bees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
