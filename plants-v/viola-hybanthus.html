Viola hybanthus

Viola hybanthus, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy blooms. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola hybanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
