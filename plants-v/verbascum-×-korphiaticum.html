Verbascum × korphiaticum

Verbascum × korphiaticum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum blattellum.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, woolly leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely appreciated in horticulture.
