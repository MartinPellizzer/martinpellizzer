Vincentia olukondae

Vincentia olukondae, commonly known as the Kondae vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Vincentia olukondae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
