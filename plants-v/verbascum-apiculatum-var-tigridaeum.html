Verbascum apiculatum var. tigridaeum

Verbascum apiculatum var. tigridaeum, commonly known as the tigridian mullein, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens.
