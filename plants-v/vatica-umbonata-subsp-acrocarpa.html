Vatica umbonata subsp. acrocarpa

Vatica umbonata subsp.

acrocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 40 meters tall, characterized by its dense, heavy timber that is highly valued for its strength and durability.

The leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. The flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in spikes, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.
