Vilfa plebeia

Vilfa plebeia, commonly known as the common vilfa, is a small herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, and it is often found growing in clusters along the edges of water bodies.

The species is characterized by its low-growing habit and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
