Vernonia hypochaeris

Vernonia hypochaeris, commonly known as golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in some traditional practices.

The plant's ability to thrive in disturbed soils makes it a common weed in agricultural areas.
