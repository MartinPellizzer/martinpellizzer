Viola brevistipulata subsp. minor

Viola brevistipulata subsp.

minor, commonly known as the small-leaved violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with short, hairy stipules.

The plant produces delicate purple or violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardening.
