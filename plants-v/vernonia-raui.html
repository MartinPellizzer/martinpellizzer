Vernonia raui

Vernonia raui, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Vernonia raui is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
