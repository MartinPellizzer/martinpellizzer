Vernonia elisabethvilleana

Vernonia elisabethvilleana, commonly known as the Elizabethville Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, arranged in dense, conical clusters.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.
