Vicia purpurascens

Vicia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often appearing in clusters. Vicia purpurascens is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
