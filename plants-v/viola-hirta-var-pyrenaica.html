Viola hirta var. pyrenaica

Viola hirta var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive white or pale yellow throat. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cool, moist climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Violaceae family.
