Volkameria sesamoides

Volkameria sesamoides, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing three seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along riverbanks.

The plant is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
