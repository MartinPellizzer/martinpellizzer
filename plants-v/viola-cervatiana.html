Viola cervatiana

Viola cervatiana, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers that are typically violet or white in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Viola cervatiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
