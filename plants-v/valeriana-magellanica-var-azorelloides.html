Valeriana magellanica var. azorelloides

Valeriana magellanica var. azorelloides, commonly known as the Azorean valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Azores.

It features aromatic, cylindrical stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in summer. Its root system is fibrous and contains essential oils responsible for its characteristic scent.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
