Veronica stenocarpon

Veronica stenocarpon, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
