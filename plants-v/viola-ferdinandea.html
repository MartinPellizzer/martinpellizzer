Viola ferdinandea

Viola ferdinandea, commonly known as the Ferdinand's violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-20 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it forms dense, low-growing mats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its beautiful blooms and medicinal properties.
