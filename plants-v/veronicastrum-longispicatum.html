Veronicastrum longispicatum

Veronicastrum longispicatum, commonly known as blue mist spirea, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 2 to 4 meters tall, characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
