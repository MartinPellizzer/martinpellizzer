Valerianella × zoltanii

Valerianella × zoltanii, commonly known as the edible groundsel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy, lobed leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It is valued for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste and are used in salads or as a garnish.

Valerianella × zoltanii is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
