Viola tricolor var. declinata

Viola tricolor var. declinata, commonly known as the purple heart viola, is a variant of the common violet characterized by its distinct coloration.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter, often white or pale underside. The flowers are typically violet on the outside and white or cream on the inside, creating a striking contrast. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
