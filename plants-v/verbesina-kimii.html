Verbesina kimii

Verbesina kimii, commonly known as Kimii's beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant has rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and grassland habitats.

Verbesina kimii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African communities.
