Vernonia borinquensis

Vernonia borinquensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia borinquensis plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
