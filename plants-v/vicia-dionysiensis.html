Vicia dionysiensis

Vicia dionysiensis, commonly known as the Sicilian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean shrublands.

Vicia dionysiensis is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
