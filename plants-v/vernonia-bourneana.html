Vernonia bourneana

Vernonia bourneana, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight stem and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. It is known for its hard, durable wood, which has been used traditionally for tools and construction.

Vernonia bourneana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and preventing soil erosion.
