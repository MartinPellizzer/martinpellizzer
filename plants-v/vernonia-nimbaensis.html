Vernonia nimbaensis

Vernonia nimbaensis, commonly known as the Nimba vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Nimba Mountains in West Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in semi-arid to sub-humid environments. Its foliage is generally narrow and grass-like, with dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
