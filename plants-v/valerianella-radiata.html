Valerianella radiata

Valerianella radiata, commonly known as dwarf sorrel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in early spring. Valerianella radiata is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, shady environments.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also be found in wildflower meadows.
