Viola hirtipes var. lanata

Viola hirtipes var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shady garden beds.
