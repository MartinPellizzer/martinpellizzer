Vernonia platylepis

Vernonia platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Vernonia platylepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
