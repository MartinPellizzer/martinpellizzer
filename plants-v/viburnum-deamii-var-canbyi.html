Viburnum deamii var. canbyi

Viburnum deamii var. canbyi, commonly known as Canby's snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental berries and wildlife value, attracting birds and other animals.
