Viola alpestris subsp. polychroma

Viola alpestris subsp.

polychroma, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its delicate, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to cold and harsh environmental conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
