Vicia ochroleuca

Vicia ochroleuca, commonly known as yellow vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange pea-like flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Vicia ochroleuca is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
