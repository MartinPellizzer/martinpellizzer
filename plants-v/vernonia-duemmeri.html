Vernonia duemmeri

Vernonia duemmeri, commonly known as the desert ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Vernonia duemmeri plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
