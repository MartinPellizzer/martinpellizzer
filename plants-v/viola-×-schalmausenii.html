Viola × schalmausenii

Viola × schalmausenii, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in various colors such as purple, blue, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-30 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
