Valeriana plantaginea var. minor

Valeriana plantaginea var. minor, commonly known as lesser valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
