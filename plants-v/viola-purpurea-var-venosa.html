Viola purpurea var. venosa

Viola purpurea var. venosa, commonly known as the purple violet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in woodland areas and meadows. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

The varietal name 'venosa' refers to the distinctive veins on its leaves, which are more pronounced compared to other varieties.
