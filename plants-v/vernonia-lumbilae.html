Vernonia lumbilae

Vernonia lumbilae, commonly known as the coastal vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in coastal environments with high salt exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Vernonia lumbilae plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and provides habitat for various insects and birds.
