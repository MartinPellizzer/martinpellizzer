Vitis dapsilis

Vitis dapsilis, commonly known as the grapevine, is a flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend support structures. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into clusters of berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Vitis dapsilis is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in winemaking and as a food source.
