Vernonella platyseta

Vernonella platyseta, commonly known as the broad-leaved vervain, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. Its flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Vernonella platyseta is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
