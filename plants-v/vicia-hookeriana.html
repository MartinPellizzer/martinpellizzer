Vicia hookeriana

Vicia hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Vicia hookeriana is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically significant in soil enrichment and habitat restoration.
