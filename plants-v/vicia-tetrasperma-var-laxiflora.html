Vicia tetrasperma var. laxiflora

Vicia tetrasperma var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or violet. The plant produces small, hairy pods containing several oblong seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. Native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and soil stabilization.
