Verbena hastata var. pinnatifida

Verbena hastata var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
