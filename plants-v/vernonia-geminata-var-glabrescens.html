Vernonia geminata var. glabrescens

Vernonia geminata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth vernonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish brown flowers, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
