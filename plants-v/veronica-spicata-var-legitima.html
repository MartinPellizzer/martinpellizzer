Veronica spicata var. legitima

Veronica spicata var. legitima, commonly known as the upright speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
