Viola tricolor var. saxatilis

Viola tricolor var. saxatilis, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple, yellow, and white, often in a pattern resembling the traditional tricolor flag. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
