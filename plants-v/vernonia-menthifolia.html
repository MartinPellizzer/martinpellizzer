Vernonia menthifolia

Vernonia menthifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and fluffy white to pale blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia menthifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
