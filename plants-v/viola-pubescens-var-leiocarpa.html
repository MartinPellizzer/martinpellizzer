Viola pubescens var. leiocarpa

Viola pubescens var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white violets, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically range from white to pale purple in color. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
