Viola variegata f. nipponica

Viola variegata f. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with distinctive white or pale green veins. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
