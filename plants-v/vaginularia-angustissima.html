Vaginularia angustissima

Vaginularia angustissima, commonly known as the narrow-bodied siphonophore, is a colonial marine organism belonging to the phylum Ctenophora.

It forms long, transparent chains of repeating units called "nectophores," which are used for propulsion. The colony is typically less than 1 meter in length and lacks a true digestive system, relying on its prey-capturing structures. It is found in temperate and tropical coastal waters, where it plays a role in marine food webs.

Its delicate, translucent body makes it a challenging subject for observation and study.
