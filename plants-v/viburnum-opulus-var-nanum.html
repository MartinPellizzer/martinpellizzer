Viburnum opulus var. nanum

Viburnum opulus var. nanum, commonly known as dwarf ninebark, is a deciduous shrub native to northern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white, flat-topped flowers that appear in early summer. The plant produces red, persistent berries that provide food for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall.

Viburnum opulus var. nanum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
