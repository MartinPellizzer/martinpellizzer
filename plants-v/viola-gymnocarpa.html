Viola gymnocarpa

Viola gymnocarpa, commonly known as the naked-seeded violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America, particularly the eastern United States. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The fruit is a capsule that contains small, winged seeds, which aid in wind dispersal.
