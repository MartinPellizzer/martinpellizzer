Vernonia hexantha var. nivea

Vernonia hexantha var. nivea, commonly known as the white vernonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or open woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
