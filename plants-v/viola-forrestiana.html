Viola forrestiana

Viola forrestiana, commonly known as the Chinese violet, is a species of flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola forrestiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
