Villadia chihuahuensis

Villadia chihuahuensis, commonly known as the Chihuahua lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Chihuahua Desert in Mexico.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant is adapted to arid conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in dry environments. It blooms during the spring and early summer, adding a splash of color to its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
