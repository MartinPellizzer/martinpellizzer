Veronica fruticans subsp. nevadensis

Veronica fruticans subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada speedwell, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the western United States. It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native environment.
