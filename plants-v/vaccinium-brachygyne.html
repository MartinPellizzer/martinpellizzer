Vaccinium brachygyne

Vaccinium brachygyne, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are oval-shaped, leathery, and have a slight waxy coating.

Vaccinium brachygyne thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
