Vilfa liebmannii

Vilfa liebmannii, commonly known as the Liebmann's veldtgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to regrow from its rhizomes after grazing or fire.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
