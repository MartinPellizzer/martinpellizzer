Vanilla trigonocarpa

Vanilla trigonocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican vanilla or mountain vanilla, is a climbing vine native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a small, fleshy capsule, contains numerous seeds and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its aromatic qualities and has been utilized in various cultural and culinary practices.
