Vernonia graciliflora

Vernonia graciliflora, commonly known as slender-flowered ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to brownish-purple flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woods and along roadsides.

Vernonia graciliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
