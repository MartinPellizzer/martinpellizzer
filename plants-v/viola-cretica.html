Viola cretica

Viola cretica, commonly known as the crested violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are slender and upright.

Viola cretica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
