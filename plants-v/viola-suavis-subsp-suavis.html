Viola suavis subsp. suavis

Viola suavis subsp.

suavis, commonly known as sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

The leaves and flowers are often used in herbal remedies and as a source of essential oils.
