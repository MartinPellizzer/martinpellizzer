Viola arvensis var. arvatica

Viola arvensis var. arvatica, commonly known as the field violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and it produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of violet, purple, or white. It is characterized by its erect stem and is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various regions beyond its native range.
