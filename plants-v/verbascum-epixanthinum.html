Verbascum epixanthinum

Verbascum epixanthinum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in terminal spikes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and gradually become narrower toward the top of the plant.

Verbascum epixanthinum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
