Verbesina olivacea

Verbesina olivacea, commonly known as golden beggar's ticks, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems covered in fine, sticky hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from summer to fall.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
