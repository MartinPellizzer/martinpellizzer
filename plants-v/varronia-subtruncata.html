Varronia subtruncata

Varronia subtruncata, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It has smooth, dark brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture.

The tree is valued for its fragrant wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
