Vatica tonkinensis

Vatica tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.
