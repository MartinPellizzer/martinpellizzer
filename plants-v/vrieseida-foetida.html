Vrieseida foetida

Vrieseida foetida, commonly known as the stinking violets, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its strong, unpleasant odor, which is released when the leaves are crushed. Vrieseida foetida typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
