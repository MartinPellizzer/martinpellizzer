Viola gelida

Viola gelida, commonly known as the frozen violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions with low temperatures. Its flowers are typically pale purple or white, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Viola gelida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
