Viola vaginata

Viola vaginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola vaginata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
