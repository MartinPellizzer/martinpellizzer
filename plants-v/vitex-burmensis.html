Vitex burmensis

Vitex burmensis, commonly known as the Burmese chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The fruit is a dry, woody capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
