Valvaria integrifolia

Valvaria integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf valvaria, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Valvariaceae and is characterized by its large, serrated leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and forest edges. Its leaves are simple and entire, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Valvaria integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
