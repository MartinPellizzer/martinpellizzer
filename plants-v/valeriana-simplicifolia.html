Valeriana simplicifolia

Valeriana simplicifolia, commonly known as sweet valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has aromatic, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots contain volatile oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its sedative properties.

Valeriana simplicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.
