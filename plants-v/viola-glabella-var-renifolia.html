Viola glabella var. renifolia

Viola glabella var. renifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate purple or violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
