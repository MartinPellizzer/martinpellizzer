Veronica sibirica var. yezoensis

Veronica sibirica var. yezoensis, commonly known as the Yezo speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Far East of Russia and parts of Japan.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
