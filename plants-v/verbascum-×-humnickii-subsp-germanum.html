Verbascum × humnickii subsp. germanum

Verbascum × humnickii subsp.

germanum, commonly known as the German mullein, is a hybrid plant in the genus Verbascum, native to Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its fibrous roots and robust growth habit make it adaptable to various garden environments.
