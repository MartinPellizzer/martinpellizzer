Vitex villosissima

Vitex villosissima, commonly known as the hairy verbena, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Vitex villosissima is also valued for its adaptability and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
