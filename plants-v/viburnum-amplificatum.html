Viburnum amplificatum

Viburnum amplificatum, commonly known as the Canadian arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clustered inflorescences during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum amplificatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal interest and wildlife value.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
