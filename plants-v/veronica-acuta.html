Veronica acuta

Veronica acuta, commonly known as the acute speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are blue or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Veronica acuta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
