Viguiera poeppigii

Viguiera poeppigii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its showy, purple-pink flower heads bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viguiera poeppigii is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
