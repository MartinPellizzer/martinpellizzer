Verbascum cotoneum

Verbascum cotoneum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, soft grayish-white hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a thick layer of woolly hairs.

Verbascum cotoneum is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
