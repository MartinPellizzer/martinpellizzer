Veronica trisepala

Veronica trisepala, commonly known as the three-sepal speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Veronica trisepala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
