Valeriana rigida var. tenuifolia

Valeriana rigida var. tenuifolia, commonly known as slender valerian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features aromatic, often pinkish or white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically opposite or whorled along the stems. Its roots are rich in volatile oils and have been used traditionally for their sedative properties.

Valeriana rigida var. tenuifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
