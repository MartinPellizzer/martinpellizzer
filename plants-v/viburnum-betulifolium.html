Viburnum betulifolium

Viburnum betulifolium, commonly known as mountain cranberrybush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often reddish-brown when young and turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Viburnum betulifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
