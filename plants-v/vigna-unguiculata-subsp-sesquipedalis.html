Vigna unguiculata subsp. sesquipedalis

Vigna unguiculata subsp.

sesquipedalis, commonly known as the giant cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and elongated, edible pods that contain numerous seeds.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and essential nutrients.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
