Vernonia vindex

Vernonia vindex, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces fluffy, seed-filled structures called pappus that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
