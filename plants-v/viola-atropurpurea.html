Viola atropurpurea

Viola atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a purple tinge. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Viola atropurpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in woodland gardens.
