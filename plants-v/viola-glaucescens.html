Viola glaucescens

Viola glaucescens, commonly known as the blue violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Viola glaucescens is also noted for its ability to thrive in cool climates and its preference for well-drained soil.
