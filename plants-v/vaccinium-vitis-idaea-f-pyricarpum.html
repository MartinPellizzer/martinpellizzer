Vaccinium vitis-idaea f. pyricarpum

Vaccinium vitis-idaea f. pyricarpum, commonly known as the fireberry cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to boreal and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This form is distinguished by its fiery red berries, which contrast with the typical dark blue or black berries of the species.
