Vernonia calvoana subsp. ulugurensis

Vernonia calvoana subsp.

ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
