Veronica spicata var. integrifolia

Veronica spicata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the upright speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, spike-like clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and entire, giving it a clean, refined appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
