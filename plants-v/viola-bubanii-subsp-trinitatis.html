Viola bubanii subsp. trinitatis

Viola bubanii subsp.

trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidad violet, is a perennial herb native to the island of Trinidad. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces violet flowers with purple petals and yellow markings, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
