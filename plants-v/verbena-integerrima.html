Verbena integerrima

Verbena integerrima, commonly known as the rough verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Verbena integerrima is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
