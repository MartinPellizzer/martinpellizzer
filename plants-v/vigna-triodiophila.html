Vigna triodiophila

Vigna triodiophila, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Vigna triodiophila is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, improving soil fertility.
