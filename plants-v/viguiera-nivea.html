Viguiera nivea

Viguiera nivea, commonly known as white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its white to pale purple flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viguiera nivea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun.
