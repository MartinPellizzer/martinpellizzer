Viola frusinatae

Viola frusinatae, commonly known as the Frusina violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Western Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola frusinatae is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
