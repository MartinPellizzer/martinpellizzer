Viola sandrasea subsp. sandrasea

Viola sandrasea subsp.

sandrasea, commonly known as the sandrasea violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to specific regions in southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, preferring well-drained environments.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or purple. It blooms during the spring and early summer, adding a touch of color to its natural habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
