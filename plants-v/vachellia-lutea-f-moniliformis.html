Vachellia lutea f. moniliformis

Vachellia lutea f. moniliformis, commonly known as the golden acacia or yellow thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often shed during dry periods. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Vachellia lutea f. moniliformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
