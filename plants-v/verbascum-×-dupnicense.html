Verbascum × dupnicense

Verbascum × dupnicense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
