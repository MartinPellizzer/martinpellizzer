Verbascum × silifkense

Verbascum × silifkense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their fuzzy, woolly leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The hybrid exhibits characteristics of its parent species, often displaying large, heart-shaped leaves and tall flowering stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
