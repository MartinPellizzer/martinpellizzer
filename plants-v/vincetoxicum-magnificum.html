Vincetoxicum magnificum

Vincetoxicum magnificum, commonly known as the showy elder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces clusters of small, black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Vincetoxicum magnificum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
