Viola barkalovii

Viola barkalovii, commonly known as the Barkalov's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and southern Russia. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
