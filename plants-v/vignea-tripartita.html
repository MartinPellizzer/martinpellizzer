Vignea tripartita

Vignea tripartita, commonly known as the three-part vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Vignea tripartita thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
