Verticordia nobilis

Verticordia nobilis, commonly known as the noble featherflower, is an evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its striking, feather-like inflorescences. The plant produces vivid pink to red flowers during the winter months, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Verticordia nobilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
