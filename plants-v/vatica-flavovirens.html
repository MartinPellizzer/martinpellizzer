Vatica flavovirens

Vatica flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green vatica, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its timber and ecological role in forest ecosystems.

The species is also noted for its potential in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage.
