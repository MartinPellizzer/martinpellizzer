Verbena phlogiflora f. alba

Verbena phlogiflora f. alba, commonly known as the white verbena, is a flowering plant characterized by its showy, clustered blooms.

It is a hybrid form of Verbena phlogiflora, known for its pale to white flowers that contrast with its silver-green foliage. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in full sun and well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to coastal and dry garden settings.
