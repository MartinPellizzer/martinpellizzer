Vitis megabotrya

Vitis megabotrya, commonly known as the giant grapevine, is a rare and woody vine native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and its ability to produce clusters of large, sweet, and edible berries. The plant is typically found in temperate climates with well-drained soil and ample sunlight. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential for cultivation in specialty horticulture.

Vitis megabotrya is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts due to its limited distribution and distinctive features.
