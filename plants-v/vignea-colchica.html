Vignea colchica

Vignea colchica, commonly known as the Colchicum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often with a waxy coating.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native habitat.
