Vilfa scoparia

Vilfa scoparia, commonly known as the common wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often covered in a powdery coating. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

Vilfa scoparia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
