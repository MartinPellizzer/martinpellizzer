Vicia cracca var. kitaibeliana

Vicia cracca var. kitaibeliana, commonly known as the red clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and purple or pink flower heads that bloom in early summer. The plant has a robust root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in a variety of soils but prefers well-drained, loamy ground.

This variety is often cultivated for forage and as a cover crop due to its hardy nature and ecological benefits.
