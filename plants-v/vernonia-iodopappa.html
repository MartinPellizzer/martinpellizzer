Vernonia iodopappa

Vernonia iodopappa, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically grows in coastal and dry forest habitats, often on rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow to orange in color.

Vernonia iodopappa plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing food and shelter for various native insects and birds.
