Valerianoides orubica

Valerianoides orubica, commonly known as the golden valerian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces tiny, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Valerianoides orubica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
