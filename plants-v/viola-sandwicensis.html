Viola sandwicensis

Viola sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian violet, is a rare flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream banks. It has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is adapted to the unique climate of Hawaii.

Due to habitat loss and invasive species, it is considered endangered and is protected under conservation efforts.
