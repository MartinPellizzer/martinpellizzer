Vanilla bahiana

Vanilla bahiana, commonly known as the Brazilian vanilla, is a climbing orchid native to Brazil.

It produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by dark brown pods containing the vanilla beans. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and partial shade. Its vanilla pods are used in perfumes, food, and traditional medicine.

The botanical characteristics of Vanilla bahiana make it a valuable species in both horticulture and commercial applications.
