Vignea pachystylis

Vignea pachystylis, commonly known as the broad-leafed vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its fruits are elongated pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vignea pachystylis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
