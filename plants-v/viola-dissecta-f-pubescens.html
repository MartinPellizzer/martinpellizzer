Viola dissecta f. pubescens

Viola dissecta f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The flowers are typically violet-blue in color, with five petals and a dark purple or brownish throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
