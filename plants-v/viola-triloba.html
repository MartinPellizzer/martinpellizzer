Viola triloba

Viola triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, typically varying in color from dark green to reddish purple. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of purple, violet, or white, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Viola triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
