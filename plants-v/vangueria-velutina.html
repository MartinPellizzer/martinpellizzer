Vangueria velutina

Vangueria velutina, commonly known as the velvet vangueria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, dark green foliage with a slightly velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Vangueria velutina is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
