Viola striata var. lutescens

Viola striata var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed violet, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its yellowish stems and violet or purple petals make it visually distinct from other viola species.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
