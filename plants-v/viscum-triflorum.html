Viscum triflorum

Viscum triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southern United States.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, sticky berries that are a source of food for birds and other wildlife. Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis.

Viscum triflorum is often found in forested areas and is an important part of the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
