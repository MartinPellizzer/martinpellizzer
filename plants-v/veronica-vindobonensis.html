Veronica vindobonensis

Veronica vindobonensis, commonly known as the Viennese speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Veronica vindobonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
