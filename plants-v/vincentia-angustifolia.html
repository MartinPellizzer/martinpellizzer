Vincentia angustifolia

Vincentia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed vining plant, is a flowering species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in clusters. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and greenhouses.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
