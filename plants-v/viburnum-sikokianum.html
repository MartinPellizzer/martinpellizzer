Viburnum sikokianum

Viburnum sikokianum, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters and prefers well-drained soil in partial shade.

Viburnum sikokianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
