Vernonia scorpioides var. cincta

Vernonia scorpioides var. cincta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and in wetlands, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a distinctive silver or grayish-white underside, while its flowers are typically pink to purple in color.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in wet conditions, making it a valuable species in wetland restoration efforts.
