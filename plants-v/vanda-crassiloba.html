Vanda crassiloba

Vanda crassiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaf vanda, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Vanda crassiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
