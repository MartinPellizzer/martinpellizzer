Verbascum × fragriforme

Verbascum × fragriforme, commonly known as the fragrant mullein, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its common name reflects the pleasant, spicy scent that emanates from its leaves and flowers.
