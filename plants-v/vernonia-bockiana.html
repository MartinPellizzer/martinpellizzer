Vernonia bockiana

Vernonia bockiana, commonly known as the Bock's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands, and it thrives in partial to full sunlight. Vernonia bockiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
