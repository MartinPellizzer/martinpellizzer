Viola wiedemannii

Viola wiedemannii, commonly known as the Wiedemann's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Viola.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in cool, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Viola wiedemannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its resilience in moist, well-drained soils.
