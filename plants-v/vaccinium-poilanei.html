Vaccinium poilanei

Vaccinium poilanei, commonly known as the Polian's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. Its berries, which are typically blue or purple when mature, are edible and have a tart flavor.

Vaccinium poilanei is often found in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments.
