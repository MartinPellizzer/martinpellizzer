Vernonia harperi

Vernonia harperi, commonly known as the ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia harperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
