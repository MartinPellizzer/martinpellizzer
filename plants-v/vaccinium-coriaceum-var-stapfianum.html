Vaccinium coriaceum var. stapfianum

Vaccinium coriaceum var. stapfianum, commonly known as the Stapfian huckleberry, is a variant of the huckleberry plant native to high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapted to cold and harsh conditions. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are evergreen, with a leathery texture, and it has a compact growth habit.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain and supporting local wildlife.
