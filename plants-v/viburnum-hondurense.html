Viburnum hondurense

Viburnum hondurense, commonly known as the Honduran viburnum, is a species native to Central America, particularly Honduras.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are typically white or pale green, appearing in the spring.

The fruit is a dark blue to black drupe, which is edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals.
