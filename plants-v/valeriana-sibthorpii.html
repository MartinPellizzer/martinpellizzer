Valeriana sibthorpii

Valeriana sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorp's valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are opposite and often have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

Valeriana sibthorpii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
