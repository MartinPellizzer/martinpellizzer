Veratrum intermedium

Veratrum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, crowned with clusters of white to pale green, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Its roots are thick and fleshy, containing toxic alkaloids such as veratridine and cevadine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.
