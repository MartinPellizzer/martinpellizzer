Vicia unijuga var. trifoliolata

Vicia unijuga var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf vetch, is a variant of the common vetch species.

It is a herbaceous annual plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides.

This variety is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
