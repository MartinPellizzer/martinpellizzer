Vigna debanensis

Vigna debanensis, commonly known as the Debane's cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein. Vigna debanensis thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
