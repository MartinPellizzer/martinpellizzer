Verbena litoralis f. congesta

Verbena litoralis f. congesta, commonly known as the coastal verbena or sea verbena, is a compact, flowering plant native to coastal regions.

It features small, vibrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand salt spray and wind.
