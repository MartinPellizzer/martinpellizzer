Vaccinium dictyoneuron var. oreophilum

Vaccinium dictyoneuron var. oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain blueberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
