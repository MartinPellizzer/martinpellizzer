Vernonia suaveolens var. hirsuta

Vernonia suaveolens var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable plant for pollinators and wildlife.
