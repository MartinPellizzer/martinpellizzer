Vulpia attenuata

Vulpia attenuata, commonly known as slender veldtgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and grasslands. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soils and its tolerance to drought conditions.

Vulpia attenuata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
