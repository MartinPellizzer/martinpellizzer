Viola patrinii var. angustifolia

Viola patrinii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy blooms.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
