Viola glacialis

Viola glacialis, commonly known as the glacier violet, is a hardy perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe and North America.

It thrives in cold, moist environments and is typically found in rocky soils at high elevations. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces showy, violet or purple flowers with five petals. It is well adapted to harsh climates, exhibiting frost resistance and a short growing season.

Viola glacialis plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
