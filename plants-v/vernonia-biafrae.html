Vernonia biafrae

Vernonia biafrae, commonly known as the Nigerian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Vernonia biafrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
