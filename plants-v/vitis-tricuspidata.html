Vitis tricuspidata

Vitis tricuspidata, commonly known as the Japanese grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its ability to climb using tendrils. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Vitis tricuspidata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
