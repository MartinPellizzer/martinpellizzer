Valeriana locusta var. olitoria

Valeriana locusta var. olitoria, commonly known as water pepper, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers and its distinctive, peppery scent. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Valeriana locusta var. olitoria has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
