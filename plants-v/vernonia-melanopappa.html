Vernonia melanopappa

Vernonia melanopappa, commonly known as ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, dark purple to brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Vernonia melanopappa is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
