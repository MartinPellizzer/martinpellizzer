Vincetoxicum pectinatum

Vincetoxicum pectinatum, commonly known as the pectinatum honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Botanically, it is characterized by its hairy stems and showy floral display, making it a notable ornamental plant.
