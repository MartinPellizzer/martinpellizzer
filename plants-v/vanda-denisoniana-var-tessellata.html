Vanda denisoniana var. tessellata

Vanda denisoniana var. tessellata, commonly known as the tessellated vanda, is a striking orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters of 4 to 8. The plant has thick, fleshy roots that help it store water in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

This variety is prized for its vibrant colors and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
