Viola kitaibeliana var. beirensis

Viola kitaibeliana var. beirensis, commonly known as the Beirensis Violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and delicate, spurred petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its flowers, which range in color from purple to white, bloom in early spring, adding a subtle beauty to its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
