Viola nephrophylla

Viola nephrophylla, commonly known as the kidneyleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features heart-shaped, kidney-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are violet-blue and have five petals, with a distinctive spur at the base.

Viola nephrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
