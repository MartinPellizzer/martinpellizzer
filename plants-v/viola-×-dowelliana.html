Viola × dowelliana

Viola × dowelliana, commonly known as the Downey violet, is a hybrid perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of violet, purple, and white, often with a yellow center. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Viola × dowelliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
