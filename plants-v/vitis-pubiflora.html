Vitis pubiflora

Vitis pubiflora, commonly known as the pubescent grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, wooded habitats. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible berries.

Vitis pubiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in horticulture and conservation efforts.
