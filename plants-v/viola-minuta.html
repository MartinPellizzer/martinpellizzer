Viola minuta

Viola minuta, commonly known as the small violet, is a delicate annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and thrives in moist, shady environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola minuta is often found in woodland areas and along the edges of streams.
