Viola tricolor f. sublutescens

Viola tricolor f. sublutescens, commonly known as the white-flowered viola, is a cultivar of the common violet.

It is characterized by its pale yellow, white, and violet petals, which create a striking contrast. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-20 cm, with heart-shaped leaves that are velvety in texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is particularly noted for its delicate appearance and ability to bloom in early spring.
