Viola hirta subsp. odorata

Viola hirta subsp.

odorata, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers with a distinctive fragrance.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

The subspecies is distinguished by its stronger scent compared to other viola species.
