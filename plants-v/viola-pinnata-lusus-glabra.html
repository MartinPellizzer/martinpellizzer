Viola pinnata lusus glabra

Viola pinnata lusus glabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and slightly hairy, with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring, often with five petals arranged symmetrically. Native to parts of North America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
