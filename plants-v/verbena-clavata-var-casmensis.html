Verbena clavata var. casmensis

Verbena clavata var. casmensis, commonly known as the Casmen's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces spikes of small, showy flowers in shades of violet and blue. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
