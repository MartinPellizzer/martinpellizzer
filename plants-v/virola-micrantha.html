Virola micrantha

Virola micrantha, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the early morning. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, winged seeds.

Virola micrantha plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
