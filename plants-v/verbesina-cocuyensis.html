Verbesina cocuyensis

Verbesina cocuyensis, commonly known as the Cocuy Mountain ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Verbesina cocuyensis is notable for its ornamental value and potential ecological importance in its native ecosystem.
