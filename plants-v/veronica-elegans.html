Veronica elegans

Veronica elegans, commonly known as the slender speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy blue or purple flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Veronica elegans is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
