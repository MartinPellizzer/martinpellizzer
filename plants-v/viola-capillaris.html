Viola capillaris

Viola capillaris, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and violet. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
