Vaccinium varingiifolium var. erythrinum

Vaccinium varingiifolium var. erythrinum, commonly known as the red-leaved huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in color during the fall. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
