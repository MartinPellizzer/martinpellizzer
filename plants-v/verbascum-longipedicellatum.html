Verbascum longipedicellatum

Verbascum longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stalked mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Verbascum longipedicellatum is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for respiratory ailments.
