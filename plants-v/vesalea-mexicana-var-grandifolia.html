Vesalea mexicana var. grandifolia

Vesalea mexicana var. grandifolia, commonly known as the Mexican vesalea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like blooms with yellow disc florets surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in gardens and botanical collections.
