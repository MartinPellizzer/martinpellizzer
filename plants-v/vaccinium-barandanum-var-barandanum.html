Vaccinium barandanum var. barandanum

Vaccinium barandanum var. barandanum, commonly known as the Barandane blackberry, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slight pubescence.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it is less commonly cultivated than other Vaccinium species.
