Veronica agrestis

Veronica agrestis, commonly known as common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Veronica agrestis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes.
