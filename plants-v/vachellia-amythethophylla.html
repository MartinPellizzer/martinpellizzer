Vachellia amythethophylla

Vachellia amythethophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and compound leaves that are typically narrow and feathery. The plant produces small, yellow, spherical flowers that are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and along coastal areas.

The bark and leaves have been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a source of tannins and in local medicine.
