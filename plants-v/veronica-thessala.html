Veronica thessala

Veronica thessala, commonly known as the Greek speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are borne in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Veronica thessala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
