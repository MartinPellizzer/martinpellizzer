Viola crassa var. vegeta

Viola crassa var. vegeta, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy violet flowers, usually ranging from purple to blue, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the back. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
