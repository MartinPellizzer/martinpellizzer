Valeriana officinalis var. albiflora

Valeriana officinalis var. albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower valerian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly medicinal scent and its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces pinkish-brown fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
