Viburnum plicatum f. roseum

Viburnum plicatum f. roseum, commonly known as the laceleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, pale green to white-variegated leaves that create a striking visual effect. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Viburnum plicatum f. roseum is valued for its ornamental foliage and seasonal interest in garden landscapes.
