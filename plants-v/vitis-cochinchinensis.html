Vitis cochinchinensis

Vitis cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese grapevine, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, green berries which can turn red or black when mature. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and tendrils that help it climb. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and can also be fermented into beverages.
