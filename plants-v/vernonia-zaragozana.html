Vernonia zaragozana

Vernonia zaragozana, commonly known as Zaragoza ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to reddish-brown, attracting various pollinators.

Vernonia zaragozana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
