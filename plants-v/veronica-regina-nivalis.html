Veronica regina-nivalis

Veronica regina-nivalis, commonly known as the snow queen speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Veronica regina-nivalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom in early spring.
