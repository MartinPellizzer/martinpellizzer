Viola hirta subsp. sepincola

Viola hirta subsp.

sepincola, commonly known as the Sierra foothill violet, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions, often in open woodlands or meadows.

The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals. It is adapted to cool, moist environments and blooms in early spring.

Its subspecies status reflects its distinct ecological and morphological characteristics compared to other viola species.
