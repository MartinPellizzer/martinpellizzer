Verbena rosulata

Verbena rosulata, commonly known as the roseleaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright blue or purple flowers. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Verbena rosulata is known for its long blooming period, typically from spring to early autumn.
