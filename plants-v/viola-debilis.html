Viola debilis

Viola debilis, commonly known as the slender violet, is a delicate flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Viola debilis has a short life cycle, often blooming in early spring before setting seed.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
