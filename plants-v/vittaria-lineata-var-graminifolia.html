Vittaria lineata var. graminifolia

Vittaria lineata var. graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy fern, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Vittariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, slender fronds that resemble grass. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores for propagation.

The foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
