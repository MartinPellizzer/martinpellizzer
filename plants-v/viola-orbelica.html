Viola orbelica

Viola orbelica, commonly known as the Iceland viola, is a perennial flowering plant native to Iceland and parts of northern Europe.

It produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and vibrant violet or blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subarctic regions. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Viola orbelica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
