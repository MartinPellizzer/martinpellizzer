Vitis rotundifolia

Vitis rotundifolia, commonly known as the muscadine grape, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are round or heart-shaped at the base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of dark purple to black berries. Its vines are vigorous climbers, often found growing along forest edges or in woodland areas.

The plant is notable for its high antioxidant content and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
