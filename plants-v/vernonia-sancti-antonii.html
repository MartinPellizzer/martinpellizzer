Vernonia sancti-antonii

Vernonia sancti-antonii, commonly known as the Saint Anthony's verbena, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia sancti-antonii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
