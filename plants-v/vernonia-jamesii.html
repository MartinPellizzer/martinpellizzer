Vernonia jamesii

Vernonia jamesii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Vernonia jamesii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
