Viola mistassinica

Viola mistassinica, commonly known as the Mistassini violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the boreal and subarctic regions of Canada and Alaska.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wet meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals. It is part of the Violaceae family, known for its ornamental and medicinal value.

Viola mistassinica plays an important ecological role by supporting pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
