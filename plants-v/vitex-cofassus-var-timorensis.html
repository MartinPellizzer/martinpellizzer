Vitex cofassus var. timorensis

Vitex cofassus var. timorensis, commonly known as the Timor vitex, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to the island of Timor and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles, with a distinctive purple or violet coloration. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in open woodland or along coastal areas.

The Timor vitex is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
