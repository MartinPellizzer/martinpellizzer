Viola cordata

Viola cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and velvety in texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of violet or white, often with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Viola cordata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
