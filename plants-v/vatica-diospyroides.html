Vatica diospyroides

Vatica diospyroides, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a broad, rounded canopy and smooth, greyish bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, often with a waxy surface.

Vatica diospyroides plays an important ecological role in its native forests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
