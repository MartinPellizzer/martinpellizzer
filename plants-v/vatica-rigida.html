Vatica rigida

Vatica rigida, commonly known as the hardy ironwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and sturdy, hardy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Vatica rigida is also notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
