Vochysia vismiifolia var. vismiifolia

Vochysia vismiifolia var. vismiifolia, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The tree produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
