Verbena gracilescens var. swiftiana

Verbena gracilescens var. swiftiana, commonly known as the Swift's verbena, is a compact, low-growing annual herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy texture, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically opposite along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to arid landscapes.
