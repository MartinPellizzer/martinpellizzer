Viola irinae

Viola irinae, commonly known as the Irina violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet. Viola irinae is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.
