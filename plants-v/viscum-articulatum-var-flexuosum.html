Viscum articulatum var. flexuosum

Viscum articulatum var. flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in temperate regions.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a source of food for birds and other wildlife. Its growth habit is unique, with a flexible, trailing stem that allows it to spread along branches.

This variety is distinguished by its more flexible branches compared to other mistletoe species.
