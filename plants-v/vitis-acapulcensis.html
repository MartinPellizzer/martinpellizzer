Vitis acapulcensis

Vitis acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to Mexico, specifically the states of Oaxaca and Chiapas, where it grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and other structures. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-white in color.

The fruit, which is a small, round berry, is edible and has a sweet, acidic flavor.
