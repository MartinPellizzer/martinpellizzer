Vimen myricoides

Vimen myricoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas or lowland rainforests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their essential oils.
