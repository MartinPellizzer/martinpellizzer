Viguiera guaranitica

Viguiera guaranitica, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habits. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Viguiera guaranitica produces clusters of small, purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
