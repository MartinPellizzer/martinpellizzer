Veronica kurdica var. incisocrenata

Veronica kurdica var. incisocrenata, commonly known as the Kurdish speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
