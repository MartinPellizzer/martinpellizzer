Vernonia albeomensae

Vernonia albeomensae, commonly known as the white vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Vernonia albeomensae is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological diversity of Hawaii's flora.
