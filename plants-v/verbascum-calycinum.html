Verbascum calycinum

Verbascum calycinum, commonly known as the rock mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often covered in soft, downy hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum calycinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
