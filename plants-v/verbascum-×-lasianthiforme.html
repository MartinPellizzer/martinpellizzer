Verbascum × lasianthiforme

Verbascum × lasianthiforme, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and soft, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
