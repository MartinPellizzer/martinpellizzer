Viburnum optatum var. vagum

Viburnum optatum var. vagum, commonly known as the rose-foliage viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its striking, glossy, rose-purple foliage that turns red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Viburnum optatum var. vagum is also valued for its adaptability and resistance to pests and diseases.
