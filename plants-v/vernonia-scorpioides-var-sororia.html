Vernonia scorpioides var. sororia

Vernonia scorpioides var. sororia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to golden-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture.

This species is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.
