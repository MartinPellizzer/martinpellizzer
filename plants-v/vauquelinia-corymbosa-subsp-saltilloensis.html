Vauquelinia corymbosa subsp. saltilloensis

Vauquelinia corymbosa subsp.

saltilloensis, commonly known as Saltillo hillbilly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small white flowers in the spring, which are followed by oval, dark blue to black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
