Veronica × tridentina

Veronica × tridentina, commonly known as the three-lobed speedwell, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with distinctive three-lobed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Veronica × tridentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
