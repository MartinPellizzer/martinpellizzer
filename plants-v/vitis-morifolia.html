Vitis morifolia

Vitis morifolia, commonly known as the Japanese grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnately divided, and its small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Vitis morifolia thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
