Vanillosma multiflora

Vanillosma multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora vanilla vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. These flowers are followed by small, red berries that contain the seeds.

Vanillosma multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
