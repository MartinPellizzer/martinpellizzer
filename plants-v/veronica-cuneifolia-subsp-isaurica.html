Veronica cuneifolia subsp. isaurica

Veronica cuneifolia subsp.

isaurica, commonly known as the Isaurian speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
