Vicia truncatula var. intermedia

Vicia truncatula var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate pea or narrowleaf vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used as forage for livestock.

The species is notable for its adaptability and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
