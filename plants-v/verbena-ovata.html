Verbena ovata

Verbena ovata, commonly known as beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Verbena ovata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize sandy soils and attract pollinators.
