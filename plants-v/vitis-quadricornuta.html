Vitis quadricornuta

Vitis quadricornuta, commonly known as the four-awned grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America, particularly the southeastern United States. The plant is characterized by its distinctive four-lobed leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that ripen to a dark purple color.

Vitis quadricornuta is often found growing in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
