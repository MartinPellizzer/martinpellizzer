Vulpia ciliata var. tripolitania

Vulpia ciliata var. tripolitania, commonly known as the Tripolitania hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture along the edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

This plant is notable for its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments.
