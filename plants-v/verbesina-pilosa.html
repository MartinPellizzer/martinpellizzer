Verbesina pilosa

Verbesina pilosa, commonly known as the hairy beggarticks, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution across various habitats.
