Vicia narbonensis var. platycarpos

Vicia narbonensis var. platycarpos, commonly known as the narrow-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This variety is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration efforts.
