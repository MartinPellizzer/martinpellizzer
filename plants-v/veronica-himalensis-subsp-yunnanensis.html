Veronica himalensis subsp. yunnanensis

Veronica himalensis subsp.

yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
