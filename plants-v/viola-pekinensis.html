Viola pekinensis

Viola pekinensis, commonly known as the Chinese violet, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola pekinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
