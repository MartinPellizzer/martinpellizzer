Vernonia rusbyi

Vernonia rusbyi, commonly known as Rusby's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia rusbyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
