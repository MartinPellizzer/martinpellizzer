Verbascum thapsus subsp. montanum

Verbascum thapsus subsp.

montanum, commonly known as mountain mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered with dense, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a loose panicle. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and also covered with a dense layer of soft hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
