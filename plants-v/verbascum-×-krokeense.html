Verbascum × krokeense

Verbascum × krokeense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes species known for their large, fuzzy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its fibrous leaves and medicinal properties have made it of interest in traditional herbal practices.
