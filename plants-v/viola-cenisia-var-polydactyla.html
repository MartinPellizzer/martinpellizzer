Viola cenisia var. polydactyla

Viola cenisia var. polydactyla, commonly known as the purple violet, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slight purple tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of violet and purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique coloration, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
