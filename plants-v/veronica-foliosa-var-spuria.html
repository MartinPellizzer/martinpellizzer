Veronica foliosa var. spuria

Veronica foliosa var. spuria, commonly known as the Spuried Speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
