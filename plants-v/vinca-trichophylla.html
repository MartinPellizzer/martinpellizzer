Vinca trichophylla

Vinca trichophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf periwinkle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, blue to violet flowers. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.

Vinca trichophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
