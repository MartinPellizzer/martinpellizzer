Valeriana barbulata var. gymnostoma

Valeriana barbulata var. gymnostoma, commonly known as the purple valerian, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant's leaves are opposite, with a rough, hairy texture, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in treating insomnia and anxiety.
