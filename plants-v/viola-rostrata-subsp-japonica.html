Viola rostrata subsp. japonica

Viola rostrata subsp.

japonica, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. It has a short stem with opposite leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly elongated calyx and broader petals compared to the typical Viola rostrata.
