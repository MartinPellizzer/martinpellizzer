Vernonia arachniolepis

Vernonia arachniolepis, commonly known as the silky Vernonia, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
