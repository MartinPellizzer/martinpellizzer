Veratrum taliense

Veratrum taliense, commonly known as Taliense Lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale blue flowers. The plant contains various alkaloids, making it toxic and potentially dangerous if ingested. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
