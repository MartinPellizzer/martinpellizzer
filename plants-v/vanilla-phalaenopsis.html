Vanilla phalaenopsis

Vanilla phalaenopsis, commonly known as the orchid butterfly, is a type of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces a unique vanilla-like aroma, which is a distinguishing feature of its blossoms. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight and requires well-draining potting media.

The orchid is popular as a houseplant due to its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
