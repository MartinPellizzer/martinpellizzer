Valeriana przewalskii

Valeriana przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's valerian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.
