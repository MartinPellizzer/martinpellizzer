Verbascum phlomoides var. ramosum

Verbascum phlomoides var. ramosum, commonly known as the branched mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green to brownish flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during late summer. Its leaves are large, broad, and gradually decrease in size upward the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.
