Vernonia colorata

Vernonia colorata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is known for its striking blue to purple coloration, which is particularly vivid in the fall. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Vernonia colorata is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
