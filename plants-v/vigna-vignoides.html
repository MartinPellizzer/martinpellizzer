Vigna vignoides

Vigna vignoides, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein. Vigna vignoides is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in various cuisines.

It also plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia.
