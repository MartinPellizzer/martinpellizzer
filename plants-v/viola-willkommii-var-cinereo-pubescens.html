Viola willkommii var. cinereo-pubescens

Viola willkommii var. cinereo-pubescens, commonly known as the silver-haired violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically grayish-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white throat, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Viola genus.
