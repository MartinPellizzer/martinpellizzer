Viola hondoensis

Viola hondoensis, commonly known as the Hondo violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Hondo in the Philippines.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and violet. Viola hondoensis is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
