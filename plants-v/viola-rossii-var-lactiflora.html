Viola rossii var. lactiflora

Viola rossii var. lactiflora, commonly known as the white violet, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
