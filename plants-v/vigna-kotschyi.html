Vigna kotschyi

Vigna kotschyi, commonly known as the desert cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, bean-like pods and trifoliate leaves, which are adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility. Vigna kotschyi is often used as a forage crop and has traditional uses in some local communities.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts in dry regions.
