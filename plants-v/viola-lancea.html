Viola lancea

Viola lancea, commonly known as the lanceleaf violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Viola lancea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
