Verbesina montevidensis

Verbesina montevidensis, commonly known as the Paraguay goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its showy inflorescences.
