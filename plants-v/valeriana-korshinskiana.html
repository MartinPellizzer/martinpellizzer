Valeriana korshinskiana

Valeriana korshinskiana, commonly known as the Siberian valerian, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia, particularly Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and contains essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Valeriana korshinskiana is valued for its calming effects and is often used in traditional medicine.
