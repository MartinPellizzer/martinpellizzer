Veronica multifida var. bihariensis

Veronica multifida var. bihariensis, commonly known as the Bihar Veronica, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variant is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
