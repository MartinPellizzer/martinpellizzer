Verbesina glabrata

Verbesina glabrata, commonly known as smooth beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered with fine, silky hairs.

The plant is known for its prickly seeds, which are dispersed by wind and can adhere to clothing and animals.
