Valeriana pugae

Valeriana pugae, commonly known as the pigweed valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. Valeriana pugae thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
