Valeriana pubicarpa

Valeriana pubicarpa, commonly known as the pubicarp valerian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that releases seeds when mature.
