Vernonanthura pinguis

Vernonanthura pinguis, commonly known as the fat vernon's rockrose, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has adapted to harsh, dry environments with its succulent leaves and deep root system. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils.

Vernonanthura pinguis is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
