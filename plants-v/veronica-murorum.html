Veronica murorum

Veronica murorum, commonly known as wall speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ability to thrive in harsh environments.

Veronica murorum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in difficult conditions.
