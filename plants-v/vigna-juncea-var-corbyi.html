Vigna juncea var. corbyi

Vigna juncea var. corbyi, commonly known as the dwarf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. Its trifoliate leaves and hairy stems are typical features of the Vigna genus.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
