Vignopsis lukafuensis

Vignopsis lukafuensis, commonly known as the Lukafu wild bean, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical forests and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

The seeds are enclosed in a pod and are an important food source for local wildlife.
