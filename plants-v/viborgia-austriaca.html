Viborgia austriaca

Viborgia austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Viborgia austriaca thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe.
