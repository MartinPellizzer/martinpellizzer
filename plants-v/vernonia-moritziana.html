Vernonia moritziana

Vernonia moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically blue to purple in color.

Vernonia moritziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
