Veronica riae

Veronica riae, commonly known as the Riae speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of soft, downy hair.

Veronica riae is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in well-drained, sunny environments.
