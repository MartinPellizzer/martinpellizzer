Vicia truncata var. villosa

Vicia truncata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is a perennial vine that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Vicia truncata var. villosa is also noted for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural and ecological restoration settings.
