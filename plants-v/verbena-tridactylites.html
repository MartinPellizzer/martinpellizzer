Verbena tridactylites

Verbena tridactylites, commonly known as the three-toed verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Verbena tridactylites is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
