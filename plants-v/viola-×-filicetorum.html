Viola × filicetorum

Viola × filicetorum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved violet, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Viola tricolor and Viola verecunda.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that resemble those of ivy, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of violet or purple flowers with five petals, typically in shades of violet, blue, or white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded gardens.

Viola × filicetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in horticulture for its attractive foliage and blooms.
