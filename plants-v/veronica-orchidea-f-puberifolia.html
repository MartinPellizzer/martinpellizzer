Veronica orchidea f. puberifolia

Veronica orchidea f. puberifolia, commonly known as the pubescent orchid veronica, is a hybrid plant that combines characteristics of both orchids and the genus Veronica.

It is a flowering plant native to regions with temperate climates, where it thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade. The plant features lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive pubescent texture. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the spring or early summer.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
