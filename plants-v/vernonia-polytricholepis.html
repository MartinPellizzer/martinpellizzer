Vernonia polytricholepis

Vernonia polytricholepis, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes whitish underside.

The showy purple to pink flower heads appear in late summer and attract various pollinators.
