Veronica miyabei

Veronica miyabei, commonly known as the Japanese speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often prostrate.

Veronica miyabei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
