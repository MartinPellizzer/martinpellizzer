Viburnum pubescens var. longifolium

Viburnum pubescens var. longifolium, commonly known as the snowbank viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy texture that gives it its scientific name.

Viburnum pubescens var. longifolium is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
