Valeriana tergemina

Valeriana tergemina, commonly known as the mountain valerian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often reddish or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, distinctive scent, while its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

Valeriana tergemina is known for its ability to bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to alpine landscapes.
