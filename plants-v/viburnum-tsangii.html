Viburnum tsangii

Viburnum tsangii, commonly known as the Chinese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. The plant is noted for its ornamental value, particularly its showy, red berries that appear in the fall. Viburnum tsangii prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

It is also valued for its ability to attract wildlife, including birds and insects.
