Vitis vinifera var. massiliensis

Vitis vinifera var. massiliensis, commonly known as the Massilian grape, is a rare and ancient variety of Vitis vinifera.

It is characterized by its small, thick-skinned berries and is primarily found in the Mediterranean region. The plant typically grows on vines with robust, woody stems and produces clusters of dark purple grapes. It is valued for its unique flavor profile and historical significance in viticulture.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized vineyards for its distinct characteristics.
