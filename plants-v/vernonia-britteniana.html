Vernonia britteniana

Vernonia britteniana, commonly known as the Britten's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit. The plant produces showy, purple to violet flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Vernonia britteniana is often found in prairie habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
