Valeriana septentrionalis

Valeriana septentrionalis, commonly known as blue valerian, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a sprawling, herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Valeriana septentrionalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
