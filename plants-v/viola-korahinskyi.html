Viola korahinskyi

Viola korahinskyi, commonly known as the Korahinsky violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, often in shades of purple or violet, and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Viola korahinskyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
