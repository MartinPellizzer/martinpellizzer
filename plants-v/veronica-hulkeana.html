Veronica hulkeana

Veronica hulkeana, commonly known as Hulke's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
