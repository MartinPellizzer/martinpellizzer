Vulpia sciuroides var. microstachya

Vulpia sciuroides var. microstachya, commonly known as the small-stalked vulpia, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing annual plant with slender, erect stems that often form dense mats. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.
