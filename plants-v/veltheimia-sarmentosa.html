Veltheimia sarmentosa

Veltheimia sarmentosa, commonly known as the Cape grass or velvet fern, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, cone-shaped inflorescences that bear small, pale green to white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in temperate climates.
