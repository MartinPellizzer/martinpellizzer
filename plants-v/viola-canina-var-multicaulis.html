Viola canina var. multicaulis

Viola canina var. multicaulis, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, giving it its common name. The plant produces violet flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
