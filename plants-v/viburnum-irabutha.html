Viburnum irabutha

Viburnum irabutha, commonly known as Japanese snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used ornamentally. Viburnum irabutha typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in partial to full sun.

Its foliage is dark green and turns reddish in the autumn, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
