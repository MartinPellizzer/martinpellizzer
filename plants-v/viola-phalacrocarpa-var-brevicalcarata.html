Viola phalacrocarpa var. brevicalcarata

Viola phalacrocarpa var. brevicalcarata, commonly known as the short-beaked violet, is a variant of the common violet found in specific regions of Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
