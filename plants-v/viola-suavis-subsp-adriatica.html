Viola suavis subsp. adriatica

Viola suavis subsp.

adriatica, commonly known as the Adriatic violet, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and blue. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its beauty in gardens.
