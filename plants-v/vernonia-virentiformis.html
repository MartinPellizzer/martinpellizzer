Vernonia virentiformis

Vernonia virentiformis, commonly known as the green-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Vernonia virentiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
