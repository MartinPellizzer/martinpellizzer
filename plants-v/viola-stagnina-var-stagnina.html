Viola stagnina var. stagnina

Viola stagnina var. stagnina, commonly known as the water violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically submerged or floating on the water surface. The plant produces small, delicate violet flowers with five petals, often in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshes and ponds.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and ecological health in its environment.
