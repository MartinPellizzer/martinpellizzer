Vincetoxicum apiculatum

Vincetoxicum apiculatum, commonly known as the red elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves that are typically alternate and bear small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters. The plant produces clusters of bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Vincetoxicum apiculatum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
