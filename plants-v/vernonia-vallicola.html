Vernonia vallicola

Vernonia vallicola, commonly known as the mountain ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant produces upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage, and it bears small, clustered purple to brownish-purple flower heads. Its flowers are typically visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant's resilience to harsh environmental conditions makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
