Verbascum erianthum

Verbascum erianthum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Verbascum erianthum is also known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
