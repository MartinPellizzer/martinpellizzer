Vigna unguiculata subsp. dekindtiana

Vigna unguiculata subsp.

dekindtiana, commonly known as the African cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical, hairy pods that contain edible seeds.

The plant thrives in warm climates and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for cultivation in arid areas. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The botanical features of this subspecies make it an important crop for both food security and agricultural sustainability.
