Veronica senanensis var. pubescens

Veronica senanensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy veronica, is a flowering plant native to parts of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
