Verbascum × binbogense

Verbascum × binbogense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum sinaiticum.

It is native to the Middle East and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
