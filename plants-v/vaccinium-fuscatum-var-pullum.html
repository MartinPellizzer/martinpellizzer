Vaccinium fuscatum var. pullum

Vaccinium fuscatum var. pullum, commonly known as the purple huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture and are covered in fine hairs.
