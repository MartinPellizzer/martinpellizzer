Verbena ribifolia f. longavina

Verbena ribifolia f. longavina, commonly known as the long-stemmed verbena, is a compact, flowering shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in spikes. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

This variety is a hardy perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
