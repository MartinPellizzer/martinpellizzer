Viola trinervata f. semialba

Viola trinervata f. semialba, commonly known as the white violet, is a botanical variant of the common violet.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple hues of the species. The plant is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Korea. It features heart-shaped leaves with three main veins, giving it the species name *trinervata*.

The foliage is typically dark green and covered in a fine layer of pubescence.
