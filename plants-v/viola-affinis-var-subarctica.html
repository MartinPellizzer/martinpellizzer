Viola affinis var. subarctica

Viola affinis var. subarctica, commonly known as the Arctic violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in tundra or mountainous environments. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, showy flowers that are usually violet or purple in color. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

The species is notable for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is often found in areas with limited vegetation.
