Viola heterophylla var. messanensis

Viola heterophylla var. messanensis, commonly known as the Messina violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that vary in size and shape depending on the growth conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, colorful plants.
