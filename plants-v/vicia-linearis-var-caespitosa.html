Vicia linearis var. caespitosa

Vicia linearis var. caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps, giving it a grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

Vicia linearis var. caespitosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges.
