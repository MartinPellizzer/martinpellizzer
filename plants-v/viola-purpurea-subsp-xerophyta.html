Viola purpurea subsp. xerophyta

Viola purpurea subsp.

xerophyta, commonly known as the purple violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its xerophytic adaptations.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for regions with limited water availability.
