Verbascum adenanthum

Verbascum adenanthum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mullein, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Verbascum adenanthum is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating respiratory ailments.
