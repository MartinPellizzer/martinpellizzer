Viola beckwithii subsp. glabrata

Viola beckwithii subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the smooth viol, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae. It is native to parts of East Asia, including China and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture, distinguishing it from other viola species. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
