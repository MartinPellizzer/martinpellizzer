Vernonia asteriflora var. mollissima

Vernonia asteriflora var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky vernonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
