Vernonia crassinervia

Vernonia crassinervia, commonly known as the broad-veined ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Vernonia crassinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a host plant for several native butterflies.
