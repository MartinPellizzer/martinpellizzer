Vulpia persica

Vulpia persica, commonly known as Persian vulpia, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Vulpia persica is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.
