Vanda simondii

Vanda simondii, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, often in shades of pink, white, and yellow. The plant has long, leathery leaves and produces aerial roots that help it attach to trees or rocks. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Vanda simondii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its dramatic floral displays.
