Vaccinium acuminatissimum var. singalense

Vaccinium acuminatissimum var. singalense, commonly known as the Singalense blueberry, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, acidic soils. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and dark green color.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its potential use in horticulture and food production.
