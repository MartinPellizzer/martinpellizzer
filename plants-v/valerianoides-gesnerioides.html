Valerianoides gesnerioides

Valerianoides gesnerioides, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and attractive, heart-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.
