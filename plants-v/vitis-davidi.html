Vitis davidi

Vitis davidi, commonly known as the David's grape, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its vigorous growth and climbing habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its berries are usually small and black when ripe, containing several seeds.

Vitis davidi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
