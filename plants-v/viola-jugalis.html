Viola jugalis

Viola jugalis, commonly known as the violet vernal, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully develop.

Viola jugalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
