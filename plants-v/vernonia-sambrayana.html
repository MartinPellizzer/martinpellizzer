Vernonia sambrayana

Vernonia sambrayana, commonly known as the Sambray goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Vernonia sambrayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
