Viola joergensenii

Viola joergensenii, commonly known as the Jorgensen's violet, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and showy violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in some regions.
