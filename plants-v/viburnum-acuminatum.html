Viburnum acuminatum

Viburnum acuminatum, commonly known as the arrowwood viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring dense, upright branches and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum acuminatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.

It also provides food and shelter for various wildlife species.
