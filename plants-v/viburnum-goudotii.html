Viburnum goudotii

Viburnum goudotii, commonly known as the Goudot viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Viburnum goudotii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
