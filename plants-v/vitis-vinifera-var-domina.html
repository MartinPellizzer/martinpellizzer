Vitis vinifera var. domina

Vitis vinifera var. domina, commonly known as the Dominica grape, is a less commonly cultivated variety of the European wine grape.

It is characterized by its small, dark-colored berries and a distinctive, aromatic profile that includes notes of spice and earth. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its vines are vigorous and can produce fruit in their second year, making it a promising candidate for both viticultural and culinary applications.

Due to its unique flavor and potential for adaptation, it is gaining interest among growers and sommeliers alike.
