Viola hirta var. collina

Viola hirta var. collina, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The flowers are violet to purple in hue, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variant is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and is often found in the eastern United States.
