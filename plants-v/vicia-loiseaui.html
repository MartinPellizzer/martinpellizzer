Vicia loiseaui

Vicia loiseaui, commonly known as the Loiseleur's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, slender pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in rocky and arid environments.

Vicia loiseaui is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
