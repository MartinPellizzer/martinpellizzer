Viola orientalis subsp. xanthopetala

Viola orientalis subsp.

xanthopetala, commonly known as the yellow violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive violet throat, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
