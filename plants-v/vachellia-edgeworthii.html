Vachellia edgeworthii

Vachellia edgeworthii, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa, particularly found in regions like Kenya and Tanzania.

The plant is recognized for its distinctive yellow flowers and spiny, thorn-like branches, which provide protection from herbivores. Its small, leathery leaves are alternate and often used in traditional medicine.

Vachellia edgeworthii plays an important ecological role in its habitat, contributing to soil stabilization and providing shelter for various wildlife species.
