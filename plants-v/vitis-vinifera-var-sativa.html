Vitis vinifera var. sativa

Vitis vinifera var. sativa, commonly known as the common grapevine, is a deciduous vine native to the temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, sweet berries that are used in winemaking and as table grapes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a climate with distinct seasons for optimal growth. Its deep root system allows it to access moisture from deeper soil layers, enhancing its resilience.

The berries undergo complex biochemical changes during ripening, contributing to the diverse flavors found in wine and other grape products.
