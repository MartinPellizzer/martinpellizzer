Vicia sativa subvar. onubensis

Vicia sativa subvar. onubensis, commonly known as the Onubian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment and sustainable agriculture.
