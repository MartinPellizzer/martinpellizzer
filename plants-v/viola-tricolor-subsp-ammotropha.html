Viola tricolor subsp. ammotropha

Viola tricolor subsp.

ammotropha, commonly known as the violet scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive violet, white, and yellow coloration.

The flowers are typically violet or purple, with a yellow center, and are borne on tall, slender stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and wildflower habitats.
