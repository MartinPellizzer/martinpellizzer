Vigna carinalis

Vigna carinalis, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and vigorous growth habit, often reaching heights of several meters. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Vigna carinalis is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
