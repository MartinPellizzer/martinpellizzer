Vicia dubia

Vicia dubia, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender, trailing stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. Vicia dubia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in ecological succession and soil enrichment.
