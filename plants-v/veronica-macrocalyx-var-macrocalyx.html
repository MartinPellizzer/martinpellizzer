Veronica macrocalyx var. macrocalyx

Veronica macrocalyx var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright stems that bear opposite pairs of ovate to lance-shaped leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
