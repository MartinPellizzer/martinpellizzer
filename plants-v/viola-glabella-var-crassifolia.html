Viola glabella var. crassifolia

Viola glabella var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet or purple, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are well adapted to cool climates and are often used in garden settings for their ornamental value.
