Veronica mollissima

Veronica mollissima, commonly known as softleaf speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are soft, velvety, and often covered in fine hairs, which gives it its common name.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
