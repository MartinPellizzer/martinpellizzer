Viguiera macrocalyx

Viguiera macrocalyx, commonly known as the Mexican honeyflower, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its roots are fibrous and deep. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
