Valeriana columbiana

Valeriana columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features aromatic, yellowish-green leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds responsible for its characteristic scent.

Valeriana columbiana is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
