Verticordia verticordina

Verticordia verticordina, commonly known as the red-stemmed featherflower, is a striking member of the Myrtaceae family native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, feather-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in heathlands and coastal regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
