Viola canina var. caespitosa

Viola canina var. caespitosa, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a low-growing perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces violet flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. This variety is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, forming dense mats of foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
