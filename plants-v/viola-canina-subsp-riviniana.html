Viola canina subsp. riviniana

Viola canina subsp.

riviniana, commonly known as the river viola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and violet or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as riverbanks and woodland edges. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and is often found in calcareous soils.

Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
