Viola raddeana

Viola raddeana, commonly known as the Siberian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy violet or purple flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white marking at the base. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested or alpine habitats.

Viola raddeana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its delicate appearance.
