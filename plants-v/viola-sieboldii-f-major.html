Viola sieboldii f. major

Viola sieboldii f. major, commonly known as the Japanese pansy, is a cultivar of the viola genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including violet, white, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-20 cm and produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool climates and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate regions.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
