Vernonia violacea

Vernonia violacea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine, soft hairs.

Vernonia violacea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
