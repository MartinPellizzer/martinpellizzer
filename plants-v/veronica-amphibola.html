Veronica amphibola

Veronica amphibola, commonly known as the water speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue or purple flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Veronica amphibola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
