Verbascum × kalabakense

Verbascum × kalabakense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to regions in the Mediterranean and has been cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in drought-tolerant plants.
