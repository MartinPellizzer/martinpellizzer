Viola pubipetala

Viola pubipetala, commonly known as the pubescent violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and blue. Its blossoms typically appear in early spring, adding a delicate touch to woodland gardens.

Viola pubipetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
