Viola × jindoensis

Viola × jindoensis, commonly known as the Korean violet, is a hybrid species native to Korea.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Viola × jindoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
