Vaccinium microphyllum f. langeanum

Vaccinium microphyllum f. langeanum, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a compact shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by berries that range in color from blue to red. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
