Viburnum macdougallii

Viburnum macdougallii, commonly known as MacDougall's viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

Viburnum macdougallii is valued in gardens for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
