Veronica triphyllos var. collina

Veronica triphyllos var. collina, commonly known as the mountain speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plantings.
