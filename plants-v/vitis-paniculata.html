Vitis paniculata

Vitis paniculata, commonly known as the fragrant grapevines, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and wines. Vitis paniculata is typically found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.

It is a hardy species that can thrive in a variety of soil types and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
