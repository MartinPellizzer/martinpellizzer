Vicia ludoviciana var. ludoviciana

Vicia ludoviciana var. ludoviciana, commonly known as the Louisiana vetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear trifoliate leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in elongated, drooping clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
