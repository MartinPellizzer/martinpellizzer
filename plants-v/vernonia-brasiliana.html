Vernonia brasiliana

Vernonia brasiliana, commonly known as Brazilian ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Vernonia brasiliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
