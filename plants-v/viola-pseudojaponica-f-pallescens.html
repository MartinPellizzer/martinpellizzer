Viola pseudojaponica f. pallescens

Viola pseudojaponica f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale viola, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features heart-shaped, pale green leaves with a distinctive bluish-gray hue, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, with five petals and a spurred lower petal. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
