Viburnum opulus f. xanthocarpum

Viburnum opulus f. xanthocarpum, commonly known as the yellow-fruited euonymus, is a cultivar of the European guelder rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-orange berries that contrast with its white, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively hardy, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique fruit color makes it a notable addition to landscape designs.
