Vachellia bucheri

Vachellia bucheri, commonly known as the Burkea acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has small, compound leaves that turn red or orange during the dry season, and it produces yellow flowers followed by long, flat pods. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common sight in savannas and rocky areas.

Vachellia bucheri is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of browse for wildlife.
