Vitis vinifera var. balbis

Vitis vinifera var. balbis, commonly known as the Balbis grape, is a lesser-known variety of the common grapevine.

It is characterized by its small, thick-skinned berries that are typically dark purple in color. This variety is primarily cultivated in specific regions of Europe and is valued for its unique flavor profile. The plant exhibits vigorous growth and is often used in both viticultural research and ornamental gardening.

Due to its limited cultivation, it remains a rare and specialized plant in the world of viticulture.
