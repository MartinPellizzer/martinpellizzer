Viola hirtipedoides

Viola hirtipedoides, commonly known as the rough-leaved violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, usually appearing in early spring. Its flowers have a distinctive hooded structure, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in woodland soils.

Viola hirtipedoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
