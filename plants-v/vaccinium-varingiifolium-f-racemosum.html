Vaccinium varingiifolium f. racemosum

Vaccinium varingiifolium f. racemosum, commonly known as the racemosum blueberry, is a botanical variant of the varingiifolium blueberry species.

It is characterized by its racemose inflorescence, which consists of a cluster of flowers arranged along a central stem. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for cultivation in gardens and natural habitats.
