Vernonia seyrigii

Vernonia seyrigii, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy yellow flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular florets that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Vernonia seyrigii is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
