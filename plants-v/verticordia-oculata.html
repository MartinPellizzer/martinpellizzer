Verticordia oculata

Verticordia oculata, commonly known as the "Fairy Orchid," is a striking member of the Myrtaceae family native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

This plant is characterized by its intricate, often colorful inflorescences that resemble tiny orchids, with elongated, recurved flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in heathland environments, and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate with dry summers and wet winters. The species is noted for its unique floral structure, which includes a central column and specialized staminodes that aid in pollination by insects.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, Verticordia oculata is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
