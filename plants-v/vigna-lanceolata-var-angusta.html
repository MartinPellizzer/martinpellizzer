Vigna lanceolata var. angusta

Vigna lanceolata var. angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional seeds and is often cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
