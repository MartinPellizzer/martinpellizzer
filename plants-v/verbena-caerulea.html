Verbena caerulea

Verbena caerulea, commonly known as blue verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant produces showy blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Verbena caerulea is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to reseed itself reliably.
