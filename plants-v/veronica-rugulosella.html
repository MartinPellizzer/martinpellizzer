Veronica rugulosella

Veronica rugulosella, commonly known as the rough speedwell, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grassy areas, woodland edges, and along paths, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine, rough hairs. Its flowers are blue or violet, arranged in clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Veronica rugulosella is often found in disturbed or moist habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
