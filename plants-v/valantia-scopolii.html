Valantia scopolii

Valantia scopolii, commonly known as the nightshade or scopolia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in rocky or limestone soils. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while the fruit is a small, dry capsule containing several seeds.

Valantia scopolii is also noted for its chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
