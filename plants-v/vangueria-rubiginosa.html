Vangueria rubiginosa

Vangueria rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed vangueria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems covered in a distinctive reddish or brownish bark. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

Vangueria rubiginosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species found in various habitats across its native range.
