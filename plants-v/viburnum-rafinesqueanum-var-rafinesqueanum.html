Viburnum rafinesqueanum var. rafinesqueanum

Viburnum rafinesqueanum var. rafinesqueanum, commonly known as the Allegheny blackhaw, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green above and lighter green beneath, with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. Its bark is thick, ridged, and dark reddish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

Viburnum rafinesqueanum var. rafinesqueanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and supporting local biodiversity.
