Vitis helferi

Vitis helferi, commonly known as the Helfer's grape, is a species of flowering plant in the family Vitaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in the southeastern region. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and other structures. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and greenish-white in color.

The fruit, a small grape-like berry, is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in local culinary traditions.
