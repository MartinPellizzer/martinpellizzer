Viola septentrionalis var. grisea

Viola septentrionalis var. grisea, commonly known as the gray violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive grayish-white underside, and it produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet and purple. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in clusters along the ground.

The gray violet is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
