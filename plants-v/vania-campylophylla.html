Vania campylophylla

Vania campylophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed vanda, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments.

Vania campylophylla is a climbing orchid that requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil for optimal growth.
