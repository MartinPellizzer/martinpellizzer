Vernonia uhligii

Vernonia uhligii, commonly known as the golden ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or wetland habitats. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Vernonia uhligii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
