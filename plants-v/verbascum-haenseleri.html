Verbascum haenseleri

Verbascum haenseleri, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It features tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in clusters at the top of the stems, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Verbascum haenseleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
