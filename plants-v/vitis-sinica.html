Vitis sinica

Vitis sinica, commonly known as the Chinese grapevine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and red to black berries that are edible. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Vitis sinica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for various health benefits.
