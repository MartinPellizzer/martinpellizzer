Vernonanthura phaeoneura

Vernonanthura phaeoneura, commonly known as the dark-winged orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark violet petals and sepals, with a distinctive white lip that sets it apart from other orchid species. Native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the warmer months, attracting specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
