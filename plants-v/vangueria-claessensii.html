Vangueria claessensii

Vangueria claessensii, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Vangueria claessensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and birds.
