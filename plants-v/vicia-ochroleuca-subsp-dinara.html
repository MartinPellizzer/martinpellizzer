Vicia ochroleuca subsp. dinara

Vicia ochroleuca subsp.

dinara, commonly known as the Dinara vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with rounded leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in elongated racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that mature to a brownish color.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
