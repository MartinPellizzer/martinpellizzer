Vicia elegantissima var. aristata

Vicia elegantissima var. aristata, commonly known as the aristate vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are pinnate, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
