Verbena spathulata var. grandiflora

Verbena spathulata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the largeflower verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green hue. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
