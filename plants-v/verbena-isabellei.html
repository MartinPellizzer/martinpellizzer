Verbena isabellei

Verbena isabellei, commonly known as Isabelle's verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in arid environments.
