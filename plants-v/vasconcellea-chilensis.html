Vasconcellea chilensis

Vasconcellea chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean cucumber or sweet cucumber, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant features trailing stems with heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, edible fruits.

Its fruits are typically sweet and aromatic, often used in local cuisines for their unique flavor.
