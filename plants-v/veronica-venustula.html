Veronica venustula

Veronica venustula, commonly known as the pretty speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation areas.
