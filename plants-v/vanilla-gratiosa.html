Vanilla gratiosa

Vanilla gratiosa, commonly known as the fragrant vanilla orchid, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, vine-like stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into pods, which are the source of the vanilla flavor. Its growth requires warm, humid conditions and partial shade, making it well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its aromatic properties.
