Vatica aerea

Vatica aerea, commonly known as the sky ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and striking silver-blue foliage. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is known for its tall, straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Vatica aerea is valued for its durable timber and plays a significant role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.

It also has cultural and traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
