Vicatia millefolia

Vicatia millefolia, commonly known as common yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a sprawling growth habit with fibrous roots and a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Vicatia millefolia is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing benefits.
