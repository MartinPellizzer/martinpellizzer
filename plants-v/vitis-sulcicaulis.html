Vitis sulcicaulis

Vitis sulcicaulis, commonly known as the grooved grapevine, is a species of flowering plant in the Vitaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in wetland and swampy areas. The plant is characterized by its tendrils, which help it climb and support its growth on various structures. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a distinctive grooved appearance on the stem, giving it its scientific name.

Vitis sulcicaulis produces small, greenish-white flowers and red or black berries, which are important for wildlife in its native habitat.
