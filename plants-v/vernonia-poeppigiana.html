Vernonia poeppigiana

Vernonia poeppigiana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as grasslands and woodlands, and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in compact clusters.

Vernonia poeppigiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
