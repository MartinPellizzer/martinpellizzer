Viola truncata var. glaberrima

Viola truncata var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smoothleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks, favoring rich, loamy soils. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are smooth and often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a distinctive downward-curving spur.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
