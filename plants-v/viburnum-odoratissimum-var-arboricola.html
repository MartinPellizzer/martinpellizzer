Viburnum odoratissimum var. arboricola

Viburnum odoratissimum var. arboricola, commonly known as the Chinese snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Viburnum odoratissimum var. arboricola is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
