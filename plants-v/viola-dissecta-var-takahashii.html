Viola dissecta var. takahashii

Viola dissecta var. takahashii, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a delicate flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the woodland landscape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
