Vaccinium sylvaticum

Vaccinium sylvaticum, commonly known as the common blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other heath and cranberry plants. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which later develop into edible berries. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are commonly used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and forest edges.
