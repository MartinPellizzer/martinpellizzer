Vaccinium penaeoides f. densa

Vaccinium penaeoides f. densa, commonly known as the dense heath, is a variant of the Vaccinium penaeoides species, which belongs to the Ericaceae family.

This plant is characterized by its dense growth habit and compact, evergreen foliage. It typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland or coastal environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by edible berries.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems, supporting local wildlife and contributing to biodiversity.
