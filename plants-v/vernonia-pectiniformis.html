Vernonia pectiniformis

Vernonia pectiniformis, commonly known as the featherleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, often found in desert and arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
