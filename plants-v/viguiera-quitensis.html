Viguiera quitensis

Viguiera quitensis, commonly known as the Andean blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Viguiera quitensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
