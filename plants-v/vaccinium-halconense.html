Vaccinium halconense

Vaccinium halconense, commonly known as the Halcon blueberry, is a species of shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many other heath and blueberry plants. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool climate with high humidity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
