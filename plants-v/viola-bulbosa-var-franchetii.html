Viola bulbosa var. franchetii

Viola bulbosa var. franchetii, commonly known as the Franchet's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to regions of China and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short, rhizomatous root system that helps it survive in its natural habitat.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
