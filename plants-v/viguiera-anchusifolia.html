Viguiera anchusifolia

Viguiera anchusifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native gardens.

Viguiera anchusifolia is also known for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
