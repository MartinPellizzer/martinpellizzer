Vicia salaminia

Vicia salaminia, commonly known as the Salaminian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often found in Mediterranean regions. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Vicia salaminia is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
