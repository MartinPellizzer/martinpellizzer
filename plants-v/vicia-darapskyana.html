Vicia darapskyana

Vicia darapskyana, commonly known as the Darapsky vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Vicia darapskyana is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
