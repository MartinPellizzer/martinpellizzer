Viola leptosepala

Viola leptosepala, commonly known as the slender-sepaled violet, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing nectar that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola leptosepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
