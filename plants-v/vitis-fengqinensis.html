Vitis fengqinensis

Vitis fengqinensis, commonly known as the Fengqin grapevine, is a rare and endemic species native to China.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are small berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color.

Due to its limited distribution and unique genetic makeup, it is of significant interest to botanists and plant conservationists.
