Verbascum sinuatum var. sinuatum

Verbascum sinuatum var. sinuatum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are broad, gray-green, and also covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a loose spike.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
