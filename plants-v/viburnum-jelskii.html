Viburnum jelskii

Viburnum jelskii, commonly known as the Siberian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of eastern Siberia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and red or orange berries that appear in the fall. Viburnum jelskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
