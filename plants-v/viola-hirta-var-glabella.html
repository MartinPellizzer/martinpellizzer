Viola hirta var. glabella

Viola hirta var. glabella, commonly known as the hairy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common species in botanical gardens and wildflower habitats.
