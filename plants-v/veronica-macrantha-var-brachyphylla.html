Veronica macrantha var. brachyphylla

Veronica macrantha var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
