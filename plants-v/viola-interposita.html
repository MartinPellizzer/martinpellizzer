Viola interposita

Viola interposita, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from violet to white, typically blooming in spring. Viola interposita thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for shade gardens and conservation efforts.
