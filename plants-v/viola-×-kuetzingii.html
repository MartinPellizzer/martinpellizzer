Viola × kuetzingii

Viola × kuetzingii, commonly known as the Dutch viola, is a hybrid species belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Europe, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows. Viola × kuetzingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
