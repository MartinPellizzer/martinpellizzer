Verbascum turkestanicum

Verbascum turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkistan mullein, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
