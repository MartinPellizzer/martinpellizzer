Viola purpurea subsp. mohavensis

Viola purpurea subsp.

mohavensis, commonly known as Mohave violet, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. It is known for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in full sun.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
