Vachellia farnesiana f. micrantha

Vachellia farnesiana f. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flower clusters, which are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils and drought conditions.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of browse for livestock.
