Vignea trinervis

Vignea trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with three distinct veins. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in the axils of the leaves. Vignea trinervis is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
