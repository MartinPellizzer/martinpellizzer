Veratrum versicolor f. brunneum

Veratrum versicolor f. brunneum, commonly known as the brown-flowered white hellebore, is a rare variant of the white hellebore.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States, typically found in moist, wooded areas. The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow to brownish flowers. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its toxic alkaloids.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
