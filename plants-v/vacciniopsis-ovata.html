Vacciniopsis ovata

Vacciniopsis ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaved vacciniopsis, is a flowering plant species native to eastern Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in heathlands and sclerophyllous forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring. Its leaves are oval in shape, with a leathery texture and a glossy surface.

Vacciniopsis ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
