Vitis spectabilis

Vitis spectabilis, commonly known as the purple passionvine, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round, edible berries that ripen to a dark purple color, often used in jams and wines. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Vitis spectabilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
