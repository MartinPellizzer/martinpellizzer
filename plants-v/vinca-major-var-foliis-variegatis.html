Vinca major var. foliis-variegatis

Vinca major var. foliis-variegatis, commonly known as the variegated periwinkle, is a low-growing evergreen ground cover plant native to Europe.

It features dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation, enhancing its ornamental value in gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and is highly tolerant of moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its hardiness and spreading habit, it is often used to suppress weeds and add visual interest to shaded landscapes.
