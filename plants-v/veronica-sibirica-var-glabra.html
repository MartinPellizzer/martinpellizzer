Veronica sibirica var. glabra

Veronica sibirica var. glabra, commonly known as Siberian speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the variety glabra is distinguished by its smooth, hairless foliage.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes for its delicate appearance.
