Valeriana dubiosa

Valeriana dubiosa, commonly known as the slender valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters called cymes. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana dubiosa thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
