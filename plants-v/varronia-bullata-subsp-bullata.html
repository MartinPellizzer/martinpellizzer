Varronia bullata subsp. bullata

Varronia bullata subsp.

bullata, commonly known as the African balm or yellow balm, is a shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and forest margins.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and local ecosystems.
