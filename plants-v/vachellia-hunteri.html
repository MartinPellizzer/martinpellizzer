Vachellia hunteri

Vachellia hunteri, commonly known as the Hunter's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate, and its yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, thin pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Vachellia hunteri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
