Verbascum phlomoides var. gymnostemon

Verbascum phlomoides var. gymnostemon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features thick, woolly stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, narrowing towards the tip, and are also densely hairy. It produces showy, yellowish-green to brownish flowers in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
