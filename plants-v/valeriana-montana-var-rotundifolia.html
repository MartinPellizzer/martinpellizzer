Valeriana montana var. rotundifolia

Valeriana montana var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf valerian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often pink or white flowers and its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is typically found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its roots contain volatile oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine.

Valeriana montana var. rotundifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
