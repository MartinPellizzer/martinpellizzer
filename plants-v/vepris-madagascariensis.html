Vepris madagascariensis

Vepris madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its essential oils are valued for their medicinal properties, including antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.
