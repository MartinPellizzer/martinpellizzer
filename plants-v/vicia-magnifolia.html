Vicia magnifolia

Vicia magnifolia, commonly known as the showy vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive flower clusters. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often arranged in dense, erect spikes. Vicia magnifolia is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
