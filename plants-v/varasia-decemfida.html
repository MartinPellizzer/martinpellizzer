Varasia decemfida

Varasia decemfida, commonly known as the ten-leafed varasia, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that typically consist of ten lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with smooth, dark green bark.

Varasia decemfida is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive foliage and delicate floral display.
