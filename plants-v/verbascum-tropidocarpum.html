Verbascum tropidocarpum

Verbascum tropidocarpum, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in dense, fuzzy grayish-white hairs. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, broad, and also covered in a thick layer of soft, woolly hair.

Verbascum tropidocarpum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
