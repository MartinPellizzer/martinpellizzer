Veronica spicata var. vulgaris

Veronica spicata var. vulgaris, commonly known as common speedwell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features upright spikes of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
