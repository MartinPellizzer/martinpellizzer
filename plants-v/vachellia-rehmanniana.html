Vachellia rehmanniana

Vachellia rehmanniana, commonly known as the silver thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Vachellia rehmanniana is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
