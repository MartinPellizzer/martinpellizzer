Vicia ochroleuca subsp. baborensis

Vicia ochroleuca subsp.

baborensis, commonly known as the Baborensis vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a fibrous root system and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is often noted for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
