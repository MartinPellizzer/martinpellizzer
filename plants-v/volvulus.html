Volvulus

Volvulus, commonly known as the spiral fruit or spiral bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique spiral-shaped pods that enclose its seeds. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The botanical features of volvulus make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
