Viburnum lantana var. rugosum

Viburnum lantana var. rugosum, commonly known as roughleaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Viburnum lantana var. rugosum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
