Viola pilushanensis

Viola pilushanensis, commonly known as the Pilushan violet, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Viola pilushanensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
