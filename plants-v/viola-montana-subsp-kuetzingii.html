Viola montana subsp. kuetzingii

Viola montana subsp.

kuetzingii, commonly known as the Kuetzing's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet to purple flowers with a distinctive spurred petal.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to cooler climates and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
