Vernonia wildii

Vernonia wildii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climatic conditions.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional African medicine.
