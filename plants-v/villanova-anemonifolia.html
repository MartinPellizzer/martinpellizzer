Villanova anemonifolia

Villanova anemonifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are finely divided, giving the plant a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and support of pollinators.
