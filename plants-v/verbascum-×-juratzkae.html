Verbascum × juratzkae

Verbascum × juratzkae, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their large, fuzzy leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The hybrid is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
