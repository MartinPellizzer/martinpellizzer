Vignea chlorocarpa

Vignea chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-barked vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Vignea chlorocarpa thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
