Verbena moctezumae

Verbena moctezumae, commonly known as Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple or blue flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and often branched.

Verbena moctezumae thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
