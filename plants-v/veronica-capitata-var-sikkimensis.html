Veronica capitata var. sikkimensis

Veronica capitata var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim Veronica, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces dense clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its hardy nature.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
