Vittadinia australis var. dissecta

Vittadinia australis var. dissecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
