Viguiera palmeri var. coalcomana

Viguiera palmeri var. coalcomana, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
