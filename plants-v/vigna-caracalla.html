Vigna caracalla

Vigna caracalla, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical America.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and long, slender pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and twining stems that support its growth. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Vigna caracalla is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
