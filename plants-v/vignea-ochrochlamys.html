Vignea ochrochlamys

Vignea ochrochlamys, commonly known as the yellow-flowered vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals.

Vignea ochrochlamys is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
