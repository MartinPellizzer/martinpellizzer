Viola dumetorum

Viola dumetorum, commonly known as the velvetleaf violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clustered flowering pattern.

Viola dumetorum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
