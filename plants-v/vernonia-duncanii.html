Vernonia duncanii

Vernonia duncanii, commonly known as Duncan's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and features narrow, gray-green leaves. Its showy, yellow-orange flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Duncan's ironweed is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
