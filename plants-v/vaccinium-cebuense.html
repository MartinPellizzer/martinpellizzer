Vaccinium cebuense

Vaccinium cebuense, commonly known as the Cebu blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other blueberry species. It thrives in acidic soils and requires moderate to high levels of moisture.

Vaccinium cebuense is often found in forest understories and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
