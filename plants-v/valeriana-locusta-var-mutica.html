Valeriana locusta var. mutica

Valeriana locusta var. mutica, commonly known as the hairy valerian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves with hairy surfaces, and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.
