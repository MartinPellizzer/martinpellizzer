Viola pinnata lusus hirta

Viola pinnata lusus hirta, commonly known as the hairy wood violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves is a distinguishing botanical feature.
