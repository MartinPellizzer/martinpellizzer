Venana

Venana, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically features thick, velvety leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Venana is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
