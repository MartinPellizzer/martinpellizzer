Vachellia albicorticata

Vachellia albicorticata, commonly known as the white-barked acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The white bark is a distinguishing feature, giving the plant its common name.
