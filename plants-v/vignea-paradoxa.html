Vignea paradoxa

Vignea paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Vignea paradoxa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in warm, humid climates.
