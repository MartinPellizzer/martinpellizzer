Vitis semicordata var. scortechinii

Vitis semicordata var. scortechinii, commonly known as the scortechinii grapevine, is a rare and botanically unique variant of the semicordata grape species.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its botanical significance lies in its genetic diversity and potential for use in viticultural research.

The plant's morphology and growth habits make it an interesting subject for studying adaptation to specific ecological niches.
