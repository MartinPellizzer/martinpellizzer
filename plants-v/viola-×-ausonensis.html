Viola × ausonensis

Viola × ausonensis, commonly known as the common violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and gardens. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Viola × ausonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
