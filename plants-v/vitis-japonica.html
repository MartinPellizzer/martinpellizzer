Vitis japonica

Vitis japonica, commonly known as Japanese grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It produces small, sweet, and edible berries that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length, using its tendrils to climb supports. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Vitis japonica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in herbal medicine.
