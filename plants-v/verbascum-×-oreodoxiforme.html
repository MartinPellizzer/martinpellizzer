Verbascum × oreodoxiforme

Verbascum × oreodoxiforme, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers in the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
