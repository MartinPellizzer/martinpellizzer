Vernonia concinna

Vernonia concinna, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its sturdy stems and dense foliage make it a hardy plant that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Vernonia concinna is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
