Vernonia desertorum var. polycephala

Vernonia desertorum var. polycephala, commonly known as the desert ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue to purple in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs that help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
