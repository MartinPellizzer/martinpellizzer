Viola pubescens

Viola pubescens, commonly known as the downy violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often adorned with yellow markings. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Viola pubescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
