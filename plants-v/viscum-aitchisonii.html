Viscum aitchisonii

Viscum aitchisonii, commonly known as the Aitchison's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees, primarily in the Himalayan region.

It has a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and white, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or yellow, which contain the seeds necessary for its propagation. Viscum aitchisonii relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it provides nutrients to the host through its parasitic relationship.

Its presence is often an indicator of the health and biodiversity of the forest ecosystem.
