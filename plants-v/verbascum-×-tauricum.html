Verbascum × tauricum

Verbascum × tauricum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Verbascum blattaria and Verbascum thapsus.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant features large, velvety gray-green leaves that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Verbascum × tauricum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
