Viola carnosula

Viola carnosula, commonly known as the fat violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its compact growth habit. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Viola carnosula is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
