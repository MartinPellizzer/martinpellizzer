Vicia pseudohirsuta

Vicia pseudohirsuta, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by pods containing several seeds. Vicia pseudohirsuta is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in agricultural and ecological restoration settings.
