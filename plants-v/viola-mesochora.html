Viola mesochora

Viola mesochora, commonly known as the Alpine violet, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces violet-blue flowers with five petals. It is part of the Violaceae family, known for its ornamental and medicinal uses.

Viola mesochora is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience in harsh environments.
