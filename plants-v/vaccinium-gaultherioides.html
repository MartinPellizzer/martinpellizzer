Vaccinium gaultherioides

Vaccinium gaultherioides, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in early spring. Its berries, which are usually dark blue or black, are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

Vaccinium gaultherioides thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.
