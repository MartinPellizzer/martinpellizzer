Veronica sergievskiana

Veronica sergievskiana, commonly known as the Siberian speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Veronica sergievskiana is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
