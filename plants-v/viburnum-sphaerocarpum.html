Viburnum sphaerocarpum

Viburnum sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the globe viburnum, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels. The plant produces dark blue berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
