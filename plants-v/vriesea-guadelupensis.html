Vriesea guadelupensis

Vriesea guadelupensis, commonly known as the Guadeloupe Vriesea, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliad family.

It is native to the Caribbean, specifically the island of Guadeloupe, where it grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant features a striking rosette of sword-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, with a waxy texture. Its flowers, which are typically orange or red, bloom in a spike and are highly attractive to pollinators.

Vriesea guadelupensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
