Vernonia trinitatis

Vernonia trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidad ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Vernonia trinitatis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
