Vicia modesta

Vicia modesta, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. Its pods contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals.

Vicia modesta is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
