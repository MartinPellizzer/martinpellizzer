Valerianella congesta var. trigona

Valerianella congesta var. trigona, commonly known as the clustered valerian or trigonous valerian, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are pinnately divided and have a slightly aromatic scent.

It is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
