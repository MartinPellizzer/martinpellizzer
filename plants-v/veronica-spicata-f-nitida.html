Veronica spicata f. nitida

Veronica spicata f. nitida, commonly known as the silver tufted speedwell, is a compact, mat-forming perennial that features dense, silver-blue foliage.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, sky-blue flowers on slender, upright stems. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is particularly noted for its striking foliage, which adds visual interest throughout the growing season.
