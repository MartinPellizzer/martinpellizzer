Viola hymettia

Viola hymettia, commonly known as the Hymettian violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland soils and thrives in partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

Viola hymettia is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
