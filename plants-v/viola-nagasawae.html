Viola nagasawae

Viola nagasawae, commonly known as the Nagasawa violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and small, colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing purple or violet flowers with yellow centers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
