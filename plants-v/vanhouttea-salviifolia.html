Vanhouttea salviifolia

Vanhouttea salviifolia, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its root system is fibrous, and it often grows in disturbed soils along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
