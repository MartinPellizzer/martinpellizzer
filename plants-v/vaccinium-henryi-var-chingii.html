Vaccinium henryi var. chingii

Vaccinium henryi var. chingii, commonly known as the Ching's huckleberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible berries that are typically red or black. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
