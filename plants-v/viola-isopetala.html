Viola isopetala

Viola isopetala, commonly known as the broad-leaved violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola isopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
