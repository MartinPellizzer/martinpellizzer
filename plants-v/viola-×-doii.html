Viola × doii

Viola × doii, commonly known as the hybrid violet, is a species resulting from the cross between Viola lutea and Viola tricolor.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers typically exhibit a range of colors, including purple, blue, and white, depending on the specific cultivar. Viola × doii thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and ecological studies.
