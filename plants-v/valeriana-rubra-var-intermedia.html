Valeriana rubra var. intermedia

Valeriana rubra var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are often reddish or purple-tinged, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a strong, somewhat peppery aroma. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Valeriana rubra var. intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
