Viscum coloratum var. rubroaurantiacum

Viscum coloratum var. rubroaurantiacum, commonly known as the red-orange mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a reddish-orange hue, which distinguishes it from other mistletoe species. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by berries that are orange-red in color.

This variety is native to regions in Asia and is often found in forested areas where its host trees thrive.
