Verbascum × omissum

Verbascum × omissum, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their large, showy flowers. The hybrid mullein typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy plant suited to arid environments. Its flowers, which range from yellow to orange, are clustered in dense spikes and bloom during the summer months.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating respiratory ailments.
