Viola corsica

Viola corsica, commonly known as the Corsican violet, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean island of Corsica.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola corsica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
