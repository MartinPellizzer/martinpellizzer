Viola tenella

Viola tenella, commonly known as the creeping violet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from violet to white, typically blooming in early spring. Viola tenella thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.
