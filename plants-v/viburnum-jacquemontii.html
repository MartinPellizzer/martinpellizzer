Viburnum jacquemontii

Viburnum jacquemontii, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green above and lighter below, with a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that attract wildlife. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in partial to full shade.

Viburnum jacquemontii is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
