Vernonia guineensis var. cameroonica

Vernonia guineensis var. cameroonica, commonly known as the Cameroon ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and sometimes branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
