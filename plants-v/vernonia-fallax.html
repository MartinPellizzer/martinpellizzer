Vernonia fallax

Vernonia fallax, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist, which bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Vernonia fallax is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
