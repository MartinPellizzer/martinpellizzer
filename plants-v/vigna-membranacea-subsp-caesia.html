Vigna membranacea subsp. caesia

Vigna membranacea subsp.

caesia, commonly known as the purple wisteria vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, violet to purple flowers.

The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and green spaces.
