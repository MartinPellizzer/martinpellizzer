Vernonia aplinii

Vernonia aplinii, commonly known as the Aplin's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Vernonia aplinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
