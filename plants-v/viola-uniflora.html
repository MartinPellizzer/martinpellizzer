Viola uniflora

Viola uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces a single, showy violet flower that is usually purple or blue in color. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its delicate appearance and ornamental value.

Viola uniflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
