Vicia villosa subsp. maniatissa

Vicia villosa subsp.

maniatissa, commonly known as the Maniatissa vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft pubescence.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-purple, typically blooming in early summer. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types, including those that are dry or rocky.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas.
