Veronica alpina var. terrae-novae

Veronica alpina var. terrae-novae, commonly known as the Newfoundland speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the coastal regions of Newfoundland.

It features small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to coastal environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
