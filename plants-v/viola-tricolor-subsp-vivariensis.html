Viola tricolor subsp. vivariensis

Viola tricolor subsp.

vivariensis, commonly known as the Italian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of violet, white, and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. It has small, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
