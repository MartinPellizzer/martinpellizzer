Veronica × cassinioides

Veronica × cassinioides, commonly known as the hybrid speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
