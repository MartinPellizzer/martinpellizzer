Vernonia caducissima

Vernonia caducissima, commonly known as the pale coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early fall, producing composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Vernonia caducissima is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
