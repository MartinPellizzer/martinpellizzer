Viola crassa subsp. yatsugatakeana

Viola crassa subsp.

yatsugatakeana, commonly known as the Yatsugatake viola, is a perennial herb native to Japan. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, shaded woodland areas, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable addition to both horticultural collections and ecological studies.
