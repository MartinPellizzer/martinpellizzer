Verbascum sinuatum var. pallidiflorum

Verbascum sinuatum var. pallidiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The leaves are large, broad, and covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hair. Its flowers are yellowish-green, with pale yellow petals, and bloom in late summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils.
