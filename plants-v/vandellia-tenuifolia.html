Vandellia tenuifolia

Vandellia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf vandellia, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the wet tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
