Verticordia multiflora subsp. multiflora

Verticordia multiflora subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
