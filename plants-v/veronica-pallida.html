Veronica pallida

Veronica pallida, commonly known as pale speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in whorls along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Veronica pallida is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
