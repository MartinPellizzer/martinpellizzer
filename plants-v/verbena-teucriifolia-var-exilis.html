Verbena teucriifolia var. exilis

Verbena teucriifolia var. exilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
