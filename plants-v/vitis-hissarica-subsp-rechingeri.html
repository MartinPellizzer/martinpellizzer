Vitis hissarica subsp. rechingeri

Vitis hissarica subsp.

rechingeri, commonly known as the Caucasian grapevine, is a perennial vine native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in temperate climates.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically dark blue or black in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it has a woody vine structure that supports climbing growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in winemaking.
