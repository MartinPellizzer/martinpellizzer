Verbascum × eginense

Verbascum × eginense, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their fuzzy leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The hybrid nature of Verbascum × eginense results from crosses between different Verbascum species, leading to a variety of morphological characteristics. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid environments.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
