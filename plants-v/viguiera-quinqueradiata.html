Viguiera quinqueradiata

Viguiera quinqueradiata, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Viguiera quinqueradiata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
