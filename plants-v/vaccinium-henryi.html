Vaccinium henryi

Vaccinium henryi, commonly known as Henry's blueberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens and conservation efforts.
