Vaccinium cuneifolium

Vaccinium cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueberry, is a species of dwarf shrub native to the northwestern United States and southwestern Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like heathers and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, containing high levels of antioxidants. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Vaccinium cuneifolium thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
