Vernonia musofensis

Vernonia musofensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, showy clusters.

Vernonia musofensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
