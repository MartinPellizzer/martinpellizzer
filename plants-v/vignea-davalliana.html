Vignea davalliana

Vignea davalliana, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Vignea davalliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
