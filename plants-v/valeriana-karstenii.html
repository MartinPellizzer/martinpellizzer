Valeriana karstenii

Valeriana karstenii, commonly known as the Karsten's valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, and it has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Valeriana karstenii is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its calming effects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
