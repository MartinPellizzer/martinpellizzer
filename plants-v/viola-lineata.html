Viola lineata

Viola lineata, commonly known as the purple violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple-tinged underside and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and white. Viola lineata is often found growing in moist, well-drained soils and can tolerate partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
