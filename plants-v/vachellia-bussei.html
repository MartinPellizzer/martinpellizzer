Vachellia bussei

Vachellia bussei, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and compound leaves that are typically arranged in pairs. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous husk. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and along rocky outcrops.

Vachellia bussei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
