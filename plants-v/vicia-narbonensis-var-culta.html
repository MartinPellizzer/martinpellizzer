Vicia narbonensis var. culta

Vicia narbonensis var. culta, commonly known as the cultivated vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped leaflet at the base. The plant produces slender, erect stems that bear small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in hairy pods and are often used as a forage crop or in traditional medicine.

Vicia narbonensis var. culta is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches soil fertility.
