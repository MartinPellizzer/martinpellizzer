Vochysia racemosa

Vochysia racemosa, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and its striking yellow flowers that bloom in racemes, giving it its scientific name. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Vochysia racemosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
