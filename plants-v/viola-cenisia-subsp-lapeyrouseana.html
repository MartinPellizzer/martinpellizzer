Viola cenisia subsp. lapeyrouseana

Viola cenisia subsp.

lapeyrouseana, commonly known as the Lapeyrouse's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with yellow centers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more robust growth compared to other viola species.
