Vittadinia cervicularis var. subcervicularis

Vittadinia cervicularis var. subcervicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vittadinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes.
