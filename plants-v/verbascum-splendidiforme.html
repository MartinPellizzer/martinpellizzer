Verbascum splendidiforme

Verbascum splendidiforme, commonly known as the splendid mullein.

This plant is a flowering species native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is known for its medicinal properties historically used in traditional herbal remedies. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
