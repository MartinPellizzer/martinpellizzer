Vernonia bothrioclinoides

Vernonia bothrioclinoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are woody and branched.

Vernonia bothrioclinoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
