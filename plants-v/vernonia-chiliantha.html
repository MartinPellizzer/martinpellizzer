Vernonia chiliantha

Vernonia chiliantha, commonly known as the Chilean ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong aromatic scent.

Vernonia chiliantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
