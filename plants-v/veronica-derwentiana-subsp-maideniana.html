Veronica derwentiana subsp. maideniana

Veronica derwentiana subsp.

maideniana, commonly known as the New Zealand veronica, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
