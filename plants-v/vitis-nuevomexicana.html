Vitis nuevomexicana

Vitis nuevomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico grape, is a species of flowering vine native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clustered berries, which are typically dark blue to black when mature. Vitis nuevomexicana thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along rocky slopes and in dry woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use in drought-resistant crops.
