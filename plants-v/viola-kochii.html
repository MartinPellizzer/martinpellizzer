Viola kochii

Viola kochii, commonly known as the Koch's violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces small, delicate flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Viola kochii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
