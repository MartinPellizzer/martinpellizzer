Villebrunea rubescens

Villebrunea rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, showy red flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Villebrunea rubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
