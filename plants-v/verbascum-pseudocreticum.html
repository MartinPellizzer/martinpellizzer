Verbascum pseudocreticum

Verbascum pseudocreticum, commonly known as the Cretan mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by fluffy, seed-filled capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Verbascum pseudocreticum is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used as a source of fibers in some regions.
