Verbesina sartorii

Verbesina sartorii, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its botanical features include composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Verbesina sartorii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
