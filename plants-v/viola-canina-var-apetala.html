Viola canina var. apetala

Viola canina var. apetala, commonly known as the hairless dog violet, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the common dog violet.

It is characterized by its reduced or absent petals, giving it a distinctive appearance compared to its typical counterpart. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, downy texture.

The flowers, when present, are usually small and violet in color, blooming in early spring.
