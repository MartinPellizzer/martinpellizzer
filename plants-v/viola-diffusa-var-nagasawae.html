Viola diffusa var. nagasawae

Viola diffusa var. nagasawae, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and gardens. Its flowers are usually pale violet or white, with a distinctive purple throat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
