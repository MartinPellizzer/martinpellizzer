Vepris tabouensis

Vepris tabouensis, commonly known as African birch, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically consumed raw or used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves are often utilized in local herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
