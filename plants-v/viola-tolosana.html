Viola tolosana

Viola tolosana, commonly known as the Tolosan violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Viola tolosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
