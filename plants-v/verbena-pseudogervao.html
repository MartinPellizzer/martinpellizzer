Verbena pseudogervao

Verbena pseudogervao, commonly known as the Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Verbena pseudogervao thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
