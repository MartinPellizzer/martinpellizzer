Vitex agnus-castus f. alba

Vitex agnus-castus f. alba, commonly known as white chaste tree, is a cultivar of the chaste tree species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, contrasting with its dark green, ovate leaves. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its fruit is a cylindrical, woody capsule that splits open to release small, fluffy seeds.

The white form of this species is distinguished by its pale flowers, which make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
