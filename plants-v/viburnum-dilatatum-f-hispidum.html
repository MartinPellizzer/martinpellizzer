Viburnum dilatatum f. hispidum

Viburnum dilatatum f. hispidum, commonly known as the roughleaf arrowwood, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that attract wildlife. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
