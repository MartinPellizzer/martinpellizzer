Verbesina spilanthoides

Verbesina spilanthoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have hairy surfaces, while its stems are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

The showy yellow flowers appear in the summer and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
