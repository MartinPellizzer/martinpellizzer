Vochysia arcuata

Vochysia arcuata, commonly known as the "silverleaf vochysia," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Vochysia arcuata is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
