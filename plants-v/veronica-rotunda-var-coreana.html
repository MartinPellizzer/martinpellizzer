Veronica rotunda var. coreana

Veronica rotunda var. coreana, commonly known as Korean round vervain, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
