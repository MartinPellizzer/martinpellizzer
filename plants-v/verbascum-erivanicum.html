Verbascum erivanicum

Verbascum erivanicum, commonly known as the Armenian mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountains of Armenia and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Verbascum erivanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
