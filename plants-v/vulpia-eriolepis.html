Vulpia eriolepis

Vulpia eriolepis, commonly known as the hairy vulpia, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing annual plant with slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name. Its flowers are typically greenish or reddish and are arranged in dense clusters.

This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.
