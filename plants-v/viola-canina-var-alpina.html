Viola canina var. alpina

Viola canina var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and blue. It is part of the Violaceae family and is often found in mountainous areas across the Alps and Carpathians.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
