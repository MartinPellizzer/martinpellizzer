Vernonia subulata

Vernonia subulata, commonly known as the needleleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its tall, upright growth habit. Its leaves are linear and sharply pointed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The small, yellow flowers appear in late summer and early fall, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
