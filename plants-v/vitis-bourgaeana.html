Vitis bourgaeana

Vitis bourgaeana, commonly known as the African grapevine, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Vitis bourgaeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
