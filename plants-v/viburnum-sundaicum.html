Viburnum sundaicum

Viburnum sundaicum, commonly known as the Sunda viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Viburnum sundaicum typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple during the fall.
