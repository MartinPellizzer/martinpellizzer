Verbesina sublobata

Verbesina sublobata, commonly known as the narrow-lobed beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Verbesina sublobata is often found in moist, disturbed habitats like roadsides and wetlands.
