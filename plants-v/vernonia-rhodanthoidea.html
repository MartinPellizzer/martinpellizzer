Vernonia rhodanthoidea

Vernonia rhodanthoidea, commonly known as the redroot ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of small, yellow to orange-red flowers. Its botanical name reflects its distinctive reddish roots and showy inflorescences.

Vernonia rhodanthoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
