Vitis labrusca var. macrophylla

Vitis labrusca var. macrophylla, commonly known as the fox grape, is a cultivar of the grapevine species Vitis labrusca.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy fruit.

The berries are typically not suitable for eating raw but can be used in winemaking.
