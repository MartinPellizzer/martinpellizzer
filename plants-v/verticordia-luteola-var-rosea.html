Verticordia luteola var. rosea

Verticordia luteola var. rosea, commonly known as the pink swamp wallaby, is a species of flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to rose-colored flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in swampy or seasonally waterlogged soils, often in heathlands or along watercourses. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in wet, acidic environments.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for native pollinators.
