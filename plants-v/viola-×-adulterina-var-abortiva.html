Viola × adulterina var. abortiva

Viola × adulterina var. abortiva, commonly known as the abortive violet, is a hybrid species within the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, often violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its variability in flower color and leaf structure, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
