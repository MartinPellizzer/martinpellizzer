Veronica officinalis var. multicaulis

Veronica officinalis var. multicaulis, commonly known as the hairy speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers clustered in pairs, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
