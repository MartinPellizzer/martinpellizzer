Vitis erioclada

Vitis erioclada, commonly known as the sand grape, is a woody vine native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, downy hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along riverbanks and in rocky soils.

The fruit is used in local cuisines and has been historically valued for its flavor and nutritional content.
