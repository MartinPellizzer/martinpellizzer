Valeranda frutescens

Valeranda frutescens, commonly known as wild valerian, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Valeranda frutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming properties.
