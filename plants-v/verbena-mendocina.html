Verbena mendocina

Verbena mendocina, commonly known as Mendo verbena, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its spikes of bright blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. It has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Verbena mendocina is often used in native gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
