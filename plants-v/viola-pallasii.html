Viola pallasii

Viola pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and blue. Viola pallasii is part of the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
