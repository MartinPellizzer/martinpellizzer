Verbesina trujillensis

Verbesina trujillensis, commonly known as the Trujillo goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Verbesina trujillensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
