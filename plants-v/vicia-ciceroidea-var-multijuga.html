Vicia ciceroidea var. multijuga

Vicia ciceroidea var. multijuga, commonly known as the multijugate vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
