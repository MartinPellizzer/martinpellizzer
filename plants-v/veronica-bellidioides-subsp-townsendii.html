Veronica bellidioides subsp. townsendii

Veronica bellidioides subsp.

townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial with erect, branched stems and small, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
