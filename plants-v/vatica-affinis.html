Vatica affinis

Vatica affinis, commonly known as the yellow tonka tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It is valued for its timber, which is highly durable and used in furniture and construction.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in various indigenous cultures.
