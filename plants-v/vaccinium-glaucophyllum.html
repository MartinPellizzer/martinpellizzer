Vaccinium glaucophyllum

Vaccinium glaucophyllum, commonly known as the blueberry shrub, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants, and it typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue, followed by edible berries that are similar in appearance to blueberries.

Vaccinium glaucophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in coastal environments with high salinity and wind exposure.
