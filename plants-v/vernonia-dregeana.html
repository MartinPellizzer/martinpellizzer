Vernonia dregeana

Vernonia dregeana, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces fluffy, pale blue to purple flower clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Vernonia dregeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
