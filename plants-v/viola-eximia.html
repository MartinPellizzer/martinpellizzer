Viola eximia

Viola eximia, commonly known as the showy viola, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Viola eximia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
