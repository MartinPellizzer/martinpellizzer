Vachellia johnwoodii

Vachellia johnwoodii, commonly known as the Johnwood's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers arranged in clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Vachellia johnwoodii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.
