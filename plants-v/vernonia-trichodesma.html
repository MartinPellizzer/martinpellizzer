Vernonia trichodesma

Vernonia trichodesma, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and they bloom in late summer to early fall.
