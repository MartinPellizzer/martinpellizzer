Viola rhodopensis

Viola rhodopensis, commonly known as the Bulgarian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along forest edges, preferring moist, well-drained soil. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Viola rhodopensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
