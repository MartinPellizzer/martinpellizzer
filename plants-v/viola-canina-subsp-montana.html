Viola canina subsp. montana

Viola canina subsp.

montana, commonly known as the alpine violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces violet or purple flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
