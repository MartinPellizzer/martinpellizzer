Viola centronum

Viola centronum, commonly known as the purple heart viola, is a flowering plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy violet flowers with a distinctive purple hue. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

Viola centronum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
