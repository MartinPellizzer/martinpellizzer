Veratrum lobelianum var. eschscholtzianum

Veratrum lobelianum var. eschscholtzianum, commonly known as the Eschscholtz's white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy white to pale green flowers. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
