Viburnum lobophyllum var. silvestrii

Viburnum lobophyllum var. silvestrii, commonly known as the Sierra viburnum, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This plant is often found in chaparral and woodland areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to poor soils and harsh environmental conditions.
