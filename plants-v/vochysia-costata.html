Vochysia costata

Vochysia costata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or yellow bell, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Vochysia costata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
