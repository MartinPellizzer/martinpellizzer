Vleckia occidentalis

Vleckia occidentalis, commonly known as the western vleck, is a rare flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its unique adaptations allow it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
