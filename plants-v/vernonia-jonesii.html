Vernonia jonesii

Vernonia jonesii, commonly known as Jones' ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, dark purple to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a distinctive grayish-green color.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
