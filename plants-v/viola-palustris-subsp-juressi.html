Viola palustris subsp. juressi

Viola palustris subsp.

juressi, commonly known as the swamp violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically green with a slight violet hue.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often with a distinctive yellow or white keel. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
