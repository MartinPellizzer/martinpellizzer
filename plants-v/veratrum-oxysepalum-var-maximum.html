Veratrum oxysepalum var. maximum

Veratrum oxysepalum var. maximum, commonly known as the giant white hellebore, is a tall perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
