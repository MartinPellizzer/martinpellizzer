Viburnum fragrans

Viburnum fragrans, commonly known as sweet viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces dark blue, edible berries that are favored by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall.

Viburnum fragrans thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
