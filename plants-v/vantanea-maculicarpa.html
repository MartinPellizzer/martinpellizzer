Vantanea maculicarpa

Vantanea maculicarpa, commonly known as the spotted fruit vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces unique, fleshy, orange-red berries that are adorned with distinctive black spots. These berries are edible and often used in local cuisines for their tangy flavor.

Vantanea maculicarpa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
