Verbascum chrysorrhacos

Verbascum chrysorrhacos, commonly known as the golden-hairwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the genus Verbascum, which includes over 300 species of flowering plants commonly referred to as mullein. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Verbascum chrysorrhacos is typically perennial and has a sprawling growth habit, making it a distinctive addition to its natural environment.
