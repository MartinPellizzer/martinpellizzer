Vanda parishii var. marriottiana

Vanda parishii var. marriottiana, commonly known as the Marriott's Vanda, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, showy flowers with vibrant colors, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a pseudobulb structure with long, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
