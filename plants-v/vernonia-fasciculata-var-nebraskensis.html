Vernonia fasciculata var. nebraskensis

Vernonia fasciculata var. nebraskensis, commonly known as Nebraska ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, dark purple to brownish-red flower heads. The plant typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
