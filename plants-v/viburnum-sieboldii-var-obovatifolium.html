Viburnum sieboldii var. obovatifolium

Viburnum sieboldii var. obovatifolium, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features elliptical, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and seasonal interest in garden settings.
