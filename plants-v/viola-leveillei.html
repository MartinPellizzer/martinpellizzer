Viola leveillei

Viola leveillei, commonly known as the Sierra Madre violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Viola leveillei belongs to the Violaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

Its blooming period occurs in spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.
