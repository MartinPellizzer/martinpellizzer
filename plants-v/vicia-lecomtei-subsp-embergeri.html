Vicia lecomtei subsp. embergeri

Vicia lecomtei subsp.

embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in racemes, which are followed by elongated, hairy pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for certain insect species.
