Verticordia tumida subsp. tumida

Verticordia tumida subsp.

tumida, commonly known as the Hairy Wandflower, is a striking member of the Myrtaceae family native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its unique, wand-like inflorescence that consists of numerous small, tubular flowers clustered around a central axis.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, often found in heathland and woodland habitats. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly honeyeaters.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
