Vinsonia pervilleana

Vinsonia pervilleana, commonly known as the Pervillean vine, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in cool, humid environments. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.

However, it is relatively rare and not widely cultivated outside its natural range.
