Verbena teucrioides f. iverana

Verbena teucrioides f. iverana, commonly known as the Iverna verbena, is a compact, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically a vibrant blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters atop slender stems. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are oppositely arranged, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
