Veronica pectinata var. mardinensis

Veronica pectinata var. mardinensis, commonly known as the Mardin speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Eastern Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite leaves which are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with five petals, typical of the Veronica genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
