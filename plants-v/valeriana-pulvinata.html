Valeriana pulvinata

Valeriana pulvinata, commonly known as the purple valerian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its root system is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its calming properties.

Valeriana pulvinata is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
