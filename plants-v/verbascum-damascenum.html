Verbascum damascenum

Verbascum damascenum, commonly known as the mullein plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers in the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Verbascum damascenum has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly for respiratory ailments.
