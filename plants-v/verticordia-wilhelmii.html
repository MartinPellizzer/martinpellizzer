Verticordia wilhelmii

Verticordia wilhelmii, commonly known as the Geraldton waxplant, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It features dense, compact growth with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces vibrant, candle-like flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife.
