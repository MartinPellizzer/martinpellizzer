Vaccinium leptosepalum

Vaccinium leptosepalum, commonly known as the slender-sepal blueberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, leathery leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or dark purple in color, similar to other blueberry species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Vaccinium leptosepalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
