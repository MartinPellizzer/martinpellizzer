Vittaria minor var. minima

Vittaria minor var. minima, commonly known as the small vittaria, is a small epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows on tree trunks or branches, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Vittaria minor var. minima is often found in rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
