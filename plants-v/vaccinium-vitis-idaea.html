Vaccinium vitis-idaea

Vaccinium vitis-idaea, commonly known as cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to boreal and Arctic regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bright red berries. The plant thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas. Its flowers are bell-shaped and typically pink or white, while the berries are edible and used in various culinary traditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
