Vitekorchis buchtienii

Vitekorchis buchtienii, commonly known as the Buchtien's orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, featuring showy, often fragrant blossoms that attract specific pollinators. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, relying on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its small, tuberous roots are adapted to store moisture and nutrients in its nutrient-poor habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
