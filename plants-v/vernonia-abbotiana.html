Vernonia abbotiana

Vernonia abbotiana, commonly known as Abbot's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Vernonia abbotiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
