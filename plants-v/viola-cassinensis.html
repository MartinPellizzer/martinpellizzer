Viola cassinensis

Viola cassinensis, commonly known as the Cassin's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Viola cassinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
