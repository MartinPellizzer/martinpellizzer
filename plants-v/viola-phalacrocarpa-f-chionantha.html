Viola phalacrocarpa f. chionantha

Viola phalacrocarpa f. chionantha, commonly known as the snow viola, is a rare and frost-tolerant variant of the viola genus.

It features pale blue to white flowers with a distinctive violet-tinged center, which bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This frost-resistant form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
