Viola yamatsutae

Viola yamatsutae, commonly known as the Japanese violet, is a delicate flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a touch of color to its natural habitat.

Viola yamatsutae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
