Vincentia samoensis

Vincentia samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits make it a notable species in its native habitat.
