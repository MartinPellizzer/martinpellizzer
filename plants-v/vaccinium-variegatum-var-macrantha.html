Vaccinium variegatum var. macrantha

Vaccinium variegatum var. macrantha, commonly known as the variegated blueberry, is a variant of the trailing blueberry species.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically a deep blue or purple color when mature. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible fruits in suitable climates.
