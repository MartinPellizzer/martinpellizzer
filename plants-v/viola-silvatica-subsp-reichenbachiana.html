Viola silvatica subsp. reichenbachiana

Viola silvatica subsp.

reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Reichenbach's violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe. It typically grows in woodland and shady areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
