Voandzeia subterranea f. spontanea

Voandzeia subterranea f. spontanea, commonly known as the wild cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture and as a source of protein-rich seeds.
