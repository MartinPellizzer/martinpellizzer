Viridantha

Viridantha, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the East, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its striking pink to purple flowers emerge from the water, symbolizing purity and spiritual awakening. The plant has a thick, fibrous stem that supports its heavy blooms and aids in anchoring it in muddy substrates. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Viridantha plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms and contributing to water filtration.
