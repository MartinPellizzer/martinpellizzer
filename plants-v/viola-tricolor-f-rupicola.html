Viola tricolor f. rupicola

Viola tricolor f. rupicola, commonly known as the rock viola, is a compact, low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet, white, and yellow. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats. Its compact form and colorful blooms make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil conditions.
