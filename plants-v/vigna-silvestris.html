Vigna silvestris

Vigna silvestris, commonly known as wild pigeon pea, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful cover crop. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed or secondary growth habitats.

Vigna silvestris is also appreciated for its nutritional value, as its seeds are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary applications.
