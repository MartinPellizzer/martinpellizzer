Viola vivariensis

Viola vivariensis, commonly known as the marsh violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as marshes, meadows, and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and it produces small, showy flowers with five petals. It belongs to the Violaceae family, which includes other violets and related species.

Viola vivariensis is often found in areas with rich, loamy soil and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.
