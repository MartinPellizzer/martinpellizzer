Verbascum floccosum f. manlevense

Verbascum floccosum f. manlevense, commonly known as the Manleven's mullein, is a horticultural form of the common mullein.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is typically found in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.

This cultivar is valued for its compact growth habit and extended flowering period.
