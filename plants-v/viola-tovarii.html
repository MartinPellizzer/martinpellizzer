Viola tovarii

Viola tovarii, commonly known as the Tovar's violet, is a species of flowering plant in the Violaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico, where it thrives in rocky and woodland habitats. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Viola tovarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
