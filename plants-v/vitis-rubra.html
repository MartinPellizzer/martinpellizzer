Vitis rubra

Vitis rubra, commonly known as red currant, is a deciduous vine native to Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe, though some varieties may have black or white fruits. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Vitis rubra is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in jams, preserves, and culinary applications.
