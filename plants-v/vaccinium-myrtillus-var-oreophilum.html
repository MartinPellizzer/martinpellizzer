Vaccinium myrtillus var. oreophilum

Vaccinium myrtillus var. oreophilum, commonly known as the alpine blueberry, is a variant of the common bilberry.

It is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy surface, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

This variety is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
