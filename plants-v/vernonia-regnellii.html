Vernonia regnellii

Vernonia regnellii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish or purplish in color.

Vernonia regnellii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
