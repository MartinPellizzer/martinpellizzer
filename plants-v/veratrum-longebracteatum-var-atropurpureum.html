Veratrum longebracteatum var. atropurpureum

Veratrum longebracteatum var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-bracted white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are large, broad, and often have a reddish or purplish hue on the underside, contributing to its varietal name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
