Valantia hirsuta

Valantia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy valantia, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical or subtropical regions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Valantia hirsuta is considered a rare and endangered species.
