Viburnum furcatum var. melanophyllum

Viburnum furcatum var. melanophyllum, commonly known as the black-berried viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple to black berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
