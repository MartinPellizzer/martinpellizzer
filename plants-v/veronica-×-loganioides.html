Veronica × loganioides

Veronica × loganioides, commonly known as the blueweed or common blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Veronica × loganioides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various conditions.
