Vernonia hispidula

Vernonia hispidula, commonly known as rough vervain, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its hairy, rough-textured leaves and tall, slender stems are adapted to thrive in humid environments.

Vernonia hispidula produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.
