Vincetoxicum pisidicum

Vincetoxicum pisidicum, commonly known as the Caucasian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

Vincetoxicum pisidicum thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.
