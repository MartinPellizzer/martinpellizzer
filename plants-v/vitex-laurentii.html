Vitex laurentii

Vitex laurentii, commonly known as the sweet woodruff, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in woodland and hedgerow environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers. Its aromatic foliage has been historically used in herbal remedies and as a flavoring agent.

Vitex laurentii is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
