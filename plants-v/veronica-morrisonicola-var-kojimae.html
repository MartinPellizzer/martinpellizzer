Veronica morrisonicola var. kojimae

Veronica morrisonicola var. kojimae, commonly known as the Kojima's speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It features slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground, with small, blue to violet flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
