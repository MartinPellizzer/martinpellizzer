Viburnum atrocyaneum var. puberulum

Viburnum atrocyaneum var. puberulum, commonly known as the silky viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The leaves are typically ovate with a silvery-gray underside, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
