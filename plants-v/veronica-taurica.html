Veronica taurica

Veronica taurica, commonly known as the Tauric speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features slender stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Veronica taurica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
