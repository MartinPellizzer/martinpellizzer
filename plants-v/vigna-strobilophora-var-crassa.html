Vigna strobilophora var. crassa

Vigna strobilophora var. crassa, commonly known as the thick-fruit cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its robust, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces large, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its seeds and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional benefits in agricultural systems.
