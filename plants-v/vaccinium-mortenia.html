Vaccinium mortenia

Vaccinium mortenia, commonly known as the red evergreen huckleberry, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and cranberries. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are typically broad and oval, with a leathery texture, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Vaccinium mortenia plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
