Vaccinium corymbosum

Vaccinium corymbosum, commonly known as blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamin C. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Vaccinium corymbosum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
