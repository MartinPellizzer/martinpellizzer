Veronica libaudii

Veronica libaudii, commonly known as the alpine speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found at elevations above 1,000 meters. The plant features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern to conserve moisture.

Veronica libaudii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in alpine ecosystems.
