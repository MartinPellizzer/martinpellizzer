Viola tricolor proles albuquerqueana

Viola tricolor proles albuquerqueana, commonly known as the Albuquerque violet, is a rare and endemic species native to New Mexico.

This flowering plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and violet-pink flowers. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. The plant is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
