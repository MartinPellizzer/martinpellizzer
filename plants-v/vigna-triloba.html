Vigna triloba

Vigna triloba, commonly known as the sickle bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and elongated, curved pods that contain small, yellowish seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale purple or white, and it is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

Vigna triloba is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
