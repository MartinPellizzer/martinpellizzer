Viola pallens f. alba

Viola pallens f. alba, commonly known as the white violet, is a botanical variant of the common violet, characterized by its pale or white petals.

This plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. The flowers are small, with five petals, and the leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive violet underside.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
