Vaccinium buxoides

Vaccinium buxoides, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, similar to other Vaccinium species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
