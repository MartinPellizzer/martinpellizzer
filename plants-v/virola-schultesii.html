Virola schultesii

Virola schultesii, commonly known as the sacred tree or caapi, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myristicaceae family and is characterized by its thick, bark-like outer layer and smooth, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is particularly noted for its role in traditional Amazonian medicine and its use in the preparation of ayahuasca.

Its botanical features make it a significant species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
