Vernonia araripensis

Vernonia araripensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces showy, yellow to orange flower clusters that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Vernonia araripensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
