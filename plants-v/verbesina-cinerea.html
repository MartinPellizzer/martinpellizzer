Verbesina cinerea

Verbesina cinerea, commonly known as pale coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features hairy, gray-green leaves with a distinctive fuzzy texture. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Verbesina cinerea is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
