Vernonia stuebelii

Vernonia stuebelii, commonly known as Stuebel's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, silver-white hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its most striking feature is its dense clusters of blue to purple flower heads, which bloom in late summer.

Vernonia stuebelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
