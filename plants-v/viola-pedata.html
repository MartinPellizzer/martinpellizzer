Viola pedata

Viola pedata, commonly known as the showy viola, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with yellow markings, which bloom in early spring. Viola pedata thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
