Vigna strobilophora var. buseri

Vigna strobilophora var. buseri, commonly known as the Buser's cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its protein-rich seeds, which are used in various traditional cuisines.

Additionally, it has potential as a forage crop due to its high biomass production.
