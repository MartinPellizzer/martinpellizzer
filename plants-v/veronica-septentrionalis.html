Veronica septentrionalis

Veronica septentrionalis, commonly known as northern speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Veronica septentrionalis is adapted to moist, rocky soils and is often found in stream banks and wet meadows.
