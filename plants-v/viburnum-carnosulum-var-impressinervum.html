Viburnum carnosulum var. impressinervum

Viburnum carnosulum var. impressinervum, commonly known as the velvetleaf viburnum, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often reddish-brown underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm climates.
