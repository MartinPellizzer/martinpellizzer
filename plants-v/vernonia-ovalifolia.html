Vernonia ovalifolia

Vernonia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves and produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant and visually striking plants.
