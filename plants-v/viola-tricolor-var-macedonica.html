Viola tricolor var. macedonica

Viola tricolor var. macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian violet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of violet, white, and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
