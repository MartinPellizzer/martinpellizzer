Vicatia coniifolia

Vicatia coniifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a rhizomatous stem system that helps it spread vegetatively.

Vicatia coniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle blooms.
