Verbascum × myriocarpoides

Verbascum × myriocarpoides, commonly known as the hybrid mullein, is a perennial plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which attract various pollinators. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
