Viviania rosea var. grandifolia

Viviania rosea var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant swamp mallow, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems.
