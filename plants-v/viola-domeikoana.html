Viola domeikoana

Viola domeikoana, commonly known as the Armenian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Viola domeikoana is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
