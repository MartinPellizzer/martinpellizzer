Veronica tenuifolia subsp. tenuifolia

Veronica tenuifolia subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, often prostrate stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
