Vachellia sieberiana var. woodii

Vachellia sieberiana var. woodii, commonly known as the Wood's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves that are often shed during dry seasons, a feature adapted to conserve water. Its woody stems are used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.

The species is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
