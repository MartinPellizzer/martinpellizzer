Veronica teucrium var. satureifolia

Veronica teucrium var. satureifolia, commonly known as the purple speedwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
