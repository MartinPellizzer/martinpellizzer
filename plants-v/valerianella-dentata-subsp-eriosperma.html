Valerianella dentata subsp. eriosperma

Valerianella dentata subsp.

eriosperma, commonly known as the hairy valerian, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its aromatic foliage has been used traditionally in herbal remedies for its calming properties.
