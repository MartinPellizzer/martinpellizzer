Viola pedata subsp. pedata

Viola pedata subsp.

pedata, commonly known as the purple violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of violet-purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and butterflies, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
