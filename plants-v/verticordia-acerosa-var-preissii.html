Verticordia acerosa var. preissii

Verticordia acerosa var. preissii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal and Mediterranean climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
