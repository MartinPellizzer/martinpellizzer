Vernonia purpurata

Vernonia purpurata, commonly known as the purple ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of small, lavender-pink to purple flowers. The plant produces rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
