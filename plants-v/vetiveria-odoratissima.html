Vetiveria odoratissima

Vetiveria odoratissima, commonly known as vetiver, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and dense, fibrous root system that is highly valued for its ability to stabilize soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its essential oil, extracted from the roots, is widely used in perfumery and aromatherapy.

Vetiver is also cultivated for its ornamental value and as a natural remedy in traditional medicine.
