Viola praemorsa subsp. flavovirens

Viola praemorsa subsp.

flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellow-green hue, and its flowers are typically violet or purple with yellow markings.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
