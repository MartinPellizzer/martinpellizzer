Viola alata subsp. verecunda

Viola alata subsp.

verecunda, commonly known as the purple violets, is a member of the Violaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America. This subspecies is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically borne on short pedicels.

The leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy surface, and the plant often grows in moist, shaded environments. It is a perennial herb that thrives in cool climates and is often found in forest understories.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and flowering plants.
