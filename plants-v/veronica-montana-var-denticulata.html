Veronica montana var. denticulata

Veronica montana var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed speedwell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and toothed, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
