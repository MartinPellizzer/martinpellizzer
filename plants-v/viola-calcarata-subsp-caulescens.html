Viola calcarata subsp. caulescens

Viola calcarata subsp.

caulescens, commonly known as the rock viol, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, with heart-shaped leaves that have a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are violet or purple, with five petals and a characteristic spur at the back.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
