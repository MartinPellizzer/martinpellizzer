Veronica austriaca subsp. tenuifolia

Veronica austriaca subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Europe. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, shade-tolerant plant.
