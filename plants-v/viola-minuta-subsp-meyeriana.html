Viola minuta subsp. meyeriana

Viola minuta subsp.

meyeriana, commonly known as the Meyer's violet, is a small flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers. It is a perennial herb with a compact growth habit, often reaching heights of less than 10 centimeters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
