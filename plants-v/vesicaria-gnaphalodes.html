Vesicaria gnaphalodes

Vesicaria gnaphalodes, commonly known as the woolly bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers. The plant is an epiphytic species, often growing on trees or rocks in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are adapted for water retention, and it possesses specialized structures for capturing small aquatic organisms.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological niche within its habitat.
