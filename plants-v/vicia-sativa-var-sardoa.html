Vicia sativa var. sardoa

Vicia sativa var. sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by slender, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
