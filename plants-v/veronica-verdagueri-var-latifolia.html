Veronica verdagueri var. latifolia

Veronica verdagueri var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved speedwell, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite pairs of ovate to lanceolate leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers, typically arranged in racemes, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
