Vaccinium coelorum

Vaccinium coelorum, commonly known as the blueberry shrub, is a species of evergreen shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like heather and cranberry. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Vaccinium coelorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
