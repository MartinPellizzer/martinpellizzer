Vignea × ploettneriana

Vignea × ploettneriana, commonly known as the hybrid passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that often feature a crown-like structure at the base. The plant produces tendrils that aid in its climbing habit, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Vignea × ploettneriana thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
