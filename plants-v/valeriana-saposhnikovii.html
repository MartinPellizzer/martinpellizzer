Valeriana saposhnikovii

Valeriana saposhnikovii, commonly known as Chinese valerian, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes and roots are used in traditional medicine for their sedative and calming properties.

Valeriana saposhnikovii is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its showy blossoms and fragrant foliage.
