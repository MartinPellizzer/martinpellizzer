Vitis sicyoides var. gardneri

Vitis sicyoides var. gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's muscadine, is a rare variant of the muscadine grape.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its large, thick-skinned berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It produces compound leaves with three to five lobes and has a vigorous growth habit.

This variety is valued for its potential in horticulture and its unique flavor profile.
