Vernonia psilophylla var. megocephala

Vernonia psilophylla var. megocephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant landscapes.
